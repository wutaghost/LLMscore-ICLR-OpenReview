009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            User-level privacy is important in distributed systems. Previous research primarily
012            focuses on the central model, while the local models have received much less
013            attention. Under the central model, user-level DP is strictly stronger than the
014            item-level one. However, under the local model, the relationship between user-
015            level and item-level LDP becomes more complex, thus the analysis is crucially
016            different. In this paper, we first analyze the mean estimation problem and then
017            apply it to stochastic optimization, classification, and regression. In particular, we
018
               propose adaptive strategies to achieve optimal performance at all privacy levels.
               Moreover, we also obtain information-theoretic lower bounds, which show that
019
               the proposed methods are minimax optimal up to logarithmic factors. Unlike the
020
               central DP model, where user-level DP always leads to slower convergence, our
021            result shows that under the local model, the convergence rates are nearly the same
022            between user-level and item-level cases for distributions with bounded support. For
023            heavy-tailed distributions, the user-level rate is even faster than the item-level one.

026   1   I NTRODUCTION
027
028   Differential privacy (DP) (Dwork et al., 2006) is one of the mainstream schemes for privacy protection.
029   The traditional DP framework is item-level, which focuses on the privacy of each sample (Dwork
030   et al., 2014). However, in many real-world scenarios such as federated learning (Kairouz et al.,
031   2021; Geyer et al., 2017; McMahan et al., 2018; Wang et al., 2019; Wei et al., 2020; 2021; Huang
032   et al., 2023), each user provides multiple samples, which need to be treated as a whole for privacy
033
      protection. Therefore, in recent years, user-level differential privacy has emerged and has received
      widespread attention from researchers (Liu et al., 2020; Levy et al., 2021; Ghazi et al., 2021; 2023;
034
      Liu & Asi, 2024; Acharya et al., 2023; Zhao et al., 2024).
035
036   Existing research on user-level DP mainly focuses on central models, while local models have received
037   relatively less attention. Several existing works have proposed algorithms for some learning tasks
038   under user-level ѕµ-LDP with ѕµ < 1, based on samples from bounded domains. For example, (Bassily &
039
      Sun, 2023) focus on the stochastic optimization problem, and (Ma et al., 2024) provides a new method
      for sparse linear regression. Despite such progresses, there are still some remaining challenges. The
040
      most important one is that in industrial applications, from an accuracy-first perspective, a practical
041
      LDP protocol in a real-world LDP data collection system usually requires ѕµ вЙ• 1 (Talwar et al.,
042   2024; Apple & Google, 2021). To achieve a good privacy-utility tradeoff, a fixed LDP protocol
043   is not uniformly suitable to all ѕµ, thus a unified strategy adaptive to all privacy levels is crucially
044   needed. Apart from the adaptivity to all privacy budgets, there are some other remaining problems.
045   Real-world applications often involve samples generated from heavy-tailed distributions (Ibragimov
046   et al., 2015), thus we need to handle the tails properly. Moreover, some important problems including
047   nonparametric classification and regression have not been analyzed before.
048   In this paper, we conduct a systematic study on user-level ѕµ-LDP for a wide range of statistical tasks.
049   We analyze the mean estimation problem first, including one-dimensional and multi-dimensional
050   cases. We then apply the mean estimation methods to other tasks, including stochastic optimization,
051   classification, and regression. For each task, we provide algorithms and analyze the theoretical
052   convergence rates. Moreover, we derive the information-theoretic lower bounds based on classical
053   minimax theory (Tsybakov, 2009), which shows that the newly proposed methods are minimax rate
      optimal up to a logarithm factor. The results are shown in Table 1, in which the non-private term

      The aforementioned challenges are addressed as follows. Firstly, we design algorithms that are
077
      adaptive to all privacy levels, which is especially important for multi-dimensional mean estimation.
078   For ѕµ < 1, we conduct user splitting which divides users into groups and each group is used to
079   estimate only one component. For very large ѕµ, we conduct budget splitting, which assign each
080   component with privacy budget ѕµ/d. In the medium privacy regime, we design the splitting strategy
081   carefully to achieve a smooth transition between these two extremes. Such design enables us to
082   handle all privacy levels. Moreover, for heavy-tailed distributions, we clip each sample properly and
083   achieve a good bias-variance tradeoff based on our theoretical analysis.
084   The main contributions of this paper are summarized as follows.
085
086          вАҐ For the mean estimation problem, we use a two-stage approach for d = 1. With higher
087            dimensionality, for вДУвИЮ support, our method divides users into groups, and the strategy of
088            such grouping is tailored to the privacy level ѕµ. We then use KashinвАЩs representation to
089            obtain a tight result for вДУ2 support.
090          вАҐ We apply the mean estimation to the stochastic optimization problem and derive a rate of
091            OћГ(d/(nm(ѕµ2 вИІ ѕµ)), matches the item-level bound in (Duchi et al., 2013) under the same
092            total sample size.
093
             вАҐ For nonparametric classification and regression, we divide the support into grids and apply
094            the Hadamard transform, which is shown to be optimal under user-level LDP.
095
096   In general, the results show that the user-level LDP requirement is similar or sometimes even weaker
097   than the item-level one, which is crucially different from the central model.

533   In this paper, we have conducted a theoretical study of various statistical problems under user-level
534   local differential privacy, including mean estimation, stochastic optimization, nonparametric classifi-
535   cation, and regression. For each problem, we have proposed algorithms and provided information-
536

      total sample sizes, the errors under user-level and item-level ѕµ-LDP are nearly of the same order.
