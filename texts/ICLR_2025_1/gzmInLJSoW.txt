                                                  A BSTRACT
008
009
               Prompting and context-based fine-tuning methods, which we call Prefix Learning,
010
               have been proposed to enhance the performance of language models on various
011            downstream tasks. They are empirically efficient and effective, matching the
012            performance of full parameter fine-tuning, but the theoretical understandings are
013            limited. In this paper, we aim to address this limitation by studying their ability
014            from the perspective of prefix length. In particular, we provide a convergence
015            guarantee for training an ultra-long prefix in a stylized setting using the Neural
016            Tangent Kernel (NTK) framework. Based on this strong theoretical guarantee, we
017            design and implement an algorithm that only needs to introduce and fine-tune a
018            few extra trainable parameters instead of an infinite-long prefix in each layer of a
019
               transformer, and can approximate the prefix attention to a guaranteed polynomial-
               small error. Preliminary experimental results on vision, natural language, and math
020
               data show that our method achieves superior or competitive performance compared
021
               to existing methods like full parameters fine-tuning, P-Tuning V2, and LoRA. This
022            demonstrates our method is promising for parameter-efficient fine-tuning.

025   1   I NTRODUCTION
026
027   The advent of Large Language Models (LLMs) and Vision LLMs (vLLMs) has significantly advanced
028   the field of Artificial Intelligence (AI), with prominent examples like ChatGPT (ChatGPT, 2022),
029   GPT-4 (Achiam et al., 2023; Bubeck et al., 2023), Claude (Claude-3, 2024), Llama (Touvron et al.,
030   2023a;b), Gemini (Gemini, 2024), ViT (Dosovitskiy et al., 2020), DETR (Carion et al., 2020), BLIP
031   (Li et al., 2022; 2023a), CLIP (Radford et al., 2021). They have exhibited impressive performances
032   across a spectrum of tasks, encompassing chat systems (Maaz et al., 2023; Xu et al., 2023a; Zheng
033
      et al., 2024), text-to-image conversion (Qiao et al., 2019; Frolov et al., 2021; Zhang et al., 2023),
      AI mathematical inference (Hendrycks et al., 2020; Yu et al., 2023a; Yao et al., 2023), and many
034
      more. However, despite these advancements, pre-existing LLMs often fall short in specialized
035
      domains that demand a deeper understanding of professional knowledge (Tajbakhsh et al., 2016;
036   Devlin et al., 2018; Gururangan et al., 2020; Hu et al., 2021; Sun, 2023; Kasneci et al., 2023; Li
037   et al., 2023b; Thirunavukarasu et al., 2023; Li et al., 2024b; Wang et al., 2024). This has led to
038   the development of fine-tuning/adaptation (Shi et al., 2022; Xu et al., 2023b; Shi et al., 2024a)
039   methodologies aimed at enhancing the proficiency of these models in executing more specialized
040   tasks (Mangrulkar et al., 2022). Several notable contributions in this area, such as LoRA (Low-Rank
041   Adaptation, Hu et al. (2021)), P-Tuning (Liu et al., 2021b; 2023), and (IA)3 (Liu et al., 2022), have
042   displayed performances rivaling those of full-parameter fine-tuning techniques. This underscores the
043   potential of these fine-tuning strategies to further refine the capabilities of Large Language Models.
044   Among the methods proposed, most context-based fine-tuning methods, e.g., Prompt-Tuning (Lester
045   et al., 2021; Liu et al., 2021a), Prefix-Tuning (Li & Liang, 2021), P-Tuning (Liu et al., 2023; 2021b),
046   use enhanced input sequences (or virtual prompt, a.k.a soft prompt) to optimize their model outputs.
047   These methods are gaining significant interest due to their ease of implementation across various
048   model architectures, and also prevention of catastrophic forgetting with static pre-trained parameters
049   (Wang et al., 2023b; Sohn et al., 2023; Yang et al., 2024). We call the above approaches Prefix
050
      Learning since they improve the performance by optimizing a prefix matrix added to the input in
      each attention layer of the LLMs (see detailed formulation in Section 2).
051
052   Despite its wide use and strong empirical performance, we still have a limited understanding of
053   why and how prefix learning operates (Wang et al., 2023a; Petrov et al., 2024a;b). One common
      phenomenon in prior empirical studies is that prefix learning results in better downstream performance

077   The answer to this cannot be directly figured out via empirical evaluations, since it is impractical to
078   implement networks with ultra-long or even infinite prefixes in practice. Therefore, we first perform
079   a theoretical analysis of prefix learning. We study the optimization of ultra-long prefix learning via
080   the Neural Tangent Kernel (NTK) technique (Jacot et al., 2018), which has been used for analyzing
081   overparameterized networks and thus is suitable for ultra-long prefix learning. Based on the insights
082   gained from the analysis, we propose our method, NTK-attention, which reparameterizes prefix
083

      also conduct some empirical evaluations of our method on vision, natural language understanding,
084

086   Specifically, we have made the following contributions:
087
088   • We first perform a theoretical analysis of optimizing an ultra-long prefix in a stylized attention
089
        network; see Section 3. We consider a simplified attention network, and show that when prefix

090
        can be analyzed via NTK, which leads to our theoretical guarantee of convergence to small errors.
091

      Prefix Learning. Prefix Learning (Lester et al., 2021; Ding et al., 2021; Wang et al., 2022b; Zhou
117   et al., 2022; Liu et al., 2021a; Petrov et al., 2024a; Wu et al., 2023), including Prompt-Tuning (Lester
118   et al., 2021), Prefix-Tuning (Li & Liang, 2021), P-Tuning (Liu et al., 2023; 2021b), Reweighted
119   In-Context Learning (RICL) (Chu et al., 2023) and so on, is proposed to enhance the performance
120   of language models on the downstream tasks and to reduce the costs of computational resources of
121   fine-tuning the whole model. Those methods optimize task-specific prompts for downstream task
122   improvement. On the other hand, besides the Parameter-Efficient-Fine-Tuning (PEFT) approaches
123   (Mangrulkar et al., 2022) we mentioned above, Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) (Lewis et al.,
124   2020; Jiang et al., 2023; Gao et al., 2023b) and Chain-of-Thought (CoT) prompting (Wei et al., 2022b;
125
      Wang et al., 2022a; Fu et al., 2022) can also be considered as prefix learning. We conclude all these
      works to an optimization problem that improves the prefix based on task-specific measurements.
126
127   Neural Tangent Kernel. Neural Tangent Kernel (NTK) (Jacot et al., 2018) studies the gradient flow
128   of neural networks in the training process. They showed neural networks are equivalent to Gaussian
129   processes in the infinite-width limit at initialization. A bunch of works has explained the strong
130   performance and the learning ability of neural networks at over-parameterization, such as (Li & Liang,
131
      2018; Du et al., 2019; Song & Yang, 2019; Allen-Zhu et al., 2019; Wei et al., 2019; Bietti & Mairal,
      2019; Lee et al., 2020; Chizat & Bach, 2020; Shi et al., 2021; Zhou et al., 2021; Seleznova & Kutyniok,
132
      2022; Gao et al., 2023a; Li et al., 2024a; Shi et al., 2024c) and many more. Furthermore, Arora et al.
133
      (2019) gave the first exact algorithm on computing Convolutional NTK (CNTK), Alemohammad
134   et al. (2020) proposed Recurrent NTK, and Hron et al. (2020) presented infinite attention via NNGP
135   and NTK for attention networks. These works have demonstrated advanced performance by utilizing
136   NTK in different neural network architectures. In particular, Malladi et al. (2023) have studied the
137   training dynamic of fine-tuning LLMs via NTK and confirmed the efficiency of such methods.
138
      Theory of Understanding Large Language Models. Since the complicated transformer-based
139
      architecture and stochastic optimization process of LLMs lead the study of their behaviors to be a
140   challenge, analyzing LLMs through some theoretical guarantee helps in providing insights to improve
141   and design the next generation of AI systems. This topic includes efficient LLMs (Alman & Song,
142   2023; 2024a;b; Han et al., 2024; Kacham et al., 2023; Addanki et al., 2023; Deng et al., 2024; Shi
143   et al., 2024b), optimization of LLMs (Deng et al., 2023; Li et al., 2024a), white-box transformers (Yu
144   et al., 2023b;c; Ferrando et al., 2024; Pai et al., 2024), analysis of emergent abilities of LLMs (Brown
145   et al., 2020; Wei et al., 2022a; Allen-Zhu & Li, 2023a;b;c; 2024), etc. Especially, (Alman & Song,
146   2023) proved that the hardness of fast attention can be achieved within n1+o(1) times executions,
147   one effective way is to construct a high-order polynomial mapping based on Taylor expansion of the
148   exponential function exp(·), and it inspired the design of our NTK-Attention method.

178   Scaling Prefix Length. A rich line of studies (Liu et al., 2021b; Lester et al., 2021; Liu et al., 2023;
179   Reynolds & McDonell, 2021; Arora et al., 2022; Brown et al., 2020; Dong et al., 2022; Shi et al.,
180   2023; Von Oswald et al., 2023; Xu et al., 2024; Fu et al., 2022; Agarwal et al., 2024; Kaplan et al.,
181   2020; Hoffmann et al., 2022) have reported a common observation that as the prefix length increases,
182   the model’s ability to master complex skills also improves. Specifically, the performance of fine-tuned
183   models is enhanced when the prefix length grows within a certain range. A similar trend is observed
184   in prompting methods and in-context learning (ICL), where longer and more complex prompts lead
185
      to better inference abilities in LLMs, and providing more examples in ICL results in improved LLM
      performance. We summarize this as the scaling law in prefix learning: the longer the prefix length for
186
      fine-tuning, the larger dataset the model can fit, thus, the more complicated skill it can master. This
187
      motivates investigating prefix learning with long prefixes.
188
189   In this paper, we examine the implications of using a significantly large prefix length, denoted
190   as m ≫ L and m ≫ d, which is prevalent across various prompt-based methods. The primary
191
      objective of Prefix Learning is to enhance the LLMs’ outputs by identifying an advanced prefix
      during the generation process. For instance, the search for optimal example pairs to improve ICL
192
      (Nguyen & Wong, 2023) and the development of prompt engineering tailored for agent frameworks to
193
      address specific task requirements (dif, 2024) often necessitate the use of exceptionally long prefixes.
194   Moreover, given the modern application demands related to long-context scenarios, optimizing
195   previous tokens to improve next-token prediction can be framed as a prefix optimization problem.
196   Thus, a thorough investigation into the optimization of infinitely long prefixes is essential for
197   understanding the theoretical significance of the prefix matrix in LLMs.

303   4     NTK-ATTENTION : A PPROXIMATE I NFINITE -L ONG P REFIX ATTENTION
304
305   The preceding section discussed the convergence guarantee of training sufficiently long prefixes P in
306   attention networks (recall that the trainable parameter W is just P ⊤ ). This strong theoretical property
307
      inspires us to scale up the prefix length m. However, such prefix learning (Algorithm 1) necessitates
      a time complexity of O(mLd + L2 d) in each layer of the model, this is impractical due to a large m.
308
309   This section proposes an approximate algorithm to make long prefix learning practical. Our algorithm,
310   NTK-Attention, is designed to output an approximation of PrefixAttn(X, P ) (Eq. (1)) in time within
311   O(L1+o(1) ) and without using the long prefix matrix P . We present the derivation and motivation of
312   our algorithm in Section 4.1, formalize the NTK-Attention algorithm in Section 4.2, and provide an
313   approximation guarantee in Section 4.3.
314

      6     C ONCLUSION
531
532
      In this study, we illuminated the principles of prefix learning for fine-tuning when the prefix length
533
      is large. We conducted an in-depth theoretical analysis, demonstrating that when the prefix length
534   is sufficiently large, the attention network is over-parameterized, and the Neural Tangent Kernel
535   technique can be leveraged to provide a convergence guarantee of prefix learning. Based on these
536   insights, we proposed a novel efficient fine-tuning method called NTK-Attention, which approximates
537   prefix attention using two trainable parameters to replace the large prefix matrix, thus significantly
538   mitigating memory usage issues and reducing computational cost for long prefixes. We also pro-
539   vided empirical results to support our theoretical findings, demonstrating NTK-Attention’s superior
      performance on downstream tasks over baselines across natural language, math, and vision datasets.

            D       F URTHER D ISCUSSIONS
1314
1315
            Prior works (Arora et al., 2019; Alemohammad et al., 2020; Hron et al., 2020) had already given
1316
            exact algorithms for computing the extension of NTK to neural nets and conducted experiments
1317        showing enhanced performance from adding NTK into models, while in this paper, our contributions
1318        are not limited to this. Our theory about NTK of attention with the infinite-long prefix provides more
1319        insights. We clarify this further in the following.
1320
            Can LLMs master any advanced reasoning skill through self-planning and prompting? We
1321
            will answer that it may be possible. Since an attention network can converge on any dataset with the
1322
            infinite-long prefix, we can tell that for any advanced reasoning skill that is equivalent to training on
1323        a well-constructed dataset, there exists an ultra-long prefix matrix satisfying the training objective
1324        smaller than any positive value ϵ > 0. It’s noteworthy that this conclusion is not only suitable for
