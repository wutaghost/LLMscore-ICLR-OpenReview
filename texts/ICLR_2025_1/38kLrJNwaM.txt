009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011
               Offline preference-based reinforcement learning (PbRL) provides an effective way
012
               to overcome the challenges of designing reward and the high costs of online in-
013
               teraction. However, since labeling preference needs real-time human feedback,
014            acquiring sufficient preference labels is challenging. To solve this, this paper
015            proposes a offLine prEference-bAsed RL with high Sample Efficiency (LEASE)
016            algorithm, where a learned transition model is leveraged to generate unlabeled
017            preference data. Considering the pretrained reward model may generate incorrect
018            labels for unlabeled data, we design an uncertainty-aware mechanism to ensure the
019            performance of reward model, where only high confidence and low variance data
020            are selected. Moreover, we provide the generalization bound of reward model to
021            analyze the factors influencing reward accuracy, and demonstrate that the policy
022
               learned by LEASE has theoretical improvement guarantee. The developed the-
               ory is based on state-action pair, which can be easily combined with other offline
023
               algorithms. The experimental results show that LEASE can achieve comparable
024
               performance to baseline under fewer preference data without online interaction.

028   1   I NTRODUCTION
029
030   Reinforcement learning (RL) has been widely applied in robot control (Haarnoja et al., 2024; Ra-
031   dosavovic et al., 2024) and healthcare (Li et al., 2021; Wu et al., 2022) fields. However, in real-world
032   scenarios, RL faces two serious challenges: 1) It is dangerous for agent to interact directly with en-
033   vironment, especially human-in-loop control (Levine et al., 2020); 2) It is difficult to design a quan-
034   titative reward function to accurately reflect the human intention or preference (Hadfield-Menell
035   et al., 2017). Many researches have been explored to solve the above challenges.
036   Offline RL, learning policy from previous collected dataset without interaction with environ-
037   ment, has become an effective way for challenge 1 (Kumar et al., 2020; Kostrikov et al., 2021a).
038   Preference-based RL (PbRL), which learns the reward function from human preference data without
039   the demand for tedious hand-engineered reward design (Christiano et al., 2017), has been developed
040   recently for challenge 2. However, compared with data (s, a, s′ ), the collecting cost of preference
041   data is higher since it demands human real-time feedback to label preference, which often brings
042   high sample complexity (low sample efficiency) (Liang et al., 2022; Park et al., 2022).
043   Some data augmentation techniques have been proposed to solve problem of the limitation of pref-
044   erence data (Park et al., 2022; Hu et al., 2024). However, the above methods need online interaction
045   with environment and still demand large human feedback. Therefore, this paper aims to design a
046   high sample efficiency offline PbRL algorithm that can achieve comparable control performance
047   with baseline from a limited amount of preference data without interaction with environment.
048   In offline PbRL setting, existing methods (Shin et al., 2021; Kim et al., 2023; Zhang et al., 2024)
049   typically involve two steps: reward learning and policy learning. However, the theoretical guarantees
050   of reward generalization and policy improvement are not provided. Therefore, this paper focuses on
051   answering the following two questions: 1) In algorithm, how to use the limited preference data to
052   learn accurate reward model and guarantee agent performance? 2) In theory, what factors affect the
053   reward generalization and what is the detailed relationship between offline RL algorithm itself, the
      reward gap and the improvement of policy performance?

      For question 2, there are very fewer algorithms for offline PbRL theory. The most relevant work
078
      is Zhan et al. (2024), where the theory of policy improvement guarantee is established. However,
079   the generalization bound for reward model is not considered, and this theory is based on the whole
080   trajectory rather than state-action pair in offline algorithms, which is not conducive to the theoretical
081   analysis of specific offline PbRL algorithms since many offline RL are based on state-action pair.

085   and provide the theory of policy improvement guarantee for offline PbRL based on state-action pair.

087   baseline under fewer preference data. The contributions are given below:
088
089   1) A novel learning framework, named LEASE, is proposed for offline PbRL, where an innovative
090      preference augmentation technique is utilized to improve sample efficiency.
091   2) An uncertainty-aware mechanism is designed for screening the generated preference data so as
092      to guarantee the stability of reward training and improve the accuracy of reward prediction.
093   3) The generalization bound of reward model and the theory of policy improvement based on state-
094      action pair are developed to analyze factors that influence reward and policy performance.
095
096   In this paper, the theoretical and experimental results show that LEASE has policy improvement
097   guarantee and can achieve superior performance under fewer preference data on D4RL benchmark.

      5.1   T HE I MPLEMENTATION D ETAILS OF LEASE
310
311
      Most offline RL algorithms are based on Eq. (1). They proposed various algorithms to solve the
312   problem of distribution shift. In offline PbRL setting, the reward is unknown for agent. The accuracy
313   of reward model directly influence the performance of policy. Moreover, the unlabeled data are
314   generated through the current policy interaction with learned transition model. Therefore, the reward
315   model and policy influence each other. In practical implementation, the policy, Q-function, reward
316   and transition model are parameterized by πθ , Qω , Rψ and Tφ respectively.
317
      Algorithm 1 gives pseudocode for LEASE. The goal of LEASE is to learn better reward model
318   Rψ from fewer labeled preference dataset Dl and learn better policy πθ from offline dataset Doffline
319   without interaction with environment. The key of LEASE is how to train reward model in policy
320   learning process. Notably, the reward model only update once instead of updating constantly in this
321   process. The reward update condition is set to when the number of unlabeled data in buffer reaches
322   the maximum buffer capacity. The update time is influenced by rollout frequency and rollout batch
323   size. The reward update condition, rollout length H and selecting mechanism f (σ1 , σ2 ) are the
      important factors for reward model. The more detailed analysis can be found in Appendix B.3.

      7   R ELATED W ORK
487
488
      The algorithm for offline PbRL. Offline PbRL eliminates the demand for interaction with environ-
489   ment and handcraft designed reward. OPAL (Shin et al., 2021) is the first algorithm that combining
490   offline RL and PbRL. PT (Kim et al., 2023) utilized transformer-based architecture to design pref-
491   erence model capable of generating non-Markovian rewards. OPPO (Kang et al., 2023) directly
492   optimized the policy in a high-level embedding space without learning a separate reward function.
493   However, the above methods demands a large amount of preference dataset. Zhang et al. (2024)
494   trains a diffusion model to achieve data augmentation, but this consumers larger training cost. Our
495   method LEASE can achieve superior performance under fewer dataset and time.
496   The theory for offline PbRL. There are few algorithms that provide theoretical guarantees for
497   offline PbRL, including the generalization bound of reward model and the guarantee of policy im-
498   provement. Zhu et al. (2023) studied offline PbRL, but the analysis are restricted to linear model.
499   Zhan et al. (2024) extended to general function approximation, but the generalization analysis of
500   reward model is not provided and the theory is based on trajectory. LEASE gives the theoretical
501   analysis for reward model, and provides the theoretical guarantee for policy based on state-action
502   pair. The theory of LEASE can be easily combined with other offline RL theory.
503   Semi-supervised learning. The goal of semi-supervised learning is using unlabeled data to improve
504   model performance when labeled data is limited. Data selection techniques is used to filter the data
505   with clean labels from a noisy dataset. Han et al. (2018) selected unlabeled data with small losses
506   from one network. Li et al. (2020) used a two-component GMM to separate the dataset into a clean
507   set and a noisy set. Rizve et al. (2021) modeled the prediction uncertainty of unlabeled data to screen
508   data. Xiao et al. (2023) selected data with high confidence as clean data. Motivated by (Rizve et al.,
509
      2021) and (Xiao et al., 2023), we trained ensemble reward model to select data with high confidence
      and low variance to guarantee the quality of unlabeled data. The more related works for this study
510
      can be found in Appendix C.2.

513   8   C ONCLUSION
514
515   This paper proposes a novel offline PbRL algorithm (LEASE) with high sample efficiency. LEASE
516   can achieve comparable performance under fewer preference dataset. By selecting high confidence
517   and low variance data, the stability and accuracy of reward model are guaranteed. Moreover, this
518   paper provides the theoretical analysis for LEASE, including generalization of reward model and
519   policy improvement guarantee. This theory can be easily connected with other offline algorithms.
520
      The theoretical and experimental results demonstrate that the data selecting mechanism f (σ0 , σ1 )
      can effectively improve performance of reward model and the performance learned by LEASE can
521
      be guaranteed under fewer preference dataset. However, there is still the gap between the true and
522
      the learned reward model. Future works can focus on how to further reduce this gap under the
523   limited preference data or how to achieve conservative estimation for state-action pairs where the
524   learned reward model predicts inaccurately.

       C.2    D ISCUSSION FOR RELATED WORKS
1266
1267   Model-free offline RL. Existing model-free algorithms typically use two approaches: policy con-
1268   straint and value regularization. The goal of policy constraint is to keep the learned policy close to
1269   the behavior policy (Kumar et al., 2019). On the other hand, value regularization methods mitigate
1270   the value overestimation for out-of-distribution (OOD) data by conservatively estimating Q values
1271   (Kumar et al., 2020) or by penalizing based on the uncertainty of the Q function (An et al., 2021).
1272   However, the core challenge for offline RL arises from limited data coverage. Model-free offline
1273
       RL algorithms can only learn policies from the offline dataset, which restricts the agent’s ability to
       explore.
1274
1275   Model-based offline RL. Model-based offline RL algorithms train a dynamics model using the
1276   offline dataset and leverage this model to enhance data coverage. However, due to the limitations
1277   of the offline dataset, there is a discrepancy between the learned dynamics model and the actual
1278   dynamics. To address this, conservatism should be integrated into the algorithms to prevent the
1279
       agent from operating in areas where the predictions of the learned dynamics model are unreliable.
       One approach is to penalize the reward based on uncertainty quantification (Yu et al., 2020; Sun
1280
       et al., 2023). Another approach is to enforce lower Q-values for data generated by the dynamics
1281
       model that are deemed imprecise (Yu et al., 2021; Liu et al., 2023).

1283   Comparison with Surf (Park et al., 2022). Surf is the PbRL method similar to LEASE using data
1284   augmentation technique. The differences between them mainly includes the below three aspects:
1285
       1) Surf belongs to online RL, but LEASE belongs to offline RL. Surf generates data through in-
       teraction with environment (simulator) while LEASE generates data through the learned transition
1286
       model; 2) Surf only uses confidence for label filtering, whereas LEASE employs both confidence and
1287
       uncertainty principles, which effectively reduce pseudo-label error; 3) LEASE provides the general
1288   theoretical framework for offline PbRL, but Surf don’t provide theoretical analysis.

1292   Actual application. In actual scenarios, especially for human-in-loop-control, such as exoskeleton
1293   robot assistance or rehabilitation, designing a high sample efficient RL algorithm is greatly signif-
1294   icant. Firstly, many rewards are difficult to describe in mathematical terms in some situation, such
1295   as the comfort of interaction. Human feedback or preference is the better way to reflect the above
       indexes. In addition, in human in-the-loop control, obtaining preference data through interaction
