011                                               A BSTRACT
012
013            Molecular interactions are a common phenomenon in physical chemistry, often re-
014            sulting in unexpected biochemical properties harmful to humans, such as drug-drug
015
               interactions. Machine learning has shown great potential for predicting these inter-
               actions rapidly and accurately. However, the complexity of molecular structures
016
               and the diversity of interactions often reduce prediction accuracy and hinder gen-
017
               eralizability. Identifying core invariant substructures (i.e., rationales) has become
018            essential to improving the model’s interpretability and generalization. Despite
019            significant progress, existing models frequently overlook the pairwise molecular
020            interaction, leading to insufficient capture of interaction dynamics. To address these
021            limitations, we propose I2Mole (Interaction-aware Invariant Molecular learning),
022            a novel framework for generalizable property prediction. I2Mole meticulously
023            models atomic interactions, such as hydrogen bonds and Van der Waals forces, by
024            first establishing indiscriminate connections between intermolecular atoms, which
025            are then refined using an improved graph information bottleneck theory tailored
026
               for merged graphs. To further enhance model generalization, we construct an envi-
               ronment codebook by environment subgraph of the merged graph. This approach
027
               not only could provide noise source for optimizing mutual information but also
028
               preserve the integrity of chemical semantic information. By comprehensively lever-
029            aging the information inherent in the merged graph, our model accurately captures
030            core substructures and significantly enhances generalization capabilities. Extensive
031            experimental validation demonstrates I2Mole’s efficacy and generalizability. The
032            implementation code is available at https://anonymous.4open/r/I2Mol-C616.

035   1   I NTRODUCTION
036
037   The molecular interaction process can generate additional physical or chemical properties when
038   two or more molecules combined Varghese & Mushrif (2019); D’Souza et al. (2011); Low et al.
039
      (2022). This phenomenon is common in the fields of physics, chemistry, and medicine etc., such as
      changes in Gibbs free energy during dissolution (i.e., solute-solvent pair) Chung et al. (2022); Fang
040
      et al. (2024); Xia et al. (2023) and synergistic or adverse reactions between drugs (i.e., drug-drug
041
      pair) Lee et al. (2023b); Klemperer (1992). Due to the complexity of molecular structures and the
042   diversity of molecular interactions, conventional modeling approaches are limited and susceptible to
043   noise, undermining prediction accuracy. Meanwhile, lacks generalizability and reliability severely
044   limits their applicability. Based on this, mining the invariant core substructures of molecules (i.e.,
045   rationale) has gradually become a consensus to enhance both interpretability and generalization like
046   the CGIB Lee et al. (2023a) and MoleOOD Yang et al. (2022b).
047
      Although current methods have attracted widespread attention in predicting the properties of molecu-
048   lar pairs, two inherent shortcomings remain underexplored. The first is Insufficiency in molecular
049   interaction modeling. Existing methods demonstrate proficiency in elucidating essential structural
050   characteristics for individual molecular models. However, when molecular interactions occur, pivotal
051   substructures may exhibit considerable variation. Taking Pseudoephedrine (PSE) and Metham-
052   phetamine (Meth) as an example (Figure 1 (a)). Both are common drugs used to induce cardiac
053   stimulation, arising from their distinct functional groups affecting neurons. The hydroxyl moiety in
      PSE participates in regulating neurotransmitter release and reuptake, while the amphetamine moiety

      6         C ONCLUSION AND F UTURE OUTLOOK
531
      In this work, we introduce I2Mole, a novel framework for precise DDI prediction. I2Mole constructs
532
      a merged drug pair graph to capture complex molecular interactions, specifically addressing the
533
      imbalance between training and testing data distributions commonly encountered in real-world drug
534   scenarios. By leveraging an enhanced information bottleneck approach, I2Mole extracts invariant
535   and rational subgraphs, ensuring consistency across diverse environments. Furthermore, we devise
536   an environment codebook based on the molecular environments in the training set. This captures
537   environmental information and integrates it into data from diverse distributions, thereby significantly
538   enhancing the model’s generalization capability. Extensive experiments validate I2Mole’s superior
539   accuracy and generalizability, facilitating the rapid identification of potential DDIs and reducing the
      risks associated with drug misuse. Limitations are discussed in Appendix I.

759   A.1    D RUG -D RUG I NTERACTION (DDI) PREDICTION
760
761
      In recent years, computational approaches, particularly employing machine learning and deep learn-
      ing methods, have emerged as indispensable tools for swiftly and economically predicting potential
762
      DDIs Ryall & Tan (2015); Jaaks et al. (2022). Initially, DDI prediction models predominantly focused
763
      on drug attribute information, assuming that similar drugs would exhibit common interactions Ryu
764   et al. (2018); Deng et al. (2020). For instance, Gottlieb et al. (2012) utilized seven types of drug
765   features to construct similarity vectors, forming a DDI prediction model based on logistic regres-
766   sion. Ferdousi et al. (2017) designed a deep neural network using drug molecular similarity vectors as
767   descriptors for predicting potential DDIs. Recently, there has been a shift towards graph-based DDI
768   prediction methodologies. Zhong et al. (2019) employed Graph Convolutional Neural Networks (GC-
769   NNs) for message aggregation and an attention-based pooling method for DDI prediction. Given that
770   the interaction between two drugs is influenced by their specific substructures and functions, recent
771   efforts have focused on substructure extraction and interaction Harrold & Zavod (2014); Fu et al.
772
      (2020). For instance, Yu et al. (2022a) utilized functional group information of drug molecules as their
      substructures, while Nyamabo et al. Nyamabo et al. (2021) introduced the Substructure-Substructure
773
      Interaction for Drug-Drug Interaction (SSI-DDI) method, employing graph attention network (GAT)
774
      layers to extract substructure representations and co-attention layers to model interactions among
775   substructures.
776
777   Despite the proficiency of existing methodologies in elucidating essential structural characteristics of
778
      individual molecular models Tang et al. (2023); Lee et al. (2023c), considerable variation in crucial
      substructures may occur during molecular interactions. Notably, while some pioneering work like
779
      DSIL-DDI Tang et al. (2023) and CMRL Lee et al. (2023c) has provided foundational insights, a
780
      noticeable gap remains in comprehensively modeling intermolecular interactions. CMRL Lee et al.
781   (2023c) innovatively incorporates conditional graph information bottleneck theory to obtain rationales,
782   simultaneously considering a second drug as a conditional factor during drug subgraph generation Lee
783   et al. (2023b). However, prevailing methodologies encounter limitations in adequately capturing
784   molecular interactions, particularly at the atomic level. Moreover, integrating a comprehensive
785   profile of interacting molecules into subgraph generation poses significant challenges, including
786   overwhelming complexity and the risk of incorporating redundant information Jia et al. (2009).
787
788
      A.2    OOD GENERALIZATION
789
790
      The susceptibility of deep neural networks to significant performance degradation under distribution
791
      shifts has spurred extensive research on out-of-distribution (OOD) generalization. In response, the
792
      invariant rationalization theory has been introduced, aiming to achieve an invariant representation
793   across diverse environments Chang et al. (2020); Rojas-Carulla et al. (2018b). This theory involves a
794   rationalization module that discerns a crucial subset within the input graph, referred to as rationale,
795   essential for prediction Ying et al. (2019); Luo et al. (2020). Subsequently, through invariant learning,
796   these rationales are exposed to diverse environments, thereby fortifying the learned representation
797   against environmental fluctuations and effectively bolstering the model’s OOD capacity. 1) suffi-
798   ciency: shows sufficient predictive power for the target, 2) invariance: contributes to equal (optimal)
799   performance for the downstream tasks across all environments. Certain methods in computer vi-
800   sion Lv et al. (2022); Zhang et al. (2020); Wang et al. (2020b) achieve OOD generalization by
801
      learning domain-invariant representations. Additionally, methods such as Shen et al. (2018); He
      et al. (2021); Shen et al. (2020) aim to achieve OOD generalization by decorrelating correlated and
802
      irrelevant features, considering the statistical correlation between these features as a major factor for
803
      distribution shifts. In terms of molecular applications, DIR Wu et al. (2022d) introduces an inventive
804   method to unveil invariant rationales by intervening in the training distribution, generating multiple
805   interventional distributions, and identifying causal rationales consistent across varied distributions.
806   Similarly, MoleOOD Yang et al. (2022a) suggests that leveraging causal data-generating invariance
807   from substructures across environments, linked to specific properties, holds promise for enhancing
808   OOD generalization. However, learning a domain-invariant representation for intermolecular in-
809   teraction remains an open problem, and current discussions on OOD issues among molecules are
      limited.
