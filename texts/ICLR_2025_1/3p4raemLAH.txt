009                                            A BSTRACT
010
011           The unauthorized generation of privacy-related and copyright-infringing
012           content using generative-AI is becoming a significant concern for society,
013           raising ethical, legal, and privacy issues that demand urgent attention.
014           Recently, machine unlearning techniques have arisen that attempt to elim-
015           inate the influence of sensitive content used during model training, but
016           they often require extensive updates in the model, reduce the utility of
017           the models for unrelated content, and/or incur substantial computational
018
              costs. In this work, we propose a novel and efficient method called Single
              Layer Unlearning Gradient (SLUG), that can unlearn targeted information
019
              by updating a single targeted layer of a model using a one-time gradient
020
              computation. We introduce two metrics: layer importance and gradient
021           alignment, to identify the appropriate layers for unlearning targeted infor-
022           mation. Our method is highly modular and enables selective removal of
023           multiple concepts from the generated outputs of widely used foundation
024           models (e.g., CLIP), generative models (e.g., Stable Diffusion) and Vision-
025           Language models. Our method shows effectiveness on a broad spectrum of
026           concepts ranging from concrete (e.g., celebrity name, intellectual property
027           figure, and object) to abstract (e.g., novel concept and artistic style). Our
028           method also exhibits state-of-the-art efficiency with effective unlearning
029
              and retention on the comprehensive benchmark UnlearnCanvas. Our code
              is available at https://anonymous.4open.science/r/SLUG-6CDF.

032   1   I NTRODUCTION
033
034   Modern machine learning models, including large language models (LLMs) (Achiam et al.,
035   2023; Leiter et al., 2024), Stable Diffusion (SD) (Salimans & Ho, 2022; Yang et al., 2023) , and
036   vision language mdoels (VLMs) (Zhang et al., 2024b; Liu et al., 2024a) leverage vast amounts
037   of data for training. While these large unsupervised datasets enhance performance under
038
      scaling law (Kaplan et al., 2020), they also raise serious data privacy and legal compliance
      (gdp, 2016; Thiel, 2023) concerns as sensitive, unsafe, and unwanted data can influence the
039
      trained models (Thiel, 2023). Completely abandoning trained model weights and re-training
040
      large models from scratch using scrutinized dataset is prohibitively expensive and wasteful.
041   Machine unlearning (Cao & Yang, 2015; Nguyen et al., 2022) is an attractive alternative,
042   which refers to a broad set of techniques designed to reverse the learning process, with
043   the specific aim to efficiently remove targeted information from a trained model without
044   re-training the model from scratch.
045
      Machine unlearning has three main objectives: (1) Low computational cost, as the naïve
046
      approach of re-training models usually achieves the best result (exact unlearning) at the
047   expense of large computational cost. (2) Effective unlearning, to ensure that the model
048   forgets the intended data completely. (3) Utility retention, maintaining the original model
049   performance, in terms of accuracy and utility on data/tasks unrelated to the intended
050   unlearning data. Current unlearning methods often fall short of meeting all these objectives
051   simultaneously. Traditional approaches like fine-tuning (FT) (Warnecke et al., 2023) and
052   gradient ascent (GA) (Thudi et al., 2022) struggle to balance effective forgetting with utility
053   preservation. More recent techniques such as saliency unlearning (SalUn) (Fan et al., 2024)
      and selective synaptic dampening (SSD) (Foster et al., 2024) attempt to address this by

076   iterative updates over the model parameters, resulting in high computational costs (Fan
077   et al., 2024). Second, the significant weights targeted for updates are often spread throughout

081   In this paper, we propose a novel and efficient method for targeted unlearning, namely
082   Single Layer Unlearning Gradient (SLUG). We push the boundaries of efficiency as our
083   algorithm identifies and updates a single layer using a single gradient computation to

086   gradients of forget and retain losses with respect to the model weights using a designated
087

      concepts that are targeted to be removed from, and retained in the model, respectively. Based
088
      on these gradients, we introduce two metrics — layer importance and gradient alignment,
089
      to identify the appropriate layers for unlearning targeted concepts. To determine a suitable
090   step size for model weight updates, SLUG employs binary search along the direction
091   of forget gradients. We demonstrate that SLUG outperforms state-of-the-art methods in
092   unlearning models involving CLIP, and SD, across various tasks and architectures. We
093   also evaluate SLUG on a comprehensive unlearning benchmark UnlearnCanvas Zhang et al.
094   (2024c), showcase its superiority in efficiency and balancing trade-off between unlearning
095   targeted concept and retaining model utility. In addition to its efficiency and effectiveness,
096   our methods offers higher modularity and better interpretability compared to Fan et al.
097   (2024); Foster et al. (2024). SLUG precisely identifies layers associated with distinct concepts,
098
      which provides insights into the features learned by different layers and their functionalities,
      offering generalized guidance for new tasks and model architectures design.

102   2.1    M ACHINE UNLEARNING PRELIMINARIES
103
104   The goal of machine unlearning is to remove the influence of a specific subset of training
105   data, Df ⊂ D, on a pre-trained model Fθ ( D ) with parameters θ. The challenge is to make
106   this process more efficient than re-training the model on the retain set Dr = D \ Df . The
107   unlearning algorithm U should produce an unlearned model Fθf = U ( Fθ ( D ), D, Df ) that is
      functionally equivalent to a model retrained only on Dr , i.e., Fθr ( Dr ). We can formulate the

124   and lack interpretability. This motivates us to develop a hyperparameter-free, interpretable
125   method.

      3     S INGLE LAYER UNLEARNING GRADIENT (SLUG)
163
164   Our proposed approach SLUG performs three main steps using given unlearning query with
165   retain and forget sets: (1) calculate one-time gradients for the forget and retain losses; (2)
166   identify a single layer with high importance to the forget set and low relevance to the retain
167   set; (3) update the targeted layer along a linear path using one-time calculated gradient. The
168   framework is illustrated in Figure 1. Our approach improves the state-of-the-art along three
169   axes: (1) low computational cost, (2) effective unlearning, and (3) high retention of general
170   utility.
171   3.1   L AYER IDENTIFICATION
172
173   We are inspired by how different layers in deep networks learn distinct features; early layers
174   capture basic patterns like edges, while later layers focus on more specific details (Zeiler &
175   Fergus, 2014; Olah et al., 2017; Ghiasi et al., 2022). To efficiently unlearn, we aim to modify
176   only those layers that directly hold the information related to the unlearning task, avoiding
177   changes to layers processing abstract features unrelated to the data to be forgotten. Our
178
      goal is to identify the layers most critical to unlearning while preserving the model’s overall
      functionality. We achieve this by performing unlearning within the “nullspace” of the retain
179
      set, focusing on layers that minimally impact retained data performance while effectively
180
      removing the targeted features. This approach improves the precision of unlearning and
181   provides insights into how the model handles data retention and unlearning.
182
183   To measure the influence of each parameter, similar to (Aich, 2021; Foster et al., 2024), we
184
      use the Fisher information matrix(Kay, 1993; Hassibi et al., 1993; Kirkpatrick et al., 2017),
      approximated by its diagonal for simplicity:

      By harnessing effective unlearning in CLIP models, our technique can be extended to larger
256
      models built on CLIP, such as Stable Diffusion (Rombach et al., 2022; Salimans & Ho, 2022)
257
      and VLMs like LLaVA (Liu et al., 2024a; 2023).
258
259   Unlearn Stable Diffusion. Diffusion models, known for generating high-quality images
260   from text, use a text encoder (e.g., CLIP ViT-H/14 in Stable Diffusion) to embed prompts
261
      into a high-dimensional space. The text embedding guides the denoising process through
      cross-attention, starting from an initial noise x T and iteratively updating the noisy image at
262
      each step:

266   Here, xt is the noisy image at step t, zt is the noise added at step t, αt is a time-dependent
267   parameter controlling the noise balance, γt is the learning rate, e = f t (txt) is the text

      4.1   E XPERIMENT SETUP
280
281   Unlearning scenarios. We investigate three main types of unlearning scenarios for practical
282   and generalizable impact: (1) Unlearning identity information to counter privacy concerns;
283   (2) Unlearning copyrighted content for compliance with legal standards. We primarily focus
284   on large-scale multimodal models that include CLIP (Radford et al., 2021), Stable Diffusion
285   (Rombach et al., 2022), and VLMs (Liu et al., 2024a); and (3) Unlearning artistic style and
286   object concepts in UnlearnCavas Zhang et al. (2024c).
287   Models. We performed experiments on various models to demonstrate the broad appli-
288   cability of our unlearning method. For CLIP models, we used architectures ranging from
289   ViT-B-32 to EVA01-g-14, trained on LAION-400M dataset (Schuhmann et al., 2021), and
290   model weights sourced from the OpenCLIP repository (Cherti et al., 2023). For Stable
291   Diffusion models, we used the latest version from StabilityAI, which incorporates the
292   CLIP-ViT-H-14 trained on the LAION-5B dataset. For VLMs, we used the improved LLaVA-
293   v1.5 model from HuggingFace, which employs a CLIP ViT-L/14-336px model, trained by
294   OpenAI, as the visual extractor.
295   Datasets. We used publicly-available datasets to construct the forget, retain, and evaluation
296   sets. For unlearning target identities, we curated the forget set by filtering the LAION-400M
297   dataset to isolate 1,000 to 6,000 image-text pairs per identity. The retain set consists of a
298   single shard from LAION-400M, containing approximately 7,900 images (due to expiring
299   URLs). To assess unlearning effectiveness, we used the CelebA dataset (Liu et al., 2015),
300   sampling 100 frequently appearing celebrities from LAION-400M. Post-unlearning, model
301
      utility was evaluated using the ImageNet dataset for zero-shot classification. UnlearnCanvas
      Zhang et al. (2024c) was used to test unlearning of artistic styles and objects in Stable
302
      Diffusion.
303
304   Evaluation metrics. For CLIP models, we measure unlearning performance using forget
305   accuracy, defined as the zero-shot classification accuracy on unlearned content. Following
306   the standard zero-shot paradigm (Radford et al., 2021), predictions are based on the highest
307
      cosine similarity between image and text embeddings. Utility is assessed via zero-shot
      accuracy on ImageNet and CelebA. In addition to quantitative results for CLIP, we provide
308
      qualitative results from Stable Diffusion (image generation) and VLMs (question-answering)
309
      before and after unlearning. The UnlearnCanvas benchmark evaluates unlearning using
310   diverse metrics, including computation and storage efficiency.
311
312   Comparing methods. We compare with the state-of-the-art methods along with classical
313
      methods. For unlearning in CLIP models, we compare with classical fine tuning (FT)
      (Warnecke et al., 2023), gradient ascent (GA) / negative gradient (NG) (Thudi et al., 2022),
314
      and recent salient parameters based saliency unlearning (SalUn) (Fan et al., 2024), and
315
      selective synaptic dampening (SSD) (Foster et al., 2024). For unlearning in Stable Diffusion
316   models, we compare with 9 methods in Table 2 included in UnlearnCanvas.

376   Localizing layers. Our method efficiently identifies critical layers for updates, reducing
377   the search space from hundreds to just a few. Figures 2, 7, and 12 show which layers
      are selected for unlearning different identities. This is achieved by combining two key


                                                                         7
378
      metrics: layer importance, which measures how sensitive the forget loss is to changes
379   in each layer, and gradient alignment, ensuring updates minimally affect the retain set.
380   These metrics help identify Pareto-optimal layers that balance effective unlearning with
381   preserving model utility (explained further in Section 3). Our approach also reveals the
382   distinct roles of layers in different architectures. Across various identities (see Figure 7)
383   and architectures (see Figure 12), final projection layers of vision and language models are
384   often updated due to their role in transforming complex features into final predictions. We
385   also observe that the late attention layers in vision models and early attention layers in
386   language models are selected for updates. Vision transformers utilize attention mechanisms
387
      to focus on different parts of an image and aggregate contextual information from various
      spatial regions. The late attention layers in these models are closer to the output; thus,
388
      more specialized in refining and utilizing contextually rich, high-level features. In contrast,
389
      language models often employ attention mechanisms right from the early layers to capture
390   and process the sequential and contextual dependencies inherent in the textual data. Early
391   attention layers are crucial for establishing a foundational understanding of the language
392   structure, including syntax and semantics. By focusing on early layers, modifications can
393   influence the foundational processing of input text, effectively guiding the subsequent
394   layers’ interpretation and response to the content.

427   gradient for unlearning. Despite being extremely efficient, our method does not suffer from

512   5     C ONCLUSION
513
514   In this work, we introduced SLUG, an efficient machine unlearning method that requires
515   just a single gradient computation and updates only one layer of the model. SLUG enhances
516   unlearning feasibility on large models, especially with constrained hardware, while preserv-
517   ing overall model utility. Our experiments with CLIP, Stable Diffusion, and VLMs show
518   that SLUG outperforms existing methods, particularly in unrelated tasks, with minimal
519
      computational overhead. SLUG’s key innovation is its ability to identify and update only the
      most relevant layers, typically the late layers in vision models, when unlearning concepts
520
      like identities or copyrighted content.
521
522   This paper demonstrates that highly targeted, minimal interventions can be surprisingly
523   effective for concept removal, suggesting that knowledge in neural networks may be more
524   localized than previously thought. This has implications for our understanding of how
525
      information is encoded and stored in deep learning models. The ability to identify specific
      layers most relevant for particular concepts also provides new insights into the internal
526
      representations learned by different parts of neural networks. This contributes to the
527
      ongoing effort to improve the interpretability and transparency of AI systems.
528
529   Limitations. While SLUG shows clear advantages, there are limitations. Our experiments
530   focused on vision-language models, and further testing is needed to evaluate its general-
531
      izability to other architectures, such as pure language models or graph neural networks.
      Additionally, we did not extensively explore long-term stability, adversarial resistance (Goel
532
      et al., 2022), or the ability to unlearn more abstract concepts. More work is needed to ensure
533
      robustness in adversarial scenarios and over extended periods or retraining.

      A PPENDIX A         R ELATED WORK
811
812   Machine Unlearning (Cao & Yang, 2015; Nguyen et al., 2022) has recently emerged as a
813   critical area of research, driven by privacy concerns and regulatory requirements (gdp,
814   2016). Existing approaches mainly focus on a single task, like image classification (Liu
815   et al., 2024b; neu, 2023; Guo et al., 2020; Goel et al., 2022; Chien et al., 2022; Golatkar et al.,
816   2020b;a; Chundawat et al., 2023; Kurmanji et al., 2023; Jia et al., 2023; Shaik et al., 2023; Fan
817   et al., 2024; Foster et al., 2024), image generation (Li et al., 2024a; Gandikota et al., 2023;
818   Zhang et al., 2024a; Gandikota et al., 2024; Kumari et al., 2023; Li et al., 2024b; Lyu et al.,
819   2024; Wu et al., 2024; Wu & Harandi, 2024), and LLMs text generation (Yao et al., 2024; Liu
820   et al., 2024c). In this work, we propose a generic approach that is applicable to a wide
821   range of multi-modal models including CLIP (Radford et al., 2021) for zero-shot image
822   classification, stable diffusion models (Rombach et al., 2022) for text-to-image generation,
823   and vision-language models (Liu et al., 2024a) for visual question answering.
824   For text-to-image diffusion models, particularly Stable Diffusion (SD), the evolution of
825   unlearning approaches reveals increasing sophistication. Early methods such as ESD
826   (Gandikota et al., 2023) and CA (Kumari et al., 2023) focused on modifying the UNet
827   architecture through fine-tuning with negative guidance, but these approaches often re-
828   sulted in widespread parameter updates across multiple layers, potentially compromising
829   generation fidelity. More recent work has explored more targeted and efficient interventions.
830   UCE (Gandikota et al., 2024) introduced a training-free unified approach using closed-
831   form solutions for simultaneous debiasing, style erasure, and content moderation. FMN
832
      (Zhang et al., 2024a) achieved rapid concept removal through attention re-steering loss,
      redirecting generation from unwanted concepts to pretrained alternatives. SPM (Lyu et al.,
833
      2024) proposed an adapter-based approach using "concept-SemiPermeable Membranes"
834
      that can be flexibly transferred across different models without re-tuning. Other approaches
835   include EDiff (Wu et al., 2024), which formulates unlearning as a constrained optimization
836   problem to preserve model utility, and SEOT (Li et al., 2024b), which focuses on content sup-
837   pression through text embedding manipulation and inference-time optimization. Despite
838   these advances, existing methods still face challenges in balancing computational efficiency,
839   generalization ability, and preservation of model utility, which our work aims to address
840   through a principled single-layer approach.
841   Saliency-based Methods. Recent advances in machine unlearning have seen the emergence
842   of saliency-based approaches, which aim to identify and modify only the most relevant
843   parameters for concept removal. In image classification, methods like SSD (Foster et al.,
844   2024) employ synaptic importance measures to selectively dampen connections, while SalUn
845   (Fan et al., 2024) takes a simple and heuristic threshold-based approach. In text-to-image
846   generation, SalUn (Fan et al., 2024) extend its framework by replacing cross-entropy loss in
847   the unlearning objective to diffusion loss, requiring careful tuning of a gradient threshold
848   for parameter selection. Diff-quickfix (Basu et al., 2024) utilizes causal inference with
849
      CLIPSscore (Hessel et al., 2021) as a metric to pinpoint concept-salient model parameters.
      MACE (Lu et al., 2024) proposes tuning the prompt-related projection matrices of the cross-
850
      attention blocks in the UNet architecture using LoRA modules (Hu et al., 2022). Similarly,
851
      CRE (Dong et al., 2024) identifies concept-specific causal denoising time steps in UNet layers
852   and performs representation editing on selected layer outputs.
853
854   While these saliency-based methods represent the existing efforts in improving the efficiency
855
      of unlearning, their scope remains confined to specific tasks, such as image classification
      or text-to-image generation. Moreover, their parameter modifications often span multiple
856
      layers, which limits interpretability and flexibility in practical scenario. In contrast, our
857
      approach aims to extend efficient unlearning to foundation models that cover a diverse
858   range of tasks (e.g., CLIP, Stable Diffusion, and vision-language models). By restricting
859   model edits to a layer-specific scope, our framework introduces modularity to machine
860   unlearning, abstracting the process into distinct layer updates along gradient vectors for
861   tailored unlearning requests.

            A PPENDIX D                  J OINT UPDATE FOR UNLEARNING MULTIPLE IDENTITIES
1011
            We study the composite effect of our approach where we unlearn multiple tasks simulta-
1012
            neously. For instance, in the task of unlearning multiple identities, we use the gradients
1013        calculated for each identity on the original model and corresponding forget sets to identify
1014        the layers that are most significant for the respective identities, and then perform layer
1015        updates to simultaneously unlearn all of them. For joint updating, we follow the same
1016        updating scheme as described in Sec. 3. Firstly, different identities have different step size
1017        initialization from their respective gradients, and later on the step size is updated separately
1018        using binary search based on the unlearning result of the respective identity. We present our
1019        results in Figure. 8, where we successfully unlearn (a) {Elon Musk, Mark Zuckerberg}
1020        and (b) {Elon Musk, Taylor Swift}.
1021        We also investigate how the unlearning performance varies as the number of identities to be
1022        forgotten increases. The identified layers are then updated in parallel to achieve unlearning
1023        of N identities. Figure 9 demonstrate the effectiveness of our approach in unlearning N
1024        identities for different values of N. Figure 7 presents details on identifying layers associated
1025        with different identities and updating them to achieve unlearning of multiple identities at
            once.

1328        to the original pre-trained model, which indicates that the model’s original functions are
1329        highly preserved after our unlearning.
