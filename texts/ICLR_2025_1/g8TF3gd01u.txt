                                                                                      A BSTRACT
010
011
                             Artistic style, a unique medium for artists to express creativity through elements
012                          like form, color, and composition, poses a challenge for computer vision due to its
013                          intricate patterns and nuanced aesthetics. Contemporary models, often reliant on
014                          specific datasets, face limitations in their generalizability and precision in identi-
015                          fying individual artists’ styles. From an information theory perspective, we ex-
016                          amine the limitations of fine-tuning and investigate techniques to disentangle con-
017                          tent from style information. We note differences in artistic style representation
018                          between unimodal and multimodal models. As a result, we propose a plug-and-
019                          play approach designed to efficiently separate content information within Vision-
020
                             Language Models (VLMs), preserving stylistic details. Furthermore, we present
                             the WeART dataset, a large-scale art dataset with high-quality annotations, to eval-
021
                             uate the artistic style representation capabilities of models. Experimental results
022
                             show that our method improves the performance of VLMs in style retrieval tasks
023                          across several datasets. We will publicly release the proposed dataset and code.

050   Artistic style is the essence of an artist’s personalized expression, conveyed through visual elements
051   such as form, color, and composition to communicate themes and creativity. Despite advancements
052   in computer vision technology, recognizing and describing this unique style remains challenging,
053   as illustrated in Figure 1(a). Artistic style encompasses complex visual patterns, aesthetic prefer-
      ences, and unique techniques, which are difficult to capture through traditional quantitative analysis

054
      or natural language descriptions. With the development of generative models, accurately expressing
055   artistic style in tasks such as image transformation, style transfer, and editing has become crucial.
056   However, the subjectivity and personalized techniques inherent in artistic style make it challeng-
057   ing for general image representation methods to capture these subtle differences, often leading to
058   confusion between style and content.
059
      Currently, most artistic style representation models Wright & Ommer (2022); Wang et al. (2023);
060
      Somepalli et al. (2024); Zhang et al. (2019); Girdhar et al. (2023); Li et al. (2023) rely on classifica-
061   tion datasets of specific art forms for training. The effectiveness of these methods is limited by the
062   richness of the datasets, making it difficult to handle a broader range of art categories. Consequently,
063   these models have limited generalization ability and practicality when faced with unknown artistic
064   styles or categories. Moreover, current models often face challenges in fine-tuning their style repre-
065   sentations down to the granularity of individual artists, which results in challenges when it comes to
066   precisely discerning stylistic variations among works by different artists.
067   We identify noticeable differences in the representation of artistic styles between multimodal Vision-
068   Language Models (VLMs) Radford et al. (2021); Li et al. (2022); Zhai et al. (2023); Zhu et al.
069   (2023) and unimodal self-supervised modelsCaron et al. (2021); Oquab et al. (2023); Chen* et al.
070   (2021). VLMs can simultaneously capture both semantic information and stylistic features of im-
071   ages, whereas unimodal self-supervised models excel at recognizing the content representation of
072   images. Leveraging this disparity, we conduct an in-depth analysis of the overlapping areas between
073   style and content in image information representation, as well as the overlapping areas between
074   different modalities in multimodal models. Based on this analysis, we propose a visual representa-
075
      tion decoupling technique grounded in mutual information. The core of this method is to retain the
      stylistic features of the image while removing non-stylistic content information, enabling the model
076
      to focus more effectively on representing artistic style.
077
078   To evaluate the capability of existing models to represent artistic style, we introduce a new high-
079   quality dataset named WeART. This dataset contains 197,632 images from public museum col-
080   lections, artists’ public blogs, and personal portfolios, covering seven art categories and including
081
      works by 661 internationally renowned artists. Through extensive screening and strict selection cri-
      teria, WeART ensures the high-definition quality and integrity of each artwork. In terms of scale,
082
      diversity, and image quality, WeART provides valuable resources for the study of artistic style and
083
      promotes further research and technological advancements in this field.
084
085   Experimental results demonstrate that our method adheres to the principles of mutual information
086   analysis and achieves performance enhancements in style retrieval tasks, as shown in Figure 2(b),
087
      across two annotated artist datasets, WikiART Saleh & Elgammal (2015) and WeART. Notably, our
      decoupling approach is designed to be modular and flexible, ensuring compatibility with a variety
088
      of VLMs and fine-tuned style representation models. This not only enhances their artistic represen-
089
      tation capabilities but also improves the robustness and adaptability of these models.
090
091   In summary, the main contributions of this paper include:
092
             • Analyze existing artistic style and general image representation models from an informa-
093
               tion theory perspective, identifying key criteria for effective style models.
094
095          • Develop a comprehensive decoupling method that isolates content-independent artistic
096            styles from VLMs, enhancing the model’s stylistic representation.
097          • Introduce the WeART dataset, which improves scale, diversity, and image quality over
098            existing art datasets. Experiments show our method’s effectiveness on both WikiART and
099            WeART datasets, demonstrating cross-model compatibility.

      4.1   F EATURE S PACE C ONSISTENCY M APPING
230
231   Based on the analysis of style representation disentanglement in VLMs presented in the previous
232   chapter, our goal is to map three distinct types of information features into a shared feature space
233   and integrate them to effectively disentangle image style features. As depicted in Figure 4(a), we use
234   a large-scale image-text paired dataset to fine-tune the self-supervised model DINOv2. This ensures
235   that the image content representation features generated by DINOv2 match the dimensions and
236   scales of the representation features from the CLIP model, thus achieving feature space consistency.
237
      We select a subset of the CC3MChangpinyo et al. (2021) dataset filtered by the LLaVA Liu et al.
      (2024) project, containing 595,000 high-quality images and their corresponding text descriptions, as
238
      our training dataset.
239
240   Training commenced with the publicly available DINOv2 model, continuing its original training
241   and image augmentation strategies, within a student-teacher framework. The key addition to the
242   original model is an extra constraint: the student model’s output distribution must align with the
243
      feature output distribution of the CLIP model for the corresponding text descriptions. This ensures
      consistency between the output spaces of DINOv2 and CLIP and enhances the model’s focus on
244
      image content information.
245

      7      C ONCLUSION
532
533
      In this paper, we introduce a universal decoupling method for enhancing artistic style represen-
534   tation. By removing text-side representations and content-related information from single-modal
535   components within VLMs, we achieve the decoupling of artistic style. Additionally, we present
536   the WeART dataset, a large-scale collection of artworks with high-quality annotations, designed to
537   evaluate the artistic style representation capabilities of models. Experimental results demonstrate
538   that our decoupling approach improves performance in style retrieval tasks across multiple datasets.
539   For future work, we plan to incorporate additional artistic forms into our evaluation dataset and to
      explore more effective methods of combining representations.
