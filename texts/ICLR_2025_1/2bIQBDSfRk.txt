011                                              A BSTRACT
012
013            The ubiquitous Transformer architecture suffers from two main bottlenecks: 1)
014            low computational and memory efficiency, leading to suboptimal hardware uti-
015            lization, and 2) quadratic time complexity with respect to sequence length N ,
016
               making it slow and costly for large data contexts. We propose a novel DenseAtten-
               tion Network architecture, a straightforward simplification of the standard Trans-
017
               former block that addresses these issues and serves as a drop-in replacement for
018
               language modeling tasks. We eliminate memory-bound components in DenseAt-
019            tention, including Softmax, masking, one skip connection, and both LayerNorms,
020            as well as key, value, and output projection matrices, as they become redundant.
021            Despite these removals, it maintains exact N × N pairwise interactions between
022            tokens. By exploiting the associativity of matrix multiplications, DenseAttention
023            can be computed with O(N 2 d) or O(N d2 ) time and space complexity, depending
024            on the context. To handle the absence of Softmax and prevent numerical instabil-
025            ity, we introduce MaxNormActivation at both ends of the Transformer block. We
026            also devise Cosine Relative Positional Embeddings as a computationally efficient
027
               replacement for RoPE, and simple LocalAttention variations of the block to help
               the model focus on details in extremely long contexts.
028
029
               DenseAttention competes with FlashAttention in speed on small sequences and
               outperforms it by orders of magnitude on large contexts. We pre-train encoder
030
               language models on sequences up to 16K in length, which perform similarly or
031
               better than baseline BERT-large, while significantly improving speed and effi-
032            ciency. Finally, we achieve state-of-the-art on the LRA benchmark among the
033            Transformer-based architectures.

036   1   I NTRODUCTION
037
038   Transformer architecture (Vaswani et al., 2017) has become ubiquitous in neural networks across
039   many domains and modalities, such as NLP (Devlin et al., 2019), images (Dosovitskiy et al., 2021),
040   video (Arnab et al., 2021), speech recognition (Radford et al., 2022), and even tabular data (Arik &
041   Pfister, 2019)). But most notably, it’s the core component of Large Language (Touvron et al., 2023a;
042   Brown et al., 2020) and Multi-modal (Bai et al., 2023) Models, which demonstrate surprisingly good
043   abilities in natural language understanding, comprehension and reasoning tasks.
044   The most prominent feature which distinguishes a Transformer layer from other architectures is
045   the attention mechanism which allows for all of the inputs to simultaneously interact with each
046   other. However, it’s also the source of its limitations: O(N 2 ) time and space complexity w.r.t
047   context length N , and computational inefficiency of the constituents which make the architecture
048   work seamlessly. As reported by Ivanov et al. (2021), matrix multiplications account for 99.8% of
049   total FLOPs during BERT pretraining and only 61% of runtime, the discrepancy being caused by
050
      low arithmetic intensity of memory bound operations, namely, LayerNorms, Softmaxs and other
      activations as well as elementwise operations.
051
052   Numerous extensions and modifications to the standard Transformer (Katharopoulos et al., 2020;
053   Choromanski et al., 2022; Beltagy et al., 2020; Zhai et al., 2021; Hua et al., 2022) have been pro-
      posed in the recent years to alleviate the restrictive O(N 2 ) complexity. However, as these architec-

      Thus, we aim to achieve 3 main goals:
079
080         1. To create hardware efficient yet hardware-agnostic architecture with the arithmetic intensity
081            ratio as high as possible. An ideal algorithm should contain merely matrix multiplications
082            with no activations, normalizations and residual connections. However, while possible in
083            principle, it remains a challenging task due to numerical instabilities occurring both in
084            forward and backward pass and lagging performance of such architectures (Balduzzi et al.,
085            2017; Santurkar et al., 2018; Pascanu et al., 2013)
086         2. To create an algorithm which would efficiently process long sequences, preferrably with
087            O(N ) time and space complexity.
088         3. To make the resulting architecture as simple as possible, and closely resembling original
089            Transformer architecture as well so it can serve as a drop-in replacement for the former and
090            be easily adopted by both research and practitioners communities.
091
092   We accomplished all of these goals with DenseAttention and DenseAttention Network (DANet)
093
      blocks (Fig. 1). This architecture is a straight-forward simplification of the traditional Transformer
      architecture which does not introduce any additional elements and complexities to the module and
094
      can be freely swapped with it. On the contrary, we develop DenseAttention by removing all compu-
095
      tationally inefficient elements of the original architecture: biases in all linear layers, masks, dropout,
096   residual connection between attention and FFN. Most importantly, we remove Softmax inside self-
097   attention. It results in the whole scaled dot-product attention mechanism becoming just a compo-
098   sition of matrix multiplications, which can be done in any order by associative property of matrix
099   multiplication. This duality allows to calculate DenseAttention using either O(N 2 d) or O(N d2 )
100   FLOPs, and the second option has linear time and space complexity w.r.t sequence length.
101

102   sentations by their l∞ norm. We place it at both ends of the DANet block. We also remove all
103   projection matrices except WQ in the self-attention module as they become redundant in the ab-
104   sence of non-linearities between attention and FFN. To empirically validate the architecture, we
105   test on the challenging Long Range Arena (LRA) benchmark (Tay et al., 2021) and achieve a new
106   SOTA result across all of the transformer-based models, even competing with State-Space-Models
107   (Gu et al., 2022a). We also replace Transformer modules in BERT-large model (Devlin et al., 2019)
      with DenseAttention Network modules and pre-train it from scratch on sequences up to 16k tokens.

187   Since we aim to achieve as much computationally efficient and simple module as possible, we
188   proceed with eliminating inefficient components of original self-attention and Transformer architec-

190   The most straightforward idea which we exploit first is to abstain from using Dropout module any-
191   where in the model. Even though the module can be removed altogether at inference time, we also
192   do it for the training as we believe it won’t slow down the convergence with a large corpora dataset
193   typical for LLM pre-training. Besides, as noted by Clark et al. (2019), dropout in attention proba-
194   bilities might be the reason of redundancies among attention heads. Next, we remove the attention
195   mask before Softmax. Note that if there are no biases in FFN, Query and Output linear layers and

      3.3   L OCAL ATTENTION FOR D ENSE ATTENTION
359
      In the years following invention of Transformer, many variations of local attention, also known as
360   sliding window attention, patterns and implementations have been proposed (Zaheer et al., 2020;
361   Beltagy et al., 2020; Child et al., 2019; Roy et al., 2021; Dao et al., 2022). Recently, some of
362   the open-weights Large Language Models (Jiang et al., 2023; Team et al., 2024) started partially
363   or fully adopting some forms of local attention with the primary goal of alleviating quadratic cost
364   of full attention for large contexts with the trade-off of not being able to fully process the entire
365   sequence at once.
366   We also develop a form of local attention pattern for discretionary use with DenseAttention on
367   very long contexts, however, with the goal of improving modeling quality as opposed to increasing
368   speed. The reason of this extension is outlined by Qin et al. (2022a): in linear Transformer family
369   of models, attention scores of a query are distributed along the sequence length more uniformly as
370   compared to Softmax attention, so the model is not fully able to focus at details in the vicinity of a
371   query’s token.
372   We adopt the approach to partition the whole sequence into equal non-overlapping chunks of win-
373   dow size w, similar to Dao et al. (2022); Qin et al. (2022a). We choose this design because of
374   its simplicity and straight-forward implementation with minimal invocations of memory-intensive
375   data layouts. However, this form of chunked attention leads to all of the tokens not being able to
376   interact with up to a half of the tokens constituting their neighbourhood. To mitigate this issue,
377   we extend our local attention framework beyond one layer and propose a 3-layer structure 3. It

411
412   To prove the viability of DenseAttention architecture, we conduct two sets of experiments: 1) long
413   range sequence modeling on Long Range Arena benchmark; 2) pretraining of BERT-like encoder
414   architecture on sequences of different lengths. We train all of the models with fp16 precision, unless

      4.1               L ONG R ANGE A RENA
418
419
      Long Range Arena is a challenging suite of 6 classification benchmarks dedicated to examining the
420   abilities of efficient and long-context models on large sequence lengths spanning from 1k to 16k to-
421   kens. The tasks are diverse in nature and modalities: from synthetic and purely algorithmic, such as
422   long version of ListOps benchmark (Nangia & Bowman, 2018), to character-level text classification
423   on IMDB reviews (Maas et al., 2011). At the time of publication, the best model tested by Tay et al.
424   (2021) achieved average of 55.01%, and all of the models failed to learn above the level of change
425   on the most difficult task, Pathfinder-X (seq. len 16K) adopted from Linsley et al. (2018); Kim*
426   et al. (2020).
427   Later, novel State-Space-Models-inspired architectures (Gu et al., 2022a;b; Ma et al., 2023; 2024)
428   demonstrated by far superior performance considered to be out of reach for any Transformer-based
429   model due specific inductive biases of the SSMs. But recently, Amos et al. (2024) showed that
430   by using MLM-style pre-training the Transformer with RoPE is competitive with the SSMs. Inter-
431   estingly, even without pre-training, but with RoPE, they reached a SOTA score on the benchmark
      among all Transformer-based architectures with a large margin.

       A    C ONCLUSION & F UTURE W ORK
1081
1082
       In this paper, we propose DenseAttention Network – a general architecture which simplifies the
1083   Transformer block and can serve as a drop-in replacement in every model architecture using it. We
1084   conduct experiments on the diverse modalities spanning from logic to language modeling and image
1085   classification and from short to extremely long sequence lengths using the LRA suite of benchmarks
1086   and MLM-style language model pre-training on text data. The results show that DenseAttention is
1087   capable of generalizing to many different tasks and context sizes and achieving favorable perfor-
1088   mance in comparison with standard Transformer and its augmented variants while being faster and
1089   more computationally efficient even with no specialized, low-level computation algorithms such as
1090   in Dao et al. (2022).
1091   We acknowledge that there are other modalities and specialized architectures that would benefit
1092   from long-context efficiency improvements if the DenseAttention is ported or applied to them, such
1093   as ViT (Dosovitskiy et al., 2021) and SAM (Kirillov et al., 2023) for Computer Vision tasks, and
1094   LLAMA (Touvron et al., 2023a) for decoder-style language modeling. We hope to address them
1095   in future work. In particular, we look forward to adapting DenseAttention architecture to causal
1096   LLAMA-style LLMs and studying their scaling laws at billions parameter ranges.

1098   B    H ARDWARE E FFICIENCY
1099
1100   All calculations performed by a hardware accelerator such as a NVIDIA GPU are either compute-
1101   bound or memory-bound (Williams et al., 2009). It depends on whether the operation in question
1102   spends the majority of time directly on computation or on data movements between High-Bandwidth
1103   Memory (HBM) and processing units. Customary unit of measurement for computational perfor-
1104   mance is TeraFLOPs (TFLOPs) per second and for memory it’s bandwidth (throughput) in TB/s.
1105   Arithmetic intensity unifies both and is calculated as number of FLOPs/number of bytes accessed. It can be at-
1106   tributed both to hardware accelerator (usually referred to as ops:byte ratio in this case) and to a
1107   computational kernel, e.g. layer of neural network, and it’s necessary but not sufficient for the ker-
1108
       nel to maintain the arithmetic intensity higher than the accelerator in order to be computationally
       intensive (Docs, a). Otherwise, processing units stay idle part of the time waiting for the data to be
1109
       brought from or written to HBM.
1110
1111   In latest generations of GPUs, FLOPs count rapidly grows but memory bandwidth progression falls
1112   behind, which results in latest generations of GPUs having much higher arithmetic intensity. Thus,
1113   it’s increasingly hard for existing Deep Learning (DL) primitives to achieve hardware efficiency.
1114
       Most operations besides matrix-matrix multiplications are inherently memory limited even on older
       GPUs. For example, the arithm. intensity of ReLU is 0.25 FLOPS/B, and for LayerNorm it’s ¡ 10
1115
       FLOPS/B on NVIDIA V100 as stated in Docs (b). Moreover, GPUs feature fast Tensor Cores (312
1116
       TFLOPs for half-precision formats in NVIDIA A100) specialized for matrix multiplications, and
1117   general purpose cores with significantly lower throughput (19.5 TFLOPS in NVIDIA A100) which
1118   in turn process non-MatMul operations even slower as reported in He (2022).
1119
1120
       So, from the view of computational efficiency, all activations, elementwise operations and reductions
       are detrimental to high ratios of hardware utilization.

       E.1   D ISCUSSION OF THE LRA TASKS
1245
1246   The Long Range Arena is, in fact, not a single benchmark but a suite of 6 challenging and diverse
1247   tasks designed to test modeling capabilities across different domains. Below is a brief description of
1248   each task.
1249   ListOps(Nangia & Bowman, 2018). This is a purely logical synthetic task which is dedicated to
1250   modeling evaluation results of long hierarchically structured sequences. Each sequence has length
1251   up to 2000 symbols and consists of whole numbers from 0 to 9, mathematical operators, such as
1252   MAX, MIN, MEDIAN and SUM MOD, and parentheses.
1253
       Text Classification (IMDB) (Maas et al., 2011). This task tests Natural Language Understanding
1254
       (NLU) abilities of models by letting them classify the sentiment of movie reviews in the IMDB
1255   dataset. To make the task more challenging, the texts of the reviews are split into tokens not on
1256   a word level, but on a character (or byte) level. This leads to much longer sequences of 4K max
1257   length.
1258
       Document Retrieval (AAN) (Radev, 2013). This task tests the abilities of producing encoded rep-
1259
       resentations of the textual information and further matching/ retrieving them. Namely, given a pair
1260
       of the documents from ACL Anthology Network (AAN; Radev et al., 2013) dataset, a model should
1261   independently process them and, based on their final embeddings, classify if the two documents
1262   have a citation link. As in the IMDB tasks, individual input texts are tokenized on a character (byte)
1263   level with max sequence length 4K.
1264
       Image Classification (CIFAR-10) (Krizhevsky & Hinton, 2009). This is an image classification
1265
       task with 10 classes on a classical CIFAR-10 benchmark with one specific condition: images should
1266
       be ingested into models as 1-d sequences, thus setting the input length to 1024 tokens (pixels) and
1267   making the task more challenging.
1268
1269   Pathfinder (Kim* et al., 2020) . This is a binary classification task of 32x32 pixels grayscale images
1270
       with corresponding sequence length 1024 tokens, which, formally, makes it similar to CIFAR-10
       task. However, it’s different on a conceptual level, as the task measures a model’s ability to discern
1271
       spatial dependencies. Given a multitude of intertwined, dashed line paths, a model should correctly
1272
       determine if two rounded dots are connected by a dashed line.
1273
1274   Pathfinder-X (Pathfinder-128). It’s a version of Pathfinder task with 16K (128x128) pixels images
1275   which makes it significantly more challenging. At the time of publication of the original LRA
1276
       paper Tay et al. (2021), none of the tested models managed to achieve a score above chance on this
       benchmark.
1277
1278   Therefore, the Long Range Arena arguably represents a wide range of tasks, spanning from logic
1279   and reasoning to language modeling and image classification. To perform well on all of the 6 bench-
1280   marks, a model’s architecture should be powerful and versatile enough to generalize to different
1281   modalities.

            CONTEXT.

1355
       To ensure numerical stability, we scale weight matrices of FFN layers to have a constant l∞ norm
       after each optimizer step during pre-training. After pretraining, we merge each weight with its fi-
1356
       nal scaling factor so there is no additional overhead at the inference time. The choice of the norm
1357
       type is motivated largely by the bounds it provides for the layer outputs as in the case with the
1358   DenseAttention layer. The scaling factor of a layer is a standalone non-trainable scalar decoupled
1359   from its corresponding weight tensor at the train time. This means that the weight itself doesn’t get
1360   re-scaled constantly which would otherwise induce tug-of-war dynamics with the direction of gra-
1361   dient. This way, the weight also has natural proportions compared to ADAM optimizer’s ((Kingma
1362   & Ba, 2015)) weight update as it would in the absence of scaling. By employing this technique, we
1363   eliminate the need for weight decay and warmup. We also use constant learning rate 2 × 10−4 in all
1364   training runs.
1365   We observed that scaling the Queries weight in the DenseAttention hinders loss convergence speed
1366   to a certain degree so we proceeded with scaling just FFN layers.
1367
       The models which continued training with seq. len. 1k slightly outperform their counterparts which
1368
       stopped after seq. len. 512. on sequences of this same size 512 which indicates that training on
1369
       longer contexts is indeed beneficial for modeling quality. However, performance degrades when
1370   models trained on N = 512 or N = 1024 get tested on seq. len. 128 which is a consequence of the
1371   models’ specialization on the longer sequences.

1375
