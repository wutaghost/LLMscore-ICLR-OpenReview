                                                  A BSTRACT
013
014
               Voxel-wise contrastive learning (VCL) is a prominent approach in semi-
015            supervised medical image segmentation. Based on the initially generated pseudo-
016            labels, VCL pulls voxels with the same pseudo-labels toward their prototypes
017            while pushes those with different labels apart, thereby learns effective represen-
018            tations for the segmentation task. However, in multi-organ segmentation (MoS),
019            the complex anatomical structures of certain organs often lead to many unreliable
020            pseudo-labels. Directly applying VCL can introduce confirmation bias, result-
021            ing in poor segmentation performance. A common practice is to first transform
022            these unreliable pseudo-labels into more reliable complementary ones, which rep-
023
               resent classes that voxels are least likely to belong to, and then push voxels away
               from the prototypes of their complementary labels. However, we find that in this
024
               approach, if voxels with unreliable pseudo-labels are originally close in feature
025
               space, they can end up far apart after being pushed away from their complemen-
026            tary prototypes. This disruption of the semantic relationships among voxels can
027            be detrimental to the MoS task. In this paper, we propose DVCL, a novel distance-
028            aware VCL method for semi-supervised MoS. DVCL is based on the observation
029            that voxels close to each other in the feature space (’neighbors’) likely belong
030            to the same semantic category, while distant ones (’outsiders’) likely belong to
031            different categories. In DVCL, we first identify neighbors and outsiders for all
032            voxels with unreliable pseudo-labels, and then pull their neighbors into the same
033            clusters while pushing outsiders away. In this way, neighbors of unreliable vox-
034
               els remain their neighbors and outsiders remain outsiders. This approach helps
               maintain useful semantic relationships among unreliable voxels while still enjoy-
035
               ing the advantages of VCL. We conduct extensive experiments on four datasets to
036
               validate the effectiveness. Extensive experiments on four datasets demonstrate the
037            superior performance of DVCL compared to state-of-the-art methods.

042   Medical image segmentation is a critical task in computer-aided diagnosis (Meng et al., 2022; Zhao
043   et al., 2022). However, considering the large amount of effort and expertise required for labeling
044   data (Greenspan et al., 2016), semi-supervised learning (SSL) is often employed. In SSL, voxel-

046   proven highly effective. Based on the pseudo-labels generated by the initial model, VCL pulls voxels
047   with the same pseudo-labels toward their prototypes while pushing those with different pseudo-

049   the segmentation task.

051   is more challenging than single-organ segmentation due to complex anatomical structures, includ-

053   ferent shapes (e.g., the stomach has a more complex shape than the liver despite similar volumes),

      the prototypes of its complementary labels. An example of using complementary labels is shown in

107   Then the critical question is, among a large number of voxels with unreliable pseudo-labels, which
      kind of information should be maintained during VCL? Our answer to this questions is: neighbors

108
      remain neighbors, and outsiders remain outsiders. Correspondingly, we propose a novel distance-
109   aware VCL (DVCL) technique. The rationale behind DVCL is simple: for a voxel with an unreli-
110   able pseudo-label, we wish to maintain two types of information. Its close voxels in feature space
111   (referred to as neighbors), which likely belong to the same semantic category, should still be its
112   neighbors after implementing DVCL, while its far-away voxels (referred to as outsiders), which
113   likely belong to different categories, should remain its outsiders after DVCL. Specifically, in DVCL,
114   we first identify, for each voxel with an unreliable pseudo-label, its neighbors and outsiders. Dur-
115   ing contrastive learning, for all unreliable voxels, we pull their neighbors into the same clusters
116   while pushing outsiders away. This approach helps maintain useful semantic relationships among
117
      unreliable voxels while still enjoying the advantages of contrastive learning.
118   An example of using DVCL is shown in the bottom figure of Fig.1(b). Take voxel B as an example
119   (Query). Before implementing DVCL, voxel A is one of its neighbors, and voxel C is one of its
120   outsiders. Then, after DVCL, voxel A is pulled to the same cluster of B, while voxel C is pushed
121   away from it. In this way, the neighbors and outsiders of this query voxel B are kept the same.
122   One interesting thing we should emphasize is related to voxel D, which is neither a neighbor nor
123
      an outsider for the query voxel B. However, this does not mean it remains in the same location
      after DVCL. As voxel D, in most conditions, belongs to the neighbor or an outsider for some other
124
      voxels, it will be moved accordingly to maintain their relationships.
125
126   Additionally, in the image space, an entropy-based selection module (ESM) is designed to adaptively
127   separate all pseudo-labels into two groups, i.e., a reliable one and an unreliable one. Furthermore,
128   reliable pseudo-labels are assigned higher weights, while unreliable pseudo-labels receive lower
129
      weights, effectively reweighting the unsupervised loss. We conduct extensive experiments on four
      datasets to demonstrate the effectiveness of DVCL. Compared to state-of-the-art methods, our pro-
130
      posed DVCL achieves superior performance. Additionally, we believe that DVCL can be applied to
131
      other segmentation tasks where there are a large number of unreliable pseudo-labels. Overall, our
132   contributions can be summarized as follows:
133
134         • We propose a novel Distance-aware Voxel-based Contrastive Learning technique. By main-
135           taining useful semantic relationships among unreliable voxels, discriminative features for
136           segmentation can be learned.
137
            • We propose an entropy-based learning approach to efficiently utilize pseudo-labels that
138
              have low confidence yet are accurate in image space.
139
140         • Extensive experiments on four medical multi-organ benchmarks demonstrate the effective-
141           ness of our method and show that we advance the state-of-the-art approaches.

      2   R ELATED WORK
145
146   Semi-supervised multi-organ segmentation. Recently, semi-supervised methods for MoS have
147
      emerged (Zhou et al., 2019; Xia et al., 2020; Ma et al., 2023c). For example, Wen et al. Wen et al.
      (2024) proposed a DCL-Net to utilize global and local VCL to enhance model performance. For a
148
      more detailed summary of semi-supervised MoS methods, please refer to Appendix A.
149
150   Semi-supervised contrastive learning. Recently, VCL has achieved significant success in extract-
151   ing powerful features from unlabeled data in SSL (Wang et al., 2021; 2022b; Feng et al., 2024;
152   Deng et al., 2024). For example, Wang et al. (2021) proposed a VCL algorithm for SSL semantic
153
      segmentation that enhances similarity between embeddings of same-class voxels while distinguish-
      ing different-class voxels using pseudo-labels. However, unreliable pseudo-labels can introduce
154
      confirmation bias in VCL, ultimately reducing task performance. Wang et al. (2022b) proposed
155
      an uncertainty-guided contrastive learning method that discard unreliable pseudo-labels based on
156   threshold. More recently, works such as (Wang et al., 2022c; Du et al., 2023; Feng et al., 2024; Deng
157   et al., 2024) have focused on leveraging unreliable pseudo-labels for learning rather than filtering
158   them out, often employing complementary labels. For a more detailed summary of complementary
159   label VCL methods, please refer to Appendix A. However, this approach can disrupt the relation-
160   ships among some unreliable voxels, and these relationship are helpful when learning features. In
161   contrast, our method emphasizes preserving these semantic relationships among unreliable voxels.
      This capability is a key reason for the superiority of our method.

235   In the CPS method, the CPS loss is computed at the image level by considering all pseudo-labels
236
      without discrimination. However, overfitting to incorrect pseudo-labels can result in confirmation

237

238

239   information. Thus, we propose an entropy-based learning approach to mitigate confirmation bias
240   and effectively utilize the pseudo-labels, as shown in Fig.3 left, including entropy-based selection
241   module (ESM) and entropy-weighted cross pseudo supervision (ECPS) loss.

      4.3       A BLATION STUDY
504
505   In this subsection, we conduct various ablations to better understand our design choices. For all the
506   ablation experiments the models are trained on FLARE 2022 dataset with 10% labeled ratio.
507
      Importance of Each Components. We analyze the effectiveness of different components of our
508
      method. At the core of our approach is the combination of two strategies: an entropy-based learning
509
      (EBL) approach and distance-aware voxel-wise contrastive learning (DVCL). We deactivate each
510   component and then evaluate the resulting models, as shown in Table 2. As illustrated, both EBL
511   and DVCL significantly enhance performance, particularly for challenging organs (i.e., Sto; Pan;
512   LAG; RAG). Furthermore, the integration of both EBL and DVCL yields the highest mean Dice
513   (84.11%) and mean Jaccard (73.49%).
514
      Importance of Neighbors and Outsiders. Although voxels from different organs might become
515
      neighbors due to similar features in the feature space, by carefully choosing the value for the num-
516   ber of neighbors,K, we can effectively reduce such misclassifications and ensure that the selected
517   neighbors are as likely as possible to belong to the same class. As shown in Table 3, the optimal
518   values are K = 10 and K’ = 15, which yield the highest mean Dice and mean Jaccard. When K is
519   small, the query voxel has fewer neighbors. Although these neighbors have high similarity, they
520   may only contain limited semantic information. Conversely, when K is large, the number of neigh-
521   bors increases, potentially introducing misclassified samples with high similarity but belonging to
522   different categories.
523   Analysis on The Nearest-neighbor Voxel-wise Contrastive Learning. In Table 4, we com-
524   pare DVCL with existing nearest neighbor VCL methods (Wu et al., 2023; Dwibedi et al., 2021;
525   Chongjian et al.) and find that DVCL exhibits superior performance. This improvement can be at-
526   tributed to two main factors. First, for unreliable voxels, DVCL not only considers similar neighbors
527   but also introduces outsiders, which provide valuable inter-class contrastive information. Second,
528   DVCL transcends traditional positive and negative sample pair comparisons by employing con-
529   trastive learning between two feature sets. Moreover, the results of ablation studies of the contrastive
530
      learning strategycan be found in Appendix.M.

532   5       C ONCLUSION
533
534   In this work, we introduce a semi-supervised VCL method for MoS, termed DVCL, to make suffi-
535   cient use of voxels with unreliable pseudo-labels. Contrary to the previous works that use comple-
536   mentary labels, DVCL helps maintain useful semantic relationships among unreliable voxels while
537   still enjoying the advantages of VCL. Additionally, we propose an entropy-based learning approach
538   to efficiently utilize pseudo-labels that have low confidence yet are accurate in image space. Com-
539   parative experiments against state-of-the-art methods and extensive ablation studies demonstrate the
      effectiveness of our method.

      A    R ELATED WORK
776
777   Semi-supervised Multi-organ Segmentation. Accurately segmenting multiple organs in CT scans
778   is crucial for a variety of medical procedures (Gao et al., 2023). Lee et al. (Lee et al.) proposed a
779   lightweight volumetric ConvNet for multi-organ segmentation in CT scans called 3D UX-Net for
780   segmenting 13 organs from CT scans. 3D UX-Net is a supervised network that relies on the quantity
781   and quality of labeled data. Recently, a number of semi-supervised learning methods were developed
782   for multi-organ segmentation in CT scans (Zhou et al., 2019; Xia et al., 2020; Ma et al., 2023c). To
783   alleviate the class-imbalance problem, Lin et al. Lin et al. (2022) proposed a a novel framework
784   to leverage label distribution to encourage the network to put more effort into small organ. Wang
785
      et al. Wang & Li (2023) proposed a dual-debiased heterogeneous co-training (DHC) framework
      that leverages the pseudo-labels dynamically to guide the model to solve data and learning biases.
786
      Chen et al. Chen et al. (2023a) designed a data augmentation strategy (MagicNet) that encourages
787
      unlabeled images to learn an organ’s semantic information regarding its position relative to labeled
788   images. Furthermore, Wen et al. Wen et al. (2024) proposed a two-stage dual contrastive learning
789   network (DCL-Net) to utilize global and local contrastive learning to strengthen the relations among
790   images and classes. In this work, we also use contrastive learning to fully utilize unlabeled data
791   and propose a feature interaction method for unreliable voxels to deeply explore their semantic
792   relationships, thereby improving the segmentation performance of difficult organs.
793
      Complementary Label VCL.
794
795         • U2 PL (Wang et al., 2022c): Instead of discarding unreliable pixels, they are effectively
796           treated as negative samples for the least likely categories. This enables a semi-supervised
797           semantic segmentation framework that leverages these uncertain predictions to enhance
798           learning.
799         • Lion (Du et al., 2023): They introduce a negative learning module to extract knowl-
800           edge from complementary labels, thereby maximizing the utility of underestimated, low-
801           confidence unlabeled data. Furthermore, they propose an innovative NC-Loss to enhance
802           the model’s performance.
803         • BaCon (Feng et al., 2024): To address the issue of class imbalance across multiple
804           categories, they build upon the complementary label approach and propose a Balanced
805           Distribution-related Temperature Adjusting (BTA) method. This approach dynamically
806           modulates the class-wise temperature of positive pairs in VCL.
807         • CCL (Deng et al., 2024): They propose a Contrastive Complementary Labeling (CCL)
808           method, which constructs reliable negative pairs using the complementary labels of low-
809           confidence data. This approach supplies discriminative information for subsequent con-
              trastive learning while effectively avoiding the introduction of potential noise.

      Self-supervised Learning. A particular kind of self-supervised training – known as instance dis-
811   crimination has become popular. For example, Wu et al. (Wu et al., 2018) proposed an unsupervised
812   feature learning approach by maximizing distinction between instances via a novel non parametric
813   softmax formulation. Debidatta Dwibedi et al. (Dwibedi et al., 2021) used an explicit support set
814   (also known as a memory queue/bank) for the purpose of nearest neighbor mining(NNCLR). Fur-
815   thermore, Yang et al. (Yang et al., 2021) only use a few neighbors from the feature bank to cluster
816   the target features with a consistency regularization. Recently, Ge et al. (Chongjian et al.) computed
817   cross-attention scores to explore soft neighbors during contrastive learning (SNCLR). Building on
818   these approaches, Wu et al. (Wu et al., 2023) proposed a neighbor-guided consistent and contrastive
819
      learning framework for semi-supervised setting (NCCL). However, this approach can disrupt the
      relationships among some unreliable voxels, and these relationship are helpful when learning fea-
820
      tures. However, these existing methods select only semantically similar positive samples based on
821
      neighbors, while we introduce not only similar samples but also dissimilar ones, providing valuable
822   inter-class contrastive information.

919   2017 (Zhuang & Shen, 2016) . The dataset contains 20 3D CT volumes with corresponding an-
920   notations of seven cardiac structures, i.e., the myocardium of the left ventricle (MYO), left atrium
921   blood cavity (LAC), right atrium blood cavity (RAC), left ventricle blood cavity (LVC), right ven-
922   tricle blood cavity (RVC), ascending aorta (AA), and pulmonary aorta (PA). In our experiments, we
923   divide the dataset into training, validation, and test sets at a ratio of 6:2:2. For the training set, the
924   proportion of labeled data is set to 10%and 50%.
925
926
927
      D     DATA P REPROCESSING AND I MPLEMENTATION D ETAILS
928
929   Data Preprocessing. We apply hierarchical steps for data preprocessing before network training.
930
      1) the orientation of all CT scans is standardized in the left-posterior-inferior (LPI) direction. 2)
      The voxel values are clipped into the range of [–325, 325] Hounsfield units (HU) to enhance the
931
      contrast of the foreground organs and suppress background interference. 3) The voxel spacing is
932
      standardized to [1.25, 1.25, 2.5]. 4) Min-max normalization is implemented using the formula
933   (x − x0.5 )/(x99.5 − x0.5 ), where x0.5 and x99.5 represent the 0.5th and 99.5th percentiles of x,
934   respectively.
935
936
      Implementation Details. All experiments are conducted using the PyTorch platform and trained
      on 2 NVIDIA A100 GPUs. For network training, we use the stochastic gradient descent (SGD)
937
      optimizer with a weight decay of 0.0001 and momentum of 0.9 (Luo et al., 2021b). To mitigate
938
      the overfitting problem, we apply basic data augmentation, including random cropping and flip-
939   ping (Wang & Li, 2023). The model uses randomly cropped patches as inputs, with a patch size of
940   64 × 128 × 128. During network training, we adopt a polynomial learning rate policy that scales the
941   initial learning rate by (1 − maxiteration
                                          iteration )
                                                     0.9
                                                         (Chen et al., 2023a), where iteration and max iteration
942   represent the current iteration and maximum number of iterations, respectively. For the MMWHS
943   dataset, the segmentation model is trained with a batch size of 4, comprising an equal split of 2
944   labeled and 2 unlabeled instances, over a total of 10,000 iterations with an initial learning rate of
945   0.03. For the FLARE 2022, AMOS and BTCV datasets, we opt for a batch size of 8, including 4
946   labeled and 4 unlabeled instances, and conduct training for 20,000 iterations with an initial learning
947
      rate of 0.1. Note that, for all the evaluated methods, we make no additional modifications during
      the training process for fair evaluations. During inference, the final volumetric segmentation is gen-
948
      erated using a sliding-window strategy. The stride used is 32 × 80 × 80, and the sliding-window
949
      approach employs a patch size of 64 × 160 × 160.

952   E    SEMI - SUPERVISED SEGMENTATION METHODS IN C OMPARISON
953
954   For experiments, we compare our method to thirteen state-of-the-art semi-supervised segmentation
955   methods: (1) deep adversarial networks (DAN) (Zhang et al., 2017), (2) MT (Tarvainen & Valpola,
956   2017), (3) uncertainty-aware mean teacher (UA-MT) (Yu et al., 2019), (4) the shape-aware semi-
957   supervised network (SASSnet) (Li et al., 2020), (5) the dual-task consistency (DTC) framework (Luo
958   et al., 2021a), (6) CPS (Chen et al., 2021), (7) calibrating label distribution (CLD) for segmentation
959   (Lin et al., 2022), (8) the dual-debiased heterogeneous co-training (DHC) framework (Wang & Li,
960
      2023), (9) MagicNet (Chen et al., 2023a), (10) Uncertainty-guided pixel contrastive learning (UG-
      PCL) framework (Chen et al., 2021), (11) Using unreliable pseudo-labels (U2 PL) framework (Wang
961
      et al., 2022c), (12) Balanced feature-level contrastive learning (BaCon) framework (Feng et al.,
962
      2024), (13) Contrastive complementary labeling (CCL) framework (Deng et al., 2024), and the fully
963   supervised 3D U-Net (Çiçek et al., 2016). Note that among all the above evaluated methods, several
964   methods use a contrastive learning objective, including UGPCL (Chen et al., 2021), U2 PL (Wang
965   et al., 2022c), BaCon (Feng et al., 2024), and CCL (Deng et al., 2024). For all semi-supervised
966   methods, we utilize 3D U-Net as the backbone.

1215   with clear boundaries between different categories, which is a notable advantage of VCL (Wang
1216   et al., 2021; Alonso et al., 2021). As shown in Fig 9(a)(top left), features with high classification
1217   confidence, such as node2 and node13, are treated as reliable voxels. However, voxels like node12,
1218   node4, node5, and node6, which are prone to classification confusion and have low confidence, tend
1219   to cluster together in the feature space without forming well-defined feature boundaries. As a result,
1220   they are typically classified as unreliable voxels. When Complementary Label VCL is introduced,
1221   these unreliable voxels, although prone to classification confusion, can be confidently identified
1222   as not belonging to category 2. This causes their features to be pushed away from the feature
1223
       center of node2. However, this random repulsion operation can disrupt semantic relationships. For
       example, after applying VCL, node4 and node12 may be confused, but node4 and node6 should
1224
       be distinguishable. Yet, with Complementary Label VCL, node4 and node6 are unintentionally
1225
       drawn closer together, as shown in Fig 9(a)(top right). The same applies to node5 and node6.
1226   This misalignment may increase the burden on contrastive learning, potentially undermining its
1227   effectiveness.
1228
1229
       To address the aforementioned issues, DVCL introduces the concepts of ”neighbors” and ”out-
       siders,” which ensure semantic separation between node4 and node6 while maintaining the semantic
1230
       consistency of node4. Through the visualization of feature maps and the changes in inter-class dis-
1231
       tances, it can be observed that after applying DVCL, the semantic relationships between previously
1232   unreliable voxels in VCL are preserved, and the feature classification becomes clearer. Addition-
1233   ally, we observe that, compared to Complementary Label VCL, the intra-class distance between
1234   unreliable voxels significantly decreases after applying DVCL, resulting in more distinct and distin-
1235   guishable feature classification boundaries.

       M     A BLATION STUDIE OF THE CONTRASTIVE LEARNING STRATEGY.
1308
1309
       We chose to use the likelihood of class relationships, rather than directly pulling together or push-
1310
       ing apart voxel features as in traditional contrastive learning, because we argue that introducing
1311   contrastive learning between two feature sets goes beyond the limitations of traditional positive-
1312   negative sample comparisons. This approach enables us to more effectively capture distributional
1313   differences between groups, thereby providing a clearer understanding of the semantic structural
1314   relationships between samples. We adopted negative log-likelihood (NLL) as the loss function, in-
1315   spired by self-supervised learning (Wu et al., 2018). To further validate the effectiveness of our
1316   strategy, we conducted comparative experiments with different contrastive learning strategies (in-
1317   cluding InfoNCE, SimCLR, and KL divergence). The results demonstrate that our strategy achieves
1318   the best performance, as shown in Table.8 .
