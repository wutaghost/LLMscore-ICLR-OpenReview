009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011
               Multi-view clustering effectively integrates information from multi-view data rep-
012            resentations, yet current methods face key challenges. They often lack inter-
013            pretability, obscuring how clusters are formed, and fail to fully leverage the com-
014            plementary information across views, limiting clustering quality. Additionally,
015            large-scale data introduces high computational demands, with traditional methods
016            requiring extensive post-processing.To address these issues, we propose a novel
017            Fast Tensor-Based Multi-View Clustering with Anchor Probability Transition Ma-
018            trix ((FTMVC-APTM). By selecting anchor points and constructing bipartite sim-
019            ilarity graphs, we can capture the relationships between data points and anchors
020
               in different views and reduce computational complexity. Through probability ma-
               trices, we efficiently transfer cluster labels from anchors to samples, generating
021
               membership matrices without the need for post-processing. We further assem-
022
               ble these membership matrices into a tensor and apply a Schatten p-norm con-
023            straint to exploit complementary information across views, ensuring consistency
024            and robustness. To prevent trivial solutions and ensure well-defined clusters, we
025            incorporate nuclear norm-based regularization. Extensive experiments on various
026            datasets confirm the effectiveness and efficiency of our method.

      1   I NTRODUCTION
030
031
032   In recent years, multi-view clustering (MVC) has gained increasing importance in machine learning
033
      and data analysis. As data sources expand through various sensors, imaging technologies, and social
      media platforms, multi-view data has become widespread across many fields. Unlike single-view
034
      clustering, which may miss important patterns by focusing on only one data perspective, MVC
035
      integrates information from multiple views to uncover the true underlying structure of the data (Chao
036   et al., 2021).
037
038   Current MVC approaches can be categorized into four main types: subspace learning, graph-based
039
      methods, co-training methods, and multi-kernel learning. Subspace learning reduces the data to
      lower dimensions, which helps in handling high-dimensional datasets. However, this approach
040
      may fail to capture complex relationships between different views (Zheng et al., 2023; Gao et al.,
041
      2020a). Graph-based methods, on the other hand, build similarity graphs and apply spectral cluster-
042   ing. While effective, these methods can be computationally expensive due to the graph construction
043   and eigendecomposition steps involved (Wei et al., 2017; Yang et al., 2023). Co-training improves
044   clustering by combining classifiers from different views, especially when these views provide com-
045   plementary information (Jiang et al., 2013). Multi-kernel learning captures non-linear relationships
046   across views by learning a combined kernel, integrating information from multiple data sources
047   (Tzortzis & Likas, 2012).
048   Despite their strengths, several challenges limit the practical application of these methods. Many
049   existing approaches follow a two-step process: first, learning a fusion graph or spectral embedding,
050   and then performing clustering. This separation often results in suboptimal performance, as the
051   two steps are not jointly optimized. Moreover, many methods require complex post-processing
052   to generate the final cluster labels, which increases computational complexity, particularly for large
053   datasets (Brbić & Kopriva, 2018; Li et al., 2019). The separation of steps and the additional overhead
      make these methods less scalable for real-world applications (Yu et al., 2023).

054
      To address these computational complexity issues, anchor graph-based methods have been proposed.
055   These methods reduce the graph size by selecting a smaller subset of points (anchors) to represent
056   the original data. By constructing a bipartite graph between the data points and the anchors, these
057   methods significantly lower the computational burden during graph construction (Li et al., 2015).
058   Li et al. (2024c) introduced tensor-anchor graph factorization by combining the concepts of tensors
059   and anchor points. Additionally, Feng et al. (2024) proposed a depth tensor factorization method,
060   which builds on depth matrix factorization to mine deeper, hidden information embedded in the
061   anchor graph tensor. However, these methods rely on anchor graph data instead of raw data and pro-
062   vide only limited improvement in computational efficiency (Li et al., 2023; 2024a). After selecting
063
      anchors, Yu et al. (2023) constructed a probabilistic bipartite graph using both original and anchor
      data to derive a consensus matrix directly from the anchor label matrix. However, this method ne-
064
      glects the complementary information between multi-view data, which affects the overall clustering
065
      performance.
066
067   To overcome these challenges, we propose a novel method called Fast Tensor-Based Multi-View
068   Clustering with Anchor Probability Transition Matrix (FTMVC-APTM), which simplifies the pro-
069
      cess and improves efficiency by directly using a probability transition matrix to derive the mem-
      bership matrix from the anchor label matrix, eliminating the need for complex post-processing.This
070
      approach significantly reduces computational overhead and streamlines the entire clustering process
071
      while maintaining interpretability. To prevent trivial solutions and ensure well-defined clusters, we
072   apply nuclear norm regularization to the membership matrix. Additionally, we apply a Schatten p-
073   norm regularization to the tensor formed by the membership matrices across different views, thereby
074   fully utilizing the complementary information between views and greatly improving clustering per-
075   formance. The main contributions of our work are as follows:
076
077          • We propose a novel approach using probability matrices to directly compute member-
078            ship matrices, avoiding the need for complex post-processing and enhancing clustering
079            interpretability. This simplification enhances clustering efficiency, particularly for large
080            datasets.
081
             • Our method incorporates both nuclear norm and Schatten p-norm regularization to ensure
082
               balanced and robust clustering results. The nuclear norm promotes clear clusters and pre-
083            vents trivial solutions, while the Schatten p-norm handles varied data distributions and
084            mitigates the impact of noisy views. These techniques contribute to high-quality clustering
085            outcomes.
086
087          • We conduct extensive experiments on multiple datasets to demonstrate the effectiveness and
088
               efficiency of our method. Results show that our approach outperforms existing methods in
               terms of both clustering accuracy and computational speed, highlighting its practical value
089
               for real-world applications.

      2.1   N UCLEAR N ORM IN M ULTI -V IEW C LUSTERING
095
096   In multi-view clustering, imbalanced sample allocation can lead to two extremes: overly concen-
097   trated clustering and overly dispersed clustering. In the case of overly concentrated clustering, all
098   data points are assigned to a single cluster. This results in a cluster assignment matrix where one
099   column has non-zero entries while the rest remain zero. Such a matrix structure reflects limited
100   diversity in the clustering, as the model essentially identifies only one cluster, providing little insight
101   into the underlying data structure. Conversely, in overly dispersed clustering, the data points are
102   evenly spread across all clusters, leading to a matrix where each column has equal entries. This
103   uniform distribution makes it hard to discern meaningful groupings because the clustering fails to
104
      differentiate between the data points based on their inherent similarities.
105   To address this issue, Yu et al. (2023) introduced the nuclear norm as a regularization term to pro-
106   mote a balanced distribution of samples across clusters. The nuclear norm ∥Y ∥∗ , defined as the
107   sum of the singular values of the matrix Y , helps prevent extreme cases of over-concentration or
      over-dispersion by encouraging a more evenly distributed clustering. Formally, the nuclear norm is

      2.2   A NCHOR G RAPH -BASED M ULTI -V IEW C LUSTERING
134
135   Anchor graph-based methods are widely adopted in multi-view clustering due to their ability to
136   reduce computational complexity while maintaining performance. These methods select a smaller
137   set of anchor points from the original data, constructing an n × m anchor graph that improves
138   efficiency, especially in large datasets (Li et al., 2023). The concept of anchor points in multi-view
139   clustering was first introduced by Liu et al. (2010), laying the groundwork for later advancements.
140   Building on this, Li et al. (2015) proposed methods that replace the original data matrix with an
141   anchor graph for each view and apply spectral clustering.
142   Further developments have expanded the use of anchor graphs in more sophisticated ways. Li et al.
143   (2024c) introduced tensor-anchor graph factorization, which combines tensor structures with anchor
144   points to capture more complex multi-view relationships. This method leverages both tensors and
145   anchor points to enhance the clustering process. Li et al. (2023) proposed a depth tensor factor-
146   ization method, building on matrix factorization techniques to uncover deeper, hidden information
147   within anchor graph tensors. While this approach improves the ability to capture underlying data
148   structures, its computational efficiency remains suboptimal when compared to other methods that
149
      more effectively leverage multi-view data.However, the reliance on anchor graphs rather than raw
      data provides only limited gains in computational efficiency. Yu et al. (2023) introduced a proba-
150
      bilistic bipartite graph by combining original and anchor data to directly derive a consensus matrix
151
      from the anchor label matrix. Although this method reduces computational complexity by using an-
152   chor points, it fails to fully exploit the complementary information between different views, which
153   can limit overall clustering performance.

      3.1   M OTIVATION AND O BJECTIVES
163
164   Multi-view clustering aims to enhance clustering accuracy and robustness by leveraging comple-
165   mentary information from multiple data representations. However, many existing methods lack
166   interpretability, making it difficult to understand how clusters are formed, especially when deal-
167   ing with complex datasets. In addition, traditional methods often suffer from high computational
168   complexity and require extensive post-processing, particularly for large-scale data.
169   To address these challenges, we propose a method that uses probability transition matrices com-
170   bined with anchor label matrices to directly generate membership matrices. This approach not only
171   simplifies the clustering process but also provides more straightforward and interpretable results by
172   clearly showing how the anchor points relate to the final clusters, eliminating the need for complex
173   post-processing.
174   Our method begins by selecting anchor points for each view from the original data matrix X v ∈
175   Rn×pv , where n is the number of data points and pv is the dimensionality of the v-th view. The
176   anchor points U v ∈ Rm×dv , with m ≪ n, are a subset of representative points that capture the
177   data distribution in a more compact form, thereby reducing computational complexity. By selecting
178   a smaller set of anchors, we efficiently approximate the full dataset while retaining its structural
179   properties.
180   Next, using the method in Nie et al. (2023), we construct bipartite similarity graphs that map the
181   relationships between the data points in X v and the anchor points in U v . The bipartite graph is
182   characterized by the similarity matrix B v ∈ Rn×m , which encodes the relationships between the
183   n data points and the m anchors for each view. Specifically, the bipartite graph is constructed as
184   follows:

356   3.3   C OMPLEXITY A NALYSIS
357
358   The proposed FTMVC-APTM algorithm consists of several stages: (1) Compute the similar bipartite
359   graph B v ;(2) updating the anchor label matrix Z v ; (3) updating the membership matrix F v for each
360   view and the auxiliary variable J ;
361   B v needs to be computed only once and its computational complexity is O(nmV ).In the update
362   phase, let the number of iterations be t. The first step is to update the anchor label matrix Z v . This
363   step has a complexity of O(nmcV ), where n is the number of data points and m is the number
364   of anchor points. Next, the update of the membership matrix F v requires matrix multiplications,
365   resulting in a complexity of O(nm2 cV ). The auxiliary variable J , used for the Schatten p-norm
366   regularization, adds an additional complexity of O(2V nclog(V c) + V 2 cn), due to the computations
367   involving the norm regularization.Considering that V, c are small constants, m ≪ n,thus the com-
368
      putational complexity of the scheme MVCt should be O(t(nm2 cV + nmcV + V 2 cn)), which is
      proportional to the magnitude of n, showing the efficiency of the FTMVC-APTM.
369
370   The appendix includes a comparison of the computational complexity and running time of the
371   FTMVC-APTM with the comparison methods to demonstrate the efficiency of our method again.

463   4.5   PARAMETER A NALYSIS
464
465   We conducted experiments to evaluate the influence of key parameters on our clustering method.
466   Specifically, we analyzed how varying the Schatten p-norm parameters β and p, as well as the
467   anchor rate and the nuclear norm regularization parameter λ, affects clustering performance.
468   As shown in Figure 1, the clustering accuracy remains relatively stable across different values of β
469   and p, demonstrating the robustness of our method to these parameters. However, we observe that
470   the optimal performance is generally achieved when p is between 0.4 and 0.6.
471
      In Figure 2, we examine the impact of the anchor rate and λ on clustering accuracy. The results
472   indicate that the accuracy is not significantly affected by changes in the anchor rate, highlighting
473   the robustness of our method to this parameter. For the BBCSport, MSRC, and Yale datasets, the
474   optimal performance is achieved when λ is between 0.5 and 1.0. In contrast, the WebKB dataset
475   achieves optimal results when λ is between 1.75 and 2.25.

      In this paper, we proposed a Fast Tensor-Based Multi-View Clustering with Anchor Probabil-
      ity Transition Matrix (FTMVC-APTM), which simplifies the clustering process by directly using
532
      anchor-based probability transition matrices. This eliminates the need for complex post-processing
533
      and improves computational efficiency. By integrating nuclear norm and Schatten p-norm regular-
534   ization, the method ensures well-defined clusters while fully utilizing complementary information
535   from multiple views. Extensive experiments show that FTMVC-APTM consistently outperforms ex-
536   isting methods in terms of both accuracy and speed, particularly on large datasets. Future work may
537   focus on further optimizing the method towards a parameter-free approach, reducing the reliance
538   on manual parameter tuning and improving its adaptability across diverse datasets. In conclusion,
539   FTMVC-APTM provides an efficient and scalable solution to multi-view clustering, making it suit-
      able for various practical scenarios.
