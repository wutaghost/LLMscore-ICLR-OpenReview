011                                                A BSTRACT
012
               We reveal that feed-forward network (FFN) layers significantly contribute to the
013
               latencies of Vision Transformers (ViTs). This effect scales up quickly as the
014            model size escalates, and hence presents a major opportunity in efficiency optimiza-
015            tion for ViTs via structural reparameterization on FFN layers. However, directly
016            reparameterizing the linear projection weights is difficult due to the non-linear
017            activation in between. In this work, we propose an innovative channel idle mecha-
018            nism that establishes a linear pathway through the activation function, facilitating
019            structural reparameterization on FFN layers during inference. Consequently, we
020            present a family of efficient ViTs embedded with the introduced mechanism called
021            ReParameterizable Vision TransFormers (RePaFormers). This technique brings
022
               remarkable latency reductions with small sacrifices (sometimes gains) in accuracy
               across various MetaFormer-structured architectures investigated in the experiments.
023
               The benefits of this method scale consistently with model sizes, demonstrating
024
               increasing efficiency improvements and narrowing performance gaps as model
025            sizes grow. Specifically, the RePaFormer variants for DeiT-Base and Swin-Base
026            achieve 67.5% and 49.7% throughput accelerations with minor changes in top-1
027            accuracy (-0.4% and -0.9%), respectively. Further improvements in speed and
028            accuracy are expected on even larger ViT models. In particular, the RePaFormer
029            variants for ViT-Large and ViT-Huge enjoy 66.8% and 68.7% inference speed-ups
030            with +1.7% and +1.1% higher top-1 accuracies, respectively. RePaFormer is the
031            first to employ structural reparameterization on FFN layers to expedite ViTs to
032            our best knowledge, and we believe that it represents an auspicious direction for
033
               efficient ViTs. Codes are provided in the supplementary material.

      Vision Transformer (ViT) (Dosovitskiy et al., 2021) and its advanced variants (Touvron et al., 2021;
037
      Liu et al., 2021; Tolstikhin et al., 2021; Ryoo et al., 2021; Yu et al., 2022; Liu et al., 2022; Dehghani
038
      et al., 2023) have achieved remarkable performance on various computer vision tasks. However, the
039   high computational cost and memory demand of ViTs hinder their wide deployment in real-world
040   scenarios, especially in computing resource-constrained environments.
041
042
      To improve efficiency for ViTs, several techniques have been proposed, such as token pruning (Rao
      et al., 2021; Liang et al., 2021; Kong et al., 2022a;b; Fayyaz et al., 2022) and token merging (Bolya
043
      et al., 2023; Zong et al., 2022; Marin et al., 2023; Xu et al., 2024; Kim et al., 2024) methods that
044
      gradually reduce the number of image tokens as the layer goes deep; hierarchical architectures (Fan
045   et al., 2021; Pan et al., 2021; Liu et al., 2021; Dong et al., 2022; Ryali et al., 2023) that extract
046   feature information at multiple scales; hybrid architectures (Mehta & Rastegari, 2022a; Chen et al.,
047   2022a; Maaz et al., 2022; Li et al., 2022; Zhang et al., 2023) that embed efficient convolutional neural
048   networks (CNNs) into ViTs. Meanwhile, knowledge distillation methods (Touvron et al., 2021; Hao
049   et al., 2022; Wu et al., 2022; Chen et al., 2022b) are introduced to further optimize and improve
050   efficient ViTs’ performance. However, these efficient ViT methods overlook a powerful network
051   simplification technique: structural reparameterization.
052   Structural reparameterization (Ding et al., 2019; 2021b; Zhu et al., 2023) is typically utilized in
053   CNNs to transform a network’s structure during different phases of training and testing. Specifically,
      structural reparameterization merges multi-branch convolutions or adjacent linear projections via

      However, since structural reparameterization relies on linear algebra operations to simplify the
088
      network structure, the non-linear activation function between the two linear transformations makes
089   reparameterization infeasible on FFN layers. In addition, the LayerNorm (Lei Ba et al., 2016) in the
090   FFN layer prevents further reparameterizing the normalization and shortcut into linear projection
091   weights due to the sample-specific nature of LayerNorm.
092
      To address the aforementioned challenges, we introduce an innovative channel idle mechanism. In
093
      particular, in each FFN layer, only a small subset of feature channels undergo the activation function
094
      to provide necessary nonlinearity while the rest channels remain idle, as shown in Figure 1(b).
095   Consequently, these idle channels bridge a linear pathway through the activation function, facilitating
096   structural reparameterization during inference. Moreover, inspired by Yao et al. (2021), we substitute
097   the LayerNorm with BatchNorm (Ioffe & Szegedy, 2015) and add another BatchNorm before the
098   second linear projection. These BatchNorms can be reparameterized into their corresponding linear
099   projection weights, which further allows reparameterization of the shortcut.
100
      With the proposed channel idle mechanism, a family of ReParameterizable Vision Transformers
101   (RePaFormers) are developed, whose FFN layers can be reparameterized to condensed structures
102   during inference as Figure 1(c) shows. RePaFormers achieve ferocious real-time accelerations of
103   up to 133.4% post-reparameterization. Extensive experiments on various MetaFormer-structured
104   backbones have validated the effectiveness of our method, demonstrating its potential to enhance the
105   practical utility of MetaFormer-structured models in resource-constrained environments. Moreover, as
106   Figure 2 illustrates, the experimental results further indicate that our method delivers more significant
107   acceleration and narrower performance disparity as the model complexity increases, highlighting the
      potential of applying our method on large foundation models.

127     In conclusion, the contributions of our work are threefold: 1) We discover that FFN layers dominate
128     in the total latency of various MetaFormer-structured models. The proportion of FFN layers in
129     the computation significantly increases as the model size grows. 2) We propose a novel channel
130
        idle mechanism to construct a linear pathway in the FFN layer during training, which enables
        reparameterizing the linear projection weights during testing without accuracy loss. 3) With the
131
        proposed mechanism, we develop highly efficient RePaFormer models based on existing MetaFormer-
132
        structured architectures. Our approach achieves greater efficiency and a narrower accuracy gap when
133     the model size escalates. To our best knowledge, RePaFormer is the first method that successfully
134     applies structural reparameterization on FFN layers for efficient ViTs, and achieves significant
135     acceleration (∼68%) while having positive gains in accuracy (1∼2%) instead of accuracy drops, on
136     large and huge ViTs.

140     2.1                     E FFICIENT V ISION T RANSFORMER M ETHODS
141    Vision Transformer (ViT) (Dosovitskiy et al., 2021) adapts the Transformer (Vaswani et al., 2017)
142    architecture for computer vision, achieving success on various computer vision tasks. However,
143    ViT suffers a substantial computational complexity. To alleviate the computational burden, several
144    techniques that focus on structural design for efficient ViTs have been proposed. Spatial-wise token
145    reduction methods are developed to identify less important tokens and subsequently prune (Rao
146    et al., 2021; Liang et al., 2021; Kong et al., 2022a; Fayyaz et al., 2022; Xu et al., 2022; Meng et al.,
147    2022; Tang et al., 2022; Xu et al., 2023) or merge (Bolya et al., 2023; Zong et al., 2022; Marin
148
       et al., 2023; Xu et al., 2024; Kim et al., 2024) them during inference. As a result, the number of
       tokens participating in the self-attention computation is reduced. Meanwhile, hybrid architectures
149
       that combine self-attentions with computationally efficient convolutions (Graham et al., 2021; Mehta
150
       & Rastegari, 2022a; Chen et al., 2022a; Li et al., 2022; Cai et al., 2023; Vasu et al., 2023; Zhang
151    et al., 2023; Shaker et al., 2023) are introduced to reduce the computationally expensive self-attention
152    operations while introducing regional biases into ViTs. In addition to hybrid ViTs, MetaFormer
153    (Yu et al., 2022) figures out that ViTs benefit from their architectural design, which consists of one
154    token mixer layer and one multi-layer perception layer, and the token mixer can be replaced by more
155    efficient operations, such as average pooling (Yu et al., 2022) or linear projection (Tolstikhin et al.,
156    2021). However, these approaches overlook the structural reparameterization method, which can
157    effectively compress a network that contains many consecutive linear transformations, such as FFN
158    layers in ViTs. Our work is the first to apply structural reparameterization on FFN layers for ViTs.
159
        2.2                     S TRUCTURAL R EPARAMETERIZATION
160
161     Structural reparameterization is an effective network simplification technique that is typically em-
        ployed in multi-branch CNNs (Ding et al., 2019; Guo et al., 2020; Ding et al., 2021a;b). It converts


                                                                                                                                3
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


162
      an over-parameterized network block into a compressed structure during testing, thereby reducing
163   the model complexity and increasing the speed for the inference stage. For instance, after reparame-
164   terizing its multi-branch convolutions and shortcuts into a single branch, RepVGG-B0 (Ding et al.,
165   2021b) achieves 71% speed-up with no accuracy loss. Although some recent studies claim to adopt
166   structural reparameterization for enhancing ViTs’ efficiency (Vasu et al., 2023; Wang et al., 2024;
167   Tan et al., 2024), they primarily construct a hybrid architecture consisting of both convolutions and
168   self-attentions and only perform reparameterization on the convolutional part. A recent state-of-the-
169   art method, SLAB (Guo et al., 2024), proposes to progressively substitute LayerNorms in ViTs with
170   BatchNorms and subsequently reparameterize BatchNorms into linear projection weights. Unlike
171
      these methods, we are the first to apply structural reparameterization on FFN layers.

174   3.1   M ETA F ORMER - STRUCTURED M ODELS
175
176   We start by revisiting the MetaFormer (Yu et al., 2022) architecture, which can be regarded as
177
      a general architecture for a variety of ViT models. Specifically, given an input X ∈ RN ×C to a
      MetaFormer block, where N represents the number of tokens and C denotes the number of feature
178
      channels, the input X is sequentially processed by a token mixer (TokenMixer) layer and a feed-
179
      forward network (FFN) layer, with a pre-layer Layer Normalization (LN) (Lei Ba et al., 2016) and a
180   shortcut (He et al., 2016) for each layer as
181
182
                                         Y = TokenMixer(LN(X)) + X,
                                                                                                           (1)
183                                      Z = FFN(LN(Y)) + Y,
184   where Y is the token mixer output and Z is the FFN output as well as the input to the next block. The
185   token mixer is utilized to aggregate image tokens, which can be multi-head self-attention (Dosovitskiy
186   et al., 2021), average pooling (Yu et al., 2022), convolution (Li et al., 2023), etc.
187
      In each FFN layer, the LN normalized feature Y is processed by two linear projections with a
188
      non-linear activation function in between as
189
190                           Z = FFN(LN(Y)) + Y = Act(LN(Y)WIn )WOut + Y,                                 (2)
191            In     C×ρC      Out     ρC×C
      where W ∈ R            ,W ∈ R            are the linear projection weights, and Act(·) is usually the
192   GELU (Hendrycks & Gimpel, 2016) activation function. ρ is the FFN expansion ratio, which is
193   typically set to 4. The biases are omitted for simplicity since they are inherently linear and do not
194   interfere with the reparameterization process.
195
      Figure 1(a) illustrates the MetaFormer block. We classify all the models that adhere to this specific
196
      framework as MetaFormer-structured models and use this term throughout this paper.

      In this paper, we investigate the latency compositions of various MetaFormer-structured models and
      observe that FFN layers significantly contribute to the overall latency. The observations highlight the
532
      critical need for accelerating FFN layers to enhance the efficiency of ViTs, where structural reparam-
533
      eterization emerges as a potential solution. We introduce a novel channel idle mechanism to facilitate
534   the reparameterization of FFN layers during inference. The proposed mechanism is employed
535   on various MetaFormer-structured models, resulting in a family of RePaFormers. RePaFormers
536   demonstrate consistent scalability with more accelerations and narrower accuracy disparities as the
537   backbone model size escalates. Importantly, RePaFormer achieves accuracy gains while improving
538   the inference speed on large-scale ViT backbone. We believe that RePaFormer presents a promising
539   direction for expediting ViTs and we invite the community to further explore its effectiveness on even
      larger MetaFormer-structured foundation models.

775   B    L IMITATIONS
776
777   Despite the exceptional performance of RePaFormers on large backbone models, there is a notable
778   decrease in accuracy as the model size shrinks. For example, as demonstrated in Table 2, the accuracy
779   of RePa-DeiT-Tiny decreases significantly from 72.1% to 64.2%. This performance drop is primarily
780   attributed to the reduced nonlinearity in the backbone, which is a consequence of keeping channels
781   idle. In smaller models, both the number of layers and the number of feature channels are limited,
782   resulting in substantially fewer activated channels compared to larger models. After applying the
783
      channel idle mechanism with a high idle ratio (e.g., 75%), tiny models would lack sufficient non-linear
      transformations. However, as the model size increases, both the number of layers and feature channels
784
      expand, enhancing the model’s robustness and mitigating the impact of reduced nonlinearity.
785
786   In conclusion, while our method may not be optimally suited for tiny models, it significantly enhances
787   the performance of large MetaFormer-structured models. We sincerely invite the research community
788   to further investigate and validate the effectiveness of our approach on large foundational models, such
789
      as SAM (Kirillov et al., 2023) or GPT (Radford et al., 2019; Brown et al., 2020). This exploration
      could provide valuable insights into the scalability and adaptability of our method across various
790
      advanced computational frameworks.

795
      As explained in Appendix B, tiny-size models are less robust and rely on sufficient nonlinearities
796
      for a decent feature extraction capability. To validate this, we further present the performance of
797   tiny-size ViT models with various idle ratios. As Table 8 shows, our RePaFormers demonstrate

800   shows effectiveness in these cases.

803   D    C OMPARISON AGAINST R EP VGG- STYLE REPARAMETERIZATION
804
805   The differences between our structural reparameterization method and RepVGG-style (Ding et al.,
806   2021b) structural reparameterization are threefold:
807
808         1. Different reparameterization solutions: The key difference is that RepVGG reparameter-
809            izes horizontally across parallel convolutional kernels, while RePaFormer reparameterizes
               vertically on consecutive linear projection weights.


                                                         15
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


810
               For instance, RepVGG reparameterizes two parallel convolutional branches with kernels
811            W1Conv and W2Conv by summing them:
812
                                               Conv
813                                           WRep  = W1Conv + W2Conv .                                (8)
814
815
               On the contrary, as demonstrated in Equation 6, RePaFormer reparameterizes two consecu-
               tive projection weights W1FFN and W2FFN by multiplying them:
816
                                                 FFN
817                                             WRep = W1FFN · W2FFN .                                 (9)
818
819           (In the above example, we omit the BatchNorm and suppose W1Conv and W2Conv have been
820           padded to the same shape.)
821        2. Different target components: RepVGG and RepVGG-style methods apply reparameteri-
822           zation to multi-branch convolutional layers in CNNs, while our RePaFormer targets FFN
823           layers in ViTs. Their application targets are distinct.
824        3. Different scopes: Although some previous works (Vasu et al., 2023; Wang et al., 2024) have
825           attempted to adapt RepVGG-style reparameterization on ViTs by incorporating multi-branch
826           convolutions into the ViT backbone, they only reparameterize the convolutional parts. The
827           main scope of these works is to construct novel mobile-friendly architectures. In contrast,
828           our method is the first to apply structural reparameterization to FFN layers and accelerate
829           existing ViTs/MetaFormers of all sizes.
830
831   Moreover, our channel idle mechanism cannot be regarded as a special case of a dual-branch structure
832
      in RepVGG. In RepVGG, all branches must be linear so that they can be reparameterized, whereas in
      our approach, one branch is linear while the other one is nonlinear.
