039                                                                A BSTRACT
040
041                Diffusion models have emerged as a formidable tool for training-free conditional
042                generation. However, a key hurdle in inference-time guidance techniques is the
043                need for compute-heavy backpropagation through the diffusion network for estimat-
044                ing the guidance direction. Moreover, these techniques often require handcrafted
045
                   parameter tuning on a case-by-case basis. Although some recent works have intro-
                   duced minimal compute methods for linear inverse problems, a generic lightweight
046
                   guidance solution to both linear and non-linear guidance problems is still missing.
047
                   To this end, we propose Dreamguider, a method that enables inference-time guid-
048                ance without compute-heavy backpropagation through the diffusion network. The
049                key idea is to regulate the gradient flow through a time-varying factor. Moreover,
050                we propose an empirical guidance scale that works for a wide variety of tasks,
051                hence removing the need for handcrafted parameter tuning. We further introduce
052                an effective lightweight augmentation strategy that significantly boosts the perfor-
053                mance during inference-time guidance. We present experiments using Dreamguider
                   on multiple tasks across multiple datasets and models to show the effectiveness of

058   1   I NTRODUCTION
059
060   Generative modeling utilizing Denoising Diffusion Probabilistic Models (DDPMs) Sohl-Dickstein
061   et al. (2015); Ho et al. (2020); Dhariwal & Nichol (2021); Song et al. (2021b) has massively improved
062   over the past few years. Multiple works have extended the use of diffusion models for text-to-image
063   synthesis Balaji et al. (2022); Rombach et al. (2021); Saharia et al. (2022b), 3D synthesis Poole
064   et al. (2022); Jun & Nichol (2023), video generation Ho et al. (2022); Blattmann et al. (2023); Wu
065
      et al. (2023a), as well as for conditioning to solve inverse problems. Moreover, like conditional
      generative adversarial networks (GANs)Goodfellow et al. (2020); Arjovsky et al. (2017), DDPMs
066
      can be adapted to tasks based on a labels Rombach et al. (2021); Dhariwal & Nichol (2021) or visual
067
      prior-based conditioning Saharia et al. (2022a). However, like conditional GANs Wang et al. (2018);
068   Radford et al. (2015), DDPMs also need to be trained with annotated pairs of labels and instructions
069   to obtain satisfactory results. This poses a limitation in many cases where there is a lack of paired
070   data to train large diffusion models. Due to this reason, there has been recent interest in models that
071   can perform conditional generation without the need for explicit training Yu et al. (2023); Chan et al.
072   (2016); Nguyen et al. (2017); Graikos et al. (2022).
073
      Progressing towards this direction is prior research in plug-and-play models. First introduced in
074   Nguyen et al. (2017), the initial research on plug-and-play models Nguyen et al. (2017); Graikos
075   et al. (2022) enabled conditional sampling from GANs trained with unlabeled data. For this, a
076   pre-trained classifier Simonyan & Zisserman (2014); Hossain et al. (2019) or a captioning model was
077   used to estimate the deviation between the GAN-generated image and a given label, and based on
078   this deviation, the GAN input noise was modulated until the generated sample satisfied the given
079   text or class label. A similar approach that has been attempted for diffusion models to facilitate
080   conditional sampling from unconditional diffusion models is classifier guidance Dhariwal & Nichol
081   (2021); Graikos et al. (2022), where a noise-robust classifier is trained along with the diffusion
082
      model to guide the sampling towards a particular direction. However, classifier guidance brings
      in the computational costs of training a classifier, which is often undesirable. Some recent works
083
      have performed conditional generation without explicit training for the condition by utilizing the
084
      implicit guidance capabilities of the diffusion model Chung et al. (2023b); Yu et al. (2023); Nair
085   et al. (2023); Bansal et al. (2023); Chung et al. (2023a). Diffusion posterior sampling (DPS) Chung
086   et al. (2023b) proposed a technique of using an L2 norm-based loss function to solve linear inverse
087   problems using unconditional diffusion models. However, DPS often requires a large number of
088   sampling steps for photorealistic results. Freedom Yu et al. (2023), yet another work, proposed the
089   use of general loss functions during sampling to achieve training-free conditional sampling. Some
090   variants of DPS have also been proposed in the literature Song et al. (2023). All the aforementioned
091   loss-guided posterior sampling techniques involve a guidance function at each timestep that requires
092   backpropagation through the diffusion UNet. Recently, He et al. (2023) proposed Manifold Preserving
093
      Guided Diffusion Models (MGD) that remove the need for backpropagating through the diffusion
      U-Net by performing a gradient descent with respect to the Minimum Mean Square Error (MMSE).
094
      Although MGD He et al. (2023) works remarkably well for linear tasks that require more guidance
095
      towards the start of the guidance process, it may fail in some tasks where guidance happens earlier,
096   for example, face semantics-to-image and sketch-to-image, where stronger guidance is required
097   from a much earlier stage. Moreover, like Yu et al. (2023); Nair et al. (2023), MGD also requires a
098   case-by-case handcrafted parameter. Hence, a generic lightweight method that works well for both
099   linear and non-linear guidance functions is still missing. Moreover, the need to find a handcrafted
100   guidance parameter on a case-by-case basis still remains an open challenge.
101
      In this paper, we introduce a new framework that can adaptively perform zero-shot generation using
102   diffusion models without the need for any manual intervention by the user. We found a rather
103   simple fix to the problem during the initial timesteps of diffusion, i.e., by utilizing the gradient
104   with respect to the diffusion output noise in initial steps of inference. Combined with the guidance
105   with respect to the MMSE estimate, we found that the combination generalizes well to tasks that
106   require guidance at very early stages of guidance. Figure 2 presents the visualization of our approach
107   over existing works present in the literature. Utilizing the correction term along with the correction
      with respect to the MMSE estimate significantly boosts the performance in non-linear tasks. We

158   2.1   T RAINING - FREE C ONDITIONAL S AMPLING USING D IFFUSION M ODELS
159
160   Recently, there has been a rise in multiple works that propose utilizing unconditional diffusion
161   models for conditional sampling Nair et al. (2023); Bansal et al. (2023); Chung et al. (2023c);
      Kawar et al. (2022). The earlier works proposed solving linear inverse problems using diffusion

162
      models with the help of priors dependent on the inverse transform of degradation. Recently, diffusion
163   posterior sampling Chung et al. (2023b) considered the degradation to be conditioned on a Gaussian
164   distribution given any intermediate timestep and derived an L2 norm-based regularization at each
165   intermediate timestep to solve for linear inverse problems. Recent works such as Freedom Yu et al.
166   (2023) explored an energy-based perspective and extended guidance to non-linear functions using
167   general loss functions. Universal diffusion guidance Aggarwal et al. (2018) extended this guidance
168   process to stable diffusion and improved the performance by using forward-backward guidance. More
169   recent works, such as manifold-guided diffusion He et al. (2023), further proposed to constrain the
170   manifold space by projecting for the latent space alone.

225   2.4   S HORTCOMINGS OF THE E XISTING M ETHODS
226
      Although the energy-based guidance theory supports guidance as a function of the current latent
227   estimate, almost all loss-based guidance techniques derive the distance function as a function of
228   xt rather than xt−1 and derive the gradient based on the previous sample. Although this approach
229   works for many tasks, it requires backpropagating through the neural network and modeling the score
230   function for the guidance correction term. This limits the use of classifier guidance since existing
231   diffusion architectures that produce photorealistic results are often very bulky. One can see why the
232   existing framework utilizes the derivative with respect to the previous sample works by taking a better
233   look at Equation (5). As we can see, a reciprocal distance over the previous timestep diffusion latent
234   xt is a perfectly valid distance guidance function. In the next section, we elaborate on Dreamguider.

296   3.3   D IFFERENTIAL AUGMENTATION C LASSIFIER G UIDANCE
297
      A common practice while performing classifier guidance to augment diffusion models with specific
298
      regularization for guidance is to use the noisy estimate at timestep t and utilize it to compute the
299   loss function to regularize the current prediction. However, in many cases, such guidance can
300   give results with artifacts and color shifts, as portrayed in Figure 3 and Figure 5, due to excessive
301   guidance or insufficient guidance at intermediate timesteps that shift the results off manifold or
302   cause color shifts. One effective solution for this is to imitate different versions of artifacts or
303   color shifts on both the source image and the target image and utilize these augmented versions
304   for a boost in performance. Hence, to perform guidance with a much more robust guidance loss,
305   we introduce DiffuseAugment, an augmentation strategy for diffusion guidance during inference
306   time. Specifically, given an intermediate sample xt and condition y, we augment x̂t and y with
307
      differentiable augmentations denoted by
308                                                         \hat {x}^{aug}_t,y^{aug}= T(\hat {x}^{aug}_t,y^{aug}).                                                                                                                      (15)
309
310   We choose three different types of augmentations for T comprising random cutouts, random trans-
311   lations, and color saturations. Please note that the augmentation of y is dependent on the input
312   signal. For label-based conditioning such as identity or text, we do not perform augmentation for
313   y. For image space augmentations, we augment y with the same random augmentation as that of
314   x. While computing the effective loss, we find the average across all augmentations. We find that
315   DiffuseAugment significantly boosts the sampling fidelity and quality of the reconstructed image.
316   We present these results in Section 5.

490   6    L IMITATIONS AND F UTURE W ORKS
491
492   Although we illustrated the working across various tasks for pixel space diffusion models, the direct
493   approach cannot be used for latent diffusion models for the task of linear inverse problems, and
494   one might have to apply multiple steps of time travel sampling to fix this issue, making a large
495
      computational overhead of the overall sampling time. We emphasize that this problem arises due to
      the reconstruction error in the VAE that encodes the image to the latent space. In the future, we will
496
      attempt to improve upon this with better optimization techniques. Moreover, although the proposed
497
      empirical estimate based on distance over gradients works for most tasks and shows the existence of
498   an optimal parameter estimate, a thorough mathematical evaluation and the most optimal parameters
499   are still missing. We leave this problem up to future works to estimate the optimal guidance parameter.

502   7    C ONCLUSION
503
504   In this paper, we proposed an improvement to existing loss-guided techniques for zero-shot conditional
505   generation with an unconditional diffusion model. Specifically, we proposed a sampling technique
506
      that removes the need to backpropagate through the diffusion U-Net in order to tackle sampling for
      general inverse problems. We also present an empirical function for automatic scaling parameters
507
      that removes the need for manual scaling parameter tuning, which was previously a huge hurdle
508
      in using classifier-free guidance. The newly proposed scaling parameter also removes the need
509   for model-specific tuning of start and end guidance steps. We also introduced a differentiable data
510   augmentation method that significantly improves the sampling fidelity. We illustrated the working of
511   our method across 4 linear and 3 non-linear tasks across faces and real image domains. Our sampling
512   technique produces photorealistic samples with much lower sampling time and higher fidelity than
513   existing methods.
