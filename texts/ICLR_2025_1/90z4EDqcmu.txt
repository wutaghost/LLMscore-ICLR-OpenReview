009                                              A BSTRACT
010
011            In this work, we introduce FlexGen, a flexible framework designed to generate
012            controllable and consistent multi-view images, conditioned on a single-view im-
013            age, or a text prompt, or both. FlexGen tackles the challenges of controllable
014            multi-view synthesis through additional conditioning on 3D-aware text annota-
015            tions. We utilize the strong reasoning capabilities of GPT-4V to generate 3D-
016            aware text annotations. By analyzing four orthogonal views of an object arranged
017            as tiled multi-view images, GPT-4V can produce text annotations that include 3D-
018
               aware information with spatial relationship. By integrating the control signal with
               proposed adaptive dual-control module, our model can generate multi-view im-
019
               ages that correspond to the specified text. FlexGen supports multiple controllable
020
               capabilities, allowing users to modify text prompts to generate reasonable and
021            corresponding unseen parts. Additionally, users can influence attributes such as
022            appearance and material properties, including metallic and roughness. Extensive
023            experiments demonstrate that our approach offers enhanced multiple controllabil-
024            ity, marking a significant advancement over existing multi-view diffusion models.
025            This work has substantial implications for fields requiring rapid and flexible 3D
026            content creation, including game development, animation, and virtual reality.

029   1   I NTRODUCTION
030
031   Recent progress in generative models (Song et al., 2020b; Ho et al., 2020) has significantly ad-
032   vanced 2D content creation, thanks to the rapid increase in 2D data volumes. However, 3D content
033
      creation remains challenging due to the limited accessibility of 3D assets, which are essential for
      diverse downstream applications, including game modeling (Gregory, 2018; Lewis & Jacobson,
034
      2002), computer animation (Parent, 2012; Lasseter, 1987), and virtual reality (Schuemie et al.,
035
      2001). Previous 3D generation methods primarily concentrate on optimization-based techniques us-
036   ing multi-view posed images (Wang et al., 2021; Yariv et al., 2021; Ge et al., 2023; Verbin et al.,
037   2022), or employ SDS-based distillation approaches derived from 2D generative models (Lin et al.,
038   2023; Poole et al., 2022; Liang et al., 2023). Although effective, these methods often demand sig-
039   nificant optimization time, limiting their practicality in real-world applications.
040
      Multi-view diffusion models (Liu et al., 2023; Long et al., 2024; Shi et al., 2023a;b; Wang & Shi,
041
      2023) have demonstrated the potential of pre-trained 2D generative models for 3D content creation
042   through the synthesis of consistent multi-view images. Despite their promising performance, the
043   controllable generation of multi-view images remains under-explored. Most existing multi-view
044   diffusion models typically rely on a single-view image, which lacks 3D-aware controllable guidance
045   and proves insufficient for robust multi-view image generation. For example, the generation of
046   unseen regions continues to pose a significant challenge, often simply replicating information from
047   the input view to unseen areas.
048   Several studies focus on conditional 3D generation. For instance, Coin3D (Dong et al., 2024) utilizes
049   basic shapes as 3D-aware guidance, whereas Clay (Zhang et al., 2024) leverages sparse point clouds
050   and 3D bounding boxes. However, these guidance methods are not user-friendly. Other research (Xu
051   et al., 2024b;a) employs sparse multi-view images as 3D-aware guidance for 3D generation. These
052   methods effectively supplement most unseen regions due to the incorporation of additional views,
053   thereby achieving promising results. Nevertheless, acquiring sparse multi-view images is not always
      feasible in practice. These approaches typically depend on a pre-trained multi-view diffusion model

157   2.1   D IFFUSION M ODELS FOR M ULTI - VIEW S YNTHESIS
158
159   Recent research has extensively explored the generation of multi-view images using diffusion mod-
160   els to achieve efficient and 3D-consistent results. These efforts include both text-based methods,
161   such as MVDiffusion (Deng et al., 2023), MVDream (Shi et al., 2023b) and ImageDream (Wang &
      Shi, 2023), and image-based methods like SyncDreamer (Liu et al., 2023), Wonder3D (Long et al.,


                                                        3
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


162
      2024) and Zero123++ (Shi et al., 2023a). MVDiffusion, for instance, leverages text conditioning
163   to simultaneously generate all images with a global transformer, facilitating cross-view interactions.
164   Similarly, MVDream incorporates a self-attention layer to capture cross-view dependencies, en-
165   suring consistency across different views. For image-based approaches, SyncDreamer constructs
166   a volume feature from the multi-view latent representation to produce consistent multi-view color
167   images. Wonder3D enhances the quality of 3D results by explicitly encoding geometric information
168   and employing cross-domain diffusion. Several methods (Tang et al., 2024; Zhang et al.; Zheng
169   et al., 2024; Xu et al.) adopt these approaches to first generate multi-view images of an object and
170   then reconstruct the 3D shape from these views using sparse reconstruction techniques. Despite
171
      achieving reasonable results, these methods still face limitations in controllable generation.
172
173   2.2   C ONTROLLABLE G ENERATIVE MODELS
174
      In recent years, adding conditional control to generative models has garnered increasing attention
175
      for enabling controllable generation. These efforts span both 2D and 3D domains. For instance,
176   several 2D methods (Hess, 2013; Brooks et al., 2023; Gafni et al., 2022; Kim et al., 2022; Par-
177   mar et al., 2023) focus on text-guided control by adjusting prompts or manipulating CLIP features.
178   Additionally, ControlNet (Zhang et al., 2023) allows for a series of 2D image hints for control
179   tasks through a parallel model architecture. However, similar controllable capabilities in 3D gen-
180   eration (Bhat et al., 2024; Cohen-Bar et al., 2023; Pandey et al., 2024) remain largely inapplicable.
181   Coin3D (Dong et al., 2024) introduces a framework for generating 3D assets guided by basic shapes
182   and text. Nonetheless, basic 3D shapes are not user-friendly for general users, whereas text serves
183   as a more intuitive and accessible conditional input. In this work, we integrate text prompts into
184
      the multi-view diffusion model for controllable multi-view image generation, enabling the model to
      generate controllable unseen regions when a single-view image is simultaneously provided.
185

187   3     M ETHOD
188
189   FlexGen is a flexible multi-view generation framework that supports conditioning based on text,
190   single-view images, or a combination of both. By incorporating 3D-aware text annotations derived
191   from GPT-4V, our method effectively achieve controllable multi-view images generation, including
192
      reasonable unseen part generation, texture controllable generation and materials editing. We begin
      with a succinct problem formulation in Section 3.1. Then, we introduce how to annotate 3D-aware
193
      caption in Section 3.2. After that, adaptive dual-control module is introduced to add textual condition
194
      into the framework in Section 3.3. Finally, we introduce training and inference in Sectin 3.4. An
195   overview framework of FlexGen is shown in Figure 3.

      3.1   P ROBLEM F ORMULATION
198
199   Given a single-view image I or a user-defined prompt T that describes an object or both, our goal
200   is to develop a generative model G that produces a tiled image Iout . This image consists of a
201   2 × 2 layout, representing 4 views of an object - the front, left, back, and right - with each view
202   at a resolution of 512 × 512. The multi-view images are aligned with both the single-view image
203   and the prompt, ensuring consistency among them, as illustrated in Figure 1. Inspired by (Li
204   et al., 2023b), our generative model G is based on a large pre-trained text-to-image diffusion model.
205
      The design of the 2 × 2 image grid aligns better with the original data format used by the 2D
      diffusion model, facilitating the utilization of more prior knowledge. Regardless of the focal length
206
      and pose of the input image I, we consistently generate orthographic images with a fixed focal
207
      length and an elevation angle of 5◦ . For example, when processing an input image captured at an
208   elevation angle α and azimuth angle β, FlexGen generates multi-view images at azimuth angles
209   {β, β + 90◦ , β − 90◦ , β + 180◦ }, all with a fixed elevation of 5◦ .

296   3.4   T RAINING AND INFERENCE
297
298
      Building on the adaptive dual-control module, our framework accommodates both prompt and im-
      age conditions to guide the generation of multi-view images. To increase flexibility, we introduced
299
      a condition switcher during training that supports both single-mode and dual-mode conditions, al-
300
      lowing for seamless transitions between different input scenarios. With a configurable probability,
301   inputs can be left empty: when the text prompt is absent, it defaults to an empty string to ensure
302   uninterrupted processing by the model. Similarly, in the absence of an image input, we substitute it
303   with a black image.
304
      During inference, this design facilitates flexible and controllable multi-view generation. When both
305
      modalities are available, the image and prompt collaborate to provide complementary information,
306
      enriching the generated output. If only the image is supplied, the model functions in an image to
307   multi-view mode, generating multiple views based solely on the visual input. Conversely, when only
308   the text is available, the model operates as a text to multi-view generator, producing views that align
309   with the textual description.

526   5   C ONCLUSION
527
528   In this work, we present FlexGen, a multi-view diffusion model designed to generate consistent
529   and controllable multi-view images guided by a single image, text, or a combination of both. To
530   achieve this, we harness the powerful recognition capabilities of GPT-4V to perform 3D-aware text
531   annotations by reasoning over orthogonal views of an object, arranged as tiled multi-view images.
532   Additionally, we introduce an adaptive dual-control module that enables text-based conditioning
533   to be incorporated directly into the generation phase. By embedding spatial relationships, texture,
534   and material descriptions into the text annotations, we achieve enhanced control over the output.
535   Extensive experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of our proposed approach.
536   Limitations and future works. Although FlexGen introduces the innovative capability to jointly
537   control both image and text inputs, our method occasionally encounters difficulties with complex
538   user-defined instructions. This limitation likely stems from the constraints imposed by the availabil-
539   ity of high-quality datasets. In the future, we plan to expand the dataset size and enhance the control
      capabilities to more effectively accommodate intricate instructions.
