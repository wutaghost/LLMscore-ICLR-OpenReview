011                                               A BSTRACT
012
013            The forex market, with its daily trading volume reaching nearly trillions of dollars,
014            presents significant opportunities for the application of advanced predictive ana-
015            lytics. Traditional forex forecasting methods often overlook the interdependencies
016
               between currencies and struggle with long-range data dependencies, leading to
               challenges in capturing the true market dynamics. To overcome these limitations,
017
               we propose a novel Spatial-Temporal Graph Attention Network with Hierarchical
018
               Transformer (STGAT). Our model innovatively combines spatial graph convolu-
019            tions with a dual-view temporal transformer-based mechanism, utilizing a tempo-
020            ral linearity graph attention network to account for currency relations in a time-
021            sensitive manner. By integrating a linear attention mechanism for enhanced effi-
022            ciency and capturing both local and global sequential data embeddings, STGAT
023            provides a framework based on a hierarchical transformer for predicting exchange
024            rates. We validate our approach on exchange rates of seventeen currencies over
025            2,092 trading days, demonstrating superior performance compared to state-of-the-
026            art models.

029   1   I NTRODUCTION
030
031   The daily trading volume of the forex market reached nearly trillions of dollars Islam & Hossain
032   (2021). The exchange rates of various currencies provide a large amount of important data for
033
      the currency exchange market. The forex rate (FR) not only affects the profits of enterprises and
      financial institutions, but also influences the development of the international trade market and the
034
      adjustment of national monetary policy Wang et al. (2021). Therefore, FR prediction is crucial to
035
      managing financial risk, making informed investment decisions, planning economic policies, and
036   ensuring competitive international pricing and profitability Pradeepkumar & Ravi (2018); Jarusek
037   et al. (2022).
038
039
      Traditional solutions for forex prediction are based on time-series analysis models. Recent advances
      in deep learning present a promising prospect in the prediction of forex rates using long-short-term
040
      memory (LSTM)-based methods Datta et al. (2021); Islam & Hossain (2021); Dautel et al. (2020).
041
      In addition, some work Islam & Hossain (2021); Dautel et al. (2020) investigates the performance
042   of the gated recurrent unit and LSTM models in predicting exchange rates. Following it, Bi-LSTM
043   Datta et al. (2021) is applied to forecast the exchange rates of 22 different currencies against the US
044   dollar. Moreover, the paper Mao et al. (2024) studies the performance of a hybrid model combin-
045   ing a convolutional neural network with transformer Vaswani (2017) and CNN-LSTM models for
046   exchange rate prediction.
047
      However, such the above works for FR prediction have two challenges. First, they treat FR prediction
048   as independent of each other, which is contrary to true market function, and makes forecasting chal-
049   lenging. FR is interconnected due to economic policies and trade relationships Hartmann (1998);
050   Auboin & Ruta (2013); Dell’Ariccia (1999). For example, if the U.S. Federal Reserve raises interest
051   rates, it can strengthen the USD, which in turn impacts other currencies pegged to the USD or those
052   in countries with close economic ties to the United States. Figure 1 shows that the currencies GBP,
053   ERU and CHF share highly coincident price movements, and the same scenario can be found for the
      currencies HKD and MOP. In addition, for trading, if two countries trade extensively, changes in the

229   2.3.3    T EMPORAL G RAPH ATTENTION M ECHANISM

231   Temporal Forex Graph Construction with k-means. We model currency interdependence via
232   graph structure, where nodes represent currencies and edges represent relations between currencies.
233
      We suppose that currencies within the same cluster at each time step should have similar behavioral
      patterns at time step t, and hence constitute the spatial relationships represented by edges in our
234
      spatial-temporal graph. In addition, k-means is widely used to reveal temporal and spatial patterns
235
      in some applications, such as power grid systems Gharavi & Hu (2017) and traffic flow analysis Tian
236   et al. (2024). Inspired by them, we construct this spatial-temporal currency graph using the k-means
237   algorithm.
238
239

246   To ensure the existence of unique identifiers for nodes at different time steps in the final generated
247   spatial-temporal graph, we combine the time step t with the local index i of a node to generate the
248   global index global indexti = i + t × n, where i is the local index of a node at time step t; n is
249   the number of nodes in each time step; t × n is the offset of the time step. Finally, we create the
250   undirected fully connected edge within the cluster and update the edge index.
251   To create edges across clusters, in addition to representing the spatial correlation between different
252   nodes, we need to reveal the temporal correlation of the same node over the complete time period.
253   We connect the feature representations of the same node at neighboring time steps. Such one-way
254   edges ensure that the direction of the edge points from time step t to time step t + 1, which in turn
255   ensures that edges across time steps can represent temporal dependencies that are consistent with the
256   temporal order. Different from the previous section, this section treats the feature representation of
257
      the same node (currency) in neighboring time steps as two separate nodes, which in turn generates
      edge indexes across time steps.

409   we have chosen the US dollar, the Euro, and the pound sterling to analyze the performance of our
410   model when dealing with mainstream currencies. The United States dollar play an important role in
411   forex, and it is widely used in international trade and financial markets Chinn et al. (2024). Euro is
412   the second largest reserve currency after the US dollar Chinn et al. (2024). Pound sterling has a high

416
417          • Head in Multiple Attention Mechanisms: The model has the best performance when
418            the number of heads in the multi-head attention mechanism is 64. However, we find that

420            that appropriately increasing the number of Attention heads can help the model to capture
421
               more complex spatio-temporal features in general, but the enhancement of the number
               of Attention heads has limited contribution to the overall performance beyond a certain
422
               threshold.
423

425            does not significantly improve the overall performance, but rather slightly increased the
426            prediction error. This is because a fact that with too many attention heads increase com-
427            plexity but fails to capture more useful features, leading to overfitting or instability of the
428            model.

430          • Hidden Channels: The model performance increases significantly when the number of
431            hidden channels is increased to 4 or 8, and the model has the best overall performance
               when the number of hidden channels is 64.
