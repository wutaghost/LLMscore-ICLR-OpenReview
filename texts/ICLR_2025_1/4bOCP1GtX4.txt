009                                                          A BSTRACT
010
011            Inspired by the hospital expert consultation model, this paper proposes a conversational medical
012            visual language model for orthopedics, named WenXinGPT (Multi-disciplinary Collaboration). The
013            core concept of this work focuses on aligning medical visual and textual representations to leverage
014            high-quality data for generating expert consultation dialogues across hospital departments. The
015            primary objective is to uncover orthopedic knowledge within medical intelligent models and enhance
016            their reasoning abilities in an interpretable manner without requiring additional training. Our research
017            particularly emphasizes zero-shot scenarios, and the results from experiments on 16 datasets provided
018            by Peking Union Medical College Hospital demonstrate that the proposed WenXinGPT framework
019
               excels at mining and utilizing medical expertise within large language models, while also expanding
               their reasoning capabilities. Based on these findings, we conducted manual evaluations to identify
020
               and categorize common errors in our methods, along with ablation studies aimed at understanding
021
               the impact of various factors on overall performance.

024   1    I NTRODUCTION
025
026   Large Language Models (LLMs) have revolutionized the field of natural language processing (NLP) by achieving
027
      unprecedented performance across a wide array of tasks (Radford et al., 2019; Brown et al., 2020; Chang et al.,
      2024). Models such as GPT-3 (Brown et al., 2020) and ChatGPT (Ouyang et al., 2022) have demonstrated remarkable
028
      capabilities in generating human-like responses, enabling advancements in machine translation, summarization, and
029
      conversational agents (Raffel et al., 2020). These models leverage deep neural architectures and vast datasets to capture
030   complex linguistic patterns and semantic nuances (Vaswani et al., 2017; Liu et al., 2019), setting new benchmarks in
031   NLP research.
032
033
      The success of LLMs in textual domains has spurred interest in extending these models to handle multimodal data,
      integrating information from diverse sources such as text, images, and audio (Alayrac et al., 2022; Li et al., 2022b;
034
      Yang et al., 2024a). Multimodal models like MiniGPT-4 (Zhu et al., 2023), mPLUG-Owl (Ye et al., 2023), and LLaVA
035
      (Liu et al., 2023) have made significant strides by combining visual and textual information to perform tasks such as
036   image captioning, visual question answering, and cross-modal retrieval (Li et al., 2023c; Gong et al., 2022). These
037   models harness the synergy between different modalities to enhance understanding and generate more informative and
038   context-aware responses.
039
      Despite these advancements, the application of multimodal LLMs in specialized domains like medicine remains
040
      underexplored (Esteva et al., 2021; Miotto et al., 2018). The medical field poses unique challenges, including the need
041
      for high accuracy, interpretability, and compliance with ethical standards (Topol, 2019; Albahri et al., 2023). Medical
042   data often consist of complex multimodal information, such as radiological images coupled with clinical notes, which
043   require sophisticated models capable of integrating and interpreting heterogeneous data sources (Azizi et al., 2021;
044   Schlemper et al., 2019). Moreover, there is a scarcity of large, high-quality medical datasets due to privacy concerns
045   and regulatory constraints, hindering the training of robust models (Yang et al., 2019; Amiri et al., 2024).
046
      Previous research on medical multimodal models, such as Visual-Med-Alpaca (Jiang et al., 2023), has made some
047
      progress by integrating medical image analysis with language models to assist in diagnostic processes. Similarly,
048   studies have explored the use of deep learning for radiology report generation and medical image captioning (Jing et al.,
049   2018; Li et al., 2018; Chen et al., 2020). However, these models primarily rely on English-language diagnostic reports,
050   which presents a significant barrier to their adoption in non-English-speaking regions, particularly in Chinese medical
051   contexts (Li et al., 2019; Zhou et al., 2021). This language barrier not only hinders research but also limits the practical
052   deployment of such models in clinical settings where the local language is essential for effective communication
053   (Wang et al., 2018; Huang et al., 2019). Additionally, current approaches often provide a single, final output without
      accommodating the iterative and interactive nature of medical decision-making processes (Kim et al., 2024; Wachter &


                                                         1
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


054
      Cassel, 2021). The lack of multi-turn dialogue and intermediate reasoning steps reduces the utility of these models for
055   healthcare professionals who rely on sequential and context-rich information (Gaube et al., 2021; Xie et al., 2020).
056
057   To address these challenges, we propose WenXinGPT, a novel multimodal large model tailored for orthopedic medical
058
      image diagnosis and interactive dialogue in Chinese. Our model is inspired by research in reinforcement learning and
      multi-agent systems (Silver et al., 2016; Vinyals et al., 2019), which enable agents to learn optimal policies through
059
      interaction with the environment and other agents. By incorporating these principles, WenXinGPT supports multi-round
060
      interactions that mirror real-world diagnostic processes, allowing for dynamic question-answering and decision support
061   (Li et al., 2022a; Yu et al., 2019). The model not only provides intermediate outputs during the diagnostic dialogue but
062   also integrates underlying prompt systems to guide the conversation and ensure a smoother workflow for healthcare
063   professionals (Sun et al., 2021; Peng et al., 2022). This design contrasts with existing models, such as XrayGPT
064   (Nguyen et al., 2022a), which often leave a substantial portion of interaction design to the user, making the systems
065   less accessible to doctors unfamiliar with complex AI operations (Larasati et al., 2023; Van Berkel et al., 2023). Our
066   contributions are as follows:
067
068          • We introduce WenXinGPT, a multimodal large model specifically designed for Chinese medical image
069            diagnosis, addressing the lack of such models in non-English-speaking healthcare contexts.
070          • WenXinGPT integrates a multi-round interactive dialogue system, allowing for intermediate outputs that
071            enhance decision support in clinical workflows.
072
             • We implement an underlying prompt system that simplifies doctor-model interaction, making AI assistance
073
               more accessible and practical for medical professionals.

078   2.1   M ULTIMODAL L ARGE L ANGUAGE M ODELS
079
080   The integration of visual and linguistic modalities in large language models (LLMs) has garnered significant attention in
081   recent years. To enable LLMs to process and comprehend visual information, various techniques have been developed.
082   BLIP-2 (Li et al., 2023c) introduced Q-Former, which utilizes learnable query vectors to extract visual features from
083   frozen image encoders, marking a notable advancement in aligning visual and language modalities. MiniGPT-4
084   (Zhu et al., 2023) demonstrated that further fine-tuning with detailed image descriptions can significantly improve
085   the usability of multimodal models. LLAVA (Liu et al., 2023) extended this work by exploring diverse multimodal
086
      instruction-following data, including conversations, detailed descriptions, and complex reasoning tasks, with the goal
      of constructing a general-purpose visual assistant. Other notable contributions include Kosmos-2 (Peng et al., 2023)
087
      and VisionLLM (Chen et al., 2023), which focused on enhancing image comprehension, particularly in referring and
088
      grounding tasks.
089
090   Following the success of Image LLMs, researchers have shifted their focus to video comprehension, leading to the
091   development of video-compatible LLMs. Notable examples include VideoChat (Li et al., 2023a), Video-LLaMA (Li
092
      et al., 2023b), and Video-ChatGPT (Zhou et al., 2023), which employ a two-stage training strategy. In the first stage,
      large-scale datasets are used to align video features with the LLM’s feature space, and in the second stage, instruction
093
      tuning is performed using a limited set of annotated datasets. While these models have demonstrated impressive video
094
      comprehension abilities, their capabilities in temporal reasoning and describing specific video segments remain limited.
095   This is largely due to the nature of datasets such as WebVid (Bain et al., 2021), which typically consist of one-event
096   videos with noisy textual annotations. To address this limitation, our approach, WenXinGPT, introduces a fine-tuned
097   interactive dialogue system that enhances the ability to handle detailed medical images and multiple rounds of dialogue
098   in a clinical setting.

      2.2   M EDICAL M ULTIMODAL M ODELS
101
102   In the medical field, multimodal models have also begun to emerge, though at a slower pace. Visual-Med-Alpaca (Jiang
103   et al., 2023) is one of the few models designed to integrate medical images with diagnostic language models. This
104   model, while promising, relies heavily on English diagnostic reports, limiting its application in non-English medical
105   contexts, such as Chinese healthcare. Other notable efforts, such as XrayGPT (Nguyen et al., 2022a), have focused on
106   generating reports from radiological images but lack the flexibility for multi-round interactions required in medical
107   diagnoses. Additionally, these models often provide a single final output, which may not align well with iterative
      diagnostic processes typically used by healthcare professionals.

      WenXinGPT builds on these foundations by specifically addressing the gap in Chinese-language medical multimodal
109   models. Drawing inspiration from reinforcement learning and multi-agent systems (Silver et al., 2016; Vinyals et al.,

      The validity and applicability of the WenXinGPT model are currently mainly evaluated on Chinese medical data.

718   which needs to be further explored and verified. Although manual evaluation and ablation studies are mentioned in this
