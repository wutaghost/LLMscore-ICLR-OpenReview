                                                   A BSTRACT
008
009
               Instance segmentation is critical in biomedical imaging for accurately distinguish-
010
               ing individual objects, such as cells, which often overlap and vary in size. Recent
011            query-based methods—where object-specific queries guide segmentation—have
012            shown strong performance in this task. While U-Net has been a go-to architec-
013            ture in medical image segmentation, it was neither specifically designed for in-
014            stance segmentation nor explored in the context of query-based approaches. In
015            this work, we present IAUNet, a novel architecture that brings instance aware-
016            ness to U-Net with query-based mechanisms to achieve superior pixel-to-instance
017            clustering. The key design includes lightweight Instance Activation (IA) layers,
018            which generate guided object queries by highlighting semantically important re-
019
               gions. Additionally, we propose a Parallel Dual-Path Transformer decoder that
               refines object-specific features across multiple scales, allowing us to assign multi-
020
               ple queries from different scale levels to a specific object. Finally, we introduce the
021
               2025 Revvity Full Cell Segmentation Dataset, comprising hundreds of manually
022            labeled cells from brightfield images. This dataset is unique in capturing the com-
023            plex morphology of overlapping cell cytoplasm with an unprecedented level of
024            detail, making it a valuable resource and benchmark for advancing instance seg-
025            mentation in biomedical imaging. Experiments on multiple public datasets and
026            our own show that IAUNet outperforms most state-of-the-art fully convolutional,
027            transformer-based, and query-based models, setting a strong baseline for medical
028            image instance segmentation tasks.

      Mask R-CNN He et al. (2018) has set the standard for instance segmentation in natural images
127   through its proposal-based approach. Building on Faster R-CNN Ren et al. (2016), Mask R-CNN
128   adds a dedicated mask prediction branch, enabling end-to-end segmentation of individual instances.
129   The process begins with detecting object bounding boxes, followed by applying Region of Interest
130   (RoI) operations, such as RoI-Pooling Girshick et al. (2014) or RoI-Align He et al. (2018), to extract
131   detailed region features for object classification and mask generation. While these two-stage, region-
132   based methods have achieved high performance across various benchmarks, they are often hindered
133   by inefficiencies from generating numerous redundant region proposals, limiting their scalability in
134   practical, real-world applications.
135   The latest iteration, YOLOv8 Reis et al. (2024), represents a state-of-the-art solution for both ob-
136   ject detection and instance segmentation, significantly improving COCO Mean Average Precision
137   (mAP) scores. YOLOv8 introduces the C2f (Cross Stage Partial Fusion) building block, designed
138   for more efficient feature extraction and fusion, enhancing both detection and segmentation tasks.
139   Following this, YOLOv9 Wang et al. (2024) builds on YOLOv8 by introducing the GELAN (Gra-
140   dient Enhanced Layer Aggregation Network) and PGI (Progressive Gradient Interpolation), which
141
      further enhance multi-scale feature fusion and improve the model’s performance during training. In
      addition, the YOLO family employs an advanced data augmentation scheme, notably Mosaic Aug-
142
      mentation Hao & Zhili (2020), where images are transformed by stitching together four different
143
      images. This augmentation pushes the model to learn better generalization by exposing it to objects
144   in diverse positions, levels of occlusion, and environments.
145
146   In biomedical image segmentation, where objects in microscopy typically have complex shapes, ran-
147
      dom orientations, and varying sizes, traditional axis-aligned bounding boxes perform poorly Foll-
      mann & König (2020), Kirillov et al. (2016). For instance, CellPose Stringer et al. (2021) provides a
148
      novel approach by generating topological maps through a simulated diffusion process. The method
149
      uses a U-Net architecture Ronneberger et al. (2015) to predict horizontal and vertical gradients, as
150   well as a binary map of cell pixel predictions. These predicted gradients are then used to create a
151   vector field that groups pixels by their directional flow towards the cell’s center of mass. By tracking
152   these gradients, CellPose successfully segments individual cells, although this method often requires
153   an additional size model to predict object diameters and scale images, especially when faced with
154   high variability in object sizes.
155
      Query-based methods have gained prominence with the introduction of DETR Carion et al. (2020),
156   which demonstrated the potential of a Transformer-based encoder-decoder architecture to achieve
157   competitive results in detection and segmentation tasks. Unlike traditional region-based methods,
158   query-based models rely on object queries to predict object instances directly, eliminating the need
159   for handcrafted representations like bounding boxes. This shift marked a significant advancement in
160   the efficiency and performance of instance segmentation models. Extensions such as Mask2Former
161   and FastInst Cheng et al. (2022a) He et al. (2023) introduced masked attention for improved conver-
      gence and segmentation accuracy, while Mask DINO Li et al. (2022) unified object detection and

169   3   M ODEL OVERVIEW
170
171   Instance segmentation is a critical task in computer vision, particularly for applications such as
172   biomedical imaging, where identifying individual objects in complex environments is essential. In-
173   stance segmentation can be formulated as a task of grouping related pixels for each of the N defined
174   objects in an image. This process can may resemble clustering, where each object is represented
175   as a cluster center, and the goal is to assign associated pixel features to their corresponding ob-
176   ject. The object representation serves as the centroid, and pixels belonging to the same object are
177
      grouped together based on feature similarity. Recent works, such as DETR Carion et al. (2020) and
      Mask2Former Cheng et al. (2022a), have shown that a good instance representation is crucial in ac-
178
      curate segmentation tasks. Inspired by these models, we represent each object as a D-dimensional
179
      feature vector, forming instance embeddings also known as instance queries. These queries act
180   as cluster centers in the D-dimensional feature space, guiding the assignment of pixel features to
181   specific instances.
182
183
      To effectively model both mask and instance features, we propose a convolutional Pixel decoder ??
      with decoupled branches. One branch handles mask features, representing per-pixel embeddings of
184
      the entire image. The other branch models instance features and outputs a corresponding instance
185
      embeddings for guidance. Similar to a standard U-Net, our decoder incorporates skip connections
186   to enrich semantic information from earlier layers, ensuring that both pixel and instance features
187   benefit from multi-scale contextual information.
188
189
      The Transformer decoder addresses the clustering idea by iteratively updating the mask and instance
      features in parallel and subsequently refining instance queries. Unlike traditional methods that per-
190
      form multi-scale feature fusion before decoding, we utilize U-Net’s hierarchical decoding structure,
191
      making the process iterative. In this approach, features from each decoder layer are passed sequen-
192   tially to the next, allowing instance queries to be refined in a stepwise manner across multiple scales.
193   The final instance mask predictions are decoded from the refined mask features and object queries.
194

196   4   P IXEL D ECODER
197
198   Multi-scale and high-context features have proven to be crucial for segmentation tasks Chen et al.
199   (2017) Wang et al. (2020b) Kirillov et al. (2019). In the biomedical domain, U-Net, with all its
200   variants, still holds the ground as the most superior network for accurate segmentation.n. This is
201   primarily due to the design of U-Net’s decoder, which maintains high semantic consistency through
202   the use of skip connections that transfer important features across layers.
203
      We introduce a simple U-Net-like pixel decoder to propagate feature maps. Our pixel decoder works
204   with three types of features: main features, mask features, and instance features 1. The main features
205   serve a similar role to those in the vanilla U-Net, aggregating spatial context across the image. The
206   instance and mask features, however, are specifically designed to support instance segmentation and
207   are tightly integrated with the Transformer decoder. The mask features act as per-pixel embeddings,
208   capturing rich semantic information, while the instance features are responsible for generating object
209   queries at each level. Since both the mask and instance features are derived from the main feature
210   map, they remain aligned, ensuring parallel information flow between pixel-level and object-level
211   representations.
212   At each pixel decoder layer, given the main feature map X, we combine it with a skip connection
213   from the encoder. The combined features are then passed through a simple double depth-wise con-
214   volution with residual connection to retain lightweight nature of pixel decoder. The result is a refined
215   main feature map X, which we then use to decouple both mask features Xm and instance features
      Xi .

      5   G UIDED I NSTANCE Q UERIES
248
249
      Central to this refinement process are guided instance queries, which ensure accurate object seg-
      mentation. Object queries play a crucial role in the Transformer decoder Liang et al. (2023) Carion
250
      et al. (2020). Since object queries are used to embed information about the object, they serve as the
251
      basis for accurate instance segmentation. Models like DETR Carion et al. (2020) and Mask2Former
252   Cheng et al. (2022a) utilize either zero-initialized or learnable embeddings to describe instances
253   without relying on prior knowledge of the image semantics.
254
255
      In contrast, we introduce query guidance to avoid convergence into suboptimal local minima and
      to guide the model toward learning more informative object representations. At each level of the
256
      decoder, the model learns to generate guided queries, which capture denser and more accurate object
257
      representations. These instance embeddings get progressively refined through the decoder while
258   preserving high-resolution object features.
259
260   At each decoder stage, the Instance Activation (IA) layer 1 generates N guided instance queries
261
      a ∈ RN ×H×W . Given the instance features Xi from the Pixel decoder, the IA layer produces
      activation maps by highlighting important regions for each object. Formally, IA can be defined as:
262
263
264                                   a = softmax(f (Xi )) ∈ RN ×H×W                                    (1)
265
266
      where f (x) is a simple 3x3 convolution followed by a softmax function to normalize the activations.
267   After obtaining normalized instance activation maps a ∈ RN ×H×W , we select N object queries
268   from the instance features Xi with high foreground probabilities from instance activations. We then
269   perform an element-wise multiplication with the Xi feature map to generate the final object queries:
      q = a · XiT ∈ RN ×256 . Thus, each object gets encoded into a 256-dimensional vector.

      In the IAUNet model, we implement a Parallel Dual-Path Transformer Decoder that updates both ob-
278
      ject queries and pixel features in parallel. The key component of our Transformer decoder includes
279   double-center clustering, where the object gets represented with two queries.
280
281   At each decoder layer l, we generate new instance queries q from the instance features Xi and
282
      concatenate them with N instance queries from the previous layer (“support” queries) to obtain a
      total of 2N instance queries 2. Each object is represented with two queries (“two cluster centers”).
283

284
      and instance features Xm ∈ RL×256 and Xi ∈ RL×256 , where L = Hl × Wl for the l-th decoder
285   layer.
286
287   The Parallel Dual-Path Transformer performs parallel mask and instance features update and query
288
      update. The new instance queries hold rich object features and act as primary cluster centers. While
      the previous instance queries function as support centers. Such dual representation allows the model
289

      9   L IMITATIONS AND C ONCLUSION
524
525
526
      In this work, we introduced IAUNet, a novel architecture combining U-Net with query-based mech-
      anisms for instance segmentation. The model’s Instance Activation layers generate guided object
527
      queries, while the Parallel Dual-Path Transformer Decoder refines features across multiple scales.
528
      IAUNet outperforms leading models, especially in handling medium and large objects, and sets a
529   new baseline for biomedical imaging tasks, as demonstrated on the 2025 Revvity Full Cell Segmen-
530   tation Dataset.
531
532
      IAUNet faces challenges with small object segmentation, similar to other query-based methods
      Cheng et al. (2022a); He et al. (2023). Additionally, IAUNet could be optimized to handle a higher
533
      number of instances per image. Future research should focus on developing more efficient solutions
534
      for small object segmentation.
