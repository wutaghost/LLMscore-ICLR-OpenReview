009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011
               In recent years, large vision models have made significant advancements and
012            excelled in tasks such as detection, segmentation, and tracking. This is partly
013            due to vision models‘ good representation of visual objects. Although the re-
014            cently proposed SAM (the Segment Anything Model Kirillov et al. (2023)) or the
015            one/few-shot models based on SAM have wide applicability across many tasks,
016            some researchers have found that they do not perform well on certain downstream
017            tasks (Han et al. (2023); Tang et al. (2023)). In this paper, we focused on a spe-
018            cific group of these objects, which can be summarized as glass-like objects, and
019            quantitatively studied the inadequacies related to the vision models’ feature rep-
020
               resentation of glass-like objects using the representation accuracy(RA) metric we
               proposed. Then, we proposed a novel, extremely simple method that introduces
021
               almost no additional computations to address these inadequacies. The main idea
022
               is utilizing the visual properties of target objects to find representation dimensions
023            which dominate in recognizing them and leveraging these information accordingly
024            to achieve better representations of target objects. Using representation accuracy
025            and setting these representations as reference in one-shot segmentation tasks, our
026            experiments demonstrated the substantial effectiveness of our method.

      1   I NSTRUCTION
030
031
032   Research in Vision Foundation Models (VFMs) has made tremendous strides in recent times. Fueled
033
      by extensive image-text contrastive pre-training, CLIP ( Radford et al. (2021)) and ALIGN ( Jia
      et al. (2021)) demonstrate robust zero-shot transfer capabilities across a wide array of classification
034
      tasks. DINOv2 ( Oquab et al. (2023)) showcases remarkable proficiency in visual feature matching,
035
      enabling it to comprehend intricate information at both the image and pixel levels, solely from raw
036   image data. Furthermore, the Segment Anything Model (SAM) ( Kirillov et al. (2023)) has achieved
037   impressive class-agnostic segmentation performance by training on the SA-1B dataset, comprising
038   1 billion masks and 11 million images.
039
      However, unlike Large Language Models (LLMs), which seamlessly integrate various language
040
      tasks using a unified model structure and pre-training approach, VFMs face challenges when di-
041
      rectly addressing diverse perception tasks. For instance, researchers have found that SAM performs
042   rather poorly in some downstream vision tasks, e.g. camouflaged objects( Han et al. (2023)), trans-
043   parent objects( Tang et al. (2023)), mirror objects( Tang et al. (2023)), etc. It is worth emphasizing
044   that in the testing of the transparent and mirror object segmentation task, Tang et al. manually se-
045   lected the masks generated by SAM that were closest to the target objects but the performance of
046   SAM still fell far short compared to specialized models, with nearly a 25 % difference in mean inter-
047   section over union (mIoU) compared to the previous state-of-the-art models for each task averagely.
048   This suggests that raw features extracted by existing Vision Foundation Models may not effectively
049   represent such objects.
050   To enhance the transferability of those Vision Foundation Models, efforts have been made by Per-
051   SAM ( Zhang et al. (2023)) and Matcher ( Liu et al. (2023)) which employ a systematic approach
052   where each object category is prompted with a single reference photo (one-shot). This process in-
053   volves several key steps, including computing similarity in the patch level between the target image
      and reference images by features encoded by VFMs (ViT,DINOv2), extracting prompted points or


                                                         1
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


054
      boxes to guide SAM in generating segmentation masks, and utilizing the similarity information to
055   select segmentation masks and produce the final results.
056
057   Despite their significant advancement over previous models in traditional one/few-shot datasets, we
058
      found that they performed poorly on the one-shot glass and mirror segmentation tasks, just like SAM
      does. For the glass segmentation dataset, Matcher based on DINOv2 and SAM achieved an mIoU
059
      of averagely only around 40%, and for the mirror segmentation task, Mathcer’s mIoU was merely
060
      around 25%, which is far below the 85% achieved on the traditional one-shot dataset FSS-1000. This
061   indicates that existing VFMs do not represent glass surface objects as well as traditional objects. In
062   this article, we quantitatively analyze how this representation gap manifests at the patch level, clearly
063   showing the deficiencies of VFMs in these downstream segmentation tasks with experiments of the
064   representation accuracy index.
065
      After analyzing the inadequacies of existing vision models in representing glass-like objects, we
066
      propose a novel and rather simple method to improve these feature representations. The main idea
067   is to utilize the visual properties of glass-like objects by employing a comparative approach to iden-
068   tify those dominant feature dimensions when recognizing these hard-to-detect objects. Specifically,
069   because glass barriers have transparent attributes and mirrored objects have reflective qualities, we
070   can perform comparative analysis on the same scene by adding and removing glass barriers, exam-
071   ining both the interior and exterior aspects of the mirrored scene. This allows us to extract the slight
072   feature dimensions that truly characterize glass-like objects.
073   By processing these important dimensions, we significantly enhance accuracy and validity of the
074   representations, which substantially improves segmentation precision in further tasks. Experimen-
075   tal results demonstrate the effectiveness and broad applicability of our method. In the task of
076   glass(transparent) object segmentation, there is a average around 1% improvement in representa-
077   tion accuracy, with consistent improvement on the one-shot model Matcher( Liu et al. (2023)). For
078   the mirror segmentation task, there is a consistent increase in representation accuracy, and an average
079   improvement of around 3% on Matcher.
080   Our main contributions can be summarized as follows:
081
082
      (i) We propose a new metric, representation accuracy to quantitatively calculated the representation
      accuracy of a specific vision model. We assessed the representation accuracy of several images
083
      with the encoder (DINOv2) of the recent state-of-the-art (SOTA) one-shot model across the whole
084
      datasets on the glass-like tasks, highlighting the inadequacies of existing vision models in represent-
085   ing glass-like objects.
086
087   (ii) We proposed a novel, clearly motivated, and easily implementable method that utilizes the visual
088
      properties of objects to extract the most important feature dimensions and process them accordingly
      to achieve more accurate and effective representations with almost no additional computation.
089
090   (iii) Extensive and thorough ablation experiments across three datasets demonstrate the substantial
091   effectiveness and broad applicability of our method, providing inspiration for future research.

252   4   M ETHODOLOGY
253
254   As mentioned in the problem analysis section, glass-like objects almost always have a complex
255   background mixed within them, resulting in many dimensions of the features encoded by the vision
256   model being used to store this background information. This leads to numerous errors in patch
257   matching when calculating similarity using these features. To address this issue, we propose a novel
258   and targeted approach.
259   Since the transparency and reflectivity of glass-like objects interfere with the vision model, we can
260   leverage these two visual properties to design a series of comparative scenarios. For example, we can
261   add and remove glass barriers (e.g., windows) in the same location or examine the inside and outside
262   of mirrors in the same place, seeking the dimensions where the average feature values change the
263   most with consistent direction. These dimensions may, to some extent, better represent glass-like
264   objects. Subsequently, we can further process these dimensions for either the reference images or
265   the target images, such as feature dimensions reduction, to enhance the accuracy of the matching
266
      process.
267   Specifically, we set up five pairs of images comparing the addition and removal of glass barriers,
268   along with six pairs of images contrasting the inside and outside of mirrors, labeling target regions
269   within these images. We then averaged features of patches in target regions of each image and cal-
      culated dimensions with the largest absolute value changes for each pair of features. By identifying

417   6     C ONCLUSION
418
419
      In this paper, we first tested the accuracy of existing vision foundation models in representing glass-
420   like objects. Specifically, we introduced a metric called representation accuracy and pinpointed
421   significant inadequacies in current glass-like object representations after testing these representa-
422   tions applied to a one-shot segmentation model. We analyzed the reasons for these deficiencies
423   and concluded that background information within the glass severely interfered with vision models’
424   representations of glass-like objects. To address this issue, we leveraged the visual properties of
425   glass-like objects, designing several sets of contrastive scenarios to identify dimensions that best
426   represent glass-like objects, and applied a simple method to these dimensions with almost no addi-
427   tional computational overhead. Remarkably, using only eleven pairs of contrastive scenarios, getting
428
      only seven dimensions and very simple operations resulted in a significant improvement in model’s
      performance, proving the correctness of our ideas and the substantial effectiveness of our approach.
429
430   Limitations and Future Research.The number of contrastive scenarios we designed was limited
431   and rather straightforward, and or processing method was simple with minimal additional compu-
      tation. We believe that, moving forward, the design of more diverse and rich contrastive scenarios,
