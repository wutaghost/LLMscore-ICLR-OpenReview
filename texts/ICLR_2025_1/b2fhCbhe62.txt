011                                                A BSTRACT
012
013             Emotion recognition systems face significant challenges in real-world applications,
014             where novel emotion categories continually emerge and multiple emotions often
015             co-occur. This paper introduces multi-label fine-grained class incremental emotion
016
                decoding, which aims to develop models capable of incrementally learning new
                emotion categories while maintaining the ability to recognize multiple concurrent
017
                emotions. We propose an Augmented Emotional Semantics Learning (AESL)
018
                framework to address two critical challenges: past- and future-missing partial label
019             problems. AESL incorporates an augmented Emotional Relation Graph (ERG) for
020             reliable soft label generation and affective dimension-based knowledge distillation
021             for future-aware feature learning. We evaluate our approach on three datasets
022             spanning brain activity and multimedia domains, demonstrating its effectiveness
023             in decoding up to 28 fine-grained emotion categories. Results show that AESL
024             significantly outperforms existing methods while effectively mitigating catastrophic
025             forgetting.

028   1    I NTRODUCTION
029
030    Accurately decoding human emotional states remains a fundamental challenge in affective computing
031    research. While conventional deep learning approaches have demonstrated promising performance (Li
032    & Deng, 2020; Poria et al., 2017), they struggle to adapt to the evolving nature of real-world scenarios,
033
       where novel emotion categories continuously emerge to capture increasingly nuanced emotional
       experiences. As eloquently expressed by the renowned novelist Jeffrey Eugenides (Eugenides, 2003),
034
      “Emotions, in my experience, are not covered by single words. I do not believe in ’sadness’, ’joy’, or
035
      ’regret’" – this profound observation underscores the inherent complexity of human emotions. Indeed,
036    individuals typically experience a sophisticated blend of multiple emotions simultaneously when
037    responding to emotional stimuli (Fu et al., 2022). Motivated by these insights, we introduce a novel
038    research paradigm: multi-label fine-grained class incremental emotion decoding.
039
      As illustrated in Figure 1, multi-label class incremental emotion decoding aims to develop a unified
040
      model capable of incrementally learning and integrating knowledge from both existing and emerging
041
      emotion classes while comprehensively decoding multiple concurrent emotional states. Unlike
042   traditional single-label class incremental learning (SLCIL), the multi-label class incremental learning
043   (MLCIL) faces unique challenges in addressing catastrophic forgetting, primarily stemming from
044   past- and future-missing partial label problems. Consider the past-missing partial label scenario: the
045   left screenshot (The corgi is diving) in task 3’s training dataset contains the label Adoration, yet this
046   emotion remains imperceptible to the model during the current task. Similarly, in the future-missing
047   partial label case, the right screenshot (The father is combing his daughter’s hair) in task 1’s dataset
048   encompasses the label Joy, which is inaccessible to the model in the current task. Existing MLCIL
049   approaches either rely on storing historical instances (Kim et al., 2020; Liang & Li, 2022), limiting
050
      their practical applicability, or overlook the critical future-missing partial label problem (Du et al.,
      2022), leading to suboptimal performance.
051
052   To address these challenges, we propose a novel Augmented Emotional Semantics Learning (AESL)
053   framework. First, we tackle the past-missing partial label problem by introducing an augmented
      Emotional Relation Graph (ERG) module with graph-based label disambiguation. Upon encountering

      have explored adaptive architectures (Wang et al., 2023) and meta-learning approaches (Zhang et al.,

142   Multi-label Class Incremental Learning. MLCIL addresses the crucial challenge of simultaneously

144   various approaches: (Dong et al., 2023) proposed an attention-based knowledge restore and transfer
145
      framework, while AGCN (Du et al., 2022) employed GCN for label relationship learning. Online
      class incremental learning has been addressed through specialized replay buffer designs in PRS (Kim
146

147
      (Zhang et al., 2021) and knowledge distillation (Liu et al., 2022) for MLCIL. However, MLCIL

      3.2   F RAMEWORK OVERVIEW
163
164   As shown in Figure 2(a), AESL processes each incremental task through four interconnected mod-
165   ules. The ERG module first constructs and maintains the emotion relationship graph, providing the
166   foundation for emotional semantics learning. The graph autoencoder (GAE) then learns emotion
167   embeddings from ERG, which guide the feature decoupling module to extract label-specific features.
168   Finally, relation-based knowledge distillation preserves previously learned knowledge while accom-
169   modating new emotion categories. These components work jointly to address both past-missing and
170
      future-missing partial label problems in MLCIL.

417   Comparative Studies. Tables 2, 3, and 4 show the results on subject 1 of Brain27 (more results are
418   shown in Appendix C), Video27 and Audio, respectively. We can observe that AESL shows obvious
419   superiority under different datasets and protocols, in terms of three widely used metrics mAP, maF1
420
      and miF1 (Zhang & Zhou, 2013). Especially for Brain27, AESL has a relative improvement of 9.6%
      in mAP (4 protocols averaged) and 9.7% in maF1 compared with the second place method. Figure 4
421
      exhibits the comparison curves of AESL and comparing methods, which indicates that our method is
422
      consistently optimal at each task of incremental learning. Among the compared methods, EWC and
423   LwF are traditional SLCIL methods. We can find that EWC is not suitable for direct application to
424   MLCIL task due to its poor performance. In contrast, LwF achieves impressive performance on the
425   Audio28 dataset. We infer that knowledge distillation, which essentially provides soft labels for old
426   classes, contributes to overcoming catastrophic forgetting in MLCIL. Besides, it is noticeable that
427   rehearsal-based methods, including ER, RS, PRS, OCDM and KRT-R, are not satisfactory, especially
428   for maF1 and miF1. This is because just saving the labels of current task aggravates the partial
429   label problem in subsequent training, which illustrates that data replay is not suitable for MLCIL.
430   Furthermore, AGCN serves as a strong baseline which can rank 2nd for most cases, and our method
431
      surpasses AGCN by incorporating effective label semantics learning and introducing knowledge
      distillation from the affective space.

      5   C ONCLUSION
533
534   In this paper, we have proposed a novel AESL framework for multi-label class incremental emotion
535   decoding. In detail, we developed an augmented ERG generation method with label disambiguation
536   for handling the past-missing partial label problem. Then, knowledge distillation from affective
537   dimension space was introduced for alleviating the future-missing partial label problem. Besides,
538   we constructed an emotional semantics learning module to learn indispensable label embeddings
539   for subsequent semantic-specific feature extraction. Extensive experiments have illustrated the
      effectiveness of AESL.

792   Details of these compared methods are as follows.
793
      EWC (Kirkpatrick et al., 2017): A Single-Label Class Incremental Learning algorithm that reduces
794
      catastrophic forgetting by constraining important parameters uses the Fisher information matrix to
795
      compute the importance of parameters.
796
797   LWF (Lee et al., 2019): The first algorithm to apply knowledge distillation to the Single-Label Class
798   Incremental Learning task uses the old model as a teacher and minimizes the KL divergence between
799
      the probability distributions of the outputs of the new and old models.
800   ER (Lee et al., 2019): A Single-Label Class Incremental Learning algorithm based on data replay,
801   where the construction of a data buffer employs a random sampling strategy.
802
      RS (Vitter, 1985): A Single-Label Class Incremental Learning algorithm based on data replay, where
803   the construction of a data buffer utilizes a reservoir sampling strategy.
804
805   AGCN (Vitter, 1985): A Multi-Label Class Incremental Learning algorithm based on graph con-
806
      volutional neural networks, where the graph adjacency matrix continuously expands as the tasks
      progress.
807
808   PRS (Kim et al., 2020): A Multi-Label Class Incremental Learning algorithm based on data replay,
809   which improves upon the reservoir sampling strategy to ensure that the number of samples for each
      class in the data buffer is as balanced as possible.

      D    L IMITATIONS
957
958
      At the application level, in the affective HCI tasks in real-life scenarios, in addition to learning new
959   emotion categories, we also need to adapt to new subjects. Therefore, it is necessary to further
960   consider the continuous learning of emotions from different subjects, which can be regarded as
961   domain incremental learning. At the experimental level, the impact of the order of learning emotion
