009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            Recently, multi-modal masked autoencoders (MAE) has been introduced in 3D
012            self-supervised learning, offering enhanced feature learning by leveraging both
013            2D and 3D data to capture richer cross-modal representations. However, these
014            approaches have two limitations: (1) they inefficiently require both 2D and 3D
015            modalities as inputs, even though the inherent multi-view properties of 3D point
016            clouds already contain 2D modality. (2) input 2D modality causes the recon-
017            struction learning to unnecessarily rely on visible 2D information, hindering 3D
018            geometric representation learning. To address these challenges, we propose a 3D
019
               to Multi-View Learner (Multi-View ML) that only utilizes 3D modalities as inputs
               and effectively capture rich spatial information in 3D point clouds. Specifically,
020
               we first project 3D point clouds to multi-view 2D images at the feature level based
021
               on 3D-based pose. Then, we introduce two components: (1) a 3D to multi-view
022            autoencoder that reconstructs point clouds and multi-view images from 3D and
023            projected 2D features; (2) a multi-scale multi-head (MSMH) attention mechanism
024            that facilitates local-global information interactions in each decoder transformer
025            block through attention heads at various scales. Additionally, a novel two-stage
026            self-training strategy is proposed to align 2D and 3D representations. Empiri-
027            cally, our method significantly outperforms state-of-the-art counterparts across
028            various downstream tasks, including 3D classification, part segmentation, and
029            object detection. Such performance superiority showcases that Multi-View ML
030
               enriches the model’s comprehension of geometric structures and inherent multi-
               modal properties of point clouds.

034   1   I NTRODUCTION
035
036   3D vision is a critical research area with applications in autonomous driving (Duan et al., 2019; Liu
037   et al., 2023; Li et al., 2022) and robotics (Zou et al., 2021; Zhou et al., 2021), thanks to its ability
038   to model and interpret the real world. However, manually collecting and annotating 3D point cloud
039
      data is both expensive and time-consuming, posing significant challenges for developing effective
      3D models. Consequently, self-supervised learning (SSL) of 3D representations has emerged as a
040
      key research direction.
041
042   Recent works have leveraged 2D images to enhance 3D self-supervised learning (Guo et al., 2023;
043   Chen et al., 2023; Qi et al., 2023; Wang et al., 2023). Specifically, methods such as Joint-MAE (Guo
044   et al., 2023) and PiMAE (Chen et al., 2023) utilize masked images and point clouds as inputs to
045
      reconstruct complete images and point clouds. However, we argue that using both 2D and 3D
      modalities as inputs in these MAE-based methods presents two significant drawbacks. First, incor-
046
      porating both 2D and 3D modalities as input for training is redundant and inefficient. Point clouds
047
      inherently encapsulate multi-modal data, as they can be directly translated into multi-view depth
048   images or rendered as 2D images. This eliminates the necessity for input images for 2D recon-
049   struction. Secondly, using 2D images as input may cause the network to rely heavily on visible 2D
050   information when predicting masked regions. Specifically, when 2D view information is directly
051   provided, it inadvertently ”leaks” viewpoint semantic information to the network. Consequently, the
052   underlying multi-view geometric information is neglected, preventing the network from developing
053   a comprehensive understanding of how the 2D view should appear from different angles. As a result,
      the model is unable to develop multi-view geometric representations, which is essential for effective

054
      3D representation learning, as highlighted in previous studies (Hamdi et al., 2021b;a; Su et al., 2015;
055   Robert et al., 2022).
056
057   To address these limitations, we propose leveraging the intrinsic multi-modal nature of point clouds
058
      without the redundant use of input images, thereby enhancing the efficiency and effectiveness of
      3D self-supervised learning. More formally, we introduce an innovative method called Multiview
059
      Masked Learner (Multiview-ML), designed to extract the multi-view 2D information inherently
060
      embedded within 3D point clouds, thus advancing 3D representation learning. Specifically, we
061   input point clouds into an initial encoder to obtain intermediate 3D encoded tokens. Using the
062   provided pose information, we then project these intermediate 3D tokens onto 2D image tokens
063   through our proposed view-based feature projection (see Figure 2a). The projected multi-view 2D
064   tokens and the original 3D tokens, augmented with modality, positional, and pose embeddings, are
065   then fed into a fusion encoder to obtain the corresponding encoded 3D and multi-view 2D features.
066   Subsequently, we pass these features into two separate decoders to independently reconstruct the
067   complete multi-view 2D images and 3D point clouds.
068   Furthermore, to enhance the integration of local attention mechanisms for reconstruction, we in-
069   troduce a Multi-Scale Multi-Head (MSMH) attention mechanism—a simple yet effective module
070   that provides broader local and global attention. Unlike previous standard attention mechanisms,
071   MSMH organizes tokens into distinct, non-overlapping local groups. Self-attention is then applied
072   within each subgroup rather than across all individual tokens. Additionally, we implement a multi-
073   scale design with varying group sizes, allowing smaller groups to capture fine-grained local details
074   while larger groups capture broader global context. Finally, we concatenate the multi-scale attention
075
      features to ensure the model effectively acquires both local and global information.
076   Moreover, unlike previous methods (Pang et al., 2022; Guo et al., 2023), which primarily focus
077   on reconstructing raw masked inputs, our approach involves reconstructing masked 2D and 3D
078   representations within a latent space, drawing inspiration from the SimSiam (Chen & He, 2021).
079   We propose a two-stage training procedure to enhance multi-modal masked representation learning:
080   Stage One: 3D to multi-view autoencoder (teacher model) takes the complete point cloud as input
081
      and outputs both the reconstructed point cloud and multi-view images. The autoencoder extracts
      latent features that contain rich geometric information, effectively restoring the point cloud and
082
      multi-view images. Stage Two: We introduce a student network based on a masked autoencoder,
083
      which leverages masked point clouds to predict features generated by the teacher encoder in the first
084   stage. Notably, our objective function is designed to predict latent space features instead of 3D point
085   clouds. This ensures that the student model learns well-aligned and contextualized representations
086   across modalities.
087
      We conducted extensive experiments to validate our Multiview-ML approach. Our method signif-
088
      icantly outperforms previous approaches across downstream tasks such as 3D shape classification,
089
      part segmentation, and object detection, underscoring its effectiveness in learning robust 3D geo-
090   metric representations. A key insight from our study is the limited effectiveness of using 2D images
091   as input for 3D geometric learning through MAE. We believe this finding presents an intriguing op-
092   portunity in the design space for developing learning strategies for 3D representation learning. Our
093   key contributions are summarized as follows:
094
      ⋄ We propose a 3D to multi-view autoencoder that reconstructs point clouds and multi-view images
095
      solely from 3D point clouds, eliminating the need for additional input images and enhancing the
096   learning of 3D geometric features.
097
098   ⋄ We propose a Multi-Scale Multi-Head (MSMH) attention mechanism that integrates local and
099
      global contextual information by organizing distinct, non-overlapping local groups at multiple scales
      within the reconstructed features.
100
101   ⋄ We develop a two-stage training strategy for multi-modality masked feature prediction, enhanc-
102   ing the alignment between 2D and 3D representations by having the student network predict the
103   teacher’s latent features from masked point clouds.
104   ⋄ Our method outperforms existing approaches across various downstream tasks, underscoring the
105   importance of leveraging the inherent multi-view 2D information present in point clouds for effective
106   3D representation learning.

      2     R ELATIVE W ORK
109
110   Masked Autoencoder for Point Cloud. MAE (He et al., 2022) masks random patches of an input
111   image and reconstructs the missing pixels, effectively learning modality-specific representations.
112   This approach has achieved significant success across various domains (Devlin et al., 2018; Rad-
113   ford et al., 2019a;b). Recently, Point-BERT (Yu et al., 2022) and Point-MAE (Pang et al., 2022)
114   adapted MAE to reconstruct masked 3D point clouds. Furthermore, Zhang et al.(Zhang et al., 2022)
115   proposed PointM2AE, which uses pyramid architectures to capture both fine-grained and high-level
116   semantic features of 3D shapes. However, these methods primarily focus on a single 3D modality.
117   In contrast, multi-modality MAE(Gong et al., 2022; Bachmann et al., 2022) has drawn attention
118
      to learning multiple modalities complementary representations. Yet, research on multi-modality
      masked autoencoders for point cloud learning remains limited. Few Recent frameworks, such as
119
      Joint-MAE (Guo et al., 2023) and PiMAE (Chen et al., 2023), propose 2D-3D MAE approaches by
120
      reconstructing 2D and 3D inputs. However, we argue that utilizing 2D input is redundant, as 3D
121   data already includes multi-view 2D information. Unlike previous works that require both 3D point
122   clouds and corresponding images for 3D representation learning, Multiview-ML uses only 3D point
123   clouds as input, fully leveraging multi-view information to enhance learning efficiency.
124
      Multimodal Feature Learning. Recent studies (Xue et al., 2023; Morgado et al., 2021; Radford
125
      et al., 2021; Afham et al., 2022; Jing et al., 2020) have demonstrated that pre-trained multimodal
126
      models offer highly transferable representations, significantly boosting performance across various
127   downstream tasks. For example, CrossPoint (Afham et al., 2022) employs contrastive learning to
128   align point clouds with their corresponding 2D images in a shared latent space. MVTN (Hamdi
129   et al., 2021a) enhances 3D object understanding by exploiting multi-view image correspondences.
130   Sautier et al.(Sautier et al., 2022) introduce an object-level contrastive loss between 2D and 3D
131   representations, while TAP(Wang et al., 2023) presents a 3D-to-2D generative pre-training strategy.
132   Unlike prior one-stage approaches that focus solely on reconstructing point clouds, our two-stage
133   strategy emphasizes better alignment between 2D and 3D representations. In this work, we propose
134   a novel two-stage pre-training framework that enhances the alignment of 2D and 3D representations,
135
      ensuring that our model learns well-aligned and contextualized multimodal features.

528   5     C ONCLUSION
529
530   This paper introduces Multiview-ML, an innovative technique for 3D representation learning that
531
      capitalizes on the multi-view nature of 3D point clouds. Unlike conventional approaches, our
      method uniquely employs masked point clouds to reconstruct their original forms and generate mul-
532
      tiple depth images from different views. This approach harnesses the rich, multi-view features and
533
      spatial information within point clouds, distinguishing our method from existing multi-modal MAE
534   techniques. Additionally, we introduce a multi-scale multi-head attention mechanism that enhances
535   the interplay between local and global perspectives within each decoder transformer block, utilizing
536   attention heads across various scales. Finally, a novel self-training strategy is proposed, aiming to
537   generate aligned and context-rich masked 2D and 3D representations based on the initial learning ob-
538   jectives. Our experimental results showcase the superior performance of Multiview-ML over current
539   leading self-supervised 3D learning methods across various downstream applications, highlighting
      its effectiveness in fully leveraging the multi-view attributes of 3D data.
