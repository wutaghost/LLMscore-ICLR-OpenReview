009                                              A BSTRACT
010
011            Video action recognition has aroused the research interest of many scholars, and
012            has been widely applied in public surveillance, video review, sports events and
013            other fields. However, the high similarity of video backgrounds and the long time
014            span of action bring serious challenges to action recognition. In this work, we pro-
015            pose a spatio-temporal diffusion transformer (STD-Former) to improve the recog-
016
               nition accuracy of long-distance and fine-grained actions from redundant back-
               grounds. STD-Former utilizes a two-branch network to extract the spatiotemporal
017
               and temporal information of video respectively. First, we present a parallel trans-
018
               former module to capture the spatiotemporal feature of actions through attention
019            mechanism and convolutional structure in the spatiotemporal branch. Secondly, a
020            cross transformer module integrating the feature of spatiotemporal branch is con-
021            structed to explore the long-distance temporal dependency relationship of actions
022            in the temporal branch. In addition, inspires by the advantage of the diffusion
023            principle in exploring long-term temporal dependency, we design a novel plug-
024            and-play spatiotemporal diffusion module that feeds back the feature extracted
025            from the temporal branch to the spatiotemporal branch, thereby enhancing the
026            ability of model to capture motion information from a large number of redun-
027
               dant backgrounds. Finally, in order to learn the fine-grained action information
               between adjacent video sequences, another plug-and-play significant motion ex-
028
               citation module is established to convert the spatial information of adjacent video
029
               frames into the motion feature. The experimental results on Something Something
030            V1 and V2 datasets demonstrate that STD-Former can more accurately identify
031            the fine-grained action and has favorable robustness than the current state-of-the-
032            art action recognition models.

035   1   I NTRODUCTION
036
037   Action recognition can efficiently identify and analyze video actions (Sun et al., 2023b), and has
038   been applied in public surveillance (Elharrouss et al., 2021), medical monitoring (Hang & Li, 2023),
039
      video review (Sun et al., 2023b), sports events (Tong et al., 2022) and so on. However, it still faces
      multiple challenges (Ramanathan et al., 2014). Different actions have considerable temporal vari-
040
      ance, which struggles to extract short-term motion cues and characterize the action over long time
041
      spans. Meanwhile, the intra-class differences and inter-class similarities (Akila & Chitrakala, 2019)
042   are significant for some video actions. The forms of actions with same category are various under
043   different circumstances (for instance, running). Certain actions, such as striding and walking, are
044   highly similar in their representation, making them indistinguishable according to spatial configura-
045   tion and motion characteristics.
046
      In recent years, deep learning based action recognition methods are constantly emerging, which
047
      could be classified into the convolutional neural network (CNN) and Transformer based mod-
048   els. The CNN-based action recognition models mainly included three types of architectures: 2D
049   CNN, 3D CNN, and two-stream network. The 2D CNN-based recognition methods employed two-
050   dimensional convolution to capture action appearance information and extract spatial and temporal
051   features of video, such as temporal relation network (TRN) (Zhou et al., 2017), temporal shift mod-
052   ule (TSM) (Lin et al., 2019), etc. Given that videos encompass not only static appearance features
053   but also motion and temporal features, 2D CNN-based methods may not fully capture the long-term
      temporal relationship of video actions. Hence, many researchers utilized 3D convolution to directly

054
      extract spatiotemporal feature and capture the contextual information of video actions, for instance,
055   C3D (Tran et al., 2015), I3D (Carreira & Zisserman, 2017), R(2+1)D (Tran et al., 2018), etc. Al-
056   though the 3D CNN-based method is capable of extracting spatiotemporal feature of video, they
057   usually have a large number of parameters, so their training speed is slower than that of the 2D
058   CNN-based methods.
059
      In addition, a pioneering two-stream approach proposed by Simonyan & Zisserman (2014) em-
060
      ployed two distinct models to process RGB and optical flow information concurrently. Subsequently,
061   some researchers (Feichtenhofer et al., 2016; 2017; Wu et al., 2018) paid attention to the design strat-
062   egy of two-stream network. The existing two-stream methods depended on dense sampling of video
063   and pre-extracted optical flow feature, which needed substantial storage and computational resource.
064   At the same time, although the two-stream network excels at capturing short-term motion feature,
065   the long-term action feature is also crucial for accurate action recognition.
066
      Transformer (Vaswani et al., 2017) focuses on global feature of sample through its self-attention
067   mechanism. Therefore, scholars leveraged Transformer to model long-distance action dependency
068   relationships and proposed many action recognition methods based on transformer (Fan et al., 2021;
069   Yan et al., 2022; Yang et al., 2023). A convolution-free spatiotemporal transformer method (Berta-
070   sius et al., 2021) gave a new paradigm for transformer architecture, which first integrated temporal
071   and spatial attention. Subsequently, various video feature fusion strategies (Shah et al., 2024) are
072   presented to improve the performance of model. Motion-Former (Patrick et al., 2021) integrated
073   trajectory attention and aggregated the spatial and temporal features along implicit motion paths. Li
074   et al. (2023) proposed an improved visual transformer model, which improved spatiotemporal atten-
075
      tion mechanism and temporal dependency of actions. A convolutional transformer architecture (Wu
      et al., 2021a) was proposed by embedding convolution into the self-attention and introducing a
076
      compressed projection to enhance local information representation. Convolutional projection (Yuan
077
      et al., 2021) was designed to accurately extract the feature of image patches through hierarchical
078   category token attention. The ability of transformer to capture global information could improve the
079   model performance for the long video sequences, but the inherent background redundancy in video
080   would lead to insufficient capture of fine-grained features in the transformer-based action recogni-
081   tion model.
082
      In this paper, we propose a spatio-temporal diffusion transformer (STD-Former) to accurately iden-
083
      tify fine-grained actions with long time spans. STD-Former contains two branches with the trans-
084   former architecture: a spatiotemporal branch and a temporal branch, which extract the spatiotempo-
085   ral features of video and the motion features of the moving subject, respectively. In the spatiotem-
086   poral branch, we construct a parallel transformer module to extract global spatiotemporal features
087   and enhance local temporal information via a two-dimensional convolutional structure. For the tem-
088   poral branch, multiple cross transformer modules are utilized to integrate the features from both
089   branches and model long-range temporal dependency. Furthermore, we present a spatiotemporal
090   diffusion module, which continuously feeds back the temporal information from temporal branch to
091   the spatiotemporal branch, thus strengthening the spatiotemporal features. Additionally, a motion
092
      excitation module is designed to extract the moving information from adjacent video sequences and
      embeded into two branches. To verify the performance of STD-Former, we conducted a series of ex-
093
      periments on the Something Something V1 and V2 datasets. The experimental results demonstrate
094
      that STD-Former achieves higher accuracy than most mainstream models. The main contributions
095   of this paper are as follows:
096
097   (1) In this paper, we propose a spatio-temporal diffusion transformer (STD-Former) with a spa-
098
      tiotemporal branch and a temporal branch for action recognition. The two branches respectively
      consist of multiple parallel transformer modules and cross transformer modules to extract global
099
      spatiotemporal features of video and the temporal information of actions.
100
101   (2) We devise a spatiotemporal diffusion module to explore the long-term dependency of video
102   actions through the temporal information propagation between the two branches.
103   (3) To effectively capture detail information of fine-grained actions, a lightweight salient motion ex-
104   citation module is integrated into both branches of STD-Former, which extracts key motion features
105   from adjacent video sequences.

      2     R ELATED WORK
109
110
      2.1   CNN- BASED ACTION RECOGNITION METHODS
111
112   At present, the architectures of the backbone for the existing CNN-based action recognition models
113   mainly include 2D CNN, 3D CNN, and two-stream networks. The early action recognition meth-
114   ods (Yuan et al., 2022; Sudhakaran et al., 2023; Qiu et al., 2017) were based on 2D convolution.
115   Li et al. (2020) introduced a novel temporal excitation and aggregation (TEA) approach to effec-
116   tively capture short-range and long-range temporal dynamics in videos. MSNet(Kwon et al., 2020)
117   extracted displacement tensors from adjacent video sequences for motion representation learning.
118   Wang et al. (2021) presented a novel video architecture, temporal difference network (TDN), to cap-
119
      ture multi-scale temporal information for efficient action recognition by using a temporal difference
      operator. Li et al. (2021) proposed a video classification model called CT-Net, which employed
120
      channel tensorization to enhance feature interaction and receptive fields for improving classification
121
      accuracy. Multi-view fusion network (MVFNet) (Wu et al., 2021b) modeled multi-view features
122   of video, thereby enhancing the recognition performance of model. Temporal adaptive module
123   (TAM) (Liu et al., 2021) employed an attention mechanism to fuse multi-scale features. Wang et al.
124   (2023a) introduced an adjoint enhancement network (AE-Net), which addressed the challenges of
125   motion information loss and misalignment of temporal attention through global adjoint enhance-
126   ment module. Peng & Tseng (2023) presented a multi-scale motion-aware (MSMA) module to ef-
127   fectively capture motion information at different scales. 2D CNN-based action recognition methods
128   could efficiently extract the spatial feature, but they were limited in acquiring temporal dynamics,
129   particularly long-term dependency.
130   In contrast, 3D CNN-based action recognition models can extract spatiotemporal features of video
131   directly, capturing video action patterns effectively. Considering the high computational cost of
132   3D convolution, pseudo 3D (P3D) (Qiu et al., 2017) and spatiotemporal 3D (S3D) (Xie et al., 2018)
133   decomposed 3D convolution into a combination of 2D and 1D convolutions to extract spatiotemporal
134   information. However, 3D CNN-based methods still have a large number of parameters, and the
135   training speed of models is slow.
136   Additionally, some two-stream based action recognition networks independently processed RGB
137   and optical flow information of video. Based on the two-stream architecture, Wang et al. (2016)
138   proposed a temporal segment network (TSN), which divided video sequences at fixed intervals to
139   enhance the recognition ability of key actions through chronological segmented sampling and a
140   sparse sampling strategy. Subsequently, Feichtenhofer et al. (2019) proposed SlowFast with two-
141   branch structure, where slow branch receives images at a lower frame rate to extract spatial details,
142
      and another fast branch processes at a higher rate to capture motion cues. However, the two-stream
      network requires additional calculation of optical flow information, and it susceptible to illumination
143
      variations and occlusions, thus impacting action recognition accuracy. To sum up, the CNN-based
144
      action recognition methods are difficult to capture the complex temporal features of video, due to
145   the limited receptive field of convolutional operations.

      3.1   OVERVIEW
203
204   Considering the challenge of long time spans of actions and the indistinguishable fine-grained fea-
205   tures, we integrate transformer architecture mining global features with convolutional operator that
206   can extract local information effectively, and propose a spatio-temporal diffusion transformer (STD-
207   Former) model. STD-Former employs a dual-branch structure to capture the global spatiotemporal
208   information in videos.

213   tively. The spatiotemporal branch mainly contains twelve parallel transformer module (PTM), which
214   parallelly extracts the temporal and spatial features of video by attention mechanism and convolu-
215   tional structure respectively. Meanwhile, another temporal branch is mainly composed of twelve

      3.2   PARALLEL T RANSFORMER M ODULE
238
239   The extraction of spatiotemporal information in videos is crucial to action recognition. We design
240   a PTM to capture global spatiotemporal features of videos in this paper. Figure 2 illustrates the
241   architecture of PTM which integrates a modified multi-head attention mechanism (MHA), a feed-
242   forward neural network (FFN), and a two-dimensional convolutional layer (2D Conv) in parallel
243   after dynamic position encoding (DPE).
244   Firstly, depthwise separable convolution based DPE encodes positional information of video actions,
245   which is composed of a 1×1×1 convolution, a 3×1×1 convolution and a 1×1×1 convolution. Mean-
246   while, a residual connection is added to integrate the original video information. Then, the encoded
247   features obtained by DPE are fed into the MHA, FFN and 2D convolution layer respectively. After
248   layer normalization (LayerNorm), MHA and FFN are used to extract global spatiotemporal feature
249   y1 and y2 through the weighted summation of multiple attention heads and the combination of linear
250   transformation and activation function respectively. At the same time, we extract temporal feature
251   y3 from adjacent frames of videos through a 2D convolutional layer including a 1×1, a 3×3 and a
252
      1×1 two-dimensional convolutional structure.
253   Finally, the video features acquired from MHA, FFN and 2D convolutional layer are fused. A feature
254   fusion strategy with learnable parameters is developed to enhance the global spatiotemporal features
255   of video actions.

      Inspired by the advantage of the diffusion principle for capturing long-distance relevant informa-

310   3.5   S ALIENT M OTION E XCITATION M ODULE
311
312   The change of the appearance features in videos can reflect the motion patterns of actions. To bet-
313   ter extract video temporal information, we construct a lightweight SMEM, which converts spatial
314   information from adjacent frames into motion features. Figure 5 shows the structure of SMEM. Ini-
315
      tially, the input feature is process by a 3×3 2D convolution to extract local spatial information of the
      video. Next, to extract the feature correlation of video sequences, a correlation calculation module is
316
      introduced, which contains temporal feature separation, dimension reshaping, matrix multiplication
317
      and feature concatenation to enhance the features of adjacent video sequences and aggregate motion
318   information at different moments. After the motion information is obtained, feature transformation
319   is applied to enhance the temporal features of the video. This process consists of stacked 3×3 2D
320   convolution layers, downsample operation, batch normalization (BatchNorm) and activation func-
321   tions. Finally, a 3×3 2D convolution is utilized to further strengthen temporal information of the
322   video, followed by an upsampling operation to restore the feature dimension. SMEM can capture
323   the changes of action at different granularities from spatial features, thereby enhancing the feature
      representation of fine-grained actions.

341
342   4.1   DATASET
343
344   Two mainstream datasets in the field of action recognition are used to verify the performance of
345   the proposed STD-Former. Something Something V1 (SSV1) dataset is collected in real scenes
346   and records the action information of different objects from multiple angles, including the subtle
347   changes of the moving subject, which consists of 108,499 video clips. It describes the interactive
348   actions between people and objects in different scenarios. These actions are divided into 174 cate-
349
      gories(Raghav Goyal, 2017).
350   Another Something Something V2 (SSV2) dataset also has 174 action categories, but the number
351   of videos has increased to 220,487(Raghav Goyal, 2017). This dataset not only records richer video
352   action information but also greatly reduces the impact of label noise on action recognition. Different
353   from other scene datasets that rely on video background for action prediction, there are very similar
354   action scenes in SSV1 and SSV2 datasets, but they belong to different action categories, such as
355
      picking up and putting down objects, opening and closing containers, etc. Therefore, SSV1 and
      SSV2 belong to time-dependent datasets. They put forward higher requirements on the ability of
356
      action recognition methods to capture effective spatio-temporal information of videos. After that,
357
      random inversion operations are used to increase the number of model input samples.
358
359
360   4.2   E XPERIMENTAL SETTING
361
362
      RGB video data of datasets are first sampled at an interval of 16 frames per second through a sparse
      time sampling strategy, and then are cropped to a size of 224×224. After that, random inversion
363
      operations are utilized to increase the number of model input samples. During training model, the
364
      initial learning rate and the training batch size (Batch Size) are set to 0.0001 and 16 respectively.
365   The cosine annealing strategy is employed to update the current learning rate of the network. The
366   common AdamW (Adam with Weight decay) and the soft cross-entropy function are used for the op-
367   timizer and the loss function of model respectively. In addition, STD-Former model is trained based
368   on the parameters of Contrastive Language–Image Pre-training (CLIP). The model is executed on a
369   NVIDIA RTX 4090 server, and the experimental results are obtained in PyTorch 1.10 framework in
370   this paper.

      5   C ONCLUSION
496
497
      In this paper, we propose a STD-Former model to accurately identify the fine-grained and long
      time span actions from highly similar video backgrounds. STD-Former draws on the advantage of
498
      transformer architecture in modeling global information and a dual-branch structure is established,
499
      including a spatiotemporal branch and a temporal branch. Firstly, PTM is designed to mine the
500   spatiotemporal information of videos by a parallel structure, and a two-dimensional convolution
501   layer is added to enhance the temporal features of actions in the spatiotemporal branch. Meanwhile,
502   in the temporal branch, CTM is created to extract the long-distance temporal dependencies of video
503   actions. Furthermore, a novel STDM is presented to gradually feed back the temporal information
504   of temporal branch to the spatiotemporal branch, thus enhancing the spatiotemporal features through
505   inter-branch information diffusion. Additionally, to capture the fine-grained action features between
506   adjacent video sequences, a lightweight SMEM is constructed and embedded into the two branches.
507   Finally, the extracted spatiotemporal and motion features from both branches are fused to produce
508
      action prediction. The experimental results on SSV1 and SSV2 datasets show that STD-Former has
      favorable performance for the long-distance and fine-grained actions recognition.
