                                                       A BSTRACT
032
              The feed-forward based 3D Gaussian Splatting method has demonstrated excep-
033
              tional capability in real-time human novel view synthesis. However, existing ap-
034
              proaches are restricted to dense viewpoint settings, where camera view angles are
035           less than 60 degrees. This limitation constrains their flexibility in free-viewpoint
036           rendering across a wide range of camera view angle discrepancies. To address
037           this limitation, we propose a real-time pipeline named EVA-Gaussian for 3D hu-
038           man novel view synthesis across diverse multi-view camera settings. Specifically,
039           we first introduce an Efficient cross-View Attention (EVA) module to accurately
040           estimate the position of each 3D Gaussian from the source images. Then, we
041           integrate the source images with the estimated Gaussian position map to predict
042           the attributes and feature embeddings of the 3D Gaussians. Moreover, we em-
043
              ploy a recurrent feature refiner to correct artifacts caused by geometric errors
              in position estimation and enhance visual fidelity. To further improve synthesis
044
              quality, we incorporate a powerful anchor loss function for both 3D Gaussian
045
              attributes and human face landmarks. Experimental results on the THuman2.0
046           and THumansit datasets showcase the superiority of our EVA-Gaussian approach
047           in rendering quality across diverse camera settings. Project page: https:
048           //anonymousiclr2025.github.io/iclr2025/EVA-Gaussian.

051   1   I NTRODUCTION
052
053   3D reconstruction and novel view synthesis have long been fundamental yet complex tasks in vi-
      sual data representation and computer vision. Recent advancements in fast 3D reconstruction and


                                                           1

054
      novel view synthesis for humans have shown immense potential in applications such as holographic
055   communication, real-time teaching, and augmented/virtual reality (AR/VR), where time efficiency
056   is critical for user experience and downstream processing. Nonetheless, existing methods either rely
057   on dense input views and precise templates as prior knowledge (Qian et al., 2024; Lei et al., 2024;
058   Hu et al., 2024; Wen et al., 2024; Kocabas et al., 2024; Kwon et al., 2024) or are restricted to specific
059   camera poses (Zheng et al., 2024; Tu et al., 2024). None of these approaches have fully developed
060   a pipeline for real-time human reconstruction under diverse, especially sparse, camera viewpoints,
061   which remains a significant challenge.
062   In recent years, Neural Radiance Fields (NeRFs) (Mildenhall et al., 2021) have emerged as a promis-
063   ing technique for 3D reconstruction. These models employ neural networks to predict the color and
064   density of sampled 3D points along camera rays and aggregate these predictions to synthesize novel
065   images with high fidelity. Despite their effectiveness, NeRFs suffer from substantial time con-
066   sumption during both the training and rendering phases. Although various advancements, such as
067   multi-resolution hash encoding (Müller et al., 2022) and feed-forward neural scene prediction (Yu
068   et al., 2021a; Xu et al., 2024), have been made to reduce the time for reconstruction and rendering,
069
      the achievable speeds remain insufficient for real-time applications.
070   More recently, 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) (Kerbl et al., 2023) has been introduced as a solution
071   to this rendering bottleneck. 3DGS utilizes a set of discrete Gaussian representations to model
072   complex 3D scenes and leverages the α-blending technique to enable real-time novel view rendering.
073   In the field of 3D human avatar reconstruction, previous works (Qian et al., 2024; Lei et al., 2024;
074   Hu et al., 2024; Wen et al., 2024; Kocabas et al., 2024; Kwon et al., 2024) have employed 3DGS
075
      as a representation for humans to achieve animatable full-body human avatar reconstruction. These
      methods, however, rely on precise human templates as priors, and their training and reconstruction
076
      processes can take from minutes to hours, which impedes their use in real-time applications such
077
      as holographic communication. While a feed-forward human reconstruction method (Zheng et al.,
078   2024) has achieved fast reconstruction and real-time rendering with stereo inputs, the stereo settings
079   and small angle change between camera viewpoints restrict the overall reconstruction quality under
080   sparse camera settings and lead to sub-optimal performance with more than two input views.
081
      To address these limitations and enable real-time 3D reconstruction of humans using various camera
082
      positions and different numbers of cameras, we propose EVA-Gaussian, a novel 3D Gaussian-based
083
      pipeline for real-time human novel view synthesis. Our method attaches 3D Gaussians to the sur-
084   face of human body through multi-view depth estimation and aligns their positions closely with
085   point cloud locations. A key innovation of our method is the introduction of an Efficient cross-View
086   Attention (EVA) module for multi-view 3D Gaussian position estimation (see Sec. 4.2). Specif-
087   ically, we employ a U-Net (Ronneberger et al., 2015) as the backbone and further use dedicated
088   window-embedded cross-view attention to infer multi-view position correspondences. This attention
089   mechanism enables EVA-Gaussian to effectively process multiple inputs from cameras positioned
090   at various viewpoint angles, thereby ensuring robust performance across a wide range of viewing
091   angles, even under extremely sparse camera settings. Besides, we incorporate a Gaussian attribute
092
      estimation module that takes the EVA output and the original RGB images as input to estimate the
      remaining 3D Gaussian attributes (see Sec. 4.3). Furthermore, we embed an additional attribute,
093
      referred to as feature, into each Gaussian for further feature splatting and image quality refinement,
094
      thereby mitigating the position estimation error introduced by the EVA module (see Sec. 4.4). In
095   addition, we employ an anchor loss to penalize the inconsistency between multi-view face land-
096   marks, which achieves better supervision for human faces (see Sec. 4.5). We conduct extensive
097   experiments on the THuman2.0 (Yu et al., 2021b) and THumanSit (Zhang et al., 2023) datasets. The
098   results, as exemplified in Fig. 1, demonstrate that our proposed EVA-Gaussian outperforms existing
099   feed-forward synthesis approaches in rendering quality, while enabling real-time reconstruction and
100   rendering. Moreover, our approach generalizes well to settings with diverse numbers of cameras and
101   significant changes in camera viewpoint angles. In summary, our main contributions are as follows:
102

             • We propose a novel pipeline for fast feed-forward 3D human reconstruction, called EVA-
               Gaussian, that comprises three main stages: 1) a multi-view 3D Gaussian position estima-

118   2   R ELATED W ORKS
119
120
      3DGS-based Human Reconstruction. 3D Gaussians Splatting has recently emerged as an effective
121
      technique for 3D human reconstruction. However, most previous works (Lei et al., 2024; Hu et al.,
122   2024; Wen et al., 2024; Qian et al., 2024; Kocabas et al., 2024; Pan et al., 2024) bind 3D Gaussians
123   to a predefined human mesh model, such as SMPL (Loper et al., 2023) or SMPL-X (Pavlakos et al.,
124   2019). This approach generates 3D Gaussians and human models in a canonical space and then
125   transforms them to match the target human pose using the predefined weights of the human model.
126   This iterative binding process, however, is extremely time-consuming. Moreover, these methods
127   require human templates as inputs at each frame, which incurs extra computational cost and poten-
128   tially misleads the reconstruction procedure due to the errors in pose estimation. These limitations
129   significantly hinder their applicability in real-world scenarios.
130   Fast Generalizable 3D Reconstruction. In the field of NeRF rendering, pixelNeRF (Yu et al.,
131   2021a) pioneers the approach of predicting features per pixel from a single image in a feed-forward
132   manner for 3D reconstruction. While subsequent works (Chen et al., 2021; Wang et al., 2021; Lin
133   et al., 2022) have followed this feed-forward NeRF pipeline, they still suffer from the extensive time
134   consumption of the NeRF rendering process. Besides, their reconstruction results are often unsatis-
135   factory in sparse camera settings. The introduction of 3DGS has helped mitigate the rendering speed
136
      issue of high-quality novel view synthesis methods. Notably, pixelSplat (Charatan et al., 2024) and
      Splatter Image (Szymanowicz et al., 2024) are the first to combine the feed-forward inference and
137
      3DGS, which predict 3D Gaussian attributes for each pixel and project them back to the 3D space for
138
      novel view synthesis in a real-time manner. Nevertheless, these methods still struggle with inaccu-
139   rate estimation of 3D Gaussian positions. MVSplat (Chen et al., 2024) and MVGaussian (Liu et al.,
140   2024a) address this issue by leveraging cost-volume modules, thereby achieving better novel view
141   image quality. Moreover, latentSplat (Wewer et al., 2024) attaches a latent vector to each 3D Gaus-
142   sian and uses this vector for novel view refinement through a diffusion decoder and generative loss,
143   which significantly improves image quality on extrapolation views. Despite these advancements,
144   these approaches do not fully exploit prior knowledge about human images and camera settings,
145   which limits their performance on human reconstruction and novel view synthesis. In the field of
146   3D generation, LGM (Tang et al., 2024) and GSLRM (Zhang et al., 2024) employ a transformer-
147
      based feed-forward pipeline for predicting 3D Gaussian attributes from multi-view images in real-
      world scenarios, but these networks are often too complex for real-time reconstruction. Our work
148
      utilizes a carefully designed memory-efficient attention mechanism, thereby enabling generalizable
149
      3D reconstruction at high resolutions with a much lower temporal and computational cost.
150
151   The work most closely related to ours is GPS-Gaussian (Zheng et al., 2024), which proposes a stereo
152   matching network for 3D Gaussian position estimation and employs two 3-layer U-Nets to predict
153
      3D Gaussian scales, rotations, and opacities. Although GPS-Gaussian has demonstrated the capa-
      bility for real-time human reconstruction and novel view synthesis, it suffers from severe distortions
154
      under sparse camera settings and mismatch across multiple viewpoints. Subsequent works have
155
      attempted to alleviate these issues. For instance, Tele-Aloha (Tu et al., 2024) introduces an image
156   blending and cascaded disparity estimation method for human reconstruction with four input views.
157   However, this approach is tailored to a specific system and struggles to generalize to sparser camera
158   settings. On the other hand, GHG (Kwon et al., 2024) achieves real-time 3D Gaussian-based human
159   novel view synthesis in a feed-forward manner, but it requires additional human template priors,
160   thus inheriting the limitations associated with template-based human reconstruction methods. In
161   contrast, our proposed method eliminates the need for human templates and is specifically designed
      to generalize effectively across various sparse camera settings.

      4.1   OVERVIEW
192
193   In this paper, we focus on fast human 3D reconstruction and novel view synthesis under diverse
194   camera settings. Our objective is to reconstruct a 3D scene from a set of n sparse-view RGB images
195   {Ii }ni=1 , Ii ∈ RH×W ×3 , captured from different viewpoints surrounding a human subject, where
196   the angle between any two adjacent camera views is denoted by ∆, and synthesize arbitrary novel
197   view images at any camera position in real time. To achieve this, we propose EVA-Gaussian, a
198   method that utilizes deep neural networks and 3D Gaussian Splatting to enhance novel image quality
199   while achieving real-time reconstruction.
200   Specifically, we employ 3DGS to represent each source image Ii as a set of 3D Gaussians. Each
201   pixel in the foreground corresponds to a unique 3D Gaussian. We use Ui to denote the number of
202   Gaussians for source image i. The proposed EVA-Gaussian predicts the positions and attributes of
203   3D Gaussians in the form of attribute maps {Mi }ni=1 = {Pi , Oi , Si , Qi , Fi }ni=1 from the image set
204   {Ii }ni=1 , where Pi , Oi , Si , Qi , and Fi denote the attribute maps for Gaussian positions, opacities,
205   scales, quaternions, and features of source image i, respectively. Notably, in the feature map Fi =

245   The variations in depth across the surface of human body may appear minimal. However, these nu-

249   estimation of 3D Gaussian positions to enable effective and high-fidelity human reconstruction.
250   To tackle this challenge, we employ a U-Net based architecture DθP1 to estimate the 3D Gaussian
251   position maps {Pi }ni=1 from multi-view images {Ii }ni=1 , which is expressed as:

527   6     C ONCLUSION
528
529   In this paper, we introduce EVA-Gaussian, a novel real-time 3D human reconstruction pipeline
530   that employs multi-view attention-based 3D Gaussian position estimation and comprehensive fea-
531   ture refinement. To ensure robust performance, the method is trained using both photometric loss
532   and anchor loss. Quantitative and qualitative evaluations on benchmark datasets demonstrate that
533   our EVA-Gaussian achieves state-of-the-art performance while maintaining a competitive inference
534   speed, particularly under sparse camera settings.
535   While EVA-Gaussian synthesizes high-fidelity novel views, there remain several areas for improve-
536   ment. For instance, the attention module can consume substantial GPU memory when processing a
537   large number of input views or high-resolution images. In addition, the naive reprojection of pixels
538   into 3D space may introduce conflicts in overlapping areas, leading to redundancy in the 3D rep-
539   resentation. These limitations can be effectively addressed by incorporating RGBD information or
      developing overlap area detection techniques.

      Network architectures. Our Gaussian position estimation network DθP1 utilizes a U-Net as the
733
      backbone. The architecture incorporates four stages of 2× down-sampling using average pooling to
734
      extract essential feature details. Symmetrically, the network features four stages of 2× up-sampling,
735   achieved through transpose convolutional neural networks. The EVA module is incorporated before
736   the 4×, 8×, 16× down-sampling and up-sampling blocks. The channel dimension starts at 64 prior
737   to the first down-sampling block, doubling after each down-sampling block and halving after each
738   up-sampling block, which is facilitated by two residual blocks (He et al., 2016). The Gaussian
739   attribute estimation network DθA2 also employs a U-Net backbone, but it does not include the EVA
740   modules and performs only two stages of 2× down-sampling. The architecture of the feature refiner,
741   DθR3 , mirrors that of DθA2 , but operates in a recurrent manner.
742   More training details. The training hyper-parameters are set as follows: λ1 = 1, λ2 = 1, λ3 =
743   103 , λopacity = 1, λopacity = 1, λrender = 0.25, and t = 0.05. The number of recurrent loops L
744   mentioned in Sec. 4.4 is empirically set to L = 1 to enhance temporal efficiency. Each training batch
745   contains 2 to 4 source view images, depending on the specific reconstruction task. For instance,
746   the stereo reconstruction task in Sec. 5.2 utilizes 2 source view images. For novel view image
747   supervision, 3 randomly selected views are chosen between each adjacent pair of source views to
748   compute Lrefine and Lrender . The learning rate for deep supervised pre-training and overall network
749
      training is initialized to 0.0002 and decreases linearly with the number of training epochs.

909   In this section, we further clarify the motivation of our proposed EVA module, detail its architecture,
910   and analyze its advantages over existing attention mechanisms.
911
      Attention mechanisms that leverage multi-view correspondence across different viewpoints, such
912   as epipolar attention (He et al., 2020), have proven beneficial for downstream 3D tasks like pose
913   estimation. Epipolar attention has demonstrated its effectiveness by performing attention for each
914   pixel only with sampled points along its epipolar line. This approach is based on the principle that a
915   pixel in the source image corresponds to a pixel along the epipolar line in the target image. However,
916   the sampling process and attention calculation in traditional epipolar attention are computationally
917   and temporally intensive, as shown in 5. To mitigate this problem, we propose the EVA module,
      specifically tailored for the camera settings in feed forward human 3D Gaussian reconstruction,
