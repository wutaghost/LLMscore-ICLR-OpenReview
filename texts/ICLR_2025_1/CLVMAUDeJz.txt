009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            In this paper, the distributed constrained optimal consensus problem of multi-
012            agent systems under a directed graph is investigated. We propose two projection-
013            based distributed constrained optimal consensus algorithms: one addressing set
014            constraints and the other tailored for general constraints. Only the relative state
015            is exchanged among agents in these two algorithms. In the stability analysis of
016            case with set constraints, we transform the distributed optimization problem into a
017            constrained leaderless consensus problem by adopting a sliding mode approach.
018
               Building on this foundational transformation, we further develop a projection-
               based distributed constrained optimal consensus algorithm to address general con-
019
               straints. It is shown that the proposed algorithm achieves an ergodic convergence
020
               rate of O( k1 ) with respect to the ﬁrst-order optimality residuals. Numerical simu-
021            lations are conducted to validate the effectiveness of our theoretical results.

025   1     INTRODUCTION
026
027   In the past two decades, distributed optimization of multi-agent systems has drawn extensive atten-
028   tion. As a fundamental problem of distributed optimization, distributed optimal consensus refers
029   to construct an algorithm for each agent such that the states of agents converge to the optimal solu-
030   tion of a global objective function using local information. Early distributed optimal consensus of
031   multi-agent systems can be referred to (Shi et al., 2015) and (Kia et al., 2015). The distributed opti-
032
      mization algorithm has diverse practical applications, including cooperative transportation (Chen &
      Kai, 2018), energy management (Wang et al., 2024), distributed learning (Arjevani & Shamir, 2015).
033
      For a comprehensive overview of recent advances in distributed optimization, one can refer to (Yang
034
      et al., 2019; Shorinwa et al., 2024).
035
036
      1.1   R ELATED WORK
037
038   Many previous studies have developed distributed optimization algorithms under different types of
039   constraints. In relation to the work presented in this paper, we categorize the current advancements
040   into three groups based on the types of constraints as follows:
041   Unconstrained optimization: Distributed discrete time unconstrained optimization algorithms pri-
042   marily include DGD (Nedic & Ozdaglar, 2009; Tu et al., 2022), EXTRA (Shi et al., 2015), DEXTRA
043   (Xi & Khan, 2017), Exact-Diffusion (Yuan et al., 2018; Alghunaim, 2024), Gradient-Tracking (Pu
044   & Nedić, 2021), among others. Compared with the vanishing step-sizes in DGD, EXTRA, Exact-
045
      Diffusion, and Gradient-Tracking uses constant step-size and has linear convergence speed O(C −k ).
      Be aware that due to the introduction of an integral term used to compensate gradient in equilibrium,
046
      both EXTRA and DEXTRA demonstrate second-order dynamics.
047
      Optimization with set constraints: Set constraints are considered in (Nedic et al., 2010; Liu &
048   Wang, 2015; Scaman et al., 2018; Mai & Abed, 2019; YU2, 2021). Distributed optimization prob-
049   lems with set constraints under an undirected graph are considered in (Nedic et al., 2010; Liu &
050   Wang, 2015; Scaman et al., 2018). Early projection-based algorithm considering is presented in
051   (Nedic et al., 2010). (Liu & Wang, 2015) proposes a distributed proportional-integral (PI) type
052   projection-based gradient method. Conic constraint sets are considered in (Scaman et al., 2018).
053   Different from the undirected graph case, general directed graph cases are considered in (Mai &
      Abed, 2019; YU2, 2021; Wan et al., 2020). Due to the second-order dynamics in EXTRA, DEX-


                                                        1
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


054
      TRA, Exact-Diffusion, it is of challenge for incorporating projection algorithms in environments
055   with set constraints. This is precisely why the existing algorithms with set constraints adopt vanish-
056   ing step-sizes in (Mai & Abed, 2019; YU2, 2021; Wan et al., 2020). Moreover, vanishing step-sizes
057   are used in distributed gradient-tracking algorithm due to different set constraints (Cheng et al.,
058   2023). One drawback of utilizing diminishing step sizes is a slower convergence rate. Therefore, it
059   is desired to design a projection based algorithm with constant step-sizes.
060   Optimization with general constraints: Except for the set constraints considered in (Nedic et al.,
061   2010; Liu & Wang, 2015; Scaman et al., 2018; Mai & Abed, 2019; YU2, 2021; Wan et al., 2020),
062   general constraints include equality constraints and nonlinear inequality constraints, which can be
063
      solved by primal-dual method (Gao, 2004). These nonlinear constraints bring more challenges to
      the distributed optimization algorithm design. The existing algorithms on distributed optimization
064
      algorithms addresses both the undirected graph scenario (Yang et al., 2016; Zhu et al., 2018) and
065
      the directed graph scenario (Khatana & Salapaka, 2023). Besides the difﬁculties of projection oper-
066   ation, the existence of nonlinear inequality constraints has introduced signiﬁcant complexities into
067   stability analysis, which is not considered in (Khatana & Salapaka, 2023).

069
      1.2    C ONTRIBUTION
070
071   From the above analysis, it becomes clear that existing distributed optimization algorithms have not
072   yet tackled scenarios that simultaneously incorporate directed topology graphs, ﬁxed step sizes, and
073   general constraints. In this paper, we study the distributed constrained optimal consensus problem of
074   multi-agent systems under a directed graph using only relative state. The main challenge lies in the
075   fact that the nonlinear projection operator and nonlinear constraints hinder the stability analysis. To
076   address this challenge, we introduce an original analysis framework based on the sliding mode tech-
077   nique, which transforms the distributed constrained optimal consensus problem into a constrained
078   leaderless consensus problem. Compared with existing results (Nedic et al., 2010; Liu & Wang,
079
      2015; Scaman et al., 2018; Mai & Abed, 2019; YU2, 2021; Wan et al., 2020; Gao, 2004; Yang et al.,
      2016; Zhu et al., 2018; Khatana & Salapaka, 2023), the proposed algorithm makes the following
080
      contributions:
081
082       Case with set constraints: we develop a distributed algorithm using only the interaction of
083   relative state under a balanced directed graph (Theorem 3.6). By using a sliding mode approach,
084   we transform the distributed constrained optimal consensus problem into a constrained leaderless
085
      consensus problem. Within this transformed framework, we design a novel Lyapunov function and
      conduct a comprehensive stability analysis to illustrate the effectiveness of our approach. Different
086
      from undirected graphs considered in (Nedic et al., 2010; Liu & Wang, 2015; Scaman et al., 2018),
087
      we consider the directed graph case. Compared with the methods with vanishing step-sizes (Mai &
088   Abed, 2019; YU2, 2021), our algorithm utilize the constant step-sizes which can achieve faster con-
089   vergence speed. By omitting local set constraints, our algorithm can be conducted under a directed
090   graph case, which is more general than EXTRA (Shi et al., 2015).
091
          Case with general constraints: A projection-based algorithm is proposed for the case with
092
      general constraints (Theorem 4.4). Different from the undirected graph case, our proposed algo-
093
      rithm (Yang et al., 2016; Zhu et al., 2018) can be deployed under a directed graph. Compared with
094   (Khatana & Salapaka, 2023), we only communicate the relative state information. Unlike the sin-
095   gle system optimization algorithm in (Gao, 2004), we propose a novel algorithm to tackle general
096   constraints.

517
518   In this paper, we have investigated the distributed constrained optimal consensus problem for multi-
519   agent systems under a directed graph. By employing the sliding mode method, we propose a
520   projection-based distributed constrained optimal consensus algorithm that utilizes only relative state
521
      information for the optimization problem with set constraints. Furthermore, based on this frame-
      work, we have explored the distributed optimization problem with general constraints. The simula-
522
      tion results have illustrated that all agents can converge to the optimal solution with the proposed

      algorithm.
