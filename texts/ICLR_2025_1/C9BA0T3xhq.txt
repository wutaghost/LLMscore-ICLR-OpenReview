011                                               A BSTRACT
012
013            Offline Reinforcement Learning (RL) presents unique challenges, primarily due
014            to the constraint of learning from a static dataset without additional environmen-
015            tal interaction. Traditional methods often face limitations in effectively exploiting
016
               the available data, particularly when navigating the exploration-exploitation trade-
               off inherent in RL. This paper introduces a novel algorithm inspired by Implicit
017
               Q-Learning Kostrikov et al. (2021), designed to extend the utility of the Bellman
018
               update to actions not explicitly present in the dataset. Our approach, termed Ex-
019            tended Implicit Q-Learning (EIQL), strategically incorporates actions beyond the
020            dataset constraints by allowing selection actions with maximum Q. By doing so,
021            it leverages the maximization capability of the Bellman update, while simultane-
022            ously mitigating error extrapolation risks. We demonstrate the efficacy of EIQL
023            through a series of experiments that show its improved performance over tradi-
024            tional offline RL algorithms, particularly in environments characterized by sparse
025            rewards or those containing suboptimal and incomplete trajectories. Our results
026            suggest that EIQL enhances the potential of offline RL by utilizing a broader ac-
027
               tion spectrum.

      1   I NTRODUCTION
031
032   Reinforcement learning (RL) has made significant advances in solving sequential decision problems
033
      in recent years. Training an RL agent typically requires extensive interaction with the environment,
      which can involve taking actions that are either dangerous or costly. Additionally, online interactions
034
      with the environment are time-consuming. Offline RL addresses these challenges by learning from
035
      pre-existing datasets without real-time interactions, proving invaluable in practice. However, offline
036   RL must contend with the challenge of distribution shift. Unlike conventional supervised learning,
037   which trains models to perform well on data from the same distribution as the training set, offline
038   RL aims to derive policies that outperform those in the dataset. This involves dynamic programming
039   that might query out-of-distribution (OOD) data, as policies trained on historical data are applied to
040   new and varied state spaces. This can lead to error extrapolation if not properly regularized, under-
041   scoring the need for advanced methods capable of adapting to and overcoming these distributional
042   discrepanciesLevine et al. (2020).
043   To mitigate the distribution shift in offline RL, current approaches involve penalizing OOD state-
044   action pairs or regularizing the trained policy towards the behavior policy. Works such as Kumar
045   et al. (2019) and Kumar et al. (2020) explicitly constrain policy or Q-value updates to ensure pes-
046   simism during training. However, balancing the tradeoff between staying within the support of
047   the data and avoiding suboptimal solutions when constraints are too conservative remains chal-
048   lengingKumar et al. (2019). Another approach focuses on eliminating extrapolation error through
049   in-sample learning, which updates the Bellman target using only actions present within the dataset.
050
      Implicit Q-Learning (IQL), for instance, employs in-sample learning and avoids querying the value
      of unseen actions by using expectile regression to assess the near-optimality of actions taken under
051
      the observed policy.
052
053   In this paper, we introduce a novel algorithm, Extended Implicit Q-Learning (EIQL), which builds
      on the framework of Implicit Q-Learning. EIQL occasionally allows querying actions not present in

      2     R ELATED W ORK
061
062   Offline reinforcement learning (RL) has recently gained substantial attention due to its potential
063   to learn effective policies from static datasets without additional online exploration. The primary
064   challenge in offline RL is to mitigate extrapolation errors that arise when the learning algorithm
065   queries states and actions that are not well-represented in the dataset. Various approaches have been
066   proposed to address this issue, often focusing on constraining the learned policy to remain within
067   the support of the dataset Levine et al. (2020).
068   Implicit Q-Learning (IQL) is an offline reinforcement learning algorithm designed to avoid evalu-
069   ating out-of-distribution actions while still enabling dynamic programming. By employing expec-
070   tile regression, IQL introduces minor yet effective modifications to the conventional SARSA-like
071   temporal difference learning algorithmKostrikov et al. (2021). Demonstrably, IQL achieves robust
072   performance on the D4RL benchmarkFu et al. (2020).

079   with an environment assumed to be a Markov Decision Process (MDP). An MDP is characterized
080   by a set of states S, a set of actions A, a scalar reward function R, transition dynamics p, and a
081   discount factor γ. The agent’s interaction with the MDP is governed by a policy π(a|s), which can

      either be a deterministic mapping or a stochastic distribution over actions. The primary objective
      in RL  is to derive a policy that maximizes the expected discounted return, formally expressed as

303   To enhance the robustness of the Bellman update in the value function estimation, we explore the
304   adaptation of expectile regression as an alternative to the conventional mean-based approach. This
305   modification involves fitting the expectile of the return distribution rather than the mean, offering
306   a nuanced view that potentially accommodates the asymmetry in the distribution of returns. We
307   experiment with various levels of the expectile, specifically examining τ = 0.5, which corresponds
308
      to the mean-based update, as well as other expectile levels to investigate their impacts on policy
      performance.
309

311   large-play-v2, and kitchen-partial-v0 from Fu et al. (2020). These datasets were chosen to represent

315   resultant policy efficacy in offline reinforcement learning settings. By comparing these outcomes,

      6   C ONCLUSION
379
380
      In this paper, we introduced EIQL, a novel algorithm that engages in both in-sample and out-
381   of-sample learning. This approach leverages the stability provided by learning from high-quality
382   in-sample data, while also effectively incorporating potential valuable out-of-sample actions. Our
383   algorithm maintains computational efficiency and does not significantly increase the complexity
384   compared to the baseline IQL model.
385
      Experimental results demonstrate that EIQL not only enhances performance but also represents the
386
      first application of in-sample offline reinforcement learning as a regularization strategy. This dual
387   learning capability enables our algorithm to mitigate the impact of maximum error extrapolation
388   caused by out-of-sample (OOD) data, achieving robust performance across varied scenarios.
389
390
      In future work, we plan to explore the potential of our method to improve other in-sample learning
      techniques, with the aim of assessing the generalizability of our approach across different domains
