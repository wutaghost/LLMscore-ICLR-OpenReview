011                                               A BSTRACT
012
013            Secure aggregation of user update vectors (e.g. gradients) has become a criti-
014            cal issue in the field of federated learning. Many Secure Aggregation Protocols
015            (SAPs) face exorbitant computation costs, severely constraining their applicabil-
016            ity. Given the observation that a considerable portion of SAP’s computation bur-
017            den stems from processing each entry in the private vectors, we propose Partial
018            Vector Freezing (PVF), a portable module for compressing computation costs
019
               without introducing additional communication overhead. λ-SecAgg, which inte-
               grates SAP with PVF, “freezes” a substantial portion of the private vector through
020
               specific transformations, requiring only λ1 of the original vector to participate in
021
               SAP. Eventually, users can “thaw” the public sum of the “frozen entries” by the
022            result of SAP. To avoid potential privacy leakage, we devise Disrupting Variables
023            Element for PVF. We demonstrate that PVF can seamlessly integrate with various
024            SAPs and it poses no threat to user privacy in the semi-honest and active adver-
025            sary settings. We include 7 baselines, encompassing 5 distinct types of masking
026            schemes, and explore the acceleration effects of PVF on these SAPs. Empirical
027            investigations indicate that when λ = 100, PVF yields up to 99.5× speedup and
028            up to 32.3× communication reduction.

031   1   I NTRODUCTION
032
033
      Machine learning technologies are applied in countless fields to improve service performance. How-
034
      ever, aggregating large amounts of data for big data mining raises concerns regarding data pri-
035
      vacy (Liu et al., 2021). Federated Learning (FL) (McMahan et al., 2017) keeps original data on the
036   local devices while only requiring data owners to submit local training updates to a central server.
037   Nonetheless, as Zhu et al. (2019) and Geiping et al. (2020) indicate, attackers can infer a user’s
038   local data by reversing the submitted updates. To address this issue, numerous research efforts have
039   been focusing on Secure Aggregation Protocols (SAPs) (Liu et al., 2022b) for aggregating all user’s
040   model information while preserving individual privacy.
041
      The widely discussed SAPs are based on Secure Multi-Party Computation (SMPC) (Xu et al., 2022;
042   Sotthiwat et al., 2021), Mask (Bonawitz et al., 2017), Homomorphic Encryption (HE) (Aono et al.,
043   2017) and Differential Privacy (DP) (Wei et al., 2020). For most SAPs, except for DP-based ones,
044   the computation overhead often scales proportionally with the length of the model update vectors
045   since most of these schemes involve masking (encrypting) each entry of the vector sequentially.
046   Therefore the computation time for both masking and unmasking always experiences a steep esca-
047   lation with the increase in vector length, as PracAgg (Bonawitz et al., 2017) in Figure 1, significantly
048   constraining real-world applications. Especially in recent applications that utilize FL to fine tune
049   Large Language Models (LLMs) (Ye et al., 2024) with billions of parameters, the computational
050
      overhead brought by SAP is unbearable.
051   On the other hand, DP-based solutions although have the best efficiencies, many studies (Stevens
052   et al., 2022; Wang et al., 2021) posit that the minimal noise added by DP is insufficient to thwart
053   attacks such as gradient inversion aimed at stealing users’ local data, where adversaries can recover
      flawed but recognizable handwritten digit image (Wang et al., 2021). Therefore the security of DP in

      In this work, we primarily explore methods to alleviate masking-related overhead.
071   Our proposal. We think the root cause of computation overhead in SAPs is masking each entry
072   of the original vector. While a few sparsification-related approaches (Ergun et al., 2021; Lu et al.,
073   2023) try to reduce the dimensions of uploaded vectors, they raise an inevitable trade-off of dis-
074   carding some information. In this work, we propose Partial Vector Freezing (PVF) to reduce the
075   number of entries processed in SAPs while ensuring intact aggregation of all entries in the original
076
      vector. Within the module, each user performs certain transformations on the original vector at a
      negligible computation cost to selectively freeze most entries of the user’s original vector, compress-
077
      ing the length of the vector involved in SAPs to λ1 of its original size (λ is the compression factor).
078
      The communication overhead and number of communication interactions in SAPs after integrating
079   PVF, which we call λ-SecAgg, do not increase. Further, we propose Disrupting Variables Ele-
080   ment to prevent PVF from leaking the linear relationship between vectors. PVF remains decoupled
081   from SAPs and guarantees individual user privacy under semi-honest and active adversary settings,
082   offering high portability.
083
      Our contributions can be summarized as follows:
084
085   • We propose the PVF without incurring additional communication overhead or harming security. It
086     reduces the entries processed in SAP while ensuring the aggregation of all entries in the origi-
087     nal vector, which means it can compress the computation overhead of SAP to approximately λ1 of
088     the original. Moreover, it brings up to 32.3 × (λ = 100) additional communication enhancements
089     for HE-based SAPs thanks to the decreased number of ciphertext entries.
090   • We propose the disrupting variables element to PVF to avoid potential privacy leakage.
091
      • Extensive experiments show the effectiveness of our proposal. We include 7 baselines encom-
092     passing 5 types of masking schemes for a comprehensive overhead comparison, which is largely
093     unexplored in most research endeavors and reaffirms the high portability of PVF.

099
      based (Boer & Kramer, 2020; Xu et al., 2022), HE-based (Aono et al., 2017; Ma et al., 2022; Li
      et al., 2022), DP-based (Geyer et al., 2017; Wei et al., 2020), and Mask-based (Bonawitz et al., 2017)
100
      schemes. Most efforts to reduce computation cost focus on enhancing PracAgg (Bonawitz et al.,
101
      2017), which is mainly categorized into two types: (i) improving the masking mechanism (Liu et al.,
102   2022a; Stevens et al., 2022; Liu et al., 2022c; Wei et al., 2023) to reduce masking-related overhead;
103   (ii) minimizing interaction-related overhead, including refining communication structures (Bell
104   et al., 2020; So et al., 2021) and enhancing efficiency in key agreements among users (Kalikinkar
105   et al., 2018; Kadhe et al., 2020; Ma et al., 2023). Note that the security of FL remains an open issue.
106   SAPs, though cannot fully guarantee FL security at the moment, remain a promising direction worth
107   exploration. The main objective of our work is to reduce the masking-related overhead of secure
      aggregation, thereby making it more applicable in practice.

      Compression-based Techniques. Rothchild et al. (2020) employs a Count Sketch to compress
109   model updates. Additionally, some sparsification-based approaches (Ergun et al., 2021; Lu et al.,

114
      Inconsistency Attacks (Pasquini et al., 2022), Multi-round Privacy Stealing Attacks (So et al., 2023)

123   inputs remains an unresolved challenge, and it falls beyond the scope of this work. What’s more,
124   Mozaffari et al. (2023) propose Federated Rank Learning (FRL), where the server aggregates the
125   parameter rankings instead of the model parameter updates. It can effectively resist poisoning at-
126   tacks, and enable direct aggregations without any constraints on user submissions. Therefore, FRL
127   can be combined with SAP, and we do not have to worry about whether PVF can be integrated with
128   input constraints in this work.

478   6   C ONCLUSION
479
480   We present a new perspective aimed at mitigating the formidable computation overhead of SAPs by
481   reducing the number of involved entries while ensuring intact secure aggregation of original vectors.
482   Based on this, we propose PVF, a concrete portable solution. After integrating with PVF, λ-SecAgg
483   involves only λ1 of original vectors. Moreover, we introduce the disrupting variables element to
484   improve security. Extensive experiments showcase the remarkable improvements in acceleration
485   and communication brought by PVF and its portability. Consequently, our method undoubtedly
      renders SAPs genuinely feasible, promising inspiration for future research endeavors.
