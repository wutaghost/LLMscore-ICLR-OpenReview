               Self-attention is the core mathematical operation of modern transformer archi-
               tectures and is also a significant computational bottleneck due to its quadratic
013
               complexity in the sequence length. In this work, we derive the scalar energy
014
               function whose gradient computes the self-attention block, thus elucidating the
015            theoretical underpinnings of self-attention. Our formulation reveals that the re-
016            duction across the sequence axis can be efficiently computed in parallel through
017            a tree reduction. Our algorithm, called Tree Attention, for parallelizing
018            exact attention computation across multiple GPUs enables cross-device decod-
019            ing to be performed asymptotically faster (up to 8× faster in our experiments)
020            than state-of-the-art approaches such as Ring Attention, while also requir-
021            ing significantly less communication volume and incurring 2× less peak mem-
022            ory. We demonstrate that Tree Attention speeds up decoding up to 4x
023
               on Llama 3.1-8B and can be applied to a variety of hardware and network-
               ing setups such as H100 DGX nodes, AMD MI300x nodes, and PCIe con-
024
               nected NVIDIA RTX 4090s. Our code is publicly available here: https:

028   1    I NTRODUCTION
029
030   The self-attention operation is the core computational building block of the transformer architecture
031   Bahdanau et al. (2014); Vaswani et al. (2017), which has become an ubiquitous and highly effective
032   workhorse architecture currently applied at scale to language Brown et al. (2020); Kaplan et al.
033   (2020); Hoffmann et al. (2022); Team et al. (2023); Achiam et al. (2023); Pilault et al. (2023), vision
034   Dosovitskiy et al. (2020), audio Betker (2023), and decision-making Chen et al. (2021); Reed et al.
035   (2022). Nonetheless, the quadratic time complexity of self-attention means that significant resources
036   are required to train and generate from transformer-based Large Language Models (LLMs), especially
037   for models with large context lengths.
038   During inference, the attention block largely determines the computational and memory requirements,
039   which become more demanding as the input sequence length increases. Although LLMs generate
040   one token at a time, the entire sequence of past tokens must still be stored in memory and used to
041   compute attention scores during generation. Since attention performs a similarity matching of every
042   token representation with every other, it incurs quadratic computational complexity in terms of flops.
043   There have been recent advances in training LLMs to handle extremely long contexts (up to 1M
044   tokens) Chen et al. (2023); kai (2023); Peng et al. (2023). Such models attain qualitatively new
045   capabilities such as extremely large-scale in-context learning of entire small datasets held in the
046   prompt Reid et al. (2024); Lee et al. (2024); Bertsch et al. (2024). They can also avoid putting
047   multi-modal continuous data through a lossy tokenization scheme Reid et al. (2024); Team (2024)
048   by directly operating at the byte level Xue et al. (2022); Wu et al. (2024). The issue however is that
049   performing inference on such long contexts is very expensive.
050   To speed up inference and alleviate memory requirements, recent works have attempted to alter the
051   attention mechanism itself, either by linearizing it Katharopoulos et al. (2020), or approximating it by
052   a kernel map Choromanski et al. (2020b); Peng et al. (2021); Arora et al. (2024), which reduces the
053   complexity to linear at the cost of reduced expressiveness. Others have invented alternative sequence
      mixing architectures such as state-space models which are designed to be efficiently computable in

092   The computational complexity of self-attention, introduced by Vaswani et al. (2017), poses challenges
093   for long sequences due to its quadratic dependency on sequence length, 𝑂 (𝑛2 · 𝑑). To address this,
094   attention approximation mechanisms like Linformer (Wang et al., 2020) and Performer (Choro-
095   manski et al., 2020a) reduce complexity to linear 𝑂 (𝑛) using low-rank projections and kernelized
096
      approximations on a single device. Sparse models such as Longformer (Beltagy et al., 2020) and
      BigBird (Zaheer et al., 2020) further optimize computations by restricting attention to local windows
097
      or sparsity patterns, significantly reducing resource demands while maintaining performance for
098
      specific tasks. Such methods however provide approximations to the attention mechanism while we
099   seek to parallelize an exact attention computation across the sequence axis.
100
101   Theoretical work has also contributed to improving the efficiency of both exact and approximate
102
      methods. Kernel-based approaches, such as those by Tsai et al. (2019), suggest alternative formu-
      lations to self-attention that are computationally efficient. Surveys like Tay et al. (2020) highlight
103
      these advancements, emphasizing the synergy between parallelization strategies and sparsity or
104
      approximation techniques, ensuring self-attention remains scalable even in constrained computational
105   environments. It must be noted as well that Duman Keles et al. (2022) established lower bounds

514   7     D ISCUSSION AND C ONCLUSION
515
      In this paper, we have derived the energy function for self-attention and demonstrated how the
516
      computation of the derivative of this function provides a novel and efficient method for computing
517   attention in parallel. This advantage is especially apparent when performing decoding across multiple
518   devices, in which case our Tree Attention enables us to substantially outperform SOTA Ring
519   Attention with an asymptotically superior algorithm, with ×8 speedups when we use 128 GPUs
520   on a sequence length of 5.12M. We also see that the AllReduce operation that we use involves
521   sending partially reduced objects, which greatly reduces the volume of communicated data as well as
522   the peak memory requirement. In a real-world application, using the Llama 3.1 model with 1B and
523   8B parameters, we find that decoding with a prefill stage using Tree Attention gets us ×3-5
524   speedupds compared to Ring Attention. Further, by testing our method on various types of
525
      GPUs clusters including AMD MI300xs, we show that Tree Attention generalizes very well to
      various communication protocols and network topologies.

      A     M ORE R ELATED W ORK
865
866   Recent work has attempted a Bayesian reformulation of attention by deriving a probabilistic generative
867   model which matches the operations performed in a self-attention operation Singh & Buckley (2023).
868   This follows on from a line of work which relates self-attention to the well-studied Hopfield Network
869   architecture Ramsauer et al. (2020). The idea being that while the Hopfield Network is defined
870   in terms of dynamics upon an energy landscape, this same picture can be cast into a Bayesian
871   interpretation by identifying the energy function with a variational free energy functional and hence
872   deriving the generative model that is implicit in the self-attention update.
873   In particular, consider the energy function proposed in Hoover et al. (2023), which is the logsumexp.
874   Since the gradient in the update rule of that paper is taken with respect to the input to the block, the
875   resulting function is a modified version of the self-attention operation. Similarly, the update rule in
876   Ramsauer et al. (2020) requires the tying of certain weights (K and V) in the attention operation. This
877   restricts the Hopfield derivation to modelling auto-associative retrieval networks, while transformer
878   attention is hetero-associative.
879   Another notable related work is Feng et al. (2024), where the authors made similar observations
880   as we do in section 5 about how the associative operations within the attention computation can
881   be efficiently parallelized to motivate an attention-based modified RNN architecture for sequence
882   modeling.
883   While this energy function by itself is primarily a mathematical and theoretical curiosity, we demon-
884   strate below that when combined with automatic differentiation, our formulation naturally leads to
885   highly efficient parallel algorithms for computing attention and performing decoding, especially
886   across multiple devices.
887
888   B     M ORE BACKGROUND ON THE TREE REDUCTION OPERATION
889
890   A tree reduction operation is a hierarchical strategy to perform a reduction operation (e.g., sum,
891   product, maximum, minimum) over a set of data elements efficiently, especially in parallel computing.
892   This approach reduces the overall computational complexity and enables efficient utilization of
893
      parallel processing resources. Here’s how it works:
894
             • Divide the problem into smaller tasks: The input data is divided into smaller chunks, and
895
               the reduction operation is performed pairwise between adjacent elements in these chunks.
896
897
             • Form a tree-like structure: The results from the first level of reductions are themselves
               reduced pairwise in the next level. This continues until the entire dataset has been reduced
898
               to a single result.
899
900          • Iterative or recursive aggregation: The aggregation typically follows a binary tree pattern,
901            but other fan-in numbers (e.g., k-ary trees) can also be used. Each node in the tree represents
902
               a partial reduction result, and the root of the tree holds the final result.
903
      Because a tree structure has a logarithmic depth to total number of nodes, a tree reduction can
904
      asymptotiacally reduce the number of total steps required to perform an operation when it is possible to
905   aggregate partial results, and additionally is amenable to parallelization since k-ary trees can be defined
906   to match the number of available processors for parallel processing. Additionally, many existing
907   networking topologies such as Nvidia’s NVLINK and Infiniband, due to the natural advantages of
908   tree structures, are designed with such a toplogy meaning that tree operations are natural and efficient
909   to perform.
