                                                  A BSTRACT
008
009
               Multi-modality image fusion (MMIF) integrates heterogeneous images from di-
010
               verse sensors. However, existing MMIF methods often overlook significant style
011            discrepancies, such as saturation and resolution differences between modalities,
012            resulting in overly smooth features in certain modalities. This tendency causes
013            models to misjudge and disregard potentially crucial content. To address this is-
014            sue, this paper proposes a novel style-coherent multi-modality fusion model that
015            adeptly merges heterogeneous styled features from various modalities. Specif-
016            ically, the proposed style-normalized fusion module progressively supplements
017            the complete content structure by merging style-normalized features during cross-
018            modal feature extraction. Meanwhile, a style-alignment fusion module is devel-
019
               oped to align different feature representations across modalities, ensuring con-
               sistency. Additionally, to better preserve information and emphasize critical pat-
020
               terns during fusion, an adaptive reconstruction loss is applied to multi-modal im-
021
               ages transformed into a unified image domain, enforcing mapping to a consistent
022            modality representation. Extensive experiments validate that our method outper-
023            forms existing approaches on multiple MMIF tasks and exhibits greater potential
024            to facilitate downstream applications.

027   1   I NTRODUCTION
028
029   Multi-modality image fusion (MMIF) aims to generate visually enhanced images by integrat-
030   ing complementary details from multi-modal images of the same scene captured by different de-
031   vices (Liu et al., 2020; He et al., 2023a; Yan et al., 2022; Zhou et al., 2023b). The versatility of
032   MMIF, arises from the diversity of imaging sensors, making it applicable to a wide range of tasks
033
      such as infrared and visible image fusion (IVF), medical image fusion (MIF), and biological im-
      age fusion (BIF). These fused images offer more discernible representations of objects and scenes,
034
      benefiting applications like visual enhancement (Liu et al., 2020; He et al., 2023a; Yan et al., 2022;
035
      Zhou et al., 2023b), image registration (Jiang et al., 2022; Wang et al., 2022; Xu et al., 2022), and
036   object semantic segmentation (Zhou et al., 2022; Liu et al., 2023b; 2024b; Zhang et al., 2024).
037
038   Multi-modal images have distinct visual characteristics like differences in saturation, resolution, and
039
      spectral properties due to variations in external styles. For instance, in IVF, visible images are sen-
      sitive to illumination changes, while infrared images are generally characterized by noise and lower
040
      resolution. To address the limitations of individual source images and produce a comprehensive
041
      composite, MMIF should effectively balance these external styles while preserving the unified in-
042   ternal content from all contributing sources. Recently, the rapid development of deep learning has
043   led to various learning-based MMIF methods (Jung et al., 2020; Xu et al., 2020b;a; Zhang et al.,
044   2020a;b) have been proposed and demonstrated promising results (Li & Wu, 2018; Li et al., 2021;
045   Zhao et al., 2020; Liang et al., 2022; Zhao et al., 2023b; Liu et al., 2023a). However, existing MMIF
046   methods tend to overlook the significant style discrepancies like saturation and resolution between
047   modalities and produce oversmooth features for a certain modality. Such a tendency causes models
048   to misjudge and mistakenly disregard potential salient contents, which affects fusion quality and
049   could compromise safety for night driving or medical uses. As shown in Fig. 1 (a), it leads to coarse
050
      intra-modal feature representations and significant disparities between cross-modal features, result-
      ing in existing models producing fused features and results with diminished details and textures,
051
      specifically in elements like the stone pillars along the street and the cyclist with a taillight.
052
053   To mitigate the above issues, we propose a Style-coherent Content Fusion model (SCFNet). This
      approach transforms heterogeneous features from multiple modalities into a shared style-coherent

087   On the other hand, the lack of ground truth (GT) makes it challenging to ensure the quality and
088   reliability of the fused images. Some methods guide MMIF models based on priors from down-
089   stream tasks (Liu et al., 2022; 2023b; Sun et al., 2022; Tang et al., 2022a; Zhao et al., 2023a; Wang
090   et al., 2022; Xu et al., 2020a; 2023b; Huang et al., 2022; Liu et al., 2024b; Zhang et al., 2024) or
091   image priors from the pre-trained models (Zhao et al., 2023c; Yi et al., 2024). These approaches
092   rely on explicit external guidance, which limits their generalizability. Therefore, this paper focuses
093   on developing a self-supervised loss function for MMIF, requiring only source images collected
094   in a general sensing setup. Some methods utilize crafted loss functions e.g., the pixel-based loss
095
      (Zhao et al., 2023b; 2021; Liang et al., 2022) and gradient-based loss (He et al., 2023b; Xu et al.,
      2023a), applied directly to source images to maintain visible and texture fidelity. Due to inherent
096
      differences in source images, such as intensity and contrast, directly blending them as a supervision
097
      signal leads to smoother model outputs that lose important details. Therefore, we propose an adap-
098   tive reconstruction loss function that utilizes a learnable rescaling transformation for multi-modal
099   images, allowing for comprehensive content supervision within a specific source domain. This lin-
100   ear transformation minimizes potential damage to the prior image and better unifies the diverse
101   representations of scene information integrity. In addition, it encourages the model to learn identity
102   mapping within this specific image domain, further promotes the retention of critical details.
103
      Overall, the main contributions of this paper are summarized as follows:
104
105     • We propose a style-coherent content fusion model (SCFNet) that generates fused images with
106   complete scene information and consistent external characteristics by unifying the external styles
107   of heterogeneous modalities. To the best of our knowledge, this is the first work of MMIF grounded
      in a style-based foundation.

112     • Our method attains state-of-the-art performance across various datasets. Furthermore, we sub-

      2    R ELATED W ORK
117
118
119   Multi-Modality Image Fusion. Existing deep-learning based MMIF methods are roughly divided
120   into two categories: deep unrolling networks (DUN) and deep neural networks (DNN). DUNs ad-
121   dress the ill-posedness of MMIF by unfolding iterative algorithms into neural network layers (Deng
122   & Dragotti, 2020; Gao et al., 2022; Xu et al., 2023a; Zhao et al., 2021; Ju et al., 2022; He et al.,
123   2023b). However, it is challenging to model the complex non-linear degradation process of MMIF.
124
      DNNs learn nonlinear mappings to fuse diverse modalities (Jung et al., 2020; Xu et al., 2020b;a;
      Zhang et al., 2020a;b; Liu et al., 2024a). Generative adversarial network-based methods (Goodfel-
125
      low et al., 2014; Liu et al., 2022; Ma et al., 2020; 2019; Zhang et al., 2021) constrain the distribution
126
      of the fused image to be similar to the input images for perceptually satisfactory. Encoder-decoder-
127   based methods (Li & Wu, 2018; Li et al., 2021; Zhao et al., 2020; Liang et al., 2022; Zhao et al.,
128   2023b; Liu et al., 2023a), employing CNN/Transformer blocks, learn feature fusion by translating
129   between image and latent spaces. These methods overlook cross-domain stylistic discrepancies,
130   directly proceeding with fusion and thereby compromising the integrity of the content.
131
      To address the absence of GT, some methods guide the fusion process through image registration
132
      (Jiang et al., 2022; Wang et al., 2022; Xu et al., 2020b; 2023a; Huang et al., 2022) or downstream
133   tasks such as object detection and semantic segmentation (Liu et al., 2022; 2023b; Sun et al., 2022;
134   Tang et al., 2022a; Zhao et al., 2023a; Xu et al., 2020a; 2023b; Liu et al., 2024b). Some methods
135   utilize perceptual priors (Goodfellow et al., 2014; Liu et al., 2022; Ma et al., 2020; 2019; Zhang et al.,
136   2021) or natural image statistics (Zhao et al., 2023c; Yi et al., 2024) which are introduced by pre-
137   trained models to obtain perceptually and visually satisfying images. Recently, DeRUN (He et al.,
138   2023b) introduces a gradient direction-based entropy loss that effectively captures and represents
139   textural details within images by focusing on the directional patterns of gradients. EMMA (Zhao
140   et al., 2024) introduce an equivariant image loss that utilizes an image equivariance prior to constrain
141
      the structural properties of fused images. These aforementioned self-supervised methods do not
      explicitly address inconsistent supervised signal distributions caused by inherent differences.
142
143   Disentanglement for Multi-Modal Tasks. For modalities with distinct physical carriers like audio,
144   text and images, existing methods Yao et al. (2024); Fei et al. (2021); Lee & Pavlovic (2021); Chen
145   et al. (2023); Ouyang et al. (2021) disentangle the representations of features both within and across
146   the different modalities. The goal is to obtain robust and independent feature representations that
147
      enhance the relevance of the outcomes for objectives. For MMIF, Some methods Zhao et al. (2023b;
      2020); Liang et al. (2022); Xu et al. (2021) decouple sources into common and unique features to
148
      enhance feature integration. CDD Zhao et al. (2023b) employs shared and specific multi-modal
149
      feature decomposition, followed by separate fusions of specific and shared features. DIDFuse Zhao
150   et al. (2020) uses four distinct encoders to extract scenario features and attribute latent representa-
151   tions, and then fuse each set. However, these MMIF methods overlook the unification of appearance
152   styles across modalities and do not explicitly ensure the integrity of the content during fusion.
153
      Style-Based Learning. Style-based learning methods consider domain differences as divergent
154
      stylistic attributes across domains (Huang & Belongie, 2017; Nam & Kim, 2018; Pan et al., 2018).
155
      These methods aim to extract features that exhibit invariance to stylistic variations, thereby enabling
156   effective style transfer across different scenes. Based on frequency analyses, the style and content
157   of an image are represented by the amplitude and phase in the frequency domain (Hansen & Hess,
158   2007; Oppenheim & Lim, 1981; Piotrowski & Campbell, 1982; Zhou et al., 2023a; Li et al., 2024).
159   Recently, it has been demonstrated that the properties of amplitude and phase extend to feature
160   representations (Lee et al., 2023). The style and content components (Lee et al., 2023) enable the
161   adjustment of style and content magnitudes by modulating the amplitude and phase of the pre-
      normalized features, facilitating the learning of generalized features. In contrast, this paper devotes

192   3.1      OVERVIEW
193
194   As illustrated in Fig. 2(a), our proposed SCFNet adopts a framework with a dual-branch encoder
195   and a decoder. Considering the challenges that existing CNN and transformer blocks (Zhao et al.,
196   2023b; 2020; Liang et al., 2022) encounter in extracting essential frequency features due to dis-
197   ruptions in local feature and global dependency coherence across modalities, we utilize the Fourier
198   Prior Embedding (FPE) block (Zhou et al., 2023a). This block can efficiently capture the frequency
199
      information of features, thereby facilitating frequency-based style-content decomposition within our
      core module. See Suppmentary for details of FPE. Overall, the encoder has three main components:
200
      Fourier prior embedding block, style-normalized fusion (SNF) module, and style-alignment fusion
201
      (SAF) module. To reduce the complexity and potential discrepancies that arise from separate config-
202   urations for each modality, the twin encoder branches share the same structure and parameters. The
203   decoder utilizes the standard architecture as Zhao et al. (2023b); Zamir et al. (2022). We take the
204   IVF task as an example. Given a paired visible image V and an infrared image I, the fusion process
205   to obtain the fused image F is described by F = f (V , I), where f (·) represents the proposed SCF.
206

      3.3   S TYLE -A LIGNMENT F USION M ODULE
271
272   SAF prioritizes the alignment of the source modality image domain that contains more detailed and
273   crucial information for recognition and understanding. In IVF, we aim to fuse multi-modal features
274   aligning with the visible domain. This alignment is achieved by projecting the features X̂VL and
275   X̂IL from the encoders at the last level L into a shared embedding space with a normalized style as
276   defined in Eq. 3. Within this space, the features are fused in a channel-weighted manner using W
277   to maintain spatial consistency. The alignment of the fused feature with the visible domain is then
278   performed by adjusting based on the fundamental properties of the distribution, ensuring that the
279   mean matches Mean(X̂VL ). Std(X̂VL ) aids W in adjusting the standard deviation.

296   Directly self-supervising with source images, which have significant differences, leads to ambiguous
297   solutions. Therefore, the proposed adaptive reconstruction loss function (ARL) employs the linearly
298   rescaled source images as the supervision signal to guide the model in maintaining both stylistic
299   coherence and content fidelity in the fused outputs. The learnable rescale function R(·) is designed
300   to adjust the distribution statistics of the source images, as follows:
301                                        R(X) = α(X)N(X) + β,                                           (9)
302                    +                                                 +
      where α(I) ∈ R is the learnable parameter to scale and β ∈ R is the parameter to shift.
303
304   In conjunction with SAF, input source images are regularized as supervision signals in the visible
305
      image domain. The parameter β is set to Mean(V ), anchoring the fused output to the distribution of
      the visible image domain. Meanwhile, the learnability of α provides significant flexibility, enabling
306
      dynamic adaptation to the distributional differences between the multi-modal inputs. It is obtained
307
      in a data-driven manner based on pooling and convolutional operations, as follows:
308
309                         α(X) = Conv1 Cat MaxPool(X), AvgPool(X) .                                  (10)
310
      To ensure appropriate information entropy and maintain balance, we make α(X) fall into
311
      [Max Std(V ), Std(I)), Std(X)] using the clip operator. The lower bound prevents the loss of essen-
312
      tial details by avoiding overly narrow distribution, while the upper bound prevents the introduction
313
      of unrealistic artifacts due to excessively broad variability. Overall, this entropy-aware clipping pro-
314   vides controlled supervision while preserving informational integrity. This entropy-aware clipping

      5     C ONCLUSION
532
533
      In this work, we propose SCFNet, a novel style-coherent multi-modality fusion model, to address
534   the challenge of integrating heterogeneous modalities in MMIF. To complement missing scene in-
535   formation from uncollected modalities, SNF enhances features by merging style-normalized rep-
536   resentations. SAF aligns cross-modal fused features to a designated modality, ensuring stylistic
537   consistency. Additionally, to improve the integrity and completeness of self-supervision, we employ
538   an adaptive reconstruction loss function that linearly transforms source inputs to enforce mapping
539   to a specific domain with image priors. Experiments demonstrate that our approach outperforms
      existing MMIF methods and shows strong potential for downstream applications.
