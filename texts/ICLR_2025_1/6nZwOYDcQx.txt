009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRA) has become a popular paradigm for fine-tuning
012            large models, but it still necessitates a substantial number of training parameters. To
013            address this issue, we first conduct comprehensive empirical studies on parameter-
014            efficient LoRA structure. Then, we establish design guidelines that emphasize the
015            use of serial structures, optimal placements, and nested LoRA. Based on these
016            insights, we present NoRA, a nested parameter-efficient LoRA structure that revolu-
017            tionizes the initialization and fine-tuning of projection matrices. Our NoRA’s inno-
018            vative approach involves freezing outer layer LoRA weights and employing a serial
019
               inner layer design, enabling precise task-specific adaptations while maintaining
               compact training parameters. In addition, we propose an activation-aware Singular
020
               Value Decomposition (AwSVD) that adjusts the weight matrices based on activation
021
               distributions for initialization of outer layer LoRA weights. This schema enhances
022            decomposition accuracy and mitigates computational errors. Extensive evaluations
023            across multiple linguistic and visual tasks demonstrate that NoRA outperforms
024            state-of-the-art LoRA variants, achieving significant improvements in efficiency
025            and effectiveness on models such as Mistral-7B, Gemma-7B, and LLaMA-3 8B.
026            Notably, NoRA reduces fine-tuning parameters|training-time|memory-usage by
027            85.5%|37.5%|8.9% and enhances performance by 1.9%, compared to LoRA on
028            LLaMA-3 8B. Codes are available in the supplementary materials.

031   1   I NTRODUCTION
032
033   Large Language Models (LLMs) have recently achieved remarkable performance in natural language
034   processing and related fields (Zhao et al., 2023; Touvron et al., 2023). However, the high parameter
035   size makes training and adaptation challenging, especially in resource-limited settings. To address
036   this, Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning (PEFT) techniques have been developed (Ding et al., 2023;
037   Han et al., 2024), focusing on fine-tuning a subset of model parameters. Low-Rank Adaptation
038   (LoRA) (Hu et al., 2021a) is a notable PEFT technique that uses low-rank matrices for efficient
039
      adaptation to specific tasks (He et al., 2021). It achieves significant computational and memory
      savings during fine-tuning, making it feasible to adapt LLMs on consumer-grade hardware (Mao
040
      et al., 2024).
041
042   Despite LoRA’s demonstrated utility, it faces challenges that limit its effectiveness in downstream
043   tasks. The original LoRA involves training a large number of parameters, which can lead to slow
044   convergence and potential overfitting problems. To address these issues, two main approaches have
045
      emerged in the literature: (1) Hyperparameter-based methods, which focus on adaptive rank allocation
      and optimization settings tuning. Examples include BiLoRA (Qiang et al., 2024), LoRA-dropout (Lin
046
      et al., 2024), and AdaLoRA (Zhang et al., 2023b), which employ bi-level optimization strategies,
047
      parameter dropout, and singular value-based allocation for different layer types. (2) Structural
048   modifications, which involve new components or frozen architectures. For instance, DoRA (Liu et al.,
049   2024b) and SARA (Gu et al., 2024) augment vector and mixture designs, respectively, although at the
050   expense of increased computational costs. VeRA (Liu et al., 2023a) incorporates trainable vectors on
051   random matrices, while other methods (Bałazy et al., 2024) approximate SVD decomposition and se-
052   lectively truncate singular values to balance performance and efficiency. Despite these advancements,
053   two significant challenges persist for these LoRA variants: (1) The intrinsic properties of LLMs
      are often neglected, particularly their sensitivity to activation outliers, which can potentially lead to

      Based on the above guidelines, we propose NoRA, a nested parameter-efficient LoRA design structure.
109   It features a nested LoRA structure in which the outer LoRA is initialized using AwSVD, while
110   the serial inner LoRA layers are initialized with a Gaussian distribution. NoRA aims to enhance
111   the efficiency and effectiveness of LoRA by optimizing the initialization of projection matrices and
112   fine-tuning strategies. NoRA keeps the outer LoRA fixed while innovatively reducing the number of
113   parameters and maximizing adaptation performance. First, NoRA introduces a new initialization
114   method for the LoRA projection matrices. We propose AwSVD to decompose the original matrices,
115   effectively reducing output errors while maintaining high fidelity to the pre-trained weights. This
116   initialization strategy provides a more informed starting point for the fine-tuning process, helping to
117
      accelerate convergence and improve task-specific performance (see Figure 2 (a) and Figure 1 (c), (d)).
      Second, NoRA effectively reduces training parameters by freezing the outer LoRA weights while
118
      employing a serial inner LoRA design, enabling the model to adapt more precisely to specific tasks
119
      while maintaining a compact parameter space.
120
121   We conduct experiments on multiple downstream tasks, including instruction tasks on the
122   GSM8K (Cobbe et al., 2021) and Math (Hendrycks et al., 2021) datasets using the Mistral-7B (Jiang
123
      et al., 2023), Gemma-7B (Team et al., 2024), and LLaMA-3 8B models. Additionally, we fine-tune
      the LLaMA (Touvron et al., 2023) model for commonsense reasoning, perform few-shot tuning
124
      on the CLIP (Radford et al., 2021) model, and conduct subject-driven generation on the Stable
125
      Diffusion XL (Podell et al., 2023) model. In these experiments, NoRA not only significantly reduces
126   the required parameters to as low as 4.1 million for the LLaMA-3 8B model but also enhances
127   performance, achieving an average score of 84.4%, which surpasses LoRA’s 82.8%. Furthermore, in
128   visual few-shot tasks using ViT-B/16, NoRA achieves the highest average accuracies of 80.9% (4
129   shots) and 86.1% (16 shots), demonstrating its superior efficiency and effectiveness over existing
130   methods. We summarize our contributions as follows:
131
132       • To overcome limitations of existing methods, we construct a unified design space while main-
133         taining a compact parameter set. Through comprehensive empirical research, we develop a set of
134         design guidelines that emphasize the importance of design positions (WA |wa ), serial structures,
135
            and the use of nested LoRA.
136       • We propose an AwSVD technique that adjusts weight matrices based on activation distributions,
137         effectively managing activation outliers and accelerating model convergence.
138       • We introduce NoRA, the first nested LoRA structure that optimizes the initialization and fine-
139         tuning of projection matrices. NoRA offers key advantages: significant parameter reduction,
140         enhanced training efficiency, and improved performance across diverse tasks.
141       • Through extensive evaluations across various linguistic and visual tasks, we demonstrate NoRA’s
142         superior performance, highlighting improvements in efficiency and effectiveness compared to
143         state-of-the-art LoRA variants.

      Parameter-efficient fine-tuning (PEFT) (Pfeiffer et al., 2020; Zaken et al., 2021) emerges as an
148
      effective solution for adapting large pre-trained models to downstream tasks, successfully addressing
149
      the challenges of high computational demands and training costs associated with traditional fine-
150   tuning methods (Hu et al., 2023). PEFT optimizes parameter adjustment by reducing additional
151   parameters and computational resources for specific tasks while maintaining the structure and
152   performance of the pre-trained model. The field evolves from early selective update strategies
153   (Gururangan et al., 2020) to more advanced techniques such as adapter modules and delta-weight
154   methods. These innovative approaches include adapters (Houlsby et al., 2019), which introduce
155   task-specific parameters within transformer layers. Additionally, prompt tuning (Liu et al., 2023b) and
156   prefix tuning (Li & Liang, 2021) adapt to tasks by appending task-specific vectors to inputs or various
157   layer representations. BitFit and IA3 (Zaken et al., 2021; Liu et al., 2022) focus on altering only
158
      the bias or scaling vectors within the base large language model. Overall, these methods, including
      LoRA (Hu et al., 2021a) and OFT (Liu et al., 2023c), aim to further enhance model adaptability
159
      through streamlined updates and auxiliary modules.
160
161   Low-rank Adaptation (LoRA) proves efficient in various task scenarios, using low-rank decompo-
      sition to enhance adaptation while minimizing computational overhead. However, its fixed rank

200   3.1   R EVIEW OF L OW-R ANK A DAPTATION
201
202
      LoRA is a parameter-efficient method for fine-tuning large-scale pre-trained models. It achieves
      fine-tuning of the original weights W by introducing low-rank matrix updates, aiming to preserve
203
      the stability and overall performance of the pre-trained models. The traditional LoRA forward pass
204
      for an input x ∈ Rn is:
205
206
207                                   h = Wx + ∆Wx = Wx + BAx,                                          (1)
208
209   where ∆W ∈ Rm×n is the low-rank weight update, and A ∈ Rr×n and B ∈ Rm×r are low-rank
210   matrices with r ≪ min(m, n). During training, we keep W frozen, while A and B serve as the
211   trainable parameters.
212

214

      (AwSVD) and employs a nested Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRA) architecture.

220   The specific details are as follows:
221
222      • Outer LoRA Layer: The LoRA weights for this layer are initialized using the activation-aware
223        SVD of the pre-trained weights W, with the decomposition error mitigated by a scaling matrix
224        S. Specifically, matrix B is initialized with UΣ, while matrix A is initialized with VT S−1 .
225        The parameters of this outer LoRA layer are frozen during training to maintain stability and
226        preserve the essential features of the pre-trained model, while still permitting precise adjustments
227        through the inner LoRA layer.
228
         • Inner LoRA Layer: This layer is initialized with a Gaussian distribution N (0, σ 2 ). Such
229        initialization enables the inner LoRA layer to focus on subtle perturbations within the weight
230        space, facilitating finer adjustments without altering the core weights preserved by the outer
231        LoRA layer. This approach ensures that updates are concentrated on refining and enhancing the
232        model’s ability to adapt to new tasks, leveraging minor adjustments that have a targeted impact
233        on performance.

271
272   For better understanding, we provide comparisons between our NoRA and alternative approaches
273   such as adding adapters or parallel LoRA structures:
274

      The NoRA form provides a more expressive and flexible weight update compared to adding adapter

300

301
      LoRA form (B + CA′ )A lack this hierarchical structure, limiting their ability to capture multi-scale

306

          • By setting B′ = I and rearranging terms, NoRA can approximate the parallel LoRA form.

310   The generalization capability of NoRA enables flexible adaptation to diverse scenarios, potentially
311   harnessing the strengths of both approaches. The NoRA architecture integrates the expressiveness
312   of both the additive adapter and parallel LoRA configurations while providing additional flexibility
313   and facilitating multi-scale adaptations. As previously analyzed, the nested structure of NoRA
314   is inherently flexible, allowing it to manage complex multi-scale adaptations within a controlled
315   parameter space. Moreover, NoRA’s generalization capability permits structural simplification when
316   necessary, enabling adaptation to various fine-tuning scenarios and enhancing its versatility.

      4.5   A BLATION S TUDY
487
488   Initialization Strategies. We compare various initialization methods, including random, SVD,
489   and AwSVD, for the outer LoRA matrices WA and WB in Table 4. The ablation results indicate
490   that AwSVD achieves the highest average performance on the GSM-8K and MATH datasets, with
491   scores of 69.4 and 19.1, respectively. AwSVD effectively reduces SVD approximation errors while
492   preserving the knowledge of the pre-trained model. For the initialization of inner NoRA matrices,
493   we evaluate the performance of three methods: Gaussian distribution, diagonal singular matrix, and
494   uniform initialization. As shown in Table 5, the Gaussian distribution yields superior performance,
495   surpassing the other two methods.
496   Structure Design Analysis. Table 6 demonstrates that the serial LoRA method exhibits higher task
497   accuracy compared to both parallel LoRA and adapter methods. Furthermore, Table 7 shows that
498   applying the serial LoRA method exclusively at the wa position results in improved performance.
499   Based on these findings, we derive NoRA design guidelines that emphasize the use of serial structures,
500   design layouts, and nested LoRA.
501   Training Time and Memory Usage. In evaluating LoRA, DoRA, and NoRA on a commonsense
502   reasoning task with controlled rank, NoRA displays superior efficiency in training time across different
503   batch sizes. As shown in Figure 5 (a) and (b)„ at a batch size of 4, NoRA is approximately 11 hours
504   faster per batch than DoRA and 12 hours faster than LoRA. Additionally, NoRA demonstrates reduced
505   GPU memory usage, particularly at larger batch sizes, indicating enhanced memory management and
506   efficiency.
507   Training Convergence Analysis. Figure 5 illustrates NoRA’s superior performance in terms of
508   training loss compared to DoRA. NoRA rapidly converges to a lower loss value, with the curve
509   steeply declining within the first 200 steps and maintaining a lower plateau throughout training,
510   suggesting faster convergence and potentially more stable and effective training outcomes.

527   5     C ONCLUSION
528
529   In this study, we introduce NoRA, an innovative framework for parameter-efficient fine-tuning that
530   enhances the efficiency and effectiveness of LoRA-based methods. By establishing a unified design
531   space, our comprehensive empirical analysis yields critical insights into initialization strategies,
532   structural configurations, and design placements. Furthermore, we present the activation-aware
533   SVD, which significantly reduces output errors and accelerates the training process. Comparative
534   experiments across 15 datasets and 5 models demonstrate that NoRA not only preserves the parameter
535   efficiency advantages of LoRA but also markedly improves overall performance. Future research may
536
      explore the integration of NoRA with AutoML and distillation techniques, applying it to multimodal
      models, and examining its effects on model interpretability and robustness.
537
538   Limitations. While NoRA shows strong performance across various tasks, its optimal hyperparameter
539   configurations may vary depending on the specific task and models. This limitation is common and
      widespread in other LoRA variants and parameter-efficient fine-tuning methods.

768   A     M ORE D ISCUSSIONS
769
770   A.1    E THICS S TATEMENT
771
772   This research focuses exclusively on developing efficient techniques for Large Language Models
773
      (LLMs), utilizing publicly available datasets and models. The study does not directly address human
      ethics or privacy concerns. Instead, it aims to enhance the computational efficiency and adaptability
774
      of existing LLMs, which may indirectly contribute to their broader accessibility and application.
775
776
      A.2    R EPRODUCIBILITY
777
778   The authors affirm the solid reproducibility of their results and provide specific code implementations
779   in the appendix. The main experiments represent average outcomes from multiple repetitions,
780   ensuring reliability and consistency. By presenting detailed results for different initial seeds, the
781   researchers demonstrate the robustness and repeatability of their method across various conditions,
782   further solidifying the reproducibility of their findings.
783
784   A.3    S UMMARY OF I NNOVATIONS
785
786   (1) The study introduces NoRA, a novel nested parameter-efficient Low-Rank Adaptation (LoRA)
787
      design structure that optimizes the initialization and fine-tuning strategies of projection matrices. (2)
      The researchers propose an activation-aware Singular Value Decomposition (AwSVD) technique
788
      that adjusts weight matrices based on activation distributions, effectively managing outliers and
789
      accelerating model convergence. (3) The work constructs a unified design space for LoRA variants
790   and develops comprehensive design guidelines, emphasizing the importance of specific design
791   positions, serial structures, and the use of nested LoRA for enhanced performance and efficiency.
792
793   A.4    P ERFORMANCE G AINS
794
795   As the first nested LoRA method utilizing activation-aware SVD, NoRA demonstrates significant
796   advantages in both performance and efficiency. (1) The performance gains compared to other LoRA
797   variants are substantial, with NoRA achieving an average score of 84.4% on the LLaMA-3 8B model,
798   surpassing LoRA’s 82.8%. (2) In visual few-shot tasks, NoRA achieves the highest average accuracies
799
      of 80.9% (4 shots) and 86.1% (16 shots), outperforming existing methods. (3) The improvements
      in inference speed and memory optimization are notable strengths of NoRA, reducing the required
800
      parameters to as low as 4.1 million for the LLaMA-3 8B model while enhancing performance.
801
802
      A.5    C OMPARISON TO OTHER M ETHODS
803
804   (1) While other LoRA variants like AdaLoRA, LoRA-FA, VeRA, and LoRA-XS have made advance-
805   ments in low-rank adaptation, NoRA distinguishes itself by addressing key limitations in existing
806   approaches. The unified design space and nested structure of NoRA offer unique advantages in
807   balancing parameter efficiency and task-specific adaptation. Unlike methods that focus solely on rank
808   adjustment or activation memory reduction, NoRA’s comprehensive approach to optimization, includ-
809   ing its AwSVD technique and nested structure, provides a more holistic solution to the challenges of
      fine-tuning large language models.
