011                                               A BSTRACT
012
013            Document Layout Analysis is crucial for real-world document understanding sys-
014            tems, but it encounters a challenging trade-off between speed and accuracy: mul-
015
               timodal methods leveraging both text and visual features achieve higher accuracy
               but suffer from significant latency, whereas unimodal methods relying solely on
016
               visual features offer faster processing speeds at the expense of accuracy. To ad-
017
               dress this dilemma, we introduce DocLayout-YOLO, a novel approach that en-
018            hances accuracy while maintaining speed advantages through document-specific
019            optimizations in both pre-training and model design. For robust document pre-
020            training, we introduce the Mesh-candidate BestFit algorithm, which frames doc-
021            ument synthesis as a two-dimensional bin packing problem, generating the large-
022            scale, diverse DocSynth-300K dataset. Pre-training on the resulting DocSynth-
023            300K dataset significantly improves fine-tuning performance across various doc-
024            ument types. In terms of model optimization, we propose a Global-to-Local Con-
025            trollable Receptive Module that is capable of better handling multi-scale varia-
026
               tions of document elements. Furthermore, to validate performance across differ-
               ent document types, we introduce a complex and challenging benchmark named
027
               DocStructBench. Extensive experiments on downstream datasets demonstrate that
028
               DocLayout-YOLO excels in both speed and accuracy. Code, data, and models will
029            be made publicly available.

032   1   I NTRODUCTION
033
034   With the rapid advancement of large language models and retrieval-augmented generation (RAG)
035   research Lewis et al. (2020); Ram et al. (2023); Edge et al. (2024), the demand for high-quality
036   document content parsing Wang et al. (2024b) has become increasingly critical. A central step in
037   document parsing is Document Layout Analysis (DLA), which aims to precisely locate different
038   types of regions (text, titles, tables, graphics, etc.) within a document. Over the past few years, DLA
039
      algorithms have made significant progress, performing well on common document types. However,
      when faced with diverse document formats, existing layout analysis algorithms Huang et al. (2022);
040
      Li et al. (2022) still struggle with speed and accuracy.
041
042   Currently, there are two main approaches to document parsing: multimodal methods that combine
043   visual and textual information, and unimodal methods that rely solely on visual features. Multi-
044   modal methods, which typically involve pretraining on document images using unified text-image
045
      encoders, generally achieve higher accuracy but are often slower due to the complexity of their
      architectures. In contrast, unimodal methods, which rely only on visual features, offer faster pro-
046
      cessing speeds but tend to lack accuracy due to the absence of specialized pretraining and model
047
      design for document data. To achieve robust performance on diverse real-world documents while
048   meeting the demands of real-time applications, this paper introduces the DocLayout-YOLO layout
049   detection algorithm. This method leverages the strengths of both multimodal and unimodal ap-
050   proaches to quickly and accurately identify various regions within documents. As illustrated in Fig-
051   ure 1, DocLayout-YOLO matches the speed of the mainstream unimodal method YOLOv10 (Wang
052   et al., 2024a) and surpasses all existing methods, including the unimodal DINO-4scale (Zhang et al.,
053   2023a) and YOLO-v10, as well as the multimodal LayoutLMv3 (Huang et al., 2022) and DiT-
      Cascade (Li et al., 2022), in terms of accuracy on diverse evaluation datasets. Specifically, we

081        We observe that multimodal layout analysis methods such as LayoutLMv3 and DiT-Cascade sig-

           However, for unimodal layout analysis methods, existing datasets predominantly comprise single
           document types such as PubLayNet (Zhong et al., 2019) and DocBank (Li et al., 2020b). Models
084
           fine-tuned on such datasets tend to overfit to a single distribution, failing to generalize to the diverse
085
           layouts encountered in real-world scenarios. To address this, we propose an automated pipeline
086        for constructing diverse document layout data, introducing the Mesh-candidate BestFit algorithm.
087        This algorithm synthesizes document layouts by leveraging principles from the two-dimensional bin
088        packing problem, using a rich set of base components (text, images, tables) to generate a large-scale,
089        diverse pretraining corpus, DocSynth-300K.
090
           YOLO (Jocher et al., 2023; Wang et al., 2024a), a leading algorithm in object detection, excels
091
           in both accuracy and speed on natural images. To further enhance YOLO’s performance on doc-
092        ument images, we adapt the network to the specific characteristics of document data. In diverse
093        documents, the scale of different elements can vary significantly, from small single-line titles to
094        full-page paragraphs, images, and tables. To better handle these multi-scale variations, we introduce
095        the Global-to-Local Controllable Receptive Module (GL-CRM), enabling the model to effectively
096        detect targets of varying scales. The contributions of this paper can be summarized as follows:
097
098        • This paper proposes DocLayout-YOLO, a novel model for diverse layout analysis tasks, which
099
             leverages the large-scale and diverse document layout dataset DocSynth-300K, and incorporates
             the GL-CRM to enhance detection performance.
100

      2.1 D OCUMENT L AYOUT A NALYSIS A PPROACHES
110
      Document Layout Analysis (DLA) focuses on identifying and locating different components within
111   documents, like text and images. DLA approaches are divided into unimodal and multimodal meth-
112   ods. Unimodal methods treat DLA as a special object detection problem, using generic off-the-shelf
113   detectors (Ren et al., 2015; Zhong et al., 2019; Carion et al., 2020; Jocher et al., 2023; Zhang et al.,
114   2023a). Multimodal methods improve DLA by aligning text-visual features through pre-training.
115   For example, LayoutLM (Xu et al., 2020; 2021; Huang et al., 2022) offers a unified approach with
116   various pre-training goals, achieving impressive results on various document tasks. DiT (Li et al.,
117   2022) boosts performance via self-supervised pre-training on extensive document datasets. VGT
118   (Da et al., 2023) introduces grid-based textual encoding for extracting text features.
119   2.2 D OCUMENT L AYOUT A NALYSIS DATASETS
120   Current document layout analysis datasets, such as the IIT-CDIP (Lewis et al., 2006) with 42 million
121   low-resolution, unannotated images, and its subset RVL-CDIP (Harley et al., 2015), which catego-
122   rizes 400,000 images into 16 classes, suffer from limitations in annotation detail. PubLayNet (Zhong
123
      et al., 2019) includes 360,000 pages from PubMed journals, significantly scaling up the dataset size
      for document layout analysis. DocBank (Li et al., 2020b) annotates 500,000 arXiv pages using weak
124
      supervision, while DocLayNet (Pfitzmann et al., 2022) focuses on 80,863 pages from magazine-type
125
      documents. D4 LA (Da et al., 2023) manually annotates 11,092 images from RVL-CDIP across 27
126   categories, and M6 Doc (Cheng et al., 2023) offers a diverse collection of 9,080 images annotated
127   with 74 types but is not open source due to copyright restrictions. Additional datasets such as
128   DEES200 (Yang et al., 2017), CHN (Li et al., 2020a), Prima-LAD (Antonacopoulos et al., 2009),
129   and ADOPD (Gu et al., 2024) are either not open-sourced or primarily suitable for fine-tuning. As
130   for document generation methods (Zhang et al., 2023b; Inoue et al., 2023; Hui et al., 2023; Jiang
131   et al., 2023; Kong et al., 2022; Gupta et al., 2021), most approaches focuses on academic papers.
132   Overall, current document layout analysis datasets have significant limitations in diversity, volume,
133   and annotation granularity, leading to sub-optimal pre-training models.

151   3      D IVERSE D OC S YNTH -300K DATASET C ONSTRUCTION
152
153   Existing unimodal pre-training datasets are characterized by significant homogeneity, primarily
154   comprising academic papers. This limitation substantially hinders the generalization capabilities
155   of pre-trained models. To enhance adaptability to diverse downstream document types, it is impera-
156   tive to develop a more varied pre-training document dataset.
157   The diversity of pre-training data can be primarily manifested in two dimensions: (1) Element diver-
158   sity: This includes a variety of document elements, such as text in different font sizes, tables in var-
159   ious forms, and more. (2) Layout diversity: This encompasses various document layouts, including
160   single-column, double-column, multi-column, and formats specific to academic papers, magazines,
161   and newspapers. In this paper, we propose a novel methodology termed Mesh-candidate BestFit,
      which automatically synthesizes diverse and well-organized documents by leveraging both element

167   3.1     P REPROCESSING : E NSURING E LEMENT D IVERSITY
168
169
      In the preprocessing phase, to ensure the inclusion of a diverse range of document elements, we
      utilize M6 Doc test (Cheng et al., 2023), which consists of 74 different document elements coming
170
      from about 2800 diverse document pages, as our initial data. Consequently, we fragment the pages,
171   extracting and constructing an element pool by each fine-grain category. Meanwhile, to maintain
172   diversity within elements of the same category, we design an augmentation pipeline that enlarges
173   the data pool of rare categories that have quantities less than 100 elements (Appendix A.2.2).
174
175
176
      3.2     L AYOUT G ENERATION : E NSURING L AYOUT D IVERSITY
177
      In addressing the challenge of synthesizing diverse layouts, the most straightforward approach is
178
      random arrangement. However, random arrangement yields disorganized and confusing layouts,
179
      which severely hampers the improvement on real-world documents. Regarding the layout generation
180   models based on Diffusion (Chen et al., 2024; Inoue et al., 2023) or GAN (Jiang et al., 2023; Gupta
181   et al., 2021), existing methods are limited to producing homogeneous layouts such as academic
182   papers, which is insufficient to cover various real-world document layouts.
183
      To ensure layout diversity and consistency with real-world documents, inspired by the 2D bin-
184
      packing problem, we regard available grids built by the current layout as “bins” of different sizes
185
      and iteratively perform the best matching to generate more diverse and reasonable document layouts,
186   balancing both the layout diversity (randomness) and aesthetics (such as fill rate and alignment). De-
187   tailed steps of layout generation are demonstrated as follows:
188
189   1. Candidate Sampling For each blank page, a subset is obtained through stratified sampling from
190      the element pool based on element size, serving as candidate set. Then, randomly sample an
191
         element from the candidate set and place it at a certain position on the page.
      2. Meshgrid Construction Construct the meshgrid based on the layout and filter out the invalid
192
         grids that overlaps with inserted elements. Only the remaining grids will be able to participate in
193
         matching with the candidate in subsequent steps.
194   3. BestFit Pair Search For each candidate, traverse all grids that meet the size requirement and
195      search for the Mesh-candidate pair with the maximum fill rate. Subsequently, remove the optimal
196      candidate from the candidate set and update the layout.
197   4. Iterative Layout Filling Repeat step 2 ∼ 3 until no valid Mesh-candidate satisfy the size require-
198      ment. Ultimately, random central scaling will be applied to all filled elements separately.

      2006) across different 12 document types. The training set consists of 8,868 images and the testing
297   set consists of 2,224 images. As for DocLayNet, DocLayNet contains 80,863 pages from 7 docu-

300
      Meanwhile, to quantitatively evaluate model performance across different document types, we cu-
301
      rate an in-house dataset termed DocStructBench, which is a comprehensive dataset designed for
302
      evaluation across various real-world scenario documents. It consists of four subsets categorized by
303   the source of the documents: Academic, Textbooks, Market Analysis, and Financial (examples of
304   these documents are illustrated in Figure 6). The data sources of DocStructBench are notably di-
305   verse, encompassing a broad range of domains from various institutions, publishers, and websites.
306   DocStructBench consists of 7,310 training images and 2,645 testing images. Each image has been
307   manually annotated across 10 distinct categories: Title, Plain Text, Abandoned Text, Figure, Figure
308   Caption, Table, Table Caption, Table Footnote, Isolated Formula, and Formula Caption. For exper-
309   iments on DocStructBench, we perform training on a mixture of all four subsets and report results

626   A.2.1         A LGORITHM OF L AYOUT G ENERATION
627
      The algorithm of layout generation is detailed as Algorithm 1, which iteratively searches for the
628
      best matches between the candidate and all grids (bins). After the best matching pair is found, the
629   candidate is inserted into the document and continues to iteratively search for the optimal match
630   until the number of elements reaches a threshold N (empirically set to 15). The matching threshold
631   f rthr is set to 10−4 .
632
633   A.2.2         DATA AUGMENTATION P IPELINE
634
635   In the preprocessing phase, we conduct a specifically designed augmentation pipeline for rare cate-
636   gories that have few elements in the element pool. The details are as follows:
637
638   1. Random Flip Considering the various possibilities of text orientation in different documents,
639
         we enhance the original data with random flips in both the horizontal and vertical directions at a
         probability of 0.5.
640
      2. Random Brightness & Contrast We simulate the real-world environments under a wide variety
641
         of lighting conditions and brightness levels by randomly altering the brightness and contrast of
642      elements at a probability of 0.5.
643   3. Random Cropping To guide the model to concentrate more on local features, we employ a
644      probability of 0.7 to perform random cropping on the elements within the area range of 0.5 ∼
645      0.9.
646   4. Edge Extraction We use the Sobel filter to perform edge detection and extract the contour
647      information within the elements with a probability of 0.2, thereby enhancing the richness of the
         features.
