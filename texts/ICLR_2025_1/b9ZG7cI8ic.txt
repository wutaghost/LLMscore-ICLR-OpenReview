011                                                A BSTRACT
012
013            In this work, we study the optimization and the generalization performance of the
014            widely used FedAvg algorithm for solving Federated Learning (FL) problems. We
015
               analyze the generalization performance of FedAvg by handling the optimization
               error and the Rademacher complexity. Towards handling optimization error, we
016
               propose novel constrained Polyak-Łojasiewicz (PL)-type conditions on the objec-
017
               tive function that ensure existence of a global optimal to which FedAvg converges
018            linearly after O(log(1/ϵ)) rounds of communication, where ϵ is the desired opti-
019            mality gap. Importantly, we demonstrate that a class of single hidden layer neural
020            networks satisfies the proposed constrained PL-type conditions required to estab-
021            lish the linear convergence of FedAvg as long as m > nK/d, where m is the
022            width of the neural network, K is the number of clients, n is the number of sam-
023            ples at each client, and d is the feature dimension. We then bound the Rademacher
024            complexity for this class of neural networks and establish that both Rademacher
025            complexity
                     √      and the generalization error of FedAvg decrease at an optimal rate of
               O(1/ n). We further show that increasing the number√      of clients K decreases the
026                                                       √
027            generalization error at the rate of O(1/ n + 1/ nK).

      1   I NTRODUCTION
031
      Federated learning (FL) is a distributed learning paradigm where multiple client devices collabo-
032
      rate with the help of a server to solve a joint problem while keeping the data of each client private
033                                                                             PK
      (Kairouz et al., 2021). A typical FL problem aims to solve minw k=1 Φk (w), where Φk (w) is
034
      the loss at the k th client and w refers to the joint model the clients aim to learn. A standard and most
035
      widely adopted algorithm to solve the FL problem is the Federated Averaging (FedAvg) algorithm
036
      first proposed in (McMahan et al., 2017). Consequently, the study of the convergence performance
037   of FedAvg has received wide attention (Konečnỳ et al., 2015; Stich, 2018; McMahan et al., 2017; Li
038   et al., 2020; Zhou & Cong, 2017b). However, when it comes to ensuring generalization guarantees
039   for FedAvg, the problem has not received significant attention, partially because of the challenging
040   nature of the problem (Mohri et al., 2019; Sun et al., 2023; Hu et al., 2022). To prove the gener-
041   alization guarantees for FedAvg, we need to bound (a) the optimization error (on empirical loss)
042   achieved by FedAvg, and (b) the complexity measure such as the Rademacher complexity of the
043   model (Arora et al., 2019; Mohri et al., 2019; 2018). The major challenge in guaranteeing good
044   generalization performance is to bound both (a) and (b) above, which are often contradictory, i.e.,
045
      proving optimization guarantees usually rely on restrictive assumptions on the loss landscape like
      (strong)-convexity or Polyak-Łojasiewicz (PL) inequality to be satisfied over the entire parameter
046
      space Haddadpour et al. (2019); Haddadpour & Mahdavi (2019) while the Rademacher complex-
047
      ity is large for an unbounded parameter space [see Theorem 5.10 (Mohri et al., 2018)]. Therefore,
048   bounding both (a) and (b) simultaneously is challenging, thereby making it difficult to provide sat-
049   isfactory generalization guarantees for FedAvg. To address these challenges in this work:
050   ➢ We first analyze the convergence of FedAvg and establish linear convergence under a new set of
051   assumptions that are only required to be satisfied locally. Importantly, to highlight the practicality
052   of the assumptions, we establish that the proposed assumptions are naturally satisfied by a single
053   hidden-layer Neural Network (NN).
      ➢ We then study the generalization guarantees of FedAvg for the single hidden-layer NN and show

      In the following, we discuss specific challenges and the drawbacks of the current state-of-the-art
      with respect to challenges (a) and (b) discussed above.
059
060   Convergence of FedAvg. As discussed earlier, several works have analyzed the convergence perfor-
061   mance of FedAvg under various settings. In the non-convex regime, multiple works have established
062   the convergence of FedAvg to a stationary point (local optimal) (Konečnỳ et al., 2015; Stich, 2018;
063   McMahan et al., 2017; Li et al., 2020; Zhou & Cong, 2017b). However, the local optimal does not
064
      guarantee a small empirical loss, and hence cannot be used to provide generalization guarantees.
      Some works have shown convergence of FedAvg to global optimal but under restrictive assumptions
065
      of (strong) convexity (Stich, 2018; Qu et al., 2020). In Haddadpour et al. (2019), the authors provide
066
      convergence of FedAvg to the global optimal by imposing the PL condition on the objective func-
067   tion, which is unfortunately not satisfied by several loss functions (e.g., log-logistic loss) over the
068   whole parameter space. Importantly, assuming that the PL inequality is satisfied globally (without
069   any restriction on the parameter space Haddadpour & Mahdavi (2019)) leads to a large Rademacher
070   complexity, thus leading to worse generalization guarantees. This leads to the following question:
071
          Q1: Can we develop conditions that are satisfied locally (on a restricted parameter space)
072
         rather than globally and provide convergence guarantees for FedAvg? Are there models that
073                                        satisfy such a condition?
074
075
076
      To address Q1, we provide new weaker conditions (a constrained variant of the PL-inequality) on
      the global and local loss functions. Importantly, we prove that there exists a globally optimal point
077
      within a ball of radius ρ around initialization to which FedAvg converges linearly. Moreover, we
078
      also establish that there exist NN architectures that satisfy the conditions proposed in our work.
079
080   Generalization guarantees for FedAvg: The generalization performance of centralized machine
081   learning algorithms has been extensively studied (Mohri et al., 2018; Bousquet & Elisseeff, 2002;
082
      Emami et al., 2020). However, the study of generalization guarantees of FL algorithms is rather
      limited (Mohri et al., 2019; Hu et al., 2022; Yuan et al., 2021a). Notably, these studies often
083
      overlook the impact of the optimization algorithm Sun et al. (2023), and often rely on assumptions
084
      like Binary loss Hu et al. (2022); Mohri et al. (2019) and the Bernstein condition (Yuan et al.,
085   2021a). Additionally, generalization bounds for meta-learning and FL are established in Fallah
086   et al. (2021); Chen et al. (2021) under stringent assumptions such as strong convexity and bounded
087   loss functions. Recently, Sun et al. (2023) has investigated the generalization of FedAvg via the
088   lens of uniform stability. We note that these analyses impose strong assumptions such as bounded
089   gradient and heterogeneity on the data, which are usually not satisfied by many problems of
090   practical interest. Moreover, the optimization guarantees provided in Sun et al. (2023) are weaker
091   compared to the linear convergence established in our work. Based on the above observations, we
092   ask the following main question:
093
094      Q2: Can we provide generalization guarantees for FedAvg? If so, what is the impact of (a)
095       the number of samples per client, (b) the model size, and (c) the number of clients on the
096                                    generalization performance?
097
098   We address Q2 by deriving Rademacher complexity when each client employs a single hidden-layer
099   NN for FedAvg implementation. We show that the local assumptions developed to address Q1 play
100   an important role in bounding the Rademacher complexity for FedAvg. Importantly, our analysis
101   captures the effect of data samples and NN size, and the number of clients on the generalization
102   performance of FedAvg. It is worth mentioning that to address both Q1 and Q2, we do not make
103
      some standard assumptions that are typically used in many existing works Li et al. (2019); Stich
      (2018); Yu et al. (2019); Haddadpour et al. (2019); Qu et al. (2020); Woodworth et al. (2020a;b);
104
      Hu et al. (2022); Mohri et al. (2019) such as: (i) (strongly) convex loss, (ii) bounded loss, (iii)
105
      bounded gradients (iv) bounded heterogeneity, and (v) interpolation 1 . In this work, we have not
106   assumed the existence of a global optimal point; rather, it is part of our conclusion.
107

      Contributions. The major contributions of our work include:
109   ➢ Answer to Q1: For the first time, we show that FedAvg converges linearly to the optimal
110   solution (see Corollary 3.2) if the local loss functions at each client and the global loss function
111   satisfy a novel local PL-type assumption introduced in Assumption 2.4. It is important to note that
112   the existence of a global optimal in our analysis is a part of our conclusion, not an assumption. To
113   the best of our knowledge, both conditions introduced in Assumption 2.4 are new. It is also worth
114   noting that these conditions do not follow from any of the existing results, even in the special case of
115   centralized setting, i.e., for K = 1 (Chatterjee, 2022; An & Lu, 2023). In addition, we also establish
116   that a single hidden-layer NN satisfies the two conditions proposed in Assumption 2.4. Specifically,
117
      we establish the conditions on the width of the NN as a function of the number of samples, number
      of clients, and the feature dimension, and on the eigenvalues of the Jacobian of the loss functions
118
      (or the scaling factor of the final output layer) such that the proposed conditions are satisfied. To our
119
      knowledge, these results are novel (see Theorems 4.5).
120   ➢ Answer to Q2: To address Q2, we derive an upper bound on the Rademacher complexity for
121   a class of single hidden layer NNs by utilizing the fact that the FedAvg iterates stay within a ρ-ball
122   around the initialization. We point out that this is made possible by the conditions provided in As-
123   sumption √ 2.4. In particular, we show that the Rademacher complexity approaches zero if the radius
124   ρ = O( n)2 and m = O(n3 ), where n is the number of samples at each client and m is the width
125   of the√NN. We show that the generalization error regardless of the data heterogeneity diminishes as
126   O(1/ n). We finally corroborate our theoretical findings through numerical experiments.

192   convergence: (i) interpolation, i.e., there exists w∗ such that Φi (w∗ ) = 0 for all samples i ∈ [n]

199   is important to note that our condition is fundamentally different from the PL∗ -inequality in the
200   following way:
201
202   • There is a stark difference between our proposed condition and the the PL-condition (or P L∗
203     condition), which is defined as ∥∇Φ(w)∥2 ≥ µ(Φ(w) − Φ(w∗ )) for all w ∈ Rd (and w ∈
204     B[w0 , ρ] for P L∗ condition). In the PL-condition (and local PL), the constants do not depend on
205
        the initialization and radius as the condition is universally satisfied. Another important assumption
        made in the local/global PL-condition is the existence of a global optimal point w∗ . In contrast,
206
        our proposed condition does not require this assumption; instead, we prove the existence of a
207
        global optimal point under our novel condition.
208
      • It is important to note that the PL-condition must be satisfied over the entire parameter space,
209
        which can restrict its applicability to certain loss functions such as logistic loss Karimi et al.
210
        (2016). On the other hand, our novel condition is assumed only over a small neighborhood around
211     the initialization, making it more broadly applicable. Later we show that parameters such as
212     initialization and the radius ρ can be chosen so that the condition is easily (compared to the PL
213     inequality) satisfied.

522   7                 C ONCLUSIONS
523
524   In this work, we addressed the problem of generalization along with convergence guarantees of
525
      the widely used FedAvg algorithm for solving Federated Learning (FL) problems. We proved the
      generalization bound by handling the optimization error and the Rademacher complexity. The opti-
526
      mization error was handled by proposing a novel and new constrained Polyak-Łojasiewicz (PL) type
527
      conditions on the (local) loss functions. Under these new conditions, we showed that there exists
528   a global optimum to which the FedAvg converges linearly after O(log(1/ϵ)) rounds of communi-
529   cation, where ϵ is the desired optimality gap. Importantly, we demonstrated that a class of single
530   hidden layer NNs satisfy the proposed conditions that are required to establish the linear conver-
531   gence of FedAvg as long as m > nK    d , where m is the number of neurons in the hidden layer, n is
532   the number of samples at each client, K is the number of clients, and d is the feature dimension.
                                                                                                √
533   Finally, we showed that the generalization error of FedAvg decreases at the rate of O(1/ n) by
534   proving a bound on the Rademacher Complexity using the fact that the neural network parameters
535   are constrained to a neighbourhood around the initialization.
