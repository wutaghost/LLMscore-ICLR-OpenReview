009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            Single-cell multi-omics technologies (e.g., scRNA-seq and scATAC-seq data) have
012            provided more comprehensive insights for understanding cellular conditions and
013            activities in recent years. However, multimodal representation learning for tran-
014            scriptomics data remains a challenging problem due to heterogeneous relationships
015            and label scarcity in reality. In this work, we propose a novel approach named
016            Geometric Relation Exploration with Cross-modal Supervision (GRACE) for re-
017            alistic multimodal single-cell matching. In particular, we map both multimodal
018            data into a shared embedding space by maximizing the log-likelihood of ZINB
019
               distributions. To reduce the semantic gap between multimodal data, we construct a
               geometric graph using mutual nearest neighbors to indicate cross-modal relations
020
               between samples for distribution alignment. Furthermore, to extract most pairwise
021
               information, we explore high-order relations in the geometric graph, which would
022            be incorporated into a meta-learning paradigm for robust optimization. In addition,
023            to further mitigate label scarcity, we introduce a nonparametric way to generate
024            label vectors for unlabeled data for cross-modal supervision across different modal-
025            ities. Extensive experiments on several benchmark datasets validate the superiority
026            of the proposed GRACE compared to various baselines. In general, compared to
027            the second-best method, GRACE exhibits an average performance improvement of
028            6.71% and 14.17% for the R2A task and A2R task, respectively. Code is available
029            at https://anonymous.4open.science/r/GRACE.

032   1   I NTRODUCTION
033
034   Modern single-cell multi-omics technologies (Chappell et al., 2018; Wen et al., 2022b) have made
035   extensive achievements, which enable the measurement of cells from various modalities for under-
036   standing cell biology in health and disease. Among them, single-cell RNA-sequencing (scRNA-seq)
037   (Kolodziejczyk et al., 2015), single-cell ATAC-sequencing (scATAC-seq) (Pott & Lieb, 2015), and
038   single-cell DNA methylome sequencing (Karemaker & Vermeulen, 2018) quantify the gene expres-
039
      sion, chromatin accessibility and DNA methylation of individual cells, respectively. There have
      also been sequencing technologies for the joint measurement of multi-omics information from the
040
      same cell such as CITE-seq (Stoeckius et al., 2017) and ASAP-seq (Mimitou et al., 2021). To
041
      understand and integrate data from various modalities, it is highly anticipated to develop a unified
042   cell representation learning framework, which maps multimodal data to a common embedding space
043   while preserving the original semantic relationships.
044
045
      In literature, several multimodal single-cell data integration approaches are proposed (Lin et al.,
      2022; Li et al., 2023). Despite their tremendous progress, these single-cell multimodal integration
046
      approaches require a large number of labeled single-cell data (Wang et al., 2021; Huang et al.,
047
      2021) to boost the performance. Nevertheless, multimodal single-cell data frequently originate
048   from different sources (Kiselev et al., 2019), where cell type information is not provided for all
049   the data. In reality, there always exists a large number of economic unlabeled multimodal data (Qi
050   et al., 2020). Moreover, the existing multimodal single-cell data integration approaches focus on
051   transferring knowledge about cell types, while ignoring the more challenging problem of matching
052   single-cell multimodal representations. This motivates us to study an underexplored problem of
053   realistic multimodal single-cell data matching, which learns unified cell representations with cellular
      semantics incorporated by jointly using both labeled and unlabeled multimodal data.

054
      In practice, formalizing a framework for realistic multimodal single-cell data matching remains
055   challenging since two questions are required to tackle : (1) How to obtain modality-invariant
056   representations for multimodal single-cell data? Note that scRNA-seq and scATAC-seq data offer
057   different perspectives on cell-level descriptions (Green et al., 2022). Therefore, the distribution
058   discrepancy in the hidden space (Andonian et al., 2022; Liu et al., 2021; Patel et al., 2023) usually leads
059   to the semantic gap between the two modalities. (2) How to learn discriminative cell representations
060   under label scarcity? Due to the absence of annotation information, cell representations of unlabeled
061   data could be of poor quality without proper semantics incorporated (Li et al., 2020). This leads
062   to insufficient supervision during the learning process. In addition, the heterogeneous relationship
063
      across multimodal data makes the problem more complicated.
064   To address these issues, we propose a new approach named Geometric Relation Exploration with
065   Cross-modal Supervision (GRACE). Our GRACE utilizes separate auto-encoders to transform
066   multimodal high-dimensional single-cell data into a unified embedding space. To preserve the most
067   information, we reconstruct the original count data with likelihood maximization by incorporating
068   underlying zero-inflated negative binomial (ZINB) (Clivio et al., 2019) distributions. The core of
069
      our GRACE is to explore hierarchical geometric relations between unlabeled multimodal samples.
      In particular, we construct a geometric graph among unlabeled samples by employing mutual
070
      nearest neighbors in the hidden space to illustrate the distribution discrepancy across modalities,
071
      which is minimized with the help of a memory bank. Furthermore, we investigate high-order
072   relations in the geometric graph for extra supervision, which is integrated into a meta-learning
073   framework (Vanschoren, 2018) for robust optimization. To mitigate label scarcity, we present a
074   nonparametric strategy for generating label distributions by comparing unlabeled cell representations
075   with support representations. These label distributions would be refined for informative signals for
076   effective discriminative learning across modalities. Extensive experiments on a range of benchmark
077   datasets validate the superiority of the proposed GRACE in comparison to competing baselines. The
078   contribution of this work can be summarized as follows:
079
      • Problem Formulation. We study an underexplored problem of realistic multimodal single-cell data
080
        matching, which extends multimodal learning into biological data understanding.
081
082
      • Novel Methodology. On the one hand, GRACE explores hierarchical geometric relations among
        cross-modal unlabeled samples, which are incorporated into a meta-learning paradigm to ensure
083
        robust distribution alignment. On the other hand, GRACE introduces a nonparametric manner to
084
        generate label vector distributions for discriminative learning across different modalities.
085
      • Multifaceted Experiments. Extensive experiments on a range of benchmark datasets validate the
086
        superiority of the proposed GRACE compared with diverse baseline methods in different settings.

092   Stein-O’Brien et al., 2018) and statistical models (Shen et al., 2009; Stuart et al., 2019; Welch et al.,
093
      2017). Matrix factorization (Wang & Zhang, 2012; Xu et al., 2020) is a powerful tool for dimension

094
      statistical models (Xiao et al., 2022) frequently employ intricate data distributions to characterize
095

096   made significant strides in single-cell data integration (Tang et al., 2023). Some existing approaches

100   passing mechanism to generate discriminative representations. However, it’s imperative to underscore
101   that none of the existing works are suitable for our problem setting. They fall short in effectively
102   leveraging both labeled and unlabeled multimodal data for efficient representation matching.

137   3.1     F RAMEWORK OVERVIEW
138
139   This work investigates the realistic problem of realistic multimodal single-cell data matching, which
140
      is challenging due to heterogeneous relationships between modalities and label scarcity in practice.
      In brief, we propose a new approach named GRACE for this problem, which mainly consists of
141
      three modules as follows: (1) Joint Representation Learning, which leverages separate auto-encoders
142
      to compass high-dimensional and sparse single-cell data from different modalities with likelihood
143   maximization; (2) Geometric Relation Exploration, which builds a geometric graph using mutual
144   nearest neighbors at different orders and then explore relations among the hierarchical neighbors
145   for robust semantics alignment with a bi-level meta learning paradigm; (3) Nonparametric Semi-
146   supervised Learning, which reconstructs label vector distributions by comparing unlabeled data with
147   labeled data, providing supervision for cross-modal consistency. The overview of our GRACE can be
148   found in Figure 1. Next, we elaborate on the details of each module in the proposed approach.
149

188   3.3   G EOMETRIC R ELATION E XPLORATION FOR S EMANTICS A LIGNMENT

193

194

195

      3.5   S UMMERIZATION
339   Consistency Learning. Finally, we adopt cross-modal consistency learning (Feng et al., 2023;
340   Radford et al., 2021) to enhance the discriminability of cell representations. In particular, we enforce
341   the consistency of representation for each unlabeled pair compared with the other samples in a

533
534   This paper investigates an underexplored problem of realistic multimodal single-cell data matching
535   and proposes a novel approach named GRACE, which maps high-dimensional multimodal biological
536   data into a common embedding space under label scarcity conditions. Extensive experiments
537   conducted on several benchmark datasets verify the superiority of the proposed method over numerous
538   baselines and prove that GRACE is scalable to diverse multi-omics single-cell data analysis tasks.
539   In future works, we plan to extend our framework to a broader range of applications such as spatial
      transcriptomics single-cell data analysis and multimodal single-cell foundation models.

      B    R ELATED W ORK
855
      Multimodal Single-cell Data Integration. Integrating multimodal single-cell data from various
856
      sources is an essential problem for understanding biological processes. Early attempts usually
857
      focus on matrix factorization (Duren et al., 2018; Jin et al., 2020; Stein-O’Brien et al., 2018) and
858   statistical models (Shen et al., 2009; Stuart et al., 2019; Welch et al., 2017). Matrix factorization
859   provides an effective tool for generating low-dimensional features from high-dimensional omics data
860   while statistical models usually introduce a range of assumptions about underlying distributions for
861   probabilistic inference. For example, scMoMaT (Zhang et al., 2023b) adopts matrix tri-factorization
862   to identify multimodal biomarkers associated with cell types. Recently, deep learning-based methods
863   have achieved extensive progress including auto-encoder-based methods and graph neural network-
      based methods. scMoGNN (Wen et al., 2022a) constructs a graph model to depict the correlation


                                                       16
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


864
      between genes and cells and then employs cell-feature graph convolution to learn discriminative cell
865   and gene representations. Nevertheless, these approaches are data-hungry with the requirement of
866   extensive labeled data, which is hard to get in realistic scenarios. Towards this end, this work for the
867   first time, centers on a practical issue of semi-supervised multimodal single-cell data matching and
868   proposes an effective approach dubbed GRACE to solve the problem.
869
      Semi-supervised Learning. Due to its ability to address label scarcity, semi-supervised learning (Wu
870
      et al., 2023; Assran et al., 2021) has gained significant attention with wide applications including
871   semantic segmentation (Qiao et al., 2023; Chen et al., 2023) and object detection (Hua et al., 2023;
872   Zhang et al., 2023a). Research semi-supervised learning approaches can be broadly categorized
873   as consistency regularization approaches (Miyato et al., 2018; Sohn et al., 2020; Xie et al., 2020)
874   and pseudo-labeling approaches (Berthelot et al., 2019a;b; Hu et al., 2021b). Pseudo-labeling
875   approaches annotate unlabeled samples using a weak model and incorporate confident pseudo-labels
876   and their corresponding sample into the training set. Moreover, dual learning, dynamical thresholding,
877   and curriculum learning are utilized to ensure accurate and unbiased pseudo-labels for reliable
878   optimization. In contrast, consistency regularization approaches usually incorporate perturbation of
879
      various sources including input (Xie et al., 2020), network parameters (Ouali et al., 2020) and deep
      features (Ke et al., 2019), and then encourage model invariance under perturbation. Nevertheless,
880
      these approaches primarily focus on single-modality classification problems, which are not applicable
881
      to multimodal data matching. In this work, we propose a new nonparametric strategy to reconstruct
882   informative and reliable label vectors for discriminative learning across modalities.
