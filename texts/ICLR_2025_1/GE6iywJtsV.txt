009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            Shape-based virtual screening is a widely utilized method in ligand-based de novo
012            drug design, aiming to identify molecules in chemical libraries that share similar
013            3D shapes but simultaneously possess novel 2D chemical structures compared to
014            the reference compound. As an emerging technology, generative model is an alter-
015            native way to do de novo drug design by directly generating 3D novel structures.
016            However, existing models face challenges in reliably generating valid drug-like
017            molecules under specific conformation constrains. Here, a novel diffusion model
018
               constrained with 3D reference shape, Diff-Shape, was proposed to generate struc-
               tures whose 3D conformations are similar to a given reference shape, thereby
019
               avoiding the computational cost of screening large database of 3D conformations.
020
               This model utilizes a zero-weighted graph control module, taking in various forms
021            of point clouds of reference shape to guide diffusion process of 3D molecular gen-
022            eration. The results show that our model is capable of generating molecules with
023            high shape similarity but still low 2D graph similarity to the query structure and it
024            significantly out-performs existing shape-based generative models.

027   1   I NTRODUCTION
028
029   Ligand-based drug design (LBDD) task focuses on molecular modelling without relying on knowl-
030   edge of protein structure. Typically, it elucidates the relationship of a compound’s structure and
031   physico-chemical attributes to its biological activity and identifies ”actives” on the basis of 3D phar-
032   macophore or 3D shape similarity. For example, 3D shape similarity search is a commonly used
033
      ligand-based virtual screening tool to identify molecules with similar shape to a reference structure
      and has shown promising results for scaffold-hopping tasks.(Rush et al., 2005) However, the effec-
034
      tiveness of virtual screening is tied to the chemical space of searched chemical libraries, restraining
035
      its capacity to explore novel chemical space.
036
037   In recent years, generative model has emerged as new paradigm for de novo drug design and has
038   revolutionized computer-aided drug design (CADD) by enabling efficient exploration of chemical
039
      space and goal-directed molecular optimization (MO) in a data driven manner. Various neural net-
      work architectures have been applied in generative models to directly generate drug-like molecules
040
      at 1D sequence, 2D graph or 3D conformation level with or without protein structure, including
041
      recurrent neural networks (RNN)(Segler et al., 2018; Li et al., 2018; Cho, 2014), variational auto-
042   encoders(Ma et al., 2018; Jin et al., 2020), generative adversarial networks(De Cao & Kipf, 2018),
043   3D convolutional networks (CNNs)(Kuzminykh et al., 2018), flow-based model(Shi et al., 2020)
044   and, more recently, diffusion based models(Qian et al., 2024; Lin et al., 2022) etc. Among these
045   models, shape conditioned generative model represents an alternative way for doing ligand based
046   de novo design and in contrast to conventional database screening methods, it can explore a much
047   larger chemical space beyond the databases of known chemicals.
048   The early attempts on shape conditioned generative model were to build models not directly gener-
049   ating 3D conformations. Skalic et al.(Skalic et al., 2019) and Imrie et al.(Imrie et al., 2021) trained
050   networks to generate 1D SMILES strings and 2D molecular graphs, respectively, conditioned on
051   CNN encodings of 3D pharmacophores ignoring Euclidean symmetries. Zheng et al. used super-
052   vised molecule-to-molecule translation on SMILES strings for scaffold hopping tasks and evaluated
053   the generated scaffolds’ 3D shape similarity to the reference.(Zheng et al., 2021) Papadopoulos et al.
      sampled molecules with high shape similarity to a target by SMILES based reinforcement learning

054
      in REINVENT, requiring re-optimization of the agent model for each target shape.(Papadopoulos
055   et al., 2021) Roney et al. fine-tuned a 3D generative model on the hits of a shape based virtual
056   screen of more than 1010 drug-like molecules to shift the learned distribution towards a particular
057   shape.(Roney et al., 2022) Yet, this expensive screening approach must be repeated for each new ref-
058   erence shape. The first transferable shape-conditioned 3D generative model, SQUID, proposed by
059   Coely et al., utilized auto-regressive fragment based generation with heuristic bonding geometries,
060   allowing to predict torsion of connecting bonds of growing fragments to make sure the growing 3D
061   conformation properly aligned to the reference shape.(Adams & Coley, 2022) However, its perfor-
062   mance was only demonstrated on small drug-like molecules up to 27 atoms. Ning et al. proposed
063
      another attempt for shape-constraint molecular generations, ShapeMol(Chen et al., 2023), an equiv-
      ariant diffusion model conditioned with a shape-embedding from encoder, they demonstrated that
064
      ShapeMol out-performed SQUID on shape-conditioned virtual screening.
065
066   Recently, various diffusion models have been used for 3D molecule generation in both LBDD and
067   SBDD scenarios, utilizing an iterative denoising process. Welling et al. proposed the equivariant
068   diffusion model (EDM) for 3D molecule generation, improving significantly over previous results
069
      in one-shot or auto-regressive settings.(Hoogeboom et al., 2022) The MDM(Huang et al., 2023)
      and GCDM21(Morehead & Cheng, 2024) modified EDM by limiting the message-passing compu-
070
      tations to neighboring nodes and changing a more expressive denoising model. Xu et al. proposed
071
      GeoLDM(Xu et al., 2024), a diffusion model on latent space coded by an equivariant autoencoder
072   instead of feature space as defined in EDM. Although these methods have shown promising results,
073   they ignored the connectivity in molecular generations, leading to sub-optimal performance on com-
074   plex molecules. To overcome this issue, Frossard et al. proposed a mixed graph and 3D denoising
075   diffusion model, MIDI(Vignac et al., 2023), by simultaneously generating a molecular graph and
076   its corresponding 3D coordinates. So far, these diffusion models are conditioned in two ways: con-
077   ditional guidance existed in the sampling process not in the training stage or using direct control
078   during the training stage with specific model architectures. In former case, an additional end-to-
079   end differential scoring function or model is needed to guide the pre-trained generative model to
080
      sample molecules with targeted properties, for example, the KGDiff model by Xu et al.(Qian et al.,
      2024) and SLIVER model by Mey et al.(Runcie & Mey, 2023) In later case, models such as DiffS-
081
      BDD(Schneuing et al., 2022), DiffBP(Lin et al., 2022), and TargetDiff(Guan et al., 2023) were used
082
      to generate molecules that bind to a specific protein pocket, the DiffLinker(Igashov et al., 2024) was
083   proposed to generate linkers between molecular fragments. Although these methods improved the
084   conditioned generation in various tasks, all of them need to be trained from scratch for each task
085   which may lead to high computation cost.
086
      In the image generation field, ControlNet(Zhang et al., 2023) achieved success by leveraging the
087
      well-established encoding layers of large stable diffusion models, which were pre-trained with bil-
088
      lions of images, to generate images under the guidance of input conditions. This approach allowed
089   ControlNet to learn a diverse range of conditional models.(Zhang et al., 2023) Inspired by Control-
090   Net, we introduce a novel diffusion model called Diff-Shape, which combines a pre-trained uncon-
091   ditional diffusion model with a graph control module for shape constrained 3D molecule generation.
092   One unique feature of our method is that the pre-trained unconditional molecule generative model
093   can be directly incorporated into Diff-Shape framework, therefore avoiding the computation cost of
094   training from scratch. By taking in reference shape as point clouds with noise and partially obscured
095   bond information, the graph control module guides diffusion process of molecular generation. To
096   showcase the utility of Diff-Shape in drug design, several tasks of the shape-conditioned generation
097
      of chemically diverse molecular structures were highlighted. Our results demonstrate that the Diff-
      Shape model significantly out-performed existing shape based generative models in terms of the
098
      shape similarity of the generation set to the reference shape, and, at the same time, can achieve bal-
099
      anced performance between high 3D shape similarity and relatively low 2D graph similarity which
100   is the key requirement for identification of new scaffold.

113   For a given 3D graph G = {V, E, R}, set V represents node features which include scalar features
114   for all nodes hv , ∀v ∈ V , such as atom types and charges for atom etc; set E corresponds to edge
115   features including scalar features like bond types for all edges he(v,w) , ∀e(v,w) ∈ E, and set R refers
116   to atomic coordinates rv , ∀v ∈ V . The adjacency Adj of G is defined as a matrix where ’1’ is set
117   for bond existence between two atoms, otherwise is ’0’.

152   Inspired by the well-known ControlNet for conditioned image generation, supposing an uncondi-
153   tioned 3D molecule generative model was already trained as a prior model, then GrCN is designed
154   to include a 3D shape constrain module and a 3D molecule generation module as shown in Figure
155   1, where the 3D generation module is a copy of the prior model with locked parameters θ and the
156   constrain module contains another locked copy of the prior model and a trainable copy with param-
157   eters θc . The trainable copy in constrain module takes a condition 3D graph Gc as input. Within the
158
      GrCN model, the locked parameters preserve the features in the prior model learned with millions
      of molecules, while the trainable copy infuses conditioning graph information into prior model to
159
      guide the output 3D graph in a profound way.

179   2.2   D IFF -S HAPE M ETHODOLOGY
180
181   The goal of Diff-Shape method is to generate 3D molecule conformations with similar 3D shape to a
182   template molecule while try to keep low 2D graph similarity simultaneously. The template molecule
183   is represented as a three-dimensional molecular graph GT M = {VT M , ET M , RT M }. Several ways
184   of fuzzy operation were applied on GT M to increase its structural obscurity aiming to output solu-
185   tions having low 2D similarity. As shown in Figure S3, in total, seven different fuzzy operations
186
      were adopted in Diff-Shape to achieve varying effects. These operations include: (1) none fuzzy
      level, in which the original reference molecular graph is used; (2) fuzzy element level, a whitened
187
      molecular graph in which all atoms were changed to carbon element and the original bond type in-
188
      formation was retained; (3) fuzzy element and bond level, a whitened molecular graph that all bond
189   types are changed to single bond; (4) point cloud level, a whitened three-dimensional point cloud in
190   which all bonds are removed; (5) mixed point cloud level, a whitened molecular graph in which all
191   bonds are changed to single bond and some bonds are partially removed. (6) coloured point cloud
192   level, the original three-dimensional point in which all bonds are removed; and (7) coloured mixed
193   point cloud level, the original element types are kept and other changes are the same with mixed
194   point cloud level. Our result demonstrates that these fuzzy operations play crucial role in generating
195   novel structures compared to the reference structure.
196   In Diff-Shape, GrCN is implemented as illustrated in Figure 2 for a given time step t. A pre-
197   trained MIDI model is employed as the prior model. The algorthim of MIDI for unconditioned
198   generations are briefly introduced in Appendix A1. The embedding blocks of the trainable copy of
199   prior model are utilized to process the condition 3D graph GT M , each attaching a zero MLP layer
200   and additionally, two MLPs are used for the scalar features V, E and one E3 MLP for the R set. The
201   E3 MLP can maintain the coordinate equivariance for R = {r0 . . . rn } by scaling each ri relative to
202   its norm ∥ ri ∥2 as shown in Eq (5).
203
204                                  ri = ri ∗ M LP (∥ ri ∥2 )/(∥ ri ∥2 +ϵ)                              (5)
205
206   Subsequently, the center of geometry is subtracted from the coordinates R of the graph G. The graph
207   embedding Gt+1 = {At+1 ⊕ C t+1 , E t+1 , Rt+1 } is used as the input of time step t and combined
208   with the embedding of GT M to be fed into the trainable module. Two locked copies of prior model
209   are created, one is used in the 3D generation module and the other one is employed in the constraint
210   module.
211   In the trainable unit of constraint module, a zero initialized MLP layer is attached to each trainable
212   encoder block. Similar to ControlNet, the output of each zero MLP layer is added to the locked
213   GT block of decoder in constraint module. This collecting mechanism is served as skip-connection,
214   linking the output of the trainable encoder layer to the input of the decoder layer in the opposite
215   order. Finally, the graph output of constraint module Gc = {Vc , Ec , Rc } is combined with the
      output of 3D generative module Gy to form the final output of GrCN.

285   2.4   C OMPUTATIONAL D ETAILS
286
287   Diff-Shape was trained on the GEOM-Drugs dataset consisting of 304339 molecules. For each
288   molecule, 5 conformations with lowest energies were selected as our reference datasets. The ref-
289   erence datasets were split into training, valid and test set in a ratio of 8:1:1. For each molecule M
290
      in training set, it was trained by using its own shape graph GT M = {VM , EM , RM } as the basis
      of creating shape condition. To increase the transferability of Diff-Shape model, we added noise
291
      on both VM ,EM and RM with a standard deviation of 0.3 in default, and various fuzzy operations
292
      were adopted to construct shape conditions. For fuzzy operations on the mixed point cloud level,
293   the connections between atoms are randomly masked with a ratio of 0.5. The weights of compo-
294   nents in loss function on atom type A, formal charge C, edge E and coordinate R are 0.4, 1.0,
295   2.0 and 3.0, respectively. The maximal diffusion step was set to 500. Evaluation metrics for Diff-
296   Shape generated molecules includes the 2D graph similarity Simg and 3D shape similarity Sim3D
297   with template molecules. The 2D similarity was calculated by the Tanimoto similarity of ECFP-4
298   fingerprints between generations and templates, while the 3D shape similarity was calculated with
299   the ROCS software of OE Toolkit package in default settings.30(Grant et al., 1996) For the tasks
300   of structure-based drug design, we also evaluate the docking score of generations in target pockets
301
      with the GLIDE module of Schrodinger software package.(Halgren et al., 2004) In our study, we
      utilized an unconditioned MIDI model as the baseline. We conducted a performance comparison
302
      between Diff-Shape and two other shape-conditioned generative models: SQUID and ShapeMol.
303
      The λ parameter of SQUID was set to 0.3 for optimal performance in our practice.(Adams & Co-
304   ley, 2022) ShapeMol was evaluated with and without guidance using a shape similarity-based score
305   function during the diffusion process to improve its performance. These two variants of Shape-
306   Mol are denoted as ”ShapeMol-NoGuide” and ”ShapeMol-Guide-0.5”, respectively, in which the
307   guidance weight was set to 0.5 in the guidance mode. Furthermore, we evaluated Pocket2Mol and
308   DiffSBDD in the context of structure-based drug design. Both models were employed with their
309   default settings for the evaluation.

504   4   C ONCLUSION
505
506   In current study, we introduce a novel diffusion-based 3D molecular generative model, called as
507   Diff-Shape, for shape-controlled 3D molecule generation. By directly incorporating a pre-trained
508   unconditional 3D molecular generative model and a graph control module taking reference shape as
509
      input, the condition guided diffusion process of molecular generation can be achieved. In tasks of the
      shape-conditioned generations of chemically diverse molecular structures, our results demonstrate
510
      that the Diff-Shape model can efficiently generate drug-like molecular conformations which has
511
      not only high 3D similarity to the reference shape but also possess reasonable chemical novelty
512   compared to the reference structure.

      A.2   F UZZY OPERATIONS ON SHAPE CONDITION
757
758   As shown in Figure S3, in total, seven different fuzzy operations were adopted in Diff-Shape to
759   achieve varying effects. These operations include: (1) none fuzzy level, in which the original ref-
760   erence molecular graph is used; (2) fuzzy element level, a whitened molecular graph in which all
761   atoms were changed to carbon element and the original bond type information was retained; (3)
762   fuzzy element and bond level, a whitened molecular graph that all bond types are changed to sin-
763   gle bond; (4) point cloud level, a whitened three-dimensional point cloud in which all bonds are
764
      removed; (5) mixed point cloud level, a whitened molecular graph in which all bonds are changed
      to single bond and some bonds are partially removed. (6) coloured point cloud level, the original
765
      three-dimensional point in which all bonds are removed; and (7) coloured mixed point cloud level,
766
      the original element types are kept and other changes are the same with mixed point cloud level.
767   Our result demonstrates that these fuzzy operations play crucial role in generating novel structures
768   compared to the reference structure.
