008                                               A BSTRACT
009
010
               Point cloud registration is a critical and challenging task in computer vision. It is
               difficult to avoid poor local minima since the cost function is significantly non-
011
               convex. Correspondences tainted by significant or unknown outliers may cause
012
               the probability of finding a close-to-true transformation to drop rapidly, leading to
013            point cloud registration failure. Many registration methods avoid local minima by
014            updating the scale parameter of the cost function using graduated non-convexity
015            (GNC). However, the update is usually performed in a fixed manner, resulting in
016            limited accuracy and robustness of registration, and failure to reliably converge to
017            the global minimum. Therefore, we present a novel method to robust point cloud
018            registration based on Adaptive Graduated Non-Convexity (AGNC). By monitor-
019            ing the positive definiteness of the Hessian of the cost function, the scale in grad-
020            uated non-convexity is adaptively reduced without the need for a fixed optimiza-
021
               tion schedule. In addition, a multi-task knowledge sharing mechanism is used to
               achieve collaborative optimization of non-convex cost functions at different levels
022
               to further improve the success rate of point cloud registration under challenging
023
               high outlier conditions. Experimental results on simulated and real point cloud
024            registration datasets show that AGNC far outperforms state-of-the-art methods in
025            terms of robustness and accuracy, and can obtain promising registration results
026            even in the case of extreme 99% outlier rates. To the best of our knowledge, this
027            is the first study that explores point cloud registration considering adaptive gradu-
028            ated non-convexity.

      1   I NTRODUCTION
032
033
      Point cloud registration is a critical and challenging task in computer vision. Its goal is to transform
      point clouds with arbitrary coordinate systems into a common coordinate system to obtain full cov-
034
      erage of an object or scene. Point cloud registration can be used for scene reconstruction (Yu et al.,
035
      2023; Mei et al., 2023), object recognition (Jiang et al., 2023; Yuan et al., 2024; Nie et al., 2024),
036   autonomous driving (Lu et al., 2019; Liu et al., 2024), and medical imaging processing (Chen et al.,
037   2022c; Ginzburg & Raviv, 2022; Ma et al., 2023).
038
039
      The point cloud registration problem can be easily solved when the true correspondences between
      point clouds are known. But in reality, solvers yield subpar estimates since the correspondences are
040
      either uncertain or include a large number of outliers (Bustos & Chin, 2017; Chen et al., 2022b;
041
      Jiang et al., 2023). High outlier rates (sometimes exceeding 99%) are a typical feature of point
042   cloud keypoint detection and registration, which poses a great challenge to point cloud registration
043   (Huang et al., 2020; Qin et al., 2022; Yuan et al., 2023). This challenge is common, where matching
044   often produces false correspondences due to noise, occlusions, and sensor errors. For example,
045   in autonomous driving, LiDAR scanning is often interfered by dynamic objects such as cars and
046   pedestrians and contain a lot of background noise (Bogdoll et al., 2022). Registration methods must
047   effectively handle these outliers to ensure proper functioning of safety systems. Given the inherent
048   ambiguity in point cloud data association and the potential measurement errors that may produce
049   outliers, the performance of point cloud registration depends on how well it handles these outliers.
050   Over the past few decades, a lot of research has been done on point cloud registration with cor-
051   respondences tainted by outliers. Typical methods are iteratively reweighted least squares (IRLS)
052   (Wang et al., 2023; Huang et al., 2024), random sample consensus (RANSAC) (Fischler & Bolles,
053   1981; Barath & Matas, 2021), and M-estimators (Le & Zach, 2020; Li et al., 2023; Sidhartha et al.,
      2023). When the percentage of outliers in the input is low, a set of optimal parameters can be easily


                                                         1
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


054
      obtained by minimizing the residual sum of squares, and the cost can be optimized using popular
055   IRLS solvers. However, in the presence of a large number of outliers, standard IRLS with a fixed
056   threshold often produces results that are biased toward the outliers. As a result, the transformation
057   estimates are far from the ground truth transformation.
058
      For outliers, RANSAC has been widely used for registration problems. The main reasons are its
059
      algorithmic simplicity and its ability to handle contaminated data containing more than 50% outliers.
060
      But there are still a few issues that need to be fixed. On the one hand, the random sampling has a
061   slow convergence speed. On the other hand, the predefined inlier threshold leads to low accuracy in
062   registering high proportion outlier point clouds. To address these issues, many variants have been
063   proposed to speed up the computation time (Yang et al., 2021; Chen et al., 2022b), improve the
064   solution stability (Zhang et al., 2023), and automatically determine the threshold (Wei et al., 2023).
065
      M-estimators and IRLS are mathematically equivalent (He et al., 2013), and M-estimators are also
066
      sensitive to the threshold. However, the threshold can be determined heuristically based on the prob-
067   lem. One approach is to add graduated non-convexity (GNC) (Nielsen, 1997; Zach & Bourmaud,
068   2018; Jin et al., 2024), which smooths the non-convex cost function by gradually reducing the scale
069   parameter. Because it eliminates the competition from subpar solutions, it has shown to be the most
070   promising strategy. In existing registration methods with GNC (Yang et al., 2020; Le & Zach, 2020;
071   Gold & Rangarajan, 1996), parameter updates follow a basic and straightforward rule, multiplying
072   by a given scaling factor constant during each iteration. The gradual optimization plan is carefully
073   designed, which requires prior knowledge of the problem. An incorrect plan may lead to unneces-
074   sary long invalid runs in the registration instance. On the other hand, little attention has been paid to
075
      how the scaling factor is determined (Hazan et al., 2016; Le & Zach, 2020).
076   In this study, we introduce a robust point cloud registration method based on adaptive graduated
077   non-convexity (AGNC). Different from previous GNC-based methods that rely on a predetermined
078   update rule to adjust the shape of the cost function, we propose a new adaptive update rule to de-
079   termine the scaling factor. The update rule aims to effectively adjust the shape of the cost function
080   to minimize GNC iterations, thereby potentially improving the robustness of the method without
081
      sacrificing accuracy. To overcome the severe failure cases caused by high outlier rates, we propose
      a preventive measure. In the initial stage of AGNC, we achieve the co-optimization of non-convex
082
      cost functions at different levels through a multi-task knowledge sharing mechanism to jump out
083
      of the local minimum. This measure further reduces the failure rate of point cloud registration.
084   Through performance evaluation on multiple datasets, we demonstrate the accuracy and robustness
085   of AGNC to registration problems with outliers. Extremely high outlier percentages (such as 99%
086   of correspondences being outliers) are acceptable to AGNC. To the best of our knowledge, this is
087   the first study to explore point cloud registration considering adaptive graduated non-convexity.
088
      The contributions of this work are as follows:
089
090          • We propose a novel approach to robust point cloud registration based on adaptive graduated
091            non-convexity. The adaptive reduction of the graduated non-convexity scale occurs through
092            monitoring the positive definiteness of the Hessian of the cost function.
093          • We achieve collaborative optimization of non-convex cost functions at different levels
094            through a multi-task knowledge sharing mechanism to further improve the success rate
095            of point cloud registration under challenging high outlier rates.
096          • Extensive experimental results on the different datasets demonstrate that our method can
097            achieve superior registration precision and is robust to 99% outliers.

      2.1   P OINT C LOUD R EGISTRATION
102
103   Robust Methods. RANSAC, as a well-known robust method, is embedded in the point cloud reg-
104   istration problem. It attempts to find reasonable samples and correctly identify them via iterations.
105   Some methods perform preprocessing before RANSAC, considering the use of deterministic geo-
106   metric methods (Bustos & Chin, 2017) or random game theory methods (Tam et al., 2012) to remove
107   outliers. Potential outliers can also be selected for further processing, such as selecting potential in-
      lier correspondences through geometric consistency checks (Barath & Matas, 2021). Some methods


                                                         2
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


108
      perform transformation parameter search based on consensus maximization (Campbell et al., 2017)
109   and Branch and Bound (BnB) techniques (Yang et al., 2021; Chen et al., 2022a). However, in the
110   case of high outlier rates, all of the aforementioned techniques become intractable and accuracy is
111   severely hampered.
112
      M-estimators. The M-estimators method treats the point cloud registration problem as the mini-
113
      mization of a robust cost function. Cost functions include Geman-McClure (GM), Huber, Cauchy,
114
      Welsch, Tukey, etc. In the optimization, M-estimators give small weights (close to 0) to outliers
115   and large weights (close to 1) to inliers. Therefore, the impact of outliers on the cost is largely
116   discounted. (Zhou et al., 2016) proposed fast global registration (FGR), which uses the GM cost
117   function and introduces Black-Rangarajan duality and GNC to solve the non-convex optimization
118   problem. This duality provides a way to convert traditional line process methods and robust statis-
119   tical methods into each other (Black & Rangarajan, 1996). In fact, when the proportion of outliers
120   exceeds 80%, FGR tends to fail. (Enqvist et al., 2012) proposed sequential optimization of a range of
121   surrogate functions instead of directly optimizing non-convex functions. GNC has achieved success-
122   ful applications in computer vision (Black & Rangarajan, 1996; Nielsen, 1997; Zach & Bourmaud,
123
      2018), and its wide applicability still needs to be explored.
124   Deep Learning Methods. The deep learning method first learns a high-dimensional feature space
125   representation of the point cloud, then matches key points to generate hypothetical correspondences,
126   and finally uses a differentiable registration module to obtain the best alignment (Wang et al., 2022;
127   Yu et al., 2024; Liu et al., 2024; Wang et al., 2024). Many deep learning-based point cloud regis-
128   tration methods have been proposed, such as PointNetLK (Aoki et al., 2019), SpinNet (Ao et al.,
129
      2021) and FINet (Xu et al., 2022). The assumed correspondence can be obtained based on the fea-
      tures extracted from feature descriptors such as fully convolutional geometric features descriptor
130
      (FCGF) (Choy et al., 2019). For outliers in the hypothesized correspondences, some methods (Yu
131
      et al., 2021; Chen et al., 2022b; Qin et al., 2023; Mei et al., 2023) use spatial consistency metrics to
132   eliminate outliers. Deep learning methods often have problems with generalization ability and the
133   requirement for a large amount of training data.

      2.2   G RADUATED N ON -C ONVEXITY
136
137   GNC is a commonly used method for optimizing non-convex cost functions and has been success-
138   fully applied in a variety of fields such as computer vision and machine learning (Black & Rangara-
139   jan, 1996; Nielsen, 1997). The fundamental idea of GNC is to continuously replace the original
140   non-convex cost function with simpler functions, which leads to fewer local minima (Hazan et al.,
141   2016; Yang et al., 2020). First, a simpler coarse-grained version of the objective is generated and
142   minimized. Then, the version of the objective is gradually refined in stages, and the solution of the
143   previous stage is used as the starting point for the optimization of the next stage. It eliminates the
144
      need for an initial guess and increases the probability of converging to the global minimum.
145   Let us explain GNC with an example. The GM function is a popular cost because of its robustness.
146   The GM function and the surrogate function containing the scale parameter µ are as follows:

      3.2   A DAPTIVE G RADUATED N ON -C ONVEXITY
191
192   Although GNC has been successful in early computer vision applications, most of them use a simple
193   fixed update rule (Nielsen, 1997; Ochs et al., 2013; Hazan et al., 2016; Yang et al., 2020). The scale
194   µ is decreased by a predetermined step size at each iteration, that is, µk+1 = µζk , where ζ > 1.
195   The performance of GNC depends critically on the update method used for the scale parameter µ.
196   Imagine that if ζ is close to 1, the movement in the cost function landscape becomes slow. This
197   conservative strategy ensures that each step in the optimization process moves firmly along the red
198   curve and finally reaches the global minimum at µf inal . However, this method requires a large
199
      number of update stages to gradually reduce µ, which undoubtedly increases the computational cost
      of the entire optimization process. In contrast, if we choose a larger value of µ, the movement in the
200
      cost function landscape becomes very fast. But this fast-moving strategy also brings the risk that the
201
      algorithm may not fully explore all areas in the cost landscape and get stuck in a local minimum.
202
203   In this paper, we propose a robust point cloud registration method with adaptive graduated non-
204   convexity. At each stage, we seek to use the largest µ possible while ensuring that each step update
205
      of the algorithm lies within the expected convergence range of the global minimum, significantly
      improving the accuracy and reliability of point cloud registration. To accomplish this, we look at the
206
      Hessian of the cost function Eq. 2.

286   To overcome the severe failure cases caused by high outliers, we propose a preventive measure. In-
287
      spired by human learning, humans often use their experience of solving one problem to help solve
      other problems (Chen et al., 2018; Xu et al., 2020). Improve the optimization performance of mul-
288
      tiple related tasks by sharing knowledge between tasks (Gupta et al., 2015; Liao et al., 2023; Yang
289
      et al., 2023). We regard the cost functions at different stages of the optimization process as different
290   tasks, whose function landscapes or optimal solutions have certain similarities. A promising candi-
291   date solution that helps on one task may also help on another task. Therefore, in the initial stage of
292   AGNC, we implement the collaborative optimization of non-convex cost functions at different levels
293   through a multi-task sharing mechanism to jump out of the local minimum. This measure further
294   improves the success rate of point cloud registration under challenging high outliers. The multiple
295   AGNC optimization problem can be expressed as:

525   5    C ONCLUSION
526
527   We have proposed a novel robust point cloud registration approach based on adaptive graduated
528   non-convexity. Without requiring a set optimization plan, the scale of graduated non-convexity
529   is adaptively lowered by keeping an eye on the positive definiteness of the Hessian of the cost
530   function. Experimental results have shown that this method outperforms compared methods in terms
531   of robustness and accuracy, can obtain promising registration results even in 99% outlier rates.
