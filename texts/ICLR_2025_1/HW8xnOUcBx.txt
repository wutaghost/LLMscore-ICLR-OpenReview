                                                 A BSTRACT
029
030
               Fitting parametric human body models to 3D point cloud is crucial for applica-
031
               tions such as virtual reality and human-robot interaction but remains challenging
032
               due to the lack of contextual guidance, often leading to imprecise results. To ad-
033            dress this, we propose a hybrid approach that incorporates body part segmentation
034            into the fitting process, enhancing pose estimation and segmentation accuracy.
035            Our method starts with an initial segmentation, assigning each point to a specific
036            body part. This segmentation guides a two-step optimization in fitting an SMPL-X
037            model: first, approximating the initial pose and orientation using body part cen-
038            troids, and second, refining the model by considering the entire point cloud. After
039            fitting, we reassign body parts to the point cloud through nearest-neighbor match-
040            ing, resulting in more accurate segmentation. This enhanced segmentation serves
041
               as pseudo ground truth to fine-tune the segmentation network in a self-supervised
               manner, creating a feedback loop where improvements in pose fitting lead to bet-
042
               ter segmentation and vice versa. We evaluate our approach on four challenging
043
               datasets – PosePrior, EgoBody, BEHAVE, and Hi4D – demonstrating significant
044            improvements over leading methods, including a tenfold increase in pose model-
045            ing accuracy and a 15% enhancement in segmentation accuracy after fine-tuning.
046            Our contributions are twofold: (1) introducing a novel hybrid method that unifies
047            pose fitting and body part segmentation on point clouds, enabling mutual enhance-
048            ment through iterative refinement; and (2) developing a self-supervised technique
049            for fine-tuning segmentation networks using pseudo ground truths derived from
050            fitted models. This work advances the state of the art in human body fitting to
051            point clouds, facilitating more accurate human representations in complex envi-
052
               ronments and benefiting applications that require precise human modeling. We
               will make the source code publicly available.

      1   I NTRODUCTION
055
056
057   Capturing the intricate details of human movement and shape is fundamental to a wide range of
058   applications, from creating realistic avatars in virtual reality (Thalmann & Musse, 2012; Slater &
059   Sanchez-Vives, 2016) to enhancing human-robot interactions (Argall et al., 2009; Billard & Kragic,
060   2019). Fitting a parametric human body model to 3D point cloud data (measured by, e.g., Lidar or
061   Kinect) without contextual guidance can be challenging and often yields imprecise results (Bogo
062   et al., 2016; Kolotouros et al., 2019). However, having prior information about body parts can
063
      significantly improve this process (Varol et al., 2017; Zanfir et al., 2018; Xu et al., 2020). Thus, this
      paper focuses on incorporating body part segmentation into the body fitting process.
064
065   In recent years, parametric models like SMPL-X (Loper et al., 2015) have become instrumental
066   in representing 3D human poses and shapes. These models are typically fitted to point cloud data
067   obtained from depth sensors or multi-view stereo systems using optimization techniques such as
068   gradient descent (Bogo et al., 2016; Pavlakos et al., 2019) or neural networks (Kolotouros et al.,
069
      2019; Kocabas et al., 2020). Simultaneously, advancements in body part segmentation —- assigning
      each point in a cloud to a specific body part —- have provided valuable context for understanding
070
      human poses and interactions in applications like computer graphics, personalized healthcare, and
071
      autonomous systems (Li et al., 2018; Xu et al., 2020; Takmaz et al., 2023).
072
073   Despite these advancements, current methods often struggle when applied to real-world data featur-
074   ing complex poses, occlusions, multiple interacting individuals, or human-object interactions (Trum-
075
      ble et al., 2018; Moon et al., 2019; Hassan et al., 2019a). This is largely due to existing approaches
      being trained on simplified datasets with isolated bodies in controlled environments (Mahmood
076
      et al., 2019; Andriluka et al., 2014). Consequently, their performance diminishes when confronted
077
      with the diversity and unpredictability of real-world scenarios (Mehta et al., 2017; Dabral et al.,
078   2018).
079
080   To address these limitations, we propose a hybrid approach that unifies human pose fitting and body
081
      part segmentation on point clouds, enabling each process to iteratively inform and enhance the other.
      Our method begins with an initial segmentation of the point cloud using a state-of-the-art network,
082
      Human3D (Takmaz et al., 2023), which provides a preliminary assignment of 3D points to specific
083
      body parts. This segmentation acts as a guide for the fitting of the SMPL-X model through a two-step
084   optimization process (Feng et al., 2023b).
085
086   The first optimization step uses the centroids of the body parts to approximate the initial pose and
087
      orientation of the model (Bogo et al., 2016; Xiang et al., 2019). This step is crucial since it provides
      a strong starting point for the model parameters, similar to assembling the edge pieces of a puzzle
088
      first. The second step refines the model by considering the entire point cloud, adjusting the pose and
089
      shape parameters to achieve a more precise fit (Pavlakos et al., 2019; Kolotouros et al., 2019). This
090   refinement fills in the puzzle’s interior. After fitting the model, we leverage the SMPL-X mesh to
091   reassign body parts to the point cloud through nearest-neighbor matching (Ge et al., 2019; Remelli
092   et al., 2020). This results in a more accurate segmentation than the initial prediction, effectively up-
093   dating our ”puzzle sketch” based on the assembled pieces. We then use this enhanced segmentation
094   as a pseudo ground truth to fine-tune the segmentation network. This self-supervised learning ap-
095   proach allows the network to improve its performance on new, unlabeled data, such as point clouds
096   derived from in-the-wild captures (Varol et al., 2017; Zhang et al., 2021).
097   This reciprocal process creates a feedback loop where improvements in pose fitting lead to better
098   segmentation, which in turn facilitates more accurate fitting in subsequent iterations (Zanfir et al.,
099   2018; Kocabas et al., 2020). By integrating these two tasks, we address the challenges of generaliz-
100   ing to diverse datasets and enhance the robustness of both processes in real-world conditions.
101
      We evaluate our approach on four challenging datasets: PosePrior (Zhang et al., 2021), EgoB-
102   ody (Zhang et al., 2022), BEHAVE (Bhatnagar et al., 2022), and Hi4D (Yin et al., 2023). These
103   datasets encompass a wide range of complex scenarios, including intricate poses, partial occlu-
104   sions, close human interactions, and human-object interactions (Hassan et al., 2019a; Xiang et al.,
105   2019; Pumarola et al., 2021). Our experiments demonstrate significant improvements in both pose
106   modeling accuracy and body part segmentation performance compared to leading methods (Kolo-
107   touros et al., 2019; Kocabas et al., 2020; Zanfir et al., 2018), especially in the complicated scenes.
      Specifically, we observe a tenfold average improvement in pose modeling accuracy and a 15% en-

109   on EgoBody (Zhang et al., 2022) and BEHAVE (Bhatnagar et al., 2022).

125   2   R ELATED W ORK
126
127   Estimating human body pose and shape from 3D point clouds is vital in computer vision, with
128   applications in virtual reality, animation, and human-computer interaction. While extensive research
129
      has been conducted on fitting parametric human models to 2D images (Bogo et al., 2016; Kanazawa
      et al., 2018; Kolotouros et al., 2019), we focus on methods that directly operate on 3D point cloud
130
      data. Point clouds capture detailed geometric information and avoid the ambiguities inherent in 2D
131
      projections, making them valuable for precise human modeling.
132
133   Methods for Fitting Human Poses onto Point Clouds. Several approaches have been developed
134   to fit human poses to point clouds. Bhatnagar et al. (2020) introduced IP-Net, which combines
135
      implicit representations with parametric models to reconstruct clothed human bodies from partial
      scans. IP-Net learns a continuous occupancy field representing the human body, allowing for de-
136
      tailed reconstructions even with incomplete data. Wang et al. (2021b) proposed PTF, a method that
137
      fits SMPL models to point clouds by considering local geometric features. By leveraging local point
138   distributions, PTF improves fitting accuracy in areas with high curvature or fine-grained details. Zuo
139   et al. (2021) presented a self-supervised approach for 3D human motion reconstruction from depth
140   sequences. Their method leverages temporal coherence without requiring ground truth annotations,
141   effectively reconstructing dynamic human motions. Cai et al. (2023) developed PointHPS, a hierar-
142   chical point-based network that directly regresses SMPL parameters from point clouds. PointHPS
143   achieves state-of-the-art results by utilizing a point-based encoder-decoder architecture.
144   The common challenges among these methods include sensitivity to initialization, reliance on
145   large annotated datasets, and difficulties in handling complex poses, occlusions, and interactions.
146   Regression-based methods often require extensive training data to cover the variability in human
147   shapes and poses, which may not be feasible for all applications. Additionally, they may not ef-
148   fectively capture subtle variations in body shape or handle occlusions caused by interactions with
149   objects or other people.
150   Our approach differs by integrating body part segmentation into the fitting process, leveraging initial
151   segmentation to improve model initialization and fitting accuracy. By using body part information,
152   we can distinguish between symmetric limbs and reduce ambiguities in global orientation, which
153   is a common issue in optimization-based methods. This integration allows us to achieve robust
154   initialization without the need for multiple optimization runs from different starting points, as done
155   in methods like SMPLify-X (Pavlakos et al., 2019). Furthermore, our method improves the fitting
156   accuracy by focusing on both global and local alignment between the model and the point cloud.
157   The initial segmentation guides the fitting process, making it more resilient to noise and outliers. By
158
      considering the centroids of body parts during initialization and refining the fit using the entire point
      cloud, we enhance the ability of the model to capture complex poses and interactions.
159
160   Datasets and the Challenge of Annotations. High-quality datasets with accurate annotations are
161   crucial for training and evaluating human body modeling methods. However, obtaining datasets
      with detailed body part segmentation on point clouds is particularly challenging. Existing datasets


                                                         3
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


162
      like Hi4D (Yin et al., 2023), BEHAVE (Bhatnagar et al., 2022), and EgoBody (Zhang et al., 2022)
163   provide valuable data capturing complex human poses, interactions, and motions. Hi4D offers high-
164   fidelity 4D data for human reconstruction in motion, BEHAVE focuses on human-object interac-
165   tions, and EgoBody captures human activities from an egocentric perspective. Despite their richness,
166   these datasets often lack detailed body part segmentation annotations necessary for training segmen-
167   tation networks. Annotating body parts in 3D point clouds is labor-intensive and time-consuming,
168   making it impractical for large datasets. This scarcity of annotated data limits the effectiveness of
169   supervised learning methods for body part segmentation on point clouds.
170   Our method addresses this challenge by generating improved body part segmentations through the
171   fitted SMPL-X models. After fitting the model to the point cloud, we use the correspondences
172   between the model vertices and the point cloud to assign body part labels, effectively creating pseudo
173   ground truth annotations. This process enhances the initial segmentation obtained from networks
174   like Human3D (Takmaz et al., 2023). By utilizing these improved segmentations, we can fine-
175   tune segmentation networks in a self-supervised manner. This approach does not require additional
176   manual annotations and enables the network to generalize better to diverse and complex datasets.
177
      Our method contributes to the enrichment of existing datasets with high-quality segmentation labels,
      facilitating better training data for future research and applications.

      This section presents our method for fitting the SMPL-X parametric human body model to 3D point
183
      clouds by leveraging initial body part segmentation. Our approach consists of a two-step process:
184   an initialization phase that provides a reliable starting point for optimization and a fitting phase
185   that refines the model parameters to represent the human subjects in the point cloud accurately.
186   We further utilize the fitted models to enhance body part segmentation through a self-supervised
187   fine-tuning process.
188
189
      3.1   P ROBLEM D EFINITION
190
191
      Given a 3D point cloud P = {pi }N i=1 capturing one or more human subjects, our goal is to estimate
192   the pose and shape parameters of the SMPL-X model (Pavlakos et al., 2019), resulting in accurate
193   3D meshes that align with the point cloud. The challenge lies in initializing the model parameters to
194   avoid local minima during optimization and handling ambiguities due to the symmetry of the human
195   body. To address this, we propose a method that leverages initial body part segmentation obtained
196   from Human3D (Takmaz et al., 2023). This segmentation provides valuable information that aids in
197   distinguishing between symmetric body parts and improves the initialization of the SMPL-X model.
198
199   3.2   OVERVIEW OF THE A PPROACH
200
201   Our method comprises the following main components:
202
203         1. Initial Body Part Segmentation: Segment the input point cloud into body parts using
204            Human3D (Takmaz et al., 2023), assigning each point a body part label.
205
            2. Model Initialization: Compute centroids of the segmented body parts and use them to
206
               initialize the SMPL-X model parameters, including pose, shape, and global orientation.
207
208         3. Model Fitting: Refine the SMPL-X model by optimizing an objective function that bal-
209            ances data fidelity and regularization terms, fitting the model to the entire point cloud.
210
            4. Improved Body Part Segmentation: Use the fitted SMPL-X meshes to reassign part la-
211
               bels to the point cloud via nearest-neighbor matching, resulting in enhanced segmentation.
212
213         5. Self-Supervised Fine-Tuning: Fine-tune the Human3D segmentation network using the
214            improved segmentation as pseudo ground truth, enhancing its performance on new data.
215
      We provide detailed descriptions of each component in the following subsections.

      3.8   S UMMARY
318
319   Our method effectively combines body part segmentation and model fitting to enhance the accuracy
320   of both processes. By initializing the SMPL-X model using body part centroids and refining it

323   supervised manner. This iterative process addresses challenges in pose estimation and segmentation,
      particularly in complex real-world scenarios.

      We evaluate our proposed method on several challenging datasets to demonstrate its effectiveness
327   in modeling human poses and improving body part segmentation from point clouds. Our experi-
328   ments are designed to assess the accuracy and efficiency of our method compared to baseline ap-
329   proaches, as well as to highlight the improvements in segmentation accuracy achieved through our
330   self-supervised fine-tuning process.
331
      We evaluate our method through a series of steps and compare the results with the baseline models,
332
      ArtEq (Feng et al., 2023b) and Human3D (Takmaz et al., 2023). First, we analyze the perfor-
333   mance of our pose modeling approach across various conditions and configurations, benchmarking
334   it against ArtEq. Next, we examine the improvements in body part segmentation facilitated by the
335   pose modeling, comparing these to the initial segmentation accuracy of Human3D. Finally, we as-
336   sess the impact of fine-tuning Human3D using the enhanced part segmentations as pseudo-ground
337   truths. Our evaluations are conducted on four distinct datasets, detailed below.

      To thoroughly evaluate the generalization capabilities of our method, we conduct experiments on
339   four diverse datasets, each presenting unique challenges:

            4. Hi4D (Yin et al., 2023): Features high-resolution scans of humans engaged in close inter-

               actions, such as fighting or dancing, requiring the method to identify boundaries between

519   5    C ONCLUSION
520
521
      In this paper, we introduce SegFit, a novel hybrid approach for fitting parametric human body models
522   to diverse 3D point clouds. SegFit combines body part segmentation, based on Human3D (Takmaz
523   et al., 2023), with SMPL-X modeling (Pavlakos et al., 2019) to iteratively enhance both segmen-
524   tation and pose fitting accuracy. Our method demonstrates significant improvements over state-of-
525   the-art techniques such as PTF (Wang et al., 2021b) and ArtEq (Feng et al., 2023b), especially in
526   complex, real-world scenarios where occlusions, human-object interactions, and multiple human
527   instances are prevalent.
528   By incorporating an initial body part segmentation and refining it through SMPL-X model fit-
529   ting, SegFit creates a feedback loop where better pose fitting leads to improved segmentation and
530   vice versa. This self-supervised cycle enhances the robustness and generalization capabilities of
531   the method across diverse datasets, as shown by our experiments on the PosePrior (Zhang et al.,
532   2021), BEHAVE (Bhatnagar et al., 2022), EgoBody (Zhang et al., 2022), and Hi4D (Yin et al.,
533   2023) datasets. Our results indicate up to a tenfold improvement in pose estimation and a 15%
534   increase in segmentation accuracy. Furthermore, the segmentation fine-tuning process with pseudo
535   ground truths provides a versatile solution for unlabeled data, paving the way for more accurate
536
      self-supervised human modeling.
537   Our work sets a new benchmark for body fitting on point clouds, with the potential to significantly
538   advance human representation in complex environments. Future work will explore potential im-
539   provements to the pose fitting accuracy, such as by introducing a penetration loss term for scenes
      where humans interact with each other or with objects in their environment.
