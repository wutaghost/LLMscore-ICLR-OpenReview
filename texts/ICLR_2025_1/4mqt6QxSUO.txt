009                                                A BSTRACT
010
011            We present a novel, theoretically grounded framework for automated SARS-
012            CoV-2 detection from pulmonary Computed Tomography (CT) scans, integrating
013            cutting-edge concepts from statistical learning theory, optimal transport, and in-
014            formation geometry. Our approach begins with a submodular optimization-based
015            image selection protocol, utilizing a continuous greedy algorithm. The feature ex-
016            traction process employs a Riemannian geometry-inspired attention mechanism,
017            where feature integration is formulated as geodesic interpolation on a manifold in-
018
               duced by the Fisher Information Metric. We introduce a unified decision-making
               framework based on proper scoring rules and Bregman divergences, encompass-
019
               ing multiple voting schemes with proven consistency and asymptotic normality
020
               properties. To address domain shift, we develop an adversarial domain adap-
021            tation technique using the Wasserstein-Fisher-Rao distance, complemented by a
022            graph-based regularization term derived from Gromov-Wasserstein theory. Theo-
023            retical analysis provides convergence guarantees for the adversarial training pro-
024            cess and establishes generalization bounds in terms of optimal transport distances.
025            Empirical evaluation demonstrates the superiority of our approach over existing
026            methods, achieving better performance on benchmark datasets. This work not
027            only advances the field of automated medical image analysis but also contributes
028            fundamental theoretical insights to the broader domains of machine learning and
029
               optimal transport theory.

038   lenges in terms of time, expertise, and consistency, especially during peak pandemic periods. This
039   predicament has catalyzed research into automated diagnostic systems, with deep learning at the

046   data.
047
      In response to these challenges, we present a novel, theoretically grounded framework for auto-
048   mated SARS-CoV-2 detection from CT scans. Our approach integrates cutting-edge concepts from
049   statistical learning theory, optimal transport, and information geometry to create a robust and math-
050   ematically rigorous methodology. At the core of our framework is a submodular optimization-based
051   image selection protocol, which utilizes a continuous greedy algorithm with provable (1 − 1e )-
052   approximation guarantees. This ensures optimal selection of CT slices, addressing the variability in
053   scan quality and content. Our feature extraction process employs a Riemannian geometry-inspired
      attention mechanism, where feature integration is formulated as geodesic interpolation on a man-

471   4.3   I MAGE P REPARATION S ETUP
472
      Despite the advanced capabilities of deep learning models to process complex features directly from
473
      raw data, image preprocessing is still essential for several reasons: Data Quality and Consistency:
474   Preprocessing aids in ensuring the input data is of superior quality, devoid of noise or artifacts. This
475   is crucial to prevent unwanted variations in the training dataset that could potentially impair model
476   performance. Normalization: Preprocessing steps such as image scaling and intensity normaliza-
477   tion are vital for harmonizing pixel values across the dataset, which can facilitate more consistent
478   model training and convergence. Feature Extraction: Certain preprocessing tasks, like image filter-
479   ing, are instrumental in augmenting the visibility and extraction of important features within images,
480   thereby enhancing the model’s learning efficiency and predictive accuracy. Efficiency: Implement-
481   ing suitable preprocessing can diminish the computational load and decrease the training duration
482
      for deep learning models, thereby enhancing their practical efficiency.
483   In our study, we implemented a series of image preprocessing steps designed to optimize model
484   efficacy and enhance the accuracy of predictions in detecting SARS-CoV-2: Image Rescaling: All
485   images within the dataset were resized to uniform dimensions of 512 × 512 pixels. This standard-
      ization is crucial for ensuring model compatibility and maintaining consistency across the dataset.

486
      Sharpening Filters: We employed sharpening filters to improve the definition and clarity of the im-
487   ages. This process highlights essential features in the X-ray images, minimizes noise, and facilitates
488   the extraction of significant patterns by the model. Color Space Transformation: We converted
489   images from the Blue-Green-Red format to the Red-Green-Blue format. This transformation is vi-
490   tal for achieving color consistency across the dataset and for the accurate interpretation of image
491   data. Pixel Intensity Scaling: The pixel values were normalized to ensure they fall within a specific
492   range, enhancing the model’s learning efficiency. This normalization is integral to achieving faster
493   and more stable convergence during training. Consistent Labeling: To support supervised learning
494   approaches, we meticulously labeled all images in the dataset. Proper labeling is imperative to train
495
      the model effectively and achieve high accuracy in predictions.

      4.4   AUGMENTING DATASET
498
499
      The image augmentation process is dynamically integrated during the training phase of our model,
500
      implemented in real-time as the model structure evolves. By embedding a combination of prepro-
501
      cessing and augmentation techniques directly into the training pipeline, the model is better equipped
502   to handle variations in image quality, size, and perspective, thereby significantly improving the ac-
503   curacy of SARS-CoV-2 detection. In the augmentation strategy, equal importance was given to
504   rotation, flipping, shearing, and zooming. This strategy was designed to ensure a uniform applica-
505   tion of these techniques, aiming to inject a diverse set of transformations into the training set without
506   disproportionately favoring any single technique. By distributing equal weightage across these op-
507   erations, our approach promotes a balanced dataset that encapsulates a broad spectrum of real-world
508   variabilities and distortions likely to be encountered by the model. Such a balanced implementa-
509   tion not only contributes to enhancing the robustness of the model but also boosts its generalization
510
      capabilities across different input conditions, crucial for accurate real-world applications.
