009                                              A BSTRACT
010
011
               Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs) are considered to have enormous potential in the
012            future development of Artificial Intelligence (AI) due to their brain-inspired and
013            energy-efficient properties. In the current supervised learning domain of SNNs,
014            compared to vanilla Spatial-Temporal Back-propagation (STBP) training, online
015            training can effectively overcome the risk of GPU memory explosion and has
016            received widespread academic attention. However, the current proposed online
017            training methods cannot tackle the inseparability problem of temporal dependent
018            gradients and merely aim to optimize the training memory, resulting in no perfor-
019            mance advantages compared to the STBP training models in the inference phase. To
020
               address the aforementioned challenges, we propose Efficient Multi-Precision Firing
               (EM-PF) model, which is a family of advanced spiking models based on floating-
021
               point spikes and binary synaptic weights. We point out that EM-PF model can
022
               effectively separate temporal gradients and achieve full-stage optimization towards
023            computation speed and memory footprint. Experimental results have demonstrated
024            that EM-PF model can be flexibly combined with various techniques including
025            random back-propagation, parallel computation and channel attention mechanism,
026            to achieve state-of-the-art performance with extremely low computational overhead
027            in the field of online learning.

      1   I NTRODUCTION
031
032
033
      Spiking Neural Networks (SNNs), as the third-generation neural network towards brain-inspired in-
      telligence (Maass, 1997), have gained widespread attention from researchers in Artificial Intelligence
034
      (AI) community. SNNs utilize spiking neurons as the basic computing unit to transmit discrete spike
035
      firing sequences to the postsynaptic layer. Due to the fact that spiking neurons only emit spikes when
036   the membrane potential exceeds the firing threshold, compared to the activation values in traditional
037   Artificial Neural Networks (ANNs), spike sequences have sparse and event-driven properties, which
038   can demonstrate superior computational efficiency and power consumption ratio on neuromorphic
039   hardware (Merolla et al., 2014; Davies et al., 2018; Pei et al., 2019).
040
      Spatial-Temporal Back-propagation (STBP) is the most significant training algorithm in the supervised
041
      learning domain of SNNs currently (Wu et al., 2018). By introducing the concepts of temporal
042   dimension and surrogate gradient, STBP simultaneously tackles the Markov property and non-
043   differentiable problem of SNNs existed in the forward propagation and firing process. However,
044   although STBP training has significantly improved the learning performance and universal property
045   of SNNs (Wang et al., 2023; Qiu et al., 2024; Shi et al., 2024), as its back-propagation chains are
046   inseparable due to the temporal dependencies, its GPU memory will inevitably boost linearly with
047   the number of time-steps. This phenomenon greatly increases the training burden and hinders the
048   further application of SNNs to complex scenarios (Kim et al., 2020) and advanced spiking models
049   (Hao et al., 2024).
050   To address this problem, researchers have transferred the idea of online learning to the STBP training
051   framework (Xiao et al., 2022; Meng et al., 2023), which means that by detaching the temporal
052   dependent gradient terms, SNNs can immediately perform back-propagation at any time-step. This
053   scheme ensures that the corresponding GPU memory is independent of the training time-steps and
      remains constant, effectively alleviating the problem of computation memory explosion. However,


                                                        1

054
      the current proposed methods based on online learning still have two main defects: (i) the discrepancy
055   between forward and backward propagation, (ii) ineffective online deployment.
056
057   The reason for the first defect is that the surrogate function of spiking neurons is generally related
058
      to the value of membrane potential and the spike sequence is usually unevenly distributed in the
      temporal dimension, making the temporal dependent gradients different from each other and unable to
059
      merge with the back-propagation chain along the spatial dimension. In this case, when online learning
060
      frameworks detach temporal dependent gradients, it will lead to inconsistency between forward and
061   backward propagation, resulting in learning performance degradation. The second defect refers to the
062   fact that current online learning methods mainly focus on optimizing training memory, but cannot
063   bring any optimization regarding computation time or memory during the inference phase. This is
064   because under the framework of firing binary spikes, it is difficult to introduce parallel computation
065   or weight quantization techniques to improve inference speed or optimize memory usage without
066   sacrificing learning precision.
067   Based on the above discussion, we propose Efficient Multi-Precision Firing (EM-PF) model for
068   online training, it adopts a learning framework with inverted numerical precision, which combines
069   floating-point spikes with binary synaptic weights. On the one hand, EM-PF model solves the
070   non-differentiable problem of the firing process and enhances the uniformity of the spike sequence,
071   significantly improving the separability of the backward gradients compared to vanilla spiking models.
072   On the other hand, the EM-PF model can be flexibly combined with various techniques for optimizing
073   computational costs, achieving full-stage optimization including training and inference phases. Our
074   contributions are summarized as follow:
075
             • Compared to vanilla spiking models, we theoretically point out that EM-PF model has more
076
               superior backward gradient separability, which is conducive to achieving high-performance
077            online learning.
078
079
             • We further propose variant versions based on vanilla EM-PF model. Among them, the EM-
               PF model based on membrane potential batch-normalization can more effectively regulate
080
               the degree of gradient separability and be reparameterized into vanilla EM-PF model in the
081
               inference phase. In addition, it can further improve the network performance by combining
082            channel attention mechanism.
083
084
             • By combining random back-propagation, parallel computation and other techniques, EM-PF
               model can achieve comprehensive optimization in terms of time and memory overhead
085
               during training and inference phases, which goes beyond the optimization scope of vanilla
086
               online training.
087
088          • We achieve state-of-the-art (SoTA) performance on various datasets with different data-scale
089
               and data-type. For example, we reach top-1 accuracy of 79.91% on CIFAR-100 dataset
               under the condition of saving 15× parameter memory.

092   2   R ELATED W ORKS
093
094   Recurrent learning algorithms for SNNs. Considering the similarity in computational mechanisms
095   between SNNs and Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs), Wu et al. (2018) and Neftci et al. (2019)
096   transferred the Back-propagation Through Time (BPTT) method from RNNs to the supervised
097   learning field of SNNs and utilized surrogate functions to tackle the non-differentiable problem
098   existed in the spike firing process, which is called the STBP training algorithm. On this basis,
099
      Li et al. (2021), Guo et al. (2022b) and Wang et al. (2023) respectively attempted to start from
      the perspective of regulating the distribution about the backward gradient and membrane potential,
100
      introducing progressive surrogate functions and penalty terms. Deng et al. (2022) proposed a
101
      target learning function which comprehensively considers the SNN output distribution within each
102   time-step, which is particularly suitable for neuromorphic sequential data. To further improve
103   the learning stability and performance of SNNs, various BatchNorm (BN) modules (Zheng et al.,
104   2021; Duan et al., 2022; Guo et al., 2023) and attention mechanisms (Yao et al., 2023; Qiu et al.,
105   2024) have been proposed successively, which capture the representation information contained in
106   spike sequences from multiple dimensions, including spatial-wise, temporal-wise and channel-wise.
107   Recently, advanced spiking models have become a focus of academic attention. Researchers have
      proposed a variety of neuron models with stronger dynamic properties and memory capabilities


                                                        2
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


108
      around membrane-related parameters (Fang et al., 2021), firing mechanism (Yao et al., 2022) and
109   dendrite structure (Hao et al., 2024), promoting deeper exploration towards brain-inspired intelligence.
110   In addition, a spatial-temporal back-propagation algorithm based on spike firing time (Bohte et al.,
111   2002; Zhang & Li, 2020; Zhu et al., 2023) has also attracted widespread attention. However, this
112   series of methods are currently limited by high computational complexity and unstable training
113   process, which cannot be effectively applied to complex network backbones and large-scale datasets.
114
      Online learning algorithm for SNNs. Although STBP learning algorithm promotes SNNs to
115   join the club of high-performance models, it also brings severe computational burden to SNNs
116   during the training phase, especially the GPU memory that will increase linearly with the number of
117   time-steps. Xiao et al. (2022) transferred the idea of online learning to the domain of SNN direct
118   training, which splits the back-propagation chain by ignoring the backward gradients with temporal
119   dependencies, making the training GPU memory independent of time-steps. On this basis, Meng et al.
120   (2023) proposed a selective back-propagation scheme based on online learning, which significantly
121   improves training efficiency. Yang et al. (2022) combined online learning with ANN-SNN knowledge
122   distillation, further accelerating the training convergence speed of SNNs. Zhu et al. (2024) proposed
123
      a brand-new BatchNorm module suitable for online learning, which enhances the stability of gradient
      calculation by considering the global mean and standard deviation in the temporal dimension. To
124
      enrich the neurodynamic property of online learning, Jiang et al. (2024) introduced the difference
125
      of membrane potential between adjacent time-steps as a feature term into the backward gradient
126   calculation. Inspired by the architecture of reversed network, Zhang & Zhang (2024) and Hu et al.
127   (2024a) respectively proposed reversible memory-efficient training algorithms from spatial and
128   temporal perspectives. This type of algorithm can ensure computational consistency between online
129   and STBP learning under the condition of occupying constant GPU memory, but it requires bi-
130   directional computation towards all intermediate variables, which inevitably increases computational
131   overhead. In addition, it is worth noting that most of the online learning methods mentioned above
132   neglect the optimization of computation time and memory usage during the inference phase, thus
133   failing to demonstrate their advantages over STBP training when deploying SNN models.

359
360   Channel Attention mechanism (Hu et al., 2018; Wang et al., 2020; Guo et al., 2022a) is usually
361   inserted after the convolutional layers to optimize the network performance. We transfer the idea
362   of ECA (Wang et al., 2020) to the online training based on EM-PF model, then propose Spiking
363   Efficient Channel Attention (SECA) mechanism, as shown in Eq.(9).
364
              &\textbf {\texttt {SECA}}(\bm {I}^l[t]) = \textbf {\texttt {Sigmoid}}( \underbrace {\textbf {\texttt {Conv1d}}}_{\in \ \mathbb {R}^{1\times 1\times K}}( \underbrace {\textbf {\texttt {Sign}}(\textbf {\texttt {GAP}}(\bm {I}^l[t])}_{\in \ \mathbb {R}^{B\times 1\times C}} ))) \odot \bm {I}^l[t],\ \bm {I}^l[t]\in \mathbb {R}^{B\times C\times H\times W}. \label {eq05}   (9)

366
367   Here the input current I l [t] ∈ RB×C×H×W will be compressed to RB×C×1×1 through the Global

369   the attention scores among different channels, ultimately merging with the shortcut path. Considering
370   that the EM-PF model can convey enough information representation at each time-step, we enable
371   the spike sequence to share the weight of SECA in the temporal dimension. Due to its extremely low
372   parameter quantity (only 1 Conv1d layer with 1 × 1 × K parameters), SECA can further enhance the
373
      learning ability of SNNs under the condition of hardly affecting its online deployment.

480   In this paper, we systematically analyze the deficiencies of traditional online training, then propose a
481   novel online learning framework based on floating-point spikes and binary synaptic weights, which
482   effectively tackles the performance degradation problem caused by temporal dependent gradients

484   optimization techniques. Experimental results have verified that our proposed scheme can break
485   through the limitations of previous methods and provide further inspiration for the future development
      of online learning.
