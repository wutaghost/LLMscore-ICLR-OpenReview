009                                             A BSTRACT
010
011           Currently, Large language models (LLMs) have achieved remarkable performance
012           across various language tasks, largely due to their training on extensive datasets
013           and their considerable model size. These models exhibit in-context learning abili-
014           ties, which is to learn through few-shot learning. However, the underlying reason-
015           ing process remains ambiguous, it is unclear whether the model simply retrieves
016           relevant information and instructions from its training data to generate similar re-
017           sponses, or whether it generalizes examples to form overarching rules, which are
018
              then applied to produce accurate answers. Another method for improving few-
              shot learning is Chain-of-Thought prompting that complement steps by steps in-
019
              struction for LLMs, so they can follow this instruction to solve many reasoning
020
              tasks. Several approaches for evaluating the reasoning abilities of LLMs typically
021           involve task-solving through code generation, which enables models to formal-
022           ize problems and leverage a code compiler to solve them precisely. However,
023           these methods are constrained to specific task types and are insufficient for a com-
024           prehensive assessment of the model’s broader reasoning capabilities. Therefore,
025           this paper proposes a method to enhance in-context learning capabilities through
026           two main stages: generating general rules from the provided examples and uti-
027           lizing LLMs to verify these general rules, thereby aiming to improve reliability
028           and accuracy. At the same time, this approach seeks to investigate the induc-
029
              tive and deductive reasoning abilities, and can improve our understanding of the
              model’s reasoning by generating and applying general rules to provide transpar-
030
              ent, clearly explained responses. The proposed method demonstrates competitive
031
              performance on the 1D-ARC benchmark and several traditional language tasks,
032           suggesting its potential for more robust evaluation of LLM reasoning abilities.
