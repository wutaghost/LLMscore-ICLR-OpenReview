011                                               A BSTRACT
012
013            In this paper, we introduce Epi-Attention, a novel context-aware attention mech-
014            anism designed to enhance the relevance of features in neural networks by incor-
015            porating external contextual information. Unlike traditional attention mechanisms
016            that rely solely on the input sequence, Epi-Attention dynamically adjusts the sig-
017            nificance of features based on additional evidence provided by external contexts.
018
               This approach allows the model to emphasize or diminish the relevance of specific
               features, leading to better capture and reflect the internal properties of specific
019
               classes. This mechanism provides a nuanced interpretation of feature relevance
020
               that aligns with domain knowledge, enabling the model to focus on contextually
021            significant features in a way that resonates with expert understanding. We formal-
022            ize the problem and present two variants of the proposed mechanism: Scaled Dot-
023            Product Epi-Attention and Self-Epi-Attention, both of which re-evaluate feature
024            importance considering either external or internal information, respectively. By
025            leveraging the dynamic aspect of Epi-Attention, models can highlight local cor-
026            relations that are characteristic of certain classes, offering a more transparent and
027            interpretable decision-making process compared to global correlations favorized
028            by classical approaches such as Decision trees, Logistic regression and Neural
029
               Networks. We demonstrate the efficency of Epi-Attention through three different
               applications (dynamic feature relevance, processing mixed datatypes and multi-
030
               source datasets) with respectively benchmark datasets, including the Wisconsin
031
               Breast Cancer, Bank Marketing and ABIDE-II datasets. Our results show signif-
032            icant improvements in model interpretability over traditional models that aligns
033            with domain knowledge. Furthermore, we discuss the potential of Epi-Attention
034            for enhancing explainability in complex machine learning tasks, paving the way
035            for more robust and transparent neural network architectures.

039
040
      Attention mechanisms Vaswani et al. (2017) have become a core component in deep learning, signif-
041   icantly enhancing the ability of models to focus on the most relevant aspects of input data Hassanin
042   et al. (2024); Xiao et al. (2024); Bo et al. (2024). However, conventional attention mechanisms,
043   including those applied in models like transformers Vaswani et al. (2017), often assign static im-
044   portance weights across the entire dataset. This global approach can be misaligned with real-world
045   scenarios involving heterogeneous data, where local correlations, class-specific properties, and ex-
046   ternal context are critical. As a result, traditional attention methods may fail to capture the nuanced
047   patterns needed for tasks involving mixed data types, class imbalance, or varying subpopulations.
048   In Addition, explainability is crucial in deploying machine learning models in domains such as
049   healthcare and finance, where decision-making must be transparent and aligned with expert knowl-
050   edge. Traditional models, including logistic regression, MLPs, decision trees, and random forests,
051   often suffer from several challenges:
052
053         1. Static Feature Importance: These models apply static importance weights to features
               across all data points, failing to capture class-specific insights.

055            relationships within specific classes or subpopulations—that reflect the internal properties
056            of each class. Traditional models struggle to capture these finer details, missing valuable
057            insights that could improve interpretability.
058
            3. Bias in Imbalanced Datasets: Importance weights often align with global correlations,
059            reflecting the properties of the most represented class. This global focus can obscure crit-
060            ical features in underrepresented classes, leading to biased models and misalignment with

      which focuses on internal consistency within a given input, further refining feature relevance without

      relying on external context.

083   2   R ELATED WORK
084
085   Attention mechanisms have become a crucial component in various deep learning models for tasks
086   such as machine comprehension, action recognition, emotion recognition, and natural language pro-
087   cessing. Traditionally, attention mechanisms have been used to focus on specific parts of the input
088
      data and summarize it with fixed-size vectors Britz et al. (2017). However, recent advancements
      have introduced more sophisticated attention mechanisms that take into account contextual infor-
089
      mation to improve performance. For instance, in Seo et al. (2017), a Bi-Directional Attention Flow
090
      (BIDAF) network has been introduced. It utilizes a multi-stage hierarchical process to represent
091   context at different levels of granularity and incorporates a bi-directional attention flow mecha-
092   nism to obtain a query-aware context representation without premature summarization. Similarly,
093   Liu et al. (2017) and Liu et al. (2018) proposed Global Context-Aware Attention LSTM networks
094   for 3D action recognition and skeleton-based action recognition, respectively. These networks se-
095   lectively focus on informative joints in action sequences with the assistance of global contextual
096   information, improving attention representation iteratively Pang et al. (2023); Liu et al. (2021). In
097   the realm of natural language processing, Xing et al. (2017) introduced a Topic Aware Sequence-to-
098   Sequence (TA-Seq2Seq) model that utilizes joint attention mechanisms to summarize hidden vectors
099
      of input messages and synthesize topic vectors from topic words obtained from a pre-trained LDA
      model Zhao et al. (2011). Furthermore, Yu et al. (2022) highlighted the limitations of traditional
100
      Bi-Attention mechanisms in explicitly modeling interactions between contexts, queries, and keys of
101
      target sequences. To address this, they proposed a Tri-Attention framework that incorporates context
102   as the third dimension in calculating relevance scores, enhancing attention performance in natural
103   language processing tasks Vaswani et al. (2017). Moreover, attention mechanisms have also been
104   applied in emotion recognition tasks. Lee et al. (2019) introduced Context-Aware Emotion Recogni-
105   tion Networks (CAER-Net) that leverage both human facial expressions and contextual information
106   in a joint manner for improved emotion recognition. Additionally, Zhong et al. (2019) proposed
107   a Knowledge-Enriched Transformer (KET) for emotion detection in textual conversations, where
      contextual utterances are interpreted using hierarchical self-attention and external commonsense


                                                        2
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


108
      knowledge is dynamically leveraged using a context-aware affective graph attention mechanism. In
109   summary, attention mechanisms have evolved from simple uni-directional approaches to more so-
110   phisticated context-aware mechanisms that consider global context, topics, and external knowledge
111   to enhance performance across various domains such as machine comprehension, action recognition,
112   emotion recognition, and natural language processing. The introduction of Tri-Attention frame-
113   works represents a significant advancement in explicitly modeling interactions between contexts,
114   queries, and keys, leading to improved attention performance in complex tasks.

299
300   In classical attention mechanisms Vaswani et al. (2017), self-attention has demonstrated its efficacy
301
      across various tasks, such as reading comprehension and learning task-independent sentence rep-
      resentations. In our framework, the concept of Epi-Attention is particularly valuable for assessing
302
      the consistency of the input sequence x. Self Epi-Attention regulates the information conveyed by
303
      the input sequence x itself, enhancing the most relevant features while minimizing inconsistencies.
304   Unlike classical self-attention, which evaluates each element in isolation, Self Epi-Attention handles
305   conflicts by considering the impact of all elements x − [xj ] (all elements but xj ) on the relevance of
306   xj within the sequence x.

311          • Ei : ci is internal information = xi (evidence)

      Thus, self Epi-Attention aims to preserve the internal consistency of an observation xi .

521   7   C ONCLUSION AND F UTURE WORK
522
523   In this work, we proposed Epi-Attention, a novel attention mechanism that dynamically adjusts fea-
524   ture relevance by leveraging both internal feature correlations and external contextual information.
525   The key innovation of Epi-Attention lies in its ability to tailor feature weighting not just globally
526   across a dataset but dynamically at the instance or group level, where contextual relevance becomes
527   paramount. Unlike traditional models, which often apply static importance weights, Epi-Attention
528   enables a more nuanced, context-aware feature relevance evaluation that adapts to local correlations
529   and external signals.
530   Also, we showed how this concept of context-driven feature relevance can be applied to a variety of
531   challenging real-world scenarios. The core contribution of Epi-Attention lies in its ability to offer
532   dynamic feature relevance evaluation, where the importance of features is not fixed but evolves ac-
533   cording to the specific characteristics of the data and the context in which it is situated. This makes
534   Epi-Attention especially powerful in scenarios where feature importance varies across subpopula-
535   tions, conditions, or instances.
536   Looking forward, future work could expand on the use of Epi-Attention in other domains and data
537   modalities, further exploring its potential for enhancing model transparency and context-driven
538   decision-making. The promising results shown in this paper point to a broader application of
539   context-aware attention mechanisms in machine learning, especially in fields where explainability
      is crucial.
