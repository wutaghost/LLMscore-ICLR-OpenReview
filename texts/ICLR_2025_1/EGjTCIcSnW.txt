009                                                          A BSTRACT
010
011            Although vision-language models (VLMs) have achieved significant success in various applications
012            such as visual question answering, their resilience to prompt distractions remains as an under-
013            explored area. Understanding how distractions affect VLMs is crucial for improving their real-world
014            applicability, as inputs could be filled with noisy and irrelevant information in many practical scenarios.
015            This paper aims to assess the robustness of VLMs against both visual and textual distractions in
016
               the context of science question answering. Built on the ScienceQA dataset, we developed a new
               benchmark that introduces distractions in both the visual and textual contexts. To evaluate the
017
               reasoning capacity of VLMs amidst these distractions, we analyzed the performance of ten state-of-
018
               the-art models, including GPT-4o. Our findings reveal that most VLMs are vulnerable to various
019            types of distractions, experiencing noticeable degradation in reasoning capabilities when confronted
020            with distractions. Notably, models such as InternVL2 demonstrates a higher degree of robustness to
021            these distractions. We also found that models exhibit greater sensitivity to textual distractions than
022            visual ones. Additionally, we explored various mitigation strategies, such as prompt engineering, to
023            counteract the impact of distractions. While these strategies improved model resilience, our analysis
024            shows that there remain significant opportunities for further improvement.

      1    I NTRODUCTION
028
      Despite the impressive capabilities of vision-language models (VLMs) in understanding image context and generating
029
      human-like text (Liu et al., 2023c; Dai et al., 2023; Hu et al., 2023), their susceptibility to irrelevant information
030   remains a critical challenge. In real-world applications, it is common for vision and text inputs to be noisy and filled
031   with distractions. Such distractions can lead to significant performance degradation, potentially resulting in incorrect
032   interpretations or responses with hallucination from VLMs (Zhou et al., 2024; Chen et al., 2024b).
033
      Existing benchmarks for VLMs are typically designed under the assumption that inputs in both visual and textual
034
      domains are carefully curated without distractions. This assumption, however, fails to reflect real-world scenarios.
035
      Previous research (Shi et al., 2023) has demonstrated that large language models (LLMs) are vulnerable to textual
036   distractions. With the rapid development of VLMs, it is crucial to understand how these models handle distractions not
037   only in the textual domain but also in the visual domain. Compared to LLMs, VLMs face the additional challenge of
038   potential distractions from bi-modal inputs, making the situation even more complex.
039
      Moreover, current evaluation benchmarks (Lu et al., 2022a; Singh et al., 2019; Lu et al., 2024) do not adequately
040
      account for the presence of distractions within the input data. They often emphasize clean and well-structured datasets,
041
      which do not mirror the complexities and noise inherent in real-world data streams. This oversight limits our ability to
042   assess the true robustness and reliability of VLMs when deployed in practical settings where distractions are inevitable.
043   Consequently, there is a pressing need for specialized benchmarks that systematically introduce and evaluate various
044   types of distractions to better understand and improve VLM performance under realistic conditions.
045
      To address this gap, we present I-ScienceQA, a comprehensive benchmark designed to investigate the robustness of
046
      VLMs towards distractions. Our benchmark, built upon the ScienceQA dataset (Lu et al., 2022a), incorporates various
047
      types of distractions to simulate more realistic, noisy scenarios. Specifically, we aim to answer the following questions:
048
049       • How vulnerable are VLMs to distractions across different modalities?
050       • Which modality, visual or textual, causes greater degradation in model performance when distracted?
051       • What techniques can mitigate the impact of distractions and improve the performance of VLMs?
052
053   To build I-ScienceQA, we leveraged different generative models, including GPT-3.5-turbo (OpenAI, 2024) and Stable
      diffusion models (Rombach et al., 2021). Our benchmark comprises 8,100 samples with four scenarios of distractions

      in both visual and textual domains. Specifically, we utilized stable diffusion models to generate visual distractions,
055   such as neutral backgrounds, generic landscapes, abstract art, and everyday objects. For textual distractions, we
056   employed GPT-3.5-turbo to produce textual distractions such as contradictory information, irrelevant details. This
057   approach allowed us to simulate a wide range of real-world scenarios where VLMs might encounter noise or irrelevant
058   information. More information about the defintions of distractions can be found in section 7.
059
      Through extensive evaluation of the various state-of-the-art VLMs, our key findings include:
060
061       • VLMs exhibit varying degrees of vulnerability to distractions, with performance degradation observed across
062         different models and scenarios (see Section 4).
063       • Textual distractions tend to have a more significant impact on VLMs compared to visual distractions, particularly
064         in the “Add Hints” scenario (see Section 4).
065       • Larger models generally demonstrate better robustness against distractions, with some models like Internvl2 (8B)
066         showing minimal performance drops in certain scenarios (see Section 5.1).
067       • Prompt engineering techniques or robust encoders offer limited enhancement to VLM performance against
068         distractions, with their effectiveness varying across different models and tasks (see Section 5.3).
069       • The impact of bi-modal distractions (both visual and textual) on VLMs is nuanced, with some models showing
070         consistent performance while others exhibiting minor fluctuations (see Section 5.4).
071
072   Our research not only provides valuable insights into the current limitations of VLMs but also highlights potential areas
073   for improvement in model design and training methodologies. By addressing these challenges, we can develop more
074   robust and reliable VLMs for real-world applications.

      2.1    OVERVIEW OF I-ScienceQA
078
079   In order to create a comprehensive benchmark for assessing the robustness of VLMs, it is essential to introduce minor
080   distractions while ensuring that the hints for solving the questions remain accessible in either the textual or visual context.
081   Developing I-ScienceQA presented several challenges. Firstly, ensuring the diversity and relevance of distractions across
082   both visual and textual modalities required meticulous selection and generation strategies. Additionally, maintaining
083   the semantic integrity of the original questions while injecting distractions demanded advanced techniques in data
084   augmentation and validation. To overcome these challenges, we leveraged state-of-the-art generative models, such as
085
      GPT-3.5-turbo (OpenAI, 2024) for textual distractions and Stable diffusion models (Rombach et al., 2021) for visual
      distractions, ensuring that the introduced noise was both diverse and contextually appropriate. These efforts resulted
086
      in a robust and versatile benchmark that not only fills the gaps left by existing datasets but also provides a nuanced
087
      framework for evaluating and enhancing the resilience of VLMs in practical applications. In this paper, we introduce
088   the I-ScienceQA benchmark, consisting of 8,100 samples distributed across four distraction scenarios.
089
090   Data Collection Figure 1 illustrates the models we utilized to construct the dataset. In our study, we employed LLMs
091
      to introduce textual distractions and stable diffusion models to generate visual distractions. As depicted in Figure 1,
      we took use of GPT-3.5-turbo to generate short textual contexts or insert brief distractions into existing text. For the
092
      visual domain, we employed stable diffusion models to create various image distractions. We also applied masks to the
093
      main objects in existing images and added distractions to other areas to ensure that the models could still extract useful
094   information to answer the questions. Figure 1 shows the detailed process for data generation.
095
096   Dataset Statistics Built upon the ScienceQA dataset, our dataset is crafted as a comprehensive and diversified benchmark
097
      for evaluating the robustness of VLMs against distractions. In Table 1, we present samples from the dataset for some of
      the distraction types. Table 7 shows the dataset statistics. Specifically, the I-ScienceQA dataset contains 8,100 samples,
098
      which include 4,000 text-based distractions and 4,100 image-based distractions. This dataset encompasses 4 scenarios
099
      of distractions. The data are collected from four types of sources including stable diffusion(Rombach et al., 2021),
100   GPT-3.5, Unsplash API(Unsplash, 2024), and PromeAI(ProMeAI, 2024). It offers a broad spectrum of distractions.
101   We believe that I-ScienceQA can serve as a comprehensive benchmark for evaluating the robustness of VLMs. In the
102   following sections, we will describe how we established the I-ScienceQA benchmark.

      2.3    D ESIGN P RINCIPLE
217
218   The design of our multimodal benchmark for distractions is grounded in the principle of creating realistic and challenging
219   scenarios that accurately reflect the complexities of real-world environments where VLMs are deployed. The goal is to
220   assess the robustness and adaptability of these models by introducing a variety of distractions they might encounter in
221   practical applications. Here are the core principles guiding the benchmark’s design:
222
          • Realism and Relevance: Every element of the benchmark—from the selection of images and texts to the types of
223
            introduced distractions—is designed to closely mimic real-life conditions.
224
          • Comprehensive Challenge: The benchmark is desinged to challenge the models across multiple dimensions. This
225
            includes their ability to process and interpret visual and textual information, filter out irrelevant data, and maintain
226
            focus on the task at hand. Distractions are varied to comprehensively test the models’ capabilities.
227
          • Generative Model-Based Generation: Generative models have demonstrated their ability to generate enriched
228         samples in both the textual and visual domains. Inspired by recent study (Shu et al., 2023), we propose to leverage
229         generative models for multimodal data collection, based on the existing image-question pairs from ScienceQA.

      Table 3 presents a comprehensive evaluation of various models across different scenarios of distractions, measured under
294   both original and distraction settings. For each scenario, the exact match degradation due to distractions is quantified in
295   parentheses, providing insight into the robustness of each model against distractions. The results exhibit differing
296   degrees of degradation in model performance when exposed to distractions, highlighting the variability in the
297   models’ abilities to focus on relevant data. It is important to note that the Phi3v and InstructBLIP models, which
298   can only process inputs containing both textual and visual components, were evaluated exclusively on the Add Image
299   and Insert Image scenarios. In this discussion, we analyze the models’ performances in each scenario of distraction,
300   focusing on both the exact match score and the exact match degradation score.
301   Firstly, in the Add Image scenario, models are evaluated on their ability to handle additional visual distraction. Notably,
302   the Internvl2 (8B) model achieves the highest performance in the distraction scenario with a score of 94.45, exhibiting a
303   minimal decrease of −1.00 from its original score of 95.45. Similarly, GPT-4o maintains high performance with an exact
304   match score of 93.00 and a slight reduction of −0.50. In contrast, smaller models like Llava (7B) and Internvl2 (1B)
305   show more significant drops in performance, with exact match scores of 68.05 (−3.25) and 79.70 (−5.90), respectively.
306   These results suggest that larger models tend to be more robust against visual distractions in this context.
307   In the Insert Image scenario, where visual distraction are embedded into existing visual input, the performance trends
308   are consistent. The Internvl2 (8B) model again demonstrates robustness with a exact match scores of 94.23 and a
309   decrease of −2.67 from its original score of 96.90. Interestingly, the Qwen2-VL-Instruct (2B) and Qwen2-VL-Instruct
310   (7B) models show minimal performance degradation, with exact match scores of 63.26 (−0.54) and 68.08 (−0.32),
311   respectively. Despite smaller reduction in exact match score, these models have worse performance than other models.
312   When examining the Add Hint scenario, which involves injected textual distraction, the impact of distractions becomes
313   more pronounced. Most models experience larger decreases in performance. The Internvl2 (2B) model, for instance,
314   has an exact match score of 82.35, reflecting a significant drop of −9.05 from its original score of 91.40. Even the
315   higher-performing Internvl2 (8B) and Internvl2 (26B) models face reductions to exact match scores of 93.60 (−1.20)
316   and 92.80 (−2.40), respectively. These findings highlight that adding textual distractions poses a greater challenge
317   to the models compared to visual distractions, possibly due to the complexity of processing textual information.
318   Lastly, in the Insert Hint scenario, where textual distractions are interspersed within existing text, models generally
319   show moderate performance degradation. The Internvl2 (8B) model maintains a high exact match score of 95.90,
320   with a decrease of −1.70 from its original score of 97.60. Similarly, GPT-4o achieves a exact match scores of 84.05,
321   reflecting a decrease of −1.95. However, models like Qwen2-VL-Instruct (2B) exhibit a larger drop to a exact match
322   scores of 64.20 (−8.25), indicating vulnerability to inserted textual distractions. These results suggest that while some
323   models are adept at managing inserted hints, others may struggle, potentially due to differences in their attention
      mechanisms or the diversity of their training data.

480   6     R ELATED W ORK
481
482   Model Evaluations and Data Contamination. Visual Language Models (VLMs) have traditionally been evaluated
483   using standard Visual Question Answering (VQA) tasks such as TextVQA (Singh et al., 2019), VQAv2 (Antol
484   et al., 2015), and GQA (Hudson & Manning, 2019), which focus on foundational VQA questions. More recently,
485   studies like MM-Vet (Yu et al., 2023b), POPE (Li et al., 2023a), and MM-Bench (Liu et al., 2023d) have emerged to
      specifically evaluate VLMs, addressing key challenges like hallucination, reasoning, and robustness. These efforts


                                                            9
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


486
      have demonstrated that multimodal LLMs encounter significant issues, such as hallucination (Guan et al., 2023) and
487   insufficient robustness (Fu et al., 2023). In this papr, we introduce the I-ScienceQA benchmark, which highlights that
488   even advanced VLMs, such as GPT-4o (OpenAI, 2023), struggle with basic visual questions when irrelevant distractions
489   are present in the input.
490
      Data contamination has also become a major focus in recent work (Lovin, 2023; Bender et al., 2021; Kocoń et al., 2023;
491
      Li, 2023). Several researchers (Golchin & Surdeanu, 2023; Oren et al., 2023; Yang et al., 2023; Oren et al., 2023) have
492
      developed techniques to detect and mitigate data contamination. Furthermore, dynamic evaluation techniques have
493   been proposed (Zhu et al., 2023; Lei et al., 2023; Fan et al., 2023), leveraging various algorithms to reduce the adverse
494   effects of data contamination on model performance.
495
496
      Benchmarks with Input Perturbations. The use of input perturbations has been a common strategy in natural language
      tasks, with approaches ranging from model-agnostic input transformations (Liang et al., 2022; Ravichander et al.,
497
      2022) to adversarial example generation targeting specific models (Jia & Liang, 2017; Shi et al., 2018; Morris et al.,
498
      2020; Wang et al., 2021). Notably, prior research has constructed arithmetic reasoning benchmarks by paraphrasing or
499   rewriting sentences from clean datasets (Patel et al., 2021; Kumar et al., 2021; Shi et al., 2023).
500
501   Robustness of VLM Recent studies have increasingly concentrated on the adversarial vulnerabilities of VLMs Qi et al.
502
      (2024); Carlini et al. (2024); Schlarmann & Hein (2023); Zhao et al. (2023b); Dong et al. (2023). Schlarmann & Hein
      (2023) demonstrate that imperceptible perturbations in input images can enable attackers to manipulate LVLMs into
503
      generating specific outputs. Additionally, visual adversarial attacks designed to jailbreak LVLMs are introduced in
504
      works such as Carlini et al. (2024) and Qi et al. (2024). More recently, numerous studies have focused on training
505   adversary robust vision encoder for VLMs Schlarmann et al. (2024); Mao et al. (2023).

508   7    L IMITATIONS AND C ONCLUSION
509
510   In this paper, we introduced I-ScienceQA, a comprehensive benchmark designed to assess the robustness of Vision-
511   Language Models against distractions in both visual and textual domains. By augmenting the ScienceQA dataset with
512
      diverse forms of distractions, we simulated real-world conditions where input data is often imperfect, noisy. Our
      extensive evaluation across state-of-the-art VLMs revealed several key findings: (1) Most VLMs remain vulnerable to
513
      distractions, especially in the textual domain; (2) Larger models tend to be more robust but do not always guarantee
514
      improved performance, particularly when faced with complex bi-modal distractions; (3) Prompt engineering and robust
515   vision encoder could only partially mitigate these vulnerabilities, there remains significant room for improvement in
516   handling both textual and visual distractions.
517
518
      Our findings highlight the need for further research in developing more robust VLM models. As the use of VLMs
      expands across domains such as healthcare, education, and autonomous systems, it becomes increasingly important to
519
      build models that can handle the noisy data often encountered in real-world applications.
520
521   While our work contributes valuable insights into the challenges of distraction robustness, it also has certain limitations:
522
523       • Limited scope of distractions: Although we introduced a variety of textual and visual distractions, the dataset
524
            does not encompass all possible real-world noise. Future work could explore additional forms of noise, such as
            adversarial examples, corrupted images to further challenge the models.
525
526
          • Model evaluation focus: Our study primarily focused on pre-trained VLMs. We did not explore the effects of
            fine-tuning models on noisy datasets that may be more resilient to distractions. Fine-tuning on noisy or augmented
527
            data could provide valuable insights into improving model robustness.
528
          • Bimodal distractions: While we examined the compounded effects of bimodal distractions, we did not extensively
529
            explore how interaction effects between the two modalities influence model performance. Future research
530
            should analyze more closely how different types of visual and textual distractions interact and whether certain
531         combinations are more detrimental than others.
532
          • Defense techniques: Although we explored the use of prompt engineering and robust vision encoder as a defense
533         mechanism, our study did not delve into other possible methods to enhance model robustness, such as vision
534         segmentationLai et al. (2023). Exploring these techniques could offer more comprehensive solutions for improving
535         VLM performance in noisy environments.
536
537   In summary, while the I-ScienceQA benchmark provides a valuable tool for evaluating VLM robustness, there is much
538   work to be done in advancing models that can consistently navigate noisy, real-world data. Future research should focus
539   on expanding the range of distractions, investigating fine-tuning techniques, and exploring other defense strategies to
      create more resilient VLMs.
