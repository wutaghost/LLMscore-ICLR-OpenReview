011                                               A BSTRACT
012
013            Early identification of neurodegenerative diseases is crucial for effective diagno-
014            sis in neurological disorders. However, the quasi-periodic nature of vocal tract
015            sampling often results in inadequate spectral resolution in traditional spectral fea-
016
               tures, such as Mel Frequency Cepstral Coefficients (MFCC), thereby limiting their
               classification effectiveness. In this study, we propose the use of Constant Q Cep-
017
               stral Coefficients (CQCC), which leverage geometrically spaced frequency bins
018
               to provide superior spectrotemporal resolution, particularly for capturing the fun-
019            damental frequency and its harmonics in speech signals associated with neurode-
020            generative disorders. Our results demonstrate that CQCC, when integrated with
021            Random Forest and Support Vector Machine classifiers, significantly outperform
022            MFCC, achieving absolute improvements of 5.6 % and 7.7 %, respectively. Fur-
023            thermore, CQCC show enhanced performance over traditional acoustic measures,
024            such as Jitter, Shimmer, and Teager Energy. The effectiveness of CQCC is un-
025            derpinned by the form-invariance property of the Constant Q Transform (CQT),
026            which ensures consistent feature representation across varying pitch and tonal con-
027
               ditions, thereby enhancing classification robustness. Furthermore, the robustness
               of CQCC features against MFCC features are validated using LDA plots. These
028
               findings are validated using the Italian Parkinson’s database and the Minsk2019
029
               database of Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis, underscoring the potential of CQCC
030            to advance the classification of neurodegenerative disorders.

      Neurodegenerative disorders have become a significant and escalating health concern as popula-
036   tions age globally. These disorders are marked by the gradual loss of neuronal function, leading to
037   debilitating cognitive and motor impairments. Despite the availability of advanced medical tech-
038   nologies, diagnosing and managing these diseases remain significant challenges. The complexities
039   of these conditions, coupled with the limitations of current therapies, emphasize the urgent need for
040   innovative approaches to both diagnosis and treatment.
041
      Neurodegeneration stands out as the central pathological process in the majority of brain-related
042   conditions (Jeong et al., 2024). Conditions like Parkinson’s Disease (PD) and Amyotrophic Lateral
043   Sclerosis (ALS) continue to be major clinical challenges, especially within the elderly demographic.
044   (Garofalo et al., 2020). The World Health Organization’s report on Neurological Disorders: Public
045   Health Challenges indicates that nearly one billion people worldwide are affected. (Bosco et al.,
046   2011). The formidable blood-brain barrier (BBB) continues to pose a major challenge in the ef-
047   fective management of neurodegenerative disorders (NDs). The WHO has noted that, despite the
048   availability of highly effective and affordable treatments, up to 9 out of 10 individuals with NDs in
049   developing countries remain untreated. Enhancing health systems is essential to provide better care
050
      for those with neurological disorders. Despite ongoing efforts in modern science to develop medical
      or surgical interventions, the results have been largely disappointing. This underscores the critical
051
      need for further research in this field.
052
053   Language deficits are frequently observed in numerous neurodegenerative conditions, often emerg-
      ing early as a prominent symptom. Therefore, identifying and characterizing language impairments

205   For simplicity, we examine the continuous-time forms of the Fourier Transform (FT), Short-Time
206   Fourier Transform (STFT), and Constant-Q Transform (CQT). If y(t) and Y (ξ) are a Fourier trans-
207

      (Gambardella, 1968), (Quatieri, 2015):
208

212   indicating that scaling the time domain by a factor of β corresponds to scaling the frequency domain
213   by the inverse factor β1 . This shows that the structure of the energy spectral density (ESD) remains
214   unchanged, which is why this property is referred to as "form-invariance." However, this property
215   does not extend to the conventional STFT, where the analysis window function depends solely on
      the time variable.

443   6   C ONCLUSIONS
444
445   This study comprehensively assessed various characteristics to distinguish between healthy and
446   pathological speech using SVM and RF classifiers. The findings underscore that CQCCs emerged
447   as the most effective feature, achieving the highest accuracy in classification tasks. Particularly no-
448   table was RF’s performance, significantly outperforming SVM, which highlights RF’s capability in
449   leveraging intricate time-frequency representations inherent in CQCC. It also demonstrated substan-
450   tial efficacy, surpassing traditional measures such as Jitter, Shimmer, and Teager Energy in accuracy.
451   This underscores the relevance of the quality features of the cepstral to accurately identify pathologi-
452
      cal speech conditions. Comparison with MFCC further validated the superiority of CQCC, showing
      considerable improvements in both the RF and SVM classifiers. Furthermore, the evaluation in
453
      new databases (D1 and D3) reaffirmed the robustness of CQCC in handling complex pathological
454
      classifications, providing further validation of their utility in clinical applications. Future research
455   directions should focus on validating these findings in diverse pathological datasets and exploring
456   advanced machine learning techniques to further improve classification precision.
