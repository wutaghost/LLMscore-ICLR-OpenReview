009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011
               Prompt tuning has become a crucial technique for adapting pre-trained vision-
012            language models (VLMs) to various downstream tasks. Recent advancements
013            introduce multi-modal learnable prompts to enhance the creation of task-specific
014            classifiers. Despite their utility, these methods commonly encounter challenges
015            in generalizing to unseen classes, as their symmetrically designed visual prompt
016            struggles to capture task-relevant textual knowledge and lacks the flexibility in
017            adjusting to novel test class distributions. To tackle these obstacles, we propose a
018            novel Text-Guided Visual Prompt Tuning (TGVP) method, which uniquely lever-
019            ages the robust generalizability of textual knowledge to guide the generation of
020
               visual prompt. Our method introduces a simple yet effective Text-Knowledge
               Guidance Module that dynamically incorporates visual prompt with task-relevant
021
               textual knowledge through cross-attention mechanism. The generated text-guided
022
               visual prompt endows the visual encoder with semantic awareness and thus en-
023            hances both generalization and discriminability of VLMs across various scenarios.
024            Comprehensive experiments demonstrate that TGVP significantly outperforms ex-
025            isting methods in base-to-novel generalization, cross-dataset transfer, and domain
026            generalization tasks, offering a substantial improvement in VLM adaptation.

      1   I NTRODUCTION
030
031
032   Foundational vision-language models (VLMs), such as CLIP (Radford et al., 2021b) and BLIP (Li
033
      et al., 2022a), pre-trained on large-scale image-text pairs, have demonstrated remarkable general-
      ization abilities across diverse downstream vision tasks. However, training models from scratch
034
      generally requires large labeled datasets, which limits their applicability to downstream tasks with
035
      fewer samples. To overcome this, parameter-efficient adaptation techniques such as prompt tun-
036   ing (Zhou et al., 2022c), adapters (He et al., 2021), and LoRA (Hu et al., 2021) have been introduced.
037   Among these, prompt tuning has become a prominent approach for maximizing the potential of
038   VLMs, balancing parameter efficiency while effectively preserving pre-trained knowledge.
039
      CoOp (Zhou et al., 2022c) was the pioneer in introducing prompt tuning by concatenating learnable
040
      contextual tokens to class names, demonstrating its effectiveness. However, these learnable prompts
041
      face over-fitting problem, particularly with limited training data, leading to degraded generalization
042   on unseen classes. To mitigate the issue, subsequent methods have incorporated various regularization
043   techniques when updating these prompts. For instance, CoCoOp (Zhou et al., 2022b) conditions
044   on image features to enhance the learnable textual prompts with instance-level visual information.
045   Follow-up methods such as KgCoOp (Yao et al., 2023b), ProGrad (Zhu et al., 2023a), and ProReg (Zhu
046   et al., 2023b) impose stronger constraints on learnable prompts from vanilla CLIP features to ensure
047   that they effectively encapsulate essential general knowledge.
048   Alternative methods, such as PromptSRC (Khattak et al., 2023c), address the over-fitting problem by
049   enhancing cross-modal alignment in the shared vision-language space. Instead of solely updating
050   textual prompts, they take advantage of the multi-modal encoding capabilities of VLMs by learning
051   both textual and visual prompts. While these two-branch designs yield improved results, both types
052   of prompts are independently optimized to over-fit the base classes and cannot effectively handle
053   novel classes. Additionally, the lack of cross-modal interactions constrains the sufficient multi-modal
      alignment.

066   A natural question arises: which modality of prompt tuning enhances the model’s generalization
067   ability? To gain a deeper understanding of prompt learning across different modalities, we set
068   up a comparative experiment in which only one modality of prompts (either visual or text) is
069
      set as learnable and trained on base classes. Figure 1 illustrates that textual prompts result in
      less performance degradation on novel classes compared to visual prompts, exhibiting stronger
070
      generalization capability. This motivates us to incorporate textual knowledge into visual branch for
071
      enhanced the generalization capability when adapting VLMs. The previous method, MaPLee (Khattak
072   et al., 2023a), also notices this problem and establishes a mapping from textual prompts to visual
073   prompts for better alignment of two modalities. However, it has certain limitations. First, the source
074   of textual information is confined to fixed text prompts, which are uniform across both seen and
075   unseen scenarios, thereby hindering effective adaptation to unseen classes.Moreover, the simple
076   symmetrically projection mechanism is insufficient for information interaction between visual and
077   textual modalities, as textual features naturally contain semantic information while visual features
078   carry local patch information from the current image.
079   Therefore, to mitigate these limitations, we propose a novel Text-Guided Visual Prompt Tuning
080   (TGVP) approach to leverage the generalization capability of textual knowledge to guide the genera-
081   tion of visual prompts, focusing on both the knowledge source and the method of knowledge transfer.
082   We emphasize that high-level textual semantics are key to facilitating the learning of general-
083   izable visual prompts. Instead of using textual prompts to enhance their visual counterparts, we
084   utilize the text embeddings, which encode high-level semantic information, as the knowledge source
085   to guide the optimization of visual prompts. To better harness this knowledge, we propose a novel
086
      Text-Knowledge Guidance Module to dynamically select and fuse the task-relevant textual knowledge
      into the visual encoder, enabling it to be semantically aware of both seen and unseen classes. Specifi-
087
      cally, both visual prompt tokens and the CLS token cooperatively serve as queries to dynamically
088
      select the most relevant textual guidance through cross-attention mechanism. We conducted extensive
089   experiments to evaluate the proposed TGVP approach on base-to-novel generalization, cross-dataset
090   transfer, few-shot classification, and domain generalization tasks. Experimental results demonstrate
091   the significantly superior performance of TGVP compared to existing state-of-the-art methods. The
092   main contributions can be summarized as follows:
093
094          • We point out that text embeddings can be leveraged as knowledge source at the cross-
095            modal interaction rather than text prompts, thereby enhancing both discriminability and
096
               generalizability of the visual representation in VLMs.
097          • We propose a novel Text-Guided Visual Prompt Tuning mechanism, which dynamically
098            transfers textual knowledge to guide the generation of visual prompt, making it semantically
099
               aware for both seen and unseen classes.
100          • Extensive and comprehensive experiments have validated the consistent effectiveness and
101            the superior performance by significant margins.

      et al., 2021; Li et al., 2022a) have emerged as a powerful paradigm, effectively leveraging visual

      and textual modalities trained on large-scale image-text datasets. Current research underscores
109   that these models, pre-trained on extensive image-text pairs sourced from the internet, possess the

117   2.2   P ROMPT T UNING
118
119
      Prompt tuning (Gan et al., 2022; Ouali et al., 2023; Lee et al., 2023; Zang et al., 2022; Radford et al.,
      2021a) has emerged as a prominent approach for adapting pre-trained VLMs to downstream tasks by
120
      leveraging learnable tokens to encapsulate task-specific knowledge. In models like CLIP, handcrafted
121
      templates such as "a photo of a [CLASS]" are employed to encode textual embeddings for zero-shot
122   predictions. However, these handcrafted prompts often fall short of capturing the subtle nuances
123   required for downstream tasks. To overcome this limitation, textual prompt tuning techniques have
124   been developed to enhance textual embeddings by inferring a set of learnable tokens combined with
125   class tokens.
126
      For example, CoOp (Zhou et al., 2022c) replaces static handcrafted prompts with dynamic, learn-
127
      able soft prompts. To further improve the generalization capability of these learnable prompts,
128   CoCoOp (Zhou et al., 2022b) introduces image-conditional prompts that integrate image features
129   with learnable tokens. Additionally, approaches like KgCoOp (Yao et al., 2023b), ProGrad (Zhu et al.,
130   2023a) impose constraints on learnable prompts to ensure they encapsulate essential, generalized
131   knowledge. Beyond textual prompt tuning, recent advancements such as MaPLe (Khattak et al.,
132   2023a) and PromptSRC (Khattak et al., 2023c) propose joint optimization of both visual and textual
133   prompts. CLIP-Adapter (Gao et al., 2021) integrates an adapter mechanism to fine-tune both visual
134   and textual embeddings, further enhancing model adaptability.
135   However, previous unimodal prompt tuning methods often struggle with generalization to unseen
136   classes due to inadequate modeling of test class distributions, while existing multi-modal prompt
137   tuning strategies are also hampered by limited cross-modality information exchange, restricting
138   performance improvements. To tackle these obstacles, we propose Text-Guided Visual Prompt
139   Tuning (TGVP), which transfers general textual knowledge into the vision encoder via a Text-
140   Knowledge Guidance (TKG) Module. Utilizing a streamlined cross-attention mechanism, visual
141   prompts, alongside the CLS token, dynamically select relevant textual guidance. This process enables
142
      the creation of a semantic-aware vision classifier that effectively adapts to diverse downstream tasks.

209

210   tend to design visual prompts symmetrically to textual prompts, treating them as direct counterparts
211   or projections. However, this approach limits the extent to which visual prompts can internalize
212   textual knowledge, thus preventing them from serving as optimal context for target classes at a natural
213   semantic level. This restricted interaction between modalities further hinders model performance
214   enhancement. To address these challenges, we introduce TGVP, which uniquely harnesses the robust
215   generalizability of textual knowledge to guide the generation of visual prompt. As illustrated in
      Figure 2, TGVP employs a Text-Knowledge Guidance (TKG) Module that first transfers general

      4   E XPERIMENTS
286
      In this section, we present quantitative results of our method and comprehensive comparisons with
287
      other state-of-the-art methods to demonstrate the effectiveness of our proposed TGVP. Similar to
288
      previous works, we evaluate the proposed TGVP across four challenging task settings:
289
290          • Base-to-Novel Generalization. We evaluate the generalization ability of our approach in a
291            zero-shot context by dividing the datasets into base and novel classes. The model is trained
292            with a few examples from the base classes and then tested on unseen novel classes to assess
293            its performance.
294          • Cross-Dataset Transfer. To examine the transferability of our method, we conduct a
295            direct evaluation of our ImageNet-trained model across a diverse array of external datasets.
296            Adhering to established protocols, the model is trained on all 1,000 ImageNet classes under
297            a few-shot paradigm.
298          • Domain Generalization. We further test the robustness of our method by evaluating it on
299            out-of-distribution (OOD) datasets. Specifically, the model trained on ImageNet is assessed
300            on four different ImageNet variants, each representing a different type of domain shift.
301          • Few-shot Classification. This scenario allows us to compare the model’s learning capacity
302            under very limited supervision. It also helps determine whether our approach effectively
303            learns both task-specific and generalizable knowledge.
304
305   Datasets. For base-to-novel generalization, cross-dataset transfer tasks, we follow previous
306   work (Radford et al., 2021b; Zhou et al., 2022c;b) to conduct the experiments on 11 represen-
307   tative image classification datasets, including ImageNet (Deng et al., 2009) and Caltech101 (Fei-Fei
308   et al., 2004) for generic object classification; OxfordPets (Parkhi et al., 2012), StanfordCars (Krause
309   et al., 2013), Flowers102 (Nilsback & Zisserman, 2008), Food101 (Bossard et al., 2014), and FGV-
310   CAircraft (Maji et al., 2013) for fine-grained classification; SUN397 (Xiao et al., 2010) for scene
311
      recognition; UCF101 (Soomro et al., 2012) for action recognition; DTD (Cimpoi et al., 2014) for
      texture classification; and EuroSAT (Helber et al., 2019) for satellite image recognition. For do-
312
      main generalization, we utilize ImageNet as the source dataset and four ImageNet variants as target
313
      datasets including ImageNet-A (Hendrycks et al., 2021b), ImageNet-R (Hendrycks et al., 2021a),

483
484   In this work, we present a novel approach called Text-Guided Visual Prompt Tuning (TGVP) for
485   vision-language models. By leveraging the robust generalizability of textual knowledge, our method
      guides the generation of visual prompts through a unique Text-Knowledge Guidance Module. This


                                                        9
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


486
      module dynamically integrates task-relevant textual knowledge into visual prompts using a cross-
487   attention mechanism. The resulting text-guided visual prompts enhance the visual encoder’s semantic
488   awareness, thereby improving both generalization and discriminability across various scenarios.
489   Extensive experiments demonstrate that TGVP not only significantly outperforms existing methods
490   in base-to-novel generalization, cross-dataset transfer, and domain generalization tasks but also offers
491   a substantial improvement in the adaptation of vision-language models.

      A.1   M ORE I MPLEMENTATION D ETAILS
705
706   In addition to the implementation details mentioned in the main text, our code is based on Prompt-
707   SRC’s code, adhering to its deep prompt configuration, where deep text/visual prompts are set in
708   the first 9 layer with our proposed Text-knowledge Guidance Module. Additionally, during the
709   deployment of the TKG module, the textual guidance is applied to the visual prompt tokens in the
710   first nine layers and the CLS token in the ninth layer. Finally, during training, we employed the
