                                                  A BSTRACT
008
009
010
               Recent works have shown a surprising result: a small fraction of Large Language
               Model (LLM) parameter outliers are disproportionately important to the quality
011
               of the model. LLMs contain billions of parameters, so these small fractions, such
012
               as 0.01%, translate to hundreds of thousands of parameters. In this work, we
013            present an even more surprising finding: Pruning as few as a single parame-
014            ter can destroy an LLM’s ability to generate text – increasing perplexity by 3
015            orders of magnitude and reducing zero-shot accuracy to guessing. We propose
016            a data-free method for identifying such parameters, termed super weights, using
017            a single forward pass through the model. We additionally find that these super
018            weights induce correspondingly rare and large activation outliers, termed super
019            activations. When preserved with high precision, super activations can improve
020            simple round-to-nearest quantization to become competitive with state-of-the-art
021
               methods. For weight quantization, we similarly find that by preserving the su-
               per weight and clipping other weight outliers, round-to-nearest quantization can
022
               scale to much larger block sizes than previously considered. To facilitate further
023
               research into super weights, we provide an index of super weight coordinates for
024            common, openly available LLMs.

027   1   I NTRODUCTION
028
029
      Large Language Models (LLMs) have been growing in size and capability at an unprecedented
030   rate, enabling them to capture increasingly complex linguistic patterns across a wide range of tasks.
031   However, with this increase in model scale, new and unexpected behaviors have emerged. Dettmers
032   et al. (2022) discovered that once LLMs reach a certain scale, a small set of hidden state features
033   contains outliers of exceptionally large magnitude. These outliers account for a small percentage of
034   all activations but are crucial for preserving the compressed model’s quality (Dettmers et al., 2022;
035   Xiao et al., 2023; Wei et al., 2023; Shao et al., 2024).
036   However, not all outliers are equally important. In this paper, we study a tiny yet important set of
037   outliers in LLMs, termed super weights. In Llama-7B, pruning the super weight, a single scalar,
038   completely destroys the model’s ability to generate text; the average accuracy of zero-shot down-
039   stream tasks effectively plummets to zero. Conversely, pruning the other top 7,000 outliers, includ-
040   ing outliers that are larger than the super weight, affects no more than a few percentage points.
041
      Intriguingly, super weights behave similarly across model families and sizes. For one, the super
042   weight is always found in the mlp.down proj weight, always in an early layer. We also find
043   that the super weight amplifies input activation inliers to ultimately produce the exceptionally large
044   magnitude activation observed by Sun et al. (2024) – we term this the super activation. This super
045   activation persists throughout the model at exactly the same magnitude and position regardless of the
046   prompt, and we find this is uniquely enabled by skip connections. Finally, super weights suppress
047   stopword likelihood. Taken together, pruning the super weight destroys quality by dampening the
048   super activation and shifting almost all logit probability mass to stopwords.
049   Both super weights and super activations, which we collectively refer to as super outliers, are critical
050   to model quality. Fortunately, there are no more than a handful of scalar super outliers per tensor;
051   in light of this, we revisit round-to-nearest quantization, equipped only with the ability to hold
052   out and restore super outliers. This yields a data-free, hardware-friendly method. For activation
053   quantization, we find this technique competitive with SmoothQuant; for weight quantization, we
      can scale round-to-nearest to much larger block sizes with higher quality.

081   2.1   O UTLIERS IN LLM S
082
      LLM outliers are widely observed in existing literature. Kovaleva et al. (2021) notes weight out-
083   liers, which emerge gradually, beginning early in pre-training, and cause abnormal spikes at select
084   dimensions in the output embedding vectors. Disabling those outliers significantly degrades both
085   the training loss and the downstream task performance. Bondarenko et al. (2021) notes activation
086   outliers, which encourage specific attention patterns, such as attending to the special separator to-
087   ken. However, Sun et al. (2024) first observes an exceptionally extraordinary outlier; in particular,
088   they discover massive activations in LLMs that persist across layers in a fixed position, which Yang
089   et al. (2024) hypothesizes is caused by gated linear units (GLU) and its variants, such as GEGLU
090   and SwiGLU. To mitigate these massive activations, Sun et al. (2024) proposes a learnable atten-
091
      tion bias, and (Son et al., 2024; Yang et al., 2024) inserts certain prefixes. To complement these
      mitigation studies, our focus is instead to leverage, rather than mitigate, these super activations.
092

      2.2   O UTLIER - AWARE QUANTIZATION METHODS
095
      Quantization is one of the most popular techniques for reducing LLM resource consumption. How-
096   ever, quantizing LLMs is non-trivial, due to outliers that increase the range of values. Existing
097   works typically study two settings for LLM quantization: (1) Weight-only quantization, where only
098   weights are quantized into low-bit integers; (2) Weight-activation quantization, where both activa-
099   tion and weights are quantized.
100
      For weight-only quantization, several common solutions including using smaller block sizes, to
101
      limit the number of values any single outlier can impact (Dettmers et al., 2024; Shao et al., 2024;
102   Dettmers & Zettlemoyer, 2023; Frantar et al., 2022; Dettmers et al., 2023); scaling sensitive weights,
103   via a grid-searched channel-wise scaling, Lin et al. (2024); or clipping outliers via learned optimal
104   thresholds (Shao et al., 2024; Lin et al., 2024). The most common approach is to extract and store
105   sensitive weight outliers in higher-precision (Dettmers et al., 2024; Kim et al., 2024; Dettmers et al.,
106   2022). However, decomposed, mixed-precision arithmetic for hundreds of thousands of weights
107   is unfriendly for hardware and incurs significant latency penalties. We take a different approach,
      handling at most a half dozen scalars to maintain hardware friendliness.

123   For activation quantization, there are an increased number of even more aggressive outlier values,
124   making activation quantization more challenging. To tackle this, previous work rotates (Liu et al.,
125   2024; Ashkboos et al., 2024; Chee et al., 2023), clips (Wei et al., 2022) or shifts (Wei et al., 2023;

130   data-free, with a small change to a naive round-to-nearest method.
131   Recent studies have discovered that activation outliers are associated with weight outliers. The hid-
132   den dimensions where activation outliers emerge have a high correlation to sensitive weight channels
133   (Heo et al., 2024; Lee et al., 2024). Along these lines, activation magnitudes have been used as an in-
134   dicator to find salient weight channels to preserve in weight quantization (Lin et al., 2024). We find
135   the relationship between activations and weights is even more striking: Rather than channel-wise
136   pairs, we find relationships between two individual scalars – up to six weights and one activation.

      3     S UPER W EIGHTS
139
140
      Many studies corroborate the importance of weight outliers, showing that a small percentage of the
141
      largest magnitude outliers are essential to model quality. This percentage can be as small as 0.01%,
142
      but for these billion-parameter models, 0.01% can still include hundreds of thousands of weights.
143   Our investigation reveals a surprising fact about even this group of weight outliers: There exists a
144   single, scalar weight that, despite not being the largest, holds more importance than thousands of
145   other outlier weights combined. We call this single scalar weight a super weight.
146
      In our analysis, we find that the super weight is necessary for quality, having an outsized influence on
147
      quality if removed. Without the super weight, LLMs fail to generate text, resulting in qualitatively
148
      (Figure 1) and quantitatively (Table 1) gibberish responses. In particular, zero-shot dataset accuracy
149   is reduced to guessing, and perplexity increases by several orders of magnitude (Prune SW). To
150   quantify this influence, we prune all other outlier weights (Prune Non-SW), comparing the impact
151   of a single super weight against 7,000 other outliers. Remarkably, the accuracy drop associated with
152   pruning this single weight is much greater than the effect of all other outliers combined.
153
154
      3.1   I DENTIFICATION OF S UPER W EIGHTS
155
156   Super weights create super activations. Sun et al. (2024) first discover a handful of exceptionally
157   massive activations, which are crucial to model quality. Massive activations persist across many
158   layers, feature constant magnitude, and always exist at the same position, regardless of input. We
159   find a further intriguing property: The activation’s channel aligns with our super weight’s, and
160   the activation first appears right after our super weight. To confirm whether this is correlation or
161   causation, we prune the super weight and check the massive activation’s magnitude. Per Figure 4,
      we discover that pruning the super weight drastically reduces the massive activation’s magnitude.

      With further investigation, we reveal the mechanism of super weights and super activations. Sun
192   et al. (2024) explained super activations as bias terms, but they did not explain how super activations
193   are created and why they are always in the same positions. Through empirical analysis, we find that
194   before down projection, the Hadamard product of the gate and up projection creates a relatively
195   large activation, which aligns with the findings of Yang et al. (2024). More importantly, the super
196   weights further amplify it and create super activations.

201   rather than a set of validation data or use-case examples.

233     3.2                                      M ECHANISMS OF SUPER WEIGHTS
234
235     We find that super weights (1) induce super activations, which have lasting effects throughout the
236     entire model, and (2) suppress stopword likelihood (Figure 2).
237
238
239     Super weights (partially) operate via super activations. To assess whether the super weight’s
240     impact on model quality is solely mediated by the super activations or also by activations of other
241
        tokens, we conducted an experiment involving the removal of super weights (SW) and restoration
        of super activations (SA). Note that a super weight should influence the same channel for all tokens.
242
243     We conduct an ablation experiment with three conditions: (1) the original model, (2) remove the
244     super weight (Prune SW), i.e., setting the weight scalar as zero, (3) remove the super weight and
245     restore the super activation at the layer where it first appears (Prune SW,+SA). The third condition
246     allows us to isolate the impact of super weights on super activations only.
247     Results are shown in Table 1. Specifically, when we restore the super activations, the average ac-
248     curacy recovers to 49.94 from 35.14, indicating that the restoration of super activations salvaged
249     approximately 42% of the quality loss. These findings suggest that while the super activations con-
250     tribute substantially to the model’s performance, they do not fully account for the super weight’s
251     overall influence on quality.
252
253
254
        Super weights affect output token probability distributions. We studied the impact of super
        weights with respect to the output token probability distribution, averaged over 500 prompts from
255
        Lambaba validation set. We find that when super weights are removed, the stopword probabilities
256
        are amplified, e.g., with Llama-7B, the probability of “the” is amplified by around 2×, “.” by 5×,
257     and “,” by 10× (Figure 5, Appendix Figure 11).
258
259     To dive deeper on how SW impact the output token distribution, we conduct a case study with a
260
        prompt “Summer is hot. Winter is ”. The correct next token should be “cold”, which is a word
        that has strong semantic meaning. With the original model with SW, it correctly predicts the next
261
        token “cold” with a high probability 81.4%. However, when the SW is removed, the model’s top
262
        prediction is a stopword “the” with a non-confident low probability of 9.0%. This indicates that the
263     SW is essential for the model to make a correct and confident prediction of meaningful words.
264

267     weights impact the model’s quality, especially, how does increasing the magnitude affect model
268     quallity. We multiply the super weights by a scaling factor ranging from 0.0 to 3.0. Results in
269     Figure 6 show that amplifying super weights can improve model accuracy, to some extent. See full

303     Quantization is a powerful technique for compressing models and reducing memory requirements.
304     However, the presence of outliers can significantly degrade quantization quality, for both weight
305     quantization and activation quantization. As we mentioned before, we refer to these problematic
306     outliers, both super weights and super activations, as super outliers. As we have shown above, these
307     super outliers carry disproportionate importance for model quality, making their preservation during
308     quantization critical. Quantization generally maps continuous values to a finite set of values; we
309     consider one of the simplest forms – namely, asymmetric round-to-nearest quantization:

397
398   We compare our method with SmoothQuant in Table 3. For three Llama models on both datasets,
399   we achieve over 70% of SmoothQuant’s improvement over naive quantization. On C4 with Llama-
400   7B and on Wikitext with Llama-30B, we attain above 80% of SmoothQuant’s improvement. Our
401   method demonstrates that a significantly simplified approach to quantization can achieve competitive
402
      results compared to more complex methods. Unlike SmoothQuant, which applies scales to every
      activation channel, our method focuses solely on addressing one critical activation outlier.
403
404   We extended our evaluation to include additional LLMs: OLMo (1B and 7B), Mistral-7B, and
405   Llama-2-7B. Results are shown in Table 4 and Appendix Table 7. These models represent a di-
406   verse set of architectures and training paradigms, allowing us to assess the generalizability of our
407   quantization method. Since SmoothQuant does not report on this set of models, we compare our
408
      results with naive W8A8 quantization. Across all models and datasets, our method consistently out-
      performs naive W8A8 quantization. Our method demonstrates remarkable performance on OLMo
409
      models.
410
411   Notably, OLMo models use non-parametric LayerNorm, making them incompatible with the
412   SmoothQuant method, which relies on LayerNorm weights to apply the per-channel scales. On
413   Mistral-7B, the improvements are smaller. We hypothesize that this is because the LayerNorm of
414
      these models may have learned weights that aggressively suppress the super activation, resulting in
      a more uniform distribution of activation magnitudes.
415
416   These results underscore the critical importance of the super activation in maintaining model per-
417   formance during quantization. By addressing this single activation with minimal computational
418   overhead, our method captures a significant portion of the benefits achieved by more complex quan-
419   tization schemes. This finding suggests that the super activation plays a disproportionately large role
420
      in preserving model quality during the quantization process.

458   To evaluate the effectiveness of the proposed super weight-aware quantization method, we compare
459   it with the traditional round-to-near quantization approach. We assess the models on a suite of
460   zero-shot downstream tasks, with results illustrated in Figure 7.
461
      In the traditional round-to-near quantization, we observe a clear trend: as the block size increases,
462
      model quality significantly degrades. This decline likely results from the increased quantization er-
463
      ror introduced when larger blocks of weights are quantized together, which allows outliers to impact
464   more weights. In contrast, our super weight-aware quantization method demonstrates much greater
465   robustness to larger block sizes. As the block size increases, the degradation in model quality is
466   noticeably smaller compared to the round-to-near method. This robustness stems from our method’s
467   ability to preserve the most critical weight (the super weight) while minimizing the influence of out-
468   lier weights on the overall quantization process. By clipping outliers and focusing on inlier weights,
469   our method maintains higher fidelity in representing the model’s parameters.
470   A key advantage of our method is its ability to support larger block sizes with less loss in model
471   quality. This capability leads to a lower average bitrate and smaller file sizes, which are essential for
472   deploying models in resource-constrained environments, such as mobile devices or edge computing
473   scenarios.

477
478
479   Our study of Large Language Models has revealed the critical role of super outliers – specifically,
480   the super weight and its induced super activation. Although these super outliers are small in number,
481   identifying and preserving them is essential for model quality; pruning the super weight completely
482
      destroys the model’s ability to generate text, and retaining the super weight can significantly improve
      the quantized model’s quality.
483
484   Our findings shed light on how these outliers influence model behavior and provide practical strate-
485   gies for their detection and management. By sharing a directory of super weights, we furthermore
      hope to inspire further research into their properties and implications.

        A.5                S UPER W EIGHTS AND ATTENTION S INKS
745
746     Given that super activations are typically observed on the first token of an input sequence, we hy-
747     pothesized a potential relationship between super weights and the well-documented phenomenon of
748     attention sinks (Xiao et al., 2024; Son et al., 2024). To test this hypothesis, we conducted experi-
749     ments comparing attention weight patterns in the presence and absence of super weights. Contrary
750     to our initial expectations, we find that attention sinks persist even when super weights are removed
751     from the model, while not preserving model quality.
