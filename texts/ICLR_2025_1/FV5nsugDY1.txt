009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            Visual object tracking is a research hotspot in the field of computer vision, and
012            has been widely applied in video surveillance, human-computer interaction, un-
013            manned driving and other fields. At present, the object trackers based on Trans-
014            former have good performance, but they still face the challenge of confusing target
015            and background in the feature extraction process. To address this issue, we pro-
016            pose a Hybrid Contrastive Transformer Tracker (HCTrack) in this paper, which
017            combines contrastive learning to improve the ability of distinguishing the target
018
               and the background in video. Furthermore, a hybrid feature interaction module
               is presented to realize multi-level information exchange between the features of
019
               template and search regions and capture the target-related semantic information of
020
               the search frames comprehensively. Additionally, we design a redundant informa-
021            tion pruning module to adaptively eliminate the redundant backgrounds according
022            to the global scene information, thereby reducing the interference of the back-
023            ground to the target feature. HCTrack achieves superior tracking accuracy on the
024            GOT-10k and TrackingNet datasets compared to other state-of-the-art trackers,
025            while maintaining fast inference speed, as the contrastive learning strategy is only
026            adopted during training model.

029   1    I NTRODUCTION
030
031   Object tracking aims to locate the target in each frame of a continuous video sequence while pro-
032   viding scale and position information. It plays a pivotal role in understanding and analyzing various
033
      moving objects of videos, and has been widely applied in video surveillance, human-computer inter-
      action, unmanned driving, and so on. However, tracking object in the real-world scenarios presents
034
      numerous challenges such as lighting variations, scale adjustments, motion blur, and interference
035
      from similar objects within complex scenes.
036
037   In recent years, there were a great many deep learning-based trackers, which could be broadly cat-
038   egorized into two groups: trackers based on Siamese network and trackers based on Transformer.
039
      SiamFC(Bertinetto et al., 2016) firstly integrated Siamese network into object tracking by utilizing
      a convolutional neural network (CNN) to extract the feature of the search frame and the template.
040
      SiamRPN++(Li et al., 2019a) and SiamDW(Zhang & Peng, 2019) enhanced the feature representa-
041
      tion capability of model to improve the tracking performance. Subsequently, anchor-based(Fu et al.,
042   2021a; Li et al., 2018; Zhu et al., 2018) trackers and anchor-free(Han et al., 2021; Xu et al., 2020;
043   Zhang et al., 2020) trackers employed different target scale regression strategies to enhance adapt-
044   ability to the scale change of target. In addition, some template update strategies(Fu et al., 2021b;
045   Yang et al., 2023; Wang et al., 2023) were introduced to accommodate variations in the appearance
046   of the target during tracking. However, due to the limited local receptive field of CNN, it is difficult
047   to obtain global information of samples, so that the Siamese-based trackers are unable to accurately
048   locate the targets in the complex scenarios such as occlusion, motion blur, and background interfer-
049   ence.
050   In contrast, as the core module of Transformer, attention mechanism can effectively capture the
051   global contextual information of video. Currently, the existing Transformer-based object tracking
052   methods could be divided into two-stage and single-stage Transformer-based trackers. The two-
053   stage Transformer-based trackers(Chen et al., 2021; Yan et al., 2021) still struggle to accurately
      extract the target- specific feature during the feature extraction, which could lead to a loss of some

054
      effective detail information. The single-stage Transformer-based trackers(Chen et al., 2022; Song
055   et al., 2023; Wu et al., 2023; Zhao et al., 2024) utilized a Transformer-based backbone to interact
056   with the template feature and the features of search frames, and directly extract the target-related
057   information of search frames in the process of extracting feature. However, the existing Transformer-
058   based trackers tended to confuse the target with the background due to their premature interaction,
059   which would result the misclassification.
060
      Contrastive learning is a learning method by comparing the similarity between different samples,
061   which can enhance the discriminability of model by distinguishing between the positive and negative
062   samples. Similar to contrastive learning, object tracking requires maximizing the similarity among
063   the same objects in different frames of the same video sequence and the difference among differ-
064   ent objects. Therefore, we propose a Transformer-based tracker incorporating contrastive learning,
065   called as a hybrid contrastive Transformer tracker (HCTrack), to guide the model to accurately track
066   the objects in this paper. Firstly, we develop a contrastive learning strategy, which constructs the pos-
067   itive and negative sample pairs and utilizes their semantic label information to effectively improve
068   the similarity among same-class targets and the dissimilarity among different-class targets. Further-
069
      more, considering that most existing tracking methods using synchronous or asynchronous feature
      extractors only could extract limited target information, we present a hybrid feature interaction style
070
      involving a semantic self-association module and a cross-layer semantic association module (CSA).
071
      CSA associates the template features across multiple layers during the deep feature extraction of
072   search frame to accurately capture the target-related semantic information. Additionally, in order to
073   reduce the focus of the model on the background region, we devise a redundant information prun-
074   ing (RIP) module, which adaptively prunes the redundant background region features based on the
075   scene complexity. Additionally, in order to reduce the focus of the model on the background region,
076   we devise a redundant information pruning (RIP) module, which adaptively prunes the redundant
077   background region features based on the scene complexity. The experimental result of HCTrack
078   on GOT-10k and TrackingNet datasets demonstrates that HCTrack has competitive performance in
079   comparison to other state-of-the-art object tracking methods. The main contributions of this paper
080
      are as follows:
081   (1) In order to mitigate the confusion between the target and the background, we introduce the
082   contrastive learning into the transformer-based tracker, and propose a hybrid contrastive transformer
083   tracker (HCTrack). Our designed contrastive learning strategy can enhance the ability of model
084   to discriminate the targets and backgrounds in the process of objects tracking by using the label
085   information of the constructed positive and negative samples.
086   (2) A semantic self-association module and a cross-layer semantic association module are presented,
087   which make full use of multi-level template features and guide model to accurately learn the target-
088   related feature.
089
      (3) A redundant information pruning module is established for pruning the redundant background
090   information, thereby reducing on the impact of a large number of complex backgrounds on the target
091   during tracking .

096   2.1   S IAMESE - BASED T RACKERS
097
098   Siamese network has received a lot of attention in the field of target tracking because of their strong
099   scalability and dual advantages in speed and accuracy. After SiamFC (Bertinetto et al., 2016) intro-
100   ducing siamese network into the object tracking is proposed, numerous improved tracking methods
101   have been derived from it. DSiame(Guo et al., 2017) handled changes of the target over time through
102   online update, and inspired a series of approaches(Zhang et al., 2019; Li et al., 2019b; Zheng et al.,
103   2023; Zhao et al., 2024) that utilized spatio-temporal relationship. SiamMDM(Yang et al., 2023)
104
      developed a dynamic template update module and a score-based model for predicting target motion
      trajectory. (Wang et al., 2023) proposed a dynamic template updating strategy based on spatio-
105
      temporal information and designed a tracking confidence network to decide whether to update.
106
107   Later, inspired by the object detection methods, SiamRPN(Li et al., 2018) introduced region pro-
      posal network(Girshick, 2015) into object tracking, improving the adaptability of model to scale


                                                         2
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


108
      variations. On the basis of SiamRPN, Ren et al. proposed a classical structure Faster-RCNN(Ren
109   et al., 2016) and Cai & Vasconcelos gave a cascading structure Cascade R-CNN(Cai & Vasconcelos,
110   2018). SiamRPN++(Li et al., 2019a) and SiamDW(Zhang & Peng, 2019) applied deep backbone
111   networks in the object tracking framework to improve the model’s ability to represent features more
112   effectively. Meanwhile, some methods introduced anchor-free networks to adapt to the changing
113   shape of the target. SiamFC++(Xu et al., 2020) removed anchor priors and utilized a quality as-
114   sessment branch to refine the target localization results of the classification branch. Although the
115   Siamese network-based object tracking methods perform well, it is difficult to obtain global informa-
116   tion from video sequences due to the limited receptive field of CNN. Consequently, these methods
117
      face the challenges in accurately locating targets with significant appearance changes in complex
      scenarios such as occlusion, motion blur, and background interference.

120   2.2   T RANSFORMER - BASED T RACKERS
121
122   Inspired by the significant success of Transformer(Vaswani, 2017) architecture in various
123   tasks(Dosovitskiy, 2020; Zhang et al., 2024; Zong et al., 2023; Venkataramanan et al., 2023), schol-
124
      ars began applying it in the field of object tracking. At present, the Transformer-based object track-
      ing methods can be broadly categorized into two types: two-stage and single-stage Transformer-
125
      based tracking methods.
126
127   The two-stage Transformer-based object tracking methods mainly contains a feature extraction mod-
128   ule and a feature fusion module. TransT(Chen et al., 2021) extracted the features of template and
129   search frames in the feature extraction stage, and then interacted them by a Transformer structure
130
      in the feature interaction stage, thus capturing the target-related information in the search features.
      Yan et al. introduced a spatiotemporal Transformer network, named STRAK(Yan et al., 2021),
131
      which extended the traditional Transformer encoder-decoder framework to capture spatiotemporal
132
      features of video. Cao et al. proposed a Hierarchical Feature Transformer (HiFT)(Cao et al., 2021),
133   which integrated multi-level features to efficiently learn the global dependency relationship among
134   different levels of features. Videotrack(Xie et al., 2023) leverages the self-attention mechanism to
135   simultaneously process both the spatial and temporal dimensions of videos, effectively enhancing
136   its ability to capture features over long sequences. Afterwards, some two-stage Transformer-based
137   object tracking methods utilized Transformer structure not only in the feature fusion stage but also
138   as a backbone of feature extraction stage, such as SwinTrack(Lin et al., 2022). Since the feature
139   extraction and fusion process are separated, the feature of search area could not perceive the tar-
140   get information during the feature extraction, so the two-stage Transformer tracking models tend to
141
      overlook target-related detail information.
142   Subsequently, researchers developed single-stage Transformer-based object tracking approaches,
143   which integrated the feature extraction and fusion phases. Cui et al. proposed Mixformer(Cui et al.,
144   2022), which introduced a hybrid attention module to extract discriminative target-related feature
145   and facilitate information exchange between the template and search area. A unified framework
146   OSTrack(Ye et al., 2022) was proposed for the feature extraction and relationship modeling, which
147
      contained an candidate elimination module to discard irrelevant spatial features, thereby improving
      the inference speed of model. (Song et al., 2023) introduced a compact Transformer-based tracker
148
      (CTTrack), which employed an asymmetric hybrid attention mechanism to strengthen feature. Pro-
149
      ContEXT(Lan et al., 2023) synergistically utilized the spatial and temporal contextual information,
150   and (Wu et al., 2023) introduced a masked auto-encoder into the Transformer-based object track-
151   ing. LiteTrack(Wei et al., 2024) aimed to enhance tracking speed and made the Transformer-based
152   tracking model applicable to resource-constrained devices. (Zhang et al., 2023) leveraged semantic
153   information from language to compensate for the instability of visual information, and proposed a
154   language-based evaluation tracking method for selecting high-quality target samples. (Song et al.,
155   2023) added a lightweight masked encoder to the single-stage tracking framework, guiding the en-
156   coder to capture invariant features. (Gao et al., 2023) proposed a generalized relationship model
157   by adaptively selecting template and search area tokens for more flexible interactions. (Kang et al.,
158
      2023)designed a bridging module which integrated the high-level semantic information from deep
      features into the shallow high-resolution features to ensure high accuracy while maintaining fast
159
      operation on different devices.
160

      extraction capability, leading to potential confusion between the target and background during the

187   2.3   C ONTRASTIVE L EARNING
188
      Contrastive learning(Oord et al., 2018; Khosla et al., 2020; Seo et al., 2024) is to learn feature
189   representation of samples by comparing the similarities or differences among different samples.
190   Recently, contrastive learning has also been applied in the object tracking.(Wu et al., 2021) proposed
191   a progressive unsupervised object tracking learning framework, which distinguished between target
192   and background regions in video sequences through contrastive learning. (Pi et al., 2022) proposed
193   an instance-aware module to enhance the separability among instances and the compactness within
194   instances through using contrastive learning mechanisms. (Zeng et al., 2023) employed contrastive
195   learning to learn discriminative representation of targets in the drone object tracking. (Bhat et al.,
196   2019) applied contrastive learning to improve object-background discriminability in visual tracking,
197
      enabling the tracking model to learn a robust object-specific appearance model online.
198   However, the number of positive samples is relatively small and all of them are based on CNN in the
199   aforementioned methods. In this paper, we introduce contrastive learning into the Transformer-based
200   object tracking method, which leverages contrastive learning in a simple yet effective manner to
201   enhance the discriminability of model, thereby improving tracking accuracy without compromising
202   inference speed.

253   In HCTrack, we construct a hybrid feature extraction and interaction module to extract the features of
254   templates and search frames and realize their information interaction. Figure 2 shows the structure of
255   HFEI, which contains several semantic self-association (SSA) modules, several cross-layer semantic

257   multi-head self-attention, a skip connection and a multilayer perceptron.
258   Firstly, the three types of images pass through a block embedding layer respectively to attain tem-
259   plate feature token, dynamic template feature token, and search feature token, as shown in Figure 2.
260   Then, the template feature token and dynamic template feature token are concatenated and processed
261   by nine SSA modules for the template feature extraction, where the output of the sixth SSA module
262   is separated as the middle template feature token tm , and dynamic template feature token dm . The

267   token sm , the current template feature token tm , the current dynamic template feature token dm , the

339   3.5   C ONTRASTIVE L EARNING M ODULE
340
341   Contrastive learning mainly mines the similarity among samples, which brings the same sample
342   and its augmented data closer in the embedding space, and pushes the different samples farther
343
      apart, thereby enhancing the discriminability among samples. Therefore, we combine the contrastive
      learning with the Transformer-based object tracking to assist in training the model. Specifically, the
344
      contrastive learning strategy in this paper involves three key components: construction of positive
345
      and negative samples, contrastive head, and contrastive loss function.
346
347   Construction of positive and negative samples. In general, the positive samples are from the
348   same image through data augmentation, and negative samples come from different images in self-
349
      supervised contrastive learning method. However, applying this approach directly to object tracking
      may ignore the temporal characteristics of video, potentially resulting in samples from the same
350
      video being treated as negative samples. To address this issue, we adopts a different way that the
351
      template images, the dynamic template images, and the search frame images from the same video
352   sequence are treated as positive samples. These images contain the same target with richer vari-
353   ations, compared to the positive samples generated through data augmentation, which makes the
354   model more robust. Meanwhile, the negative samples are drawn from other video sequences, which
355   prevent the samples containing the same target from being misclassified as negative samples.
356
      Contrastive head. HCTrack employs three contrastive heads with shared weights, which process
357
      the search area feature token sm , the template feature token tm and the dynamic template feature
358   token dm respectively, as shown in Figure 1. The contrastive heads are composed of region of in-
359   terest pooling, a 3×3 convolution, a Rectified Linear Unit (ReLU), a 1×1 convolution, and batch
360   normalization. Additionally, the output template feature vector, dynamic template feature vector,
361   and search area feature vector are concatenated and reorganized. The feature vectors from the same
362   video are grouped together as positive samples, while feature vectors from different videos are classi-
363   fied as negative samples. The positive and negative samples are then used to compute the contrastive
364   learning loss function.
365   Contrastive loss function. In this paper, we give an improved contrastive learning loss function
366   based on Info Noise Contrastive Estimation (InfoNCE) (Oord et al., 2018). The InfoNCE loss
367   function is as follows:

      4.1   I MPLEMENT D ETAILS
413
      Model. HCTrack utilizes the first 9 encoder layers of ViT-Base(Dosovitskiy, 2020) as the backbone
414   network and is initialized by using MAE(He et al., 2022) pre-trained parameters. The search region
415   image size is 256×256, and after passing through the patch embedding layer, the token length in the
416   backbone network is 256 with a channel dimension of 768. The template image size is 128×128,
417   and after passing through the patch embedding layer, the token length in the backbone network is 64
418   with a channel dimension of 768. The redundancy pruning module retains 90% of the energy.
419
      Training. For the tests on GOT-10k and TrackingNet datasets, the training datasets of HCTrack
420   are different. To obtain the tracking result on GOT-10k test set GOT-10k dataset is only used for
421   training according to GOT-10k protocol. For TrackingNet test set, a combination of COCO2017,
422   TrackingNet, LaSOT, and GOT-10k datasets is used for training, which is consistent with most
423   methods. The preprocessing process of the training data is same as that of OSTrack(Ye et al., 2022),
424   including image cropping and data augmentation. The model is performed for 300 epochs, where
425   6 × 104 image pairs are processed in each epoch. Every image pair includes a static template image,
426   a dynamic template image, and a search image. In addition, ADAM optimizer with a weight decay
427   of 1 × 10−4 is used, and the learning rate is initially set to 2 × 10−4 and dropped to 2 × 10−5 at
428
      240 epochs.
429   Inference. At the beginning of testing, the first frame is set as both the initial static template and the
430   dynamic template. The dynamic template is updated with the image that has the highest confidence
431   score in an interval of 200 frames, where the confidence of each image is determined by the value
      output by the score head.

      5   C ONCLUSION
509
510
      In this paper, we present a Hybrid Contrastive Transformer Tracker (HCTrack) to address the chal-
511
      lenges of confusion of targets and backgrounds in the Transformer-based object tracking. By in-
512   tegrating contrastive learning with a Transformer-based tracking framework, HCTrack effectively
513   enhances the ability to differentiate between targets and backgrounds, thereby improving tracking
514   accuracy. Furthermore, a hybrid feature interaction module is constructed to realize comprehensive
515   information exchange between template and search regions. In addition, we design a redundant
516   information pruning module to reduce background interference by adaptively eliminating target-
517   irrelevant redundant features based on global scene information. Experimental results on the GOT-
518   10k and TrackingNet datasets demonstrate that HCTrack achieves competitive performance in terms
519   of tracking accuracy, while maintaining fast tracking speed.
