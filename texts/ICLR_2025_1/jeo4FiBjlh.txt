009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            Many discrete multi-view clustering methods based on anchor graphs use the an-
012            chor graph decomposition or spectral clustering to obtain the final clustering la-
013            bels, such methods achieve good results but lack interpretability. Morever, some
014            of them are poorly balanced. To this end, first, we start from the perspective of
015            label transmission to convert labels of the anchors to the labels of the samples,
016            which has better interpretability. Second, we find a new and remarkable use of
017            the nuclear norm, i.e., maximizing the nuclear norm can ensure the balanced clus-
018
               ters, which has the rigorous theoretical proof. Simultaneously, a novel optimisa-
               tion method based on the first order Taylor expansion is proposed for the nuclear
019
               norm. Finally, we introduce the tensor Schatten p-norm to fully exploit the spa-
020
               tial structural and complementary information between views, which can obtain
021            aligned label matrices. Extensive experiments have verified the superiority of the
022            proposed method compared with the state-of-the-art methods.

025   1   I NTRODUCTION
026
027   Clustering as an unsupervised machine learning method aims at classifying unlabelled data into sets
028   with specific meanings. This technique has been widely adopted across various fields due to its
029   efficiency. In the early stages, most research focused on single-view data. However, the cluster-
030   ing results were often suboptimal due to incomplete information about the objects. In recent years,
031   multi-view data, which allows obtaining object feature information from multiple perspectives, has
032   garnered significant attention. Multi-view data provides a more complete and comprehensive repre-
033
      sentation of information. For example, a video can be described through text, images, voice, etc.,
      and a news story can be translated into multiple languages such as Chinese, English, French, and
034
      more. As a result, multi-view clustering has developed rapidly and is now a prominent research area.
035
036   Numerous methods have been proposed for multi-view clustering, including subspace clustering,
037   spectral clustering, and non-negative matrix factorization clustering. Among them, graph-based
038   clustering methods have experienced significant development due to their excellent performance in
039
      capturing the spatial structure of samples, making them highly favored by researchers. The critical
      step in graph-based clustering is the construction of an affinity matrix (or similarity matrix) of size
040
      N ×N , where N represents the number of samples. This matrix is used to represent the relationships
041
      between different sample points. Since similarities must be calculated for each sample, the time
042   complexity of this process can reach O(N 2 ), and the graph factorization process also has a high time
043   complexity of O(N 3 ). This results in substantial memory usage and processing time, especially for
044   large datasets. To address this issue, some methods like Li et al. (2015) and Qiang et al. (2021)
045   utilize anchor graph-based clustering. The anchor graph typically has a size of N × m, where m is
046   the number of anchor points, and m << N , representing the relationship between anchor points and
047   sample points. This approach significantly reduces the computational complexity, making it feasible
048   to handle large-scale datasets efficiently.
049   The methods for processing multiple anchor graphs in multi-view clustering have become a hot
050   research topic. Some approaches construct a unified graph for multi-view data instead of separate
051   graphs for each view (Chen et al., 2020; Lin et al., 2021), while others construct individual graphs
052   for each view and use multi-view learning to fuse them into a unified graph (Nie et al., 2017; Qiang
053   et al., 2023; Zhan et al., 2018). Although these methods achieve promising clustering results, they
      rely on a unified ”global graph” for each view, assuming the same spatial structure across views. In

054
      reality, the hard labels and clustering results differ between views, and each view has a unique spatial
055   structure. These methods fail to fully explore the spatial structures and complementary information
056   across views.
057
058
      Additionally, we observe that some graph-based or anchor graph-based methods (Kang et al., 2021;
      Liang et al., 2019; Wang et al., 2021) cannot directly obtain the final clustering results, often re-
059
      quiring post-processing methods such as k-means. These post-processing methods not only reduce
060
      efficiency but also constrain the clustering performance, limiting the ability to achieve optimal re-
061   sults. Moreover, we found that many multi-view clustering methods based on anchor graphs use
062   the anchor graph decomposition or spectral clustering to obtain the final clustering labels, such as
063   Yang et al. (2024) and Qiang et al. (2021), such methods achieve good results but lack interpretabil-
064   ity. And, they are also unevenly balanced, making them less efficient for handling data with varied
065   distributions.
066
      To address these issues, this paper uses the label transmission strategy to design the clustering model
067   by mining the relationship between the labels of anchors and the labels of samples, which has bet-
068   ter interpretability. Besides, the introduce of the nuclear norm ensures that this model can ob-
069   tain discrete and balanced clustering results directly, eliminating the need for time-consuming and
070   performance-limiting post-processing methods. In addition, the tensor Schatten p-norm used in Gao
071   et al. (2020); Yang et al. (2022); Lei et al. (2024); Lu et al. (2023); Li et al. (2023) explores low-rank
072   representations between views, which can ensure that each graph retains the spatial structural infor-
073   mation of its corresponding view without distortion. Our contributions are summarized as follows:
074
075
             • We propose a label transmission strategy to establish the relationship between anchor points
               and sample points based on an anchor graph, so that the anchor labels can be transferred to
076
               the sample labels, which has more interpretable than previous methods.
077
078          • Different from the previous approach of minimizing the nuclear norm, we propose the
079            maximization of the nuclear norm to ensure the discretised and class-equilibrated in the
080            clustering process, which has the rigorous theoretical proof.
081          • For the optimization of the nuclear norm, we propose an innovative and highly efficient
082            optimization approach grounded in the first-order Taylor expansion.
083
             • We introduce the tensor Schatten p-norm to fully explore the spatial complementary in-
084            formation between views. This can ensure that labels of samples from different views are
085            more likely to align, thereby enhancing clustering performance.

090   2.1   G RAPH - BASED C LUSTERING
091
092   There are a number of methods that perform weighted embedding of multiple graphs to obtain
093
      fused graphs. Liang et al. (2019; 2022), by exploiting the consistency and inconsistency between
      individual views for multi-view graph learning to obtain a unified graph common to all views, the
094
      authors of these two methods believe that there are consistent as well as inconsistent parts between
095
      the views, and thus design an objective function so that the model can learn the consistency between
096   views to obtain a common graph of fused terms. Wang et al. (2019) recognises that the importance
097   of the views is not consistent, and thus chooses to fuse multiple graphs by weighting them thus
098   obtaining a consistent representation. Qiang et al. (2023) proposed a very concise graph-based
099   clustering method using an objective function with only one item in order to obtain a weighted
100   multi-view aggregated graph with good results.
101
      Even though these methods give good clustering results, multi-graph fusion is not always a good
102   choice for multi-view. Fusion of graphs involves treating the hard labels in each view as consistent,
103   but this is not reasonable. The clustering results of different views will often not be identical, and
104   methods that directly use the results of multi-graph fusion to obtain hard labels do not fully exploit
105   the complementary information between views and ignore the spatial structure information of each
106   view. At the same time, we also note that these methods use full-sample graphs, and the computa-
107   tional complexity of this construction process and decomposition process is O(N 2 ) or even O(N 3 ),
      especially on large-scale data this problem is particularly significant.

      2.2   A NCHOR G RAPH - BASED C LUSTERING
109
110   In order to reduce the computational complexity, methods such as Li et al. (2015) propose an anchor
111   graph-based clustering approach. The number of anchor points m used to construct the anchor graph
112   is much smaller than the number of samples N (m << N ), which can significantly reduce the
113   space and time complexity of the model. Similarly, in the anchor graph-based approach, Kang et al.
114   (2021) proposed a framework that can be applied to both single and multiple views. They similarly
115   recognise that different views may play different roles and choose to weight different views, but
116
      the key graph remains applicable across views using a uniform graph. Yang et al. (2024); Qiang
      et al. (2021); Shi et al. (2021), on the other hand, weights the anchor graphs for different views
117
      according to their importance and fuses the anchor graphs for each view to get a unified graph.
118
      These three discrete clustering methods do not fully explore the spatial structure information and
119   complementary information between views. In addition, they all use matrix decomposition to obtain
120   the final labelling matrix, which makes the interpretability worse. Also in addition to this, these
121   methods above they try to make the hard labels consistent for each view clustering, this requirement
122   is too high and different from the actual situation, resulting in clustering results are not good.

388          • SMSC (Hu et al., 2020): Combines non-negative embedding and spectral embedding. Non-
389            negative embedding is learned to obtain the final clustering results directly, avoiding per-
390            formance degradation due to post-processing.
391          • SwMC (Nie et al., 2017): A unified graph is obtained by weighted fusion of different views,
392            minimizing the difference between a common indication matrix and the similarity matrix.
393          • LMVSE (Kang et al., 2020): Fuses the graphs of all views to get a unified graph and uses
394            spectral clustering for the final result.
395
             • FPMVS-CAG (Wang et al., 2021): Unifies anchor learning with anchor graph construction,
396
               optimizing jointly with linear time complexity, followed by post-processing to obtain the
397            final clustering results.
398
             • CSMSC (Luo et al., 2018): The self-representation matrix is divided into consistent and
399
               exclusive parts, with the former reflecting low-rank results and the latter highlighting the
400
               unique variations of each view.
401
402
             • GMC (Wang et al., 2019): Fuses multiple graphs by weighting them to obtain a consistent
               representation.
403
404          • ETLMSC (Wu et al., 2019): Constructs a probability matrix from the tensor, with spectral
405            clustering providing the final result.
406          • MSC-BG (Yang et al., 2022): Constrains two-part graphs using tensor Schatten p-norms to
407            efficiently capture spatial structure and complementary information between views.
408          • Orth-NTF (Li et al., 2024): Applies orthogonal non-negative tensor factorization with ten-
409            sor Schatten p-norm constraints.
410
411   The experimental results of the nine comparison algorithms on six datasets are shown in Table 1.
412
      Our method consistently achieves promising results across all datasets, validating its effectiveness.
413
             • In summary, our proposed model outperforms the comparison algorithms in most cases.
414
415          • Additionally, we obtain clustering results directly without post-processing, outperform-
416
               ing algorithms such as SMSC, LMVSE, FPMVS-CAG, and ETLMSC that rely on post-
               processing for final results. This demonstrates better stability compared to methods requir-
417
               ing post-processing.
418
419          • Compared to methods like SwMC, LMVSE, and GMC, which fuse views into a unified
420
               common graph, our method preserves the anchor graph for each view, fully leveraging
               spatial structure and complementary information using tensor Schatten p-norms, resulting
421
               in improved performance.
422
423
             • Although MSC-BG also employs tensor Schatten p-norm constraints, our approach ac-
               counts for the uniqueness of different views by preserving each view’s individual graph.
424
425          • While Orth-NTF uses non-negative matrix factorization with tensor Schatten p-norm con-
426            straints, our approach leverages anchor graphs to explore the spatial structure relationship
427
               between anchor and sample points more effectively.

      5                                              C ONCLUSION
516
517
      In this paper, we propose a multi-view clustering method based on anchor graphs, which directly
      obtains the final clustering results. Specifically, the label transmission strategy is proposed to cap-
518
      ture the relationship between samples and anchors, which has better interpretability. Moreover,
519
      maximizing the nuclear norm can ensure the balanced clusters, followed by a novel optimization
520   method. In addition, by utilizing tensor Schatten p-norm, it fully exploits the spatial structural infor-
521   mation and the complementary information between views to enhance clustering performance. The
522   contribution of each view is reassessed, and individual views are weighted according to their signif-
523   icance. Experiments conducted on four standard datasets and two large-scale datasets demonstrate
524   the excellent performance of the proposed algorithm, affirming its superiority. Additionally, a series
525   of parametric and convergence analysis experiments are presented to illustrate the robustness and
526   effectiveness of the method.
