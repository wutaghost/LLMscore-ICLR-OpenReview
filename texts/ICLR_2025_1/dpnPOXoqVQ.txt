                                                  A BSTRACT
010
011
               Semi-supervised learning with manifold regularization is a classical family for
012
               learning from the labeled and unlabeled data jointly, where the key requirement
013            is the support of unknown marginal distribution enjoys the geometric structure of
014            a Riemannian manifold. Usually, the Laplace-Beltrami operator-based manifold
015            regularization can be approximated empirically by the Laplacian regularization
016            associated with the whole training data and its graph Laplacian matrix. However,
017            the graph Laplacian matrix depends heavily on the pre-specifying similarity metric
018            and may result in inappropriate penalties when facing redundant and noisy input
019            variables. In order to address the above issues, this paper proposes a new Semi-
020            Supervised Meta Additive Model (S2 MAM) under a bilevel optimization scheme
021
               to automatically identify the informative variables, update the similarity matrix,
               and achieve the interpretable prediction simultaneously. Theoretical guarantees
022
               are provided for S2 MAM including the computing convergence and the statistical
023
               generalization bound. Experimental assessments on synthetic and real-world
024            datasets validate the robustness and interpretability of the proposed approach.

      1    I NTRODUCTION
028
029
      Manifold regularization provides an elegant and effective framework to develop semi-supervised
030
      learning (SSL) models by utilizing a large amount of unlabeled data with limited labeled data jointly
031   (Belkin & Niyogi, 2004; Belkin et al., 2005; 2006; Geng et al., 2012; Van Engelen & Hoos, 2020). The
032   key assumption of manifold regularization is that the support of intrinsic marginal distribution has the
033   geometric structure of a Riemannian manifold (Belkin & Niyogi, 2004; Belkin et al., 2006; Johnson
034   & Zhang, 2007; 2008)). Usually, the Laplace-Beltrami operator-based manifold regularization can be
035   approximated empirically by the Laplacian regularization associated with the whole training data and
036   the corresponding similarity (adjacent) matrix (Belkin & Niyogi, 2004; Belkin et al., 2006; Roweis
037   & Saul, 2000), where the similarity matrix is constructed by the principles of Gaussian fields and
038   harmonic functions (Zhu et al., 2003b) or the local and global consistency (Zhou et al., 2003). Typical
039
      manifold regularization schemes include Laplacian regularized least squares (LapRLS) and Laplacian
      regularized support vector machine (LapSVM) (Belkin et al., 2006). Moreover, Nie et al. considered
040
      a flexible manifold embedding for semi-supervised dimension reduction (Nie et al., 2010), and Qiu et
041
      al. further developed an accelerated version (called fast flexible manifold embedding (f-FME)) by
042   reconstructing a smaller adjacency matrix with low-rank and sparse constraints (Qiu et al., 2018).
043
044   Despite rapid progress, it is still scarce to validate the intrinsic manifold assumption (Belkin &
045
      Niyogi, 2004; Belkin et al., 2006; Johnson & Zhang, 2007; 2008) for different types of data, e.g.,
      data with redundant or even noisy variables. Moreover, the investigation for the robustness and
046
      interpretability of manifold regularization is far below its empirical applications only concerning
047
      the prediction accuracy. The existing manifold regularization models require that the similarity
048   matrices are pre-specified before the semi-supervised training procedures, where the adaptivity and
049   robustness of manifold learning are unexplored. For real applications, there unavoidably involve some
050   abundant irrelevant and even noisy variables, and the pre-specified similarity metric associated with
051   the whole variables can not reflect the true adjacent relations properly. The uninformative and noisy
052   variables often result in a large deviation in estimating manifold structure, and then seriously degrade
053   the prediction capability of manifold regularization methods. As illustrated in Figure 1, the clean
      unlabeled data are beneficial to better fit the decision curve, while the randomly added noisy variables

073   resulting in the degraded performance, is the computation bias of the similarity matrix through the
074   whole input variables directly (Nie et al., 2019; 2021). This motivates the following open questions:
075
      “How to alleviate the impact of redundant and even noisy variables on SSL models with manifold
076
      regularization? How to design a new manifold regularization scheme enjoying the robustness,
077
      interpretability, and prediction effectiveness simultaneously?"
078
079   Intuitively, we can handle the above questions by a two-stage framework, i.e., selecting the informative
080   variables firstly (e.g., via Lasso (Tibshirani, 1994), SpAM (Ravikumar et al., 2009)), and then
081
      implementing the manifold regularization approaches with the refined input variables. However,
      this variable selection strategy is independent of the intrinsic manifold structure and its accuracy is
082
      difficult to be guaranteed due to the scarcity of labeled data. Inspired by meta learning for coreset
083
      selection (Borsos et al., 2020; Zhou et al., 2022), this paper considers assigning all input variables
084   with masks for both labeled and unlabeled data, where merely those truly informative variables are
085   left for modeling and constructing the similarity matrix.
086
087
      Nevertheless, there are several challenges along this way: 1) It is NP-hard to learn the discrete mask
      variables taking values in {0, 1} directly. 2) The bilevel optimization usually needs the computation
088
      on Hessian and Jacobian matrices, which leads to a heavy computation burden. 3) Most kernel-based
089
      manifold regularization models construct the Gram matrix based on sample distance, which lacks the
090   result’s interpretability, e.g., screening the key variables associated with the response.
091
092
      1.1   C ONTRIBUTION
093
094   To address the aforementioned challenges, we inject the meta learning strategy and sparse additive
095   models into manifold regularized SSL framework, and formulate a new Semi-Supervised Meta
096   Additive Model (S2 MAM) to realize automatic variable masking and sparse approximation for
097   high-dimensional inputs even with noisy variables.
098   The core technique is to update the decision function and similarity matrix simultaneously with proper
099   masks on input variables, where the masks of S2 MAM are learned by a probabilistic meta strategy.
100   Moreover, an efficient implementation is employed here to solve the bilevel optimization problem,
101   which avoids the heavy computing burden on the implicit hypergradient calculation (Pedregosa,
102   2016), Neumann series and some variants with Hessian-vector or Jacobian-vector products (Lorraine
103   et al., 2020; Ghadimi & Wang, 2018).
104   The main contributions of this paper are summarized below:
105
106          • New statistical modeling. To the best of our knowledge, our S2 MAM is the first meta
107            learning method for manifold regularized additive models, where a novel bilevel optimization
               scheme is formulated for robust estimation and data-driven automatic variable selection

      1.2    C OMPARISONS WITH THE R ELATED W ORKS
132
      Semi-supervised dimensionality reduction. Recently, some efforts were made towards constructing a
133   flexible similarity matrix against feature corruptions for SSL with manifold regularization (Chen et al.,
134   2018; Nie et al., 2019). By rescaling the regression coefficients as variable weights, Chen et al. (Chen
135   et al., 2018) developed an efficient SSL method to obtain important variables, which is called rescaled
136   linear square regression. Another weighting approach in (Nie et al., 2019) is called auto-weighting
137   semi-supervised learning (AWSSL), which adaptively assigns continuous weights on variables to
138   update the similarity matrix. After the dimension reduction process, a specific classifier is employed
139   for downstream tasks. A robust graph learning (RGL) method (Kang et al., 2020) combined label
140   ranking regression and label propagation into a unified framework for weight graph construction and
141
      semi-supervised learning. Semi-supervised adaptive local embedding learning (SALE) (Nie et al.,
      2021) adaptively constructs two affinity graphs (based on labeled data and all embedding samples)
142
      separately to explore the local and global structures. Different from these works, this paper considers
143
      to automatically assign discrete masks 0/1 on input features (variables) for screening the truly active
144   variables.
145
146   Sparse additive models. Additive models (Stone, 1985; Hastie & Tibshirani, 1990), as natural
147
      nonparametric extensions of linear models, have been burgeoning in high-dimensional data analysis
      due to their attractive properties, i.e., overcoming the curse of dimensionality, the flexibility of
148
      function approximation, and the ability of variable selection (Meier et al., 2009; Christmann & Hable,
149
      2012; Yuan & Zhou, 2016; Chen et al., 2020). In recent years, many sparse additive models have
150   been proposed from various theoretical or empirical motivations, see e.g., (Lv et al., 2018; Haris
151   et al., 2022; Bouchiat et al., 2024; Duong et al., 2024). Naturally, the paradigm of additive models
152   can be applied to semi-supervised learning settings. As far as we know, there are only three papers
153   that touched on this topic (Culp & Michailidis, 2008; Culp et al., 2009; Culp, 2011). However, all
154   of them don’t consider the robustness on manifold learning against noisy variables, and ignore the
155   data-driven variable structure discovery. These strong restrictions on the pre-defined similarity matrix
156   and variable structure may result in degrading seriously of existing models under complex noise
157   circumstances.
158   Meta learning for sample/variable selection. The meta-based masking policy was developed in
159   (Borsos et al., 2020), where a bilevel neural network is designed for automatic supervised coreset
160   selection. Furthermore, its improved version with probabilistic bilevel optimization is proposed for
161   supervised classification (Zhou et al., 2022), especially for corrupted and imbalanced data. Indeed,
      Zhou et al. (Zhou et al., 2022) also provide an example of variable selection, while it is limited to


                                                                          3

162
      the supervised learning case and doesn’t concern the impact of noisy variables. To the best of our
163   knowledge, there has been no any endeavor before to explore the meta-based masking policy for
164   semi-supervised additive models.

533   5      C ONCLUSION
534
535   This paper proposes a semi-supervised meta additive model, called S2 MAM, to improve the robustness
536   and interpretability of manifold regularization (Belkin et al., 2006) under the redundant and noisy
537   input variable settings. Compared with the existing SSL with manifold regularization (Belkin et al.,
538   2006; Nie et al., 2019), the proposed approach is capable of realizing variable selection, interpretable
539   and robust estimation simultaneously. Theoretical and empirical evaluations verify its superiority
      over some state-of-the-art learning models.

928   B.2     BASELINES & PARAMETER S ETTINGS
929
930   B.2.1    R EGRESSION TASKS
931
      The baselines for regression tasks include:
932
933           • Lasso (Tibshirani, 1994), is a type of supervised linear regression model that is used for
934             variable selection with sparsity-induced regularization. The regularization parameter λ is
935             tuned across [10−4 , 10−3 , 10−2 , 10−1 , 1].
936
              • SpAM (Ravikumar et al., 2009), is an additive supervised nonparametric model for high-
937
                dimensional nonparametric regression and classification tasks. The regularization parameter
938             λ is tuned across [10−4 , 10−3 , 10−2 , 10−1 , 1].
939
940
              • DAN (Dinh & Ho, 2020) is designed to identify a subset of relevant features in deep
                learning models. The core technology involves the use of the adaptive group Lasso selection
941
                procedure with group Lasso as the base estimator, which is proven to be selection-consistent
942
                for a wide class of networks.
943
944           • LapRLS (Belkin et al., 2006), learns a semi-supervised linear model using the labeled
945
                data by minimizing a regularized least squares objective function. The regularization term
                incorporates the graph Laplacian matrix, which captures the smoothness assumption that
946
                similar points should have similar labels. The regularization parameters λ1 and λ2 are both
947
                tuned across [10−4 , 10−3 , 10−2 , 10−1 , 1].
948
949           • Variational autoencoder (VAE) (Goodfellow et al., 2014), is designed as a semi-supervised
950
                generative model by first learning an unsupervised embedding of the data and then using the
                embeddings as input to a supervised multilayer perceptron.
951
952           • Co-training regressor (COREG) (Zhou & Li, 2005), is a co-training algorithm for regression
953             tasks that uses two k-NN regressors with different distance metrics. During the training
954             process, each regressor generates labels for each other.
955           • Semi-supervised deep kernel learning (SSDKL) (Jean et al., 2018), is a semi-supervised
956             regression model based on minimizing predictive variance in the posterior regularization
957             framework. It combines the hierarchical learning of networks with the probabilistic modeling
958             capabilities of Gaussian processes.
959
960   For fairness, a network with a [d − 100 − 50 − 50 − 2] structure is employed here for the downstream
961
      regression task. Following (Jean et al., 2018), the same base network is shared for all deep semi-
      supervised models including VAE and SSDKL. The learning rates for neural network and Gaussian
962
      process are 10−3 and 10−1 , respectively. The training process of VAE, COREG, and SSDKL follows

2392   F     L IMITATIONS AND D ISCUSSIONS
2393
2394
       This paper proposes a new bilevel manifold regularization for semi-supervised learning tasks with
       an automatic feature masking mechanism. Theoretically, we establish its foundations of learning
2395
       theory including the computing convergence and the generalization error analysis. As far as we
2396
       know, this is the first work for bounding the excess risk of semi-supervised additive model. And our
2397   results show better convergence performance than (Zhou et al., 2022). Empirically, we verify the
2398   effectiveness of the proposed approach on synthetic datasets and real-world datasets. We designed
2399   the novel optimization algorithm for the proposed manifold regularized sparse additive model (see
2400   Appendix E.4). In the implemented codes, we further provide the settings of spline-based additive
2401   models.
2402
       However, there still exist some limitations including the computational difficulties on large-scale
2403   datasets and the assumption of bounded output. Fortunately, as introduced in Appendix B.7, S2 MAM
2404   can also deal with high-dimensional data with data preprocessing. An interesting approach for dealing
2405   with high-dimensional data like images is to extract the feature vectors first, which has been widely
2406   employed in some supervised (Su et al., 2023) and semi-supervised works (Qiu et al., 2018; Nie et al.,
2407   2019; Kang et al., 2020; Nie et al., 2021). And the random Fourier technique (Rahimi & Recht, 2007;
2408   Wang et al., 2023) could be further considered to accelerate the computation process. Theoretically,
2409   the bounded condition of the response can be relaxed to include the unbounded output, e.g., replacing
2410   it by the 1 + ϵ moment bounded assumptions (Feng, 2021; Feng & Wu, 2022)). The neural additive
2411
       modeling strategy (Agarwal et al., 2021; Yang et al., 2020) is also another interesting and effective
       direction to better improve the non-linear approximation ability and prediction performance of
2412
       S2 MAM. In addition, the current generalization analysis just focuses on the basic model of S2 MAM,
2413
       which can be further improved to match the bilevel manifold regularization tightly.
