009                                                A BSTRACT
010
011            Multi-view clustering aims to improve the final performance by taking advan-
012            tages of complementary and consistent information of all views. In real world,
013            data samples with partially available information are common and the issue re-
014            garding the clustering for incomplete multi-view data is inevitably raised. To deal
015            with the partial data with large scales, some fast clustering approaches for incom-
016            plete multi-view data have been presented. Despite the significant success, few
017            of these methods pay attention to learning anchors with high quality in a unified
018
               framework for incomplete multi-view clustering, while ensuring the scalability
               for large-scale incomplete datasets. In addition, most existing approaches based
019
               on incomplete multi-view clustering ignore to build the relation between anchor
020
               graph and similarity matrix in symmetric nonnegative matrix factorization and
021            then directly conduct graph partition based on the anchor graph to reduce the space
022            and time consumption. In this paper, we propose a novel fast incomplete multi-
023            view clustering method for the data with large scales, termed Efficient Incomplete
024            Multi-view clustering via flexible anchor Learning (EIML), where graph construc-
025            tion, anchor learning and graph partition are simultaneously integrated into a uni-
026            fied framework for efficient incomplete multi-view clustering. To be specific, we
027            learn a shared anchor graph to guarantee the consistency among multiple views
028            and employ a adaptive weight coefficient to balance the impact for each view.
029
               The relation between anchor graph and similarity matrix in symmetric nonnega-
               tive matrix factorization can also be built, i.e., each entry in the anchor graph can
030
               characterize the similarity between the anchor and original data sample. We then
031
               adopt an alternative algorithm for solving the formulated problem. Experiments
032            conducted on different datasets confirm the superiority of EIML compared with
033            other clustering methods for incomplete multi-view data. paragraph.

036   1    I NTRODUCTION
037
038   In real application, data are usually represented with different features from multiple views. This
039   kind of data is usually named multi-view data and integrating the various information for clustering
040   has shown to be a critical task in the unsupervised learning field. By investigating the complementary
041   and diverse information among different views, a large number of clustering methods for multi-view
042   data have been given Zhang et al. (2021a); Kumar et al. (2011); Qin et al. (2022); Chen et al. (2022);
043   Zhao et al. (2023); Wang et al. (2023); Yu et al. (2023); Jia et al. (2023) in recent years. For instance,
044   Kumar et al. Kumar et al. (2011) guaranteed that different representations are able to agree with each
045
      other by co-regularizing the clustering hypotheses. Ye et al. Ye et al. (2016) maximized the sum
      of weighted similarities among multiple clusterings to study the underlying clustering. Nie et al.
046
      Nie et al. (2017) simultaneously learned the local structure as well as performed semi-supervised
047
      classification or clustering. Luo et al. Luo et al. (2018) studied specificity and consistency in the
048   representations from different views. Chen et al. Chen et al. (2020) jointly explored the affinity
049   matrix as well as the low-rank representation tensor. Zhou et al. Zhou et al. (2020) utilized the
050   predefined kernels to learn a consistent representation or a shared kernel and then achieved the
051   unified clustering results. The vital part of clustering for multi-view data is to study the consistency
052   of different views by learning a unified representation. Most existing multi-view clustering works
053   make the assumption that data samples from different views are available Zhao et al. (2017); Zhang
      et al. (2021b).

072   However, data samples in most applications often lack the information for some views Xu et al.
073   (2023; 2019); Qin et al. (2023); Lv et al. (2022). Then the approaches based on integrity have diffi-
074   culty in dealing with incomplete data from multiple views. In order to handle such problem, several
075   methods of incomplete multi-view clustering have been presented Zhang et al. (2020). We can con-
076
      clude these methods into three strategies including graph construction, matrix factorization and deep
      learning. The methods based on graph construction aim to produce a similarity matrix shared by dif-
077
      ferent views. For instance, Liu et al. Liu et al. (2017) simultaneously learned a representation and
078
      filled in the blank value. The methods based on matrix factorization make full use of L1 constraint
079   and nonnegative matrix factorization to learn a consensus representation Li et al. (2014). Shao et al.
080   Shao et al. (2015) integrated weighted matrix factorization and L2,1 regularization to obtain better
081   clustering performance. The methods based on deep learning use a deep neural network to recover
082   the missing data and the feature representation. As a representative, Lin et al. Lin et al. (2021)
083   employed contrastive learning for integrating data recovery and feature learning. However, most
084   existing methods easily suffer from the high computation and space cost, which inevitably restricts
085   their availability on the datasets with large scales.
086   To cope with the above issue, many methods for the data with large scales have been proposed.
087   Wang et al. Wang et al. (2011) built a constrained factor matrix for exploring the cluster structure.
088   Kang et al. Kang et al. (2020) employed K-means to obtain the anchors and then collocated them for
089   a unified representation. Li et al. Li et al. (2022) adopted the consistent learned anchors for handling
090   the clustering problems of incomplete multi-view data. Nie et al. Nie et al. (2020) simultaneously
091   performed clustering on column and row of the original dataset. Wang et al. Wang et al. (2022b)
092   used the guidance of consensus anchors to study the anchor graph shared by different views. Sun et
093
      al. Sun et al. (2021) exploited the underlying distribution of the data to construct the anchor graph.
      Among these existing methods, the approaches based on anchor have achieved attention due to the
094
      scalability and efficiency. This kind of methods usually employs the original data and the generated
095
      anchors to build the corresponding anchor graph, resulting in more satisfied clustering performance.
096   Despite great success, the above methods neglect learning high-quality anchors in a unified frame-
097   work for incomplete multi-view clustering, while ensuring the scalability for incomplete datasets
098   with large scales. In addition, few of the existing approaches based on incomplete multi-view clus-
099   tering pay attention to building the relation between anchor graph and similarity matrix in symmetric
100   nonnegative matrix factorization and then directly performing graph partition based on the anchor
101   graph for reducing the space and computation consumption.
102   In this paper, we propose a novel fast incomplete multi-view clustering method for the data with
103   large scales, termed Efficient Incomplete Multi-view clustering via flexible anchor Learning (EIML),
104   where graph construction, anchor learning and graph partition are simultaneously considered in a
105   unified framework for efficient incomplete multi-view clustering as Fig. 1. These three parts can
106   boost each other, which promotes the quality of the clustering and improves the efficiency for large
107   scale datasets. To be specific, we learn a shared anchor graph to guarantee the consistency among
      multiple views and adopt a adaptive weight coefficient to balance the impact for each view. The

      relation between anchor graph and similarity matrix in symmetric nonnegative matrix factorization
109   can also be built, i.e., each entry in the anchor graph can describe the similarity between the anchor
110   and original data sample. In particular, we constrain the factor matrix to be a cluster indicator

      As a summary, the proposed EIML has the main contributions in the following:
114
115          • We give a new insight to the community of incomplete multi-view clustering for large
116            scale datasets, i.e., graph construction, anchor learning and graph partition in efficient in-
117            complete multi-view clustering can boost each other, which are able to be integrated into
118            a problem. The combination of these three issues is the focus in our work. While most
119            existing work treat graph construction, anchor learning and graph partition as separated
120            problems in incomplete multi-view clustering for the datasets with large scales.
121
             • We propose a novel fast incomplete multi-view clustering method for large scale data
122            termed as EIML, where graph construction, anchor learning and graph partition are simul-
123            taneously considered in a unified framework for efficient incomplete multi-view clustering.
124            The relation between anchor graph and similarity matrix in symmetric nonnegative matrix
125            factorization is also built, i.e., each entry in the anchor graph is able to characterize the
126            similarity between the anchor and original data sample.
127
             • Based on the relation between anchor graph and similarity matrix, we constrain the factor
128            matrix with rigorous interpretation to be cluster indicator representation by introducing the
129            orthogonal constraint on the actual bases and use the alternative algorithm for solving the
130            formulated problem. Extensive experiments are performed on different datasets to demon-
131            strate the superiority of EIML in terms of effectiveness and efficiency.

      2   E FFICIENT I NCOMPLETE M ULTI - VIEW C LUSTERING VIA F LEXIBLE

137   This section begins with introducing the motivation and formulation of the proposed EIML, then
138   moves on to the detailed optimization process for EIML. We lastly conduct the analysis about the
139   computation complexity to demonstrate the efficiency of EIML.
140   Motivation: For large-scale incomplete data clustering, reducing the redundancy of the data is the
141   key to increase efficiency. Some existing works denote each data sample with a linear combination of
142   the others and the global relation can be well exploited in this manner. However, the relatively high
143   storage and computation time produced in this way inevitably limit the scalability of incomplete
144   multi-view clustering for large-scale dataset. Actually, relatively less data samples are enough to
145   reconstruct the latent space. Therefore, selecting some data samples from the original dataset as
146   anchors or landmarks for reconstructing the relation structure is commonly used in the existing
147
      works.
148   Nevertheless, some existing incomplete multi-view clustering approaches usually conduct strategies
149   based on heuristic sampling, where the anchor selection and graph construction are separated. Then
150   the graph is constructed after selecting the anchors for different views. In this manner, the comple-
151   mentary information among different views is not able to be well explored and further algorithm is
152   needed to obtain a shared graph. Afterwards, the clustering algorithm (spectral clustering) is usually
153
      needed to achieve the final clustering results. This multiple-stage process significantly affects the
      quality of the anchors. Besides, few of the existing methods pay attention to building the relation
154
      between anchor and similarity matrix in symmetric nonnegative matrix factorization. As is known,
155
      each entry of a similarity matrix can describe the similarity between data samples in the dataset.
156   Performing symmetric nonnegative matrix factorization for the similarity matrix can directly lead
157   to the final partition. Then building the relation between anchor and similarity matrix can take ad-
158   vantages of directly obtaining the final clustering results in incomplete multi-view clustering. How
159   to learn anchors with high quality in a unified framework and build the relation between anchor
160   graph and similarity matrix in symmetric nonnegative matrix factorization to ensure the scalabil-
161   ity on large-scale dataset for incomplete multi-view clustering remains a considerably challenging
      issue.

      Formulation: Different from most existing works for incomplete multi-view clustering, we learn
163   anchors instead of selecting them based on the available data samples in the dataset. The proposed
164   EIML integrates graph construction, anchor learning and graph partition into a unified framework
165   for efficient incomplete multi-view clustering. Then the discriminative anchors are automatically
166   learned and the final partition can be achieved in this manner. Based on the assumption that multiple
167   views are sampled from a latent space, the anchors from multiple views are expected to be consistent
168   in this space. Given multi-view dataset {Xp }vp=1 , we construct the projection Bp ∈ Rdp ×m as the
169   consensus anchor guidance to integrate complementary information from different views into the
170   shared anchor graph Z ∈ Rm×n , where dp and m are the dimension of the data and the total
171
      number of anchors for the p-th view, respectively. The indicator representation Ap ∈ {0, 1}n×np is
      adopted to mark the unavailable data samples. The above process can be formulated as follows:

      2.1                   C OMPLEXITY A NALYSIS
271
272   The computation burden of EIML consists of the optimization cost of each variable. To be specific,
273   the time complexity for optimizing Bp is O(m2 d + mnd) at each iteration. Optimizing the weight
274   α of each view costs O(mnd). The time cost to learn the shared anchor graph Z is O(mnd). For
275   optimizing F , the time cost is O(mnk). The time cost to update G is O(mk 2 ). Then the total time
276   complexity of the proposed EIML is O((m2 d + mnd + mnk + mk 2 )t), where t denotes the number
277   of iterations for these parts. Due to n ≫ m and n ≫ k, the computation cost of EIML is nearly
278   linear to the size of the dataset O(n).

396   We compare EIML with some representive incomplete multi-view clustering approaches in the fol-
397   lowing. BSV Ng et al. (2001) uses mean value filling to perform spectral clustering for each view
398   and then gives the best single view result. MIC Shao et al. (2015) learns latent subspaces from
399   different views and then performs optimization on a shared representation. MKKM-IK Ma et al.
400
      (2021) simultaneously imputes the missing part and performs kernel K-means algorithm. DAIMIC
      Hu & Chen (2018) deals with the problem of missing view by introducing a view-specific weight
401
      representation and then aligns the basis representations. APMC Guo & Ye (2019) uses the presented
402
      data samples from different views as anchors and achieves the final result by spectral clustering. PIC
403   Wang et al. (2019) pads the similarity matrix by solving the problem of missing view. EEIMVC
404   Liu et al. (2021) produces the base representations with low dimensions and then adopts a unified
405   framework to simultaneously impute these representations and optimize a shared representation fea-
406   ture. V 3 H Fang et al. (2020) exploits the unique and consistent par among different incomplete
407   views, which is motivated by genetics. IMVC-CBG Wang et al. (2022a) adopts a scalable anchor
408   graph framework for the problem of incomplete multi-view clustering. FIMVC-VIA Liu et al.
409   (2022) learns view-specific anchors and builds a consensus anchor graph shared by different views
410   for incomplete multi-view clustering.
411   In the experiment, we use four metrics to evaluate the experimental results, which include accuracy
412   (ACC), NMI, F1-score and Purity. We repeat each algorithm for total 20 times and then report the
413   mean and standard deviation of the results. The parameters for the compared methods of incomplete
414   multi-view clustering are set as their recommended ones. We run all experiments on AMD Ryzen 5
415   Six-Core Processor 3.60 GHz.

417   3.2       PARAMETER S ELECTION
418
419   There are total two parameters appeared in EIML, including the trade-off parameter λ and the
420
      number of anchors m. We then perform experiments on different datasets to study how these
      two parameters influence the final clustering performance. We set λ and m in the range of
421
      [0.001, 0.1, 1, 10, 100, 1000] and [k, 2k, 3k, 5k, 7k], respectively. Here, k corresponds to the total
422
      number of clusters in dataset. According to Figs. 2-3, we find that better performance is achieved
423   when λ = 1 under the same m on different datasets. Besides, the clustering result of EIML is rela-
424   tively stable over different parameter values on these datasets, which shows that EIML is generally
425   robust to the trade-off parameter λ. It can also be observed that different number of anchors m has
426   relatively little influence on the clustering performance under the same λ for these datasets.

534   we propose EIML in this work for efficient incomplete multi-view clustering. It simultaneously
535   considers graph construction, anchor learning and graph partition in a unified framework, in which

      several benchmark datasets show the effectiveness and efficiency of EIML under different metrics.
