009                                                A BSTRACT
010
011            Recently, diffusion models have been extensively studied as powerful generative
012            tools for image translation. However, the existing diffusion model-based image
013            translation approaches often suffer from several limitations: 1) slow inference due
014            to iterative denoising, 2) the necessity for paired training data, or 3) constraints
015            from learning only one-way translation paths. To mitigate these limitations, here
016            we introduce a novel framework, called Implicit Bridge Consistency Distilla-
017            tion (IBCD), that extends consistency distillation with a diffusion implicit bridge
018
               model that connects PF-ODE trajectories from any distribution to another one.
               Moreover, to address the challenges associated with distillation errors from consis-
019
               tency distillation, we introduce two unique improvements: Distribution Matching
020
               for Consistency Distillation (DMCD) and distillation-difficulty adaptive weight-
021            ing method. Experimental results confirm that IBCD for bidirectional translation
022            can achieve state-of-the-art performance on benchmark datasets in just one step
023            generation.

026   1    I NTRODUCTION
027
028   Diffusion Models (DMs) (Ho et al., 2020; Song et al., 2021a;b), which learn the score function
029   of clean data, have demonstrated remarkable generation performance through iterative denoiso-
030   ing. They have shown superior performance compared to the classical generation models such as
031   Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) (Goodfellow et al., 2014a), Variational Autoencoders
032   (VAEs) (Kingma & Welling, 2014), etc. Furthermore, DMs have been widely explored across vari-
033
      ous domains, e.g., text-to-image generation (Rombach et al., 2022), inverse image problems (Chung
      et al., 2023), image editing (Mokady et al., 2022), and so forth.
034
035   Typically, diffusion models (DMs) can be classified into two groups based on the type of govern-
036   ing equation: Stochastic Differential Equations (SDEs) and Probability Flow Ordinary Differen-
037   tial Equations (PF-ODEs). Although PF-ODEs generally require fewer neural function evaluations
038   (NFEs) during sampling compared to SDEs, they still involve numerous iterative steps, leading to
039
      slow inference speeds. To address this issue, various techniques have been explored to accelerate
      the inference speed of DMs. One prominent approach is distillation-based methods, where a stu-
040
      dent neural network learns the ODE trajectories generated by a pre-trained teacher diffusion model,
041
      enabling one-step generation (Salimans & Ho, 2022; Song et al., 2023; Kim et al., 2024b). How-
042   ever, these methods primarily focus on learning deterministic paths from Gaussian noise to specific
043   data distributions, which restricts their applicability to arbitrary distributions, especially in unpaired
044   scenarios.
045
      On the other hand, Schrödinger Bridge (Schrödinger, 1932) offers a promising approach for translat-
046
      ing between two arbitrary distributions using entropy-regularized optimal transport. Various meth-
047
      ods have been developed for translating between data distributions, such as those proposed in (Wang
048   et al., 2021; Chen et al., 2021; Liu et al., 2022), though many of these methods are limited to paired
049   settings. In contrast, DDIB (Su et al., 2023) addresses image-to-image translation by concatenating
050   the ODE trajectories of two distinct DMs, making it suitable for unpaired settings, yet it still relies
051   on numerous iterative steps. More recently, UNSB (Kim et al., 2024a) has been introduced to di-
052   rectly tackle unpaired image-to-image translation by regularizing Sinkhorn paths. However, UNSB
053   faces limitations due to its dependence on multiple iterative steps, unidirectional translation, and the
      use of adversarial training with a discriminator, which can lead to training instability.

067   To address the limitations of the existing method, we aim at the development of a bidirectional
068   one-step generator that enables translation between two arbitrary distributions in unpaired settings
069   without relying on adversarial losses (see a comparison in Table 1). To achieve this, we propose
070   Implicit Bridge Consistency Distillation (IBCD), an extension of the concept of consistency distilla-
071   tion (CD) that incorporates a diffusion implicit bridge model for translating between arbitrary data
072
      distributions. Unlike CD, which learns paths from Gaussian noise to data, IBCD connects trajecto-
      ries from one arbitrary distribution to another one using a Probability Flow Ordinary Differential
073
      Equation (PF-ODE), allowing for flexible and efficient distribution translation.
074
075   However, simply extending CD can result in reduced distillation efficacy due to error accumula-
076   tion, as well as challenges related to model capacity and training, which arise from integrating two
077   trajectories and introducing bidirectionality. To address this, we propose a regularization method
078
      called Distribution Matching for Consistency Distillation (DMCD). Furthermore, we introduce a
      novel weighting scheme based on distillation difficulty, which applies a stronger DMCD penalty
079
      specifically to samples where the consistency loss alone proves insufficient. By integrating these
080
      advanced components, along with an additional cycle loss, our approach significantly enhances the
081   reality-faithfulness trade-off, achieving state-of-the-art performance in a single step, as shown in
082   Figure 1. The main contributions of our work are as follows:
083
084            • We propose a novel unpaired image-to-image translation framework, termed Implicit
085              Bridge Consistency Distillation (IBCD), which enables bidirectional translation using only
086              a single neural function evaluation (NFE), achieving state-of-the-art performance on bench-
087              mark datasets.
088            • We introduce additional improvements, including Distribution Matching for Consistency
089              Distillation (DMCD) and an adaptive weighting scheme based on distillation difficulty,
090              to effectively mitigate distillation errors inherent in the process. Additionally, the incor-
091              poration of cycle-loss further enhances image translation performance, resulting in more
092              accurate and reliable translations.

134   Consistency Distillation (CD). The aim of the consistency distillation (CD) (Song et al., 2023) is

137   for any time point input within the same PF-ODE trajectory, i.e., f (xt , t) = f (xt′ , t′ ) for all t, t′ ∈
138   [ϵ, T ], with the boundary condition fθ (xϵ , ϵ) = xϵ . Here, ϵ is a small positive number, to avoid
139

      3     M ETHODS
191
192   Building upon existing approaches, our goal is to develop a distillation method for a one-step model
193   that enables bidirectional mapping between arbitrary probability distributions in an unpaired setting,
194   by leveraging pre-trained diffusion models. Specifically, given two domains XA , XB , and unpaired
195   datasets SA = {xa ∈ XA }, SB = {xb ∈ XB }, our translator fθ is designed to perform two translation
196   functions: fθ (xa , cb ) : XA → XB and fθ (xb , ca ) : XB → XA , where ca and cb represent class
197   embeddings for the target translation domain. In the following, we introduce our framework for
198   distilling bidirectional DDIB into a unified one-step model in an unpaired setting and then discuss
199   the associated challenges. To address these, we present a novel distillation approach by incorporating
200
      distribution matching with a new adaptive weighting factor and a cycle loss to enable bidirectional
      reconstruction.

261   3.2      L OSS F UNCTION FOR I MPLICIT B RIDGE C ONSISTENCY D ISTILLATION
262
263   While our IBCD framework facilitates one-step bidirectional transport of the student model in
264
      unpaired settings, it faces certain challenges. First, the consistency loss relies on a local consis-
      tency strategy (categorized by Kim et al. (2024b)), which aligns consistency only between adjacent
265
      timesteps by recursively using the student’s output. This can lead to the accumulation of local er-
266
      rors, resulting in a growing discrepancy between the student’s prediction fθ (xt , t, c) and the true
267   boundary value xϵ(c) as the distance from the boundary condition timestep increases. This issue
268   is particularly pronounced in IBCD due to its doubled trajectory length compared to standard CD.
269

      Second, the student not only has to perform a bidirectional task but also has to learn two different
271   ODE trajectories. Considering that the two ODEs in the teacher model share time steps and are sepa-
272   rated only by conditions, this increased complexity can not only affect model capacity but also make
273   training more difficult. The degradation of consistency distillation performance due to the addition
274   of bidirectional features has also been reported by Li & He (2024). Third, unlike EGSDE (Zhao
275   et al., 2022), which can freely adjust the trade-off between reality and faithfulness by weighting
276   expert contributions, vanilla IBCD lacks an explicit mechanism to control this balance, potentially
277   limiting its ability to adapt to diverse scenarios.

283   difficulty adaptive weighting factor D̂(·, ·). This adaptive weighting scheme helps to focus the op-
284   timization on challenging samples, thereby enhancing the overall performance and stability of the
285   student model during training. The resulting DMCD is given by:

311   applying the DMCD loss forcefully only to trajectories where the IBCD loss alone is insufficient.

530
531
      In this work, we introduced a novel unpaired bidirectional one-step image translation framework,
532
      Implicit Bridge Consistent Distillation (IBCD). By distilling the diffusion implicit bridge through an
533
      extended consistency distillation framework, we achieved bidirectional translation without the need
534   for paired data or adversarial training. Our approach addresses the limitations of traditional consis-
535   tency distillation through the proposed Distribution Matching for Consistency Distillation (DMCD)
536   and distillation difficulty adaptive weighting strategies. Empirical evaluations on both toy and high-
537   dimensional datasets demonstrate the effectiveness and scalability of IBCD. We believe that IBCD
538   represents a significant advancement in the field of general one-step image translation, providing
539   a versatile and efficient solution for various image tasks, including image restoration, especially in
      scenarios with limited paired data.
