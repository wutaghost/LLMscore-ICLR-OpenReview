                                                    A BSTRACT
027
028
                 Diffusion-based methods have demonstrated remarkable capabilities in generating
                 a diverse array of high-quality images, sparking interests for styled avatars, virtual
029
                 try-on, and more. Previous methods use the same reference image as the target. An
030
                 overlooked aspect is the leakage of the target’s spatial information, style, etc. from
031              the reference, harming the generated diversity and causing shortcuts. However, this
032              approach continues as widely available datasets usually consist of single images
033              not grouped by identities, and it is expensive to recollect large-scale same-identity
034              data. Moreover, existing metrics adopt decoupled evaluation on text alignment
035              and identity preservation, which fail at distinguishing between balanced outputs
036              and those that over-fit to one aspect. In this paper, we propose a multi-level, same-
037              identity dataset RetriBooru, which groups anime characters by both face and cloth
038              identities. RetriBooru enables adopting reference images of the same character and
039
                 outfits as the target, while keeping flexible gestures and actions. We benchmark
                 previous methods on our dataset, and demonstrate the effectiveness of training
040
                 with a reference image different from target (but same identity). We introduce a
041
                 new concept composition task, where the conditioning encoder learns to retrieve
042              different concepts from several reference images, and modify a baseline network
043              RetriNet for the new task. Finally, we introduce a novel class of metrics named
044              Similarity Weighted Diversity (SWD), to measure the overlooked diversity and
045              better evaluate the alignment between similarity and diversity.

048   1 I NTRODUCTION
049   Recent advancements in latent diffusion models (Rombach et al., 2022) have ushered in new capabili-
050   ties in image generation, including face generation, virtual try-on, anime creation, etc. Previously,
051   the focus was on optimization-based methods which involved tuning entire networks (Ruiz et al.,
052   2023), tuning low-rank matrices (Hu et al., 2021), or incorporating text embeddings (Gal et al., 2022).
053   Yet, they suffer from slow inference and their hyper-parameters do not generalize to all intended
      subjects. The latest research has shifted towards injecting specific subject information directly into


                                                                1
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


054
      the generation process, enhancing the relevance and accuracy of the output. Current methods can
055   be categorized into two main approaches. The first approach, exemplified by IP-Adapter (Ye et al.,
056   2023), integrates additional networks that do not specifically learn to comprehend reference images.
057   The second approach, e.g. InstantID (Wang et al., 2024b), adopts specialized inputs such as facial
058   key-points, which while effective for maintaining facial identity, lacks versatility in other contexts
059   like changing outfits. However, we observe a potential defect of the predominant training pipeline,
060   which adopts the same image for both reference conditioning and target. This setup can inadvertently
061   leak target information to the conditioning encoder during training, which could lead to shortcut
062   learning, e.g., spatial information (Xue et al., 2023; Zhong et al., 2023). While this pipeline may
063
      aid in convergence and stabilize generation, it raises concerns about whether the model is learning
      the intended concepts or merely exploiting the leaked information. In this paper, we propose a shift
064
      from learning a passive condition encoder to a retrieval encoder, enabled by new annotations. This
065
      new task encourages the encoder to understand and actively retrieve subject concepts in the intended
066   manner, without depending on non-characteristic target information.
067
068   Avoiding target leakage. To learn to retrieve the specified information and reduce leakage from the
069
      target, a good conditional image could be chosen to maximize the shared characteristic information
      we would like to learn, and minimize the unwanted information. For example, vision-language
070
      models are used to be challenged by distinguishing between Chihuahua and Muffins (Fan et al., 2024),
071
      partially due to their similar spatial structures (near-round shapes in brown with black dots), which
072   are heavily exploited during pre-training. If the non-characteristic information was well permuted, e.g.
073   hiding Chihuahua partially, closing Chihuahua eyes, cutting muffins, resulting in a diverse and less
074   learnable spatial inductive bias, we could force the models to learn from characteristic information as
075   intended, such as furs on Chihuahua faces and cake crumbs on muffins, as well as their dog paws and
076   paper cups, respectively. Applying the same ideology to subject-driven generation, what if we can
077   access to conditional images with different spatial information, activities etc. from the target, while
078   maintaining the identity? In this sense, the conditional encoder is forced to learn from the definitive
079   concepts and features of the identity. However, such identity-preserving annotations are scarce. For
080
      human appearance, current identity-focused datasets are either limited to faces, or feature inconsistent
      clothing options. Meanwhile, virtual try-on datasets provide detailed clothing labels but suffer from a
081
      lack of diversity in body poses and background settings (Sec. 2). It remains a significant challenge to
082
      keep track of whole-body human identities with unlimited motions and poses, accounting for both
083   facial and clothing identities, where the later one can lead the classification at whole-body scale too.
084
085   Costs and generalization. Another practical consideration is the trade-off between computational
086
      costs and the generalization ability. For instance, InstantID (Wang et al., 2024b) requires 48 NVIDIA
      H800 GPUs, with a batch size of only two, to train adapter structures for face generation with
087
      key-point features on their large, private dataset. Extending this to more general semantic features
088
      could significantly increase computational demands. Researchers, when validating prototype methods
089   and primary results, demand a smaller and easier dataset, in order to obtain valuable feedback on the
090   way to convergence. However, an easier benchmark would still require a decent ability to generalize,
091   so that the observations and conclusions drawn can also apply to larger scale datasets and other
092   domains. Taking both into consideration, anime figures data offers a valuable testing ground due to
093   its easier details and good mimicry of human figures. It has less compressed latent space after VAE,
094   which is less prone to degeneration at smaller resolutions compared to human faces and body parts.
095   This allows for quicker iterations and faster algorithm development in anime styles, while fitting into
096   tighter speed and memory constraints. On the other hand, anime figures are animated humans, whose
097
      samples share a large similarity in body structures, facial expressions, activities, etc. with human
      samples, facilitating human-related research. What is more, anime itself also serves as the medium of
098
      plenty artistic creations in books, movies and games, which have proven popular and successful for
099
      both commercial and recreational applications, sparking generative methods to create anime subjects.
100
101   Therefore, we propose RetriBooru, a multitask, multiconcept anime dataset based on Danbooru, one
102   of the largest anime website with higher-quality anime images and precise tags. More importantly,
103
      We propose a labeling pipeline by leveraging existing tags and VQA models to construct same-outfit
      clusters for anime characters (Sec. 3). This combination of data and annotation would be very
104
      costly to obtain otherwise. With annotated clothing identities, RetriBooru not only enables training
105
      with a different reference image of the same identity as target, but also empowers more advanced
106   tasks such as composing faces and clothes so changing one’s outfits. In order to retrieve and inject
107   desired information into the generation, we modify ControlNet (Zhang et al., 2023) to build a retrieval

      encoder, namely RetriNet (Fig. 4). Simultaneous work follow a similar design, achieving good results
109   in other generation tasks with other geometric or layout control (Sec. 2). We take this one step further
110   and let both appearance and geometric information be retrieved and injected.
111
112
      Evaluating prompt-reference fusion. In evaluating the generation quality, common CLIP scores for
      image and text similarity also tend to encourage copying and pasting the reference subject and follow
113
      prompts elsewhere. This is not the ideal integration of reference images and text prompts. Even worse,
114
      in rare cases, if half of the generated images have high identity preservation but low text alignment,
115   and vice versa for the other half, we obtain no useful pictures with high information from reference
116   images and good prompt fidelity, or otherwise poorly combined, but still have decent CLIP-I and
117   CLIP-T averages. We tackle this problem in Sec. 4, where we propose a class of metrics named
118   Similarity Weighted Diversity to evaluate how well the prompts and reference images are combined.
119   When used with different pairs of metrics, new metrics can measure the quality of simultaneous
120   reference and prompt similarity, which we refer as similarity-diversity balance.
121   We summarize our contributions as follows: a) We propose an anime dataset RetriBooru and the
122   generalizable construction pipeline, which enables tracking and clustering of annotated outfits of the
123   same character, and facilitates experimenting methods on referenced generation. b) Given the clothing
124   annotation, we propose a new training pipeline for subject-driven generation by using a reference
125   image different from the target but with the same identity, and verify its effectiveness by previous
126   methods. c) We propose a new concept composition task, which learns to retrieve specific aspect
127   information or various concepts from different reference images, and provide baseline results with
128   our baseline architecture RetriNet. d) We propose a new class of metrics (SWD) to better evaluate
129
      referenced generation methods, explain its theoretical meaning, and demonstrate its effectiveness
      in evaluation. We conduct major experiments on RetriBooru using various methods and settings,
130
      drawing intriguing conclusions that also generalize to other domains with a larger scale.

136   Reference architectures. Initial approaches to inject information into the generation process apply
137   to the attention blocks in the denoising U-Net. For example, in Prompt-to-Prompt (Hertz et al., 2022)
138   and in Tune-A-Video (Wu et al., 2023), one can control the subject to ride a car instead of a bike
139   while preserving the rest of the results. However, like in CustomDiffusion (Kumari et al., 2023), these
140
      methods are usually limited to more general concepts without as much geometric identity information
      as faces or clothes. IP-adapter (Ye et al., 2023) and FastComposer (Xiao et al., 2023), both aiming
141
      at subject face generation, do utilize image encoders, but with a shared feature vector for all blocks
142
      of the U-Net (as opposed to different vectors for different blocks in our RetriNet). MasaCtrl (Cao
143   et al., 2023) adopts a parallel encoder with modified self-attention blocks so that the reference image
144   is edited according to the new prompt. DreamTuner (Hua et al., 2023) tries to enhance referenced
145   generation with an additional fine-tuning. StableVITON (Kim et al., 2023) adds body-related masks
146   and segmentation for the virtual try-on application.
147   Virtual try-on. Starting from reproducing single objects (Chen et al., 2023; Li et al., 2023a), tasks
148   of combining multiple objects or people have emerged (Liu et al., 2022; Xiao et al., 2023; Kumari
149   et al., 2023; Ma et al., 2023), including virtual try-on, where given images of a person and an outfit, a
150   generated image of the person wearing the clothes seamlessly is expected. It is more difficult than
151   other composition tasks since there are more interactions between the body and clothes (Zhu et al.,
152
      2023; Li et al., 2023b; Institute for Intelligent Computing, 2023; Kim et al., 2023). A unique technical
      characteristic of virtual try-on is the fixed pose in the reference image as layout control, and only
153
      the appearance needs to be modified. For diffusion models, generating local appearances based on a
154
      given layout is much easier compared to generating geometry and appearance at the same time.
155
      Evaluation metrics. Recent evaluation metrics are quite similar among the literature (Ruiz et al.,
156   2023; Chen et al., 2023; Ruiz et al., 2023; Li et al., 2023a; Kumari et al., 2023; Ma et al., 2023), using
157   CLIP (Radford et al., 2021) or DINO (Caron et al., 2021) features to measure similarity. CLIP-I
158   is used to measure the similarity between the reference image and the generated image. CLIP-T is
159   used to measure the similarity between the prompt and the generated image. Besides human user
160   preference tests, (Achlioptas et al., 2023) aims to improve similarity scores. Nevertheless, those
161   metrics offer simple trade-off between one another and fail to evaluate many more subtle aspects for
      referenced generation. In Sec. 4, we propose a new class of metrics to ameliorate this situation.

      3      R ETRI B OORU DATASET

178   As discussed in Sec. 1, a concepts-labeled dataset is required to train a referenced generation
179   model for retrieving given concepts interpretably. The motivation is to minimize the use of leaked
180   information of the target, when training on the same image as both the reference and the target,
181
      which can form potential training shortcuts and cause overfitting. Existing datasets are single-tasked,
      lacking in size and types of identity, and may be harder to iterate on than anime figures (faces, for
182
      example). With the further annotated RetriBooru dataset, one can not only utilize cloth tags to
183
      constrain customization, apply masks and face boxes to refine details, but also leverage reference
184   image(s) to serve as conditioning and train a more generalizable, robust condition encoder.

192   It is very costly to collect multiple identities with multiple clothing images, and approaches that rely
193   merely on CLIP features do not have enough sensitivity to reliably classify clothing identities. To
194   ameliorate this, we leverage current large models to obtain clothing labels for our dataset with filtering
195   heuristics. We construct our RetriBooru dataset from the Danbooru 2019 Figures dataset (Branwen
196
      et al., 2020), which cropped 855880 images of single anime figures from Danbooru by anime character
      detection model (Li & Shahjahan, 2018). We perform filtering by cloth tags, visual annotations using
197
      existing robust segmenter and detector, and clothing clustering using a visual-question answering
198
      (VQA) model. We use Instruct-BLIP (Liu et al., 2023) with Vicuna-7B as the VQA model, ask it
199   descriptive questions for short answers, e.g., “list top two colors of the clothing”, and cluster images
200   of the same character wearing the same outfits using a strict heuristic that matches the output answers.
201   Please see Supp. A.1 for processing details. In the end, RetriBooru retains 116729 training samples,
202   where each training sample could serve as either the target image or a reference image for another
203   target. Each of the reference image has the same character, outfits, and is drawn by the same artist
204   as the target image. We show details of the dataset in Fig. 2. On the left is a sample image and its
205   annotations, including basic information, mask and face bounding box, prompts and cloth tags, and a
206   list of IDs which can access to other samples of the same identities. On the right are the distributions
207
      of the number of same-identity samples for each sample, top-15 characters present, and top-30 most
      frequent cloth tags. Each sample has at least one reference image in the “similar” entry, and there
208
      are 4232 samples which have ≥15 reference images. We also compare with proposed datasets in
209
      previous work in Tab. 1. Note that the larger the datasets are, the less functionalities they enable
210   due to annotation costs, and vice versa. In comparison, RetriBooru hits the best balance between
211   size and capabilities, where there are multi-images of an identity (similar images to the target), as
212   well as concept-aware labeling (two concepts per sample, face and cloths). CustomConcept101
213   (Kumari et al., 2023) is the most similar dataset to ours serving the same functionalities, but only
214   contains image clusters of 101 realistic objects, in total 642 images. Using automated pipeline and
215   strict answer matching to group multiple images by identities, RetriBooru is able to collect accurate
      annotations, and enable learning to compose separated concepts which will be further discussed.

      Training. We adopt FastComposer (Xiao et al., 2023), ControlNet (Zhang et al., 2023), IP-Adapter
      (Ye et al., 2023), and Kandinsky (Razzhigaev et al., 2023) to finetune on our dataset (initialized with
231
      SD v1.5 weights), as they are the latest open source methods that process conditional images with
232
      distinct designs: FastComposer injects identifiers to help place objects (in conditional images) into
233   one image; ControlNet learns pixel-wise control and exploits spatial structures on purpose, passing
234   conditioning to the U-Net via zero convolutions; IP-Adapter passes reference images to a frozen
235   encoder and additionally learn to adapt the features, adding the same outputs to all layers in U-Net;
236   Kandinsky adopts a prior network (taking prompts and reference) and U-Net (target and reference)
237   that can be pre-trained separately. Due to the limited computing power, we finetune with resolution
238   256 and FP16 for all methods without rigorous hyper-parameter searching, using 8 NVIDIA V100
239   GPUs. Note that we do not pursue the peak performance but conduct sufficient training for our
240   analysis, and we do not leverage extra training tricks such as additional loss updates on face parts.
241
      To verify our motivation and assumptions, we develop two training settings: -a denotes existing
      training pipeline, which applies two different transforms the same image as target and reference
242
      (conditional) image, and -b adopts a same-identity but different image from target as the reference
243
      image, everything else unchanged. It would be more fair and informative to compare two settings
244   within the same method to better evaluate the impact of target information leakage.
245

246

249   between generated images and reference image, generated images and text prompt to evaluate identity
250   preservation and text alignment, denoted as CLIP-I and CLIP-T. We also multiply two quantities
251   element-wisely to roughly evaluate the balance. See Supp. A.3 for detailed training and evaluation
252   settings, including fixed parameter choices for the models.

287   4 S IMILARITY W EIGHTED D IVERSITY
288   Although our new training pipeline enabled by annotating clothes has been justified, there lies a
289   discrepancy between qualitative results and metric scores. The goal of subject-driven generation is
290   to generate images flexibly while maintaining subject identity (object or personal) and combining
291   text prompts well. As discussed in Sec. 2, existing metrics measure image and text similarities
292   separately. However, observe that the better qualitative results of -b setting are only roughly reflected
293
      in Tab. 2, and decoupled averages of the metrics are not good measures of qualities of subject driven
      images. Oftentimes, some generated images tend to resemble reference images while others follow
294
      texts, especially when generation parameters are not well-optimized, yielding inflated averaged
295
      scores. We provide a class of metrics to combine similarity and diversity metrics, which require both
296   extracting the desired information from reference images and combining reference information with
297   texts. Hence, it is better suited for referenced generation. Our metrics also benefit from continued
298   improvements in the precision of similarity and diversity measurements. For instance, CLIP-I is not
299   yet an optimal measurement identity preservation because preserving other irrelevant style, layout
300   and semantic information also contributes to CLIP-I. In practice, these irrelevant information usually
301   interfere with text prompts, resulting in artifacts.
302   A reference image generation model can be written as a function f : r, θr → g, where r represents
303   reference image(s), θ is the collection of all generation parameters for the run (the prompt, scheduler,
304   guidance scale etc.), and g is the generated image. We denote div and sim (diversity, similarity)
305   measurement functions as div : r, g → R, sim : r, g → R. Under this notation, for a pair of
306   measurement functions div and sim, we define:

359   5    C ONCEPT C OMPOSITION TASK
360   We extend our -b setting in Sec. 3.1, using multiple reference images from the “similar” entry
361   of the target. In the following sections, we refer to directly using various reference images as
362   the “reconstruction” task, given its unchanged training goal despite various reference. We further
363   propose a concept composition task, where different reference images carry different concepts for the
364   condition encoder to retrieve and compose. As shown in Fig. 1, we use annotated bounding boxes
365
      and masks to separate desired concepts from the reference images, and pass to the condition encoder
      along with the corresponding texts. Note that we randomly choose N reference images and obtain
366
      either “face” or “clothes” concepts independently, instead of building N reference images by dividing
367
      each of N/2 images into two concepts. In rare cases, we replace the “clothes” reference with its
368   whole image, denoted as “figure” reference, if the face bounding box occupies the majority of the
369   image. See Supp. A.4 for implementation details. Likewise, for reconstruction task, we directly pick
370   N reference images and pass them to the condition encoder with texts “figure”. Concept composition
371   is a more difficult task than reconstruction, as it needs to not only recognize the concepts in the
372   reference, but also learn to compose them properly.
373   Expectation for N . Ideally, We want N to be as large as it can be supported by sufficient annotations.
374   When the reference image is the same as the target, the conditional encoder cannot constrain what
375   information is passed and learned, forming shortcuts based on inductive bias such as geometric
376   structures, shapes and gestures, leading to overfitting. Hence, information from inductive bias is
377   leaked to the model for convergence, while the characteristics of an identity is insufficiently learned.
      However, when the reference image maintains only the identity but has other attributes differently

526   6 C ONCLUSION
527   In this paper, we propose a new dataset RetriBooru, which enables new training pipelines and tasks
528   with extra identity annotations. We successfully show that using same-identity pairs (reference and
529   target image have the same identity but are not the same image) results in more robust generalizations,
530
      and also enable training for composing different concepts from different reference images, using
      a newly designed model. Moreover, we propose a new group of metrics to weight similarity
531
      (image identity) and diversity (text prompt), which aligns well with visualizations and provide better
532
      evaluation. Our dataset facilitate research with constrained computes and provide enough samples to
533   yield meaningful analysis that can generalize. Our study sheds light on improving current training
534   procedure and objectives.
