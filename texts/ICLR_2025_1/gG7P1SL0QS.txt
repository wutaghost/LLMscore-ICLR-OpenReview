009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            This work optimizes DP-SGD from a geometric perspective. Differential privacy
012            (DP) has become a prevalent privacy model in a wide range of machine learning
013            tasks, especially after the debut of DP-SGD. However, DP-SGD, which directly
014            perturbs gradients in the training iterations, fails to mitigate the negative impacts
015            of noise on gradient direction. As a result, DP-SGD is often inefficient. In this
016            work, we first generalize DP-SGD and theoretically derive the impact of DP noise
017            on the training process. Our analysis reveals that, in terms of a perturbed gra-
018
               dient, only the noise on a direction has eminent impact on the model efficiency
               while that on magnitude can be mitigated by optimization techniques, i.e., fine-
019
               tuning gradient clipping and learning rate. Besides, we confirm that traditional
020
               DP introduces biased noise on the direction when adding unbiased noise to the
021            gradient itself. Overall, the perturbation of DP-SGD is actually sub-optimal from
022            a geometric perspective. Motivated by this, we design a geometric perturbation
023            strategy GeoDP within the DP framework, which perturbs the direction and the
024            magnitude of a gradient, respectively. By directly reducing the noise on the di-
025            rection, GeoDP mitigates the negative impact of DP noise on model efficiency
026            with the same DP guarantee. Extensive experiments on two public datasets (i.e.,
027            MNIST and CIFAR-10), one synthetic dataset and three prevalent models (i.e.,
028            Logistic Regression, CNN and ResNet) confirm the effectiveness and generality
029
               of our strategy.

      1   I NTRODUCTION
033
      Although deep learning models have numerous applications in various domains, such as personal
034
      recommendation, smart manufacturing, and healthcare, the privacy leakage of training data from
035
      these models has become a growing concern. There are already mature attacks which successfully
036   reveal the contents of private data from deep learning models (Carlini et al., 2021; Gong et al., 2021).
037   For example, a white-box membership inference attack can infer whether a single data point belongs
038   to the training dataset of a DenseNet with 82% test accuracy (Nasr et al., 2019). These attacks pose
039   imminent threats to the wider adoption of deep learning in business sectors with sensitive data,
040   especially in healthcare and fintech.
041
      To address this concern, differential privacy (DP), which can provide quantitative amount of privacy
042   preservation to individuals in the training dataset, is embraced by the most prevalent model training
043   technique, i.e., stochastic gradient descent (SGD). Referred to as DP-SGD (Li et al., 2021; Zeighami
044   et al., 2022; Liu et al., 2021; Bao et al., 2022), this algorithm adds random DP noise to gradients in
045   the training process so that attackers cannot infer private data from model parameters with a high
046   probability.
047
      However, a primary drawback of DP-SGD is the ineffective training process caused by the over-
048   whelming noise, which deteriorates the model efficiency. Although much attention (Abadi et al.,
049   2016; Mironov, 2017; Fu et al., 2023) has been paid to reducing the noise scale, the majority of
050   existing solutions, which numerically add DP noise to gradients, do not fully exploit the geometric
051   nature of SGD (i.e., descending gradient to locate the optima). As reviewed in Section E.3, SGD
052   exhibits a distinctive geometric property — the direction of a gradient rather than the magnitude
053   determines the descent trend. By contrast, regular DP algorithms, such as the Gaussian mecha-
      nism (Dwork et al., 2014), were originally designed to preserve numerical (scalar) values instead

522   6   L ITERATURE REVIEW
523
524   As a privacy-preserving technique for training various models, DP-SGD is an adaptation of the tradi-
525   tional SGD algorithm to incorporate differentially private guarantees. Chaudhuri et al. 2011 initially
526   introduced a DP-SGD algorithm for empirical risk minimization. Abadi et al. 2016 were one of the
527   first to introduce DP-SGD into deep learning. Afterwards, DP-SGD has been rapidly applied to var-
528   ious models, such as generative adversarial network (Ho et al., 2021), Bayesian learning (Heikkilä
529   et al., 2017), federated learning (Zhang et al., 2022), graph neural networks (Zhang et al., 2024b).
530   More comprehensive related works on DP, SGD and DP-SGD are described in Appendix E.
531
532   7   C ONCLUSION
533
534   In this work, we first theoretically analyze the impact of DP noise on the training process of SGD,
535   which shows that the perturbation of DP-SGD is actually sub-optimal because it introduces biased
536   noise to the direction. This inspires us to reduce the noise on direction for model efficiency improve-
537   ment. We then propose our geometric perturbation mechanism GeoDP. Its effectiveness and gener-
538   ality are mutually confirmed by both rigorous proofs and experimental results. As for future work,
539   we plan to study the impact of mainstream training optimizations, such as Adam optimizer (Tang
      et al., 2024), on GeoDP.
