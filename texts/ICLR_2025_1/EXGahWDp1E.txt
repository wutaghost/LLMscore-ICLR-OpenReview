011                                                   A BSTRACT
012
013                Constrained optimization problems arise in various engineering system operations
014
                   such as inventory management and electric power grids. However, the requirement
                   to repeatedly solve such optimization problems with uncertain parameters poses
015
                   a significant computational challenge. This work introduces a learning scheme
016
                   using Bayesian Neural Networks (BNNs) to solve constrained optimization prob-
017                lems under limited labeled data and restricted model training times. We propose
018                a semi-supervised BNN for this practical but complex regime, wherein training
019                commences in a sandwiched fashion, alternating between a supervised learning
020                step (using labeled data) for minimizing cost, and an unsupervised learning step
021                (using unlabeled data) for enforcing constraint feasibility. Both supervised and
022                unsupervised steps use a Bayesian approach, where Stochastic Variational Infer-
023                ence is employed for approximate Bayesian inference. We show that the proposed
024                semi-supervised learning method outperforms conventional BNN and deep neural
025
                   network (DNN) architectures on important non-convex constrained optimization
                   problems from energy network operations, achieving up to a tenfold reduction in
026
                   expected maximum equality gap and halving the optimality and inequality (fea-
027
                   sibility) gaps, without requiring any correction or projection step. By leveraging
028                the BNN’s ability to provide posterior samples at minimal computational cost, we
029                demonstrate that a Selection via Posterior (SvP) scheme can further reduce equal-
030                ity gaps by more than 10%. We also provide tight and practically meaningful
031                probabilistic confidence bounds that can be constructed using a low number of
032                labeled testing data and readily adapted to other applications.

      1       I NTRODUCTION
036
037
      Constrained optimization problems are fundamental in the optimal operation of various engineer-
      ing systems, such as supply chains, transportation networks, and power grids. Learning a forward
038
      mapping between the inputs and outputs of these problems can significantly reduce computational
039
      burdens, especially when rapid solutions are required, such as in electricity market clearing or real-
040   time transportation planning.
041
042   Recent advancements in machine learning (ML) have led to considerable efforts to solve optimiza-
043
      tion problems using deep neural networks (DNNs) Khadivi et al. (2023); Kotary et al. (2021); Fa-
      jemisin et al. (2024). The idea of learning input-to-output mappings has been explored via su-
044
      pervised and unsupervised methods, particularly in power system applications Zamzam & Baker
045
      (2020); Donti et al. (2021); Park & Van Hentenryck (2023); Fioretto et al. (2020); Kotary et al.
046   (2021); Rolnick et al. (2022). Additionally, constraint penalization approaches have been proposed
047   to enforce feasibility in predicted outputs within DNN loss functions AI4OPT (2023).
048
049
      Supervised DNN models rely on labeled datasets obtained by solving numerous instances of op-
      timization problems. This data generation step poses a significant limitation due to the prohibitive
050
      computational time required for moderate to large problem instances. For example, Park & Van Hen-
051
      tenryck (2023) report that generating labeled data to train supervised DNN models for a medium-
052   sized power grid problem takes over three hours1 . Unsupervised methods aim to address the labeled

055   high training time requirements. Methods requiring projection steps to recover feasible solutions
056   involve time-consuming constraint correction within the framework Donti et al. (2021); Gupta et al.
057   (2022); Zamzam & Baker (2020), resulting in slower training and prediction. Moreover, unsuper-

075

078
      signing optimization proxies with guaranteed confidence bounds in the setting of limited total time
      requirement and limited labeled sample availability. For practical applicability, the total time must
079
      account for training and testing data generation, model training time and prediction time. Our major
080
      contribution is the development of a semi-supervised Bayesian Neural Network (BNN) approach
081   for this setting, that can be used to give tight confidence bounds on predictions. First, we pro-
082   pose using BNNs instead of DNNs for learning input-to-output mappings, as they provide intrin-
083   sic uncertainty quantification and allow the integration of prior beliefs Papamarkou et al. (2024).

088   bounds Audibert et al. (2007). We intentionally restrict ourselves to 10 minutes of training time
089
      on a single CPU core to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed learning scheme under low-
      data, low-compute settings. For various power grid optimization problem instances, we show that
090
      (i) supervised BNNs outperform standard supervised DNN approaches under limited training time;
091
      (ii) the proposed Sandwich BNN enforces feasibility better than supervised BNNs without requiring

093   pected error bounds are tight and practically useful for constraint satisfaction studies without extra
094   computational effort.

098   In recent years, Deep Neural Networks (DNNs) have been applied to solve various optimization
099   problems with physics-based constraints, particularly in energy networks Zamzam & Baker (2020);

102   machine learning proxies, enabling instantaneous solutions to a large number of problem instances
103   Park & Van Hentenryck (2023); Donti et al. (2021); Gupta et al. (2022); Zamzam & Baker (2020).
104   Outside the realm of optimization proxies, several semi-supervised learning methods have been pro-
105   posed to leverage unlabeled data for improving ML model performance Yang et al. (2022). These
106   approaches include augmenting unlabeled data with inexpensive pseudo-labels and developing un-
107   supervised loss functions to be minimized alongside supervised loss functions Sharma et al. (2023);
      Yang et al. (2022). Data augmentation has been used in image classification with Bayesian Neu-

358   (PDL) Park & Van Hentenryck (2023) and DC3 Donti et al. (2021) due to their significantly higher
359   training times and computational demands, which violate the premise of this paper. For example,
360   PDL requires over 125 minutes of training time for the ACOPF problem on a 118-node power net-
361   work using a Tesla RTX6000 GPU. Methods that require solving alternating current power flow to
362   recover solutions (e.g., Zamzam & Baker (2020)) are also excluded, as they result in high prediction
363   times compared to DNN or BNN forward passes5 .
364   To demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed BNN learning methods, we conduct simulation
365   studies on both our models and the ML4OPF models using an M1 Max CPU with 32 GB RAM,
366   without any GPU. This setup highlights performance improvements due to the learning mechanism

474    6                         C ONCLUSIONS
475
476   In conclusion, this paper introduces a semi-supervised Bayesian Neural Network (BNN) approach
477   to address the challenges of high labeled data requirements and limited training time in learning
478   input-to-output maps for constrained optimization problems. The proposed Sandwich BNN method
479   incorporates unlabeled data through input data augmentation, ensuring constraint feasibility without
480   relying on a large number of labeled instances. We provide tight confidence bounds by utilizing
481   Bernstein’s inequality, enhancing the method’s practical applicability. Results show that BNNs out-
482   perform DNNs in low-data, low-compute settings, and the Sandwich BNN more effectively enforces
483
      feasibility without additional computational costs compared to supervised BNNs.
