                                                                                 A BSTRACT
034
035
036                The remarkable development of text-to-image generation models has raised no-
037
                   table security concerns, such as the infringement of portrait rights and the gener-
                   ation of inappropriate content. Concept erasure has been proposed to remove the
038
                   model’s knowledge about protected and inappropriate concepts. Although many
039
                   methods have tried to balance the efficacy (erasing target concepts) and specificity
040                (retaining irrelevant concepts), they can still generate abundant erasure concepts
041                under the steering of semantically related inputs. In this work, we propose RealEra
042                to address this ”concept residue” issue. Specifically, we first introduce the mech-
043                anism of neighbor-concept mining, digging out the associated concepts by adding
044                random perturbation into the embedding of erasure concept, thus expanding the
045                erasing range and eliminating the generations even through associated concept
046                inputs. Furthermore, to mitigate the negative impact on the generation of irrel-
047                evant concepts caused by the expansion of erasure scope, RealEra preserves the
048
                   specificity through the beyond-concept regularization. This makes irrelevant con-
                   cepts maintain their corresponding spatial position, thereby preserving their nor-
049
                   mal generation performance. We also employ the closed-form solution to optimize
050
                   weights of U-Net for the cross-attention alignment, as well as the prediction noise
051                alignment with the LoRA module. Extensive experiments on multiple benchmarks
052                demonstrate that RealEra outperforms previous concept erasing methods in terms
053                of superior erasing efficacy, specificity, and generality.

      1    I NTRODUCTION
055
056
057
058
059
      In recent years, the surge of generative artificial intelligence (GAI) has brought historic opportunities
060
      for the development of various fields, especially in text-to-image generation (T2I) (Nichol et al.,
061   2022; Ramesh et al., 2022; Rombach et al., 2022; Saharia et al., 2022b). The T2I diffusion models
062   have produced images of remarkable quality, gratitude to its training on large-scale Internet datasets.
063   However, these unfiltered large-scale datasets contains abundance of Not-Safe-For-Work (NSFW)
064   content (Hunter, 2023; Zhang et al., 2023), as well as images involving intellectual property (Jiang
065   et al., 2023; Roose, 2022; Setty, 2023) or portrait rights (Somepalli et al., 2023). Diffusion models
066   even learn and memorize these concepts (Carlini et al., 2023; Kumari et al., 2023), making it easy
067   for users to generate harmful or infringing content, and leading to the spread of disinformation and
068   greater harm to the society.
069   To address this security issue, researchers have designed several safety mechanisms for T2I dif-
070   fusion models. An intuitive solution is to retrain the model using the filtered images (Rombach,
071   2022), which whereas not only requires expensive computational costs but also leads to a decrease
072   in generation quality. In addition, the NSFW safety checker which tries to filter out the inappropriate
073   results after generation (Rando et al., 2022), while the classifier-free guidance aims at eliminating
074   the concept generation in inference phase (Schramowski et al., 2023). However, they can be easily
075
      circumvented by malicious users due to the open-source model parameters and code.
076   Recently, some methods propose to erase these concepts by fine-tuning T2I diffusion models
077   (Gandikota et al., 2023; 2024; Zhang et al., 2024; Kumari et al., 2023; Heng & Soh, 2024; Lu
078   et al., 2024; Lyu et al., 2024). Specifically, for text inputs containing inappropriate concepts, they
079   adjust the internal parameters of generation model through fine-tuning, so that the generated content
080   no longer contains these concepts. Previous work has reached a consensus on the need to solve the
081
      trade-off between efficacy and specificity in concept erasure. Given a text input containing the era-
      sure concept, efficacy means that the model outputs irrelevant content while maintaining the overall
082
      naturalness. While specificity implies that if the text input has no relation to the erasure concept, the
083
      output should remain identical to the original model before erasure.
084
085   Despite their appealing performance, they fail to produce surprising results when encountering im-
086   plicitly associated input concept. As shown in Figure 1, for text inputs closely associated in se-
087
      mantics but not explicitly containing the erasure concept, previous methods still generate objects of
      erasure concept. For example, when it comes to concept of ”Tom Cruise”, if we input ”A still of
088
      Mission Impossible”, which is Tom Cruise’s most iconic work, previous method can still generate a
089
      portrait of Tom Cruise. Note that ”Mission Impossible” is a concept closely associated with ”Tom
090   Cruise”, which whereas doesn’t explicitly include ”Tom Cruise”. We define this as the concept
091   residue issue, i.e., erasure concept still exists in some implicitly associated concepts. This fails to be
092   tackled by previous methods, which to some extent considered as the word-level erasure, and thus
093   becomes the motivation of this work.
094
      In this paper, we propose a novel concept erasure framework, named RealEra, which prevents the
095
      diffusion model from regenerating erasure concepts with semantically related inputs. Specifically,
096   we first mine associated concepts by randomly sampling within the vicinity space of erasure concept.
097   By introducing the stochasticity into erasure concept’s embedding and shifting it to an associated
098   concept, RealEra steers the associated concept to the anchor concept. Meanwhile, erasing one con-
099   cept from diffusion models should prevent the catastrophic forgetting of others, whereas simply sup-
100   pressing the generation of erasure concept leads to severe concept erosion. To maintain specificity
101   preservation, we introduce the beyond-concept regularization, which turns the erasure concept into
102   concepts that are far away and irrelevant by sampling perturbation outside the neighborhood range.
103   This makes irrelevant concepts maintain their corresponding spatial position, thereby preserving
104
      their normal generation performance. Subsequently, we employ the closed-form solution to opti-
      mize weights of U-Net for the cross-attention alignment, as well as the prediction noise alignment
105
      with the LoRA module. RealEra achieves superior performance in both erasing assigned concepts,
106
      and preserving the generation ability of other unrelated concepts.
107
      Our contributions can be summarized as follows:

119   2.1   T EXT- TO -I MAGE G ENERATION
120
121   Training on large-scale datasets, the text-to-image (T2I) generation models have achieved great suc-
122   cess recently. T2I generation involves creating visual images from textual prompts, which has made
123
      significant advances with diffusion models. Various methods have been developed to achieve high-
      resolution text-to-image generations. As a pioneer work, GLIDE (Nichol et al., 2022) trains a 3.5B
124
      text-conditional diffusion model at a 64 × 64 resolution, as well as a 1.5B parameter text-conditional
125
      up-sampling diffusion model to increase the resolution to 256 × 256. DALL-E 2 (Ramesh, 2023)
126   proposes transforming a CLIP (Radford et al., 2021) text embedding into a CLIP image embed-
127   ding with a prior model, and then decoding this image embedding into the image. Imagen (Saharia
128   et al., 2022a) adopts a cascaded diffusion model and T5, a large pretrained language model, as text
129   Encoder to generate images. Stable Diffusion (SD) (Rombach et al., 2022) is built on the latent
130   diffusion model, which operates on the latent space instead of pixel space, enabling SD to generate
131   high-resolution images. SD v1.x employs 123.65M CLIP as text encoder and trains at different steps
132   on the laion-improved-aesthetics or laion-aesthetics v2 5+ datasets. SD v2.x uses laion-aesthetics v2
133   4.5+ datasets, a larger dataset and 354.03M OpenCLIP (Cherti et al., 2023), a more powerful CLIP
134
      text encoder.
135
136   2.2   C ONCEPT E RASURE IN D IFFUSION M ODELS
137
      T2I models (Nichol et al., 2022; Ramesh et al., 2022; Rombach et al., 2022; Saharia et al., 2022b) are
138
      mostly trained on large-scale web-scraped datasets, such as LAION-5B (Schuhmann et al., 2022).
139   Unfiltered datasets can cause T2I models to learn and generate a series of inappropriate content that
140   violates copyright and privacy. To alleviate this concern, many studies explore and devising vari-
141   ous solutions: training datasets filtering (Rombach, 2022), post-generation content filtering (Rando
142   et al., 2022), classifier-free guidance (Schramowski et al., 2023), and fine-tuning pretrained mod-
143   els (Gandikota et al., 2023; 2024; Zhang et al., 2024; Kumari et al., 2023; Heng & Soh, 2024; Lu
144   et al., 2024; Lyu et al., 2024). The Stable Diffusion 2.0 (Rombach, 2022) applies an NSFW detector
145   to filter inappropriate content from the training dataset, but this leads to high retraining costs and
146   generation quality decrease. Post-generation content filtering adopts the safety checker to filter out
147
      NSFW content, but this can easily be disabled by users. SLD (Schramowski et al., 2023) suppresses
      the generation of inappropriate content during the inference process with negative guidance, based
148
      on the classifier-free guidance.
149
150   Recently, some researches erase inappropriate concepts by fine-tuning the parameters of the T2I
151   models. ESD (Gandikota et al., 2023) fine-tunes the pretrained model by guiding the model output
152   away from the erasure concept with a negative conditioned score, so that the model learns from its
153
      own knowledge to steer the diffusion process away from the undesired concept. FMN (Zhang et al.,
      2024) utilizes attention re-steering to fine-tune UNet to minimize each of the intermediate attention
154
      maps associated with the erasure concepts. AC (Kumari et al., 2023) fine-tunes the model to match
155
      the prediction noise between the erasure concepts and corresponding anchor concepts, so that steer-
156   ing the erasure concepts towards anchor concepts. SA (Heng & Soh, 2024) incorporates EWC and
157   generative replay to forget the erasure concept and remember the retention concepts, respectively.
158   UCE (Gandikota et al., 2024) employs a closed-form solution to optimize the cross-attention weights
159   of pretrained models, thereby mapping erasure concepts to anchor concepts. MACE (Lu et al., 2024)
160   trains a separate LoRA module for each erasure concept by combining a closed-form method and
161   minimizing activation values of erasure concept, and fuses multiple LoRA modules to achieve mass
      concepts erasure. SPM (Lyu et al., 2024) proposes to train a lightweight adapter for each erasure

163   and utilizes an facilitated transport mechanism to regulate the multiple concepts erasure. However,
164   these methods have not focused on the erasure performance of implicitly associated concepts while
165   ensuring overall erasure performance.

      3.1   L ATENT D IFFUSION M ODEL
171
      To enhance the generative efficiency of the diffusion model, Latent Diffusion Model (LDM) (Rom-
172   bach et al., 2022) proposes to shift the diffusion process from the pixel space of the images to the
173   low-dimensional latent space, so it needs to train a VAE (Kingma, 2013) model to encode and de-
174   code images. In addition, in order to achieve conditional generation, text or image is converted
175   into condition embedding and fed into the diffusion model, then the diffusion process is controlled
176   conditionally by the attention mechanism in U-Net. Given a user’s input prompt, it is first encoded
177   into text embedding by the text encoder. The text embedding will then be projected into K and V
178   vectors respectively by the projection matrix WK and WV . Then, the K vectors dot-product with
179   Q vectors from the noisy image to get the attention maps. The image-text fusion feature is obtained
180
      by multiplying the attention maps and V vectors, then the final predicted noise is derived from the
      subsequent network structure of U-Net. The final training optimization objective is:

188   3.2   L OW-R ANK A DAPTATION
189
190   To reduce the cost of downstream transfer learning for large-scale models, the concept of parameter-
191   efficient fine-tuning (PEFT) (Mangrulkar et al., 2022) has been introduced. Low-Rank Adaptation
192   (LoRA) (Hu et al., 2021), as a structure in PEFT, enhances parameter efficiency by freezing the pre-
193
      trained weight matrices and integrating additional trainable low-rank matrices within the network.
      This method is based on the observation that pre-trained models exhibit low “intrinsic dimension”.
194
      Given the pretrained weights matrix of the diffusion model W ∈ Rm×n , LoRA constrain its update
195
      with a low-rank decomposition W ′ = W + BA, where B ∈ Rm×r and A ∈ Rr×n , and satisfying
196   r ≪ min(n, m). In training phase, only A and B are trainable and receive gradient updates, while
197   W ′ is frozen. W ′ and BA multiply the same input and sum them to output. Thus, as for input x and
198   output h:
199                                        h = W ′ x = W x + BAx,                                      (2)
200
      where LoRA adopts a random Gaussian initialization for A and zero for B.

      The previous methods mainly focus on mapping the erasure concepts to the anchor one. How-
      ever, as for erasure concept, there are still multiple related concepts within its neighborhood that
208
      can easily condition the diffusion model to generate erasure concepts, e.g., ”airport” and ”An-225
209
      Mriya” for ”airplane”, and ”Mission Impossible” for ”Tom Cruise”. Therefore, to prevent the model
210   from generating erasure concept through these associated concepts, we propose the mechanism of
211   Neighbor-Concept Mining. Specifically, when fine-tuning the model, we add random perturbations
212   to the input embedding of erasure concept, shifting them towards associated concepts in the adjacent
213   semantic space. We fine-tune the diffusion model by mapping both these mined-out concepts and
214   erasure concept to the anchor concept. Regarding the addition of stochasticity introduced by ran-
215   dom sampling, we design the following scheme: suppose we have the prompt pc corresponding to
      the erasure concept c, whose corresponding embedding is e, and the defined perturbation as η. For

267   4.2     S PECIFICITY P RESERVATION WITH B EYOND -C ONCEPT R EGULARIZATION
268
269   Despite delving into the associated concepts, directly mapping them to the anchor concept will
      greatly affect the generation of other unrelated concepts. Although we can balance the performance

271   still sub-optimal. We argue that when the mapping relationships of most data points within the
272   erasure concept neighborhood are changed, concepts in a larger range in the same manifold space

278   model’s generative capability.

      6   L IMITATIONS
520
521
522   We focus on the phenomenon that may lead to the regeneration of erasure concepts in concept
523   erasing and define it as the “concept residual”. We achieve excellent erasing results and effectively
524   preserve other concepts, but the trade-off between efficacy and specificity is yet a challenge since
525
      associated concepts inevitably expand the scope of erasing. In addition, the canonical definition of
      associated concepts and the controllability of erasure scope are also issues worth exploring by the
526
      relevant communities in the future.

530   7   C ONCLUSION
531
532
      This paper focuses on solving the challenge of concept residue, and proposes the novel RealEra
533
      method, aiming to achieve semantic-level concept erasure in the diffusion model. The modified
534   diffusion model can circumvent malicious users from generating inappropriate content by feeding
535   implicitly associated concepts, defined as the Concept Residue issue. RealEra shifts the erasure con-
536   cept into associated concepts by sampling perturbation in its neighborhood, while sampling outside
537   the neighborhood to maintain the generation ability of unrelated concepts. Extensive evaluations
538   have demonstrated the superior efficacy and generality of RealEra over existing concept erasing
539   methods. We hope our work will inspire future research on comprehensively and precisely erasing
      inappropriate concepts in generative models.
