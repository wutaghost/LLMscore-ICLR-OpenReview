                                                  A BSTRACT
008
009
              Large Language Models (LLMs) have greatly advanced the field of General Ar-
010
              tificial Intelligence, yet their security vulnerabilities remain a pressing issue, par-
011
              ticularly in fine-tuned models. Adversarial attacks in black-box settings—where
012           model details and training data are obscured—are an emerging area of research,
013           posing a substantial threat to private models’ integrity. In this work, we uncover
014           a new attack vector: adversaries can exploit the similarities between open-source
015           LLMs and fine-tuned private models to transfer adversarial examples. We intro-
016           duce a novel attack strategy that generates adversarial examples on open-source
017           models and fine-tunes them to target private, black-box models. Our experiments
018           show that these attacks achieve success rates comparable to white-box attacks,
019           even when private models have been trained on proprietary data. Furthermore, our
020
              approach demonstrates strong transferability to other models, including LLaMA3
              and ChatGPT. These findings highlight the urgent need for more robust defenses
021
              when fine-tuning open-source LLMs.
