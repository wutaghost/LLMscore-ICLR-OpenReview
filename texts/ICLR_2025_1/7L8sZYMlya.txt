009                                                A BSTRACT
010
011            Since the advent of knowledge distillation, much research has focused on how the
012            soft labels generated by the teacher model can be utilized effectively. Allen-Zhu
013            & Li (2020) points out that the implicit knowledge within soft labels originates
014            from the multi-view structure present in the data. Feature variations within sam-
015            ples of the same class allow the student model to generalize better by learning
016
               diverse representations. However, in existing distillation methods, teacher models
               predominantly adhere to ground-truth labels as targets, without considering the
017
               diverse representations within the same class. Therefore, we propose incorporating
018
               an intra-class contrastive loss during teacher training to enrich the intra-class in-
019            formation contained in soft labels. In practice, we find that intra-class loss causes
020            instability in training and slows convergence. To mitigate these issues, margin loss
021            is integrated into intra-class contrastive learning to improve the training stability
022            and convergence speed. Simultaneously, we theoretically analyze the impact of this
023            loss on the intra-class distances and inter-class distances. It has been proved that
024            the intra-class contrastive loss can enrich the intra-class diversity. Experimental
025            results demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed method.

      Knowledge distillation (KD) is a technique in deep learning where a smaller model is trained to
030   replicate the behavior of a larger, more complex model Hinton et al. (2015); Ji et al. (2023). This
031   approach is valuable for compressing large models Kim et al. (2018); Jin et al. (2021); Gu et al.
032   (2023), transfer learning Vapnik et al. (2015); Noroozi et al. (2018) and enhancing the performance
033   of smaller modelsBuciluǎ et al. (2006); Romero et al. (2014). Therefore, KD has gained popularity
034   across various tasks, such as image classification Mobahi et al. (2020); Yuan et al. (2020), natural
035   language processing Rashid et al. (2020); Yang et al. (2020), and multimodal learning Dai et al.
036   (2022).
037   Soft labels play a crucial role in knowledge distillation by offering more comprehensive information
038   regarding the data distribution Menon et al. (2021); Zhou & Song (2021). They encapsulate the
039   knowledge of the teacher model, encompassing not only the relative probabilities of different classes
040   but also the intra-class variances, which are not reflected in the hard labels. Allen-Zhu & Li (2020)
041   proposes the multi-view data assumption, which is validated by real-world datasets, and demonstrates
042   that students can learn features of other classes present in the soft labels. These points also constitute
043   the core hypothesis of this paper, that soft labels express the variances among samples of the same
044
      class, which is absent in ground-truth labels.
045   In fact, existing methods for training teacher models can be roughly divided into two categories. One
046   targets the ground-truth (possibly with regularization) Tian et al. (2019); Chen et al. (2021a), and the
047   other involves joint training of teacher and student models Chen et al. (2020); Xu et al.; Neitz et al.
048   (2020); Yuan et al. (2021), where the teacher’s soft labels could be adjusted based on feedback from
049   the student. These two methods are neither designed for intra-class diversity nor provide parameters
050
      to control intra-class variation. The soft labels generated by the teacher model may fail to capture a
      significant amount of valuable intra-class information.
051
052   Therefore, we propose incorporating an intra-class contrastive loss as an auxiliary loss during teacher
053   training. This can enrich the intra-class information within soft labels, preventing the soft labels
      from being overly similar to the ground truth due to the model’s strong fitting capability. Similar to

      Knowledge Distillation has gained significant attention Buciluǎ et al. (2006); Hinton et al. (2015)
101
      in the field of deep learning. It was initially proposed for model compression Kim et al. (2018);
102   Jin et al. (2021), and later widely adopted for knowledge transfer Vapnik et al. (2015); Zagoruyko
103   & Komodakis (2016), or as a trick to enhance performance Guo et al. (2023); Jin et al. (2023). In
104   traditional knowledge distillation, one teacher model teach one student model. Self-distillation Zhang
105   et al. (2019); Lee et al. (2019) is a variant of distillation where a single model acts both as the teacher
106   and the student. During training, a tradeoff between the ground-truth and the model’s previous outputs
107   is used as the target for subsequent training. Typically, the tradeoff parameter in self-distillation
      varies across epochs. In ensemble distillation You et al. (2017); Zhu et al. (2018), the outputs from

108
      multiple teachers are integrated and used to instruct the student. In mutual learning (peer learning)
109   Zhang et al. (2018); Chen et al. (2020), multiple models learn from each other or use the aggregated
110   knowledge as a common teacher.
111
112
      Although there are many variants of distillation, a significant focus remains on the dark knowledge
      hidden in soft labels. Most explanations regarding dark knowledge are empirically validated. However,
113
      theoretical analyses of this concept are diverse and subject to debate. Phuong & Lampert (2019)
114
      studied the distillation mechanism with assumption that both the teacher and the student models are
115   linear. Similarly, Allen-Zhu & Li (2020) hypothesized that data adhere to a multi-view structure,
116   where samples from different classes may share similar features. They demonstrated the effectiveness
117   of soft labels, which stems from their ability to enable learning of information from other classes
118   present in the samples. Research by Mobahi et al. (2020) analyzed the teacher model in self-
119   distillation using Green’s function, and regard self-distillation as a form of regularization. On a
120   statistical front, Menon et al. (2021) and Zhou & Song (2021) treated the generated soft labels as
121   posterior probabilities and posited the existence of the Bayes probability. Additionally, there are other
122   studies analyzing soft labels from the perspective of transfer risk Ji & Zhu (2020); Hsu et al. (2021).
123   Contrastive Learning has emerged as a powerful mechanism for learning effective representations
124   Logeswaran & Lee (2018); Oord et al. (2018); Tian et al. (2020a) by contrasting positive pairs
125   against negative pairs Mikolov et al. (2013). This technique is primarily used in unsupervised or
126   self-supervised learning environments where labeled data is scarce or expensive to obtain. Contrastive
127   earning hinges on the idea that an encoder should map semantically similar (positive) samples closer
128   in the embedding space, while semantically different (negative) samples should be farther apart
129
      Hadsell et al. (2006); Sohn (2016a). Sohn (2016b) introduces multi-class N-pair loss instead of
      traditional triplet loss. Wu et al. (2018) proposes instance discrimination to learn an embedding
130
      that can repell each pair of two training data. He et al. (2020) uses a dynamic dictionary with a
131
      momentum-updated encoder to efficiently handle large-scale data in contrastive learning so that more
132   negative samples can be used. Gutmann & Hyvärinen (2010) assumes that the data originate from a
133   distribution that can be parametrically clustered.
134
135
      In addition to empirical work, there are some theoretical results in contrastive learning. Saunshi
      et al. (2019) analyzed the generalization error bounds for downstream classifiers with representations
136
      obtained from contrastive learning, based on the assumption of latent classes. Saunshi et al. (2019)
137
      argue that reducing the mutual information between views is beneficial to downstream classification
138   accuracy. Tian et al. (2020b) proved that contrastive loss can help align features from positive pairs
139   and features from different classes will uniformly distributed on the hypersphere. Parulekar et al.
140   (2023) demonstrated that the representations learned by minimizing InfoNCE Loss, even with a
141   limited number of negative samples, are consistent with the clusters inherent in the data. They further
142   proved that when combined with a two-layer ReLU head, the learned representations can achieve
143   zero downstream error on any binary classification task that preserves clustering. HaoChen et al.
144   (2021) analyzed contrastive learning by constructing an augmentation graph and demonstrated that
145   features obtained by minimizing spectral contrastive loss have provable accuracy guarantees under
146
      linear probe evaluation.

174   The loss function for the student model is defined as:

186   approximate the soft label q. The dark knowledge in the soft label q originates from the teacher’s
187   output pt . Existing distillation methods mostly focus on how the student aligns with the teacher,
188   paying less attention to the learning objectives of the teacher model. In most distillation approaches,
189   the teacher typically learns from real labels (with regularization). If the network has strong fitting
190   capability, it may also result in the teacher’s soft labels being very similar to the real labels. This can
191   potentially lead to the reduction of intra-class information within soft labels, consequently resulting
192
      in performance degradation.
193   Therefore, we propose incorporating intra-class contrastive learning into teacher training to extract
194   intra-class information. Traditional contrastive learning aims to maximize inter-class distance and
195   minimize intra-class distance to learn discriminative and robust representations. Intra-class contrastive
196   learning encourages the teacher model to learn embeddings where samples from the same class are
197   dispersed appropriately, while still being distinguishable within their respective classes, thus enriching
198
      the soft label information in knowledge distillation.

427   with the learning rate being reduced three times at epochs [30, 60]. When training the student model,

      6     C ONCLUSION
474
475
476   In this work, we introduced the Margin-Based Intra-Class Contrastive Distillation approach, which
477
      integrates intra-class contrastive learning with traditional knowledge distillation. This method not
      only enriches the soft labels with nuanced intra-class variations but also maintains a balance between
478
      inter-class and intra-class distinctions, which is vital for the robust generalization of the student
479
      model. Our method significantly enhances the performance of the student model by leveraging
480   enriched soft labels, demonstrating superior results across standard image classification datasets.
481   Both the integration of margin loss and the design of pipeline ensure the stability and efficiency of the
482   learning process, addressing potential issues related to convergence and model training dynamics. The
483   theoretical results also confirm the feasibility of our method and highlight the role of the parameter
484   λ in balancing the tradeoff. Overall, our approach provides a compelling framework for effectively
485   utilizing the soft labels in knowledge distillation, paving the way for future innovations in model
      compression and efficient learning strategies.
