009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011
               Diffusion models have emerged as the de facto choice for generating high-quality
012            visual signals across various domains. However, training a single model to predict
013            noise across various levels poses significant challenges, necessitating numerous
014            iterations and incurring significant computational costs. Various approaches, such
015            as loss weighting strategy design and architectural refinements, have been intro-
016            duced to expedite convergence and improve model performance. In this study, we
017            propose a novel approach to design the noise schedule for enhancing the training
018            of diffusion models. Our key insight is that the importance sampling of the log-
019            arithm of the Signal-to-Noise ratio (log SNR), theoretically equivalent to a modi-
020
               fied noise schedule, is particularly beneficial for training efficiency when increas-
               ing the sample frequency around log SNR = 0. This strategic sampling allows
021
               the model to focus on the critical transition point between signal dominance and
022
               noise dominance, potentially leading to more robust and accurate predictions. We
023            empirically demonstrate the superiority of our noise schedule over the standard
024            cosine schedule. Furthermore, we highlight the advantages of our noise schedule
025            design on the ImageNet benchmark, showing that the designed schedule consis-
026            tently benefits different prediction targets. Our findings contribute to the ongoing
027            efforts to optimize diffusion models, potentially paving the way for more efficient
028            and effective training paradigms in the field of generative AI.

031   1   I NTRODUCTION
032
033
      Diffusion models have emerged as a pivotal technique for generating high-quality visual signals
034
      across diverse domains, including image synthesis (Ramesh et al., 2022; Saharia et al., 2022; Rom-
035
      bach et al., 2022) , video generation (Ho et al., 2022; Singer et al., 2023; Brooks et al., 2024), and
036   even 3D object generation (Wang et al., 2022; Nichol et al., 2022). One of the key strengths of diffu-
037   sion models lies in their ability to approximate complex distributions, where Generative Adversarial
038   Networks (GANs) may encounter difficulties. Despite the substantial computational resources and
039   numerous training iterations required for convergence, improving the training efficiency of diffu-
040   sion models is essential for their application in large-scale scenarios, such as high-resolution image
041   synthesis and long video generation.
042   Recent efforts to enhance diffusion model training efficiency have primarily focused on two direc-
043   tions. The first approach centers on architectural improvements. For instance, the use of Adaptive
044   Layer Normalization (Gu et al., 2022), when combined with zero initialization in the Transformer
045   architecture Peebles & Xie (2023), has shown promising results. MM-DiT (Esser et al., 2024) ex-
046   tends this approach to multi-modality by employing separate weights for vision and text processing.
047   Similarly, U-shaped skip connections within Transformers (Hoogeboom et al., 2023; Bao et al.,
048   2022; Crowson et al., 2024) and reengineered layer designs (Karras et al., 2024) have contributed to
049   more efficient learning processes.
050   The second direction explores various loss weighting strategies to accelerate training convergence.
051   Works such as eDiff-I (Balaji et al., 2022) and Ernie-ViLG 2.0 (Feng et al., 2022) address training
052   difficulties across noise intensities using a Mixture of Experts approach. Other studies have investi-
053   gated prioritizing specific noise levels (Choi et al., 2022) and reducing weights of noisy tasks (Hang
      et al., 2023) to enhance learning effectiveness. Recent developments include a softer weighting

078   Despite these advances, the fundamental role of noise scheduling in diffusion model training re-
079   mains underexplored. In this study, we present a novel approach focusing on the fundamental role
080
      of noise scheduling, which is a function that determines how much noise is added to the input data
      at each timestep t during the training process, controlling the distribution of noise levels that the
081
      neural network learns to remove. Our framework provides a unified perspective for analyzing noise
082
      schedules and importance sampling, leading to a straightforward method for designing noise sched-
083   ules through the identification of curves in the p(λ) distribution, as visualized in Figure 1. Through
084   empirical analysis, we discover that allocating more computation costs (FLOPs) to mid-range noise
085   levels (around log SNR = 0) yields superior performance compared to increasing loss weights dur-
086   ing the same period, particularly under constrained computational budgets.
087

088
      caled variants, through comprehensive experiments using the ImageNet benchmark with a consistent
089   training budget of 500K iterations (about 100 epochs). Our results, measured using the Fréchet In-
090   ception Distance (FID) metric at both 256 × 256 and 512 × 512 resolutions, demonstrate that noise
091   schedules with concentrated probability density around log SNR = 0 consistently outperform alter-
092   natives, with the Laplace schedule showing particularly favorable performance.
093

094
095          • A unified framework for analyzing and designing noise schedules in diffusion models, of-
096            fering a more systematic approach to noise schedule optimization.
097
             • Empirical evidence demonstrating the superiority of mid-range noise level focus over loss
098            weight adjustments for improving training efficiency.
099
             • Comprehensive evaluation and comparison of various noise schedules, providing practical
100
               guidelines for future research and applications in diffusion model training.

344
345   To explore the robustness of the adjusted noise schedule to different resolutions, we also designed
346   experiments on Imagenet-512. As pointed out by Chen (2023), the adding noise strategy will cause

352   1.0}, and determined that b = 0.75 was the most effective, resulting in an FID score of 9.09. This
353   indicates that despite the need for hyperparameter tuning, adjusting the noise schedule can still stably

362   3.5   A BLATION S TUDY

364   We conduct an ablation study to analyze the impact of hyperparameters on various distributions of
365   p(λ), which are enumerated below.
366   Laplace distribution, known for its simplicity and exponential decay from the center, is straight-
367   forward to implement. We leverage its symmetric nature and adjust the scale parameter to center
368   the peak at the middle timestep. We conduct experiments with different Laplace distribution scales
369   b ∈ {0.25, 0.5, 1.0, 2.0, 3.0}. The results are shown in Figure 3. The baseline with standard cosine
370   schedule achieves FID score of 17.79 with CFG=1.5, 10.85 with CFG=2.0, and 11.06 with CFG=3.0
371   after 500K iterations. We can see that the model with Laplace distribution scale b = 0.5 achieves
372
      the best performance 7.96 with CFG=3.0, which is relatively 26.6% better than the baseline.

423

      E FFICIENT D IFFUSION T RAINING
444
      Generally speaking, the diffusion model uses a network with shared parameters to denoise different
445   noise intensities. However, the different noise levels may introduce conflicts during training, which
446   makes the convergence slow. P2 (Choi et al., 2022) improves image generation performance by
447   prioritizing the learning of perceptually rich visual concepts during training through a redesigned
448   weighting scheme. Min-SNR (Hang et al., 2023) seeks the Pareto optimal direction for different
449   tasks, achieves better convergence on different predicting targets. HDiT (Crowson et al., 2024)
450   propose a soft version of Min-SNR to further improve the efficiency on high resolution image syn-
451   thesis. Stable Diffusion 3 (Esser et al., 2024) puts more sampling weight on the middle timesteps by
452   multiplying the distribution of logit normal distribution.
453   On the other hand, architecture modification is also explored to improve diffusion training.
454   DiT (Peebles & Xie, 2023) proposes adaptive Layer Normalization with zero initialization to im-
455   prove the training of Transformer architectures. Building upon this design, MM-DiT (Esser et al.,
456   2024) extends the approach to a multi-modal framework (text to image) by incorporating separate
457   sets of weights for each modality. HDiT (Crowson et al., 2024) uses a hierarchical transformer
458   structure for efficient, linear-scaling, high-resolution image generation. A more robust ADM UNet
459
      with better training dynamics is proposed in EDM2 (Karras et al., 2024) by preserving activation,
      weight, and update magnitudes. In this work, we directly adopt the design from DiT (Peebles &
460
      Xie, 2023) and focus on investigating the importance sampling schedule in diffusion models.
461
462
463   N OISE S CHEDULE D ESIGN FOR D IFFUSION M ODELS
464
      The design of the noise schedule plays a critical role in training diffusion models. In DDPM, Ho
465
      et al. (2020) propose linear schedule for the noise level, which was later adopted by Stable Dif-
466
      fusion (Rombach et al., 2022) version 1.5 and 2.0. However, the linear noise schedule introduces
467   signal leakage at the highest noise step (Lin et al., 2024; Tang et al., 2023), hindering performance
468   when sampling starts from a Gaussian distribution. Improved DDPM (Nichol & Dhariwal, 2021)
469   introduces a cosine schedule aimed at bringing the sample with the highest noise level closer to
470   pure Gaussian noise. EDM (Karras et al., 2022) proposes a new continuous framework and make
471   the logarithm of noise intensity sampled from a Gaussian distribution. Flow matching with optimal
472   transport (Lipman et al., 2022; Liu et al., 2022) linearly interpolates the noise and data point as the
473   input of flow-based models. Chen (2023) underscored the need for adapting the noise schedule
474   according to the image resolution. Hoogeboom et al. (2023) found that cosine schedule exhibits
475
      superior performance for images of 32 × 32 and 64 × 64 resolutions and propose to shift the cosine
      schedule to train on images with higher resolutions.
476
477
478   5     C ONCLUSION
479
480   In this paper, we present a novel method for enhancing the training of diffusion models by strate-
481   gically redefining the noise schedule. Our theoretical analysis demonstrates that this approach is
482   equivalent to performing importance sampling on the noise. Empirical results show that our pro-
483   posed Laplace noise schedule, which focuses computational resources on mid-range noise levels,
484   yields superior performance compared to adjusting loss weights under constrained computational
485   budgets. This study not only contributes significantly to the development of efficient training tech-
      niques for diffusion models but also offers promising potential for future large-scale applications.
