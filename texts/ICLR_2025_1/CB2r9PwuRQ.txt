011                                                A BSTRACT
012
013            Emotional Support Conversation (ESC) is a rapidly advancing task focused on al-
014            leviating a seeker’s emotional distress. The intricate interplay between cognition,
015            emotion, and behavior presents substantial challenges for existing approaches,
016
               which often struggle to capture the dynamic evolution of the seeker’s internal
               state during conversations. To address this, we propose CausalESC, a model de-
017
               signed to dynamically represent the seeker’s internal states, by assuming that the
018
               generative process governing the mutual influence among these factors follows a
019            first-order Markov property, with i.i.d. random variables. The model comprises
020            a prior network, that disentangles the seeker’s emotions, cognition, and behav-
021            ior, and a posterior network, which decouples the support strategy factors. The
022            prior network also models the psychological causality of the seeker within each
023            conversation round. To account for the varying effects of support strategies on
024            the seeker’s intrinsic states, we incorporate a support intervention module to cap-
025            ture these impacts. Additionally, a holistic damping transfer mechanism is de-
026            signed to regulate the complex interactions among cognition, emotion, behavior,
027
               and strategy, ensuring that changes remain within a reasonable range. Our model
               effectively breaks causal cycles and achieves causal representation learning. Both
028
               automatic and human evaluations demonstrate the effectiveness of our model, em-
029
               phasizing the advantages of modeling the evolution of the seeker’s internal state
030            under support strategies.

      In recent years, mental health problems have become increasingly prevalent, yet access to profes-
036   sional psychological counselors remains limited and costly. Consequently, there is an urgent need for
037   a chatbot capable of alleviating psychological issues. Emotional Support Conversation (ESC) (Liu
038   et al., 2021) is designed to reduce individuals’ distress by generating appropriate support strategies,
039   as illustrated in Fig. 1. This task holds significant potential in various fields, including mental health
040   support and social assistance.
041
      An increasing number of researchers are investigating ESC tasks. For instance, MISC(Tu et al.,
042   2022) uses COMET to infer the fine-grained mental state of the seeker and then employs a mixture
043   strategy to generate emotional support text. Similarly, GLHG(Peng et al., 2022) also utilizes com-
044   monsense knowledge to model local and global hierarchical relationships. Additionally, PAL(Cheng
045   et al., 2023) leverages the seekers’ persona to generate more informative and personalized responses.
046   Meanwhile, TransESC (Zhao et al., 2023b) constructs a state transition graph to model semantic,
047   strategy, and emotional transition, thereby generating effective responses.
048   While previous studies have attempted to model the psychological state of the seeker, they often fall
049   short by representing it as static snapshots, neglecting the continuous and dynamic evolution of the
050   seeker’s internal state throughout the conversation. Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)(Rothbaum
051   et al., 2000) emphasizes the importance of understanding the dynamic relationship between the indi-
052   vidual’s internal state and their environment during the therapeutic process, as well as the interaction
053   mechanisms among emotions, cognition, and behavior. This concept is illustrated in the left sub-

      2   R ELATED W ORK
128
      Emotional Support Conversation Since being proposed by (Liu et al., 2021), the ESC task has
129
      gathered significant attention. MISC(Tu et al., 2022) integrates commonsense knowledge and em-
130
      ploys mixed-strategy to guide response generation. GLHG(Peng et al., 2022) utilizes a graph-based
131   reasoner to model the hierarchical relation between global cause and local intention, capturing the
132   multi-source information. SUPPORTER(Zhou et al., 2023) formulates ESC as a process of eliciting
133   positive emotion and designs a mixture-of-expert-based mechanism with a reinforcement learning
134   approach. TransESC(Zhao et al., 2023b) focuses on the fine-grained turn-level transition of ESC,
135   including semantics, strategy, and emotion transition. KEMI(Deng et al., 2023) retrieves mental
136   health knowledge from a pre-trained knowledge graph and evaluates the model from the perspective
137   of mix-initiative. MFF-ESC(Bao et al., 2024) perceives emotional intensity transitions and proposes
138   an information network that integrates text semantics, feedback, and emotional intensity streams.
139
      However, all the aforrmentioned methods model the seeker’s state only statically. In contrast, we
      disentangle the seeker’s emotion, cognition, and behavior to simulate the dynamic evolution of the
140
      seeker’s internal state during the conversation.
141
142   Causal Disentangled Representation Learning In recent years, causal disentangled representa-
143   tion learning has garnered increasing attention from researchers, with its primary objective being
144   the discovery of high-level causal variables from low-level observations (Schölkopf, 2022). Most
145
      approaches in this field combine Structural Causal Models (SCM)(Pearl, 2009) with deep learning.
      For example, CausalVAE (Yang et al., 2021) employs a causal layer to transform exogenous vari-
146
      ables into causal endogenous factors that correspond to causally related concepts in data. (Li et al.,
147
      2021) present a novel causal hidden Markov model for sequential medical images for future disease
148   prediction. (Zhao et al., 2023c) analyze physical factors in multimodal traffic flow and proposes a
149   causal propose causal conditional hidden Markov model to predict traffic flow. For dialogue data,
150   (Su et al., 2024) propose a temporal causal disentanglement model that effectively decouples the
151   dialog content and realizes the temporal accumulation of emotions, enabling more accurate emotion
152   recognition. The concepts in the above causal disentangled learning are all directed acyclic, but
153   in our task, cognition, emotion, and behavior are interdependent, presenting a challenge for causal
154   representation learning. In our model, we assume the mutual influence of emotion, behavior, and
155   cognition follows a first-order Markov process with i.i.d., effectively resolving the issue of circular-
156
      ity.

530   5   C ONCLUSION AND F UTURE W ORK
531
532
      This paper proposes a new model, CausalESC, for capturing the evolution mechanism of cognitive,
533
      emotional, and behavioral during the dialogue process. The key contribution lies in breaking the
534   causal loop problem by assuming that the mutual influence of emotion, behavior, and cognition
535   follows a first-order Markov property with i.i.d.. variables. Additionally, a support intervention
536   module is proposed to consider the impact of the strategy on the seeker state, and a novel module
537   is developed to capture the complex transfer process. Experimental results on both automatic and
538   human evaluations show the superiority of our approach. In the future, we will explore more fine-
539   grained states of the seeker, including physiological responses, and capture the evolution of these
      states.

      C.1    E XPERIMENTAL S ETUPS
749
      For a fair comparison with previous work, we use the 90M BlenderBot(Roller et al., 2021) as the
750   base model. AdamW (Loshchilov & Hutter, 2019) is employed as the optimizer, and training is
751   conducted for 4 epochs with a batch size of 20. The learning rate starts at 2e-5 and incorporates a
752   linear warmup over 120 steps. The latent variable has a dimension of 48, with the dimension of the
753   cognitive, emotional, and behavioral factors each being 16. During the training process, we use the
754   KL annealing method to mitigate the KL vanishing problem. Training and testing are performed on
755   a single GeForce RTX 3090 GPU. For inference, the decoding algorithm utilized Top-p and Top-k
      sampling with parameters set to p = 0.3 and k = 30.
