011                                                A BSTRACT
012
013            Large language models (LLMs) have demonstrated remarkable performance across
014            various real-world tasks. However, they often struggle to fully comprehend and
015
               effectively utilize their input contexts, resulting in responses that are hallucinated.
               This difficulty increases for contexts that are long or contain distracting information,
016
               which can divert LLMs from fully capturing essential evidence. To address this
017
               issue, many works use prompting to help LLMs comprehend contextual information
018            more reliably. For instance, iterative prompting highlights key information in two
019            steps that first ask the LLM to identify important pieces of context and then derive
020            answers accordingly. However, textual prompting methods are constrained to
021            highlighting key information implicitly in token space, which is often insufficient
022            to fully steer the model’s attention. To improve model reading comprehension,
023            we propose SteerPrompt, a method that automatically identifies key contextual
024            information and explicitly highlights it by steering an LLM’s attention scores. Like
025            prompting, SteerPrompt is applied at inference time and does not require changing
026
               any model parameters. Our experiments on open-book QA demonstrate that
               SteerPrompt effectively enables models to grasp essential contextual information,
027
               leading to substantially improved problem-solving performance, e.g., an average
028
               improvement of 7.95% for LLAMA3-70B-Instruct. Code will be publicly available.

031   1    I NTRODUCTION
032
033   Large language models (LLMs) exhibit remarkable performance across various natural language
034   processing (NLP) tasks and artificial intelligence (AI) applications (Brown et al., 2020; Touvron et al.,
035   2023; OpenAI, 2023). Despite their remarkable capabilities, recent studies reveal that LLMs often
036   encounter challenges in fully understanding their input contexts, overlooking or showing insensitivity
037   to crucial contextual information (Kasai et al., 2023; Li et al., 2023; Si et al., 2023; Zhou et al., 2023;
038   Yu et al., 2024; Zhang et al., 2024). Consequently, the models tend to fabricate answers (also known
039
      as hallucination), resulting in responses that are inconsistent with the presented contexts (Zhou et al.,
      2023; Yu et al., 2024). This becomes particularly problematic when models are presented prompts
040
      containing lengthy background contexts (Liu et al., 2023) or complex questions, such as in open-book
041
      question answering (QA) (Kwiatkowski et al., 2019; Shi et al., 2023b; Peng et al., 2023). In these
042   information-dense scenarios, lengthy contexts can overwhelm LLMs, which contain many details
043   with varying degree of relevance (Wan et al., 2024; Zhang et al., 2024). Some sentences are crucial
044   for providing the correct answer, while others, though irrelevant, can distract models from fully
045   understanding the essential information.
046
      To improve reading comprehension of models, most prior work explores well-designed prompts
047
      to guide the LLM to use contextual knowledge more reliably (Zhou et al., 2023; Wan et al., 2024;
048   Radhakrishnan et al., 2023). In particular, iterative prompting in chain-of-thought (COT; Wei
049   et al., 2022) fashion can help LLMs decompose complex task-solving into more interpretable
050   and manageable intermediate steps, thus yielding better performance (Radhakrishnan et al., 2023).
051   Motivated by this, it is natural to design multi-step iterative prompting to guide LLMs to pay more
052   attention to relevant contextual parts and derive answers accordingly. Specifically, for open-book QA
053   tasks, iterative prompting can be decomposed into two steps: (i) identifying key information and (ii)
      deriving answers using the key information.

      Iterative prompting can work effectively for black-box LLMs of significantly large sizes (e.g., >100B)
      (Radhakrishnan et al., 2023). However, for LLMs of smaller sizes (e.g., LLAMA3-70B, Meta, 2024),
077
      it remains unclear if this strategy can guide models to fully attend to the extracted key information and
078
      subsequently improve performance. First, step-by-step generations typically result in longer contexts.
079   However, key information is only highlighted in token space by appending the short predicted key
080   sentences, which are often not strong enough to fully steer the model’s attention. As illustrated in the
081   left part of Figrue 1, even though the model correctly predicts the key sentence which is appened in the
082   subsequent prompt, it still fails to provide the correct answer. Moreover, errors can propagate across
083   steps, further compromising performance. Therefore, we aim to develop an alternative inference
084   method that emulates iterative prompting while addressing these limitations.
085   Motivated by this, we propose SteerPrompt, an inference-only method that (i) automatically identifies
086   key contextual parts, and (ii) explicitly highlights them through attention score manipulation for
087   improving models’ reading comprehension and performance on open-book QA tasks. Specifically,
088   SteerPrompt integrates iterative question-decomposition prompting and attention steering approaches
089   (Zhang et al., 2024). Given the original context and question, SteerPrompt first prompts an LLM to
090   identify the key information (sentences) through free-text generation. Then, instead of appending
091   those key sentences to the initial prompt, SteerPrompt maps those sentences back to the original
092   context using semantic embeddings (Figure 1 right). By using the original sentences for highlighting,
093
      it avoids more lengthy input for the next step, and potentially reduces the unreliable key sentences
      generations, mitigating the error propagation. Finally, to guide the model to attend to the selected key
094
      sentences, SteerPrompt highlights them through attention steering that upweights their corresponding
095
      attention scores at the selected attention heads as done by Zhang et al. (2024). Unlike existing
096   attention steering work, our method does not necessitate human annotation on the highlighting part,
097   rectifying its critical limitation. Additionally, we also design an efficient coarse-to-fine search scheme
098   for identifying effective attention heads for steering, which reduces the searching overhead by 4.5×
099   compared to the greedy method of previous work (Zhang et al., 2024).
100
      We conduct experiments to evaluate the effectiveness of SteerPrompt using Vicuna-7B (Chiang
101
      et al., 2023), LLAMA3-8B-Instruct, and LLAMA3-70B-Instruct (Meta, 2024) on both single- and
102   multi-hop open-book QA tasks from Natural Questions (NQ; Kwiatkowski et al., 2019) and Hot-
103   potQA (Yang et al., 2018b). Empirical evidence shows that it can be a much easier task for the
104   LLMs to select the contextual key sentences than comprehending the full context (see analysis in
105   Section 5.1). SteerPrompt converts the challenging bottleneck of contextual comprehension into
106   an easier problem of key-sentence selection in a steerable way, consistently providing significant
107   performance improvements over baseline prompting strategies. For example, SteerPrompt achieves
      an average improvement of 8.99% on exact-match (EM) score over iterative prompting for LLAMA3-

164   Our proposed method – Steerable Prompting (SteerPrompt), integrates iterative prompting and at-
165   tention steering. This integration synergistically combines the advantages of both techniques while
166   mitigating their respective limitations. For multi-step iterative prompting, incorporating attention
167   steering externalizes the highlighting of key information through an inference-only operation, effi-
168   ciently enhancing model reading comprehension with improved reliability and controllability. For
169   post-hoc attention steering, equipping it with iterative prompting enables the automatic identifica-
170   tion of contextually relevant key information, thereby addressing its significant reliance on human
171   annotations.

342
343   5.1     I SOLATING THE EFFECT OF S TEER P ROMPT ’ S TWO COMPONENTS
344
345
      SteerPrompt consists of two primary components: automatic key sentence identification, and explicit
      highlighting key sentences. To underscore the necessity of both components, we conduct the
346
      comparison between following methods: (i) direct prompting with the original context; (ii) iterative
347
      prompting that appends the identified key sentences appended to the context; (iii) highlighting the
348   entire context by attention steering approach but without key-sentence identification; (iv) SteerPrompt
349   that highlights the identified key sentences. Moreover, we present additional results about iterative
350   prompting to provide comprehensive evaluation for it. Specifically, within each context of the NQ
351   dataset, there is one gold sentence that entails the answer. We also evaluate the performance of
352   iterative prompting when appending the gold sentence.
353
      The results in Table 3 indicate that SteerPrompt can benefit from using the identified key sentence,

395   5.2   C OMPARISON BETWEEN PROFILING STRATEGIES
396
397   To illustrate the effectiveness of the coarse-to-fine profiling strategy introduced in Section 3.2, we
398   evaluate several different profiling approaches as follows:
399
      • Greedy search proposed by Zhang et al. (2024): This strategy involves selecting the top-k heads
400   from all the attention heads in the models. The evaluation times for this strategy is L × H.
401
402   • Group search inspired by Ainslie et al. (2023): Here, 8 adjacent heads from one layer form a group.
403
      Then, we evaluate them group-wise, and select the top-k head groups. The evaluate times for this
      strategy is LH/8.
404
405   • Coarse-to-fine search: This strategy initially selects the top-l layers and then chooses the head set
406   only from the heads within these layers. The evaluation times for this strategy is L + lH.
407   where L is the number of layers, and H is the number of attention heads per layer. We compare them
408   with a Vicuna-7B (Chiang et al., 2023) that has 32 layers, and 32 heads per layer. The results in Table
409   5 show that coarse-to-fine profiling significantly outperforms all the other strategies while reducing
410   the total evaluation times by 4.5× compared to the original greedy search in Zhang et al. (2024).

457   We conduct ablation study to discuss the performance of SteerPrompt given different number of

459
      Varying the number of steered heads. Figure 2a presents the performance variation of SteerPrompt

473   performance variation. Therefore, we set δ as its default value log(100), which is the same as Zhang

476   6                  R ELATED W ORK
477
478
      Large language models exhibit remarkable performance on (context-free) knowledge-intensive tasks,
479
      such as open-domain question answering (QA) (Kwiatkowski et al., 2019) and commonsense reason-
480   ing (Mihaylov et al., 2018; Clark et al., 2018), indicating that they encode substantial knowledge about
481   open-world facts (Zhou et al., 2023) in their parameters. Despite their proficiency in memorization,
482   different kinds of hallucinations in the output are observed, including factual knowledge hallucination
483   (Huang et al., 2023; Yu et al., 2024), hallucination in summarization (Maynez et al., 2020; Pagnoni
484   et al., 2021), hallucination in logical operations (Lyu et al., 2023; Huang et al., 2023). In this work,
485   we focus on the factual knowledge hallucination due to models’ unawareness of relevant knowledge
      or overlooking contextual information.


                                                                                              9
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


486
      Retrieval-augmented LLMs. To address the problem of missing relevant knowledge, one popular
487   method is to use retrieval-augmented LMs that supplement missing knowledge from external sources
488   (Shi et al., 2023b; Peng et al., 2023). Retrieval augmentation requires that LLMs are sensitive to
489   the input context and reliably understand the contexts. However, recent work shows that even if the
490   relevant knowledge is presented, the model may still not fully comprehend the given evidence (Zhou
491   et al., 2023; Yu et al., 2024; Wan et al., 2024).
492
      Prompt-based strategies. To improve the reading comprehension of the models, various prompting
493   strategies are designed to guide the model to detect the key information (Wei et al., 2022; Radhakr-
494   ishnan et al., 2023), or focus on the given evidence (Zhou et al., 2023), while these extracted key
495   information is only added as additional tokens in the input, and models still cannot grasp these new
496   tokens.
497
      Model-based strategies. Besides using prompting to improve the reading comprehension, some
498
      works augment an LLM’s training data (Köksal et al., 2023; Hu et al., 2024; Chen et al., 2024).
499   Alternatively, Shi et al. (2023a) proposes a context-aware decoding method to downweight the output
500   distribution associated with the model’s prior knowledge.
501
502
      To the best of our knowledge, we are the first work to integrate key information prompting and
      explicit token highlighting during inference without any additional training.
503

505   7   C ONCLUSION
506
507   In this paper, we address the challenge of contextual reading comprehension in open-book QA tasks
508   and introduce SteerPrompt, an inference-only method that automatically identifies crucial information
509   pieces within contexts and explicitly highlights them through steering a model’s attention scores.
510   SteerPrompt guides the model to focus on the essential information within contexts, leading to
511
      substantially improved model reading comprehension and performance. Remarkably, by integrate
      iterative prompting and attention steering techniques, SteerPrompt synergistically combines their
512
      advantages while mitigating their respective limitations.
