009                                                A BSTRACT
010
011            The Neural Tangent Kernel (NTK) has emerged as a valuable tool for analyzing the
012            training and generalization properties of neural networks. While the behavior of the
013            NTK in the infinite-width limit is well understood, a comprehensive investigation is
014            still required to comprehend its dynamics during training in the finite-width regime.
015            In this paper, we present a detailed exploration of the NTK’s behavior through the
016            examination of its trace during training.
017            By conducting experiments on standard supervised classification tasks, we observe
018            that the NTK trace typically exhibits an increasing trend and stabilizes when
019
               the network achieves its highest accuracy on the training data. Additionally, we
               investigate the phenomenon of “grokking”, which has recently garnered attention,
020
               as it involves an intriguing scenario where the test accuracy suddenly improves long
021
               after the training accuracy plateaus. To shed light on this phenomenon, we employ
022            the NTK trace to monitor the training dynamics during grokking. Furthermore,
023            we utilize the NTK trace to gain insights into the training dynamics of semi-
024            supervised learning approaches, including the employment of exponential moving
025            average mechanisms. Through these investigations, we demonstrate that the NTK,
026            particularly its trace, remains a powerful and valuable tool for comprehending the
027            training dynamics of modern finite-width neural networks.

030   1    I NTRODUCTION
031
032   Deep neural networks have proven to be highly successful in a wide range of machine learning
033
      tasks, particularly in the domain of image classification. Understanding the dynamics of these
      networks during training and their generalization capabilities is of paramount importance to further
034
      advance their performance and interpretability. In recent years, the field of deep learning has seen
035
      the rise of a crucial analytical tool known as the Neural Tangent Kernel (NTK) (Jacot et al., 2018),
036   primarily in the infinite-width limit under suitable initialization scales and infinitesimal learning rate.
037   Empirically, researchers adopt neural networks of finite-width, therefore the behavior of the NTK in
038   the finite-width regime necessitates a more comprehensive investigation.
039
      Notably, in the infinite width limit, Jacot et al. (2018) show that when the network depth is fixed
040
      the NTK is determined by the initialization and is fixed throughout the whole training process.
041
      Theoretically, Hanin & Nica (2019); Littwin et al. (2021) show that the NTK at initialization is also
042   influenced by the network depth even in the infinite width limit. Recently, Fort et al. (2020); Loo et al.
043   (2022) empirically study the NTK of finite width neural network during the whole training process.
044   By defining a “kernel distance” function, they show that after a rather quick chaotic data-dependent
045   kernel learning process, the network learns a kernel that has the linearized training accuracy that
046   matches the performance of the neural network. However, the defined “kernel distance” S(K1 , K2 )
047   (Fort et al., 2020; Loo et al., 2022) is not a distance function in mathematics as S(K1 , K2 ) = 0 does
048   not imply K1 = K2 . But interestingly, one can show that if in addition Tr(K1 ) = Tr(K2 ) then
049   K1 = K2 . Moreover, the NTK trace has an efficient approximation method, making it a feasible and
050
      orthogonal approach to prior works (Fort et al., 2020; Loo et al., 2022).
051   To understand the behavior of the NTK in finite-width neural networks, we conduct experiments that
052   evaluate the NTK trace along the training process on 3 settings: standard supervised classification
053   tasks, grokking, and semi-supervised learning. The latter 2 settings grokking and semi-supervised
      learning are closely related to standard supervised classification, which we will briefly discuss as

054
      follows. Grokking is first observed in (Power et al., 2022), where a supervised classification task
055   is trained to perform arithmetic tasks. It refers to a sudden and unexpected great improvement in
056   test accuracy long after the training accuracy has reached a plateau. Semi-supervised learning uses
057   a small amount of labeled data and a large amount of unlabeled data in training. It usually adopts
058   the pseudo-labeling strategy, where during training the network will assign pseudo labels to some
059   unlabeled data and thus transform it into a supervised learning problem.
060
      Our contributions can be summarized as follows:
061
062         1. We present an efficient approximation for NTK trace and link it to the margin of a kernel
063            SVM problem.
064         2. On standard supervised classification tasks, we find the NTK trace usually rises and stabilizes
065            when top training accuracy is met. We also observe that in the grokking settings, when the
066            NTK trace is stabilized the test accuracy attains its maximum.
067         3. The NTK trace also helps us understand training dynamics in semi-supervised learning
068            approaches, including the use of the exponential moving average (EMA) mechanism.

      2   R ELATED W ORK
072
      Neural Tangent Kernel. Jacot et al. (2018) provide neural tangent kernel (NTK) as a tool to
073
      understand the dynamics of neural networks training under suitable initialization and small learning
074
      rates by considering the infinite-width limit. In this limiting regime, the NTK becomes deterministic
075   and fixed during training when the width is taken to infinity (Zou & Gu, 2019; Ji & Telgarsky, 2019;
076   Chen et al., 2019). As modern neural networks are in the finite-width regime, researchers have
077   gained more and more interest in the behavior of NTK in modern finite-width architectures (Shan &
078   Bordelon, 2021; Atanasov et al., 2021; Seleznova et al., 2024; Hanin & Nica, 2019; Littwin et al.,
079   2021; Fort et al., 2020; Loo et al., 2022). For example, Hanin & Nica (2019); Littwin et al. (2021)
080   have given finite-width (and depth) correction of neural network’s NTK at initialization. And Fort
081   et al. (2020); Loo et al. (2022) study the evolution of NTK during modern finite-width neural network
082   training using a “kernel distance”. In this paper, we study dynamics of NTK using its trace.
083
084   Neural network training dynamics. Researchers have found that there has usually been some
085
      implicit optimization process during the supervised training dynamics of neural networks (Nacson
      et al., 2019; Xu et al., 2021; Soudry et al., 2018; Banburski et al., 2019; Lyu & Li, 2019; Blanc et al.,
086
      2020). For example, Lyu & Li (2019) show the network implicitly maximizes the margin during
087
      the training using sgd under loss like cross-entropy loss and Blanc et al. (2020) study the implicit
088   regularization of the network under MSE loss using sgd. Very recently, Power et al. (2022) find cases
089   in supervised learning that the network generalizes long after it overfits the training data, which they
090   termed the phenomenon grokking. There have been attempts to understand the training dynamics
091   of grokking: Liu et al. (2022a) study the dynamics using physics-inspired effective theory, Nanda
092   et al. (2023) study the process using trigonometric series, and Tan & Huang (2023) study the process
093   using the network’s robustness. In this paper, we study the dynamics of neural networks and their
094   associated NTK using the trace of NTK.

420   early stages of training, which leads to a less stable training process. Furthermore, it indicates
421   that the weakly augmented labeled and unlabeled data share similar NTK traces, implying that the
422   predicted pseudo-labels may have similar distributions to the labeled ones. Additionally, the NTK
423   traces of weakly and strongly augmented samples are similar, suggesting that the network generalizes
424   well on strongly augmented samples. Finally, the NTK traces of strongly augmented samples and
425   pseudo-labeled samples exhibit resemblances, indicating a high efficiency of pseudo-labeling.

471
472
      Through the analysis of the NTK trace, we gained valuable insights into the network’s training
473
      progress and its correlation with accuracy. Our findings in standard supervised image classification
474   settings demonstrated that the NTK trace typically exhibited an increasing trend, eventually stabilizing
475   when the network achieved its highest accuracy on the training data. This observation underscores the
476   intrinsic connection between the NTK dynamics and the network’s training performance. Furthermore,
477   our exploration of the phenomenon referred to as “grokking” yielded further insights. Through the
478   utilization of the NTK trace, we closely monitored the training dynamics in grokking scenarios and
479   made a noteworthy observation: the test accuracy reaches its peak when the NTK trace stabilizes.
480   In the final phase of our study, we investigated the training dynamics of semi-supervised learning,
481   with a particular emphasis on examining the effectiveness of exponential moving average (EMA)
482
      mechanisms. By leveraging the NTK trace, we gained a deeper and more accurate understanding
      of the intricate behavior displayed in semi-supervised learning scenarios, as well as the influence
483
      of EMA on the training process. This analysis provided valuable insights into the dynamics of
484
      semi-supervised learning and shed light on the role of EMA in enhancing training performance. One
485   limitation of our work is not analyzing the whole property of NTK, just its trace.
