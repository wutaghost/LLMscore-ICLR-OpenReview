009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011
               The rapid increase in the size of large language models (LLMs) has significantly
012
               escalated their computational and memory demands, posing challenges for effi-
013
               cient deployment, especially on resource-constrained devices. Structured pruning
014            has emerged as an effective model compression method that can reduce these
015            demands while preserving performance. In this paper, we introduce FASP (Fast
016            and Accurate Structured Pruning), a novel structured pruning framework for LLMs
017            that emphasizes both speed and accuracy. FASP employs a distinctive pruning
018            structure that interlinks sequential layers, allowing for the removal of columns in
019            one layer while simultaneously eliminating corresponding rows in the preceding
020            layer without incurring additional performance loss. The pruning metric, inspired
021            by Wanda, is computationally efficient and effectively selects components to prune.
022
               Additionally, we propose a restoration mechanism that enhances model fidelity by
               adjusting the remaining weights post-pruning. We evaluate FASP on the OPT and
023
               LLaMA model families, demonstrating superior performance in terms of perplexity
024
               and accuracy on downstream tasks compared to state-of-the-art methods. Our
025            approach achieves significant speed-ups, pruning models such as OPT-125M in
026            17 seconds and LLaMA-30B in 20 minutes on a single NVIDIA RTX 4090 GPU,
027            making it a highly practical solution for optimizing LLMs.

031   1    I NTRODUCTION
032
033   Large language models (LLMs) have profoundly transformed various aspects of daily life in recent
034   times, showcasing remarkable capabilities across diverse applications, including machine translation,
035   conversational agents, text generation, as well as image and video synthesis (OpenAI, 2023; Meta
036   AI, 2023; Gemini Team et al., 2023; Arefeen et al., 2024; Li et al., 2024). However, the increasing
037   size of these models imposes substantial demands on computational and GPU memory resources,
038   creating significant barriers to the deployment of advanced LLMs. This challenge becomes even
039
      more pronounced when considering the deployment of LLM technologies on mobile devices, such as
      smartphones, particularly in light of the rising trend driven by the release of advanced AI capabilities
040
      like Apple intelligence (Apple Inc, 2024). In light of these challenges, the compression of LLMs has
041
      become a critical requirement to mitigate the resource demands associated with their deployment,
042   thereby enhancing their accessibility and operational efficiency across diverse platforms.
043
044   Pruning techniques represent one of the most prevalent methods for compressing LLMs (Frantar &
045
      Alistarh, 2023; Sun et al., 2023; Ma et al., 2023a; Shen et al., 2024; Fang et al., 2024), effectively
      reducing model size and computational demands while maintaining performance integrity. Among
046
      the various pruning strategies, structured pruning stands out for its systematic approach of removing
047
      entire components, such as neurons or channels, which directly enhances computational efficiency and
048   is compatible across diverse hardware platforms. In contrast, unstructured pruning targets individual
049   weights, necessitating support for sparse data structures and corresponding computational methods.
050   Similarly, semi-structured pruning often relies on specific hardware architectures, such as the 2:4
051   semi-structure utilized in NVIDIAŌĆÖs Ampere architecture (Mishra et al., 2021). Additionally, the
052   inference acceleration achieved through semi-structured pruning is less effective than that obtained
053   with structured pruning at the same level of sparsity (Ashkboos et al., 2024). Therefore, this work
      will concentrate on structured pruning.

072   Several existing structured pruning methods have shown varying effectiveness in optimizing LLMs.
073   For example, LLM-Pruner (Ma et al., 2023a) employs structured pruning to selectively remove
074
      non-critical coupled structures based on gradient information, and it requires a fine-tuning step to
      recover the performance of the pruned models, which may take several hours. SliceGPT (Ashkboos
075
      et al., 2024) replaces each weight matrix with a smaller dense matrix, thereby reducing the embedding
076
      dimension of the network. However, this approach requires the application of principal component
077   analysis for each linear layer, which can be time-consuming. Additionally, it introduces two auxiliary
078   transformation matrices in each decoder block, which mitigates the overall effectiveness of the
079   pruning. FLAP (An et al., 2024) formulates structured importance metrics and adaptively searches for
080   a globally compressed model. Although it is relatively efficient, it often compromises performance.
081   Shen et al. (2024) introduces a neural architecture search-based approach (denoted as NASLLM
082   hereafter) to identify subnets of the original LLMs and implements a reformation algorithm to rectify
083   the inherited weights. However, due to its searching nature, this method can be time-consuming.
084   Given the limitations of current structured pruning methods, particularly concerning time efficiency
085   and the performance of pruned models, we propose a novel approach, FASP, that emphasizes both
086   speed and accuracy. First, we introduce an innovative pruning structure that connects two sequential
087   linear layers. This design enables the pruning of a subset of columns in the later layer, allowing for the
088   corresponding rows in the preceding layer to be removed without adversely impacting performance.
089   Inspired by Wanda (Sun et al., 2023), we develop a pruning metric for selecting columns to prune,
090   which is both simple and efficient. Finally, we implement an effective restoration mechanism to
091
      recover the performance of the pruned model by leveraging inherited weights, thereby enhancing
      overall model fidelity.
092
093   We conduct comprehensive experiments on the OPT (Zhang et al., 2022) and LLaMA (Touvron
094   et al., 2023) model families, comparing the proposed FASP against state-of-the-art techniques. Our
095   results demonstrate that FASP achieves superior performance in terms of perplexity and accuracy
096   on downstream tasks across these models. Specifically, we evaluate the pruning time for models of
097
      varying sizes, finding that FASP requires approximately 17 seconds to prune the OPT-125M model
      and about 20 minutes for the LLaMA-30B model on a single NVIDIA RTX 4090 GPU, highlighting
098
      substantial improvements in pruning speed. These findings emphasize the advantages of our method
099
      in practical applications, showcasing its potential to optimize LLMs efficiently while maintaining
100   high performance.

103   2       BACKGROUND AND R ELATED W ORK
104
105   Pruning techniques in neural networks can be broadly categorized into unstructured and structured
106   pruning. Unstructured and semi-structured pruning remove individual weights or partial substructures
107   of a model to reduce its size. These methods, such as SparseGPT (Frantar & Alistarh, 2023),
      Wanda (Sun et al., 2023), FPSTAPruner (Zhao et al., 2024) and MaksLLM (Fang et al., 2024), often


                                                          2
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


108
      achieve high sparsity levels without severely degrading model performance. However, they come with
109   practical limitations. Unstructured pruning may provide limited computational speedup and memory
110   savings due to its irregular sparsity structure and the necessity of storing indices. Additionally,
111   the inference of semi-structured sparsified LLMs typically requires specialized hardware, such as
112   NVIDIAŌĆÖs Ampere architecture GPUs (Mishra et al., 2021), which restricts their applicability in
113   real-world scenarios.
114
      In contrast, structured pruning entirely removes substructures from rows or columns in the weight
115   matrices to attention heads, layers, or blocks. This results in direct memory savings and inference
116   speedups that are achievable on any hardware, making it more practical for deployment. However,
117   structured pruning imposes stricter constraints on what can be pruned, and the modelŌĆÖs performance
118   typically degrades more significantly. To compensate for this, retraining is traditionally required to
119   restore the modelŌĆÖs accuracy (Ma et al., 2023b). Retraining, though, is computationally expensive and
120   time-consuming, particularly for LLMs. This makes pruning with retraining less feasible in practice,
121   especially in scenarios requiring fast model adaptation and deployment. Given these challenges,
122   there is a growing need for structured pruning methods that avoid retraining while maintaining model
123
      performance. Recent research has focused on developing such methods to improve the practicality of
      pruning large-scale models.
124
125   One line of work focuses on pruning larger components of LLMs. For instance, FinerCut (Zhang
126   et al., 2024) prunes entire attention or feed-forward network blocks through a greedy algorithm that
127   exhaustively evaluates and removes the blocks with minimal impact on the modelŌĆÖs output. Similarly,
128   ShortGPT (Men et al., 2024) removes entire decoder layers to achieve sparsity. Another direction
129
      focuses on pruning the weights of the linear layers in LLMs. SliceGPT (Ashkboos et al., 2024)
      introduces a rotation-based pruning method where the weights are first rotated by orthogonal matrices
130
      derived from principal component analyses (PCA) of the activations and the less important portions
131
      of the weights are pruned. However, this method introduces additional memory and computation
132   overhead, as the orthogonal matrices must be stored to transform the residual connections during
133   inference. Moreover, SliceGPTŌĆÖs pruning strategy solely relies on the activations and thus requires a
134   large calibration dataset and high precision (64-bit) for PCA calculations, making it computationally
135   expensive and memory-intensive. FLAP (An et al., 2024) proposes a novel pruning metric that
136   measures the stability of the channels to determine which parts of the network to prune. A bias update
137   mechanism is then used to compensate for the pruned parts. However, FLAP does not update the
138   remaining weights after pruning, which contains much more parameters than the bias vector. Thus,
139   incorporating bias-only compensation misses vast opportunities to remedy the performance loss due
140
      to pruning. NASLLM (Shen et al., 2024) explores network architecture search (NAS) methods for
      finding optimal subnets within LLMs and employs the alternating direction method of multipliers
141
      (ADMM) to determine the optimal updates for the remaining weights. However, these involve slow
142
      and inefficient search iterations, requiring approximately 5 hours to prune the LLaMA-7B model, as
143   reported in the paper.

277
278   In this section, we validate the performance and efficiency of FASP through comprehensive experi-
279   ments. We begin by detailing our experimental settings, followed by a comparison of perplexity and
280   zero-shot results for the pruned models obtained via FASP and various baseline methods, as well as
281
      the time taken for pruning.
282   Models and baseline methods. We compare FASP with state-of-the-art methods including
283   SliceGPT (Ashkboos et al., 2024), NASLLM (Shen et al., 2024), FLAP (An et al., 2024), and
284   LLM-Pruner (Ma et al., 2023b) on the LLaMA (Touvron et al., 2023) and OPT (Zhang et al.,
285   2022) families with sizes ranging from 125m to 30B downloaded from HuggingFaceŌĆÖs Transformers
286   library (Wolf et al., 2019).
287   Datasets and benchmarks. We evaluate the perplexity of the models under various sparsity pruned
288   by 128 randomly drawn calibration samples from the WikiText2 (Merity et al., 2016) dataset with
289   the 2048 sequence length. Additionally, following the settings in (An et al., 2024; Ma et al., 2023b;
290   Shen et al., 2024), we compare the zero-shot accuracy on standard common reasoning datasets
291   including BoolQ (Clark et al., 2019), PIQA (Bisk et al., 2020), HellaSwag (Zellers et al., 2019),
292   WinoGrande (Sakaguchi et al., 2021), OpenbookQA (Mihaylov et al., 2018), ARC-easy and ARC-
293
      challenge(Clark et al., 2018).
294   Implementation details. We implement FASP using the PyTorch framework (Paszke et al., 2019) and
295   perform pruning on NVIDIA RTX 4090 GPUs, each equipped with 24GB of memory. To evaluate
296   its performance, we utilize the official implementations of SliceGPT and FLAP. Since the code for
297   NASLLM has not been released, we rely on the results reported in their paper for comparison. For
298   LLM-Pruner, which necessitates a costly fine-tuning process to recover performance, we directly
299
      reference the results provided by the NASLLM authors. Our observations indicate that, on a single
      GPU, FASP can effectively prune 30B-level LLMs, while SliceGPT is limited to pruning models up
300
      to 13B due to the high memory requirements associated with PCA computations and the storage of
301
      additional orthogonal matrices.

445   5   D ISCUSSION
446
447   In this section, we reflect on the limitations of FASP. Despite its strengths, one limitation is the
448   decision to forgo pruning the rows of WQ and WK in the self-attention layers. While our experiments
449   demonstrated that pruning these layers degrades performance, future work could explore more
450   sophisticated strategies for dealing with these components, such as adaptive sparsity levels or selective
451   pruning criteria that may mitigate the performance loss.
452
453   6   C ONCLUSION
454
455   In this paper, we present FASP, a fast and accurate structured pruning method designed for LLMs.
456   By capitalizing on the inherent connections between sequential layers, FASP allows for efficient
457   pruning with minimal performance drop. Our proposed pruning structure and metric streamline
458   the pruning process, while the restoration mechanism effectively recovers model fidelity. Through
459   comprehensive experiments on the OPT and LLaMA model families, we demonstrate that FASP
460   significantly outperforms existing pruning techniques both in terms of speed and accuracy. Notably,
461
      FASP is capable of pruning large-scale models like LLaMA-30B in a fraction of the time required by
      other methods, while maintaining competitive performance on perplexity and zero-shot evaluation
462
      tasks. These results highlight the potential of FASP to improve the deployment efficiency of LLMs
463
      across diverse hardware platforms, including those with limited computational resources.
