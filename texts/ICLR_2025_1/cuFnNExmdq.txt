011                                                A BSTRACT
012
013            Transformer-based models have emerged as powerful tools for multivariate time
014            series forecasting (MTSF). However, existing Transformer models often fall short
015            of capturing both intricate dependencies across variate and temporal dimensions
016            in MTS data. Some recent models are proposed to separately capture variate and
017
               temporal dependencies through either two sequential or parallel attention mecha-
               nisms. However, these methods cannot directly and explicitly learn the intricate
018
               inter-series and intra-series dependencies. In this work, we first demonstrate that
019
               these dependencies are very important as they usually exist in real-world data. To
020            directly model these dependencies, we propose a transformer-based model UniTST
021            containing a unified attention mechanism on the flattened patch tokens. Addition-
022            ally, we add a dispatcher module which reduces the complexity and makes the
023            model feasible for a potentially large number of variates. Although our proposed
024            model employs a simple architecture, it offers compelling performance as shown
025            in our extensive experiments on several datasets for time series forecasting.

028   1    I NTRODUCTION
029
030   Inspired by the success of Transformer-based models in various fields such as natural language
031   processing (Touvron et al., 2023a; Chiang et al., 2023; Almazrouei et al., 2023; MosaicML, 2023;
032   Touvron et al., 2023b; OpenAI, 2022; Google, 2023; Touvron et al., 2023b) and computer vision
033   (Wu et al., 2020; Liu et al., 2021b; Jamil et al., 2023), Transformers have also garnered much
034   attention in the community of multivariate time series forecasting (MTSF) (Nie et al., 2023; Liu
035   et al., 2024; Wu et al., 2021; Zhang and Yan, 2023; Zhou et al., 2022; Carlini et al., 2023; Han
036
      et al., 2024). Pioneering works (Li et al., 2021; Wu et al., 2021; Zhou et al., 2022) treat multiple
      variates (aka channels) at each time step as the input unit for transformers, similar to tokens in the
037
      language domain, but its performance was even inferior to linear models (Zeng et al., 2023; Han
038
      et al., 2023). Considering the noisy information from individual time points, Variate-Independent
039   and Patch-Based (Nie et al., 2023) methods are subsequently proposed and achieve positive results
040   by avoiding mixing noises from multiple variates and aggregating information from several adjacent
041   time points as input. Nevertheless, these methods neglect the cross-variate relationships and interfere
042   with the learning of temporal dynamics across variates.
043
      To tackle this problem, iTransformer (Liu et al., 2024) embeds the entire time series of a variate into
044
      a token and employs "variate-wise attention" to model variate dependencies. However, it lacks the
045   capability to model intra-variate temporal dependencies. Concurrently, several approaches (Zhang
046   and Yan, 2023; Carlini et al., 2023; Yu et al., 2024) utilize both variate-wise attention and time(patch)-
047   wise attention to capture inter-variate and intra-variate dependencies, either sequentially or parallelly.
048   Yet, they may raise the difficulty of modeling the diverse time and variate dependencies as the errors
049   from one stage can affect the other stage and eventually the overall performance.
050
      Additionally, either two parallel or sequential attention mechanisms cannot explicitly model
051   the direct dependencies across different variates and different times, which we show in Fig-
052   ure 1. Regardless of how previous works apply time-wise attention and variate-wise atten-
053   tion parallelly or sequentially, they would still lack the green links to capture cross-time cross-
      variate dependencies (aka inter-series intra-series dependencies) simultaneously as in our model.

107   Recently, many Transformer-based models have been also proposed for multivariate time series
      forecasting and demonstrated great potential (Liu et al., 2021a; Wu et al., 2021; Li et al., 2021; Zhang


                                                                            2

108
      and Yan, 2023; Zhou et al., 2022; Li et al., 2019). Several approaches (Wu et al., 2021; Li et al., 2021;
109   Zhou et al., 2022) embed temporal tokens that contain the multivariate representation of each time step
110   and utilize attention mechanisms to model temporal dependencies. However, due to the vulnerability
111   to the distribution shift, these models with such channel mixing structure are often outperformed
112   by simple linear models (Zeng et al., 2023; Han et al., 2023). Subsequently, PatchTST (Nie et al.,
113   2023) considers channel independence and models temporal dependencies within each channel to
114   make predictions independently. Nonetheless, it ignores the correlation between variates, which may
115   hinder its performance. To model variate dependencies, in the past two years, several works have
116   been proposed (Liu et al., 2024; Zhang and Yan, 2023; Carlini et al., 2023; Han et al., 2024; Yu et al.,
117
      2024; Wu et al., 2023). iTransformer (Liu et al., 2024) models channel dependencies by embedding
      the whole time series of a variate into a token and using "variate-wise attention" without explicitly
118
      modeling on temporal dependencies.
119
120   Additionally, several methods proposed different modules to capture both time and variate depen-
121   dencies. However, they can either sequentially or parallelly capture time and variate dependencies
122   and are not able to capture them simultaneously. In the later Section 3, we show the importance
123
      of simultaneously capturing both time and variate dependencies by providing empirical evidence
      in real-world data. Crossformer (Zhang and Yan, 2023) uses the encoder-decoder architecture
124
      with two-stage attention layers to sequentially model cross-time dependencies and then cross-variate
125
      dependencies. CARD (Carlini et al., 2023) employs the encoder-only architecture utilizing a similar
126   sequential two-stage attention mechanism for cross-time, cross-channel dependencies and a token
127   blend module to capture multi-scale information. Leddam (Yu et al., 2024) designs a learnable
128   decomposition and a dual attention module that parallelly model inter-variate dependencies with
129   "channel-wise attention" and intra-variate temporal dependencies with "auto-regressive attention". In
130   summary, these works generally model intra-variate and inter-variate dependencies separately (either
131   sequentially or parallelly), and aggregate these two types of information to get the outputs. In con-
132   trast, our model has a general ability to directly capture inter-variate and intra-variate dependencies
133   simultaneously, which is more effective. We provide more discussion on the comparison between our
134
      model and previous models in Section 4.2.
135   Moreover, CATS (Lu et al., 2024) constructs auxiliary series and capture inter-series dependencies
136   from auxiliary series. In contrast, our method is applied directly on the original series with considering
137   all multivariate as a unified sequence. CrossGNN (Huang et al., 2023), as a GNN-based method,
138   proposes a cross interation layer to capture cross-scale interation on the time dimension and cross-
139   variate interaction on the variate dimension. However, it still relies on a sequential manner to capture
140
      cross-time and cross-variate dependencies. Similar to it, TimeXer (Wang et al., 2024) sequentially
      capture cross-time and cross-variate dependencies by ingesting external information from exogenous
141
      variables. With the same goal as our work, LIFT (Zhao and Shen, 2024) also aims to capture
142
      cross-time and cross-variate dependencies simultaneously. However, it requires directly calculations
143   on leading indicators for each pair of variates and applies leading indicators lagged variates, which
144   may need massive computational costs. In contrast, our proposed method UniTST can model the
145   cross-time and cross-variate from the time series sequence without explicit calculation on leading
146   indicators.

      4.2   D ISCUSSION AND C OMPARISON WITH P REVIOUS M ODELS
271
272   Our proposed model is an encoder-only transformer model containing a unified attention mechanism
273   with dispatchers. The model explicitly learns both intra-variate and inter-variate temporal dependen-
274   cies among different patch tokens through attention, which means that it can directly capture the
275   correlation between two time series at different periods from different variates. In contrast, these
276   dependencies cannot be directly and explicitly captured by previous works which claim that they
277   model variate dependencies (Liu et al., 2024; Zhang and Yan, 2023; Carlini et al., 2023; Yu et al.,
278
      2024).
279   For example, iTransformer (Liu et al., 2024) captures variate dependencies using the whole time
280   series of a variate as a token. It loses the ability to capture the fine-grained temporal dependencies
281   across channels or within a channel. Crossformer (Zhang and Yan, 2023) and CARD (Carlini et al.,
282   2023) both propose to use a sequential two-stage attention mechanism to first capture dependencies
283   on time dimensions and then capture dependencies on variate dimensions. This sequential manner
284
      does not directly capture cross-time cross-variate dependencies simultaneously, which makes them
      less effective as shown in their empirical performance. In contrast, our proposed model uses a more
285
      unified attention on a flattened patch sequence with all patches from different channels, allowing
286
      direct and explicit modeling cross-time cross-variate dependencies. In addition, Yu et al. (2024)
287   propose a dual attention module with an iTransformer-like encoder to inter-variate dependencies and
288   an auto-regressive self-attention on each channel to capture intra-variate dependencies separately.
289   In this way, it also cannot directly capture cross-variate temporal dependencies between two patch
290   tokens at different time steps from different variates (e.g., H (i,k) , while our model is able to directly
291   capture these dependencies.
292
      Worth noting that our proposed model is a more general case to directly capture intra-variate and
293
      inter-variate dependencies at a more fine-grained level (i.e., patch level from different variates at
294   different times). Moreover, our model employs simple architectures that can be easily implemented
295   while the empirical results show the effectiveness of our model in Section 5.1. Additionally, we
296   provide the analysis of computational complexity in Appendix B.

      6     C ONCLUSION AND F UTURE W ORK
504
      In this work, we first point out the limitation of previous works on time series transformers for
505
      multivariate forecasting: their lack of ability to effectively capture inter-series and intra-series
506
      dependencies simultaneously. We further demonstrate that inter-series and intra-series dependencies
507   are crucial for multivariate time series forecasting as they commonly exist in real-world data. To
508   mitigate this limitation of previous works, we propose a simple yet effective transformer model
509   UniTST with a dispatcher mechanism to effectively capture inter-series and intra-series dependencies.
510   The experiments on 13 datasets for time series forecasting show that our model achieves superior
511   performance compared with many representative baselines. Moreover, we conduct the ablation
512   study and model analyses to verify the effectiveness of our dispatcher mechanism and demonstrate
513   the importance of inter-series intra-series dependencies. Our study emphasizes the necessity and
514   effectiveness of simultaneously capturing inter-variate and intra-variate dependencies in multivariate
515
      time series forecasting, and our proposed designs represent a step toward this goal.

      B    D ISCUSSION ON C OMPUTATIONAL C OMPLEXITY
692
693
      Moreover, we provide the computational complexity analysis of different models. As Feedforward
      networks in different models have similar complexities, we mainly analyze the computational
694
      complexity of the attention mechanism. For UniTST, the designed attention mechanism uses cross-
695
      attention with dispatchers to reduce the complexity. It results in the complexity as O(kN p) where
696   k is the number of dispatchers, N is the number of variates, and p is the number of patches within
697   a variate. For iTransformer, it utilizes self-attention on the variate dimension, which leads to the
698   complexity as O(N 2 ). Additionally, PatchTST uses self-attention on the time dimension and treats
699   each variate independently. As a result, the complexity is O(N p2 ). We can see that different models
700   have different advantages in different scenarios. For example, when handling data with a long time
701   series but with fewer variates, iTransformer should be faster than others as it doesn’t depend on p.
      Comparing UniTST and PatchTST, when p is relatively small, then the complexity should be similar


                                                                                  13
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


702
      (we set k as 10 in our experiments). UniTST may be slower than iTransformer when the length of the
703   time series and the number of variates are both extremely large. For this extreme scenario, we leave
704   further investigation for future work.
