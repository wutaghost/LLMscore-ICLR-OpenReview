009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            Existing certified training methods can only train models to be robust against a
012            certain perturbation type (e.g. l∞ or l2 ). However, an l∞ certifiably robust model
013            may not be certifiably robust against l2 perturbation (and vice versa) and also has
014            low robustness against other perturbations (e.g. geometric transformation). To this
015            end, we propose the first multi-norm certified training framework CURE, consisting
016            of a new l2 deterministic certified training defense and several multi-norm certified
017            training methods, to attain better union robustness when training from scratch
018
               or fine-tuning a pre-trained certified model. Further, we devise bound alignment
               and connect natural training with certified training for better union robustness.
019
               Compared with SOTA certified training, CURE improves union robustness up to
020
               22.8% on MNIST, 23.9% on CIFAR-10, and 8.0% on TinyImagenet. Further, it
021            leads to better generalization on a diverse set of challenging unseen geometric
022            perturbations, up to 6.8% on CIFAR-10. Overall, our contributions pave a path
023            towards universal certified robustness.

026   1   I NTRODUCTION
027
028   Though deep neural networks (DNNs) are widely deployed in various vision applications, they
029   remain vulnerable to adversarial attacks (Goodfellow et al., 2014; Kurakin et al., 2018). Though many
030   empirical defenses (Madry et al., 2017; Zhang et al., 2019a; Wang et al., 2023) against adversarial
031   attacks have been proposed, they do not provide provable guarantees and remain vulnerable to
032   stronger attacks. Therefore, it is important to train DNNs to be formally robust against adversarial
033
      perturbations. Various works (Mirman et al., 2018; Gowal et al., 2018; Zhang et al., 2019b; Balunović
      & Vechev, 2020; Shi et al., 2021; Müller et al., 2022a; Hu et al., 2023; 2024; Mao et al., 2024)
034
      on deterministic certified training against l∞ and l2 perturbations have been proposed. However,
035
      those defenses are mostly limited to a certain type of perturbation and cannot easily be generalized
036   to other perturbation types (Yang et al., 2022). Certified robustness against multiple perturbation
037   types is essential because this better reflects real-world scenarios where adversaries can use multiple
038   lp perturbations. Also, Mangal et al. (2023) argue that lp robustness is the bedrock for non-lp
039   robustness. In this work, we also show that training with multi-norm robustness can lead to stronger
040   universal certified robustness by generalizing better to other perturbation types such as geometric
041   transformations (Section 5.1).
042   To this end, we propose the first multi-norm Certified training for Union RobustnEss (CURE)
043   framework, consisting of a new l2 deterministic certified training defense and several multi-norm
044   certified training methods. Inspired by SABR (Müller et al., 2022a), our l2 defense first finds the l2
045   adversarial examples in a slightly truncated l2 region and then propagates the smaller l∞ box using
046   the IBP loss (Gowal et al., 2018). For multi-norm certified training, we propose several methods
047   based on multi-norm empirical defenses (Tramer & Boneh, 2019; Madaan et al., 2021; Croce & Hein,
048   2022). Our proposed methods successfully improve both the union and universal certified robustness,
049   as illustrated in Table 1, 2, and 2.
050   However, the aforementioned methods achieve sub-optimal union robustness since they do not exploit
051   the connections between certified training for different lp perturbations as well as natural training.

      Neural network verification. We consider deterministic verification methods that analyze a neural
111   network by abstract interpretation (Gehr et al., 2018; Singh et al., 2018; 2019a), optimization via linear
112   programming (LP) (De Palma et al., 2021; Wang et al., 2021; Müller et al., 2022b), mixed integer
113   linear programming (MILP) (Tjeng et al., 2017; Singh et al., 2019b), and semidefinite programming
114   (SDP) (Raghunathan et al., 2018; Dathathri et al., 2020). Many of them are incomplete methods,
115   sacrificing some precision for better scalability since the neural network verification problem is
116   generally NP-complete (Katz et al., 2017). In our work, we analyze multi-norm certified training
117   using deterministic and incomplete verification methods.
118   Certified training. For l∞ certified training, a widely-used method IBP (Mirman et al., 2018; Gowal
119   et al., 2018) minimizes a sound over-approximation of the worst-case loss, calculated using the Box
120   relaxation method. Wong et al. (2018) applies DeepZ (Singh et al., 2018) relaxations, estimating
121   using Cauchy random projections. CROWN-IBP (Zhang et al., 2019b) integrates efficient Box
122   propagation with precise linear relaxation-based bounds during the backward pass to estimate the
123   worst-case loss. Balunović & Vechev (2020) consists of a verifier that aims to certify the network
124   using convex relaxation and an adversary that tries to find inputs causing verification to fail. Shi
125
      et al. (2021) proposes a new weight initialization method for IBP, adds Batch Normalization (BN) to
      each layer and designs regularization with a short warmup schedule. Besides this, SABR (Müller
126
      et al., 2022a) and TAPS (Mao et al., 2024) are unsound improvements over IBP by connecting
127
      IBP to adversarial attacks and adversarial training. For l2 deterministic certified training, recent
128   works (Xu et al., 2022; Hu et al., 2023; 2024) are based on Lipschitz-based certification methods.
129   They design specialized architectures for l2 certified robustness, which is not naturally robust against
130   l∞ perturbations. In Table 5, we show our l2 certified defense has better l2 robustness compared
131   with Hu et al. (2023) on CIFAR-10. To the best of our knowledge, CURE is the first deterministic
132   framework for multi-norm certified robustness, compatible with diverse model architectures.
133
      Robustness against multiple perturbations. Adversarial Training (AT) usually employs gradient
134   descent to discover adversarial examples and incorporates them into training for enhanced adversarial
135   robustness (Tramèr et al., 2017; Madry et al., 2017). Numerous works focus on improving robustness
136   (Zhang et al., 2019a; Carmon et al., 2019; Raghunathan et al., 2020; Wang et al., 2020; Wu et al.,
137   2020; Gowal et al., 2020; Zhang et al., 2021; Debenedetti & Troncoso—EPFL, 2022; Peng et al.,
138   2023; Wang et al., 2023) against a single perturbation type while remaining vulnerable to other
139   types. Tramer & Boneh (2019); Kang et al. (2019) observe that robustness against lp attacks does not
140   necessarily transfer to other lq attacks (q ̸= p). Previous studies (Tramer & Boneh, 2019; Maini et al.,
141   2020; Madaan et al., 2021; Croce & Hein, 2022; Jiang & Singh, 2024) modified Adversarial Training
142
      (AT) to enhance robustness against multiple lp attacks, employing average-case (Tramer & Boneh,
      2019), worst-case (Tramer & Boneh, 2019; Maini et al., 2020; Jiang & Singh, 2024), and random-
143
      sampled (Madaan et al., 2021; Croce & Hein, 2022) defenses. There are also works (Nandy et al.,
144
      2020; Liu et al., 2020; Xu et al., 2021; Xiao et al., 2022; Maini et al., 2022) that use preprocessing,
145   ensemble methods, mixture of experts, and stability analysis to solve this problem. For multi-norm
146   certified robustness, Nandi et al. (2023) study the certified multi-norm robustness with probabilistic
147   guarantees. They apply randomized smoothing, which is expensive to compute in nature, making
148   it impractical for real-world applications. Our work in contrast to these works, proposes the first
149   deterministic certified multi-norm training for better multi-norm and universal certified robustness.

151   3     BACKGROUND
152
153   In this section, we provide the necessary background for CURE. We consider a standard classification
154   task with samples {(xi , yi )}Ni=0 drawn from a data distribution D. The input consists of images
155   x ∈ Rd with corresponding labels y ∈ Rk . The objective of standard training is to obtain a classifier
156   f parameterized by θ that minimizes a loss function L : Rk × Rk → R over D.
157
158   3.1   N EURAL NETWORK VERIFICATION
159
160   Neural network verification is used to formally prove the robustness of a neural network. The
161   portion of the samples that can be proved robust is called certified accuracy. Box or interval
      bounded propagation (Gowal et al., 2018; Mirman et al., 2018) (IBP) is a simple yet effective


                                                          3
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


162
      verification method. Essentially, IBP calculates an over-approximation of the network’s reachable set
163   by propagating an over-approximation of the input region Bp (x, ϵp ), p ∈ {2, ∞} through the network,
164   and then verifies whether all reachable outputs result in the correct classification. For instance, we
165   consider a network f = Lj ◦ σ ◦ Lj−2 ◦ . . . ◦ L1 , with linear layers Li and ReLU activation functions
166   σ. We then propagate Bp (x, ϵp ) layer by layer (see Gowal et al. (2018); Mirman et al. (2018) for
167   more details). For the output o = {oi , oi }i<k
                                                  i=0 , the lower bound of the correct class should be higher
168   than the upper bounds of other classes (∀i ∈ [0, k), i ̸= y, oi − oy < 0) to be provably robust.
169
170   3.2   T RAINING FOR ROBUSTNESS
171
172   A classifier is adversarially robust on an lp -norm ball Bp (x, ϵp ) = {x′ ∈ Rd : ∥x′ − x∥p ≤ ϵp } if
173   it classifies all points within the adversarial region as the correct class. That is, f (x′ ) = y for all
174   perturbed inputs x′ ∈ Bp (x, ϵp ). Training for robustness is formulated as a min-max optimization
175
      problem. Formally, the optimization problem against a specific lp attack can be expressed as follows:
176
177                                  min E(x,y)∼D           max        L(f (x′ ), y)                       (1)
                                       θ                  ′
                                                       x ∈Bp (x,ϵp )
178
179
180
      The inner maximization problem is impossible to solve exactly. Thus, it is often under or over-
      approximated, referred to as adversarial training (Madry et al., 2017; Tramèr et al., 2017) and certified
181
      training (Gowal et al., 2018; Müller et al., 2022a), respectively. Further, the optimization described
182
      above is specific to certain p values and tends to be vulnerable to other perturbation types. To address
183   this, previous research has introduced various methods to train networks adversarially robust against
184   multiple perturbations (l1 , l2 , and l∞ ) simultaneously. In our work, we concentrate on how to train
185   networks to be certifiably robust against multiple lp (l2 , l∞ ) perturbations.
186

206   3.4   U NION CERTIFIED ACCURACY AND UNIVERSAL CERTIFIED ROBUSTNESS
207
208   Union certified accuracy. We focus on the union threat model ∆ = B2 (x, ϵ2 ) ∪ B∞ (x, ϵ∞ ) which
209   requires the DNN to be certifiably robust within the l2 and l∞ adversarial regions simultaneously.
210   Union accuracy is then defined as the robustness against ∆(i) for each xi sampled from D. In this
211   paper, similar to the prior works (Croce & Hein, 2022), we use union accuracy as the main metric to
212
      evaluate the multi-norm certified robustness.
213   Universal certified robustness. We measure the generalization ability of multi-norm certified
214   training to other perturbation types, including rotation, translation, scaling, shearing, contrast, and
215   brightness change of geometric transformations (Balunovic et al., 2019; Yang et al., 2022). We define
      the average certified robustness across these adversaries as universal certified robustness.

      In this section, we present our multi-norm certified training framework CURE. First, we introduce
219   a new deterministic l2 certified training defense. Building on this, we propose several methods for
220   multi-norm certified training against l2 , l∞ perturbations, which serve as the base instantiations of
221   our framework. After that, we design new techniques to improve union-certified accuracy. We note
222   that the techniques inside CURE are applicable to l1 perturbations as well.

259   4.2    I MPROVED MULTI - NORM CERTIFIED TRAINING

261   The methods proposed above are suboptimal as they fail to fully explore the relationship between
262   worst-case IBP losses across different perturbations, certified training (CT), and natural training (NT).
263   To address this, we introduce the following improvements to enhance the union robustness of CURE:
264   (1) We identify a tradeoff between l2 and l∞ perturbations and propose a bound alignment technique
265   to mitigate this, improving multi-norm robustness. (2) We analyze and connect certified and natural
266
      training to attain better union accuracy. (3) To facilitate the procedure for multi-norm robustness with
      pre-trained single norm models, we propose the first certified fine-tuning method, demonstrating its
267
      ability to quickly improve union accuracy using CURE (Table 1).

      Quick certified fine-tuning of single-norm pre-trained classifiers for multi-norm robustness. In
346
      practice, as the model architectures and datasets become larger, multi-norm certified training from
347
      scratch becomes more expensive. Also, there are many pre-trained models available with single norm
348   certified training. In adversarial robustness, Croce & Hein (2022) shows it is possible to obtain
349   state-of-the-art multi-norm robustness by fine-tuning a pre-trained model for a few epochs, which
350   reduces the computational cost significantly. In this work, we propose the first fine-tuning certified
351   multi-norm robustness scheme CURE-Finetune. Starting from a single norm pre-trained model,
352   we perform the bound alignment technique by optimizing LScratch for a few epochs. Because of the
353   lq − lr tradeoff, certifiably finetuning a lq pre-trained model on lr perturbations reduces lq robustness.
354   Thus, we want to preserve more lq robustness when doing certified fine-tuning, which makes bound
355   alignment useful here. By regularizing on the correctly certified lq subset with LScratch , we can prevent
356
      losing more lq robustness when boosting lr robustness, which leads to better union accuracy. We note
      that CURE-Finetune can be adapted to any single-norm certifiably pre-trained models. As shown
357
      in Table 1, compared with other methods, we can quickly obtain a superior multi-norm certified
358
      robustness by performing fine-tuning on pre-trained l∞ models for a few epochs.

518
519   Time cost of CURE. The extra training costs of GP are small, taking 6, 24, 82 seconds using a
520   single NVIDIA A40 GPU on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and TinyImageNet datasets (Table 7), respectively.
521   Compared with the total training cost of CURE-Scratch, it only accounts for ∼ 6% of the total
522   cost. For runtime comparison of different methods, we have a complete runtime analysis (Table 6) in
523   Appendix C for the MNIST experiment. We observe that CURE-Joint has around two times the cost
524
      of other methods. CURE-Finetune has the smallest time cost per epoch, which shows the efficiency
      of our proposed techniques.
525
526   Limitations. For l2 certified training, we use a l∞ box instead of l2 ball for bound propagation,
527   which leads to more over-approximation and the potential loss of precision. Also, we notice drops in
528   clean accuracy in both training from scratch and fine-tuning with CURE methods. In some cases,
529   union accuracy improves slightly but clean accuracy and single lp robustness reduce. Both BA and
530
      GP techniques lead to a slight decrease in clean accuracy on experiments of three datasets. There is
      no negative societal impact of this work.

535   We propose the first framework CURE with a new l2 deterministic certified defense and multi-norm
536   training methods for better union robustness. Further, we devise bound alignment, gradient projection,

538   Extensive experiments on MNIST, CIFAR-10, and TinyImagenet show that CURE significantly
539   improves both union accuracy and robustness against geometric transformations, paving the path to
      universal certified robustness.
