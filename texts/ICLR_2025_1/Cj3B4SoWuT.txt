009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            The time complexity of the standard attention mechanism in transformers scales
012            quadratically with sequence length. We propose a probabilistic framework for
013            attention, enabling us to derive a novel low-rank linear re-parameterisation of both
014            bidirectional and causal cases, based on defining a latent variable model. Our
015            method can be seamlessly integrated as a drop-in replacement for the standard
016
               attention mechanism. Additionally, this framework provides a natural extension for
               combining local standard attention with our global linear attention. This approach
017
               allows us to extend the context length of existing large pre-trained models with
018
               only a few additional training steps. The resulting “Latte Transformer” achieves
019            performance comparable to standard attention and other state-of-the-art models,
020            while maintaining linear time and memory complexity, along with constant-time
021            next-token prediction during inference.

024   1    I NTRODUCTION
025
026   Transformers (Vaswani et al., 2017) are extensively used in sequence modelling, with widespread
027
      applications in natural language processing (NLP) (Devlin et al., 2019; Radford et al., 2019; Touvron
      et al., 2023), and computer vision (Khan et al., 2022; Dosovitskiy, 2020; Liu et al., 2021). The
028
      transformer is based on an attention mechanism that compares each element of a sequence with every
029
      other. This pairwise interaction gives state-of-the-art results on tasks such as language modelling but
030   it comes at the cost of quadratic time and (in standard implementations) quadratic space complexity.
031   While the quadratic space complexity can be reduced to linear in the sequence length by using
032   non-vectorised operations (Rabe & Staats, 2021), there is no method, with the same properties as the
033   standard attention, to reduce the quadratic time complexity, hindering the application of transformers
034   to very long sequences. Another disadvantage of standard attention is its slow inference for the next
035   token prediction, with time complexity scaling linearly with the length of the conditioning sequence
036   (context window), making it expensive for long sequences.
037   The attention mechanism takes an input sequence of token vectors x1 , . . . , xT ; xs ∈ RD and
038   transforms this to a new sequence according to an input-dependent linear function:

      The quadratic cost of attention comes from the matrix multiplication of Q ∈ RT ×D and K T ∈ RD×T
071   in equation 3. The softmax(·) function prevents us from leveraging the associativity       of matrix
072
      multiplication, resulting in operations involving the matrix A = softmax QK T ∈ RT ×T . To

076   enhancing performance while preserving linear complexity. We also demonstrate how this allows
077   us to extend the context length of a pre-trained large language model with only modest additional
078   compute.

      2     L ATTE ATTENTION
081
082
      To overcome the quadratic complexity of standard attention we propose Latent Attention (Latte).
083   Instead of comparing the similarity between each token xs and xt , Latte compares how similar
084   each xs is with learnt latent tokens, see Figure 1. First, in Section 2.1 we consider a non-causal
085   model before presenting the causal approach in Section 2.2, which is needed for auto-regressive
086   models (Radford et al., 2019). We further show in Section 2.3 how to improve performance by
087   combining a local attention mechanism with global latent attention, which we call Latte Macchiato.

      From equation 12 it immediately follows that we can calculate x̃t+1 (i.e. infer the future token)
163   directly from αt,l , βt , ṽt,l , whereas standard attention requires the full sequence x1 , . . . , xt . In this

166   numerically stable implementation of the recursion is given in Appendix B.3. Next token inference in
167   Causal Latte requires O(LD) memory, compared to O(T D) in standard attention. Similarly, the time
168   complexity is O(LD), compared to O(T D) in standard attention. Next token inference is therefore
169   significantly faster than standard attention, assuming L ≪ T . For training we can calculate all terms
170   x̃1 , . . . , x̃T in total time O(T LD) and total space O(T L + LD). This is compared to O(T 2 D) time

      Recent Work. Recent studies have investigated state-based models for mixing tokens within a
263
      sequence. Although inspired by first-order differential equations, these models can also be viewed
264   as linear recurrent neural networks (Gu et al., 2021; Smith et al., 2022). Gated sequence models
265   like Mamba (Gu & Dao, 2023) improve the performance of these models on text by introducing
266   input-dependent weights in the recurrence. In contrast, other approaches combine local attention
267   mechanisms with recurrent layers (Ma et al., 2023; De et al., 2024; Zuo et al., 2022). Our method
268   aligns more closely with the latter class of models, but differs in how we integrate local attention
269   with global linear context. Furthermore, using our Latte-Macchiato method we can easily extend the
      context size of a large pre-trained model in linear time.

      4.5        E XTENDING A P RE - TRAINED M ODEL
476
      Training large models from scratch is computationally expensive, even when the sequence mixing
477   layer (e.g., attention) has linear time and memory complexity. Recent work has demonstrated
478   preliminary success in distilling pre-trained quadratic self-attention layers into sub-quadratic layers,
479   such as Mamba (Bick et al., 2024). However, unlike Latte, these architectures (Gu & Dao, 2023)
480   significantly differ from the attention mechanisms used in standard transformers, making knowledge
481   distillation from pre-trained transformers more complex. Other research has modified relative
482   embeddings in standard attention to enable sequence extrapolation, but the computational cost
483   remains quadratic (Sun et al., 2022). We use Latte-Macchiato with SWA weights taken from a pre-
484   trained large model and show that training only the Latte-specific weights for 1.6B tokens is sufficient.

      5       C ONCLUSION
528
529   We introduced a latent attention mechanism that scales linearly with sequence length and serves as
530   a drop-in replacement for standard attention. While previous approaches have explored low-rank
531
      representations, to the best of our knowledge, none have interpreted these methods as a latent variable
      model, from which a straightforward formulation for both bidirectional and causal variants can
532
      be derived, giving SOTA results in language modelling. Furthermore, our framework extends this
533
      approach by integrating local sliding window attention with global latent attention. This allows one
534   to now easily take a pre-trained large language model and considerably extend its usable context
535   length with only modest additional training and at minimal additional run-time cost.
536
537
      Our initial focus has been on language modelling and long-range classification tasks and future work
      will consider tasks such as question-answering and extensions to multimodal models.
