009                                                 A BSTRACT
010
011            Dataset Distillation (DD) has been applied to various downstream tasks and re-
012            cently scaled to ImageNet-1k, highlighting its potential for practical applications.
013            However, in real-world scenarios, robustness to unseen domains is essential, and
014            the robustness of models trained on synthetic datasets remains uncertain. To ad-
015            dress this, we propose a novel task, Dataset Distillation for Domain Generalization
016            (DD for DG), and evaluate the unseen domain generalization of models trained on
017            synthetic datasets distilled by state-of-the-art DD methods using the DomainBed
018
               benchmark. Additionally, we introduce a new method for this task, which inter-
               prets DD through the lens of image style transfer, achieving superior performance
019
               in unseen domain generalization compared to baseline approaches.

      1    INTRODUCTION
023
024
      To mitigate the burdensome costs associated with large-scale datasets such as storing, transmission,
025   and model training, Dataset Distillation (DD) (Wang et al., 2018) is introduced, which aims to re-
026   duce the size of a dataset by synthesizing a smaller dataset that approximates the original dataset
027   in terms of the performance of the model trained on it. The DD methods simulate the training pro-
028   cess of the model on the original dataset for optimizing the synthetic dataset (Zhao et al., 2021;
029   Cazenavette et al., 2022; Zhao & Bilen, 2023), whose are applied to small-scale datasets such as
030   CIFAR-10/100 (Krizhevsky & Hinton, 2009) and Tiny-ImageNet (Le & Yang, 2015). These meth-
031   ods have been applied to many downstream tasks, such as continual learning (Yang et al., 2023; Gu
032   et al., 2024), privacy preservation (Dong et al., 2022; Chen et al., 2022), federated learning (Liu
033
      et al., 2023; Xiong et al., 2023), and medical imaging (Li & Kainz, 2024). And recently, the advent
      of the decoupling paradigm (Yin et al., 2023; Loo et al., 2024) has paved the way for the distilla-
034
      tion of larger-scale datasets, such as ImageNet-1k/21k (Krizhevsky et al., 2012; Ridnik et al., 2021),
035
      which highlights the potential of using the distilled datasets as an alternative to large-scale datasets
036   in practice for model training and research, etc.
037
038   On the one hand, the model in real-world applications requires the robustness to unseen domains,
039
      as the input data distribution might differ from the distribution encountered during training. Do-
      main Generalization (DG) is proposed to address this challenge and numerous methods have been
040
      studied to improve the generalization to unseen domains. These methods focus on learning domain-
041
      invariant features or designing augmentation processes across multiple seen domains, utilizing tech-
042   niques such as domain adversarial training (Ganin & Lempitsky, 2015; Ganin et al., 2016; Li et al.,
043   2018b; Zhao et al., 2020), domain-invariant representation learning (Li et al., 2018a), and domain
044   augmentation approaches (Zhou et al., 2021b). In addition, various datasets exhibiting domain shifts
045   caused by changes in viewpoint (Fang et al., 2013), or image style (Li et al., 2017; Venkateswara
046   et al., 2017; Peng et al., 2019) have been proposed to evaluate the performance of these meth-
047   ods. The DomainBed benchmark (Gulrajani & Lopez-Paz, 2021) integrates these datasets, offering
048   a comprehensive evaluation for DG methods, and also shows that Empirical Risk Minimization
049   (ERM) (Vapnik, 1998) is a strong baseline compared to these methods.
050   In order to use the distilled dataset as an alternative to the original large-scale dataset in practice, the
051   robustness of the model trained on the synthesized dataset to unseen domains is also required. How-
052   ever, it remains unclear how the model trained on the distilled dataset is robust to unseen domains
053   compared to that trained on the original dataset and whether existing DG methods remain effective
      when applied to distilled datasets. To investigate this, we evaluate the unseen domain generalization

080
081   Inspired by the trade-off relationship between unseen domain generalization performance and
082   dataset distillation efficiency, we propose a novel task, Dataset Distillation for Domain General-
083   ization (DD for DG), and introduce a new approach for this task exploiting the advantages of the
084
      two straightforward approaches. Formally, the proposed task, DD for DG, aims to provide a small
      synthetic dataset that approximates the unseen domain generalization performance of the model
085
      trained on the original dataset, i.e., efficiently distills the original dataset, which improves the ro-
086
      bustness of the model to unseen domains when trained on it. To achieve this goal, we introduce
087   an approach that constructs a single synthetic dataset across domains and learns a domain transfer
088   module that transfers the synthetic dataset into each domain in the original dataset. This approach
089   consists of two processes: a Domain Transfer Learning (DTL) process and a Domain Style Mixing
090   (DSM) process. The DTL process learns to transfer the synthetic images into the style of each do-
091   main in the original dataset by interpreting the dataset distillation loss as a style loss in image style
092   transfer (Gatys et al., 2016; Dumoulin et al., 2017). The DSM process augments the domain transfer
093   process to boost unseen domain generalization performance by mixing the learned domain styles.
094   Our contributions are summarized as follows:
095
096          • We address the unseen domain generalization performance of state-of-the-art Dataset Dis-
097            tillation (DD) methods on the DomainBed benchmark, using two straightforward ap-
098            proaches: DD across domains and DD per domain. The evaluation results highlight the
099            vulnerability of synthetic datasets to unseen domains and the trade-off between generaliza-
100            tion performance and distillation efficiency.
101
             • We propose a novel task, Dataset Distillation for Domain Generalization (DD for DG),
102
               which aims to provide a small synthetic dataset that approximates the robustness to unseen
103            domains in the original dataset. We also introduce a new approach for this task, consisting
104            of two processes: Domain Transfer Learning (DSM) and Domain Style Mixing (DSM).
105
106          • The proposed approach outperforms state-of-the-art DD methods in unseen domain gener-
107            alization, and we conduct ablation studies to further show the effectiveness of the DTL and
               DSM processes in enhancing performance.

      2   RELATED WORK
109
110
111
112   Dataset Distillation aims to produce a small synthetic dataset that approximates the original dataset
113   in terms of the performance of a model trained on that dataset. Building on the first work proposed
114   by Wang et al. (2018), which uses a bi-level optimization approach on small-scale datasets such as
115   MNIST and CIFAR-10, the distillation dataset has been scaled up to the down-scaled version of
116   ImageNet-1k (Krizhevsky et al., 2012) using efficient approaches such as matching the loss gradi-
117   ent (Zhao et al., 2021), training trajectory (Cazenavette et al., 2022), and feature distribution (Zhao
118
      & Bilen, 2023). Recently, larger-scale datasets beyond full-scale ImageNet-1k have started to distill
      with attempts to decouple the optimization process (Yin et al., 2023; Shao et al., 2024; Sun et al.,
119
      2024; Loo et al., 2024; Sun et al., 2024). We mainly focus on DD methods that distill full-scale
120
      ImageNet-1k in this paper.
121
122   The full-scale ImageNet-1k is first distilled by SRe2 L (Yin et al., 2023), by decoupling the opti-
123   mization process into the squeeze and recover processes. They propose to use the batch normal-
124
      ization statistics as a regularization loss to optimize the synthetic dataset with cross-entropy loss.
      G-VBSM (Shao et al., 2024) extends the recover process of SRe2 L using diverse squeeze backbone
125
      models and convolutional layer feature statistics and proposes the data densification loss which con-
126
      straint the synthetic dataset has full rank for the diversity of synthetic images. D3S (Loo et al., 2024)
127   reframes DD as a domain shift problem and uses the KL divergence between the feature statistics
128   of the original and synthetic dataset as a regularization loss. RDED (Sun et al., 2024) replaces the
129   optimization process with a patch-wise scoring, top-k selection, and concatenation process using
130   image patches in the original dataset.
131
      Domain Generalization has been introduced to mitigate the performance degradation caused by
132
      the domain gap between the source and unseen target domain. Extensive research has explored
133   this task, with most approaches based on the premise that domain-invariant features learned from
134   multiple seen domains can also generalize effectively to unseen domains. This goal is typically
135   achieved through domain adversarial training methods (Ganin & Lempitsky, 2015; Ganin et al.,
136   2016; Li et al., 2018b; Zhao et al., 2020; Li et al., 2018a) and domain-invariant-feature learning (Li
137   et al., 2018a). Data augmentation strategies—commonly used as regularization techniques to pre-
138   vent overfitting—also have been explored to enhance the generalization of models to unseen do-
139   mains, such as an augmentation method specifically designed to reduce the domain gap (Volpi &
140   Murino, 2019), employing an off-the-shelf style transfer network for data augmentation (Yue et al.,
141
      2019; Zhou et al., 2021a), and introducing input/feature levels perturbations using gradients/style
      representations (Shankar et al., 2018; Zhou et al., 2021b; 2023). As a benchmark for evaluating
142
      the domain generalization methods, DomainBed (Gulrajani & Lopez-Paz, 2021) has been widely
143
      used and it also argues that Empirical Risk Minimization (ERM) (Vapnik, 1998) is a strong baseline
144   compared to existing DG methods.
145
146   Style Transfer is a task that transfers the style of one image to another while preserving the content
147
      of the former. From the framework proposed by Gatys et al. (2016), the stylized image is obtained
      by minimizing the style and content losses from a random noise image, where the style/content
148
      losses are calculated from the style/content representations of the style/content image and the target
149
      image. The ImageNet-1k pre-trained VGG-19 (Simonyan & Zisserman, 2015) is commonly used
150   as a feature extractor for style representation and content representations, and the former is defined
151   as the feature correlation calculated by the Gram matrix and the latter is defined as the feature
152   map itself. The MSE loss on the style/content representations are used for the style/content losses,
153   respectively, and the style loss can be extended to other forms such as batch norm statistic matching
154   and channel-wise mean and variance matching (Dumoulin et al., 2017; Huang & Belongie, 2017).
155
      To alleviate the limitation of requiring separate optimization processes for multiple styles, a frame-
156   work that transfers the style of a given content image in a single forward pass from a learned style
157   transfer network is proposed Dumoulin et al. (2017); Huang & Belongie (2017). They transfer the
158   given content image to multiple styles using a channel-wise affine transform in the style transfer
159   network, either by learning the style representation within the network from the given style images
160   or by taking the style image as input and extracting the style representation within the network. Also
161   Wang et al. (2021) points out that the ResNet architecture (He et al., 2016) produces unstable style
      loss due to the residual connection, and propose a technique to a alleviate this issue.

181   3     DATASET D ISTILLATION FOR D OMAIN G ENERALIZATION
182
183   We propose a new task, Dataset Distillation for Domain Generalization (DD for DG), which aims
184   to distill a small dataset that can train a model robust to unseen domains, and introduce an approach
185   for this task, consisting of two processes: Domain Transfer Learning (DTL) and Domain Style Mix-
186   ing (DSM), which are explained in the following sections. First, we define the formal definition
187
      of the proposed task and the baseline methods in Section 3.1. Then, we introduce the fundamental
      step of the proposed approach, interpreting the dataset distillation loss as a style loss in image style
188
      transfer (Gatys et al., 2016; Dumoulin et al., 2017), in Section 3.2. We then introduce DTL, which
189
      learns a domain transfer module to adapt the synthetic images to each domain in Section 3.3. Fi-
190   nally, Section 3.4 explains DSM, which combines learned domain styles to enhance unseen domain
191   generalization performance. The overall architecture of the proposed approach is shown in Figure 1.
192

335   where the αce and αstyle denotes the hyperparameters for the cross-entropy loss and the style loss on
336   the synthetic images, respectively, and βce and βper-style denotes the hyperparameters for the cross-
337   entropy loss and the style loss on the style transfer network, respectively.
338
      Technical Details. Due to the memory constraint, it is impractical to train the entire synthetic
339
      dataset S in a single batch, so the training is conducted in pre-defined batch sizes, either by iterating
340   over the classes or instances. The former strategy, class-first iteration, is employed in SRe2L (Yin
341   et al., 2023), where each batch contains a single instance across different classes. In contrast, G-
342   VBSM (Shao et al., 2024) uses the latter strategy, instance-first iteration, where each batch contains
343   instances from the same class, and use the data densification loss, which imposes full rank of the
344   batch, for the diversity of synthetic images.
345
      In our work, we jointly optimize the synthetic dataset S and the style transfer network ψ. The
346   class-wise iteration strategy limits the use of data densification loss, while the instance-wise strategy
347   induces a training bias to the domain transfer network ψ, as it favor latter classes in the iteration
348   process. To resolve this, we implement an epoch loop on the class-wise iteration strategy by a fixed
349   number of iteration steps for each loop over the synthetic dataset S. This epoch loop is terminated
350   when each synthetic data is trained by the total iteration steps, as shown in Algorithm 1.
351
352   3.4     D OMAIN S TYLE M IXING
353
354   Beyond the domain transfer learning, we propose the Domain Style Mixing (DSM) process, which
355   mixes the style of the learned domain style to encourage the model to robust to unseen domain. This
356
      kind of attempt to mix the style of multiple domains to boost the domain generalization performance
      is also proposed in Zhou et al. (2021b), which mixes the style of the images in the batch by the linear
357
      interpolation with the mixing ratio λ from the beta distribution. Where the previous work mixes
358
      the style representation in the CNN feature extractor, which requires the additional component in
359   the model, the proposed method mixes the learned style representation during the style transfer
360   process, which is computed in the style transfer process. The style mixing process itself is similar
361   to the previous work, which mixes the style of the images in the batch by the beta distribution,
362   ψ(S; λθe + (1 − λ)θe′ ), where e and e′ denote mixing domain indexes and λ ∼ Beta(0.1, 0.1)
363   denotes the mixing ratio sampled from beta distribution.

530   5     CONCLUSION
531
532
      We investigate the problem of Domain Generalization (DG) on the synthetic dataset distilled by
533
      Dataset Distillation (DD), and propose a novel task, Dataset Distillation for Domain Generalization
534   (DD for DG), which aims to provide distilled dataset that can be train a model robust to unseen
535   domain generalization. We evaluate the unseen domain generalization performance of the model
536   trained on the synthetic dataset distilled by current DD methods, and propose a novel approach
537   consisting of two processes, Domain Transfer Learning (DTL) and Domain Style Mixing (DSM),
538   by interpret the current DD method from the perspective of Style Transfer. The experimental results
539   and ablation study show that the proposed approach can improve the unseen domain generalization
      performance of the model trained on the synthetic dataset distilled by our approach.
