009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            Neighborhood refinery aims to enhance the neighbor relationships by refining the
012            original distance matrix to ensure pairwise consistency. Traditional context-based
013            methods, which encode instances alongside their local neighbors in a contextual
014            affinity space, are limited in capturing global relationships and are vulnerable to the
015            negative impacts of outliers in the neighborhood. To overcome these limitations,
016
               we propose a novel Neighbor-aware Geodesic Transportation (NGT) for the neigh-
               borhood refinery. NGT first constructs a global-aware distribution for each instance,
017
               capturing the intrinsic manifold relationships among all instances. This is followed
018
               by an optimization transportation process that utilizes the global-aware distribution
019            within the underlying manifold, incorporating global geometric spatial information
020            to generate a refined distance. NGT first involves Manifold-aware Neighbor Encod-
021            ing (MNE) to project each instance into a global-aware distribution by constraining
022            pairwise similarity with the corresponding affinity graph to capture global relation-
023            ships. Subsequently, a Regularized Barycenter Refinery (RBR) module is proposed
024            to integrate local neighbors into a barycenter, employing a Wasserstein term to
025            reduce the influence of outliers. Lastly, Geodesic Transportation (GT) leverages
026            geometric and global context information to transport the barycenter distribution
027
               along the geodesic paths within the affinity graph. Extensive evaluations on several
               tasks, such as re-ranking and deep clustering, demonstrate the superiority of our
028
               proposed NGT.

031   1    I NTRODUCTION
032
033   Neighborhood Refinery (Iscen et al., 2017; Bai et al., 2019; Shao et al., 2023; Chen et al., 2024; Yu
034   et al., 2023; Luo et al., 2024; Liu et al., 2019) is a critical task in machine learning and computer
035   vision, which refines the similarity or distance among neighborhood samples by leveraging the
036   underlying manifold structure. Specifically, given an initial distance matrix obtained from Euclidean
037   space, neighborhood refinery adjusts the pairwise similarity by ensuring consistent relationships
038   among instances, resulting in a more informative refined distance matrix. Due to the higher coherence
039
      exhibited by the neighborhood samples obtained from the refined matrix, neighborhood refinery can
      be effectively applied to image retrieval tasks (Lee et al., 2022; Radenović et al., 2019; Yang et al.,
040
      2021; Tolias et al., 2016) and self-supervised framework (Dwibedi et al., 2021; Koohpayegani et al.,
041
      2021; Van Gansbeke et al., 2020; Niu et al., 2022) for re-ranking and deep clustering, e.g., the refined
042   query-to-gallery similarity matrix can be used to rerank initial retrieval results, and high-confidence
043   pseudo-labels can be generated by the refined distance matrix for providing substantial and diverse
044   supervisory signals. Despite the importance of neighborhood refinery, it remains a challenging task
045   to efficiently identify the robust neighbors within the data manifold.
046
      Recently, several innovative methods have been developed to enhance the robustness of neighborhood
047
      refinery, notably through diffusion-based and context-based approaches. To reveal the intrinsic
048   relationship within the underlying data manifold, diffusion-based methods (Iscen et al., 2017; 2018;
049   Bai et al., 2019; Zhang et al., 2023; Yang et al., 2019) perform similarity propagation within the
050   k-nearest neighbor graph. However, the negative impact of outliers inevitably propagates throughout
051   the graph, leading to a decrease in the discrimination of the resulting similarity matrix used for
052   neighborhood refinery. In addition, context-based methods (Kim et al., 2022; Yu et al., 2023; Chen
053   et al., 2024; Zhong et al., 2017; Zhang et al., 2020) are proposed to map the original feature into a
      contextual affinity feature space by encoding each instance with its local neighbors. Since consistent


                                                         1
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


054
      neighbors are supposed to share high similarity within the contextual affinity space, they are effective
055   in adjusting the similarity matrix. Despite their effectiveness, these methods do not adequately
056   capture global relationships, making the feature representation susceptible to neighborhood outliers.
057   Thus, it is imperative to improve global awareness of the manifold and reduce the impact of outliers
058   to improve the performance of the neighborhood refinery.
059
      Therefore, a reasonable approach is to construct an affinity graph from the original Euclidean space
060
      to intrinsically capture the data manifold information, in which the global-aware distribution can be
061   formulated under the constraint of edge weights in a diffusion manner. To comprehensively perceive
062   the manifold structure, the pairwise distances can be measured by transporting the global-aware
063   distributions along the affinity graph. Moreover, considering the high consistency within the local
064   neighbors, the influence of outliers can be further mitigated by integrating the neighbor distributions
065   into a barycenter. Unlike trivial solutions that simply average neighboring distributions, the Euclidean
066   distance from the barycenter to local neighbors can be jointly minimized with the Wasserstein
067   distance (Solomon et al., 2015; Lin et al., 2020; Cuturi & Doucet, 2014) to enhance the perception
068   of the neighborhood structure. After obtaining the robust barycenter distributions, we incorporate
069
      global geometric spatial information to distinguish instances that are not in the close neighborhood
      domain more effectively. This can be achieved by transporting (Cuturi, 2013; Janati et al., 2020)
070
      the global-aware distributions along the geodesic path within the affinity graph, where the resulting
071
      distance can be used for neighborhood refinery.
072
073   In this work, we propose a novel Neighbor-aware Geodesic Transportation (NGT) to exploit the
074   underlying manifold information for neighborhood refinery, consisting of Manifold-aware Neighbor
075
      Encoding (MNE), Regularized Barycenter Refinery (RBR) and Geodesic Transportation (GT). First,
      by taking advantage of the diffusion process, Manifold-aware Neighbor Encoding (MNE) embeds
076
      each instance into a manifold-aware feature space. The resulting distributions are regularized by
077
      the affinity weights of a k-nearest neighbor graph constructed from the original Euclidean space,
078   allowing the global manifold relationships between each instance and the entire dataset to be captured.
079   Afterward, a Regularized Barycenter Refinery (RBR) is proposed to integrate local neighbors into
080   a barycenter to mitigate the negative effects of outliers. Formally, the Euclidean distance from
081   barycenter to local neighbors and the Wasserstein distance serving as a regularization term is jointly
082   minimized. This approach implicitly considers the pairwise connections between neighborhood
083   instances, enabling us to obtain more robust and global-aware distributions. Finally, we introduce
084   Geodesic Transportation (GT) to measure the pairwise distances among distributions by formulating
085   it as an optimal transport problem. Specifically, distributions are transported along geodesic paths
086
      within the affinity graph, inherently incorporating the geometric and global context information of the
      data manifold. To maintain consistency in Euclidean space, we combine the resulting transportation
087
      distance with the original Euclidean distance to perform neighborhood refinery.
088
089   The proposed Neighbor-aware Geodesic Transportation (NGT) can be easily adapted to the image
090   retrieval tasks for re-ranking and the self-supervised framework for deep clustering. Extensive
091   experiments have shown the superiority of our proposed method. Specifically, NGT achieves the
092
      mAP of 81.1%/91.7% on ROxf(M) and RPar(M) respectively. Moreover, after conducting NGT to
      deep clustering tasks, the obtained performance surpasses the top-performs model by 2.4%/1.1%/2.1%
093
      in NMI/ACC/ARI on CIFAR-20 respectively.

096   2     R ELATED W ORK
097
098   Neighborhood refinery tackles the challenge of identifying semantically similar neighbors for each
099   sample by considering the distance relationships among all samples in the feature space. This process,
100   known as re-ranking in the context of image retrieval, serves as a post-processing approach that can
101   significantly enhance retrieval performance. Similarly, it can also provide richer supervisory signals
102   for training in deep clustering, improving both feature learning and clustering results.
103
104   2.1   R E - RANKING
105
106   Image retrieval seeks to identify images with similar content from a large database, while re-ranking
107   is a training-free technique that improves the overall retrieval performance by refining the initial
      ranking list through a second retrieval or optimization process. Existing re-ranking methods can


                                                         2
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


108
      be broadly classified into four categories: Query Expansion, learning-based, context-based, and
109   diffusion-based methods. Query expansion methods (Chum et al., 2007; Gordo et al., 2017; Radenović
110   et al., 2019; Shao et al., 2023) focus on integrating neighbor image features through various averaging
111   and weighting strategies to build a more effective query. Meanwhile, learning-based methods involve
112   approaches utilizing self-attention mechanisms (Ouyang et al., 2021; Gordo et al., 2020) for adjustable
113   weight aggregation, along with techniques that leverage graph neural networks (Liu et al., 2019; Shen
114   et al., 2021a) to enhance sample perception across the entire feature space.
115   The insight that the contextual information captured by k-nearest neighbors can enhance retrieval
116   performance has prompted the development of context-based re-ranking. Shen et al. (2012); Sarfraz
117   et al. (2018) update the distance measure using the rank lists of k-nearest neighbors. Additionally, Yu
118   et al. (2023); Chen et al. (2024); Kim et al. (2022); Zhang et al. (2020); Zhong et al. (2017) encode
119   each instance into a contextual affinity space to perform neighborhood refinery, ensuring similar
120   images have higher consistency. Leveraging the intrinsic manifold structure of data, diffusion-based
121   methods serve as powerful neighborhood refinery techniques. Prominent works (Iscen et al., 2018;
122   Yang et al., 2019; Luo et al., 2024; Zhang et al., 2023) employ the affinity graph to represent the
123
      underlying data manifold and have shown remarkable performance in image retrieval, while Bai et al.
      (2019); Chang et al. (2019); Zhou et al. (2012) further incorporate the hypergraphs to effectively
124
      aggregate higher-order information, leading to better performance.
125
126
127   2.2   D EEP C LUSTERING AND S ELF -S UPERVISED L EARNING
128
      Deep clustering seeks to simultaneously learn image representations and conduct clustering in an
129
      integrated manner, while neighborhood refinery can serve as an adaptive module to provide richer
130   information during the training stage. Over the past few decades, substantial research endeavors
131   (Metaxas et al., 2023; Cai et al., 2023; Shiran & Weinshall, 2021; Li et al., 2022; 2024) have been
132   directed towards this task. Recently, driven by self-supervised learning, deep clustering has advanced
133   significantly. For instance, IDFD (Tao et al., 2021), MoCo (He et al., 2020), SimCLR (Chen et al.,
134   2020) and ProPos (Huang et al., 2023) leverage pretext tasks and construct contrastive losses for
135   representation learning, while approaches such as BYOL (Grill et al., 2020) and SwAV Caron et al.
136   (2020) introduce a non-contrastive paradigm. The incorporation of self-supervised methods has
137   enriched the learned features and enhanced clustering performance. Building on these approaches,
138
      the supervisory information embedded in neighboring samples has also been utilized to guide feature
      learning. Dwibedi et al. (2021); Navaneet et al. (2022); Yu et al. (2023) demonstrate that leveraging
139
      semantically similar neighbor instances can further enhance the robustness and quality of learned
140
      features. Thus, employing neighborhood refinery techniques to effectively identify and aggregate
141   neighbor information represents an important direction for deep clustering.

144   3     M ETHODOLOGY
145
146   The Neighborhood Refinery aims to adjust for better neighborhood relationships by utilizing infor-
147   mation from the underlying structure of the data manifold. We propose a novel Neighbor-aware
148   Geodesic Transportation (NGT) (Figure 1). comprising Manifold-aware Neighbor Encoding (MNE),
149
      Regularized Barycenter Refinery (RBR), and Geodesic Transportation (GT) to capture both global re-
      lationships and local features. Global relationships refer to interactions across the entire dataset, while
150
      local features reflect immediate connections between closely situated entities. MNE first embeds
151
      each instance into a manifold-aware space under the supervision of an affinity graph constructed from
152   Euclidean space to enhance the perception of the global manifold structure. After that, to mitigate the
153   negative effect of outliers, RBR integrates local neighbors into a robust barycenter distribution by
154   adding a Wasserstein regularization term to constrain the pairwise relationships among neighbors.
155   Finally, GT transports the distributions along the geodesic path within the affinity graph, combining
156   geometric and global context information to generate the refined distance matrix.
157

344   where d(i, j) is the Euclidean distance, and θ is the balance weight. All pairwise distances among
345   barycenter distributions can be calculated in a parallel manner, resulting in a refined distance matrix
346   that can be used for neighborhood refinery.

      Time Complexity Analysis: Our method involves three main components: MNE, RBR, and GT. For
457
      MNE, we approximate the solution to the optimization problem in an iterative manner with Equation 5,
458   achieving a time complexity of O(n3 ). Similarly, RBR introduces entropy regularization term to find
459   the fixed-point relationship for Equation 6, the iterative solution also results in a complexity of O(n3 ).
460   The GT module leverages the Sinkhorn-Knopp algorithm to handle the optimal transport problem,
461   maintaining the same complexity. Consequently, the total time complexity of NGT is O(n3 ). The
462   comparisons of time complexity and re-ranking latency conducted on ROxford are shown in Table 6,
463   our method achieves a latency of 3,360 ms on GPU, demonstrating its outstanding performance. As
464   for the large-scale datasets, we have implemented a coarse-to-fine re-ranking strategy, where our
465   method is applied only to re-rank the top-k images in the initial ranking list. Specifically, the latency
466
      remains within 4 seconds when k equals 5,000, underscoring the effectiveness of our method.

      5       C ONCLUSION
532
533
      Searching for the most informative neighbors within the data manifold is a crucial problem in the
534
      field of machine learning and computer vision. In this paper, we propose a novel Neighbor-aware
535   Geodesic Transportation strategy to address the problem, which consists of Manifold-aware Neighbor
536   Encoding, Regularized Barycenter Refinery and Geodesic Transportation. Extensive evaluations on
537   several tasks, such as re-ranking and deep clustering, demonstrate the superiority of our proposed
538   NGT. In future work, we will leverage intermediate properties of the transport process to reduce
539   unnecessary computations, thereby improving the efficiency of the neighborhood refinery algorithm.

       where the N (xi ) denotes the set of neighborhood samples of xi within the Euclidean space. Since
1211   k-nearest neighbors may unavoidably include negative samples in the computation of group-aware
1212   loss, this error could accumulate throughout the training process. Thus exploiting the robust neighbors
1213   (Yu et al., 2023; Dwibedi et al., 2021) from the data manifold constructed by the batch of image
1214   features can help improve the quality of neighborhoods and provide a more precise supervision signals.
1215   Intuitively, the original k-nearest neighbors can be replaced with our proposed NGT neighborhood,
1216   such that the the negative influence can be mitigated with the precise neighboring information brought
1217   by NGT. Similar to previous neighbor guided self-supervised learning strategy, our algorithm consists
1218   of two parts, the first t0 epochs for instance-aware pre-training and the rest epochs for group-aware
1219
       concordance training with NDT to find refined neighborhood, the overall loss is formulated as:
1220                                   Ltotal = 1(t < t0 )LI + 1(t ≥ t0 )LG .                              (66)

1222   For the deep clustering tasks, the performance are measured on five widely used benchmarks,
1223   including CIFAR-10 (Krizhevsky et al., 2009), CIFAR-20 (Krizhevsky et al., 2009), STL-10 (Coates
1224   et al., 2011), ImageNet-10 (Chang et al., 2017), and ImageNet-Dogs (Chang et al., 2017). Where
1225   CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-20 are moderate scale dataset both containing 60,000 images. Following the
1226   settings in Huang et al. (2023); Yu et al. (2023), we resize the images to the scale of 32 × 32 for
1227   CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-20, 96 × 96 for STL-10 and ImageNet-10, 224 × 224 for ImageNet-Dogs.
1228   We strictly follow Huang et al. (2023); Shen et al. (2021b); Yu et al. (2023); Li et al. (2021; 2022) to
1229   perform fair comparison. Specifically, we conduct the same augmentation strategies and adopt the
1230   stochastic gradient optimizer and the cosine decay learning schedule with first 50 epochs for warming
1231   up. The entire network is trained for 1000 epochs, with the initial 800 epochs utilizing standard BYOL
1232   loss LI , followed by the remaining 200 epochs employing LG , which incorporates our proposed
1233   method. In order to enhance training efficiency and mitigate the potential error accumulations, we
1234
       employ a straightforward 0-1 matrix to calculate the Geodesic Transportation distance. For the
       architecture, we use ResNet-18 on CIFAR-10 and CIFAR-20 and ResNet-34 on the rest of datasets
1235
       for a fair comparison, The base learing rate is 0.05 and batchsize we use is 128 for CIFAR-10 and 64
1236
       for others, which is smaller than the existing methods. The select the hyper parameter of k1 = 10
1237   and k2 = 4 for the first four datasets, and k1 = 25 and k2 = 8 for the challenging ImageNet-Dogs
1238   with many categories that are not easy to distinguish. We train the model with 4 GTX 3090 GPU,
1239   the performance converges within 200 epochs of training, which takes about 4 hours. During the
1240   evaluation procedure, the labels of features encoded by the target encoder are assigned by k-means to
1241   validate the clustering performance for a fair comparison.
