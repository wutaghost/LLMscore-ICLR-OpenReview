009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            Traditional classification models are typically optimized solely for their specific
012            training task without considering the properties of the underlying probability dis-
013            tribution of their output space. As the use of these models for downstream tasks
014            becomes more prevalent, it becomes advantageous to have a framework that can
015            transform the output space of such models to a more convenient space without
016            sacrificing performance. In this paper, we introduce DeepKDE, a novel method
017            which enables the transformation of arbitrary output spaces to match more de-
018
               sirable distributions, such as Normal and Gaussian Mixture Models. We explore
               the properties of the new method and test its effectiveness on ResNet-18 and vi-
019
               sion transformers trained on CIFAR-10 and Fashion MNIST datasets. We show
020
               that DeepKDE models succeed in transforming the output spaces of the original
021            models while outperforming them in terms of accuracy.

024   1   I NTRODUCTION
025
026   Leveraging pre-trained models to enhance performance in downstream tasks is a widely used tech-
027   nique. In this framework, a large model is trained on abundance of data, and the resulting output
028   can then serve as a starting point for a new task such as classification (Plested & Gedeon, 2022),
029   anomaly detection (Chalapathy & Chawla, 2019), clustering (Ren et al., 2022) and more. However,
030   the optimization of the primary model focuses on refining its architecture, hyperparameters, and
031   training process to best suit the primary task. These optimizations are not designed to match any
032   underlying probability distribution thus resulting with an arbitrary and intractable probability distri-
033   bution. It would be advantageous to reshape the output space of such models to align with a more
034
      convenient underlying probability function. This can enable downstream algorithms to benefit from
      the knowledge of the feature space distribution.
035
      Reshaping a feature space to match a specific distribution is commonly done in various learning
036
      tasks. However, the current approach is usually task-specific and not a generic solution to transform
037   the output of any arbitrary model.
038   In image generation tasks, deep generative models such as Variational Auto Encoders (VAE)
039   (Kingma & Welling, 2014), Normalizing Flows (Rezende & Mohamed, 2015) and Diffusion Models
040   (Ho et al., 2020), link between the input space and a feature space with a known underlying proba-
041   bility density function, so new data points could be easily sampled during inference. In the context
042   of clustering tasks, the process of combining representation learning using deep neural networks
043   (DNN) together with a clustering method is referred to as deep clustering. Some deep clustering
044   methods aim to generate a feature space that follows a multimodal distribution such as Gaussian
045
      Mixture Model (GMM). Notable methods in this category include Xie et al. (2016) and Yang et al.
      (2017) who proposed methods to construct deep clustering using autoencoder networks. Mukherjee
046
      et al. (2018) proposed a methodology to train clustering Generative Adversarial Networks and Jiang
047
      et al. (2017) used VAE with GMM as a prior. In unsupervised anomaly detection tasks, optimiza-
048   tion was done for deep representation learning along with one class objective such as a hyperplane
049   (Chalapathy et al., 2018) or a hypersphere (Ruff et al., 2018).
050   Looking at the aforementioned methods, we can identify two modes of operations: the first involves
051   a joint optimization of DNN parameters and reshaping the feature space to meet a target distribution
052   (Yang et al., 2017; Jiang et al., 2017; Chalapathy et al., 2018; Ruff et al., 2018). The second involves
053   a two-step procedure starting with optimizing DNN parameters, followed by refining the feature
      space to best suit the task (Xie et al., 2016). Given the growing size of the state-of-the-art models


                                                         1
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


054
      and the increasing complexity of the tuning methods, it is more practical to adopt the two-step mode
055   of operation.
056   In this paper we introduce the DeepKDE a novel deep method. We take state-of-the-art classification
057   models and use DeepKDE networks to reshape their output space to match underlying probability
058   density functions such as Normal distributions or GMMs while also improving their classification
059   performance. The workflow begins with taking a primary tuned model and projecting the input data
060   to a predefined layer within the model that captures meaningful features. The projected data is then
061   fed as an input to a DeepKDE network. The DeepKDE network is trained with a loss function op-
062   timized to match a desired probability density function while incorporating constraints to preserve
063
      the information contained in the input data (Figure 1). Our work focuses on classification models
      but can be extended to other tasks.

      2.2   A RCHITECTURE
197
198   A DeepKDE basic block consists of three consecutive layers: A linear layer followed by batch
199   normalization (Ioffe & Szegedy, 2015) and Parametric Rectified Linear Unit (PReLU) (He et al.,
200   2015b). The DeepKDE net architecture is constructed as an autoencoder and can be formally ex-
201   pressed by:
202
203                               Block(x) = PReLU(BatchNorm(Linear(x)))
204                               Latent(x) = Linear(Block(...Block(x)))                                 (13)
205
                               DeepKDE(x) = Linear(Latent(x))

429   4   C ONCLUSIONS AND F UTURE W ORK
430
431   In this paper we introduced the DeepKDE, a method for transforming the output space of classifica-
      tion models to match more desirable distributions, such as Normal and Gaussian Mixture Models.


                                                         8
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


432
      We formulated the theoretical foundations of our method, explored its properties, and tested its ef-
433   fectiveness on ResNet-18 and ViT classification models trained on CIFAR-10 and Fashion MNIST
434   datasets. Our results demonstrate that DeepKDE models succeed in transforming the data while
435   enhancing the classification performance compared to the original models. Furthermore, the results
436   can be further explained by using the knowledge about the underlying probability of the new space.
437   Our aim in introducing this method is to establish a new application flow in downstream tasks such
438   as anomaly detection and clustering.
