011                                                A BSTRACT
012
013            Abstract
014            Transformer-based models have achieved significant success in time series forecast-
015            ing by modeling global dependencies through self-attention mechanisms. However,
016            these models often rely on fixed patch settings with locality constraints, tokenizing
017            time series into spatially connected sub-series. This approach can hinder the capture
018            of semantic relationships and lead to computational inefficiencies, especially when
019            dealing with long sequences with complex temporal dependencies. In this work,
020
               we introduce TimeCAT—a Time series Context-Aware Transformer that dynami-
               cally groups input sequences into semantically coherent groups, enabling efficient
021
               modeling of both local and global dependencies. By appending group and global
022
               tokens, TimeCAT facilitates fine-grained information exchange through a novel
023            Context-Aware Mixing Block, which utilizes self-attention and MLP mixing opera-
024            tions. This hierarchical approach efficiently models long sequences by processing
025            inputs in structured contexts, reducing computational overhead without sacrificing
026            accuracy. Experiments on several challenging real-world datasets demonstrate
027            that TimeCAT achieves consistent state-of-the-art performance, significantly im-
028            proving forecasting accuracy and computational efficiency over existing methods.
029            This advancement enhances the Transformer family with improved performance,
030            generalization ability, and better utilization of sequence information.

033   1    I NTRODUCTION
034
035   Time series forecasting plays a pivotal role in various domains such as finance (Zhang et al., 1998),
036   weather prediction (Rasp & Lerch, 2018), energy management (Ahmed & Khalid, 2019), and
037   healthcare (Cheng et al., 2017). Accurate long-horizon forecasting is essential for informed decision-
038   making and strategic planning in these fields. The advent of deep learning has spurred significant
039
      advancements in modeling complex temporal patterns, with Transformer-based models emerging
      as a powerful tool due to their ability to capture long-range dependencies through self-attention
040
      mechanisms (Vaswani, 2017; Zhou et al., 2022a; Wu et al., 2023; Liu et al., 2024).
041
042   Despite their success, existing Transformer-based approaches face fundamental challenges that limit
043   their effectiveness in time series forecasting. A central issue lies in the design of input tokenization
044   strategies. As illustrated in Figure 1-(a), traditional methods employ point-wise tokenization (pixel-
045
      level), patch-wise tokenization (fixed-length sub-series), or series-wise tokenization (entire sequence
      as a single token). Point-wise tokenization, while fine-grained, incurs prohibitive computational
046
      costs due to the quadratic complexity of self-attention with respect to sequence length. Patch-wise
047
      tokenization (Nie et al., 2023) reduces computational burden but may impede the model’s ability
048   to capture long-range dependencies effectively, as it imposes locality constraints. Furthermore,
049   modeling the multivariate interactions using such methods is not straightforward and poses significant
050   challenges. Series-wise tokenization (Liu et al., 2024) captures holistic temporal patterns but struggles
051   with modeling local context and becomes impractical for long sequences or large datasets due to
052   high computational demands. Furthermore, the current fixed-length path-based and whole-series
053   tokenization methods present significant challenges for time series foundation models, either due to
      computational overhead or feasibility issues (Goswami et al., 2024; Das et al., 2023).

071
      To address these challenges, we observe that time series data inherently exhibit hierarchical temporal
072
      structures and varying semantic characteristics across different segments. This insight motivates a
073
      context-aware approach where sequences are dynamically partitioned into semantically coherent
074   groups based on the input context. By doing so, we can model both local patterns within groups and
075   global trends across the entire sequence more effectively.
076
077
      In this paper, we introduce TimeCAT—a Time series Context-Aware Transformer designed to
      capture complex temporal dependencies while maintaining computational efficiency. As depicted in
078
      Figure 1-(b), our approach dynamically groups sequences based on their semantic content, enabling
079
      efficient hierarchical mixing at different levels of context. We propose a novel Context-Aware Mixing
080   Block that facilitates three hierarchical levels of information exchange: (1) Intra-Group Mixing
081   focuses on capturing local dependencies within each group by applying self-attention mechanisms to
082   tokens specific to that group. (2) Inter-Group Mixing enables interactions across different groups
083   through mixing layers that aggregate information, thereby enhancing the model’s ability to learn
084   cross-group dependencies. Finally, (3) Global-Level Mixing incorporates a global token that collects
085   information from all groups, capturing overarching temporal trends and facilitating interactions across
086   variables throughout the entire sequence.
087   Our hierarchical processing scheme exploits both local and global patterns in a structured manner,
088   addressing the limitations of traditional tokenization strategies. As illustrated in Figure 1-(c), the
089   integration of group tokens and a global token allows TimeCAT to holistically model temporal
090   dynamics, effectively balancing computational efficiency with accurate representation learning. By
091   capturing rich interactions across different token types and levels of context, TimeCAT enhances the
092   modeling of complex temporal patterns inherent in time series data. Our work makes the following
093   key contributions:
094   • We propose a novel dynamic grouping mechanism that segments time series data into semanti-
095     cally meaningful groups based on input context, enabling efficient intra-group and inter-group
096     interactions without compromising temporal context.
097
      • We introduce a Context-Aware Transformer architecture that utilizes a hierarchical mixing block, fa-
098
        cilitating fine-grained information exchange across intra-group, inter-group, and global levels. This
099     design captures complex temporal patterns while significantly reducing computational complexity.
100
101   • Extensive experiments on challenging real-world datasets demonstrate that TimeCAT outperforms
102
        state-of-the-art models in both forecasting accuracy and computational efficiency, validating its
        effectiveness in modeling complex time series data.
103
104   By addressing the fundamental challenges in time series forecasting with Transformers, TimeCAT
105   sets a new direction for efficient and accurate modeling of temporal data. Our approach leverages
106   hierarchical context and dynamic grouping to overcome the limitations of existing methods, making
107   it well-suited for real-world applications requiring long-horizon forecasting, including the time series
      foundation models.

      2     R ELATED W ORK
109
110   Transformer and MLP-based Time Series Forecasting Transformers have been widely adopted
111   in time series forecasting (Zhou et al., 2021; 2022b; Zhang & Yan, 2023; Chen et al., 2021; 2024).
112   PatchTST (Nie et al., 2022) introduced a Transformer architecture that splits input time series into
113   fixed-length patches, applying self-attention for temporal information extraction. However, PatchTST
114   lacks cross-channel interactions. Extensions have addressed these limitations, such as varied patch
115   sizes for multi-resolution representations (Zhang et al., 2024), and representing the entire time series
116   as a single token to capture holistic information (Liu et al., 2024). While the latter effectively models
117   inter-variable interactions, it may lose temporal dynamics and is impractical for long sequences or
118
      large datasets. Alternatively, TimeMixer (Wang et al., 2024) uses a pure MLP-based mixing module
      to explore multi-scale representation learning, showing that distinct temporal patterns enhance
119
      forecasting. However, building large foundational time series models with MLP backbones poses
120
      challenges (Liang et al., 2024).
121
122   Recently, tokenization has gained attention as a crucial element in Transformer-based foundation
123   models (Qian et al., 2022). MOMENT, a family of time series foundation models, emphasizes
124
      tokenization to model temporal dynamics (Goswami et al., 2024). Das et al. (2023) propose a
      decoder-only Transformer focusing on efficient token structures, while Garza & Mergenthaler-
125
      Canseco (2023) introduce TimeGPT-1, achieving state-of-the-art results by leveraging tokenization to
126
      model complex temporal relationships. These works highlight tokenization’s key role in developing
127   robust Transformer-based time series models, which still primarily focus on fixed-length tokenization.
128
129   Token Merging & Clustering Methods To enhance token-based foundation models’ efficiency,
130   various token merging and clustering approaches have been proposed in both time series and image
131   domains. Götz et al. (2024) introduce a token merging mechanism to reduce complexity by grouping
132   tokens, significantly speeding up pretrained models on multivariate time series datasets but acting as a
133   low-pass filter, potentially degrading prediction accuracy. In the image domain, "ToMe" (Bolya et al.,
134   2022) merges redundant tokens in Vision Transformers (ViT) for a speed-accuracy trade-off without
135   retraining. Additionally, Fan et al. (2024) propose clustering tokens based on semantic relevance,
136   reducing computational costs but potentially affecting performance due to limited inter-cluster
137
      interactions and reliance on a global token.
138   These works inspire our approach, which integrates context-aware token grouping within a
139   Transformer-based backbone to enable multi-scale information interactions. Unlike fine-grained,
140   fixed-length patch-based methods (Nie et al., 2023) or coarse-grained whole-sequence tokeniza-
141   tion (Liu et al., 2024), our approach introduces novel intra-group, inter-group, and global-level
142   operations for efficient, fine-grained interactions.
