                                                 A BSTRACT
008
009
               Adam and its variants, including AdaBound, AdamW, and AdaBelief, have gained
010
               widespread popularity for enhancing the learning speed and generalization perfor-
011
               mance of deep neural networks. This optimization technique adjusts weight vec-
012            tors by utilizing predetermined exponential decay rates (i.e.,ő≤1 = 0.9, ő≤2 = 0.999)
013            based on the first moment estimate and the second raw moment estimate of the
014            gradient. However, the default exponential decay rates might not be optimal, and
015            the process of tuning them through trial and error with experience proves to be
016            time-consuming. In this paper, we introduce AdamE, a novel variant of Adam
017            designed to automatically leverage dynamic exponential decay rates on the first
018            moment estimate and the second raw moment estimate of the gradient. Addition-
019            ally, we provide theoretical proof of the convergence of AdamE in both convex
020
               and non-convex cases. To validate our claims, we perform experiments across
               various neural network architectures and tasks. Comparative analyses with adap-
021
               tive methods utilizing default exponential decay rates reveal that AdamE consis-
022
               tently achieves rapid convergence and high accuracy in language modeling, node
023            classification, and graph clustering tasks.

026   1   I NTRODUCTION
027
028   Adam Kingma & Ba (2014), a widely adopted stochastic optimization method, has been applied
029   across various domains in recent years, including object detection Kim et al. (2022), natural language
030   processing Gu et al. (2022), and node classification Meng et al. (2021). Despite its widespread use,
031   Adam‚Äôs performance in training deep neural networks (DNNs) can be sensitive to improper learning
032   rates, whether too large or too small. To address this issue, several improved variants have been
033
      proposed, such as AdaBound Luo et al. (2019), RAdam Liu et al. (2019), Padam Chen et al. (2018),
      AdamW Loshchilov & Hutter (2017), and AdaBelief Zhuang et al. (2020).
034
035   Building on the principles of adaptive optimization, we introduce a novel technique, AdamE, which
036   incorporates adaptive exponential decay rates. This approach dynamically adjusts the decay rates
037   for both the first and second moments of the gradient. The primary contributions of our study are as
038   follows:
039
            ‚ÄĘ Introduction of AdamE: We propose a new optimization algorithm, AdamE, designed for
040
              DNNs. Compared to the standard Adam optimizer and other mainstream methods, AdamE
041
              exhibits superior adaptability to current gradient values, resulting in faster convergence and
042           improved model stability.
043
            ‚ÄĘ Theoretical analysis of AdamE: We provide a detailed theoretical analysis of AdamE‚Äôs
044
              convergence properties in both convex and non-convex stochastic optimization settings,
045
              demonstrating its enhanced convergence behavior.
046
047
            ‚ÄĘ Experimental validation: The effectiveness of AdamE is validated through experiments
              on established datasets, including WikiText-2, BBBP, Cora, Citeseer, and DBLP. Extensive
048
              experimental results show that AdamE achieves state-of-the-art performance.

513   7   C ONCLUSIONS
514
515   In this study, we systematically analyze the impact of varying exponential decay rates on the con-
516   vergence and generalization performance of the Adam optimization algorithm. Building on this
517   analysis, we introduce an enhanced version of the algorithm, termed AdamE, and provide a compre-
518   hensive exposition of its design. The effectiveness of AdamE is demonstrated through experiments
519   on the quadratic function fitting problem, along with a theoretical investigation of its convergence
520   properties in convex settings.
521   We further evaluate the empirical performance of AdamE across a range of deep learning tasks,
522   including language modeling, node classification, and graph clustering. Both the theoretical analyses
523   and experimental results consistently highlight the superior performance of the proposed AdamE
524   algorithm.
