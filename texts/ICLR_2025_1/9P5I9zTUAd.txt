009                                                 A BSTRACT
010
011            With the proliferation of large language models (LLMs), the comprehensive align-
012            ment of such models across multiple tasks has emerged as a critical area of research.
013            Existing alignment methodologies primarily address single task, such as multi-
014            turn dialogue, coding, mathematical problem-solving, and tool usage. However,
015            AI-driven products that leverage language models usually necessitate a fusion
016
               of these abilities to function effectively in real-world scenarios. Moreover, the
               considerable computational resources required for proper alignment of LLMs
017
               underscore the need for a more robust, efficient, and encompassing approach to
018
               multi-task alignment, ensuring improved generative performance. In response to
019            these challenges, we introduce a novel technique termed Mixture-of-Instructions
020            (MoI), which employs a strategy of instruction packing combined with diverse
021            system prompts to boost the alignment efficiency of language models. We have
022            also compiled a diverse set of seven benchmark datasets to rigorously evaluate the
023            alignment efficacy of the MoI-enhanced language model. Our methodology was
024            applied to the open-source Qwen-7B-chat model, culminating in the development
025            of Qwen-SFT-MoI. This enhanced model demonstrates significant advancements
026            in generative capabilities across coding, mathematics, and tool use tasks.
027
028   1    I NTRODUCTION
029
030   The rise of large language models (LLMs) has underscored the importance of effective training
031   techniques, encompassing Pre-training, Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT), and Reinforcement Learning
032   from Human Feedback (RLHF), to expand their knowledge and ensure alignment with human values
033   for greater reliability. However, despite advancements, LLMs often falter in specialized tasks such
034   as coding, mathematics, and reasoning. With the growing access to high-quality, domain-specific
035   datasets(Luo et al., 2023c; Li et al., 2023a; CodeFuse, 2023; Wei et al., 2023; Yue et al., 2023; Yu
036   et al., 2023; Luo et al., 2023a; Zeng et al., 2023; Qin et al., 2023), this study investigates how targeted
037   SFT can boost LLM capabilities in these areas while preserving existing competencies.
038   We examined a case where the Qwen-7B-chat (Bai et al., 2023)model failed to learn Python code for
039   the Boyer-Moore majority vote algorithm. Training with standard code dataset proved ineffective,
040   with the model producing suboptimal solutions due to prompt-induced knowledge conflicts. To our
041   surprise, we found that simply changing to a different system prompt and retraining could easily
042   overcome this issue, indicating that altering prompts can resolve knowledge conflicts and improve
043   task performance across tasks.
044   Leveraging our initial findings, we developed a strategy that assigns unique system prompts to
045   different tasks and integrates these varying prompts into a unified instruction set for model alignment.
046   We introduce the Mixture of Instructions (MoI) methodology, a structured approach to amalgamate
047   open-source datasets for comprehensive multi-task training, spanning dialogue, code, math, and
048   agent-based tasks, each with bespoke prompts. This not only recasts training as a multi-task learning
049   (Chung et al., 2024; Wei et al., 2021; Raffel et al., 2020; Sanh et al., 2021) endeavor but also tackles
050
      challenges such as dataset bias, where improvement in one task could detriment another. Through
      MoI, we adeptly use diverse prompts to ensure balanced task performance, effectively navigating the
051
      complexities of multi-task learning.
052
053   Ultimately, we integrated the MoI with multi-turn dialogue, code generation, mathematics reasoning
      and tool usage datasets for comprehensive training, resulting in a significantly enhanced language

153   2.2   PACKING I NSTRUCTIONS WITH BALANCED S AMPLING
154
155
      Building on the effectiveness of system prompts, we investigate whether dividing the training dataset
156   into multiple tasks and setting specific system prompts for each improves model alignment. Multi-task
157   learning, as shown by numerous studies (Radford et al., 2019; Sanh et al., 2021; Chung et al., 2024),
158   demonstrates that pre-trained + fine-tuned language models inherently possess multi-task capabilities.
159   However, when aggregating datasets from multiple tasks for sequence training, we found that models
160   trained on combined tasks performed worse than those trained on individual tasks as shown in Table
161   2. This highlights a common issue in multi-task learning: training on combined data can lead to
      model bias towards certain tasks, enhancing performance in some while degrading it in others.

      2.3   C HUNK - BASED ATTENTION M ASKING
202
203
      Although a balanced sampling scheme can mitigate dataset biases and promote domain-specific
204   learning in models, both packing and balanced sampling methods combine multiple training examples
205   into a single sequence without applying an attention mask to prevent tokens from attending across
206   packed example boundaries. This phenomenon, known as attention cross-contamination(Krell et al.,
207   2021; Zhao et al., 2024), can affect model performance. Our experiments, shown in Table 8, indicate
208   that attention cross-contamination lowers the model’s performance on complex reasoning tasks.
209   However, it can also enhance the model’s abilities in code and mathematical tasks.
210   Zhao et al. (2024) reveals that achieving optimal training results during pre-training requires making
211   the packed training examples as similar as possible and isolating them using attention masks. Our
212   proposed chunk-based attention masking approach shares similarities with this scheme, but we
213   construct chunks within each balanced sampled packed instruction based on the number of domains
214   (such as the four domains in this paper) and ensure that each chunk is sorted in the same order.
215   By applying chunk-based attention masking, we block interference between different chunks while
      ensuring high similarity between the data in each pair of chunks. Our experimental results, shown in

237   2.4   M IXTURE OF I NSTRUCTIONS
238
239   Ultimately, we propose the Mixture of Instructions(MoI) scheme, which comprises several key
240
      components. First, we set unique system prompts for each domain-specific task to ensure tailored
      guidance. Next, we employ balanced sampling techniques to concatenate task data from multiple
241
      domains into proportionally balanced datasets. During training, we apply a chunk-based attention
242
      mask to isolate unrelated contexts, thereby achieving optimal alignment. By explicitly modifying the
243   optimization objective of standard SFT, MoI achieves superior multi-task learning alignment.
244

      What is the impact of attention masking in the training of MoI? We utilized the method
      outlined in (Dao, 2023) to create a 2D Flash Attention mask, which was then input into the
472
      flash_attn_varlen_func function to prevent cross-contamination in attention. Table 8 shows
473
      that SFT with masking significantly improves reasoning ability on the T-Eval dataset. However, it
474   also leads to a considerable decline in performance on the GSM8K, MATH, and MBPP validation
475   sets. We hypothesize that this issue stems from attention contamination. The final MoI solution,
476   employing a chunk-based attention mask, effectively balances task metrics and mitigates attention
477   contamination.
478
      Why is MoI effective? We hypothesize that training with mixed instructions can significantly alter
479
      the attention distribution of an SFT model, thereby enhancing its alignment performance. To test
480   this, we conducted a case study by replacing the initial chat model’s weights with those from an
481   SFT-trained model, specifically targeting attention and other weights, and observed performance
482   changes on a specific dataset. As shown in Table 9, replacing the chat model’s attention weights
483   with MoI’s attention weights resulted in a performance decline, a phenomenon not observed in other
484   SFT models. This suggests that the MoI model’s attention mechanisms are unable to extract the
485   most relevant knowledge from the original chat MLP. Additionally, the experiments in A.11 also
      demonstrate the robustness of MoI’s attention to irregular system prompts.

488
      4.1   LLM S FOR V ERSATILE TASKS : M ATHEMATICS , C ODING , AND T OOL U TILIZATION .
489
490   Solving complex mathematical tasks is challenging for open-source LLMs. Research focuses on
491   fine-tuning open-source models (e.g., Touvron et al. (2023a;b); Bai et al. (2023)) with insights from
492   advanced proprietary LLMs (OpenAI, 2023; 2022). WizardMath (Luo et al., 2023a) employs a
493   reinforced evol-instruct method, combining supervised fine-tuning with PPO training to enhance
494   reasoning. Meanwhile, MAmmoTH (Yue et al., 2023) uses Chain of Thought and Program-of-
495
      Thought to enable LLMs to work with external tools, like Python interpreters, for mathematical
      problem-solving.
496
497   LLMs have been specifically designed for code generation tasks, such as StarCoder (Li et al., 2023a)
498   and DeepSeek-Coder (Guo et al., 2024). Alternatively, code-generating LLMs can be derived
499   from fine-tuning general-purpose models, exemplified by CodeLlama (Rozière et al., 2023) and
500   WizardCoder(Luo et al., 2023c). Our approach aligns with the latter, fine-tuning general-purpose
501
      LLMs to enhance code generation.
502   LLMs in real-world scenarios must adeptly select and apply tools from numerous APIs. Gorilla
503   (Patil et al., 2023) pairs LLMs with a broad API set, while ToolAlpaca (Tang et al., 2023) documents
504   3938 tool uses from over 400 real-world APIs in 50 categories. ToolLLM (Qin et al., 2023) further
505   contributes with ToolBench, a rich dataset featuring 16464 real-world APIs across 49 categories from
506   RapidAPI Hub, accommodating diverse single and multi-tool use cases.
507
508   4.2   M ULTI -TASK L EARNING
509
      During multi-task learning, dataset bias (Hadsell et al., 2020) is a phenomenon where two or
510
      more tasks pull the model parameters in different directions, thus impacting the multi-task learning
511
      performance. GPT-2(Radford et al., 2019) shows language models are unsupervised multitask learners
512   and Flan-T5(Chung et al., 2024) has demonstrated that language models can learn up to 1836 tasks.
513   Liu et al. (2024) proposes concatenating multiple datasets during training and using focal loss to
514   balance data bias among multiple tasks. Dong et al. (2023) proved multi-task learning of LLM lead
515   to conflicts, while sequential training results in catastrophic forgetting. In our work, we propose a
516   method to mitigate dataset bias that focuses on the data rather than the model.
517
518   4.3   S UPERVISED F INE - TUNING IN THE ALIGNMENT OF LLM
519
520   The training process of LLMs is categorized into pretraining and fine-tuning(Touvron et al., 2023b),
521   with Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT) as key components of the fine-tuning phase. Some researchers,
522
      such as (Wei et al., 2021), refer to SFT as instruction-tuning, while Ouyang et al. (2022) defines LLM
      alignment as ’helpfully and safely following user instructions.’ Additionally, studies like (Luo et al.,
523
      2023b; Zhang & Wu, 2024; Kotha et al., 2023; Razdaibiedina et al., 2023) have examined catastrophic
524
      forgetting during fine-tuning. Furthermore, Wang et al. (2024) explores how incorporating character
525   definitions in system prompts can assist language models in achieving multi-domain alignment
526   while Ge et al. (2024) propose a novel persona-driven data synthesis methodology for SFT. Lu et al.
527   (2024)reveals that the intrinsic capabilities of LLMs confine the knowledge within role-play. In our
528   work, we explored how to efficiently use multiple system prompts to better continue aligning a model
529   that has undergone SFT.
530
531
      5     C ONCLUSION
532
533
      In this work, we highlight the significant role of system prompts in enhancing language model
534   performance during SFT. By refining prompts, we mitigated issues in code generation with the
535   Qwen-7B-chat model, leading to better handling of complex tasks. Our innovative multi-task learning
536   strategy , Mixture of Instructions(MoI), which utilizes curated datasets with tailored prompts, success-
537   fully tackled the dataset bias seen in multi-task training. The MoI method further ensured consistent
538   performance across tasks. Our finalized Qwen-SFT-MoI model showcased substantial improvements
539   over the original, underscoring MoI’s potential for improving language model adaptability and
      proficiency.
