009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            Text-centric visual question answering (VQA) has made great strides with the devel-
012            opment of Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs), yet open-source models
013            still fall short of leading models like GPT4V and Gemini. A key contributing
014            factor to this disparity is the absence of extensive, high-quality instruction tuning
015            data. To this end, we introduce a new approach for creating a massive, high-quality
016            instruction-tuning dataset, Square-10M, generated by leveraging the versatile mul-
017            timodal capabilities of closed-source MLLMs. The data construction process,
018            termed Square, consists of four steps: Self-Questioning, Answering, Reasoning,
019
               and Evaluation. Our experiments with Square-10M led to three key findings: 1)
               Our model, TextSquare, considerably surpasses open-source previous state-of-the-
020
               art text-centric MLLMs and sets a new standard on OCRBench (62.2%). It even
021
               outperforms top-tier models like GPT4V and Gemini on six out of ten text-centric
022            benchmarks. 2) We demonstrate the importance of VQA reasoning data in offering
023            comprehensive contextual insights for specific questions, improving accuracy and
024            substantially mitigating hallucinations. Specifically, TextSquare scores an average
025            of 75.1% across four general VQA and hallucination evaluation datasets, outper-
026            forming previous state-of-the-art models. 3) Notably, the phenomenon observed in
027            scaling text-centric VQA datasets reveals a vivid pattern: an exponential increase
028            of instruction tuning data volume is directly proportional to the improvement in
029            model performance, thereby validating the necessity of the dataset scale and the
030
               high quality of Square-10M.

033   1   I NTRODUCTION
034
035   Recent research on multimodal large language models (MLLMs) (Ye et al., 2023a; Feng et al., 2023b;
036   Liu et al., 2024d; Feng et al., 2023a) has yielded significant advancements in text-centric visual
037   question-answering(VQA), with several closed-source state-of-the-art (SOTA) models (OpenAI,
038   2023; DeepMind, 2023) leading the way. Two representative examples are GPT4V (OpenAI, 2023)
039
      and Gemini (DeepMind, 2023), which have shown exceptional performance and even surpassed
      human capabilities in some aspects. Nevertheless, as illustrated in Figure 1, open-source models
040
      still significantly trail behind their closed-source counterparts. This gap can be attributed to various
041
      factors, including model architecture, the scale of model parameters, image resolution, the volume of
042   pretraining and instruction tuning data, and training strategies.
043
044   Recent studies (Chen et al., 2024; Nayak et al., 2024; Chen et al., 2023; Zhang et al., 2023) have
045
      delved into the challenges of insufficient instruction tuning data. For instance, Monkey (Li et al.,
      2023c) employed expert models to generate image descriptions, which GPT-4 then summarized to
046
      create high-quality, detailed image captions. LLaVAR (Zhang et al., 2023) and TG-Doc (Wang et al.,
047
      2023) used GPT-4 to generate conversations for text-rich images by integrating OCR results into
048   the instructions. ShareGPT4V (Chen et al., 2023) constructs a high-quality image caption dataset
049   through GPT4V to improve the image caption ability for MLLMs. While these efforts have achieved
050   remarkable success, they also left some challenges unresolved. Image caption data and VQA data
051   belong to different domains, with inconsistencies in the granularity and scope of image content
052   presentation. Furthermore, the scale of synthetic data remains relatively small, preventing MLLMs
053   from fully realizing their potential. The exploration of methods that leverage large-scale text-centric
      VQA data for instruction tuning remains limited.

080   To bridge the gap, this paper proposes a strategy termed Square to acquire extensive, high-quality
081
      text-centric VQA data from advanced closed-source MLLMs, constructing a dataset (Square-10M)
      comprising tens of millions of instances for instruction tuning. The Square strategy consists of
082
      four steps: Self-Questioning, Answering, Reasoning, and Evaluation. The self-questioning step
083
      involves utilizing the MLLM’s capabilities in text-image analysis and understanding to generate
084   textual-related questions. The answering step involves answering these questions, leveraging various
085   prompting techniques such as Chain-of-Thought and few-shot prompting. The reasoning step entails
086   probing the model for the reasoning behind its answers, leveraging the powerful reasoning abilities of
087   MLLMs. The evaluation step involves evaluating the question-answer pairs, assessing the validity of
088   the questions, the relevance to the textual content of images, and the correctness of answers, thereby
089   improving data quality and mitigating hallucinations. Overall, Square comprehensively leverages the
090   various capabilities of MLLMs, significantly enhancing data quality.
091   Besides, enriching the diversity of images is also crucial. We collect a diverse set of text-rich images
092   from various public sources, including natural scenes, charts, tables, receipts, books, slides, PDFs,
093   documents, products, and web images. Subsequently, deduplication is performed on this collection.
094   By applying the Square strategy to these images, Square-10M is constructed.
095
      Based on Square-10M, our model (TextSquare) achieves remarkable results. First, as shown in
096   Figure 1, TextSquare performs comparably or even better than advanced closed-source models and
097   substantially surpasses recent open-source models on various benchmarks. Notably, the image
098   resolution of TextSquare is 700, and the parameters are 8.6B. Second, our experiments validate
099   the beneficial impact of reasoning data on VQA tasks, demonstrating its ability to enhance model
100   performance while mitigating hallucinations. With reasoning data for instruction tuning, TextSquare
101   has a strong reasoning capability to provide elaborate explanations in VQA scenarios. Additionally,
102   our scaling experiments reveal the relationships between instruction tuning data scale, training
103   convergence loss, and model performance. Whereas a few instruction tuning data can effectively
104
      engage MLLMs, it is insufficient. Large amounts of high-quality data can further significantly reduce
      convergence loss and improve performance. The performance of TextSquare grows and the loss
105
      of convergence decreases while continuously scaling up the instruction tuning data, which also
106
      demonstrates the effectiveness of Square-10M.
107
      In summary, the main contributions of this paper can be categorized into four points:

      3.1     OVERVIEW OF THE S QUARE S TRATEGY
155
156   Figure 3 presents an overview of our proposed Square. Square generally consists of three stages for
157   synthesizing high-quality instruction tuning data for text-centric VQA: (1) Data Collection: we gather
158   a vast collection of text-rich images. (2) Data Generation: it involves self-questioning, answering, and
159   reasoning, utilizing the images procured. In this phase, the MLLM generates VQA pairs predicated
160   on the images, accompanied by the rationale behind the answers. (3) Data Filtering: we focus on
161   eliminating nonsensical questions and erroneous answers by leveraging the evaluation capabilities of
      MLLMs.

197   3.2      DATA C OLLECTION
198
199   The data collection process aims to cover a variety of text-rich scenarios in the real world. We
200
      collect 3.8 million unlabeled text-rich images (Figure 2), showcasing diverse properties. For instance,
      images categorized as Chart and Table focus on textual elements with intense statistical information;
201
      Slide, Screenshot, and WebImage are tailored for the interaction between text and prominent visual
202
      messages; Document, PDF, Receipt, and e-commerce images are characterized by fine, dense text;
203   Street-View images are derived from natural scenes. This comprehensive image collection provides a
204   representative cross-section of text-rich images in real-world scenarios, forming the foundation of
205   our research on text-centric VQA.

208   3.3      DATA G ENERATION : S ELF -Q UESTIONING , A NSWERING , AND R EASONING
209
210   To construct the Square-10M dataset, we harness the multi-modal understanding capabilities of
211   Gemini, one of the most advanced LLMs. For each selected image, Gemini generates VQA pairs and
212
      the reasoning context through three stages:
213   Stage 1: Self-Questioning. Gemini is prompted to formulate profound, meaningful questions about
214   each image. We prompt Gemini to first comprehensively analyze the image and generate questions
215   based on its interpretation, as shown in Figure 3. To bolster understanding of visual text, we also
      incorporate text extracted via OCR expert models into the prompts.

      Stage 2: Answering. Gemini is then directed to answer the generated questions. We leverage various
217   prompting techniques to enrich contextual information and improve the reliability of the responses,
218   such as Chain-of-Thought and few-shot prompting, exemplified in Figure 3.
219
220
      Stage 3: Reasoning. We require Gemini to elucidate the reasoning behind its answers. This process
      fosters a deeper connection between the questions and visual elements, mitigating hallucinations
221
      and ensuring accurate responses. The reasoning also provides additional context for individual
222
      questions, potentially aiding research into in-context learning mechanisms. An illustrative prompt for
223   self-reasoning is presented in Figure 3.

      3.4   DATA F ILTERING : S ELF -E VALUATION AND A NSWERING C ONSISTENCY
226
227   Despite the efficacy of self-questioning, answering, and reasoning, some generated content may be
228   hallucinatory or contain meaningless questions and erroneous answers. We establish filtering rules
229   based on the evaluative capabilities of LLMs to select high-quality VQA pairs. This comprehensive
230   filtering system encompasses three aspects:
231   Self-Evaluation of MLLMs. Advanced MLLMs, including Gemini, are utilized to assess the
232   meaningfulness of questions and the adequacy of answers. An example of self-evaluation prompting
233   is illustrated in Figure 3.
234
      Multi-Prompt Consistency. We augment the prompt and context space during Data Generation,
235
      ensuring that a valid VQA pair remains semantically consistent under diverse prompts. If answers
236
      vary significantly in meaning, the VQA pair is discarded, as shown in Figure 3.
237
238   Multi-Context Consistency. Similar to Multi-Prompt Consistency, VQA pairs are further verified
239   by appending varied contexts to the question. Given the generated question, three types of answers
240
      are produced by Gemini with different contexts: (1) Answering with reasoning. Gemini answers the
      question with a detailed explanation prepended (i.e., content generated in the stage of Reasoning).
241
      (2) In-Context answering. Gemini answers the question with chain-of-thought or few-shot prompts
242
      prepended. (3) Naive answering. Gemini answers the question with no extra context. VQA pairs will
243   be removed if the generated answers are not semantically consistent.

      3.5   MLLM S ELECTION
246
247   Since a comprehensive comparison of all available VLMs to find the optimal VLM is not possible
248   considering the scale of the dataset, we apply the Square strategy to different VLMs (including
249   Gemini-pro, GPT-4V, Qwen-VL-Plus, and Claude 3) and perform a manual comparison on the
250   sampled data. We collect 1,000 QA pairs from each VLM, and perform human evaluation on the
251   generated data. Specifically, for each VLM, the questionnaire consists of 1,000 cases, each of which
252   includes a ”Yes or No” question: Is the ”Question” meaningful to the image and can the ”Answer”
253
      correctly respond to the ”Question” ? Overall, we have collected 10 questionnaires, and the results are
      Gemini-pro (94.9%), GPT-4V(95.2%), Qwen-VL-Plus(92.8%),and Claude 3(92.1%). Considering
254
      the time cost, price and quality of the data generated, Gemini-pro is our best choice for a full-scale
255
      attempt at the Square strategy.

      3.6   DATA Q UALITY E VALUATION : H UMAN V ERIFICATION
258
259   Harmful information. In order to minimize the proportion of harmful information, we have set the
260   Gemini’s security level to the maximum. Besides, our dataset is about visual text and with quality
261   assessment and adequate filtering, there is little harmful content in the dataset. Considering the
262   large size of the dataset, we did not have enough resources to conduct a full human assessment. We
263   sampled the dataset (100,000 samples) and did not find harmful information.
264   Faulty information. Faulty information is almost unavoidable for very large generated datasets(e.g.,
265   ShareGPT4V[1], Monkey[2]), and there is no guarantee that even manually labelled data is completely
266   correct. To verify the effectiveness of the Square strategy in eliminating factual errors, we performed
267   a manual evaluation on 1,000 samples. Our proposed Square strategy improves the accuracy of the
268   generated data from 82.6% to 94.9%, significantly reducing the probability of faulty information.
269   What’s more, TextSquare greatly mitigates the model hallucinations, which is beneficial to the
      development of MLLMs.

      4.1   M ODEL A RCHITECTURE
274
275   TextSquare’s architecture follows the framework of InternLM-Xcomposer2 (Dong et al., 2024),
276   comprising three integral components: (1) A vision encoder modified from OpenAI CLIP ViT-L
277   (Radford et al., 2021), with an increased resolution of 700 to better capture fine-grained features. (2)
278   A LLM based on InternLM2 (Cai et al., 2024), utilizing InternLM2-7B-Chat as the practical variant.
279   (3) A projector that semantically aligns vision and text.
280
281   4.2   S UPERVISED F INE -T UNING WITH S QUARE -10M
282
      TextSquare is achieved by performing Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT) with Square-10M. The SFT
283
      process entails three stages: initially, all components are unfrozen and trained at a resolution of
284
      490. Subsequently, we increase the input resolution to 700 and focus on training the Vision Encoder
285   to adapt to the higher resolution. In the final stage, full-parameter fine-tuning is performed at a
286   resolution of 700. TextSquare demonstrates that with our Square-10M dataset, a model with 8B
287   parameters and normal-size image resolution can perform exceptionally on text-centric VQA tasks,
288   outperforming most available MLLMs and even closed-source SOTA models.

292   5.1   I MPLEMENTATION D ETAILS
293
294   The training data contains Square-10M and in-domain datasets (consistent with Monkey’s SFT data).
295   The training process is divided into three phases, using the same data and the AdamW (Loshchilov
296   & Hutter, 2017) optimizer with 64 A100-80G GPUs. In the first phase, we fine-tune InternLM-
297   Xcomposer2 with full parameters, and the learning rate decreases from 1e-5 to 1e-6, taking about
298   9520 GPU hours. In the second phase, we scale up the image resolution to 700 and train only VIT,
299
      with the learning rate decreasing from 1e-4 to 1e-5, taking about 7280 GPU hours. In the third stage,
      we perform full-parameter fine-tuning at 700 image resolution, and the learning rate drops from 1e-5
300
      to 1e-6, spending about 12350 GPU hours.

      5.5   R ELATIONSHIPS BETWEEN I NSTRUCTION T UNING DATA S CALE , C ONVERGENCE L OSS ,
487         AND M ODEL P ERFORMANCE
488
489   To explore the relationship between instruction tuning data scale, convergence loss, and model
490   performance, we conduct a series of 10 experimental sets with different volumes. These experiments
491   utilize Square-10M and specialized in-domain instruction tuning datasets. The average performance
492   of the models is evaluated on DocVQA, ChartQA, InfoVQA, WTQ, and SROIE. As shown in
493   Figure 5(a)(b), we observe a consistent decline in convergence loss with increasing data scale, albeit
494
      at a decelerating rate. The relationship between the convergence loss and the instruction tuning
      data scale approximately conforms to a logarithmic function. Similarly, Figure 5(c)(d) illustrates
495
      that model performance is enhanced with the expansion of instruction tuning data, yet the rate of
496
      improvement diminishes. This relationship also aligns with a logarithmic function. Holistically, there
497   is a corresponding scaling law in the instruction tuning phase in text-centric VQA scenarios: model
498   performance is directly proportional to the logarithm of the data scale. This insight is instrumental in
499   guiding the development of larger datasets and in forecasting model performance.

      6     L IMITATION
503
      While yielding notable outcomes across diverse scenarios, our approach encounters certain limitations.
504
      Primarily, the processing of large-scale datasets necessitates an extensive array of GPUs for prolonged
505   training periods. This requirement significantly escalates the overall training costs. Furthermore,
506   despite the advancements introduced by the Square strategy in enhancing the quality of synthetic data,
507   it falls short of achieving the nuanced accuracy and complexity characteristic of human-generated
508   data.

      7     C ONCLUSION
511
512
      This paper presents the Square strategy for constructing a high-quality text-centric instruction tuning
513
      dataset(Square-10M). Leveraging this dataset, TextSquare significantly surpasses recent open-source
514   models and achieves performance comparable to GPT4V across various benchmarks. Furthermore,
515   we derive the relationship between the scale of instruction tuning datasets, convergence loss, and
516   model performance, offering insights into developing even larger datasets. Our data-centric approach
517   reevaluates the significance of instruction-tuning data in text-centric VQA, underscoring that both
518   the quantity and quality of data are pivotal for optimal model performance. We maintain a steadfast
519   belief in the potential for advancing the quantity and quality of data as a means to bridge the divide
520   between open-source models and their industry-leading counterparts.

      A.2.1     M ULTI - MODAL L ARGE L ANGUAGE M ODELS
779   Recent work has increasingly focused on introducing visual knowledge into LLMs (Zhu et al.,
780   2023; Bai et al., 2023; Dai et al., 2024). General attempts connect a visual encoder and an LLM
781   with intermediate modules like Projector (Liu et al., 2024c), Q-Former (Li et al., 2023a), Perceiver
782   Resampler (Alayrac et al., 2022), etc, and go through pre-training alignment and instruction fine-
783   tuning for vision-language understanding.
784   Several researches (Ye et al., 2023a; Feng et al., 2023b;a; Yu et al., 2023; Wei et al., 2023; Wan et al.,
785   2024; Luo et al., 2024; Liu et al., 2024a) propose to enhance MLLMs’ capabilities in understanding
786   textual elements (OCR, text-centric VQA, etc). Among them, mPLUG-DocOwl (Ye et al., 2023a)
787   creates novel instruction tuning datasets to enhance the tuning process. TextMonkey (Liu et al., 2024d)
788   adopts shifted window attention and filters out significant tokens. DocPedia (Feng et al., 2023a), and
789   HRVDA (Liu et al., 2024a) enlarges input resolution to bridge the gap between MLLMs and visual
790   document understanding. Despite the extraordinary progress of existing open-source MLLMs, they
791   still suffer from the huge gap against SOTA closed-source models like GPT4V (OpenAI, 2023) and
792
      Gemini Pro (DeepMind, 2023).
793
794
      A.2.2     T EXT-C ENTRIC V ISUAL Q UESTION A NSWERING
795   Text-centric Visual Question Answering aims to understand the interactions between the image’s
796   textual and visual elements. Donut (Kim et al., 2022) first proposes an end-to-end training method
797   based on a Transformer without OCR. Pix2Struct (Lee et al., 2023) introduces a variable-resolution
798   input representation to adapt to document images. DoCo (Li et al., 2024) enhances the visual
799   representation of the image encoder in MLLMs by aligning the document object of multi-modal
800   inputs. BLIVA (Hu et al., 2024b) enlarges the input token space by concatenating learned query
801   embeddings and encoded patch embeddings. Several studies (Feng et al., 2023b; Wang et al., 2023;
802
      Zhang et al., 2023) have performed data-centric attempts in this regard. UniDoc (Feng et al., 2023b)
      construct 600k document-oriented image-text pairs from PowerPoint presentations. LLaVAR (Zhang
803
      et al., 2023) and TG-Doc (Wang et al., 2023) prompt text-only GPT-4 to generate conversations for
804
      text-rich images by integrating OCR results into the instructions. These researches are restricted to
805   small-scale annotations or generation based on uni-modal inputs.
806
807   A.2.3     G ENERATING I NSTRUCTION -T UNING DATA VIA LLM S
808
809   The success of LLMs has inspired recent work to employ them as training data generators (Chen
      et al., 2023; 2024; Wang et al., 2022; Shao et al., 2023). In this regard, we anchor on generating

810
      instruction-tuning data. Self-Instruct (Wang et al., 2022) took the initial step towards synthesizing
811   instructions via language models and improving the instruction-following capabilities. Llama-GPT4
812   (Peng et al., 2023) uses GPT-4 to generate instruction-tuning data for LLM fine-tuning. Synthetic
813   Prompting (Shao et al., 2023) leverages a few handcrafted examples to prompt LLMs to generate
814   more examples. Bonito (Nayak et al., 2024) converts unannotated text into task-specific training
815   datasets for instruction tuning. Recently, ALLAVA (Chen et al., 2024) employs GPT4V to generate
816   reasoning instructions and detailed answers from unlabeled images. All of the above attempts suffer
817   from the low quality of the generated data and are typically performed on a small scale. In contrast,
818   we collect millions of text-rich images and devise comprehensive generating methods and filtering
819
      rules to ensure the quality of the instruction tuning dataset.
