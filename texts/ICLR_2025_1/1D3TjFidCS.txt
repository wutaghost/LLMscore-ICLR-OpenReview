011                                              A BSTRACT
012
013            The Logarithmic Linear Unit (LogLU) presents a novel activation function for
014            deep neural networks by incorporating logarithmic elements into its design, intro-
015            ducing non-linearity that significantly enhances both training efficiency and ac-
016
               curacy. LogLU effectively addresses common limitations associated with widely
               used activation functions include ReLU, Leaky ReLU, and ELU, which suffer
017
               from issues like the dead neuron problem and vanishing gradients. By enabling
018
               neurons to remain active with negative inputs and ensuring effective gradient flow
019            during backpropagation, LogLU promotes more efficient convergence in gradient
020            descent. Its capability to solve fundamental yet complex non-linear tasks, such
021            as the XOR problem, with fewer neurons demonstrates its efficiency in captur-
022            ing non-linear patterns. Extensive evaluations on benchmark datasets like Cal-
023            tech 101 and Imagenette, using the InceptionV3 architecture, reveal that LogLU
024            not only accelerates convergence but also enhances model performance compared
025            to existing activation functions. These findings underscore LogLU’s potential as
026            an effective activation function that improves both model performance and faster
027
               convergence.

      1   I NTRODUCTION
031
032   Deep learning has become highly popular in recent years for its ability to recognize complex patterns
033
      within data. LeCun et al. (2015). At the core of deep learning models are layers of neurons, A
      neural network processes input data by passing it through layers of weighted connections, where
034
      activation functions are applied to determine the output at each node. The choice of activation
035
      function is critical, as it influences how well a neural network learns, handles non-linearities, and
036   performs in various tasks Goodfellow et al. (2016). An effective activation function enables the
037   model to approximate complex relationships, This ability is a key reason for deep learning’s better
038   performance.
039
      While popular activation function like Rectified Linear Unit (ReLU) Nair & Hinton (2010), Leaky
040
      ReLU Xu et al. (2015), and Exponential Linear Unit (ELU) Clevert et al. (2015) have been widely
041
      used, they each present limitations. For instance, ReLU faces the dead neuron problem, where neu-
042   rons stop learning if they constantly receive negative inputs. Although Leaky ReLU addresses this
043   problem by permitting small negative values, it introduces the vanishing gradient problem, limiting
044   its effectiveness in deep networks Maas (2013). ELU, on the other hand, provides a smooth output
045   for negative inputs but increases computational complexity due to its exponential calculation.
046
      In this paper, we introduce a new activation function i.e., Logarithmic Linear Unit (LogLU), which
047
      addresses the limitations of existing activation functions. LogLU is designed to solve both the dead
048   neuron and vanishing gradient problems while maintaining computational simplicity. It enables neu-
049   rons to remain active even with negative inputs, preventing dead neurons and ensuring that gradients
050   stay sufficiently large during backpropagation. This enhances the training of deep neural networks,
051   resulting in quicker convergence and improved overall performance. One of the most notable fea-
052   tures of LogLU is its ability to solve classic XOR function approximation problems using a single
053   neuron McCulloch & Pitts (1943). This highlights its efficiency in capturing non-linear relationships
      with fewer resources compared to other activation functions. LogLU’s unique properties allow deep

      6   C ONCLUSION
421
422   This research study focuses on evaluating the impact of various nonlinear activation functions on
423
      the performance of output neurons in deep learning models. We specifically examine the perfor-
      mance of well-established activation functions, including ReLU, Leaky ReLU, and ELU, each of
424
      which presents certain limitations. To overcome these challenges, we introduce a novel activation
425
      function, the Logarithmic Linear Unit (LogLU), designed to enhance the efficiency of model train-
426   ing. The ability of LogLU to keep neurons active with negative inputs and maintain robust gradient
427   flow during backpropagation enables more efficient convergence in gradient descent, particularly in
428   solving complex non-linear tasks. Through extensive evaluations on benchmark datasets, including
429   Caltech 101 and Imagenette, which are relevant for large-scale applications, we demonstrate that
430   LogLU accelerates convergence and improves model performance when integrated into the Incep-
431   tionV3 architecture. The empirical results show that LogLU consistently outperforms traditional
      activation functions in terms of convergence speed, stability, accuracy, and loss reduction.
