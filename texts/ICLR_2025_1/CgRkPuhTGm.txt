009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            Time series forecasting is critical across various domains, including energy, trans-
012            portation, weather prediction, and healthcare. Although recent advances using
013            CNNs, RNNs, and Transformer-based models have shown promise, these ap-
014            proaches often suffer from architectural complexity and low computational ef-
015            ficiency. MLP-based networks offer better computational efficiency, and some
016
               frequency-domain MLP models have demonstrated the ability to handle periodic
               time series data. However, standard MLP-based methods still struggle to directly
017
               model periodic and temporal dependencies in the time domain, which are essential
018
               for accurate time series forecasting. To address these challenges, we propose the
019            Skip and Split MLP Network (SSNet), featuring innovative Skip-MLP and Split-
020            MLP components that enable MLP models to directly capture periodicity and tem-
021            poral dependencies in the time domain. SSNet requires fewer parameters than
022            traditional MLP-based architectures, improving computational efficiency. Empir-
023            ical results on multiple real-world long-term forecasting datasets demonstrate that
024            SSNet significantly outperforms state-of-the-art models, delivering better perfor-
025            mance with fewer parameters. Notably, even a single Skip-MLP unit matches the
026            performance of high-performing models like PatchTST.

      1   I NTRODUCTION
029
030
      Time series forecasting has been extensively applied in various practical scenarios, including eco-
031   nomics (Clements et al., 2004; Ghysels & Marcellino, 2018), energy (Deb et al., 2017; Koprinska
032   et al., 2018; Hu et al., 2022), transportation (Cirstea et al., 2022; Cai et al., 2020), weather (Hewage
033   et al., 2020; Taylor et al., 2009), and retail (Fildes et al., 2022). In particular, Long-Term Time Se-
034   ries forecasting presents significant real-world demands, where accurate modeling of periodic and
035   seasonal patterns is crucial for improving performance. PatchTST (Nie et al., 2022) partitions time
036   series into multiple small patches to enhance the information of local sequence features and reduce
037   the computational cost associated with excessively long sequences. TimesNet (Wu et al., 2022) uses
038   the Fast Fourier Transform to perform multi-scale decomposition of time series data and processes
039
      the 2D feature maps with CNN networks to capture rich periodic information. TimeMixer (Wang
      et al., 2024) leverages down-sampling techniques to hierarchically process sequences and aggregates
040
      seasonal and trend information through Seasonal Mixing and Trend Mixing. These methods utilize
041
      different approaches to capture periodic information and model both local and global information.
042   DLinear (Zeng et al., 2023), based on trend and seasonality decomposition, employs a single linear
043   layer to demonstrate the MLP-based model’s excellent capability in time series forecasting. How-
044   ever, due to its symmetric point-mapping structure, MLPs struggle to model periodic and seasonal
045   patterns directly and are limited in distinguishing the temporal dependencies within sequences.
046
      To address these limitations, we propose two novel network architectures, Skip-MLP and Split-
047
      MLP, which extend traditional MLPs. These architectures consist of fundamental Skip Connected
048   Layers and Split Connected Layers, as illustrated in Figure 1. Skip-MLP skips over sequences
049   of a specific length (referred to as skip-size, generally related to the sequence’s periodicity) to cap-
050   ture periodic information. The Split-MLP layer segments sequences into sub-sequences of specific
051   lengths, establishing separate connections between these sub-sequences to focus on extracting local
052   features. When used in conjunction with Skip-MLP layers, it can further capture global sequence
053   information. Through matrix derivation, we demonstrate that the sparse Skip Connection Layer and
      Split Connected Layer can be represented as combinations of multiple small, dense Fully Connected

055   hances prediction performance and computational efficiency.

057   To leverage the strengths of both Skip-MLP and Split-MLP layers, we designed the SSNet net-

      extracts significant periodic patterns and their intensities, (2) a parallel multi-scale Skip-MLPs net-

060

061   consistently achieved state-of-the-art performance across various real-world forecasting scenarios
062   while maintaining linear time complexity. Our contributions are summarized as follows:
063
064          • We introduce two novel MLP networks, Skip-MLP and Split-MLP, which address the lim-
065            itations of traditional MLPs in capturing periodic information and can handle global and
066            local data information more effectively with fewer parameters.
067          • We propose the SSNet model architecture, which adaptively captures and integrates critical
068            periodic patterns, effectively differentiating between local and global information, making
069            it well-suited for long-term multivariate time series forecasting.
070          • Extensive experimental results demonstrate that SSNet achieves state-of-the-art perfor-
071            mance in long-term time series forecasting tasks, with fewer parameters and higher com-
072            putational efficiency.

074   2   R ELATED W ORK
075
076   Research on MLPs Multi-Layer Perceptrons (MLPs) are fundamental structures in deep learn-
077   ing and have been extensively studied. The MLP-Mixer(Tolstikhin et al., 2021) achieves compet-
078   itive performance in image classification by employing Token-mixing MLPs and Channel-mixing
079   MLPs to extract features from positional tokens and channels, respectively, thereby demonstrat-
080   ing the efficient visual representation capabilities of MLPs. The gMLP(Liu et al., 2021) intro-
081   duces gating mechanisms to simulate the self-attention mechanisms found in Transformers, achiev-
082
      ing performance that is competitive with or even exceeds that of Transformers in both language
      and vision tasks. ResMLP(Touvron et al., 2022) simplifies networks based on Vision Transform-
083
      ers (ViT)(Dosovitskiy, 2020) by incorporating MLPs and residual connections, leading to high-
084
      performance image classification. sMLPNet(Tang et al., 2022) utilizes 1D MLPs applied axially,
085   with parameter sharing between rows or columns, which reduces the number of model parame-
086   ters and computational complexity through sparse connections and weight sharing. Recently, the
087   KAN(Liu et al., 2024b) model has sparked considerable discussion due to its superior interpretabil-
088   ity and improved performance in certain prediction scenarios, positioning it as a strong contender
089   among MLP variants. We propose enhancements to the traditional MLP architecture through Skip
090   Connected Layers and Split Connected Layers, which enhance its ability to handle sequential data
091   and improve computational efficiency.
092   Time Series Forecasting Time series prediction has been extensively studied, with traditional
093   methods such as Exponential Smoothing(Gardner Jr, 1985), Holt-Winters(Chatfield, 1978) and
094   ARIMA(Asteriou & Hall, 2011) providing robust theoretical foundations and good interpretability.
095   However, these methods are limited by their assumptions and struggle with the complexities of real-
096   world time series data. Recent research has increasingly focused on deep learning-based approaches
097   for time series forecasting. RNN-based models (LSTNet(Lai et al., 2018), DeepAR(Salinas et al.,
098   2020)) capture sequential dependencies but encounter challenges such as gradient vanishing and
099
      forgetting in long sequences. CNN-based models (MICN(Wang et al., 2023), SCINet(Liu et al.,
      2022a), TimesNet(Wu et al., 2022)) leverage CNN characteristics to capture 2D features but are of-
100
      ten limited by receptive field sizes, impacting global feature extraction. Transformer-based models
101
      (PatchTST(Nie et al., 2022), FEDFormer(Zhou et al., 2022b), Stationary(Liu et al., 2022b)) benefit
102   from self-attention mechanisms to extract rich temporal features and show strong prediction perfor-
103   mance, though they face high computational complexity with longer sequences. MLP-based models
104   (LTST-Linear(Zeng et al., 2023), TimeMixer(Wang et al., 2024), NBEATS(Iori et al., 2024)) are
105   generally simpler and more computationally efficient but struggle with directly modeling periodic
106   and local features. We propose the SSNet architecture for time series data modeling, which adap-
107   tively performs multi-scale modeling, effectively integrates both local and global information, and
      achieves high computational efficiency and predictive performance.

      Problem Definition Multivariate time series prediction involves forecasting future values for a
111   window of size H, given historical data from a look-back window of size L. A time series with
112   T time steps and N variables is represented as [X1 , X2 , . . . , XT ] ∈ RN ×T , where Xt ∈ RN
113   denotes the N -dimensional values at time step t. Given the current time step t, the input sequences
114   fed into the model is Xt = [Xt−L+1 , Xt−L+2 , . . . , Xt ] ∈ RN ×L , and the output will be Yt =
115   [Xt+1 , Xt+2 , . . . , Xt+H ] ∈ RN ×H .

297   SS-MLPs SS-MLPs are composed of 2K − 1 SS-MLP Blocks, with residual connections applied
298   between consecutive layers. The output of the n-th layer is the sum of the SS-MLP output at that

302   odic information, we adopt a design where the skip sizes are narrower at both ends and wider in the

        5                            C ONCLUSIONS
477
       In this paper, we introduce two innovative architectures, Skip-MLP and Split-MLP, which effectively
478
       overcome the limitations of conventional MLPs in capturing periodic information and distinguish-
479
       ing between global and local features. These architectures are distinguished by a reduced param-
480    eter count and enhanced computational efficiency. Building on these foundations, we developed
481    the SSNet temporal network, which adaptively identifies significant periodicities through an auto-
482    correlation method, extracts multi-scale periodic features via Skip-MLPs, integrates this multi-scale
483    information, and systematically reduces prediction bias using multiple SS-MLP Blocks with resid-
484    ual connections. SSNet demonstrates significant improvements over existing methods in various
485    long-term time series forecasting tasks, achieving superior prediction performance while utilizing
       fewer parameters and minimizing runtime.

794
795
               • ETT (Electricity Transformer Temperature): The ETT dataset is collected from a power
796              grid, recording electricity transformer temperatures alongside other features such as load,
797              oil temperature, and ambient conditions. It is divided into four sub-datasets: ETTh1,
798              ETTh2, ETTm1, and ETTm2. The ETTh datasets represent hourly data, while the ETTm
799              datasets record data every 15 minutes. Each sub-dataset spans two years and contains
800              7 features, including power load, transformer oil temperature, and ambient temperature.
801              This dataset is particularly suited for both short-term and long-term time-series forecasting
802              tasks.
803            • Weather: The Weather dataset contains meteorological data recorded every 10 minutes
804              throughout the year 2020. It includes 21 meteorological indicators, such as air temperature,
805              humidity, wind speed, and pressure. The dataset provides a high-resolution view of weather
806              patterns and is commonly used in environmental forecasting and anomaly detection tasks.
807            • Electricity: The Electricity dataset contains hourly electricity consumption data from 321
808              clients between 2012 and 2014. This dataset is challenging due to its high variability and
809              non-stationary characteristics, making it a popular choice for evaluating the robustness of
                 forecasting models.
