009                                              A BSTRACT
010
011            3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) has emerged as a pivotal technique for 3D scene
012            representation, providing rapid rendering speeds and high fidelity. As 3DGS gains
013            prominence, safeguarding its intellectual property becomes increasingly crucial
014            since 3DGS could be used to imitate unauthorized scene creations and raise copy-
015            right issues. Existing watermarking methods for implicit NeRFs cannot be di-
016
               rectly applied to 3DGS due to its explicit representation and real-time rendering
               process, leaving watermarking for 3DGS largely unexplored. In response, we pro-
017
               pose WATER-GS, a novel method designed to protect 3DGS copyrights through
018
               a plug-and-play strategy. First, we introduce a pre-trained watermark decoder,
019            treating raw 3DGS generative modules as potential watermark encoders to en-
020            sure imperceptibility. Additionally, we implement novel 3D distortion layers to
021            enhance the robustness of the embedded watermark against common real-world
022            distortions of point cloud data. Comprehensive experiments and ablation studies
023            demonstrate that WATER-GS effectively embeds imperceptible and robust water-
024            marks into 3DGS without compromising rendering efficiency and quality. Our
025            experiments indicate that the 3D distortion layers can yield up to a 20% improve-
026            ment in accuracy rate. Notably, our method is adaptable to different 3DGS vari-
027
               ants, including 3DGS compression frameworks and 2D Gaussian splatting.

      Considering the unique characteristics of 3D generative scenes (3DGS), robust watermarking tech-
061   niques must possess the following properties. 1): Flexibility: The watermark should be embedded
062   into the 3DGS parameters and extracted from the 2D rendered images. In industries where 3D
063   technology is widely applied (e.g., gaming, filmmaking, and graphic design), users primarily ac-
064   cess content through 2D renderings rather than 3D models. Therefore, each rendered image must
065   carry the same copyright information. 2) Fidelity: The watermark should be implicitly embedded
066   within the pre-trained 3DGS file without compromising the quality of the renderings. As 3DGS
067   is a real-time renderable representation, the entire file is uploaded online for others to download.
068   Moreover, 3DGS files or point clouds from different frameworks may have various formats, neces-
069
      sitating that the watermarking method be compatible with diverse 3DGS pipelines without altering
      the original file format and attributes. 3) Robustness: The watermarking method should maintain
070
      robustness, enabling the extraction of watermark information even from compressed or partially dis-
071
      torted 3D files. Previous NeRF watermark studies, such as (Luo et al., 2023b), have focused solely
072   on distortions in rendered images, neglecting distortions within the NeRF network itself. We argue
073   that distortions in the original 3D model parameters are even more critical, as watermark bits are
074   embedded directly within them.
075
      While imperceptibility is crucial for watermarking methods, the heightened demand for robustness
076
      distinguishes watermarking from steganography (Zhu et al., 2018). Therefore, in addition to invisi-
077
      bility, we prioritize enhancing the robustness of 3DGS watermarks. Instead of embedding messages
078   in specific parameters, we propose a watermarking method that fine-tunes the entire model using a
079   pre-trained decoder, as shown in Fig. 1. By integrating watermark messages into various parameters,
080   the embedded messages remain consistent across different viewpoints rendered from 3DGS models.
081   Additionally, we introduce 3D distortion layers during the fine-tuning stage to bolster watermark
082   robustness. Previous works primarily addressed distortions in 2D renderings, neglecting the impact
083   on point cloud files or 3DGS models. Our 3D distortion layers are designed to ensure effective wa-
084   termark extraction even under severe 3D data distortions. For instance, when fine-tuned with these
085   layers, WATER-GS improves extraction accuracy from 74.38% to 95.14% under Gaussian noise dis-
086
      tortion applied to 3D points. In summary, the watermark extraction accuracy can reach 95% across
      various distortions.

104   2.1   3D G AUSSIAN S PLATTING
105
106   In recent advancements, 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) (Kerbl et al., 2023) has gained tremendous
107   traction as a promising paradigm to 3D view synthesis, reconstructing and representing 3D scenes
      using millions of 3D Gaussians endowed with learnable shape and appearance attributes. Compared


                                                         2
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


108
      to implicit representations like NeRF (Mildenhall et al., 2021), 3DGS offers remarkable accelera-
109   tion in training and rendering while utilizing fewer resources and maintain high-fidelity quality. By
110   leveraging explicit 3D Gaussian representations and differentiable tile-based rasterization (Lassner
111   & Zollhöfer, 2020), 3DGS optimizes 3D Gaussians during training to accurately fit their local re-
112   gions. This not only enhances flexibility and editability, but also enables high-fidelity, real-time
113   rendering for 3D scene reconstruction.
114
      In response to 3DGS’s contributions to 3D representation, recent advancements and derivative works
115   have emerged. Mip-Splatting (Yu et al., 2024) introduces a 2D Mip filter and a 3D smoothing filter
116   to address aliasing and dilation issues, effectively eliminating high-frequency artifacts. DreamGaus-
117   sian (Tang et al., 2023) presents a generative framework that incorporates UV space, significantly
118   improving content creation efficiency. Additionally, Scaffold-GS (Lu et al., 2024) and HAC (Chen
119   et al., 2024a) utilize anchor points to distribute local 3D Gaussians, enhancing scene coverage while
120   reducing redundancy. The applications of 3DGS technology extend beyond its initial domain, find-
121   ing success in fields like autonomous driving (Tang et al., 2023; Zhou et al., 2024), simultaneous
122   localization and mapping (SLAM) (Yan et al., 2024; Keetha et al., 2024), multi-modal genera-
123
      tion (Ling et al., 2024; Chen et al., 2024b), and 2D scenarios (Zhang et al., 2024). Despite its
      widespread adoption, research on watermarking and copyright protection in 3DGS remains limited.

      2.2   3D D IGITAL WATERMARK
127   The limited popularity of 3D data has hindered the development of watermarking technologies com-
128   pared to traditional media such as images, videos, and audio. Early approaches to 3D digital wa-
129   termarking utilized methods like principal component analysis (PCA) (Jolliffe & Cadima, 2016),
130   geometric techniques like analyzing vertex curvatures (Lipuš & Žalik, 2019; Praun et al., 1999;
131   Liu et al., 2019), and frequency-domain transform approaches (Ohbuchi et al., 2002; Hamidi et al.,
132   2019). Following the success of HiDDeN (Zhu et al., 2018), a deep image watermarking method
133   that outperformed traditional techniques, various extensions have emerged. For instance, 3D-to-2D
134   Watermarking(Yoo et al., 2022) adopts similar principles to address mesh watermarking problem.
135   Recently, NeRF-based watermarking method (Li et al., 2023; Luo et al., 2023b; Song et al., 2024;
136   Huang et al., 2024b) have attracted increasing attention. For example, StegaNeRF (Li et al., 2023)
137   embeds watermark information into the parameters of pre-trained NeRFs. CopyRNeRF (Luo et al.,
138   2023b) encodes watermark messages into implicit tensors and concatenates them with color repre-
139   sentations. NeRFProtectorr (Song et al., 2024) embeds binary messages directly during the creation
140   of NeRFs. However, watermarking for emerging 3DGS has yet to be explored. Our work pioneers
141   robust watermarking for 3DGS by utilizing the 3DGS generative module as an watermark encoder,
142
      allowing creators to embed watermark information both effectively and imperceptibly.

      Problem Setup. NeRF-based watermark methods (Luo et al., 2023b; Song et al., 2024) typically
184   embed messages within model weights, allowing extraction from renderings at various viewpoints.
185   In contrast, 3DGS utilizes an explicit 3D representation, where each point’s attributes carry clear
186   physical meanings. Furthermore, the entire point cloud files of trained 3DGS are uploaded online,
187   meaning that the watermark embedding process must not alter the structure of the 3DGS file or the
188   format of existing attributes. Therefore, we assume that a complete (or partially distorted, as long as
189   it doesn’t affect the rendered images) 3DGS file is accessible during the watermark extraction stage,
190   enabling extraction from each viewpoint’s rendering. Creators can either download a pre-trained de-
191   coder from the cloud or train one themselves. In addition to the proposed WATER-GS, we explore
192
      other intuitive solutions as our baselines illustrated in Fig. 2.
      Watermarking Training Datasets. Inspired by previous work on generative model watermark-
193
      ing (Yu et al., 2021; Zhao et al., 2023), we propose embedding binary strings within training images
194
      using a watermark encoder before training the 3DGS. Furthermore, we aim to explore whether the
195   resulting renderings retain the same quality when the training images undergo 2D watermarking.
196   Concatenating Watermarks with Attributes. Traditional deep watermarking methods design en-
197   coders to extract deep features from watermark messages and cover images. These features are then
198   concatenated with the raw cover image to generate a watermarked image. CopyRNeRF (Luo et al.,
199   2023b) propose transforming secret watermark messages into higher dimensions and fusing them
200   with the spatial information and color representations of NeRFs. Furthermore, we aim to explore
201   whether an encoder can align watermark messages with specific attributes of 3DGS, such as the
202   spherical harmonic coefficients h, and extract these messages from 2D renderings.
203   As described in Section 3, both solutions mentioned above incorporate an additional encoder to em-
204   bed watermarks. However, experiments in Section 4.3 demonstrate that neither approach success-
205   fully extracts watermarks from 2D renderings. Rather than explicitly introducing a new encoder,
206   we propose that the original 3DGS generative network can serve as a potential encoder. Figure 3
207   illustrates the overall processing pipeline of the proposed WATER-GS method. Our approach aims
208   to embed l-bit binary messages m ∈ {0, 1}l within the 3DGS file prior to publication. In the fol-
209
      lowing sections, we first outline the process for obtaining a universal decoder in Section 3.1. We
      then describe the method for embedding watermarks within 3DGS and discuss the roles of the 3D
210
      distortion layers in Section 3.2.

      4.1   E XPERIMENTAL SETUP
352
353   Datasets. We conduct experiments on real-world datasets: LLFF (Mildenhall et al., 2019), Mip-
354   NeRF360 (Barron et al., 2021) and Tanks&Temples (Knapitsch et al., 2017). We choose the train
355   and truck scenes from the Tanks&Temples dataset, similar to the (Kerbl et al., 2023). We present
356   average values across all testing viewpoints in our experiments.
357
      Evaluation Metrics. We evaluate the performance of our watermarking method from two perspec-
358   tives: robustness and imperceptibility. For robustness, we utilize the Bit Error Rate (BER) metric to
359   assess the extraction accuracy of the watermark message. To evaluate imperceptibility, we employ
360   metrics such as PSNR, MS-SSIM and VGG-LPIPS (Simonyan & Zisserman, 2014; Zhang et al.,
361   2018), which measure the distortion between rendered images from the raw and watermarked 3DGS
362   model.
363
      Implementation Details. Our method fine-tunes the model for 10K to 30K iterations to embed
364   the watermark into pre-trained scene. The entire fine-tuning process takes approximately half to
365   multiple hours according to the size of scenes. The watermark decoder utilizes the HiDDeN (Zhu
366   et al., 2018) structure, trained on the COCO (Lin et al., 2014) dataset. Experiments are conducted
367   using NVIDIA GeForce RTX 3090 GPU and PyTorch.
368
      Baselines. We compare our WATER-GS with three baselines for a fair assessment: 1) “HiDDeN
369
      + 3DGS”, which involves training the 3DGS model with watermarked images processed by HiD-
370   DeN (Zhu et al., 2018); 2) “MBRS + 3DGS”, which uses watermarked images processed by the
371   SOTA image watermark method MBRS (Jia et al., 2021); 3) “CONCAT + 3DGS”, which concate-
372   nates the watermark message with spherical harmonic (SH) coefficients, ensuring minimal impact
373   on image rendering.

      5   C ONCLUSION
530
531
      In this paper, we present a novel plug-and-play approach for safeguarding the copyright of 3D
      Gaussian Splatting (3DGS). Our method leverages a universal decoder, enabling creators to em-
532
      bed unique signatures as watermarks into variants 3DGS models, which can be accurately extracted
533
      from rendered images captured from any viewpoints. To enhance the robustness of the embedded
534   watermarks, we incorporate 3D distortion layers into the fine-tuning process, effectively mitigating
535   potential distortions encountered during rendering. To the best of our knowledge, WATER-GS is the
536   first method to address robust watermarking for 3DGS while pioneering the application of distor-
537   tion layers in 3DGS point cloud data. Experimental results validate the superiority of our approach
538   and demonstrate its seamless integration into diverse 3DGS variants. Future work will focus on
539   further enhancing the visual quality of watermarked 3DGS models and improving robustness under
      complex real-world conditions.

      A.1   H YPER - PARAMETERS
705
706   Decoder network architecture. We use the network architecture proposed by HiDDeN (Zhu et al.,
707   2018). The encoder E consists of four stacked layers of convolution followed by ReLU activation
708   functions and the decoder D consists of seven stacked layers of convolution followed by ReLU
709   activation functions. The middle channels are set to 64. During training stage, the images are
710   cropped to 256 × 256 and the model is trained for 300 epochs.
711   Distortion layer strength factors. For Gaussian noise, we add noise to position parameters xyz,
712   the kernel width σ is set to 0.01. For Dropout and Crop noise, we drop a percentage p = 10% of
713   points from original 3DGS parameters Θ. Creators can fine-tune this factors to change the strength
714   of distortion. Fine-tuning the model with stronger distortion degrees can enhance robustness of
715   watermark while sacrifice the image quality.
716   Loss weights. The total loss Ltot is consist of Lrgb and Lm , balanced by γ. We prefer to remain
717   hyper-parameters of raw frameworks unchanged and control the γ to fit different scenes and datasets.
718   In summary, we use a smaller γ for the fewer points 3DGS. In this paper, we set γ = 0.1 for
719   LLFF (Mildenhall et al., 2019) dataset and γ = 0.3 for others.
720
      Fine-tuning iterations. Excessive training epochs can degrade image quality; therefore, we set the
721   training duration to range between 10,000 and 30,000 iterations. When the bit error rate (BER)
722   decreases to 0.0%, we implement early stopping of the fine-tuning process.
