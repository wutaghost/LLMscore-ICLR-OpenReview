009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011
               With the powerful representation ability of neural networks, deep clustering (DC)
012
               has been widely studied in machine learning communities. However, current re-
013
               search on DC has rarely laid emphasis on the inter-cluster representation struc-
014            tures, i.e. ignoring the performance degradation caused by the low uncorrelation
015            between different clusters. To tackle this problem, a Uniform quasi-Low-rank Hy-
016            persphere Embedding based DC (ULHE-DC) method is proposed herein, which
017            promotes learning an inter-cluster uniform and intra-cluster compact representa-
018            tion in a novel geometric manner. Specifically, clusters are uniformly distributed
019            on a unit hypersphere via minimizing the hyperspherical energy of the centroids,
020            and the embeddings belonging to the same cluster are simultaneously collapsed to
021            a quasi-low-rank subspace through intra-cluster correlation maximization. Addi-
022
               tionally, a pre-training based optimization scheme is proposed, in which an auto-
               encoder (AE) is pre-trained and the parameters of the encoder of AE are inher-
023
               ited to initialize the feature extractor for clustering, aiming at engaging the model
024
               learning cluster-oriented representation more efficiently. Experimental results val-
025            idate the strong competitiveness of the proposed method, compared with several
026            state-of-the-art (SOTA) benchmarks.

030   1   I NTRODUCTION
031
032   Clustering is widely studied in numerous machine learning communities (Ehsan & René, 2013;
033   Mathilde et al., 2018), such as computer vision, data mining, etc. As an unsupervised learning (Xu
034   & Wunsch, 2005) based technology, clustering aims at learning a partition, ensuring similar samples
035   belonging to the same cluster while grouping dissimilar ones into different clusters, and naturally
036   possesses the technological advantage (i.e. annotation-free) compared with supervised learning.
037   Conventional clustering methods, such as k-means (MacQueen, 1967), Gaussian mixture model
038   (GMM) (Bishop, 2006), kernel k-means (Liu et al., 2016) and spectral clustering (SC) (Ng et al.,
039
      2001), group samples based on the intrinsically similar features or linear transformation of the raw
      data. However, these methods suffer from issues caused by the inflexibility of the hand-crafted
040
      feature or the incapacity to model the non-linear nature, and generally come under the performance
041
      degeneration and high computational complexity when dealing with high-dimensional and large-
042   scale data.
043
044   Noting the superiority of deep neural networks (DNNs) on the ability of nonlinear representation,
045
      deep clustering (DC) methods have been proposed recently, which integrate deep learning to ef-
      fectively learn more discriminative representation and capture the non-linear property. In general,
046
      the basic framework of DC typically comprises the auxiliary loss and clustering loss, respectively
047
      learning feasible features and inducing the cluster formation of feature embeddings. Specifically,
048   the auxiliary loss can generally be the reconstruction loss (Dizaji et al., 2017; Lv et al., 2021), the
049   variational loss (Jiang et al., 2017), or the adversarial loss (Mukherjee et al., 2019). The clustering
050   loss can be the loss of any existing clustering algorithms, such as k-means, GMM, and hierarchical
051   clustering. Nonetheless, DC needs to tackle with the following two optimization problems: 1) Intra-
052   cluster compactness minimization. Features of samples belonging to the same cluster should be
053   highly correlated. 2) Inter-cluster discriminability maximization. Samples belonging to different
      clusters should be embedded in the feature space with extremely low correlation.

054
      However, most existing DC approaches mainly focus on the first issue and learn suitable embed-
055   dings with the DNNs trained through a clustering-oriented loss function, which causes that hard
056   samples near the cluster boundaries cannot supply enough representation guidance. In addition, few
057   researches on DC explicitly pay attention to the second problem. Coincidentally, recent studies (Hu
058   et al., 2014; De et al., 2016) on the supervised tasks have similar properties, which performed the
059   minimization of the Euclidean distance between the deep intra-class embeddings but keeping the
060   inter-class ones apart. More recently, an orthogonal low-rank embedding (OLE) (Lezama et al.,
061   2018) loss was proposed to encourage the neural networks to learn more discriminative features,
062   subspaces of which are intra-class low-rank regularized but inter-class orthogonalized at the same
063
      time. The OLE promotes the network to learn one-dim representations for each category but lim-
      its the utilization of the whole space, compared with the uniform embeddings of cluster centroids.
064
      Besides, the nuclear norm in the OLE loss function is non-smooth, which potentially raises difficul-
065
      ties during the gradient descent based optimization. To alleviate these problems, a representation
066   learning framework based on maximal coding rate reduction (Yu et al., 2020) was proposed to learn
067   subspaces with maximal dimensions, trained with a determinant based smooth loss. Whereas, the
068   determinant operator will result in the computational complexity explosion when the batch size is
069   relatively large.
070
      Addressing the above issues, a Uniform quasi-Low-rank Hypersphere Embedding based DC
071
      (ULHE-DC) method is proposed in this paper, including pretraining and clustering two stages.
072   Firstly, an autoencoder is trained by minimizing the reconstruction and normalizing each embed-
073   ding on the unit hypersphere, transforming data to low dimensional representation space. Then, the
074   encoder is finetuned by using the basic clustering loss. Additionally, the ULHE is designed as a
075   regularizer for the clustering loss, composed of the minimization of the hyperspherical energy be-
076   tween cluster centroids and the maximization of the correlation between members of each cluster,
077   which respectively stimulate the learning preference of the model to uniformly embed the cluster
078   centroids on hypersphere, enhancing the inter-cluster discriminability and diversity, and generate
079   quasi-low-rank and compact embeddings of members belonging to the same cluster. In particular,
080
      the formulation of ULHE based loss is smooth and computationally friendly. Main contributions
      can be summarized as follows:
081
082
             • A novel framework named Uniform quasi-Low-rank Hypersphere Embedding based DC
083            (ULHE-DC) is proposed to optimize the cluster-oriented presentation structure, which can
084            be efficiently implemented with a mini-batch based learning strategy.
085
             • ULHE is established to enhance the inter-cluster discriminability and diversity with min-
086
               imizing the hyperspherical energy, encouraging the centroids being uniformly embedded
087
               on the hypersphere; meanwhile, it enforces the feature embeddings of the same cluster
088            squashed in a quasi-low-rank subspace through the maximization of intra-cluster correla-
089            tion.
090
             • Extensive experiments validate the effectiveness and superiority of ULHE-DC via compar-
091
               ing with several state-of-the-art (SOTA) DC approaches on four benchmarks.

094   2   R ELATED W ORK
095
096   Deep Clustering. DC is a family of clustering algorithms that adopt DNNs to learn cluster-oriented
097   representations. From the perspective of the type of DNNs, DC approaches can be divided into four
098   categories: AE-based, Variational autoencoder (VAE) (Kingma & Welling, 2013) based, generative
099
      adversarial network (GAN) (Goodfellow et al., 2014) based, and clustering DNN (CDNN) based.
      As an extensively studied branch of DC, AE-based DC integrates prior knowledge into the objective
100
      function of AE. The clustering loss functions are mainly the objective of k-means (Yang et al., 2017;
101
      Fard et al., 2020), the variant objective of k-means (Jabi et al., 2021), or the other kinds of loss (Ji
102   et al., 2017). The superiority of AE-based DC is that the scheme of conventional clustering and
103   the regularization of feature embedding can be reasonably employed to the training procedure of
104   AE. VAE-based DC (Jiang et al., 2017; Dilokthanakul et al., 2016) prefers learning a representation,
105   which follows a predefined distribution of the cluster structure, but suffers from high computational
106   complexity. GAN-based methods (Chen et al., 2016; Zhou et al., 2018; Mukherjee et al., 2019)
107   enforce the embedding of the deep feature in a similar way as VAE-based ones. However, the model
      collapse problem and the training challenge of GAN also exist. CDNN-based algorithms (Peng


                                                         2
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


108
      et al., 2017; Guérin & Boots, 2018; Deng et al., 2023) train the extractor merely with the clustering
109   loss, which may result in obtaining corrupted feature space, that is, a convergent loss possibly makes
110   no sense. Recently, those existing DC approaches have been proposed mainly from the perspective
111   of network architectures, various clustering loss or tricks in deep learning. The proposed ULHE reg-
112   ularizer is introduced to restrain the latent embeddings, which keeps intra-cluster members compact
113   and inter-cluster ones relatively uniform on a unit hypersphere.
114
      Minimum Hyperspherical Energy (MHE). Drawing inspiration from the Thomson problem in
115   physics, MHE (Liu et al., 2018) is defined to seek the equilibrium state of the distribution of mutually
116   repelling electrons through minimizing the potential energy. More generally, lower energy indicates
117   more diverse and more uniform distribution. MHE has been extensively researched, which shows
118   noteworthy effectiveness in many applications. MHE was firstly proposed and used as a generic
119   regularization for neural networks in (Liu et al., 2018), regularizing the networks to avoid represen-
120   tation redundancy. Analogously, the compressive minimum hyperspherical energy (Lin et al., 2020)
121   and the hyperspherical uniformity regularization (Liu et al., 2021) were established. A MHE-based
122   active learning algorithm (Cao et al., 2023) was designed to effectively characterize the decision
123
      boundaries for data learning. Besides, MHE has been widely applied in image recognition (Chen
      et al., 2020; Li et al., 2020), speaker verification, adversarial robustness (Pang et al., 2019), etc. In
124
      DC, maximizing the inter-cluster discriminability is approximated to enhance the diversity of clus-
125
      ters, which can be implemented through embedding the centroids as evenly as possible, and MHE
126   provides a solution from a geometric perspective.

131   3.1   F RAMEWORK OVERVIEW
132
      Given an unlabeled dataset X = {xi ∈ RD }N        i=1 , deep clustering aims to assign N samples to K
133
      clusters. Note that K is priorly given in this study. In deep clustering, samples are generally mapped
134
      to a much lower dimension feature space with an embedding network Fw := xi → zi , zi ∈ Rd (d
135   D). With parameters w well optimized by minimizing the clustering loss function, the embedding
136   network is expected to extract more suitable feature for clustering.
137
138
      The proposed ULHE-DC method aims to learn cluster-oriented features based on an AE networks
      and includes the pretraining and clustering two stages. Firstly, the AE is pretrained to extract fea-
139
      sible features with the reconstruction loss Lrec and a normalized loss Lnorm to embed data on
140
      a unit hypersphere. After pretraining, ULHE-DC finetunes the encoder part of AE both with the
141   clustering objective and the ULHE based regularization loss Lunif and Lcmpt , making the learned
142   representations cluster-friendly.

      C ONCLUSION
477
478
      In this paper, a uniform quasi-low rank embedding based deep clustering method (ULHE-DC) is
479
      proposed. To address the problem of low uncorrelation between different clusters, an inter-cluster
480   uniformity regularization is applied to enhance the discriminability and diversity of the represen-
481   tation structures, which is implemented via the minimization of the hyperspherical energy of the
482   centroids. Additionally, ULHE-DC establishes an intra-cluster compactness regularization to em-
483   bed features of the same cluster in a quasi-low-rank subspace, and simultaneously improve the in-
484   stability potentially existing in the optimization of the uniformity regularization loss. Furthermore,
485   an efficient mini-batch based optimization strategy is designed for ULHE to yield better clustering
      performance. The experimental results show that ULHE-DC outperforms those SOTA approaches.
