009                                                A BSTRACT
010
011            Recent advances in retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) furnish large language
012            models (LLMs) with iterative retrievals of relevant information to strengthen their
013            capabilities in addressing complex multi-hop questions. However, these methods
014            typically accumulate the retrieved natural language text into LLM prompts, impos-
015            ing an increasing burden on the LLM to grasp the underlying knowledge structure
016            for high-quality multi-step reasoning. Despite a few attempts to reduce this burden
017            by restructuring all retrieved passages or even entire external corpora, these efforts
018
               are afflicted with significant restructuring overhead and potential knowledge loss.
               To tackle this challenge, we introduce a new structured paradigm (K NOW T RACE)
019
               from the perspective of explicit knowledge tracing, which treats LLM as an agent
020
               to progressively acquire desired knowledge triplets during iterative retrievals and
021            ultimately trace out a specific knowledge graph conditioned on the input question.
022            This paradigm clearly unveils the logical relationships behind the unstructured text
023            and thus can directly facilitate LLM’s inference. Notably, it also naturally inspires
024            a reflective mechanism of knowledge backtracing to identify supportive evidence
025            and filter out useless retrievals in the correct trajectories, thus offering an effective
026            way to stimulate LLM’s self-taught finetuning. Extensive experiments demonstrate
027            the superiority of our paradigm over three standard multi-hop question answering
028            benchmarks. Our code is available at https://github.com/xxrep/SRAG.

      1   I NTRODUCTION
032
033
      Large language models (LLMs) (Brown et al., 2020; Chowdhery et al., 2023; Touvron et al., 2023;
      Dubey et al., 2024) encapsulate a wealth of human knowledge into their massive parameters and have
034
      shown impressive performance across a wide range of language tasks through the form of question
035
      answering. Despite their remarkable capabilities, LLMs continue to struggle with factual errors
036   (Welleck et al., 2020; Mallen et al., 2023; Zhang et al., 2023) when the input question exceeds their
037   knowledge boundaries. As a prominent solution for this problem, Retrieval-Augmented Generation
038   (RAG) (Lewis et al., 2020) empowers LLMs to incorporate new knowledge through the retrieval
039   of relevant information. One-time retrieval-then-read (Borgeaud et al., 2022; Izacard et al., 2023)
040   usually suffices to fulfill the information needs of single-hop questions, while the complex multi-hop
041   question answering task still remains challenging due to their demands for intensive knowledge and
042   multi-step reasoning capabilities, thus drawing significant attention within the research community.
043   To address the complex multi-hop questions, a feasible strategy is extending RAG into a multi-round
044   iterative process (Trivedi et al., 2023; Press et al., 2023; Shao et al., 2023; Yao et al., 2023; Yang
045   et al., 2024; Jiang et al., 2024). This process alternates between two stages: (1) performing partial
046   LLM reasoning to guide subsequent retrieval, and (2) utilizing the retrieved information to enhance
047   further reasoning, continuing until the available information is sufficient to deduce the final answer.
048   Benefiting from such workflow of interleaving retrievals with LLM reasoning, the iterative RAG can
049   narrow semantic gaps between the input questions and their requisite knowledge (Shao et al., 2023).
050   However, more is not always better—while the above iterative RAG methods can periodically bring
051   in new external passages, they also present a great challenge for the LLMs in handling such ever-
052   growing text due to the complexity and diversity of natural language expressions. Most of existing
053   methods simply accumulate all these passages into LLM prompts, thereby struggling to perceive
      the underlying knowledge structure (i.e., logical connections among informative entities) for high-

054
      quality multi-step reasoning (Braine, 1978). Several recent works try to mitigate this issue with
055   an auxiliary process of reorganizing all retrieved passages (Li & Du, 2023; Cheng et al., 2024) or
056   even entire external corpora (Edge et al., 2024; Sarmah et al., 2024) into specific structures (e.g.,
057   graphs and hierarchies), yet these approaches come with two inherent drawbacks: (1) they typically
058   necessitate extensive LLM invocations for the intricate restructuring operations such as information
059   extraction and refinement (Sarmah et al., 2024), thus incurring significant computational overhead;
060   (2) such restructuring is decoupled from the question-specific reasoning process, potentially leading
061   to the loss of question-relevant knowledge due to the lack of explicit reasoning guidance. In light
062   of these limitations, a critical concern arises: is there an elegant way to seamlessly incorporate the
063
      information restructuring process into the iterative RAG for higher-quality multi-step reasoning?
064   In this paper, we give an affirmative response by introducing K NOW T RACE, a fresh RAG paradigm
065   that can coherently trace out question-specific knowledge structures to bolster multi-step reasoning.
066   At a high level, we draw upon a profound insight from constructivist theory (Fosnot, 2013): learning
067   is never merely about accumulating information, but involves actively absorbing crucial knowledge
068   to construct and expand one’s cognitive schema. Inspired by this principle, K NOW T RACE leverages
069
      the LLM as an active knowledge organizer (rather than a passive information receiver) to explicitly
      trace question-relevant knowledge triplets from retrieved passages and progressively form a concrete
070
      knowledge graph (KG) for structured retrieval-augmented generation. More specifically, instead of
071
      simply accumulating textual information, our paradigm can be described as a reasoning-guided KG
072   expansion process. As shown in Fig. 1(c), the LLM alternates between: (1) knowledge exploration
073   to determine a set of entities along with their respective relation guidance based on the current KG
074   (initially from the input question) for next retrieval; (2) knowledge completion to fill in these entity-
075   relation pairs based on the retrieved passages for enriching the current KG—until it grasps adequate
076   knowledge to solve the question or reaches the predefined maximum number of expansion rounds.
077
      Owing to the perspective of explicit knowledge tracing, K NOW T RACE is able to adaptively maintain
078   a transparent KG throughout the multi-step reasoning process for each input question. Such evident
079   structure endows the LLM with an intelligible context to facilitate its inference capability, while also
080   providing a graphical explanation of the entire reasoning trajectory. In particular, for the positive
081   trajectories that ultimately arrive at correct answers, the acquired KGs can naturally induce a post-
082   hoc reflective mechanism to identify the evidence subgraphs via knowledge backtracing (Fig. 1(c)).
083   Notably, this offers a simple yet effective way to distill higher-quality reasoning rationales from the
084   correct trajectories, enabling us to further improve K NOW T RACE in a self-taught manner. Different
085   from recent self-taught methods (Zelikman et al., 2022; Yuan et al., 2023; Hosseini et al., 2024) that
086
      indiscriminately finetune on the entire correct trajectories, our paradigm can selectively filter out the
      procedural impurities (i.e., irrelevant knowledge and useless retrievals) for better self-improvement.
087
      Overall, by showcasing its dual advantages in multi-step inference and self-improvement, this work
088
      highlights the great significance of structured knowledge tracing for retrieval-augmented generation.
089
090   Our contributions are summarized as follows:
091
          • We introduce a structured RAG paradigm (K NOW T RACE), which to the best of our knowledge
092
            is the first work to seamlessly enhance multi-step reasoning through explicit knowledge tracing.
093
094
          • Based on the acquired question-specific knowledge structures (i.e., graphs), we further propose
            a post-hoc reflective mechanism (knowledge backtracing) to distill high-quality rationales from
095
            the correct trajectories, which can be used to guide the self-improvement of K NOW T RACE.
096
097       • We conduct extensive experiments on three multi-hop question answering benchmarks. Under
098
            different configurations of LLMs and retrieval models, K NOW T RACE consistently outperforms
            current RAG methods across all the datasets. The backtracing-guided finetuning further boosts
099
            the performance. Moreover, we also explore prompting strategies of this structured paradigm.

      Iterative RAG. Retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) has been demonstrated as a promising tech-
132
      nique to improve the performance of LLMs in knowledge-intensive NLP tasks (Lewis et al., 2020)
133   (as shown in Fig. 1(a)). Early RAG approaches (Zhu et al., 2021; Lazaridou et al., 2022; Gao et al.,
134   2023) typically conduct only one-time retrieval, which struggles to gather all information related to
135   the input questions, especially for the complex multi-hop questions. To address this, a new genre of
136   iterative RAG works has recently been developed. Self-Ask (Press et al., 2023) adopts a step-by-step
137   approach to break down complex questions and solve each sub-question through the Google search.
138   IRCoT (Trivedi et al., 2023) treats the output of each reasoning step as a retrieval query, collecting
139   more passages to augment the subsequent reasoning steps. Similarly, Iter-RetGen (Shao et al., 2023)
140   combines the reasoning output with the original question for further retrieval. Despite their prowess,
141
      these iterative methods inherently disregard the significance of the underlying knowledge structures
      behind the textual passages, which are essential elements of high-quality reasoning (Braine, 1978).
142
143   Structure-enhanced RAG. In light of the limitation of iterative RAG methods, several recent works
144   employ an auxiliary restructuring process for retrieved passages (Li & Du, 2023; Panda et al., 2024;
145   Liu et al., 2024; Cheng et al., 2024) or even entire text corpora (Edge et al., 2024; Sarmah et al., 2024;
146   Peng et al., 2024). In particular, these methods first utilizes LLMs to conduct intricate restructuring
147
      operations such as entity recognition and relation extraction; according to the constructed knowledge
      structures, the LLMs then switches to inference mode for answering the input question. Unlike these
148
      methods that separate structuring from reasoning, our K NOW T RACE paradigm seamlessly integrates
149
      structuring and reasoning into a coherent process, thereby eliminating the limitations of substantial
150   restructuring overhead and potential knowledge loss.
151
152   Self-taught finetuning. Self-taught finetuning provides an impressive way to enhance the reasoning
153
      capabilities of LLMs by finetuning them on their self-generated correct solutions (Zelikman et al.,
      2022; Yuan et al., 2023; Singh et al., 2024; Hosseini et al., 2024). Specifically, the self-improvement
154
      process (Zelikman et al., 2022; Hosseini et al., 2024) corresponds to a simple loop: use an LLM to
155
      infer a set of questions; collect all the generations that ultimately yield the correct answers; finetune
156   on these collected data; restart to collect new generations from the newly finetuned LLM. Note that
157   when a new dataset is collected, the model being finetuned is the original base LLM, rather than the
158   one from the previous iteration. Such self-taught process is designed based on a priori assumption:
159   the LLM generations that lead to correct answers reveal high-quality reasoning rationales. However,
160   for the complex multi-step reasoning scenarios such as MHQA, a long-horizon reasoning trajectory
161   may still contain irrelevant generations. In our paradigm, a reflective mechanism is naturally inspired
      to identify and filter out this noise for more effective self-improvement.

      3.1   OVERVIEW
165
166   This work introduces K NOW T RACE, a new structured paradigm that can self-organize the question-
167   relevant knowledge structures (i.e., KGs) in a coherent manner to enhance the MHQA performance.
168   As shown in Fig. 1, K NOW T RACE reformulates the LLM reasoner as an active knowledge organizer,
169   which iteratively operates in two phases (knowledge exploration and knowledge completion) to trace
170   the desired knowledge triplets, until an adequate KG is acquired to conclude with a definitive answer.
171
      We present a detailed description of this entire inference process in Section 3.2. Moreover, benefit-
      ing from the transparent graph structures, a post-hoc reflective mechanism (knowledge backtracing)
172
      is naturally inspired to filter out extraneous knowledge and futile retrievals from the correct trajec-
173
      tories. By leveraging the distilled high-quality rationales as procedural supervision, we can further
174   improve K NOW T RACE via refined self-taught finetuning (Section 3.3). For the sake of clarification,
175   all notations used in this paper are listed in Appendix A.
176
177   3.2   E XPLICIT K NOWLEDGE T RACING FOR S TRUCTURED M ULTI -S TEP R EASONING
178
179   Given a multi-hop question q and a textual corpus C, K NOW T RACE autonomously acquires a set of
180   q-relevant knowledge triplets from C to form an explicit KG Gq = {(es , r, eo )|es , eo ∈ Eq , r ∈ Rq },
181   in which Eq and Rq denotes the sets of entities and relations, respectively, and each triplet (es , r, eo )
182   represents that there is a relation r between subject entity es and object entity eo . The entire inference
183   framework can be described as an iterative explore-then-complete process (Algo. 1), which consists
184
      of two LLM-driven phases: knowledge exploration and knowledge completion.
185   Knowledge exploration. During this phase, K NOW T RACE leverages the LLM’s planning capability
186   (Bohnet et al., 2024) to determine the actions for each iteration: either to provide a definitive answer
187   as the final prediction or to explore more knowledge for KG expansion. Formally, at the l-th iteration
188   (1 ≤ l ≤ L), K NOW T RACE integrates the input question q and the KG Gql−1 acquired from the pre-
189   vious l − 1 iterations (initially Gq0 is empty) into an instruction prompt Iexp . This prompt is designed
190   to elicit such a coherent generation from an LLM M : first, M assesses whether Gql−1 is adequate for
191   deriving the final answer, and accordingly sets a boolean FLAG; if the FLAG is true, M then outputs
192   the answer a, along with a chain of thought t (Wei et al., 2022) to reveal the final reasoning process;
193   otherwise, M then presents specific guidance on how to expand the current KG, that is, it adaptively
194
      determines the expansion points (i.e., entities) and the corresponding directions (i.e., relations), pro-
      ducing a set of entity-relation pairs that indicate the knowledge desired for the next reasoning step.
195

235   Knowledge prompting strategy. Since K NOW T RACE aims to enhance LLM’s inference with self-
236   organized KG, one essential consideration lies in how to integrate KG information into LLM prompt.
237   On this matter, we investigate three strategies to describe Gq for the prompt Iexp in Eq. (1):
238     ◦ KG-to-Triplets. We directly feed the elementary knowledge triplets into the LLM.

241       and then regard these connected paths as the descriptions of KG.
242     ◦ KG-to-Text. An additional generative model is employed to transform the acquired triplets into
243       natural language, allowing the LLM to process the KG as standard text.

      3.3   R EFLECTIVE K NOWLEDGE BACKTRACING FOR S ELF -TAUGHT F INETUNING
256
257
      Self-taught finetuning is an attractive process, in which the LLM can post-refine its own performance
258   without human intervention. In line with the concept of self-training (Nigam & Ghani, 2000), recent
259   techniques (Zelikman et al., 2022; Singh et al., 2024; Hosseini et al., 2024) improve the reasoning
260   capabilities of LLMs by finetuning them on their own generations that arrive at the correct answers.
261   Nevertheless, despite its effectiveness for one-time generation tasks, this process is inherently flawed
262   when applied to recent RAG systems in MHQA scenario: for a complex multi-hop question, even if
263   the final prediction is correct, the corresponding long-horizon reasoning trajectory may still contain
264   multiple unnecessary LLM generations, which could diminish the efficacy of subsequent finetuning.
265   To address this limitation, the key challenge lies in how to grasp the reasoning rationales and remove
266   the procedural impurities in correct trajectories. Based on our perspective of explicit knowledge trac-
267   ing, the structured K NOW T RACE can progressively acquire a KG for the input question as reasoning
268   progresses. In this regard, the acquired KG directly forms procedural records of the entire multi-step
269   reasoning process. Such transparent records then naturally inspire us to design a post-hoc reflective
      mechanism (knowledge backtracing) to identify the supportive generations from correct trajectories.

288   Formally, given a positive inference sample (q, Gq , [t, a]) of K NOW T RACE that yields correct answer
289   (i.e., the prediction a exactly matches the ground-truth answer â), we aim to grasp the key reasoning
290   rationales from all LLM generations in Eq. (1) and (2). Due to the structured nature of our paradigm,
291   the rationales of each sample (q, Gq , [t, a]) essentially correspond to a knowledge subgraph Sq ⊆ Gq
292   that exactly supports the final prediction. In light of this, a simple yet effective backtracing process
293
      can be adopted for rationalization: first, since the ground truth label â could verify the rationality of
      the final LLM generation (i.e., [t, a]) (Wei et al., 2022; Xi et al., 2024), we accordingly select the
294
      entities that appear in [t, a] as the informative target entities; then, we trace back along the graph
295
      structure of Gq from these entities to the initial entities (defined in Section 3.2), thereby inducing the
296   expected subgraph Sq that consists of all the supportive knowledge. This subgraph showcases which
297   triplets contribute to the final prediction, and thus offers guidance for filtering irrelevant generations:
298
299
          ◦ (Unavailing Exploration) For each prompt-generation pair (Iexp (·), P) in Eq. (1), we remove
            (ei , ri ) ∈ P (or even entire P) that fails to produce any supportive knowledge triplets in Sq .
300
301       ◦ (Extraneous Completion) For each prompt-generation pair (Icom (·), T∗ ) in Eq. (2), we remove
302         the generated knowledge triplets (or even entire T∗ ) that are not included in Sq .
303   In this way, the designed mechanism endows K NOW T RACE with the ability to reflect on reasoning
304   trajectories and distill high-quality rationales. We incorporate this mechanism into the standard self-
305   improvement technique (Zelikman et al., 2022), forming a backtracing-guided self-training process.
306   As shown in Algo. 2, K NOW T RACE can bootstrap its reasoning ability through the following loop:
307   (1) collect inference samples that lead to correct answers from a labeled MHQA dataset; (2) conduct
308   backtracing mechanism to filter out irrelevant generations; (3) finetune the base LLM on the distilled
309   generations for the next round of inference. We highlight that the backtracing mechanism is naturally
310
      built on the structures acquired by our K NOW T RACE, thus showcasing another unique advantage of
      the perspective of explicit knowledge tracing. In other words, K NOW T RACE not only self-organizes
311
      clear structures to enhance inference, but also offers an effective way to stimulate self-improvement.

329   4.1.1    DATASETS AND M ETRICS
330
331   Datasets. We evaluate K NOW T RACE on three standard MHQA datasets in the open-domain setting:
332   HotpotQA (Yang et al., 2018), 2WikiMultihopQA (2Wiki) (Ho et al., 2020), and MuSiQue (Trivedi
333
      et al., 2022). For each dataset, we follow (Trivedi et al., 2023; Li & Du, 2023) to randomly sample
      100 questions from the development set for hyperparameter tuning, and another 500 questions are
334
      randomly sampled as the test set. For the open-domain corpora, we use Wikipedia 2017 (Yang et al.,
335
      2018) for HotpotQA and Wikipedia 2018 (Karpukhin et al., 2020) for the other two datasets. More
336   details about these datasets can be found in Appendix B.
337
338   Metrics. We employ the exact match (EM) and the F1 score as evaluation metrics. The EM accuracy
339
      is calculated as the proportion of correct answers in the test set, where a prediction is deemed correct
      if it exactly matches one of the ground truth answers. The F1 score evaluates the overlap between
340
      the tokens in the prediction and the answer. We apply normalization to both the predictions and the
341
      answers when computing these two metrics, following the implementation of (Yao et al., 2023).

      4.1.2    BASELINES
344
345   We compare K NOW T RACE with a series of advanced RAG approaches, which can be classified into
346   three categories: (1) one-time retrieval-augmented chain-of-thought (Wei et al., 2022), i.e., RA-CoT;
347   (2) unstructured iterative approaches: IRCoT (Trivedi et al., 2023), ReAct (Yao et al., 2023), Self-
348   Ask (Press et al., 2023) and Iter-RetGen (Shao et al., 2023); (3) restructuring-based approaches:
349   SG-Prompt (Li & Du, 2023) and ERA-CoT (Liu et al., 2024).
350   Here, we briefly describe two representative baselines related to our work. As an unstructured RAG
351   approach, IRCoT (Trivedi et al., 2023) interleaves retrieval-augmented chain-of-thought reasoning
352   and reasoning-guided retrieval until the final answer is reported or the maximum allowed number of
353   reasoning steps is reached. A recent restructuring-based work, ERA-CoT (Liu et al., 2024), uncover
354   the knowledge structure behind textual passages using a fully LLM-driven process: first, it identifies
355   all entities involved in the text; then, it extracts both explicit and implicit relations between entities;
356   next, it scores the reliability of the relations and removes those falling below a predefined threshold;
357
      after completing this intricate process, it performs the final answer prediction. More descriptions of
      all baselines can be found in Appendix C.

507   Knowledge prompting strategies. Since this work highlights the significance of explicit knowledge
508
      structures (i.e., KGs) for LLM inference, a meaningful concern lies in how to incorporate KGs into
      LLM prompts. Here, we explore three types of prompting strategies, including KG-to-Triplets, KG-
509
      to-Paths, and KG-to-Text, which correspond to elementary triplets, connected paths, and rewritten
510
      natural language, respectively, as described in Section 3.2. Table 3 presents the EM/F1 results of our
511   paradigm with these three strategies. For fair comparisons, we utilize LLaMA3-8B-Instruct as
512   the base LLM and BM25 as the retriever. One the one hand, the simplest KG-to-Triplets works well,
513   while organizing independent triplets into paths (i.e., KG-to-Paths) does not lead to consistent gains.
514   We find that the path extraction process typically duplicates some triplets, which may distract LLM
515   inference (Shi et al., 2023; Ji et al., 2023). On the other hand, converting KGs back into natural text
516   with LLM (i.e., KG-to-Text) also results in inferior performance, which we attribute to the absence
517   of priori structural templates in the prompts. For example, one can directly specify that the contexts
518   take the form of (subject, relation, object) in the triplet prompting strategy. Overall, KG-to-Triplets
519
      exhibits the dual advantages of simplicity and effectiveness, as it can offer a priori structural template
      without bringing duplicate information, making it the main choice for our experiments.

522   5                  D ISCUSSION AND C ONCLUSION
523
524   Limitations. Although our paradigm showcases advantages in both multi-step reasoning and self-
525   taught finetuning, there are still two major limitations in this work. First, despite its effectiveness
526   in MHQA task, the applicability of our explicit knowledge tracing in other complex scenarios, such
527   as mathematics and decision-making tasks, is unexplored. Second, K NOW T RACE can leverage the
528
      designed backtracing mechanism to foster self-taught finetuning, but how to proactively correct
      erroneous trajectories without finetuning remains an open challenge in our paradigm. We expect
529
      future studies to mitigate these issues.
530
531   Conclusion. In this work, we introduce K NOW T RACE, a simple yet effective RAG paradigm to en-
532   hance the multi-step reasoning capabilities of LLMs for more advanced MHQA performance. Our
533   design idea is to seamlessly integrate the knowledge structuring with the multi-step reasoning from
534
      the perspective of explicit knowledge tracing. Benefiting from this perspective, our K NOW T RACE
      not only acquires question-specific KGs to facilitate inference, but also naturally inspires a reflective
535
      backtracing mechanism to stimulate self-improvement. Extensive experiments over three standard
536
      MHQA benchmarks comprehensively demonstrate the superiority of our proposals. Under differ-
537   ent configurations of LLMs and retrieval models, our paradigm consistently outperforms a series of
538   existing RAG approaches, and the backtracing-guided finetuning further elevates the overall perfor-
539   mance, thereby showcasing the rationality of our perspective of explicit knowledge tracing.

838   B    DATASETS
839
840   We use the following three widely-used multi-hop question answering datasets for evaluation:
841   HotpotQA. This is a comprehensive dataset collected from the English Wikipedia, comprising ap-
842   proximately 113k crowd-sourced questions. The unique characteristic of HotpotQA lies in its con-
843   struction, which requires answers to be derived from the introductory paragraphs of two distinct
844   Wikipedia articles. For each question, the dataset includes the corresponding gold paragraphs from
845   these articles, alongside a curated list of sentences identified by crowdworkers as critical supporting
846   evidence necessary to accurately resolve the query. Note that our evaluation is conducted under the
847   open-domain setting (Trivedi et al., 2023), and thus does not use these gold information.
848   2WikiMultihopQA (2Wiki). This dataset consists of complex 2-hop questions that require either
849   compositional reasoning or comparative analysis. Both structured and unstructured information
850   from Wikipedia and Wikidata are combined for data construction. In terms of difficulty, 2WikiMul-
851   tihopQA is challenging for multi-hop models and it ensures that multi-hop reasoning is required.
852   MuSiQue. The multi-hop questions in this dataset is constructed by carefully selecting and com-
853   posing single-hop questions obtained from a large collection of single-hop questions. In terms of
854   difficulty, MuSiQue is a more challenging dataset, since it contains 2 to 4 hop questions.

      C    BASELINES
858
859   RA-CoT (Wei et al., 2022). This is the simplest approach, which conducts one-time retrieval with
860
      the input question as the query, and use the retrieved text to guide LLM to perform CoT reasoning.
861   ReAct (Yao et al., 2023). This approach integrates reasoning, action, and observation steps in an
862   iterative process until a final answer is reached. Actions in this process include generating queries
863   to search for relevant information or concluding with a final answer. Observations are formed by
      concatenating the results from these actions and serve as inputs for subsequent reasoning steps.


                                                                 16
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


864
      Self-Ask (Press et al., 2023). This work adopts an iterative approach to break down complex ques-
865   tions into simpler sub-questions. At each iteration, sub-questions are generated based on the current
866   stage of reasoning, followed by retrieving relevant information and answering these sub-questions.
867   This process continues until the answer is finalized.
868
      Iter-RetGen (Shao et al., 2023). This work leverages the LLM output from the previous iteration
869
      as the query to retrieve more relevant knowledge. It conducts retrievals by concatenating the output
870
      from the previous iteration with the original question.
871
872   SG-Prompt (Li & Du, 2023). This work first constructs a semantic graph structures through infor-
873   mation extraction from all retrieved text, and then leverages this symbolic information (including
874
      entities and semantic relations) to enhance LLM’s inference process.

1023   G     D ISCUSSION ON M ORE R ELATED W ORKS
1024
1025   Several recent works also leverage structured information to enhance the training of language mod-
       els or guide their reasoning processes. (Wang et al., 2023b) and (Misra et al., 2023) focus on con-


                                                              19
       Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


1026
       structing masked knowledge structures as training data for (pre-)training language models, aiming to
1027   imbue the models with structural reasoning capabilities. Specifically, they construct training datasets
1028   by first restructuring Wikipedia documents and then masking specific (predefined or random-walk-
1029   generated) entities within the structures. In contrast, our method does not rely on such structural
1030   pretraining or dataset construction, but instead operates directly on unstructured text, actively trac-
1031   ing relevant knowledge in the form of triplets during multi-step inference.
1032
       GE-Reasoning (Park et al., 2023) and Semi-Structured CoT (Su et al., 2023) focus on parsing input
1033   questions into masked structured chains and subsequently rely either on existing external knowledge
1034   graphs to fill missing triplets or rewrite missing triplets as natural language queries to retrieve an-
1035   swers from external text databases. However, such approaches heavily depend on the accuracy of the
1036   initial parsing—errors at this stage can propagate—thereby necessitating careful filtering and con-
1037   sistency operations (Su et al., 2023). In contrast, K NOW T RACE adopts a more flexible perspective
1038   of adaptively tracing knowledge triplets during the multi-step reasoning process, rather than solely
1039   relying on the one-time parsing of the input question. This adaptive exploration can reduce error
1040   propagation and enhance robustness.
1041   CoK (Wang et al., 2023a) retrieves candidate knowledge triplets from a pre-constructed KG and
1042   combines them with human annotations, aiming to design effective exemplars that induce fact gen-
1043   eration capabilities of LLMs. In contrast, our work pursues a different objective, i.e., tracing and
1044   expanding structured knowledge directly from unstructured text during multi-step reasoning process
1045   to enhance the multi-step reasoning capabilities of LLMs.
1046   Overall, our K NOW T RACE framework actively traces knowledge triplets relevant to the input ques-
1047   tion during multi-step reasoning process. Such a perspective enables more flexible LLM inference
1048   and does not require additional structural training or one-time parsing of the input. The progres-
1049   sive expansion of structured knowledge memory in our K NOW T RACE framework not only enhances
1050   LLM inference, but also provides a transparent record of the reasoning Procedure. This transparency
1051   allows for the natural backtracing mechanism to distill higher-quality rationales, which can further
1052   be leveraged for post-training (e.g., self-improvement). The proposed framework is orthogonal to
1053
       the above techniques, and one can integrate them to further enhance the reasoning capabilities of
       LLMs. For instance, K NOW T RACE could use models pre-trained with structural reasoning as the
1054
       backbone or incorporate pre-parsed question structures to assist in the knowledge exploration phase.
