009                                              A BSTRACT
010
011            Diffusion models facilitate powerful control over the generative process. Here
012            we introduce training-free guidance, a method for sampling from a broad class of
013            conditional distributions that can be considered generalisations of inpainting. The
014            method is grounded in annealed Langevin dynamics which ensures convergence
015            to the exact conditional distribution, unlike existing methods for inpainting which
016            rely on heuristics. We demonstrate training-free guidance using pretrained uncon-
017            ditional models for image, protein structure, and protein sequence generation and
018            improve upon state-of-the-art approaches. We show the versatility of training-free
019
               guidance by addressing a wide range of tasks, including multi-motif scaffolding
               and amino acid mutagenesis of T cell receptors.

      1     I NTRODUCTION
024
025   Denoising diffusion probabilistic models (DDPMs) are a powerful class of generative models (Sohl-
026
      Dickstein et al., 2015; Ho et al., 2020) that have gained popularity across many domains (Hooge-
      boom et al., 2022; Kong et al., 2020). Diffusion models are often trained to generate samples from a
027
      data distribution unconditionally, but practical applications generally require sampling from a con-
028
      ditional data distribution (Rombach et al., 2022; Karras et al., 2022).
029
030   One such conditional sampling task is inpainting, a well-studied problem where the conditioning
031   specifies the exact values of a subset of the sample. This task is important in several settings, in-
032
      cluding image editing (Pathak et al., 2016; Liu et al., 2018) and protein engineering, where it is
      more commonly referred to as motif scaffolding (Didi et al., 2023; Lin et al., 2024). In this work
033
      we consider inpainting alongside a suite of other tasks such as editing sequences and optimising an
034
      element-wise sample score. These conditions are not typically considered as extensions of inpaint-
035   ing, but we show that they are in fact expressible as a set of inpainting conditions linked by logical
036   connectives (i.e. AND/OR), possibly with varying weights. We refer to these tasks as generalised
037   inpainting.
038
      Most methods for conditional sampling require new models (Dhariwal & Nichol, 2021), or modified
039
      training procedures (Ho & Salimans, 2022), which can be costly. Several plug-and-play methods
040
      have been proposed for both inpainting (Song & Ermon, 2019) and generalised inpainting tasks like
041   sequence editing (Meng et al., 2021), but these methods tend to be heuristically motivated and do
042   not sample from the exact conditional distribution.
043
044
      In this work we present training-free guidance (TFG) of diffusion models, a method of sampling
      from the exact conditional distribution for generalised inpainting tasks. We show that TFG improves
045
      upon the current state-of-the-art for standard inpainting when applied to image and protein structure
046
      generation, and give examples of further generalisations of the inpainting problem.

470   5     L IMITATIONS AND F URTHER W ORK
471
472   In this work we have developed training-free guidance, a new method of exact sampling for gen-
473   eralised inpainting conditions. We now highlight some limitations to the approach and suggest
474   possible avenues for further research.
475   We have restricted ourselves here to Langevin dynamics as an approach for annealed MCMC. The
476   sampling time scales linearly with the number of Langevin steps, which limits the applicability of
477   TFG in scenarios where speed of generation is critical. Improving the efficiency of MCMC methods
478   is a rich field (Andrieu et al., 2010; Neal, 2012) with direct implications for such contexts.
479
      Despite the broad nature of generalised inpainting tasks, they do not comprise all interesting condi-
480   tional tasks, and we give two possible directions of further generalisation. First, generalised inpaint-
481   ing tasks are straightforward functions in the original data space, not a latent space. Future work
482   could explore the applicability of TFG to conditioning on preferred values in latent spaces, with
483   consequences for latent diffusion models (Rombach et al., 2022). Second, we have not explored
484   condition-forcing terms that cannot be expressed solely in terms of the score, but also require esti-
485   mates of the raw probabilities pt (x). These probabilities can be accessed by training energy-based
      diffusion models (Du et al., 2023), enabling sampling from a wider range of conditions.

      The RFDiffusion motif scaffolding benchmark contains 25 motif scaffolding tasks defined by contig
      strings. We generate 50 backbone designs for each task and use the insilico validation pipeline pub-
879
      lished by Lin et al. (2024) to attribute design success. This pipeline uses the inverse folding method
880
      ProteinMPNN (Dauparas et al., 2022) to decode eight sequences from each generated backbone, and
881   ESMFold (Lin et al., 2023) to refold these sequences. For each backbone design, the best refolding
882   structure is considered that with the lowest scRMSD to the original designed backbone. Backbone
883   designs are deemed a success if they satisfy pLDDT > 70, pAE < 5, scRMSD < 2.0 Å and
884   motif bb rmsd < 1.0 Å. To assess the diversity of designed backbones we also report the num-
885   ber of unique successes, which is determined using single linkage hierarchical clustering to group
886   structures based on structural similarity (TM-score).
887   We note that the reimplementation of the original RFDiffusion benchmark includes minor differ-
888   ences, particularly the motifs specified by tasks 6exz, 6e6r and 5trv are shifted by one, one and
889   two residues respectively. We choose to match our generations to the Lin et al. (2023) implementa-
890   tion since this allows us to reuse their published evaluation pipeline.
