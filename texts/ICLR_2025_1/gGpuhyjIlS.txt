011                                                  ABSTRACT
012
013            Reducing serving costs and latency is a fundamental challenge for deploying large-scale
014            models in business applications. To cope with this demand, the Learngene framework
015            encapsulates shareable information from large models into a compact unit called a
016
               learngene. This unit serves to initialize downstream models, enabling them to inherit
               the knowledge from the large model efficiently, hopefully diminishing deployment
017
               expenses. However, existing learngene methods are constrained by their strong
018
               dependence on the architecture of large model and overlook the features of target tasks,
019            resulting in suboptimal adaptability of downstream models to deployment requirements.
020            In this paper, we present Task-Aware Learngene (TAL), a novel method based on graph
021            hypernetworks that predicts model parameters conditioned on desired model scales and
022            task-specific characteristics. Extensive experiments demonstrate that TAL effectively
023            scales model initialization parameters, selectively utilizes shareable information
024            pertinent to target tasks, and consistently outperforms random initialization and existing
025            parameter prediction methods. Furthermore, TAL exhibits promising transfer learning
026            capabilities for unseen tasks, underscoring its effectiveness in condensing large model
027
               knowledge while being aware of downstream requirements.

      1    INTRODUCTION
031
032   Large-scale foundation models Dosovitskiy (2020); Radford et al. (2021) have revolutionized multi-task
033
      learning by enabling the fine-tuning of a single model across various downstream tasks Dosovitskiy
      (2020); Liu et al. (2021). This paradigm rests on the belief that the shareable information among different
034
      tasks allows foundation models to provide effective parameter initialization for arbitrary tasks. However,
035
      fine-tuning typically necessitates maintaining the entire model’s parameters and updating them for a
036   specialized task Mahabadi et al. (2021), which usually comes at a significant cost. Furthermore, the size
037   of the fine-tuned model may not always align with the scale of the downstream task Houlsby et al. (2019),
038   posing unnecessary computational burdens, particularly in real-time deployments with stringent latency
039   or memory footprint constraints. These challenges have sparked great interest in developing techniques
040   that target specific issues left by the pre-training and fine-tuning paradigm, such as parameter-efficient
041   fine-tuning methods Houlsby et al. (2019); Li & Liang (2021); Lester et al. (2021) and model compression
042   methods Liu et al. (2017); Jiao et al. (2019); Touvron et al. (2021).
043   Beyond conventional strategies, a new concept of learngene Wang et al. (2022) offers an alternative
044   parameter initialization scheme. Inspired by the intergenerational transfer of information in biology, a
045   learngene philosophically acts as a functional unit that encapsulates shareable information from a pretrained
046   model (referred to as the ancestry model in learngene-based methods). Instead of replicating the entire
047   pretrained model to obtain a parameter initialization, downstream models (termed descendant models)
048   inherit the learngene, that is, initialized by learngene in some way. Thus the Learngene framework is
049   expected to confer many advantages, such as efficient knowledge transfer and adaptability to descendant
050
      models of flexible scales, however, the exploration for its central challenges in the representation and
      extraction of learngene remains in its early stages.
051
052   The initial learngene work Wang et al. (2022) posited that the memorization of shareable information
053   can be localized, that is, certain neurons exhibit greater generalizability. Consequently, the approach aims
      to identify these neurons and directly extract them, along with their trained parameters, to form a learngene.

079   By combining the learngene with an arbitrary number of randomly initialized neurons, the descendant
080   model is effectively initialized. More advanced efforts Shi et al. (2024); Xia et al. (2024b;a), focused on
081   the Transformer architecture, seeking to identify a single module that can approximate a linear mapping
082   to all modules in the ancestry network, thereby serving as a learngene. This kind of approach enables
083   a more compact representation of information across the entire network while ensuring that the process
084   of flexibly initializing the descendant model does not dilute the efficacy of the learngene.
085   However, current methods heuristically translate shareable information into shareable parameters,
086   thus learngene only providing parameter initialization information to descendant models with specific
087   architectures — typically those identical to the ancestry model — which leads to downstream models
088   that remain insufficiently flexible. Recently, Graph HyperNetworks (GHNs) Zhang et al. (2018); Knyazev
089   et al. (2021) have demonstrated the ability of encoding the network architectures. A GHN is composed of
090   an encoder that learn the compact latent representations from different network architectures and a decoder
091   that then uses those latent representations to reconstruct the networks. Once trained, the hypernetwork’s
092   weights encode the network architectures, which to some extent resonates with the need for learngene
093
      to integrate structural information, but its applicability in the Learngene framework is not straightforward.
      More importantly, it would be preferable if the process of using the learngene to initialize a descendant
094
      model not only supports variable architectures with scaling but also customizes the parameters based
095
      on the specific downstream task it targets.
096
097   In this paper, we explore a GHN-based learngene method to encode shareable information to predict
098   initial parameters for descendant models across flexible scales. Our approach enables the learngene to
099
      dynamically adjust its parameter initialization in response to the requirements of descendant models, thereby
      significantly enhancing the scalability of existing learngene methods. By leveraging a hypernetwork, we
100
      achieve an end-to-end mechanism for the representation and transfer of shareable knowledge, seamlessly
101
      integrating structural information into the parameter initialization process. Taking one step beyond existing
102   learngene methods, we introduce a fine-tuning procedure that imbues the learngene with task-specific
103   awareness, allowing it to modulate the expression of shared knowledge tailored to targeted downstream
104   tasks. Crucially, our approach is the first to explore dual customization of descendant models based on
105   both scale and task characteristics, yielding a significantly improved quality-cost trade-off for information
106   sharing. We systematically investigate the effectiveness of TAL. Extensive experiments show the
107   superiority of TAL. For example, descendant models initialized with TAL outperform those initialized
      using LoGAH Zhou et al. (2024) by an average of 24.39% across Decathlon datasets Rebuffi et al. (2017).

      3     METHOD
163
164
      Fig. 2 illustrates the overall pipeline of Task-Aware Learngene (TAL). First, we train the TAL model on
165
      a large dataset under the guidance of an ancestry model to transfer knowledge. Then, we tune the trained
166   TAL model on multi-task datasets to effectively filter and convey the task-specific knowledge from the
167   ancestry model to descendant models. Finally, the trained TAL model can predicting descendant model
168   parameters for various tasks, even unseen ones and supports model customization at different scales. Next,
169   we introduce the problem definition for TAL.
170
171   3.1   PROBLEM DEFINITION
172
173   We are committed to addressing the challenges that large-scale foundation models face when adapting
174   to downstream tasks. First, fine-tuning typically necessitates maintaining the entire model’s parameters
175   and updating them for a specialized task, which usually comes at a significant cost. Additionally, in
176   resource-constrained environments, the large memory footprint of the foundation model can hinder its
177
      deployment and using large models for small-scale tasks may result in unnecessary computational burdens
      and resource wastage.
178
179   TAL seeks to inherit knowledge from ancestry models, while selectively expressing in specific tasks. This
180   enables descendant models initialized with TAL to adapt to various tasks in resource-limited environments.
181   These descendant models not only inherit task-specific and shared knowledge from the large model but
182   also have the flexibility to adjust their size according to task requirements, thereby better accommodating
183
      diverse task scenarios.
184
185   3.2   TASK-AWARE LEARNGENE
186

187

188

189   task-specific knowledge based on different tasks. Specifically, learngene receives both model configuration

191   out task-specific knowledge which previously inherited from the ancestry model and inject it into model
192   computational graphs, thereby producing task-specific computational graphs.

      5     RELATED WORK
474
475   5.1   PARAMETER PREDICTION
476
477
      Model parameter prediction is usually achieved through hypernetworks. A lot of research is devoted to
      extending the parameter prediction capabilities of hypernetworks to make them applicable to unseen model
478
      architectures or datasets. Among many methods, Graph HyperNetworks (GHN) Zhang et al. (2018);
479
      Knyazev et al. (2021) have garnered considerable attention due to their outstanding performance and high
480   flexibility. GHN-2 Knyazev et al. (2021) and GHN-3 Knyazev et al. (2023) further improved the parameter
481   prediction capabilities of GHN by improving the learning process of the model computation graph. The
482   latest LoGAH method Zhou et al. (2024) introduces low-rank approximation (LoRA) technology, allowing
483   GHN to predict the parameters of larger models using smaller hypernetworks. This progress has greatly
484   improved the efficiency and ability of GHN in handling large-scale model parameter prediction tasks.
485   Our task-aware learngene (TAL) incorporates modules to process task information, enabling a single TAL
      model to customize models of varying scales for different tasks.


                                                                9
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


486
      5.2   LEARNGENE
487
488   The Learngene method Wang et al. (2022), inspired by biological gene inheritance, focuses on extracting
489   compact components, known as Learngene, from large-scale pretrained models (referred to as ancestry
490   models) and using them to initialize descendant models. Existing methods such as Vanilla-LG Wang et al.
491   (2022), TLEG Xia et al. (2024b), Learngene Pool Shi et al. (2024) and SWS Xia et al. (2024a) employ
492   different strategies to select and expand Learngene to build descendant models. In the Vanilla-LG method,
493   the key layers of the ancestry model are screened and extracted as Learngene and are spliced together
494
      with randomly initialized layers to construct descendant models. The TLEG method employs a linear
      expansion of the layers of Learngene to initialize different descendant models. The Learngene Pool method
495
      refines a large-scale pretrained model into multiple small models, regarding the layers of these small
496
      models as Learngene instances, which are then spliced together to construct different descendant models.
497   In task-aware learngene (TAL), learngene is no longer a sub block of the ancestry model, the encoder part
498   of TAL model is regarded as learngene. Model generation becomes a process of encoding and decoding,
499   with the ancestry model and multi-task common knowledge injected through the learngene. TAL model
500   can handle both model and task information, initializing models of flexible scales for different tasks.
501
502   5.3   MULTI-TASK LEARNING
503
504   The goal of multitask learning is to train a model to perform well on multiple tasks simultaneously. It needs
505   to solve multiple challenges Arivazhagan et al. (2019), such as catastrophic forgetting and deal with the
506   disproportionate task sizes, resulting in overfitting in low-resource tasks and underfitting in high-resource
507   tasks. An efficient multi-task learning method was proposed Mahabadi et al. (2021), using hypernetworks
508
      to achieve a common adapter between tasks. In existing work on model parameter prediction, the focus
      is often limited to predicting model parameters of varying sizes for a single task, without attempting to ini-
509
      tialize models with different parameters for multiple tasks. Our work represents a significant advancement
510
      in the field, as it enables the prediction of parameters for different tasks and models of varying sizes.
511
512
513   6     CONCLUSION
514
515   In this paper, we propose a novel method based on graph hypernetworks called Task-Aware Learngene that
516   predicts model parameters conditioned on desired model scales and task-specific characteristics. Experi-
517   mental results on various datasets demonstrated the effectiveness of TAL’s ability to predict parameters. Un-
518   trained descendant models initialized using TAL achieved significant improvements across various datasets
519
      compared to the previous LoGAH initialization methods. Remarkably, the accuracy of these untrained
      descendant models even surpassed the performance of models trained using other initialization methods.

643
644   The Visual Domain Decathlon Challenge tests the ability of visual recognition algorithms to handle images
645   from different visual domains. It includes 10 datasets in total:
646
647         1. ImageNet-1K (IN-1K) is the largest dataset in the Decathlon Challenge, containing 1,000
               categories and 1.2 million images.

      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025

             2. CIFAR-100 (C100) contains 60,000 32 × 32 color images for 100 object categories.
649
650
             3. Aircraft (Airc.) contains 100 images for each of 100 different aircraft model variants, such as
                the Boeing 737-400 and the Airbus A310.
651
652          4. Daimler Pedestrian Classification (DPed) consists of 50,000 grayscale pedestrian and
653             non-pedestrian images, cropped and resized to 18 × 36 pixels.
654          5. Describable Textures (DTD) is a texture database consisting of 5,640 images, organized into
655             47 categories such as bubbly, cracked andmarbled.
656          6. German Traffic Signs (GTSRB) contains cropped images for 43 common traffic sign categories
657             in different image resolutions.
658          7. Omniglot (OGlt) consists of 1,623 different handwritten characters from 50 unique alphabets.

             8. SVHN is a real-world digit recognition dataset with around 70,000 32 × 32 images.
