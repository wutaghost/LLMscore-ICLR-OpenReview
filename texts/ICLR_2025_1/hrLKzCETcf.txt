009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011
012            Training neural networks which are robust to adversarial attacks remains an im-
013            portant problem in deep learning, especially as heavily overparameterized models
014
               are adopted in safety-critical settings. Drawing from recent work which refor-
               mulates the training problems for two-layer ReLU and polynomial activation net-
015
               works as convex programs, we devise a convex semidefinite program (SDP) for
016
               adversarial training of two-layer polynomial activation networks and prove that
017            the convex SDP achieves the same globally optimal solution as its nonconvex
018            counterpart. The convex adversarial SDP is observed to improve robust test ac-
019            curacy against ℓ∞ attacks relative to the original convex training formulation on
020            multiple datasets. Additionally, we present scalable implementations of adver-
021            sarial training for two-layer polynomial and ReLU networks which are compat-
022            ible with standard machine learning libraries and GPU acceleration. Leveraging
023            these implementations, we retrain the final two fully connected layers of a Pre-
024            Activation ResNet-18 model on the CIFAR-10 dataset with both polynomial and
025
               ReLU activations. The two ‘robustified’ models achieve significantly higher ro-
               bust test accuracies against ℓ∞ attacks than a Pre-Activation ResNet-18 model
026
               trained with sharpness-aware minimization, demonstrating the practical utility of
027
               convex adversarial training on large-scale problems.

032   1   I NTRODUCTION
033
034
      While neural networks have demonstrated great success in many application areas, they are prone
035
      to learning unstable outputs. In particular, adversarial attacks can easily deceive neural networks
036   (Goodfellow et al., 2014). The problem of adversarial robustness has motivated a number of ap-
037   proaches (Foret et al., 2020; Xu et al., 2023) which can incur significant computational overhead
038   and often require training a model from scratch for optimal results.
039
      A related problem is the lack of interpretability and optimality guarantees in neural network training.
040
      Towards understanding these issues, recent works have developed convex reformulations of certain
041
      neural network architectures (Pilanci & Ergen, 2020; Bartan & Pilanci, 2023; 2021). These convex
042   programs offer insight into how neural networks learn (Mishkin & Pilanci, 2023; Lacotte & Pilanci,
043   2020), while also providing guarantees for both the global optimality of weights and computational
044   complexity of training.
045
      We emphasize that convexity of the loss landscape is useful beyond guaranteeing local optima are
046
      global. Indeed, a major pain-point of machine learning is that parameters extrinsic to the model (e.g.
047
      the learning rate, momentum or batch size) significantly impact performance. Convex reformula-
048   tions are optimizer-agnostic, so off-the-shelf solvers (Diamond & Boyd, 2016; Agrawal et al., 2018)
049   will reliably converge to the global optimum without hyperparameter tuning. Given that convexly
050   trained neural networks enjoy greater interpretability and reproducibility, there is motivation to study
051   them in the context of adversarial robustness. For example, Bai et al. (2022) derives a convex ad-
052   versarial training problem for two-layer ReLU networks by modifying the original convex training
053   problem introduced by Pilanci & Ergen (2020). However, to the best of our knowledge, there is no
      convex reformulation of adversarial training for polynomial activation networks.

      1.1    C ONTRIBUTIONS
055
056   We contribute (1) a convex reformulation for adversarial training of polynomial activation networks
057   based on the main result of Bartan & Pilanci (2021), (2) a proof that our reformulation achieves the
058   same optimal value as the nonconvex adversarial training formulation, (3) an exact solver of the con-
059   vex SDP for small-scale problems using the open-source Python library CVXPY (Diamond & Boyd,
060   2016), and (4) PyTorch implementations of adversarial training for both polynomial and ReLU ac-
061   tivation networks. While we only present convex adversarial training programs for two-layer net-
062
      works, we demonstrate that robust classification accuracy of deep image classification models (e.g.
      a Pre-Activation ResNet model from He et al. (2016)), can be greatly improved by retraining the
063
      final two layers with the developed convex adversarial training programs, even outperforming the
064
      same architecture trained with sharpness-aware minimization.

      These results corroborate the observations in Bartan & Pilanci (2021); Rodrigues & Givigi (2022);
480   Bartan & Pilanci (2023) that polynomial activation networks excel when data is scarce. While
481   the optimization problem in (11) is costly to solve, it is both computationally feasible and highly
482   performant in data-constrained applications. For applications where data is more abundant, the
483   ReLU formulation of (15) is cheaper and likely to provide better results. This is due to the difference
484   in expressive power between polynomial and ReLU activation networks. Specifically, the high bias
485   of polynomial activation networks prevents overfitting when little data is available. On the other
      hand, ReLU activation networks exhibit low bias and high variance, making it difficult to train a

490   5   C ONCLUSION
491
492   Our results demonstrate that the convex adversarial training program for two-layer polynomial acti-
493   vation networks is performant on small-scale classification datasets. Further, when the input dimen-
494
      sion is large (on the order of 102 or 103 ), the robust polynomial activation network still improves
      robust test accuracy over sharpness-aware minimization when trained on a small random sample of
495
      the data. Convex adversarial training of two-layer ReLU networks is feasible with large amounts
496
      of data, yielding significant improvements in robustness to adversarial attacks over sharpness-aware
497   minimization. However, we find that when training data is constrained, ReLU networks are less
498   effective.
499
500
      Adversarial training methods such as projected gradient descent (Madry et al., 2019) involve com-
      puting multiple descent steps for each training batch, which drastically increases the computational
501
      overhead of training (Schwinn et al., 2020). The convex programs (11) and (15) analytically pe-
502
      nalize the worst-case value such descent methods approximate. While convex adversarial programs
503   are currently limited by neural network depth, they can still offer significant performance increases
504   with less computational overhead than iterative descent methods.
505
506
      Future research directions could include layer-wise convex adversarial training for deep neural net-
      works, derivations of convex adversarial programs for deeper architectures, or extensions of these
507
      techniques for regression tasks.
