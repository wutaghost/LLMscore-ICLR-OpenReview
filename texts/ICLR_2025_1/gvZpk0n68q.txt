009                                                 A BSTRACT
010
011            Autoregressive-based attention methods have made a significant advance in scene
012            text recognition. However, the inference speed of these methods is limited due
013            to their iterative decoding scheme. In contrast, the non-autoregressive methods
014            adopt the parallel decoding paradigm, making them much faster than the autore-
015            gressive decoder. The dilemma is that, though the speed is increased, the non-
016            autoregressive methods are based on the character-wise independent assumption,
017            making them perform much worse than the autoregressive methods. In this paper,
018            we propose a simple non-autoregressive transformer-based text recognizer named
019
               NAText , by proposing a progressive learning approach to force the network to
               focus on hard samples and learn the relationship between characters. Further-
020
               more, we redesign the query composition by introducing positional encoding of
021
               the character center. And it has more clear physical meanings than the conven-
022            tional one. Experiments show that our NAText helps to better utilize the positional
023            information for 2D feature aggregation. With all these techniques, the NAText has
024            achieved competitive performance to the state-of-the-art methods. The code will
025            be released.

028   1    I NTRODUCTION
029
030
      Reading and processing text from natural scenes has a lot of applications in reality, such as read-
031   ing road signs, billboards, product labels, logos, etc. Due to its high-demanding characteristics,
032   scene text recognition has attracted a lot of researchers and has been studied for years. Recently,
033   autoregressive methods have achieved great success in scene text recognitionYue et al. (2020)Li
034   et al. (2019)Zhong et al. (2022)Lee et al. (2020b). Structurally, they usually consist of an encoder
035   to extract image features and an autoregressive decoder to transcribe the encoded features into text
036   sequence. By the attention mechanism and autoregressive decoding style, the autoregressive models
037   can extract robust and discriminative features for scene text.
038   Although the autoregressive models have many advantages in recognition accuracy, the employment
039   of the iterative decoding style results in extremely low efficiency, especially for long text. In contrast,
040   the non-autoregressive models adopt a parallel decoding paradigm. They share similar decoder
041   structure with their autoregressive counterparts but run much faster. As there is no free lunch, while
042   increasing the speed, the performance suffers greatly. For example, in machine translation, the naive
043   non-autoregressive model performs 4% lower than autoregressive modelsGu et al. (2018). In scene
044   text recognition, we notice that in some recent workQiao et al. (2021)Bautista & Atienza (2022)
045
      the non-autoregressive recognizers perform about 2% lower than autoregressive models. This is
      consistent with our experimental findings that the non-autoregressive model performs 1.7% lower in
046
      regular text and 3.4% lower in irregular text. For scene text recognition, such performance drop is
047
      considerable. Despite the non-satisfactory performance, the huge advantage in decoding speed is too
048   attracting that some of the most recent workYu et al. (2020a)Fang et al. (2021a)Qiao et al. (2021)
049   on scene text recognition still attempts to adopt such parallel decoding scheme. To remedy the
050   performance degeneration, they either introduce large language modelsFang et al. (2021a) to correct
051   the error prediction in a post-process manner or design a heavy predictionQiao et al. (2021) pipeline.
052   These methods are all designed to be very complex and require considerable computational burden.
053   In a sense, they do not fundamentally solve the problem of why non-autoregressive models get
      inferior performance. Therefore, in this case, we try to answer the question: Is it possible to design

070   In this paper, we propose NAText as a solution to the above question. NAText is short for Non-
071   Autoregressive scene Text recognizer. It uses a simple encoder-decoder structure without extra
072   modules and extra post-process. We start by digging into the inferior performance and find that
073   the harder situations usually suffer more significant drop, e.g., the irregular text(Table-6 and the

075   three techniques. First, we argue that the independent assumption adopted by the non-autoregressive
076
      model is the main reason to blame. For hard cases, the character-wise inter-dependency provides
      rich information for prediction. We drop the independent assumption entirely and design incremen-
077
      tal learning to enforce mutual constraints on character predictions. Specifically, during training, we
078
      sample some characters and replace them with their ground truth token embeddings, and force the
079   remaining characters to be learned under this condition. In this way, the network will gradually
080   capture the character-wise relationships. Second, we design progressive sampling to force the train-
081   ing to focus on hard characters. During sampling, the confident predictions are more likely to be
082   replaced, leaving the hard characters to be learned. Together with the first technique, we name this
083   learning scheme the progressive sampled learning. Third, to better capture each character’s visual
084   information, we follow the recently proposed DAB-DETRLiu et al. (2022) to adopt a re-designed
085   decoder structure in which the character center explicitly models the positional information. It uni-
086   fies the physical meaning of the positional encoding from the image features and query embeddings.
087
      This is in contrast to the inconsistency of the positional encoding of query and encoded features of
      traditional decoders.
088
089   We experiment on six popular scene text recognition benchmarks to verify the effectiveness of NA-
090   Text. Detailed exploration into each part is also conducted. In summary, this paper’s contributions
091   mainly include: 1) We propose NAText as a simple and powerful non-autoregressive scene text rec-
092   ognizer. It is both fast and strong compared to most recent work. 2) We research deep into the reason
093
      behind the inferior performance of non-autoregressive decoding and propose progressive sampled
      learning to overcome it. 3) We re-design the decoder structure to utilize the positional information
094
      that leads to better visual perception.

098
099   Based on the topic of our method, we roughly divide the current methods into autoregressive and
100   non-autoregressive methods.
101
      Autoregressive Text Recognition. Autoregressive methods can be grouped into 1D-attention based
102   and 2D-attention based. Earlier methods usually encode the image features to 1D feature sequence
103   and use 1D attention in the decoding period. For example, the R2 AMLee & Osindero (2016) design
104   an autoregressive CNN that can capture broader features as the feature extractor and a 1D-attention-
105   based decoder to transcribe the sequence. FANCheng et al. (2017) employs a focusing attention
106   mechanism to automatically draw back the attention drift. Fang et al. (2018) proposes a fully CNN-
107   based network to extract visual and language features separately. However, these methods usually
      lack the ability to process irregular text(e.g., curved, rotated). To this end, recent methodsLee et al.

      (2020b)Fang et al. (2021a)Bautista & Atienza (2022)Qiao et al. (2021) of scene text recognition
109   usually encode the image into 2D features and adopt the 2D attention in the decoder. With the help
110   of 2D attention, they consistently show strong performance on irregular text recognition. In this
111   paper, we also choose the 2D attention-based transformer to build our baseline method. We mainly
112   focus on the design of the decoder query and show that by re-designing the query, the simple and
113   concise structure can also lead to powerful performance.
114
      Non-Autoregressive Text Recognition. Non-autoregressive methods predict the target sequence
115   at a single iteration or constant time independent of the sequence length. They can be categorized
116   into three groups: the CTC-Based methods, the segmentation-based methods, and the attention-
117   based. The attention-based non-autoregressive methods have been widely applied in machine trans-
118   lationGhazvininejad et al. (2019)Gu et al. (2018)Wang et al. (2019)Qian et al. (2021), auto speech
119   recognitionTian et al. (2020)Chi et al. (2021)Chan et al. (2020) and capture generationGuo et al.
120   (2021). In comparison, there is less workQiao et al. (2021)Fang et al. (2021a)Yu et al. (2020a)
121   for the research of non-autoregressive model on scene text recognition. Recent methodsFang et al.
122   (2021a)Yu et al. (2020a) on scene text recognition that is relative to the non-autoregressive model
123
      mainly pay attention to the employment of language models to assist the text recognition. They
      usually design a complex multi-model system to get a high-performance text recognizer, but the
124
      efficiency of the model is often overlooked. In contrast, our work is focused on the nature of the
125
      non-autoregressive model itself. This work aims to explore how we can design a simple and power-
126   ful non-autoregressive model that keeps the merit of high efficiency and high performance.
127
128   Masking Technique. The masking technique has been widely applied to the pre-training of trans-
129
      formersDevlin et al. (2018)Joshi et al. (2020)Song et al. (2019)Lewis et al. (2019)Song et al. (2020).
      Different from these works, the masked tokens in NAText are replaced with their ground truth em-
130
      beddings. They are ignored in loss calculation. For the masking technique, the most relevant works
131
      are Mask-PredictGhazvininejad et al. (2019) and GLMQian et al. (2021) proposed for machine
132   translation. They both randomly mask tokens to replace and the remaining tokens are predicted
133   under such condition. However, the random sampling that is suitable for machine translation has
134   little effect on scene text recognition. The reason is that the task of scene text recognition has to
135   deal with lots of noised inputs, e.g., blurry, occluded, and incomplete while the inputs of machine
136   translation are clean. Based on the task characteristics, we follow the idea of hard sample mining
137   and propose progressive sampling to feed more informative samples for training, which is proved
138   crucial for scene text recognition. Besides, they need multiple decoding times, either for training
139   or testing. While we design a two-stage decoding scheme to avoid repetitive decoding. In all, the
140
      masking technique in NAText is specially designed for scene text recognition. It is both concise and
      effective.

147
      The structure of NAText is depicted in Figure 1. The NAText adopts the transformer based encoder-
      decoder structure. Given an input image, the encoder will extract the image features and generates
148
      the coarse sequence prediction. Then the predicted sequence is fed to the decoder to generate the
149
      final refined result. Along with the sequence output, the character coordinate will be predicted by
150   the regression head.
151
152   NAText mainly optimizes the decoder structure. Compared to conventional text recognizers, we
153
      highlight two differences in structure. The first is the parallel decoding style. Parallel decoding does
      not need much modification to the decoder structure. It only needs to discard the masking operation
154
      used to guarantee uni-directional self-attention. The second is that we introduce the concept of
155
      location query into the decoder structure. It has the exact physical meaning and makes the decoding
156   process easier to interpret while also performing better.
157

      et al. (2022) to introduce the positional query(embedding) into the decoding process. For clarity, we

257   Progressive sampling. Following the designed factorization in Eq-7, we find that the naive random
258   sampling even leads to worse performance. The reason is twofold. First, as most characters are
259   easy samples, the training hardly focuses on the informative samples. Such scheme is inefficient
260
      and leads to low performance. Second, the model is trained and tested under different conditions.
      During training, the model is always encouraged to predict with the help of extra knowledge while
261
      during testing, there is not. In other words, the model is tested in a more difficult condition than
262
      training. Therefore, we design progressive sampling scheme in which the characters are sampled
263
      based on their predicted confidence. Specifically, given the predicted sequence Ŷ , the ground truth
264
      sequence Y and the confidence C = {c1 , c2 , ..., cT } corresponding to Ŷ , we first determine the

269   The designed progressive sampling can well solve the above problems. First, the remaining char-

273   in parallel. This is in accord with our expectations for progressive learning, by which the learning

276   3.4   O PTIMIZATION O BJECTIVE
277
278   We further design a two-stage decoding scheme to simplify the progressive sampled learning. The
279   extra bonus is that the first stage decoding will also bring performance improvement.
280   To get the characters’ predicted confidence for progressive sampling, the direct thought is a two-pass
281   decoding manner. In the first pass, the confidence is generated. In the second pass, the progressive
282   sampling is applied. We argue that this way is tedious and unnecessary. In our two-stage decoding
283   scheme, we directly use the encoder network to make a coarse sequence prediction, which serves as
284   the predicted confidence for the progressive sampling. The coarse prediction can also serve as the
285   prior knowledge of the decoding process. The pipeline of the two-stage proceeds as follows, given
286
      the image, features output from the encoder X, and the target sequence Y , we first use a prediction
      head FFN to get the per-pixel classification S = FFN(X). Then we apply the connectionist temporal
287
      classification(CTC) loss as the supervision between the predicted logits S and target Y .

      4.2   DATASETS
351
352   We use two publicly available synthetic datasets, i.e., Mjsynth(MJ)Jaderberg et al. (2014), and Syn-
353   thText(ST)Gupta et al. (2016) as training datasets and test on six standard benchmarks: IIIT 5k-word
354   (IIIT5K) Mishra et al. (2012), CUTE80 (CUTE) Risnumawan et al. (2014), Street View Text (SVT)
355   Wang et al. (2011), SVT-Perspective (SVTP)Phan et al. (2013), ICDAR 2013 (IC13) Karatzas et al.
356   (2013), and ICDAR 2015 (IC15) Karatzas et al. (2015).
357   IIIT5K is a large natural scene dataset collected from Google, containing 5000. CUTE contains
358   288 cropped high-resolution images, many of which are curved or irregular text images. SVT is
359   a Google Street View dataset, which consists of 647 patches for testing. SVTP consists of 639
360   patches, cropped from side view snapshots in Google Street View. In SVTP, many patches encounter

414   Query Design. We experiment with different designs of queries to demonstrate the effectiveness of
415   NAText. The query design mainly influences the cross attention in the decoding process. NAText
416   uses the concatenation of content embedding and positional embedding to form the query embed-
417
      ding. We denote it as CAT(cq , pq ). Note that the positional embedding for NAText has a clear
      physical explanation. It is the encoding of the characters’ 2D coordinate. Such positional embed-
418
      ding has never been adopted in the conventional recognizer. We choose several types of designs
419
      for comparison. (1) cq :The query contains only the content part. The cross attention is obtained
420   by computing the dot product of the projection of query content embedding cq and image features
421   X. (2) pq : the query contains only the positional part. The cross attention is obtained by com-
422   puting the dot product of query positional embedding pq and image positional embedding Xp . (3)
423   ADD(cq , pq ): The query is the summation of the content embedding and the positional embedding.
424   The conventional recognizer usually uses the cq -only for the query. It does not mean that they di-
425   rectly drop the positional information. Rather, the content query will still be added by a sinusoidal
426   encoding that represents the sequential order. The results are shown in Table-4. We can see that
427   only using the positional embedding performs the worst, both in the regular and irregular text. The
428
      conventional style cq -only will be further improved when the positional information is introduced.
      And we find that the concatenation performs better than the add operation. It is consistent with the
429
      conclusion in previous workLiu et al. (2022).

534
535   In this paper, we propose a simple and powerful non-autoregressive text recognizer NAText. It
536   elegantly solves the problem that non-autoregressive model often performs inferior to its counterpart.
537   Specifically, We rectify the basic assumption and design a progressive sampled learning to help non-
538   autoregressive model to perform better. We also introduce positional encoding that has clear physical
539   meaning for better visual perception. Experiments on various datasets verify the effectiveness of our
      method.
