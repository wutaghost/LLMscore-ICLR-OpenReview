009                                                  A BSTRACT
010
011
              Large language models (LLMs) like ChatGPT, Gemini, or LLaMA have been trending re-
012
              cently, demonstrating considerable advancement and generalizability power in countless
013           domains. However, LLMs create an even bigger black box exacerbating opacity, with in-
014           terpretability limited to few approaches. The uncertainty and opacity embedded in LLMs’
015           nature restrict their application in high-stakes domains like financial fraud, phishing, etc.
016           Current approaches mainly rely on traditional textual classification with posterior inter-
017           pretable algorithms, suffering from attackers who may create versatile adversarial samples
018           to break the system’s defense, forcing users to make trade-offs between efficiency and ro-
019           bustness. To address this issue, we propose a novel cascading framework called Genshin
020           (General Shield for Natural Language Processing with Large Language Models), utiliz-
021
              ing LLMs as defensive one-time plug-ins. Unlike most applications of LLMs that try to
              transform text into something new or structural, Genshin uses LLMs to recover text to its
022
              original state. Genshin aims to combine the generalizability of the LLM, the discrimina-
023
              tion of the median model, and the interpretability of the simple model. Our experiments
024           on the task of sentimental analysis and spam detection have shown fatal flaws of the cur-
025           rent median models and exhilarating results on LLMs’ recovery ability, demonstrating that
026           Genshin is both effective and efficient. In our ablation study, we unearth several intriguing
027           observations. Utilizing the LLM defender, a tool derived from the 4th paradigm, we have
028           reproduced BERT’s 15% optimal mask rate results in the 3rd paradigm of NLP. Addition-
029           ally, when employing the LLM as a potential adversarial tool, attackers are capable of
030           executing effective attacks that are nearly semantically lossless. We conduct detailed case
031           analyses using the SHAP interpreter, which could yield insights for systemic enhance-
032
              ments. Lastly, we provide discussions on the architecture of Genshin, underscoring the
              necessity of each component and outlining the current limitations.

038   Masked language modeling (MLM) proposed in BERT (Devlin et al., 2018), which is inspired by the "Cloze"
039   test, reveals the phenomenon of redundancy in natural languages. Although recently the new paradigm of
040   the GPT series developed by OpenAI has made significant progress than the MLM paradigm, the nature of
041   MLM’s representative mechanism remains valuable.
042   For example, "password", "pa*skey" and "ρassωOrd" can convey the same concept of "a key to someone’s
043   account" to human receivers, but the latter ones are much more difficult for classical pattern-matching ma-
044   chines to identify. This redundancy keeps certain symmetrical invariance in the language space, where a
045   small degree of disturbance on the original text can keep the corresponding meaning of the text intact. The
046   malicious attackers may utilize this to change the sentence’s appearance while keeping it human-readable,

068   Until recently, LLMs have shown salient potential in symmetry-persistent textual transformation and manip-
069   ulation, compared with their weakness in logistical inference. This is understandable due to LLMs’ natural
070
      essence, which generates the most fluent tokens rather than executes solid causal inference. Besides, LMs
      have shown great discrimination abilities with efficiency on downstream tasks. Finally, IMs provide ex-
071
      planations of the mechanisms inside the black box of the corresponding model. We present an exemplary

      demonstration of Genshin in Fig. 1.
073
074   Due to the intrinsic symmetry of LLMs, there is another potential threat. In the future, attackers may also
075   leverage LLMs not as a shield but as a spear. This kind of attack can be entirely invisible to pattern-
076
      matching machines. The race between defenders and attackers leads to infinity, demonstrating the necessity
      of Genshin.
077
078   Our goal is to alleviate the deficiency of current LLMs, by combining the power of median-sized language
079   models (LMs) and interpretable models (IMs). When our framework receives any new textual information,
080   it first calls an LLM to recover it to its original state, if it has been injected with toxic tokens. Then the
081   recovered text is sent to an LM to make accurate predictions. If requested, an IM will be operated to explain
082
      the predictions of the LM, providing solid insights and interpretations.
083   Our main contributions are as follows: (1) A novel framework called Genshin is proposed to provide a
084   general shield for all kinds of adversarial textual attacks. This shield is also capable of becoming a spear in
085   the future; (2) To the best of our knowledge, this is the first attempt to use LLMs as symmetrical recovery
086   one-time plug-ins, aiming to transform the content to its natural status without meaningful information
087   losses; (3) We also leverage LMs and IMs to achieve both efficiency and explainability which current LLMs
088
      lack. We visualize our interpreting results to provide insight and enlightening.

092   Language Models There are 4 paradigm shifts for language models. In the early days, statistical models
093   based on the Markov assumption (A.A. Markov, 1953) dominated the region of NLP, predicting the next state

094
      with the nearest former state (Zhao et al., 2023). The essence of the Markov assumption is compressible
095
      information, where history can be concentrated into constant storage. Second, distributed representations
096   like word2vec (Mikolov et al., 2013a;b) start to mine the hidden features of the textual corpus, introducing
097   the idea of vectorization into NLP territory. Third, pre-trained language models (PTMs) like BERT and GPT-
098   1/2/3 (Radford et al., 2018; 2019; Brown et al., 2020) significantly boost the growth of NLP applications,
099   by separating the training stage and fine-tuning stage. Finally, large language models such as ChatGPT
100   (OpenAI, 2023), LLAMA (Touvron et al., 2023), Claude-3 (Anthropic, 2024), and GPT-4 (Achiam et al.,
101   2023) have emerged with novel abilities that can solve real-world tasks with versatile prompts. LLMs’ zero-
102   shot abilities (Kojima et al., 2022) also pave the way for prompt engineering (Liu et al., 2023), optimizing
103   the deficiency of LLMs. For instance, few-shot prompting (Brown et al., 2020) and instructions help LLMs
104
      form structural output. Retrieval-augmented Generation (RAG) (Lewis et al., 2020) helps LLMs acquire
      knowledge and minimize hallucination. Chain-of-thought (CoT) (Wei et al., 2022) and intelligent agents
105
      (Park et al., 2023; Team, 2023; Wen et al., 2023) help LLMs cultivate rational minds and stabilize the
106
      reasoning process.

      Textual Adversarial Attacks Adversarial attacks make LMs vulnerable by creating different kinds of
      adversarial samples(Wang et al., 2019; Biggio et al., 2013; Szegedy et al., 2013). Textual adversarial attacks
110
      can be operated on 3 levels: char-level, word-level, and sentence-level. All textual attacks can be described
111
      as some versions of insertion, replacement, or deletion. OpenAttack (Zeng et al., 2020) proposes an open-
112   source textual adversarial attack toolkit, implemented with lots of existing attacking methods. There are
113   also studies for adversarial attacks on LLMs (Shayegani et al., 2023; Weng, 2023). The most well-known
114   adversarial attack is the "magic" prompt, instructing the LLM to forget all safety measures and play a user-
115   specified role to produce harmful content.
116
117   Interpretable Machine Learning Interpretable Machine Learning consists of interpretability and explain-
118   ability (Molnar, 2020). Interpretability means a "white box", where the model’s inner mechanism is trans-
119   parent and understandable to users. There are few classical interpretable models such as the Generalized
120   Additive Model, Decision Tree, Naive Bayes, K-NN, etc (Hastie et al., 2009). But when it comes to neural
121   networks, interpretability withers away when the net scales exponentially according to the scaling law (Ka-
122   plan et al., 2020). Explainability aims to provide insight from opaque models, especially neural networks.
123   Model-agnostic methods like LIME (Ribeiro et al., 2016) and SHAP (Lundberg & Lee, 2017) explain clas-
124   sification results by highlighting the importance of different input elements. Instance-based methods explain
125
      results by locating the more relevant training sample with crafted influence functions (Han et al., 2020).
      There are also specialized methods only for interpreting language models (Sun et al., 2021). Attention-
126
      based methods aggregate and visualize the attention score toward token importance. Probing explains each
127
      layer of neural networks by applying different downstream tasks. Explanation generation explains smaller
128   models with an LLM.

133   The textual world is filled with natural or intended noises, where humans might encounter typographical
134   errors or indirectly express their concepts. The LM often fails in these infinite heavy-tailed scenarios causing
135   OOD (Out-of-domain) tokenization problems, leading to an inevitable trade-off between robustness and

139   where the LLM defender first acts as a one-time recovery tool to denoise the text, from risky informa-

160   Attacking Strategies We designed 3 kinds of attacks that might bypass the current system:
161
162          • Char-level disturbance (Char Attacker): we randomly disturb each minimal-sized token (charac-
163
               ters) in the sentence, with a probability of α (disturbance ratio). This is effective due to redundancy
               in natural languages.
164
165          • Word-level disturbance (Word Attacker): this is similar to char-level disturbance, except hap-
166            pening on words as tokens. For simplicity, the disturbance of characters and words is implemented
167            by random replacement in the following experiments.
168          • Similarity-based disturbance (LLM Attacker): we prompt LLMs to disturb some words using
169            their synonyms or similar-shaped tokens. In this way, we can provide safety even if the attackers
170            are implementing their attacks through LLMs as well.
171
172   Prompt Design We design our attacker and defender prompts with 4 components: task description,
173   demonstration, input, and structural output control. The task description part describes the purpose of the
174   task, like "try to recover the following text to its original state". The demonstration part provides examples
175   of detailed situations, like "there could be adding, deleting, swapping or replacing characters/words". The
176   input part is parameterized as "«text»" ready to be replaced by any new instance in real-time. The structural
177   output control part is crucial, making sure the LLM’s output is JSON-parsable, like "output your recovered
178   output text as JSON format".

397   7   D ISCUSSION
398
399
      Enhancing Robustness through Large Language Models The augmentation of our framework, Gen-
400   shin, with Large Language Models (LLMs) is driven by the requirements for improved robustness, which
401   traditional models like BERT fail to offer due to their reliance on tokenization. Textual attacks can easily
402   disrupt these tokenizers, leading to ineffective content moderation. LLMs enhance robustness comprehen-
403   sively, mitigating the impacts of such adversarial attacks. Additionally, incorporating LLMs as a one-time
404   plugin allows the median-sized Language Models (LMs) in our system to focus on maximizing accuracy
405   and performance in content analysis and classification tasks. Let’s ponder this: "What if the attackers also
406   leverage an LLM to create adversarial samples?" In such cases, a system without an LLM shield layer will
407   be defenseless.
408
409   The Necessity of Interpretable Models In Genshin, the integration of Interpretable Models (IMs) ad-
410   dresses the opaqueness of traditional machine learning outputs. Traditional models often struggle to provide
411   insights into the exact location and nature of issues in detected sensitive or problematic text. Our IMs not
412
      only identify such text but also highlight specific segments for further review, significantly aiding content
      moderators. This increases the transparency and trustworthiness of the model’s outputs, leading to more
413
      efficient and precise content moderation.
414
415
      The Role of Median-sized Language Models as an Intermediate Layer In the architecture of Genshin,
416
      LMs serve as an intermediate layer, rather than directly applying LLMs for content review followed by IMs.
417
      This design is primarily driven by computational efficiency and cost-effectiveness. IMs such as LIME and
418   SHAP require thousands of repetitive calls from previous language models. LMs, being tremendously less
419   resource-intensive than LLMs, can be invoked multiple times with minimal cost. As a result, LMs act as an
420   efficient intermediate layer, handling the majority of text classification tasks by pinpointing key information.
421   In contrast, the high computational cost of LLMs necessitates their limited use. So in our framework, LLMs
422   will only be called once per content review cycle.

423
      Limitations of Genshin While our Genshin framework forms a general shield against all attackers in
424
      practice, there are clear limitations beyond its reach. One limitation is the lack of controllability of the
425   LLM attacker, such as creating a large disturbance or a specified disturbance ratio attack. Second, the
426   general LLM defender prompt could be inexperienced for domain-specific tasks like medical records, so
427   including Retrieval-augmented Generation (RAG) into the LLM defender could further enhance the model’s
428   robustness. Third, Genshin can only recover textual information while the world is full of multi-modal
429   information, which can restrict its applications for more general purposes.
430
431
      8   C ONCLUSION AND F UTURE W ORK
432
433   In this work, we propose a novel framework called "Genshin", to make trade-offs between LLMs, LMs, and
434   IMs. Our experiments demonstrate the current flaws of classical models and the effectiveness of the Genshin
435   framework. In our ablation study, we successfully reproduced the optimal 15% mask rate results of BERT,
436   previously established in the 3rd paradigm of NLP. Additionally, our experiments with the LLM as a potential
437   attacking tool reveal that attackers can execute successful strategies nearly semantically information lossless.
438   This dual application highlights the robustness and versatility of the Genshin framework in addressing both
439   defensive and offensive scenarios in language processing. In the case study, we interpret multiple cases
440   providing insight for system improvement. Finally, we have discussed the design of Genshin, the necessity
441
      of each component, and the limitations at present.
442   For future work, we want to explore the controllability of the LLM attacker and more prompting skills. Next,
443   novel interpretable algorithms might provide data insights into how the attacks take effect and a deeper
444   understanding of how the model operates. Genshin is also promising to be applied to Optical Character
445   Recognition (OCR) or Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR) results, recovering mistakes made by preced-
446   ing models. Although we mainly focus on the defense of median-sized models, the safety of LLMs is still
447
      worth exploring. With the growth of multi-modal LLMs, how to recover an edited photo, recording or video
      remains a critical area of research.
