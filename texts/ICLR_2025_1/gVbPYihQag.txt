009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            Recent successes in diffusion probabilistic models have demonstrated their strength
012            in modelling and generating different types of data, paving the way for their
013            application in generative time series forecasting. However, most existing diffusion
014            based approaches rely on sequential models and unimodal latent variables to capture
015            global dependencies and model entire observable data, resulting in difficulties
016            when it comes to highly stochastic time series data. In this paper, we propose a
017            novel Stochastic Diffusion (StochDiff) model that integrates the diffusion process
018
               into time series modelling stage and utilizes the representational power of the
               stochastic latent spaces to capture the variability of the stochastic time series
019
               data. Specifically, the model applies diffusion module at each time step within
020
               the sequential framework and learns a step-wise, data-driven prior for generative
021            diffusion process. These features enable the model to effectively capture complex
022            temporal dynamics and the multi-modal nature of the highly stochastic time series
023            data. Through extensive experiments on real-world datasets, we demonstrate
024            the effectiveness of our proposed model for probabilistic time series forecasting,
025            particularly in scenarios with high stochasticity. Additionally, with a real-world
026            surgical use case, we highlight the modelŌĆÖs potential in a medical application.

029   1   I NTRODUCTION
030
031   The recent advancements of diffusion probabilistic models has demonstrated their superior abilities
032   in capturing complex data distributions and generating high quality samples across different types of
033
      data including image, text, and audio (Ramesh et al., 2021; Kong et al., 2021; Rombach et al., 2022;
      Saharia et al., 2022; Ruiz et al., 2023). This strength of diffusion based models in data modelling
034
      and generation has led to their wide applications in generative time series forecasting where models
035
      extract temporal dynamics from historical data and approximate the distribution of future values based
036   on learned features and auxiliary information.(Rasul et al., 2021; Shen and Kwok, 2023; Alcaraz and
037   Strodthoff, 2023; Fan et al., 2024; Li et al., 2024).
038
039
      Existing diffusion based time series forecasting models predominantly employ sequential architectures
      such as Recurrent Neural Networks (RNN) (Rasul et al., 2021; Fan et al., 2024), Transformers (Li
040
      et al., 2024), or a State Space Model (Alcaraz and Strodthoff, 2023) to first capture temporal dynamics
041
      from historical sub-series and learn the underlying patterns that can be used for predicting future
042   values. Once the temporal features are extracted, diffusion modules are applied to generate future
043   time series based on these features in a post-hoc fashion, with little integration between the temporal
044   modelling and the diffusion process. Although these models have achieved success with various
045   homogeneous time series data where relationships between the historical and future data are mostly
046   consistent, they still face certain challenges: (1) For heterogeneous time series data like clinical
047   patient data, high variability between each individual can introduce extra stochasticity and make it
048   challenging to model the historical data. (2) The latent variable of the diffusion models is commonly
049   sampled from a unimodal distribution, which exhibits limited capability to encode the full temporal
050
      dynamics and uncertainty of real-world time series data.
051   To address these challenges, we propose a novel diffusion based time series forecasting model called
052   Stochastic Diffusion (StochDiff). Unlike existing approaches that apply diffusion process only after
053   time series modelling, StochDiff directly integrates the diffusion process into the modelling stage to
      capture the intrinsic characteristics and multi-modal behaviors of complex data, rather than relying


                                                        1
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


054
      on a post-hoc diffusion process. Specifically, the diffusion module is applied at each time step of
055   sequential framework, enabling the model to effectively learn both temporal dependencies and
056   stochasticity at every step of time series. This step-wise application of diffusion process allows
057   the latent variable to encode the specific distribution of individual values at each time step, making
058   it more effective for the model to learn the individualized, complex patterns in highly stochastic
059   time series data. Additionally, an auxiliary variable is learned to complement the latent variable,
060   forming the prior distribution of conditional generation in reverse diffusion process. This variable,
061   referred to as data-driven prior, is learned from both temporal dynamics extracted by sequential
062   framework and the data at each time step. This prior enables the diffusion module to capture intrinsic
063
      characteristics and the multi-modal behavior of the complex data, which is beneficial for modelling
      highly stochastic time series. Finally, a Gaussian Mixture Model is fitted on sampled future data to
064
      obtain more accurate point-wise forecasting results during the inference stage.
065
066   We conduct extensive experiments on six real-world datasets across various domains. Results
067   show that StochDiff can achieve competitive performance on homogeneous time series data and
068   superior performance on heterogeneous time series data compared to existing state-of-the-art diffusion
069
      based time series forecasting models (Ōł╝ 15% and Ōł╝ 14% improvements in predictive accuracy on
      two clinical datasets). Furthermore, a case study using real-world surgical data further highlights
070
      the modelŌĆÖs effectiveness in handling individual variability and efficiency in multi-step prediction,
071
      demonstrating its potential in a medical application.

084   2    R ELATED W ORKS
085
086   The Diffusion Probabilistic Models (DPM) is firstly proposed by Sohl-Dickstein et al. (2015), where
087
      a forward and reverse diffusion process is used to create a prior distribution and transform it back
      to complex data distribution by leveraging the thermodynamic analogy. Building on DPM, the
088
      Denoising Diffusion Probabilistic Model (DDPM) (Ho et al., 2020) refines these ideas into more
089
      practical and effective generative modelling approach using neural networks. This has paved the way
090   for the widespread research into diffusion models and their applications in generating various types
091   of data such as image, audio, and text (Ramesh et al., 2021; Kong et al., 2021; Rombach et al., 2022;
092   Ramesh et al., 2022; Saharia et al., 2022; Ruiz et al., 2023; Zhang et al., 2023).
093
      The strength of diffusion models in data modelling and generation has led to their applications in
094
      time series forecasting tasks. The very first work is TimeGrad (Rasul et al., 2021) which combines a
095
      diffusion module with RNN to autoregressively generate future time series data. TimeDiff (Shen and
096   Kwok, 2023), on the other hand, applies the non-autoregressive approach and introduces a future
097   mix-up strategy to enhance the time series forecasting. MG-TSD (Fan et al., 2024) applies TimeGrad
098   on different granularity of time series data to improve the model in long-period time series forecasting
099   tasks. Instead of Recurrent networks, Transformer and state space models are also utilized to capture
100   the temporal dynamics of time series data. TMDM (Li et al., 2024) focuses on Transformer based
101   forecasting models, and improves their performance with the diffusion generative module. SSSD
102   (Alcaraz and Strodthoff, 2023) develops structured state space model and combines with diffusion
103   model to improve the accuracy for time series forecasting tasks. Besides time series forecasting,
104
      diffusion models have also been applied to other time series analysis tasks including imputation and
      generation (Tashiro et al., 2021; Kollovieh et al., 2024).
105
106   The existing diffusion based time series forecasting models typically apply the the diffusion process
107   after the entire observed time series is processed by sequential modules, such as RNN in TimeGrad.
      In these models, the sequential modules first extract temporal features from observed time series,


                                                         2
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


108
      then the diffusion modules focus on generating the future time series based on the learned temporal
109   features. In contrast, our proposed model integrates the diffusion process directly into modelling
110   stage by applying diffusion module at each time step of sequential model, improving its ability to
111   model highly stochastic time series data and forecast more accurate and diverse future values.

      This allows the sequential module to extract the intrinsic temporal properties that can be used to
217   predict future dynamics. However, several challenges exist for this strategy: Firstly, when modelling
218   highly stochastic data, such as clinical data which are highly variable between different patients. The
219   model may not be able to learn informative knowledge from historical sub-sequences, leading to
220   failures in forecasting. Additionally, the prior distribution over the latent variable p(xN ) is commonly
221   fixed to be the standard Gaussian distribution N (0, I) in order to simplify the computation and
222   parameter estimation. This unimodal latent variable may introduce inflexibility and inexpressiveness

224   highly stochastic and non-linear real-world time series data.
225   To address these challenges and develop an efficient learning paradigm for highly stochastic time
226   series, we integrate diffusion process into time series modelling stage by applying the diffusion
227   module at each time step of sequential module, enabling the model to effectively learn both
228   temporal dependencies and stochasticity of data at each time step of time series. To enhance the
229   step-wise learning, we build on previous works (Chung et al., 2015; Aksan and Hilliges, 2019) to
230   design a data-driven prior variable at each time step of time series data:

311   4.4     I MPROVED P OINT-W ISE S OLUTION
312
313   Similar to other probabilistic models, diffusion models always generate a data distribution instead of
314   point estimation. This introduces the problem of choosing a single result from the distribution for a
315   downstream application. Existing diffusion models directly use the median values as the final results
316   to represent the central tendency of predictions. However, this strategy might introduce errors given a
317
      complex data distribution such as mixture of Gaussians. To address this issue, inspired by (Hu et al.,
      2022), we propose an additional step to train a Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM) on the outputs and
318
      identify the largest cluster center as the selected point-wise result.

466   Real-world application has become a crucial benchmark to evaluate the practical benefits of machine
467   learning models. While many models achieve state-of-the-art results on various datasets, their

469   bridging this gap for our model by simulating the Cochlear Implant Surgery process and evaluating
470
      the modelŌĆÖs ability to assist with the operation.

472   The raw data, containing 218 channels, can be converted into a univariate signal called the ŌĆśCochlear

534   In this paper, we propose StochDiff, a novel diffusion based forecasting model for stochastic time
535   series data. By applying step-wise modelling and data-driven prior knowledge, StochDiff is better
536   able to model the complex temporal dynamics and uncertainty from highly stochastic time series data.
537   Experimental results on six real-world datasets demonstrate the competitive performance of StochDiff
538   when compared with state-of-the-art diffusion based time series forecasting models. Additionally, a
539   case study on cochlear implant surgery showcase the modelŌĆÖs effectiveness and efficiency in predicting
      highly stochastic patient data, highlighting its potential in a real-world medical application.

732   D       N ETWORK D ETAILS & E XPERIMENT S ETUP
733
734   The proposed StochDiff consists of several components that responsible for various learning tasks.
735   The Recurrent backbone (LSTM) is the basis of time series modelling: it takes time series data xt at
736   time t and its corresponding prior knowledge ztŌłÆ1 as inputs, then extracts the temporal dynamics and
737   stores them inside 128-length hidden units ht . A Fully Connected Network (FCN) with non-linear
738   activation function projects htŌłÆ1 into distributional parameters ┬Ą, ╬┤. Then, another FCN projects
739   these parameters into a 128-length vector zt , representing the prior latent variable. Meanwhile, the
740
      approximate posterior is estimated with similar networks that takes both xt and htŌłÆ1 as the inputs.
      The prior knowledge is learnt based on the principle of variational inference and its implementation
741
      surrogate, VAE. The diffusion module is responsible for the data reconstruction and generation.
742
      The forward diffusion process is mainly involved with mathematical calculations. And during the
743   reverse diffusion process, a data prediction network Attention-Net is designed for multivariate time
744   series modelling. The Attention-Net (as showed in Figure 5) consists of a series of residual blocks
745   adapted from existing works (van den Oord et al., 2016; Kong et al., 2021; Alcaraz and Strodthoff,
746   2023). Each residual block contains two different attention mechanisms to generate original data:
747   Firstly, a self-attention is applied to learn channel correlations for xt . Then, a cross-attention
748   properly combines the outputs of self-attention with the conditional latent zt to integrate the temporal
749   dynamics, uncertainties and multi-modalities into the data prediction process. Finally, a feed forward
750   network projects the outputs of residual blocks into the expected outputs - the predicted data. Through
751
      the backpropagation, the model will optimize the parameters in each part to learn the expected
      components.

            ŌĆó Prior Knowledge Learning: The current prior knowledge at each time step is provided to
844
              diffusion model as an auxiliary condition during the generation process. This design can
845
              be improved by directly integrating this prior knowledge into latent variable and using it as
846           the end/beginning point of diffusion process (gil Lee et al., 2022). On the other hand, the
847           current model projects the learnt hidden units into corresponding prior knowledge using a
848           MLP with non-linear activation function. A more robust architecture may possibly improve
849           the prior knowledge learning comparing to this design.
850
851         ŌĆó Sequential Backbone: The current model uses LSTM as the sequential backbone, which
852           may lead to one limitation: Although the local variance/uncertainty at each step can be
853           efficiently extracted, the long term features still rely on the LSTM to capture. Thus, as
854
              we explained in section 5.3, on long-period homogeneous datasets (e.g. Electricity), our
              model did not show superior performance comparing with baselines, such as MG-TSD or
855
              TMDM, which focus on these homogeneous datasets to develop certain mechanisms to
856
              extract temporal features (e.g. MG-TSD proposed to learn temporal features with multiple
857           granularity). In the future, a combination of different sequential modules can be proposed
858           to learn different features in time series data (e.g., Liu et al. (2024) proposed a network
859           consists of TCN and Transformer to simultaneously learn global and local features).
860
861         ŌĆó Efficiency: The current model apply parallelism and DDIM (Song et al., 2021) sampling
862           strategy during the forecasting stage to enhance the sample speed. This strategy improves
863           the time efficiency at the cost of increased memory requirements. In the future, a lightweight
              model design could be explored to achieve efficiency in both time and memory.
