009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            Federated Learning (FL) often struggles with error accumulation during local
012            training, particularly on heterogeneous data, which hampers overall performance
013            and convergence. While dataset distillation is commonly introduced to FL to
014            enhance efficiency, our work finds that communicating distilled data instead of
015
               models can completely get rid of the error accumulation issue, albeit at the cost of
               exacerbating data heterogeneity across clients. To address the amplified hetero-
016
               geneity due to distilled data, we propose a novel FL algorithm termed FedDual-
017
               Match, which performs dual matching in the way that local distribution matching
018            captures client data distributions while global gradient matching aligns gradients
019            on the server. This dual approach enriches feature representations and enhances
020            convergence stability. It proves effective for FL due to a bounded difference in
021            the testing loss between optimal models trained on the aggregation of either dis-
022            tilled or original data across clients. At the same time, it can converge to within
023            a bounded constant of the optimal model loss. Experiments on controlled hetero-
024            geneous dataset MNIST/CIFAR10 and naturally heterogeneous dataset Digital-
025            Five/Office-Home demonstrate its advantages over the state-of-the-art methods
026
               that communicate either model or distilled data, in terms of accuracy and conver-
               gence. Notably, it maintains accuracy even when data heterogeneity significantly
027
               increases, underscoring its potential for practical applications.

030   1   I NTRODUCTION
031
032   Federated learning (FL) is a distributed paradigm that enables collaborative optimization across
033   devices while preserving data privacy (Bonawitz, 2019). It has been widely adopted in areas of
034   healthcare (Xu et al., 2021), finance (Li et al., 2020), and the Internet of Things (IoT) (Kairouz
035   et al., 2021). Due to the communication cost constraint, federated clients typically run multiple local
036
      training epochs before communicating with the server. However, client heterogeneity can cause
      weight drifts, which can be further accumulated during communication, leading to performance
037
      drop and convergence instability. To mitigate these challenges, various methods have been proposed
038
      for heterogeneous federated learning, such as regularization techniques (Li et al., 2020), weighted
039   aggregation (Wang et al., 2020a), and personalization strategies (T Dinh et al., 2020). Despite these
040   efforts, error accumulation persists. Recently, dataset distillation techniques have been introduced
041   to FL to enhance efficiency (Xiong et al., 2023; Pi et al., 2023), where client data is condensed
042   into smaller yet information-dense subsets with similar training utility. In addition to improving
043   efficiency, in this work, we find that communicating distilled data instead of models can effectively
044   get rid of the error accumulation, as the model can be fully optimized with aggregated distilled
045   data on the server in a way akin to the centralized training. However, an issue that accompanies
046   this change is that the selective extraction and integration of client data features may exacerbate
047
      heterogeneity among distilled datasets compared to the original client data (Huang et al., 2023). To
      delve into this issue and the underlying paradigm shift in communication, one will naturally ask the
048
      following two questions for FL:
049
050          вАҐ How can we fully exploit distilled data and mitigate the amplified heterogeneity?
051          вАҐ Why can distilled data replace model for communication?
052
053   To answer the first question, we propose a novel FL framework, called FedDualMatch, that can
      leverage complementary strengths of gradient and distribution matching to effectively address the

066   ingly, gradient matching, described in Fig. 1 (b), can control the client drift (Zhao et al., 2020) and

      the mean distribution of the distilled data with that of the given data on each client in multiple layer-

071   amplifies the heterogeneity of distilled data between clients, exacerbating the client drift. To rec-
072   tify this issue, we propose global gradient matching (GGM) on the server side, which constrains
073   the global modelвАЩs gradients for consistency between the aggregated distilled dataset and individual
074   clientsвАЩ distilled datasets and thus improves the convergence stability of the global model. Fig. 2 de-
075   scribes how the dual matching strategies are integrated into our FedDualMatch to sufficiently distill
076   data features and meanwhile enhance convergence stability.
077   For the second question, we conduct a theoretical analysis of the communication of distilled datasets
078   in terms of effectiveness and convergence. Specifically, we show that under the assumptions of
079   bounded distributional distance and bounded gradient distance, the testing loss difference between
080   the optimal model trained on aggregated distilled datasets and that trained on all the given datasets
081   in the centralized setting can be bounded by a small positive constant. It means that training on
082   the aggregated distilled dataset closely approximates the ideal centralized training, thus supporting
083   the effectiveness of distilled data communication. At the same time, the global model can converge
084   to the sub-optimality gap of the final model is bounded by a constant primarily determined by the
085
      bounded distributional distance.
086   We provide extensive experiments to evaluate the efficacy and effectiveness of FedDualMatch under
087   controlled or natural heterogeneity. In controlled experiments with artificial heterogeneity, we set up
088   the data distributions of MNIST and CIFAR10 via a Dirichlet distribution. Our experimental studies
089   on them show that it outperforms those existing federated learning methods, based on either distilled
090   data communication or traditional model communication, in terms of convergence speed and model
091
      accuracy. Further, experimental results on naturally heterogeneous datasets, such as Digit-Five and
      Office-Home, demonstrate that it exhibits strong stability and adaptability when handling real-world
092
      data heterogeneity. To summarize, we make the following contributions:
093
094          вАҐ We propose FedDualMatch, a novel federated learning framework that combines gradient
095            and distribution matching for distilled dataset communication. It excels in getting rid of
096            error accumulation while reducing client drift caused by the amplified heterogeneity from
097            distilled data.
098          вАҐ We provide theoretical analysis on the effectiveness and stable convergence of distilled
099            dataset communication.
100
             вАҐ We conduct extensive experiments which show that our proposed algorithm outperforms
101            the state-of-the-art on both controlled and naturally heterogeneous datasets and particularly
102            maintains accuracy on data with increasing heterogeneity.

142   Dataset-distillation-based federated learning: Recent advancements in FL have leveraged dataset
143
      distillation via either gradient/trajectory matching or distribution matching to enhance communi-

153   advancements, the primary use of dataset distillation is to enhance communication efficiency, with-
154
      out exploring its potential to eliminate error accumulation. Additionally, almost all the methods
      perform a single type of matching, suffering inherent limitations of that type. Our work fills these
155
      gaps by combining complementary strengths of gradient and distribution matching to unleash the
156
      full potential of dataset distillation in heterogeneous federated learning.

520    7                     C ONCLUSION
521
522   We propose a federated learning framework, termed FedDualMatch, that leverages distilled data
523   for communication to eliminate the error accumulation caused by data heterogeneity across clients.
524   ItвАЩs characterized by dual matching, i.e., distribution matching on clients and gradient matching on
525   server. Moreover, we introduce layer-wise feature alignment for distribution matching and global
526   model fine-tuning for gradient matching. By design, FedDualMatch excels in facilitating distri-
527
      bution knowledge extraction and convergence stability. We further conduct a theoretical analysis to
      understand the rationale behind the practicality of communicating distilled data in federated learning
528
      in terms of effectiveness, convergence, and privacy. Extensive experiments in both controlled and
529
      real-world heterogeneity settings demonstrate its superior and stable performance, and prominent
530   advantages over the state-of-the-arts on highly heterogeneous data.
531
532   Limitation: The performance of FedDualMatch shows stagnation in low-heterogeneity scenarios,
533
      indicating space for improvement. Additionally, current data distillation techniques require substan-
      tial computing resources, which may be beyond the computational capacity of edge devices.
