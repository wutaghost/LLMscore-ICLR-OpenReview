011                                              A BSTRACT
012
013            Advancements in Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) have signif-
014            icantly improved medical task performance, such as Visual Question Answer-
015
               ing (VQA) and Report Generation (RG). However, the fairness of these models
               across diverse demographic groups remains underexplored, despite its importance
016
               in healthcare. This oversight is partly due to the lack of demographic diversity
017
               in existing medical multimodal datasets, which complicates the evaluation of fair-
018            ness. In response, we propose FMBench, the first benchmark designed to evaluate
019            the fairness of MLLMs performance across diverse demographic attributes. FM-
020            Bench has the following key features: (1): It includes four demographic attributes:
021            race, ethnicity, language, and gender, across two tasks, VQA and RG, under zero-
022            shot settings. (2): Our VQA task is free-form, enhancing real-world applicability
023            and mitigating the biases associated with predefined choices. (3): We utilize both
024            lexical metrics and LLM-based metrics, aligned with clinical evaluations, to as-
025            sess models not only for linguistic accuracy but also from a clinical perspective.
026
               Furthermore, we introduce a new metric, Fairness-Aware Performance (FAP), to
               evaluate how fairly MLLMs perform across various demographic attributes. We
027
               thoroughly evaluate the performance and fairness of eight state-of-the-art open-
028
               source MLLMs, including both general and medical MLLMs, ranging from 7B to
029            26B parameters on the proposed benchmark. We aim for FMBench to assist the
030            research community in refining model evaluation and driving future advancements
031            in the field. All data and code will be released upon acceptance.

034   1   I NTRODUCTION
035
036   Significant progress has been made in Multimodal Large Language Model (MLLM) (Wang et al.,
037   2024; Yao et al., 2024), exemplified by models such as the InternVL series and Mini-CPM (Yao
038   et al., 2024; Chen et al., 2024). These advancements in general MLLMs have also spurred de-
039
      velopments in the medical domain, as seen with LLaVA-Med (Li et al., 2024). Although general
      and medical MLLMs are commonly assessed on vision-language tasks like Visual Question An-
040
      swering (VQA) and report generation (RG), their fairness across diverse demographic groups has
041
      been less explored (Hu et al., 2024), despite its critical importance in clinical applications (Cheng
042   et al., 2024). Previous studies on fairness in the medical field have predominantly focused on clas-
043   sical single-modality tasks and have not sufficiently addressed multimodal tasks (Chen et al., 2023).
044   Given that MLLMs are trained on large-scale and diverse datasets, a pertinent question arises: Do
045   MLLMs perform fairly on medical multimodal tasks?
046
      To the best of our knowledge, there is currently no public benchmark for evaluating fairness compre-
047
      hensively in medical multimodal tasks, which include various demographic attributes. To address
048   this gap, our main contributions are:
049
050         • We introduce FMBench, the first benchmark specifically designed to evaluate the fairness
051           of MLLMs on medical multimodal tasks, including VQA and RG. FMBench comprises a
052           dataset of 30,000 medical VQA pairs and 10,000 medical image-report pairs, each anno-
053           tated with detailed demographic attributes (race, gender, language, and ethnicity) to facili-
              tate a thorough evaluation of MLLM fairness.

             • We propose Fairness-Aware Performance (FAP), a novel metric designed to assess the
055            equitable performance of MLLMs across different demographic groups, filling the gap left
056            by the lack of existing metrics to evaluate MLLM fairness on open-form multimodal tasks.
057
             • We benchmark eight mainstream MLLMs, ranging from 7B to 26B parameters, includ-
058
               ing both general-purpose and medical models. These models are evaluated using tradi-
059            tional lexical metrics, clinician-verified LLM-based metrics, and our proposed FAP metric.
060            Experimental results reveal that traditional lexical metrics are insufficient for open-form
061            multimodal tasks and may even conflict with clinician-verified metrics. Furthermore, all
062            MLLMs exhibit inconsistent performance across different demographic attributes, indicat-

      2     R ELATED W ORK
066
067
      Medical Visual Question Answering. Medical Visual Question Answering (MedVQA) involves
068   answering questions based on medical images and associated queries (Zhang et al., 2023). Recent
069   developments include datasets such as VQA-RAD (Lau et al., 2018), Path-VQA (He et al., 2020),
070   SLAKE (Liu et al., 2021a), and OmniMedVQA (Hu et al., 2024). These datasets, however, lack
071   demographic information, complicating the evaluation of model fairness across different popula-
072   tion groups. Additionally, they predominantly feature closed-form answers, which contrasts with
073   the open-ended responses required in real clinical scenarios (Oh et al., 2024). The absence of de-
074   mographic data and reliance on closed-form questions underscore the need for a dataset capable of
075   assessing real-world performance and fairness in medical VQA tasks.
076   Medical Report Generation. Much of the research in medical report generation (RG) has con-
077   centrated on radiology, particularly chest X-ray report generation (Liu et al., 2021b; Chen et al.,
078   2020; Boecking et al., 2022). These studies typically do not assess fairness across diverse popula-
079   tion groups, thus limiting their generalizability and real-world applicability (Seyyed-Kalantari et al.,
080   2020; Badgeley et al., 2019). This limitation largely stems from the lack of comprehensive demo-
081   graphic data in major RG datasets, which hampers fairness assessments (Huang et al., 2021; Irvin
082
      et al., 2019). Addressing this, our work introduces FMBench, the first benchmark to comprehen-
      sively evaluate fairness in medical report generation tasks.
083
084   Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs). MLLMs have shown significant advancements
085   in vision-language tasks (OpenAI, 2023), with models like LLaVA (Liu et al., 2024), InternVL
086   (Chen et al., 2024), and MiniCPM-V (Yao et al., 2024), including medical-specific versions such as
087   LLaVA-Med (Li et al., 2024). Despite being trained on diverse, web-scale datasets, these models
088
      still encounter issues with unfairness and social biases across different demographic groups (Cheng
      et al., 2024). Given the critical role of fairness in healthcare, where biased predictions can result
089
      in detrimental outcomes, our work presents the first benchmark specifically designed to assess the
090
      fairness of MLLMs in medical multimodal tasks.

      Constructing QA Pairs. To construct QA pairs based on clinical reports, we follow the method
134
      outlined by (Oh et al., 2024), querying an LLM with existing clinical reports to generate QA

137   Ophthalmologist. Please generate 3 concise Question and Answer

139   belong the following three categories and each category only
140   appear one time: 1. Primary Condition or Diagnosis, 2. Testing
141   or Treatment, 3. Medical Condition. The given clinical report is
142   <Clinicial Report>. We illustrate the construction process and show three example QA
143
      pairs in Figure 1 (b).
144   Post-processing. To enhance the quality of the generated open form QA pairs, we instruct Llama3.1-
145   70B-Instruct to perform a self-check of its initial output of these QA pairs in conjunction with the
146   report. Overall, our benchmark includes 10k image-report pairs, and 30k VQA pairs with 3 types,
147   4 different demogrphy attributes. This allowed us to comprehensively assess the fairness of MLLM
148   performance on two mulitmodal tasks, VQA and report generation.

150   3.3      FAIRNESS -AWARE P ERFORMANCE
151
      To evaluate the fairness of Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) across various demo-
152
      graphic groups in Visual Question Answering (VQA) and Report Generation (RG) tasks, traditional
153   metrics such as BLEU and METEOR (Papineni et al., 2002; Banerjee & Lavie, 2005) prove insuffi-
154   cient as they primarily assess linguistic correctness rather than fairness. Moreover, merely averaging
155   performance across different demographic groups can obscure significant disparities. To address
156   this, we introduce the Fairness-Aware Performance (FAP) metric, designed to quantitatively assess
157   the fairness of MLLM performance.
158

159   reflect the effectiveness of MLLMs on specified tasks. In this study, we utilize the GREEN score
160   (Ostmeier et al., 2024) to evaluate each group’s performance. These scores are weighted (Wi )

244   4.2     Z ERO - SHOT E VALUATION
245
      To assess the performance and fairness of MLLMs on VQA and report generation tasks, we
246
      conduct zero-shot evaluations across eight open-source MLLMs. For the VQA task, we utilize
247
      medical images and accompanying questions as instruction inputs to all MLLMs, comparing the
248   generated text against the ground truth answers. For the report generation task, we employ the
249   same textual instruction: ‘You are a professional Ophthalmologist. Please
250   generate the clinical report for the given fundus image. ’, using the
251   medical image as input to the MLLMs to generate clinical reports. These are then compared with the
252   clinical reports from the original sample. We evaluate their performance using lexical metrics and
253   LLM-based metrics for each demographic attribute and also assess their fairness using our proposed
254   Fairness-Aware Performance (FAP) metric. We all know that in clinical applications, the medical
255   report generation task is more difficult and important compared to medical VQA. However, main-
256
      stream medical MLLM research currently focuses predominantly on medical VQA tasks. Through
      our FMBench, we hope to bring new thinking to the community and conduct in-depth research on
257
      such tasks, highlighting the crucial need for advancements in medical report generation.

      6   C ONCLUSION
474
475   In this work, we introduce FMBench, the first benchmark designed to evaluate the fairness of Multi-
476   modal Large Language Models (MLLMs) on medical multimodal tasks. FMBench includes four de-
477
      mographic attributes, encompassing ten groups in total, and features 30,000 image-question-answer
      pairs for VQA evaluation and 10,000 image-report pairs for report generation. It provides a com-
478
      prehensive assessment of MLLM fairness across these tasks. We also identify limitations in cur-
479
      rent metrics for fairly evaluating VQA and report generation and propose the Fairness-Adjusted
480   Performance (FAP) score as a new metric for assessing fairness. Our findings indicate that exist-
481   ing MLLMs demonstrate unstable performance across demographic groups, even when trained on
482   large-scale, diverse datasets. Moreover, their performance on both VQA and report generation tasks
483   is unsatisfactory. Notably, we observe that medical-specific MLLMs generate text with high lexical
484   accuracy but low semantic correctness (as indicated by the GREEN score), while general MLLMs
485   like MiniCPM produce more semantically accurate text but with lower lexical scores. This dis-
      crepancy reveals the shortcomings of current metrics and underscores that current medical MLLMs

      often mimic medical style without truly understanding medical content. We hope that FMBench and
