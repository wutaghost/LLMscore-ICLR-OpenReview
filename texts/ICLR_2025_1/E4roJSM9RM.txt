009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011
               Diffusion transformer (DiT), a rapidly emerging architecture for image genera-
012            tion, has gained much attention. However, despite ongoing efforts to improve
013            its performance, the understanding of DiT remains superficial. In this work, we
014            delve into and investigate a critical conditioning mechanism within DiT, adaLN-
015            Zero, which achieves superior performance compared to adaLN. Our work studies
016            three potential elements driving this performance, including an SE-like structure,
017            zero-initialization, and a “gradual” update order, among which zero-initialization
018            is proved to be the most influential. Building on this insight, we heuristically
019            leverage Gaussian distributions to initialize each condition modulation, termed
020
               adaLN-Gaussian, leading to more stable and effective training. Extensive exper-
               iments following DiT on ImageNet1K demonstrate the effectiveness and gener-
021
               alization of adaLN-Gaussian, e.g., a notable improvement of 2.16% in FID score
022
               over adaLN-Zero.

026   1   I NTRODUCTION
027
028   Diffusion transformer (DiT) (Peebles & Xie, 2023) has recently emerged as a powerful architecture
029   for image synthesis, and has gained vast attention for its superior performance over UNet-based
030   diffusion models (Dhariwal & Nichol, 2021; Rombach et al., 2022). As DiTs continues to drive
031   breakthroughs in image generation, there is a growing interest in pushing its performance boundaries
032   even further. Current efforts could be roughly categorized into two categories: 1) those incorporating
033
      advanced techniques (Chu et al., 2024; Ma et al., 2024b; Lu et al., 2024; Tian et al., 2024; Zhu et al.,
      2024), like VisionLLama (Chu et al., 2024), which introduces language model-based tricks such as
034
      RoPE2D (Su et al., 2024) and SwishGLU (Shazeer, 2020), to boost the performance; and 2) those
035
      leveraging stronger and more informative conditions (Esser et al., 2024; Chen et al., 2023; 2024a;
036   Ma et al., 2024a; Li et al., 2024), such as PixArt-α (Chen et al., 2023) that extends DiTs to the
037   text-to-image realm to enable more exquisite image generation.
038
039
      Despite these advances, our understanding of the mechanisms driving DiT’s performance remains
      superficial. One critical aspect that requires further investigation is adaLN-Zero, an important con-
040
      ditioning mechanism that significantly enhances DiT’s performance compared to the original adaLN
041
      (20.02 vs. 24.13 in FID). Fully understanding the underlying mechanism of adaLN-Zero is essential
042   and may provide deeper insights for further optimizing DiT, especially given the increasing preva-
043   lence of DiT in the field of diffusion generation (Karras et al., 2022; Dhariwal & Nichol, 2021;
044   Karras et al., 2024).
045
      In this work, we uncover the mechanism behind adaLN-Zero’s performance boost, providing key
046
      insights into DiT’s conditioning process. By studying the differences between adaLN-Zero and
047
      adaLN, our analysis studies three elements that collectively contribute to the performance enhance-
048   ment: 1) an Squeeze-and-Excitation-like (SE-like) structure (Hu et al., 2018), 2) zero-initialized
049   value (a well-optimized location in the optimization space), and 3) a “gradual” update order of
050   model weights. The SE-like structure arises from introducing scaling element α and the latter two
051   stem from adaLN-Zero’s zero-initialization strategy for α. By empirical experiments, we find that
052   a good zero-initialized location itself plays a more significant role among the three elements. We
053   reveal that compared to other initialization, zero-initialization enables the weights that derive α to
      morphologically more closely approximate the well-trained distribution which resembles a Gaus-

054
      sian distribution. Interestingly, we find all the weights of condition modulations in DiT’s blocks
055   gradually form Gaussian-like distributions as training progresses.
056
057   Based on these findings, we propose to replace adaLN-Zero by initializing the weights of each
058
      condition modulation with Gaussian distributions, which we call adaLN-Gaussian. To validate the
      effectiveness and generalization of adaLN-Gaussian, we conduct comprehensive experiments fol-
059
      lowing DiT on ImageNet1K (Russakovsky et al., 2015), testing across different training durations,
060
      DiT variants, and DiT-based models. Our contributions can be summarized as follows:
061
062   • We study three key factors that collectively contribute to the superior performance of adaLN-Zero:
063   an SE-like structure, a good zero-initialized value, and a gradual weight update order. Among them,
064
      we find that the a good zero-initialized value plays the most pivotal role.
065   • Based on the distribution variation of condition modulation weights, we heuristically leverage
066   Gaussian distributions to initialize each condition modulation, termed adaLN-Gaussian.
067
      • Extensive experiments following DiT on ImageNet1K across different settings demonstrate
068   adaLN-Gaussian’s effectiveness and generalization, showing a promising pathway for future gen-
069   erative models.

076   Transformer in Diffusion. With the extensively demonstrated scalability and remarkable capa-
077   bilities of transformers (Vaswani et al., 2017; Dosovitskiy et al., 2020), they have recently been
078   introduced into diffusion generation (Chai et al., 2023; Gao et al., 2023; Mo et al., 2023; Feng
079   et al., 2023; 2024; Bao et al., 2023; Fei et al., 2024; Chen et al., 2024b; Levi et al., 2023; Crowson
080   et al., 2024). Gao et al. propose an asymmetric masking diffusion transformer to explicitly enhance
081   contextual relation learning among object semantic parts. DiffiT (Hatamizadeh et al., 2023) intro-
082
      duces hybrid hierarchical vision transformers with a U-shaped encoder and decoder. More recently,
      DiT (Peebles & Xie, 2023) replaces the widely-used UNet with transformers in diffusion generation,
083
      empirically demonstrating excellent performance and promising scalability. Subsequently, more ef-
084
      forts have been devoted to improving diffusion transformers. Following this research line, FiT (Lu
085   et al., 2024) and VisionLLama (Chu et al., 2024) introduce large language model (LLM) techniques,
086   such as RoPE2D (Su et al., 2024) and SwishGLU, to further enhance DiT. SiT (Ma et al., 2024b)
087   proposes a scalable interpolant framework built on the backbone of DiTs. SD-DiT (Zhu et al.,
088   2024) incorporates masking operations into DiT to accelerate model convergence and improve per-
089   formance. Pixart-α and Pixart-σ (Chen et al., 2023; 2024a) extends DiT to text-to-image synthesis
090   and produces high-quality and exquisite images. U-DiT(Tian et al., 2024) argues that the effective-
091   ness of the U-Net inductive bias is meaningful but has been neglected in DiTs, reintroducing the
092   U-shaped architecture to enhance performance. Different from these efforts, our work is motivated
093
      by elevating the understanding of DiT given its great prevalence in the generation realm, and focuses
      primarily on a crucial conditioning mechanism called adaLN-Zero.
094
095   Weight Initialization. In a neural network, weight initialization is a crucial operation as it directly
096   determines the initial position in the optimization space (Narkhede et al., 2022). Typically, good
097   initialization aids model training. Common methods include random initialization with (truncated)
098   normal or uniform distributions. Glorot & Bengio introduced a properly scaled uniform distribution
099
      for initialization, known as “Xavier" initialization, in Jia et al. (2014). However, this strategy is
      not suitable for the ReLU activation function (Nair & Hinton, 2010), as ReLU can map negative
100
      values to zero, thereby altering the entire variance. To address this, He et al. proposed “Kaiming"
101
      initialization, which assumes that half of the neurons are activated while the rest are zero. In the
102   deep learning era, the zero-initialization strategy can be traced back to Goyal et al. (2017), where
103   it was used to accelerate large-scale training potentially via nullifying certain output pathways to
104   implicitly adjust the propagation of backward signals in a supervised learning setting. More re-
105   cently, it has been widely adopted in diffusion generation (Ho et al., 2020; Rombach et al., 2022)
106   to ease optimization. In DiT (Peebles & Xie, 2023), the impact of zero-initialization is particularly
107   notable, leading to significant performance improvement. Motivated by this, we delve deeper into
      the underlying reasons, hoping that our findings will inspire further research.

      To unveil the underlying mechanism, we perform a detailed comparison between adaLN-Zero and
      adaLN. In Fig. 2 we find that adaLN-Zero introduces two additional steps: first, it introduces scaling
127
      element α (as denoted in DiT) for all transformer blocks; second, it zero-initializes corresponding
128
      linear layers to output zero vectors for all α. Given these differences, one may naturally wonder:
129   how do these two steps contribute to the performance gap between adaLN-Zero and adaLN in DiT?
130
131   3.1             D ECOUPLING ADA LN-Z ERO BY E VALUATING S TEP O NE IN I SOLATION
132
133   To answer this question, we decouple adaLN-Zero by introducing only the first step and initial-
134   izing the linear layer’s weights by default. For convenience, we denote this intermediate state as
135   adaLN-Step1 as shown in Fig. 2 (middle). Then we train the three variants following the same train-
136   ing setting in DiT (Peebles & Xie, 2023) on ImageNet1K for 400K iterations using the largest and
137   best-performing model, i.e., DiT-XL/2. Similarly, we measure FID (Heusel et al., 2017) by using
138
      ADM’s TensorFlow evaluation suite (Dhariwal & Nichol, 2021) following DiT and compare the
      performance of adaLN-Step1 with adaLN-Zero and adaLN in Fig. 1. One can see that adaLN-Step1

181   3.2     H OW Z ERO - INITIALIZATION I MPROVES THE P ERFORMANCE
182
183
      For a typical initialization strategy, e.g., kaiming initialization (He et al., 2015), its fundamental role
      is to determine the initial location of the model in the optimization space. Particularly, in the case
184
      of zero-initialization, besides this function, Goyal et al. suggest that it also has an additional role.
185
      Specifically, it can implicitly adjust the model structure by nullifying certain output pathways at the
186   beginning of training, more importantly, causing the forward/backward signals to initially propa-
187   gate through the identity shortcut (He et al., 2016), thereby easing the optimization at the start of
188   training (Goyal et al., 2017). However, is this additional role really responsible for the performance
189   gap between adaLN-Zero and adaLN-Step1? To answer this question, we first examine how this
190   additional role specifically impacts optimization through the lens of gradient update. Afterward,
191   we decouple this impact on gradient update during training to highlight the fundamental role of
192   zero-initialization.
193
194   3.2.1    Z ERO - INITIALIZATION ’ S I MPACT ON G RADIENT U PDATE
195

197
      our gradient derivation. First, we use only one DiT block, easing the computations of complex
      chain rules. Second, we replace the multi-head self-attention and pointwise feedforward modules
198
      within the DiT block with simple linear transformations, respectively. Though this replacement
199
      alters the structure of the DiT block, from the view of backpropagation it does not affect the gradient
200   flow of other modules but itself which is not our emphasis. Therefore this adjustment could be
201   acceptable. Finally, for a linear layer, we omit the bias term in both the forward and backward passes.
202   These alterations significantly simplify our analysis without negatively impacting the conclusions.

529
530   We study three key factors contributing to the performance discrepancy: an SE-like structure, a good
531   zero-initialized value, and a "gradual" update order of model weights. Moreover, our empirical ex-
532
      periments suggest that a good zero-initialized value itself plays a more significant role among these
      factors. Finally, inspired by the observed distribution variations in condition modulation weights,
533
      we propose adaLN-Gaussian which uses Gaussian distributions to initialize condition modulations.
534
      We conduct extensive experiments with DiT on ImageNet1K, demonstrating the effectiveness and
535   generalization of adaLN-Gaussian.
