009                                              A BSTRACT
010
011            Visual prompt-based large vision models exhibit remarkable performance in a
012            range of vision tasks. However, visual prompting large vision models are com-
013            putationally intensive and resource-demanding due to their large parameter sizes
014            and the complexity of processing visual prompts, resulting in inefficiencies in
015            speed and memory usage. To tackle these challenges, we propose the Efficient
016            Painter model, which leverages a novel context-aggregated attention based trident
017            block to alleviate cross-task gaps and reduce memory and computation overhead.
018
               Furthermore, we introduce a cross-blocks feature union module to capture global
               contextual information at different levels and speed up training. This architecture
019
               mitigates training costs and memory requirements during inference. Our model
020
               strikes a balance between speed and memory efficiency, achieving a 19× reduction
021            in floating point operations per second (FLOPs). Moreover, our model is 9×
022            smaller in model size and runs 4.1× and 27× faster during training and inference,
023            respectively. Comprehensive experiments demonstrate that our design effectively
024            processes additional visual prompts and outperforms baseline methods on standard
025            benchmarks like SIDD and LoL in zero-shot settings, improving performance by
026            0.4% and 1.2% respectively.

029   1   I NTRODUCTION
030
031   Visual prompt-based Large Vision Models (LVMs) have recently emerged as a powerful approach for
032   various vision tasks without relying on language instructions. Unlike traditional models like CLIP
033
      Radford et al. (2021) and Flamingo Alayrac et al. (2022) that depend on language guidance, visual
      prompt-based LVMs Wang et al. (2023a;b; 2024) operate solely on continuous visual inputs. These
034
      models reduce quantization errors from discretization and enable effective in-context visual learning
035
      through masked image modeling techniques by aligning the output space to be as continuous as the
036   input images.
037
038   Standard LVMs typically utilize encoder-decoder architectures, which are computationally intensive
039
      and resource-demanding due to their complex designs. This issue is magnified in visual prompt-
      based LVMs, as visual prompt-based LVMs must handle additional visual prompt inputs, increasing
040
      computational complexity and resource consumption. During inference, processing high dimensional
041
      vision prompts leads to higher latency, negatively impacting performance in real-time applications.
042   Conventional methods like quantization and pruning Liu et al. (2021); Mao et al. (2023); Molchanov
043   et al. (2019) aim to reduce computational load but often fail to improve inference time significantly.
044   Recent studies Kao et al. (2022); Mehta & Rastegari (2022b) have identified redundant parameters
045   and frequent memory access operations in multi-head self-attention (MHSA) as bottlenecks, however,
046   they result in reduced accuracy and minimal speed improvement. While some work Pan et al. (2022;
047   2023); Wu et al. (2022) focus on redesigning transformer blocks to enhance performance, they do
048   not simultaneously address memory and computational efficiency or reduce parameter redundancy.
049   Therefore, these methods not only fail to achieve overall efficiency, but the distinctive manner in
050
      which visual prompt-based LVMs incorporate visual tokens into the models also makes them directly
      inapplicable. Applying these methods would impair the model’s in-context visual learning capabilities.
051
      Consequently, such barriers complicate the application of classical architectural optimizations.
052
053   Building upon the issues outlined above, the primary challenge lies in achieving a delicate balance
      between overall model efficiency in computation and memory and the in-context learning (ICL)

096   Efficient ViTs. ViTs Wang et al. (2021a) exhibit powerful capabilities in various vision tasks.
097   However, ViTs are hindered from deployment due to the limited throughput in resource-limited
098   environments. Traditional approaches such as DeiT Touvron et al. (2021), and MobileViT Mehta &
099
      Rastegari (2022a) leverage knowledge distillation or architectural downsize fail to enhance actual
      inference speed or throughput. Conversely, post-training techniques like Token Merging (ToMe)
100
      Bolya et al. (2023) and DiffRate Chen et al. (2023) optimize token usage to reduce the model’s
101
      FLOPs. Despite lowering theoretical computational complexity, they do not improve memory
102   efficiency significantly due to additional parameters introduced in Multi-head Self-attention and Feed-
103   Forward Layers. Recent strategies like EfficientSAM Zhao et al. (2023) aim to balance parameter
104   reduction and computation demands by transferring the capabilities of heavy-weight models to
105   smaller ones using masked image pretraining. However, it incurs significant computational and
106   memory overhead during training, making it intolerable in scenarios with limited resources and in
107   vision tasks spanned across multiple semantic contexts. In addition, these methods focus primarily
      on specific tasks such as SAM, which limits their general applicability.

      LVMs with ICL. ICL Brown et al. (2020) enables models to adapt to new tasks by leveraging
109   contextual information during inference. Recent research has demonstrated that LVMs are particularly
110   effective for ICL due to their self-attention mechanisms, which are adept at modeling long-range
111   dependencies across images and texts. Prominent ICL ViTs, such as Perceiver IO Jaegle et al. (2021)
112   and Flamingo Alayrac et al. (2022), excel in few-shot learning by effectively utilizing contextual
113   examples. Recent innovations, including Visual Prompting Bahng et al. (2022) and PromptGIP
114   Liu et al. (2024), leverage visual prompts for task guidance. However, these methods typically

116   applications. In this paper, we explore the development of a visual prompt-based ViT designed to
117
      improve both time and memory efficiency while achieving ICL capabilities. Our approach aims to
      address existing challenges and enhance the applicability of visual-prompt ICL models in resource-
118
      limited scenarios.

190   4.1    OVERVIEW
191
192   Designing an Efficient LVM capable of ICL from visual prompts across diverse visual tasks presents
193   significant challenges due to the varying context information required for different visual tasks. While
194
      substituting a more efficient backbone seems like a straightforward approach, transferability will
      diminish during training.
195
196   To overcome such deficiencies, our core design is built on three key components: (a) Trident
197   block with CAA; (b) CBFU with context-based order optimization; (c) context-aware multiscale
198   reconstruction loss function. The overall architecture is depicted in Fig. 3.

352   the iterations needed to reach an optimal solution. Our central objective is to perceive the contextual
353
      inductive bias in parallel and embody prior knowledge delivered from a heavy encoder. Meanwhile,
      our model benefits from different network configurations and can be quickly adapted to other low-level
354
      image-processing tasks.

520
521   Large Vision Models with ICL ability that employ pure vision guidance demonstrate great potential
522   for solving cross-task visual tasks. In this paper, we introduce Efficient Painter, an architecture
523   designed to optimize the speed-accuracy trade-off of a popular LVM: Painter. By leveraging an
524   improved trident block with CAA and a new CBFU module, Efficient Painter significantly reduces
525   training GPU hours to one-third and increases the throughput by 27 to 44 times, while maintaining a
526
      comparable precision. In addition, Efficient Painter surpasses some current vision generalist models
      in low-level tasks, providing a viable solution for deploying LVMs on resource-constrained devices.
