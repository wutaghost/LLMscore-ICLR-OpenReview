011                                              A BSTRACT
012
013            In the field of natural language description tasks, one challenge for text-to-image
014            modeling is to generate images that are both of high quality and diversity and
015
               maintain a high degree of semantic consistency with the textual description. Al-
               though significant progress has been made in existing research, there is still po-
016
               tential for improving image quality and diversity. In this study, we propose an
017
               efficient attention-based text-to-image synthesis model based on generative ad-
018            versarial network named Dice-GAN. To improve the diversity of image gener-
019            ation, we design a diversity injection module, which injects noise several times
020            during the image generation process, fuses the noise with the textual information,
021            and incorporates a self-attention mechanism to help the generator maintain global
022            structural consistency while enhancing the diversity of the generated image. To
023            improve the semantic consistency, we designed a consistency enhancement mod-
024            ule, which enhances the semantic consistency of image generation by combining
025            word vectors and a hybrid attention mechanism to achieve dynamic weight ad-
026
               justment for different image regions. We conducted experiments on two widely
               used benchmark datasets, CUB and COCO. Dice-GAN demonstrated significant
027
               superiority in improving the fidelity and diversity of image generation compared
028
               to the existing approaches.

031   1   I NTRODUCTION
032
033   Generating images from textual descriptions in natural language is a challenging cross-modal gen-
034   erative task. In recent years, with the advancement of Generative Adversarial Network (GAN)
035   technology (Goodfellow et al., 2014), the field has been significantly developed (Xu et al., 2018; Li
036   et al., 2019b; Liang et al., 2020; Jiang et al., 2024). Furthermore, the outcomes of studies focus-
037   ing on text-driven image generation have found wide-ranging applications across various domains,
038
      encompassing diverse image synthesis tasks such as image manipulation (Liu et al., 2020; Zhang
      et al., 2020a), facial synthesis (Karras et al., 2018; Zhang et al., 2020b), image restoration (Denton
039
      et al., 2016; Yu et al., 2019a), and image enhancement (Zhang et al., 2017b; 2018a;b). As a result,
040
      this research area has become one of the most active research topics in the past few years.
041
042   Nonetheless, text-to-image generation confronts two principal challenges:
043   First, the text-to-image synthesis process involves a complex one-to-many mapping relationship. As
044   textual descriptions typically cover only a fraction of an image’s features, generating images intro-
045   duces significant uncertainty. This uncertainty naturally leads to varied image outputs. However,
046   existing models (Hinz et al., 2020; Qi et al., 2021) often introduce noise at the network’s incep-
047   tion to exploit this diversity. Unfortunately, the effectiveness of noise diminishes over the course
048   of training, potentially compromising image diversity. A notable consequence of this challenge is
049   the repetitive generation of identical or highly similar images when provided with the same textual
050
      input, as illustrated in Fig. 1. This example highlights instances of limited diversity. The recur-
      ring issue of producing similar images from identical textual prompts underscores the limitations of
051
      current approaches in maintaining diversity throughout image generation.
052
053   Second, maintaining the visual quality and semantic consistency of images remains a major chal-
      lenge when it comes to the generation of complex scenes. Current approaches (Cheng et al., 2020;

      the features of the input sentences. However, most of the attention mechanisms in these approaches
      focus primarily on the spatial dimension and tend to ignore the interrelationships between feature

      channels. Since each feature channel is directly related to the final image modality, neglecting a
080
      reasonable ordering of the importance of individual channels may lead to confusion in the generated
081   image, which in turn leads to a degradation of semantic consistency.
082
083   To address with the above problems, this paper proposes a novel generative adversarial network
084
      (Dice-GAN). To alleviate the problem of degradation of image diversity, we introduce a diversity
      injection (DI) module into the generator. This module injects both noise broadcasts and sentence
085
      vectors into the generator to balance the effect of noise on the visual process. In addition, the
086
      modules incorporate a self-attention mechanism to enhance the structural rationality of the layout of
087   the synthesized image, thus enhancing the diversity of the image generation. To improve the visual
088   quality and semantic consistency of image generation, we design a consistency enhancement (CE)
089   module. This module allows the model to dynamically adjust the weights of different image regions
090   according to the semantic information of the input text by fusing word vectors and hybrid attention
091   mechanisms, thus rationalizing the interplay between primary and secondary visual features, so
092   that the generated images can better achieve semantic consistency while ensuring visual quality.
093   Ultimately, extensive experiments on two datasets show that Dice-GAN can generate images with
094   high diversity, high-quality visual performance, and enhanced semantic consistency aligned with
095
      user-provided textual descriptions.
096   The main contributions of this study can be summarized in the following three aspects:
097
098        1. We design a diversity injection module. This module injects noise and sentence vectors into
099           the generator multiple times during the image generation process and incorporates a self-
100           attention mechanism to balance the effect of noise, thus enhancing the diversity of image
101           generation.
102
           2. We propose a consistency enhancement module. This module orchestrates the intricate
103
              interplay between primary and secondary visual features by dynamically adjusting the
104           weights assigned to different image regions, thus improving semantic consistency while
105           ensuring the visual quality of image generation.
106
107        3. We conducted extensive experimentation on two widely used benchmark datasets. The out-
              comes demonstrate that Dice-GAN surpasses existing approaches in terms of performance.

112   2   R ELATED WORK
113
114
      Within the contemporary realm of image generation, text-to-image generation techniques can be
115
      categorized into two main groups based on the complexity and structural intricacies involved in the
116   generation process: single-stage generation models and multi-stage generation models. The single-
117   stage generation model directly crafts an image from a provided textual description by leveraging
118   a generator and a discriminator. This approach is characterized by its simplicity and directness in
119   image generation. In contrast, the multi-stage generation model adopts a more hierarchical approach
120   to generation. This model comprises several pairs of generators and discriminators, each allocated
121   to a distinct phase in the image creation process. Typically executed sequentially from coarse to
122   intricate details, each stage iteratively refines and enhances the image based on the preceding stage,
123   culminating in the synthesis of a high-fidelity image.
124   In single-stage generative modeling, GAN-INT-CLS (Reed et al., 2016) achieves the first single-
125   stage text-to-image synthesis task for training conditional GANs by combining an image-text match-
126   ing discriminator and text streaming interpolation learning, followed by DCGAN (Radford et al.,
127   2016) which demonstrates its strong potential in unsupervised learning by combining deep convo-
128   lutional networks. An auxiliary classification loss is introduced in TAC-GAN (Dash et al., 2017) to
129   enhance the image quality. HDGAN (Zhang et al., 2018b) innovatively introduces a hierarchically
130
      nested discriminator in the network structure to assist the generator in training and capturing com-
      plex image semantic information. DF-GAN (Tao et al., 2022) employs a target-aware discriminator
131
      to enhance the semantic consistency between text and image. DE-GAN (Jiang et al., 2024) employs
132
      a conditional channel attention module to integrate the textual and visual information to make the
133   final generated image more visually logical and thus better semantically aligned with the given text.
134
135   Among the multistage generative models, StackGAN (Zhang et al., 2017b) and its improved Stack-
136
      GAN++ (Zhang et al., 2018a) use a two-stage generative process to achieve high-quality image syn-
      thesis. StyleGAN (Karras et al., 2019) enhances the model’s ability to control the diversity of the
137
      generated images by introducing a style blending mechanism and Adaptive Instance Normalization
138
      (AdaIN) technique, which enables more flexible production of images with rich variations. Mani-
139   GAN (Li et al., 2020) introduces the affine combination module and detail correction module in the
140   multistage model structure to generate images with higher semantic consistency. LAPGAN (Denton
141   et al., 2015) utilizes cascaded convolutional networks in the framework of the Laplace pyramid to
142   generate images in a coarse-to-fine manner, with each layer using GAN to train a separate generative
143   convolutional network model. RiFeGAN (Cheng et al., 2020) enriches the semantic descriptions by
144   embedding a priori knowledge and combines it with a caption-matching approach to achieve the gen-
145   eration of images with a high degree of matching to the descriptions. DAE-GAN (Ruan et al., 2021)
146   efficiently solves the problem by integrating a global refinement module and an aspect-aware local
147
      refinement module. The problem is that aspect information may be ignored during text-to-image
      synthesis.
148
149   In the field of image processing, the attention mechanism has become a key technique to improve
150   the representation ability of neural networks (Chen et al., 2018; Hu et al., 2018; Wang et al., 2020;
151   Woo et al., 2018). It has been applied in several subfields, including image translation (Ma et al.,
152   2020; Yang et al., 2020; Yang & Qi, 2021; Emami et al., 2020), image caption generation (Shrimal
153
      & Chakraborty, 2021; Yang et al., 2021b) and visual questioning (Gao et al., 2019; Lee et al., 2021;
      Yang et al., 2021a; 2019; Yu et al., 2019b). Especially in text-to-image generation tasks, cross-
154
      modal attention mechanisms play an important role in improving the visual quality of images and
155
      ensuring the semantic consistency between the generated images and the given text descriptions.
156   AttnGAN (Xu et al., 2018) achieves conditional generation at the word level by introducing word-
157   level attention to help the generator produce images with a stronger relevance to the given text.
158   SAGAN (Zhang et al., 2019a) introduces a self-attention generative adversarial network, which
159   generates high-quality images by applying remote dependency modeling to the image generation
160   task in an attention-driven manner. Unlike the above models, ControlGAN (Li et al., 2019a) is
161   able to decouple different visual attributes by introducing a word-level spatial and channel-attention
      mechanism-driven generator and enables the model to focus on generating image subregions corre-

      sponding to specific textual descriptions. DR-GAN (Tan et al., 2022), on the other hand, combines a
163   spatial self-attention mechanism in a semantic disentanglement module to help the generator extract
164   the critical information required for image generation. Despite the performance improvement of
165   these attention mechanism-based models, they still have a large number of hidden states during the

      Compared to the existing approaches, our proposed Dice-GAN utilizes a single-stage model struc-

354   4.1   E XPERIMENTAL SETUP
355
356   Datasets. To assess the efficacy of our proposed Dice-GAN model, we conducted experiments
357
      using two prominent benchmark datasets: the CUB dataset (Wah et al., 2011) and the MS-COCO
      dataset (Lin et al., 2014). The CUB dataset comprises 11,788 images representing 200 distinct bird
358
      species. These images are segregated into training and testing sets, with the training set comprising
359
      8,855 images and the test set containing 2,933 images. Each bird image is accompanied by ten
360   unique textual descriptions. In contrast, the MS-COCO dataset consists of 80,000 training images
361   and 40,000 test images, each paired with five language descriptions.
362
363
      Implementation details. In our experimental setup, we utilized Ubuntu 20.04 as the operating
      system, PyTorch 1.11.0 as the deep learning framework, and Cuda 11.3 for GPU acceleration. For
364
      hardware, we harnessed the computational power of the NVIDIA RTX 4090 graphics card, boasting
365
      24GB of video memory. For the image generation task, we configured the output image resolution
366   to be 256 × 256 pixels to ensure the generated images exhibit adequate clarity. We employed the
367   Adam optimizerr (Kingma & Ba, 2015) to optimize the network parameters, with β1 = 0.0001 and
368   β2 = 0.9 settings. The learning rate for the generator was set to 0.0001, while for the discriminator,
369   it was set to 0.0004.
370
      Evaluation metrics. Building upon prior research (Xu et al., 2018; Zhu et al., 2019), we employ
371
      two established evaluation metrics, namely the Inception Score (IS) (Salimans et al., 2016) and the
372   Fréchet Inception Distance (FID) (Heusel et al., 2017) and CLIPScore (Hessel et al., 2021) to assess
373   the performance of our proposed Dice-GAN model. The IS computation leverages the pre-trained
374   Inception v3 network to gauge the visual quality and diversity of generated images. It evaluates the
375   quality and diversity by analyzing the KL divergence between the conditional and marginal distri-
376   butions of the generated images. A higher IS value signifies that the model’s generated images not
377   only possess high visual quality but also demonstrate diversity in category distribution. Conversely,
      the FID also utilizes the pre-trained Inception v3 network, assessing the authenticity of generated


                                                        7
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


378
      images and their alignment with provided textual descriptions. This evaluation metric calculates
379   the Fréchet distance between the distribution of synthesized images and the distribution of real im-
380   ages in the feature space. Unlike the IS metric, a lower FID value indicates a closer resemblance
381   between the generated and real images in terms of statistical features, reflecting higher fidelity in
382   the generated images and stronger semantic consistency. CLIPScore is a metric for assessing the
383   consistency between image and text descriptions by calculating the cosine similarity between the
384   CLIP embedding vectors of the generated images and text descriptions. The value of CLIPScore
385   ranges from 0 to 1, where 1 indicates perfect consistency between the image and text descriptions,
386   and 0 means no correlation. Therefore, the higher the CLIPScore value, the higher the consistency
387
      between the image and the text description. To fully evaluate the performance of the DI module in
      improving image diversity, we computed the average LPIPS distance between 3K pairs of images,
388
      each generated from the same sentence. Higher LPIPS values indicate greater differences between
389
      images, thus reflecting better diversity.

      4.2     C OMPARISON OF EXPERIMENTAL RESULTS
393
      In this research, to provide an objective assessment of the Dice-GAN model’s performance in text-
394   to-image synthesis, we conduct a comparative analysis against a selection of cutting-edge text-to-
395   image synthesis approaches. These methods include AttnGAN (Xu et al., 2018), DM-GAN (Zhu
396   et al., 2019), DF-GAN (Tao et al., 2022), DE-GAN (Jiang et al., 2024), StackGAN (Zhang et al.,
397   2017a), StackGAN++ (Zhang et al., 2019b) and StyleGAN (Karras et al., 2019).
398
      Furthermore, we extend our comparative study to diffusion models such as CogView (Ding et al.,
399
      2021), DALL-E (Ramesh et al., 2021), and ShiftDDPMs (Zhang et al., 2023), which has also demon-
400   strated notable success in the domain of text-to-image synthesis. Since the training process of
401   CogView and DALL-E is extremely complex and requires a large amount of computing resources,

526   5        C ONCLUSION
527
528
529   This paper introduces Dice-GAN, a novel model tailored for text-to-image synthesis. Operating
530   within a single-stage GAN framework, Dice-GAN directly produces high-resolution images. Central
531
      to its architecture is the Diversity Injection (DI) Module, which merges injection noise and textual
      data within the generator, heightening the stochastic nature of the generated images. Leveraging
532
      a self-attention mechanism, the DI module prioritizes global structural coherence, elevating visual
533
      quality while preserving image diversity. Furthermore, a Consistency Enhancement (CE) Module
534   is proposed to fine-tune channel weights during image generation. By integrating word vectors
535   into the conditional channel attention mechanism, the CE module bolsters features aligned with
536   textual descriptions. Spatial attention mechanisms are also incorporated to capture inter-regional
537   relationships within images, ensuring spatial consistency and coherence during synthesis, thereby
538   enhancing semantic fidelity. Through experiments on standard datasets, Dice-GAN demonstrates
539   commendable performance in image synthesis, underscoring its efficacy in text-to-image generation
      tasks.
