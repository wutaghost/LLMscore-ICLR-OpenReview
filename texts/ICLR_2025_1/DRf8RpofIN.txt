009                                                A BSTRACT
010
011            Bilevel Optimization (BLO) is a widely-used approach that has numerous applica-
012            tions, including hyperparameter optimization, meta-learning. However, existing
013            gradient-based method suffer from the following issues. Reverse-mode differ-
014            entiation suffers from high memory requirements, while the methods based on
015            the implicit function theorem require the convergence of the inner optimization.
016            Approximations that consider a truncated inner optimization trajectory suffer from
017            a short horizon bias. In this paper, we propose a novel approximation for hyper-
018            gradient computation that sidesteps these difficulties. Specifically, we accumulate
019
               the short-horizon approximations from each step of the inner optimization trajec-
               tory. Additionally, we demonstrate that under certain conditions, the proposed
020
               hypergradient is a sufficient descent direction. Experimental results on a few-shot
021
               meta-learning and data hyper-cleaning tasks support our findings.

057   In the current work we develop a novel gradient-based algorithm Bengio (2000). The challenge
058
      is that the exact hypergradient calculation is computationally demanding Franceschi et al. (2017).
      Specifically, Forward-Mode differentiation (FMD) is memory demanding, since it increases linearly
059
      with the number of hyperparameters. This limits the application of the method for large-scale
060
      problems with millions of hyperparameters, such as meta-learning. By contrast, Revers-Mode
061   Differentiation (RMD) perfectly scales to problems with millions of hyperparameters, but it requires
062   the full inner optimization trajectory of model parameters to be saved, which is computationally
063   costly. Moreover, RMD suffers from gradient vanishing or explosion Antoniou et al. (2018), which
064   leads to training instability. Truncation of the optimization trajectory was proposed to alleviate high
065   memory consumption Shaban et al. (2019) while calculating an approximate hypergradient. However,
066   this approach suffers from short horizon bias Wu et al. (2018). Following Micaelli & Storkey (2020),
067   we define greediness as finding the optimal hyperparameters on a local scale, rather than on a global
068   scale.
069   Alternatively, an implicit differentiation may be used to compute the hypergradient Lorraine et al.
070   (2020); Luketina et al. (2016); Pedregosa (2016). This approach mitigates the need for unrolling,
071   but it heavily relies on Implicit Function Theorem, which requires the convergence of the inner
072   optimization Grazzi et al. (2020); Blondel et al. (2022). The challenge of this family of methods is
073   computing inverse Hessian-vector product. This computation may be approximated with Neumann
074   series Lorraine et al. (2020) or conjugate gradients Pedregosa (2016).
075   In this paper, we propose an alternative approach to hypergradient computation. We generalize the
076   method from Luketina et al. (2016). Namely, the proposed approach resolves the following issues
077   simultaneously: short horizon bias, high memory requirements, applicability to large-scale problems
078   with millions of hyperparameters, and independence of inner optimization convergence. Overall, our
079   contributions are as follows:
080
            1. we introduce a procedure that aggregates the greedy gradients calculated at each iteration of
081
               the inner objective, which satisfies the requirements above.
082
083
            2. We provide a theoretical analysis of the proposed approach. Under some assumptions, a
               sufficient descent condition holds.
084
085         3. We empirically prove the effectiveness of the proposed approach on a Meta-Learning task.
086

      5.1   BASELINES
271
272   For comparison, we consider the following list of baselines that are efficient in terms of space and
273   latency:
274
275          • T1 − T2 Luketina et al. (2016). The method performs an unrolled differentiation using
276            only the last step of inner optimization, so it performs a JVP.
277          • IFT Lorraine et al. (2020). The method combines the implicit function theorem (IFT) with
278            efficient approximations of the inverse Hessian. The number of JVPs is controlled by the
279            number of terms taken from the Neumann series.
280          • FO. The method uses only the first-order gradient from equation 5, namely ∇α Lval (wT , α).
281            Note that it is not applicable for tasks for which the outer objective does not depend explicitly
282            on the vector of hyperparameters α.
283          • Full. The method computes the true hypergradient defined in equation 5.

399   6     F UTURE WORK AND EXTENSIONS
400
401   Hyperparameter γ estimation. One of the future work directions is an exploration of the optimal
402   value of the hyperparameter γ. While we have not yet conducted a comprehensive analysis, current
403
      experiments suggest that γ = 0.9 offers strong performance, comparable or superior to baseline
      methods. A straightforward approach to tuning this hyperparameter is through grid or random search.
404
      However, theoretical framework proposed in Section 4 establishes a relation between γ and matrices
405
      of parameter gradients Ak . This fact can be used for potential analytical methods to derive its optimal
406   value.
407
408
      Extension to Other Optimization Algorithms. The proposed method can be viewed as an ex-
409
      tension of the T 1 − T 2 method, leveraging a longer optimization horizon and the inclusion of
410   momentum. The momentum term establishes connections with other optimization algorithms, and
411   future extensions could incorporate advanced optimization techniques such as adaptive moment
412   estimation Kingma & Ba (2015). Additionally, a promising direction for further research is to explore
413   neural network-based optimization methods, as demonstrated in Andrychowicz et al. (2016), which
414   could potentially improve the adaptability of the proposed method.
415
416   Validation Loss Surface and Horizon Length in Hyperparameter Optimization. Our experi-
417   ments demonstrate that, across multiple tasks, the proposed method outperforms more sophisticated
418   approaches such as the IFT method, despite its simplicity in both computational and implementation
419   aspects. This raises a question: how complex is the underlying hyperparameter optimization problem,
420   and do we truly require accurate hyperparameter gradient approximations over a long horizon?
421   While several studies theoretically explore hyperparameter optimization with long horizon Micaelli
422
      & Storkey (2020); Wu et al. (2018), the complexity of the validation loss surface for real-world
      problems remains an open question and needs to be investigated.

425   7     C ONCLUSION
426
427   The paper presents an approximation of the true hypergradient for gradient-based bilevel optimization
428   that avoids the high memory cost and short horizon bias. Additionally, the method does not require
429   the assumption of convergence to an optimal solution for the inner optimization. The proposed
430   method exploits an aggregation of greedy gradients calculated at each step of the inner trajectory.
431   Our theoretical findings suggest that the approximation satisfies the sufficient descent condition.
      Empirically, the introduced method outperforms the baselines in terms of validation accuracy, having
