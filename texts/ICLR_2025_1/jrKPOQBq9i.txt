009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            Dirichlet energy is intuitive and commonly used to measure over-smoothing. How-
012            ever, Dirichlet energy can only capture information about the first-order derivative
013            of features. In light of this, we propose a series of node similarity measures which
014            are the energy of higher-order derivatives of features and generalize Dirichlet
015            energy. After we rigorously analyze the property of proposed measures and its
016            application to establish the sharp decay rate of Dirichlet energy under continuous
017            diffusion or discrete random walk which is closely related to the first nonzero eigen-
018
               value of graph Laplacian. Lastly, to address over-smoothing with respect to these
               measures, we propose a normalization termed PoincareNorm which generalizes
019
               PairNorm to control our proposed measures. We consider the semi-supervised
020
               node classification task in the scenario without missing features, PoincareNorm
021            outperforms existing normalization methods.

024   1   I NTRODUCTION
025
026   Graph neural networks(GNNs) have emerged recently and successfully applied to many fields such
027   as protein prediction (Gligorijević et al., 2021) and social recommendation (Fan et al., 2022). Though
028   vanilla graph convolutional network(GCN) (Kipf and Welling, 2017), graph attention network(GAT)
029   (Veličković et al., 2018) and a class of neural networks following the paradigm of message passing
030   type (Gilmer et al., 2017) have achieved great success in many graph-based tasks, they suffer from
031   over-smoothing when stacking layers. Many works theoretically analyze the loss of expressive
032   power for attention-based mechanism (Wu et al., 2023) and graph convolutional network (Oono and
033
      Suzuki, 2020)(Cai and Wang, 2020). Works are emerging to address over-smoothing such as residual
      connection (Li et al., 2019) (Liu et al., 2020) (Chen et al., 2020)(Xu et al., 2018) originated from
034
      (He et al., 2016), regularization such as dropout (Papp et al., 2021) (Rong et al., 2020)(Fang et al.,
035
      2023). Nguyen et al. (2023) think that positive Ollivier-Ricci curvature will cause over-smoothing
036   and they delete edges with highly positive curvature. From the spectrum view over-smoothing is
037   closely related to the first nonzero eigenvalue of graph Laplacian (Jamadandi et al., 2024) (Giraldo
038   et al., 2023).
039
      Simplified graph convolution(SGC) (Wu et al., 2019) separates transformation and propagation.
040
      With propagation only they can stack layers and achieve competing performance with GCN, Wang
041
      et al. (2021) further use heat kernel to propagate and Li et al. (2022) consider more propagation
042   kernel. Another mainstream to address over-smoothing is to consider different ordinary differential
043   equations(ODE) and partial differential equations(PDE) to change the dynamic system of GNNs.
044   Rusch et al. (2023b) use gradient gating to control the update of features. Kang et al. (2024) apply
045   fractional time derivative to evolving dynamic system. More equation-based GNNs such as Allen-
046   Cahn equation (Wang et al., 2023), reaction-diffusion equation (Choi et al., 2023). For more equation-
047   based graph neural networks we refer to (Rusch et al., 2022)(Maskey et al., 2023)(Behmanesh et al.,
048   2023)(Thorpe et al., 2022)(Chamberlain et al., 2021)(Xhonneux et al., 2020).
049   Normalization of features has also been proven successful in graph neural networks. a few works
050   are devoted to address over-smoothing such as PairNorm (Zhao and Akoglu, 2020) and GroupNorm
051   (Zhou et al., 2020). These works keep normalized features away from trivial under the measure of
052   over-smoothing. NodeNorm (Zhou et al., 2021a) think of degradation of graph convolutional network
053   as variance inflammation and normalize over feature dimension for every node similar to layernorm
      (Ba et al., 2016), GraphNorm (Cai et al., 2021) think that usual instancenorm (Ulyanov et al., 2017)


                                                         1
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


054
      applied to GNN will cause loss of information due to standard shift, instead they normalize the feature
055   values across all nodes in each graph with a learnable shift.
056
057   Many measures are proposed to quantitively measure over-smoothing, among which Dirichlet energy
058
      is intuitively and most commonly used (Rusch et al., 2022)(Zhou et al., 2021b), however, Dirichlet
      energy can only capture information about the first-order derivative of features. Also, Dirichlet energy
059
      based normalization PairNorm can not control the energy of higher-order derivatives well. Based on
060
      this observation our contributions are as follows
061
062          • We propose a series of node similarity measures to measure over-smoothing more finely,
063            this measure generalizes Dirichlet energy. We prove that these node similarity measures
064            satisfy the condition of (Rusch et al., 2023a). Then we theoretically illustrate the relation
065            between proposed node similarity measures and use this relation to build up the decay rate of
066            Dirichlet energy under continuous heat diffusion and general discrete random walk. Results
067
               of the decay rate will show that self-loop is a necessary condition for over-smoothing. Also,
               the decay rate under continuous heat diffusion can give a more concrete analysis of over-
068
               smoothing of the work (Wang et al., 2021) and indicate the relation between over-smoothing
069
               and the first nonzero eigenvalues of graph Laplacian.
070
             • In light of these measures, We use Poincare inequality to construct normalization termed
071
               PoincareNorm to control measures proposed by us, we note that this normalization is a
072
               generalization of PairNorm. We consider the semi-supervised node classification task in
073            the scenario without missing features, PoincareNorm outperforms existing normalization
074            methods.

      6    C ONCLUSION
470
471   In this work, we generalize some existing node similarity measures including Dirichlet energy, and
472   propose measures called the energy of higher-order derivatives. We rigorously establish relations
473   between the energy of higher-order derivatives. Using this relation we establish the decay rate of
474   Dirichlet energy of diffusion under heat kernel and discrete random walk, and we show that self-loop
475   is a necessary condition for over-smoothing. These results and techniques can also be easily used
476   to establish the decay rate for other dynamical systems. Future work can design GNNs in light of
477   these measures. To address over-smoothing in light of the energy of higher-order derivatives, we
478   propose a normalization termed PoincareNorm which is a generalization of PairNorm. PoincareNorm
479
      outperforms existing normalizations on the semi-supervised node classification task in the scenario
      with missing features and can control the energy of higher-order derivatives well.
