015             we demonstrate that, for multi-index models, focusing solely on the lowest degree

017             Specifically, we consider the task of learning target functions of form fвИЧ (x) =

      1    I NTRODUCTION
030
031
      In many learning problems, the target function exhibits or is assumed to exhibit a low-dimensional
032
      structure. A classical model of this type is the multi-index model, where the target function depends
033   only on a P -dimensional subspace of the ambient space Rd , with P typically much smaller than d.
034   When the relevant dimension P = 1, the model is known as the single-index model, which dates
035   back to at least Ichimura (1993). Both single- and multi-index models have been widely studied,
036   especially in the context of neural network and stochastic gradient descent (SGD) in recent years,
037   sometimes under the name вАЬfeature learningвАЭ(Ben Arous et al. (2021); Bietti et al. (2022); Damian
038   et al. (2022); Abbe et al. (2022; 2023); Damian et al. (2024); Oko et al. (2024); Dandi et al. (2024)).
039   In Ben Arous et al. (2021), the authors show that for single-index models, the behavior of online
040   SGD can be split into two phases: an initial вАЬsearchingвАЭ phase, where most of the samples are used
041   boost the correlation with the relevant (one-dimensional) subspace to a constant, and a subsequent
042   вАЬdescendingвАЭ phase, where the correlation further increases to 1. They introduce the concept of the
043   information exponent (IE), defined as the index of the first nonzero coefficient in the Taylor expan-
044   sion of the population loss around 0, which corresponds to the lowest degree in the Hermite expan-
045   sion of the link function in Gaussian single-index models. They prove that the sample complexity
046   of online SGD is OћГ(d) when IE = 2 and OћГ(dkвИТ1 ) when IE = k вЙ• 3. After that, various lower and
047   upper bounds have been established for single-index models in Bietti et al. (2022); Damian et al.
048
      (2023; 2024). Similar results for certain multi-index models have also been derived in Abbe et al.
      (2022; 2023); Bietti et al. (2023); Oko et al. (2024). In all cases, the sample complexity of online
049
      SGD scales with dIEвИТ1 when IE вЙ• 3.1

055   In this setting, there are two types of recovery: recovering each direction vkвИЧ (strong recovery)
056   and recovering the subspace spanned by {vkвИЧ }k . The former notion is stronger, because once the
057   directions are known, the learning task essentially reduces to learning the one-dimensional ѕХk :
058   R вЖТ R for each k вИИ [P ]. However, strong recovery is not always possible. To see this, consider
059   the case ѕХk (z) = h2 (z), where hL is the L-th (normalized) Hermite polynomial. One can show
060   that this corresponds to decomposing the projection matrix (a second-order tensor) of the subspace
061   span{vkвИЧ }k . If the model is isotropic in the relevant subspace, recovering the directions is impossible
062   due to the rotational invariance (see Section 3.1 for more discussion). In contrast, when ѕХk (z) =
063
      h2 (z) + h4 (z), the identifiability property of the fourth-order tensor decomposition problem allows
      strong recovery via tensor power method or (stochastic) gradient descent (Ge et al. (2018); Li et al.
064
      (2020); Ge et al. (2021)). Note that in both examples, the information exponent is 2, indicating that
065
      information exponent alone does not distinguish between these two scenarios.
066
067   This leads to a natural question: Can we combine the above results for orthogonal multi-index
068   models by first using the second-order terms to recover the subspace and then using the higher-order
069   terms to learn the directions? Ideally, the first stage would require at most OћГ(d poly(P )) samples,
070   consistent with the case IE = 2, and once the subspace is recovered, later steps would also cost
071   at most d poly(P ) samples.2 This would yield an overall OћГ(d poly(P )) sample (and also time)
072
      complexity for strong recovery of the ground-truth directions. Note that the d-denpendence matches
      the IE = 2 case and the strong recovery guarantee aligns with the results for IE > 2. In this work,
073
      we prove the following theorem, providing a positive answer to this question.

119   1.2     O UR CONTRIBUTIONS
120
121   We summarize our contributions as follows:
122
123       вАҐ We demonstrate that information exponent alone is insufficient to characterize certain structures
124         in the learning task and show that for a specific orthogonal multi-index model, if we consider
125
            both the lower- and higher-order terms, the sample complexity of strong recovery using online
            SGD can be greatly improved over the vanilla information exponent-based analysis.

364   4     F ROM GRADIENT FLOW TO ONLINE SGD
365
366   In this section, we discuss how to convert the previous gradient flow analysis to an online SGD
367   one. Our actual proof will be based directly on the online SGD analysis, but the overall idea is still
368   proving that the online SGD dynamics of certain important quantities closely track their population
369   gradient descent (GD) counterparts. Our choice of learning rate ќЈ will be much smaller than what
370   needed for GD to track GF вАФ the bottleneck comes from the GD-to-SGD conversion, not the GF-
371   to-GD one. In other words, provided that SGD tracks GD well, the number of steps/samples it needs
372   to finish each substage is roughly the amount of time GF needs, divided by the step size ќЈ.
373   The rest of this section is organized as follows. In Section 4.1, we collect a few useful lemmas for
374   controlling the difference between noisy dynamics and their deterministic counterparts. The idea
375   behind them has appeared in Ben Arous et al. (2021) and is also used in Abbe et al. (2022). Here,
376   we simplify and slightly generalize their argument and provide a user-friendly interface. When used
377   properly, it reduces the GD-to-SGD proof to routine calculus. Then, in Section 4.2, we discuss how
      to apply those general results to analyze the dynamics of online SGD in our setting.

520   In this work, we study the task of learning multi-index models of form fвИЧ (x) = k=1 ѕХ(vkвИЧ ¬Јx) with

529   gradually learn the directions using those terms of different order sequentially.
