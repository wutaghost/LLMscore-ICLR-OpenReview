                                                                 A BSTRACT
035
                 3D cars are commonly used in self-driving systems, virtual/augmented reality,
036
                 and games. However, existing 3D car datasets are either synthetic or low-quality,
037              presenting a significant gap toward the high-quality real-world 3D car datasets
038              and limiting their applications in practical scenarios. In this paper, we propose the
039              first large-scale 3D real car dataset, termed 3DRealCar, offering three distinctive
040              features. (1) High-Volume: 2,500 cars are meticulously scanned by smartphones,
041              obtaining car images and point clouds with real-world dimensions; (2) High-
042              Quality: Each car is captured in an average of 200 dense, high-resolution 360-
043              degree RGB-D views, enabling high-fidelity 3D reconstruction; (3) High-Diversity:
044              The dataset contains various cars from over 100 brands, collected under three
045
                 distinct lighting conditions, including reflective, standard, and dark. Additionally,
                 we offer detailed car parsing maps for each instance to promote research in car
046
                 parsing tasks. Moreover, we remove background point clouds and standardize the
047
                 car orientation to a unified axis for the reconstruction only on cars and controllable
048              rendering without background. We benchmark 3D reconstruction results with
049              state-of-the-art methods across each lighting condition in 3DRealCar. Extensive
050              experiments demonstrate that the standard lighting condition part of 3DRealCar
051              can be used to produce a large number of high-quality 3D cars, improving various
052              2D and 3D tasks related to cars. Notably, our dataset brings insight into the fact that
053              recent 3D reconstruction methods face challenges in reconstructing high-quality
                 3D cars under reflective and dark lighting conditions. Our dataset is available here.

089   1    I NTRODUCTION
090
091   Cars, as both daily objects and vehicles, are of significant interest to researchers, especially in the
092   field of autonomous driving. Autonomous perception systems are typically trained on daily scene
093   datasets that are collected frequently. However, these datasets often exhibit long-tailed distributions,
094   with far fewer instances of corner-case scenarios, like car accidents. Consequently, this imbalance
095   leads to the autonomous perception system generalizing well in the most frequently occurring scenes.
096   This means that the system is likely to perform poorly in rare situations, posing significant safety risks
097   to drivers. To build a reliable system, it is essential to have a simulator that can simulate photorealistic
098
      hazardous scenes. Moreover, high-quality 3D cars are necessary for a realistic simulator.
099   Recent 3D car reconstruction methods (Wang et al., 2023a; Zhou et al., 2023; Xie et al., 2023) mainly
100   reconstruct cars from self-driving datasets (Sun et al., 2020; Caesar et al., 2020; Geiger et al., 2013).
101   To apply reconstructed cars to real-world scenes, the reconstructed 3D car should be high-quality.
102   However, it is very challenging to obtain such high-quality 3D cars for the following reasons: (1)
103   Previous 3D car reconstruction methods produce low-quality 3D cars, primarily because they train
104
      on self-driving datasets with low-resolution car images and a limited number of trainable views. (2)
      Manually crafting a high-quality 3D car model requires specialized artists, which is time-consuming.
105
      (3) There is no large-scale 3D real car dataset that can be utilized to produce a bulk of 3D cars.
106
107   Moreover, existing 3D car datasets are either synthetic or only contain a few posed images, as shown
      in Figure 2. SRN-Car (Chang et al., 2015) and Objaverse-Car (Deitke et al., 2023) collect 3D car

      computer-aided design (CAD) models from the Internet, but these models are synthetic and contain
109   non-photorealistic texture. Although MVMC (Zhang et al., 2021) is a real car dataset, it collects only
110   ten views on average for each car. On the contrary, our collected 3DRealCar dataset provides an
111   average of 200 dense RGB-D views per car for high-quality 3D car reconstruction.
112
      We also show that the recent state-of-the-art 3D generative method, MVDream (Shi et al., 2023b), as
113
      depicted in Figure 2, fails to generate high-quality cars due to the multi-view inconsistency introduced
114
      by generative models (Rombach et al., 2022; Stability.AI, 2023; Liu et al., 2023c; Sun et al., 2023).
115   Thus, the existing 3D generation methods cannot be employed to generate high-quality 3D real car
116   assets.

118
      In this work, we collect a large-scale 3D real car dataset in the wild, termed 3DRealCar, which
      contains dense high-quality views and rich diversity. During data collection, we employ smartphones
119
      with ARKit (Apple, 2021) to scan cars parked on roadsides or parking lots, obtaining posed RGB-D
120
      images and point clouds of cars. In particular, we scan around the cars in three loops to obtain dense
121   views. Note that we collect car data with the consent of owners. In Table 1 and Figure 1, we show
122   our dataset possesses striking characteristics compared with previous 3D car datasets. We capture
123   dense RGB-D images in high resolution, which promotes the reconstruction of high-quality 3D cars.
124   Furthermore, we scan cars under three different lighting conditions, resulting in the surfaces of cars
125   having different lighting effects, such as reflective, standard, and dark, where we denote the standard
126   as the smooth lighting condition without obvious specular highlight. Figure 2 shows some examples
127   of three lighting conditions in our dataset. Note that the number of instances in our dataset is the
128   largest in existing datasets. Therefore, our collected 3DRealCar dataset has a rich diversity in terms
129
      of car types, colors, brands, and lighting conditions. We also provide car parsing map annotations
      with thirteen classes for each instance, which enable our dataset to be applied in car component
130
      understanding tasks.
131
132   To construct a high-quality dataset, we filter out the images that are out of focus, occluded, or
133   blurred. To facilitate the 3D reconstruction solely on cars, we remove the point clouds of the
134   background. We also adjust the orientation of the car facing along the x-axis before the reconstruction
135
      for controllable rendering. Based on the high-quality posed RGB-D images, point clouds, and multi-
      grained annotations, we can apply the dataset to various tasks related to cars. Figure 3 shows our
136
      dataset supports over 10 tasks, including several popular 2D and 3D tasks to promote the advancement
137
      of car-related research.
138
139   We leverage existing state-of-the-art methods to benchmark 3D reconstruction and car parsing tasks
140   of our 3DRealCar dataset. We also conduct extensive experiments to demonstrate that the reflective
141
      and dark lighting conditions in our dataset are challenging to existing methods, which brings a new
      challenge for 3D reconstruction in awful lighting conditions. Furthermore, we demonstrate that
142
      our 3DealCar dataset can bring real-car prior and enhance existing 3D generation and downstream
143
      methods. Overall, the contributions of this work can be summarized below:
144
145   • We propose the first large-scale 3D real car dataset, named 3DRealCar, which contains 2,500 car
146   instances and their point clouds with actual sizes in real-world scenes.
147   • 3DRealCar contains RGB-D images and point clouds with detailed annotations, supporting re-
148   searchers to investigate various tasks in both 2D and 3D.
149
      • We conduct 3D reconstruction and car parsing benchmarks to advance car-related tasks. Notably,
150
      we observe that existing methods face challenges under the extreme lighting conditions of 3DRealCar.
151
152   • Extensive experiments demonstrate our 3DRealCar dataset can enhance real-car prior and improve
153   the performance of existing 3D generation and novel view synthesis methods.

194   3D Reconstruction with Neural Field. 3D reconstruction aims to create a 3D structure digital
195   representation of an object or a scene from its multi-view images, which is a long-standing task in
196
      computer vision. One of the most representative works in 3D reconstruction is Neural Radiance
      Fields (NeRFs) (Mildenhall et al., 2021), which demonstrates promising performance for novel view
197
      synthesis. Afterward, this method inspires a new wave of 3D reconstruction methods using the
198
      volume rendering method, with subsequent works focusing on improving its quality (Verbin et al.,
199   2021; Barron et al., 2021; 2022b; Guo et al., 2022; Suhail et al., 2022; Chen et al., 2022b; Wang et al.,
200   2023b; Barron et al., 2023), efficiency (Fridovich-Keil et al., 2022; Müller et al., 2022; Reiser et al.,
201   2021; Sun et al., 2022; Kerbl et al., 2023a; Chen et al., 2022a; Garbin et al., 2021), applying artistic
202   effects (Fan et al., 2022; Wang et al., 2022; Jain et al., 2022; Zhang et al., 2022b), and generalizing to
203   unseen scenes (Yu et al., 2021; Chen et al., 2021; Wang et al., 2021; Johari et al., 2022; T et al., 2023;
204   Chibane et al., 2021). Particularly, Kilonerf (Reiser et al., 2021) accelerates the training process
205   of NeRF by dividing a large MLP into thousands of tiny MLPs. Furthermore, Mip-NeRF (Barron
206   et al., 2021) proposes a conical frustum rather than a single ray to ameliorate aliasing. Mip-NeRF
207
      360 (Barron et al., 2022a) further improves the application scenes of NeRF to the unbounded scenes.
      Although these NeRF-based methods demonstrate powerful performance on various datasets, the
208
      training time always requires several hours even one day more. Instant-NGP (Müller et al., 2022)
209
      uses a multi-resolution hash encoding method, which reduces the training time by a large margin.
210   3DGS (Kerbl et al., 2023a) proposes a new representation based on 3D Gaussian Splatting, which
211   reaches real-time rendering for objects or unbounded scenes. 2DGS (Huang et al., 2024) proposes a
212   perspective-accurate 2D splatting process that leverages ray-splat intersection and rasterization to
213   further enhance the quality of the reconstructions. Scaffold-GS (Lu et al., 2023) proposes an anchor
214   growing and pruning strategy to accelerate the scene coverage, which effectively reduces redundant
215   Gaussians and improves rendering quality. However, there is not yet a large-scale 3D real car dataset
      so far. Therefore, we present a 3D real car dataset, named 3DRealCar in this work.

234   3D Generation with Diffusion Prior. Some current works (Jun & Nichol, 2023; Nichol et al., 2022)
235   leverage a 3D diffusion model to learn the representation of 3D structure. However, these methods
236   lack generalization ability due to the scarcity of 3D data. To facilitate 3D generation without direct
237   supervision of 3D data, image or multi-view diffusion models are often used to guide the 3D creation
238   process. Notable approaches like DreamFusion (Poole et al., 2022b) and subsequent works (Metzer
239   et al., 2023; Lin et al., 2023) use an existing image diffusion model as a scoring function, applying
240   Score Distillation Sampling SDS loss to generate 3D objects from textual descriptions. These methods,
241   however, suffer from issues such as the Janus problem (Poole et al., 2022b; Metzer et al., 2023) and
242
      overly saturated textures. Inspired by Zero123 (Liu et al., 2023c), several recent works (Stability.AI,
      2023; Shi et al., 2023a; Liu et al., 2023e; Kong et al., 2024; Zheng & Vedaldi, 2023; Melas-Kyriazi
243
      et al., 2024; Liu et al., 2023b;a; Qian et al., 2023) refine image or video diffusion models to better
244
      guide the 3D generation by producing more reliable multi-view images. However, these generative
245   methods fail to generate high-quality cars due to the lack of the prior of real cars.

      3.1   DATA C OLLECTION AND A NNOTATION
251   As shown in Figure 4, our dataset is collected using smartphones, specifically iPhone 14 models,
252   adopting ARKit APIs (Apple, 2021) to scan cars for their point clouds and RGB-D images. The data
253   collection process is conducted under three distinct lighting conditions, such as standard, reflective,
254   and dark. These lighting conditions represent the lighting states of vehicle surfaces. It is important to
255   note that all data collection is performed with the consent of owners. During the scanning process,
256   the car should be stationary while we meticulously circle the car three times to capture as many
257   views as possible. For each loop, we adjust the height of the smartphone to obtain images from
258   different angles. Furthermore, we try our best to make sure captured images contain the entire car
259
      body without truncation. To preserve the privacy of owners, we make license plates and other private
      information obfuscated. To construct a high-quality dataset, we filter out some instances with blurred,
260
      out-of-focus, and occluded images. We also provide detailed annotations for car brands, types, and
261
      colors. Particularly, we provide the car parsing maps for each car with thirteen classes in our dataset
262   as shown in Figure 1 for the advancement of car component understanding tasks.

      Orientation Rectification. As shown in Figure 4, we utilize Colmap (Schonberger & Frahm, 2016)
297
      to reconstruct more dense point clouds and obtain accurate camera poses and intrinsics because we
298
      find that the estimated poses by the smartphone are not accurate. However, after the removal of the
299   background point clouds, we find that the car orientation of the point cloud is random, which leads to
300   the subsequent render task being uncontrollable. Given camera poses P = {pi }N       1 , where N is the
301   number of poses, we use Principal Component Analysis (PCA) (Abdi & Williams, 2010) to obtain
302   a PCA component T ∈ R3×3 . The PCA component is the principal axis of the data in 3D space,
303   which represents rotation angles to each axis. Therefore, we leverage it to rectify the postures of cars
304   parallel to the x-axis. However, this process cannot guarantee cars facing along the x-axis. Therefore,
305   in some failure cases, we manually interfere and adjust the orientation along the x-axis. With the
306   fixed car orientation, we can control rendered poses for the subsequent tasks.
307   Point Cloud Rescaling. The size of the point clouds reconstructed by Colmap (Schonberger &
308   Frahm, 2016) does not match the real-world size, which inhibits the reconstruction of a practically
309   sized 3D car. To address this, we calculate the bounding box of the scanned foreground point clouds
310   to obtain its actual size in the real-world scene. Then, we rescale the rectified point clouds into the
311   real size. In addition to the rescaling of the point clouds, we also need to adjust the camera poses. We
312   rescale translations of camera poses using a scale factor calculated by the ratio of scanned point cloud
313
      size and Colmap point cloud size. After these rescaling processes, we use rescaled point clouds to
      reconstruct a 3D car model through recent state-of-the-art methods, like 3DGS (Kerbl et al., 2023b).

366   Single Image to 3D Generation (Poole et al., 2022a; Sun et al., 2023; Tang et al., 2023): Given a text
367   prompt or single image, recent 3D generation methods generate 3D objects with Score Distillation
368   Sampling (SDS) (Poole et al., 2022a) and diffusion generative models (Rombach et al., 2022; Liu
369   et al., 2023c; Stability.AI, 2023). However, these methods cannot generate high-quality 3D cars due
370   to the lack of the prior of real cars in 3D-based diffusion models. Therefore, we would demonstrate
371   the value of our dataset by improving recent 3D generation for real cars.

      6    C ONCLUSION
532
533
      In this paper, we propose the first large-scale high-quality 3D real car dataset, named 3DRealCar.
534   The collected dense and high-resolution 360-degree views for each car can be used to reconstruct a
535   high-quality 3D car. Extensive experiments demonstrate the efficacy and challenges of our 3DRealCar
536   in 3D reconstruction. Thanks to the reconstructed high-quality 3D cars from our dataset and car-part
537   level annotations, our dataset can be utilized to support various tasks related to cars. In addition, the
538   benchmarking results can serve as baselines for prospective research. Although 3DRealCar currently
539   only has car exterior views, we intend to provide both exterior and interior views in the future to
      further promote the reconstruction of more intact 3D cars.

      A    B ROADER I MPACTS S TATEMENT
811
812
      The introduction of our 3DRealCar dataset has profound effects on self-driving research. We expect
813   this dataset can encourage extensive research to promote the advancement of the community.
814
815   Research Impacts. By providing dense 360-degree views of cars with point clouds as initialization,
816
      our 3DRealCar can be used to reconstruct high-quality 3D real cars for 3D printing and simulation in
      corner-case scenes. By providing detailed car parsing map annotations, our dataset can be leveraged
817
      to segment 2D car components or point clouds. Note that our 3DRealCar is the first dataset providing
818
      3D car parsing annotations. In our 3D reconstruction benchmarking experiments, the reflective and
819   dark lighting conditions of our dataset bring challenges to existing methods to reconstruct 3D cars
820   under awful lighting conditions. We expect our dataset to encourage widespread collaboration and
821   accelerate the exploration of 3D real car reconstruction, parsing, and simulation.
822
      Societal Impacts. We collect our 3DRealCar dataset with the consent of the owners. In addition, we
823
      blur license plates and other private information. We try our best to hide and preserve the privacy of
824
      owners. Therefore, our dataset would not have any privacy violation problems. Due to our dataset
825   focusing on a car class, we believe our dataset has the potential to be employed in future self-driving
826   research and improve self-driving systems further.

      E    S IMULATED L IDAR S CENES
944
      As depicted in Figure 12, we can insert our car point clouds into lidar scenes to simulate corner-case
945
      scenes, like a car passing or parking horizontally in front of the ego car. To better differentiate the
946   inserted cars, we set them with dense point clouds and red color. In a practical scene, the vehicular
947   point clouds should be sparse and only have one size that could be scanned by the lidar. Therefore,
948   when we apply the inserted vehicular point clouds into a scene, we should make the vehicular point
949   clouds sparse and only contain one side. By training on a variety of simulated scenarios, including
950   rare or dangerous situations that are difficult to collect in real life, the self-driving system can learn to
951   handle unexpected events more effectively.
952
953   F    3D C AR PARSING
954
955   As shown in Figure 13, our dataset is the first to provide 3D car parsing annotations for parsing
956   car components in 3D space. Thanks to that we provide 2D car parsing maps for every instance in
957   our 3DRealCar dataset, we can lift 2D parsing maps to 3D and segment each component for point
958   clouds and meshes. The primary purpose of these 3D car parsing maps is to enable precise and
959   comprehensive analysis of vehicle structures, which is crucial for applications such as autonomous
960   driving, vehicle design, vehicle editing, and virtual reality simulations. By using these detailed 3D
961   parsing maps, developers and researchers can improve object recognition algorithms and enhance
962   collision detection systems. Furthermore, this dataset facilitates the training of machine learning
963
      models to better understand the spatial relationships and physical attributes of car components,
      leading to more advanced and reliable automotive technologies.
