011                                             A BSTRACT
012
013           This paper proposes novel binarized convolutional neural networks (BCNNs)
014           named QB-Net and QSB-Net, specifically designed to Quadruple the number of
015
              channels and incorporate a so-called Smooth downsampling in BCNNs for low-
              cost mobile environments. The proposed models combine FP32 depthwise sep-
016
              arable (DS) convolutions with binarized 1 × 1 pointwise convolutions, offering
017
              reduced computational costs in the pointwise convolutions. To enhance the de-
018           graded performance of the above naive combination, the proposed models start
019           with a small number of channels in shallow layers and expand them during down-
020           sampling by a factor of four, effectively managing model complexity in the down-
021           sampling. The proposed model structure maintains low computational costs in
022           the shallow blocks and increases model complexity in the deep blocks, provid-
023           ing a wider dynamic range to manage information in the frequency domain. As a
024           result, the proposed models overcome the limitations of existing BCNNs, deliver-
025           ing improved performance while reducing the total computational costs. For fur-
026
              ther performance enhancements, we propose a novel smooth downsampling with
              heightwise and widthwise sequential downsampling steps, doubling the number of
027
              channels at each step. Besides, we show that the channelwise self-attention (SE)
028
              is applicable with minimal additional computational costs in the proposed mod-
029           els. Besides, multiple binarized convolutions in the fully-connected (FC) layer
030           reduce storage costs without requiring 8-bit quantized convolutions. Experimen-
031           tal results demonstrate the efficiency of the proposed models in terms of perfor-
032           mance, computational costs, and inference latency on real hardware. Notably,
033           the QSB-Net-Large with SE achieve 71.2% Top-1 accuracy on ImageNet-1K and
034           69.2 mean intersection over union (mIoU) in the semantic segmentation on the
035           PASCAL VOC dataset, outperforming other counterparts.

      1   I NTRODUCTION
039
      Although substantial parallelism in GPUs or specialized accelerators can achieve a considerable
040
      speedup, edge devices lack sufficient parallelism for accommodating the increasing model com-
041
      plexity. BCNNs binarize both weights and activations into −1 and +1 in binarized convolutions. As
042   a result, multiply-accumulate operations are replaced by bitwise XNOR and bit-counting operations,
043   leveraging bit-level parallelism in CPU-based devices. While earlier BCNNs experienced significant
044   accuracy drops compared with their FP32 counterparts, recent BCNNs achieve good performance
045   comparable to mobile-friendly CNNs. However, as computational costs increase within BCNNs, the
046   expected inference speedup could not be satisfactory. For example, whereas ReActNetA (Liu et al.,
047   2020) adopts binarized 3 × 3 spatial convolutions, its FP32 counterpart, MobileNetV1 (Howard
048   et al., 2017) utilizes FP32 DS spatial convolutions. Compared with FP32 DS convolutions, bina-
049   rized 3 × 3 spatial convolutions require more computations, failing to provide faster inference on
050
      real hardware. Therefore, novel lightweight structures should be studied to minimize computational
      costs while avoiding significant performance degradation in BCNNs.
051
052   Conventional CNNs typically double the number of channels or adopt customized channel expan-
053   sion during downsampling (He et al., 2016; Ridnik et al., 2021) to mitigate information loss. In
      BCNNs, however, we expect that the binarization error and low resolution of BCNNs require a dif-

081
082
            1. New Structure for Channel Quadrupling: To reduce the computations in pointwise con-
083            volutions, the proposed models utilize 3 × 3 DS convolutions and binarized pointwise
084            convolutions. However, the existing channel expansion with the above structure shows sig-
085            nificant performance degradation. To overcome the problem, the proposed models have
086            a small number of channels in the shallow blocks and increase the number of channels
087            by a factor of four during downsampling. The small number of channels in the shallow
088            blocks reduces the total computations, as shown in Figure 1 (b). The channel quadrupling
089            can provide a wider dynamic range in deep blocks, enhancing the ability to manage the
090            information in the frequency domain and achieving good performance in BCNNs.

092            smooth downsampling processes two sequential steps involving heightwise and widthwise
093            1-D downsampling, producing noticeable performance enhancements. Each 1-D down-
094            sampling layer can double the number of channels, resulting in quadrupled channels after
095
               the two steps of downsampling in Figure 1 (a). Experimental results show that channelwise
               self-attention in DS convolutions is effective, as shown in Figure 1 (b). Besides, 8-bit quan-
096
               tization can be valid for the FP32 DS convolutional and FC layers. Notably, the usage of
097
               multiple binarized convolutions in the FC layer can reduce storage costs, which can solve
098            the issue of the increasing number of channels in the FC layer.
099

100
               els for image classification and semantic segmentation. The experiments on a Raspberry Pi
101            4B (RPi 4B) and a Samsung Exynos processor using Larq Compute Engine (LCE) (Ban-
102            nink et al., 2021) show that the proposed models have significant inference speedup com-
103            pared with the baseline model and mobile-friendly CNNs.

106   mantic segmentation on the PASCAL VOC dataset, outperforming other counterparts. The above
107   results prove that the proposed channel quadrupling and smooth downsampling can be effective in
      BCNNs.

      2   R ELATED W ORKS
109
110   Courbariaux et al. (2016) showed that BCNNs can reduce memory usage and energy consumption by
111   nearly 32× compared with baselined FP32 models. Many existing works have focused on improv-
112   ing the performance of BCNNs due to their significant performance drops. XNOR-Net (Rastegari
113   et al., 2016) adopted a scaling factor for both weights and input features of binarized convolutional
114   layers, achieving 51.4% Top-1 accuracy with binarized ResNet18 (He et al., 2016) on ImageNet-1K.
115   Bi-RealNet (Liu et al., 2018) utilized the single skipped shortcut, enhancing the Top-1 accuracy of
116   binarized ResNet18 up to 56.4%. XNOR-Net++ (Bulat et al., 2019) advanced BCNNs by introduc-
117   ing heightwise and widthwise scaling factors for the output features. Real-to-Bin (Martinez et al.,
118
      2019) enhanced the performance up to 65.4% by incorporating a self-attention block.
119   Whereas the above BCNNs are based on ResNet (He et al., 2016), MobiNet (Phan et al., 2020a)
120   and ReActNetA (Liu et al., 2020) followed the structure of MobileNetV1 (Howard et al., 2017).
121   However, MobiNet produced only 53.5% Top-1 accuracy on ImageNet-1K. ReActNetA (Liu et al.,
122   2020) achieved 69.4% Top-1 accuracy by deploying binarized 3 × 3 convolutions instead of us-
123   ing FP32 DS convolutions. The outstanding performance of Real-to-Bin and ReActNetA showed
124
      the effectiveness of teacher-student training (Hinton et al., 2015) in BCNNs. There have been sev-
      eral works to develop specific activation functions and training methods for BCNNs. IR-Net (Qin
125
      et al., 2020) introduced a binarization method and so-called error decaying estimator for the train-
126
      ing. SI-BNN (Wang et al., 2020) proposed a binarization function with trainable thresholds. BNSC-
127   Net (Wu et al., 2021) decomposed 2-D convolutions to employ additional skip connections. RB-
128   Net (Liu et al., 2022) reshaped pointwise convolutions and incorporated a balanced activation func-
129   tion. ReCU (Xu et al., 2021) employed a rectified clamp unit to improve its training results. SA-
130   BNN (Liu et al., 2021) mitigated the weight flip issues in BCNNs. AdaBin (Tu et al., 2022) adap-
131   tively optimized weights and features to follow the value distributions of its FP32 baseline model.
132   ReBNN (Xu et al., 2023) introduced weighted reconstruction loss to reduce the weight oscillation
133   during training. Whereas PokeBNN (Zhang et al., 2022) achieved impressive results by adopting
134   ResNet50 as its baseline and teacher models, its hyperparameter for the clipping bound and stride
135
      configuration in the first convolutional layer introduced a different optimization strategy. Different
      from Zhang et al. (2022), the proposed QSB-Net shows a novel strategy for channel quadrupling and
136
      smooth downsampling. Therefore, we conclude that the fundamental techniques and contributions
137
      of the proposed models are totally different from those of PokeBNN.

160   methods and hyperparameters, we adopt a straight-through estimator (STE) (Bengio et al., 2013) to
161   approximate the derivatives of the sign conversion. Besides, we assume that the output features of
      the binarized convolution are scaled with channelwise learnable scaling factors.

      4.1   M OTIVATIONS
185
186   The depth multiplier for multiplying the number of channels in all blocks dramatically increases the
187   total computations, having long inference latency in mobile-friendly BCNNs. Whereas most exist-
188   ing BCNNs have focused on training techniques and specific blocks on the baseline model structure,
189   the efficient BCNN structures were not sufficiently studied. To reduce the total computations, the
190   structural development of BCNNs should mainly increase the model complexity of the efficient part
191   that significantly contributes to model performance. On the other hand, we hypothesize that informa-
192   tion loss during downsampling can impact model performance. Although channel expansion during
193   downsampling can improve representation capacity, it is questionable whether the doubled or cus-
194
      tomized channel expansions in conventional CNNs are the most effective in BCNNs. Besides, the
      binarization error could compound the effects of information loss during downsampling. Therefore,
195
      we develop a novel strategy for configuring channel expansion and downsampling in BCNNs.

      Figure 4 illustrates the OPs of the convolutions in each block, representing the main idea of the
353
      QB-Net and QSB-Net. Whereas the counterparts such as MobileNetV1 (Howard et al., 2017) and
354
      ReActNetA (Liu et al., 2020) reduce OPs in the downsampling blocks, the channel expansion in
355   QB-Net does not decrease OPs in the downsampling block. Moreover, additional computations are
356   performed in the TypeDS and TypeQS blocks. We expect that the representation capacity in a block
357   of BCNNs during downsampling can have a significant impact on the model performance. The
358   low resolution of binarized convolutions could degrade representation capacity, so it is thought that
359   the low resolution can be compensated with additional model complexity in the proposed blocks.
360   The proposed model structures increase model complexity for deep blocks during downsampling.
361   Several existing works show that increasing representation capacity during downsampling is helpful
362   for achieving better performance in BCNNs. For example, XNOR-Net (Rastegari et al., 2016) and
363
      Bi-RealNet (Liu et al., 2018) deploy FP32 1 × 1 convolutional layer for the shortcut connection.
      However, the shortcut connection with FP32 1 × 1 convolutions significantly increases the total
364
      OPs, showing long latencies on real mobile hardware (Bannink et al., 2021). We note that the
365
      channel quadrupling can increase the representation capacity during downsampling without using
366   the FP32 1×1 convolutions. It is expected that the proposed structure using the channel quadrupling
367   can have a wider dynamic range in the frequency domain from the increased complexity in deep
368   blocks. Although the number of channels in the deep blocks increases, the total computations are
369   significantly reduced by having a small number of channels in shallow blocks.
370
      To show the effects of the increased representation capacity and wider dynamic range in deep blocks,
371
      the frequency domain histograms are compared. Figure 5 shows the frequency domain histograms
372   of proposed models and counterparts in the outputs of the third (left) and final (right) downsampling
373   blocks. Notably, the proposed models have wider dynamic ranges than ReActNetA (Liu et al.,
374   2020). The proposed QSB-Net-Large and QSB-Net-Large(SE1) have dynamic ranges comparable
375   to those of FP32 models. However, the similarity between Figures 5 (g) and (h) indicates that the
376   self-attention block does not widen the dynamic range in the frequency domain. Therefore, we
377   conclude that the role of the channel quadrupling is critical to obtain wide dynamic ranges in the
      frequency domain. Besides, the feature maps of deep blocks are visualized in Appendix A.8.

400   4.5        A PPLICABILITY OF T ECHNIQUES FOR B ETTER M ODELS
401
402   The applicability of the techniques for achieving enhanced performance can be considered with
403   small additional computations. Whereas DSCONVs during downsampling reduce the computa-
404   tional costs by removing channelwise operations, existing BCNNs (Phan et al., 2020a; Liu et al.,
405
      2020) adopted the channelwise spatial convolutions. However, the channelwise operations signifi-
      cantly increase computational costs, so it could be better to deploy the self-attention (SE) block (Hu
406
      et al., 2018) after the DSCONVs, applying the attention mechanism to different channels with small
407
      computational costs. Because the number of channels is 2048 in the FC layer, its parameters are
408   doubled compared with MobileNetV1 (Howard et al., 2017) and ReActNetA (Liu et al., 2020). Any
409   fixed-point format in the FC layer can reduce storage costs, so we performed an experiment to know
410   whether the FC layer with 8-bit quantized weights is applicable, which is explained in Ablations
411   Studies. Besides, the FC layer with 8-bit quantized weights can be implemented with 8 binarized
412   convolutions, which is described in Appendix A.2. The usage of binarized convolutions in the FC
413   layer reduces storage costs of the FC layer by ×4 without using 8-bit fixed-point operations.

      5.3   A BLATION S TUDIES
490
491   Deployments of 1 × 1 convolutions: In a modification of QB-Net-Large, when the number of input
492   channels was 16 or 32, binarized pointwise convolutions were adopted after DS convolutions. In
493   this case, the Top-1 accuracy on ImageNet-1K was 68.0%, which was significantly degraded by
494   1.8%. In another modification of QB-Net-Large, when the number of input channels was 16, 32,
495   or 128, binarized 3 × 3 convolutions were adopted after DS convolutions. In this case, its Top-1
496   accuracy was 70.0%, which was only increased by 0.2%. Based on the above empirical findings,
497
      we concluded that when the number of channels in shallow blocks was small, the accuracy can be
      significantly degraded. Therefore, we determined that instead of binarized pointwise convolutions,
498
      binarized 3 × 3 convolutions were used when the number of input channels was 16 or 32.
499
500   Effects of ×8 channel expansion in deep blocks: A modification of QB-Net-Small changed the
501   number of output channels of the last 13-th and 14-th blocks to 4048 (C = 4048). The modification
502   produced 69.5% Top-1 accuracy, having 2.6% performance enhancement. This study shows that
503
      the increasing complexity of deep blocks can significantly enhance model performance. However,
      we thought that the storage costs of the FC layer and computational costs of binarized pointwise
504
      convolutions were significant, so we did not consider the case with C = 4048.
505
506   Learnable bias deployed before DS convolutions: In a modification of QB-Net-Large, when the
507   learnable bias layer was not deployed just before DS convolutions, Top-1 accuracy was degraded by
508   2.1% on ImageNet-1K. The above result indicates that the calibration using the learnable bias can
509
      be effective in the DS convolutions of the proposed models.
510   Quantization of DS convolutions and FC layer: In a modification of QSB-Net-Large(SE2), we
511   applied 8-bit quantization to the weights and input features for DS convolutions in each block.
512   In the two-stage training, its training result surprisingly achieved up to 71.5% Top-1 accuracy on
513   ImageNet-1K. Then, we applied 8-bit quantization to the weights and input features for the DS con-
514   volution and FC layers, where the tuning adopted the learning rate of 10−4 and 25 epochs on the
515
      pretrained model. In the evaluation, Top-1 accuracy was 70.8%, demonstrating that 8-bit quantiza-
      tion was applicable to the proposed models.
516
517   Training from scratch without teacher: To know the model performance without using the
518   teacher-student training, the proposed models were trained from scratch with the scheduler of co-
519   sine annealing with warmup during 600 epochs, which was based on the recipe of QuickNet (Ban-
520   nink et al., 2021). QB-Net-Large, QSB-Net-Large, and QSB-Net-Large(SE1) had 65.8%, 67.0%,
521
      and 67.5% Top-1 accuracies on ImageNet-1K, which indicates that the teacher-student training was
      critical for enhancing model performance. Whereas the proposed models were slower than Quick-
522
      Net (Bannink et al., 2021), Top-1 accuracies of the proposed models without using the teacher-
523
      student training significantly outperformed 63.3% Top-1 accuracy of QuickNet in Table 3.

526   6     C ONCLUSION
527
528   This paper proposes new BCNNs having low computational costs and high performance using chan-
529   nel quadrupling and smooth downsampling. The proposed structure using the channel quadrupling
530
      has low-cost computations in shallow blocks and a wider dynamic range in the frequency domain
      due to the increased model complexity in deep blocks. The techniques for achieving better models,
531
      such as the self-attention (Hu et al., 2018) and the quantization of DS convolutional and FC layers,
532
      are applicable to the proposed models. Using multiple binarized convolutions in the FC layer, the
533   proposed models can significantly reduce storage costs without using an 8-bit quantized FC layer.
534   When the self-attention is applied to the DS convolutions during downsampling, its performance on
535   ImageNet-1K reaches up to 71.1% Top-1 accuracy, only requiring 0.63 × 108 OPs. The small laten-
536   cies on an RPi 4B and a Samsung Exynos processor prove that the proposed models are suitable for
537   implementing high-speed inference on real hardware. Considering the above performance enhance-
538   ments and structural benefits, it is concluded that the proposed models are efficient for mobile-based
539   applications. Additional explanations and visualizations of the proposed models are included in
      Appendixes A.8, A.9, and A.10.

1446   A.11    E STIMATION OF E NERGY C ONSUMPTION ON R EAL H ARDWARE
1447
1448   We performed an energy consumption analysis on real hardware using an RPi 4B on Manjaro OS.
1449   Due to the limitations of using the power management ICs (PMIC), we cannot use power profiling
1450   tools. Therefore, we employed an alternative method, where we measured the current drawn from
1451   the USB power line using a current probe over 500 test runs. To assess the impact of running a
1452   benchmark evaluation program in Appendix A.7, we compared the current consumption under two
1453   conditions: with and without executing the program on the RPi 4B. When the models were not
1454
       being evaluated, the current consumption was approximately 713 mA @ 5V. In contrast, during the
       execution of the models listed in Table 3, the current consumption ranged from 912 mA to 936 mA.
1455
       Considering the resolution of the current probe, we thought that these variations were not significant.
1456
1457   While other system activities such as parameter uploading and log dumping were also performed
       during the measurements, their impact on the comparison was not great, considering the negligible


                                                           27

1458
       relative difference. Based on these observations, we conclude that the systematic energy consump-
1459   tion is largely proportional to inference latency because no substantial differences in system power
1460   consumption were detected during the evaluations.
