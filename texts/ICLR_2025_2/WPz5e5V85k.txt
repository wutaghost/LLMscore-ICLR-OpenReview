009                                              A BSTRACT
010
011            The proximal algorithm is a powerful tool to minimize nonlinear and nonsmooth
012            functionals in a general metric space. Motivated by the recent progress in studying
013            the training dynamics of the noisy gradient descent algorithm on two-layer neural
014            networks in the mean-field regime, we provide in this paper a simple and self-
015            contained analysis for the convergence of the general-purpose Wasserstein prox-
016            imal algorithm without assuming geodesic convexity of the objective functional.
017            Under a natural Wasserstein analog of the Euclidean Polyak-≈Бojasiewicz inequal-
018
               ity, we establish that the proximal algorithm achieves an unbiased and linear con-
               vergence rate. Our convergence rate improves upon existing rates of the proximal
019
               algorithm for solving Wasserstein gradient flows under strong geodesic convex-
020
               ity. We also extend our analysis to the inexact proximal algorithm for geodesi-
021            cally semiconvex objectives. In our numerical experiments, proximal training
022            demonstrates a faster convergence rate than the noisy gradient descent algorithm
023            on mean-field neural networks.

      1.1   C ONTRIBUTIONS
095
096   In this work, we give a simple and self-contained convergence rate analysis of the Wasserstein
097   proximal algorithm (2) for minimizing the objective function satisfying a PL-type inequality without
098   resorting to any geodesic convexity assumption. Below we summarize our main contributions.
099   вАҐ To the best of our knowledge, current work is among the first works to obtain an unbiased and
100     linear convergence rate of the general-purpose Wasserstein proximal algorithm for optimizing a
101     functional under merely a PL-type inequality. When restricted to ¬µ-convex (¬µ > 0) objective
102     functional, our result yields a sharper linear convergence rate (in function value and minimizer
103     under W2 distance) than the existing literature (Yao & Yang, 2023; Cheng et al., 2024).
104   вАҐ The linear convergence guarantee provides a new training scheme for two-layer wide neural net-
105     works in the mean-field regime. Our numeric experiments show a faster training phase (up to
106     particle discretization error) than the (forward) noisy gradient descent method.
107
      вАҐ We also analyze the inexact proximal algorithm for geodesically semiconvex objectives under
        PL-type inequality.

      1.2   L ITERATURE R EVIEW
109
110   Recently, various time-discretization methods have been proposed for minimizing a functional over
111   a single distribution. Different from the proximal algorithm, some explicit forward schemes that
112   can be seen as gradient descent in Wasserstein space are proposed (Chewi et al., 2020; Liu & Wang,
113   2016). For example, Chewi et al. (2020) studies a gradient descent algorithm for solving the barycen-
114   ter problem on the Bures-Wasserstein manifold of Gaussian distributions. The Langevin algorithm,
115   as another forward discretization of Wasserstein gradient flows via its stochastic differential equation
116
      (SDE) recursion, is widely used in the sampling literature. Numerous works (Durmus et al., 2019;
      Vempala & Wibisono, 2022; Chewi et al., 2024) have been devoted to the analysis of the Langevin
117
      algorithm under different settings and its variants (Zhang et al., 2023; Wu et al., 2022). However,
118
      Langevin algorithms are naturally biased for a positive step size. Salim et al. (2021) introduced
119   a hybridized forward-backward discretization, namely the Wasserstein proximal gradient descent,
120   and proved convergence guarantees for geodesically convex objective, akin to the proximal gradient
121   descent algorithm in Euclidean spaces.
122
      Existing rate analysis for proximal algorithm. Though convergence rate analysis for Langevin al-
123
      gorithms under strong convexity is well-developed, it is until recently that the convergence rate of the
124
      proximal algorithm on geodesically convex objectives is obtained. One advantage of the proximal
125   algorithm is that it ensures a dimension-independent convergence guarantee directly for any starting
126   distribution. Yao & Yang (2023); Cheng et al. (2024) proved an unbiased linear convergence result
127   for the ¬µ-strongly convex objective. The condition is relaxed to geodesic convexity and quadratic
128   growth of functional in (Yao et al., 2024). However, convergence analysis for non-geodesically
129   convex objective functionals is missing.
130
      Convergence rate of different time-discretizations under PL-type inequality. Vempala &
131   Wibisono (2022) obtained a biased linear convergence result for Langevin dynamics under the log-
132   Sobolev inequality (LSI) and smoothness condition. Nitanda et al. (2022) extended this result to
133   MFLD with similar techniques. Proximal Langevin algorithm proposed by Wibisono (2019), attains
134   a biased linear convergence rate under the LSI, while an extra smoothness condition of the second
135   derivative of the sampling function is required. Proximal sampling algorithm (Chen et al., 2022),
136   assuming access to samples from an oracle distribution, achieves an unbiased linear convergence for
137   sampling from Langevin dynamics under the LSI, while the analysis requires geodesic semiconvex-
138   ity (cf. Definition A.3). Fan et al. (2023); Liang & Chen (2024) improved the results, however, they
139
      still concentrate on sampling on a fixed function and cannot be applied to MFLD.

531   5   C ONCLUSION
532
533   In this work, we provided a convergence analysis of the Wasserstein proximal algorithm without
534   assuming any geodesic convexity, which improves upon the existing rates when strong geodesic
535   convexity indeed holds. We also analyzed the inexact gradient variant under an extra geodesic
536   semiconvexity condition. Applying to the proximal training of mean-field neural networks, linear
537   convergence of the entropy-regularized total objective is guaranteed, which is faster than the noisy
538   gradient descent algorithm as observed in our empirical experiments. One future work would be
539   the study of particle discretization effect of the Wasserstein proximal algorithm in the setting of
      MFLD (Kook et al., 2024; Fu & Wilson, 2024).
