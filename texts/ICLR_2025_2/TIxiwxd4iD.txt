011                                             A BSTRACT
012
013           This paper presents a comprehensive exploration into the classification of Ben-
014           gali music genres, utilizing a novel dataset, ‘BanglaGITI: Bangla Genre-wise
015           Indexed Tracks and Interpretations’, specifically curated to capture the rich di-
016
              versity of Bengali musical heritage. Our study is structured around a comparative
              analysis of traditional Machine Learning (ML) techniques, advanced Deep Learn-
017
              ing (DL) methodologies, and innovative ensemble approaches that integrate the
018
              strengths of both ML and DL through Transfer Learning. Our dataset includes a
019           total of 1410 audio files across 6 different genres. For the ML segment, features
020           such as Mel-frequency cepstral coefficients (MFCCs), zero-crossing rate (ZCR),
021           root mean square(RMS), chroma, tempo and spectral bandwidth were leveraged
022           to encapsulate the unique characteristics of Bengali music. These features serve
023           as a foundation for employing classic ML classifiers that demonstrate robust per-
024           formance in genre classification tasks. Our methodology includes Decision Tree,
025           Random Forest, Gradient Boost and KNN. Conversely, our DL models are de-
026           signed around the extraction and analysis of Log-Mel spectrograms, capitalizing
027
              on their ability to represent complex musical structures in a manner that is both
              comprehensive and conducive to DL techniques. This approach allows for the
028
              deep neural networks to learn from a richer representation of audio data, poten-
029
              tially uncovering nuanced patterns inherent in Bengali music genres. DL tech-
030           niques feature pre-trained CNN-based models such as DenseNet, ResNet and VG-
031           GNet. Furthermore, our paper innovates by proposing ensemble models that com-
032           bine the predictive capabilities of ML and DL methods respectively, aiming to
033           harness their complementary strengths for enhanced classification accuracy. The
034           ensemble models resulted in achieving almost 80% accuracy in ML and state of
035           the art 96% accuracy in DL methods while the precision recall and F1-score of
036           96.09%, 96.05% and 96.04% respectively. Our findings not only shed light on the
037           efficacy of different computational approaches in the realm of music genre classi-
038
              fication but also contribute to the understanding of Bengali music through the lens
              of machine intelligence. The use of our self-made dataset, which is among the first
039
              of its kind for Bengali music, adds a significant value to the study, offering a new
040
              benchmark for future research in this area. Through this comprehensive study, our
041           aim is to provide insights that will guide the development of more sophisticated
042           and culturally nuanced music classification systems.
