009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            Image restoration aims to recover clean images from degraded versions. While
012            Transformer-based approaches have achieved significant advancements in this
013            field, they are limited by high complexity and their inability to capture omni-
014            range dependencies, hindering their overall performance. In this work, we develop
015            Modumer for effective and efficient image restoration by revisiting the Trans-
016            former block and Modulation design, which processes input through a convolu-
017            tional block and projection layers, and fuses features via element-wise multiplica-
018
               tion. Specifically, within each unit of Modumer, we integrate the cascaded Modu-
               lation design with the downsampled Transformer block to build the attention lay-
019
               ers, enabling omni-kernel modulation and mapping inputs into high-dimensional
020
               feature spaces. Moreover, we introduce a bioinspired parameter-sharing mech-
021            anism to attention layers, which not only enhances efficiency but also improves
022            performance. Additionally, a dual-domain feed-forward network strengthens the
023            representational power of the model. Extensive experiments demonstrate that the
024            proposed Modumer achieves state-of-the-art performance on ten different datasets
025            for five image restoration tasks: image motion deblurring, image deraining, image
026            dehazing, image desnowing, and low-light image enhancement. Furthermore, our
027            model yields promising performance on all-in-one image restoration tasks.

030   1   I NTRODUCTION
031
032   As a longstanding task, image restoration aims to recover a high-quality image from its degraded
033
      counterpart. It has been quite a challenging problem as infinite solutions correspond to a single
      input. In recent years, convolutional neural networks (CNNs) have produced promising results on
034
      this ill-posed problem by learning direct mappings from the degraded input and restored output (Qin
035
      et al., 2020; Ruan et al., 2022; Lee et al., 2021; Liu et al., 2018). However, the shortcomings of
036   convolutional operators are obvious. Due to poor receptive field scaling (Cho et al., 2021; Chen et al.,
037   2024), CNNs are unable to capture long-scale dependencies for powerful image representations.
038
039
      Recently, Transformers have significantly advanced the state-of-the-art performance of low-level
      tasks (Song et al., 2022; Chen et al., 2023a; Zamir et al., 2022a). Despite having the great power
040
      to capture content-aware global perceptive fields, the self-attention (SA) layer features quadratic
041
      complexity to the input, limiting their applications in real-world scenarios. Many attempts have
042   been made to enhance the efficiency of this expensive mechanism. SwinIR (Liang et al., 2021),
043   Uformer (Wang et al., 2022), and Stripformer (Tsai et al., 2022) reduce the complexity of Trans-
044   former models by confining the SA operation to a fixed spatial range. Restormer (Zamir et al.,
045   2022a) tactfully switches the operation dimension from the spatial domain to channels. Afterward,
046   a few works explore adopting both channel SA and spatial SA in cascading or parallel manners to
047   improve representational ability (Chen et al., 2024; Zhang et al., 2024; Chen et al., 2023c). Nonethe-
048   less, these methods impede the inherent potential of SA, originally proposed for superior global fea-
049   ture modeling, leading to a deterioration in restoration performance. Moreover, they mostly operate
050
      within a single scale and cannot capture multi-scale receptive fields within a single unit.
051   Most recently, the Modulation mechanism (Ma et al., 2024b), as illustrated in Figure 1 (b), consid-
052   ering context modeling using a large-kernel convolutional block and modulating the projected input
053   via element-wise multiplication, has become popular in high-level vision tasks (Hou et al., 2024;
      Guo et al., 2023a; Yang et al., 2022). These approaches are computationally efficient and implement-

084   Additionally, we present a dual-domain feed-forward network (DFFN) to improve dual-domain rep-

091   The unit of our U-shaped Modumer is built upon the above modulation-based SA block and DFFN.
092   Unlike other Transformer-based restoration algorithms that utilize a uniform block throughout the
093   model, we adopt a channel-wise modulation-based SA block at the initial scale to enable more effi-
094
      cient global feature modeling. For lower-resolution features at deeper scales, we apply spatial-wise
      blocks, effectively capturing spatial details. Based on these designs, Modumer achieves state-of-the-
095
      art performance on several image restoration tasks with lower complexity and fewer parameters (see
096
      Figure 2). For deraining, Modumer outperforms the previous state-of-the-art method (Zhou et al.,

099   capability of deblurring. Modumer also exhibits the potential on the CSD (Chen et al., 2021) dataset
100   for the desnowing task and is superior to the previous best model (Cui et al., 2024a) by 0.74 dB in
101   terms of PSNR. Also, on the Haze4k (Liu et al., 2021b) dataset for dehazing, it obtains 34.69 dB
102   PSNR, an improvement of 0.54 dB over the previous state-of-the-art method (Cui et al., 2024a).

107            dimensional representational capability. A bioinspired parameter-sharing mechanism is

      2                           R ELATED W ORKS
132   2.1                                 I MAGE RESTORATION
133   As a fundamental vision task, image restoration aims to reconstruct a sharp image from a degraded
134   observation (Cho et al., 2021; Ruan et al., 2022). To resolve this heavily ill-posed problem, many
135   conventional algorithms have been proposed based on hand-crafted features and assumptions to re-
136   duce the solution space (He et al., 2010). Recently, deep learning methods have remarkably boosted
137   the performance of various image restoration tasks by learning generalizable features from large-
138   scale collected data. These methods can be roughly divided into CNN-based and Transformer-based
139   categories. CNN-based methods leverage attention mechanisms to attend to informative informa-
140   tion for different dimensions (Qin et al., 2020; Zamir et al., 2021; Cui et al., 2023a), e.g., pixel,
141
      spatial, and channel. Also, they employ advanced techniques to enlarge the receptive fields and
      model multi-scale features (Son et al., 2021; Liu et al., 2020; Nah et al., 2017; Jiang et al., 2020; Cui
142
      et al., 2023c), such as the encoder-decoder architecture, atrous convolution, and multi-stage learning
143
      strategy. Subsequently, Transformer methods scale the receptive field to global features via the SA
144   layer (Tsai et al., 2022; Guo et al., 2022). To enhance its efficiency on low-level vision tasks, a few
145   algorithms confine the SA region to fixed windows or strips (Wang et al., 2022; Liang et al., 2021;
146   Song et al., 2022), which impedes the inherent potential of SA. Moreover, they cannot model multi-
147   scale features within a single unit, limiting their capability for removing degradations of different
148   sizes. In this paper, we apply SA to downsampled embedding spaces to capture global dependencies
149   and use the cascaded modulation operation to complement the missing local information.
150
      2.2                                 M ODULATION DESIGN
151
152   The modulation mechanism (Ma et al., 2024b; Guo et al., 2023a) considers context modeling using
153
      a large-kernel convolutional unit and modulates the projected inputs using element-wise multipli-
      cation, which has exhibited cutting-edge performance in high-level vision tasks. FocalNet (Yang
154
      et al., 2022) utilizes a stack of depth-wise convolutional layers to implement hierarchical contex-
155
      tualization and uses gated aggregation to selectively gather contexts. Afterward, EfficientMod (Ma
156   et al., 2024b) adopts a simpler method for context modeling using a series of linear projections and
157   depth-wise convolution. MambaOut (Yu & Wang, 2024) and Conv2former (Hou et al., 2024) use
158   7 × 7 depth-wise convolutions to extract contextual features. Recently, StarNet (Ma et al., 2024a)
159   uncovers that the strong representational capacity of element-wise multiplication originates from
160   implicitly high-dimensional spaces. However, the receptive fields of the context branch in these
161   methods are limited. In contrast, our method involves long-range contextual signals by applying SA
      to downsampled embedding spaces, striking a balance between complexity and accuracy. Moreover,

      This study presents an effective and efficient Transformer model for image restoration, termed Mod-
534   umer. The model incorporates the different downsampled self-attention layers with cascaded mod-
535   ulation designs, which can model omni-receptive field features, obtain a better balance between
536   complexity and accuracy, and map features into high-dimensional spaces. Moreover, we investi-
537   gate a bioinspired parameter-sharing mechanism in attention layers, improving efficiency and per-
538   formance. In addition, we introduce a feed-forward network to facilitate intra- and inter-domain
539   interactions. Extensive experimental results on ten datasets for general image restoration and two
      all-in-one settings demonstrate the effectiveness of our model.

949   Deployment strategy for attention. We apply channel-wise modulation block in the first scale
950
      while using spatial-wise block in other scales, as spatial-wise SA is more expensive than channel ver-

951
      features. Table 13 shows that our strategy achieves the best performance. Moreover, we experiment
952
      by using only regular channel attention Zamir et al. (2022a) in all scales, achieving a 0.32 dB lower
953   performance than our full model. These results validate the efficacy of our design.

955   DFFN. We conduct more ablation studies for DFFN by removing or substituting certain operators.
956
      Table 14 shows that removing the spatial branch in inter-domain fusion achieves 31.76 dB PSNR,
      suggesting the significance of dual-domain feature fusion. Removing the attention weights leads to
957
      31.73 dB PSNR, which is even lower than the result of using a single branch, e.g., frequency branch
958
      (31.76 dB), demonstrating the importance of coordinating the fusion process.

960   Modulation design. In this part, we perform ablation studies for the modulation design. We use the
961   plain depth-wise convolutions with the same kernel size to supplant the filter operation, achieving
962
      31.69 dB PSNR, which is 0.13 dB lower than our design.
963   Parameter-sharing mechanism. In our model, we share the parameters across CMB. We carry out
964   experiments to apply the parameter-sharing strategy in deeper scales, achieving lower performance
965   than our design (see Table 15). We also attempt to further share the parameters among DFFN in the
966   first scale, obtaining only 30.53 dB PSNR. Therefore, we only apply the mechanism in CMB for
967   better performance.
968   Position of downsampling. In CMB, we apply downsampling after the convolutions, which can
969   fully learn the spatial connectivity, as the channel-wise SA layer cannot model the real spatial pixel

971   while achieving 0.11 dB lower PSNR. Finally, we choose to place downsampling after convolutions
      in the CMB of our model.
