009                                                A BSTRACT
010
011            Tabular average reward Temporal Difference (TD) learning is perhaps the sim-
012            plest and the most fundamental policy evaluation algorithm in average reward
013            reinforcement learning. After at least 25 years since its discovery, we are finally
014            able to provide a long-awaited almost sure convergence analysis. Namely, we
015            are the first to prove that, under very mild conditions, tabular average reward
016            TD converges almost surely to a sample-path dependent fixed point. Key to this
017
               success is a new general stochastic approximation result concerning nonexpansive
               mappings with Markovian and additive noise, built on recent advances in stochastic
018
               Krasnoselskii-Mann iterations.

021   1   I NTRODUCTION
022
023   Temporal Difference learning (TD, Sutton (1988)) is the most fundamental algorithm in Reinforce-
024   ment Learning (RL, Sutton & Barto (2018)). In this paper, we investigate the almost sure convergence
025   of TD in its simplest form with a tabular representation, in average reward Markov Decision Processes
026   (MDPs, Bellman (1957); Puterman (2014)). Namely, we investigate the following iterative updates
027                          Jt+1 = Jt + ╬▓t+1 (Rt+1 ŌłÆ Jt ),                          (Average Reward TD)
028                      vt+1 (St ) = vt (St ) + ╬▒t+1 (Rt+1 ŌłÆ Jt + vt (St+1 ) ŌłÆ vt (St )),
029
      where {S0 , R1 , S1 , . . . } is a sequence of states and rewards from an MDP with a fixed policy and
030
031
      a finite state space S, Jt Ōłł R is the scalar estimate of the average reward, vt Ōłł R|S| is the tabular
      value estimate, and {╬▒t , ╬▓t } are learning rates. This iterative update algorithm, known as average
032
      reward TD, dates back to at least Tsitsiklis & Roy (1999). Surprisingly, despite its simplicity and
033
      fundamental importance, its almost sure convergence had not been established in the 25 years since its
034   inception until this work. Even more surprisingly, the theoretical analysis of average reward TD with
035   linear function approximation has seen more progress than that of the tabular version we consider
036   here. In this paper, after presenting the necessary background in Section 2, we will elaborate on the
037   difficulty in analyzing tabular average reward TD with existing techniques in Section 3, offering
038   insight into why progress on this topic has been unexpectedly slow. Then we proceed to our central
039   contribution, where we prove that under mild conditions, the iterates {vt } in (Average Reward TD)
040   converge almost surely to a sample-path-dependent fixed point.
041   This almost sure convergence is achieved by extending recent advances in the convergence analysis
042   of Stochastic Krasnoselskii-Mann (SKM) iterations (Bravo et al., 2019; Bravo & Cominetti, 2024) to
043   settings with Markovian and additive noise. This line of research originates from the seminal work
044   Cominetti et al. (2014), which introduces a novel fox-and-hare race model to analyze Krasnoselskii-
045   Mann (KM) iterations (KrasnoselŌĆÖskii, 1955). By extending this line of work to Markovian settings,
046   we not only establish the almost sure convergence of average reward TD, but also pave the way for
047   further analysis of other RL algorithms through the lens of SKM iterations.

449   6   R ELATED W ORK
450
451   It is now clear that our success fundamentally originates from the novel fox-and-hare racing model
452   introduced by Cominetti et al. (2014). This fox-and-hare model is too complicated to be detailed
453   here, but it is for sure an entirely different paradigm from the ODE and Lyapunov based methods in
454   RL (Bertsekas & Tsitsiklis, 1996; Konda & Tsitsiklis, 1999; Borkar & Meyn, 2000; Srikant & Ying,
455   2019; Borkar et al., 2021; Chen et al., 2021; Zhang et al., 2022; Meyn, 2022; Zhang et al., 2023; Liu
456
      et al., 2024; Meyn, 2024). Bravo & Cominetti (2024) is the first to introduce this SKM based method
      in RL, which analyzes a synchronous version of RVI Q-learning (Abounadi et al., 2001). The method
457
      in Bravo & Cominetti (2024) is only applicable to synchronous RL algorithms because it requires
458
      Martingale difference noise. By extending Bravo & Cominetti (2024) to Markovian noise, we are the
459   first to use the SKM method to analyze asynchronous RL algorithms.
460
461   PoissonŌĆÖs equation has been very powerful in dealing with Markovian noise. In particular, the noise
462
      representation (17) is not new. However, our work bounds the error terms in (17) differently from
      previous works concerning the almost sure convergence. Namely, Benveniste et al. (1990); Konda &
463
      Tsitsiklis (1999) use stopping times to bound the error terms while Borkar et al. (2021) use scaled
464
      iterates. Instead, we rely on the 1-Lipschitz continuity (Assumption 4.2) to bound the growth of
465   the error terms directly. Moreover, previous works with such error decomposition (e.g., Benveniste
466   et al. (1990); Konda & Tsitsiklis (1999); Borkar et al. (2021)) usually only need to bound terms like
467   P          (1)                      P       (1)                                    (1)  P          (1)
         k ╬▒k  k . For our setup, besides   k ╬▒k  k , we also need to bound terms like  n =      k ╬▒k,n  k
468          (1)     P     (1)
469
      and  n = i ╬▒i  kŌłÆ1 , which appear novel and more challenging.
470
      The (Average Reward TD) algorithm has inspired the design of many other temporal difference
471   algorithms for average reward MDPs, for both policy evaluation and control, including Konda &
472   Tsitsiklis (1999); Yang et al. (2016); Wan et al. (2021a); Zhang & Ross (2021); Wan et al. (2021b);
473   He et al. (2022); Saxena et al. (2023). We envision that our work will shed light on the almost sure
474   convergence of those follow-up algorithms.

      7   C ONCLUSION
477
478
      After more than 25 years since the discovery of (Average Reward TD), we have finally established
479
      its almost sure convergence to a potentially sample-path dependent fixed point. This result highlights
480   the underappreciated strength of SKM iterations, a tool whose potential is often overlooked in
481   the RL community. Addressing several follow-up questions could open the door to proving the
482   convergence of many other RL algorithms. Do SKM iterations converge in Lp ? Do they follow
483   a central limit theorem or a law of the iterated logarithm? Can they be extended to two-timescale
484   settings? And can we develop a finite sample analysis for them? Resolving these questions could
485   pave the way for significant advancements across reinforcement learning theory. We leave them for
      future investigation.
