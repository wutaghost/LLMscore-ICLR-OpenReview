009                                                     A BSTRACT
010
011                Classifier-Free Guidance (CFG), which combines the conditional and uncondi-
012                tional score functions with two coefficients summing to one, serves as a practical
013                technique for diffusion model sampling. Theoretically, however, denoising
014                with CFG cannot be expressed as a reciprocal diffusion process, which may
015                consequently leave some hidden risks during use. In this work, we revisit the
016                theory behind CFG and rigorously confirm that the improper configuration of the
017                combination coefficients (i.e., the widely used summing-to-one version) brings
018
                   about expectation shift of the generative distribution. To rectify this issue, we
                   propose ReCFG1 with a relaxation on the guidance coefficients such that denoising
019
                   with ReCFG strictly aligns with the diffusion theory. We further show that our
020
                   approach enjoys a closed-form solution given the guidance strength. That way, the
021                rectified coefficients can be readily pre-computed via traversing the observed data,
022                leaving the sampling speed barely affected. Empirical evidence on real-world
023                data demonstrate the compatibility of our post-hoc design with existing state-
024                of-the-art diffusion models, including both class-conditioned ones (e.g., EDM2
025                on ImageNet) and text-conditioned ones (e.g., SD3 on CC12M), without any
026                retraining. We will open-source the code to facilitate further research.

029   1       I NTRODUCTION
030
031   Diffusion probabilistic models (DPMs) (Sohl-Dickstein et al., 2015; Ho et al., 2020; Song et al.,
032   2020), known simply as diffusion models, have achieved unprecedented capability improvement
033
      of high-resolution image generation. It is well recognized that, DPMs are the most prominent
      generative paradigm for a broad distribution (i.e., text-to-image generation) (Podell et al., 2024;
034
      Chen et al., 2024; Esser et al., 2024). Among DPM literature, Classifier-Free Guidance (CFG) (Ho
035
      & Salimans, 2021) serves as an essential factor, enabling better conditional sampling. Vanilla con-
036   ditional sampling via DPMs introduces the conditional score function st (x, c) = ∇xt log qt (xt |c),
037   resulting in poor performance in which synthesized samples appear to be visually incoherent and not
038   faithful to the condition, even for large-scale models (Rombach et al., 2022). By drawing lessons
039   from Bayesian theory, CFG employs an interpolation between conditional and unconditional score
040   functions with a preset weight γ, i.e.,
041
                                 st,γ (x, c) = γ∇xt log qt (xt |c) + (1 − γ)∇xt log qt (xt ),                     (1)
042
043   in which ∇xt log qt (xt ) is the unconditional score function by annihilating the condition effect.
044   By doing so, DPMs turn out to formulate the underlying distribution with a gamma-powered
045   distribution (Bradley & Nakkiran, 2024), i.e.,
046
                                               qt,γ (x|c) = qt (x|c)γ qt (x)1−γ ,                                 (2)
047
048   which is proportional to qt (x)qt (c|x)γ . Enlarging γ > 1 focuses more on the classifier effect
049   qt (c|x), concentrating on better exemplars of given condition and thereby sharpening the gamma-
050   powered distribution. In other words, CFG is designed to promote the influence of the condition.
051   However, inspired by seminal works (Bradley & Nakkiran, 2024), we argue theoretically that
052   denoising with CFG cannot be expressed as a reciprocal of vanilla diffusion process by adding

078   2024a; Bradley & Nakkiran, 2024), while being largely ignored in practice.

      In this work, we first revisit the formulation of native CFG, theoretically confirming its flaw
080   that we concluded above and summarizing as Theorem 1. Then, to quantitatively reveal the
081   consequent expectation shift phenomenon by CFG, we employ a toy distribution, enjoying closed-
082   form description of the behavior on the gamma-powered distribution. Under the toy settings, we
083   analytically calculate the function of the precise value of expectation shift in correspondence with γ,
084   as summarized in Theorem 2. Motivated by theoretical compatibility and canceling the expectation
085   shift, we apply relaxation on the guidance coefficients in native CFG by circumventing the constraint
086   that two coefficients sum to one, enabling a more flexible control on the induced distributions. To
087   be more concrete, we propose to formulate the underlying distribution with two coefficients, i.e.,
088                                      qt,γ1 ,γ0 (x|c) = qt (x|c)γ1 qt (x)γ0 .                              (3)
089
090
      Aiming at consistency with the diffusion theory and thus better guidance efficacy, we specially
      design the constraints on γ1 and γ0 , and theoretically confirm the feasibility. We further provide
091
      a closed-form solution to the constraints, and propose an algorithm to analytically determine γ0
092
      from a pre-computed lookup table in a post-hoc fashion. Thanks to the neat formulation, we can
093   employ pixel-wise γ0 according to the lookup table involving guidance strength γ1 , condition c and
094   timestep t, as demonstrated in Fig. 1. We name the above process ReCFG. Experiments with state-
095   of-the-art DPMs, including both class-conditioned ones (e.g., EDM2 (Karras et al., 2024b)) and
096   text-conditioned ones (e.g., SD3 (Esser et al., 2024)) under different NFEs and guidance strengths
097   show that our ReCFG can achieve better guidance efficacy without retraining or extra time cost
098   during inference stage. Hence, our work offers a new perspective on guided sampling of DPMs,
099   encouraging more studies in the field of guided generation.

103   DPMs and conditional generation. Diffusion probabilistic model (DPM) introduces a new scheme
104   of generative modeling, formulated by forward diffusing and reverse denoising processes (Sohl-
105   Dickstein et al., 2015; Ho et al., 2020; Song et al., 2020). It is trained by optimizing the variational
106   lower bound. Benefiting from this breakthrough, DPM achieves high generation fidelity, and even
107   beat GANs on image generation. Conditional generation (Choi et al., 2021; Huang et al., 2023)
      takes better advantage of intrinsic intricate knowledge of data distribution, making DPM easier to

      scale up and the most promising option for generative modeling. Among the literature, text-to-
109   image generation injects the embedding of text prompts to DPM, faithfully demonstrating the text
110   content (Podell et al., 2024; Chen et al., 2024; Esser et al., 2024).
111
112
      Classifier-Free Guidance. Classifier-Free Guidance (CFG) serves as the successor of Classifier
      Guidance (CG) (Dhariwal & Nichol, 2021), circumventing the usage of a classifier for noisy
113
      images. Both CFG and CG attempt to formulate the underlying distribution by concentrating more
114
      on condition influence, achieving better conditional fidelity. Despite great success in large-scale
115   conditional generation, CFG faces a technical flaw that the guided distribution is not theoretically
116   guaranteed to recover the ground-truth conditional distribution (Du et al., 2023; Karras et al., 2024a;
117   Bradley & Nakkiran, 2024). There exists a shifting issue that the expectation of guided distribution is
118   drifted away from the correct one. This phenomenon may harm the condition faithfulness, especially
119   for extremely broad distribution (e.g., open-vocabulary synthesis).

125   Let x0 ∈ RD be a D-dimensional random variable with an unknown distribution q0 (x0 |c), where
126   c ∼ q(c) is the given condition. DPM (Sohl-Dickstein et al., 2015; Song et al., 2020; Ho et al., 2020)

128   noise, i.e., the following transition distribution holds for any t ∈ (0, T ]:

134   decreasing with respect to timestep t (Kingma et al., 2021).

      Variance of lookup table over condition c. Note that we need to pre-compute the lookup table
420   consisting of expectation ratios for all conditions c, which is time-consuming and impractical for
421   open-vocabulary distributions (e.g., text-conditioned DPMs). In Tab. 3 we report the mean and

461   4.5    D ISCUSSIONS
462
463   Classifier-Free Guidance is designed from Bayesian theory to facilitate conditional sampling, yet
464   appears incompatible with original diffusion theory. Therefore, we believe ReCFG is attached to
465   great importance on guided sampling by fixing the theoretical flaw of CFG. Despite the success
466
      on better conditional fidelity, our algorithm has several potential limitations. We need to pre-
      compute the lookup table by traversing the dataset to achieve rectified coefficients for each
467
      condition. Although we conduct extensive ablation studies on the number of traversals and variance
468
      over condition c, providing an adequate strategy especially for open-vocabulary datasets on text-
469   conditioned synthesis, the optimal strategy is unexplored. Besides, we at present cannot provide
470   precise control on variance of ReCFG, and turn to employ empirical values. Therefore, how
471   to further conquer these problems (e.g., employing a predictor network ω(c, t) for better γ0 on
472   open-vocabulary datasets according to Eq. (37)) will be an interesting avenue for future research.
473   Although leaving the variance behavior unexplored, we hope that ReCFG will encourage the
474   community to close the gap in the future.

      5      C ONCLUSION
477
478
      In this paper, we analyze the theoretical flaws of native Classifier-Free Guidance technique and the
479
      induced expectation shift phenomenon. We theoretically claim the exact value of expectation shift on
480   a toy distribution. Introducing a relaxation on coefficients of CFG and novel constraints, we manage
481   to complete the theory of guided sampling by fixing the incompatibility between CFG and diffusion
482   theory. Accordingly, thanks to the closed-form solution to the constraints, we propose ReCFG, a
483   post-hoc algorithm aiming at more faithful guided sampling by determining the coefficients from a
484   pre-computed lookup table. We further study the behavior of the lookup table, proposing an adequate
485   strategy for better time efficiency in practice. Comprehensive experiments demonstrate the efficacy
      of our method on various state-of-the-art DPMs under different NFEs and guidance strengths.

         Algorithm 1 Pseudo-code to achieve lookup table of ReCFG in a PyTorch-like style.
1082
1083    1 def calculate_lookup_table(net, gnet, data_loader, timesteps):
        2     """Defines the function to maintain the lookup table.
1084    3
1085    4     Args:
        5         net: Noise prediction model for conditional score function.
1086    6         gnet: Noise prediction model for unconditional score function.
1087    7         data_loader: Dataloader to calculate score functions.
        8         timesteps: All timesteps under the given sampling trajectory.
1088    9
1089   10     Returns:
       11         coeffs: Lookup list under all timesteps and conditions.
1090   12     """
1091   13     sum1_dict, sum2_dict = dict(), dict()
       14     # Iterate for the whole dataloader.
1092   15     for x, c in data_loader:
1093   16         # Iterate for all timesteps.
       17         sum1s, sum2s = list(), list()
1094   18         for nfe_idx, t in enumerate(timesteps):
1095   19              # Forward process.
       20              noise = torch.randn_like(x)
1096   21              x_t = alpha_t * x + sigma_t * noise
1097   22
       23              # Calculate score functions first.
1098   24              eps_cond, eps_uncond = net(x_t, c, t), gnet(x_t, t)
1099   25
       26              # Calculate the expectation in Eq. (34).
1100   27              sum1s.append(eps_cond.mean(dim=0, keepdim=True))
1101   28              sum2s.append(eps_uncond.mean(dim=0, keepdim=True))
       29
1102   30         # Save the results.
1103   31         update_dict(sum1_dict, sum2_dict, c, sum1s, sum2s)
       32
1104   33     # Calculate coefficients according to Eq. (34) for all timesteps.
1105   34     coeffs = {c: sum1_dict[c] / sum2_dict[c] for c in sum1_dict}
       35
1106   36     return coeffs

        1 def guided_sampler(sampler, net, gnet, gamma_1, noise, c, timesteps, coeffs):
1113    2     """Defines the guided sampling with lookup table.
        3
1114
        4     Args:
1115    5         sampler: Native sampler without guidance, e.g., DDIM sampler.
1116    6         net: Noise prediction model for conditional score function.
        7         gnet: Noise prediction model for unconditional score function.
1117    8         gamma_1: Guidance strength similar to CFG of type ‘float‘.
1118    9         noise: Initial random noise to denoise.
       10         c: Input label.
1119   11         timesteps: All timesteps under the given sampling trajectory.
1120   12         coeffs: Pre-calculated lookup table.
       13
1121   14     Returns:
1122   15         x: A batch of samples by guided sampling.
       16     """
1123   17     # Calculate gamma_0.
1124   18     gamma_0s = (1. - gamma_1) * coeffs[c]
       19     # Ensure gamma_0 <= 0 and gamma_1 + gamma_0 >= 1.
1125   20     gamma_0s = clamp(gamma_0s, gamma_1)
       21
1126
       22     # Guided sampling using gamma_1 and gamma_0.
1127   23     x = noise
1128   24     for t, gamma_0 in zip(timesteps, gamma_0s):
       25         # Calculate score functions and apply guided sampling.
1129   26         eps_cond, eps_uncond = net(x, c, t), gnet(x, t)
1130   27         eps = eps_cond * gamma_1 + eps_uncond * gamma_0
       28         x = sampler(x, eps, t)
1131   29
1132   30     return x
