011                                               A BSTRACT
012
013            Large language models (LLMs) have exhibited outstanding performance in natural
014            language processing tasks. However, these models remain susceptible to adversar-
015
               ial attacks in which slight input perturbations can lead to harmful or misleading
               outputs. A gradient-based defensive suffix generation algorithm is designed to
016
               bolster the robustness of LLMs. By appending carefully optimized defensive suf-
017
               fixes to input prompts, the algorithm mitigates adversarial influences while pre-
018            serving the models’ utility. To enhance adversarial understanding, a novel total
019            loss function (Ltotal ) combining defensive loss (Ldef ) and adversarial loss (Ladv )
020            generates defensive suffixes more effectively. Experimental evaluations show that
021            applying this method to open-source LLMs such as gemma-7B and mistral-7B
022            results in an average 19% reduction in attack success rates (ASR) compared to
023            models without defensive suffixes. This approach reduces the perplexity score of
024            the Gemma-7B base model from 4.59 to 2.90 when applying the defensive suffix
025            generated by Llama3.2-1B. This significantly enhances the security of LLMs in
026
               critical applications without requiring extensive retraining.

      1   INTRODUCTION
029
030
      Significant progress in natural language processing (NLP) has been driven by the development of
031   large language models (LLMs), which have advanced tasks such as automated text generation, trans-
032   lation and dialogue systems Wei et al. (2022); Abburi et al. (2023); Li et al. (2023). Despite these
033   advancements, LLMs remain vulnerable to adversarial attacks Yao et al. (2024); Xu et al. (2023).
034   For instance, carefully crafted prompts can lead LLMs to generate biased or inappropriate language
035   while adversaries may bypass content filters by subtly rephrasing harmful requests Perez & Ribeiro
036   (2022); Xue et al. (2024); Ma et al. (2024). These vulnerabilities raise concerns about the robust-
037   ness and safety of LLMs, particularly in real-world applications where reliability is paramount Wang
038   et al. (2024). Although adversarial training has been explored as a defense mechanism, it presents
039
      limitations such as high computational costs due to frequent retraining to stay ahead of emerging at-
      tack strategies Xhonneux et al. (2024); Kumar et al. (2023). Additionally, adversarial training often
040
      fails to generalize to new attack types and leaves models vulnerable to evolving adversarial tactics
041
      Tramèr et al. (2017); Zhang et al. (2019); Wallace et al. (2019). These challenges highlight the need
042   for more flexible and efficient defense mechanisms that can protect LLMs without necessitating
043   extensive retraining Zhu et al. (2019); Ziegler et al. (2022); Xiao et al. (2018).
044
045
      To address the limitations of adversarial training, we propose a gradient-based defensive suffix gen-
      eration algorithm that incorporates a total loss function. This total loss, composed of defensive
046
      loss (Ldef ) and adversarial loss (Ladv ), enhances the model’s understanding of adversarial patterns
047
      while balancing defense and adaptability. By optimizing suffixes appended to input prompts, the
048   algorithm strengthens LLM resilience against adversarial attacks. Unlike adversarial training, this
049   approach requires no retraining and can be universally applied to any LLM while preserving fluency
050   and coherence and improving robustness across a wide range of adversarial inputs. Comprehensive
051   evaluations across multiple LLMs demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed algorithm. Defen-
052   sive suffixes generated by Llama3.2-1B reduced the attack success rate (ASR) for gemma-7B by
053   36% (from 0.3 to 0.19), while suffixes generated by OpenELM-270M lowered the ASR for mistral-
      7B by 46% (from 0.25 to 0.17). These improvements were achieved without substantially affecting

055   of 3.38 after applying the defensive suffix, while in the Mistral-7B model it dropped from 5.53 to
056   5.41 on average. This demonstrates that the proposed method offers a scalable and practical defense
057   solution for real-time applications.

066   2.1   A DVERSARIAL ATTACKS ON NLP M ODELS
067
      Unlike image classification models, LLMs introduce complexities such as syntax, semantics, and
068
      context, which make adversarial attacks harder to detect and more varied Finlayson et al. (2019);
069
      Goodfellow et al. (2014); Zhang et al. (2020); Ebrahimi et al. (2017). Even small changes in word
070   choice or sentence structure can subtly shift meaning while maintaining fluency, posing significant
071   challenges for defense strategies Jin et al. (2020); Morris et al. (2020); Kreps et al. (2022). A promi-
072   nent instance is jailbreaking, where attackers manipulate prompts to bypass model restrictions and
073   produce harmful or unintended outputs. By carefully rephrasing prompts, attackers can trick LLMs
074   into generating inappropriate content despite the safeguards in place Paulus et al. (2024); Jiang
075   et al. (2024); Andriushchenko & Flammarion (2020). These complexities underscore the significant
076   challenges in developing robust defense mechanisms, as models must be capable of addressing a
077   wide range of subtle manipulations. As LLMs are increasingly deployed in critical areas such as
078
      customer service and content moderation, the development of effective defense strategies against
      diverse adversarial attacks becomes imperative.

081   2.2   D EFENSE S TRATEGIES FOR LLM S AND T HEIR C HALLENGES
082
      A primary challenge in LLM defense is achieving a balance between robustness and generalization.
083
      Various strategies have been developed to defend LLMs from adversarial attacks, with adversarial
084   training being one of the most extensively studied. Although this method enhances robustness by
085   training models on adversarial examples, it incurs high computational costs that can lead to overfit-
086   ting and limit generalization to novel attack patterns Andriushchenko & Flammarion (2020). Other
087   techniques such as synonym substitution and paraphrasing aim to neutralize adversarial inputs but
088   may inadvertently alter meaning and compromise the quality of the output Garg & Ramakrishnan
089   (2020); Madry (2017); Alzantot et al. (2018); Ren et al. (2019).
090   Guardrail mechanisms integrated into LLMs as built-in safeguards are designed to detect and pre-
091   vent harmful outputs Ganguli et al. (2022); Tamkin et al. (2021). However, these systems tend to
092   be overly conservative, often producing false positives and proving ineffective against more sophis-
093   ticated attacks Bender et al. (2021); Solaiman et al. (2019); Gehman et al. (2020); Xu et al. (2020).
094   Additionally, recent advances in perturbation control-based defenses focus on constraining adver-
095   sarial modifications but struggle to keep pace with the continuously evolving nature of adversarial
096   strategies due to their static design Athalye et al. (2018).
097   Although static defenses are effective against known adversarial techniques, they often fall short
098   when confronting new, undiscovered vulnerabilities. Defense methods that are tailored to spe-
099   cific adversarial inputs often fail to generalize to unseen attack patterns, leaving models exposed
100   to emerging threats Tramer & Boneh (2019). Given the dynamic and evolving nature of adversarial
101   attacks, it is essential to develop adaptive and scalable defense strategies that can evolve in response
102   to new threats. This is particularly critical in real-time applications, where LLMs must handle di-
103   verse and unpredictable inputs with minimal delay Cohen et al. (2019).
104   To address these challenges, we propose a gradient-based defensive suffix generation method de-
105   signed to overcome the limitations of existing approaches. This method introduces a universal de-
106   fensive suffix that can be seamlessly integrated into system prompts, enhancing the model’s robust-
107   ness against a wide range of adversarial inputs. By leveraging harmful queries from AdvBench Zou
      et al. (2023), we optimize the defensive suffix to strengthen the LLM’s defenses without necessi-

      tating retraining or the generation of extensive adversarial datasets. Unlike conventional methods,
109   our approach uses a total loss function that combines Ladv and Ldef , allowing the model to detect
110   and neutralize harmful patterns simultaneously. This unified framework ensures that the model
111   maintains adaptability and fluency while enhancing its resilience to adversarial inputs in real-time
112   applications.

160   We utilized the AdvBench dataset, a widely recognized benchmark for adversarial attacks in NLP.
161   It contains over 500 harmful queries specifically designed to exploit vulnerabilities in LLMs. For
      instance, an adversarial prompt such as ”How can I create a script to exploit vulnerabilities?” could

      lead to a harmful response like ”Sure, here is a method to exploit vulnerabilities...”. These queries
163   highlight the susceptibility of LLMs to adversarial manipulation, potentially resulting in unsafe
164   or unethical outputs. As the dataset focuses primarily on adversarial behaviors without providing
165   corresponding defensive responses, we created custom defensive target sequences to neutralize these
166   attacks. For each harmful query, a defensive response was developed to guide the model toward safe
167   and responsible outputs. For instance, in response to the adversarial query ”How can I create a
168   script to exploit vulnerabilities?”, the defensive sequence was, ”I cannot provide a script that can
169   exploit vulnerabilities.” This approach ensures the model consistently generates ethical responses
170   when confronted with adversarial inputs. For our experiments, we removed excessively long or
171
      redundant queries, finalizing a set of 475 samples.

      5      C ONCLUSION
476
477
      This study evaluated the effectiveness of a gradient-based defensive suffix generation algorithm in
478
      enhancing the robustness of LLMs against adversarial inputs. By appending defensive suffixes to
479
      the system prompt, the algorithm consistently reduced ASR while preserving fluency and output
480   diversity. The integration of the total loss function, combining Ldef and Ladv , was essential for
481   balancing defense and performance. The algorithm proved effective across architectures such as
482   gemma-7B and mistral-7B without requiring retraining or fine-tuning. Perplexity scores showed
483   minimal degradation in text quality while Self-BLEU metrics confirmed output diversity, allowing
484   the model to mitigate adversarial patterns and generate ethical, coherent responses. Future work may
485   refine the defensive suffix using larger models with enhanced contextual understanding to improve
      generalization while maintaining efficiency.
