                                                  A BSTRACT
036
037            Editability and fidelity are two essential demands for text-driven image editing,
038
               which expects that the editing area should align with the target prompt and the
               rest should remain unchanged separately. The current cutting-edge editing meth-
039
               ods usually obey an “inversion-then-editing” pipeline, where the input image is
040
               first inverted to an approximate Gaussian noise zT with T steps, based on which
041            a sampling process is performed using the target prompt. Nevertheless, we argue
042            that it is not a good choice to use a near-Gaussian noise as a pivot for further
043            editing since it almost lost all structure fidelity. To verify this, we conduct a pi-
044            lot experiment and find that the target prompt has different guiding degrees to-
045            wards those latents on the inversion trajectory. Thus, a structure-preserving while
046            sufficient-for-editing point is a more suitable pivot. Based on this, we propose a
047            novel editing paradigm dubbed ZZEdit, which first locates such a pivot during the
048            inversion trajectory and then mildly strengthens target guidance via the proposed
049
               ZigZag process. Concretely, our ZigZag process fulfills denoising and inversion
               iteratively, which gradually approaches the target while still holding background
050
               fidelity. Afterwards, to achieve the same number of inversion and denoising steps,
051
               we perform a pure sampling process under the target prompt. Extensive experi-
052            ments highlight the effectiveness of our ZZEdit paradigm in diverse image editing
053            scenarios compared with the existing “inversion-then-editing” pipeline.

      1   I NTRODUCTION
055
056   Recent years, large-scale text-guided diffusion models (Saharia et al., 2022; Rombach et al., 2022;
057   Ramesh et al., 2022a; Yu et al., 2022; Gu et al., 2022) have attracted growing attention in computer
058   vision and graphics community, showing efficiency for high-quality text-to-image (T2I) synthesis.
059   To replicate this success into text-guided image editing and enable users to manipulate input images
060   according to their text prompt, early attempts usually take additional user-provided masks (Gafni
061   et al., 2022; Nichol et al., 2021; Ramesh et al., 2022b; Avrahami et al., 2023b; Mokady et al., 2022)
062   or box (Li et al., 2023). Besides, (Zhang et al., 2023; Qin et al., 2023) take more conditions for
063   fine-grained control over images e.g., depth maps, canny edges, poses, and sketches. Another line
064
      of research aims for text-only interactive image editing (Hertz et al., 2022; Tumanyan et al., 2023;
      Dong et al., 2023; Mokady et al., 2023; Cao et al., 2023; Ju et al., 2024; Bar-Tal et al., 2022; Meng
065
      et al., 2022). Since the last setting operates with minimal input conditions (i.e., only image and text)
066
      but also shows promising results for real image editing, we follow their trend in this work.
067
068   The current text-only image editing methods usually obey the “inversion-then-editing” pipeline.
069   Specifically, as the reverse of DDIM sampling (Song et al., 2020), deterministic DDIM inversion
070
      gradually adds noise to the image feature z0 for T steps until it becomes an approximate Gaussian
      noise zT . Then, Classifier-free Guidance (CFG) (Ho & Salimans, 2022) is applied for the sampling
071
      process, which denoises the inverted zT under the guidance of the target prompt. Here, we raise
072
      a question that is it a good choice to directly invert the input image to a near-Gaussian noise? To
073   answer it, as seen in Fig. 2, we conduct a pilot experiment, which probes into the DDIM inversion,
074   reconstruction, and editing process and discovers that the target prompt has different guidance de-
075   grees to those latents on the inversion/reconstruction trajectory. Thus, for the sake of both editability
076   and fidelity, we argue that instead of using a near-Gaussian latent as a pivot, a more subtle way is
077   to seek a point which keeps certain structure information while still having sufficient editability for
078   subsequent denoising processes. From another perspective, we believe that different editing exam-
079   ples need to corrupt the input image to different degrees for subtle editing.
080   In view of the above considerations, this paper proposes a simple yet effective editing paradigm,
081   dubbed ZZEdit, where the insight behind is mildly strengthening guidance at a structure-preserving
082   while sufficient-for-editing point. Specifically, starting from z0 , we are constantly increasing the
083   inversion degree, while looking for the first step on the inversion trajectory whose response to the
084   target prompt is greater than that to the source one. We use this step as the editing pivot for our
085   ZZEdit, which meets the requirements of maintaining structure and editability all at once. Then, as
086   the core of ZZEdit, we propose a ZigZag process, which performs one-step editing and inversion
087
      alternately to mildly deepen the target editing from the selected pivot. Last, a pure editing process
      is conducted to ensure equal-step inversion and sampling. Note that our ZZEdit enjoys a unique
088
      advantage in maintaining vital structure (e.g., background) information while ensuring editability
089
      since we opt for a suitable intermediate inverted latent rather than a near-Gaussian latent, making it
090   easier to alleviate the accumulated reconstruction error (Mokady et al., 2023; Dong et al., 2023).
091
092   As a novel editing paradigm, our ZZEdit can be applied to the existing inversion-based editing
093
      methods painlessly. Though simple, our ZZEdit is still principled, which casts a glance of applying
      dynamic trajectory of inversion and sampling to boost the existing editing methods. As shown in
094
      Fig. 1, we compare our ZZEdit with the typical “inversion-then-editing” pipeline using two meth-
095
      ods P2P (Hertz et al., 2022) and PnP (Tumanyan et al., 2023). Specifically, P2P supports DDIM
096   inversion and Null-Text inversion (NTI) (Mokady et al., 2023), in which the latter delivers better
097   results by optimizing unconditional textual embeddings. However, P2P+NTI often leads to lower
098   editability for shape editing (see the first row) or color leak (see the last row). Besides, PnP+DDIM
099   sometimes yields background drift. In contrast, when our ZZEdit are equipped with these editing
100   methods, more elegant editability and fidelity are achieved. To sum up, our main contributions are:
101
102          • We provide new empirical insights about locating a structure-preserving while sufficient-
103
               for-editing point during the inversion trajectory as the editing pivot.
104          • We propose a novel zero-shot image editing paradigm named ZZEdit, where a Zigzag pro-
105            cess is designed to mildly enhance the target guidance at the suitable editing pivot.
106          • Extensive qualitative and quantitative experiments demonstrate that our ZZEdit is versatile
107            across different editing methods, including P2P (Hertz et al., 2022) and PnP (Tumanyan
               et al., 2023), which achieves state-of-the-art editing performance.

      2   R ELATED W ORKS
109
110
      Text-driven Image Generation. Recent years, diffusion models (Song et al., 2020; Ho et al., 2020)
111   has shown its capacity in text-to-image (T2I) generation. DALLE-2 (Ramesh et al., 2022a) proposes
112   a two-stage model: a prior generating a CLIP (Radford et al., 2021) image embedding given a text
113   caption, and a decoder producing an image conditioned on the image embedding. Building on
114   the strength of diffusion models in high-fidelity image generation, Imagen (Saharia et al., 2022)
115   discovers that large frozen language models trained only on text data are effective text encoders
116   for text-to-image generation. Further, to enable diffusion models training on limited computational
117   resources while retaining quality, Stable Diffusion (Rombach et al., 2022) trains models in the latent
118   space of powerful pretrained autoencoders.
119   Text-driven Image Editing. Different from the general text-driven image generation, a group of
120   methods turn the model for single-image editing. SDEdit (Meng et al., 2022) first adds noise to the
121   input (e.g., stroke painting), then subsequently denoises through the prior from stochastic differen-
122   tial equation (SDE). DiffusionCLIP (Kim et al., 2022) proposes a text-guided image manipulation
123   method using the pretrained diffusion models and CLIP loss. To further improve the editing fidelity,
124   some approaches require a mask region (Avrahami et al., 2023a; 2022; Nichol et al., 2021), where
125
      the background out of the mask can remain the same while it can be time-consuming for users to
      provide a mask. Then, for text-only intuitive image editing, DiffEdit (Couairon et al., 2022) and
126
      MasaCtrl (Cao et al., 2023) automatically infer a mask according to the target prompt. P2P (Hertz
127
      et al., 2022) and PnP (Tumanyan et al., 2023) demonstrate that fine-grained control can be achieved
128   by cross-attention layers and manipulating spatial features and their self-attention inside the model
129   respectively. Besides, Imagic (Kawar et al., 2023) and UniTune (Valevski et al., 2022) conduct
130   fine-tuning on Imagen (Saharia et al., 2022) to capture the image-specific appearance, which also
131   does not need edit masks either. Further, Instructpix2pix (Brooks et al., 2023) proposes an edit-
132   ing method following instructions, where GPT-3 (Brown, 2020) and Stable Diffusion are used for
133   dataset construction. Pix2Pix-Zero (Parmar et al., 2023) can perform image-to-image translation
134   without manual prompting. Moreover, another line of techniques proposes to insert new concepts
135   into a pretrained T2I model (e.g., a specified person, bag, cup) for personalize usage (Ruiz et al.,
136
      2023; Gal et al., 2022; Dong et al., 2022; Kumari et al., 2023; Smith et al., 2023; Tewel et al., 2023).
137   Inversion in Editing Models. The commonly-used DDIM inversion scheme (Song et al., 2020;
138   Dhariwal & Nichol, 2021) conducts DDIM sampling in the reverse direction, which is effective
139   for unconditional generation (reconstruction). However, when the classifier-free guidance (Ho &
140   Salimans, 2022) is applied for editing purposes, the accumulated error of DDIM inversion would
141   magnify and lead to a poor reconstruction, thus bringing unsatisfied editing results. To address this,
142
      several methods (Dong et al., 2023; Mokady et al., 2023) propose to perform optimization on in-
      verted latents, where Null-text inversion (NTI) (Mokady et al., 2023) optimizes the unconditional
143
      textual embedding while Prompt-Tuning inversion (PTI) (Dong et al., 2023) optimizes the condi-
144
      tional embedding. There are also some techniques (Ju et al., 2024; Garibi et al., 2024; Wallace et al.,
145   2023) improve DDIM inversion without fine-tuning.
146
147   Different from the above methods, this paper takes a close look at the latent trajectory of the existing
148
      “inversion-then-editing” pipeline, which usually takes an approximate-Gaussian latent as the editing
      pivot. However, we argue that it is usually a suboptimal solution. Without additional training or fine-
149
      tuning, we propose a new editing paradigm ZZEdit, which enhances editing mildly at a structure-
150
      preserving while sufficient-for-editing point, which considers structure and editability in the latent
151   trajectory all at once.

189
190   Given an input image I and a target prompt Ptgt , text-driven image editing tries to achieve two fun-
191   damental needs: editability and fidelity. The former aims to change visual content to be consistent
192   with the textual description of Ptgt , while the latter requires the rest to remain unchanged. In this
193   section, we first analyze the existing “inversion-then-editing” pipeline in Sec. 4.1, which shows the
194   target prompt Ptgt has distinct guidance degrees to those latents on the inversion trajectory. Then,
195   the overview of the proposed ZZEdit is given in Sec. 4.2, which first locates a structure-preserving
196   while sufficient-for-editing point as the editing pivot and then performs a mild target guiding pro-
197   cess still holding structure information based on this pivot. We elaborate on these two parts in
198
      Sec. 4.3 and Sec. 4.4. Our ZZEdit paradigm can be applied painlessly in those methods which obey
      ”inversion-then-editing” and improve their performance.

      4.2         OVERVIEW OF T HE P ROPOSED ZZE DIT
243
244   Although the editability of different latent on inversion trajectory is distinct, the existing “inversion-
245   then-editing” pipeline directly inverts the input image to a near-Gaussian latent, which almost loses
246   all structure fidelity. As contrast, this paper seeks a structure-preserving while sufficient-for-editing
247   point for the subsequent target guidance. Specifically, as seen in the left part of Fig. 3, we propose a
248   new editing paradigm, dubbed ZZEdit, which can be divided into three steps:
249   (i) We first locate a suitable point in inversion trajectory as an editing pivot, which is with certain
250   structure information while still sufficient for editing, which is elaborated in Sec.4.3.
251
      (ii) A mild guiding process, named ZigZag process is conducted to enhance target guidance at the
      pivot. Specifically, it consists of alternately performing one-step denoising and one-step inversion,
252
      which is detailed in Sec.4.4.
253
      (iii) The remaining comprises a diffusion process guided by the target prompt to achieve equal-step
254   inversion and sampling. Note that when equipping the existing editing method with our ZZEdit, the
255   denoising process needs to retain the characteristics of the method, such as P2P (Hertz et al., 2022)
256   injecting cross-attention maps and PnP (Tumanyan et al., 2023) injecting self-attention maps. We
257   summarize applying our ZZEdit to the existing text-driven image editing methods in Alg. 1.

532   6     C ONCLUSION
533
534   We presented a novel zero-shot image editing paradigm, dubbed ZZEdit. Based on the observation
535   that different latents on the inversion trajectory have distinct response levels to the target prompt,
536   we proposed to select a point maintaining both structure information and sufficient editability as the
537   editing pivot. Then, a ZigZag process was designed to execute sampling and inversion alternately,
538   which mildly approach the target. Finally, we conducted a sampling process solely to keep the same-
539   step inversion and sampling. Comprehensive experiments have shown that our method achieves
      outstanding outcomes across a broad spectrum of text-driven image editing methods.
