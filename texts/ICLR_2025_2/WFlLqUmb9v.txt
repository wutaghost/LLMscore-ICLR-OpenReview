                                                 A BSTRACT
010
011
               Time-series forecasting is a long-standing challenge in statistics and machine
012
               learning, with one of the key difficulties being the ability to process sequences
013            with long-range dependencies. A recent line of work has addressed this by apply-
014            ing the short-time Fourier transform (STFT), which partitions sequences into mul-
015            tiple subsequences and applies a Fourier transform to each separately. We propose
016            the Frequency Information Aggregation (FIA-Net), a model that can utilize two
017            backbone architectures: the Window-Mixing MLP (WM-MLP), which aggregates
018            adjacent window information in the frequency domain, and the Hyper-Complex
019            MLP (HC-MLP), which treats the set of STFT windows as hyper-complex (HC)
020            valued vectors. and employ HC algebra to efficiently combine information from
021
               all STFT windows altogether. Furthermore, due to the nature of HC operations,
               the HC-MLP uses up to three times fewer parameters than the equivalent stan-
022
               dard window aggre- gation method. We evaluate the FIA-Net on various time-
023
               series benchmarks and show that the proposed methodologies outperform ex-
024            isting state-of-the-art meth- ods in terms of both accuracy and efficiency. Our
025            code is publicly available on https://anonymous.4open.science/r/
026            research-1803/.

029   1   I NTRODUCTION
030
031   Time series forecasting (TSF) is a long-standing challenge that plays a key role in various domains,
032   such as energy management Rajagukguk et al. (2020), traffic prediction Chen & Chen (2019), and
033   financial analysis Sezer et al. (2020). With the development of deep learning, myriad neural net-
034   work (NN) architectures have been proposed and have gradually improved the accuracy on the TSF
035   problem. Two key architectures that have been used for TSF are recurrent NNs (RNNs) Zhang
036   & Man (1998); Graves (2012); Chung et al. (2014) and transformers Vaswani et al. (2018); Zhou
037   et al. (2022a); Wu et al. (2021); Zhang & Yan (2023), each of which aims to capture long-term
038   dependencies through a different functional feature extraction procedure. While both methods were
039
      proven useful, RNNs struggled with long-term dependencies Pascanu et al. (2013) or non-stationary
      data patterns. While transformer architectures may overlook important temporal information due to
040
      permutation invariance Kim et al. (2024), they require many parameters and may suffer from long
041
      runtime. Additional NN-based approaches for TSF consider graph NNs (GNNs) Wu et al. (2020)
042   and decomposition models Oreshkin et al. (2019).
043
044   Recent advancements have demonstrated promising results in processing and extracting features
045
      from the frequency domain Yi et al. (2023a). Techniques leveraging frequency-based transforma-
      tions have been applied in various contexts, ranging from computational efficiency improvements
046
      Wu et al. (2021) to seasonal-trend decomposition Zhou et al. (2022a). To better process the fre-
047
      quency domain data, Yi et al. (2023b) developed a complex-valued MLP, which demonstrated su-
048   perior capability in capturing both temporal and cross-channel dependencies. To better handle non-
049   stationarities in the data, Shen et al. (2024); Tu et al. (2024); Zeng et al. (2023b) substituted the
050   standard FFT with the Short-Time Fourier Transform (STFT) Gabor (1946), which divides the se-
051   quence into separate windows and transforms each window individually into the frequency domain.
052   While showing better suitability for nonstationary time series data, the STFT yields a set of win-
053   dows, each of which represents exclusive information about the sequence. However, in practice,
      adjacent windows are highly correlated, albeit processed separately by current STFT-based models.

      To incorporate the overlooked shared information, we propose the FIA-Net, a novel TSF model
055   designed to handle long-term dependencies in the data by aggregating information from subsets
056   of STFT windows. The FIA-Net has an MLP backbone that processes the STFT windows in the
057   frequency domain. We propose two novel MLP architectures. The first is termed window-mixing
058   MLP (WM-MLP), which mixes each STFT window with its neighboring bands. The second is the
059   HC-MLP. The HC-MLP leverages HC algebra to efficiently combine information from all STFTs
060   together. By using HC algebra, the FIA-Net is implemented with three times fewer parameters than
061   the equivalent WM-MLP.
062   The main contributions of this paper are as follows:
063
064         • We construct the FIA-Net with the WM-MLP backbone. The resulting TSF model cap-
065           tures inter-window dependencies in the frequency domain and benefits from a forward pass
066           complexity of O(L log L/p) operations, where L is the lookback window length and p is
067           the number of STFT windows.
068         • We propose a novel HC-MLP backbone that expands the receptive field of the WM-MLP
069           while requiring a fraction of the total parameters.
070         • To reduce the model size and complexity, we filter the STFT windows, leaving only the
071           top-M frequency components. We show that accuracy is maintained even when M is
072           significantly smaller than the total number of components.
073
            • We provide an array of experiments that demonstrate the performance of the model and its
074
              efficiency. We show that the FIA-Net improves upon existing models’ accuracy by up to
075           20%.
076
            • We provide an ablation study, in which we explore the effect of operating over the complex
077
              plane and compare the performance of the two considered MLP backbones.

081
082   Time-Series Forecasting The first notable works on TSF utilize classical statistical linear models
083   such as ARIMA. Box & Jenkins (1968); Box & Pierce (1970) which consider series decomposition.
084   These were then generalized to a non-linear setting in Watson (1993). To overcome the limita-
085   tions posed by the classical models, deep learning was incorporated, where initially, sequential deep
086   learning was performed using RNN-based models. Two key RNN models are long-short term mem-
087
      ory networks Graves (2012), which introduce a sophisticated gating mechanism, and the DeepAR
      model Zhang & Man (1998), which connects the RNN model with AR modeling. While RNNs have
088
      demonstrated expressive power for sequential modeling, they often suffer from low efficiency and
089
      high runtimes in both the forward and backward passes Pascanu et al. (2013). To address these lim-
090   itations, two popular architectural advancements emerged: transformers and GNNs. Transformer-
091   based approaches such as Informer Zhou et al. (2023), Reformer Kitaev et al. (2020), and PatchTST
092   Nie et al. (2023) leverage the attention mechanism to effectively capture temporal dependencies
093   while introducing innovative methods to reduce the complexity of attention operations.
094
      In contrast, GNNs have been applied to better model dependencies among time series variables by
095
      representing them as nodes in a graph. This approach is particularly effective for capturing spatio-
096   temporal patterns. For instance, AGCRN Bai et al. (2020) proposed an adaptive graph convolution
097   mechanism that dynamically adjusts graph structures based on inter-series relationships. Similarly,
098   MTGNN Wu et al. (2020) integrates graph convolutions with temporal convolutional layers to jointly
099   learn spatial and temporal dependencies. However, GNNs were not specifically developed to im-
100   prove upon RNNs but rather to address unique challenges in spatio-temporal modeling.
101

104   framework to separate trend and seasonal components by leveraging the Fourier Transform on sub-
105   sequences, allowing it to isolate periodic patterns more effectively. ETSformer Woo et al. (2022)
106   combines exponential smoothing and applies attention in the frequency domain to enhance season-
107   ality modeling by capturing both short- and long-term dependencies. In FiLM Zhou et al. (2022b),
      Fourier projections are used to reduce noise and emphasize relevant features. Additionally, SFM

137   Hyper-complex Numbers HC numbers extend the complex number system to higher dimensions
138

139

140
      able performance on popular datasets. The merit of HC numbers to extract relevant information in
141   time-series was explored in Saoud & Al-Marzouqi (2020), in which an HC-net was used to analyze
142   brainwave data, and in Kycia & Niemczynowicz (2024), which explored HC-network for financial
143   data. In this work, we explore the utility of HC architectures for the efficient processing of STFT
144   windows in the frequency domain.

150   windows. We begin by discussing the existing gap in current frequency domain TSF methods,
151   followed by a brief introduction to frequency domain MLPs Yi et al. (2022). We then outline the
152   FIA-Net components, presenting the novel complex MLP backbone, discussing a simple frequency
153   compression step that reduces the MLP input dimension, and outline the complete model.

246   Complete Model The complete FIA-Net, as shown in Figure 3, operates as follows: Given an input
247   X ∈ RB×L×D , the dimension of X is expanded through a learned embedding layer, resulting in
248   XE ∈ RB×L×D×E . This expanded representation is then fed into an STFT block that uses the real
249   input to perform R − STFT on XE . The transformed signal is passed through the SM block, whose

      Even though the WM-MLP backbone integrates valuable information that benefits the FIA-Net’s
263
      accuracy, information is not only shared between two adjacent STFT windows. In fact, the stronger
264   the dependencies on the long-term past, the more information is shared between two distant windows
265   on the frequency axis. Ideally, we would like to aggregate information between all p STFT windows.
266   Unfortunately, a straightforward extension of the WM-MLP requires O(p2 ) weight matrices, which
267   may impair the training procedure and increase model complexity. To address that, we interpret the
268   set of windows as an HC vector and propose an HC-based MLP that efficiently processes the set of
269   STFT windows. We begin with a short introduction on HC-algebras, followed by the construction
      of the proposed MLP backbone for the FIA-Net.

317   between gathered windows. In such cases, the WM-MLP, which incorporates short-term information

320

446   5.3.3   R EDUNDANCY OF C OMPLEX R EPRESENTATION
447
448   We study the effect of the real and imaginary components on prediction quality. We fix the hyper-
449
      parameters E = 128, p = 13, NF F T = 16, M = Mmax , and compare several scenarios, such that
      each scenario considers the masking of a different component, either in the data, the parameters,
450
      or both. The masking occurs in both training and inference. As seen in Table 3, the elimination
451
      of either the real or imaginary components in the data does not significantly affect the downstream
452   accuracy, which may hint at redundancy in the learning procedure. Furthermore, this redundancy is
453   maintained when we consider the intersection omission of the real/imaginary parts of both the data
454   and the MLP weights. This phenomenon can be explained through the Kramers-Kronig relation
455   (KKR) Kronig (1926); Kramers (1927), which provides a representation of the real component of
456   an analytic complex-valued function in terms of its complex components and vice versa. Roughly
457   speaking, for a complex-valued function c(ω) = Re{c}(ω) + iIm{c}(ω), the KKR are given by
458
                                 1 ∞ Im{c}(σ)                           1 ∞ Re{c}(σ)
                                   Z                                      Z
459                Re{c}(ω) =                    dσ, Im{c}(ω) = −                          dσ.
460
                                 π −∞ ω − σ                             π −∞ ω − σ
461   Thus, we conjecture that masking one component forces the other to recover both in the learning
462   procedure by implicitly approximating the KRR. We therefore believe that a sophisticated system
463   design that considers a KRR-based architecture may lead to the sufficiency of a single component
464   in the forecasting task but leaves a complete study of that subject to future work. This phenomenon
465   is further explored in Appendix 8.

482   6   C ONCLUSION
483
484   This paper presents FIA-Net, a new model for long-term time series forecasting using STFT window
485   aggregation in the frequency domain and HC MLPs. The proposed methodology shows superior
      performance over existing SoTA on standard benchmark datasets. We show that treating the set of


                                                           9
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


486
      STFT windows as a single HC tensor, which is processed by a novel HC-MLP, significantly reduces
487   the total amount of parameters, with no degradation in the TSF accuracy. We study various schemes
488   to increase model efficiency by, for example, choosing the top-M magnitude frequency components.
489   Experimental results show that the omission of one of the complex representation components does
490   not induce notable segregation in performance, which may be explained by the KKR. For future
491   work, we aim to leverage the KKR equations to propose a forecasting model that only considers the
492   real component in the complex representation while operating over the complex plane. Additionally,
493   we plan to further investigate the relationship between the number of adjacent STFT windows in the
494   WM-MLP backbone and the statistical properties of the datasets.

      In this section we extend the discussion on HC numbers, considering additional values of p beyond
813   p = 4. We couple the presentation with the construction of the corresponding HC-MLP in the
814   considered base. Recall that the base of a HC number, i.e., the number of its components is given
815   by b = 2p. While hyper-complex number can be defined for any value of b, most research has been
816   performed on b that is given by a power of 2, as the resulting structure of the (algebraic) field. The
817   addition of two HC numbers is simply given by the component-wise summation. In what follows,
818   we focus on HC multiplication and additional properties. For more information on the HC number
819   system,, we refer the reader to Kantor & Solodovnikov (1989).
