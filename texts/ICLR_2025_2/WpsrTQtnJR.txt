009                                                 A BSTRACT
010
011            When modelling a real-world dataset as a graph, groups of highly correlated data
012            items correspond to densely connected vertex sets (clusters), and efficient algo-
013            rithms that find these clusters have broad applications in various data analysis
014            tasks. In this paper we study densely connected clusters in graphs and introduce
015            two sparsification algorithms that preserve the structure of these clusters in both
016            undirected graphs and directed ones. We show that our algorithms significantly
017            speedup the running time of existing clustering algorithms while preserving their
018
               effectiveness.

      1   I NTRODUCTION
021
022
      Graph clustering is a fundamental technique in data analysis with wide-ranging applications in ma-
023   chine learning and data science. A classical graph clustering problem involves partitioning the
024   vertices of a graph into sets of highly connected vertices to minimise the normalised cut value.
025   However, many real-world clustering tasks are defined by alternative objective functions, tailored to
026   the specific needs and constraints of the problem at hand. One such example involves uncovering
027   the vertex sets (clusters) that are densely connected to each other, and these clusters are connected
028   through bipartite-like graphs. For example, when representing the migration or trade datasets with a
029   graph, a pair of densely connected clusters captures regional migration or trade patterns (Cucuringu
030   et al., 2020; Laenen & Sun, 2020; He et al., 2022), and the importance of these densely connected
031
      clusters extends to various other real-world datasets (Bennett et al., 2022; Concas et al., 2022).
032   In this paper we study densely connected clusters in both undirected graphs and directed ones. We
033   first study the case for undirected graphs, and present an efficient algorithm that sparsifies an input
034   graph while preserving its densely connected clusters. For any undirected G = (V, E) and a pair of
035   disjoint non-empty subsets V1 , V2 ⊂ V , let ϕG (V1 , V2 ) be

089   To examine the significance of Theorems 1 and 2, we first highlight that our algorithms preserve the
090   cut values w(Ai , Bi ) between the pairs of vertex sets Ai and Bi for 1 ≤ i ≤ k; this objective is very
091
      different from the one for most graph sparsification problems, which only preserve the cut values
      between vertex set S and V \ S. Secondly, our algorithms preserve k pairs of densely connected
092
      clusters, and the value of k in the output graph is the same as the original input graph. Thirdly,
093
      our second result works for directed graphs; this result is very interesting on its own since most
094   sparsification algorithms are only applicable for undirected graphs.
095
096   The design of our algorithms is based on several reductions and sampling routines that can be im-
097
      plemented locally when the degree sequence of the underlying graph is available with an oracle. As
      such one can run our algorithms online while exploring the underlying graph with existing local al-
098
      gorithms that find densely connected clusters (e.g., (Andersen, 2010; Li & Peng, 2013)), resulting in
099
      direct improvement on the running time of the existing algorithms. To demonstrate this, we conduct
100   experimental studies and show that our algorithms can be directly applied to significantly speed up
101   the running times to the ones presented in (Macgregor & Sun, 2021), while preserving similar output
102   results.
103

107   pair of densely connected clusters. Cucuringu et al. (2020) proved that densely connected clusters
      in a digraph can be uncovered through spectral clustering on a complex-valued Hermitian matrix
