                                                  A BSTRACT
011
               The performance of mini-batch stochastic gradient descent (SGD) strongly de-
012
               pends on setting the batch size and learning rate to minimize the empirical loss in
013            training the deep neural network. In this paper, we present theoretical analyses of
014            mini-batch SGD with four schedulers: (i) constant batch size and decaying learn-
015            ing rate scheduler, (ii) increasing batch size and decaying learning rate scheduler,
016            (iii) increasing batch size and increasing learning rate scheduler, and (iv) increas-
017            ing batch size and warm-up decaying learning rate scheduler. We show that mini-
018            batch SGD using scheduler (i) does not always minimize the expectation of the
019            full gradient norm of the empirical loss, whereas it does using any of schedulers
020            (ii), (iii), and (iv). Furthermore, schedulers (iii) and (iv) accelerate mini-batch
021
               SGD. The paper also provides numerical results of supporting analyses showing
               that using scheduler (iii) or (iv) minimizes the full gradient norm of the empirical
022
               loss faster than using scheduler (i) or (ii).

025   1   I NTRODUCTION
026
027   Mini-batch stochastic gradient descent (SGD) (Robbins & Monro, 1951; Zinkevich, 2003;
028   Nemirovski et al., 2009; Ghadimi & Lan, 2012; 2013) is a simple and useful deep-learning opti-
029   mizer for ﬁnding appropriate parameters of a deep neural network (DNN) in the sense of minimizing
030   the empirical loss deﬁned by the mean of nonconvex loss functions corresponding to the training set.
031
      The performance of mini-batch SGD strongly depends on how the batch size and learning rate
032   are set. In particular, increasing batch size (Byrd et al., 2012; Balles et al., 2016; De et al., 2017;
033   Smith et al., 2018; Goyal et al., 2018; Shallue et al., 2019; Zhang et al., 2019) is useful for train-
034   ing DNNs with mini-batch SGD. In (Smith et al., 2018), it was numerically shown that using an
035   enormous batch size leads to a reduction in the number of parameter updates.
036
      Decaying a learning rate (Wu et al., 2014; Ioffe & Szegedy, 2015; Loshchilov & Hutter, 2017;
037
      Hundt et al., 2019) is also useful for training DNNs with mini-batch SGD. In (Chen et al., 2020),
038   theoretical results indicated that running SGD with a diminishing learning rate ηt = O(1/t) and
039   a large batch size for sufﬁciently many steps leads to convergence to a stationary point. A prac-
040   tical example of a decaying learning rate with ηt+1 ≤ ηt for all t ∈ N is a constant learning
041   rate ηt = η > 0 for all t ∈ N. However, convergence of SGD with a constant learning rate is
042   not guaranteed (Scaman & Malherbe, 2020). Other practical learning rates have been presented for
043   training DNNs, including cosine annealing (Loshchilov & Hutter, 2017), cosine power annealing
044   (Hundt et al., 2019), step decay (Lu, 2024), exponential decay (Wu et al., 2014), polynomial decay
045   (Chen et al., 2018), and linear decay (Liu et al., 2020).
046   Contribution: The main contribution of the present paper is its theoretical analyses of mini-batch
047   SGD with batch size and learning rate schedulers used in practice satisfying the following inequality:

531
      This paper presented theoretical analyses of mini-batch SGD under batch size and learning rate
532
      schedulers used in practice. Our results indicated that using increasing batch sizes and decaying
533
      learning rates guarantees convergence of mini-batch SGD and using both batch sizes and learning
534   rates that increase accelerates mini-batch SGD. That is, using increasing batch sizes and decaying
535   learning rates with warm-up guarantees fast convergence of mini-batch SGD in the sense of min-
536   imizing the expectation of the full gradient norm of the empirical loss. This paper also provided
537   numerical results to support the analysis results that increasing both batch sizes and learning rates
538   accelerates mini-batch SGD. One limitation of this study is that the numbers of models and datasets
539   in the experiments were limited. Hence, we should conduct similar experiments with larger numbers
      of models and datasets to support our theoretical results.
