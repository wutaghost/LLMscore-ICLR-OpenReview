                                                 A BSTRACT
033
034
035           Diffusion models are now commonly used for producing high-quality and diverse
036           images, but the iterative denoising process is time-intensive, limiting their usage in
037           real-time applications. As a result, various acceleration techniques have been devel-
038           oped, though these primarily target UNet-based architectures and are not directly
039           applicable to Transformer-based diffusion models (DiT). To address the specific
040           challenges of the DiT architecture, we first analyze the relationship between the
041           depth of DiT blocks and the quality of image generation. While skipping blocks
042
              can lead to large degradations in generation quality, we propose the ∆-Cache
              method, which captures and stores the incremental changes of different blocks,
043
              thereby mitigating the performance gap and maintaining closer alignment with the
044
              original results. Our analysis indicates that the shallow DiT blocks primarily define
045           the global structure of images such as compositions and outlines, while the deep
046           blocks mainly refine details, and the role of middle blocks lies between the two.
047           Based on this, we introduce a denoising property alignment method that selectively
048           bypasses computations of different blocks at various timesteps while preserving
049           performance. Comprehensive experiments on PIXART-α and DiT-XL demonstrate
050           that ∆-DiT achieves a 1.6× speedup in 20-step generation and enhances perfor-
051           mance in most cases. In the 4-step consistent model generation scenario, and with
052           a more demanding 1.12× acceleration, our approach significantly outperforms
053
              existing methods.

      1     I NTRODUCTION

056
      In recent years, the field of generative models has experienced rapid advancements. Among these,
057   diffusion models (Ho et al., 2020; Rombach et al., 2022; Song et al., 2021b) have emerged as pivotal,
058   attracting widespread attention for their ability to generate high-quality and diverse images (Dhariwal
059   & Nichol, 2021). This has also spurred the development of many meaningful applications, such
060   as image editing (Kawar et al., 2023; Zhang et al., 2023a), 3D generation (Tang et al., 2023b;a;
061   Mo et al., 2023), and video generation (Wu et al., 2023a; Khachatryan et al., 2023; Blattmann
062   et al., 2023; Luo et al., 2023b). Although diffusion models have strong generation capabilities, their
063   iterative denoising nature results in poor real-time performance. Subsequently, numerous inference
064   acceleration frameworks have been proposed, which include general model compression methods
065
      for denosing network (Fang et al., 2023; Zhang et al., 2024a; Shang et al., 2023; So et al., 2023;
      Kim et al., 2023; Salimans & Ho, 2022; Luo et al., 2023a; Zhao et al., 2023; Sauer et al., 2023), fast
066
      sampling solver (Song et al., 2021a; Lu et al., 2022a;b; Zhang & Chen, 2023; Karras et al., 2022), and
067
      cache-based acceleration methods (Ma et al., 2023; Li et al., 2023b). However, almost all of these
068   acceleration techniques are designed for the UNet-based (Ronneberger et al., 2015) architecture.
069
070   Recently, Diffusion Transformers (DiT) (Peebles & Xie, 2023) have emerged as dominant foun-
071
      dational models, exemplified by PIXART-α (Chen et al., 2023), SD3.0 (Esser et al., 2024), and
      Sora (Brooks et al., 2024). Despite this success, the acceleration of DiT inference is under-explored.
072
      Existing methods, such as early stopping (Moon et al., 2023), require retraining and are unsuitable
073
      for small-step generation. DiT’s isotropic architecture, with no long skip connections found in
074   UNet, makes it difficult to apply UNet-based acceleration techniques. For instance, cache-based
075   methods (Ma et al., 2023; Li et al., 2023b) may result in information loss, as DiT lacks the long
076   shortcuts that facilitate feature reuse in UNet. Moreover, skipping computations for branches can
077   introduce significant degradations. To address this, we propose ∆-Cache, a caching method tailored
078   for transformer architectures that caches ∆ change between different blocks instead of the original
079   feature maps, preventing large degradation and making caching more effective for DiT.

119   2    R ELATED W ORK
120
121   Efficient Diffusion Model. To improve the real-time performance of diffusion models, various
122   lightweight and acceleration techniques have emerged. Currently, methods for accelerating diffusion
123   models for image generation can be broadly categorized into three perspectives: a lightweight
124
      denoising model, and reduced denoising timestep, and the intersection of the model and timestep
      dimension. Similar to traditional model compression, many efforts focus on pruning (Fang et al.,
125
      2023; Zhang et al., 2024a), quantization (Shang et al., 2023; So et al., 2023; He et al., 2023; Li et al.,
126
      2023c), and distillation (Kim et al., 2023; Salimans & Ho, 2022; Luo et al., 2023a; Zhao et al., 2023;
127   Sauer et al., 2023) to obtain a smaller yet comparable denoising network. Besides, reduced denoising
128   timestep is a unique dimension for diffusion models. Most methods currently focus on exploring
129   efficient ODE solvers (Song et al., 2021a; Lu et al., 2022a;b; Zhang & Chen, 2023; Karras et al., 2022),
130   aiming to obtain high-quality images with fewer sampling steps. LCM (Song et al., 2023; Luo et al.,
131   2023a) proposes consistency loss and knowledge distillation to achieve the goal of fewer steps. Lastly,
132   there’s a focus on jointly optimizing denoising modes and timesteps. For instance, OMS-DPM (Liu
133   et al., 2023) and Autodiffusion (Li et al., 2023a) simultaneously optimize skips and allocate noise
134   estimation networks of specific sizes for each timestep. However, most of the aforementioned work
135
      is implemented and validated on the UNet architecture. One previous work (Moon et al., 2023)
      proposes an early stopping strategy for DiT, which cannot be easily transferred to fewer timestep
136
      settings. Therefore, there is currently a lack of novel acceleration methods specifically designed for
137
      the DiT architecture.
138
139   Cache Mechanism. The cache mechanism is a key concept in computer systems, designed to
140   temporarily store data for reuse, improving processing efficiency. In Large Language Models, the
141
      KV cache (Zhang et al., 2023b; Ge et al., 2023) is widely used, caching key and value matrices
      from attention blocks to accelerate inference. Cache-based techniques have also been applied to
142
      diffusion models, with DeepCache (Ma et al., 2023) accelerating UNet by caching feature maps
143
      from up-sampling blocks, and Faster Diffusion (Li et al., 2023b) optimizing computation by caching
144   outputs from UNet encoders. On a finer granularity, studies such as (Zhang et al., 2024b; Wimbauer
145   et al., 2024; So et al., 2024) focus on caching feature maps within specific blocks to save computations.
146   These methods target feature maps at various locations and in differing quantities as caching objectives,
147   and most of them are targeted at the UNet architecture. However, this paper introduces a feature map
148   offsets caching method, specifically tailored to the isotropic architecture of DiT.

331   Denoising property alignment framework. Therefore, in terms of outline and detail generation,
332   we can align the ∆-Cache with the denoising property. As shown in Figure 3, image denoising
333   at various timesteps (from t = T to t = 0) is presented. Since denoising properties emphasize
334   outline generation in the early stages, and the deeper blocks in the ∆-Cache method are more suited
335   for generating outlines, ∆-Cache is applied to the deep blocks during the outline generation stage.
336   Conversely, as diffusion models focus more on detail generation in the later stages, and the shallow
337   blocks of the ∆-Cache method are more appropriate for detail generation, ∆-Cache is applied to the
338   shallow blocks during the detail generation stage.

      5.4     A BLATION S TUDY
463
464   Compatibility with fast sampling solvers. Our experiments use the default solver, DPMSolver++(Lu
465   et al., 2022b), but we also demonstrate compatibility with more advanced solvers. As shown in
466   Table 4, the performance improvements are consistent across different solvers. Notably, for all
467   three solvers—EulerD (Karras et al., 2022), DEIS (Zhang & Chen, 2023), and DPMSolver++ (Lu
468   et al., 2022b)—we observe significant gains, particularly in FID scores. EulerD shows a substantial
469   improvement (FID: 39.688 → 35.735), as does DEIS (FID: 37.675 → 35.882).
470   Effect of opposite denoising property alignment. The ∆-DiT framework uses ∆-Cache for deep
471   blocks during early sampling and for shallow blocks during later stages. In this experiment, we
472   reverse the cache order, applying ∆-Cache to shallow blocks in the early stages and deep blocks in the
473   later stages. As shown in Table 5, for PIXART-α, although the CLIP score shows a minor difference,
474   FID and IS significantly deteriorate (FID: 35.882 → 41.374; IS also drops). While CLIP score reflects
475   semantic alignment with text, FID and IS better capture the image’s finer details, highlighting the
476
      effectiveness of the original caching strategy in enhancing image quality.
477   Illustration of the increasing bound b. Figure 6 illustrates the effect of the bound value on generation
478   outcomes. As b increases from 0 to 20, FID and IS improve and reach their optimal values around
479   b = 16, while the CLIP score peaks at b = 8. Given that a decreasing CLIP score can significantly
480   impact image-text alignment, we empirically determine that setting b = 12 offers the best trade-off
481   between FID, IS, and CLIP score, balancing both image quality and semantic alignment.
482   Illustration of the increasing number of cached blocks Nc . Figure 7 depicts the effect of the
483   number of cached blocks (Nc ) on generation performance. As Nc increases from 0 to 28, FID
484   reaches its best value around Nc = 14, while IS and CLIP score peak around Nc = 21. To balance
485   performance and acceleration, we select Nc = 21, which results in over 37% MACs reduction, as
      shown in Table 1.

      6    C ONCLUSION AND L IMITATION
534   This paper considers the unique structure of DiT and proposes a training-free cache mechanism,
535   ∆-Cache, specifically designed for DiT. Furthermore, we qualitatively and quantitatively explore
536
      the relationship between shallow blocks in DiT and outline generation, as well as deep blocks and
      detail generation. Based on these findings and the denoising properties of diffusion, we propose the
537
      denoising property alignment acceleration method, ∆-DiT, which applies ∆-Cache to different part
538
      blocks of DiT at various denoising stages. Extensive experiments confirm the effectiveness of our
539   approach. We believe that more refined search or learning strategies will yield even greater benefits.

      In ∆-DiT, there are two hyperparameters: the boundary b for detail generation and contour generation,
