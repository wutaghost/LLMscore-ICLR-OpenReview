                                                    Abstract
010
011
                Enhancing the versatility of pretrained diffusion models through advanced
012             conditioning techniques is crucial for improving their applicability. We
013             present APCtrl, a novel conditional image generation approach that formu-
014             lates the latent zt at timestep t as the projection zt = ProjDt (zt+1 ) onto the
015             denosing set Dt . For conditional control, APCtrl integrates the condition
016             set Ct , defined by a latent control network Aθ (·, ·). Our method simplifies
017             conditional sampling to recursive projections zt = ProjIt ◦ ProjDt (zt+1 ),
018             where each projection step integrates both the diffusion and condition
019             priors. By employing Alternative Projection, our approach offers several
020             key advantages: 1. Multi-Condition Generation: easily expandable with
021             additional conditional sets; 2. Model and Sampling Agnosticism: works
022             with any model or sampling method; 3. Unified Control Loss: simplifies
023             the management of diverse control applications; 4. Efficiency: delivers
024
                comparable control with reduced training and sampling times. Extensive
                experiments demonstrate the superior performance of our method.

190   3.1    Alternative Projection
191
192   The alternative projection method is an iterative process used to identify a point that belongs
193   to the intersection of two sets, S1 and S2 . Although projecting onto each set separately is
194   easy, projecting directly onto their intersection S1 ∩ S2 is challenging. Let the projection
195   operators onto S1 and S2 be denoted by ProjS1 and ProjS2 , respectively. The alternative
196   projection method is straightforward: beginning with any point, the vector x is iteratively
197   updated by applying the composition of projections, such that z = ProjS1 ◦ ProjS2 (z).

306
      found in SDv1.5 and was initialized using the SDv1.5 checkpoint. The training process
307   was conducted for fewer than 24 GPU hours on a single NVIDIA 3090 GPU. We employed
308   approximately 118,000 images from the COCO2017 dataset (Lin et al., 2014) across various
309   tasks. For human pose estimation, we specifically selected a subset of around 6,500 images

526   5      Conclusions
527
528   Achieving controllable generation remains one of the significant challenges in diffusion
529   models. We propose a novel direction to address this challenge. Our approach begins with
530
      reinterpreting the diffusion sampling process as a series of recursive projections onto the
      denosing set, denoted as Dt . Consequently, a conditional control diffusion model can be
531
      viewed as a sequence of recursive projections onto the intersection of feasible sets, Dt ∩ Ct ,
532
      where Ct represents the condition set. We employ an alternative projection technique to
533   effectively implement these projections onto the intersection set Dt ∩ Ct . This methodology
534   offers several distinct advantages over previous efforts: 1. Multi-Condition Generation:
535   Multi-condition generation can be easily implemented. 2. Model and Sampling Agnosti-
536   cism: APCtrl maintains agnosticism regarding both the underlying model backbones and
537   the sampling process. 3. Unified Control Loss: It allows for a unified control loss, facili-
538   tating the management of various control applications. 4. Efficiency: APCtrl significantly
539   reduces both training and sampling times. We have conducted a comprehensive evaluation
      of our framework, yielding state-of-the-art results.
