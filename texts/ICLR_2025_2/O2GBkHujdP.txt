011                                                  A BSTRACT
012
013
               Recent studies have shown that many nonconvex machine learning problems meet a so-
014
               called generalized-smooth condition that extends beyond traditional smooth nonconvex
015            optimization. However, the existing algorithms designed for generalized-smooth noncon-
016            vex optimization encounter significant limitations in both their design and convergence
017            analysis. In this work, we first study deterministic generalized-smooth nonconvex opti-
018            mization and analyze the convergence of normalized gradient descent under the general-
019            ized Polyak-Łojasiewicz condition. Our results provide a comprehensive understanding
020            of the interplay between gradient normalization and function geometry. Then, for stochas-
021            tic generalized-smooth nonconvex optimization, we propose an independently-normalized
022            stochastic gradient descent algorithm, which leverages independent sampling, gradient
023
               normalization and clipping to achieve an O(ϵ−4 ) sample complexity under relaxed as-
               sumptions. Experiments demonstrate the fast convergence of our algorithm.

027   1   I NTRODUCTION
028
      In modern machine learning, the convergence of gradient-based optimization algorithms has been well stud-
029
      ied in the standard smooth nonconvex setting. However, it has been shown recently that smoothness fails to
030   characterize the global geometry of many nonconvex machine learning problems, including distributionally-
031   robust optimization (DRO)(Levy et al., 2020; Jin et al., 2021), meta-learning (Nichol et al., 2018; Chayti &
032   Jaggi, 2024) and language models (Liu et al., 2023; Zhang et al., 2019). Instead, these problems have been
033   shown to satisfy a so-called generalized-smooth condition, in which the smoothness parameter can scale
034   with the gradient norm in the optimization process (Zhang et al., 2019).
035
      In the existing literature, various works have proposed different algorithms for solving generalized-smooth
036   nonconvex optimization problems. Specifically, one line of work focuses on the classic stochastic gradient
037   descent (SGD) algorithm (Li et al., 2024; Reisizadeh et al., 2023). However, the convergence of SGD either
038   relies on adopting very large batch size or involves large constants, and the practical performance of SGD
039   is often poor due to the ill-conditioned smoothness parameter when gradient is large. Another line of work
040   focuses on clipped SGD, which adapts to the generalized-smooth geometry by leveraging gradient clipping
041   and normalization (Zhang et al., 2019; 2020). However, to establish convergence guarantee, these studies
042   rely on the strong assumption that the stochastic approximation error is bounded almost surely.
043   Motivated by the algorithmic and theoretical limitations discussed above, this work aims to explore the
044   interplay between algorithm design and the geometry of generalized-smooth functions, and develop algo-
045   rithms tailored for generalized-smooth nonconvex optimization. To achieve this overarching goal, we need
046   to address several fundamental challenges. First, even in deterministic generalized-smooth nonconvex op-

048
      generalized-smooth problems. Thus, we want to answer the following question.
049
050   • Q1: In deterministic nonconvex optimization, how can we adapt algorithm hyperparameters to align with
051     the Polyak-Łojasiewicz geometry of generalized-smooth problems? What are the convergence rates?

054   either impractical due to poor performance or relying on strong assumptions to establish convergence guar-

      antee. Therefore, we aim to answer the following question.
056
      • Q2: Can we develop a novel algorithm tailored for stochastic generalized-smooth optimization that
057     achieves fast convergence in practice while providing convergence guarantee under relaxed assumptions?

059   In this work, we provide comprehensive answers to the above questions and develop new algorithms as well
060   as convergence analysis in generalized-smooth nonconvex optimization. In light of the above discussions,
061   we summarize our key contributions as following.

063   1.1   O UR C ONTRIBUTION
064   We first consider deterministic generalized-smooth nonconvex optimization, and study the convergence of
065   normalized gradient descent under the generalized Polyak-Łojasiewicz (PŁ) condition. Our result char-
066   acterizes the algorithm convergence rate under a broad spectrum of function geometry characterized by the
067   generalized-smooth and PŁ conditions, and provides deep insights into adapting algorithm hyper-parameters
068   (such as learning rate and gradient normalization scale) to the underlying function geometry.
069
      We then consider stochastic generalized-smooth nonconvex optimization, for which we propose a novel
070   Independently-Normalized Stochastic Gradient Descent (I-NSGD) algorithm. Specifically, I-NSGD lever-
071   ages normalized gradient updates with independent sampling and gradient clipping to reduce the bias and
072   enhance stability. Consequently, we can establish convergence of I-NSGD with O(ϵ−4 ) sample complexity
073   under a relaxed assumption on the approximation error of stochastic gradient and constant-level batch size.
074   This makes the algorithm well-suited for solving large-scale problems. We further study the convergence
075   behavior of I-NSGD under the generalized PŁ condition.
076   We compare the numerical performance of our I-NSGD algorithm with other state-of-the-art stochastic algo-
077   rithms in applications of nonconvex phase retrieval and nonconvex distributionally-robust optimization, both
078   of which are generalized-smooth nonconvex problems. Our results demonstrate the efficiency of I-NSGD in
079   solving generalized-smooth nonconvex problems and match our theoretical guidance.

      Generalized-Smoothness. The concept of generalized-smoothness was introduced by Zhang et al. (2019)
083   with the (L0 , L1 )-smooth condition, which allows a function to either have an affine-bounded hessian norm
084   or be locally L-smooth within a specific region. This idea was extended by Chen et al. (2023), who pro-
085   posed the L∗asym (α) and L∗sym (α) conditions, controlling gradient changes globally with both a constant
086   term and a gradient-dependent term associated with power α, thus applying more broadly. Later, Li et al.
087   (2024) introduced ℓ-smoothness, which use a non-decreasing sub-quadratic polynomial to control gradient
088   differences. Also, Mishkin et al. (2024) proposed directional smoothness, which preserves L-smoothness
089   along specific directions.
090   Algorithms for Generalized-Smooth Optimization. Motivated by achieving comparable lower bounds
091   presented in Arjevani et al. (2023) under standard assumptions, algorithms for solving generalized-smooth
092   problems can be categorized into two main series. The first series focus on adaptive methods. In deter-
093   ministic non-convex settings, Zhang et al. (2019; 2020) showed that Clipped GD can achieve a rate of

      5   C ONCLUSION
450   In this work, we study convergence of normalized gradient descent under generalized smooth and general-
451   ized PŁ condition. We propose independent normalized stochastic gradient descent for stochastic setting,
452   achieving same sample complexity under relaxed assumptions. Our results extend the existing boundary of
453   first-order nonconvex optimization and may inspire new developments in this direction. In the future, it is in-
454   teresting to explore if the popular acceleration method such as stochastic momentum and variance reduction
455   can be combined with independent sampling and normalization to improve the sample complexity.
