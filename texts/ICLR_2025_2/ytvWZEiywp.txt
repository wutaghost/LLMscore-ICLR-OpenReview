009                                                    A BSTRACT
010
011            This paper introduces EVINCE (Entropy and Variation IN Conditional Exchanges), a
012            framework that optimizes multi-LLM dialogues using conditional statistics and information
013            theory. EVINCE introduces dual entropy optimization to balance perspective diversity
014            with prior knowledge, providing quantitative measures for modulating LLM interactions.
015            Through information-theoretic metrics and mutual information optimization, the framework
016
               demonstrates consistent improvement over single-LLM performance in applications ranging
               from disease diagnosis to news debiasing. We present theoretical foundations and empirical
017
               validation for this structured approach to LLM collaboration.

020   1    I NTRODUCTION
021
022   Current Large Language Models (LLMs) (e.g., Anthropic (2024); OpenAI (2023); Gemini (2023)) demon-
023   strate remarkable capabilities but face significant challenges, including hallucination (generating false infor-
024   mation), bias (reflecting societal prejudices), and limited reasoning (difficulties in complex problem-solving
025   and logical inference). These challenges critically limit LLMs’ reliability and applicability in real-world
026   scenarios.
027   Multi-agent dialogue systems offer a promising direction to address these limitations. By fostering diversity
028   and debate among LLMs, these systems can potentially mitigate biases and enhance reasoning capabilities
029   through structured interaction and collaborative intelligence. However, most prior works in multi-agent
030   debate (e.g., Abdelnabi et al. (2024); Chan et al. (2023); Fu et al. (2023); Li et al. (2023); Liang et al. (2023);
031   Michael et al. (2023); Smit et al. (2024)) merely follow traditional mixture of experts approaches Jacobs et al.
032   (1991). These methods primarily use redundancy to mask errors Freund & Schapire (1997) and leverage
033
      error diversity to improve response quality. Their debates often lead to problems such as argument repetition,
      reinforcement of low-quality reasoning, or inconsistent outputs Wang et al. (2024); Zhang et al. (2024).
034
035   While traditional approaches focus on error reduction, recent work SocraSynth Chang (2023a) explored
036   dynamic modulation of debate “contentiousness” to generate diverse perspectives and then refine with
037   reasoning to improve results. By leveraging both adversarial and collaborative interactions between LLMs,
038   SocraSynth demonstrates quantifiable improvements across various domains, including healthcare Chang
039
      & et al. (2023), sales planning Tsao (2023), and emotional behavior modeling Chang (2024). However,
      its qualitative approach to modulating contentiousness in linguistic behavior limits precise control and
040
      optimization of these interactions.
041
042   In this paper, we advance this line of research by introducing EVINCE (Entropy and Variation IN Conditional
043   Exchanges), which provides quantitative foundations for multi-agent dialogue moderation. EVINCE builds
044   on three theoretical pillars:
045   1. Inclusiveness Exploration: We develop methods to ensure dialogues explore all potential perspectives.
046      We use conditional statistics to “free” an LLM agent from its default “maximum likelihood” next-token

                                                          1
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


047
         prediction behavior, allowing it to adopt specific stances. We introduce a dual entropy optimality theory
048
         to balance the exploration of new ideas with adherence to priors, thus optimizing information exchange
049      between agents for comprehensive and stable discourse.
050
      2. Information Flow Dynamics: We introduce information theory-based metrics to quantify and optimize
051
         dialogue dynamics. These measure information diversity (entropy), novelty (statistical divergence scores),
052      and inter-agent persuasion (mutual information). These metrics enable us to assess and enhance the quality
053      and efficiency of information flow within the multi-agent system, fostering rich and productive exchanges.
054   3. Reasoning Quality and Coherence: We establish frameworks to assess the logical structure and coherence
055      of multi-agent reasoning. This pillar evaluates argument validity, analytical depth, and dialogue coherence.
056      We synergistically integrate the CRIT algorithm Chang (2023b), which combines Socratic methods with
057      formal reasoning techniques, enhances our ability to conduct critical thinking through evaluating argument
058      quality, information-source credibility, and overall “reasonableness” within the dialogue. This integration
059      ensures that the collective reasoning of LLM agents is not only diverse but also logically sound and aligned
060      with the dialogue’s objectives.
061   The contributions of this paper are:
062
063   1. Framework Design: Unlike approaches using debate merely for accuracy via redundancy, EVINCE
064      facilitates information discovery, bias mitigation, and decision-making requiring both breadth and depth of
065
         information.
066   2. Theoretical Foundations: We establish EVINCE’s theoretical basis in conditional Bayesian statistics,
067      mutual information, and dual entropy. Our novel dual entropy theory demonstrates how productive
068
         decision-making should start with diverse inputs and stable objectives, then converge through information
         exchange to optimal predictions.
069
070
      3. Empirical Validation: Through studies in disease diagnosis (Section 3) and news debiasing (Appendix G),
         we validate EVINCE’s theories and derive practical maxims for optimizing information flow and prediction
071
         accuracy.

145   Figure 1 specifies the detailed operations of EVINCE. EVINCE employs two equally competent LLM
146
      instances, LLMA and LLMB , which can be different models (e.g., GPT and Claude) or independent
      instances of the same model. Given an information set S and a class-label set C, EVINCE outputs a
147
      confidence distribution over C with justifications. For example, S could represent a patient’s symptoms and
148
      C a set of diseases, with EVINCE moderating a dialogue to predict the patient’s disease(s).
149
150   Moderation Subroutines: EVINCE employs four key subroutines to manage the exploration-exploitation
151   tradeoff, ensuring information diversity, quality, and stability:
152
153
      • CRIT: Evaluates argument quality and source credibility. Low scores can trigger dialogue termination.
154
      • WD (Wasserstein distance): Assesses prediction diversity, expected to decrease over time.
155
      • MI (Mutual Information): Evaluates dialogue convergence. Stagnation can trigger termination.
156
      • Additional metrics: KL divergence, Jensen-Shannon divergence, and cross entropy ensure evaluation
        consistency (see Table 1 in Appendix A).
157
158   When all metrics plateau, indicating no further improvement, EVINCE terminates the dialogue. At this
159   stage, both LLM instances are prompted to collaboratively deliver a conciliatory conclusion that includes
160   comprehensive arguments and counterarguments. They also provide a list of missing information that, if
161   obtained, could enhance prediction accuracy and reliability, potentially using Retrieval-Augmented Generation
162   (RAG) techniques.
163
164
      Dialogue Iterations (Steps 1 and 2): Given the contentiousness level set for a dialogue iteration, each
      LLM generates a new prediction distribution on C with supporting arguments, considering the other LLM’s
165
      previous prediction and accumulated reasoning as context. The contentiousness level updates follow Maxims
166
      #2.2 and #3. The top-k predictions from LLMs are then calibrated using reasoning quality assessment
167   following Maxim #4.
168
169   Final Output (Step 3):
170
      • Combine final predictions from both LLMs.
171
      • Weight predictions based on supporting argument quality (evaluated by CRIT).
172
      • Handle uncertainty by soliciting missing information when final prediction entropy is high (Maxim #4.4).
173
174   EVINCE facilitates a structured debate between AI models, unearthing all perspectives related to the prediction
175   tasks at hand while balancing diverse viewpoints and fostering consensus to produce accurate, well-reasoned
176   predictions.

178   2.2   M AXIMS WITH T HEORETICAL F OUNDATIONS
179
      Progress towards the optimality goal is guided and measured by metrics introduced in Section 2.1. This
180
      section explains how these metrics can be used in complementary ways to facilitate proper trade-offs between
181
      diversity and convergence, exploration and exploitation, and several other factors. In the EVINCE algorithm
182   presented in Figure 1, we have annotated the steps to which these four maxims are applied.
183
184   Maxim #1. Orchestrate Two Equally Competent LLMs in Structured Debate: Integrating two equally
185   competent LLMs ensures a balanced exchange of insights and avoids bias. This adversarial setup fosters
186   diversity in predictions, each supported by justifications, promoting critical evaluation and uncovering
187   potential blind spots.

                                                        4
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


188
      Methods: Choosing LLMs with comparable performance on a shared validation set, a balanced debate can be
189
      ensured. Suitable models (in 2024) include GPT-4, Claude, and Gemini. Conditioning different instances of
190   the same LLM to support opposing stances on a subject matter can also be effective due to the theoretical
191   justification of in-context learning with conditional Bayesian statistics Xie et al. (2021).
192
193   Maxim #2. Fostering Exploration through Diverse Perspectives: While LLMs default to maximum
194
      likelihood predictions favoring popular outcomes, context conditioning can shift them toward exploration
      over exploitation. High initial contentiousness encourages dynamic debate and challenges to prevailing views,
195
      mitigating confirmation bias through contrary queries and diverse top-k predictions.
196
197   Methods: Set high initial contentiousness to stimulate exploration, enabling LLMs to challenge each other’s
198   predictions. Monitor mutual information and agreement diversity to ensure productive debate.
199   Maxim #3. Refining High-Quality Perspectives: Once new insights plateau (measured by mutual in-
200   formation, Wasserstein distance, and KL divergence), shift from exploration to exploitation by reducing
201   contentiousness.
202
      Methods: Algorithm Update(∆) guides this transition to a conciliatory stage, focusing on strengthening
203
      well-supported arguments for the final output.
204
205   Maxim #4. Combine Predictions Weighted by Diversity and Quality: Combine the probability distri-
206   butions from two LLMs by weighting them based on distributional diversity and argument quality. While
207   LLMs face fundamental challenges in generating accurate probabilities (Peng & Zhang (2024); Scholten et al.
208   (2024)), we can use reasoning-calibrated confidence scores as an effective proxy for weighting predictions.
209   Methods: Following these four maxims:
210
          • Maxim #4.1 Prediction Reliability: Use entropy-based measures to estimate reliability. Lower entropy
211
            suggests higher confidence and greater reliability in predictions.
212
          • Maxim #4.2 Argument Quality: Evaluate argument quality using CRIT, identifying logical fallacies
213
            and assessing the relevance and credibility of evidence.
214
          • Maxim #4.3 Aggregation: Apply a weighted aggregation method, such as a Bayesian model, to combine
215
            predictions, accounting for both probabilistic insights and argument quality.
216
          • Maxim #4.4 Diagnosis and RAG: If the final prediction has high entropy, indicating uncertainty,
217
            diagnose the issue and perform Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) to obtain missing information.

240   Problem Statement: Given a set of symptoms S and a context κ, the goal is to generate and rank top-k
241
      disease predictions from a set of C possible diseases. This is represented as P = LLM(S, κ), where each
      LLM generates confidence scores for its top-k predictions (k ≤ |C|) based on the input symptoms S and
242
      context κ.
243
                                              P = (p(top 1 to k ∈ C | S, κ)) .                                  (1)
244
245
      Context κ allows dual entropy adjustment through three parameters: temperature, top-k value, and con-
      tentiousness level ∆. A distribution tends toward high entropy when these parameters are set high, and toward
246
      low entropy when set low.
247
248   Note: Maxim #4 states that the initial confidence distribution P from LLMs (Eq.1) represents confidence
249   scores rather than absolute probabilities. In the EVINCE framework, these scores are calibrated through
250   multi-iteration debates where predictions must be supported by arguments and examined by counterargu-
251
      ments. The CRIT algorithm (detailed in Appendix B) evaluates reasoning quality to adjust these confidence
      scores—reducing credibility when high-confidence predictions lack strong supporting evidence.
252
253   Since EVINCE and CRIT can employ different LLMs, and CRIT focuses solely on evaluating reasoning
254   quality via Socratic methods, this collaborative approach uses reasoning quality as a proxy for confidence
255   calibration. This sidesteps the fundamental challenge of generating reliable absolute probabilities from LLMs
256   (Peng & Zhang (2024); Scholten et al. (2024)).
257   For example, if an LLM outputs confidence scores P = (0.5, 0.3, 0.2) for its top-3 predicted diseases, and
258   CRIT evaluates their supporting arguments as having strength (50%, 100%, 50%), the calibrated scores
259   become (0.25, 0.3, 0.1). After normalization, the final distribution is P = (0.38, 0.46, 0.16).

      4   C ONCLUDING R EMARKS
404
405
406   The power of EVINCE lies not only in its enhanced accuracy but also in its ability to elucidate the decision-
407   making process and identify missing information, providing critical insights for correcting errors.
408   The core strength of EVINCE is built on three theoretical pillars: Inclusive Exploration, Information Flow
409   Dynamics, and Reasoning Quality and Coherence. Grounded in conditional statistics and information theory,
410   these pillars enable LLMs to go beyond conventional “maximum likelihood” behaviors, exhibiting human-like
411   iterative adaptability in linguistic tasks. The integration of the CRIT system, which combines Socratic
412   methods with formal reasoning techniques, further strengthens critical thinking and ensures logically sound,
413   goal-oriented collective reasoning.
414   EVINCE leverages the advanced reasoning capabilities of post-GPT-4 LLMs to balance the exploration
415   of diverse perspectives with the exploitation of existing knowledge, thereby maximizing their potential.
416   Some may question how combining two LLMs, which are prone to hallucinations, can reduce those very
417   hallucinations. EVINCE accomplishes this by effectively enhancing contextual understanding and improving

      A PPENDIX B: CRIT, C RITICAL T HINKING , E VALUATIVE P HASE OF EVINCE
633
634   EVINCE uses the Socratic method to evaluate the “reasonableness” of a set of arguments that support a
635   subject matter. The Socratic method is a questioning technique used in teaching and philosophy to encourage
636   critical thinking and self-discovery Wikipedia (2023). The method involves asking a series of questions to
637
      explore complex ideas and help individuals arrive at their own understanding of a concept. It is based on the
      belief that knowledge cannot be simply imparted, but must be discovered through a process of questioning
638
      and dialogue.
639
640   To illustrate how these methods can practically be applied, let’s use the example of critical reading. Critical
641   reading is a crucial component of critical thinking, which involves evaluating the quality and credibility of
642   written materials, from research papers to blog posts Lai et al. (2017); Paul & Binker (1990). It requires a
643
      systematic and analytical approach, asking relevant questions, and using effective prompts to gain deeper
      understanding of the text Elder & Paul (2010).
644
645   To aid in critical reading, we introduce a prompt template called CRIT Chang (2023b), which stands for
646   Critical Reading Inquisitive Template. Given a document d, CRIT evaluates it and produces a validation score
647   Γ. Let Ω denote the conclusion or claim of d, and let R be the set of reasons supporting the claim. We define
648   (γr , θr ) = V(r ⇒ Ω) as the causal validation function, where γr denotes the validation score, θr the source
649
      credibility score, for each reason-to-conclusion argument r ⇒ Ω. Table 2 presents the pseudo-code of Γ =
      CRIT(d), which generates the final validation score Γ for document d with justifications.
650
651   EVINCE uses CRIT to evaluate argument quality of the participating LLMs involved in the debate. The input
652   to CRIT from each LLM is first its stance on the debate subject, e.g., a set of predicted diseases, and the
653   arguments are its reasons to arrive at the prediction. Each document in the case of EVINCE is the prediction
654   set as the conclusion Ω, the arguments as set R, and the opposing LLM’s counterarguments as R′ . With this
655
      document, CRIT is able to produce validity and credibility scores in Γ for the LLM.
656   For detailed prompts, examples, and an empirical study verifying the effectiveness of CRIT, please consult
657   Chang (2023b).

959   A PPENDIX F: E XPLAINABILITY AND R ECTIFICATION
960
961
962
      The power of EVINCE lies not only in its enhanced accuracy but also in its ability to elucidate the decision-
      making process and identify missing information, providing critical insights to correct errors.
963
964   Although the final joint prediction for Hepatitis C reached a high consensus of 37.5%, it deviates from the
965   actual condition of Jaundice, which the Kaggle dataset reports with 10% confidence. EVINCE provides
966   general practitioners with alerts and suggests remedial actions (see Appendices D.8 and E.9) to address this
967   discrepancy. Recommended actions include querying additional symptoms from the patient and conducting
968
      specific laboratory tests.
969   EVINCE initiates debates with high contentiousness, encouraging dual prediction entropy between LLMs, as
970   supported by the EDT theorem. It utilizes normalized mutual information (MI) to track shared knowledge
971   accumulation throughout the debate, while Wasserstein distance (WD) and Jensen-Shannon divergence (JSD)
972   quantify dissimilarity between LLM predictions.
973   These metrics (EDT, WD, JSD, MI) provide a comprehensive view of debate progress. WD and JSD assess
974   the potential for further communication and refinement, while MI monitors shared understanding, aiding in
975   determining the optimal stopping point.
976
      The asymmetric nature of KL divergence and cross entropy warrants further investigation. Despite eventual
977
      convergence in our case studies, discrepancies observed in the second round (one direction increasing while
978   the other decreases) suggest potential value in exploring asymmetric information. Future work will re-evaluate
979   the use of these metrics if asymmetry proves beneficial.
980
981
      Besides generating final joint disease predictions, EVINCE provides:
982    • Recommendations for additional symptom inquiries and lab tests to improve accuracy.
983
984    • Suggestions to query symptom onset, duration, severity, trends, and associated symptoms (documented in
985
         Appendices D.8 and E.9).
