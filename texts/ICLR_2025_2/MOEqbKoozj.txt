                                                                                                         A BSTRACT
008
009
               Model-free reinforcement learning algorithms have seen remarkable progress, but
010
               key challenges remain. Trust Region Policy Optimization (TRPO) is known for
011
               ensuring monotonic policy improvement through conservative updates within a
012            trust region, backed by strong theoretical guarantees. However, its reliance on
013            complex second-order optimization limits its practical efficiency. Proximal Policy
014            Optimization (PPO) addresses this by simplifying TRPO’s approach using ratio
015            clipping, improving efficiency but sacrificing some theoretical robustness. This
016            raises a natural question: Can we combine the strengths of both methods? In
017            this paper, we introduce Simple Policy Optimization (SPO), a novel unconstrained
018            first-order algorithm. SPO integrates the surrogate objective with Total Variation
019            (TV) divergence instead of Kullback-Leibler (KL) divergence, achieving a bal-
020
               ance between the theoretical rigor of TRPO and the efficiency of PPO. Our new
               objective improves upon ratio clipping, offering stronger theoretical properties and
021
               better constraining the probability ratio within the trust region. Empirical results
022
               demonstrate that SPO outperforms PPO with a simple implementation, particu-
023            larly for training large, complex network architectures end-to-end.

      1   I NTRODUCTION
045
046
      Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL) has achieved great success in recent years, notably in board
047
      games (Silver et al., 2016; 2017; 2018), video games (Mnih et al., 2015; Berner et al., 2019; Vinyals
048   et al., 2019; Ye et al., 2020), autonomous driving (Kiran et al., 2021; Zhu & Zhao, 2021; Teng et al.,
049   2023), and robotics (Makoviychuk et al., 2021; Brunke et al., 2022; Han et al., 2023). Policy gradient
050   (PG) methods (Sutton & Barto, 2018; Lehmann, 2024), as a major paradigm in RL, have been widely
051   adopted by the academic community. One main practical challenge of PG methods is to reduce the
052   variance of the gradients while keeping the bias low (Sutton et al., 2000; Schulman et al., 2015b). In
053   this context, a widely used technique is to add a baseline (usually the value function) when sampling
      an estimate of the action-value function (Greensmith et al., 2004). Another challenge of PG methods

072   2015a). Given that the training data strongly depends on the current policy, a large step size may
073   result in a collapse of policy performance, whereas a small one may impair the sample efficiency of
074   the algorithm.
075
      To address these challenges, in Trust Region Policy Optimization (TRPO), Schulman et al. (2015a)
076
      proved that minimizing a certain surrogate objective guarantees policy improvement with non-trivial
077
      step sizes. Subsequently, the TRPO algorithm was derived through a series of approximations,
078   which impose a trust region constraint during the policy iterations, leading to monotonic policy im-
079   provement in theory. However, given the complexity of second-order optimization, TRPO is highly
080   inefficient and can be hard to extend to large-scale RL environments. Proximal Policy Optimization
081   (PPO) (Schulman et al., 2017) is designed to enforce comparable constraints on the difference be-
082   tween successive policies during the training process, while only using first-order optimization. By
083   clipping the current data that exceeds the probability ratio limit to a constant, PPO attempts to re-
084   move the high incentive for pushing the current policy away from the old one. It has been shown that
085   PPO can be effectively extended to large-scale complex control tasks (Ye et al., 2020; Makoviychuk
086
      et al., 2021).
087   Despite its success, the optimization behavior of PPO remains insufficiently understood. Although
088   PPO aims to constrain the probability ratio deviations between successive policies, it often fails to
089   keep these ratios within bounds (Ilyas et al., 2018; Engstrom et al., 2020). In some tasks, the ratios
090   can even escalate to values as high as 401 (Wang et al., 2020). Furthermore, studies have revealed
091   that PPO’s performance is highly dependent on “code-level optimizations” (Andrychowicz et al.,
092
      2021). The implementation of PPO includes numerous code-level details that critically influence its
      effectiveness (Engstrom et al., 2020; Huang et al., 2022a;b).
093
094   In this paper, we propose a new algorithm named Simple Policy Optimization (SPO) designed to
095   more effectively bound probability ratios through a novel objective function. The differences in
096   optimization behavior between PPO and SPO are illustrated in Fig. 2. Our main contributions are
097   as follows:
098
099
                                                 • We theoretically prove that optimizing a tighter performance lower bound using Total Vari-
                                                   ation (TV) divergence constrained space results in more consistent policy improvement.
100
101                                              • To overcome PPO’s limitation in constraining probability ratios, we propose a new objec-
102                                                tive function, leading to the development of the proposed SPO algorithm.
103
104                                              • Experiments benchmark various policy gradient algorithms across different environments,
105
                                                   showing that SPO can achieve competitive performance with a simple implementation,
                                                   improved sample efficiency, and easier training of deeper policy networks.

525   6     C ONCLUSION
526
527   In this paper, we introduced Simple Policy Optimization (SPO), a novel unconstrained first-order
528   algorithm that effectively combines the strengths of Trust Region Policy Optimization (TRPO) and
529   Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO). By incorporating TV divergence into the surrogate objective,
530   SPO maintains optimization within the trust region, benefiting from TRPO’s theoretical guarantees
531   while preserving the efficiency of PPO. Our experimental results demonstrate that SPO can achieve
532   competitive performance across various benchmarks with a simple implementation. Moreover, SPO
533   can simplify the training of deep policy networks, addressing a key challenge faced by existing
534   algorithms. These findings indicate that SPO is a promising approach for advancing model-free
535
      reinforcement learning. In future work, SPO holds potential for impactful applications in areas such
      as autonomous driving, robotic control, game AI, and financial modeling. With further research and
536
      refinement, we believe, SPO could drive innovation and breakthroughs across these fields.

      A    R ELATED W ORK
703
704
      Previous studies have explored policy improvement within the trust region. For instance, Queeney
705   et al. (2021) developed an off-policy sample reuse method that combines the stability of on-policy
706   approaches with the sample efficiency of off-policy methods, implementing a mechanism similar
707   to the clipping mechanism in PPO. Achiam et al. (2017) proposed constrained policy optimization
708   (CPO), which restricts policy improvement to a safe region, based on new theoretical results. There
709   has also been work focused on developing novel clipping mechanisms to prevent aggressive policy
710   updates (Wang et al., 2020; Cheng et al., 2021). In terms of implementation, some studies have
711   designed adaptive learning rates based on TV divergence or KL divergence (Heess et al., 2017;
712   Queeney et al., 2021; Rudin et al., 2022), which have been shown to effectively enhance the stability
713
      of clipping objectives. Additionally, some work has explored non-parametric methods for policy
      optimization (Vuong et al., 2018; Song et al., 2019). Empirically, code-level optimizations have
714
      proven to be effective in improving the performance of PPO and the implementation details of PPO
715
      have been widely adopted (Huang et al., 2022a;b; Dhariwal et al., 2017).
716
717
718   B    I MPLEMENTATION D ETAILS
719
720   Our code implementation is primarily based on the high-quality open-source reinforcement learning
721   libraries, CleanRL (Huang et al., 2022b), with all code runs on an NVIDIA GeForce RTX 3090.
722
      For details on code-level optimizations, please refer to the mujoco and atari folders in the supple-
      mentary materials. We emphasize that no further code-level tuning is applied to SPO, except for
723
      modifications to the policy loss. Anyone can run our code to reproduce our results.
