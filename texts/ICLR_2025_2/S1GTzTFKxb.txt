026                                                                      A BSTRACT
027
028                   In the process of multimodal interaction, effective spatial-temporal information of
029                   correlated targets is crucial for RGB-T tracking. However, most existing methods
030                   only utilize spatial information for template-search matching or merely introduce
031                   an additional dynamic template with sparse temporal perception. These approaches
032
                      overlook rich temporal cues across consecutive video frames, such as target ap-
                      pearance changes and motion trajectory. To establish effective spatial-temporal
033
                      associations during multimodal interaction, we propose a video-level RGB-T track-
034
                      ing paradigm via prompt learning, termed PromptTrack. It densely models the
035                   spatial-temporal relationships of targets in multimodal contexts by incorporating
036                   streaming spatial-temporal prompts within a continuous sequence of video frames.
037                   Specifically, PromptTrack learns target changes and motion trajectory from histori-
038                   cal frames through streaming temporal prompt for each modality, and then learns
039                   multimodal spatial prompt conditioned on temporal prompt to effectively leverage
040                   multimodal complementary information. Benefiting from the proposed spatial-
041                   temporal prompt learning method, PromptTrack exhibits superior target location
042                   capability and robustness in complex tracking scenarios. The novel prompt-based
043
                      tracking paradigm can also be effortlessly extended to other tracking domains
                      such as RGB-D and RGB-E. Extensive experiments on three prevailing benchmark
044
                      datasets demonstrate our method achieves new state-of-the-art performances. In
045
                      particular, PromptTrack achieves Precision score of 76.2% and Success score of
046                   60.7% on LasHeR dataset while running at a real-time speed of 35 FPS. Codes and
047                   models will be released.

050   1     I NTRODUCTION
051
052   RGB-T tracking is a fundamental task in visual object tracking (VOT), which aims to continuously
053   locate a target in subsequent frames of multimodal video stream, typically given its initial bounding
      box in the first frame. The superior capability of leveraging complementary information from


                                                                                  1
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


054
      visible (RGB) and infrared (TIR) images to handle more complex tracking scenarios, such as low
055   illumination, adverse weather conditions, and cluttered backgrounds, has attracted significant attention
056   from researchers. It is widely applied in various fields, including autonomous driving (Zhang et al.,
057   2015), video surveillance (Tian et al., 2005), and robotic vision (Itti, 2004).
058
      The key to RGB-T tracking is how to effectively explore spatial-temporal information in a multimodal
059
      context. The complementary spatial information from aligned multimodal data helps identify the
060
      precise location of the target, while temporal information from the video stream provides insight into
061   appearance changes and motion trajectory of the target over time. According to the extent of utilizing
062   spatial and temporal information, existing RGB-T tracking methods can be roughly classified into
063   non-temporal and temporally-sparse methods, as illustrated in Figure 1 (a) and (b). Non-temporal
064   methods focus on utilizing complementary spatial information during template-search matching.
065   TBSI (Hui et al., 2023) exploits templates as the bridge to target-relevant contexts, enabling cross-
066   modal spatial interaction between RGB and TIR search regions. ViPT (Jiawen et al., 2023) generates
067   spatial prompts for RGB template and search images using an extra TIR-modality prompter. This
068   paradigm significantly advances the development of RGB-T tracking but may struggle with major
069
      target appearance changes due to relying solely on the initial template. Therefore, some methods
      (Figure 1 (b)) introduce an additional dynamic template to enhance tracking robustness against target
070
      appearance changes. STMT (Sun et al., 2024) and TATrack (Wang et al., 2024) attempt to fuse initial
071
      and dynamic multimodal templates to construct a unified target representation to adapt to certain
072   appearance changes. Despite achieving improvement, this temporally-sparse paradigm often fails in
073   situations where the target is occluded or similar distractors are present, due to its substantial reliance
074   on appearance information.
075
      Drawing inspiration from the way humans perceive and track targets in complex environments—by
076
      relying on continuous changes of the target across consecutive video frames—we aim to leverage
077
      historical information and complementary spatial information for RGB-T tracking. To this end, we
078   present a novel video-level RGB-T tracking paradigm via prompt learning, termed PromptTrack,
079   to establish effective spatial-temporal associations during multimodal interaction. As illustrated in
080   Figure 1 (c), RGB and TIR prompts (green and red blocks) are introduced into the model to learn
081   target changes and motion trajectory from dense historical frames that are stored in the template
082   memory. These prompts provide prior information to guide spatial-temporal associations of targets in
083   multimodal contexts and are propagated within a continuous sequence of video frames over time.
084   To implement the above paradigm, we design a multimodal tracking framework based on spatial-
085   temporal prompt learning. Specifically, a distinct group of learnable tokens as temporal prompts
086   are incorporated into the input for RGB and TIR modalities. The historical template images and
087   current search images are patched into images tokens. These image tokens and prompt tokens are
088   concatenated and input into a multimodal encoder for feature extraction and multimodal interaction.
089   During the process of the multimodal encoder, the temporal information about targets is learned
090   during the interaction through self-attention transformer blocks (Vaswani et al., 2017) within each
091   modality. The complementary spatial information is effectively leveraged through multimodal
092
      spatial prompt generation blocks, which also utilize temporal prompts. The interacted temporal
      prompts are propagated for the next timestep with the video streaming. The intra-modal relationship
093
      modeling and inter-modal interaction facilitate the thorough exploration of multimodal spatial-
094
      temporal information. Benefiting from the proposed method, PromptTrack exhibits superior target
095   location capability and robustness in complex multimodal tracking scenarios, effectively mitigating
096   issues such as low illumination and distractors. Due to the generality of token forms, the novel
097   tracking framework can also be effortlessly extended to other tracking domains such as RGB-D
098   and RGB-E. We conduct extensive experiments to demonstrate the effectiveness and scalability of
099   our method. The experimental results show that our method achieves significant improvements in
100   tracking performance across various complex scenarios, such as +6.0% in Precision and +4.4% in
101   Success on the LasHeR dataset compared to the most advanced tracker TATrack (Wang et al., 2024).
102   In summary, the contributions of our work are as follows: (1) A novel video-level RGB-T tracking
103   paradigm via prompt learning is proposed to establish multimodal spatial-temporal associations,
104   which can also be extended to RGB-D and RGB-E domains. (2) An effective multimodal tracking
105   framework is designed by utilizing streaming temporal prompt and multimodal spatial prompt for
106   precise target location in complex scenarios. (3) Extensive experiments demonstrate the effectiveness
107   of our method, achieving new state-of-the-art performance on three prevailing benchmark datasets.

      2       R ELATED WORK
109
110
      2.1       RGB-T TRACKING
111
112   In past years, RGB-T tracking methods have shifted from siamese-based architectures to transformer-
113   based architectures. Benefiting from the one-stream transformer encoder (Ye et al., 2022) for joint
114   feature extraction and template-search matching, researchers have tried to fuse multimodal templates
115   and search images to exploit complementary information from RGB and TIR modality. TBSI (Hui
116   et al., 2023) achieves cross-modal interaction of search images by using a fused templates as the
117   bridge. UnTrack (Wu et al., 2024) learns RGB and TIR common latent space through low-rank
118
      factorization and reconstruction techniques. BAT (Cao et al., 2024) designs a bi-directional adapter
      on top of ViPT (Jiawen et al., 2023) to mutually enhance cross-modal interaction. However, these
119
      methods focus on utilizing spatial information for multimodal template-search matching, overlooking
120
      rich temporal cues across consecutive video frames. Recent methods introduce an additional dynamic
121   template to strengthen the robustness against significant target appearance changes. TATrack (Wang
122   et al., 2024) constructs a basic template-search matching branch with the initial template and an online
123   branch with the dynamic template, enabling template interaction to embed with temporal information.
124   STMT (Sun et al., 2024) samples a dynamic template from the previous frame and enables search
125   regions to interact with both the initial template and the dynamic template through cross-attention
126   mechanism. Despite certain improvements, these temporally-sparse methods still struggle in complex
127   situations such as heavy occlusion, motion blur, and similar distractors. In contrast, PromptTrack
128   densely learns both appearance changes and motion trajectory cross consecutive frames in multimodal
129
      contexts, serving as prior guidance for the current frame to eliminate distractors.
130
131   2.2       P ROMPT LEARNING
132
      Prompt learning has demonstrated significant potential in enhancing model understanding of tasks by
133   incorporating learnable prompts in computer vision. CoOp (Zhou et al., 2022b) and CoCoOp (Zhou
134   et al., 2022a) embed learnable context prompts into the input data to help the vision model better
135   capture contextual information. MaPLe (Khattak et al., 2023) leverages multimodal prompts to
136   improve the model’s generalization capability in image recognition. These methods focus on learning
137   fixed contextual prompts, whereas we aim to learn spatial-temporal target information by dynamic
138   prompts as the video stream progresses. In RGB tracking, EVPTrack (Shi et al., 2024) employs
139   generated tokens from the initial template to propagate information across frames. HIPTrack (Cai
140   et al., 2024) directly generates temporal prompts based on historical search features. These approaches
141
      typically require complex generation and interaction modules of temporal tokens. In contrast,
      our proposed temporal prompt tokens are directly inserted into the input token sequence for each
142
      modality, obviating the need for additional modules. This streamlined design reduces our framework’s
143
      complexity in multimodal environments, while achieving notable performance improvements.
144
145   For multimodal tracking task, ViPT (Jiawen et al., 2023) learns modality-related prompts to adapt
146   frozen RGB-modality models through spatial fovea operations. OneTracker (Hong et al., 2024) regard
147
      the multimodal information as a kind of prompt and provide dominant RGB tracker with additional
      modality-specific information in a prompt-tuning manner. QueryNLT (Shao et al., 2024) proposes
148
      a visual-language tracking framework with joint appearance and language prompt modulation,
149
      leveraging the complementarity between historical visual cues and language expressions. Similarly,
150   we design a multimodal spatial prompt generation module to exploit the alignment characteristics of
151   search images, with temporal prompts guiding the generation process. Notably, our spatial prompts
152   are bidirectional, fully leveraging complementary information from RGB and TIR modalities.

199   3.2                      S TREAMING TEMPORAL PROMPT LEARNING
200
      Revisiting. Benefiting from the powerful capabilities of the self-attention mechanism (Vaswani
201
      et al., 2017), most top-performing trackers employ the one-stream paradigm for feature extraction
202   and relationship modeling. Specifically, for each modality, both the initial template image Z0 and
203   search image X are initially segmented into non-overlapping patches, flattened, projected into
204   template tokens z0 = [z1 , ..., zNz ] ∈ RNz ×D and search tokens x = [x1 , ..., xNx ] ∈ RNx ×D ,
205   where Nz and Nx denote the respective number of tokens for each image, and D represents the
206   dimension. These tokens are concatenated into a sequence of template-search tokens [z0 ; x] =
207   [z1 , ..., zNz , x1 , ..., xNx ] ∈ R(Nz +Nx )×D , and then fed into an L-layer modality-shared transformer
208   encoder. Ultimately, the extracted search features from both modalities are passed to the head for
209   prediction. This process leverages the similarity of target-relevant features to perform template-search
210   matching solely on the spatial dimension within sparse frames, thereby highlighting a gap in video
211   object tracking across consecutive frames.

214   is illustrated in Figure 2. As the video stream progresses, each frame is cropped into an intermediate
215   template image zm (0 < m < t) according to the predicted bounding box and then stored in

262   our proposed multimodal spatial prompt (MSP) block between transformer blocks of the original
263
      encoder. This method constructs a multimodal encoder that effectively facilitates multimodal spatial
      interactions within the MSP block. The model structure of PromptTrack is illustrated in Figure 3.
264

265

266   extracted RGB and TIR search features (tokens) are fed into the head to predict the bounding box of
267   the target, and the updated temporal prompt tokens are split for the next timestep.

      3.4   H EAD AND LOSS
310
311   We adopt the same structure of the Center head as described in OSTrack (Ye et al., 2022) to predict
312   the bounding box, which consists of stacked Conv-BN-ReLU layers. The overall loss function is:
313
                                       L = Lcls + λGIoU LGIoU + λL1 LL1                               (11)
314
315   where Lcls represents the weighted focal loss (Law & Deng, 2018) for classification, LGIoU denotes
316   the generalized IoU loss (Rezatofighi et al., 2019), and LL1 corresponds to the bounding box
317   regression loss. Additionally, the weighted factors λGIoU and λL1 are set to 2 and 5, respectively.

321   4.1   I MPLEMENTATION DETAILS
322
323   We adopt the ViT-Base (Kolesnikov et al., 2021) as the original encoder, which is pretrained on
      popular single object tracking datasets (SOT) (Ye et al., 2022). Our MSP block is inserted after the


                                                           6
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


324
      3-rd, 6-th, 9-th, and 12-th blocks of the encoder for multimodal interaction. The template image size
325   is 128 × 128 and the search region size is 256 × 256. The number of learnable temporal tokens N p for
326   each modality are set to 4, corresponding to the variables needed to represent a bounding box. We crop
327   the new template image centered on the predicted box and push it into the historical template queue.
328   The weights of the transformer blocks and head are initialized with the pretrained OSTrack-256
329   model (Ye et al., 2022). The inserted blocks and learnable temporal tokens are initialized with random
330   weights. The total batch size is 16. The learning rate is set to 4 × 10−5 for the transformer blocks
331   and head, and 4 × 10−6 for the remaining components. We use the AdamW optimizer (Loshchilov &
332   Hutter, 2017) with weight decay of 1 × 10−4 . Horizontal flip and brightness jittering are used for
333
      data augmentation.
334   Training. The training process requires 20 epochs based on the LasHeR training set (Li et al., 2021),
335   with each epoch comprising 40k samples. The process is divided into two stages: the first stage
336   involves training for 10 epochs to learn the relationship modeling between image tokens and initial
337   temporal tokens, with each sample containing k template images and 1 search image. The second
338   stage consists of another 10 epochs, where each sample contains k template images and 2 search
339
      images, focusing on learning temporal information propagation conditioned on the updated temporal
      tokens. The sample interval is set to 400 within a single video sequence. All training stages are
340
      performed on two NVIDIA 3090 GPUs using Python 3.9, Pytorch 2.0.0, and CUDA 11.7. The same
341
      training configuration is employed across all experiments, including ablation studies. The inference
342   speed (FPS) is evaluated on one NVIDIA 3090 GPU, 12th Gen Intel(R) Core(TM) i9-12900K CPU
343   and 64GB of memory.

      4.2    M ETRICS FOR RGB-T TRACKING
346
347   We adopt two widely used metrics, Precision rate (PR) and Success rate (SR), to evaluate tracking
348   performances. PR is the percentage of video frames in which the Euclidean distance between the
349   center coordinates of the predicted box and the ground truth (GT) within a certain threshold (typically
350   20 pixels). SR is the proportion of frames where the Intersection over Union (IoU) between the
351   predicted bounding box and the ground truth exceeds a predefined overlap threshold.

475
       In this paper, we have presented a novel video-level RGB-T tracking paradigm via prompt learning,
       which learns rich temporal cues and complementary spatial information across consecutive frames.
476
       Our PromptTrack significantly improves performance in complex tracking scenarios by incorporating
477
       streaming temporal prompts about appearance changes and motion trajectories of targets from
478    historical templates. The multimodal spatial information is efficiently utilized conditioned on the
479    temporal information, eliminating the need for complex spatial fusion module designs. The prompt-
480    based framework can be also extended to other multimodal tracking domains. We hope PromptTrack
481    can facilitate the exploration of spatial-temporal information for multimodal tracking in the future.
482
483    Limitations. One limitation of our method is the requirement for substantial storage resources to
484    retain all historical templates, as well as significant computational resources to perform interactions
485    between all input images. It would be interesting to explore the use of extracted target tokens for
       storage and computation.
