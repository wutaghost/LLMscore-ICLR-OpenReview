                                                   A BSTRACT
013
014
               Sharp generalization bound for neural networks trained by gradient descent (GD)
015
               is of central interest in statistical learning theory and deep learning. In this pa-
016
               per, we consider nonparametric regression by an over-parameterized two-layer
017            NN trained by GD. We show that, if the neural network is trained by GD with
018            early stopping, then the trained network renders a sharp rate of the nonparametric
019            regression risk of O(ε2n ), which is the same rate as that for the classical kernel
020            regression trained by GD with early stopping, where εn is the critical popula-
021            tion rate of the Neural Tangent Kernel (NTK) associated with the network and
022            n is the size of the training data. It is remarked that our result does not require
023            distributional assumptions on the covariate as long as the covariate lies on the
024            unit sphere, in a strong contrast with many existing results which rely on spe-
025
               cific distributions such as the spherical uniform data distribution or distributions
               satisfying certain restrictive conditions. As a special case of our general result,
026                                                                                          d

027            when the eigenvalues of the associated NTK decay at a rate of λj ≍ j − d−1 for
028
               j ≥ 1 which happens under certain distributional assumption such as the train-
               ing features follow the spherical uniform distribution, we immediately obtain the
029                                                d

030
               minimax optimal rate of O(n− 2d−1 ), which is the major results of several existing
               works in this direction. The neural network width in our general result is lower
031
               bounded by a function of only d and εn , and such width does not depend on the
032
               minimum eigenvalue of the empirical NTK matrix whose lower bound usually
033            requires additional assumptions on the training data. Our results are built upon
034            two significant technical results which are of independent interest. First, uniform
035            convergence to the NTK is established during the training process by GD, so that
036            we can have a nice decomposition of the neural network function at any step of

046   1   I NTRODUCTION
047
048   With the stunning success of deep learning in various areas of machine learning (LeCun et al., 2015),
049   generalization analysis for neural networks is of central interest for statistical learning learning and
050   deep learning. Considerable efforts have been made to analyze the optimization of deep neural net-
051   works showing that gradient descent (GD) and stochastic gradient descent (SGD) provably achieve
052   vanishing training loss (Du et al., 2019b; Allen-Zhu et al., 2019b; Du et al., 2019a; Arora et al., 2019;
053   Zou & Gu, 2019; Su & Yang, 2019). There are also extensive efforts devoted to generalization anal-
      ysis of deep neural networks (DNNs) with algorithmic guarantees, that is, the generalization bounds


                                                         1
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


054
      for neural networks trained by gradient descent or its variants. It has been shown that with sufficient
055   over-parameterization, that is, with enough number of neurons in hidden layers, the training dynam-
056   ics of deep neural networks (DNNs) can be approximated by that of a kernel method with the kernel
057   induced by the neural network architecture, termed the Neural Tangent Kernel (NTK), while other
058   studies such as (Yang & Hu, 2021) show that infinite-width neural networks can still learn features.
059   The key idea of NTK based generalization analysis is that, for highly over-parameterized networks,
060   the network weights almost remain around their random initialization. As a result, one can use the
061   first-order Taylor expansion around initialization to approximate the neural network functions and
062   analyze their generalization capability (Cao & Gu, 2019; Arora et al., 2019; Ghorbani et al., 2021).
063   Many existing works in generalization analysis of neural networks focus on clean data, but it is a
064   central problem in statistical learning that how neural networks can obtain sharp convergence rates
065   for the risk of nonparametric regression where the observed data are corrupted by noise. Consider-
066   able research has been conducted in this direction which shows that various types of DNNs achieve
067   optimal convergence rates for smooth (Yarotsky, 2017; Bauer & Kohler, 2019; Schmidt-Hieber,
068   2020; Jiao et al., 2023; Zhang & Wang, 2023) or non-smooth (Imaizumi & Fukumizu, 2019) tar-
069
      get functions for nonparametric regression. However, most of these works do not have algorithmic
      guarantees, that is, the DNNs in these works are constructed specially to achieve optimal rates with
070
      no guarantees that an optimization algorithm, such as GD or its variants, can obtain such constructed
071
      DNNs. To this end, efforts have been made in the literature to study the minimax optimal risk rates
072   for nonparametric regression with over-parameterized neural networks trained by GD with either
073   early stopping (Li et al., 2024) or ℓ2 -regularization (Hu et al., 2021; Suh et al., 2022). However,
074   most existing works either require spherical uniform data distribution on the unit sphere (Hu et al.,
075   2021; Suh et al., 2022) or certain restrictive conditions on the data distribution.
076
      It remains an interesting and important question for the statistical learning and theoretical deep
077
      learning literature that if an over-parameterized neural network trained by GD can achieve sharp
078   risk rates for nonparametric regression with milder assumptions or restrictions on the distribution
079   of the covariate, so that theoretical guarantees can be obtained for data in more practical scenar-
080   ios. In this paper, we give a confirmative answer to this question. We present sharp risk rate for
081   nonparametric regression with an over-parameterized two-layer NN trained by GD with early stop-
082   ping, which is distribution-free in spherical covariate. Throughout this paper, distribution-free in
083   spherical covariate means that there are no distributional assumptions about the covariate as long as
084   the covariate lies on the unit sphere. Furthermore, our results give confirmative answers to certain
085   open questions or address particular concerns in the literature of training over-parameterized neural
086
      networks by GD with early stopping for nonparametric regression with minimax optimal rates, such
      as the characterization of the stopping time in the early-stopping mechanism, the lower bound for
087
      the network width, and the constant learning rate used in GD. Benefiting from our analysis which
088
      is distribution-free in spherical covariate, our answers to these open questions or concerns do not
089   require distributional assumptions about spherical covariate. Section 3 summarizes our main results
090   with their significance and comparison to existing works.

      is trained, while the weights of the second layer are randomly initialized and then fixed in the training
