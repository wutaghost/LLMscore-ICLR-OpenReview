009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            Segment Anything Models (SAMs) like SEEM and SAM have achieved great
012            performance on various downstream datasets at the cost of crafting spatial and
013            semantic prompts. Previous prompt learning methods can learn prompts automati-
014            cally but largely focus on learning semantic prompts, while how to learn effective
015            spatial prompts that are important to SAMs is largely under-explored. Inspired by
016            Hough Voting that detects a complex object by voting from its parts, we propose
017            Hough Voting-based Spatial Prompt Learning (HoughSpaPL) that designs three
018
               types of voting mechanisms to learn three distinct spatial prompts for different sub-
               regions of the visual concept (e.g., things and stuff), which capture complementary
019
               spatial clues and vote together to guide SAMs to generate a precise segmentation
020
               mask for the visual concept. Following the same philosophy, we design Hough
021            Voting-based Semantic Prompt Learning (HoughSemPL) that learns distinct se-
022            mantic prompts for different sub-regions of the visual concept, which capture
023            complementary semantic clues and vote together to predict a accurate semantic
024            label for the generated mask. Extensive experiments show that our proposed tech-
025            niques achieve superior prompt learning performance over popular segmentation
026            datasets. Codes will be released.

029   1   I NTRODUCTION
030
031   Segment Anything Models (SAMs), like Segment Everything Everywhere Model (SEEM) Zou
032   et al. (2023) and Segment Anything Model (SAM) Kirillov et al. (2023), have recently shown great
033
      zero-shot generalization performance in image segmentation across various downstream tasks and
      datasets Cordts et al. (2016); Zhou et al. (2017). The remarkable success of SAMs are largely
034
      attributed to the novel “Promptable Segmentation” framework, in which the model takes handcrafted
035
      prompts as inputs and predicts the expected segmentation masks accordingly. Specifically, promptable
036   segmentation usually involves two categories of prompts comprising (1) spatial prompts such as
037   points, and (2) semantic prompts like free-form texts, where the former provides spatial clues while
038   the latter provides semantic information which together guide the model to segment and classify the
039   expected visual concepts.
040
      On the other hand, manually crafting proper prompts for each downstream task and dataset not
041
      only requires domain expertise Zhou et al. (2022b;a); Liu et al. (2021) but also is often tedious
042   and time-consuming Zhou et al. (2022b;a); Parisot et al. (2023). To tackle this issue, prompt
043   learning (Zhou et al., 2022b;a; Liu et al., 2021) has been extensively studied, which learns effective
044   prompts by optimizing learnable prompt vectors with few-shot data, such as CoOp (Zhou et al.,
045   2022b), LOCN (Parisot et al., 2023) and PLOT (Chen et al., 2022). However, previous prompt
046   learning methods Zhou et al. (2022b); Parisot et al. (2023); Chen et al. (2022) largely focus on
047   learning semantic prompts that provide semantic clues for category classification, while how to learn
048   effective spatial prompts that could provide spatial clues to guide SAMs to locate and segment the
049   expected visual concepts accurately is largely under-explored.
050   Inspired by Hough Voting Ballard (1981); Woodford et al. (2014); Qi et al. (2019); Samet et al.
051   (2022) that detects a complex object based on the voting from its parts, we propose to learns distinct
052   spatial prompts for different sub-regions of the visual concept (e.g., things and stuff), which capture
053   complementary spatial clues and vote together to guide SAMs to generate a precise segmentation
      mask for the visual concept, as shown Figure 1. To this end, we propose Hough Voting-based Prompt

113   2     R ELATED WORKS
114
115   Segmentation Anything Models (SAMs) have achieved impressive zero-shot generalization and
116   interactive segmentation performance over a wide range of datasets. SAMs such as SAM Kirillov et al.
117   (2023), SEEM Zou et al. (2023), and Semantic-SAM Li et al. (2023) represent significant advances
118
      in image segmentation by introducing a promptable architecture. They process handcrafted spatial
      prompts (i.e., points, boxes, or masks) and/or semantic prompts (i.e., textual descriptions) to generate
119
      corresponding segmentation masks, facilitating the application in various scenarios. Fast-SAM Zhao
120
      et al. (2023) enhances inference speed, while HQ-SAM Ke et al. (2024) improves segmentation
121   quality. However, acquiring effective prompts for SAMs is a non-trivial problem that is largely
122   under-explored. We focus on learning effective prompts that improves the SAMs’ performance on
123   task-specific or domain-specific downstream datasets with limited labeled samples.
124
      Prompt Learning aims to transfer a foundation model towards downstream domain by optimizing
125
      prompts with limited labeled data. It has been extensively researched in NLP foundation mod-
126
      els Zhong et al. (2021); Li & Liang (2021); Lester et al. (2021); Liu et al. (2023) and image
127   classification foundation models (CFMs) Zhou et al. (2022b); Parisot et al. (2023); Zang et al. (2022),
128   but is still in its early stages Huang et al. (2024) in SAMs. For NLP foundation models, prompt
129   learning learns contextual text tokens to append and improve the raw questions through various
130   techniques Jiang et al. (2020); Shin et al. (2020); Zhong et al. (2021); Li & Liang (2021); Lester et al.
131   (2021). In CFMs, prompt learning Zhou et al. (2022b); Parisot et al. (2023); Bulat & Tzimiropoulos
132   (2022); He et al. (2022) optimizes text tokens to improve image classification based on image descrip-
133   tions. Our research is built upon SSprompt Huang et al. (2024), which is the first to introduce prompt
134   learning for SAMs. Different from SSprompt that directly learns semantic/spatial prompts for SAMs,
135
      we design a more effective prompt learning method. It explores the idea of Hough voting to learn
      complementary spatial and semantic prompts that focus on multi-granularity clues with different
136
      spatial focus, and vote together to guide SAMs to segment effectively.
137
138   Hough Voting (Hough, 1959; Ballard, 1981) was widely-used in shape detection and object recog-
139   nition. It reformulates the detection of patterns as the identification of peaks in a parametric space,
140   which was further extended to allowing for recognize arbitrary shapes. Hough Voting was employed
141
      in various tasks including the implicit shape model for object recognition (Leibe et al., 2008), plane
      extraction from 3D point clouds (Borrmann et al., 2011), and 6D pose estimation (Sun et al., 2010;
142
      Kehl et al., 2016). Learning-based extensions of Hough voting also emerged, where weighted
143
      votes were introduced to indicate significance in object detection (Maji & Malik, 2009). Hough
144   forests (Gall et al., 2011; Gall & Lempitsky, 2013) combined ensembling with Hough Voting to
145   improve detection accuracy. HoughNet (Samet et al., 2020; 2022) aggregates feature votes within
146   neural network, resulting in precise object detection. Hough voting has also been applied to 3D object
147   recognition (Knopp et al., 2010; Woodford et al., 2014; Velizhev et al., 2012; Knopp et al., 2011; Qi
148   et al., 2019). Inspired by Hough Voting, this work aims to learn complementary spatial prompts and
149   semantic prompts that focus on multi-granularity spatial clues of objects, and vote together to guide
150   SAMs to generate a precise segmentation mask for the objects.

247   3.2     H OUGH VOTING - BASED P ROMPT L EARNING
248
249   We focus on prompt learining for SAMs with a few labeled downstream domain data. We identified
250   that one instance can be complementarily described through different views of spatial prompts
251   and their semantic prompts, which focus on regions with various attributes. Inspired by Hough
252   Voting (Ballard, 1981; Woodford et al., 2014; Qi et al., 2019; Samet et al., 2022) that detects a
253   object based on the voting from its parts, we propose to learns distinct spatial prompts for different
254   sub-regions of the object, which capture complementary spatial clues and vote together to guide
255   SAMs to generate a precise segmentation mask for the object. To this end, we design Hough voting
256
      based prompt learning (HoughPL) that introduces Hough Voting-based Spatial Prompt Learning
      (HoughSpaPL) and Hough Voting-base Semantic Prompt Learning (HoughSemPL), as illustraed in
257
      Figure 3. The two prompt learning methods complement each other by learn three spatial/semantic
258
      prompts with different spatial/semantic focus respectively and vote together to prompt SAMs .

404
      Prompt Learning for SAM on Common Datasets. Table 1 reports the image segmentation results
405   on three widely-used common datasets: Cityscapes, Mapillary Vistas, and ADE20K. HoughPL
406   demonstrates significant performance gains over the baseline across these datasets. Specifically,
407   HoughPL outperforms the state-of-the-art methods by 5.5 in mIoU for semantic segmentation, 5.5 in
408   AP for instance segmentation, and 5.5 in PQ for panoptic segmentation on average. This highlights
409   the superiority of HoughPL in effectively transferring pretrained SAM to various segmentation
410   dataset with limited labeled dataset. The superior performance of HoughPL largely attributed to its
411   ability of capturing multi-granularity spatial and semantic information, which allows it to focus on
412   multi-granularity spatial clues from the inner, middle, and outer regions of objects. Additionally,
413
      HoughPL facilitates comprehensive contextual understanding, enabling it to adapt flexibly to diverse
      segmentation scenarios across datasets. All compared methods improve the performance while
414
      HoughPL achieves the most substantial perfomance gains, demonstrating that HoughPL can effec-
415
      tively align the pretrained model’s representation with the various segmentation tasks by efficiently
416   learning complementary spatial and semantic prompts from multi-granularity spatial and semantic
417   clues. Note that due to the inefficiency of converting the high-resolution Mapillary Vistas dataset to
418   COCO-style instance annotations, we only report the results for panoptic and semantic segmentation
419   on this dataset.
420
      Prompt Learning for SAM on Adverse-Condition Datasets. Table 2 reports the experimental
421
      results on autonomous driving datasets under various weather and time conditions. Notably, HoughPL
422   consistently surpasses all other methods by significant margins, which demonstrate its effectiveness
423   and robustness in adapting SAM to adverse conditions with considerable variations in lighting and
424   scene content. The performance gains achieved by HoughPL in adverse conditions highlight the
425   effectiveness of the designed voting mechanisms as well as learning complementary spatial and
426   semantic prompts with multi-granularity spatial focus from limited labeled samples.

      5    C ONCLUSION
513
      This paper presents HoughPL, a novel prompt learning framework that employs voting mechanisms
514
      to adapt SAMs towards various downstream segmentation dataset. By designing inner-region voting
515
      mechanisms, middle-region voting mechanisms, and outer-region voting mechanisms that capture
516   multi-granularity spatial cues, HoughPL learns multiple complementary spatial and semantic prompts
517   and votes them together to guide SAMs for more effective segmentation. By effectively guiding
518   SAMs to adapt to downstream datasets with limited labeled data, HoughPL significantly improves
519   segmentation performance in both common and challenging adverse-condition scenarios. Exten-
520   sive experiments across multiple widely-adopted segmentation datasets demonstrate that HoughPL
521   consistently outperforms state-of-the-art methods by clear margins.
