010                                                A BSTRACT
011
012            Stock market prediction has remained an extremely challenging problem for many
013            decades owing to its inherent high volatility and low information noisy ratio.
014            Existing solutions based on machine learning or deep learning demonstrate superior
015            performance by employing a single model trained on the entire stock dataset to
016
               generate predictions across all types of stocks. However, due to the significant
               variations in stock styles and market trends, a single end-to-end model struggles to
017
               fully capture the differences in these stylized stock features, leading to relatively
018
               inaccurate predictions for all types of stocks. In this paper, we present MIGA, a
019            novel Mixture of Expert with Group Aggregation framework designed to generate
020            specialized predictions for stocks with different styles by dynamically switching
021            between distinct style experts. To promote collaboration among different experts
022            in MIGA, we propose a novel inner group attention architecture, enabling experts
023            within the same group to share information and thereby enhancing the overall
024            performance of all experts. As a result, MIGA significantly outperforms other
025            end-to-end models on three Chinese Stock Index benchmarks including CSI300,
026            CSI500, and CSI1000. Notably, MIGA-Conv reaches 24 % excess annual return on
027
               CSI300 benchmark, surpassing the previous state-of-the-art model by 8% absolute.
               Furthermore, we conduct a comprehensive analysis of mixture of experts for stock
028
               market prediction, providing valuable insights for future research.

      1    I NTRODUCTION
032
033
      The stock market, a complex dynamic system, has long attracted investors worldwide who seek
034
      to generate high returns through risk-taking and achieve their investment objectives. However,
035
      due to the inherent volatility and noisy characteristics of financial markets (Fama, 1970; Malkiel,
036   2003), forecasting stock prices accurately and making profitable investment decisions are extremely
037   challenging tasks. These challenges are considered to stem from the interplay of multiple stock
038   factors, including market participants’ behavior (Shiller, 2003), macroeconomic variables (Chen,
039   1988), and the difficult-to-quantify flow of information (Grossman & Stiglitz, 1980).
040
      Over past decades, machine learning (ML) and deep learning (DL) methods have shown remarkable
041
      capabilities in stock market prediction by learning from a dataset comprised of various stock factors
042   in an end-to-end manner. Most existing ML or DL methods adopt an advanced network architectures
043   derives from some basic networks like LSTM (Hochreiter, 1997) and Transformer (Vaswani et al.,
044   2017). For instance, FactorVAE (Duan et al., 2022) introduces a novel approach to predicting excess
045   returns and benchmark returns separately using Variational Auto-Encoders (Kingma, 2013). MAS-
046   TER (Li et al., 2024) proposes a transformer-based model that addresses complex stock correlations
047   through alternating intra-stock and inter-stock information aggregation. These methods adopt stock
048   market prediction as a supervised learning task, where the historical stock factors is always seemed
049   as features and the return rate is applied as the target for a single end-to-end model. Therefore, the
050
      inherent temporal characteristics of stock market enable end-to-end models to effectively predict
      future stock returns.
051
052   However, in the context of financial markets, stocks of different styles often tend to exhibit significant
053   variations in their features of stock factors. For instance, large-cap stocks are stable and offer
      predictable growth, appealing to risk-averse investors seeking modest returns. But mall-cap stocks

096   The results demonstrate that our MIGA models exhibit superior performance, outperforming all other

098   excess annual return with a long-only portfolio. Furthermore, we provide an in-depth analysis of the
099   Mixture of Expert for stock prediction, providing valuable insights for future work.

106   The router assigns weights for each data point, while each expert generates its own prediction. The
107   final output of the MoE is the weighted aggregation of the predictions from all experts. In this section,
      we present the methodology by which MIGA generates predictions and undergoes training process.

221   Load Balance Consideration Automatically learned routing strategies are susceptible to load
222
      imbalance, which can lead to the phenomenon of routing collapse (Shazeer et al., 2017a). Specifically,
      this occurs when the model consistently favors a subset of experts, thereby depriving other experts of
223
      adequate training opportunities and hindering their ability to contribute to the overall performance of
224
      the model. In many existing MoE architectures designed for training large language models (Zoph
225   et al., 2022; Dai et al., 2024), token load balance auxiliary loss (Shazeer et al., 2017a) is employed to
226   ensure that all tokens are distributed as evenly as possible among experts. However, this approach is
227   not directly applicable to stock market prediction tasks, as there is no analogous concept of tokens in
228   stock market. Hence, we reduce this imbalance by minimizing the distance between the router output

244
245   To instantiate MIGA, we leverage PyTorch to implement the entire MoE architecture and utilize

      in LMIGA at 2e-3, 1 respectively. Following previous work (Li et al., 2024), we set the history stock

461
462   Scaling up the number of experts To investigate the effect of expert aggregation size on model
463   performance, we conducted an experiment using the MIGA architecture, with results illustrated in
464   Figure 4. The findings indicate that increasing the number of experts leads to improved ICIR and
465   RankICIR scores, suggesting that this approach can substantially enhance the stability of the model’s
466   prediction. To further explore the upper limit of the model’s potential, we selected 8 out of 63 routed
467
      experts (organized into 7 groups of 9 experts each) for in-depth analysis in this paper.
468   Impact of inner group attention To evaluate the efficacy of inner group attention, we compare
469   MIGA with a mixture of isolated experts that does not incorporate inner group attention. As shown
470   in Table 3, MIGA outperforms the mixture of isolated experts and demonstrates a more balanced
471   performance across the CSI300, CSI500, and CSI1000 benchmarks. This improvement can be
472   attributed to the enhanced overall level of experts achieved through inner group attention, highlighting
473
      the effectiveness of inner group attention in facilitating more efficient and effective knowledge sharing
      among experts in Mixture of Experts (MoE) systems.

476    3.6          A NALYSIS ON E XPERT S PECIALIZATION
477
      To evaluate the expert specialization in MIGA, we conduct individual predictions on the test set using
478
      each expert in MIGA-Conv and compare their excess annual returns across various stock benchmarks
479
      and portfolios. As shown in Figure 5 and 6 (in Appendix A), the majority of experts demonstrate
480   accurate predictions, with only 7 out of 63 experts failing to generate excess returns. This suggests
481   that each expert has acquired sufficient predictive capabilities during MIGA’s training. Furthermore,
482   different experts exhibit varying levels of proficiency across different areas. For instance, in MIGA-
483   Conv (Figure 5), Expert 3 achieves an impressive excess return of nearly 30% on CSI300 but fails
484   to replicate this performance on CSI500 and CSI1000. In contrast, Expert 39 delivers a relatively
485   high excess return on CSI500, but only mediocre results on CSI300 and CSI1000. This highlights
      the specialization of MIGA’s experts for different types of stocks. Moreover, a similar specialization

      4    R ELATED WORK
492
493
      Stock market prediction Recent advancements in stock market prediction have been propelled
      by end-to-end methods such as Temporal Convolutional Network (TCN) (Hewage et al., 2020),
494
      LSTM (Greff et al., 2016) and Transformer Vaswani et al. (2017). Although these end-to-end models
495
      were not originally designed for stock market forecasting, subsequent research (Ding et al., 2020;
496   Nelson et al., 2017; Sun et al., 2023) has validated their effectiveness in this domain, which motivated
497   us to propose MIGA and investigate its model structure.
498
499
      Mixture of Experts The mixture-of-experts (MoE) approach has been widely adopted and extensively
      researched since its introduction over two decades ago (Jacobs et al., 1991). Many MoE models
500
      have achieved remarkable success in various challenging fields, including computer vision (Riquelme
501
      et al., 2021; Wang et al., 2021) and natural language processing (Shazeer et al., 2017b; Du et al.,
502   2022). The effectiveness of this approach is further demonstrated by the success of MoE-based large
503   language models, such as DeepSeekMoe (Dai et al., 2024) and Mixtral (Jiang et al., 2024). However,
504   despite the broad applicability of MoE methods, a notable gap exists in the development of MoE
505   frameworks specifically tailored to quantitative investment in stochastic financial markets. which
506   inspire us to further explore it and propose MIGA.
507
508
      5    CONCLUSION
509
510
      This paper presents MIGA, a series of Mixture of Experts with Group Aggregation models that
511   combines different expert for stock market prediction. Our approach demonstrates the potential of
512   integrating Mixture of Experts framework in quantitative investment, enabling end-to-end models to
513   tackle stochastic stock market. MIGA achieves state-of-the-art performance on 3 Chinese Stock Index
514   benchmarks, substantially outperforming existing end-to-end forecasting approaches. Furthermore,
515   we conduct systematic analysis of the benefits aspire for this work to provide valuable insights for
516   future research, contributing to the development of more advanced real-world market solution.
