009                                              A BSTRACT
010
011            This paper tackles the challenge of generating clothing-disentangled 3D charac-
012            ters from a single image. Existing approaches typically employ multi-layer 3D
013            representations to model the body and each garment and then iteratively opti-
014            mize these representations to fit the observations, which is time-consuming and
015            not scalable. To address this, we propose the first feed-forward method enabling
016            efficient and robust clothing disentanglement. Our approach first generates the
017            multi-view images for each component of the clothed character and then employs
018
               a generalizable multi-view reconstruction method to create the 3D models of each
               component. For high-quality disentanglement, we propose a two-stage disentan-
019
               glement approach that first disentangles each component in the 2D image space
020
               and then generates the multi-view images for each part. During the 2D component
021            disentanglement stage, we introduce a novel multi-part diffusion model that allows
022            information exchange among different components. Additionally, for component
023            combination, we incorporate a novel combination attention mechanism into the
024            multi-view diffusion model, enabling the integration of information from multiple
025            parts to create the final combined character. For training, we have contributed a
026            large clothing-disentangled character dataset consisting of more than 10k anime
027            characters. Extensive experiments demonstrate that our proposed approach not
028            only facilitates efficient and high-quality disentangled 3D character generation
029
               with distinct clothing layers but also supports various cloth editing applications.

      1   I NTRODUCTION
033
      The creation of 3D clothed character models is critically important across a range of applications,
034
      including film, augmented and virtual reality (AR/VR), and video gaming. In many applications,
035
      it is crucial to model the character’s body and clothing separately, allowing users to freely change
036   garments, thereby achieving controllable editing capabilities. However, manually creating such
037   clothing-disentangled 3D characters is labor-intensive and time-consuming. Artists typically need to
038   create individual components and then assemble them into complete clothed characters. Moreover,
039   since the created clothing is often tailored to specific characters, it cannot be directly transferred
040   to others, which may lead to issues such as penetration or misalignment. Therefore, automatically
041   generating these clothing-disentangled character models from simple inputs (e.g., a single image)
042   while enabling seamless clothing interchangeability presents a significant challenge.
043   Existing methods for creating clothing-disentangled 3D characters typically rely on optimization-
044   based approaches. Based on input 3D avatar scans or textual descriptions of clothed avatars, these
045   methods typically employ multi-layer 3D representations to model the body and each garment and
046   then iteratively optimize these representations to fit the observations. The optimization leverages
047   the powerful 2D diffusion model to implement Score Distillation Sampling (SDS) loss, which en-
048   ables the disentangled reconstruction of clothing and the human body. Although these methods have
049   achieved impressive results, they typically require multiple optimization processes to fully disentan-
050
      gle all clothing layers, with each optimization being quite time-consuming. This significantly limits
      their scalability and practicality.
051
052   In this paper, we propose the first feed-forward approach for efficiently generating clothing-
053   disentangled 3D characters from a single image. The proposed approach achieves disentangled
      reconstruction significantly faster than existing optimization-based methods, reducing the process

089   performance. During 2D component disentanglement, our key insight is that different clothing

117   Clothed human modeling. In the initial stages of research on clothed human modeling, most
118   works utilized parametric human meshes Bogo et al. (2016). These parametric models were en-

124   the recently proposed 3D Gaussian representations Kerbl et al. (2023) have significantly enhanced

127
      Although achieving remarkable results, these methods typically treat clothing and the human body
128   as a unified entity, which hinders effective clothing manipulation and limits the range of applications.
129   To achieve decoupled modeling of clothing and the body, some studies Yu et al. (2018a); Pons-Moll
130   et al. (2017); Yu et al. (2019); Chen et al. (2021); Jiang et al. (2020); Hu et al. (2023); Corona et al.

136
      scope of 3D clothing datasets, these clothing models often lack diversity. Unlike parametric models

137
      fuse observations from RGB-D sequences, achieving high-quality reconstructions of clothing. Ad-
138

139   generate multi-layer 3D assets from a given single-layer clothed 3D human mesh. Others Zhang
140   et al. (2023); Wang et al. (2023a); Dong et al. (2024) have even investigated generating multi-layer
141   representations (e.g., NeRF and Mesh) directly from textual descriptions of clothing. Furthermore,
142   Feng et al. (2022) proposed methods for reconstructing clothing disentangled human models from
143   monocular videos using photometric loss. Although high-quality decoupled reconstructions have
144   been achieved, these methods typically rely on per-scene optimization, requiring extensive optimiza-
145   tion for each case, which limits their scalability and practicality. Unlike these optimization-based
146   approaches, this paper aims to achieve rapid, feed-forward-based decoupled reconstruction.

204
205
206   We aim to generate the clothing disentangled 3D character models from a single image. In contrast
207
      to optimization-based methods, this paper proposes a novel feed-forward approach that not only
      enables efficient clothing disentangled character generation in seconds but also intrinsically sup-
208
      ports 3D virtual try-on applications without the need for additional clothing combination networks.
209
      Figure 2 presents an overview of our approach. Given an input image of the clothed character, a
210   multi-part diffusion model is proposed to produce disentangled images of the human body and each
211   clothing part (Section 3.1). The disentangled images are then fed into a multi-view diffusion model
212   to generate multi-view images for each part. To compose the body and clothing parts, a novel multi-
213   part attention module is introduced to guide the composition process (Section 3.2). Based on these
214   multi-view images, the off-the-shelf feed-forward multi-view reconstruction methods are adopted to
215   produce the 3D model of each part, and an optional optimization method is proposed to combine all
      3D models (Section 3.3).

      3.1   2D CLOTHING DISENTANGLEMENT
217
218   To disentangle the human body and each clothing part from the input image, existing methods typ-
219   ically rely on clothing segmentation (i.e., semantic segmentation), which outputs the pixel-wise
220   labels of each type. However, the clothing segmentation based methods have two main limita-
221   tions. First, the segmented image of each type is often incomplete due to the occlusion. Second,
222   the obtained part images usually only occupy a small part of the original image, resulting in a low
223   resolution. To address these issues, we propose a novel 2D part disentanglement method based
224
      on diffusion models, which can generate complete and high-resolution part images from the input
      image. Before introducing our approach in detail, we first briefly describe the diffusion model.
225
226
      Diffusion models. Diffusion models (Sohl-Dickstein et al., 2015; Ho et al., 2020) are a category
227
      of generative models parameterized by Markov chains, comprising forward and reverse processes.
228   In the forward process, a sample x0 drawn from the data distribution p(x) is progressively noised
229   through a sequence {xt | t ∈ (0, T )}, where each xt is computed as xt = αt x0 + σt ϵ and T denotes
230   the number of train steps. Here, ϵ represents random noise sampled from a normal distribution
231   N (0, 1), and αt , σt are constants defining the noise schedule, culminating in complete Gaussian
232   noise. Conversely, the reverse process iteratively denoises the noisy image, reconstructing xt−1
233   from xt by estimating the noise ϵ. This estimation is achieved by a noise predictor ϵθ , parameterized
234   using a UNet architecture. Please refer to Ho et al. (2020); Sohl-Dickstein et al. (2015) for more
235   details about diffusion models.
236   Under the diffusion model scheme, our goal to disentangle each part from the input image y can be
237   formulated as follows:

239                       f (y) = p x1T , ..., xN         pθ x1t−1 , ..., xN      1         N

      calculate the distribution pθ , which enables to generate the disentangled part images based on the

244
245   To leverage the strong image prior learned from billions of images, we base our model on the pre-
246   trained stable diffusion models Ho et al. (2020). The original diffusion model only supports single
247   image generation based on the conditional image. To enable multi-part image generation, we addi-
248
      tionally introduce the part type c condition into the stable diffusion models, and then the diffusion
      model can generate a specified part image based on the input image and the corresponding part type,
249
      i.e., xc = f (y, c). Specifically, we adopt the one-hot encoding to represent the part type, which
250
      is further augmented with the positional encoding and then concatenated with the time embedding.
251   Intuitively, using the multi-part diffusion model to generate the different part images separately will
252   lose the strong correlation among different parts since there are fixed patterns in how people wear
253   their clothes. To address this, we introduce a novel multi-part attention module to facilitate informa-
254   tion propagation across different parts, implicitly encoding multi-part dependencies.
255
      Rather than adding a new layer, we achieve this by extending the original self-attention layers to
256
      be multiple parts aware, which allows connections to other parts within the attention layers. This
257   module not only enhances the part decomposition quality but also achieves faster convergence. The
258   specific calculation of queries, keys, and values of part c in the multi-part attention layer is as
259   follows:

262   where Q, K and V denote query, key and value embeddings matrices, zc denotes the latent embed-
263   dings of part c in transformer blocks, and ⊕ denotes concatenation operation.

265   3.2   M ULTI - VIEW GENERATION AND PART COMBINATION
266
267   To reconstruct the 3D models of each part, we propose to first generate the multi-view images based
268   on the disentangled image, which can be used as the input of the multi-view based reconstruction
269   methods. To achieve this, similar to the previous works, we introduce the multi-view attention layers
      to the diffusion models to enhance the multi-view consistency. Specifically, we modify the original


                                                             5
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


270
      self-attention layers to be multi-view aware, which allows information propagation across different
271   views within the attention layers. With this design, the multi-view diffusion model can generate
272   multi-view consistent images for each part.
273
274
      In addition to generating multi-view images for each part, the composition of parts is also an in-
      dispensable component for many applications, such as virtual try-on. Existing 2D part composition
275
      methods typically train a dedicated network to combine the images of different parts. These meth-
276
      ods usually are designed for single view images and how to extend them to multi-view images is
277   unknown. Moreover, adding an additional network to handle part composition increases the overall
278   complexity and redundancy of the method.
279
280
      Thus, in this paper, we propose to introduce a part composition module into the multi-view dif-
      fusion model to achieve efficient and high-quality multi-view part composition. Specifically, we
281
      introduce a combination attention module after the multi-view attention layer in the UNet to guide
282
      the combination process, which can exchange the information of multiple parts. To generate the
283   combined images, an alternative method is directly fusing the information of multiple parts from
284   the corresponding conditioned part images. However, as shown in the experiments, this design can
285   not achieve high-quality combination since the network needs to simultaneously learn multi-view
286   generation and part combination from the input part images. To address this, we propose to in-
287   troduce a special condition image specifically for part combination. Intuitively, the network learns
288   multi-view generation from input part images and part combination from special condition images,
289   thereby achieving improved performance. The calculation of queries, keys, and values of each part
290   is similar to Equation 2.

480
481   The proposed approach still has the following limitations. Although we have contributed a large
482   clothing-disentangled 3D dataset, its size remains insufficient compared to existing image datasets.
483   Therefore, exploring how to train disentangled models using 2D image data is our next step. Addi-

494   5   C ONCLUSION
495
496   In this paper, we propose a novel method for generating clothing-disentangled 3D characters from
497   a single image. Unlike previous optimization-based methods, we introduce a feed-forward decou-
498   pling reconstruction approach that initially generates multi-view images of each part, followed by
499
      individual reconstruction using a generalized multi-view reconstruction method. To achieve high-
      quality decoupling, we propose a two-stage method that first decouples each part in the 2D image
500
      space, and then generates multi-view images for each part. A novel cross-part diffusion model is
501
      introduced to enhance information exchange among different components during the 2D component
502   decoupling stage. To generate the final combined character, we introduce a novel combination atten-
503   tion mechanism into the multi-view diffusion model that integrates information from multiple parts.
504   For training, we have created a large dataset of more than 10k clothing-disentangled characters. Ex-
505   periments show that our method achieves efficient, high-quality generation of clothing-decoupled
506   3D characters, while also supporting clothing editing applications.

772   In the second part, we provide details about the animation. While the primary focus of this paper is
773   on clothing-disentangled generation, we have implemented a straightforward animation pipeline to
774   animate the generated 3D characters. Specifically, we begin by using OpenPose Cao et al. (2019)
775   to estimate the 2D human poses from the generated multi-view images of the 3D character. Subse-
776   quently, we fit a parametric human model (SMPL Bogo et al. (2016)) to these estimated multi-view
777   2D poses. To animate the reconstructed 3D Gaussian characters, we identify the closest vertex on the
778
      SMPL model for each Gaussian point and utilize the corresponding skinning weight of that vertex
      for animation. Finally, we animate the 3D Gaussian characters using the SMPL motion parameters.
779
      Exploring more sophisticated animation methods will be a focus of our future research.
780
781   In the third part, we provide the details of the experiments. Following previous works Long et al.
782   (2023); Shi et al. (2023), we use 256x256 resolution for both generated disentangled 2D cloth images
783   and multi-view images. The combination condition is a predefined constant matrix, which matches
784
      the shape of the disentangled images and has all its values set to 128. This matrix serves as a
      guide for the combination of different parts in the diffusion model. The total number of generated
785
      images is calculated as (Npart + 1) ∗ Nview , where Npart represents the number of parts, and Nview
786
      represents the number of views. The additional one denotes the generated multi-view images of
787   the combined character. The additional term accounts for the generated multi-view images of the
788   combined character. In our specific setup, with Npart = 4 and Nview = 4, we produce a total
789   of 20 images. The number of body parts, being four, is fixed throughout our experiments. The
790   optimization process of Section 3.3 typically takes around 2 minutes.
