011                                                A BSTRACT
012
013            Pose skeleton images are an important reference in pose-controllable image gen-
014            eration. In order to enrich the source of skeleton images, recent works have
015
               investigated the generation of pose skeletons based on natural language. These
               methods are based on GANs. However, it remains challenging to perform diverse,
016
               structurally correct and aesthetically pleasing human pose skeleton generation with
017
               various textual inputs. To address this problem, we propose a framework with
018            GUNet as the main model, PoseDiffusion. It is the first generative framework
019            based on a diffusion model and also contains a series of variants fine-tuned based on
020            a stable diffusion model. PoseDiffusion demonstrates several desired properties that
021            outperform existing methods. 1) Correct Skeletons. GUNet, a denoising model of
022            PoseDiffusion, is designed to incorporate graphical convolutional neural networks.
023            It is able to learn the spatial relationships of the human skeleton by introducing
024            skeletal information during the training process. 2) Diversity. We decouple the key
025            points of the skeleton and characterise them separately, and use cross-attention to
026
               introduce textual conditions. Experimental results show that PoseDiffusion outper-
               forms existing SoTA algorithms in terms of stability and diversity of text-driven
027
               pose skeleton generation. Qualitative analyses further demonstrate its superiority
028
               for controllable generation in Stable Diffusion.

031   1    I NTRODUCTION
032
033   As an important external control condition in controllable image generation, 2D pose skeleton images
034   are critical to the quality of the generated images. For example, ControlNet(Zhang et al., 2023),
035   HumanSD(Ju et al., 2023), GRPose(Yin et al., 2024) and other models used to create controllable
036   photos need to be provided with one or more 2D pose skeleton images for reference. In addition,
037   many pose sequence generation tasks also need to be provided with the first frame of the pose skeleton.
038   However, in order to obtain the pose skeletons, current methods rely mainly on extracting them from
039
      existing images using pose detection models, such as DWPose(Yang et al., 2023) and OpenPose(Cao
      et al., 2017). This limits the diversity and operability of obtainable pose skeletons. In practice, there
040
      are also blender-based applications which allow users to adjust and model the pose by dragging and
041
      dropping key points. However, these approaches are time-consuming and require a lot of manual
042   effort. They are also difficult to implement an end-to-end process due to the large amount of manual
043   intervention. In order to get pose skeleton images more easily and flexibly, it is necessary to create a
044   framework for generating pose skeletons directly from natural language.
045
      In recent years, many researchers have explored methods for generating 2D human pose skeletons
046
      from textual descriptions. Zhang et al. (2021) trained a generative adversarial network capable of
047
      generating single-person poses from text. Roy et al. (2022) proposed the DE-PASS, a dataset with
048   detailed labelling of the facial details in poses, and trained a generative adversarial network for
049   generating single-person poses conditioned on natural language on it, which designed a RefineNet to
050   improve the generation of facial gestures. However, they share a common problem, i.e., the generated
051   pose skeletons suffer from misplaced key points and disproportionate bone lengths. Fig. 1 shows
052   several illustrations of erroneous skeletons generated by a GANs-based method(Zhang et al., 2021).
053   Fig. 1(a) shows a disproportionate skeleton where the two arms of the character are not the same
      length. Fig. 1(b) shows a skeleton with misplaced key points. The key point that should be on the

069   To address the above challenges, we propose PoseDiffusion, a framework capable of generating
070   2D single-person pose skeletons from various texts. We propose to introduce a denoising diffusion
071   probabilistic model for tasks of text-driven human pose skeleton generation. Unlike classical text-to-
072   image models that use 3-channel feature maps to represent an RGB image, we propose to represent
073
      each key point as a separate feature channel, thus enabling individual prediction of the position of each
      key point. The above approach can significantly increase the diversity and stability of the generated
074
      pose images. In addition, to avoid the model always generating deformed skeletons, PoseDiffusion’s
075
      GUNet model introduces the key point and skeleton information into the generation process through
076   a GCN. As a result, our model generates correct keypoint locations and appropriate skeleton lengths.
077   Furthermore, in order to verify the effectiveness of introducing the skeleton information, we propose
078   two fine-tuned Stable Diffusion-based variants.
079

080
      First, we demonstrate significant improvements in text-driven 2D pose skeleton generation. Second,
081
      we show the generation capabilities of two fine-tuned variants. In addition, we discuss additional
082   possibilities of PoseDiffusion for multiplayer pose generation.

091

096   2.1   P OSE OR K EYPOINT- GUIDED T EXT- DRIVEN I MAGE G ENERATION
097
098   With the advent of Stable Diffusion(Rombach et al., 2022)(SD), work on text-driven image generation
099
      has made tremendous progress. To avoid pose distortion of people or objects in the generated images,
      many works use pose skeletons to guide text-driven image generation. ControlNet(Zhang et al., 2023)
100
      incorporates additional inputs such as pose information into the SD, allowing the user to precisely
101
      control image details during the generation process. HumanSD(Ju et al., 2023)introduces a specific
102   human pose coding module to improve the generation accuracy of fine-grained pose manipulation
103   tasks. T2I-Adapter(Mou et al., 2024) is an insertion module that combines textual cues with additional
104   visual guidance signals to help the generative model better map complex textual descriptions to
105   image content. GRPose(Yin et al., 2024) uses GNNs to process the relationship of skeleton points,
106   which helps the SD model better understand the reference poses, thus generating images with better
107   pose consistency. All of the above work requires an additional picture of the pose skeleton. Current
      approaches mainly rely on extracting the pose skeleton from existing images using pose detection


                                                         2
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


108
      models such as DWPose(Yang et al., 2023) and OpenPose(Cao et al., 2017), etc. This limits the
109   diversity and tractability of the available pose skeletons. To address this problem, we need to delve
110   into its upstream work, synthesising high-quality human pose skeletons from diverse texts, thereby
111   enriching the source of pose skeletons for text-driven image generation.
112
113   2.2   G ENERATING H UMAN P OSE S KELETONS FROM NATURAL L ANGUAGE
114
115   Previous work has done some research on generating human poses from natural language. Zhou
116   et al. (2019) change the pose of a person in a given image based on a textual description. Zhang
117   et al. (2021) trained a generative adversarial network capable of generating complex poses from
118
      natural language descriptions. Although they achieved good results in terms of action complexity
      and versatility, they did not take into account the spatial relationships between different key points of
119
      human poses, resulting in the generation of poses that are subject to problems such as deformities or
120
      skeletal disproportions. To solve this problem, we propose a new text-driven human pose skeleton
121   generation pipeline based on denoising diffusion probabilistic model(DDPM)(Ho et al., 2020). Where
122   the denoising model is based on U-Net, and introduces a graph neural network to introduce the spatial
123   information of the pose. Benefiting from the properties of the DDPM, our pipeline is able to generate
124   more diverse samples.
125
126   2.3   T EXT- CONDITIONED U-N ET
127
128   U-Net was initially applied to solve the problem of medical image segmentation(Ronneberger et al.,
129   2015; Oktay et al., 2018; Zhou et al., 2018), and more recent work combines U-Net with diffusion
130   processes for image generation(Rombach et al., 2022; Dhariwal & Nichol, 2021; Ju et al., 2023;
131
      Zhang et al., 2023). These works achieve state-of-the-art performance on a variety of tasks such as
      unconditional image generation, text-driven image generation(Rombach et al., 2022), and image-
132
      driven image generation, and inspired our work. Text-conditioned U-Net injects textual information
133
      into image latent features through a cross-attention mechanism, thus effectively guiding the image
134   content during the generation process. The downsampling aims at gradually compressing the spatial
135   dimensions of the input feature maps to extract higher-level global semantic information, while the
136   upsampling is used to progressively recover the spatial resolution, combining the global semantic
137   information with the detailed features, to generate an output image of the same size as the input
138   image.

      3.2   P IPELINE OVERVIEW
188
189
      Our proposed PoseDiffusion pipeline is shown in Fig. 3. First, we construct a text-based pose skeleton
190   generation pipeline using the denoising diffusion model(Nichol & Dhariwal, 2021)(DDPM). The
191   basic of the denoising model is GUNet, a U-UNet-like model. GUNet introduces spatial information
192   of skeletons such as key point locations and connectivity relationships by a spatial module. To
193   enable GUNet to handle textual conditions, we use a cross-attention mechanism to fuse cross-modal
194   features. In addition to this, we change the input dimension of GUNet to match the skeleton features
195   represented using a set of heatmaps. The above design significantly improves the pose skeleton
196   generated by PoseDiffusion. We will describe each part of the pipeline in the following subsections.

      3.4   GUN ET AND C OMPONENTS
245
      Since our skeleton generation task requires spatial information of the skeleton during denoising
246   process, it makes CNNs, a structure for processing image data, unable to model the structure of
247   pose skeleton. To solve this problem, Wen et al. (2023) proposed a U-Net-like structure based on
248   GNNs. However, GNNs are unable to perform detailed feature extraction on the representation of the
249   pose skeleton, which is a kind of 2D image data. Therefore, we propose GUNet that combines the
250   advantages of both CNN and GNN, as shown in Fig. 4.
251
      Text Encoder. In this paper, we use BERT(Devlin, 2018) to obtain the embedding of the text.
252   Specifically, the sentence T describing a pose skeleton is firstly be spilt by the tokenizer of BERT to
253   get Ttoken = {t1 , t2 , .tL .., }. Then, Ttoken is input to the embedding layer of BERT for encoding,
254   using the embedding of CLS token as the sentence representation, i.e., T ∈ R1×768 .
255
      Pose Encoder and Pose Decoder. Pose Encoder and Decoder are the Pose2Heatmap and
256
      Heatmap2Pose modules in Fig. 3. These are two modules that require no training and enable

261   the key point Ki is (µx , µy ), then, the pixel value at coordinate (x, y) is computed by Eq. 6

      4.1   DATASET & BASELINE M ODELS
325
326   We use the COCO (Lin et al., 2014) to train and evaluate our models. This dataset contains over
327   100k annotated images of everyday scenes, each accompanied by five natural language descriptions.
328   Among these, about 16k images feature a single person, with each person annotated by the coordinates
329   of 17 keypoints. To ensure data quality, we filtered approximately 12k single-person images, selecting
330   only those with at least 8 visible keypoints. For each image, one of the five descriptions was randomly
331   selected as the image’s label, forming image-text pairs. These pairs were then divided into training
332
      and validation sets with a 4:1 ratio.
333   Our baseline models come from three main sources. First, we fine-tuned Stable Diffusion in two ways:
334   SD1.5-T2P and PoseAdapter, which introduces a pose coding layer. Second, we modified GUNet by
335   removing the graph convolutional neural network layer, resulting in a model we denote as UNet-T2H,
336   where the human posture skeleton is represented as a set of heatmaps. Third, we included two
337   models based on WGAN, focusing on different methods of human posture skeleton representation:
338
      WGAN-LP for heatmap prediction and WGAN-LP R for coordinate regression prediction.
339   The experimental results of the baseline model will be discussed in Sections 4.2 and 4.3.

505   5    C ONCLUSIONS
506
507   This paper focuses on text-driven 2D pose generation based on the diffusion model, and proposes a
508   PoseDiffusion architecture that includes GUNet, PoseAdapter and so on. GUNet combines UNet
509   with GCN to capture the spatial information of the pose. Extensive experiments and in-depth analyses
510
      verify the effectiveness of the proposed framework in text-driven 2D human pose generation. In
      addition, we also analyse the reasons for the differences in effectiveness presented by GUNet and
511
      PoseAdapter, as well as the fact that GUNet’s decoupled generation approach holds greater potential
512
      in the direction of future multi-person pose generation.
513

515   6    F UTURE
516
517   Future work should further explore the task of multi-person pose generation and detail control. In
518   multi-person pose generation, the inability to distinguish the key points of different individuals leads
519
      to unsatisfactory results in generating interactions such as holding hands or hugging. Therefore, the
      decoupling feature of GUNet can be considered to solve this problem. Meanwhile, the decoupling
520
      feature also has great potential for fine-grained control of poses.
