009                                                A BSTRACT
010
011            Speculative decoding has emerged as a critical technique for accelerating infer-
012            ence in large language models, achieving significant speedups while ensuring con-
013            sistency with the outputs of the original models. However, there is currently a lack
014            of theoretical guidance in speculative decoding. As a result, most existing works
015            are dualistic target-draft model paradigm, which significantly restricts the hinders
016            potential application scenarios. In this paper, we propose a polybasic speculative
017            decoding framework supported by a solid theoretical foundation. We first deduce a
018
               theorem to control the ideal inference time of speculative decoding systems which
               is then serve as a design criterion that effectively expands the original dualistic
019
               speculative decoding into a more efficient polybasic speculative decoding. We
020
               further theoretically analyze the sampling process, identifying variables that can
021            be optimized to enhance inference efficiency in multi-model systems. We demon-
022            strate, both theoretically and empirically, that this system accelerates inference
023            for the target model, and that our approach is orthogonal to the majority of ex-
024            isting speculative methods, allowing for independent application or combination
025            with other techniques. Experimentally, we conducted comprehensive evaluations
026            across a wide range of models, including those from the Vicuna, LLaMA2-Chat,
027            and LLaMA3 families. Our method achieved remarkable latency speedup ratios
028            of 3.31×-4.01× for LLaMA2-Chat 7B, up to 3.87× for LLaMA3-8B, and up to
029
               4.43× for Vicuna-7B, while maintaining the distribution of the generated text.
               Code is available in supplementary materials.

      1    I NTRODUCTION
033
034
      Large Language Models (LLMs) have become the core driving force in the field of natural language
035
      processing (NLP), demonstrating remarkable performance in various applications. However, the
036   scale and complexity of these models also bring significant computational challenges, especially
037   in real-time application scenarios. Inference acceleration has become a key issue in deploying and
038   applying these models. Among numerous acceleration techniques, speculative decoding(Stern et al.,
039   2018) (Leviathan et al., 2023) has emerged as a critical technique, gaining widespread application
040   in large-scale model deployment.
041
      In recent years, the field of NLP has witnessed significant advancements in speculative sampling
042   methods, leading to the emergence of a “draft-then-verify” paradigm. This approach encompasses
043   various drafting strategies, such as the utilization of small-scale draft models to facilitate speculative
044   sampling in LLMs (Leviathan et al., 2023) (Xia et al., 2023a)(Chen et al., 2023a) (Kim et al., 2024)
045   (Svirschevski et al., 2024), the implementation of tree structures to organize tokens generated by
046   draft models (Miao et al., 2024) (Du et al., 2024) (Stern et al., 2018), the employment of unified
047   models serving as both draft and target models (Yi et al., 2024) (Cai et al., 2024), and the integra-
048   tion of early exiting techniques with speculative sampling methodologies (Elhoushi et al., 2024).
049   For token verification, researchers have predominantly employed three primary methods: greedy
050
      sampling, speculative sampling(Leviathan et al., 2023), and typical acceptance(Cai et al., 2024).
051   However, existing methods are limited to a dualistic relationship of cooperation between a draft
052   model and a target model. The disparity in inference capabilities between these two models results
053   in a small token average acceptance length, restricting the speedup ratio of speculative sampling.
      Although Chen et al. (2023b) propose cascading large and small models as the draft model, during

      2.1   BACKGROUND
098
099   Speculative decoding has emerged as a prominent paradigm for accelerating inference in large lan-
100   guage models. The field can be systematically categorized into two primary domains: drafting
101   methodologies and verification techniques.
102
103   Drafting Methodologies Drafting approaches are bifurcated into independent and self-drafting
104   strategies. Independent drafting employs distinct models for token generation, which can be either
105   fine-tuned or tuning-free. Fine-tuned drafters, exemplified by SpecDec (Xia et al., 2023b) and BiLD
106   (Kim et al., 2024), undergo task-specific optimization. Conversely, tuning-free drafters such as
107   Speculative Decoding (Leviathan et al., 2023) and StagedSpec (Spector & Ré, 2023) utilize pre-
      existing models without additional training.


                                                        2
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


108
      Self-drafting methodologies leverage the intrinsic architecture of the target model. These encompass
109   FFN Heads approaches, including Blockwise (Stern et al., 2018) and Medusa (Cai et al., 2024); Early
110   Exiting techniques, such as PPD (Yang et al., 2023) and Self-Speculative (Zhang & Chen, 2023);
111   and Mask-Predict methods, exemplified by Parallel Decoding (Santilli et al., 2023) and Lookahead
112   Decoding (Zhao et al., 2024).
113
114   Verification Techniques Verification methods, crucial for maintaining the fidelity of drafted to-
115   kens, are categorized into three principal approaches. Greedy Decoding algorithms, both lossless
116   and approximate, are represented by works such as SpecDec (Xia et al., 2023b) and BiLD (Kim
117   et al., 2024). Speculative Sampling, introduced by Leviathan et al. (2023), offers both lossless and
118
      approximate variants, with notable extensions including DistillSpec (Zhou et al., 2023) and Online
      Speculative (Liu et al., 2023). The Token Tree Verification approach, as demonstrated by SpecIn-
119
      fer (Miao et al., 2024) and StagedSpec (Spector & Ré, 2023), presents an alternative verification
120
      paradigm.

307   3.2   A LGORITHM
308
309   We propose a theoretical framework for polybasic speculative decoding, founded on the compo-
310   sition of dualistic speculative decoding units. This framework establishes a hierarchical structure
311   of models, where combinations of varying model sizes yield draft models with enhanced inference
312   capabilities. By calculating the average acceptance token length and ideal inference time for each
313   dualistic unit, and applying the criteria established in Theorems 3.2 and 3.3, we can optimize the
314
      selection of dualistic processes to construct polybasic speculative decoding systems with superior
      acceleration ratios. This approach allows for the systematic design of more efficient large language
315
      model inference systems. Based on the framework, we propose a construction method for a poly-
316
      basic speculative decoding that can reduce the inference time of the original dualistic system and
317   improve the acceleration ratio.
318
319   First, we can select a suitable dualistic speculative decoding, such as EAGLE (Li et al., 2024a;b),
320
      SpS (Leviathan et al., 2023), etc. We choose EAGLE as the smallest draft model. EAGLE is
      a method that performs speculative sampling at the feature layer, achieving impressive inference
321
      acceleration.
322
323   Then, we selected a 4-bit quantization LLM as the intermediate model M2 . This choice is motivated
      by both Theorem 3.2 and Theorem 3.3. The 4-bit quantization LLM can maintain good accuracy
