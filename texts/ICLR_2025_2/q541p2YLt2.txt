009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            Transformers have been making significant progress across various domains, and
012            recently, with scaling up of models like LLMs, they have achieved even greater
013            success. Recent findings have shown that the softmax function in the self-attention
014            used to re-weight the attention logits into probability vectors causes attention en-
015            tropy collapse, where the attention is concentrated on a single token, and it leads
016
               to unstable training. In this work, we first demonstrate that the (non-Lipschitz)
               softmax-based attention leads to the attention entropy collapse but the Lipschitz-
017
               kernel-based attention does not. We show that the Lipschitzness of the attention
018
               plays an important role in keeping the attention entropy stable regardless of the
019            variance of the attention logits. Moreover, we argue that the underlying reason
020            why the attention entropy collapse leads to the training instability is that as the
021            attention probabilities become more concentrated, it causes the attention matrix to
022            gradually increase, leading to gradient exploding.

      1   I NTRODUCTION
026
027
      Since Transformer (Vaswani et al., 2017) was introduced in the field of Natural Language Process-
      ing (NLP), it has recently demonstrated strong performance in various areas, including computer
028
      vision (Dosovitskiy et al., 2021), speech (Dong et al., 2018), and other fields as well. A key factor
029
      contributing to the success of Transformers across diverse fields is their capacity to effectively cap-
030   ture long-range dependencies via the self-attention mechanism. Specifically, the softmax function
031   transforms attention logits into probability distributions, reflecting the relative importance of each
032   element based on its relevance to the others. This mechanism facilitates the remarkable performance
033   of Transformers, normalizing large values impact on the weighted sum and effectively emphasizing
034   relative importance. However, this can lead to attention entropy collapse, negatively impacting the
035   model’s performance and training stability.
036   Attention entropy collapse arises when the query-key inner product (also called the attention logit)
037   is converted into a probability vector, leading to an excessive focus on a single token. As a result of
038   attention entropy collapse, the model struggles to effectively learn the relationships between inputs,
039   resulting in degraded performance (Zhai et al., 2023). Entropy collapse as being caused by the
040   softmax function, which exponentially magnifies the differences between query-key inner products
041   when re-weighting them into probabilities (Wang et al., 2021; Shen et al., 2023). However, it is
042   challenging to identify the reason why the softmax function is prone to attention entropy collapse
043   and why entropy collapse causes instability in training.
044   In this work, we demonstrate that the (non-Lipschitz) softmax-based attention leads to the attention
045   entropy collapse but the Lipschitz-kernel-based attention does not. In other words, the Lipschitzness
046   of the attention plays an important role in keeping the attention entropy stable. Figure 1 shows
047   that softmax-based self-attention and its variant applying Layer Normalization (Gilmer et al., 2023;
048   Dehghani et al., 2023) lead to entropy collapse, but Lipschitz kernel self-attention (ReLU, ELU+1)
049   ensures stable training. Additionally, as the entropy collapses to zero, the gradient explodes, and
050
      the loss diverges. Previous approaches primarily focused on interpreting the results; however, we
      emphasize bridge that, during entropy collapse, when attention probabilities take the form of one-
051
      hot vectors, the norm of the corresponding matrix increases, leading to gradient exploding.
052
053   First, softmax-based self-attention is more susceptible to entropy collapse compared to Lipschitz-
      kernel-based self-attention due to its increased sensitivity to variance in attention logits. We conduct


                                                         1
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


054
      controlled experiments to examine how both functions re-weight into different types of probabil-
055   ity distributions when the variance of the attention logits increases. As variance increased, softmax
056   produced highly imbalanced distributions, while the Lipschitz kernel maintained stable entropy. We
057   also observed that as the variance of softmax inputs grows during training (Jiang et al., 2023), the
058   distribution becomes more concentrated, resulting in a one-hot-like vector. In contrast, the Lipschitz
059   kernel stabilizes weight distribution despite high variance in logits. Therefore, compared to the Lip-
060   schitz kernel function, softmax, which is highly sensitive to the variance of attention logits, tends to
061   increase this sensitivity during training, leading to attention entropy collapse.
062   In addition, as shown in Figure 1, as attention entropy reaches zero during training, the gradient
063   norm explodes, preventing model convergence. It is crucial to analyze how attention entropy col-
064   lapse contributes to these instability issue. The softmax function outputs exponentially larger values
065   in response to its inputs, making it not Lipschitz continuous, and as a result, self-attention is not
066   Lipschitz, which can lead to very large gradients (Kim et al., 2021; Dasoulas et al., 2021). It is
067   noted that when the attention probabilities distribution becomes highly concentrated, the norm of
068   attention probabilities matrix increases, causing larger gradients in the backward pass and leading to
069
      exploding gradients. Through experiments, we observe that softmax-based self-attention increases
      attention probabilities norms, leading to exploding gradients, while Lipschitz kernel self-attention
070
      keeps norms and gradients stable due to lower function variation.

      If the probability distribution becomes skewed, with most of the probability mass concentrated on a
163   single token, the entropy gradually decreases. This issue is referred to as attention entropy collapse.
164   This issue similarly appears in LLMs, where, for example, a few activation units exhibit significantly
165   larger values than others, and these large activations lead to the concentration of attention probability
166   on their corresponding tokens (Sun et al., 2024). Additionally, entropy collapse, where attention
167   concentrates on a few tokens, is linked to grokking, where performance suddenly improves after a
168   long period of LLMs performance (Hoffmann et al., 2023). There are several approaches aimed at
169   addressing the problems of entropy collapse.
170
171   Normalization One of the causes of attention entropy collapse is that when the magnitude of
172   attention logits is large for a specific token or a small set of tokens, the softmax output becomes
173
      sharply peaked, focusing almost all attention on those few tokens while others receive nearly zero. A
      representative method to mitigate this issue is QK-LayerNorm (Gilmer et al., 2023; Dehghani et al.,
174
      2023), which applies layer normalization to both the query and key before passing them into the
175
      softmax function, effectively reducing their magnitudes. To mitigate the issue caused by significant
176   statistical variations in input values, NormSoftmax (Jiang et al., 2023) normalizes the input vectors
177   to the softmax function, ensuring they have unit variance. Also, σReparam prove that as the ℓ2 -norm
178   of the product of the query and key weights increases, the bound becomes tighter, resulting in low
179   entropy. By reparameterizing the parameters using spectral normalization, it was possible to prevent
180   the entropy from reaching zero.
181
182   Activation Function There are also approaches that address the entropy collapse issue by modify-
183   ing the softmax function from a different perspective. ReLUFormer (Shen et al., 2023), by replacing
184   the exponential function in softmax with ReLU, they resolved the issue of insufficient key-value
185
      memory slots caused by the centralized softmax function. Additionally, in Wang et al. (2021) the
      attention scores between embedding vectors are not normally distributed because of softmax func-
186
      tion, so the exponential function is replaced with a periodic function e.g sine, cosine to resolve the
187
      training instability. In this work, we focus on the variance of attention logits, the most sensitive
188   factor causing entropy collapse in softmax-based self-attention, and reveal that the Lipschitz kernel
189   function, a simple method without parameters, is highly robust to entropy collapse.

269   We find that the softmax function is susceptible to entropy collapse, but when approximated with
      a Lipschitz kernel, it remains robust against such collapse. Additionally, due to this collapse, the

      softmax-based methods show that gradient norm increases rapidly, causing the loss to diverge and
271   making the learning process unstable. Figure 1 shows that, in the case of self-attention using the
272   softmax function, the entropy gradually decreases in the early steps, eventually reaching zero, while
273   the gradient increases sharply. This confirms that entropy collapse makes the model’s learning un-
274   stable, and unlike the gradient vanishing observed in large models, the gradient is found to explode.
275   As attention entropy reaches zero, it indicates that the attention probabilities in each layer have
276   transformed into one-hot vectors, as shown in Figure 2 (Top). Furthermore, even after applying
277   QK-LayerNorm (Gilmer et al., 2023; Dehghani et al., 2023), a representative method to prevent the

283   Why is Softmax Vulnerable to Entropy Collapse Compared to Lipschitz Kernels The main
284   difference between the softmax function and Lipschitz kernel functions lies in their handling of in-
285   put sensitivity and output stability (Kim et al., 2021). Due to its exponential nature, softmax function
286   is highly sensitive to small variations in input. As the variance between values increases, the expo-
287   nentiation leads to increasingly larger differences. This can cause entropy collapse, where attention
288   becomes overly concentrated on a few tokens, reducing diversity and destabilizing the training pro-
289   cess. In contrast, Lipschitz kernel functions maintain a controlled, proportional relationship between
290   input and output changes, ensuring that the attention distribution remains balanced and stable. The-
291
      oretically, this stability arises because Lipschitz kernel functions have bounded gradients, which
      prevent the gradients from exploding or vanishing during training. By limiting the rate at which
292
      the output can change relative to the input (as defined by the Lipschitz constant), these functions
293
      promote smoother training and help maintain a diverse and effective attention mechanism. Thus, in
294   self-attention, the softmax function distorts the probability distribution, concentrating most values
295   in one area and creating positive skewness. In contrast, the Lipschitz kernel function limits changes,
296   leading to a more evenly spread distribution with lower skewness. The detailed description regarding
297   experiment on the difference in skewness between the two functions is in the Appendix C.

458   4   C ONCLUSION
459
460   In this paper, we reproduced the instability of Transformer training and entropy collapse in small-
461   scale models and found that this instability is heavily influenced by the attention method used to
462   re-weight the attention logits. Through experiments, we confirm that Lipschitz kernel self-attentions
463   maintain higher and more stable entropy during training compare to the softmax-based attentions.
464   Our theoretical and experimental results demonstrate that the softmax function, as the attention logit
465
      variance increases, re-weights the logits into more extreme distributions, eventually resembling one-
      hot vectors. Additionally, our experiments show that, compared to the Lipschitz kernel attentions,
466
      the attention logit variance in the softmax attention increases more during training, making it more
467
      prone to entropy collapse. Furthermore, as the attention matrix becomes sparse, its norm increases
468   together with the gradient, leading to training instability.
