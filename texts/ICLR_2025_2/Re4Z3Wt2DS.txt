010
011            SchroћИdinger bridge (SB) has evolved into a universal class of probabilistic gen-
012            erative models. Recent studies regarding the Sinkhorn algorithm through mirror
013            descent (MD) have gained attention, revealing geometric insights into solution ac-
014            quisition of the SB problems. In this paper, we propose a variational online MD
015            framework for the SB problems, which provides further
                                                                  вИЪ stability to SB solvers. We
016            formally prove convergence and a regret bound O( T ) of online mirror descent
017
               under mild assumptions. As a result of analysis, we propose a simulation-free SB
               algorithm called Variational Mirrored SchroћИdinger Bridge (VMSB) by utilizing
018
               the Wasserstein-Fisher-Rao geometry of the Gaussian mixture parameterization
019
               for SchroћИdinger potentials. Based on the Wasserstein gradient flow theory, our
020            variational MD framework offers tractable gradient-based learning dynamics that
021            precisely approximate a subsequent update. We demonstrate the performance of
022            the proposed VMSB algorithm in an extensive suite of benchmarks.

072   In this paper, we explore a new way of stable SchroћИdinger bridge acquisition through the lens of
073
      online mirror descent (OMD; Srebro et al., 2011). As illustrated in Fig. 1, we utilize a constrained
      space C equipped with the Wasserstein metric, allowing a new formulation similar to the classical
074
      mirror descent algorithm. As an online learning algorithm, we postulate the optimization errors of
075
      an SB solver and propose an OMD framework to reduce these errors in terms of regrets. To this end,
076   we propose a new simulation-free SB algorithm called Variational Mirrored SchroћИdinger Bridge
077   (VMSB). Learning of VMSB is based on an approximation of the MD updates that solve iterative
078   subproblems by Wasserstein gradient dynamics. We introduce a gradient computation method of
079   parameterized SB models based on gradient flows with respect to Wasserstein-Fisher-Rao (WFR)
080   geometry (Liero et al., 2018). Our framework allows us to efficiently perform OMD, which is more
081   tolerant of unreliable objective estimation (Lei & Zhou, 2020). Our experiments show that the
082   proposed VMSB outperforms existing SB solvers in benchmark problems.
083   Our contributions. Our work complements earlier studies on SB, building on the theoretical and
084   technical insights derived from a geometric perspective that views MD solutions as gradient flows
085   across the Wasserstein space. To the best of our knowledge, VMSB is the first SB algorithm based
086   on online mirror descent that verifies its ability to solve high-dimensional real-world SB problems.
087   Table 1 shows that VMSB is a simulation-free SB solver that brings solid convergence results in

093   вАҐ We demonstrate our algorithm on a variety of SBPs demonstrating the effectiveness of the learning

533
534   In this paper, we have presented an OMD framework developed to solve SBPs with robustness.
535   Our geometric interpretation of the dual space allowed us to construct a robust OMD algorithm with
536   theoretical guarantees for convergence and regrets. We substantially reduced the computational chal-
537   lenge in the MD framework using the WFR geometry. The proposed method demonstrated stable
538   benchmark performance, exhibiting enhanced stability. We argue that the VMSB algorithm offers a
539   promising approach for solving probabilistic generative modeling in the context of learning theory.
      The limitations and potential directions for future research are thoroughly discussed in Appendix D.

1649   D    L IMITATIONS AND D IRECTIONS FOR F UTURE R ESEARCH
1650
1651   Computation. We have presented performance regarding efficiency and scalability up to 1,000
1652   dimensions in the experiments. The computational of VMSB requires quadratic time for computing
1653
       the Wasserstein gradient flow (asymptotically O(K 2 ny )) and memory footprints of {Ykx }K    k=1 for
       estimating with internal Gaussian particles (asymptotically O(Kny )). For fast computation, we uti-
1654
       lized the JAX automatic differentiation library (Bradbury et al., 2018) for computing gradients and
1655
       Hessians in Proposition 2. For a small number of dimensions less than or equal to 20, this overhead
1656   is negligible; VMSB can run on a 4-core CPU, and the training can be reasonably trained within
1657   10 minutes. For a large number of dimensions, such as 512, the wall clock time for finishing the
1658   FFHQ dataset in the image-to-image transfer experiment was less than 30 minutes using parallel
1659   computing of a single NVIDIA TITAN RTX GPU. While the Wasserstein gradient flow theory in
1660   the subspace of P2 (Rd ) enables us to estimate the mirror descent update more accurately, its com-
1661   putational efficiency is not yet comparable to well-established automatic differentiation libraries. If
1662   numerical computation for high order derivatives are readily available with low computational cost
1663   in future, we will be able to train more stable and reliable probabilistic models.
1664   Limitations. GMM-based SB models, due to the lack of deep structural processing, tend to focus
1665   on instance-level associations in images in coupling rather than the subinstance- or feature-level
1666   associations that are intrinsic to deep generative models. As a result, while VMSB produces statisti-
1667   cally valid representations of optimal transportation within the given architectural constraints, these
1668   outcomes may be perceived as somewhat вАЬsynthetic.вАЭ Nevertheless, GMM-based models still hold
1669   an irreplaceable role in numerous problems such as latent diffusion and variational methods, due to
1670
       their simplicity and distinctive properties (Korotin et al., 2024). As we successfully demonstrated in
       two distinct ways of interacting with neural networks for solving unpaired image transfer, we hope
1671
       our theoretical and empirical findings help novel neural architecture studies.
