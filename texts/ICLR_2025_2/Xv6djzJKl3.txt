009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            Few-shot segmentation (FSS) aims to segment new category images given only a
012            few labeled samples. Most previous works concentrate on the design of intricate
013            query decoders to perform feature matching or aggregation between the support
014            and query. In this paper, we revisit a widely overlooked aspect of existing FSS
015            methods, i.e., the exploration of fixed pre-trained backbone features. We find that
016            treating all feature channels equally is suboptimal and propose a Task-specific
017            Channel-wise Modulation Network (TCMNet) to focus more attention on task-
018
               aware channels, facilitating more effective utilization of pre-trained features. The
               proposed TCMNet enjoys several merits. First, we design a self-modulation block
019
               that injects the gradient information into channel-wise attention layers, thereby
020
               enhancing the discriminability between target and background features. Second,
021            a cross-calibration block is introduced to align the support features toward the
022            query according to the target gradient and representations, which mitigates the
023            impact of intra-class diversity. Extensive experimental results on COCO-20i
024            and Pascal-5i benchmarks demonstrate that the TCMNet, as a general plugin,
025            consistently achieves significant improvements over different query decoders and
026            also achieves state-of-the-art results. In addition, the decent performance achieved
027            by exploring the backbone features may inspire another direction for developing
028            more comprehensive FSS models.

      1   I NTRODUCTION
032
033
      Semantic segmentation has achieved conspicuous achievements benefiting from large-scale annotated
      datasets (Lin et al., 2014; Mottaghi et al., 2014; Kirillov et al., 2023) and elaborate deep-learning
034
      techniques (Long et al., 2015; Vaswani et al., 2017; Ronneberger et al., 2015; He et al., 2016).
035
      However, the dependence on extensive annotated data constrains the capabilities of segmentation
036   models to predefined training categories, severely limiting their practical applications. To overcome
037   such inherent category sensitivity and in pursuit of human-like intelligence of learning from scarce
038   samples, few-shot segmentation (FSS) (Shaban et al., 2017) is proposed to derive segmentation
039   models capable of quickly generalizing to novel classes.
040
      Concretely, FSS aims at segmenting new category images (i.e., query images) with only a handful
041
      of labeled reference images (i.e., support images). Tackling diverse query images with extremely
042   limited support reference poses great challenges as: (1) Significant intra-class diversity between
043   support and query targets is frequently encountered, as shown by the two persons in Figure 1. (2)
044   Cluttered query backgrounds often contain distractors, such as training classes (Lang et al., 2022a)
045   or similar interfering objects (e.g., colored boxes in Figure 1 (b)). These factors elevate the risk of
046   errors or incompleteness in target segmentation, constituting two fundamental challenges in FSS.
047
      The current top-performing FSS frameworks usually comprise a ImageNet (Russakovsky et al.,
048   2015) pre-trained Siamese backbone (Liu et al., 2020a) to encode support and query images into
049   the shared feature space, as well as a support-guided query decoder to excavate the query target
050   through cross-image feature matching (Shi et al., 2022a; Li et al., 2021; Zhang et al., 2021c) or
051   aggregation (Min et al., 2021; Hong et al., 2022). To alleviate the challenges discussed above, most
052   recent research has delved into the design of the decoder, yielding considerable progress such as
053   prototypical learning-based (Liu et al., 2020b; Li et al., 2021; Wu et al., 2021; Wang et al., 2024)
      or affinity learning-based decoders (Zhang et al., 2021c; Wang et al., 2023b; Shi et al., 2022a;

122   various existing FSS query decoders. Furthermore, the explicit modulation also expedites model
123
      convergence. In summary, our contributions can be concluded as follows: (i) We jump out of
      the design of query decoder and steer toward a new perspective of FSS, i.e., employing gradient

      information to modulate the pre-trained backbone features for more reasonable utilization. (ii) We
125
      put forward a novel Task-specific Channel Modulation network (TCMNet), that can be integrated into
126   various FSS methods as a general plugin, to coherently tackle two foundational FSS challenges. (iii)
127   Extensive experimental under different settings demonstrate that our TCMNet consistently elevates
128   the performance of several FSS methods and achieves state-of-the-art results.

132   2.1   S EMANTIC S EGMENTATION
133
134   Semantic segmentation aims to classify each pixel within the given image into a specific category
135   and has been widely applied to autonomous driving (Kerner, 2016), medical image processing (Ron-
136   neberger et al., 2015), and so on. The seminal Fully-Connected Network (FCN) (Long et al., 2015)
137   achieved significant advances in semantic segmentation and inspired a lot of works (Zhao et al., 2017;
138
      Xiao et al., 2018; Ronneberger et al., 2015). Numerous architectures enhance context recognition
      by expanding the receptive field of CNNs through dilated convolutions (Chen et al., 2017; 2018),
139
      global pooling (Liu et al., 2015), and pyramid pooling (Chen et al., 2017; Yang et al., 2018). Besides
140
      CNN-based architectures, the emergence of the Vision Transformer (ViT)(Dosovitskiy et al., 2020)
141   has spurred the development of transformer-based segmentation models(Strudel et al., 2021; Zheng
142   et al., 2021; Zhang et al., 2022b; 2021d). Notably, MaskFormer (Cheng et al., 2021b) utilizes the
143   transformer decoder (Carion et al., 2020) for mask classification using a set prediction approach. This
144   framework has been refined by numerous subsequent studies (Cheng et al., 2021a; Zhang et al., 2023;
145   Luo et al., 2023; Sun et al., 2023). Among them, the Segment Anything Model (SAM) (Kirillov
146   et al., 2023) proposes the prompt segmentation paradigm and achieves astonishing segmentation
147   performance after training on extremely huge datasets. Despite their success, these methods struggle
148   to generalize to novel classes in low-data scenarios.
149
150   2.2   F EW-S HOT S EMANTIC S EGMENTATION
151
152   Few-shot segmentation (FSS) (Shaban et al., 2017) is designed to segment new category images
153
      with only a few labeled samples as references. Most of the recent FSS frameworks consist of two
      fundamental components, i.e., a Siamese backbone (Liu et al., 2020a) to extract features and a
154
      query decoder to excavate the target within the query image under the guidance of support features.
155
      Current researches mainly focus on the design of the query decoder, which can be roughly divided
156   into two categories: prototypical learning decoders and affinity learning decoders. Inspired by
157   PrototypicalNet (Snell et al., 2017), prototypical learning decoders adopt a single (Zhang et al., 2020;
158   Wang et al., 2019; Cao et al., 2022; Liu et al., 2022c; Jiao et al., 2022) or multiple prototypes (Lang
159   et al., 2022b; Yang et al., 2020; Liu et al., 2022b;a; Zhang et al., 2021a; 2022a; Okazawa, 2022; Wang
160   et al., 2022) to represent the target and then conduct feature comparison or aggregation to mine the
161   query target. To capture fine-grained support information, affinity learning decoders (Wang et al.,
      2020; Min et al., 2021; Hong et al., 2022; Wang et al., 2023b) constructs pixel-level associations

194   3.1     P ROBLEM D EFINITION
195
      Few-shot Segmentation (FSS) aims to perform novel category object segmentation with only a few
196
      densely-annotated samples. Most existing FSS methodologies leverage the meta-training paradigm to
197
      enhance the model generalization. Specifically, the datasets are divided into the training set Dtrain
198   and testing set Dtest with class set Ctrain and Ctest respectively. Note that the two class sets are
199   disjoint, i.e., Ctrain ∩ Ctest = ∅. To train the FSS model in the K-shot setting (K=1 or K=5 in
200   this paper), a set of episodes are sampled from Dtrain , each of which consists of the support set
201   S = {Isk , Msk }Kk=1 and the query set Q = {Iq , Mq }, where I and M denote the RGB image and
202   corresponding ground-truth mask, respectively. The FSS model is optimized to predict the target
203   mask of the query image Iq under the supervision of Mq . For testing, the trained model is evaluated
204   on Dtest across all the sampled episodes without further optimization.

      3.2.1         OVERVIEW
209   Most of the previous FSS methods adopt the ImageNet pre-trained backbone to extract the features,
210   keeping it fixed during both meta-training and meta-testing. The extracted features (Fs and FQ
211   in Figure 3) are fed directly into the query decoder for prediction. We propose modulating the
212   features using gradient information before inputting them into the query decoder. The modulated
213   features (F̂s and F̂Q in Figure 3) are less susceptible to the impact of intra-class diversity and
214   cluttered backgrounds. The proposed Task-specific Channel Modulation network (TCMNet), as
215   shown in Figure 3, comprises three major procedures, i.e., 1) channel-wise importance assessment, 2)
      importance-guided self-modulation, 3) support-to-query cross-calibration. We employ the gradient

217   2), the Self-Modulation Block (SMB) leverages the importance as auxiliary cues of channel-wise

219   procedure 3) adapts the support features to bridge the intra-class feature gap according to the holistic
220   representation as well as grad vector discrepancy. The details are as follows.

      4.1     DATASETS AND E VALUATION M ETRICS
304   We evaluate the proposed TCMNet on two popular few-shot segmentation benchmarks, i.e., Pascal-
305   5i (Shaban et al., 2017) and COCO-20i (Nguyen & Todorovic, 2019). Among them, Pascal-5i is
306   built based on the PASCAL VOC 2012 dataset (Everingham et al., 2010) with additional annotations
307   from SBD (Hariharan et al., 2014). We follow the baseline works (Tian et al., 2020; Lang et al.,
308   2022a; Peng et al., 2023) to divide the 20 categories into four folds, with three folds for training and
309   one for testing. COCO-20i is a larger dataset with more categories and more complex scenes built
310   from MSCOCO dataset (Lin et al., 2014). 80 categories are partitioned for cross-validation, with
311   60 classes used for training and 20 classes for testing. When testing, 1000 episodes are randomly
312
      sampled for performance evaluation. For a fair comparison, we follow the common practice to
      mean intersection-over-union (mIoU) and foreground-background intersection-over-union (FBIoU)
313
      as quantitative metrics.

      4.5              D ISCUSSION ON TASK - SPECIFIC C HANNEL - WISE M ODULATION .
467
468
      We further discussed the design of TCMNet from three perspectives. (1) Why channel-wise? In the
469   absence of category-aware classifiers, query decoders of current FSS models indiscriminately use all
470   channels for feature matching. This implicit learning-based paradigm of seeking key channels not only
471   slows down convergence, but also tends to induce channel bias toward the training categories, and the
472   overfitting caused by channel bias is also a common issue in conventional channel attention methods,
473   e.g., SENet (Hu et al., 2018). We resort to gradients to identify the features that contribute the most to
474   correct predictions and focus attention on them, effectively functioning as a category-aware classifier.
475   Figure 4(a) shows that this explicit channel-wise manipulation can enable the model to converge
476   faster to higher performance. Given the gradient guidance, we further explore the effectiveness of the
477
      self-modulation strategy in the spatial dimension. As shown in Table 8, spatial self-modulation yields
      a slight performance improvement when adapting to support features, while it degrades performance
478
      when applied to query features. We conjecture that this is due to gradient information focusing
479
      attention on the most discriminative regions, hindering complete target extraction. (2) Modulation
480   strategy. In addition to the proposed self-modulation, we tested various methods of modulating
481   backbone features using gradient information as shown in Table 9. We find that simply weighting
482   features point-wise (2nd ) or channel-wise (3rd ) significantly degrade the performance. We deem the
483   reason is that the drastic changes damage the semantic information contained in backbone features.
484   The proposed self-modulation strategy leverages gradient information on the basis of inter-channel
485   relationships, which approach enhances task-relevant features while preserving the original semantic
      information. (3) Visualizations of gradients. In Figure 5(b), we visualize the features of channels

506   5    C ONCLUSION
507   In this paper, we steer toward a different perspective of FSS that shifts our focus from the design
508   of the query decoder to the better utilization of backbone features. We propose the task-specific
509   channel-wise modulation network (TCMNet), which can serve as a generic plugin to combine with
510   different query decoders, including a self-modulation block to enhance the target awareness of
511   features and a cross-calibration block to bridge the intra-class variation. The decent performance on
512   four different baseline methods indicates that exploring backbone features is another avenue for FSS
513   in addition to query decoder design.
