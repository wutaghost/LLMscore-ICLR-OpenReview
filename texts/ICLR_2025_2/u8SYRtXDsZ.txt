009                                                                                                                                 A BSTRACT
010
011                                Recently, Transformer-based models have performed remarkably well in audio-
012                                visual segmentation (AVS) tasks. However, previous methods exhibit abnormal
013                                behavior and unsatisfactory results when using cross-attention. By analyzing at-
014                                tention maps, we identify two primary challenges in existing AVS models: 1)
015                                attention dissipation, caused by anomalous attention weights after Softmax over
016                                limited frames, and 2) narrow attention patterns in early decoder stages lead to
017
                                   inefficient utilization of attention mechanism. In this paper, we introduce AVES-
                                   Former, the first real-time audio-visual segmentation transformer that simultane-
018
                                   ously achieves fast, efficient, and lightweight. Our model proposes an efficient,
019
                                   prompt query generator to rectify cross-attention behavior. Moreover, we propose
020                                an early focus (ELF) decoder, which enhances efficiency by incorporating convo-
021                                lution operations tailored for local feature extraction, thus reducing computational
022                                overhead. Extensive experiments demonstrate that AVESFormer effectively miti-
023                                gates cross-attention issues, substantially improves attention utilization, and out-
024                                performs the previous state-of-the-art, achieving a superior trade-off between per-
025                                formance and speed. The code can be found in the supplementary material.

044       Audio-Visual Segmentation (AVS) (Zhou et al., 2022) has emerged as a novel multi-modality task
045       that plays a crucial role in robot sensing, video surveillance, and other scenarios. It aims to segment
046       fine-grained pixel-level sounding objects with corresponding audio-visual modalities. However,
047       existing AVS methods primarily focus on improving performance, often at a high cost of model size
048       and computational overhead (Gao et al., 2024; Mao et al., 2023b; Liu et al., 2023b; Huang et al.,
049       2023; Liu et al., 2023a; 2024a; Li et al., 2023b). Besides, default AVS setting directly processing T

          frames at a time (Zhou et al., 2023; Li et al., 2023a) is also unfitted for immediate response. These
          drawbacks render them unsuitable for applications with real-time requirements.

          2023a). However, AVS models often rely on modified attention variants despite the prevalence of

103   while reducing the computational cost of the latter.

      2     R ELATED W ORK
109
110
      Real-Time Audio-Visual Segmentation. Audio-Visual Segmentation (AVS) is a more fine-
111   grained and complicated task than sound source localization (SSL) (Chen et al., 2021a; Hu et al.,
112   2020; Qian et al., 2020b) as it aims to locate the sounding object and show pixel-level predictions.
113   However, few research works focus on real-time scenario where only 1 frame is given at a time
114   instead of T frames. AVSBench (Zhou et al., 2022) is the first to propose audio-visual segmentation
115   benchmark, introducing temporal pixel-wise audio-visual interaction (TPAVI) module to facilitate
116   interaction between audio-visual information. AVSegFormer (Gao et al., 2024) is the first to develop
117   a novel transformer architecture for AVS. They introduce audio queries into the transformer decoder
118   to attend to corresponding visual features. CATR (Li et al., 2023a) performs bidirectional com-
119
      binatorial dependence fusion to fully enhance spatial-temporal dependencies. (Chen et al., 2024)
      incorporates contrastive loss into audio-visual semantic segmentation with positive and negative
120
      pairs and uses larger resolution with extra data to reach higher performance.
121
122   Nevertheless, these methods encounter issues when dealing with single frame image and audio,
123   making them hardly work for real-time scenario. In detail, many research works meet a failure case
124   in cross attention with vision as query and audio as key and value. We call this failure case Attention
125
      Dissipation. AVSegFormer (Gao et al., 2024) fails to deliver satisfactory results when firstly trying
      Cross-Attention Mixer (CRA). (Chen et al., 2024) generates a plain attention map when visualizing
126
      Softmax attention map in their work. To tackle this problem, researchers propose different cross
127
      attention variants to amend it. TPAVI in AVSBench performs modality fusion by the dot-product
128   of vision and audio, which can be regarded as a linear attention. AVSegFormer employs a query
129   generator and perform channel attention to expand audio features and to avoid audio as key and
130   value. Chen et al. (2024) proposes Sigmoid attention to replace Softmax. Stepping-Stones (Ma et al.,
131   2024) proposes Adaptive Audio Query Generator, which obtains audio-conditioned query by cosine
132   similarity to enrich audio features. Although many alternative attention methods are proposed, the
133   underlying problem still remains unexplored. These attention variants can achieve some results, but
134   their expression ability is still not sufficient to match default attention(Tay et al., 2022). Therefore,
135   it is necessary to amend the behaviour of cross attention.
136
137   Efficient Vision Transformer. ViT (Dosovitskiy et al., 2020) and its variants (Liu et al., 2021;
138
      Touvron et al., 2021; Wang et al., 2022) have demonstrated significant improvements in computer
      vision. However the high computational cost makes them inferior to CNN in real-time inference
139
      scenario. To mitigate this gap, previous works attempt to design more efficient architectures to
140
      reduce computational burden. MobileViT (Mehta & Rastegari, 2021) combines CNN and ViT by
141   integrating global feature fusion of transformer in CNN. MobileFormer (Chen et al., 2022) bridges
142   MobileNet (Howard et al., 2017) and ViT in a parallel design to leverage advantages from both ar-
143   chitectures. EfficientFormer (Li et al., 2022b) finds insufficient operations in transformer and slims
144   the model size in a latency-driven manner. LVT (Xiao et al., 2021) adopts dilated convolution in
145   attention mechanisms to enhance model performance and efficiency. LIT (Pan et al., 2022) gives a
146   more detailed analysis of self-attention heads and applies MLP to build local dependencies. Effi-
147   cientViT (Cai et al., 2022) proposed to aggregate multi-scale features via small-kernel convolutions.
148   These methods have made contributions to the development of efficient ViT architectures. We ben-
149
      efit greatly from their contributions to the analysis of AVS tasks.

153   3.1   PRELIMINARIES
154
155   This paper considers real-time audio-visual segmentation, which is different from common AVS
156   task settings. Traditional AVS tasks deal with a clip of video frames, which contains T visual
157   frames xvisual ∈ RT ×3×H×W , and corresponding audio signals xaudio ∈ RT ×D , where H and W
158
      are the height and width of the image and D is the audio dimension.
159   However, it’s impractical for real-time inference on a whole bunch of T frames at a time. Users
160   expect an immediate response as a single input is given instead of waiting for the entire T frames
161   to be processed together. Meanwhile, the limited memory of edge devices is insufficient to handle
      the entire video clip. Therefore, this paper aims at a more practical AVS scenario, called real-time

      4.2   E ARLY F OCUS D ECODER
291
292   Our approach is based on the audio-visual cross-attention patterns, as shown in Figure 7. In the early
293   stages, audio features generate narrow local responses on attention maps. In the early stages, audio
294   features generate narrow local responses on attention maps. As it goes deeper, the attention region
295   enlarges gradually and, in the end, forms shaped regions suitable for segmentation. Therefore, we
296   propose a novel early focus (ELF) decoder. Since the early stage primarily captures local patterns,
297   attention to high computational cost is replaced by convolution to capture local semantics. In early
298   decoder stage l, visual feature Fvisual is processed by convolution:

      Dataset. We evaluate our method on the AVSBench dataset (Zhou et al., 2022; 2023), which is
      composed of AVSBench-Object and AVSBench-Semantic. AVSBench-Object is designed for audio-
313
      visual segmentation tasks with pixel-level annotations with two subsets: single sound source seg-
314
      mentation (S4) subset and multiple sound source segmentation (MS3) subset. AVSBench-Semantic
315   is an expanded version of AVSBench-Object, providing additional semantic masks to facilitate
316   audio-visual semantic segmentation (AVSS). See Appendix A.2.1 for more experimental details.
317
318   Implementation Details. Our model is trained on NVIDIA RTX 3090 GPU. From the aspect
319   of real-time inference, we employ ResNet-50 and ResNet-18 (He et al., 2016) pre-trained on Ima-

321   (PVT-v2) (Wang et al., 2022) is unsuitable for real-time applications, we do not adopt it as the vi-
322   sual backbone. We employ Vggish (Hershey et al., 2017) pre-trained on AudioSet (Gemmeke et al.,
323   2017) to encode audio input. Jaccard index J and F-score F are adopted as evaluation metrics. See
      Appendix A.2.1 for more experimental details.

      A.1.2    C ODE IMPLEMENTATION
811
812   To make a fully comprehensive understanding of attention dissipation, we provide a PyTorch-like
813   pseudo-code for easy verification and implementation of cross-attention dissipation. Algorithm 1
814   provides the pseudo-code of attention dissipation in the AVS task. For the current frame, we calcu-
815   late the attention matrix with the use of visual features as query and audio as key and value.
816
817   Algorithm 1 Pseudo-code of Attention Dissipation in a PyTorch-like style.
818   # image, audio: visual and audio feature
819   # attn: attention matrix
      # out: output of attention
820
      import torch
821   import torch.nn as nn
822   import torch.nn.functional as F
823   def cross_attention(image:torch.Tensor, audio:torch.Tensor):
         """
824      :param image: torch.tensor with shape [B, C, H, W]
825      :param audio: torch.tensor with shape [B, C]
         :return: fused feature and attention weight
826      """
827      image = image.flatten(2).transpose(1, 2)
828      audio = audio.unsqueeze(1)
829      q = image
         k = audio
830      v = audio
831
         attn = torch.matmul(q, k.transpose(1, 2))
832      attn = F.softmax(attn, dim=-1)
         out = torch.matmul(attn, v)
833
834      return out,attn

      Dataset. We evaluate our method on the AVSBench dataset (Zhou et al., 2022; 2023), which is
      composed of AVSBench-Object and AVSBench-Semantic. AVSBench-Object is designed for audio-
842
      visual segmentation tasks with pixel-level annotations. Videos are sourced from YouTube, cropped
843
      into 5 seconds, and sampled at one frame per second to compose the image data. There are two
844   subsets in AVSBench-Object: single sound source segmentation (S4) subset and multiple sound
845   source segmentation (MS3) subset. The S4 subset contains 4,932 videos: 3,452 for training, 740
846   for validation and 740 for testing. The labels contain 23 categories, including humans, vehicles,
847   animals and kinds of instruments. Note that annotations in S4 training set is only given in the first
848   frame. Meanwhile, MS3 subset is composed of multiple sound sources, including 424 videos, 286
849   for training, 64 for validation and 64 for testing. MS3 shares the same categories as S4.
850
851   Implementation Details. During training, we use the original image size as 224×224. We apply
852   horizontal flipping on S4 and MS3 for data augmentation. Since the S4 sub-set only contains anno-
853   tations on the first frame in the training split, we only use the first frame to provide supervision. We
854   use the AdamW optimizer and a polynomial learning rate decay with power = 0.9. On S4 and MS3,
855
      the learning rate is set to 0.0005, and on AVSS, it is set to 0.0001. Following previous practice Gao
      et al. (2024), we train MS3 for 60 epochs since it is relatively small, while the S4 and AVSS subsets
856
      are trained for 30 epochs. Batch size is set to 16 for S4 and MS3 and 8 for AVSS. We adopt two
857
      ResNet He et al. (2016) backbones (ResNet-50 and ResNet-18) for the segmentation network. For
858   the audio backbones, we use VGGish Hershey et al. (2017) frozen during the training. The prompt
859   query generator (PQG) receives the feature from the audio backbone as prompt. The number of
860   queries is set to 16, and the number of layers is set to 3. At the output end, the audio feature prompt
861   is discarded. The transformer decoder is adopted from Multi-Scale Deformable (MSDeform) at-
862   tention Zhu et al. (2020). The first two attention blocks are replaced by convolution to form ELF
863   decoder. Convolution blocks are attached with residual connection and LayerNorm Yu et al. (2024).
      As for the segmentation loss, on S4 and MS3, we set λIoU = 1.8 and on AVSS λIoU = 1.0 with


                                                        16
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


864
      λDice = 1.0 and λaux = 0.1. For inference, since the end-to-end real-time scenario does not support
865   inferring on a bunch of frames (because we want to segment one image at a time on the device), the
866   latency of all models is measured under one single frame, that is, T = 1. Nevertheless, some of
867   the methods employ temporal information within multiple frames, which would be lost in a single
868   frame scenario; we still keep their performance the same for comparison.

870   Evaluation Metrics. Following Zhou et al. (2022), we adopt Jaccard index J and F-score F
871   to evaluate. J indicates the mean intersection over union (mIoU) Everingham et al. (2015) be-
872   tween segmentation prediction and ground truth. F measures the precision and recall by F =
      (1+β 2 ×precision×recall)        2
        β 2 ×precision+recall , where β = 0.3.
