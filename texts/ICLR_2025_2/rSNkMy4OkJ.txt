009                                              A BSTRACT
010
011            Exposure to distressing images on social media, such as gore and other graphic
012            content, can lead to significant mental health issues and disturbances. This paper
013            introduces a novel dataset specifically curated to include such harmful images,
014            aiming to facilitate the development of machine learning models capable of de-
015            tecting and filtering these types of content. By training on this dataset, the pro-
016            posed models demonstrate the ability to accurately identify and flag disturbing
017            images, thereby contributing to the mitigation of mental health risks associated
018
               with prolonged exposure to harmful visual content on social media platforms. The
               proposed dataset is benchmarked on various state of the art models with the ac-
019
               curacy 70.15%. This work represents a critical step towards creating safer online
020
               environments and protecting users’ mental well-being.

026
027   Social media platforms provide an excellent means of connecting with people across the
028   globeBoyd & Ellison (2007), fostering communication and community in ways previously unimag-
029   inableFardouly et al. (2015). These platforms enable users to share experiences, stay informed,
030   and engage with diverse perspectives, making the world feel more interconnected than ever before.
031   However, alongside these benefits lies the potential for exposure to harmful content. One partic-
032   ularly concerning issue is the continuous viewing of certain images, which can negatively impact
033
      mental healthGillespie (2018); Ghosh & Anwar (2021). The constant barrage of idealized images
      and distressing visuals can lead to feelings of inadequacy, anxiety, and depression, highlighting the
034
      darker side of our digital interactionsGorwa et al. (2020).
035
036   Some social media platforms have implemented restrictions, such as NSFW (Not Safe For Work)
037   filters, to limit exposure to explicit or inappropriate contentHampton & Wellman (2003); Qayyum
038   et al. (2024). These measures aim to create a safer online environment and protect users from po-
039
      tentially harmful material. By flagging or hiding content deemed inappropriate, platforms strive to
      reduce the likelihood of users encountering distressing images or videos. These restrictions reflect a
040
      growing recognition of the need for responsible content management in an era where digital media
041
      consumption is ubiquitousLewis & Seko (2016); Liu et al. (2021a). Despite these efforts, users can
042   still encounter images that may disturb their mental health. The continuous exposure to idealized
043   lifestyles, graphic content, or distressing visuals can have a profound impact on individuals. For
044   instance, constantly viewing images that portray unrealistic body standards or extravagant lifestyles
045   can lead to feelings of inadequacy, jealousy, and low self-esteem. Similarly, exposure to violent or
046   traumatic content can induce anxiety, stress, or even trigger past traumas. This highlights the ongo-
047   ing challenge of ensuring a truly safe and supportive digital space, as the effectiveness of content
048   restrictions is often limited by the sheer volume and variety of content shared online. Additionally,
049   the algorithms used to detect harmful content are not foolproof and may fail to identify all problem-
050
      atic material, further complicating efforts to protect users’ mental healthPrimack et al. (2017).
051   Motivation: The rapid expansion of social media has revolutionized connectivity and information
052   sharing but has also exposed users to distressing and harmful visual content, such as gore and graphic
053   scenes, which pose significant mental health risks including anxiety, depression, and PTSD. Current
      detection systems are inadequate due to the complexity and variability of such content, compounded

173   3     M ETHODOLOGY
174
175   Data was collected from Reddit and Google utilizing the Selenium Chrome web driver. Specifically,
176
      data was extracted from the Reddit channel r/medicalgore and from various Google search results.
      The images were collected over the period from January 2021 to June 2024.
177
178   Data Annotation:The primary objective of the annotation process was to classify images as either
179   mentally disturbing or non-disturbing. Three annotators, each holding a Master’s degree in psychol-
180   ogy with first class grades in all subjects, carried out the annotation. These annotators also have
181   relevant work experience in mental health and social media.
182   To minimize labeling errors, the dataset was divided into batches and each data point was labeled by
183   at least two annotators. If both annotators agreed on a label, it was accepted as final. In cases where
184   there was disagreement, the annotators discussed the image until a consensus was reached.
185
186   3.1   A NNOTATION G UIDELINES
187
188   The primary objective of the annotation process is to classify images as either mentally disturbing
189   or non-disturbing. To achieve this, we employed three annotators, each holding a Master’s degree
190
      in psychology and possessing relevant work experience in mental health and social media. The
      following guidelines were established to ensure consistency and reliability in the annotation process:
191
192   Classification Criteria:
193
194          • Mentally Disturbing: Images likely to cause psychological discomfort, anxiety, or dis-
195
               tress.
196          • Non-Disturbing: Images that do not evoke significant negative psychological responses.
197
198   Annotation Process: Each image is reviewed independently by two annotators who assign labels
199
      based on their initial assessment. If the annotators’ labels differ, a discussion is initiated to resolve
      discrepancies. In cases where consensus cannot be reached after discussion, a third annotator is
200
      consulted.
201
202   Labeling Considerations:
203
204
             • Context: Consider the context in which the image appears, as an image that might be
               disturbing in one context may not be in another.
205
206          • Content: Focus on the visual content, such as the presence of blood, injuries, or other
207            graphic elements.
208          • Emotional Impact: Assess the potential emotional impact on an average viewer without
209            specialized training in medicine or psychology.
210
211   Quality Control: Regular cross-checks and discussions among annotators are conducted to ensure
212
      consistency in labeling. Periodic reviews of annotated batches are performed to identify and rectify
      any discrepancies or errors.
213
214   Ethical Considerations: Annotators should be aware of their own psychological state and take
215   breaks as needed to prevent desensitization or emotional distress. Additionally, images should be
      anonymized to protect the privacy of individuals depicted.

314   The performance of machine learning models trained on our novel dataset, curated to include dis-
315   tressing content such as gore and graphic materials, was evaluated with an accuracy of 70.15%. We
316   conducted a detailed error analysis focusing on false positives and false negatives.
317
      False Positives: False positives occur when the model incorrectly flags non-disturbing images as
318   harmful. Several categories of images contributed significantly to false positives. Medical images,
319   for instance, including surgical procedures, wound treatments, or anatomical illustrations, were often
320   misclassified. These images, although graphic, are typically not intended to disturb the viewer but
321   may visually resemble harmful content, leading to misinterpretation by the model. Similarly, scenes
322   from horror movies designed to appear disturbing but fictional also led to false positives. The model
323   sometimes flagged these images due to their graphic nature, mistaking them for real distressing
      content. Additionally, images of artistic and theatrical makeup, where individuals wear elaborate

425   6     L IMITATIONS
426
427   Despite the promising results and potential impact of this work, several limitations must be ac-
428   knowledged. Firstly, the dataset used in this study is entirely sourced from Reddit. While Reddit
429   is a significant and diverse platform, its content and user base may not be fully representative of
430   other social media platforms. This reliance on a single source may limit the generalizability of the
431   models developed using this dataset. Therefore, future research should consider incorporating data
      from multiple social media platforms to enhance the robustness and applicability of the findings.

      Another important limitation is the absence of textual context. The dataset and subsequent models
433   focus solely on visual content, specifically graphic and distressing images. In real-world scenarios,
434   such images are often accompanied by textual content that can provide crucial context for under-
435   standing the severity and nature of the content. The lack of textual context in this study means that
436   the models may not perform as effectively when deployed in environments where text and images
437   are intertwined. Incorporating multimodal data, including both images and text, in future research
438   could improve the accuracy and utility of content filtering models.

      We also emphasize the importance of transparency and accountability in the research and develop-

      We introduce a novel dataset specifically curated to include mentally disturbing images from social

      The further contributions which can improve the work could be expanding the dataset to include con-

471   generalizability of the developed models. By incorporating data from various sources, future models
472   could be better equipped to handle a wider array of graphic content and improve overall detection
473   accuracy. Integrating multimodal data, including both visual and textual content, represents a cru-
474   cial step forward. Images on social media often come with captions, comments, and other textual
