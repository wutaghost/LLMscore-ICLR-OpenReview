009                                              A BSTRACT
010
011            Real-world image super-resolution (Real-ISR) aims at restoring high-quality (HQ)
012            images from low-quality (LQ) inputs corrupted by unknown and complex degra-
013            dations. In particular, pretrained text-to-image (T2I) diffusion models provide
014            strong generative priors to reconstruct credible and intricate details. However,
015            T2I generation focuses on semantic consistency while Real-ISR emphasizes pixel-
016            level reconstruction, which hinders existing methods from fully exploiting diffu-
017            sion priors. To address this challenge, we introduce ConsisSR to handle both se-
018
               mantic and pixel-level consistency. Specifically, compared to coarse-grained text
               prompts, we exploit the more powerful CLIP image embedding and effectively
019
               leverage both modalities through our Hybrid Prompt Adapter (HPA) for semantic
020
               guidance. Secondly, we introduce Time-aware Latent Augmentation (TALA) to
021            mitigate the inherent gap between T2I generation and Real-ISR consistency re-
022            quirements. By randomly mixing LQ and HQ latent inputs, our model not only
023            handle timestep-specific diffusion noise but also refine the accumulated latent rep-
024            resentations. Last but not least, our GAN-Embedding strategy employs the pre-
025            trained Real-ESRGAN model to refine the diffusion start point. This accelerates
026            the inference process to 10 steps while preserving sampling quality, in a training-
027            free manner. Our method demonstrates state-of-the-art performance among both
028            full-scale and accelerated models. The code will be made publicly available.

      With the increasing prevalence of image capturing devices in our daily lives, there emerges a grow-
      ing need to capture clean, high-resolution images. Nevertheless, real-world images invariably con-
034
      tend with various degradation. To address this issue, real-world image super-resolution (Real-ISR)
035
      techniques adeptly reconstruct the high-quality (HQ) image from the low-quality (LQ) input.
036
037   Various methods (Dong et al., 2014; 2015; Kim et al., 2016; Lim et al., 2017; Zhang et al., 2018b; Dai
038   et al., 2019; Niu et al., 2020) harness convolutional neural networks (CNN) to achieve remarkable
039
      performance, followed by transformer models (Liang et al., 2021; Zhang et al., 2022). Some others
      employ GANs’ adversarial training to generate more photo-realistic images (Ledig et al., 2017;
040
      Wang et al., 2018). However, most of the above methods assume LQ inputs with basic bicubic
041
      down-sampling, limiting their efficacy in handling complex and unknown degradations encountered
042   in Real-ISR. To tackle this problem, some methods manage to model the real-world degradations
043   with complex degradation models, including degradation shuffle from BSRGAN (Zhang et al., 2021)
044   and high-order degradation from Real-ESRGAN (Wang et al., 2021). Other GAN-based (Liang
045   et al., 2022a;b; Chen et al., 2022) or GAN-prior (Menon et al., 2020; Pan et al., 2021; Chan et al.,
046   2021) models also achieve impressive performance. While these methods enhance the perceptual
047   quality of the reconstructed images, their generative capacity is limited and the training process in
048   GANs is unstable, which may occasionally lead to unrealistic artifacts.
049   Recently, emerging diffusion models (DM) (Sohl-Dickstein et al., 2015; Ho et al., 2020; Song et al.,

051   cluding StableDiffusion (SD) (Rombach et al., 2022), have exhibited superior performance in image
052   generation against GANs. Later with ControlNet (Zhang et al., 2023), various SD-based super-
053   resolution (SDSR) methods can leverage pre-trained diffusion priors. Some of them (Wang et al.,
      2024a; Lin et al., 2023; Sun et al., 2023) neglect semantic embedding, resulting in sub-optimal per-

      2.1   R EAL -W ORLD I MAGE S UPER -R ESOLUTION
111
112   While deep learning-based image super-resolution has made notable advancements (Dong et al.,
113   2015; Lim et al., 2017; Zhang et al., 2018b; Dai et al., 2019; Niu et al., 2020; Liang et al., 2021;
114   Zhang et al., 2022), most of them rely on simple bicubic degradation, which restricts their effective-
115   ness in handling complex and unknown degradations encountered in real-world scenarios. They
116   also encounter challenges such as overly smoothed details when minimizing fidelity objectives.
117   Real-world image super-resolution seeks to reconstruct photo-realistic image details by optimizing
118
      not just fidelity objectives but also perception objectives. Some works explore complex degrada-
      tion models to approximate the real-world degradations, including degradation shuffle from BSR-
119
      GAN (Zhang et al., 2021) and high-order degradation from Real-ESRGAN (Wang et al., 2021).
120
      Further in (Menon et al., 2020; Pan et al., 2021; Chan et al., 2021), they leverage pretrained Style-
121   GAN (Karras et al., 2019) as generative priors for Real-ISR. Even though they excel at robustly
122   removing degradation in Real-ISR tasks, their limited generative capacity often hinders them from
123   generating realistic details.
124
      Emerging diffusion models Dhariwal & Nichol (2021); Rombach et al. (2022) exhibit a remarkable
125
      capability to generate high-quality images. When it comes to ISR tasks, several approach train
126
      their DMs from scratch on pixel space (Choi et al., 2021; Saharia et al., 2022). While the former
127   necessitates tens to hundreds of diffusion steps, others (Xia et al., 2023; Chen et al., 2024) apply
128   DM on compact latent space, but their generative ability is greatly restricted by the transformer
129   backbone. Resshift (Yue et al., 2024) constructs a Markov chain to shift the residual between LQ
130   and HQ images with only 15 steps. Additionally, SinSR (Wang et al., 2024b) advances this strategy
131   to single-step with consistency preserving distillation. However, without strong diffusion priors,
132   these methods still struggle to generate realistic and intricate textures.
133
134   2.2   SD- BASED S UPER -R ESOLUTION
135
136   For pretrained DMs, ControlNet (Zhang et al., 2023) introduces an effective conditioning method,
137   enabling broader applications. Based on this, various SD-based super-resolution (SDSR) methods
138
      have achieved unprecedented success. Some of them (Wang et al., 2024a; Lin et al., 2023; Sun
      et al., 2023) do not apply semantic embedding to guide the diffusion process, leading to sub-optimal
139
      performance. PASD (Yang et al., 2024) employ pretrained models including BLIP2 (Li et al., 2023)
140
      together with CLIP (Radford et al., 2021) text encoder to provide semantic guidance. Similarly,
141   CoSeR (Sun et al., 2024) train a cognitive encoder to approximate the cascaded outputs of BLIP2
142   and CLIP. But they both involve the image captioning process, which focus on coarse-grained clas-
143   sification information while neglecting the color or texture details, as shown in Figure 4. SeeSR (Wu
144   et al., 2024) fine-tunes RAM (Zhang et al., 2024) model and separately integrates text prompt and
145   image prompts with additional cross-attention layers. However, given CLIP’s proficiency in map-
146   ping text and image to a joint embedding space, the T2I priors from the SD model can also be
147   applied to image prompts. Our decouple cross-attention harnesses our robust CLIP image encoder
148   and exploits the domain alignment in CLIP embeddings, adapting our cross-attention for both text
149
      and image prompts.
150   Furthermore, all the aforementioned SDSR methods follow the training protocol from DDPM (Ho
151   et al., 2020). We review the drawback in this training process, which assumes all inputs as HQ
152   latent data with Gaussian noise. Through the proposed Time-aware Latent Augmentation (TALA),
153   includes residuals between LQ and HQ latents we enable our ConsisSR to effectively eliminates
154   diffusion noise while refining LQ latent to HQ latent.

187   As shown in Figure 3, we demonstrate our training augmentation on the left and the network archi-
188   tecture on the right. Firstly, we equip the diffusion UNet with ControlNet (Zhang et al., 2023) to

201
      SD originates from the T2I generation task, hence original cross-attention is only tailored to text
      prompts. However, given that CLIP can inherently map images and text to a joint latent space,
202
      our intuition suggests that we can leverage the LQ image as conditioning. To better align the T2I
203
      generation task with Real-ISR, we propose the hybrid prompt adapter (HPA) to incorporate the input
204   image prompt with the text prompt to enhance semantic consistency.
205
206   Although other SDSR methods also attempt to incorporate semantic guidance, including
207
      CoSeR (Sun et al., 2024), PASD (Yang et al., 2024), they both involve the image captioning pro-

208
      details. As shown in Figure 4, we present various text descriptions in a Venn diagram pattern. Fol-
209
      lowing Radford et al. (2021), we calculate the cosine distances between the CLIP image embedding
210   and different text embeddings to gauge the similarity between the input image and each text descrip-
211   tion. It can be observed that while BLIP2 offers a reasonable caption, it falls short in capturing the
212   detailed information like color and accessory (white dog with red collar). The most precise and elab-
213   orate description exhibits the highest similarity, indicating the intricate details inherent in the image
214   embedding itself. Therefore, we incorporate the more powerful CLIP image embedding with text
215   prompt in our Hybrid Prompt Adapter, providing fine-grained semantic guidance for our denoising
      network, as depicted in Figure 5.

      3.4     T IME -AWARE L ATENT AUGMENTATION
253
254   While diffusion models excel at approximating complex data distribution, ensuring the consistency
255   of the generated results in this process is challenging. However, the SDSR task strongly emphasizes
256   the pixel-level consistency of the generated images with targets, necessitating better handling of LQ
257   latent representations, especially for the early steps which focus on structure refinement (Wang et al.,
258   2024a). To achieve this goal, we propose Time-aware Latent Augmentation (TALA) to improve
259   sampling consistency with the HQ target.
260   For T2I generation tasks, there are no specific requirements for detailed composition, including
261   proportions and relative positions of different elements, but rather a focus on semantic similarity.
262   However, for Real-ISR tasks, it is essential to prioritize structural consistency over blindly pursuing
263   visual aesthetics. Although ControlNet (Zhang et al., 2023) introduces an effective conditioning
264   method to provide pixel-level guidance, most SDSR methods still overlook certain drawbacks in the
265   training of diffusion models.
266   To take a deeper insight, we take our ConsisSR as an example and truncate the predicted x̂t→0
267   at each step and decode them into images. We further quantified their reconstruction quality over
268   timesteps in Figure 6. We can clearly observe that sampling consistency, represented by PSNR and
269   SSIM, remarkably improves and remains stable until 40% (timestep 600). Subsequently, at the cost
      of consistency, the diffusion model steadily enhances texture details, leading to an improvement in

309   3.5                    GAN-E MBEDDING S TRATEGY FOR I NFERENCE ACCELERATION
310
311   Existing SDSR methods often require a large number of timesteps for inference. But unlike T2I
312   generation tasks, SDSR involves LQ inputs and does not require starting from pure Gaussian noise.
313   To address this issue, we propose GAN-Embedding (GANEmb) strategy for inference acceleration.
314
      We harness the pre-trained Real-ESRGAN (Wang et al., 2021) to pre-refine the LQ latent. Initiating
      the reverse process from this enhanced latent allows our model to skip early refinement steps and
315
      focus on detail generation. This compresses the inference process to a minimum of 10 steps while
316
      preserving sampling quality, all in a training-free manner.
317
318   Some methods attempt to reduce the inference steps by adjusting the noise schedule or sampling
319   strategy Yue et al. (2024); Wang et al. (2024b); Sun et al. (2023). But they necessitate retraining
320
      the model, which sacrifices their ability to generate the most realistic and detailed textures. Instead,
      our GANEmb directly improves the inference process based on the trained SDSR model without
321
      additional training.
322
323   As discussed in the previous section, we empirically divide the inference process into the initial 40%
      sampling steps for consistency preservation and the last 60% sampling steps for detail generation.

529
530
531
      Pretrained T2I diffusion models offer generative priors for Real-ISR task, yet they typically empha-
      size semantic consistency while neglecting pixel-level fidelity. To bridge the gap between T2I gen-
532
      eration and Real-ISR tasks, we present ConsisSR, which adeptly exploit semantic and pixel-level
533
      consistency. Utilizing the powerful CLIP image embeddings, our Hybrid Prompt Adapter (HPA)
534   seamlessly integrates both text and image modalities, providing semantic guidance for diffusion
535   process. Furthermore, we introduce Time-aware Latent Augmentation (TALA) to improve pixel-
536   level consistency in early timesteps. By randomly selecting LQ and HQ latent inputs, our model
537   handles timestep-specific diffusion noise and refines latent states in the meantime. Additionally, our
538   GAN-Embedding strategy, leveraging pretrained Real-ESRGAN, accelerates the diffusion process
539   to just 10 steps without sacrificing quality. Our innovative approach achieves state-of-the-art results
      among both full-scale and accelerated diffusion models.
