009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            We present InfiniteMesh, a feed-forward framework for efficient high-quality
012            image-to-3D generation with view interpolation. Recent advancements in Large
013            Reconstruction Model (LRM) have demonstrated significant potential in extract-
014            ing 3D content from multi-view images produced by 2D diffusion models. Never-
015            theless, challenges remain as 2D diffusion models often struggle to generate dense
016            images with strong multi-view consistency, and LRMs often exacerbate this multi-
017            view inconsistency during 3D reconstruction. To address these issues, we propose
018
               a novel framework based on LRM that employs 2D diffusion-based view interpo-
               lation to enhance the quality of the generated mesh. Leveraging multi-view images
019
               produced by a 2D diffusion model, our approach introduces an Infinite View Inter-
020
               polation module to generate interpolated images from main views. Subsequently,
021            we employ a tri-plane-based mesh reconstruction strategy to extract robust tokens
022            from these multiple generated images and produce the final mesh. Extensive ex-
023            periments indicate that our method generates high-quality 3D content in terms of
024            both texture and geometry, surpassing previous state-of-the-art methods.
025

027   1   I NTRODUCTION
028
029   3D generation from a single image has become increasingly vital across various fields, including
030   virtual reality, gaming, and robotics Pang et al. (2024). Recent advancements in 2D diffusion models
031   Ho et al. (2020); Song et al. (2021); Blattmann et al. (2023a) and Large Reconstruction Models
032   (LRMs) Hong et al. (2023); Li et al. (2023); Tang et al. (2024); Wang et al. (2024); Xu et al. (2024a)
033
      have opened new avenues for 3D content creation. Several works, such as Poole et al. (2022); Lin
      et al. (2023); Qian et al. (2023); Seo et al. (2023); Qiu et al. (2024); Chen et al. (2024a;b), leverage
034
      2D diffusion models to generate 3D content through a Score Distillation Sampling (SDS) pipeline.
035
      An alternative approach involves creating multi-view images using 2D diffusion, followed by the
036   application of reconstruction algorithms to obtain 3D content from these images Liu et al. (2023a);
037   Shi et al. (2023b); Liu et al. (2023b); Wang & Shi (2023); Shi et al. (2023a); Long et al. (2024).
038
039
      Nonetheless, current state-of-the-art (SoTA) methods typically produce a limited number of multi-
      view images (usually four or six), which restricts the generation of geometric and textural details.
040
      Approaches such as Blattmann et al. (2023b); Voleti et al. (2024); Chen et al. (2024c) have intro-
041
      duced video diffusion strategies to directly increase the number of generated multi-view images,
042   however, they are often plagued by the challenge of multi-view inconsistency, as illustrated in Fig.
043   1 (SV3D and V3D). Besides, They also require significant training costs, including GPU memory,
044   etc., which greatly limit their application.
045
      To address these limitations, we introduce InfiniteMesh, a novel LRM-based image-to-3D frame-
046
      work, designed to improve 3D generation quality through 2D diffusion-based view interpolation.
047
      InfiniteMesh generates a large number of multi-view images with two steps. Firstly, InfiniteMesh
048   employs a 2D diffusion model for N main views generation (N is 4), then, an Infinite View In-
049   terpolation (IVI) module is incorporated to generate interpolated images with superior multi-view
050   consistency from main views, enriching representational details. Finally, a tri-plane-based mesh
051   reconstruction model utilizes these views to extract robust tokens, and produce a final mesh that
052   shows high-quality geometry and texture. We validate our approach using the Google Scanned Ob-
053   jects (GSO) dataset Downs et al. (2022) and images collected from the web, demonstrating that
      InfiniteMesh outperforms existing baseline methods.

      2     R ELATED W ORKS
091
092   2.1   3D G ENERATION
093
      Recent advancement in diffusion models Sohl-Dickstein et al. (2015) has brought image genera-
094
      tion to a new height Ho et al. (2020); Song et al. (2021); Rombach et al. (2022); Blattmann et al.
095
      (2023a). Numerous works have focused on leveraging diffusion models for 3D generation. A main-
096   stream approach is directly training 3D generators using 3D ground truth Zhou et al. (2021); Zheng
097   et al. (2023); Wang et al. (2023); Gupta et al. (2023); Shue et al. (2023). For instance, Zhou et al.
098   (2021) and Zheng et al. (2023) trained diffusion models to directly generate 3D voxels. In Wang
099   et al. (2023) and Shue et al. (2023), a 3D-aware tri-plane diffusion model is introduced to produce
100   NeRF Mildenhall et al. (2021) representations. Nonetheless, 3D diffusion methods tend to be time-
101   consuming during optimization, and often show low quality in terms of texture and geometry.
102   To deal with this, some studies have explored the utilization of 2D diffusion-based generators for
103   3D generation. DreamFusion Poole et al. (2022) was the first to use 2D diffusion models to generate
104   3D content through SDS. Building upon this work, Lin et al. (2023); Qian et al. (2023); Seo et al.
105   (2023); Qiu et al. (2024); Chen et al. (2024a;b) have adopted the SDS pipeline to optimize various
106   3D representations such as NeRF, mesh, and gaussian splatting Kerbl et al. (2023). However, per-
107   forming 3D generation tasks with 2D diffusion models often encounters issues related to multi-view
      inconsistency, indicating room for improvement.

      2.2    M ULTI - VIEW D IFFUSION M ODELS
109
110   Researchers have made great efforts to improve diffusion models in multi-view images generation.
111   Zero123 Liu et al. (2023a) was the first to encode camera pose as an additional condition to gen-
112   erate images from different specific views. On this basis, MVDream Shi et al. (2023b) replace
113   self-attention in the Unet architecture with multi-view attention to facilitate multi-view consistency.
114   Other works Liu et al. (2023b); Wang & Shi (2023); Shi et al. (2023a); Long et al. (2024) share a
115   similar idea to generate 3D-aware and multi-view consistent 2D representations. These multi-view
116
      images can be further processed using techniques such as NeRF Mildenhall et al. (2021) and Gaus-
      sian Splatting Kerbl et al. (2023) to obtain 3D representations. Nevertheless, existing multi-view
117
      diffusion models are constrained to generating a limited number of images from a single input im-
118
      age. Recent advancements Blattmann et al. (2023b); Voleti et al. (2024); Chen et al. (2024c) have
119   sought to outcome this limitation by utilizing temporal priors in video diffusion models to boost the
120   number of generated images. Despite these improvements, such strategies often neglect the connec-
121   tivity between frames, resulting in inconsistencies and diminishing the quality of the generated 3D
122   content.
123
124   2.3    L ARGE R ECONSTRUCTION M ODELS
125
126   The advent of large-scale 3D datasets Deitke et al. (2023; 2024) has significantly advanced the field
127   of image-to-3D generation, bringing generalized reconstruction models to new heights. LRM Hong
128
      et al. (2023) was a pioneer that demonstrates the superiority of Transformer Vaswani et al. (2017)
      backbone in mapping image tokens to predict tri-plane NeRF under multi-view supervision. Build-
129
      ing upon this foundation, Instant3D Li et al. (2023) extends the input to multi-view images, largely
130
      enhancing the quality of image-to-3D generation through multi-view diffusion models. Inspired by
131   Instant3D, subsequent methods such as LGM Tang et al. (2024) and GRM Xu et al. (2024b) further
132   refine it by replacing NeRF representations with 3D Gassian Splatting Kerbl et al. (2023) to improve
133   the rendering efficiency. Recently, CRM Wang et al. (2024) and InstantMesh Xu et al. (2024a) take
134   advantage of FlexiCubes Shen et al. (2023) to improve both efficiency and quality of image-to-3D
135   generation.

      3     I NFINITE M ESH
138
139
      As illustrated in Figure 2 (a), given a single input image x0 , the architecture of our proposed In-
140   finiteMesh consists of 4 primary components: 1) a multi-view diffusion model to generate main
141   multi-view images, 2) an Infinite View Interpolation (IVI) module to perform view interpolation
142   between any two neighbouring views, and 3) a tri-plane based large reconstruction model to recon-
143   struct a high-quality 3D mesh. The details of each component are elaborated below.
144

146
147   In this paper, we follow Long et al. (2024) to train a four-view generation model based on multi-view
148   2D diffusion, which takes a single image as input, and generate outputs from four viewpoints (front,
149   right, back, and left) to maximize multi-view consistency.
150

      5    L IMITATION AND C ONCLUSION
474
475
476   In this paper, we introduce InfiniteMesh, a novel LRM-based image-to-3D framework to produce
477   high-quality 3D content. Particularly, we propose an innovative multi-view diffusion-based IVI
478   module to perform view interpolation, followed by a tri-plane-based mesh reconstruction to obtain
479   the final mesh. Our experimental results indicate the superior performance of InfiniteMesh, demon-
480   strating its ability to generate 3D meshes with exceptional texture and geometric fidelity, compared
481   to existing SoTA methods.
482   Based on our view interpolation strategy, we can achieve further view expansion of diverse trajecto-
483   ries by further applying the IVI module between the generated images. However, the performance
484   of IVI module depends on the generation qualities of main view images in the first step. We be-
485   lieve improvements can be made by incorporating view super-resolution concept into multi-view
      diffusion at the feature level, which will be a primary focus of our future work.

668   is because we only increase the computational load in the image encoder’s transformer (cross-modal

670
