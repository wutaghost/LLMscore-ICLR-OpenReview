037                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 A BSTRACT
038
039           Diffusion models have demonstrated impressive generation capabilities, particularly
040           with recent advancements leveraging transformer architectures to improve both
041           visual and artistic quality. However, Diffusion Transformers (DiTs) continue to
042           encounter challenges related to low inference speed, primarily due to the iterative
043           denoising process. To address this issue, we propose BlockDance, a training-free
044           approach that explores feature similarities at adjacent time steps to accelerate
045
              DiTs. Unlike previous feature-reuse methods that lack tailored reuse strategies for
              features at different scales, BlockDance prioritizes the identification of the most
046
              structurally similar features, referred to as Structurally Similar Spatio-Temporal
047
              (STSS) features. These features are primarily located within the structure-focused
048           blocks of the transformer during the later stages of denoising. BlockDance caches
049           and reuses these highly similar features to mitigate redundant computation, thereby
050           accelerating DiTs while maximizing consistency with the generated results of the
051           original model. Furthermore, considering the diversity of generated content and
052           the varying distributions of redundant features, we introduce BlockDance-Ada, a
053           lightweight decision-making network tailored for instance-specific acceleration.
              BlockDance-Ada dynamically allocates resources and provides superior content

               quality. Both BlockDance and BlockDance-Ada have demonstrated effectiveness
055            across diverse generation tasks and models, achieving an acceleration ranging from
056            25% to 50% while preserving generation quality.

059   1    I NTRODUCTION
060
061   Diffusion models have been recognized as a pivotal advancement for both image and video generation
062   tasks due to their impressive capabilities. Recently, there has been a growing interest in shifting the
063   architecture of diffusion models from U-Net to transformers (OpenAI, 2024; Labs, 2024; Zhou et al.,
064
      2024). This refined architecture empowers these models not just to generate visually convincing and
      artistically compelling images and videos, but also to better adhere to scaling laws.
065
066   Despite the remarkable performance of these transformer-based diffusion models, their applicability
067   to real-time scenarios remains constrained by slow inference speed, primarily due to the iterative
068   nature of the denoising process. Existing acceleration approaches primarily focus on two paradigms:
069   I) reducing the number of sampling steps through novel scheduler designs (Song et al., 2021; Lu et al.,
070
      2022) or step distillation (Ren et al., 2024; Lin et al., 2024); II) minimizing computational overhead
      per step through the employment of model pruning (Fang et al., 2023; Kim et al., 2023), model
071
      distillation (Gupta et al., 2024; Zhang et al., 2024a), or the mitigation of redundant calculations (Wim-
072
      bauer et al., 2024; Ma et al., 2024b). This paper aims to accelerate DiTs by mitigating redundant
073   computation, as this paradigm can be plug-and-play into various models and tasks. Although feature
074   redundancy is widely recognized in visual tasks (He et al., 2022; Meng et al., 2022), and recent works
075   have identified its presence in the denoising process of diffusion models (Ma et al., 2024b; Li et al.,
076   2023), the issue of feature redundancy within DiT models—and the potential strategies to mitigate
077   these redundant computation—remains obscured from view.

107   the reused set often includes low-similarity features, leading to structural distortions in the image and

      the most similar features, i.e. Structurally Similar Spatio-Temporal (STSS) features. To be specific,
109   during the denoising process, structural content is typically generated in the initial steps when noise
110   levels are high, whereas texture and detail content are frequently generated in subsequent steps
111   characterized by lower noise levels (Ho et al., 2020; Hertz et al., 2023). Thus, we hypothesize that
112   once the structure is stabilized, the structural features will undergo minimal changes. To validate
113   this hypothesis, we decouple the features of DiTs at different scales, as illustrated in Figure 2 (b).
114   The observation reveals that the shallow and middle blocks, which concentrate on coarse-grained
115   structural content, exhibit minimal variation across adjacent steps. In contrast, the deep blocks,
116   which prioritize fine-grained textures and complex patterns, demonstrate more noticeable variations.
117
      Thus, we argue that allocating computational resources to regenerate these structural features yields
      marginal benefits while incurring significant costs. To address this issue, we propose a strategy of
118
      caching and reusing highly similar structural features subsequent to the stabilization of the structure
119
      to accelerate DiTs while maximizing consistency with the generated results of the original model.
120
121   Considering the diverse nature of generated content and their varying distributions of redundant
122   features, we introduce BlockDance-Ada, a lightweight decision-making network tailored for Block-
123
      Dance. In simpler content with a limited number of objects, we observe a higher presence of
      redundant features. Therefore, frequent feature reuse in such scenarios is adequate to achieve
124
      satisfactory results while offering increased acceleration benefits. Conversely, in the context of
125
      intricate compositions characterized by numerous objects and complex interrelations, there are fewer
126   high-similarity features available for reuse. Learning this adaptive strategy is a non-trivial task, as
127   it involves non-differentiable decision-making processes. Thus, BlockDance-Ada is built upon a
128   reinforcement learning framework. BlockDance-Ada utilizes policy gradient methods to drive a
129   strategy for caching and reusing features based on the prompt and intermediate latent, maximizing
130   a carefully designed reward function that encourages minimizing computation while maintaining
131   quality. Accordingly, BlockDance-Ada is capable of adaptively allocating resources.
132   The main contributions of this paper are summarized in the following:
133
134          • We introduce BlockDance, a novel, training-free, and efficient algorithm for accelerating
135            Diffusion Transformers (DiTs). This algorithm caches and reuses Structurally Similar
136            Spatio-Temporal (STSS) features to minimize redundant computation during inference,
137            offering compatibility with various models through a plug-and-play approach.
138          • Regarding the varying complexity of generated content, we investigate the feasibility of
139            instance-specific BlockDance strategies, and propose BlockDance-Ada, a method that
140            adaptively saves computation through employing reinforcement learning techniques.
141          • BlockDance has been validated across diverse datasets, including ImageNet, COCO2017,
142            and MSR-VTT. It has been tested in tasks such as class-conditioned generation, text-to-
143            image, and text-to-video using models such as DiT-XL/2, Pixart-α, and Open-Sora. Our
144
               experimental results reveal that our method can achieve a 25%-50% acceleration in inference
               speed with training-free while maintaining comparable generated quality. Furthermore, the
145
               proposed BlockDance-Ada generates higher-quality content at the same acceleration ratio.

148   2   R ELATED W ORK
149
150   Diffusion transformer. Diffusion models (Ho et al., 2020; Dhariwal & Nichol, 2021; Song & Er-
151   mon, 2020) have emerged as key players in the field of generation due to their impressive capabilities.
152
      Previously, U-Net-based (Ronneberger et al., 2015) diffusion models have demonstrated remarkable
      performance across various applications, including image generation (Rombach et al., 2022; Podell
153
      et al., 2024) and video generation (Wu et al., 2023; Singer et al., 2023; Blattmann et al., 2023).
154
      Recently, some research (Peebles & Xie, 2023; Chen et al., 2024a; Zheng, 2024; Ma et al., 2024a;
155   Li et al., 2024; Labs, 2024; Zhou et al., 2024) has transitioned to transformer-based (Vaswani et al.,
156   2017) architectures, i.e. Diffusion Transformers (DiTs). This framework excels in generating visually
157   convincing and artistically compelling content, better adheres to scaling laws, and shows promise in
158   efficiently integrating and generating multi-modality content. However, DiT models are still hindered
159   by the inherent iterative nature of the diffusion process, limiting their real-time applications.
160
161   Acceleration of diffusion models. Efforts have been made to accelerate the inference process of
      diffusion models, which can be summarized into two paradigms: reducing the number of sampling


                                                        3
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


162
      steps and reducing the computation per step. The first paradigm often involves designing faster
163   samplers (Song et al., 2021; Lu et al., 2022; Zhao et al., 2023) or step distillation (Meng et al.,
164   2023; Luo et al., 2023; Sauer et al., 2023; Lin et al., 2024; Sauer et al., 2024; Ren et al., 2024).
165   The second paradigm focuses on model-level distillation (Gupta et al., 2024; Zhang et al., 2024a),
166   pruning (Kim et al., 2023; Fang et al., 2023), or reducing redundant computation (Ma et al., 2024b;
167   Bolya & Hoffman, 2023; Li et al., 2023; Wimbauer et al., 2024; So et al., 2024; Zhang et al., 2024b).
168   Several studies (Ma et al., 2024b; Li et al., 2023) have unearthed the existence of redundant features
169   in U-Net-based diffusion models, but their coarse-grained feature reuse strategies include those
170   low-similarity features, leading to structural distortions and text-image misalignment. In contrast, we
171
      investigate the feature redundancy in DiTs and propose reusing structurally similar spatio-temporal
      features to achieve acceleration while maintaining high consistency with the base model’s results.
172
173
      Reinforcement learning in diffusion models. Efforts have been dedicated to fine-tuning diffusion
174
      models using reinforcement learning (Sutton & Barto, 2018) to align their outputs with human
175
      preferences or meticulously crafted reward functions. Typically, these models (Xu et al., 2023; Black
176   et al., 2023; Fan et al., 2023; Lee et al., 2023; Prabhudesai et al., 2023; Kirstain et al., 2023) aim to
177   improve the prompt alignment and visual aesthetics of the generated content. This paper explores
178   learning instance-specific acceleration strategies through reinforcement learning.

202   Features in the transformer. The number of denoising steps is related to the number of network

204   block sequentially computes its output based on the input from the previous block. The shallow

209   3.2   F EATURE S IMILARITY AND R EDUNDANCY IN D I T S
210
211   The inference speed of DiTs is constrained by its inherently iterative nature of inference, limiting its
212
      practical applicability. This paper aims to reduce redundant computation to accelerate DiTs.
213   Upon revisiting the denoising process in various DiT models, including DiT-XL/2 (Peebles & Xie,
214   2023), PixArt-α (Chen et al., 2024a), and Open-Sora (Zheng, 2024), two key findings emerged: I)
215   There are significant feature similarities between consecutive steps, indicating redundant computation
      in the denoising process, as illustrated in Figure 2 (a); II) This high similarity is primarily manifested


                                                          4
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


216
      in the shallow and middle blocks (between 0 and 20 blocks) of the transformer, while deeper blocks
217   (between 21 and 27 blocks) exhibit more variations, as depicted in Figure 2 (b). We attribute this
218   phenomenon to the fact that structural content is generally produced in the initial steps, while textures
219   and details are generated in the later steps.
220
      To confirm this, we visualize the block features of PixArt-α using Principal Components Analy-
221
      sis (PCA), as shown in Figure 3. At the initial stages of denoising, the network primarily focuses
222
      on generating structural content, such as human poses and other basic forms. As the denoising
223   process progresses, the shallow and middle blocks of the network still concentrate on generating
224   low-frequency structural content, while the deeper blocks shift their focus towards generating more
225   complex high-frequency texture information, such as clouds and crowds within depth of field. Con-
226   sequently, after the structure is established, the feature maps highlighted by blue boxes in Figure 3
227   exhibit high consistency across adjacent steps. We define these computation as redundant computa-
228   tion, which relate to the low-level structures that the shallow and middle blocks of the transformer
229   focus on. Based on these observations, we argue that allocating substantial computational resources
230   to regenerate these similar features yields marginal benefits but leads to higher computational costs.
231
      Thus, our goal is to design a strategy that leverages these highly similar features to reduce redundant
      computation and accelerate the denoising process.

253   3.3                T RAINING - FREE ACCELERATION APPROACH
254
255   We introduce BlockDance, a straightforward yet effective method to accelerate DiTs by leveraging
256   feature similarities between steps in the denoising process. By strategically caching the highly similar
257   structural features and reusing them in subsequent steps, we reduce redundant computation.
258   Specifically, we design the denoising steps into two types: cache step and reuse step, as illustrated in
259   Figure 4. During consecutive time steps, a cache step first conducts a standard network forward based
260   on zt+1 , outputs zt , and saves the features Fti of the i-th block. For the following time step—the reuse
261   step—we do not perform full network forward computation; instead, we carry out partial inference.
262   More specifically, we reuse the cached features Fti from the cache step as the input for the (i + 1)-th
263   block in the reuse step. Therefore, the computation of the first i blocks in the reuse step can be saved
264   due to the sequential inference characteristic of the transformer blocks, and only the blocks deeper
265   than i require recalculation.
266   To this end, it is crucial to determine the optimal block index and the stage of the denoising process
267   where reuse should be concentrated. Based on the insights from Figure 2 and Figure 3, we set the
268   index as 20 and focus the reuse on the latter 60% of the denoising process, after the structure has
269   stabilized. These settings enable the decoupling of feature reuse and specifically reuse the structurally
      similar spatio-temporal features. Thus, we set the first 40% of denoising steps as cache steps and

      evenly divide the remaining 60% of denoising steps into several groups, each comprising N steps.
271   The first step of each group is designated as a cache step, while the subsequent N − 1 steps are reuse
272   steps to accelerate inference. With the arrival of a new group, a new cache step updates the cached
273   features, which are then utilized for the reuse steps within that group. This process is repeated until
274   the denoising process concludes. A larger N represents a higher reuse frequency. We term this cache
275   and reuse strategy as BlockDance-N , which operates in a training-free paradigm and can effectively
276   accelerate multiple types of DiT models while maintaining the quality of the generated content.

      Accelerate PixArt-α for text-to-image generation. The results on the 25k COCO2017 validation
393   set, as shown in Table 1, demonstrate the efficacy of BlockDance. We extend ToMe (Bolya &
394   Hoffman, 2023) and DeepCache (Ma et al., 2024b) to PixArt-α as baselines. For ToMe, we reduce
395   the computational cost by removing 25% of the tokens through the merge operation. For DeepCache,
396   we reuse features at intervals of 2 throughout the denoising process, specifically reusing the outputs
397   from the first 14 blocks out of the 28 blocks in PixArt-α. With N = 2, BlockDance accelerates
398   PixArt-α by 25.4% with no significant degradation in image quality, both in terms of visual aesthetics
399   and prompt following. Different speed-quality trade-offs can be modulated by N .
400   Compared to ToMe, BlockDance consistently outperforms ToMe by a clear margin regardless
401   of the reuse frequency N . This can be attributed to DiTs featuring a more attention-intensive
402   architecture than the U-Net-based one, thus the continuous use of token merging in DiTs exacerbates
403   quality degradation. Compared to DeepCache, BlockDance achieves better performance across all
404   image metrics at comparable speeds by focusing on high-similarity features. We specifically reduce
405   redundant structural computation in the later stages of denoising, avoiding dissimilar feature reuse and
406   minimizing image quality loss. However, DeepCache reuses features throughout the entire denoising
407
      process and does not specifically aim at highly similar features for reuse. This leads to the inclusion
      of dissimilar features in the reused set, resulting in structural distortions and a decline in prompt
408
      alignment. Compared to TGATE (Zhang et al., 2024b), which accelerates by reducing the redundancy
409
      in cross-attention calculations. Blockdance supports DiTs that do not incorporate cross-attention,

532   5         C ONCLUSION
533
534   In this paper, we introduce BlockDance, a novel training-free acceleration approach for DiTs that
535   leverages the redundancy across adjacent denoising steps. During the denoising process, by caching
536   and reusing structure-level features after the structure has stabilized, i.e., structurally similar spatio-
537   temporal features, BlockDance significantly accelerates DiTs with minimal quality loss and maintains
538   high consistency with the base model. Additionally, we propose BlockDance-Ada, a variant of
539   BlockDance that dynamically allocates computational resources based on instance-specific reuse
      policies, further enhancing the efficiency of DiTs inference while maintaining superior image quality.

776
777   Limitation. Although BlockDance accelerates various DiT models across various generative tasks
778   in a plug-and-play manner, its application is limited in scenarios with very few denoising steps (e.g.,
779   1 to 4 steps), due to the reduced similarity of features between adjacent steps. However, in scenarios
780
      where most base models use a larger number of steps during inference, training a distilled version
      with fewer steps for each base model incurs high training costs and time consumption, whereas our
781
      method requires no additional training costs and operates in a plug-and-play manner.
