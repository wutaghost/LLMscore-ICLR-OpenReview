011                                              A BSTRACT
012
013            We present PT-LiRPA (Probabilistically Tightened LiRPA), a novel approach
014            that enhances existing linear relaxation-based perturbation analysis (LiRPA)
015            methods for neural network verification. PT-LiRPA combines LiRPA ap-
016            proaches with a sampling-based underestimation technique to compute probabilis-
017
               tically optimal intermediate bounds, resulting in tighter linear lower and upper
               bounds. Notably, we show that this approach preserves the soundness of veri-
018
               fication results while significantly tightening the bounds for generic non-linear
019
               functions. Additionally, we introduce a new metric, ∆∗ , to quantify the tightness
020            for LiRPA bounds and to bound the magnitude of the possible error in the sample-
021            based overestimation, thus complementing the probabilistic bound of statistical
022            results we use. Our empirical evaluation, conducted on several state-of-the-art
023            benchmarks, including those from the International Verification of Neural Net-
024            works Competition, demonstrates that PT-LiRPA achieves higher or comparable
025            verified accuracy with lower verification times. The significantly tighter bounds
026            and better efficiency allow us to verify instances where state-of-the-art methods
027            could not provide a specific answer.

030   1   I NTRODUCTION
031
032   Deep neural networks (DNNs) and recently large language models (LLMs) have revolutionized
033   various fields, from healthcare and finance to natural language processing, enabling unprecedented
034   capabilities, for instance, in image recognition (O’Shea & Nash, 2015) and autonomous navigation
035   (Tai et al., 2017). However, their opacity and vulnerability to the so-called “adversarial inputs”
036   (Szegedy et al., 2013) raise significant concerns, particularly when they are deployed in safety-
037
      critical applications such as autonomous driving, medical diagnosis, or financial decision-making.
      Hence, developing methods to ensure that these models can be trusted, even in edge cases, is crucial.
038
      Provable safety guarantees involve formal verification (FV) techniques that mathematically ensure
039
      a system under a given amount of input perturbation will not produce harmful outcomes, offering a
040   higher level of assurance than empirical testing alone.
041
042   Existing FV approaches tackle the problem in two main ways. The first solution consists of encoding
043
      the linear combinations and the non-linear activation functions of a DNN as a set of constraints for
      an optimization problem (Katz et al., 2017; Wu et al., 2024). The second method relies on interval
044
      bound propagation (IBP) (Lomuscio & Maganti, 2017; Gowal et al., 2018; Gehr et al., 2018) and
045
      consists of determining each neuron’s reachable set, i.e., the lower and upper bound values until the
046   output layer. However, due to the non-linear and non-convex nature of the DNN, computing the
047   exact bounds of a neural network has been proven to be NP-hard (Katz et al., 2017). To address
048   this challenging problem, a recent line of works called linear relaxation-based perturbation analysis
049   (LiRPA) algorithms (Zhang et al., 2018; Xu et al., 2020b; Wang et al., 2021; Xu et al., 2020a)
050   proposes a perturbation analysis based on a sound DNN linear relaxation. In detail, for a given
051   DNN, the idea is to compute a linear relaxation of any non-linear activation function in the network.
052   Thus for any possible input x ∈ C (with C, for instance, an ℓ∞ ball around the original input
053   x0 ), we can obtain two linear bounds for the output f (x), an upper and lower bound, such that
      f (x) = aT x + c ≤ f (x) ≤ f (x) = aT x + c. Hence these approaches compute sound, over-

241   2.3   R ELATED W ORK
242
243   In recent years, significant research has been dedicated to increasing the quality of linear bounds of
244   the most popular activation functions, such as ReLU, and more general activation functions. More
245   specifically, (Xu et al., 2020a) proposes a framework for deriving and computing near-optimal sound
246   bounds with linear relaxation-based perturbation analysis for neural networks. This framework is the
247
      base of all the most famous state-of-the-art formal verification tools such as CROWN (Zhang et al.,
      2018), α-CROWN (Xu et al., 2020b), β-CROWN (Wang et al., 2021), the top performer on last
248
      years VNN-COMP (Müller et al., 2022; Brix et al., 2023). Recently, different approaches have tried
249
      to incorporate a sampling-based approach to enhance either the linear relaxation of arbitrary non-
250   linear functions (Paulsen & Wang, 2022; Biktairov & Deshmukh, 2023) or the verification process
251   (Balunovic et al., 2019). For instance, (Paulsen & Wang, 2022) proposed a method synthesizing lin-
252   ear bounds for arbitrary complex activation functions, such as GeLU (Hendrycks & Gimpel, 2016)
253   and Swish (Ramachandran et al., 2017), by combining a sampling technique with an LP solver to
254   synthesize candidate lower and upper bound coefficients and then certified the final result via SMT
255   solvers (Gao et al., 2013). However, no tightness optimality guarantees are returned for the bounds
256   computed. To address such an issue, (Biktairov & Deshmukh, 2023) presented a combination of an
257   efficient sampling-based approach and linear programming solvers for finding linear bounds arbi-
258
      trarily close to optimum in terms of tightness for Lipschitz-continuous functions. Unlike LinSyn,
      their approach provides optimality guarantees based on LP verification for the generated bounds
259
      and does not heavily rely on using the SMT solver, resulting in superior running time performance.
260
      However, they still require an LP solver to provide optimality guarantees.
261
262   In contrast, the scope of this paper is to provide a method to probabilistically enhance the tightness of
263   existing LiRPA linear bounds without relying on any LP or SMT solvers. Hence, our methodology
264
      and comparative analysis will be predominantly based on the available auto LiRPA (Xu et al.,
      2020a), which recently has also incorporated the improvements of (Paulsen & Wang, 2022; Bik-
265
      tairov & Deshmukh, 2023), and α, β−CROWN (Zhang et al., 2018; Xu et al., 2020b; Wang et al.,
266
      2021) frameworks. This allows us to effectively derive and assess the tightness of our linear bounds
267   approach with respect to recent state-of-the-art approaches also employed in the VNN-COMP.

528   5     C ONCLUSION
529
530   We introduced PT-LiRPA, a novel probabilistic method that enhances the formal verification of
531   deep neural networks by combining existing linear relaxation-based perturbation approaches with
532   a sampling-based technique. Our approach provides tighter linear bounds while maintaining prov-
533   able guarantees on the soundness of the result returned, significantly improving both the accuracy
534   and computational efficiency of verification. Moreover, we presented a new analytical formula, ∆∗ ,
535
      which offers a dual assurance of optimality for LiRPA bounds and qualitative insights into the error
      margin of sample-based estimations. Empirical results demonstrate that PT-LiRPA outperforms
536
      existing methods, particularly in terms of verification time, while also successfully addressing pre-
537
      viously unsolved instances. Inspiring future directions involves studying the impact of this novel
538   approach for verification guarantees for other realistic tasks, such as deep reinforcement learning or
539   explainability of AI models.
