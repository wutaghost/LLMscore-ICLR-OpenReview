011                                               A BSTRACT
012
013            Bilevel optimization (BO) has recently gained significant attention in various ma-
014            chine learning applications due to its ability to model the hierarchical structures
015            inherent in these problems. Several gradient-free methods have been proposed to
016
               address stochastic black-box bilevel optimization problems, where the gradients of
               both the upper and lower-level objective functions are unavailable. However, these
017
               methods suffer from high query complexity and do not accommodate more general
018
               bilevel problems involving nonsmooth regularization. In this paper, we present a
019            query-efficient method that effectively leverages Bregman distance to solve nons-
020            mooth stochastic black-box bilevel optimization problems. More importantly, we
021            provide a non-asymptotic convergence analysis, showing that our method requires
022            only O(d1 (d1 + d2 )2 ѕµвИТ2 ) queries to reach the ѕµ-stationary point. Additionally,
023            we conduct experiments on data hyper-cleaning and hyper-representation learning
024            tasks, demonstrating that our algorithms outperform existing bilevel optimization
025            methods.

092   2.1   H YPERGRADIENT BASED B ILEVEL OPTIMIZATION
093
094   Bilevel optimization Bracken & McGill (1973) has been extensively studied for decades. Various
095   strategies have emerged in recent years for addressing stochastic bilevel optimization problems. The
096   first class of methods relies on a two-loop structure: an inner loop solves the lower-level problem,
097   and an outer loop updates the upper-level variable using an approximate stochastic hypergradient.
098   Bracken & McGill (1973); Chen et al. (2021a); Ji et al. (2021) employed SGD updates for the
099
      lower-level problem, where each outer-loop iteration estimates the Hessian-inverse-vector product
      by solving a linear system or use the explicit gradient method. Another class of methods focuses
100
      on single-loop algorithms, which alternately/simultaneously update the upper-level and lower-level
101
      variables. Specifically, Hong et al. (2023) introduced a method that uses Neumann approximations
102   for the inverse Hessian, coupled with a single SGD step for the lower-level problem. Shi et al. (2024)
103   proposed a new definition of the stationary point and proposed a new method for the lower-level
104   constrained bilevel optimization problem. Guo et al. (2021b); Yang et al. (2021); Khanduri et al.
105   (2021) incorporated momentum acceleration to improve convergence rates. However, these methods
106   are not truly single-loop, as estimating the Hessian-inverse-vector product via the Neumann series
107   requires an additional iterative subroutine. More recently, DagreћБou et al. (2022) proposed a novel
      framework for bilevel optimization that allows simultaneous updates of the lower-level variables, the

108
      linear system loss variables, and the upper-level variables. However, its applicability to black-box
109   bilevel optimization has not yet been explored.

      2.2   B REGMAN D ISTANCE -BASED M ETHODS
112
113   The Bregman distance-based method (Bregman, 1967; Censor & Lent, 1981; Censor & Zenios,
114   1992; Beck & Teboulle, 2003) is an approach commonly used in optimization and machine learning,
115   particularly in settings where traditional Euclidean distances are not the most suitable measure of
116   divergence or similarity between points. This method leverages Bregman divergences, a generalization
117   of distance measures tailored for convex functions and widely applied in problems such as convex
118   optimization, iterative algorithms, and regularization techniques. Recently, the mirror descent method
119   has been extended to address minimax or bilevel optimization challenges. Specifically, Babanezhad &
120   Lacoste-Julien (2020) proposed an algorithm rooted in mirror descent principles for tackling convex-
121
      concave minimax scenarios. Rafique et al. (2022) designed a series of mirror descent strategies
      suitable for minimax optimization in weakly convex contexts. Additionally, a novel approach
122
      based on mirror descent principles (Mertikopoulos et al., 2019) has emerged for addressing certain
123
      nonconvex-nonconcave minimax issues characterized by non-monotonic variational inequalities.
124   More recently, Huang et al. (2022) proposed a class of enhanced bilevel optimization methods
125   based on Bregman distance to solve the nonconvex-strongly-convex stochastic bilevel optimization
126   problems. In addition to first-order algorithms, some existing works focus on zeroth-order algorithms
127   for solving minimax/mini optimization problems. For example, Paul et al. (2023) proposed almost
128   sure convergence of zeroth-order mirror descent algorithm and can find an ѕµ-stationary point with the
129   total complexity of O(ѕµвИТ2 ). Maheshwari et al. (2022) proposed a gradient descent-ascent algorithm
130   with random reshuffling under convex-concave setting and obtained an ѕµ-stationary point with the
131   total complexity of O(ѕµвИТ4 ). Under the nonconvex-concave setting, Xu et al. (2024) proposed the
132
      ZO-AGP algorithm and its iteration complexity of obtaining an ѕµ-staitionary point is bounded by
      O(ѕµвИТ4 ). However, the Bregman distance-based method in the black-box bilevel optimization has not
133
      been fully discussed.

471   6.3              R ESULTS AND D ISCUSSION
472
473   All the results are presented in Figure 1, 2 and Table 2, 3. Across all experiments, our method
474   consistently demonstrates superior performance compared to ZDSBA and HOZOG, both in terms
475   of test accuracy and query efficiency. This performance advantage is particularly evident when
476   considering query costs, where our approach significantly reduces the number of queries required to
477
      achieve comparable or better accuracy. One key reason for this improvement is that HOZOG, while
      effective, depends on zeroth-order gradient approximations to compute the lower-level solution for
478
      each perturbed upper-level variable. This process inherently results in a much higher query demand,
479
      as zeroth-order methods are typically less query-efficient due to their reliance on sampling techniques.
480   Similarly, ZDSBA employs a zeroth-order method for solving the lower-level problem, further
481   exacerbating the query overhead and slowing convergence. In contrast, our method introduces a more
482   query-efficient framework by reducing the reliance on expensive zeroth-order computations. The
483   observed improvements in query efficiency and accuracy strongly support the theoretical convergence
484   guarantees we established in Theorem 1. These findings not only validate the effectiveness of our
485   approach but also highlight its potential for application in large-scale problems where query efficiency
      is critical.
