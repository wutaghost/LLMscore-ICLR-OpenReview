011                                                A BSTRACT
012
013            Recent literature has found that an effective method to customize or further improve
014            large language models (LLMs) is to add dynamic adapters, such as low-rank
015            adapters (LoRA) with Mixture-of-Experts (MoE) structures. Though such dynamic
016            adapters incur modest computational complexity, they surprisingly lead to huge
017            inference latency overhead, slowing down the decoding speed by 2.5+ times. In
018            this paper, we analyze the fine-grained costs of the dynamic adapters and find
019            that the fragmented CUDA kernel calls are the root cause. Therefore, we propose
020
               LoRA-Switch, a system-algorithm co-designed architecture for efficient dynamic
               adapters. Unlike most existing dynamic structures that adopt layer-wise or block-
021
               wise dynamic routing, LoRA-Switch introduces a token-wise routing mechanism.
022
               It switches the LoRA adapters and weights for each token and merges them into
023            the backbone for inference. For efficiency, this switching is implemented with an
024            optimized CUDA kernel, which fuses the merging operations for all LoRA adapters
025            at once. Based on experiments with popular open-source LLMs on common
026            benchmarks, our approach has demonstrated similar accuracy improvement as
027            existing dynamic adapters, while reducing the decoding latency by more than 2.4
028            times.

032   1    I NTRODUCTION
033
034   Large language models (LLMs) have demonstrated remarkable capabilities in language understanding
035   and generation. To customize the pretrained models to vertical domains or further enhance their
036   capabilities, various adapter techniques such as Low-Rank Adapters (LoRA) (Hu et al., 2021),
037   LLaMA-Adapter (Zhang et al., 2023), and Prompt Tuning (Lester et al., 2021) have been employed
038   with great success. These methods are acclaimed for boosting the accuracy of LLM without extensive
039
      training, thus facilitating efficient model customization and performance enhancement. Among these,
      dynamic adapters (Feng et al., 2024; Gou et al., 2024; Liu et al., 2023a; Luo et al., 2024) represent an
040
      even more potent strategy to augment the capacity of adapters. By integrating conditionally computed
041
      lightweight adapters into the pretrained model, dynamic adapters allow for selective fine-tuning of
042   adapter parameters. This technique not only maintains the original strengths of the model but also
043   substantially increases its adaptability and capacity.
044
045
      However, we found that despite the relatively minor impact of dynamic adapters on parameter size
      and computing complexity (typically adding only 1-5% of the origin model), they may introduce
046
      significant latency overhead. For instance, the dynamic adapters that we studied all increase decoding
047
      inference latency by 250-950%. The seemingly modest computational complexity of the low-rank
048   matrices employed results in substantial extra CUDA kernel execution latency, surpassing that of
049   models without dynamic adapters. This dramatic increase in latency is primarily attributed to the
050   prolonged execution time of context operations during CUDA kernel runs, which considerably
051   exceeds the actual computation time. Dynamic adapters often require four or more additional CUDA
052   kernel calls for each layer, in stark contrast to just a single call needed for the forward computation of
053   the original backbone matrix. This excessive number of context operations substantially amplifies the
      latency overhead, leading to a severe escalation of inference latency.

054
      Reducing the inference latency overhead of dynamic adapters is challenging. Existing dynamic
055   adapters (Dou et al., 2024; Feng et al., 2024; Gao et al., 2024; Gou et al., 2024; Li et al., 2024; Liu
056   et al., 2023a; Luo et al., 2024; Wu et al., 2024a; Yang et al., 2024) adopt block-wise or layer-wise
057   routing structures, where activated LoRA adapters must be computed separately. If we were to
058   pre-merge activated LoRA adapters into the backbone weights for forward computation, akin to the
059   strategy employed by LoRA (Hu et al., 2021), it would fundamentally reduce the number of CUDA
060   kernel calls. However, this merging changes the parameters of the LLM model, and for the next input,
061   different adapters might be activated. Thus, after processing the current input, it becomes necessary
062   to unmerge the activated adapters from the LLM model that was altered. The routing dynamicity
063
      involved in such merging processes becomes prohibitively costly. For instance, the widely adopted
      dynamic adapter structure, the Mixture of Experts (MoE), determines the activated adapters based on
064
      the output of the last layer. Each layer of dynamic adapters must then wait for its router to compute
065
      the gating scores before it can proceed to merge the activated LoRA adapters. This fragmented
066   operational approach can inadvertently introduce even greater overhead costs.
067
068   Our approach to addressing the challenge is based on a holistic system-algorithm co-design. Specifi-
069
      cally, we have developed a MoE-based dynamic adapters structure that facilitates token-wise adapter
      routing. Each token is associated with k weighted paths of LoRA adapters, activated prior to the de-
070
      coding of the token. This setup ensures that, although the model is enhanced with dynamic structures,
071
      the inference process for each token remains relatively static due to the pre-determined adapters.
072   To further enhance the efficiency, we pre-merge the activated LoRA adapters into the pretrained
073   model’s backbone before each token’s decoding. This strategy fundamentally reduces the CUDA
074   kernel execution overhead, thereby significantly lowering latency. With this innovative setup, we have
075   re-engineered the inference process to seamlessly switch and merge adapters for each token, aligning
076   the process closely with the original pretrained LLM’s token decoding. Another pivotal component
077   of our system is the development of a fused CUDA kernel, named SGMM, which efficiently manages
078   the activated and inactivated adapters. This engineering solution ensures a smooth integration of
079   dynamic adapters, optimizing both performance and efficiency.
080   We evaluate our LoRA-Switch design across a range of benchmarks, comparing it against multiple
081   state-of-the-art dynamic adapter baselines. The experiment results demonstrate that our approach are
082   comparable with well-established strong baselines. Notably, our method significantly reduces the
083   running overhead associated with other dynamic adapter alternatives, achieving an average speedup
084   of 2.4 times in decoding latency.
085   In summary, our contributions are as follows:
086
087   • We uncover the high latency overhead introduced by dynamic adapters, which is a practical issue
088     usually neglected by existing approaches. We analyze the fundamental reasons behind such high
089     overhead, providing insights on the computational bottlenecks.
090
      • We introduce a novel architecture for dynamic adapters, named LoRA-Switch. This design enhances
091
        the capacity of LLM adapters while minimizing the latency overhead, thereby offering an optimal
092     balance between performance and efficiency.
093
094   • Through extensive experiments, we demonstrate that LoRA-Switch not only achieves accuracy on
095     par with existing dynamic adapters across a variety of general and domain-specific tasks, but it also
096
        cuts down decoding inference latency by more than 2.4 times.

      2.3   C HALLENGE OF R EDUCING L ATENCY OVERHEAD OF DYNAMIC A DAPTERS
163
164   A straightforward way to reduce inference latency overhead is to reduce the times of CUDA kernel
165   context operations. Like LoRA (Hu et al., 2021), one couple pre-merge adapters into the original
166   matrix and then perform token decoding computation. We implement this simple strategy in MoRAL
167   by directly merging activated adapters layer by layer before computing, although it decreases the
168   number of CUDA kernel launches. However, the additional operations introduced higher latency,
169   where the decoding latency is 4.5 ms/token, which is still 88% higher than the original LLM model.
170
      This is because merging a LoRA adapter into the backbone matrix requires an additional invocation
      of a CUDA kernel to perform the matrix multiplication for the up and down projections. Due to these
171
      inherent computational complexities, optimizing the existing structure of dynamic adapters further is
172
      challenging.
173
174   To effectively leverage the structure of dynamic adapters, we introduce LoRA-Switch. Unlike the
175   existing layer-wise or block-wise dynamic routing mechanisms, our approach LoRA-Switch utilizes
176
      token-wise routing strategy, which fundamentally reduces the number of CUDA kernel calls, thereby
      reducing decoding inference latency.

193
194   As shown in Figure 2, given a pre-trained LLM, we first extend it with LoRA-Switch to enhance
195   its model capacity and then finetune it on either general or domain-specific datasets. During the

199   efficient unmerging of inactivated adapters from the backbone, significantly reducing the number

201   origin pretrained model, optimizing performance without compromising efficiency.

287   3.4   SGMM K ERNEL
288
289
      The straight-forward way to merge the LoRA adapter into the backbone is to merge them layer by
      layer. This approach requires multiple calls to the CUDA kernel, which introduces additional latency
290
      due to kernel launches. Moreover, smaller kernel computations underutilize GPU thread blocks,
291
      leading to a low GPU throughput. We observe the layer-by-layer merging operations can be handled
292   concurrently and introduce a CUDA kernel called Segmented Gather Matrix Multiplication (SGMM)
293   to finally handle the merging of LoRA adapters of LoRA-Switch, adapted from the concept of SGMV
294   proposed by Punica (Chen et al., 2023).
295
      SGMM is designed to execute a batched GEMM operations, which can be summarized by the
296
      following Equation 11:
297
298                          f∗ = f + Fused_LoRA_DOWN × Fused_LoRA_UP,                                  (11)
299
300   where f∗ is the resultant updated matrix of the backbone; f is the original weight matrix of the
301   backbone; Fused_LoRA_DOWN and Fused_LoRA_UP are the adapter matrices for weight matrix
302   f . The addition operation within the SGMM kernel is performed in place, significantly reducing the
303
      additional memory overhead. This optimization ensures that memory usage is minimized, enhancing
      the overall computational efficiency of our system.
304
305   When wrapping these operations into a single CUDA kernel, taking full advantage of the GPU’s com-
306   putational resources is challenging. Each thread block should be fully utilized, and the computational
307   load needs to be balanced across them. To achieve this, we divide the matrix multiplication into
308   multiple GEMM tiles and assign them to different thread blocks. On the other hand, these thread
309
      blocks must switch context with global memory/shared memory frequently, thus causing significant
      latency. To tackle this, we adopt a pre-fetch buffer mechanism to hide loading latency. It fetches data
310
      from higher-level memory before the next matrix operation to boost memory access efficiency.
311
312   The input parameters for SGMM are arrays of pointers to the LoRA matrices and the backbone
313   matrices, which respectively store the corresponding entries of each layer’s LoRA matrix and the
314   shape of each matrix segment. When launching the kernel, SGMM applies as many thread blocks as
315
      possible and divides the large matrix multiplication into multiple GEMM tiles of the same shape,
      with each tile operating matrix computation. Based on the input pointers and shapes of the original
316
      matrices, SGMM calculates the address and size of each tile in global memory and assigns each
317
      of them to a thread block. The optimal tiling scheme related to hardware is selected to ensure the
318   full utilization of each thread block. To hide the loading latency of tile switching, SGMM uses two
319   buffers in shared memory and CUDA core registers, respectively; one for the current tile’s matrix
320   computation and the other for pre-fetching the next tile’s data into the buffer. This design stems from
321   the same shape of sequential tiles, which makes us able to predict the next tile to be processed. In
322   our methodology, thread blocks are executed concurrently, facilitating the simultaneous processing
323   required for our computational tasks. This parallel execution enables the efficient merging and
      unmerging of LoRA weights, a critical operation in our approach.

492   5   R ELATED W ORK
493
494   Parameter efficient fine-tuning (PEFT). PEFT stands out as the optimal approach for fine-
495   tuning pretrained large language models (LLMs). Among the myriad of popular techniques are
496   Adapters (Houlsby et al., 2019), Prefix Tuning (Li and Liang, 2021), Prompt Tuning (Lester et al.,
497   2021), P-tuning (Liu et al., 2023b), and LoRA (Hu et al., 2021), each offering unique advantages.
498   This paper specifically explores the efficacy of LoRA, which has demonstrated superior performance
499
      and have the most efficient inference performance.
500   Dynamic adapters. Existing dynamic adapters can be categorized based on their gating strategies
501   into block-wise and layer-wise types, where adapters are added either to every block or to every
502   layer, respectively. Block-wise methods such as MOLA (Gao et al., 2024), MoELoRA (Luo et al.,
503   2024), MoCLE (Gou et al., 2024), and MOELoRA (Liu et al., 2023a) integrate adapter branches
504   within a single Transformer block. Conversely, layer-wise approaches like MoRAL (Yang et al.,
505
      2024), PESC (Wu et al., 2024b), and LoRAMoE (Dou et al., 2024) incorporate adapters within the
      MLP’s linear layers. While these methods introduce a minimal amount of parameters, they typically
506
      result in high inference latency. In contrast, our work introduces LoRA-Switch, a novel token-wise
507
      dynamic adapter approach that effectively integrates with system optimizations to significantly reduce
508   inference latency. This innovation represents a substantial improvement over traditional dynamic
509   adapter configurations.

512   6   C ONCLUSION
513
514   This paper addresses the significant challenge of inference latency in dynamic adapters for large
515
      language models (LLMs). Through a comprehensive analysis, we have identified the underlying
      factors contributing to increase latency in traditional block-wise and layer-wise adapter models. Our
516
      proposed solution, LoRA-Switch, introduces a novel token-wise dynamic adapter configuration that
517
      leverages system-level optimizations to dramatically reduce inference latency without compromising
518   the model’s adaptability and performance. The effectiveness of LoRA-Switch has been validated
519   through rigorous experiments, demonstrating substantial improvements in efficiency across both
520   general and domain-specific datasets. These findings not only enhance our understanding of dynamic
521   adapter architectures but also set a new benchmark for future research in efficient LLM tuning.
