009                                                   A BSTRACT
010
011                Medical ontology graphs, which typically organize and relate comprehensive
012                medical concepts in a hierarchical structure, are able to map a rich set of ex-
013                ternal knowledge onto the specific medical codes observed in electronic health
014                records (EHRs). Through the connectivity in ontologies, healthcare predictive
015                models can utilize the ancestor, descendant, or sibling information to add supple-
016                mentary contexts to medical codes, thereby augmenting expressiveness of EHR
017
                   representations. However, existing approaches are limited by the heterogeneous
                   isolation of different ontology systems (e.g., conditions vs. drugs), that different
018
                   types of ontology concepts have to be learned individually, and only the homoge-
019
                   neous ontology relationships can be exploited. This limitation restricts the existing
020                methods from fully leveraging the cross-ontology relationships which could sub-
021                stantially enhance healthcare representations. In this paper, we propose OntoFAR,
022                a framework that fuse multiple ontology graphs, utilizing the collaboration across
023                ontologies to enhance medical concept representation. Our method jointly repre-
024                sents medical concepts cross multiple ontology structures by performing message
025                passing in two dimensions: (1) vertical propagation over levels of ontology hier-
026                archy, and (2) horizontal propagation over co-occurring concepts in EHR visits.
027                Additionally, OntoFAR leverages the large language models (LLMs) pre-trained
028
                   on massive open world information to understand each target concept with its on-
                   tology relationships, providing enhanced embedding initialization for concepts.
029
                   Through extensive experimental studies on two public datasets, MIMIC-III and
030
                   MIMIC-IV, we validate the superior performance of OntoFAR over the state-of-
031                the-art baselines. Beyond accuracy, our model also exhibits the add-on compat-
032                ibility to boost existing healthcare prediction models, and demonstrate a good
033                robustness in scenarios with limited data availability.1

036   1       I NTRODUCTION
037
038   With the ubiquity of electronic health records (EHRs) in modern healthcare systems, developing
039   machine learning models to analyze comprehensive medical histories from large-scale patient pop-
040   ulations has shown great potential in enhancing a wide range of predictive tasks (Choi et al., 2016a;
041   Poulain & Beheshti, 2024; Jiang et al., 2023; Moghaddam et al., 2024). However, the inherent com-
042
      plexity of EHRs characterized by diverse, sparse, and temporal code appearance presents a challenge
      for learning expressive and robust medical concept representation. A promising solution to this chal-
043
      lange is to integrate external medical ontology graphs. to enhance representation learning.
044
045   By providing a rich domain contexts for medical codes and their interrelationships, ontologies offer
046   a rich source of knowledge bases to explain the medical code we observed in EHR. For instance, the
047   ICD ontology classifies diseases based on symptoms, complaints, and causes, while the Anatomical
048
      Therapeutic Chemical (ATC) ontology categorizes drugs according to organ systems, ingredients,
      and functions. These ontologies provide hierarchical concepts that define specific codes, such as
049
      diabetes, with general to specific classification. By incorporating the related concepts in different
050
      hierarchies, the models can better capture the relevance between the medical code to improve the ro-
051   bustness of representations, especially in challenging scenarios such as learning rare disease. There-
052   fore, recent studies have increasingly explored the augmentation of EHR representation through
053

      the integration of supplementary ontology graphs (Choi et al., 2017; Shang et al., 2019; Ye et al.,
055   2021; Zhang et al., 2020). However, a critical limitation in the current literature lies in the inability
056   to accommodate multiple ontology systems in a unified learning framework. Specifically, exist-
057   ing methods typically focus on learning each ontology as an independent structure where only the
058   vertical message passing (e.g., top-down concept aggregation) is facilitated. As a result, only the
059   intra-ontology relationship linking the homogeneous concepts (e.g., disease-to-disease) is adopted
060   for representing each medical code. To this end, there exists a need of fusing multiple ontologies and
061   introducing the cross-ontology relationship (e.g., disease-to-drug, disease-to-procedure), so that the
062   rich and diverse medical knowledge bases can be fully leveraged to augment EHR representations.
063   To address the gap of fusing diverse ontology graphs for augmenting EHR representation, we
064   propose a multifaceted graph learning architecture, OntoFAR (Hierarchical Ontology Fusion for
065   Augmenting Representation), aiming to enable graph message passing across multiple ontologies in
066   both vertical and horizontal dimensions. Specifically, OntoFAR facilitates (1) intra-ontology con-
067   cept association through vertical propagation across hierarchical levels within each ontologies, and
068   (2) inter-ontology concept fusion through horizontal propagation to connect co-occurring concepts
069
      in EHR data over all the ontology levels in parallel. By introducing the horizontal message passing
      as a new dimension, OntoFAR is advantageous in (1) connecting different ontologies at all levels
070
      of hierarchy, (2) capturing concept co-occurrence at all levels of EHR granularity, and (3) mining
071
      EHR patterns integrally with ontology structure learning. Furthermore, OntoFAR leverages the pre-
072   trained knowledge of large language models (LLMs) to initialize dense embeddings that can benefit
073   from extensive open-world information. Last, our ontology representation framework can serve as
074   a add-on component to most healthcare predictive models (e.g., RETAIN) for performance boosting
075   and robustness enhancement.
076
      To demonstrate the significance of our work, we conducted extensive experiments on two widely
077
      used EHR datasets, MIMIC-III and MIMIC-IV performing the task of sequential diagnosis predic-
078   tion, including performance enhancement analysis when integrating to EHR models, baseline com-
079   parisons, data insufficiency tests, and interpretative case studies. The results demonstrate that Onto-
080   FAR, as a plug-in medical concept encoder, significantly improves the encoding phase of healthcare
081   predictive models, leading to enhanced predictive healthcare performance.

      ontology SNOMED to integrate multi-source medical codes, whereas KAMPNet (An et al., 2023)
105
      employs contrastive learning to achieve effective EHR representation learning.

168   3.2    M ODEL OVERVIEW
169
      We present an overview of our proposed method, OntoFAR, a hierarchical multi-ontology fusing
170   encoder of medical concepts designed to enhance the representation of EHR data. The entire frame-
171   work depicted in Figure 1 is summarized in three key steps:
172
173
      Step 1: Formulating the Meta-KG denoted by G, a heterogeneous knowledge graph (KG) that joins
      multiple ontology graphs through each level of hierarchy. The initialization of node embeddings in
174
      Meta-KG is gained through LLM prompting and dense retrieval. This Meta-KG has L horizontal
175
      inter-ontology parallel graphs at each level for horizontal massage passing, and two vertical inter-
176   ontology graphs for each ontology, one bottom-up and one top-down, for vertical massage passing.
177
178   Step 2: Carrying out Horizontal Message Passing (HMP) over inter-ontology concept edges using
179
      EHR information. We use two options to construct horizontal graphs in Meta-KG: (1) regular graph
      structure where edges are defined based on the co-occurrence probabilities of concepts; and (2)
180
      hypergraph structure where each edge links all the concepts in a EHR visit.
181
182   Step 3: Performing Vertical Message Passing (VMP) over intra-ontology concept edges derived
183   from parent-child relationships in ontology hierarchy. Within each ontology system, the process
184   begins with a bottom-up propagation which passes up the embedding of each concept to its ances-
185
      tor first, then concludes with top-down propagation where concept embeddings are passed down
      yielding the final embeddings for leaf nodes using GRAM (Choi et al., 2017).

      with a multi-hot vector ut = {0, 1}N , which represents clinical events existence in the visit. The
335   sequence of visit representations {v1 , v2 , . . . , vt } serves as input to a main encoder, Encoder(·),
336   such as a Transformer or Retain, producing the encoded hidden embedding ht for the t-th visit.
337   The patient representation hp ∈ Rd is derived by aggregating visit embeddings using a function,
338   Aggregate(·), which may be summation, averaging, or attention-pooling. The final prediction is
                                                                                                        (L)
339   computed by applying a Sigmoid function to the linear transformation of hp , with W ∈ RN ×d
340                  (L)
      and b ∈ RN         as the weight and bias, respectively. The output ŷt+1 is the predicted vector in
341     N (L)
      R       . The loss at each timestamp is calculated using cross-entropy between the ground truth yt+1
342
      and predicted visit ŷt+1 . Algorithm 1 in Appendix A.1 outlines the OntoFAR process.

534   6         C ONCLUSION
535   We introduced OntoFAR, a multi-ontology fusion framework to augment medical concept represen-
536   tation in EHR models. OntoFAR extracts cross-ontology relationships through message passing in
537   two dimensions: vertical and horizontal, and initializes concept embeddings with LLM prompting
538   and dense retrieval. The proposed framework improves EHR prediction accuracy over state-of-the-
539   art methods. Additionally, we showcase the robustness of OntoFAR in data-limited scenarios and
      validate its add-on compatibility to enhance existing healthcare models.
