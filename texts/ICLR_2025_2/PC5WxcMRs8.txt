011                                               A BSTRACT
012
013              Search engines enable the retrieval of unknown information with texts. However,
014              traditional methods fall short when it comes to understanding unfamiliar visual
015
                 content, such as identifying an object that the model has never seen before. This
                 challenge is particularly pronounced for large vision-language models (VLMs): if
016
                 the model has not been exposed to the object depicted in an image, it struggles
017
                 to generate reliable answers to the user’s question regarding that image. More-
018              over, as new objects and events continuously emerge, frequently updating VLMs
019              is impractical due to heavy computational burdens. To address this limitation,
020              we propose Vision Search Assistant, a novel framework that facilitates collabo-
021              ration between VLMs and web agents. This approach leverages VLMs’ visual
022              understanding capabilities and web agents’ real-time information access to per-
023              form open-world Retrieval-Augmented Generation via the web. By integrating
024              visual and textual representations through this collaboration, the model can pro-
025              vide informed responses even when the image is novel to the system. Extensive
026
                 experiments conducted on both open-set and closed-set QA benchmarks demon-
                 strate that the Vision Search Assistant significantly outperforms the other models
027
                 and can be widely applied to existing VLMs.

      1       I NTRODUCTION
032
       The advent of Large Language Models (LLMs) (Achiam et al., 2023; OpenAI, 2024; Anthropic,
033
      2024; Touvron et al., 2023; Schulman et al., 2022; Chiang et al., 2023) has significantly enhanced the
034   human capacity to acquire unfamiliar knowledge through powerful zero-shot Question-Answering
035   (QA) capabilities. Building upon these advancements, techniques such as Retrieval-Augmented
036   Generation (RAG) (Yu et al., 2023; Shi et al., 2023; Trivedi et al., 2022) have further reinforced
037   LLMs in knowledge-intensive, open-domain QA tasks. Concurrently, recent progress in visual in-
038   struction tuning (Liu et al., 2023b;a; Zhu et al., 2023) has led to the development of large Vision-
039   Language Models (VLMs) that aim to equip LLMs with visual understanding capabilities. By scal-
040   ing model parameters and training on extensive text-image datasets, VLMs such as LLaVA-1.6-
041   34B (Liu et al., 2023a), Qwen2-VL-72B (Bai et al., 2023), and InternVL2-76B (Chen et al., 2024b)
042
      have achieved state-of-the-art performance on the OpenVLM leaderboard1 . However, LLMs and
      VLMs are subject to the limitations imposed by their knowledge cutoff dates. They may provide
043
      incorrect answers when asked about events or concepts that occurred after their knowledge cutoff
044
      dates (Figure 1) To overcome this limitation for LLMs, they are often connected to web agents (Liu
045   et al., 2023d; Nakano et al., 2021; Chen et al., 2024a; Deng et al., 2024; Bai et al., 2024), which
046   enable internet access and information retrieval, allowing them to obtain the most up-to-date data
047   and improve the accuracy of their responses. Such agents are designed to interpret natural language
048   instructions, navigate complex web environments, and extract relevant textual information from
049   HTML documents, thereby enhancing the accessibility and utility of vast amounts of web-based
050   textual data for a wide range of applications.
051   However, for VLMs facing unseen images and novel concepts, their ability to learn and use up-to-
052   date multimodal knowledge from the internet remains a pressing challenge. As the existing web

087   In this work, we enable the VLM to answer a question regarding an unseen image or novel concept,
088   which behaves like a human searching the Internet. It 1) understands the query, 2) decides which
089   objects in the image it should look at and infers the correlations among the objects, 3) respectively
090   generates the texts to search, 4) analyzes the contents returned from the search engine based on
091   the query and inferred correlations, and 5) judges if the obtained visual and textual information is

      the concrete designs of such a framework, we make contributions by answering the following three
093

094
095   • What to search: shall we search the descriptions of the whole image or some critical objects?
096   • How to search: shall we search once and summarize a huge amount of returned content or search
097     progressively to obtain more related content?
098
      • By what to conclude: shall the final answer be generated with the eventually summarized web
        knowledge or all the knowledge acquired through the entire searching process?
099
100   By exploring such three aspects, we propose Vision Search Assistant, a framework based on VLM-
101   agent collaboration, which empowers an arbitrary VLM to become a multimodal automatic search
102   engine. We integrate VLMs into web agents to understand what the user wants, where to look, what
103   to search, how to learn from the returned multimodal information, and whether to conclude or search
104

106     generated textural descriptions of critical visual objects and their underlying correlations. Through
107     this step, we obtain a correlated formulation for each critical object, which is a textual represen-
        tation that considers its correlations with other objects.

151    Vision-Language Models. Pioneering models such as Flamingo (Alayrac et al., 2022), BLIP-2 (Li
152   et al., 2023), LLaVA (Liu et al., 2023b), and MiniGPT-4 (Zhu et al., 2023) have been instrumental in
153
      training vision-language models for the tasks such as image captioning and visual question answer-
      ing. Recent works focus on higher-quality datasets (Gong et al., 2023) and developing lightweight,
154
      trainable models (Gao et al., 2023) to enhance efficiency and accessibility. Further progress includes
155
      extending large language models (LLMs) to additional modalities and domains, such as audio pro-
156   cessing (Huang et al., 2023; Chen et al., 2023a), and more modalities (Han et al., 2024). Addi-
157   tionally, KOSMOS-2 (Peng et al., 2023), InternVL2 (Chen et al., 2024b), MiniGPT-2 (Chen et al.,
158   2023b), and LLaVA-1.5 (Liu et al., 2023a) incorporate region-level information by encoding visual
159   regions to embeddings of language models. However, despite scaling model parameters and train-
160   ing data, VLMs’ ability to handle unseen images remains limited, as they heavily rely on previously
161   seen text-image pairs. To overcome this, we propose to enhance VLMs’ performance on novel data
      by improving generalization without relying solely on extensive training pairs.

162
      Web Search Agents. The development of web search agents has progressed through integrating
163   advanced learning techniques, enhancing autonomy, and optimizing efficiency in web automation.
164   Early models like WebGPT (Nakano et al., 2021) and WebGLM (Liu et al., 2023d) primarily fo-
165   cused on retrieving information for question-answering tasks, while newer models, such as AutoWe-
166   bGLM (Lai et al., 2024), address deployment challenges with compact designs. Despite their strong
167   web navigation skills, larger models such as WebAgent (Gur et al., 2023) are constrained by size.
168   Incorporating reinforcement learning (Bai et al., 2024) and behavior cloning (Zheng et al., 2024; Pa-
169   tel et al., 2024) has further boosted the efficiency of web agents, as demonstrated by MindAct (Deng
170   et al., 2024), which integrates cognitive functionalities for complex task execution. While these ad-
171
      vances are leading to more scalable and versatile solutions for real-world use, current web agents
      still struggle with processing visual content directly from the web. We introduce Vision-Language
172
      Models to enable web agents to effectively interpret and interact with visual data, significantly ex-
173
      panding their capabilities in handling complex, multimodal tasks. We hope it can make web agents
174   more powerful and adaptable in real-world applications.
175
176   Retrieval-Augmented Generation. Integrating retrieval from large corpora into language mod-
177
      els has become essential for knowledge-intensive tasks like open-domain question answering. In-
      stead of relying solely on pre-trained data, the retriever-reader architecture (Chen et al., 2017; Guu
178
      et al., 2020) enables models to fetch relevant information based on an input query, which the lan-
179
      guage model then uses to generate accurate predictions. Recent research has enhanced retrievers
180   (Karpukhin et al., 2020; Xiong et al., 2020a; Qu et al., 2020; Xiong et al., 2020b; Khalifa et al.,
181   2023), improved readers (Izacard & Grave, 2020b; Cheng et al., 2021; Yu et al., 2021; Borgeaud
182   et al., 2022), jointly fine-tuned both components (Yu, 2022; Izacard et al., 2022; Singh et al., 2021;
183   Izacard & Grave, 2020a), and integrated retrieval directly within language models (Yu et al., 2023;
184   Shi et al., 2023; Trivedi et al., 2022).
185
      Therefore, we propose the Vision Search Assistant framework, which introduces an open-world
186   retrieval-augmented generation framework that extends beyond text-based retrieval to operate across
187   both vision and language modalities on the web. It enables VLMs to access real-time, dynamic in-
188   formation, improving their ability to handle novel, cross-modal queries. By pushing the boundaries
189   of retrieval beyond static knowledge sources, we address the challenge of incorporating web-based,
190   multimodal data into generative tasks, offering a more adaptable and scalable solution for RAG.

259   referred to as the Planning Agent. The web pages returned from the search engine are analyzed,

       In this paper, we seek to improve the generalization ability of VLMs of novel images and extend
479
      the capacity of web agents to solve visual content tasks. Through the synergistic collaboration
