009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            Multivariate time series anomaly detection (MTAD) approaches predominantly
012
               use performance enhancements that are not practical. E.g., a) point adjustment
               (PA) technique is employed which uses ground truth to forcefully convert false
013            negatives to true positives and unrealistically inflates precision, and b) significant
014            data leakage is incurred when anomaly score threshold is determined using the
015            test data and test labels. This paper first presents real-world performance of exist-
016            ing MTAD techniques without PA and threshold learning (TL) on test data which
               shows anomalies in real-world benchmarks result in significant distribution shift
017
               between normal and anomalous data; and with PA and TL, even untrained deter-
018            ministic methods can perform on par or even beat baseline techniques. Next it
019            introduces six synthetic benchmarks based on real-world systems, where anoma-
020            lous data and normal data have statistically almost same distributions. It then
021            presents, sparse model identification enhanced anomaly detection (SPIE-AD), a
               novel model recovery and conformance based zero-shot MTAD approach that out-
022            performs state-of-art MTAD on three real-world benchmarks without using PA
023            and TL on test data. Extensive peformance results show that SPIE-AD outper-
024            forms SOTA MTAD techniques on both standard and novel benchmarks.

026   1   I NTRODUCTION
027
028   Time series anomaly detection is essential for a safe and effective operation of unmanned aerial
029   vehicles (UAV), autonomous cars (AC), and autonomous drug delivery (ADD) systems due to a
030
      complex amalgamation of interacting perception, decision making and actuations. Such complex-
      ity makes testing for “all possible” operational scenarios practically infeasible. Test cases ignored
031   during pre-deployment evaluation but that occur during deployment, called ”unknown unknowns”
032   (U2), are a major cause of accidents (Maity et al., 2023). U2 detection is a special case of zero-shot
033   anomaly (ZSA) detection, when anomaly data is unavailable during model training. We present
034   SPIE-AD, SParse model Identification Enhanced Anomaly Detection which continually mines un-
035
      derlying sparse physical dynamics and checks its conformance with the original lab tested system.
036   U2s can potentially occur due to: a) hardware changes/failures, which may not be monitored, e.g.
037   mechanical failure in an aircraft resulting in an elevator getting stuck (F 8Stuck) or moving slow
      (F 8Slow), b) unwanted software executions: which may not immediately affect the input/output
038
      behaviour in anomalous ways, e.g. a change in the gravity parameter of a quadcoptor’s altitude con-
039   trol software (UAVSimG), and c) untested usage scenarios manifested as external inputs to the
040   system, which may not have a deviant measurement distribution parameter, e.g. an electromagnetic
041   attack on a sensor decreasing its fidelity (UAVEMA) or a phantom meal, where an user of a insulin de-
042   livery ADD announces a meal without ingesting any to trick it for a high insulin dose. As such, U2s
      may not result in an out-of-distribution (OOD) input or output, rather in an OOD inter-relationship
043
      among measured variables, necessciating a multi-variate time series analysis for the U2 detection.
044
045   Multi-variate time series anomaly detection (MTAD) is of recent research interest with a plethora of
      techniques ranging from statistical regression methods e.g ARIMA (Schmidt et al., 2018), Kalman
046   filter (Huang et al., 2023), principal component analysis based techniques (Shyu et al., 2003), meth-
047   ods that use autoencoders (Borghesi et al., 2019), long short term memory (LSTM) based deep
048   learning (DL) techniques, transformers (Tuli et al., 2022) and most recently large language models
049   (LLMs) (Alnegheimish et al., 2024). The general technique (Figure 1 Panel A) has three steps: a)
050
      training: that creates a high dimensional latent space representation of the normal operation us-
      ing data that may or may not have anolmalies but do not have anomaly labels, b) validation, that
051   uses data with anomalies but without anomaly labels to learn a anomaly score threshold such that
052   two fairly separated clusters are found in the validation set using the peaks over threshold method
053   guided by the extreme value theory (Siffer et al., 2017), and c) evaluation, where anomaly score
      of successive overlapping / non-overlapping windows of test data are computed and compared with

      Technical difficulty in ZSA detection violating A1: To the best of our knowledge, there is only one
090   solution for zero-shot MTAD (Audibert et al., 2020). However, as identified by (Kim et al., 2022),
091   it has poor realistic performance. Solutions for univariate zero-shot anomaly detection including
092   techniques with LLMs (Alnegheimish et al., 2024) are available which as admitted by the authors are
093   very difficult to adapt to MTAD. The technical challenge is to detect anomalies with no knowledge
      about anomalous data distribution, which preempts any discriminative feature learning methods.
094
095   b) A2: Unrealistic evaluation method- According to (Kim et al., 2022; Wu & Keogh, 2023),
096   the reported results in nearly all state-of-the-art MTAD techniques have point adjustment (PA) (Su
      et al., 2019). This technique assumes that anomalies occur in contiguous segments, and if the MTAD
097
      method detects one point in this segment as anomalous, then every point in that segment should be
098   considered as anomalous even if the MTAD method marks them as normal. The PA method inflates
099   the precision by a significant amount Wu & Keogh (2023) in nearly all MTAD methods as seen
100   in Figure 2), which shows the implementation of two most recent MTAD technique on benchmark
101
      datasets (SMAP, SMD, MSL discussed in more detail in Evaluation section) with code available
      from (Liu et al., 2024). These results are also supported by (Kim et al., 2022), which proposed an
102   alternate evaluation criteria P A%K, where PA is only employed if the original technique identifies
103   K% of time points in an anomaly segment as anomaly. K = 0 indicates application of PA in its
104   original form, while K = 100 indicates no PA.
105   Technical difficulty in ZSA detection violating A2: As highlighted in (Kim et al., 2022), in many
106   real-world datasets, anomaly injection and manual labelling may result in several anomaly data-
107   points to have similar distribution as normal data. So, if a MTAD method focuses only on latent
      features of data, its at inherent disadvantage in detecting anomalies.

174   Problem Definition: We consider n sensors each with time series X i for sensor i forming a vector
175
      X(t) over time where t ∈ 0 . . . N/µ, where µ is the sampling frequency. The dataset consists of
      three sets: a) training set Xtrain, where no anomaly labels are available, b) Xtest, where there
176   is a mix of anomalous and normal data and a corresponding label set y(t), where y(t) = 1 if the
177   time point t in the test data is anomalous or y(t) = 0 if normal. The zero-shot anomaly detection
178   problem is to use Xtrain to learn a machine that can provide ỹ(t) which is an accurately estimate
179   of y(t) for the test set Xtest without using any part of the test set Xtest during model training.
180   Method: The main hypothesis of SPIE-AD is that input / output time-series data from autonomous
181   systems must satisfy physical/chemical/mechanical/physiological properties of the real world sys-
182   tem. Such properties are typically expressed using sparse non-linear dynamical systems:

282   2.5      C OMPUTATIONAL COMPLEXITY
283
284   There are two model recovery cores of SPIE-AD: SINDY-MPC and LTC-NN. SINDY-MPC uses the
285   sequential threshold ridge regression (STRidge) (Kaiser et al., 2018) strategy. The computational
286   complexity of Ridge regression in the worst case is O(N n2 ), where N is the number of samples and
      n is the dimension of the multivariate signal if the number of regularization parameters is less than
287
      N (Wang & Pilanci, 2023), which is the case in the example of anomaly detection. The sequential
288   threshold runs Ridge regression multiple times until a desired reconstruction accuracy is obtained.
289   If we fix a maximum Q number of times that the sequential threshold can run then the overall
290   computational complexity of SINDY-MPC is O(QN n2 ).
291   For the LTC-NN architecture, the computation complexity of forward pass is O(V + V (|Θ| + q)) +
292   O(|X|N ), where N is the number of samples in the data, V , q, Θ, X are as in Figure 4. Complexity
293   of backward pass is O(V PLT C N + V (|Θ| + q)Pdense N ), where PLT C is the number of parameters
294   in the LTC cell, and Pdense is the number of parameters in each neuron of the dense layer. SINDY-
295   MPC on a single CPU thread was 11.3 (± 2.1) times faster than the neural architecture on GPU. The
296   overall computational complexity is O((N/W )QN n2 ) for SPIE-ADS and O((N/W )V PLT C N +
297
      V (|Θ| + q)Pdense N ) , where W is the window size of CRIE.

      AnomalySimpleton: We propose an untrained deterministic thresholding algorithm that exploits
356
      PA and test data distribution i.e. data leakage to provide anomaly detection performance on par with
357   state-of-the-art learning techniques. In this method, a specific window W of data is selected from the
358   train data. Statistical properties of the train data window W such as mean ψtrain , standard deviation
359   σtrain , and skewness κtrain is computed. For each test data window of length W , the same statistics
360
      are computed. If the deviation of the test statistics is more than P% of the train statistics, then the
      test data window is classified as anomalous else it is not anomalous. The window W and the test
361   statistics P is used to obtain two maximally separated clusters in the test data. This is done through
362   brute force search over several W and P options. For each benchmark real world data this window
363   and threshold seach is performed from scratch.

      In this paper, we introduced SPIE-AD a methodology for identi-
514   fying ’unknown-unknown’ (U2) errors in AI-enabled autonomous
515   systems. U2 can arise due to unpredictable human interactions and
516   complex real-world usage scenarios, potentially leading to critical safety incidents through unsafe
517   shifts in the distribution of the inter-relationships among the variables in operational data. SPIE-AD
518
      performs zero shot anomaly detection and hence does not require signature of the U2 scenario or
      detection. Validation across diverse contexts such as zero-day vulnerabilities in unmanned aerial
519   vehicles, hardware failures in autonomous insulin delivery systems, and design deficiencies in air-
520   craft pitch control systems such as Maneuvering Characteristics Augmentation Systems (MCAS),
521   demonstrates our framework’s efficacy in preempting unsafe data distribution shifts due to unknown-
522   unknowns. This methodology not only advances unknown-unknown error detection in AAS but also
      sets a new benchmark for integrating physics-guided models and machine learning to ensure sys-
523
      tem safety. Mining the underlying model of a dynamical system has several applications including
524   detection of stealth cheating scenarios in AI systems much like the Volkswagon emission cheating
525   case, or also biometric liveness detection.
526
      We have not only shown efficacy of SPIE-AD on U2 datasets but also demonstrated its generality
527   in detecting any anomalous scenarios through the usage of standard real world datasets. We will
528   make our dataset public through the MTAD tools and techniques github page (Liu et al., 2024) for
529   the general research community to develop novel ZSA detection schemes.
530   Limitations: SPIE-AD faces challenges in determining point anomalies that last very few samples.
531   In the SMD SMAP and MSL datasets, anomalies that last < 5 samples are missed consistently.
532   Moreover, as seen in Figure 5 SPIE-ADS performance is sensitive to the window size chosen for the
533
      CRIE algorithm. Hence, an important future work is to formally evaluate the sensitivity of SPIE-AD
      to window length.
534
535   Ethical Considerations: One of the components of SPIE-AD is recovering underlying model. One
536
      of the applications of SPIE-AD is digital twins. An unethical usage is impersonation. Thus, careful
      ethical evaluation is required when integrating such systems in medical practice. Another issue is
537   that SPIE-AD is only a ZSA detection mechanism. In its current form it cannot be used to explain
538   the reasons behind the U2 occurrence. Such black box models can become problematic if false
539   positives lead to usage of critical intervention. Hence proper safeguards should be placed to vet the
      U2 decisions from SPIE-AD.
