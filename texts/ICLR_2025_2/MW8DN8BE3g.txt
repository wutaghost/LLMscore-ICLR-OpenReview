009                                                A BSTRACT
010
011            High-definition (HD) map construction methods play a vital role in providing pre-
012            cise and comprehensive static environmental information essential for autonomous
013            driving systems. The primary sensors used are cameras and LiDAR, with input
014            configurations varying among camera-only, LiDAR-only, or camera-LiDAR fusion
015            based on cost-performance considerations, while fusion-based methods typically
016            perform the best. However, current methods face two major issues: high costs
017            due to separate training and deployment for each input configuration, and low
018            robustness when sensors are missing or corrupted. To address these challenges, we
019
               propose the Unified Robust HD Map Construction Network (Uni-Map), a single
               model designed to perform well across all input configurations. Our approach
020
               designs a novel Mixture Stack Modality (MSM) training scheme, allowing the
021
               map decoder to learn effectively from camera, LiDAR, and fused features. We
022            also introduce a projector module to align Bird’s Eye View features from different
023            modalities into a shared space, enhancing representation learning and overall model
024            performance. During inference, our model utilizes a switching modality strategy
025            to adapt seamlessly to any input configuration, ensuring compatibility across vari-
026            ous modalities. To evaluate the robustness of HD map construction methods, we
027            designed 13 different sensor corruption scenarios and conducted extensive exper-
028            iments comparing Uni-Map with state-of-the-art methods. Experimental results
029            show that Uni-Map outperforms previous methods by a significant margin across
030
               both normal and corrupted modalities, demonstrating superior performance and ro-
               bustness. Notably, our unified model surpasses independently trained camera-only,
031
               LiDAR-only, and camera-LiDAR MapTR models with a gain of 4.6, 5.6, and 5.6
032
               mAP on the nuScenes dataset, respectively. The source code will be released.

079   These combinations are summarized into 6 cases and illustrated in Fig. 2 (left). We compare Uni-Map

      mAP (61.2 vs. 22.5). Experimental results show that Uni-Map exhibits stronger robustness on various

085
      scheme, projector module, and the switching modality strategy are simple yet effective plug-and-play

      techniques compatible with existing pipelines.

128   (BEV) representation as an ideal feature space for multi-view perception due to its remarkable ability
129   to mitigate scale-ambiguity and occlusion challenges. Various techniques have been proposed and
130   utilized to project perspective view (PV) features into the BEV space by leveraging geometric priors,
131   such as LSS Philion & Fidler (2020), Deformable Attention Li et al. (2022b) and GKT Chen et al.
132   (2022). However, camera-only methods suffer from a lack of explicit depth information. LiDAR-only
133   methods Wang et al. (2023); Li et al. (2022a); Liu et al. (2023a); Liao et al. (2023b;a) benefit from the
134   accurate 3D geometric information from the LiDAR input. However, they struggle to deal with data
135
      sparsity and sensing noise problems robustly. Recently, camera-LiDAR feature fusion in the unified
      BEV space has attracted much attention Liao et al. (2023a;b); Zhou et al. (2024); Dong et al. (2024).
136
      BEV-level fusion uses two independent streams that encode the raw inputs from the camera and
137
      LiDAR sensors into features within the same BEV space. This fusion at the BEV level incorporates
138   complementary modality features, surpassing unimodal input approaches in performance.
139
140   While significant progress has been made using various methods with different input configurations
141
      (camera-only, LiDAR-only, camera-LiDAR fusion) chosen based on cost-performance considerations,
      a common challenge persists. Current methods necessitate training and deploying separate models for
142
      each input configuration, leading to considerable costs in development, maintenance, and deployment.
143
      In this paper, we introduce a novel Unified Robust HD map construction approach to address this
144   issue. This method enables training a single model capable of operating on any input configuration,
145   thereby streamlining the process.

      Robustness Under Sensor Failures. Sensor failures can significantly impact the accuracy of HD map
      tasks, thereby jeopardizing the safety of autonomous driving. While Camera-LiDAR fusion methods
148
      have shown promising performance, which can make use of both the semantic-rich information from
149
      cameras and the explicit geometric information from LiDAR, existing fusion methods often assume
150   access to complete sensor information from both cameras and LiDAR, leading to low robustness in the
151   face of sensor missing or corruptions. This means that their performance may degrade significantly or
152   even fail entirely when sensor data is incomplete or corrupted. Recently, there have been a few studies
153   that focus on benchmarking and improving the robustness under natural corruptions, particularly in
154   various BEV perception algorithms such as 3D object detection Li et al. (2022b); Liu et al. (2023b);
155   Ge et al. (2023), BEV segmentation Zhang et al. (2022); Zhou & Krähenbühl (2022), occupancy
156   prediction Wei et al. (2023b); Huang et al. (2023), and depth estimation Wei et al. (2023a). However,
157   approaches addressing sensor failures for HD map construction are still under exploration.
158   In this paper, to explore the camera-LiDAR fusion model robustness on the HD map construction
159   task, we design 13 types of camera-LiDAR corruption combinations that perturb both camera and
160   LiDAR inputs separately or concurrently. Our proposed Uni-Map model demonstrates improved
161   robustness under various sensor failure scenarios. To the best of our knowledge, Uni-Map is the first
      study to explore the robustness of HD map construction task under multi-sensor corruptions.

178   3       M ETHODOLOGY
179
180
      Uni-Map pursues a novel Unified Robust HD Map construction approach, which can train an all-
181   in-one model capable of operating with various input configurations. For this purpose, we feed the
182   model decoder with the features from all input configurations at the training stage and process one
183   specific feature based on the deployed input configuration during inference. The overview framework
184   of Uni-Map is shown in Fig. 3. Given different sensory inputs, we first apply modality-specific
185   encoders to extract their features. These multi-modal features are then transformed into a unified
186   BEV representation that preserves both geometric and semantic information. Then, we incorporate
187   a projector module to align BEV features from different modalities into a shared space, thereby
188   enhancing representation learning. Additionally, we introduce a novel Mixture Stack Modality
189
      training scheme, enabling the map decoder module to glean rich knowledge from the camera, LiDAR,
      or fused features. Specifically, the mixture stack BEV features are fed into the decoder and prediction
190
      heads for the HD Map construction task. During inference, we employ a switching modality strategy,
191
      enabling Uni-Map to make precise predictions using arbitrary modality inputs.

264   different modalities that share the same map decoder and ground truth labels, the projector module is

267   increases the diversity of the BEV feature space accessible to the decoder module, thereby improving
268   the model’s generalization ability and robustness across different input configurations. Third, this
269   scheme allows the map decoder module to process BEV features of different modalities. As a result,
      Uni-Map can flexibly handle various input configurations during inference.

      4.1       E XPERIMENTAL S ETTINGS
305
306   Datasets. We evaluate our method on the widely-used challenging nuScenes Caesar et al. (2020)
307   dataset following the standard setting of previous methods Liao et al. (2023a); Zhou et al. (2024).
308   The nuScenes dataset contains 1,000 sequences of recordings collected by autonomous driving cars.
309   Each sample is annotated at 2Hz and contains 6 camera images covering 360◦ horizontal FOV of the
310   ego-vehicle. Following Liao et al. (2023a); Li et al. (2022a); Gao et al. (2024), three kinds of map
311   elements are chosen for fair evaluation – pedestrian crossing, lane divider, and road boundary.
312   Evaluation Metrics. We adopt the evaluation metrics consistent with previous works Liao et al.
313   (2023a); Li et al. (2022a); Zhang et al. (2024c), where average precision (AP) is used to evaluate
314   the map construction quality and Chamfer distance DChamf er determines the matching between
315   predictions and ground truth. We calculate the APτ under several DChamf er thresholds (τ ∈ T =
316   {0.5m, 1.0m, 1.5m}), and then average across all thresholds as the final mean AP (mAP) metric.
317   The perception ranges are [−15.0m, 15.0m]/[−30.0m, 30.0m] for X/Y-axes.
318   Implementation Details. Uni-Map is trained with 4 NVIDIA RTX A6000 GPUs. During the training
319   phase, the GT labels are duplicated twice and stacked to form 3B batch dimension, matching with the
320   stacked feature map from Eq. 4. The design choice of the MSM scheme is discussed in the ablation
321   studies. For the projector module, we use a two-layer perceptron whose dimension is C->C/2->C.
322   We adopt the AdamW optimizer Loshchilov & Hutter (2019) for all our experiments. We set the
323   mini-batch size to 16, and use a step-decayed learning rate with an initial value of 4e−3 . The inference
      time is measured on a single NVIDIA RTX A6000 GPU with batch size 1.

462   4.4       ROBUSTNESS OF MULTI - SENSOR CORRUPTIONS
463
      To explore the camera-LiDAR fusion model robustness, we design 13 types of camera-LiDAR
464
      corruption combinations that perturb both camera and LiDAR inputs separately or concurrently.
465   Camera-LiDAR corruption combinations are grouped into camera-only corruptions, LiDAR-only
466   corruptions, and their combinations, covering the majority of real-world corruption cases. The
467   definition of multi-sensor corruption is detailed in A.2. Fig. 5 shows the results of three Camera-
468   LiDAR corruption combinations. We have the following observations. (1) In the sensor missing , Uni-
469   Map can prevent the model from collapsing owing to the switching modality strategy. Quantitatively,
470   when facing a missing LiDAR sensor, Uni-Map still achieves 54.9 mAP, which outperforms the
471   original MapTR Liao et al. (2023a) by 34.5 mAP. (2) In case of the corruption of the camera and
472   LiDAR sensor individually or simultaneously, Uni-Map shows stronger robustness. For example,
473
      in the face of camera frame lost and LiDAR crosstalk, compared to the MapTR fused model, the
      Uni-Map model achieved significant improvements in 8.5 mAP (29.3 vs. 20.8). These results
474
      demonstrate that the MSM training scheme enhances the generalization ability of the map decoder.
475
      By stacking BEV features from different modalities into the same map decoder, the diversity of the
476   BEV feature space accessible to the decoder increases, thereby improving the model’s robustness.
477   All in all, Uni-Map shows stronger robustness on our designed 13 types of camera-LiDAR corruption
478   combinations.

519   5    C ONCLUSION
520
521   In this paper, we propose a novel Unified Robust HD Map Construction Network (Uni-Map), which
522   can train an all-in-one model to operate on arbitrary input configurations. The core components of Uni-
523   Map, i.e. MSM training scheme, projector module, and the switching modality strategy, are simple
524   yet effective plug-and-play techniques compatible with existing pipelines. Extensive experiments
525   demonstrate that Uni-Map can achieve high performance in different input configurations while
526   reducing the training and deployment costs of the model. Moreover, Uni-Map shows stronger
527   robustness on our designed 13 types of camera-LiDAR corruption combinations. We hope that our
528
      method can be applied to more autonomous driving perception tasks.
529   Ethics Statement. Our work can boost the performance and robustness of HD map construction
530   task. Although our method significantly improves the robustness of the HD map model, the overall
531   robustness is still low. Special caution is needed in deploying our methods onto vehicles on the
532   road to ensure safety. Therefore, future research is necessary to further investigate more advanced
533   robustness methods.
534   Reproducibility. To ensure the reproducibility of our work, we have included a comprehensive
535   Reproducibility Statement. Specifically, for the novel model and algorithms presented in this work,
536   we will make them open source upon paper acceptance. Additionally, all multi-sensor corruption
537   details and more experimental results can be found in Appendix A. For the datasets used in our
538   experiments, we follow the standard protocol of the open source work MapTR Liao et al. (2023a) .
539   This Reproducibility Statement is intended to guide readers to the relevant resources that will aid in
      replicating our work, ensuring transparency and clarity throughout.

776   A.2   M ULTI - SENSOR C ORRUPTIONS
777
778   To explore the camera-LiDAR fusion model robustness, we design 13 types of camera-LiDAR
779   corruption combinations that perturb both camera and LiDAR inputs separately or concurrently.
780
      Camera-LiDAR corruption combinations are grouped into camera-only corruptions, LiDAR-only
      corruptions, and their combinations, covering the majority of real-world corruption cases. Specifically,
781
      we design 3 types of camera-only corruptions by utilizing the clean LiDAR point data and three
782
      camera failure cases such as Unavailable Camera (all pixel values are set to zero for all RGB images),
783   Camera Crash, and Frame Lost. Moreover, we design 4 types for LiDAR-only corruptions by utilizing
784   the clean camera data and the corrupted LiDAR data as the input. The LiDAR corruption types
785   include complete LiDAR failure which means LiDAR data are unavailable (Since no model can
786   work when all points are absent, we approximate this scenario by only retaining a single point
787   as input), LiDAR Incomplete Echo, LiDAR Crosstalk, and LiDAR Cross-Sensor. Note that our
788   implementation of complete LiDAR failure is close to the real-world situation. Lastly, we design 6
789   types of camera-LiDAR corruption combinations that perturb both sensor inputs concurrently, using
790   the previously mentioned image/LiDAR sensor failure types. We establish several corruption severity
791
      levels (i.e., three levels including easy, moderate, and hard) for each type of corruption. Furthermore,
      for a comprehensive evaluation, we report metrics for each corruption type by averaging over three
792
      severity levels. Description and severity level setups in 2 types of camera corruption and 3 types of
793
      LiDAR corruption are shown in Tab. 5. Visualization results of camera/LiDAR sensor corruptions
794   are shown in Fig. 8.
