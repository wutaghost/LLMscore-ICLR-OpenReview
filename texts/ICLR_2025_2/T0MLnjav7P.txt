009                                                A BSTRACT
010
011            This paper addresses the phase retrieval problem, which aims to recover a signal
012            vector x\ from m measurements yi = |hai , x\ i|2 , i = 1, . . . , m. A standard ap-
013            proach is to solve a nonconvex least squares problem using gradient descent with
014            random initialization, which is known to work efficiently given a sufficient num-
015            ber of measurements. However, whether O(n) measurements suffice for gradient
016            descent to recover the ground truth efficiently has remained an open question.
017
               Prior work has established that O(n poly(log n)) measurements are sufficient. In
               this paper, we resolve this open problem by proving that m = O(n) Gaussian
018
               random measurements are sufficient to guarantee, with high probability, that the
019
               objective function has a benign global landscape. This sample complexity is op-
020            timal because at least ⌦(n) measurements are required for exact recovery. The
021            landscape result allows us to further show that gradient descent with a constant
022            step size converges to the ground truth from almost any initial point.

      This problem has attracted high interest due to its broad applications in X-ray crystallography (Elser

034
035   The phase retrieval problem is NP-hard if only very few measurements, e.g. m = n + 1 (Fickus
036   et al., 2014), are given. However, a wide range of algorithms can recover x\ up to a global phase
037
      shift provided enough measurements. Early methods with provable performance guarantees usually
      formulate it into a convex constrained optimization problem, such as a semidefinite programming
038
      problem (Candes et al., 2013; 2015a; Waldspurger et al., 2015) or basis pursuit problem (Goldstein
039
      & Studer, 2018). These methods are usually computationally challenging in high-dimensional cases.
040   To address this issue, more recent works take nonconvex approaches, such as alternating minimiza-
041   tion (Wen et al., 2012; Netrapalli et al., 2013; Waldspurger, 2018; Zhang, 2020); gradient descent
042   type algorithms, including Wirtinger flow (Candes et al., 2015b; Chen & Candes, 2015; Ma et al.,
043   2020), truncated amplitude flow (Wang et al., 2017), vanilla gradient descent (Chen et al., 2019),
044   Riemannian gradient descent (Cai & Wei, 2024); and Newton type algorithms (Gao & Xu, 2017;
045   Ma et al., 2018).
046   Convex methods mentioned above can achieve optimal sample complexity m = O(n), but require
047   an initialization close enough to the ground truth x\ . For nonconvex algorithms, O(n) sample com-
048   plexity can be achieved with a careful initialization (Chen & Candes, 2015; Wang et al., 2017; Wald-
049   spurger, 2018; Cai & Wei, 2024). However, such initialization can be computationally inefficient
050   when the dimension n is large. In practice, random initialization is more plausible. In the random
051   initialization regime, it is known that O(n log13 n) samples are sufficient to guarantee a nearly linear
052   convergence rate for vanilla gradient descent (Chen et al., 2019). To obtain a lower sample complex-
053   ity in this regime, a benign global landscape result was analyzed for both the intensity measurement
      (1) (Sun et al., 2018b; Cai et al., 2023) and the amplitude measurement yi = |hai , x\ i| (Cai et al.,


                                                           1
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


054
      2022b) for `2 -loss function. Both the intensity and the amplitude model have no spurious local min-
055   ima and saddle points are strict, provided O(n log n) and O(n) samples respectively. Nevertheless,
056   no global convergence guarantee of gradient descent algorithm can be easily deduced because the
057   Lipschitz gradient assumption in the classical convergence results (Lee et al., 2016) does not hold,
058   and the bounded iterates assumptions are hard to verify.
059
      In this paper, we aim at showing the global convergence of vanilla gradient descent algorithm with
060
      arbitrary initialization for general phase retrieval problem with O(n) intensity measurements. We
061   propose a new tensor based criterion to show that O(n) Gaussian samples of intensity measurements
062   (1) are sufficient to guarantee a benign global landscape for phase retrieval problem. Furthermore,
063   we show that such objective function has bounded gradient trajectories, which give us the certificate
064   to apply the general global convergence result in (Josz, 2023). By combining the global benign
065   landscape result and global convergence result, we conclude that given O(n) Gaussian samples,
066   with high probability, vanilla gradient descent initialized almost everywhere converge to the ground
067   truth of phase retrieval problem.

069   1.1   C ONTRIBUTIONS
070
071   The main contributions of this paper can be summarized as follows.
072
073
            1. We propose a new simple deterministic criterion and use it to verify the global benign
               landscape of intensity-based phase retrieval problem. In particular, with this simple crite-
074
               rion, we prove at once that the objective function admits restricted strong convexity near
075
               the ground truth, has Hessian with a negative eigenvalue near the origin, and has nonzero
076            gradient elsewhere.
077
078

               samples are sufficient to satisfy our proposed criterion. This new technique circumvents the
079

080
               reduce the logarithmic factor of the sample complexity.
081
082         3. We establish the boundedness of any gradient trajectories for intensity-based phase retrieval

               problem by revealing that its gradient trajectory only moves inside a linear subspace. This
               useful fact allows us to invoke a general global convergence result of gradient descent.

465   5   C ONCLUSION AND DISCUSSIONS
466
467   In this paper, we provided a tensor based criterion that guarantees global convergence of vanilla gra-
468   dient descent with random initialization for phase retrieval problem. We first showed that the objec-
469   tive function has a benign global landscape. Then we proved that given the number of measurements
470   m Kn, the criterion is satisfied with high probability. We also showed that the objective function
471   has bounded gradient trajectories, which allows us to utilize the proposed landscape results and a
472   general convergence result in literature to conclude our main convergence result.
473   Finally, we discuss the limitation of our paper, which also leads to a potential future direction. From
474   our proof, only a local linear convergence rate can be obtained once the iterates enter the region R30 ,
475   but there is no information on how long the gradient descent algorithm will take to enter R30 starting
476   from almost every point in R10 or R20 . A more detailed analysis is needed for how long the iterates
477   will stay in R10 and R20 . For R10 , the analysis is relatively simple. Since the gradient norm is
478   lower bounded, the objective value will drop by a constant for every iteration. The key challenge is
479   to ensure the iterates will not revisit R20 once leaving it. With the global landscape result provided
480   in this paper, we expect to obtain a nearly linear iteration complexity result with only O(n) samples
481   in a following paper.
