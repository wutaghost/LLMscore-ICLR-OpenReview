009                                              A BSTRACT
010
011            The reduction of the computational and memory overhead associated with fine-
012            tuning large language models remains a significant challenge for current research
013            in natural language processing. Achieving an optimal balance between task per-
014            formance, adaptability, and low VRAM requirement often presents a complex
015            trade-off. Parameter-efficient fine-tuning (PEFT) methods, such as LoRA, have
016            gained attention for their ability to reduce the number of trainable parameters
017            while preserving task performance. However, they have not yet achieved a no-
018
               table reduction in VRAM usage, which is still predominantly consumed by model
               weights and activations during backpropagation. In contrast, Ladder Side-Tuning
019
               (LST) has been proposed as an alternative that effectively reduces VRAM usage
020
               by freezing the backbone language model (BLM) and training only lightweight
021            side networks. Nevertheless, this reduction in memory usage often results in a
022            decline in performance, as LST typically exhibits inferior performance compared
023            to PEFT methods on the same BLM. To address these limitations, we propose
024            Symbiotic Tuning (SymTune), a novel approach that extracts intermediate out-
025            puts from the BLM and integrates symbiotic modules to enhance feature process-
026            ing capabilities. This method avoids a direct trade-off between performance and
027            VRAM efficiency, offering two key advantages: 1) robust performance across a
028            wide range of natural language tasks, and 2) reduced VRAM consumption through
029
               an improved side-tuning architecture. The experimental results demonstrate that
               SymTune provides a scalable and memory-efficient solution for fine-tuning lan-
030
               guage models.

      1   I NTRODUCTION
034
035
      The VRAM bottleneck of traditional PEFT: During model training, VRAM consumption is primar-
036   ily dominated by three components: model weights, gradients, and activations. Parameter-Efficient
037   Fine-Tuning (PEFT) methods, such as LoRA, have proven effective in transfer learning for down-
038   stream tasks by significantly reducing the number of trainable parameters, directly decreasing the
039   memory required for storing gradients. However, this reduction only addresses the gradient-related
040   VRAM usage. The memory demands for the other two components—model weights and activa-
041   tions—remain high and can still accumulate to hundreds of gigabytes. Consequently, while reducing
042   the trainable parameter count alleviates some of the memory burden, it is insufficient to fully mit-
043   igate the VRAM bottleneck in large-scale models. To address this challenge, more comprehensive
044
      solutions targeting activations and model weights are necessary.
045   Ladder Side-Tuning (LST) (Sung et al., 2022) offers a promising solution to address the memory
046   bottleneck in fine-tuning large models. By drastically reducing the number of trainable parameters,
047   LST frees up a significant portion of the VRAM typically consumed by activations during training.
048   Additionally, LST can further optimize memory efficiency through quantization techniques (Zhang
049   et al., 2024), which compress the model weights by representing them with lower-precision data
050
      types. This approach makes side-tuning particularly useful for fine-tuning in environments where
      VRAM is a limiting factor.
051
052   The Performance Limitation of Side-tuning: Side-tuning methods freeze the backbone language
053   model (BLM) and apply gradients only to the side network. While this approach is fast and memory-
      efficient, it does not perform as well as LoRA when using the same BLM. The limitation arises be-

077   To address the aforementioned issues, we propose Symbiotic tuning (SymTune), which selectively

080   Cross-Attention (ICA) and Attention Sharing (ATS). ICA involves designing a specialized cross-
081   attention mechanism that constructs task-specific representations while preserving the rich contex-
082   tual information from the backbone model. ATS aims to share additional internal signals from the
083   backbone language model (BLM), enabling the symbiotic module to better learn both the language
084
      and the downstream task. Experimental results demonstrate that a language model, when paired
      with a single symbiotic module containing only a few million parameters, achieves competitive val-
085
      idation scores across a range of natural language understanding benchmarks and multi-label tasks.
086
      Moreover, it retains the low VRAM requirements characteristic of side-tuning methods, offering a
087   resource-efficient solution without compromising task-specific performance.

091
092   Our method is inspired by contemporary Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning (PEFT) and side-tuning
093   techniques, such as LoRA (Hu et al., 2022), which have gained widespread adoption in the research
094   community and demonstrated strong results.
095
      This study focuses on two main aspects: 1) improving task performance, and 2) minimizing the
096
      computational costs of training. To this end, we conducted a comprehensive review of PEFT meth-
097
      ods, systematically comparing their respective strengths and limitations to inform the development
098   of our approach.

      LoRA, shown in Eq. 1, introduces additional parameters to learn the adjustments of parameters in
109   the model which are typically Wq , Wv , and Wo in practical implementations. These modifications
110   influence every critical step in the attention layer processing procedure while requiring a much
111   smaller number of parameters to be adjusted. Consequently, LoRA outperforms full fine-tuning on

113   datasets for these benchmarks typically contain limited amounts of data. Under such constraints, it
114   is easier to tune the model when fewer parameters need to be modified.
115   The success of these PEFT methods and some other inspiring research (Song et al., 2023; Fedus
116   et al., 2022; Chen et al., 2023) offers valuable insights: fine-tuning a language model often involves
117   reconstructing internal representations and adjusting projection matrices of the model, typically un-
118   der constraints related to data availability. Reducing the number of trainable parameters has proven
119   to be an effective strategy for facilitating optimal convergence, as it simplifies the optimization pro-
120   cess and reduces the risk of overfitting when working with limited data.
121   However, LoRA is unable to reduce the VRAM usage associated with storing activations, which
122   must be kept in VRAM for use during gradient back-propagation to compute partial derivatives. In
123   LoRA, the activation should still be kept to compute the gradient for every ∆W . Consequently,
124   the VRAM required for these activations, which can be substantial in large-scale models, remains
125   unchanged. Therefore, while LoRA is highly effective in reducing the memory required for storing
126   gradients and optimizer states, it does not alleviate the overall VRAM burden significantly.

129   2.2   S IDE -T UNING : PEFT A PPROACH WITH S INGLE -D IRECTIONAL I NFORMATION F LOW
130
131
      Ladder Side-Tuning (LST) (Sung et al., 2022) constructs a new side model based on the hidden states
132   of the BLM. Unlike LoRA, the information flow in LST is unidirectional which flows only from the
133   BLM to the side network. This approach does not alter the internal representations within the BLM,
134   demonstrating that it’s still possible to achieve good performance by simply building comprehensive
135   language modeling capability on top of the existing hidden states.
136
      Quantized Side-Tuning (QST) is an alternative method that employs a quantization mechanism to
137
      reduce the VRAM consumption associated with the BLM’s parameters. This approach achieves
138   a reduction in memory usage without substantially compromising performance when compared to
139   LST while offering even greater VRAM savings.
140
141

      into the side layer.

150   In side-tuning methods, the BLM’s weights are frozen, meaning no gradients are computed for

155   tions is significantly reduced.
156   However, side-tuning often involves a trade-off between efficiency and performance. Unlike LoRA,
157   which directly modifies the attention score computation, side-tuning methods rely heavily on the
158   BLM. While initializing the side network with pruned weights from the BLM can enhance training
159   efficiency, its performance on benchmarks like GLUE tends to be inferior to LoRA’s when using
160   the same BLM. This is primarily due to its reliance on the BLM, which only provides hidden states
161   without sharing additional internal signals. As a result, LST must establish additional connections
      to the BLM for reconstructing internal representations and achieve better model performance.

      3.1.3    W HY DO WE NEED ATS?
251
252   As previously stated, side-tuning methods such as LST and QST adapt the outputs of a BLM without
253   sharing the internal signals of the Transformer modules. Although side-tuning reduces memory con-
254   sumption during training by leveraging side networks in the back-propagation process, the absence
255   of internal connections between the side networks and the BLM can lead to suboptimal task per-
256   formance. In contrast, our approach, Symbiotic Tuning, integrates attention weights from the BLM
257   and introduces a novel attention mechanism, offering a unified attention flow between the BLM and
258
      side networks for enhancing tuning performance.

283   3.3       F ORWARD AND BACKWARD PASS : S IDE - TUNING AND BACK P ROPAGATION
284
285
      Unlike LST (Sung et al., 2022) and QST (Zhang et al., 2024), our symbiotic module is not a smaller
      version of the BLM. Instead, the first self-attention layer is replaced with an inverse cross-attention.
286
      During the forward pass, the BLM operates in evaluation mode, meaning no dropout, batch normal-
287
      ization, or activation storage is applied, and no gradients are computed for the BLM.
288
289   As shown in Figure 1, during the backward pass, gradients are propagated solely through the sym-
290   biotic module, while the BLM remains unaffected by gradient updates. This inference-only mode
291
      for the backbone significantly reduces the VRAM usage of our method, as it eliminates the need to
      store activations and reduces memory requirements during model training.

476   4.4   A BLATION S TUDY
477
478   We introduced three novel operations in our model: Inverse Cross Attention (ICA) and Attention
479   Sharing (ATS). Abandoning the ICA reverts the model to the LST approach, where the hidden states
480   of the BLM are directly added to the output of the symbiotic module’s final layer.
481   The ablation study results, which are shown in Table 6, indicate that both Cross Attention and At-
482   tention Sharing provide substantial improvements in the language model’s performance across a
483   wide range of benchmarks. The experimental results indicate that just using ICA does not signif-
484   icantly impact the outcomes. However, combined with ATS, it leads to noticeable improvements
485   in the GLUE benchmark. Additionally, the findings demonstrate that sharing attention scores is a
      reasonable and compatible approach for enhancing natural language understanding capabilities.

      5     C ONCLUSION
499
500   In this work, we introduce Symbiotic Tuning (SymTune), a novel approach that integrates additional
501
      symbiotic modules into a language model, providing a robust solution for multitask learning while
      significantly reducing the overall parameter count. Through extensive experimentation, SymTune
502
      has demonstrated a notable reduction in computational costs and GPU memory consumption com-
503
      pared to traditional full fine-tuning and existing PEFT methods. The architecture of SymTune, along
504   with its plug-and-play adaptability, enhances its efficiency and flexibility across various natural lan-
505   guage understanding tasks. These results underscore SymTune’s potential as a scalable, parameter-
506   efficient framework for multitask learning, offering significant promise for future advancements in
507   natural language understanding.
