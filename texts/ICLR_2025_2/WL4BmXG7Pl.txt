009                                                A BSTRACT
010
011            Training strategies for modern deep neural networks (NNs) tend to induce a heavy-
012            tailed (HT) empirical spectral density (ESD) in the layer weights. While previous
013            efforts have shown that the HT phenomenon correlates with good generalization
014            in large NNs, a theoretical explanation of its occurrence is still lacking. Especially,
015            understanding the conditions which lead to this phenomenon can shed light on the
016            interplay between generalization and weight spectra. Our work aims to bridge this
017            gap by presenting a simple, rich setting to model the emergence of HT ESD. In
018            particular, we present a theory-informed analysis for вҖҳcraftingвҖҷ heavy tails in the
019
               ESD of two-layer NNs without any gradient noise. This is the first work to ana-
               lyze a noise-free setting and incorporate optimizer (GD/Adam) dependent (large)
020
               learning rates into the HT ESD analysis. Our results highlight the role of learning
021
               rates on the Bulk+Spike and HT shape of the ESDs in the early phase of training,
022            which can facilitate generalization in the two-layer NN. These observations shed
023            light on the behavior of large-scale NNs, albeit in a much simpler setting. Last
024            but not least, we present a novel perspective on the ESD evolution dynamics by
025            analyzing the singular vectors of weight matrices and optimizer updates.

028   1   I NTRODUCTION
029
030
      By employing techniques from random matrix theory, Martin & Mahoney (2021a) observed an im-
031   plicit self-regularization of the training process, due to which deep NN weights exhibit HT ESDs.
032   Especially, they observed a correlation between the heaviness of tails in the ESDs and the strong
033   generalization performance of deep NNs on CV/NLP tasks. On the other hand, owing to the HT
034   nature of stochastic gradient noise (Simsekli et al., 2019; Panigrahi et al., 2019; Zhang et al., 2020),
035   further studies have shown that limiting HT distributions of weight values lead to good generaliza-
036   tion (Simsekli et al., 2020a; Gurbuzbalaban et al., 2021; Hodgkinson et al., 2022; Simsekli et al.,
037   2019). Since such a distribution of values can, in turn, result in weight matrices with HT ESDs
038   (Arous & Guionnet, 2008; Belinschi et al., 2009), the study of this broader HT phenomenon in deep
039
      learning has gained importance in recent years. In particular, the shape of HT ESDs has been suc-
      cessfully leveraged to assess the quality of pre-trained NNs (Martin & Mahoney, 2020; Martin et al.,
040
      2021; Martin & Mahoney, 2021b) (including Large Language Models (Yang et al., 2023)), and de-
041
      sign layer-wise learning rate schedulers (Zhou et al., 2023). Similarly, the shape of HT stochastic
042   noise has been shown to impact generalization (Simsekli et al., 2019; 2020b;a; Hodgkinson et al.,
043   2022; Gurbuzbalaban et al., 2021), the compressibility of NNs (Barsbey et al., 2021), and the design
044   of federated-learning algorithms (Yang et al., 2022; Li et al., 2024). The theoretical efforts (Simsekli
045   et al., 2019; 2020a;b; Gurbuzbalaban et al., 2021; Hodgkinson & Mahoney, 2021; Hodgkinson et al.,
046   2022) that aim to understand the underlying mechanisms of the HT phenomenon have focused on
047   the limiting distributions of the weight values and have not explicitly studied the ESDs.
048   In this paper, we present an alternative setup that is amenable to discrete-step analysis and is rich
049   enough to study the evolution of ESDs in two-layer NNs without any gradient noise. Our setup does
050   not rely on continuous time approximations to SGD or limiting distribution analysis but instead
051   employs a Teacher-Student setting (Arous et al., 2021; Bietti et al., 2022; Ba et al., 2022; Dandi
052   et al., 2023; Ba et al., 2023; Mousavi-Hosseini et al., 2023b) to study the effects of finite optimizer
053   steps on the weight matrix ESD (Figure 1). By training a two-layer feed-forward NN (Student)
      to learn a single-index model (Teacher) using vanilla Gradient Descent (GD) and Full-batch Adam

068   Intuition. Before diving into the details, we present the reader with a mental model of the ESD
069   evolution in this setup. At initialization, the hidden layer weight matrix ESD of the student NN
070   can be characterized by the Marchencko-Pastur distribution (i.e. random-like). The first step with a
071   large О· has been shown to result in an outlier singular value (i.e. a вҖҳspikeвҖҷ) in the ESD and results in
072   a вҖҳBulk+SpikeвҖҷ shape. The singular vector corresponding to this spike tends to align with the target
073   direction of the teacher model and results in improved generalization (Ba et al., 2022; Dandi et al.,
074   2023). By continuing to train the student NN with such large О·, our work shows that the interactions
075
      between the spike and the bulk gradually lead to a вҖҳBulk-DecayвҖҷ and finally lead to HT ESD.
076   Our theory is primarily aimed at identifying the large О· for FB-Adam, which can result in a spike
077   after the one-step update. Especially, we formulate the scale of О· (depending on hidden layer width
078   and data dimension) which can lead to a Bulk+Spike ESD after the one-step update. We also show
079   their intriguing relationship with feature-learning (Ba et al., 2022; Dandi et al., 2023) in two-layer
080   NNs, which can lead to improved generalization. Our results for FB-Adam in the feature learning
081
      context can be of independent interest to the community since prior works are limited to GD.
082   Toward understanding the shape transitions of ESD from the Bulk+Spike to an HT, we vary О· from
083   small to large values and observe that the distance between the spike and the bulk (i.e spectral gap)
084   determines the HT emergence during the early phases of training. For instance, О· = 1 leads to HT
085   ESDs just after t = 10 steps, whereas О· = 0.1 requires very long training up to t = 10000 steps (see
086   also Table 3, Table 4). Thus highlighting the necessity to study critical learning rates for one-step
087
      optimizer updates. We also analyze the correlations between the heaviness of tails (as measured
      by power-law fits) in the ESD and the generalization of the student NN after multiple steps. We
088
      showcase empirical results on the existence of a range of О· (depending on the optimizer) that can
089
      result in an HT ESD as well as improve the generalization of the student NN. In particular, О· in such
090   a suitable range results in HT ESDs whose power-law fit (as measured by the hill-climbing approach
091   (Yang et al., 2023)) lie in the range of (2, 2.5) and lead to strong generalization. This result supports
092   the observations of Martin & Mahoney (2021a); Martin et al. (2021) for well-trained deep NNs
093   where the truncated power-law fit of the ESDs lies within a range of (2, 4). Finally, we present a
094   novel perspective on HT ESD emergence using tools from the вҖҳsignal recoveryвҖҷ literature (Landau
095   et al., 2023; Benaych-Georges & Nadakuditi, 2012). In particular, we show that the alignments of
096   singular vectors of the hidden layer weight matrix and its corresponding optimizer update matrix
097   significantly influence the shape of ESD during training.
098   To summarize, our main contributions are as follows:
099
100          вҖў We present a gradient-noise-free setting to study the emergence of HT ESD in the hidden
101            layer weight matrix of two-layer NNs during the initial phases of training. To the best of
102            our knowledge, this is the first work to study the early evolution phases of the ESD across
103            discrete training steps with large learning rates. In this setup, we present a novel insight
104
               that feature learning after the first step (via the spike in the weight matrix ESD) facilitates
               HT emergence after finite steps.
105
106          вҖў We theoretically establish the scale of the learning rate at which one step of FB-Adam
107            results in a spike in the hidden layer weight matrix ESD. This result can be of independent
               interest to the community since prior results have been established only for GD.

             вҖў We empirically analyze the evolution of the hidden layer weight matrix ESD into an HT
109            distribution during training. Interestingly, we show that for a certain range of optimizer-
110            dependent learning rates, the two-layer NN can exhibit HT ESDs and generalize well.
111
             вҖў We present a novel perspective on the interactions between the bulk and spike of the ESD

               which result in a HT distribution. By analyzing the overlap(alignment) of singular vectors
113            of the weight matrix and its corresponding optimizer updates during training, our quali-

120   2   R ELATED W ORK
121
122   The heavy-tailed phenomenon. Heavy tails in machine learning have been observed and studied
123   in various forms. The most prominent empirical results are from a series of works by Martin &
124   Mahoney (2020; 2021a;b); Martin et al. (2021), which propose a heavy-tailed self regularization
125   (HT-SR) theory of deep NNs. In particular, Martin & Mahoney (2021a) proposed a вҖҳ5 + 1вҖҷ phase
126   model corresponding to the ESD evolution, and aims to model the HT-SR effect during training.
127   Their observations from extensive empirical analysis showcased a correlation between the power
128   law fits of weight matrix ESDs and generalization. Although it is still unclear if HT ESDs are
129
      necessary for generalization, Martin et al. (2021); Yang et al. (2023) have shown that the shape met-
      rics of the ESD can be effectively leveraged to identify well-trained NNs (and can be extended to
130
      large-scale models such as LLMs). From a theoretical perspective, previous efforts have primarily
131
      focused on stochastic optimization settings and proposed generalization bounds based on the tail
132   indices of the stochastic noise (Simsekli et al., 2019; 2020b;a; Gurbuzbalaban et al., 2021; Hodgkin-
133   son & Mahoney, 2021; Hodgkinson et al., 2022; Raj et al., 2023; Nguyen et al., 2019; Barsbey
134   et al., 2021). In particular, the earlier work by Simsekli et al. (2020a) employed continuous-time
135   approximation of SGD via feller-processes and studied the role of the Hausdorff Dimension on gen-
136   eralization. More recently, Hodgkinson et al. (2022) extended this analysis to discrete-time settings.
137   In concurrent works, Gurbuzbalaban et al. (2021) studied the heavy-tailed stationary distributions
138   of discrete Markov processes as an approximation to SGD iterates in the infinite data regime, while
139   Hodgkinson & Mahoney (2021) analyzed the role of multiplicative noise in such settings. While
140
      these efforts have emphasized the role of the learning rate/batch size ratio in determining the tail
      index of the iterates, a formal study on the role of these hyper-parameters is still lacking. More
141
      importantly, none of these efforts have focused on the evolution of the ESD itself. Although the
142
      limiting HT distribution of the weight values can lead to HT ESDs (Arous & Guionnet, 2008), the
143   insights cannot be directly used for a fine-grained analysis of the вҖҳ5 + 1вҖҷ phase model. Our work
144   aims to bridge these gaps and analyzes the fine-grained ESD evolution from a Marchenko-Pastur
145   (MP) fit (i.e. random initialization) вҶ’ вҖңBulk+SpikeвҖқ вҶ’ вҖңBulk-DecayвҖқ вҶ’ HT distributions and the
146   correlations with generalization.
147
148   Feature learning and large learning rates. Learning single-index models using two-layer NNs
149   under the Teacher-Student setup has provided rich insights into the sample complexity (Damian
150   et al., 2023; Mousavi-Hosseini et al., 2023a; Zweig et al., 2023; Damian et al., 2024; 2022; Abbe
151   et al., 2023) and training dynamics (Bietti et al., 2022; Wang et al., 2023; Cui et al., 2024; Moniri
152   et al., 2023) of NNs. In particular, the study of feature learning in such two-layer NNs focuses on the
153
      factors (such as optimizers (Abbe et al., 2023), loss landscapes (Damian et al., 2023), representations
      (Nichani et al., 2023) and learning rates (Dandi et al., 2023)) that facilitate sample efficient learning
154
      beyond the kernel regime (Louart et al., 2018; Gerace et al., 2020; Mei & Montanari, 2022; Hu &
155
      Lu, 2022; Goldt et al., 2022; Liu et al., 2021). Recently, Ba et al. (2022) analyzed the first step of
156   GD update in the high dimensional setting and formalized the scale of О· required to go beyond the
157   random feature regime. In particular, such a large О· is necessary for a two-layer NN to learn the
158   hidden direction of a single-index model after one step of GD (see also (Dandi et al., 2023)). This first
159   large update was shown to result in an outlier singular value (i.e. a вҖҳspikeвҖҷ) in the ESD of the hidden
160   layer weight matrix. Our work presents the first result for such large О· in the case of FB-Adam
161   and can be of wider interest in the feature learning context. Beyond the one-step analysis, Dandi
      et al. (2024) analyzed the role of two-pass GD in learning single-index models with large information

      exponents (Arous et al., 2021). However, the effects of multiple passes over the data on the ESD
163   and generalization are yet to be fully understood. Our multi-step analysis empirically highlights
164   the importance of such feature learning after the first step for the emergence of HT ESD. Thus,
165   showcasing the unexplored connections between these two areas of research.

520   6    C ONCLUSION
521   This paper presents a different angle to study the emergence of HT ESDs during NN training. Un-
522   like existing explanations using stochastic gradient noise, we show that full-batch GD or Adam can
523   still lead to HT ESDs in the weight matrices after only a few optimizer updates with large О·. Our
524   paper also connects with several ongoing studies in this field. In particular, our study analyzes the
525   вҖҳ5 + 1вҖҷ phase model (Martin & Mahoney, 2021a) of ESD evolution and sheds light on the transitions
526   from a Marchenko-Pastur (MP) fit (i.e. random initialization) вҶ’ вҖңBulk+SpikeвҖқ вҶ’ вҖңBulk-DecayвҖқ вҶ’
527   HT distributions. Our paper views the вҖңBulk-DecayвҖқ ESD as an intermediate state generated from
528   diffusing the spike into the main bulk (see also Appendix E.3). Furthermore, our study tightens the
529
      connection between ESDs and feature learning, explaining why ESD-based training methods (Zhou
      et al., 2023) can improve the generalizability of large and deep models. Our paper also presents
530
      several surprising phenomena: (1) the emergence of the HT spectra seems to require only a single
531
      spike aligned with the teacher model; (2) the emergence of the HT spectra can appear early during
532   training, way before the NN reaches a low training loss; (3) several factors, such as weight nor-
533   malization and learning rate scheduling, can all contribute to the emergence of HT ESDs. Overall,
534   by connecting the HT phenomenon with feature learning, we hope to promote further research into
535   these deeply connected characteristics of NNs.

1648   E.7    A NOTE ON THE SUITABLE RANGE OF TAIL INDEX VALUES
1649
1650
       Previous works such as Simsekli et al. (2020a); Hodgkinson et al. (2022) studied the limiting dis-
       tribution of the weight matrix values by modeling the stochastic gradient noise using levy/feller
1651
       processes. In particular, they showed that smaller tail-index values lead to smaller generalization er-
1652
       rors. The underlying idea is that when the weight values tend to an HT distribution, then the resulting
1653   ESD is also an HT (see Arous & Guionnet (2008)). Given this explanation, we note that the theo-
1654   retical results by Simsekli et al. (2020a); Hodgkinson et al. (2022) were shown to hold in practice
1655   by their numerical experiments, where the tail index tends to lie in a suitable range (depending on
1656   the PL alpha fit approaches) and represents heavy-tailed noise for good generalization. Even though
1657   our setting differs significantly from these previous works, the suitability of such a range seems to
1658   hold. In particular, when О· exceeds the thresholds given in Corollary 4.2, the model learns the single
1659   index direction and generalizes well by exhibiting HT ESD with a suitable PL Alpha. However, a
1660   significant increase in О· can lead to extreme HTs which are not desirable. The large magnitude of
1661
       W can also result in large values of MSE on unseen data, especially since the second layer weights
       are fixed and not selected as per the two-phase training strategy.

1665
1666   We found that the estimation of power-law (PL) exponents, such as PL Alpha Hill and
1667   PL Alpha KS, can be sensitive to the scale of singular values in the weight matrix. However,
1668   Figure 5c , 5d illustrates a similar trend for both estimates. We believe this does not affect our
1669   qualitative interpretation of the relationship between HT, learning rates, and generalization.
1670   Additionally, we highlight a particularly interesting observation. Considering FB-Adam based up-
1671   dates with О· = 1 after t = 10 steps and after t = 100 steps in Table 4, notice that the spike tends to
1672   get closer to the bulk in the latter (i.e as training progresses). We have observed a similar behavior
1673   for GD (see Appendix E.3) where the spike tends to merge with the bulk. Thus, the estimation of
       PL exponents should be relatively less affected by such outlier spikes as training progresses.

       G    L IMITATIONS AND F UTURE WORK
1681
1682
       Currently, our analysis on FB-Adam updates does not theoretically analyze the role of large learning
1683
       rates on the training and test losses after one or more steps. such a rigorous characterization requires
1684   new techniques to analyze the regression loss beyond the Gaussian equivalence assumption (Ba
1685   et al., 2022). Furthermore, the techniques used to calculate the PL Alpha of ESDs are subject to
1686   bias (in the case of the hill estimator) and to relatively larger variance in the case of the KS variant.
1687   Similar issues have been discussed in previous works (Yang et al., 2023; Zhou et al., 2023), and
1688   analyzing multiple approaches can provide a complete picture of the heaviness of the tails.
1689
       To this end, we discuss the following potential future efforts.
1690
1691
       Generalization with FB-Adam. Recent papers employing a similar setup have analyzed the fea-
1692
       ture matrix Z to rigorously characterize the training and test errors after a one-step GD update
1693   (Moniri et al., 2023; Cui et al., 2024). In a similar spirit, the results from our work can be utilized
1694   to theoretically explore the spectral properties of Z with FB-Adam. Additionally, since the spectral
1695   properties of Z are tightly linked to the ESD of WtвҠӨ Wt , the fundamental question on the necessity
1696   of HT ESDs for generalization can be rigorously answered.
1697
1698   Spectral gap and step complexity. In Section 5 we observed that the number of steps required for
1699   HT ESD emergence depends on the spectral gap (i.e the distance between the spike and the bulk in
1700   our setup) after the first step. While our work establishes the necessity of the spike, further analysis
1701   of the relationship between this spectral gap and the step complexity for HT ESD emergence can
1702   lead to novel insights.
1703
1704   HT phenomenon and singular vector overlaps. By presenting qualitative results that indicate
1705   HT-like distributions along the diagonals of overlap matrices, we aim to bring singular vectors into
1706
       the picture for future HT phenomenon studies. Particularly, the qualitative differences in the overlap
       matrices of left and right singular vectors remain to be explored. Furthermore, metrics to quantify
1707
       the вҖҳspreadвҖҷ of the on/off-diagonal overlap values can present a holistic picture of the interactions
1708
       between the singular spaces of the weights and optimizer updates.
1709
1710
       Towards analysis with deeper NNs and the вҖҳ5 + 1вҖҷ phase model. Our work showcased how the
1711   вҖҳ5 + 1вҖҷ phase model of the ESDs can be studied under simpler two-layer NN settings and reasonably
1712   explain the practical NN ESD dynamics. A valuable direction of research is to extend this analysis
1713   to deeper NNs (Nichani et al., 2023) with multi-index models as teacher networks, and study the
1714   variations in the shapes of ESDs across depth.
1715
1716   Designing novel techniques to improve generalization. Recently, Zhou et al. (2023) employed
1717   the layer-wise PL Alpha Hill metric to design a layer-wise learning rate scheduler based on
1718   SGD. Based on our observations on the varying effects of О· for GD/FB-Adam and the dynamics of
1719   ESD evolution, there is immense potential to further improve such schedulers. Furthermore, new
1720   regularization techniques to balance the PL Alpha Hill metrics across layers during training can
1721
       lead to NNs that satisfy the ESD shape metric criteria to be considered as вҖҳwell-trainedвҖҷ by model
       selection approaches (Martin et al., 2021; Yang et al., 2023). The intriguing consequences of such a
1722
       technique on the convergence rates and sample complexities can lead to new directions of research.
