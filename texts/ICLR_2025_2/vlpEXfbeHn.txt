009                                                A BSTRACT
010
011            Time cost is a major challenge in achieving high-quality pluralistic image com-
012            pletion. Recently, the Retentive Network (RetNet) in natural language processing
013            offers a novel approach to this problem with its low-cost inference capabilities.
014            Inspired by this, we apply RetNet to the pluralistic image completion task in com-
015            puter vision. We present RetCompletion, a two-stage framework. In the first stage,
016            we introduce Bi-RetNet, a bidirectional sequence information fusion model that
017
               integrates contextual information from images. During inference, we employ a
               unidirectional pixel-wise update strategy to restore consistent image structures,
018
               achieving both high reconstruction quality and fast inference speed. In the second
019
               stage, we use a CNN for low-resolution upsampling to enhance texture details.
020            Experiments on ImageNet and CelebA-HQ demonstrate that our inference speed is
021            10ˆ faster than ICT and 15ˆ faster than RePaint. The proposed RetCompletion
022            significantly improves inference speed and delivers strong performance, especially
023            when masks cover large areas of the image.

026   1   I NTRODUCTION
027
028
      Pluralistic image completion, also known as image inpainting, is a crucial research area with various
      applications, including object removal and photo restoration Barnes et al. (2009); Criminisi et al.
029
      (2004); Dale et al. (2009); Wan et al. (2020). CNN-based methods ?Iizuka et al. (2017); Li et al.
030
      (2017) have demonstrated impressive results by capturing local texture patterns, but they often
031   struggle to model global structures, leading to suboptimal image reconstruction quality. To overcome
032   this limitation, researchers have introduced hybrid models combining Transformers and CNNs Wan
033   et al. (2021); Zheng et al. (2022); Li et al. (2022). While these approaches significantly improve
034   reconstruction quality and produce diverse results by modeling the underlying data distribution,
035   Transformer-based pixel-wise generation involves extensive feature fusion calculations. This compu-
036   tational overhead increases inference time, limiting the practicality of these methods, especially in
037   real-time applications. Therefore, developing algorithms that maintain high-quality reconstruction
038   while improving computational efficiency remains a critical challenge in this domain.
039   Recently, Retentive Network (RetNet) Sun et al. (2023) has shown substantial potential in natural
040   language processing due to its multi-scale retention mechanism, which bridges parallel training
041   and recurrent inference. This capability enables RetNet to process information efficiently, even in
042   pixel-wise generation tasks. However, applying RetNet directly to vision tasks presents challenges,
043   as image information is not unidirectional like language data.
044   In this work, we propose RetCompletion, a novel image completion framework designed to address
045   the challenges of slow inference and inconsistent image reconstruction. RetCompletion operates in
046   two stages: the first stage leverages a Bi-RetNet architecture for low-resolution pixel-wise image
047   generation, while the second stage uses a CNN for high-resolution texture refinement. Extensive
048   experiments on datasets such as ImageNet and CelebA-HQ demonstrate that RetCompletion signifi-
049   cantly accelerates inference while maintaining high reconstruction quality.
050   The key contributions of this work are:
051
052         1. First application of RetNet to image completion: We introduce RetNet for the first time
053            in image completion tasks, utilizing its parallel training and recursive inference to accelerate
               the process.

      2     R ELATED W ORK
064
      Pluralistic Image Completion The significance of Pluralistic Image Completion lies in providing
065
      a diverse approach to image processing, allowing for the creation of images with different styles
066
      and effects, enriching the toolbox in creative and design fields, and supporting diverse choices
067   in decision-making processes. PIC Zheng et al. (2019) employs a dual-path framework based on
068   probabilistic principles: one is the reconstructive path, which utilizes the given ground truth to obtain
069   prior information about the missing parts and reconstructs the original image from this distribution.
070   The other is the generative path, where the conditional prior is coupled with the distribution from the
071   reconstructive path. ICT Wan et al. (2021) directly optimizes the log-likelihood in the discrete space in
072   the first transformer-based stage without the need for additional assumptions. RePaint Lugmayr et al.
073   (2022) applies the Diffusion model to the image inpainting task, using a pre-trained unconditional
074   DDPM Ho et al. (2020) as the generative prior and modifying the reverse diffusion iterations by
075
      sampling the unmasked regions from the given image information. Since this technique doesn’t alter
      or condition the original DDPM Ho et al. (2020) network itself, the model can generate high-quality
076
      and diverse output images for any inpainting scenario.
077
078
      Retentive Network Retentive Network Sun et al. (2023) introduces the retention mechanism with
079   a dual form of recurrence and parallelism. It has three computation paradigms,i.e., parallel, recurrent,
080   and chunkwise recurrent. We can train parallelly by using parallel paradigm while conducting
081   inference recurrently using recurrent and chunkwise paradigms. The retention mechanism utilizes
082   a rotation-based positional encoding along with a decay term to effectively model the position
083   information of tokens, known as xPos Sun et al. (2022), a relative position embedding proposed for
084   Transformer. We attempt to extend this method to two-dimensional images.

      3.4   PARALLEL T RAINING
237
      During training, we utilize both the parallel and chunkwise recurrent representations to accelerate the
238   process. Specifically, we choose to predict all masked pixels simultaneously, rather than sequentially,
239   in order to improve training efficiency.
240
241
      In this approach, masked pixels receive color information exclusively from unmasked pixels, meaning
      that masked pixels do not incorporate information predicted for other masked pixels, even those earlier
242
      in the sequence. This strategy allows for more efficient computation, as it reduces dependencies
243
      between predictions and enables faster iteration over large datasets.
244
245   By employing this parallelized method, we are able to reduce the overall training time, especially
246   when handling high-dimensional data.

      In the inference stage, we adopt a pixel-wise inference method, which has proven to be significantly
      more effective compared to predicting all pixels simultaneously. The pixel-wise approach allows the
251
      model to incorporate newly predicted pixel information step by step, improving the overall quality of
252
      the generated images. This advantage is made possible by the Bi-RetNet architecture, which enables
253   fast updates during inference, a capability that Transformer-based models lack.
254
255   We begin by performing information fusion on the initial image to generate integrated representations,

      Sf orward and Sbackward . Then, we predict the masked pixels one by one in a raster-scan manner.
      At each step, we update the retention state of the forward RetNet with the new pixel information,
257
      ensuring that each subsequent pixel prediction benefits from previous predictions.

473   4.4      I NFERENCE T IME
474
475   To ensure a fair comparison, we measured the pixel-wise completion inference time of ICT Wan
476   et al. (2021), RePaint Lugmayr et al. (2022), and our proposed method on the ImageNet Russakovsky
477   et al. (2015) dataset using a GeForce RTX 4090 GPU. The results, shown in Figure 3b, demonstrate
478   that our method achieves significantly lower inference times compared to the other two methods,
479   particularly at higher mask rates.
480   For the RePaint method, although its inference time remains consistent regardless of the mask rate,
481   it requires numerous iterative denoising steps, leading to a substantial overall inference time. On
482   the other hand, ICT Wan et al. (2021) recalculates attention for each pixel estimation, causing the
483   inference time to increase linearly as the mask rate rises. In contrast, our method leverages a recurrent
484   computation paradigm, where only the information of changed pixels is updated and integrated into
485   the state S. As a result, our method shows only a minimal increase in inference time across different
      mask rates, ensuring consistently high-speed performance.

      5    L IMITATIONS
487
488
      As illustrated in Figure 2, current image completion methods encounter significant difficulties when
489   dealing with more challenging mask types. For example, the performance on Expand-type masks
490   is particularly subpar, especially when applied to highly diverse datasets such as ImageNet. This
491   highlights a notable gap in the effectiveness of existing approaches. Recognizing this limitation, our
492   future research will place a stronger emphasis on improving the robustness and accuracy of image
493   completion techniques for these complex mask scenarios.

      6    C ONCLUSION
496
497
      We propose RetCompletion, a two-stage method for pluralistic image completion with three key
498
      innovations. First, RetNet is applied for the first time in image completion, offering efficient parallel
499
      training and recursive inference. Second, we introduce Bi-RetNet, which integrates bidirectional
500   contextual information to enhance image consistency and reconstruction quality. Third, our pixel-wise
501   inference approach significantly reduces inference time, outperforming Transformer-based methods
502   in computational efficiency. Experiments demonstrate that RetCompletion delivers superior image
503   quality over CNN-based methods and achieves comparable results to Transformer approaches, while
504   maintaining faster inference, making it highly suitable for real-time applications.
