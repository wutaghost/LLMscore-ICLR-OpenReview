009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            Recently, several point-based image editing methods (e.g., DragDiffusion, Free-
012            Drag, DragNoise) have emerged, yielding precise and high-quality results based
013            on user instructions. However, these methods often make insufficient use of se-
014            mantic information, leading to less desirable results. In this paper, we proposed a
015            novel mask-free point-based image editing method, AdaptiveDrag, which pro-
016            vides a more flexible editing approach and generates images that better align
017
               with user intent. Specifically, we design an auto mask generation module using
               super-pixel division for user-friendliness. Next, we leverage a pre-trained diffu-
018
               sion model to optimize the latent, enabling the dragging of features from handle
019
               points to target points. To ensure a comprehensive connection between the input
020            image and the drag process, we have developed a semantic-driven optimization.
021            We design adaptive steps that are supervised by the positions of the points and the
022            semantic regions derived from super-pixel segmentation. This refined optimiza-
023            tion process also leads to more realistic and accurate drag results. Furthermore,
024            to address the limitations in the generative consistency of the diffusion model, we
025            introduce an innovative corresponding loss during the sampling process. Building
026            on these effective designs, our method delivers superior generation results using
027            only the single input image and the handle-target point pairs. Extensive exper-
028
               iments have been conducted and demonstrate that the proposed method outper-
               forms others in handling various drag instructions (e.g., resize, movement, exten-
029
               sion) across different domains (e.g., animals, human face, land space, clothing).
030
               The code is provided in the supplementary materials.

      ods Abdal et al. (2019) with latent space, such as the StyleGAN utilizes the editable W space.

054
      Recently, DragGAN Pan et al. (2023) introduced a point-to-point dragging scheme to edit images,
055   providing a way to achieve fine-grained content change.
056
057   Due to the shortcomings of GAN methods Abdal et al. (2019) in terms of generalization and im-
058
      age quality, the diffusion model Ho et al. (2020) was proposed, offering improved stability and
      higher-quality image generation. DragDiffusion Shi et al. (2024b) first adopts the point-to-point
059
      drag scheme from DragGAN Pan et al. (2023) on the diffusion model. It employs LoRA Hu et al.
060
      (2021) to maintain the consistency between the original image and results, then optimizes the la-
061   tent via motion supervision and point tracking steps. However, the update strategy for point-based
062   drags in DragDiffusion has several limitations, making it challenging to achieve satisfactory editing
063   results. Firstly, users must use a brush to draw a mask, defining the area they wish to adjust. This
064   not only increases the operational complexity of image editing but also makes the results sensitive
065   to the mask region. EasyDrag Hou et al. (2024) simplifies the operation by generating the mask area
066   via the normalized gradients over the threshold g. However, the gradients of the entire image are not
067   directly related to the user’s edit points, and more critically, the disappearance or cumulative error of
068   these gradients might often result in significant distortions. Another weakness is the fixed step and
069
      feature updating region strategy in the latent optimization. For varying dragging distances, the fixed
      number of iterations cannot effectively optimize the latent representation to reach the target points,
070
      leading to the issue of ‘Drag Missing’ (left side of Fig. 1). As mentioned in Liu et al. (2024), when
071
      the feature differences in the neighboring areas are minimal, the ‘Feature maintenance failure’ oc-
072   curs. However, fixed feature updating regions inevitably blend with surrounding features together,
073   leading to increased similarity with adjacent areas. As a result, in the right part of Fig. 1, existing
074   methods fail to preserve the features at the center of the mountains during long-scale editing.
075
      In this paper, we introduce a novel point-based image editing approach called AdaptiveDrag to
076
      address the aforementioned issues. (1) Auto Mask Generation. We propose an auto-mask genera-
077
      tion scheme that integrates both image content and drag point positions. To better align the image
078   content with the mask, inspired by Mu et al. (2024), we get the image elements by the Simple
079   Linear Iterative Clustering (SLIC) Achanta et al. (2012). It segments the image into patches on the
080   feature space of the Segmentation Anything Model 2 (SAM 2) Ravi et al. (2024). Next, We propose
081   a line-searching strategy to generate the final mask, informed by the positions of the handle points
082   and target points. Ultimately, this process automates the generation of a mask that precisely cov-
083   ers the area to be edited and aligns with the user’s intent. (2) Semantic-Driven Optimization. We
084   incorporate semantic relative information into our latent optimization. Specifically, we designed a
085   position-supervised backtracking strategy to enable adaptive step iteration, effectively handling dif-
086
      ferent drag lengths. For feature region selection, we use segmentation patches from the SLIC results,
      providing a more precise area for motion supervision and point tracking steps. (3) Correspondence
087
      Sample. To address the instability of the sampling process, our method incorporates a corresponding
088
      loss function between the regions of handle points and target points. Finally, our proposed method
089   can effectively generate high-quality images based on a variety of user drag instructions.
090
091   In summary, our contributions to this paper are as follows:
092
093          • We propose a mask-free drag method, called Auto Mask Generation, via semantic-driven
094            segmentation to automatically generate a precise mask area. It offers users an easy-to-
095            operate but accurate approach to image editing without explicitly drawing the user mask.
096
097          • We design an adaptive strategy for the latent optimization process, called Semantic-Driven
098            Optimization. It employs a semantics-driven automated process for managing drag steps,
099            update regions, and update radius. Coupled with the adaptive strategy, this approach yields
100            drag results that are more aligned with the semantic features of the input image and com-
101
               patible with the target points.
102
103
             • We propose Correspondence Sample to improve the generation stability of the diffusion
               process, encouraging the semantic consistency between regions of handle and target points.

107   isting approaches in handling a variety of drag instructions (e.g., resize, movement, extension) and
      across different domains (e.g., animals, human face, land space, clothing).

110   2.1   GAN-BASED I MAGE E DITING
111
112   Interactive image editing involves modifying an input image based on specific user instructions. Ex-
113   isting control methods, which rely on text instructions Brooks et al. (2023); Lyu et al. (2023); Meng
114   et al. (2021) and region masks Lugmayr et al. (2022), suffer from precision issues, while image-
115   based referencing methods Chen et al. (2024b); Yang et al. (2023) fall short in terms of control
116   flexibility. Point-based image editing employs a series of user-specified handle-target point pairs
117
      to adjust generative image content, aligning with target point positions. For instance, Endo Endo
      (2022) introduces a latent transformer to learn the connection between two latent codes using Style-
118
      GAN Mokady et al. (2022). DragGAN Pan et al. (2023) proposes an updating scheme involving
119
      ”point tracking” and ”motion supervision” within the feature map to align handle points with their
120   corresponding target points. However, GAN-based methods often struggle with complex instruc-
121   tions and yield unsatisfactory results due to their limited model capacity.
122
123   2.2   D IFFUSION -BASED I MAGE E DITING
124
125   Recently, the impressive generative capabilities of large-scale text-to-image diffusion models have
126   led to the development of numerous methods based on these models Rombach et al. (2022); Saharia
127   et al. (2022). For interactive image editing, DragDiffusion Shi et al. (2024b) employs a point-based
128   image editing scheme based on the diffusion model, similar to DragGAN. This method utilizes
129
      LoRA for identity-preserving fine-tuning and optimizes the latent space using the loss function
      of motion supervision and point tracking. However, as shown in Fig. 1, previous methods (e.g.,
130
      DragDiffusion, EasyDrag) face two main issues: ‘drag missing’ and ‘feature maintenance failure’
131
      which result in the latent being incorrectly positioned in certain regions. FreeDrag Ling et al. (2024)
132   introduces a template feature through adaptive updating and line search with backtracking strate-
133   gies, resulting in more stable dragging. DragNoise Liu et al. (2024) presents a semantic editor that
134   modifies the diffusion latent in a single denoising step, leveraging the inherent bottleneck features
135   of U-Net. Nevertheless, these methods still have challenges when dragging over long distances
136   or across complex textures. To design a user-friendly point-based image editing method, Easy-
137   Drag Hou et al. (2024) leverages gradients in the motion supervision process that remain unchanged
138   in areas with small gradients, and it automatically generates the mask M. Moreover, some meth-
139   ods Shi et al. (2024a); Shin et al. (2024); Lu et al. (2024); Cui et al. (2024) attempt to improve the
140
      quality of results in various ways (e.g., drag by regions Lu et al. (2024), flow-based drag Shin et al.
      (2024), and the fast editing method Shi et al. (2024a)). However, these previous methods provided
141
      the mask is not always directly related to the image content, which can result in inaccurate mask
142
      generation and unsatisfactory image outcomes. In contrast to previous work, we propose a novel
143   semantic-driven point-based image editing framework that achieves precise results across different
144   drag ranges without the need for a mask.

255   3.4     S EMANTIC -D RIVEN O PTIMIZATION
256
      Building on the inversion stage and the automatic mask generation module, we propose a novel
257
      semantic-driven optimization that enhances the precision of image editing by improving the cor-
258   relation between the input images and instructions, ensuring the edits more accurately align with
259   the image’s context. Following a similar design to DragGAN and DragDiffusion, the main latent
260   optimization process in our proposed method also consists of two key steps: motion supervision
261   and point tracking, which are implemented consecutively. Next, the two steps are then repeated
262   iteratively until all handle points reach their respective targets. As illustrated in the orange box of
263   Fig 2, the design optimization module consists of two parts. First, for the repeated steps, we pro-
264   pose position-supervised backtracking, as detailed in Sec. 3.4.1, to adaptively adjust the number of
265   steps based on the positions of input drag points and predicted points in each point tracking step.
266
      The other component is the semantic region, described in Sec. 3.4.2, which is used to constrain the
      feature area during the motion supervision and point tracking steps. It leverages super-pixel patches
267
      from the auto mask generation to form regions that more accurately align with the image content.

      5   C ONCLUSION
530
531
      In this paper, we proposed a novel point-based image editing method, AdaptiveDrag, which intro-
      duces a semantic-driven framework that offers a more user-friendly and precise drag-based editing
532
      approach compared to existing methods. With the auto mask generation module, the user can con-
533
      veniently modify the images by clicking several points. Furthermore, the proposed semantic-driven
534   optimization yields high-quality results across arbitrary dragging distances and domains. The corre-
535   spondence sampling with CLoss further enhances performance by improving precision and ensuring
536   stable feature preservation. Finally, extensive experiments demonstrate AdaptiveDrag’s capability to
537   generate images that meet user satisfaction. However, our approach still has limitations in cases of
538   extremely long-distance dragging, where the results may not consistently with expectations. In our
539   experiments, an improved base model version (Stable Diffusion XL) can broaden the manipulation
      range. For reproducibility, we provide our source code location and guidance in Sec. A.4.
