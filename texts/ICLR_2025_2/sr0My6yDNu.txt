009                                              A BSTRACT
010
011
               Adversarial robustness is essential for security and reliability of machine learning
012            systems. However, adversarial robustness enhanced by defense algorithms is eas-
013            ily erased as the neural network’s weights update to learn new tasks. To address
014            this vulnerability, it is essential to improve the capability of neural networks in
015            terms of robust continual learning. Specially, we propose a novel gradient projec-
016            tion technique that effectively stabilizes sample gradients from previous data by
017            orthogonally projecting back-propagation gradients onto a crucial subspace be-
018            fore using them for weight updates. This technique can maintaining robustness by
019            collaborating with a class of defense algorithms through sample gradient smooth-
020
               ing. The experimental results on four benchmarks including Split-CIFAR100 and
               Split-miniImageNet, demonstrate that the superiority of the proposed approach in
021
               mitigating rapidly degradation of robustness during continual learning even when
022
               facing strong adversarial attacks.

026   1   I NTRODUCTION
027
028   Continual learning and adversarial robustness are distinct and important research directions in arti-
029   ficial intelligence, each of which has witnessed significant advances. The former addresses a critical
030
      challenge known as catastrophic forgetting, where a neural network trained on a sequential of new
      tasks typically exhibits a dramatic drop in its performance on previously learned tasks if the model
031
      cannot revisit the previous data Farajtabar et al. (2020). The latter focuses on developing defenses
032
      against adversarial attacks that can deceive models into confidently misclassifying objects by adding
033   subtle targeted perturbations to the input images often imperceptible to human observers Silva &
034   Najafirad (2020).
035
036
      However, the evolution of the neural network’s adversarial robustness in context of continuous learn-
      ing remains underexplored. In our experiments, we observe that adversarial robustness enhanced by
037
      well-designed defense algorithms on previous data is easily lost when the neural network updates
038
      its weights to accommodate new tasks, resulting in a phenomenon similar to catastrophic forget-
039   ting. This presents an intriguing challenge: how can we maintain the adversarial robustness during
040   continuous learning? In other words, the objective of continuous learning expands to concurrently
041   encompass classification performance and adversarial robustness.
042
      In this paper, we present a solution by proposing a novel gradient projection technique called Dou-
043
      ble Gradient Projection (DGP), which inherently enables collaboration with a class of defense al-
044
      gorithms that enhance robustness through sample gradient smoothing. DGP is grounded on a theo-
045   retical hypothesis that a neural network’s robustness can be maintained if the smoothness of sample
046   gradients from previous data remain unchanged after weight updates. Specifically, when learning
047   a new task, DGP projects the back-propagation gradients into the orthogonal direction to a crucial
048   subspace before utilizing them for weight updates. This gradient subspace consists of two sets of
049   base vectors derived from previous tasks, which are obtained by performing singular value decom-
050   position on the layer-wise outputs of the neural network and the gradients of layer-wise outputs with
051   respect to samples, respectively. Our contributions are summarized as follows:
052
053        1. We introduce the problem of robust continual learning in the scenario where data from
              previous tasks cannot be revisited.

            2. We propose the Double Gradient Projection approach that stabilizes the sample gradients
055            from previous tasks by orthogonally constraining the direction of weight updates. It can
056            maintain robustness by collaborating with a class of defense algorithms that enhance ro-
057            bustness through sample gradient smoothing.
058
            3. We validate the superiority of our approach on four image benchmarks. Furthermore, the
059            experiment results indicate that without a tailored design, direct combination of existing
060            continual learning and defense algorithms into the training procedure can be conflicting,
061            resulting that the efficacy of the former is seriously weakened.

      5    R ELATED W ORKS
465
466   The aim of robust continual learning is not to
467   achieve stronger robustness on a single dataset, but rather to maintain robustness across multiple
468   datasets encountered sequentially. One related work Bai et al. (2023) also explores the under-
469   researched direction of robust continual learning. A fundamental distinction between that work
470   and ours is their approach requires partial data from previous tasks to be accessible, thereby focus-
471   ing on the selection of a key subset of previous data and optimizing its re-utilization in new tasks. In
472   contrast, we follow the stricter yet realistic scenario in the field of continual learning that data from
473   the previous tasks cannot be revisited. Further insights in the advancements in adversarial robust-
474
      ness and continual learning can be found in dedicated surveys Silva & Najafirad (2020); Wang et al.
      (2024).

477   6   L IMITATION AND D ISCUSSION
478
479   In this work, we observe that the adversarial robustness gained by well-design defense algorithms is
480   easily erased when the neural network learns new tasks. Direct combinations of existing defense and
481   continuous learning algorithms fail to effectively address this issue, and may even give rise to con-
482
      flicts between them. Therefore, we propose a novel gradient projection technique that can mitigate
      rapidly degradation of robustness in the face of drastic changes in model weights by collaborating
483
      with a class of defense algorithms through sample gradient smoothing.
484
485   According to our experiment, the proposed approach has certain limitations. First, as the number of
      base vectors becomes large, the stability of the neural network is enhanced to hold both robustness


                                                         9
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


486
      and performance across previous tasks. This stability may restrict the plasticity of the neural net-
487   work, potentially reducing its ability to learn new tasks. Second, if there are numerous tasks and the
488   matrix consisting of orthogonal bases reaches full rank, we approximate this matrix by performing
489   SVD and selecting column vectors corresponding to a fraction of the largest singular values as the
490   new orthogonal bases to free up rank space. Three, due to the extra challenge posed by our problem,
491   the perturbation size of adversarial attacks under which the proposed method work effectively is
492   slightly smaller than typical values in adversarial robustness literature (Please see Appendix B.3 for
493   more details).

860
              • EWC is a regularization technique that utilizes the Fisher Information matrix to quantify
861

862
863           • SI computes the local impact of model parameters on global loss variations, consolidating
                crucial synapses by preventing their modification in new tasks;

            • A-GEM is a memory-based approach, similar to GEM, which leverages data from episodic

866
