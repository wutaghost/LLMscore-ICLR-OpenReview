009                                                A BSTRACT
010
011            LLMs with superior response quality—particularly larger or closed-source mod-
012            els—often come with higher inference costs, making their deployment inefficient
013            and costly. Meanwhile, developing foundational LLMs from scratch is becoming
014            increasingly resource-intensive and impractical for many applications. To address
015            the challenge of balancing quality and cost, we introduce Routoo, an architecture
016            designed to optimize the selection of LLMs for specific prompts based on per-
017            formance, cost, and efficiency. Routoo provides controllability over the trade-off
018
               between inference cost and quality, enabling significant reductions in inference
               costs for a given quality requirement. Routoo comprises two key components: a
019
               performance predictor and cost-aware selector. The performance predictor is a
020
               lightweight LLM that estimates the expected performance of various underlying
021            LLMs on a given prompt without executing them. The cost-aware selector mod-
022            ule then selects the most suitable model based on these predictions and constraints
023            such as cost and latency, significantly reducing inference costs for the same qual-
024            ity. We evaluated Routoo using the MMLU benchmark across 57 domains em-
025            ploying open-source models. Our results show that Routoo matches the perfor-
026            mance of the Mixtral 8x7b model while reducing inference costs by one-third.
027            Additionally, by allowing increased costs, Routoo surpasses Mixtral’s accuracy
028            by over 5% at equivalent costs, achieving an accuracy of 75.9%. When integrat-
029
               ing GPT4 into our model pool, Routoo nearly matches GPT4’s performance at
               half the cost and exceeds it with a 25% cost reduction. These outcomes highlight
030
               Routoo’s potential to significantly reduce inference costs without compromising
031
               quality, and even to establish new state-of-the-art results by leveraging the collec-
032            tive capabilities of multiple LLMs.

035   1   I NTRODUCTION
036
037   LLMs have achieved remarkable success across a wide range of natural language processing tasks.
038   However, this success comes with a significant trade-off between performance and cost: larger
039   models generally offer better response quality but incur higher inference costs than their smaller
040   counterparts. This increased inference cost poses challenges for deploying LLMs in real-world
041   applications where computational resources, latency, and cost-efficiency are critical considerations.
042   At the same time, developing foundational LLMs (OpenAI et al., 2024; Dubey et al., 2024; Yang
043   et al., 2024; DeepSeek-AI et al., 2024; Jiang et al., 2024; 2023a; Touvron et al., 2023) from scratch
044   is becoming increasingly capital-intensive, requiring vast computational resources and extensive,
045   high-quality data (Minaee et al., 2024). As the field approaches the limits of network size and data
046   capacity, the improvements gained from training ever-larger models are diminishing (Udandarao
047   et al., 2024). This situation underscores the need for alternative approaches that can achieve high
048   performance without incurring prohibitive development and inference costs.
049   Moreover, many practical tasks do not necessitate the intricate reasoning capabilities of the largest
050   models like GPT-4; instead, they can be efficiently handled by smaller, more cost-effective mod-
051   els (Zaharia et al., 2024; Ding et al., 2024; Shnitzer et al., 2023; Chen et al., 2023). The capabilities
052   of existing LLMs appear to be complementary to a significant degree. For example, on the MMLU
053   benchmark (Hendrycks et al., 2021), selecting the optimal open-source model for each question
      could hypothetically yield an accuracy of 97.5% at a computational cost similar to that of a 13-

      billion-parameter model (see Appendix A for details). In contrast, GPT-4 (OpenAI et al., 2024)
055   achieves an accuracy of 86.4%, while Mixtral 8×7b (Jiang et al., 2024), one of the leading open-
056   source models,1 reaches 70%. These observations suggest that integrating the knowledge of multiple
057   LLMs could lead to new state-of-the-art models without the need to train from scratch while sig-
058   nificanly reducing the inference cost. However, effectively leveraging the vast and rapidly growing
059   ecosystem of LLMs poses significant challenges. With approximately 450,000 models available
060   on Hugging Face,2 keeping track of the latest advancements and integrating them efficiently is a
061   non-trivial task. Traditional approaches like Mixture of Experts (MoE) models (Abdin et al., 2024;
062   Lieber et al., 2024; Jiang et al., 2024; Fedus et al., 2021; Shazeer et al., 2017) are limited by the need
063
      to load all expert parameters onto a single high-end machine, hindering scalability and flexibility.
      There is a need for an architecture that can dynamically and efficiently leverage multiple existing
064
      LLMs to optimize performance while controlling inference costs.
065
066   To address these challenges, we propose Routoo , an architecture designed to optimize the selection
067   of LLMs for specific prompts based on performance, cost, and efficiency. Routoo provides con-
068   trollability over the trade-off between inference cost and quality, enabling significant reductions in
069
      inference costs for a given quality requirement. By intelligently leveraging a universe of trained
      LLMs, Routoo addresses both the inference cost problem and the development cost of building
070
      LLMs from scratch, creating a composite high-performance model without the need for extensive
071
      retraining.
072
073   Routoo comprises two key components: a performance predictor and cost-aware selector. The per-
074   formance predictor is a lightweight LLM that estimates the expected performance of various under-
075
      lying LLMs on a given query without executing them. Based on these predictions and constraints
      such as cost and latency, the cost-aware selector module selects the most suitable model. For in-
076
      stance, when a task is predicted to be performed nearly equally well by a smaller model or a larger,
077
      more expensive model, Routoo will opt for the smaller model when speed and cost-efficiency are
078   prioritized. This approach ensures optimal resource utilization without compromising on quality.
079
080   Our architecture marks a significant departure from traditional MoE. While MoE relies on gating
081
      over various expert sub-networks within each layer to predict the next token, it requires all expert
      parameters to be loaded onto a single, high-end machine. This limitation hinders scalability in the
082
      number of experts. In contrast, each ’expert’ within our system operates independently and can
083
      be hosted on different machines, potentially utilizing a different neural network architecture. This
084   flexibility enables Routoo to incorporate a vast array of experts, ranging from specialized domain
085   models to general-purpose ones, and to scale to a large number of experts without the limitations
086   imposed by MoE models.
087
      We evaluate our Routoo on MMLU benchmark (Hendrycks et al., 2021). We show that it achieves
088
      competitive performance with Mixtral 8x7b (Jiang et al., 2024) while only consuming two-thirds
089
      of its inference cost. Increasing the cost budget allows Routoo to outperform Mixtral by 5% at the
090   same cost level, reaching an accuracy of 75.9%. By adding GPT4 (OpenAI et al., 2024) as one of the
091   underlying experts, our Routoo achieves competitive performance with GPT4 at half the cost and
092   exceeds it with a 25% cost reduction. These outcomes highlight Routoo’s potential to significantly
093   reduce inference costs without compromising quality, and even to establish new state-of-the-art
094   results by leveraging the collective capabilities of multiple LLMs. By providing controllability
095   over cost and quality trade-offs, Routoo offers an efficient approach to high-performance language
096   modeling without the need for expensive model training from scratch.

102               the need for training from scratch.

      Mixture-of-Experts. MoE architecture includes a gating mechanism to integrate various expert
135
      sub-networks within each layer, guiding the prediction of the next token (Shazeer et al., 2017).
136
      This approach is used in recent foundational models, including Mixtral 8x7b (Jiang et al., 2024).
137   Sukhbaatar et al. (2024) fine-tuned LLaMa 7b (Touvron et al., 2023) on four different domains to
138   create domain-specific expert LLMs. They then proposed combining the FFNs of these expert LLMs
139   to construct an MoE. Tang et al. (2024) proposed merging most parameters while scaling up MLP
140   layrers of Transformer into a weight-ensembling MoE module that dynamically integrates shared
141   and task-specific knowledge based on the input. Wang et al. (2024) introduces a fusion gate at each
142   Transformer layer to generate weights for a weighted average of outputs from a set of pre-trained
143   LoRAs.
144
145   Model Selection. Previous works in selecting the best LLMs mainly focus on identifying the one
146   that generates the most optimal output for a given input. Liu & Liu (2021); Ravaut et al. (2022)
147
      proposed specialized scoring or re-ranking models that can be used for the generation tasks (sum-
      marisation, here). Speculative decoding (Kim et al., 2023; Leviathan et al., 2023) accelerates the
148
      decoding of expensive models by using small, efficient decoders for the ’easy’ steps. This approach
149
      can complement routing methods. ROUTERBENCH (Hu et al., 2024) proposed a benchmark for
150   LLM routing task. akota et al. (2024) used meta-modeling approach to assign the appropriate model
151   to each query while considering the cost constraint.LLM-BLENDER (Jiang et al., 2023b) is an en-
152   sembling framework to reach better performance by mixing the results of LLMs with a ranking
153   module, followed by a generation module to generate from top candidates of the ranker. Frugal-
154   GPT (Chen et al., 2023) executes experts sequentially until an expert reaches the acceptable gen-
155   eration performance. ZOOTER (Lu et al., 2024) proposed a reward-guided routing approach that
156   distills rewards from training queries to train a routing function. HybridLLM (Ding et al., 2024) and
157   RouteLLM (Ong et al., 2024) propose a routing approach to direct queries to the suitable expert,
158
      utilizing one strong and one weak LLMs.
159   Different from previous work, our Routoo identifies the most suitable expert without executing the
160   inference of underlying LLMs. Additionally, our approach can be efficiently generalized to scenarios
161   involving many underlying LLMs (not necessarily a tuple of weak and strong LLMs). This allows
      Routoo model to outperform even the best closed-source models, including GPT-4 (OpenAI et al.,

351    benchmark (Hendrycks et al., 2021), which comprises multiple-choice questions across 57 diverse

354    to evaluate our models. 5 Precisely, it calculates the likelihood of each choice in the question, and
355    selects the answer with maximum likelihood, then ’accuracy’ is used as the evaluation metric. The
356    overall performance is calculated as the average accuracy of the model in 57 domains. Routoo
357
       models will be made publicly available for the AI community to test on various benchmarks.

409
410   Filtering Available Datasets. We begin by collecting various multiple-choice QA datasets, such
411
      as ARC (Clark et al., 2018), MC-TEST (Richardson et al., 2013), OBQA (Mihaylov et al., 2018),

412
      ing signal (i.e., difficulty), we employed SOLAR-10.7B-v1.0 (Kim et al., 2024) 10 on this aggregated
413
      dataset, and calculate the evaluation score.11 Queries on which the model under-performed (accu-
414   racy of 0) were retained, resulting in a set of 45,645 challenging training samples from an initial
415   pool of 101,434. Additionally, 10,000 simpler questions were randomly selected from the initial
416   dataset, bringing the total to 55,645 queries.

418

      queries using GPT4 model. These queries are generated by using seed data, which randomly sam-

421
      ation are documented in Appendix C.

447
      Our Routoo Models. We present two variations of Routoo model: Routoo (open-source) and
448
      Routoo (mix). The former refers to a model that leverages 7b, 13b ,and 34b open-source mod-
449   els available on Huggingface as the input to our universe constructor. By additionally integrating
450   GPT4 (OpenAI et al., 2024) to underlying models of Routoo (open-source), we create Routoo (mix).
451   KNN variants (open-source and mix) are similarly defined.
452
453   Overall Results. Comparison of our models and baselines are illustrated in Table 1.13 The cost of
454   Routoo (open-source) is adjusted to match Mixtral 8x7b (Jiang et al., 2023a), the best generic open-
455   source LLM (at the time of writing this paper). Our model significantly outperforms Mixtral 8x7b by
456   5.27% absolute points with the same inference cost. Also, our underlying models can be executed on
457   1 GPU (e.g. A100 with 40 GB memory), while Mixtral model requires access to high-end computing
458   resources (e.g. GPUs with 100 GB memory). Compared to LLaMa2 70b, our model achieves
459   significantly better performance (+6%), while reducing the cost by 33%. Impressively, Routoo
460   (open-source) achieves competitive performance with GPT3.5 while reducing the cost by 73.3%.
461
      Compared to closed-source LLMs, our Routoo (mix) model significantly outperforms GPT3.5 by
      14.9% absolute point. Routoo (mix) reaches competitive performance with GPT4, while reducing
462
      the cost by almost 50%. Additionally, Routoo models significantly outperforms KNN baselines,
463
      demonstrating that a LoRA fine-tuned LLM as performance predictor provides better estimations of
464   the performance scores.
465
466
      Cost-Aware Selection. Given that the cost-aware selector module can compute different rout-
467
      ing distributions within a specified budget, we illustrate the curve of performance-cost in Figure 2
468   for both open-source and closed-source baselines, alongside with variations of Routoo models.14
469   Notably, our Routoo (open-source) reaches the performance of Mixtral 8x7b (Jiang et al., 2024)
470   while decreasing the cost by 33%. Interestingly, Routoo (mix) achieves better performance than
471   GPT4 (86.64 vs. 86.40) while reducing the inference cost by nearly 25%.
472   In summary, our cost-aware selector effectively offers an optimal trade-off between cost and perfor-
473   mance, enabling both open-source and mix variants of the Routoo model to outperform individual
474   LLMs in terms of performance (or cost) during inference.

      5        C ONCLUSION AND F UTURE W ORK
502
      In this paper, we proposed our Routoo architecture, a lightweight LLM-based model that is designed
503
      to inteligently routes the input query to the most suitable expert model given other constraints e.g.
504
      cost. We evaluated our architecture on MMLU benchmark (Hendrycks et al., 2021), which is a
505   MCQA dataset with 57 different domains coverage. Routoo (open-source) achieved competitive
506   performance with Mixtral 8x7b model (Jiang et al., 2024) with two-thirds of the inference cost.
507   Increasing the budget limitation allows Routoo (open-source) to outperform Mixtral model by 5%
508   with the same level of cost. By integrating GPT4 (OpenAI et al., 2024) model to underlying LLMs,
509   our Routoo (mix) achieved competitive accuracy with GPT4 while reducing the inference cost by
510   50%, and even surpassing it while saving 25% of the cost. In general, our Routoo models provide
511   an efficient trade-off between cost and performance during the inference.
512   In future, the Routoo’s ability to assess and understand the performance of existing models allows
513   researchers to identify gaps in the AI landscape. It pinpoints domains where no existing expert
514   excels or where larger models are inefficiently juggling tasks. This insight is invaluable, enabling
515   us to strategically develop domain-specific models where they are needed most. Furthermore, our
516   architecture facilitates easy integration of additional optimization criteria, including cost, speed, and
517   privacy considerations.

883   A PPENDIX B          KNN BASELINE I MPLEMENTATION
884
885   The algorithm of our k-nearest neighbors (KNN) method comprises the following steps:
886
               • We utilized the ”text-embedding-ada-002” model from OpenAI 16 to embed the question-
887
                 answer pairs from the training data defined in Section 4.2. Each question-answer pair was
888              converted into a string using the format: <question> [QUESTION] </question>
889              <answer> [ANSWER] </answer>. For efficient inference implementation, we utilized
890              the Faiss library (Douze et al., 2024; Johnson et al., 2019).
891
               • During testing, the question field was converted into a string using the format:
892              <question> [QUESTION] </question>.
893
               • We then retrieved the top-5 related LLMs by applying cosine similarity function, then ap-
894
                 plied the cost-aware selection method defined in Section 3.3. Specifically, the estimated
895
                 scores are the cosine similarity scores between the test question and the related question-
896              answer pairs from the training data.
