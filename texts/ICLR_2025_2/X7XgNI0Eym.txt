009                                                    A BSTRACT
010
011            Recent works in volume rendering, e.g. NeRF and 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS),
012            significantly advance the rendering quality and efficiency with the help of the
013            learned implicit neural radiance field or 3D Gaussians. Rendering on top of an ex-
014            plicit representation, the vanilla 3DGS and its variants deliver real-time efficiency
015            by optimizing the parametric model with single-view supervision per iteration
016            during training which is adopted from NeRF. Consequently, certain views are
017
               overfitted, leading to unsatisfying appearance in novel-view synthesis and im-
               precise 3D geometries. To solve aforementioned problems, we propose a new
018
               3DGS optimization method embodying four key novel contributions: 1) We trans-
019
               form the conventional single-view training paradigm into a multi-view training
020            strategy. With our proposed multi-view regulation, 3D Gaussian attributes are
021            further optimized without overfitting certain training views. As a general solution,
022            we improve the overall accuracy in a variety of scenarios and different Gaussian
023            variants. 2) Inspired by the benefit introduced by additional views, we further
024            propose a cross-intrinsic guidance scheme, leading to a coarse-to-fine training pro-
025            cedure concerning different resolutions. 3) Built on top of our multi-view regulated
026            training, we further propose a cross-ray densification strategy, densifying more
027            Gaussian kernels in the ray-intersect regions from a selection of views. 4) By fur-
028
               ther investigating the densification strategy, we found that the effect of densification
               should be enhanced when certain views are distinct dramatically. As a solution, we
029
               propose a novel multi-view augmented densification strategy, where 3D Gaussians
030
               are encouraged to get densified to a sufficient number accordingly, resulting in
031            improved reconstruction accuracy. We conduct extensive experiments to demon-
032            strate that our proposed method is capable of improving novel view synthesis of
033            the Gaussian-based explicit representation methods about 1 dB PSNR for various
034            tasks. Codes are available.

      scenes with strong reflection, transparency, and fine-scale details against baseline methods.
051   Photorealistic rendering towards unbounded scenes or single object is proven to hold significant values
052   in both the industrial and academic areas, e.g. multi-media generation, virtual reality, and autonomous
053   driving. Conventional primitive-based representations such as mesh and point cloud (Botsch et al.,
      2005; Lassner & Zollhofer, 2021; Yifan et al., 2019; Munkberg et al., 2022) make real-time rendering

054
      possible with the help of efficient rasterization. Although such a rendering mechanism delivers high
055   efficiency, revealing a fine-grained and sufficiently precise appearance is still struggling, where blurry
056   artifacts and discontinuity happen. On the contrary, implicit representation (Erler et al., 2020) and
057   neural radiance field (Mildenhall et al., 2021; Barron et al., 2022; Müller et al., 2022)(NeRF), employ
058   the multi-layer perceptron (MLP) to improve the potentiality of rendering high-fidelity geometries
059   where more detailed structures are preserved. However, their inference efficiency is still limited even
060   with accelerating operators such as Instant-NGP (Müller et al., 2022).
061   Recently, 3D Gaussian-based explicit representation, e.g. Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) (Kerbl et al.,
062   2023; Jiang et al., 2024; Lu et al., 2024; Wu et al., 2024) achieve both state-of-the-art rendering
063   quality and efficiency contributed by its tailored rasterization technique, following the paradigm of
064   NeRF, i.e. training with a sample from a single camera view per iteration. Such a training strategy
065   is commonly adopted in NeRF because of its pixel-wise rendering behavior for the convenience
066   of utilization of supervision. As for 3DGS (Kerbl et al., 2023), 3D Gaussians kernels are directly
067   rasterized on the image plane and get optimized with pixel-wise losses compared to the ground truth
068   as well. However, because of the explicit characteristic of 3DGS representation, we observe the
069
      single-view training paradigm encourages 3D Gaussian kernels overfitting certain views for reducing
      training losses, making it not robust enough to precisely present all details in the scene.
070
071   In this paper, we propose MVGS, a general optimization method, empowering a large variety of
072   Gaussian-based explicit approaches for better NVS precision as shown in Fig. 1 (d), with an exemplar
073   case as shown in Fig. 1 (a), (b) and (c). The most crucial contribution of our work is altering
074   the traditional training paradigm using a single-view supervision per iteration. We propose to
075
      incorporate multiple views per iteration during training by the proposed multi-view-regulated learning.
      Specifically, the overall set of 3D Gaussians towards the scene is forced to learn the structure and
076
      appearance of multiple views jointly without suffering the overfitting issues from a specific view.
077
      Consequently, such an optimization enables 3DGS kernels to get constrained to satisfy the rendering
078   for a selection of views instead of overfitting to a certain view. To incorporate more information upon
079   multi-view supervision, we propose cross-intrinsic guidance from low resolution to high resolution
080   during training. The low-resolution training allows plenty of multi-view information as a powerful
081   constraint to build more compact 3D Gaussians, which also conveys learned scene structure for
082   high-resolution training to sculpt finer detail. Intuitively, the 3D Gaussians in overlapped 3D regions
083   of cross rays should be densified to improve reconstruction performance for these views since these
084   3D Gaussians jointly serve and play an important role in the rendering of these views. To foster
085   the effectiveness of learning multi-view information, we further propose a cross-ray densification
086
      strategy to guide the densification process, utilizing the ray-marching techniques with the guidance
      of the 2D loss maps. In addition, we propose a multi-view augmented densification strategy when
087
      discrepancies between perspectives are significant. This approach encourages 3D Gaussian to densify
088
      more primitives, enabling better fitting across various perspectives and improving performance.
089
090   Extensive experiments are conducted to demonstrate that our method effectively improves NVS
091   performance for state-of-the-art Gaussian-based methods on various tasks, including general and
092
      reflective object reconstruction, 4D reconstruction, and large-scale scene reconstruction. Particularly,
      our experiments indicate that the NVS precision improves as the number of views increases in each
093
      optimization round. Moreover, our method encourages the learned 3D Gaussians to be more compact
094
      for representing the entire scene due to our proposed multi-view regulated learning. In conclusion,
095   we summarize our contributions as below:
096
      • We first propose a multi-view regulated training strategy that can be easily adapted upon existing
097
      single-view supervised 3DGS framework and its variants optimized for a large variety of tasks, where
098   the NVS and normal precision can be consistently improved.
099
      • Inspired by the benefit introduced by multi-view supervision with different extrinsic setups, a
100
      cross-intrinsic guidance scheme is proposed to train 3D Gaussians in a coarse-to-fine way. So that
101   3D Gaussians can accommodate higher consistency with pixel-wise local features.
102
      • As densification strategy is crucial for 3DGS, we further propose a cross-ray densification strategy,
103
      emitting rays under 2D loss map guidance and densifying for overlapped 3D regions. The densified
104
      3D Gaussians in those overlapped regions facilitate the fitting of multiple views, improving the
105   performance of novel view synthesis.

116   2   R ELATED W ORK
117
118   Volume Rendering. Significant advancements have been achieved in novel-view synthesis, particu-
119
      larly since the introduction of NeRF (Barron et al., 2021; Mildenhall et al., 2021), which employs
      MLP to parameterize the geometry and view-dependent appearance with the help of implicitly defined
120
      radiance field. Moreover, the training and inference efficiency of NeRF has been enhanced using
121
      hash-grid (Müller et al., 2022) and explicitly defined samplers (Li et al., 2023). Built on top of the
122   radiance field, NeuS (Wang et al., 2021), NeuS2 (Wang et al., 2023), and HF-NeuS (Wang et al.,
123   2022) also perform more precise surface reconstruction against traditional MVS fusion such as
124   MeshMVS (Shrestha et al., 2021). Given all the advantages of neural rendering, its efficiency is still
125   not implausible. Recently, 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) (Kerbl et al., 2023) has emerged, demon-
126   strating impressive real-time NVS performance. Gaussian-based methods, typically represented by
127   3D Gaussian Splating (Kerbl et al., 2023), are recent advancements in 3D reconstruction, enabling
128   high-quality and real-time rendering.
129
130   Gaussian Splatting. 3D Gaussian splitting Kerbl et al. (2023); Wu et al. (2024) rasterizes through
131   α-blending and depth-sorting to get Gaussians projected, thus achieving real-time rendering efficiency
132
      by avoiding the complex ray marching. Thanks to its real-time rendering speed and high-quality
      reconstruction performance, 3DGS has been improved and applied to numerous tasks, such as
133
      autonomous driving, reflective object reconstruction (Jiang et al., 2024), and 4D reconstruction (Wu
134
      et al., 2024). Subsequent works focus on improving Gaussian representation, such as techniques
135   about low-pass filtering (Huang et al., 2024a) and structure grid representations (Lu et al., 2024).
136   GaussianPro (Cheng et al., 2024) proposes a normal propagation method to bridge a gap from
137   SfM initialization and mitigate densification limitations. Pixel-GS (Zhang et al., 2024b) proposes
138   a gradient-based scaling densification strategy to avoid the generation of floater near the camera.
139   However, these Gaussian-based explicit representation methods adopt a single-image optimization
140   strategy (Mildenhall et al., 2021; Kerbl et al., 2023), leading to overfitting certain views and not
141   robust to novel view synthesis, especially when challenging scenarios are encountered, e.g. dynamic,
142   reflective, or few-shot reconstruction. Built on pre-trained networks, the extracted multi-view
143
      features can solve some of the mentioned difficulties. For example, MVSplat (Chen et al., 2024)
      builds a cost volume representation to store cross-view similarities for the estimation of depth.
144
      LatentSplat (Wewer et al., 2024) proposes a representation encoding uncertainty with latent 3D
145
      Gaussian features. PixelSplat (Charatan et al., 2024) predicts a dense probability distribution over 3D
146   sampled Gaussian positions. In this paper, our proposed method provides a more general solution
147   without relying on other pre-trained networks.

243   3.2   C ROSS - INTRINSIC GUIDANCE
244
245   As shown in the bottom of Fig. 2, inspired by the benefits introduced by image pyramid (Adelson
246   et al., 1984), we propose a coarse-to-fine training scheme with different camera setups, i.e. intrinsic
247
      parameters K, by simply supplementing more rasterization planes. Specifically, a 4-layer image
      pyramid with downsampling factors S = {2k−1 | k = 4 . . . 1} could be constructed as shown
248
      in Fig. 2 (d). Empirically, the largest downsampling factor set as 8 is enough to accommodate
249
      sufficient training images for multi-view training and the smallest downsampling factor set as 1
250   means that the downsampling operation is not applied. For each layer, we have matched multi-view
251   settings Ms = {M1 , M2 , M3 , M4 }. In particular, the larger downsampling factor enables more views
252   accommodated to provide stronger multi-view constraints. In the initial three training stages, we run
253   only a few thousand iterations per stage without completely training the model. Since target images
254   are downsampled, the model cannot capture fine details during these early stages. Therefore, we treat
255   the first three training stages as coarse training. During coarse training, incorporating more multi-view
256   information imposes more powerful constraints on the entire 3D Gaussians. In this case, the rich
257   multi-view information provides thorough supervision for the whole 3DGS and encourages fast fitting
258
      with coarse texture and structure. Once the coarse training is finished, fine training is started. Thanks
      to the previous coarse training stages providing a coarse architecture of 3DGS, the fine training stage
259
      only needs to refine and sculpt fine details for each 3D Gaussian. Especially, the coarse training
260
      stages provide a large number of multi-view constrain. It conveys the learned multi-view constraint to
261   the next fine training. This scheme effectively enhances multi-view constraints and further improves
262   the novel view synthesis performance.

      3.3   C ROSS - RAY D ENSIFICATION
265
266   Due to the nature of volume rendering and the explicit representation of 3DGS, 3D Gaussians in
267   some regions have a significant impact on distinct views when rendering. For instance, the central 3D
268   Gaussians are crucial when rendering with cameras shooting the center in different poses. However,
269   finding these regions is not trivial, especially in 3D space. As illustrated in Fig. 2, we propose a cross-
      ray densification strategy, starting from 2D space and then searching in 3D adaptively. Specifically,


                                                          5
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


270
      we first calculate loss maps of multiple views and then locate the regions containing the largest
271   average loss values with a sliding window with size (h, w). Afterward, we cast rays from the vertices
272   of these regions with four rays per window. Then, we calculate the intersection points across rays
273   of different perspectives. Since we cast four rays per perspective, the intersection points can form
274   several cuboids. These cuboids are the overlapped regions containing significant 3D Gaussians that
275   play an important role when rendering for multiple views. Therefore, we densify more 3D Gaussians
276   in these overlapped regions to facilitate the training of multi-view supervision. This strategy relies
277   on the accurate searching of overlapped regions containing 3D Gaussians with high significance to
278   several views. First, we choose the loss guidance, since it highlights the lowest-quality regions that
279
      should be improved for every view. Second, the ray casting technology allows us to locate the 3D
      regions containing a set of 3D Gaussians that contributes significantly to these views. Based on the
280
      accurate location, 3D Gaussians in these regions can be seen as pivotal for the joint optimization of
281
      multiple views. Note that we follow the densification mode of the original 3DGS to density one 3D
282   Gaussian into two 3D Gaussians. In this way, we densify these 3D Gaussians to a certain amount to
283   improve the reconstruction performance jointly for these views.

286
287   To get fast convergence, avoid local minimum, and learn fine-grained Gaussian kernels while dis-
288   crepancies between different views are significant, we propose a multi-view augmented densification
289   strategy. Specifically, our strategy builds on the densification strategy of the original 3DGS with a
290   predefined threshold β used to determine which 3D Gaussians should be densified. As depicted in
291   Fig. 2, we first identify whether the training views are strongly distinct. Instead of using the original
292   camera translations directly, we normalize the camera translations of sampled views into a unit sphere.
293   This approach makes our strategy adaptable to various scenes. Then, the relative translation distances
294
      {ri | i = 1, 2, . . . , n} between each camera and another is computed, where the number of distances
      n is M 2 − M assuming that we have M training views. In our multi-view augmented densification,
295
      we have a self-adaptive criterion β̂ that can be formulated as

      3.5   I MPLEMENTATION
306
      In our experiments, we utilize novel view synthesis metrics like PSNR, SSIM, and LPIPS to evaluate
307   the performance of models. For general object reconstruction, 3DGS (Kerbl et al., 2023) and Scaffold-
308   GS (Lu et al., 2024) are selected as our baselines due to their state-of-the-art performance. For
309   reflective object reconstruction, we choose 3DGS-DR (Ye et al., 2024) as our main baseline since it
310   is the recent SOTA method to reconstruct glossy objects. As for 4D reconstruction, 4DGS (Wu et al.,
311   2024) is selected as our baseline due to its fast rendering speed and high-quality 4D reconstruction
312   performance. In large-scale scene reconstruction, Octree-GS (Ren et al., 2024) is adopted as our
313   baseline since its level-of-detail structure is suitable for this kind of scene. In our proposed method,
314   we set Ms = {48, 24, 12, 8} and τ = 1. As for the other setting, we follow the implementation setting
315
      of these baselines. Our method can be easily integrated into existing Gaussian-based methods without
      200 lines of code.

532
      In this work, we propose MVGS, a novel and universal method to improve the novel view synthesis
533
      performance for existing Gaussian-based methods. The core of MVGS lies in the proposed multi-view
534   regulated learning, constraining the optimization of 3D Gaussians with multi-view information. We
535   show that our method can be integrated into existing methods to achieve state-of-the-art rendering
536   performance. We further demonstrate our proposed cross-intrinsic guidance scheme introducing
537   powerful muti-view constraints for better results. We also prove the effectiveness of the proposed
538   multi-view augmented densification and cross-ray densification in enhancing densification to facilitate
539   the optimization of 3D Gaussians. Extensive experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of our method
      and indicate that our method achieves state-of-the-art novel view synthesis results.
