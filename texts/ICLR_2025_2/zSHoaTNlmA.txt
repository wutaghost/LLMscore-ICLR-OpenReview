009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011
               Fusing global and local semantic information in segmentation networks remains
012
               challenging due to computational costs and the need for effective long-range
013
               recognition. Based on the recent success of transformers and attention mecha-
014            nisms, this research applies attention-based methods of attention-boosting mod-
015            ules and attention-fusion networks in enhancing the performance of state-of-the-
016            art segmentation networks, such as InternImage and SERNet-Former, addressing
017            these challenges. Integrating attention-boosting modules into residual networks
018            generates baseline architectures like Efficient-ResNet, enabling them to extract
019            global context feature maps in the encoder while minimizing computational costs.
020            Attention-based algorithms can also be applied to networks utilizing vision trans-
021            formers and convolutional layers, such as InternImage, to improve the existing
022
               results of state-of-the-art networks. In this research, SERNet-Former is deployed
               on the challenging benchmarking datasets such as ADE20K, BDD100K, CamVid,
023
               and Cityscapes by depending on the attention-based methods with new implemen-
024
               tations of the network, SERNet-Former v2. Our methods have also been imple-
025            mented for InternImage-XL and improved the test performance of the network on
026            the Cityscapes dataset (85.1 % mean IoU). Respectively, the results of the selected
027            networks developed by our methods on the challenging benchmarking datasets are
028            found worth considering: 85.1 % mean IoU on the Cityscapes test dataset, 59.35
029            % mean IoU on ADE20K validation dataset, 67.42 % mean IoU on BDD100K
030            validation dataset, and 84.62 % mean IoU on the CamVid dataset.

      1   I NTRODUCTION
035
036   Segmentation is a widely applied computational task for scene understanding in the field of computer
037   vision. Each pixel or mask in an image is represented through the labeled semantic classes in
038   semantic segmentation. The labeled classes represent the ground truth of an image input predicted by
039
      state-of-the-art networks and methods. Semantic segmentation has numerous applications, including
      autonomous driving, robotics for indoor and outdoor scene recognition, medical imaging, virtual and
040
      augmented reality, real-time surveillance, and photography (Minaee et al., 2022; Borse et al., 2021;
041
      Huang et al., 2023; Erişen, 2024). Segmentation networks are developed through Fully Connected
042   Networks (FCNs), Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), Vision Transformers (ViT), as well as
043   Swin Transformers with shifted windows attention mechanisms and mostly based on the encoder-
044   decoder architectures (Borse et al., 2021; Huang et al., 2023; Wang et al., 2023; Du et al., 2022; Xie
045   et al., 2021; Liu et al., 2021; Chen et al., 2023; Erişen, 2024). Encoder-decoder architectures have
046   shown remarkable progress, and recently, ViT-based networks with attention mechanisms achieved
047   state-of-the-art performances on semantic segmentation datasets (Wang et al., 2023; Erişen, 2024).
048   Despite advancements, achieving efficient multi-scale feature fusion and overcoming computational
049   bottlenecks remain significant limiting factors in segmentation tasks. The challenge of recognizing
050   objects in segmentation is two-fold: The labeled object may either lose the spatial information or
051   feature-rich properties while processing the image throughout the network (Erişen, 2024). Recent
052   state-of-the-art networks (Borse et al., 2021; Wang et al., 2023; Chen et al., 2018; Erişen, 2024) seek
053   to resolve the discrepancies between the semantic information extracted from the global and local
      contexts of a network.

      It is another fact that increasing the number of convolution layers only sometimes returns efficient
071
      results when compared to its computational cost (Wu et al., 2019). Recent research has moved away
072
      from solely expanding convolutional neural networks and instead explores their integration with at-
073   tention mechanisms improving performance (Erişen, 2024). Hence, this study re-evaluates encoder-
074   decoder architectures combined with attention-based fusion modules. It is aimed to investigate the
075   spatial information and feature maps thoroughly within convolutional neural networks with attention
076   mechanisms to enable efficient and accurate semantic segmentation. For instance, SERNet-Former
077   (Erişen, 2024) explores fused attention-gates and skip connections to optimize spatial and channel-
078   based feature extraction within both encoder and decoder stages. InternImage (Wang et al., 2023),
079   on the other hand, deploys deformable convolution layers that work in harmony with the deformable
080   attention networks and transformer heads improving the network’s performance.
081   In this research, the residual networks are analyzed first as lightweight baselines for efficient training
082   by considering the training accuracy and loss as the initial performance parameters in the network
083   design with attention-based methods (Fig. 1) (He et al., 2016; Erişen, 2024). Different activation
084   functions and attention gates are examined first with the residual convolution layers in the encoder
085   and decoder parts. Attention gates, strategically integrated into SERNet-Former, enhance feature
086   extraction, and the designed skip connections ensure the seamless fusion of multi-scale features
087
      (Erişen, 2024). Experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness of SERNet-Former in leveraging
      the fused spatial and semantic information (Fig. 1) (Chen et al., 2018; 2017; Erişen, 2024).
088
089   Respectively, the encoder of SERNet-Former is found as the most compatible baseline to be de-
090   veloped and improved with the attention-boosting gates (AbGs) and attention-boosting modules
091   (AbMs) resulting in an efficient residual network, Efficient-ResNet, with the increased training per-
092   formance and prediction efficiency (Erişen, 2024). Building on similar objectives of combining con-
093
      volutional layers with attention mechanisms, this research also explores InternImage architectures.
      AbGs are incorporated as activation functions in InternImage variants, such as InternImage-XL,
094
      leading to significant performance improvements. However, these enhancements come at the cost of
095
      increased computational demands. Attention-fusion networks (AfNs) are incorporated into the de-
096   coder of SERNet-Former (Erişen, 2024) to enhance its functionality by effectively storing, fusing,
097   and processing rich semantic information from the encoder during the up-sampling phase. These
098   networks are purposefully designed to blend global spatial context with feature-based rich semantic
099   information (Erişen, 2024). Consequently, our methods, AbG, AbM, and AfN, exhibit significant
100   improvements with the residual networks employed in SERNet-Former and SERNet-Former v2,
101   which is recompiled in PyTorch, along with notable advancements in the InternImage-XL network.
102   To summarize, attention-boosting gates, modules, and attention-fusion networks are utilized within
103   encoder-decoder architectures, such as SERNet-Former and InternImage (Erişen, 2024; Wang et al.,
104   2023). Attention-Boosting Gates (AbGs), Attention-Boosting Modules (AbMs), and Attention-
105   Fusion Networks (AfNs) integrate rich information from various contexts by combining essential
106   semantic data to achieve maximum efficiency. The attention-boosting modules improve the perfor-
107   mance of the networks by leveraging the feature-rich information of the global context. Attention-
      fusion networks help overcome long-range dependencies in predicting and recognizing smaller ob-

      2   R ELATED WORKS
128
129
      The multi-scale problem in computer vision refers to the challenge of integrating spatial and
      channel-based semantic information of an object in segmentation networks from both global and
130
      local contexts (Erişen, 2024). DeepLabv3+ (Chen et al., 2018) is the widely known segmentation
131
      architecture developed to fuse the feature-based rich semantic data with the spatial information,
132   similar to other encoder-decoder networks like U-Net and Segnet (Badrinarayanan et al., 2017; For-
133   oughi et al., 2021). The recent success of transformers integrated with CNNs (Wang et al., 2023)
134   also revealed that additional methods, such as transformer heads and attention mechanisms (Yan
135   et al., 2024) improve the efficiency of networks concerning multi-scale representations.
136
      Respectively, Li et al. (2022a) introduced global enhancement and local refinement methods inte-
137
      grated through a Context Fusion Block against the challenge of fusing the global and local semantic
138   information and loss of features during down-sampling and up-sampling (Li et al., 2020). The
139   Guided Attention Inference Network (GAIN) developed by Li et al. (2020) employs fully convo-
140   lutional networks and CNN-based semantic segmentation architectures, integrating widely utilized
141   baselines such as ResNet-101 (He et al., 2016; Xie et al., 2017).
142
      To enhance the synthesis between global and local semantic information alongside spatial and
143
      channel-wise features, the Squeeze-and-Excitation block (SENet), introduced by Hu et al. (2020),
144   has been implemented. In a similar vein, self-attention mechanisms have been designed to extract
145   comprehensive feature information from objects at each position by aggregating features from all
146   locations within a single sample (Guo et al., 2023). Consequently, Guo et al. (2023) proposed an
147   external attention module that employs memory units, thus replacing the conventional self-attention
148   mechanisms within semantic segmentation networks. The influences of SENet and self-attention
149   mechanisms have also inspired the development of SAB Net, which presents an end-to-end semantic
150   attention-boosting framework (Ding et al., 2022). This methodology proposes a non-local semantic
151   attention framework that regularizes the discrepancies between non-local and local information by
152
      applying category-wise learning weights (Ding et al., 2022). CTNet provides an alternative strategy
      utilizing a Channel Contextual Module to explore multi-scale local channel contexts and a Spatial
153
      Contextual Module to examine global spatial dependencies in a combined configuration (Li et al.,
154
      2022b).
155
156   Additionally, CoTNet integrates transformer architectures with self-attention mechanisms by in-
157   corporating a Contextual Transformer Block, which facilitates the transformation of each discrete
158
      convolutional operator (such as 3 by 3 convolutions) into two consecutive 1 by 1 convolutions (Li
      et al., 2023). Furthermore, Ye et al. (2022) presented cross-modal self-attention, which was designed
159
      to integrate image and language expressions as inputs.
160
161   Attention-based feature fusion has emerged as an effective approach addressing the multi-scale chal-
      lenges inherent in semantic segmentation (Yang & Gu, 2023). This technique employs across-feature

      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


162
      maps to tackle the difficulties associated with small objects, which are often difficult to accurately
163   identify due to their semantic characteristics and precise spatial information (Sang et al., 2023).
164   Furthermore, Choe et al. (2021) proposed an attention-based dropout layer designed to obscure the
165   most discriminative features of the model by utilizing a drop mask and an importance map, all while
166   preserving classification accuracy.
167
      As an alternative to attention-fusion networks, Liu et al. (2022) introduced the covariance atten-
168
      tion method, which employs the covariance matrix to delineate the dependencies between local and
169   global semantic features. In a related study, Yang et al. (2021a) developed a variational structured
170   attention mechanism to integrate channel-based and spatial features. Their methodology produces
171   tensor products derived from spatial and channel-wise attention modules, facilitating the evaluation
172   of the probabilities of latent variables that connect these two attention mechanisms. Additionally,
173   Hao et al. (2022) proposed a technique known as spatial-detail-guided context propagation, which
174   seeks to reconstruct lost information in low-resolution global contexts by leveraging the spatial de-
175   tails from shallower layers in real-time. The integration of spatial information with channel-based
176   rich semantic features has also been examined through the lens of RGB-D networks and 3D point
177
      clouds (Cao et al., 2021; Chen et al., 2020; Jian et al., 2021; Yang et al., 2021b). Moreover, Huang
      et al. (2023) introduced CCNet, a crisscross attention network that presents a novel alternative to
178
      conventional attention mechanisms. Respectively, this research evaluates the potential of convolu-
179
      tional networks with attention-based mechanisms to be applied for segmentation tasks.

182   3   M ETHOD
183
184   This research aims to assess the attention-based mechanisms of attention-boosting gates (AbGs),
185
      modules (AbMs), and attention-fusion networks (AfNs) in improving efficient segmentation net-
      works. Attention-boosting gates (AbGs) and attention-boosting modules (AbMs) are designed to
186
      excite and fuse the feature-rich spatial information into AfNs as well as the existing networks, such
187
      as ResNet, for fast and accurate training without losing the progress (Erişen, 2024). Accordingly,
188   the state-of-the-art networks, SERNet-Former and InternImage, using encoder-decoder architec-
189   tures with the same motivation using convolution layers and attention-based mechanisms (Wang
190   et al., 2023; Erişen, 2024) are deployed for the experiments and analyses of these attention-based
191   methods.
192
      Residual networks pre-trained on the ImageNet dataset serve as robust baselines for segmentation
193
      tasks due to their efficiency in learning key features rapidly, despite capacity constraints for novel
194   features (Erişen, 2024). Respectively, SERNet-Former (Erişen, 2024) is found as the most efficient
195   network to deploy AbGs, AbMs, as AfNs in this research (Fig. 2). AbGs utilize attention-based algo-
196   rithms to enhance the extraction of equivariant, pixel-wise, feature-rich semantic information from
197   selected baselines, maintaining equivalent input and output sizes (Erişen, 2024). Thus, the feature-
198   rich semantic information is integrated with the spatial context of the encoder through AbMs. In
199   the modification of InternImage architectures, however, the activation function is replaced with the
200   algorithms deploying the attention-boosting gates together with the ReLU function in this research.
201   To further improve feature learning and semantic representation, we explore attention-based fusion
202   modules integrated into convolutional neural networks. These modules address limitations in smaller
203   networks by enhancing spatial and channel-based information processing through attention mech-
204   anisms. Therefore, AbMs and AfNs, deployed in SERNet-Former (Erişen, 2024), amplify signal
205   efficiency and fuse spatial and channel-based semantics during decoding, effectively addressing ca-
206   pacity issues in smaller residual networks. In InternImage, AbGs and AbMs are introduced as a layer
207   of adaptive and attention-centric design replacing the traditional activation function. Dilation-based
208
      network (DbN) was also applied to SERNet-Former architectures (Chen et al., 2018; Erişen, 2024)
      in facilitating the efficient transmission of local semantic patterns between encoder and decoder
209
      components. DbN supports the seamless transfer of spatially rich features while preserving com-
210
      putational efficiency. SERNet-Former v2 is developed in PyTorch, based on the methods applied
211   in SERNet-Former together with attention mechanisms. In brief, the methods applied in improving
212   the efficiency of residual networks and InternImage architectures in this research are highlighted as
213   follows:

215          • AbgS and AbMs are used as the attention-based activation functions in segmentation net-
               works, such as SERNet-Former and InternImage-XL

239   3.1   ATTENTION - BOOSTING GATES
240
241   Leveraging the feature-based semantic information from the global context of segmentation net-
242   works confronts the increasing computational cost of attention heads. In this research, attention-
243   boosting gates and modules are designed to excite and fuse feature-rich semantic information by
244   applying attention-based algorithms to deal with this problem. In that regard, the Sigmoid function,
245
      widely applied in attention networks, is deployed as the activation function for attention-boosting
      gates, which excite pixel-wise rich semantic information that may not be activated through ReLU
246
      layers. Thus, it is aimed at extracting the feature-rich scalar values of each pixel from the data
247
      processed through the convolutional layers of residual networks instead of generating larger global
248   attention maps. AbGs are initially designed alongside attention mechanisms and then modified into
249   the weightless mathematical operators to be adapted to networks deploying attention transformers.
250   Hence, AbGs decrease the computational cost significantly compared to the conventional trans-
251   former architectures without compromising the possibility of acquiring and processing equivariant,
252   pixel-wise, and feature-based rich semantic information in proposing efficient attention mechanisms
253   for residual networks. The use of the Sigmoid function in Equation (1), and the activation operation
254   of the gate, AbG, can be iterated in Equation (2) as follows:

290   3.3   ATTENTION - FUSION NETWORKS
291
292   The attention-based algorithms of AbG and AbM are also designed to be applied in different at-
293   tention mechanisms and networks. Thus, they are deployed in the attention-fusion networks in the
294   decoder part of SERNet-Former (Erişen, 2024). The initial layers of CNNs contain rich global
295   semantic information, featuring sharp edges and distinct shapes of objects (Erişen, 2024). Thus,
296   efficient up-sampling in the decoder relies on effectively transferring and reconstructing the matrix
297
      weights and spatially rich features from the encoder processed through attention-based transform-
      ers. This ensures precise one-to-one image processing during semantic segmentation tasks (Erişen,
298
      2024). Thus, attention-based semantic information from the global and local contexts in the decoder
299
      part is fused by attention-fusion networks leveraging the pixel-wise scalar values activated through
300   the Sigmoid function.
301
302   To address the limited feature-learning capacity of smaller residual networks, AfNs are designed
303
      with additional convolution layers (Fig. 2(b)). These layers enhance the decoder’s ability to store
      and process semantic information. AfNs combine spatial and channel-based contextual features
304
      derived from deconvolution layers with varying strides. The resulting outputs are fused through a
305
      depth concatenation layer for further refinement (Fig. 2). Skip connections are also employed to
306   streamline spatial information transfer from the encoder and integrate these features with channel-
307   based information during up-sampling operations.

      also reveal that AbM and AfN decrease the loss in the initial training epochs successfully, increasing

515   5   C ONCLUSION
516
517   Attention-boosting gates and modules are utilized in advanced segmentation networks like SERNet-
518   Former and InternImage to address the multi-scale challenge of integrating semantic information
519   across varying sizes and contexts. The Sigmoid function helps to activate the pixel-wise feature
520   maps. Respectively, attention-fusion networks improve the performance and efficiency of the de-
521   coder of SERNet-Former and SERNet-Former v2 in fusing the semantic data from different contexts
522
      significantly.
523   Limitations: It is found that SERNet-Former architectures are still the most compatible networks for
524   implementing the attention-based methods of Abg, AbM, and AfN. On the other hand, our methods
525   are also limited to the overall architecture of SERNet-Former and InternImage in this article. For
526   instance, it is not possible to deploy AfNs to InternImage as its architecture has already exploited
527   the attention-based transformers throughout the whole network. Nevertheless, it is apparent that the
528
      methods that we apply, but not limited to SERNet-Former and InternImage-XL, can contribute to the
      results of different baselines and even state-of-the-art segmentation networks without compromising
529
      computational performance.
530
531   Future work: It is also possible to deploy AfNs in the decoder of networks. Adding multi-head atten-
532   tion networks, larger transformer heads, and adapting the groups of attention-fusion modules to ViT
533   baselines can also improve the networks’ results. Our attention-based modules can also contribute to
534
      the activations of learning representations in the baselines to be deployed in video and action recog-
      nition. Respectively, we hope that our methods deployed in Efficient-ResNet, InternImage-XL,
535
      SERNet-Former, and SERNet-Former v2 inspire many researchers to develop novel and efficient
536
      state-of-the-art networks and applications in integrating CNN with novel transformer mechanisms
537   and attention-based algorithms.

673   outputs of activation layers in DbN are fused before the decoder.
