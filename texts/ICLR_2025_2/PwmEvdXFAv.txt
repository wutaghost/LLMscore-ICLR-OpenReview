011                                              A BSTRACT
012
013            This work introduces novel computational methods for entropic optimal transport
014            (OT) problems under martingale-type conditions. The problems can map to a
015            prevalent class of OT problems with structural constraints, encompassing the dis-
016            crete martingale optimal transport (MOT) problem, as the (super-)martingale con-
017
               ditions are equivalent to row-wise (in-)equality constraints on the coupling matrix.
               Inspired by the recent empirical success of Sinkhorn-type algorithms, we propose
018
               an entropic formulation for the MOT problem and introduce Sinkhorn-type algo-
019
               rithms with sparse Newton iterations that utilize the (approximate) sparsity of the
020            Hessian matrix of the dual objective. As exact martingale conditions are typically
021            infeasible, we adopt entropic regularization to find an approximate constraint sat-
022            isfied solution. We show that, in practice, the proposed algorithms enjoy both
023            super-exponential convergence and robustness with controllable thresholds for to-
024            tal constraint violations.

      The martingale-type conditions P V = W and P V ≥ W constitute a prevalent class of constrained
047
      optimal transport problems whereby a few equality or inequality constraints are placed for every site
048   in the source distribution. The large number of constraints makes the corresponding optimization
049   task quite different from optimal transport (OT). There has been a considerable body of work on the
050   mathematical property of MOT (Ghoussoub et al., 2019; Huesmann & Trevisan, 2019; Alfonsi et al.,
051   2020; Backhoff-Veraguas & Pammer, 2022; Wiesel, 2023) and OT problems with super martingale
052   conditions (Nutz & Stebegg, 2018). The MOT problem under entropic regularization is of indepen-
053   dent research interest and is studied as a Schrodinger bridge problem with martingale constraints
      (Henry-Labordere, 2019; Nutz & Wiesel, 2024).

      Motivation Super-martingale conditions are prevalent for optimal transport problems with in-
055   equality constraints from inventory management concerns (Galichon, 2018). In this case, the source
056   distribution and target distribution, respectively, model receiver and supplier in a transport model.
057   For example, suppose that the resource supplied by supplier j is of an auxiliary utility vj and
058   the receiver i needs the total utility to exceed wi . In this case, write v = [v1 , . . . , vn ]⊤ , w =
059   [w1 , . . . , wn ]⊤ and the structural constraint on the coupling matrix P reads
060
                                                     P v ≥ w.
061
062   The MOT problem appears first in financial applications in computing upper and lower bounds for
063   model-free option pricing under the calibrated market model. This task assumes an asset with known
064   initial and final distributions and an exotic option whose expected payoff is a function of the price
065   of the asset at the initial and final time. In the calibrated market model, the distribution of the asset
066   is a martingale, which gives rise to the martingale condition in MOT. The computed bounds are
067   model-free as they hold under all stochastic processes the asset undergoes.
068   In addition, fairness in machine learning is growing in importance as a benchmark for machine
069   learning algorithms (Mehrabi et al., 2021; Barocas et al., 2023). Fairness in resource allocation can
070   also be cast as martingale-type constraints similar to Si et al. (2021); Buyl & De Bie (2022).
071
072   Main approach Inspired by recent theoretical analysis and empirical success of the Sinkhorn’s
073   algorithm (Yule, 1912; Sinkhorn, 1964; Cuturi, 2013) for optimal transport, this work uses entropic
074   regularization and explores fast numerical algorithms for the OT problems with martingale-type
075   constraints in equation 1 and equation 2. In contrast to recent works in constrained optimal transport
076
      with entropic regularization (Benamou et al., 2015; Tang et al., 2024a), the constraints considered
      in this setting are special with its nd constraints embedded in the linear equation P V = W or
077
      PV ≥ W.
078
079   For OT under martingale-type constraints, entropic regularization following Fang (1992) admits a
080   dual formulation in the form of a concave maximization problem. Given the formulation, we de-
081   velop two iterative maximization algorithms with a per-iteration complexity of O(n2 ). We introduce
082
      a Sinkhorn-type algorithm utilizing the sparse structure of the Hessian matrix. We further utilize the
      fact that the full Hessian matrix admits sparse approximations and introduce a Sinkhorn-Newton-
083
      Sparse (SNS) algorithm, which performs Sinkhorn-type iterations followed by sparse Newton iter-
084
      ations. The SNS algorithm rapidly converges to the entropically optimal solution, in practice often
085   achieving exponential or even super-exponential convergence. Thus, the numerical performance of
086   the proposed approach has the same O(n2 ) per-iteration complexity, and we show it has similar
087   practical convergence properties as that of Sinkhorn’s algorithm in optimal transport.
088
      For equality constraints of the type P V = W , it often occurs that the LP does not admit a feasible
089
      solution. Thus, we propose to consider a modified LP problem for MOT, first introduced in Guo
090
      & Obłój (2019), with the defining feature that it allows for the transport plan to have constraint
091   violations under a threshold. For practical purposes, having control over the constraint violation
092   threshold has the additional benefit that it allows more flexibility in the obtained transport plan.
093
094   Contribution We summarize our contribution as follows:
095
096          • For the MOT problem, we propose a novel entropic regularization approach based on ap-
097            proximate constraint satisfaction.
098          • Following the analysis in Weed (2018), we prove that the entropically optimal MOT solu-
099            tion is exponentially close to the LP solution.
100          • We show that the approximate Hessian sparsity in Tang et al. (2024a) extends to the
101            martingale-type constraint setting.
102          • We introduce a Sinkhorn-type algorithm and a Sinkhorn-Newton-Sparse algorithm for OT
103            under martingale constraints and super-martingale constraints.

169   perform well in multiple relevance metrics (Dong et al., 2010; Dai et al., 2011; Momma et al., 2019;

175   by considering the doubly stochastic matrix P = l∈[L] pl πl . The entry pij is the probability of
176   assigning product i to position j.
177

181   P is solved, one can recover a stochastic policy (pl , πl )l∈[L] through the Birkhoff algorithm with

205
      While it might be conceptually appealing to enforce the O(n) equality constraints in equation 1, this
206
      formulation faces significant feasibility and robustness concerns. For feasibility, there is a simple
207
      observation supporting the claim: Summing over the martingale conditionPin equation P        3 for all
208   i ∈ [n] shows that µ̂, ν̂ must coincide in their respective barycenter, i.e., i ri wi =        j cj vj ,
209
      which is quite strict and one can see any perturbation might change a feasible LP scheme into
210   an infeasible one. The criteria for feasibility is more complicated than coinciding barycenter, and
211   practical post-processing of the discretization to maintain feasibility is an open problem in general.
212   In addition, exact constraint satisfaction in MOT faces robustness concerns even when the problem
213   is feasible. The construction in Brückerhoff & Juillet (2022) shows that MOT problems with exact
214   constraint satisfaction are unstable: when d ≥ 2, the optimal cost from equation 1 might fail to
215   converge even when (µ̂, ν̂) → (µ, ν). Thus, entropic LP algorithms based on equation 1 are prone
      to feasibility and stability issues coming from discretization errors in general.

347   4.3   S PARSE N EWTON ALGORITHM
348
349   For enhanced accuracy, we augment Algorithm 1 with an efficient Newton’s method which optimizes
350
      over dual variables jointly. Sparse Newton iteration performs Newton’s method with the Hessian
      matrix replaced by its sparsification, which is a type of quasi-Newton method (Nocedal & Wright,
351
      1999; Tang et al., 2024b).
352
353   The sparse Newton iteration is motivated by an approximate sparsity analysis of the Hessian matrix
354   of dual potential, which shows that the Hessian matrix admits accurate sparse approximation. We
355   define important concepts for subsequent approximate sparsity analysis. Let ∥·∥0 denote the l0
356   norm. The sparsity of a matrix M ∈ Rm×n is defined by τ (M ) := ∥M           ∥0

360   Approximate sparsity of Hessian Let P be the intermediate transport plan formed by the current
361   dual variable. We show that the approximate sparsity of the Hessian matrix ∇2 f reduces to that
362   of P . By previous discussion, the blocks ∇2y f, ∇2g f are sparse for g = (x, A, B, u). Thus, we
363   only focus on the block ∇y ∇g f . The block ∇y ∇u f is negligible as it contributes only n non-zero

      which shows that one can obtain sparse approximation of ∇2 f by sparse approximation of P .

368   We now discuss the approximate sparsity of the transport plan P . To reach the super-exponential
369   convergence stage, both Newton’s method and quasi-Newton methods rely on the current dual vari-
370   able to be close to the maximizer. Therefore, the sparse Newton iteration is only used when P ≈ Pη⋆ ,
371
      where Pη⋆ is the entropically optimal transport plan in equation 6. Therefore, the applicability of
      sparse Newton iteration to aid super-exponential convergence relies on the approximate sparsity of
372
      Pη⋆ . By the fundamental theorem of linear programming, a unique solution to equation 5 must be a
373
      basic solution (Luenberger et al., 1984). Then, assuming uniqueness, the optimal coupling matrix
374   P ⋆ for equation 4 can have 2n − 1 + nd nonzero entries. Thus under Theorem 1, Pη⋆ is O(λ, δ)-
375   sparse, where λ = O(1/n) and δ is exponentially small in η.

      needs to keep an O(1/n) fraction of entries in the Hessian matrix for an accurate Hessian approx-
