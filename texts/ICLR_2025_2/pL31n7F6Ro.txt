                                                                A BSTRACT
036
037           3D affordance segmentation aims to link human instructions to touchable regions
038
              of 3D objects for embodied manipulations. Existing efforts typically adhere to
              single-object, single-affordance paradigms, where each affordance type or explicit
039
              instruction strictly corresponds to a specific affordance region and are unable to
040
              handle long-horizon tasks. Such a paradigm cannot actively reason about com-
041           plex user intentions that often imply sequential affordances with multiple objects
042           involved. In this paper, we introduce the Sequential 3D Affordance Reasoning
043           task, which extends the traditional paradigm by reasoning from cumbersome user
044           intentions and then decomposing them into a sequence of segmentations. Toward
045           this, we construct the first instruction-based affordance segmentation benchmark
046           that includes reasoning over both single and sequential affordances, comprising
047           180K instruction-point cloud pairs. Based on the benchmark, we propose our
048           model, SeqAfford, to unlock the 3D multi-modal large language model with ad-
049
              ditional affordance segmentation abilities, which ensures reasoning with world
              knowledge and fine-grained affordance grounding in a cohesive framework. We
050
              further introduce a multi-granular language-point integration module to endow
051
              3D dense prediction. Extensive experimental evaluations show that our model
052           excels over well-established methods and exhibits open-world generalization with
053           sequential reasoning abilities.

      1    I NTRODUCTION
055
056
057   Affordance is a crucial lens through which humans and embodied agents interact with various objects
058   of the world. When provided with human instructions, affordance aims to highlight the actionable
059
      possibilities of these objects, linking visual perception with manipulation. While 2D affordance offers
      visual cues that suggest potential actions to embodied systems, 3D affordance provides a more direct
060
      and intuitive guidance for executing tasks in the realistic 3D world, and thus solidify the foundation
061
      for downstream robot manipulation tasks.
062
063   Pioneering works on 3D affordance have primarily focused on the single-object, single-affordance
064   paradigm, where one afforance map can be grounded from either an affordance category Deng et al.
065
      (2021); Nguyen et al. (2023) or a 2D demonstration image Yang et al. (2023). More recently, language
      models have been employed to pair 3D objects with natural language questions Li et al. (2024), each
066
      designed to probe a distinct affordance part. Unfortunately, current systems are incapable of actively
067
      reasoning based on complex user intentions, breaking them down into actionable primitives, and
068   formulating a chain of affordances derived from each primitive. To further enable real-world physical
069   interactions, the inherent complexity of human instructions can no longer be neglected, which often
070   involves handling multiple objects and navigating through sequential affordances. For example,
071   when agents are required to “use the microwave to reheat the food inside the bowl", they must first
072   grasp the bottle, then open the microwave before using it to contain the bottle, as shown in Figure 1.
073   From this perspective, the multi-object sequential reasoning ability is indispensable for shaping the
074   next-generation affordance systems.
075   Recently, Large-Language Models (LLMs) Alayrac et al. (2022); Zhu et al. (2023); Ouyang et al.
076   (2022) have demonstrated exceptional sequential reasoning abilities, ingrained with internalized
077   common-sense knowledge encoded from vast text data corpora. On top of that, the emergence of
078   3D Multimodal Large-Language Models (MLLMs) Qi et al. (2024); Xu et al. (2023) have further
079   expanded their possibilities in understanding various object shapes in 3D world. However, even
080   current 3D MLLMs are not panaceas for reasoning about visual affordances from 3D objects, as
081   they primarily focus on object-centered text generation tasks. This, therefore, highlights a pressing
082
      question: Can we devise a 3D multi-modal large language model to sequentially reason and segment
      multi-object affordances based on long-horizon human instructions?
083
084   In this paper, we introduce a new task called Sequential 3D Affordance Reasoning, designed to
085   narrow the gap with real-world demands. Towards this, we construct a large-scale sequential
086   affordance reasoning benchmark, containing 180K instruction-point cloud pairs. To ensure diversity
087   of instruction data, the instructions are generated in four different ways. Supported by this benchmark,
088
      we introduce our model SeqAfford that unlocks the current 3D MLLMs with sequential affordance
      segmentation abilities. To further bolster the 3D dense prediction and reasoning task, we introduce
089
      a multi-granular language-point integration module, where dense point features conditioned on
090
      segmentation tokens of the large language model are integrated with sparse point features for
091   subsequent dense prediction tasks. This module not only effectively injects the reasoning results of
092   large language models into the dense point features, but enhances the affordance segmentation task
093   with multi-granular levels of representation.
094
      To summarize, our contributions are as follows:
095
096
097
            • We introduce the Sequential 3D Affordance Reasoning task, which involves the sequential
              reasoning and segmentation of affordances based on complex human instructions. This
098
              paradigm is crucial for the development of next-generation affordance systems.
099
100
            • We develop a large-scale sequential affordance reasoning benchmark with 180K instruction-
101           point cloud pairs, serving as a comprehensive resource to advance research in affordance
102           reasoning.
103
104         • We are the first to propose a novel approach for 3D affordance segmentation using multi-
105           modal large language models, leveraging the common-sense knowledge acquired during
106           pre-training to enhance affordance reasoning. Our model achieves state-of-the-art per-
107           formance across various settings and demonstrates robust generalization capabilities to
              open-world scenarios.

      2    R ELATED W ORK
109
110
      3D Affordance Segmentation. With the rapid advancement of embodied AI, research on 3D
111
      affordance has increasingly garnered significant attention from both academia and industry. 3D
112
      AffordanceNet (Deng et al., 2021), built on point cloud data from PartNet (Mo et al., 2019), was the
113   first to construct a fine-grained 3D affordance dataset, establishing a benchmark for 3D affordance
114   research. Building on this, Yang et al. (2023) proposed leveraging universal knowledge extracted from
115   2D human-object interaction (HOI) images to assist in 3D affordance segmentation. However, these
116   methods rely solely on visual information to infer affordances, without considering that embodied
117   agents in the real world communicate with humans through language. Consequently, such methods
118   are limited in their applicability for direct deployment in embodied agents. Recently, Li et al. (2024)
119   introduced a language-based affordance segmentation task, promoting the integration of natural
120   language and affordance understanding. Following this, Chu & Zhang (2024) utilized LLMs to
121
      locate objects in 2D images and subsequently retrieve corresponding objects from a 3D dataset.
      Although these approaches leverage the reasoning capabilities of LLMs, they lack the ability to
122
      perform joint visual and language alignment, failing to bridge the gap between 2D and 3D modalities.
123
      Furthermore, their research assigns each text statement to a single specific affordance, overlooking
124   the complexity of scenarios that often require the coordination of multiple affordances. In response
125   to these limitations, we propose the integration of 3D multimodal large language models (MLLMs)
126   into affordance segmentation. This novel approach enables the model to simultaneously comprehend
127   contextual semantics and point cloud data, thereby facilitating affordance reasoning across a wide
128   range of complex scenarios. By incorporating both natural language and 3D data, our model offers
129   a more comprehensive and versatile understanding of affordances, rendering it better suited for
130   real-world applications in embodied AI.
131   Multimodal Large Language Models. Large Language Models (LLMs) have achieved remarkable
132   success in processing natural language, and researchers have been working to extend these models’
133   reasoning capabilities into the visual domain. Early efforts such as Flamingo (Alayrac et al., 2022),
134   MiniGPT-4 (Zhu et al., 2023), and LLaVA (Liu et al., 2024a), have made significant strides by
135   enabling LLMs to process both visual and textual information concurrently, thereby introducing
136   an initial level of human-like multimodal reasoning. However, for many practical applications,
137   such as visual segmentation, these models lack the necessary fine-grained perception required for
138
      detailed visual tasks. To address this issue, research efforts such as GPT4RoI (Zhang et al., 2023b),
      VisionLLM (Wang et al., 2024), InternGPT (Liu et al., 2023b), and Ferret (You et al., 2023) enable the
139
      localization of specific regions within images by encoding spatial coordinates as tokens, improving
140
      the models’ ability to reason about precise areas within the visual data. Building on these 2D MLLM
141   advancements, research has increasingly expanded into the 3D domain. Following the paradigm of
142   LLaVA, PointLLM (Xu et al., 2023) replaces the vision encoder with a 3D point encoder, enabling
143   the processing of 3D data within the latent space of LLMs and facilitating 3D object understanding.
144   Similarly, ShapeLLM (Qi et al., 2024) is built upon the enhanced 3D encoder RECON++, which
145   strengthens geometric understanding through multi-view image distillation. Other models, such
146   as 3D-LLM (Hong et al., 2023), leverage 2D foundational models, like CLIP ViT (Radford et al.,
147   2021), to process multi-view rendered images of 3D point clouds, thereby integrating the 3D world
148   into LLMs. However, despite these advancements, the majority of existing MLLMs are primarily
149
      focused on scene-level and object-level understanding, lacking the ability to recognize and segment
      fine-grained affordances of 3D objects in diverse semantic contexts. Addressing this limitation, our
150
      study aims to endow MLLMs with affordance-aware perception, enabling them to interpret and act
151
      upon 3D objects more effectively in context-sensitive scenarios.

517   6    CONCLUSION , L IMITATION , AND F UTURE W ORK
518
519
      In this paper, we have advanced the field of 3D affordance segmentation by introducing the Se-
520
      quential 3D Affordance Reasoning task. Unlike traditional paradigms that focus on single-object,
521
      single-affordance scenarios, our task addresses the complexity of sequential affordances involving
522   multiple objects and nuanced user instructions. To support this new task, we developed an extensive
523   benchmark consisting of 180K instruction-point cloud pairs, laying the foundation for future research
524   in this domain. Our proposed model, SeqAfford, integrates multimodal large language models
525   with additional affordance segmentation capabilities, utilizing world knowledge and fine-grained
526   affordance grounding in a cohesive framework. Moreover, the introduction of a multi-granular
527   language-point integration module has significantly enhanced 3D dense prediction capabilities. Exten-
528   sive experimental evaluations demonstrate that SeqAfford outperforms existing methods, showcasing
529   robust sequential reasoning abilities and remarkable generalization to open-world scenarios.
530   While our work represents a significant advancement in 3D affordance segmentation, there are still
531   some limitations. One primary constraint is the reliance on high-quality 3D point cloud data, which
532   may not always be readily available or easy to obtain in real-world settings. Additionally, our research
533   primarily focuses on object-centric reasoning and lacks scene-level fine-grained reasoning, which is
534   crucial for embodied intelligent agents.
535   For future work, we plan to address these limitations through several approaches. First, we aim to
536   develop methods for augmenting or synthetically generating diverse 3D point cloud data to improve
537   the robustness of our models in varied environments. Additionally, we intend to extend our research
538   to encompass scene-level fine-grained reasoning, enabling more sophisticated and context-aware
539   affordance segmentation that aligns with the complexities faced by embodied intelligent agents in
      real-world scenarios.
