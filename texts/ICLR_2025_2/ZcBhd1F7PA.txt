009                                                A BSTRACT
010
011            Human beings can easily understand object concepts involving attributes and af-
012            fordances. Recently, to simulate this ability, Object Concept Learning (OCL) has
013            been introduced as a new task to recognize attributes and affordances related to
014            a given object. OCL is essentially a many-to-many mapping problem: While an
015            object may possess multiple different concepts, a concept can also belong to mul-
016            tiple different objects. In this regard, the prevailing method of learning discrimi-
017            native representation—which is effective in the single-mapping cases—often fails
018
               in OCL. Inspired by the reasoning mechanism of human beings, in this paper,
               we propose Hierarchical Multi-Grained Reasoning (HGR) for OCL, aiming to in-
019
               fer object-related concepts from coarse-to-fine and counterfactual grains. Specif-
020
               ically, we first propose a coarse-to-fine hierarchical reasoning module that ex-
021            ploits multi-step learnable prompts to progressively localize object-relevant con-
022            cept information. Subsequently, multiple counterfactual samples are selected to
023            strengthen the relations between objects and concepts, which further improves the
024            reasoning performance. In the experiments, our method is evaluated on multiple
025            benchmarks. Significant performance gains and extensive visualization analysis
026            demonstrate the superiorities of our method.

029   1   I NTRODUCTION
030
031   With the development of deep neural networks, many challenging tasks, e.g., object classifica-
032   tion (Krizhevsky et al., 2012), detection (Ren et al., 2015), and segmentation (Huynh et al., 2021),
033
      have achieved many progresses. Most existing methods (Hameed & Khalaf, 2024; Gonthina &
      Prasad, 2024) often leverage the specific neural network to extract discriminative representation and
034
      construct accurate mappings between representations and corresponding categories, which is eas-
035
      ily affected by environment variances. Instead, human beings could accurately understand object
036   concepts involving attributes and affordances, which improves the performance and robustness of
037   identifying objects. To imitate this ability, a task of object concept learning (Li et al., 2023b) is re-
038   cently proposed, whose goal is to recognize the attributes and affordances related to a given object.
039   Addressing this task is beneficial for promoting the development of embodied AI.
040
      Towards this task, one straightforward solution is to follow traditional object classification to mine
041
      discriminative representations corresponding to object-related attributes and affordances, which fur-
042   ther construct a one-to-one mapping between objects and attributes (or affordances). For exam-
043   ple, the work Li et al. (2023b) designs a specific debiasing mechanism for learning discriminative
044   object-agnostic attribute representations. However, in practice, an object could have multiple differ-
045   ent attributes and affordances. Meanwhile, an attribute and affordance could also belong to multiple
046   different objects. Taking Fig. 1 (a) as an example, a cake includes ‘round’, ‘fresh’, etc. And Pizza
047   and Bowl all contain the ‘round’ attribute. Thus, for OCL, how to construct an accurate many-to-
048   many mapping between objects and concepts is a critical challenge.
049   Of course, enhancing the discrimination of the learned object representation is still beneficial for im-
050   proving the performance of object-to-concept mapping (Luo et al., 2023b; Almahairi et al., 2018).
051   However, since many-to-many mapping is full of much uncertainty, only learning discriminative rep-
052   resentation is easily affected by environment variances, which may weaken its performance and ro-
053   bustness. Therefore, simply learning discriminative representations is not sufficient for this task (Li
      et al., 2023b). However, based on current observations, humans could leverage the reasoning mech-

158   formation to guide the pre-trained model in leveraging its existing knowledge to solve new tasks. The

160   et al., 2021). This approach directly optimizes prompts within the embedding space of the model.

178   3.1     C OARSE - TO -F INE H IERARCHICAL R EASONING
179
180   Compared with concrete object categories, e.g., cake, object concept is much more abstract and
181   involves plentiful information. To deepen the understanding of object concepts, we explore imitating
182   human beings to perform coarse-to-fine reasoning to progressively localize concept-relevant object
183   content, which improves the OCL performance.
184
185   3.1.1    C OARSE - GRAINED P ROMPT G ENERATION
186
      The goal of Coarse-grained Prompt Generation is to create continuous vector representations as
187
      input prompts (see Category-agnostic Prompting) and obtain a more accurate attribute and affor-
188   dance description including global visual contexts (see Contextual Prompting), which is helpful for
189   a thorough understanding of visual content.
190
191
      Category-agnostic Prompting. To adapt the large pre-trained vision-language model to the down-
      stream recognition tasks, a common way is to use text prompt templates in CLIP, like “a photo of a
192
      [cls]”, which primarily focuses on category semantics. Nevertheless, utilizing such hard text prompt
193
      templates presents challenges in generating generic attribute and affordance textual embeddings.
194   This is because the original pretraining of CLIP focused on aligning with categorical semantics
195   rather than high-level attributes and affordances concepts of images. To overcome this limitation,
196   we aim to construct a set of learnable text prompts incorporating the prior knowledge of concepts.
197   Recently works (Hassan & Dharmaratne, 2016; Li et al., 2023b) reveal that the attributes and af-
198   fordances are shared between objects. Consequently, we construct a category-agnostic model and
199   optimize prompts focusing on aligning with attribute and affordance semantics. We employ the
200   prompt tuning (Lester et al., 2021) to construct a set of learnable text prompts h incorporating the
201   knowledge of attributes and affordances as:
202                              hα = [T1 ] [T2 ] . . . [Tn ] [is][attribute]
203                                                                                                      (1)
                                 hβ = [P1 ] [P2 ] . . . [Pn ] [afford to][affordance],
204
205   where [Ti ] and [Pi ](i ∈ 1, . . . , n) are learnable token embeddings in attribute and affordance text
206   prompt templates, respectively. This design ensures the category-agnostic text prompt template to
207   learn the shared patterns of different categories.
208   Contextual Prompting. Since the nearby environment affects the recognition of attributes and af-
209   fordances (Hassan & Dharmaratne, 2016), we devise a contextual prompt tuning approach that uses
210   visual contexts to optimize the prompt features, making the generated textual embeddings capable
211   of aligning visual content. Specifically, given an input image Xi , we extract the visual embedding
212   Fg ∈ Rd from CLIP visual encoder v(·) and feature FM ∈ Rp×d from the M -th intermediate layer
213   of CLIP visual encoder following (Zhou et al., 2023), where p and d separately denote the number
214   of patches and feature dimension. And the input text hα and hβ are sent to the text encoder f (·), ob-
215   taining the text feature embeddings Hα ∈ RNα ×d and Hβ ∈ RNβ ×d . Nα is the number of attributes
      and Nβ is the number of affordances. To encourage the text features to align with related visual

      4.1     DATASETS AND BASELINES .
354
355   We consider three different tasks ranging from object concept learning, multi-task indoor scene un-
356   derstanding and weakly supervised affordance grounding. In object concept learning, we consider

      5   C ONCLUSIONS AND L IMINATIONS
508
509
      For OCL, we explore introducing a reasoning mechanism to strengthen object concept learning.
510   Concretely, we propose a Hierarchical Multi-Grained Reasoning (HGR) method, which consists of
511   coarse-to-fine hierarchical reasoning module and counterfactual relation-enhancing module. Partic-
512   ularly, we first sent the entire image to the Coarse-to-Fine Hierarchical Reasoning module, obtaining
513   the fine-grained prompt containing instance object concepts. Subsequently, multiple counterfactual
514   samples are selected to strengthen the relations between objects and concepts, which further im-
515   proves the reasoning performance. Experiment results show the effectiveness of HGR.
516   Notably, it still has much room for reasoning ability improvement. From the experiments, we found
517   that although the CLIP improved the model’s recognition of attributes and affordances, there is still
518   much room for improvement. It is worth noting that capturing causal relationships between attributes
519   and affordances requires deeper exploration. In the future, we plan to validate and optimize our
520   method in a broader range of application scenes.

741   batch size 128 and SGD learning rate 1 for parameter optimization. For NYUd2 benchmark, the
742   counterfactual reasoning cannot be used since there is no causality annotation. Thus, we add our
743   concept extraction module to the baseline network. The experiments use the standard SGD optimizer

      affordances corresponding to the image labels have been added to the prompt template. And we set

      optimization. In addition, the metrics in OCL also include the SITE and Sα-β-ITE , which combine
747
      the actual affordance probability and counterfactual output following (Li et al., 2023b) to verify the

904   A.6     F URTHER D ISCUSSION
905
906   Although multi-label recognition is not a new problem, it is under explored in the Object Concept
907   Learning (OCL) task. Multi-label recognition mainly focus on the mapping between images and
908   categories. The main challenge of OCL lies in the many-to-many mapping relationships between
909
      objects and concepts. That is, an object could have multiple different attributes and affordances
      (concepts). Meanwhile, an attribute and affordance could also belong to multiple different objects.
910
      To overcome this, we propose a Hierarchical Multi-Grained Reasoning framework. For objects in an
911
      image, an attribute and affordance could belong to multiple different objects, which means concepts
912   exist across objects. Thus, we first design object-agnostic prompts to enhance concepts-objects
913   mapping precision. Subsequently, a mapping between object concepts is formed by integrating
914   contextual global information and fine-grained local information. Furthermore, to make the mapping
915   between objects and concepts more precise, we introduce counterfactual reasoning to identify causal
916   relationships between certain attributes and affordances. This enhances the mapping connections
917   between objects and these concepts, which in turn improves recognition performance. Significant
      performance improvements and extensive visual analysis have demonstrated the superiority of HGR.
