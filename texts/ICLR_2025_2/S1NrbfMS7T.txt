011                                               A BSTRACT
012
013            Cooperative perception systems in autonomous driving enhance each agent’s per-
014            ceptual capabilities by sharing visual information with others and demonstrated
015
               effectiveness in handling prominent challenges like occlusions and long-range de-
               tection. However, most existing cooperative systems transmit feature maps, such
016
               as bird’s-eye view (BEV) representations, which include substantial background
017
               data and are costly to process due to their high dimensionality. This paradigm in-
018            troduces a trade-off between improved perception and increased communication
019            overhead. To address this challenge, we present CoCMT, an object-query-based
020            collaboration framework that enables efficient communication while unifying ho-
021            mogeneous and heterogeneous cooperative perception tasks. Within CoCMT, we
022            introduce the Efficient Query Transformer (EQFormer) to effectively fuse multi-
023            agent object queries and implement a synergistic deep supervision approach to
024            accelerate convergence during training. Extensive experiments on the OPV2V
025            and V2V4Real datasets demonstrate that CoCMT surpasses current state-of-the-
026
               art methods in performance while offering significant communication efficiency.
               Notably, on the real-world V2V4Real dataset, our proposed CoCMT model (Top-
027
               50 object queries) requires merely 0.416 Mb bandwidth during inference. This
028
               reduces bandwidth consumption by 323 times compared to SOTA methods while
029            improving AP@70 by 1.1. The code and models will be open-sourced.

032   1   I NTRODUCTION
033
034   Accurate and efficient perception is essential for autonomous driving (AV) to ensure reliable nav-
035   igation and safe decision-making. However, single-vehicle autonomous systems face significant
036   challenges in real-world scenarios, such as occlusions and limited sensing range. Cooperative per-
037   ception systems address these issues by enabling agents to enhance their perceptual capabilities
038   through the sharing of sensing and visual information with other agents. Most research in coopera-
039
      tive perception systems (Xu et al., 2022c;a; Wang et al., 2020; Xu et al., 2022b; Wei et al., 2024) has
      primarily focused on homogeneous multi-agent perception, where all agents utilize the same type
040
      of sensors—such as LiDAR, cameras, or radars. Recent studies (Xiang et al., 2023; Lu et al., 2024)
041
      have advanced into heterogeneous multi-agent perception, facilitating collaboration between agents
042   equipped with diverse sensor types. This approach better reflects real-world conditions, significantly
043   enhancing the adaptability of cooperative perception systems and expanding their potential appli-
044   cations and impact. However, a trade-off exists between communication efficiency and perception
045   performance in cooperative perception systems (Hu et al., 2022): while intermediate fusion methods
046   improve performance, they generally demand significant communication bandwidth, as compared
047   to simpler late fusion approaches whereas only the detection results are shared across agents.
048   Most existing cooperative perception fusion methods Xu et al. (2022c;b); Wei et al. (2024) use fea-
049   ture maps—such as Bird’s-Eye-View (BEV) features—as the medium for information transmission
050   among agents. Feature maps often employ high-dimensional representations to extend perception
051   range and enhance performance; however, this also increases communication bandwidth require-
052   ments. Moreover, feature maps represent the entire scene surrounding the vehicle, where dynamic,
053   relatively sparse foreground objects are mixed with a large amount of static background information.
      Transmitting large amounts of background data offers minimal benefit to perception performance

054
      while occupying significant bandwidth. To this end, existing methods Lu et al. (2024); Xiang et al.
055   (2023) have to incorporate complex foreground extraction mechanisms to reduce the unnecessary
056   information being shared, which inevitably increases model complexity. To further reduce the fea-
057   ture redundancy, Hu et al. (2022; 2024) has focused on selecting key parts of the feature map or
058   adopting alternative representations to balance performance and communication efficiency.
059
      This paper proposes a novel object-centric framework tailored for communication-efficient col-
060
      laborative perception. The sparsity nature of query-based object representations Carion et al.
061   (2020); Li et al. (2022a) has offered several advantages over prior feature map-based strategies:
062   1) Small data size: The data size of object queries is significantly smaller than that of (BEV) fea-
063   ture maps, which can largely reduce the communication bandwidth required for transmission. 2)
064   Object-centric focus: Unlike feature maps, which contain extensive background information, object
065   queries are explicitly object-centric, encapsulating only the relevant contextual features and natu-
066   rally excluding irrelevant background data. This eliminates the need for intermediate fusion algo-
067   rithms to design complex foreground information extraction mechanisms (Hu et al., 2022; 2024). 3)
068   Modality independence: Object queries are less dependent on specific data modalities, making them
069   more versatile and effective for heterogeneous multi-agent perception tasks. These advantages make
070   object queries a more efficient and scalable choice for cooperative perception systems, especially in
071
      bandwidth-constrained and multi-modal environments.
072   However, integrating object queries from multiple agents introduces two challenges. Firstly, object
073   query-based models generate numerous initial queries, many of which are unrelated to actual objects.
074   The challenge lies in efficiently filtering out noisy queries to merely focus on high-quality object
075   queries for fusion. Moreover, object queries are unordered, meaning adjacent queries in the sequence
076   may represent distant objects, especially when integrated from multiple agents. This unordered na-
077
      ture can cause feature confusion, complicating the interaction between relevant objects. To address
      these challenges, we introduce the CoCMT framework—Communication-Efficient Cross-Modal
078
      Transformer for Collaborative Perception. This framework utilizes object query as the medium for
079
      information transmission, effectively handling both homogeneous and heterogeneous multi-agent
080   perception tasks using a unified and concise architecture. The framework is divided into two stages:
081   the single-agent independent prediction stage and the cooperative fusion prediction stage. Addi-
082   tionally, we propose a synergistic deep supervision mechanism that applies deep supervision across
083   both stages simultaneously, accelerating convergence and enhancing positive interactions between
084   stages to improve overall performances. Extensive experiments on both simulated and real datasets
085   demonstrate that our model achieves superior performance compared to State-of-the-art methods
086   while requiring order-of-magnitude smaller communication bandwidth. Our contributions are:
087   • We propose CoCMT, a novel object query-based collaborative perception framework that uses
088      object queries as intermediaries for information transmission, significantly reducing bandwidth
089      consumption while enhancing the efficiency of collaborative perception.
090   • We design the Efficient Query Transformer (EQFormer), which incorporates three masking mech-
091      anisms to limit interactions between object queries to spatially valid, proximate, and strongly
092      target-associated areas, ensuring precise and efficient attention learning for fusion.
093   • We introduce a Synergistic Deep Supervision mechanism that applies deep supervision at both the
094      individual prediction and collaborative fusion stages. This mechanism accelerates model conver-
095      gence during training and improves overall performance.
096   • Our extensive experiments on the OPV2V and V2V4real datasets validate the bandwidth effi-
097      ciency of our proposed framework. The results demonstrate that the framework significantly re-
098      duces bandwidth consumption while achieving superior performance. We also conducted com-
099      prehensive ablation studies to demonstrate the efficacy of each component in our model design.

      2.2   C HALLENGES IN C OOPERATIVE P ERCEPTION
136   Despite their advantages, cooperative perception systems introduce several challenges, such as het-
137   erogeneous feature fusion, domain gaps, communication delays, and limited communication band-
138   width, to name a few. Many studies have focused on enhancing the robustness of multi-agent co-
139   operative perception systems to maintain perception performance (Xu et al., 2022b; Xiang et al.,
140   2023; Hu et al., 2024; Wei et al., 2024; Xu et al., 2023a; Li et al., 2023; 2024a;b). For instance,
141   CoBEVFlow (Wei et al., 2024) enhances robustness to asynchronous communication by compen-
142
      sating for motion through BEV Flow. To handle broader heterogeneous multi-agent perception in
      real-world scenarios, HMViT (Xiang et al., 2023) integrates heterogeneous sensor features from
143
      connected vehicles using a heterogeneous 3D graph transformer. HEAL (Lu et al., 2024) utilizes
144
      a PyramidFusion architecture to fuse heterogeneous features in a multi-scale and foreground-aware
145   manner, and reduces the training cost for adding new heterogeneous agents through backward align-
146   ment. S2R-ViT (Li et al., 2024d) introduces sim-to-real transfer learning to minimize the sim2real
147   domain gap in collaborative perception systems.
148
      To reduce communication bandwidth, Where2comm (Hu et al., 2022) adopts a spatial confidence-
149
      aware strategy to transmit only the most critical feature information. CodeFilling (Hu et al.,
150
      2024) approximates feature maps using codebook-based representations and selects key informa-
151   tion through information filling, achieving an optimal balance between communication and perfor-
152   mance. QUEST (Fan et al., 2024) explores the use of object query as the information carrier in V2X
153   scenarios, reducing communication bandwidth. However, these studies are limited to camera only
154   homogeneous perception, and its performance degrades considerably when reducing the threshold
155   of transmitted queries. In this paper, we extend the object query-based approach to simultaneously
156   handle homogeneous and heterogeneous multi-agent perception tasks involving both LiDAR and
157   cameras, aiming to achieve better communication-performance trade-offs.

198   3     C O CMT C OOPERATIVE P ERCEPTION F RAMEWORK
199
200
      We present CoCMT, illustrated in Figure 2, divided into two stages: the 1) single-agent prediction
201   stage and the 2) cooperative fusion prediction stage. We adopt the standard query-based learning
202   objective to train the single-agent perception. In the cooperative fusion prediction stage, we pro-
203   pose the Efficient Query Transformer (EQFormer) to restrict the interaction between object queries,
204   achieved by applying several layers of attention masks. To accelerate the convergence of the frame-
205   work and enhance the mutual reinforcement between the two stages, we propose a synergistic deep
206   supervision mechanism that provides deep supervision for both stages simultaneously.

209
210   In the first stage, we employ a query-based 3D object detection model to extract object queries,
211   denoted as Qi ∈ RN ×D , where Qi represents the set of object queries extracted from agent i.
212   Each agent generates N queries with D-dimensional features. We select Qi as the core intermedi-
213   ate features in the cooperative fusion prediction stage. Notably, unlike most cooperative perception
214   models that rely solely on the backbone for feature extraction, our approach retains the task heads
215   of the model at this stage. This retention allows us to incorporate additional predictive informa-
      tion—specifically, the 3D object centers Ci ∈ RN ×3 and object class scores Si ∈ RN ×C —into the

      3.2   C OOPERATIVE F USION P REDICTION S TAGE
221
      Information Selection and Sharing. Most query-based 3D object detection models initialize a large
222
      set of object queries to improve query coverage and accelerate model training Liu et al. (2022); Li
223   et al. (2022a); Yan et al. (2023). However, during training, only a small portion of the object queries
224   maintain strong associations with actual target objects, while the majority are predicted as back-
225   ground. These background object queries do not contribute significantly to detection performance
226   yet consume substantial transmission bandwidth when shared among agents. To address this issue,
227   we apply a Top-k strategy to the object queries Qi based on the object classification scores Si output
228   from the previous stage. To balance effective fusion with reduced communication costs, we set k
229   to the maximum number of detectable objects by the connected and automated vehicles (CAVs).
230   After filtering, each CAV shares object queries Qi , object centers Ci , and object class scores Si .
231
      Additionally, the LiDAR poses of the CAVs are shared for subsequent spatial alignment.

233   vehicle, their object queries exhibit significant spatial discrepancies. To solve this issue, we apply
234   the Motion-aware Layer Normalization (MLN) (Wang et al., 2023) to spatially align object queries.

405
      we train the model in an end-to-end manner, transmitting all 900 object queries.

411
      for information exchange and employ LSS (Philion & Fidler, 2020) to construct BEV features for

      the camera and LiDAR branches, respectively.

513   5   C ONCLUSION
514
515   In this paper, we introduce the CoCMT framework to address the challenges of collaborative per-
516   ception in both homogeneous and heterogeneous multi-agent environments. By utilizing object
517
      queries as the medium for information transmission, the framework significantly reduces bandwidth
      consumption while enhancing the efficiency of collaborative perception. The Efficient Query Trans-
518
      former (EQFormer) is designed with three masking mechanisms to precisely regulate interactions
519
      between object queries, ensuring focused and efficient fusion. Additionally, the Synergistic Deep
520   Supervision mechanism applies deep supervision across both stages, accelerating model training.
521   Extensive experiments on both simulated and real-world datasets validate the bandwidth efficiency
522   of our proposed CoCMT framework, demonstrating its capability to achieve superior performance
523   compared to state-of-the-art methods with orders-of-magnitude bandwidth savings. We hope our
524   work will facilitate resource-constraint, communication-efficient collaborative perception frame-
525   works towards safer, more robust mobility systems.
526
      Limitations. The single-agent model in our framework uses a DETR-based architecture. Com-
527   pared to anchor-based models, DETR-based models converge slowly and require higher training
528   costs. We could consider using the query denoising methods mentioned in (Li et al., 2022a; Zhang
529   et al., 2022; Wang et al., 2023) to accelerate the model training. Additionally, our model is also
530   suitable for multi-modal cooperative perception, where each agent simultaneously uses both LiDAR
531   and camera sensors. In our future work, we plan to explore this capability further using real-world
532   multimodal cooperative perception datasets.
533
      Broader Impact. Our proposed CoCMT framework has the potential to significantly advance the
534   field of autonomous driving by improving the efficiency and scalability of cooperative perception
535   systems. However, the deployment of such systems also raises important considerations. First,
536   sharing information among vehicles involves transmitting sensitive information. To protect against
537   potential cyber-attacks or data breaches, robust encryption, and secure communication protocols
538   must be implemented. Furthermore, the increased reliance on automated systems may impact em-
539   ployment in the transportation sector and raise questions about accountability in the event of system
      failures. In the future, researchers and engineers should handle these challenges responsibly.
