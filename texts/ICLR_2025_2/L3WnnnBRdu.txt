011                                              A BSTRACT
012
013            Single-view 3D reconstruction is a fundamental problem in computer vision, hav-
014            ing a significant impact on downstream tasks such as Autonomous Driving, Vir-
015            tual Reality and Augment Reality. However, existing single-view reconstruction
016            methods are unable to reconstruct the regions outside the input field-of-view or
017            the areas occluded by visible parts. In this paper, we propose Hi-Gaussian, which
018
               employs feed-forward 3D Gaussians for efficient and generalizable single-view
               3D reconstruction. A Normalized Spherical Projection module is introduced fol-
019
               lowing an Encoder-Decoder network in our model, assigning a larger range to
020
               the transformed spherical coordinates, which can enlarge the field of view during
021            scene reconstruction. Besides, to reconstruct occluded regions behind the visi-
022            ble part, we introduce a novel Hierarchical Gaussian Sampling strategy, utilizing
023            two layers of Gaussians to hierarchically represent 3D scenes. We first use a
024            pre-trained monocular depth estimation model to provide depth initialization for
025            leader Gaussians, and then leverage the leader Gaussians to estimate the distribu-
026            tion followed by follower Gaussians. Extensive experiments show that our method
027            outperforms other methods for scene reconstruction and novel view synthesis, on
028            both outdoor and indoor datasets.

031   1   I NTRODUCTION
032
033   Living in a complex 3D environment, humans need to comprehend their surroundings to make even
034   the slightest movements. Physiological studies show that humans can perceive depth even with
035   monocular vision (Koenderink et al., 1995). Although binocular vision is more mature currently, it
036   tends to degrade into monocular vision when sensing distant objects in autonomous driving scenar-
037   ios. Moreover, in Virtual Reality and Augment Reality tasks, there is an expectation to reconstruct
038   a scene from one single image captured by devices such as smartphones, which also makes single-
039
      view 3D reconstruction critically crucial. Unfortunately, reconstructing the 3D structure from a 2D
      image is ill-posed due to the lack of explicit geometric cues such as epipolar geometry, which are
040
      only available with two or more views. Therefore, single-view 3D reconstruction is an important
041
      but challenging issue in computer vision.
042
043   Single-view 3D reconstruction attracts more attention recently, with the development of differen-
044   tiable rendering or neural rendering. PixelNeRF (Yu et al., 2021) introduces Neural Radiance Field
045
      into single-view reconstruction. It encodes a 2D image to obtain a feature volume, and finally
      decodes the feature volume into a radiance field using an MLP decoder. MINE (Li et al., 2021)
046
      uses multi-plane images to represent scenes, composed of RGB-α images at continuous depths,
047
      which enhances the scene representation capabilities. VisionNeRF (Lin et al., 2023) incorporates
048   Transformer blocks into its encoder to better extract global features, which are combined with local
049   features to improve the quality of reconstruction. Splatter Image (Szymanowicz et al., 2024b) in-
050   troduces Gaussian Splatting into single-view reconstruction, addressing the challenge of the above
051   methods that require costly point sampling and querying during novel view synthesis. It utilizes a
052   U-Net to encode the input image into feature maps which is similar to the previous works, and then
053   decodes the feature maps into attributes of Gaussians instead of implicit radiance fields, obtaining a
      scene represented by 3D Gaussians.

      However, these methods only obtain features within the field-of-view (FOV) of the input view
069   through their image encoders, without additional focus on regions outside the input view. This
070   limits the ability of these methods to reconstruct areas beyond the input FOV, resulting in noticeable
071   blurriness or holes when there is a significant difference between the rendering view and the input
072   view. Another problem is their inability to reconstruct occluded regions. For example, as a typical
073   3DGS-based method, Splatter Image predicts only one Gaussian for each pixel, causing the Gaus-
074   sians to cluster near visible surfaces, which limits the reconstruction of occluded regions. These
075   drawbacks result in poor performances of the above methods when reconstructing complex scenes.
076   To address the aforementioned issues, we propose a simple and novel single-view reconstruction
077   method named Hi-Gaussian. Hi-Gaussian requires only one image as input, and directly recon-
078   structs a 3D scene in a feed-forward manner, as shown in Fig. 1. Our method is based on two key
079   ideas. First, we design a Normalized Spherical Projection module, which is aimed at reconstructing
080   scenes beyond the input FOV. After our Encoder-Decoder outputs feature maps, we project every
081   2D pixel to its normalized latitude-longitude spherical coordinates and uniformly resample the fea-
082   ture maps based on the spherical coordinates, which induces less distortion. Meanwhile, we expand
083
      the FOV of the feature maps by assigning a larger range of values to the spherical coordinates, al-
      lowing 3D Gaussians to cover a larger area. Second, we introduce a novel Hierarchical Gaussian
084
      Sampling strategy, which helps to reconstruct the regions occluded by the visible parts. Specifically,
085
      we estimate leader Gaussians through our pretrained depth network and Encoder-Decoder network,
086   and then predict follower Gaussians based on the leader Gaussians. We assume that these follower
087   Gaussians follow a normal distribution, whose parameters vary across leader Gaussians. We esti-
088   mate the mean and covariance of the normal distribution and sample them from this distribution.
089   Our main contributions are summarized as follows:
090
091           • We propose a Normalized Spherical Projection module to expand the input FOV, enabling
092             reconstruction beyond the image FOV while inducing less projection distortion.
093           • We propose a Hierarchical Gaussian Sampling strategy to reconstruct occluded areas in
094             images, enhancing reconstruction fidelity and improving performance on novel view syn-
095             thesis.
096           • Experimental results show that the proposed method achieves state-of-the-art reconstruc-
097             tion accuracy on both outdoor and indoor datasets.

      2.1    I MPLICIT 3D S CENE R EPRESENTATION
102
103   One of the most representatives of implicit 3D scene representation is Neural Radiance Field
104   (NeRF) (Mildenhall et al., 2021; Müller et al., 2022), which has seen widespread development
105   in recent years due to its end-to-end differentiable nature and simple structure. NeRF utilizes
106   a coordinate-based neural network to represent the scene, and typically start training from im-
107   ages with poses while employing differentiable volume rendering to back-propagate photometric
      loss. Many following works have made improvements to NeRF in various aspects. For exam-


                                                        2
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


108
      ple, NeRF++ (Zhang et al., 2020), MipNeRF (Barron et al., 2021) and MipNeRF-360 (Barron
109   et al., 2022) introduce advanced point sampling techniques, improving the rendering quality. Pix-
110   elNeRF (Yu et al., 2021), MVSNeRF (Chen et al., 2021), SinNeRF (Xu et al., 2022) and SparseN-
111   eRF (Wang et al., 2023) enable NeRF to perform sparse-view 3D reconstruction while Vision-
112   NeRF (Lin et al., 2023) and SceneRF (Cao & de Charette, 2023) extend NeRF to single input view.
113   However, due to implicit nature of Neural Radiance Field in storing scene information through
114   MLPs, explicit scenes cannot be directly obtained. Additionally, the volume rendering process in-
115   volves sampling and querying multiple points along rays, leading to a very slow rendering speed.
116   Therefore, we do not choose implicit Neural Radiance Field as the scene representation but use an
117
      explicit representation instead.
118
119   2.2    E XPLICIT 3D S CENE R EPRESENTATION
120
      Explicit representation has been studied for decades due to its simplicity and intuitiveness. Many
121
      works explicit 3D scene representations including point clouds (Fan et al., 2017; Yang et al., 2019;
122   Wiles et al., 2020; Vahdat et al., 2022), meshes (Kanazawa et al., 2018; Wang et al., 2018; Gao
123   et al., 2022), and voxels (Choy et al., 2016; Girdhar et al., 2016; Yagubbayli et al., 2021). However,
124   these representations are not smooth enough, which may affect the quality of novel view synthesis.
125   The recently emerged method of 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) (Kerbl et al., 2023) has become
126   another mainstream approach for 3D reconstruction due to its continuous 3D representation and
127   rapid differentiable rasterization. Among 3DGS-based methods, pixelSplat (Charatan et al., 2024),
128   latentSplat (Wewer et al., 2024), MVSplat (Chen et al., 2024), and FreeSplat (Wang et al., 2024)
129   extend 3DGS to sparse-view reconstruction. Whereas TriplaneGaussian (Zou et al., 2024), Splatter
130
      Image (Szymanowicz et al., 2024b), and AGG (Xu et al., 2024) further expand 3DGS to single-
      view object reconstruction. Our method also introduces 3D Gaussian Splatting into single-view
131
      reconstruction, primarily for scene reconstruction rather than object reconstruction, which is a much
132
      harder problem due to the complexity, diversity, and large scale of the scene.
133
134
      2.3    S INGLE V IEW 3D R ECONSTRUCTION AND N OVEL V IEW S YNTHESIS
135
136   Single-view 3D reconstruction involves feeding a single image into a neural network in a feed-
137   forward manner to directly output a 3D scene. Among single-view 3D scene reconstruction meth-
138   ods, SynSin (Wiles et al., 2020) utilizes point clouds to represent scenes, requiring a GAN gener-
139   ator (Goodfellow et al., 2020) for image synthesis after projecting the point clouds onto the image
140   plane, while MINE (Li et al., 2021) and MPI (Tucker & Snavely, 2020) use multi-plane images
141   to represent scenes. In contrast, SRN (Sitzmann et al., 2019), pixelNeRF (Yu et al., 2021) and
142   SceneRF (Cao & de Charette, 2023) use Neural Radiance Fields for scene representation. They
143
      both construct pixel-aligned feature maps using an image encoder, then get point-wise features by
      projecting 3D points onto the feature maps, and finally decode them using an MLP to obtain colors
144
      and opacities. These NeRF-based methods exhibit slow inference speeds due to volume rendering.
145
      Following the emergence of 3DGS (Kerbl et al., 2023), Splatter Image (Szymanowicz et al., 2024b)
146   is designed to predict 3D Gaussians for reconstruction. However, Splatter Image estimates per-pixel
147   aligned 3D Gaussians, limiting its capability to estimate only the surfaces of visible objects in the
148   image, rather than unseen parts. To address this issue, we first design a Normalized Spherical Projec-
149   tion module to reconstruct the areas outside the input field-of-view, and then propose a Hierarchical
150   Gaussian Sampling strategy to reconstruct the occluded regions.

233

260   estimation network fΦ and estimate other attributes (∆µp , αp , Sp , Rp , cp ) through an encoder-
261   decoder network fΨ , where ∆µp denotes the position offset of each leader Gaussian. ∆µp is
262   expected to serve a dual purpose: accurately predicting positions of leader Gaussians for the visible
263   regions and potentially allowing some leader Gaussians to move into occluded parts. However, our
264   experiments show that only predicting ∆µp is not enough for reconstruction of the occluded re-
265   gions. When only one Gaussian is estimated for each pixel, these Gaussians tend to aggregate near
266
      the surface of the visible parts. This phenomenon ensures geometrical fidelity of the visible parts but
      neglects the invisible parts, leading to compromised rendering quality under significant viewpoint
267
      differences, as shown in Fig. 4 and 5.

361   is better), Structural Similarity Index Measure (SSIM, higher is better) and the Learned Perceptual
362   Image Patch Similarity (LPIPS, lower is better) as evaluation metrics.
363
364   Implementation Details. The pre-trained monocular depth estimation network fϕ in our model is
365   from UniDepth (Piccinelli et al., 2024). Our Encoder-Decoder network is composed of a UNet (Song
366   et al., 2020) and a 1 × 1 convolutional layer. In Normalized Spherical Projection module, we enlarge
367
      the input FOV by 0.25×. For Hierarchical Gaussian Sampling, M is set to 3, which is adequate
      for scene representation. Our method is trained by minimizing the loss function of Eqn. 10 and
368
      set λ1 = λ2 = λ4 = 1 and λ3 = 0.05. We train our method and all compared methods using
369
      AdamW (Loshchilov, 2017) optimizer on 4 Tesla V100 32G with learning rate of 1e-5 for 50 epochs.

      5   C ONCLUSION
527
528
      In this paper, we introduce Hi-Gaussian, a feed-forward model that performs 3D reconstruction with
      high fidelity from a single image. The proposed Normalized Spherical Projection module projects
529
      2D pixels to spherical coordinates with a larger range of values, aiding in the reconstruction of the
530
      scenes beyond the input FOV. And the proposed Hierarchical Gaussian Sampling strategy initially
531   predicts leader Gaussians and then predicts follower Gaussians based on leader Gaussians, helping
532   to reconstruct the occluded regions behind the visible parts. Both the two components enhance the
533   quality of reconstruction and novel view synthesis. In two single-view 3D reconstruction tasks,
534   which are outdoor SemanticKITTI and indoor BundleFusion respectively, our proposed approach
535   achieves state-of-the-art performances.
