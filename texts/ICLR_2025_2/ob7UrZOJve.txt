009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            Large Language Models (LLMs) have achieved remarkable performance across
012            various natural language processing tasks, primarily due to the transformer archi-
013            tecture and its self-attention mechanism. However, we observe that in standard
014            decoder-style LLMs attention matrices degenerate to single-column for deeper lay-
015            ers. Layers in this state unable to learn anything meaningful and mostly redundant;
016            we refer to these as lazy layers. The goal of this paper is to train smaller models by
017            eliminating this structural inefficiency without compromising performance.
018            Motivated by this observation, we propose Inheritune, a simple yet effective
019            training recipe for developing smaller, high-performing language models. Smaller
020            models trained with Inheritune inherits early transformer layers from a larger pre-
021
               trained model, then retrains and progressively expands the smaller model until it
               matches or exceeds the performance of the larger model. We demonstrate that
022
               Inheritune enables the training of various sizes of GPT-2 models on datasets like
023
               OpenWebText-9B and FineWeb_Edu. Models trained with Inheritune, despite
024            having significantly fewer layers, match or even surpass the performance of their
025            larger counterparts. For instance, our 16-layer GPT-2 medium variant achieves
026            comparable performance to the standard 24-layer GPT-2 medium model.

029   1   I NTRODUCTION
030
031   Large Language Models (LLMs) are built with decoder-style transformer blocks (Vaswani et al.,
032   2017). These models are typically designed to be large, with a significant portion of their parameters
033   dedicated to their depth, with multiple transformer blocks stacked with eachother building model
034   capacity. Each block or layer in the stack refines the representations learned by the previous blocks,
035   allowing the model to develop a nuanced understanding of the input data. As these models scale
036   in depth and size, their performance tends to improve Kaplan et al. (2020); Hoffmann et al. (2022),
037   benefiting from increased model capacity.
038   The causal self-attention (hereafter referred to as attention) mechanism is arguably the most crucial
039   component of a transformer block. It allows models to combine tokens as a weighted linear sum of
040   their attention scores, effectively capturing long-range dependencies and contextual relationships
041   within text data. However, as models grow in depth, they often encounter a phenomenon known as
042   attention degeneration caused by collapse in the attention rank ((Noci et al., 2022; Dong et al., 2021;
043   He et al., 2023)). Notably, this phenomenon has not been studied in the context of standard LLMs. A
044
      formal discussion on attention degeneration is provided in Section 2.
045   In this paper, we empirically analyze 24-layer GPT-2 medium and 36-layer GPT-2 large models
046   (decoder-style LLMs) Radford et al. (2019) for attention degeneration and observe that many deeper
047   layers in both models exhibit rank-1 attention matrices. Further investigation reveals that most of
048   these rank-1 matrices are also single-column, i.e. their mass is concentrated to a single column. Our
049   attention matrix analysis is presented in Figure 1. We term these deeper layers, where all attention
050
      matrices of a given layer are degenerated, as lazy layers.
051   Motivated by this new finding we aim to develop performant small base language models (LMs)
052   utilizing weights from in-efficient larger base LMs. A base LM is a decoder-style model trained solely
053   for next-token prediction without additional enhancements like instruction tuning or reinforcement
      learning with human feedback (RLHF). Our proposal is straightforward, we start by initializing our

161   phenomenon, known as rank collapse of attention matrices, results in a loss of expressive power as
      the attention mechanism attends to all tokens uniformly. Noci et al. (2022) showed that even with

      3     I NHERITUNE : O UR P ROPOSED T RAINING R ECIPE
250
      This section offers a detailed description of our method, key implementation considerations, and how
251
      it addresses the inefficiencies present in current architectures.
252
253   Recall we have established the issue of attention degeneration with two motivating examples, high-
254   lighting specific inefficiencies in pre-trained LLMs. In this paper, we turn this challenge into an
255   opportunity to create smaller base LMs that are equally performant, achieving similar or lower
256
      validation loss compared to their larger, less efficient counterparts, which we refer to as reference
      models. Our proposed solution builds on two key insights: (1) the early layers of deep LLMs provide
257
      effective model initialization, and (2) multiple lazy layers can be collapsed into fewer layers and
258
      re-trained to improve the model capacity.
259
260   Setup: We split the dataset into a training set Dtrain and a validation subset Dval . Next, we assume
261   that there exists a pre-trained reference model Mref , comprising k layers, represented by Wref =
262   {W0 , W1 , . . . , Wk−1 } trained with Dtrain for T steps. We want to train a smaller model Mtgt with
263   the same or better validation loss (lower is better) compared to its larger counterpart Mref .
264
      We now present Inheritune, our proposed training recipe for efficiently developing small base lan-
265
      guage models (LMs). Inheritune operates on the principle of zero-shot initialization and progressive
266   growth. The Inheritune process consists of three main steps, which we present below and formalize
267   in Algorithm 1:
268
269         1. Inherit: Initialize Mtgt with the first n = k/2 layers of Mref , including weights, prediction
               head, and token embeddings.


                                                        5
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


270
            2. Train: Train Mtgt for T steps on Dtrain and evaluate on Dval .
271
272         3. Grow: If needed, increase Mtgt ’s size and repeat steps 1-2 until desired performance is
273
               achieved.
274
      With our method now formally described, we turn to empirical validation. In the following sections,
275   we present comprehensive results demonstrating Inheritune’s effectiveness across various scenarios,
276   including different model sizes and data regimes. In addition, we conducted an in-depth ablation
277   study to analyze the impact of initialization on performance, providing insights into the adaptability
278   of our approach.

      4   E XPERIMENTS
282
      We evaluate Inheritune through a series of comprehensive experiments using GPT-2 xlarge (1.5B),
283
      GPT-2 large (770M) and GPT-2 medium (355M) models, Radford et al. (2019) pre-trained on
284
      the 9B tokens OpenWebText dataset (Gokaslan & Cohen, 2019). These models are trained with
285   data repetition, meaning data is randomly sampled with replacement during batch creation. This
286   experimental setup is adapted from Liu et al. (2023); Sanyal et al. (2024). For evaluation we
287   compare model(s) trained with Inheritune with baselines from three key settings: a) baseline models
288   trained from scratch with random initialization, b) baseline Models trained using various zero-shot
289   initialization techniques c) baseline models trained with knowledge distillation. Table 10 provides
290   detailed specifications for all models used in our experiments. Finally, we conduct a thorough ablation
291   study of our initialization strategy, focusing on 16-layer GPT-2 medium variant(s).
292   We provide experimental details our Inheritune training recipe using a GPT-2 large model as an
293   example; similar procedure was applied to train other models. Our methodology for applying
294   Inherituneinvolves the following steps:
295
296         1. Reference Model: We train the full 36-layer GPT-2 Large model on Dtrain for 100K steps
297            and evaluate its validation loss ( log-perplexity) on Dval . This establishes our benchmark
298            validation loss.
299         2. Model initialization We initialize an 18-layer model (n = k/2) using the trained 36-layer
300            model as reference.
301         3. Training and Evaluation: We train the 18-layer model on Dtrain for T steps and evaluate
302            its validation loss.
303
            4. Iterative Refinement: If the smaller model’s performance is inferior, we incrementally
304
               increase its size by two layers and repeat steps 2-3 until we achieve parity with the reference
305
               model’s validation loss.
306

311
312   Baselines trained with various model initialization and efficient training techniques. Here we

      6    R ELATED W ORKS
507
508
      Attention degeneration has been studied in the past through the lens of attention rank collapse
509
      Dong et al. (2021) leading to representation collapse, and attention entropy collapse Zhai et al.
510   (2023) leading training instability. This also has been studied is a theoretical setup for transformer
511   models by Noci et al. (2022); Barbero et al. (2024). Recently He et al. (2023) address rank collapse
512   in self-attention networks (SANs) without residual connections or layer norms, using two model
513   initialization techniques that enable faithful signal propagation—i.e., ΣL of A(X L ) does not collapse
514   in deeper layers. However, this approach significantly
                                                        √          slows down training. Noci et al. (2022)
515   proposes scaling residual connections by 1/ L, while Barbero et al. (2024) suggest that adding
516   additional tokens to already long sequences of repeated tokens can help mitigate degeneration. In
517   contrast to prior works, we address attention degeneration by developing smaller models that eliminate
518   structural inefficiencies and training these models to match the performance of their larger, inefficient
519
      counterparts.
520   LLM training recipes and model initialization. The stacking method Gong et al. (2019); J. Reddi
521   et al. (2023) employs a stage-wise training strategy that uses weights from initial layers to initialize
522   later layers has been shown to be effective for LLM training both empirically Gong et al. (2019);
523   J. Reddi et al. (2023); Du et al. (2024) and theoretically Agarwal et al. (2024). Knowledge distillation
524   Hinton et al. (2015) has been very successful in training small LMs in some cases Turc et al. (2020);
525
      Sanh et al. (2019) the smaller student model is also initialized with teacher layers-though this is
      often done without clear explanation or intuition. Recent works in model initialization, such as
526
      Trockman & Kolter (2023), have studied synthetic attention patterns for initialization, primarily in
527
      vision settings. However, such methods have limited success in language models. Xu et al. (2024)
528   use weight initialization for faster fine-tuning of vision models. In contrast, our proposed recipe
529   focuses on creating smaller model by eliminating specific structural inefficiency in lazy layers. This
530   distinction sets our work apart in terms of both objective and methodology.

      7    C ONCLUSION
534
      In this paper, we identified a structural flaw in the attention mechanism of deep decoder-style LLMs,
535
      where many deeper layers tend to lose rank and converge into single-column matrices. To address
536
      this, we propose Inheritune, to train smaller models that inherits early blocks from a larger model and
537   expands the architecture gradually, matching the performance of the reference model. We validated
538   Inheritune on GPT-2 models of varying sizes, achieving efficient smaller models without performance
539   loss on the OpenWebText-9B and FineWeb_edu datasets.

840   How Inheritune addresses Attention Degeneration? Recall we have discussed attention degener-
841   ation in Section 2 and attention patterns are visualized in Figure 2. Following up on our previous
842   discussions in Figure 9 and Figure 10 we demonstrate that models trained with Inheritune has lesser

847   Recall we have previously discussed that attention degeneration is connected with vanishing gradients
848   of keys and queries Noci et al. (2022). The vanishing gradients is caused when the norm of the
849   gradients Bengio et al. (1994) are so small that it fails to generate meaningful back-propagation signal.
850   Since we are training smaller models intuitively ∥WQ ∥ and ∥WK ∥ should be smaller compared to
851   their larger counterparts and hence the norm of gradients in the case of smaller models derived using
852   Inheritune is higher leading to better training.

1226   B.3   I MPLICATIONS OF L OW DATA R EGIME
1227
       In this section, we discuss some of the key implications of our work in low data regime.
1228
1229
1230
       Cheap and easy development of small base LMs. Pre-training a small base LM of 1-2B parameters
       from scratch is extremely expensive. For instance mpt-1.3B base LM is pre-trained with 440 A100
1231
       GPUs for half a day, while the Pythia-1.4B base LM Biderman et al. (2023) utilized 64 A100-40GB
1232
       GPUs for 4.6 days. Similarly, TinyLLaMA-1.1B model Peiyuan Zhang & Lu (2023) was pre-trained
1233   using 16 A100 GPUs for 3 months. Our 1.5B (1B data variant) LM shows competitive performance
1234   despite being trained with 1 A6000 GPU for less than 12 hours. The computational details are
1235   provided in Table 7, comparing the training resources of the baseline models listed in this paper.
1236   Typically small base LMs are finetuned for a specific task before deployment and are not used in it’s
1237   base form. With Inheritune we present a really easy and cheap way for developing a small base LM
1238   to be later finetuned before deployment.
1239
1240   Naive baseline for pre-training a scaled down variant of large base LMs. Typically small
1241   variants of large base LMs are pre-trained using the same pre-training data Peiyuan Zhang & Lu
       (2023); Groeneveld et al. (2024). Our recipe introduces a new perspective of identifying sufficient


                                                          23
       Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


1242
       depth without losing any generalization on the held out validation set. Next, we also show that even
1243   with a small fraction of pre-train data (randomly sampled) and few initial layers of the large base LM
1244   one can develop a small base LM. Therefore our Inheritune recipe has the potential to become the
1245   naive baseline for any pre-training pipeline aiming to develop a smaller variant of a large base LM.

       D.1   D ISCUSSION ABOUT ATTENTION S INK
1392
1393   The term "attention sink" Xiao et al. (2024) refers to the phenomenon where the first token in a
1394   sequence receives disproportionately high attention scores compared to other tokens in the attention
1395   maps. While there is some connection with Inheritune, as we have also observed that many attention
1396   matrices are not only rank-1 but also single-column (with all attention scores concentrated on the first
1397   token), this connection has not been explicitly established in Xiao et al. (2024) with respect to rank-1
1398   behavior or poor training of later layers.
1399   In contrast, as illustrated in Figure 1, we compute the maximum rank of all attention matrices within
1400   a layer. For instance, consider a layer where only 2 out of 5 attention heads exhibit attention sink
1401   behavior. This does not make the layer lazy, as attention is computed as a concatenation of activations
1402   across all heads. A lazy layer, however, has all 5 out of 5 attention heads fully degenerated, with their
1403   attention matrices being rank-1. We provide evidence that such lazy layers are indicative of poorly
       trained layers.
