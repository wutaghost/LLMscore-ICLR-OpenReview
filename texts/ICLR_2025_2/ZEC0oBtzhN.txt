009                                             A BSTRACT
010
011           Mixture of Experts (MoE) architectures offer a promising avenue for scaling neu-
012           ral networks by facilitating parameter-efficient model expansion while optimiz-
013           ing FLOP utilization. However, several challenges persist, including sub-optimal
014           expert utilization, vanishing gradients, sub-par expert specialization, and incon-
015           sistent routing decisions. We introduce BLIMEY, a novel diagnostic framework
016           that systematically quantifies these critical issues, enabling design and implemen-
017           tation of more robust and efficient expert routing algorithms. BLIMEY equips re-
018
              searchers with granular, interpretable metrics on MoE dynamics, explaining both
              the performance advantages over monolithic architectures and the persistent algo-
019
              rithmic bottlenecks, thereby offering a comprehensive diagnostic framework for
020
              MoE optimization. Leveraging BLIMEY, we establish new scaling laws, that sur-
021           pass established benchmarks like Chinchilla, achieving 3x reductions in FLOPs
022           and 5% performance gains. Furthermore, our framework unveils significant op-
023           timization potential in routing algorithms, revealing sub-optimal expert specifi-
024           cation and load imbalances in current methodologies. To accelerate innovation
025           in MoE routing and computational efficiency, we have open-sourced BLIMEY
026           framework, including its diagnostic tools and implementation libraries.
