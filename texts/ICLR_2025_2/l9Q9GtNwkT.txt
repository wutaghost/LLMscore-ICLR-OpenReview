011                                                      A BSTRACT
012
013             We contribute to the growing body of knowledge on more powerful and adaptive
014             stepsizes for convex optimization, empowered by local curvature information. We
015
                do not go the route of fully-fledged second-order methods which require the ex-
                pensive computation of the Hessian. Instead, our key observation is that, for some
016
                problems (e.g., when minimizing the sum of squares of absolutely convex func-
017
                tions), certain local curvature information is readily available, and can be used to
018             obtain surprisingly powerful matrix-valued stepsizes, and meaningful theory. In
019             particular, we develop three new methodsвАФLCD1, LCD2 and LCD3вАФwhere the
020             abbreviation stands for local curvature descent. While LCD1 generalizes gradient
021             descent with fixed stepsize, LCD2 generalizes gradient descent with Polyak step-
022             size. Our methods enhance these classical gradient descent baselines with local
023             curvature information, and our theory recovers the known rates in the special case
024             when no curvature information is used. Our last method, LCD3, is a variable-
025             metric version of LCD2; this feature leads to a closed-form expression for the
026
                iterates. Our empirical results are encouraging, and show that the local curvature
                descent improves upon gradient descent.

082   successful in practice as Adam (Duchi et al., 2011).

478   9   C ONCLUSION
479
480   We explored adaptive matrix-valued stepsizes under novel assumptions that reinforce convexity and
481   L-smoothness with extra curvature information. Under our assumptions, we proposed LCD1 and
482   LCD2, which generalize GD with constant stepsize and Polyak stepsize, respectively. Moreover,
483   we provided convergence theorems for both of these algorithms. We also proposed LCD3 which
484   displays promising experimental behavior. Our key insight is that, for some problems, we have
485   certain local curvature information that can be readily exploited. We tested the methods on these
      problems using a variety of realistic datasets, demonstrating good empirical performance.
