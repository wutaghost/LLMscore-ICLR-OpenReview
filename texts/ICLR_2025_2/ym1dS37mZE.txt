009                                              A BSTRACT
010
011            The development of Multi-modal Large Language Models (MLLMs) has sig-
012            nificantly advanced various downstream applications, including visual question
013            answering and image captioning. However, the substantial computational costs
014            associated with processing high-resolution images and videos pose a barrier to
015            their broader adoption. To address this challenge, compressing vision tokens in
016            MLLMs has emerged as a promising approach to reduce inference costs. In this
017            paper, we introduce VisToG, a novel grouping mechanism that leverages the ca-
018
               pabilities of pretrained vision encoders to group similar image segments without
               the need for segmentation masks. With the isolated attention we adopt, VisToG
019
               can identify and eliminate redundant visual tokens, which effectively reduces
020
               computational demands. Extensive experiments demonstrate that the effective-
021            ness of VisToG, maintaining 98.1% of the original performance while achieving
022            a reduction of over 27% inference time.

025   1   I NTRODUCTION
026
027   The advent of Large Language Models (LLMs) (Bai et al., 2023; Touvron et al., 2023; Achiam et al.,
028   2023; Zhu et al., 2023), has revolutionized the landscape of natural language processing (NLP), en-
029   abling unprecedented advancements in tasks ranging from text generation to machine translation.
030   With the need to incorporate information from other modalities such as vision, research on Multi-
031   modal Large Language Models (MLLMs) Liu et al. (2024); Bai et al. (2023); Zhu et al. (2023) has
032   attracted much attention. MLLMs emerged as a powerful paradigm, combining the strengths of both
033
      visual and textual modalities to achieve superior performance in tasks requiring cross-modal under-
      standing However, the remarkable capabilities of these models come with substantial computational
034
      costs, particularly during the inference phase. This computational burden is exacerbated when en-
035
      countering multi-modal inputs leading to a long input sequence, such as high-resolution images or
036   videos. This limits their practical deployment in resource-constrained environments.
037
038   Typically, LLM costs the most for the MLLM computation because of the model size difference
039
      compared with the visual encoder and visual connector. For example, the widely used ViT-L Rad-
      ford et al. (2021) only has 0.3B parameters while the language encoder typically has 7B or 13B
040
      parameters Chiang et al. (2023). Therefore, towards building an effective MLLM, current works
041
      focus on reducing the image tokens fed to the LLMs. Various approaches have been applied to
042   serve this purpose. Main stream method includes training-free and finetuning. Training-free meth-
043   ods typically utilize the off-the-shelf pre-trained MLLMs and prune the visual tokens according to
044   the attention in the transformer layers in LLMs Chen et al. (2024). While training-free method is
045   plug-and-play, their performance are far from satisfying and they are often ineffective when applied
046   to training because the in-training attention scores are unstable. Finetuning methods perform vi-
047   sual token reduction via operations on the image feature produced by the visual encoder, such as
048   Adaptive Average Pooling Yao et al. (2024), convolution block or deformable attention block as vi-
049   sual abstractor Cha et al. (2024). However, these methods all conduct visual token reduction on the
050
      image features after feeding into the visual encoder, while leaving the potential of the pre-trained
      visual encoder not fully explored.
051
052   We observe that randomly chosen image tokens can serve as a strong baseline, indicating the re-
053   dundancy of the image tokens. Further, if we deliberately modify the image tokens distribution
      following prior knowledge from humans, the performance can be greatly improved. Specifically,

      the sampled tokens covered all semantic segments of the image. Motivated by this, we propose a
055   novel visual token grouping mechanism aimed at reducing the inference costs of MLLMs by ex-
056   ploring the potential of the pre-trained vision encoder to reduce the redundant token while covering
057   all semantic groups. Our approach leverages the inherent structure and redundancy present in visual
058   data to condense the visual token representation with minimally sacrificing performance. By intelli-

060   model, thereby reducing computational overhead and accelerating inference times.

065   2.1   M ULTI - MODAL L ARGE L ANGUAGE M ODELS
066
067   The success of Large Language Models (LLMs) has advanced various applications in the field of
068   natural language processing. Its strong instruction-following ability and generalization power across
069   tasks drive the researchers to build a multi-modal counterpart. GPT-4V from OpenAI has proven the
070   potential of how an MLLM can do Yang et al. (2023). Researchers have put efforts to reimplement
071
      MLLMs similar to GPT-4V. The core design lies in how to connect the pre-trained visual encoder and
      the LLM. Resampler Bai et al. (2023) and Q-Former Li et al. (2023a); Dai et al. (2023); Zhang et al.
072
      (2024) employs learnable queries to represent visual tokens and force extracting the most relevant
073
      visual information from visual features by cross-attention layers. These works helped connect the
074   visual and text modalities and achieved remarkable progress. To make the alignment between visual
075   encoders and LLMs more effective and efficient, LLaVA Liu et al. (2024) uses a single projection
076   layer to conduct the alignment. With a meticulously curated instruction-tuning dataset, it achieves
077   remarkable performance, rivaling models trained on extremely large-scale datasets, yet it requires
078   only a manageable training cost. The reduction in substantial training expenses greatly benefits the
079   MLLM community.
080
081
      2.2   E FFICIENT INFERENCE FOR LLM/MLLM
082
083
      The auto-regressive nature of LLMs poses a great challenge on the deployment of LLMs. The
084   quadratic complexity of computing the attention makes the generation process becomes much slower
085   when the input token is longer. StreamingLLM Xiao et al. (2023) and FastGen Ge et al. (2023) prune
086   the redundant attention computation to simplify the computation. Despite their success, they are de-
087   signed for the single-modal LLM and are not proven successful when it comes to scenarios involv-
088   ing tokens from other modalities. For improving the efficiency of MLLMs, various works Li et al.
089   (2023a); Zhu et al. (2023); Zhang et al. (2024) adopt Q-Former to map the images to fixed-length
090   tokens. In the meantime, many works try to train smaller MLLMs with smaller backbone Yuan
091   et al. (2023); Zhou et al. (2024) to handle the scenario with less computational resources, MoE-
092
      LLaVA Lin et al. (2024) incorporates a Mixture of Experts to address model sparsity, enhancing
      efficiency and performance. Another line of research tries to reduce the number of visual tokens
093
      while keeping the backbone of LLM unchanged. Since LLMs contribute most to the computation,
094
      the number of input tokens to the LLM becomes the bottleneck of the inference cost. Recently,
095   DeCo Yao et al. (2024) employs 2D adaptive average pooling to down-sample the visual tokens at
096   the patch level. Honeybee Cha et al. (2024) proposes to use ResNet Block and deformable attention
097   to conduct the abstraction of the vision tokens. VoCo-LLaMA Ye et al. (2024) compress the vision
098   tokens using the LLMs and leverage the attention distillation to let LLMs restore information from
099   the specially defined VoCo tokens instead of the whole image tokens.

      3.3   I SOLATED ATTENTION
217
218   To fully leverage the potential of the pre-trained vision encoder, it’s crucial to mitigate the impact of
219   the newly introduced semantic tokens, denoted as Sem, on the original image segment tokens. This
220   ensures that the integrity of the original image representations remains intact. To achieve this, we
221   implement a technique called isolated attention, which prevents the original image segment tokens
222   from interacting directly with the newly added semantic tokens, thereby preserving their original
223   characteristics.
224   More specifically, let the attention mask M be a matrix defined as M ∈ R(M +N )×(M +N ) , where
225   each element Mi,j represents whether token i can attend to token j. If Mi,j = True, token i is
226   allowed to attend to token j, and if Mi,j = False, attention is blocked between the two tokens.
227   The isolated attention mechanism is then formally defined as follows:
228
229
230                                          False, if i ∈ Img and j ∈ Sem,
                                 Mij =                                                                     (5)
231                                          True,  otherwise.
232
233   This attention mask prevents the original image tokens Img from attending to the semantic tokens
234   Sem, while allowing other interactions to proceed normally. Consequently, this design ensures that
235   the output of the original image segment tokens remains unchanged despite the addition of the se-
236   mantic tokens. By isolating the attention in this way, the original image tokens are preserved as they
237   were before the introduction of the semantic tokens, maintaining the image’s core representation.
238   On the other hand, the semantic tokens Sem can still learn to aggregate the image features derived
239   from the pre-trained visual encoder into semantically meaningful regions that align with the provided
240   instruction or task. This approach allows the semantic tokens to specialize in creating groupings of
241   image features without disrupting the original token representations.
242
      We further conduct a series of ablation studies on the attention mask configuration to validate the
243   effectiveness of this isolated attention mechanism compared to using full attention, as detailed in
244   Sec.4. These experiments demonstrate the importance of this isolation in retaining the fidelity of the
245   image representations while allowing the semantic tokens to perform their intended role.

      3.4   I NSTRUCTION - AWARE V ISUAL T OKEN G ROUPING
248
249
      Let ϕ denote the Large Language Model (LLM), W denote the lightweight visual connector, which
250
      typically takes the form of a multi-layer perceptron (MLP), and G represent the grouping layer.
251   These components together form the foundation of our method for visual token grouping within an
252   LLM-based framework. To effectively train the proposed VisToGto perform this task, we carefully
253   design a two-stage training pipeline that ensures robust feature alignment and instruction-aware
254   token grouping.
255
      Stage 1: Pre-training for Feature Alignment
256
257   The goal of the first stage is to align the image features with the LLM by training an image tokenizer
258   that maps visual inputs into a form compatible with the LLM. Since the pre-training phase operates
259   on an image-caption dataset, the grouping mechanism is not yet emphasized during this stage, as the
260
      focus is primarily on aligning the visual representation with the caption, which serves as the natural
      language supervision. Given that the grouping mechanism is better suited to be learned from more
261
      specific instructions rather than general captions, we refrain from incorporating the grouping layer
262
      during this phase. Consequently, the pre-training phase follows the same setup as LLaVA Liu et al.
263   (2024), where only the image-caption pairs are used to train the image tokenizer.
264
265   The key insight here is that, by not introducing the grouping mechanism prematurely, we allow the
266
      model to establish a strong foundation in feature alignment between images and language. In this
      stage, the only trainable parameter is the visual connector W , i.e., Θ = W . This ensures that the
267
      visual features extracted from images are properly aligned with the LLM, setting the stage for further
268
      fine-tuning in the next phase.
269
      Stage 2: Visual Instruction Tuning


                                                         5
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


270
      Once the image tokenizer has been pre-trained and the visual features are aligned with the language
271   model, we move on to the second stage, which focuses on fine-tuning the model for instruction-
272   aware visual token grouping. At this point, we incorporate the grouping block G along with semantic
273   tokens into the Vision Transformer architecture to enable effective visual token grouping based on
274   specific instructions.
275
      In this stage, we freeze the pre-trained weights of the visual encoder to retain the feature alignment
276
      achieved in Stage 1. However, we continue updating the parameters of the lightweight visual con-
277   nector W , the grouping layer G, and the LLM ϕ. This means that the trainable parameters during
278   Stage 2 are Θ = ϕ, W, G. Importantly, by introducing the grouping mechanism during instruction-
279   tuning, we ensure that the grouping layer becomes instruction-aware, meaning that the grouping
280   process is directly influenced by the specific instructions provided to the model.
281
      The critical advantage of this approach is that the gradient of the instructions can flow back to
282
      the grouping layer, allowing it to learn how to group visual tokens based on the semantics of the
283   instructions rather than general captions. This enables the VisToGto achieve a higher level of
284   adaptability and precision when handling visual tasks that require instruction-specific groupings.

468   5   C ONCLUSION
469
470   In this paper, we have introduced VisToG, a novel grouping mechanism designed to address the
471   substantial computational costs associated with Multi-modal Large Language Models (MLLMs).
472   By leveraging pre-trained vision encoders to group similar image segments without the need for
473   additional segmentation masks, VisToG effectively reduces inference costs. Our approach utilizes
474   isolated attention to identify and eliminate redundant visual tokens, significantly decreasing com-
475   putational demands. Extensive experiments validate the efficacy of VisToG, demonstrating that it
476
      maintains 98.1% of the original performance while achieving a reduction of over 27% in inference
      time. This advancement enhances the efficiency of MLLMs and provides insights on training larger
477
      MLLMs with minimal image token redundancies.
