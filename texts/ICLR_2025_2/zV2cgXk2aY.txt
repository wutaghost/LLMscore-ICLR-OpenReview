011                                               A BSTRACT
012
013            Transformer-based time series forecasting has recently gained strong interest due
014            to the ability of transformers to model sequential data. Most of the state-of-the-art
015
               architectures exploit either temporal or inter-channel dependencies, limiting their
               effectiveness in multivariate time-series forecasting where both types of depen-
016
               dencies are crucial. We propose Sentinel, a full transformer-based architecture
017
               composed of an encoder able to extract contextual information from the channel
018            dimension, and a decoder designed to capture causal relations and dependencies
019            across the temporal dimension. Additionally, we introduce a multi-patch attention
020            mechanism, which leverages the patching process to structure the input sequence
021            in a way that can be naturally integrated into the transformer architecture, replac-
022            ing the multi-head splitting process. Extensive experiments on standard bench-
023            marks demonstrate that Sentinel, because of its ability to “monitor” both the tem-
024            poral and the inter-channel dimension, achieves better or comparable performance
025            with respect to state-of-the-art approaches.

      1   I NTRODUCTION
028
029
      Time-series forecasting is a critical task in many real-world applications, hence the interest in re-
030   searching better techniques in this field is high. Recent advancements in deep learning have provided
031   new approaches to tackle this problem. However, effectively modeling multivariate time series,
032   where complex relationships exist between multiple features, remains a challenging task. In this
033   perspective, capturing both long-term dependencies and inter-channel relationships is crucial for
034   improving forecast accuracy.
035
      Transformers (Vaswani et al., 2017) have shown promise in handling long-range dependencies in
036   various domains, including natural language processing (Brown et al., 2020) and computer vision
037   (Dosovitskiy et al., 2021). Some recent works have extended transformers to the time-series fore-
038   casting task, with mixed results. While some studies demonstrate their effectiveness in capturing
039   temporal relationships(Wang et al., 2024; Liu et al., 2024; Zhang & Yan, 2023), others highlight
040   limitations in addressing inter-channel dependencies efficiently (Nie et al., 2023; Jin et al., 2024;
041   Chang et al., 2023).
042   The current literature on transformers for time-series forecasting can be mainly divided in two
043   trends: developing new transformer architectures (Liu et al., 2024; Wang et al., 2024; Nie et al.,
044   2023; Zhang & Yan, 2023) or fine-tuning pre-trained models (Zhou et al., 2023; Chang et al., 2023).
045   While both directions are promising, in this work we focus on the first approach where we iden-
046   tify a gap in the current literature: most transformer-based approaches focus on either temporal or
047   inter-channel dependencies but only few works try to model both these dependencies (Wang et al.,
048   2024). This limits the ability of state-of-the-art models to fully exploit the intricate relationships
049   within multivariate time-series data.
050   Against this background, we designed a full transformer-based architecture capable of capturing
051   both temporal and inter-channel relationships. The encoder is responsible for modeling inter-channel
052   dependencies, learning how different features (or channels) in the time series relate to each other on
053   a patch basis across the entire sequence. This allows the encoder to generates a global context for
      each channel.

054
      On the other hand, the decoder specializes in capturing temporal dependencies searching for causal
055   relationships across the time dimension. The input to the decoder first passes through a self-attention
056   layer, and the output of this layer serves as the query for the cross-attention mechanism. To further
057   enhance prediction accuracy, the cross-attention mechanism integrates the channel-wise context gen-
058   erated by the encoder with the temporal query produced by the decoder in the previous step, enabling
059   the model to effectively forecast future time steps.
060
      Additionally, the division of the time series into patches (Nie et al., 2023) has shown to be helpful in
061   the forecasting task: it simplifies the discovery of closer relations between patches and reduces the
062   computational complexity of the model.
063
064
      Following this idea we propose a modified attention mechanism. It takes advantage of the patching
      process, replacing the traditional multi-head attention with a multi-patch attention, to shift the focus
065
      from the concept of “head” to that of “patch”. This multi-patch mechanism is utilized throughout
066
      the entire architecture: in the encoder, where each “head” independently attends to the sequence at
067   patch level, extracting the channels relationships between the various time steps, and in the decoder,
068   where each “head” independently attends to the sequence at patch level, extracting the temporal
069   relationships between the various time steps.
070
      Summarizing, our key contributions are:
071
072          • A novel multi-patch attention mechanism able to exploit the patched input, redesigning the
073            multi-head attention.
074
             • A specialized encoder-decoder architecture that is able to capture both temporal and inter-
075            channel dependencies, where the encoder focuses on inter-channel relationships and the
076            decoder captures temporal dependencies, enhancing the model’s forecasting capabilities.
077
             • An extensive evaluation on standard benchmarks for time-series forecasting, showing that
078            the proposed architecture achieves better or comparable performances with respect to other
079            state-of-the art approaches.
080
             • An ablation study demonstrating that the components we propose contribute significantly
081
               to enhance overall forecasting performance.

084   2   R ELATED W ORK
085
086   Several transformer-based architectures have proven to be effective in time series forecasting, lead-
087
      ing to the development of various methods aimed at improving performance and reducing computa-
      tional complexity.
088
089   The key ingredient that allows transformer-based architectures to achieve good result in time se-
090   ries forecasting is the attention mechanism. Several works have focused on reducing computational
091   complexity and memory usage. For instance, LogTrans (Li et al., 2019) introduces a convolutional
092   self-attention mechanism alongside a LogSparse Transformer to reduce the complexity of standard
093
      attention mechanism. Informer (Zhou et al., 2021) proposed a ProbSparse self-attention mechanism
      to address memory usage and computational overhead. In contrast, Autoformer (Wu et al., 2021)
094
      proposes an auto-correlation mechanism. FedFormer (Zhou et al., 2022) enhances transformer ar-
095
      chitectures with frequency domain information. Pyraformer (Liu et al., 2022) developes a pyramidal
096   attention module able to summarize features at different resolutions. Other works, such as SAM-
097   former (Ilbert et al., 2024) aim to limit overfitting by proposing a shallow lightweight transformer
098   model to escape local minima.
099
      PatchTST (Nie et al., 2023) introduces the concept of patch and channel independence, which has led
100
      to significant improvements in forecasting performance. In our work, we exploit the patch structure
101
      created through their idea, but we leverage it by considering the full channel relationships. Addi-
102   tionally, other works have attempted to challenge the notion of channel independence, demonstrating
103   that exploring relationships at the channel level is crucial. Crossformer (Zhang & Yan, 2023) imple-
104   ments a Two-Stage Attention mechanism designed to capture both cross-time and cross-dimension
105   dependencies. iTransformer (Liu et al., 2024) applies the attention and feed-forward network on
106   the temporal dimension to improve forecasting performance. CARD (Wang et al., 2024) introduces
107   a channel-aligned transformer combined with a signal decay-based loss function. With respect to
      CARD, which implements an encoder-only structure to model both temporal and cross-channels

      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


108
      dependencies, we propose an encoder-decoder architecture that specializes the encoder in captur-
109   ing contextual information through the channel dimension while allowing the decoder to focus on
110   modeling causal relations and dependencies across the temporal dimension.
111
112
      Another trend focuses on leveraging pre-trained models for time-series forecasting. LLMTime
      (Nate Gruver & Wilson, 2023) presents a zero-shot approach by encoding numbers as text. lag-llama
113
      (Rasul et al., 2024) proposes a foundation model for univariate probabilistic time series forecasting.
114
      GPT4TS (Zhou et al., 2023) demonstrates the universality of transformer models and emphasizes the
115   importance of freezing attention layers during fine-tuning. Time-LLM (Jin et al., 2024) introduces
116   a reprogramming framework to repurpose LLMs for general time series forecasting tasks. LLM4TS
117   (Chang et al., 2023) employs a two-stage fine-tuning strategy for utilizing pre-trained LLMs for
118   time-series forecasting. Moirai (Woo et al., 2024) introduces a novel transformer architecture and
119   a related large dataset of time series from several domains to support the requirements of universal
120   forecasting.

166   3.1   PATCHING AND E MBEDDING
167
168   Following the normalization process described earlier, the input time series undergoes a patching
169   process (Nie et al., 2023) which divides the input series into patches of size P along the time dimen-
170   sion. This patching is performed with overlapping regions determined by the stride S. After this
171   operation, the resulting tensor is represented as X ∈ RC×N ×P where N = [ L−P    S + 1] is the number
172   of patches.
173   After patching, the patched tensor is projected to the model’s latent dimension resulting in the em-
174   bedding tensor X(1) ∈ RC×N ×dmodel . This transformation is performed through a multi-layer per-
175   ceptron (MLP).
176
177
      As highlighted by Nie et al. (2023), since the computational complexity of the attention mechanism
      scales quadratically with the length of the sequence, dividing the input time series into smaller
178
      patches, the memory usage and computational complexity are reduced quadratically by a factor S.
179

427
428   We conducted an ablation study by systematically removing the different components proposed in
429   this paper, with the results summarized in Table 3. As seen in the table, each component plays
430   a critical role in achieving high performance. Specifically, the benefits introduced by Sentinel are
431   more pronounced in datasets with a large number of features. For instance, in datasets with many
      channels, such as electricity or weather in Table 3, the removal of the channel-encoder, responsible


                                                                    8
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


432
      for handling cross-channel relationships, leads to a significant drop in performance (i.e., respectively
433   −8.0% and −9.7% of MSE; −3.0% and −4.5% in terms of MSE). Conversely, in datasets with
434   fewer features, such as ETTh2 in Table 3, the impact is less severe. In these scenarios, the most
435   harmful effect is observed when the decoder is removed (i.e., −0.3% of MSE and −1.3% of MAE),
436   as the reduced feature set places less emphasis on feature importance, making the role of the decoder
437   more critical.
438
      Lastly, the final row of the table, shows the architecture using the classic multi-head encoder and
439   decoder, without the multi-patch attention. It consistently performs lower than the multi-patch at-
440   tention solution across all datasets, regardless of their properties. This strong and consistent per-
441   formance gap highlights the importance of rethinking the traditional “head” attention into a “patch”
442   attention. By replacing multi-head attention with our novel multi-patch attention, we exploit the
443   natural structure of the time-series created by the patching process, thereby increasing the overall
444   forecasting performance. All these results confirm the importance of the Sentinel components for
445   effectively processing multivariate datasets and providing improved forecasting performance.

463
464   Transformer-based architectures have been shown to experience performance degradation as the
465   lookback window increases (Zeng et al., 2023). In our evaluation, as in other works (Wang et al.,

467   shown in Figure 3 and Tables 4.4, our approach demonstrates that increasing lookback window,
468
      thereby providing the model with more historical information for forecasting, generally leads to im-
      proved overall performance. This suggests that a longer historical context can enhance the model’s
469
      ability to capture temporal patterns and make more accurate predictions.

500   5     C ONCLUSION A ND F UTURE W ORK
501
502
      In this paper, we introduced Sentinel, a fully Transformer-based architecture specifically designed
503   for multivariate time-series forecasting. Sentinel addresses a critical limitation in existing trans-
504   former models by simultaneously capturing both cross-channel and temporal dependencies, which
505   are essential for improving forecast accuracy in complex multivariate datasets. Additionally, we in-
506   troduced the concept of multi-patch attention to leverage the structure created through the patching
507   process. This novel component has proven to be highly valuable in ablation studies, significantly
508   enhancing forecasting performance. By rethinking the traditional multi-head attention and replac-
509   ing it with multi-patch attention, Sentinel is able to better utilize the patched time-series structure,
510   leading to superior results across a wide range of multivariate datasets.
511   In the near future, we aim to refine the architecture of Sentinel by focusing on both the encoder and
512   the decoder. As highlighted in the ablation study, the encoder shows a strong impact when dealing
513   with datasets with a high number of features but it tends to overfit when the number of features
514   is lower. To address this lack, we aim to develop techniques to mitigate overfitting in such cases,
515   ensuring the encoder remains effective across various sizes of the feature-space. Conversely, the
516   decoder has less impact as the number of features increases. We aim to improve its contribution
517
      in such high-feature scenarios. Another promising direction is to evaluate the few-shot learning
      capabilities of Sentinel, which could expand its applicability to a wider range of forecasting tasks.
