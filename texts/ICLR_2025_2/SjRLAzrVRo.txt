009                                                  A BSTRACT
010
011               Diffusion-based image synthesis has attracted extensive attention recently. In
012               particular, ControlNet that uses image-based prompts exhibits powerful capability
013               in image tasks such as canny edge detection and generates images well aligned with
014               these prompts. However, vanilla ControlNet generally requires extensive training of
015               around 5000 steps to achieve a desirable control for a single task. Recent context-
016               learning approaches have improved its adaptability, but mainly for edge-based
017               tasks, and rely on paired examples. Thus, two important open issues are yet to be
018
                  addressed to reach the full potential of ControlNet: (i) zero-shot control for certain
                  tasks and (ii) faster adaptation for non-edge-based tasks. In this paper, we introduce
019
                  a novel Meta ControlNet method, which adopts the task-agnostic meta learning
020
                  technique and features a new layer freezing design. Meta ControlNet significantly
021               reduces learning steps to attain control ability from 5000 to 1000. Further, Meta
022               ControlNet exhibits direct zero-shot adaptability in edge-based tasks without any
023               finetuning, and achieves control within only 100 finetuning steps in more complex
024               non-edge tasks such as Human Pose. 1

027   1       I NTRODUCTION
028
029   Image synthesis (Dhariwal & Nichol, 2021; Ramesh et al., 2022; Brock et al., 2018) is a rapidly
030   growing field in computer vision and draws significant interest from various application domains. As a
031   key approach in this area, Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) (Brock et al., 2018; Karras et al.,
032   2019; Goodfellow et al., 2020) employ a discriminator-generator pair, where the generator is trained
033   to creating enhanced images via sharpening the discriminator. However, such an adversarial approach
034
      typically has difficulty to model more complex distributions. Recently, diffusion models (Dhariwal &
      Nichol, 2021; Ramesh et al., 2022) have emerged as a powerful alternative, excelling in high-quality
035
      image generation. These models utilize a series of denoising autoencoders to progressively refine an
036
      image from pure Gaussian noise. Among these, a new model known as Stable Diffusion (Rombach
037   et al., 2022) has been proposed, which has better computational efficiency. Unlike traditional methods,
038   Stable Diffusion uses latent representations for image compression, and achieves superior image
039   quality, which includes advancements in text-to-image synthesis and unconditional image generation.
040
      ControlNet (Zhang et al., 2023) further advances image synthesis with enhanced control over image
041
      content by using conditional control as different tasks. This approach clones the encoder and middle
042
      block of Stable Diffusion, and introduces zero convolution to link with the decoders of Stable
043   Diffusion. Such a setup allows ControlNet to accept image prompt inputs, such as canny or HED
044   edge, and can generate images specific to certain tasks, demonstrating improved control from both
045   image and textual inputs. However, ControlNet’s capability for precise control requires extensive
046   training. Specifically, learning to control a new task demands about 5000 steps. Recently, Prompt
047   Diffusion was proposed in (Wang et al., 2023), which leverages in-context learning idea to enhance
048   ControlNet’s adaptability to new tasks, but requires task-specific example pairs for training.
049   Although ControlNet and its variants have achieved enhanced the generalizability, several critical
050   open issues remain unresolved to reach the full power of ControlNet. Firstly, zero-shot capability
051   of ControlNet has not yet been explored, leaving it as an open question whether it is possible to
052   control new tasks without finetuning samples. Secondly, while most existing studies have focused on

086   In this paper, we propose a novel Meta ControlNet method to address the aforementioned open
087   issues. Specifically, Meta ControlNet adopts the FO-MAML (Finn et al., 2017) framework with
088
      various image condition types serving as different meta tasks. The inner-loop training of Meta
      ControlNet takes finetune steps separately for each task. Then the outer-loop training updates meta
089
      parameters (i.e., the model initial) based on averaged gradients over all training tasks.
090
091   (Novel Layer Freezing Design) Meta ControlNet features a new layer freezing design. Typically,
092   meta learning algorithms such as ANIL (Raghu et al., 2019) freezes the earlier embedding layers
093   during the inner-loop training. As a sharp difference, Meta ControlNet freezes latter encoder block
094
      and the middle block during meta training. This idea is based on the observation that the initial
      encoder blocks are directly linked to the control images of control tasks. It is essential to finetune
095
      these encoder blocks for individual task. On the other hand, the middle and latter encoder blocks,
096
      which capture common and high-level information, can be retained and shared across tasks. Such a
097   design has been proven to be critical for Meta ControlNet to exhibit desirable performance in our
098   experiments. Note that for the meta testing phase, we recommend training all layers to achieve the
099   best possible adaptation performance.

      In the following, we highlight the superior experimental performance that Meta ControlNet achieves:
101
102    • Fast Learning of Control Ability: Our proposed design significantly enhances the efficiency
103      of ControlNet’s learning process. Our experiments demonstrate that Meta ControlNet acquires
104      control abilities within only 1000 steps, a stark improvement over the vanilla ControlNet that
105      achieves the same ability with 5000 steps. Meanwhile, this efficiency is demonstrated across
106      three meta training tasks, showcasing the method’s versatility.
107    • Zero-Shot Adaptation for Edge-based Tasks: Meta ControlNet produces generalizable model
         initial, which exhibits exceptional control capabilities in zero-shot settings, especially for edge-

      2.1   D IFFUSION M ODEL AND C ONTROL N ET
122
      With the recent advancement of score-based generative models (Ho et al., 2020; Song & Ermon,
123   2019; Song et al., 2020b;a), diffusion models have achieved remarkable performance in text-to-image
124   synthesis (Dhariwal & Nichol, 2021; Ramesh et al., 2022). Essentially, the diffusion model learns a
125   time-varying mapping that gradually transforms a random noise into the sample space via a reverse
126   diffusion process (Ho et al., 2020). Stable Diffusion (SD) (Rombach et al., 2022), as an important
127   step to achieve high-resolution image generation, utilizes a variational autoencoder to first encode
128   images into a latent space, and then learns a time-conditioned U-Net to perform the denoising process
129   on the latent space based on text prompts.
130   To allow diffusion models to receive more diverse user-specific guidance for image generation,
131   Composer (Huang et al., 2023), ControlNet (Zhang et al., 2023), GLIGEN (Li et al., 2023b) and T2I-
132   Adapter (Mou et al., 2023) were proposed as general approaches to introduce additional controlling
133   signals. Among these, ControlNet (Zhang et al., 2023) stands out by its superior performance in
134   various downstream tasks ranging from sketch (edge, skeleton) to geometry (depth, normal) guided
135   generation. Technically, ControlNet freezes the original SD model, while finetuning a duplicate of the
136   pre-trained SD to integrate additional control signals via zero-initialized convolution modules. Such
137
      an adaptation scheme significantly reduces training costs by re-using the image prior learned in the
      pre-trained SD. ControlNet-XS (Rother, 2023) further investigates the size and architectural design
138
      of ControlNet and proposes a more parameter-efficient architecture. As downstream applications, by
139
      leveraging ControlNet, Goel et al. (2023) is able to edit the structure and appearance properties for
140   each object in the image, and Chu et al. (2023); Wu et al. (2023) enforces temporal consistency for
141   video generation, Ma et al. (2023); Seo et al. (2023) make pre-trained SD aware of 3D knowledge and
142   multi-view geometry. However, for each of these tasks, an independent adapter is required for each
143   condition. The modified version Multi-ControlNet (Zhang et al., 2023) demonstrates the possibility of
144   composing multiple tasks. Uni-ControlNet (Zhao et al., 2023) proposes a unified framework allowing
145   for the simultaneous utilization of different local and global controls. Prompt Diffusion (Wang et al.,
146   2023) trains an open-domain ControlNet in an in-context learning manner. However, none of these
147   studies are designed for or have been demonstrated to have zero-shot generalization capability for
148
      unseen control tasks.
149
150   2.2   M ETA L EARNING
151
152   Meta learning focuses on few-shot learning scenarios, aiming to develop algorithms that leverage a
153
      large set of pre-defined tasks to improve performance on unseen instances with only a few or even
      zero extra training data samples. In this paper, we focus on “learning-to-initialize” approaches such as
154
      Model-Agnostic Meta Learning (MAML) (Finn et al., 2017), which learns an initialization point from
155
      which models can fast adapt to new tasks. MAML (Finn et al., 2017) algorithm involves two layers of
156   training, where at each iteration, the inner loop optimizes the parameter for each task independently
157   starting from the current initialization, whereas the outer loop estimates the gradient with respect
158   to the inner loop optimization path to update the initialization. Since differentiating through an
159   optimization algorithm is often computationally expensive, FO-MAML (Finn et al., 2017) proposes to
160   simplify the outer-loop gradient computation by directly averaging task-specific gradients evaluated
161   at the outputs of the inner loop. ANIL (Raghu et al., 2019) improves MAML via feature reusing,
      where the main feature backbone is frozen and only prediction heads are updated by each task in the


                                                         3
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


162
      inner loop. Reptile (Nichol et al., 2018; Nichol & Schulman, 2018) simplifies the process by aiming
163   for an initialization that minimizes the expected loss across all tasks, similar to joint training.
164
165   In computer vision, a popular application of meta learning is few-shot image classification, where a
166
      network is adapted to new classes using only a small number of labeled instances per class (Finn et al.,
      2017; Ravi & Larochelle, 2016; Verma et al., 2020). Li et al. (2018) also adopts MAML-based meth-
167
      ods to improve cross-domain generalization. MetaGAN (Zhang et al., 2018) and MetaDiff (Zhang
168
      & Yu, 2023) combines MAML respectively with GAN (Goodfellow et al., 2020) and diffusion
169   model (Song & Ermon, 2019; Ho et al., 2020) to facilitate few-shot image classification. More
170   recently, meta learning has been employed to accelerate implicit neural representation for visual
171   signals (Sitzmann et al., 2020; Tancik et al., 2021).

      3.1   A LGORITHM D ESIGN
179
180   In this section, we propose our algorithm Meta ControlNet, which maintains the Stable Diffusion
181   network while training its duplicates via the task-agnostic meta learning technique for obtaining
182   adaptive model initial.
183
      In particular, Meta ControlNet adopts three control tasks (HED, Segmentation, Depth) as the primary
184
      meta tasks. The training of Meta ControlNet takes the double-loop training framework of FO-
185   MAML (Finn et al., 2017), as depicted in Figure 2, and is described in detail as follows.
186
187   The inner-loop training of Meta ControlNet takes finetune steps separately for each task. During each
188
      step t, the meta parameter θt of Meta ControlNet is finetuned independently for each task based on
      gradient descent as follows:
189
190                                 (Inner Loop)    θttask = θt − α∇Ltask (θt ),
191
      where task ∈ {HED, Seg, Depth} and α represents the step size. Note that we here update the
192
      parameter only once in the inner loop to enhance efficiency.
193
194   The outer-loop training first calculates the meta gradient ∇L(θt ) as follows by taking an average of
195   the gradients across all tasks, based on each task’s finetuned parameters in the inner loop:
196                                    ∇L(θt ) = Avgtask (∇Ltask (θttask )),
197
198   where "Avg" denotes the averaging operator over all task gradients. Then the meta parameter θt is
199
      updated using the meta gradient as follows:
200                                   (Outer Loop) θt+1 = θt − α∇L(θt ),
201
      where α is the step size. This design guides Meta ControlNet to minimize the loss of the finetuned
202
      model for each task, and hence makes the model more responsive to updates and enables fast
203
      adaptability.
204
205   Novel Layer Freezing Design: A main novel component that Meta ControlNet features is the design
206   of freezing layers during training, which turns out to be critical for its superior performance. Typically,
207
      meta learning algorithms such as ANIL (Raghu et al., 2019) freeze the earlier embedding layers
      during the inner-loop training. Meta ControlNet has sharp differences in two aspects. Firstly, Meta
208
      ControlNet freezes latter encoder block and the middle block during meta training. This idea is
209
      based on the observation that the initial encoder blocks are directly linked to the control images of
210   control tasks. Given that our Stable Diffusion initial was used to process Gaussian noise rather than
211   control task-specific inputs, and different control image styles signify distinct tasks, it is essential to
212   finetune these initial encoder blocks for individual tasks. On the other hand, the middle and latter
213   encoder blocks, which capture common and high-level information, can be retained and shared across
214   tasks. Therefore, during the training process, Meta ControlNet selectively freezes the Encoder Block4
215   and Middle block of the U-Net, and focuses on training the remaining parameters. The detailed
      architecture is available in Appendix. Secondly, unlike ANIL, where freezing occurs only in the

304   Dataset: We use the generated CLIP-filtered dataset proposed by InstructPix2Pix (Brooks et al.,

      Implementations: Meta ControlNet is developed using the ControlNet codebase (Zhang et al., 2023),
308
      and utilizes the Stable Diffusion v1.5 checkpoint for finetuning. We fix learning rate to be 1 × 10−4
309   and batch size to be 256, and accumulate gradients over every 4 batches. Our model is trained on 4
310   Nvidia A100 GPUs. In our study, both our Meta ControlNet and the baseline of Prompt Diffusion
311   (PD) (Wang et al., 2023) are evaluated at the 8000-step checkpoint. Note that more finetuning steps
312   enhance image quality.
313

314
      testing phase in the outer loop for each task in order to optimize memory efficiency. Note that the
315   aforementioned batch size of 256 is the total number of images from all tasks. Namely, we randomly
316   sample 256 images across all tasks, and organize them into batches respectively for each task. This
317   strategy ensures that our algorithm does not require additional image sample during the training
318   phase.

454
455   5.1   L AYER F REEZING
456
      To evaluate the effectiveness of various freezing designs, we conduct an experimental comparison
457
      focusing on the canny and normal tasks in a zero-shot setting. The experiment compares our freezing
458
      strategy (freezing Encoder Block 4 and the Middle block in U-Net) against other methods: freezing
459   Encoder Blocks 2 to 4 plus the Middle block, freezing Encoder Blocks 1 to 3, and no freezing. The
460   U-Net architecture is available in Appendix. The comprehensive results are detailed in Figure 9, with
461   all methods under identical experimental conditions.
462
      Our results indicate that while all freezing designs facilitate control ability, freezing Encoder Block 4
463
      and the Middle block achieves the most accurate alignment with the control image and the highest
464
      image fidelity. This superiority is likely because the first three encoder blocks in U-Net are more
465   task-specific, as they are more directly connected to the control image. In contrast, the latter encoder
466   block and the Middle block contribute significantly to the high-quality image output inherent in stable
467   diffusion, making them ideal candidates for freezing across diverse tasks.
468
469
      5.2   D ECODER C ONNECTION
470
471   We evaluate our algorithm using different types of connections between the ControlNet decoder and
472   the pre-trained Stable Diffusion (SD) model decoder. Specifically, we use Meta ControlNet with all
473   decoder connected by zero convolution as our baseline. We then disconnect Decoder Blocks 4 to 1
474   from the pre-trained SD model in separate variants (each Decoder Block n corresponds to Encoder
475   Block n). These variants are trained under the same conditions as the baseline, up to the 8000-step
476   checkpoint, and are then tested on normal and canny edge tasks. Figure 10 depicts the result and
477
      suggests that disconnecting Decoder Block 4 significantly reduces image fidelity, and often results in
      images with repetitive lines or objects with strange shapes. In contrast, disconnecting the other three
478
      blocks produces results similar to our baseline. This indicates that Decoder Block 4 plays a critical
479
      role in ensuring high image fidelity, which is consistent with our design choice of freezing Encoder
480   Block 4 for this purpose.

483   6     C ONCLUSION
484
485   In our study, we propose a novel Meta ControlNet approach, which adopts the meta learning technique
      and features novel freezing layer design to learn a generalizable ControlNet initial. This method

      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


486
      exhibits rapid training convergence, and requires only 1000 steps to effectively control generative
487   imaging. Further, such a meta initial exhibits remarkable zero-shot adaptability for edge-based tasks,
488   the first demonstration in this domain. It also excels in more challenging non-edge tasks, and adapts
489   rapidly within 100 steps for the human pose task and 200 steps for the human pose map task. These
490   results not only outperform existing baselines in terms of control ability and efficiency but also
491   represent significant advancement beyond vanilla ControlNet.

      A.1   D ETAILED M ETA C ONTROL N ET A RCHITECTURE
705
706   In Figure 11, the detailed Meta ControlNet architecture is illustrated. This architecture shows that
707   during both training and testing phases, the stable diffusion part remain locked, in line with the
708   ControlNet settings. Specifically, for the ControlNet part, SD Encoder Block4 and SD Middle Block
709   are frozen during the meta training phase, while other blocks are subject to finetuning. This approach
710   is chosen because SD Encoder Blocks 1-3 are more closely linked to the control image, necessitating
711   their adaptation to capture task-specific differences. During the meta testing phase, all blocks within
712   the ControlNet part are finetuned to optimize performance.
