011                                               A BSTRACT
012
013            Although trust region policy optimization methods have achieved a lot of success
014            in cooperative multi-agent tasks, most of them face a non-stationarity problem
015            during the learning process. Recently, sequential trust region methods that up-
016
               date policies agent-by-agent have shed light on alleviating the non-stationarity
               problem. However, these methods are still less sample-efficient when compared
017
               to their counterparts (i.e., PPO) in a single-agent setting. To narrow this effi-
018
               ciency gap, we propose the Off-Policyness-aware Sequential Policy Optimization
019            (OPSPO) method, which explicitly manages the off-policyness that arises from
020            the sequential policy update process among multiple agents. We prove that our
021            OPSPO has the tightness of the monotonic improvement bound compared with
022            other trust region multi-agent learning methods. Finally, we demonstrate that our
023            OPSPO consistently outperforms strong baselines under challenging multi-agent
024            benchmarks, including StarCraftII micromanagement tasks, Multi-agent MuJoCo,
025            and Google Research Football full game scenarios.

028   1   I NTRODUCTION
029
030   Trust region learning (Kakade & Langford, 2002), as a class of policy gradient methods (Sutton
031   et al., 1999; Silver et al., 2014), have played an important role in recent advances in sinelg-agent re-
032   inforcement learning (Schulman et al., 2015; Haarnoja et al., 2018). Trust Region Policy Optimiza-
033
      tion (TRPO) (Schulman et al., 2015) and its variant Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO) (Schulman
      et al., 2017) have been widely used in many fields (Mahmood et al., 2018; Baker et al., 2019;
034
      Todorov et al., 2012) and achieved impressive experimental performance (Duan et al., 2016; Kurach
035
      et al., 2020). The effectiveness of trust region methods mainly stems from their theoretically guar-
036   anteed policy optimization process. Specifically, by restricting the policy optimization to a smaller
037   neighborhood of the current policy, trust region learning obtains a guarantee of monotonic perfor-
038   mance improvement at every iteration.
039
      Recently, many works that adopt trust region learning to multi-agent reinforcement learning
040
      (MARL) have been proposed, such as methods that use trust region learning to update each agentвАЩs
041
      policy independently (De Witt et al., 2020; Yu et al., 2022), as well as methods that coordinate policy
042   updates between agents using trust region learning (Wu et al., 2021). However, these methods update
043   the agents simultaneously, that is, all agents perform policy optimization at the same time and can
044   not observe the change of other agents, which leads to the non-stationarity problem (Hernandez-Leal
045   et al., 2017) during training and hurts performance. To this end, recent works (Kuba et al., 2021;
046   Wang et al., 2023) have proposed sequential trust region learning, which uses trust region learning to
047   sequentially execute agent-by-agent policy optimization. Sequential updates allow the later updated
048   agents to use changes made by preceding agents to optimize their own policies (Bertsekas, 2019),
049   thus stabilizing training.
050   However, the joint monotonic bounds of these trust region learning methods in MARL, both for
051   simultaneous and sequential updates, are much looser than that of their counterpart (i.e., PPO) in
052   the single agent setting, which leads to sample inefficiency (Li et al., 2022; Wang et al., 2023).
053   In this paper, we take a step toward narrowing this gap. We propose the Off-Policyness-aware
      Sequential Policy Optimization (OPSPO) method, which enjoys the tightness of the joint mono-


                                                         1
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


054
      tonic improvement bound compared with other trust region learning methods in MARL (see Tab. 1
055   and Theorem 2). Our key idea is to explicitly handle the off-policyness introduced by the sequen-
056   tial policy update process among multiple agents. Specifically, we start with a vanilla extension of
057   TRPO in MARL, and then significantly improve the joint monotonic improvement bound by per-
058   forming off-policy corrections on the state distribution and advantage estimation, respectively (see
059   Sec. 3.2). Moreover, we also propose clip range correction which corrects the clip range in clipping-
060   based surrogate objective according to the degree of off-policy (see Sec. 3.3), which further im-
061   proves the performance of our practical algorithm. We test our OPSPO on three popular cooperative
062   multi-agent benchmarks: StarCraftII (SMAC) (Samvelyan et al., 2019), multi-agent MuJoCo (MA-
063
      MuJoCo) (de Witt et al., 2020), and Google Research Football (GRF) full game scenarios (Kurach
      et al., 2020). On all benchmark tasks, our OPSPO consistently outperforms strong baselines with a
064
      large margin in both performance and sample efficiency.
065
066   In summary, we make three contributions: (i) We propose a novel sequential trust region learning
067   method in MARL, which explicitly handles the off-policyness caused by the sequential policy update
068   process among multiple agents. We further prove that our method enjoys the tightness of the joint
069
      monotonic improvement bound compared with other trust region learning methods in MARL; (ii)
      We propose a practical clipping-based algorithm with clip-range off-policy correction that can fur-
070
      ther improve performance; and (iii) Our method significantly outperforms the previous trust region
071
      learning methods on three challenging multi-agent benchmarks, including SMAC, MA-MuJoCo,
072   and GRF.

150   Trust Region Methods in MARL Next, we briefly review recent works that extend the trust

152   is a variant of PPO with centralized critics. (Wu et al., 2021) proposes Coordinate PPO (CoPPO),

158
      the simultaneous trust region policy optimization methods, such as MAPPO and CoPPO. Agent-by-

159
      considering the update orders of agents.

500   5     R ELATED W ORK
501
502   Trust Region Policy Optimization (Schulman et al., 2015) and Proximal Policy Optimization (Schul-
503   man et al., 2017) are popular trust region methods in the single-agent scenario, which have strong
504   performance mainly due to the guarantee of monotonic policy improvement (Kakade & Langford,
505   2002). In multi-agent scenarios, De Witt et al. (2020) and Papoudakis et al. (2020) empirically
506   study the performance of directly applying PPO to each agent in multi-agent tasks, and show the
507   inability of native extensions. Their work provides inspiration for subsequent research work. Yu
508   et al. (2022) propose Multi-agent PPO by introducing shared critics and many stable training tech-
509   niques, and demonstrate strong performance on a large number of multi-agent tasks. Furthermore,
510
      Wu et al. (2021) propose Coordinated Proximal Policy Optimization by considering the value de-
      composition (Sunehag et al., 2017) and coordinated adaptation of step size during the policy update
511
      process among agents, and prove the monotonic improvement guarantee. In addition, there are many
512
      other works (Wen et al., 2022; Li & He, 2023; Sun et al., 2022) that also discuss trust region methods
513   in MARL scenarios. However, these MARL algorithms suffer from non-stationarity issues due to
514   the simultaneous updating of agents. From the perspective of one agent, the environment dynamics
515   change because other agents also change their policies. As a result, the agent suffers from high
516   variance in gradients and requires more samples to converge (Hernandez-Leal et al., 2017). To this
517   end, sequential updating rather than simultaneous updating has received increasing attention from
518   researchers. Sequential update allows the later updated agents to use changes made by preceding
519   agents to optimize their own policies (Bertsekas, 2019), which makes the environment faced by later
520   agents more stable. Kuba et al. (2021) propose Heterogeneous PPO which combines the sequential
521
      update scheme (Bertsekas, 2019) with trust region methods, and demonstrates experimentally and
      theoretically the advantages offered by sequential updating. Wang et al. (2023) further propose
522
      Agent-by-agent Policy Optimization (A2PO) which systematically studies the impact of agent up-
523
      date order on performance and improves the theoretical basis of previous work (Kuba et al., 2021).
524
525   The most relevant work to ours is A2PO, which also improves TRPO under sequential policy op-
526   timization. A2PO mainly focuses on the impact of agent update order on policy optimization. In
527
      contrast, our work is entirely from an off-policy perspective and achieves the tightest bound to date
      by making off-policy corrections in three aspects: state distribution, advantage estimation, and clip
528
      range.

531   6     C ONCLUSION
532
533
      In this paper, we focus on trust region learning in the sequential policy optimization for cooperative
534   multi-agent tasks. We introduce OPSPO, a sequential policy optimization method that explicitly
535   handles the off-policyness caused by the sequential policy update process among agents. We prove
536   that the joint monotonic bound achieved by our OPSPO is the tightest compared to existing trust
537   region MARL methods. Experiments in various benchmarks demonstrate that OPSPO consistently
538   outperforms several strong baselines in performance and sample efficiency in complex tasks. For
539   future work, we plan to continue along the key idea of off-policy correction to improve the broader
      MARL methods.
