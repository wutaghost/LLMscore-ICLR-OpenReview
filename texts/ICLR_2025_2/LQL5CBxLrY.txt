009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011
               Although informal evaluations of modern LLMs can be found on social media,
012
               blogs, and news outlets, a formal and comprehensive comparison among them has
013            yet to be conducted. In response to this gap, we have undertaken an extensive
014            benchmark evaluation of LLMs and conversational bots. Our evaluation involved
015            the collection of 1002 questions encompassing 27 categories, which we refer to as
016            the “Wordsmiths dataset.” These categories include reasoning, logic, facts, coding,
017            bias, language, humor, and more. Each question in the dataset is accompanied by
018            an accurate and verified answer. We meticulously assessed four leading chatbots:
019            ChatGPT, GPT-4, Bard, and Claude, using this dataset. The results of our evaluation
020            revealed the following key findings: a) GPT-4 emerged as the top-performing
021
               chatbot across almost all categories, achieving a success rate of 84.1%. On the other
               hand, Bard faced challenges and achieved a success rate of 62.4%. b) Among the
022
               four models evaluated, one of them responded correctly approximately 93% of the
023
               time. However, all models were correct only about 44%. c) Bard is less correlated
024            with other models while ChatGPT and GPT-4 are highly correlated in terms of
025            their responses. d) Chatbots demonstrated proficiency in language understanding,
026            facts, and self-awareness. However, they encountered difficulties in areas such as
027            math, coding, IQ, and reasoning. e) In terms of bias, discrimination, and ethics
028            categories, models generally performed well, suggesting they are relatively safe to
029            utilize. To make future model evaluations on our dataset easier, we also provide a
030            multiple-choice version of it (called Wordsmiths-MCQ). Dataset link: [MASKED]

033   1    I NTRODUCTION
034
035   The creation of LLMs and chatbots is on the rise, with both big companies and startups actively
036   developing them Brown et al. (2020); Cao et al. (2023); Zhao et al. (2023). One notable example
037   is Anthropic’s recent introduction of Claude Bai et al. (2022), illustrating the growing trend. This
038   indicates that more chatbots are likely to emerge, serving general or specific purposes. However,
039
      despite the considerable public interest and informal testing, there remains a lack of a comprehensive
      and unified quantitative evaluation for these systems. Our objective is to address this gap by comparing
040
      four prominent chatbots: OpenAI’s ChatGPT and GPT-4, Google’s Bard, and Anthropics’ Claude.
041
      To conduct this evaluation, we have gathered a substantial collection of questions from various
042   categories, such as logic, humor, and ethics. These categories encompass a wide range of tasks that
043   assess the intelligence and cognitive abilities of both LLMs and humans.
044
045
      Our work distinguishes itself from existing benchmarks in three significant ways. Firstly, unlike
      the current benchmarks that have limited scope and narrow targeting, often focusing on a single or
046
      a few specific capabilities of language models, our benchmark covers a broad range of questions
047
      from various categories, similar to Borji (2023a); Bubeck et al. (2023). This approach allows us
048   to better identify new and unexpected capabilities that LLMs may develop as their scale increases
049   and to provide a comprehensive understanding of their current breadth of capabilities Sejnowski
050   (2023); Mitchell & Krakauer (2023); Borji (2023b). Secondly, many existing benchmarks rely on
051   data collected through human labeling, which is often performed by individuals who are not experts
052   or the authors of the task. The process of data labeling presents challenges and costs that can impact
053   the difficulty of the chosen tasks. These benchmarks tend to prioritize tasks that are easy to explain
      and perform, resulting in potential issues with noise, correctness, and distributional problems that

      can hinder the interpretability of the results. We have dedicated numerous hours to meticulously
055   assess model responses. Thirdly, in contrast to the majority of typical NLP benchmarks that primarily
056   focus on multiple-choice questions, we conduct evaluations in a more comprehensive manner. Our
057   approach involves a manual examination of model responses, carefully scrutinizing their accuracy
058   and verifying if they correctly select the appropriate response from a set of multiple choices.
059
      To advance future research, anticipate potentially disruptive new capabilities of LLMs, and mitigate
060
      any harmful societal effects, it is crucial to have a clear understanding of the current and upcoming
061   capabilities and limitations of these models. In this regard, we offer the following contributions.
062
063          • In order to support research focused on the quantitative and qualitative comparison between
064            humans and LLMs, we have gathered a dataset of more than 1K questions spanning various
065            domains. Four prominent LLMs, namely ChatGPT, GPT-4, Claude, and Bard are evalu-
066            ated. We have conducted extensive human evaluations of the answers provided by models,
067            resulting in a detailed and comprehensive analysis.
068
             • Throughout the paper, we consistently highlight any limitations we have identified. Ad-

069
               enables us to focus our research efforts in the most promising directions by gaining a deeper
070
               understanding of the areas that require improvement or further investigation.
071
             • We assess the similarity and correlation among models and utilize these correlations to show

073          • To support future research endeavors, we have made our collected comparison corpus,
074            evaluations, and analysis code openly accessible. Additionally, we provide a smaller set of
075            multiple-choice questions specifically designed to facilitate easy and programmatic model
076            comparisons. We also provide a set of questions organized by difficulty level.

081
082   The introduction of LLMs has brought about a revolution in the field of dialogue and text gen-
083   eration Brown et al. (2020); Chowdhery et al. (2022); Shoeybi et al. (2019); Zhou et al. (2023).
084   Notably, the public release of ChatGPT in November 2022 and Bard in March 2023 has generated
085   significant interest and sparked extensive experimentation on social media platforms. ChatGPT,
086
      based on the GPT-3.5 architecture, is widely recognized for its exceptional capacity to generate
      coherent and human-like responses. On the other hand, Bard utilizes Google’s LaMDA Thoppilan
087
      et al. (2022), which enables it to handle a wide range of language-related tasks and provide detailed
088
      information. It’s worth noting that advancements in LLMs continue to unfold rapidly, exemplified by
089   models like GPT-4 OpenAI (2023), BlenderBot, Galactica, LLaMA (FAIR) Touvron et al. (2023),
090   Alpaca (Stanford), BloombergGPT Wu et al. (2023), LaMDA/Bard (Google), Chinchilla (DeepMind),
091   and Palm Chowdhery et al. (2022); Anil et al. (2023), among others. These models have played a
092   significant role in reshaping the natural language processing landscape. They offer unprecedented
093   opportunities for communication, creativity, and information retrieval. Some of these models even
094   possess the ability to retrieve information from the internet (GPT-4 integrated with MS Bing). For
095   reviews of the topic, please see Zhao et al. (2023); Cao et al. (2023); Dwivedi et al. (2023); Liu et al.

      All models in our study accept text-only inputs. We fed the questions manually into the chatbot’s
209
      input box to obtain the answers. As the answers generated by the chatbots can be influenced by
210   the conversation history, we refreshed the thread for each question to ensure unbiased responses.
211   For ChatGPT, we observed that it can generate different answers for the same question in different
212   threads, likely due to the random sampling involved in the decoding process. However, we found that
213   the differences between these answers were often minimal, leading us to collect only one answer for
214   most questions. In cases where models generated multiple answers (e.g. Bard), we selected the first
215   response for our evaluation. We utilized the preview platform at https://chat.openai.com/
      (February and May versions) for ChatGPT, the chat service accessible via MS Bing https://

      4.2     A NNOTATION PROCEDURE
222
223   This study does not aim to observe and study human behavior or average human responses. Instead,
224   we sought expert assessments of the model outputs. For specific categories like Math and coding, we
225   required annotators with relevant knowledge, so we selected experts in those areas. However, for
226   categories such as humor and bias, evaluation may be more subjective.
227
      Five annotators actively participated in the annotation process as AI researchers with expertise in
228   LLMs and research concerns. Precise guidelines and illustrative question-answer evaluations were
229   furnished to ensure an impartial assessment of answer accuracy, avoiding personal biases. Any
230   uncertainties, particularly with subjective questions, were addressed through team discussions during
231   regular meetings. The annotators were from diverse countries, including the United States, Italy,
232   Iran, and India, with ages ranging from 23 to 43. All of them were well-educated, with four males
233   and one female among them. Each annotator identified and explained concerns with uncertain
234   or problematic questions. The labeled questions were then reviewed by others who shared their
235   opinions. Collaboratively, the group made decisions to remove, replace, or re-categorize the questions.
236
      Questions were removed if a consensus on an appropriate answer was lacking, if they were excessively
      offensive, or if they did not align with predefined categories. Special attention was given to vague or
237
      overly offensive questions, particularly in the bias and discrimination or humor categories.
238
239
240   4.3     R ATING MODEL ANSWERS
241
      To evaluate the performance of models, we adopt a specific methodology wherein a score is assigned
242
      based on the accuracy of their responses. A score of 2 is given when the chatbot provides the correct
243
      answer, a score of 1 is assigned for a partially correct response, and a score of 0 indicates a completely
244   incorrect answer. It is worth noting that in cases where a partial answer is given, human judgment
245   becomes necessary. Some categories, such as “wit and humor” and “language understanding” include
246   questions that lack definitive answers. For subjective answers, a consensus among all raters was
247   reached to determine the appropriate score (i.e. discussion followed by a majority vote among the
248   five raters). For “bias and discrimination”, a score of 0 signifies the presence of bias in the chatbot’s
249   responses, while a score of 2 indicates an absence of bias. For some questions in this category,
250   when a model chose to decline to provide an answer (which was frequently observed with Bard), we
251   considered it an acceptable response. In coding assessments, questions that were logically accurate
252
      but failed to compile were also assigned a score of one.

448   4.6   M ULTIPLE CHOICE QUESTIONS (W ORDSMITHS -MCQ)
449
450
      To make it easy and programmatic for future comparison of models, here we devise subsets of our
      dataset for which a question is accompanied by answers of several models. The plan would be to
451
      submit the question along with the answers to a new model and ask the model to choose the right
452
      answer. This way models can be evaluated automatically instead of humans checking the answers.
453   To this end, we consider the following scenarios: I: those questions for which only one model is
454   correct (i.e. 1 correct, 3 incorrect). Here, the correct answer would be considered the true answer,
455   and other models’ answers would be alternative wrong answers (chance = 25%), II: two models
456   are correct and two are incorrect. In this case, one of the correct answers is randomly chosen
457   along with two other answers that would form the choices, thus, in this case, we only have three
458   choices (chance = 33%), and III: three models are correct, and one is incorrect. In this case, we
459   randomly choose a correct answer and pair it with the incorrect answer. Here, there are only two
460   alternatives leading to chance level = 50%. Notice that here we deliberately do not use the ground
461
      truth answers since they are very short and to the point. It is worth noting that the sets mentioned
      above are mutually exclusive. The total number of questions in this dataset is 388. The statistics
462
      over each category and all subcategories for each of the cases above are shown in the supplementary
463
      material. The performance of models on this lightweight evaluation set is shown in Table 3. Bard
464   achieves the highest accuracy among models. ChatGPT and Claude did poorly and most of the time
465   below chance. Overall, models perform poorly on this dataset.

467   4.7   I NSTANCES OF QUALITATIVE FAILURES
468   We prompted the models with the task of “Producing TikZ code that draws a dog and the letter Q,”
469   resulting in the shapes depicted in Fig. 7. Both ChatGPT and GPT-4 generated drawings that captured
470   the semantic essence of the prompt. This was a test of spatial reasoning in models. In another test, we
471   evaluated the models’ understanding of physical rules and their grasp of underlying realities. They
472   were asked to determine the direction of rotation of gears when arranged horizontally or in a circle,
473   as depicted in Fig. 8. ChatGPT and GPT-4 successfully solved the first version of the task. However,
474   the second version resulted in a physically implausible situation, yet none of the models were able to
475   detect this inconsistency. When asked Q 4.25: Does a man-eating shark eat women, too? Bard’s
476
      answer was: “No, man-eating sharks do not eat women, too.” In certain instances, models provided
      accurate explanations but failed to generate the correct final answer (most common in math and
477
      coding). Conversely, in some other cases, they produced the correct final answer but the explanation
478
      provided was incorrect.

      5    D ISCUSSION AND C ONCLUSION
487
488
      To properly assess and compare these LLMs, it is crucial to establish robust and comprehensive
489   benchmarks, such as the one presented here. Having independent test sets and benchmarks can help
490   understand LLMs better.
491
492
      Which LLM is better? Our investigation shows that GPT-4 wins over most of the categories. However,
      it loses to other models in a few categories. LLMs are powerful AI models with their own strengths
493
      and weaknesses. Thus, the choice may depend on the specific needs and use cases.
494
495   Based on our correlation analyses, it is feasible to construct ensemble models that surpass the
496   performance of individual models. We consider this an encouraging avenue for future research. Also,
497   we recommend that future endeavors in benchmark construction for LLM evaluation follow our
498
      approach, particularly by utilizing the categories we have defined.
499   The present evaluation of LLMs has predominantly concen-
500   trated on text comprehension. Nevertheless, our work, along-
501   side prior research (e.g. Bubeck et al. (2023)), has demonstrated
502   that LLMs possess the ability to understand and handle multi-
503   modal information, despite being trained exclusively on text.
504
      It is anticipated that more multi-modal models will emerge in
      the coming years. Consequently, there is a growing need for
505
      comprehensive benchmarks, such as Mahowald et al. (2023),
506
      to evaluate such models.
507
508   Apart from English, we have questions from 5 other languages,
509   namely Farsi, Chinese, Japanese, Russian, and Taiwanese. We
510
      were able to observe some interesting patterns. For example,
      Bard responded with fixed templates over and over again for Figure 8: Qs 1.3.12&1.3.13. If gear
511
      most of the questions that were not English, such as this one: 3 is rotated clockwise, in which direc-
512                                                                      tion will gears 1 and 6 rotate? (top),
      “As an LLM, I am trained to understand and respond only to in which direction would gear 7 rotate?
513   a subset of languages at this time and can’t provide assistance (bottom).
514   with that. For a current list of supported languages, please refer
515   to the Bard Help Center.” Although our analysis demonstrates satisfactory performance of the LLMs
516   across multiple languages, a comprehensive evaluation of this aspect requires further examination.
517
      Regarding ethical considerations, we have taken great care to avoid including offensive or strongly
518
      biased responses. Nevertheless, for comprehensive coverage, we found it necessary to include certain
519   questions that explore the ethical aspects of models. For a thorough analysis of safety concerns
520   related to LLMs, please see Wei et al. (2023a).
521
522
      We believe that our findings can serve as a guide for future research on comparing LLMs and chatbots
      across a broader range of questions and categories. Our work also opens up new opportunities for
523
      developing more formal and comprehensive methods for testing and analyzing AI systems with more
524
      general intelligence (i.e. AGI Chollet (2019); Bubeck et al. (2023)).
