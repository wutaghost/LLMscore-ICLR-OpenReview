009                                              A BSTRACT
010
011            As context lenghts grows, the increasing size of Key and Value (KV) cache poses
012            a significant challenge to efficiently serving Large Language Models (LLMs).
013            KV cache pruning, by preserving only a small subset of important KV cache for
014            sparse inference, is a recognized effective solution. Our research revealed that
015            large activations are the key to identifying these important KV cache. However,
016            existing methods have not been successful in effectively identifying these important
017            KV cache due to neglecting the impact of Value cache, and are also incompatible
018
               with Grouped-Query Attention (GQA) architectures. To address these issues, we
               introduce an innovative KV cache pruning method that preserves these large activa-
019
               tions and is compatible with Grouped-Query Attention. Featuring a novel pruning
020
               metric, this method operates within each attention group to enhance efficiency and
021            minimize performance degradation. Experimental results demonstrate that our
022            approach not only maintains comparable accuracy with existing methods but also
023            significantly reduces KV cache requirements. Specifically, It demonstrates similar
024            accuracy while utilizing only 1/10 of the KV cache compared to existing SOTA
025            methods.

028   1   I NTRODUCTION
029
030   Large Language Models (LLMs) have garnered significant attention due to their remarkable perfor-
031   mance across various applications, such as ChatGPT (OpenAI, 2022), Claude (Anthropic, 2024) and
032   Gemini (Team, 2024). However, as the context lengths utilized by these models increase, serving them
033
      efficiently becomes an increasingly challenging task. For example, the Llama-2-7B model (Touvron
      et al., 2023), when configured with a batch size of 16 to handle a context length of 32K, requires 256
034
      GiB of VRAM solely for storing its KV cache and an additional 28 GiB for model weights. Given
035
      that the most advanced GPUs currently available offer a maximum of 80 GiB of VRAM, this results
036   in substantial inference costs due to the latency involved in accessing large amounts of KV cache and
037   the excessive consumption of valuable storage resources.
038
039
      Pruning KV cache is a pivotal approach to efficient LLM serving. Specifically, during the prefill
      phase, LLMs generate extensive KV cache, and existing pruning methods utilize various strategies
040
      to assess the importance of KV cache, subsequently evicting the majority and retaining only an
041
      important subset for sparse inference computations during the generation phase. Our analysis has
042   revealed that the key to KV cache pruning is preserving large activations. Recent studies (Sun et al.,
043   2024) have indicated that large activations have a significant impact on model inference results and
044   remove these large activations will lead to severe performance degradation. However, current methods
045   do not adequately identify the important KV cache because they focus only on large activations
046   within Key cache, neglecting those in the Value cache, leading to notable performance degradation.
047   Our experiments indicate that large activations in the Value cache also have a significant impact on
048   inference results. Furthermore, existing pruning methods are also incompatible with mainstream
049   models employing more efficient Grouped-Query Attention (Ainslie et al., 2023) architectures, such
050
      as Llama-3 (Dubey et al., 2024) and Mistral (Jiang et al., 2023).
051   To address these issues, we propose a novel KV cache pruning method, named SlimKV, which ensure
052   the retention of large activations within the KV cache while supporting both Multi-Head Attention
053   (MHA) and Grouped-Query Attention (GQA) architectures. Specifically, we introduce a new KV
      cache pruning metric that identifies large activations in both Key and Value cache and calculate the


                                                        1

054
      importance of KV cache within each group, preserving these important KV cache during inference to
055   maintain optimal model performance.
056
057   We conducted extensive experiments on various long-context LLM generation tasks using popular
058
      models with different attention architectures, including MHA and GQA. The experimental results
      demonstrate that our method not only reduces the storage requirements of the KV cache but also
059
      maintains superior model performance. Specifically, It demonstrates similar accuracy while utilizing
060
      only 1/10 of the KV cache compared to existing SOTA methods.

085   Here, Qh , K g , and V g stand for the query, key, and value vectors, respectively, which are fundamental
086   components of the attention layer. The attention weights Ah are calculated by taking the dot product
087   of the query vectors Qh with the transposed key                g

090   value vectors V g by the computed attention weights Ah . MHA can be regarded as a special case of
091

093   2.2   I MPORTANCE O F L ARGE ACTIVATIONS IN KV C ACHE
094
      Large activations refer to the neuron outputs in LLMs that have high absolute values. Recent
095
      studies (Sun et al., 2024) have indicated that removing these large activations in LLMs can lead to a
096   significant reduction in model performance, thus sustain large activations in KV cache is essential for
097   maintaining model performance during pruning. The significance of large activations within the KV
098   cache during the pruning process is profound, primarily due to their substantial impact on the outputs
099   of models. These large activations, present in both the Key and Value cache, wield considerable
100   influence over the resulting computational outcomes, and their exclusion can precipitate significant
101   performance degradations in models. We delve into how these large activations crucially influence
102   both the Key and Value cache.
103   Large Activations of Key Cache. Key cache is pivotal in shaping the distribution of attention logits.
104   Within the architecture of self-attention, Key cache interact with query vectors to compute attention
105   logits. When one specific Key cache is important, it profoundly affects the softmax computation,
106   resulting in elevated attention logits at specific positions. This increased attention indicates that the
107   associated value vectors, which are weighted according to these attention logits, will exert a stronger
      influence on the resultant output. Consequently, pruning large activations of Key cache can impair the


                                                         2
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


108
      model’s capacity to concentrate on essential aspects of the input data, potentially leading to significant
109   information loss and a decrement in model efficacy.
110
111   Large Activations of Value Cache. Similarly, large activations in the Value cache directly determine
112
      the magnitude of output components. The value vectors, when augmented by their corresponding
      attention logits, make a direct contribution to the final output. Thus, large activations in Vaue cache
113
      lead to more substantial contributions to output, particularly when aligned with high attention logits.
114
      These vital elements of Value cache contain essential information crucial for the accuracy and richness
115   of the model’s output. Neglecting these large activations during pruning could result in the loss of
116   critical output features, thereby diminishing the model’s overall effectiveness and accuracy.
117
118
119   2.3     L IMITATIONS OF E XISTING KV C ACHE P RUNING M ETHODS
120
      Current pruning methods for KV cache primarily focus on Key cache and tend to overlook Value
121
      cache that could impact model performance. Here, we explore the inherent limitations of these
122
      methods.
123
124   Neglect of Large Activations in the Value Cache. Existing approaches to KV cache pruning
125   typically utilize large activations in the attention logits as metric. This methodology, however, often
126
      overlooks large activations in the Value cache. The Value cache is feature of input data, and when
      this data, weighted by attention logits, significantly influences the output, its importance cannot be
127
      understated. By not considering large activations in Value cache, current methods might discard
128
      valuable information, potentially leading to a substantial decline in the accuracy and effectiveness of
129   the model’s output.
130
131   Incompatibility with Grouped-Query Attention. Traditional pruning methods generally prune KV
132
      cache on a per-head basis, which can lead to issues when dealing with architectures like GQA, where
      queries within a group share a common KV cache. This shared mechanism in GQA is designed to
133
      optimize processing efficiency and coherence across grouped queries, but it poses a challenge for
134
      typical pruning strategies. Since each head’s KV cache is independently assessed and pruned in
135   these traditional methods, they fail to accommodate the shared nature of KV caches in GQA settings,
136   leading to potential inefficiencies and inconsistencies in how information is retained or discarded
137   across groups.

140   3      D ESIGN OF S LIM KV
141
142   The core objective of SlimKV is to minimize the usage of the KV cache while ensuring the preser-
143   vation of model performance as much as possible. This necessitates identifying which KV cache
144   are important and retaining them. We hypothesize that not only the large activations of Key cache
145   determine the importance of KV cache but also the large activations in Value cache play a significant
146   role. Hence, a comprehensive metric needs to be designed to assess the importance of specific KV
147   cache.
148
149   3.1     G ROUPED -W ISE R ECOGNITION OF L ARGE ACTIVATIONS OF K EY C ACHE
150
151   To effectively identify Key cache with large activations, we employ a methodology that traces
152   back from large attention logits to their corresponding significant Key cache. Specifically, regions
153   with elevated attention logit values indicate a proportional impact on the model’s output, thereby
154   warranting the preservation of the Key cache associated with these logits. Consequently, our system
155   gives precedence to maintaining KV cache linked to these high-magnitude activations within the
156
      attention logits to ensure that essential contextual connections are retained during the pruning
      process. Moreover, to accommodate both GQA and MHA architectures, we introduce a grouped-wise
157
      voting mechanism to facilitate the recognition of large activations. By doing so, we curtail the
158
      quantity of KV caches during the generation phase, thus alleviating the computational load for LLMs
159   when processing extensive contexts. To delineate our methodology with precision, we propose the
160   subsequent definitions.
161
          • Prompt Length (Lprompt ): The total length of the input provided by the user.

      3.2   R ECOGNITION OF L ARGE ACTIVATIONS IN VALUE C ACHE
181
182   To identify important information in the Value cache, the most direct and effective strategy is to focus
183   on large activations, which are typically indicative of key components in the model’s output. These
184   large activations in the Value cache directly affect the magnitude of the output. The importance of
185   Value cache based on these large activations can be computed as follows:
186
187
188                                        S2 = max(|V |, dim = −1)                                        (4)
189
190   where S2 ∈ RLprefix represents the importance of the Value cache, and the max operation extracts the
191
      maximum magnitude from each Value cache. Here, V ∈ RLprefix ×dK denotes the Value matrix.
192   To comprehensively assess the importance of various KV caches, we propose an integrated metric
193   that combines the indicators S1 and S2 , which respectively reflect the importance of the Key and
194   Value cache. The overall importance of one specific KV cache is thus quantified by the product of
195   these two indicators:
196
197
                                                   S = S1 ∗ S2                                             (5)
198
199   This metric allows us to capture the joint impact of both the Key and Value components on the
200   significance of the KV cache within the system.

402   5      R ELATED W ORK
403
404   Efficient Attention. Efficient attention mechanisms have been developed to address the compu-
405   tational and memory inefficiencies of standard self-attention in LLMs. One such approach is the
406
      Grouped-Query Attention (GQA), which organizes queries into groups before computing the at-
      tention, significantly reducing the number of attention calculations required (Ainslie et al., 2023).
407
      Another variant is Multi-Query Attention (MQA), which extends the idea by allowing multiple
408
      queries to interact within the same attention framework (Shazeer, 2019). TopK Attention is an
409   adaptive mechanism that focuses only on the top-K most relevant keys for each query, reducing
410   the computational complexity from quadratic to linear with respect to the sequence length. This
411   method not only improves efficiency but also enhances model interpretability by focusing on the
412   most significant interactions (Gupta et al., 2021). Among the linear transformers, Mamba stands out
413   by utilizing a moving-average-based approach to bypass the need for the softmax calculation, thereby
414   linearizing the computational cost with respect to the input length. This model achieves comparable
415   or even superior performance to traditional attention models on several benchmark tasks (Gu and
416   Dao, 2024).
417   KV Cache Compression. Numerous previous works have sought to compress the KV cache by
418   selectively dropping KV pairs using various algorithms. StreamLLM (Xiao et al., 2024), for instance,
419   retains only the most recent tokens and attention sinks (the first few tokens), reducing the KV cache
420   size but potentially discarding important information carried by the middle tokens. The Heavy-
421   Hitter Oracle (H2O) (Zhang et al., 2023) introduces a policy that greedily drops KV pairs during
422   generation based on a scoring function derived from cumulative attention. Similarly, Adaptive KV
423
      Compression (FastGen) (Ge et al., 2023) employs a dual-phase algorithm that encompasses four KV
      cache compression policies. It initially identifies optimal policies through profiling results obtained
424
      from prompt encoding, then dynamically evicts caches during the generation phase based on these
425
      policies. ScissorHands (Liu et al., 2024) aims to identify and retain pivotal tokens that exhibit a
426   consistent attention weight pattern with previous token windows during generation steps. However,
427   these methods overlook the impact of the Value cache, focusing solely on the Key cache to retain
428   the most important KV pairs. Moreover, they are not applicable to efficient attention architectures
429   like Grouped-Query Attention and Multi-Query Attention, which are utilized by popular LLMs such
430   as Llama-3, Qwen-2, Mistral, Falcon, and others. The method proposed in this paper, referred to
431   as SlimKV, is capable of supporting efficient attention architectures while fully considering the
      preservation of important information in the KV cache.

433
434
      This paper presents a novel approach to KV cache pruning for Large Language Models (LLMs) that
435   addresses the critical challenge of efficiently managing increasing context lengths. Our proposed
436   method, SlimKV, effectively retains large activations in both Key and Value caches, thereby enhanc-
437   ing model performance while supporting both Multi-Head Attention (MHA) and Grouped-Query
438   Attention (GQA) architectures.
439
      Through extensive experimentation, we demonstrate that our method reduces more GPU memory us-
440
      age by than baseline methods, while maintaining comparable accuracy. This work not only highlights
441   the significance of preserving large activations during KV cache pruning but also contributes to the
442   ongoing efforts to optimize LLM serving, ensuring that these powerful models can be utilized more
443   efficiently in practical applications. Future research can build upon our findings to further enhance
444   the efficiency and performance of LLMs in diverse contexts.
