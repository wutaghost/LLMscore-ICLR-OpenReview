011                                               A BSTRACT
012
013            Toolpath smoothing and feedrate planning are key techniques in Computer Nu-
014            merical Control (CNC) machining, and play a significant role in machining accu-
015
               racy, efficiency, and tool life. Traditional methods typically decouple path smooth-
               ing from feedrate planning, without considering the kinematic constraints during
016
               the smoothing process. As a result, the subsequent feedrate planning process is
017
               subject to more stringent kinematic limitations, which hinders the achievement
018            of optimal speed execution. However, the integration of these two processes
019            presents a significant challenge due to severe complexity and nonlinearity of the
020            problem. Here, we propose a novel Reinforcement Learning (RL) based method,
021            termed KIRL, to address the integrated optimization problem. Experimental re-
022            sults demonstrate that KIRL can generate smoother trajectories and optimize ma-
023            chining time compared to traditional decoupled methods. To our best knowledge,
024            KIRL is the first RL-based method for solving the integrated toolpath smoothing
025            and feedrate planning optimization problem in CNC machining.

      1   I NTRODUCTION
028
029
      Computer Numerical Control (CNC) machining is a widely used manufacturing technique for pro-
030   ducing high-precision parts and components across various industries, including aerospace (Nab-
031   hani, 2001), automotive (Kim & Song, 2013), and medical devices (Lepasepp & Hurst, 2021). Tool-
032   path smoothing and feedrate planning are two critical factors that significantly impact machining
033   accuracy, efficiency, and tool life Zhang & Xu (2021). Toolpath smoothing aims to generate a
034   smooth and continuous trajectory for the cutting tool, while feedrate planning determines the speed
035   along the toolpath to minimize machining time while satisfying various kinematic constraints such
036   as maximum velocity, acceleration, and jerk (Altintas & Erkorkmaz, 2003; Beudaert et al., 2012).
037   In practice, toolpaths are commonly represented using G01 codes (Tulsyan & Altintas, 2015), which
038   consist of a series of continuous line segments. However, linear toolpaths have discontinuities in tan-
039   gent and curvature at the junctions, which typically result in low machining efficiency and machine
040   vibration (Zhao et al., 2013). To mitigate this issue, path smoothing methods are often employed to
041   create smooth transitions at each corner of the polyline path, followed by velocity planning along
042   the smoothed path curve.
043   However, the decoupled approach often yields suboptimal results, as the smoothed path may not
044   consider the machine tool’s kinematic constraints, limiting the achievable feedrate in the subsequent
045   planning stage. Recent studies have formulated the integration of toolpath smoothing and feedrate
046   planning into a holistic optimization problem to address this limitation (Yang et al., 2015; Lin et al.,
047   2019; Wu et al., 2023). By considering kinematic constraints during the smoothing process, the in-
048   tegrated approach generates toolpaths that are more suitable for high-speed execution. Nevertheless,
049   solving this integrated optimization problem is quite challenging due to its high nonconvexity and
050
      nonlinearity.
051   In this paper, we propose a novel approach called KIRL (Kinematics-Informed Reinforcement
052   Learning), which leverages Reinforcement Learning (RL) to solve the integrated toolpath smoothing
053   and feedrate planning problem in CNC machining. RL has emerged as a powerful machine learning
      technique for solving complex decision-making problems in diverse domains such as robotics, game

054
      playing, and autonomous driving (Silver et al., 2016; Mnih et al., 2015; Lillicrap et al., 2019). Here,
055   we formulate the integrated optimization problem as a Markov Decision Process (MDP), where each
056   state encapsulates the current kinematic and positional information of the tool, actions correspond to
057   adjustments in kinematic states for the next segment, and rewards are designed to balance machining
058   time and trajectory smoothness. We employ Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO) and Soft Actor-
059   Critic (SAC) (Haarnoja et al., 2018) to train RL agents that predict intermediate kinematic states.
060   Our experimental demonstrations show that KIRL can generate smoother trajectories and optimize
061   machining time effectively compared to traditional decoupled methods.
062   To summarize, the key contributions of this work are as follows:
063
064          • We propose KIRL, the first RL-based method for solving the integrated toolpath smoothing
065            and feedrate planning problem in CNC machining.
066          • We formulate the integrated optimization problem as an MDP and use PPO and SAC to
067            train RL agents to predict intermediate kinematic states.
068          • We demonstrate the effectiveness of KIRL in a series of simulated CNC machining tasks,
069            showing improved performance in trajectory smoothness and machining efficiency com-
070            pared to traditional methods.

481
482   4.4     L IMITATIONS AND F UTURE W ORK
483
484   Limitations. KIRL demonstrates promising results, but several limitations need to be addressed
485   for broader industrial adoption. First, the training process is slow due to the CPU-based simulation
      environment, resulting in long training times and low GPU utilization. Second, KIRL requires sep-


                                                       9
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


486
      arate training for each toolpath, which limits its scalability, especially in environments with many
487   unique toolpaths. Additionally, the current implementation only supports 2-axis machining, which
488   restricts its applicability to more advanced multi-axis operations. Lastly, KIRL has only been eval-
489   uated in simulation, with no real-world validation on physical CNC machines.
490
491   Future Work. Future efforts will focus on several key areas to overcome these limitations. First,
492   improving training efficiency will be critical—this could be achieved by incorporating parallel en-
493   vironment sampling to make better use of available computational resources and reduce training
494   times. Second, enabling KIRL to generalize across different toolpaths through transfer learning or
495   meta-learning could significantly improve scalability and reduce the need for retraining. Expanding
496   KIRL to support multi-axis machining, such as 5-axis and 6-axis operations, will enhance its indus-
497   trial relevance by addressing more complex machining tasks. Finally, real-world validation on actual
498
      CNC machines will be essential to assess KIRL’s performance, reliability, and potential challenges
      in practical settings.

501   5   C ONCLUSIONS
502
503   In this work, we introduced KIRL, a Reinforcement Learning-based approach for the integrated
504   optimization of toolpath smoothing and feedrate planning in CNC machining. By formulating the
505   problem as a Markov Decision Process and utilizing advanced RL algorithms like PPO and SAC,
506   KIRL effectively predicts intermediate kinematic states that balance machining efficiency with tra-
507   jectory smoothness. Experimental results demonstrated that KIRL outperforms traditional decou-
508   pled methods in generating smoother trajectories and optimizing machining time across various
509
      complex toolpaths.
