040                                              A BSTRACT
041
042           The current text-to-video (T2V) generation has made significant progress in syn-
043           thesizing realistic general videos, but it is still unexplored in identity-specific
044           human video generation with customized ID images. The key challenge lies in
045
              maintaining high ID fidelity consistently while preserving the original motion dy-
              namic and prompt following after the identity injection. Current video identity
046
              customization methods mainly rely on reconstructing given identity images on
047
              text-to-image models, which have a divergent distribution with the T2V model.
048           This process introduces a tuning-inference gap, leading to identity inaccuracy and
049           dynamic degradation. To tackle this problem, we propose a novel framework,
050           dubbed PersonalVideo, that applies direct supervision on videos synthesized by
051           the T2V model to bridge the gap. Specifically, we introduce a learnable Spatial
052           Identity Adapter under the supervision of pixel-space ID loss, which customizes
053           the specific identity and preserves the original T2V model’s abilities (e.g., motion
              dynamic and prompt following). Furthermore, we employ simulated prompt aug-

               mentation to reduce overfitting by supervising generated results in more semantic
055            scenarios, gaining good robustness even with only a single reference image avail-
056            able. Extensive experiments demonstrate our method’s superiority in delivering
057            high identity faithfulness while preserving the inherent video generation qualities
058            of the original T2V model, outshining prior approaches. Notably, our Person-
059            alVideo seamlessly integrates with pre-trained SD components, such as Control-
060            Net and style LoRA, requiring no extra tuning overhead.

063   1   I NTRODUCTION
064
065   Recently, there has been considerable scholarly interest in text-to-video (T2V) generation (Guo
066   et al., 2023; Wang et al., 2023a; Chen et al., 2024; Brooks et al., 2024), which allows for the produc-
067   tion of videos from user-defined textual descriptions. However, the identity-specific customization
068   of high-fidelity human videos has not been fully explored. It aims to customize a wide variety of
069
      engaging videos using a few users’ photos, allowing for personalized content creation that features
      them in different actions, scenes, or styles while maintaining high ID fidelity. Without the need
070
      for complex scene construction and tedious post-production special effects, this convenient way of
071
      video creation also has great potential in the film and television industry.
072
073   Identity customization has achieved significant advancements in the field of text-to-image (T2I) (Gal
074   et al., 2022; Ruiz et al., 2023; Ye et al., 2023; Li et al., 2024; Wang et al., 2024b; Guo et al., 2024).
075
      Typically, these methods use a reconstructive approach on provided reference images to inject the
      identity into the pre-trained T2I model during the customization. However, directly employing this
076
      strategy in video customization will lead to unsatisfied results due to two notable challenges:
077
078   1) Inserting consistent identity with high fidelity. Existing video customization methods (Ma
079   et al., 2024; Wei et al., 2024) naively use image reconstruction supervision during tuning to model a
080   customized T2I prior, which is then injected into the T2V model to generate identity-specific videos
081
      during inference. However, the distribution of the pre-trained T2V model often deviates from that
      of the pre-trained T2I model. Fig. 2 shows that the tuning-inference gap will lead to a degradation
082
      of learned identity. As humans are sensitive to facial features, higher fidelity and consistent identity
083
      are required in generated videos.
084
085   2) Preserving inherent motion dynamics and prompt following. During the customization, tun-
086   ing on limited static images will significantly shift the video prior of the pre-trained T2V model,
087
      making the generated videos tend to appear static and fail to follow the given prompts. Although
      some works (Wei et al., 2024; He et al., 2023; Chefer et al., 2024) solve this problem by requiring
088
      additional video input, it brings great inconvenience to users. As images from user input contain no
089
      video prior, it is important to preserve the original T2V model’s abilities.
090
091   To address these challenges, we propose a novel framework, dubbed PersonalVideo, for ID-specific
092   video generation that can achieve high ID fidelity and maintain original motion dynamics and
093
      prompt following with only a few images of an identity given. Based on a pre-trained video gener-
      ation model, our PersonalVideo injects the identity from given images into some learnable modules
094
      through an optimization process. Different from previous supervising this tuning process via recon-
095
      structing images on T2I models, our core insight is applying identity supervision directly to videos
096   generated by the T2V model thus bridging the tuning-inference gap as shown in Fig. 2.
097
098   Without ground truth for generated videos, we can only use nonconstructive supervision. Inspired
099
      by face generation (Wang et al., 2021; Richardson et al., 2021) in the realm of Generative Adver-
      sarial Networks (Goodfellow et al., 2020), directly applying human perception loss to the generated
100
      videos could reward the T2V model generating videos with high ID fidelity. During the tuning time,
101
      we first generate videos from pure noises and then align face embeddings extracted from gener-
102   ated videos and reference images via the identity encoder (Deng et al., 2019). Building on recent
103   advancements in fast sampling methods (Wang et al., 2024a; 2023b) for T2V models, this process
104   only requires four denoising timesteps with manageable tuning costs and negligible quality loss.
105   Furthermore, during this process, we can incorporate simulated prompts augmentation, which su-
106   pervises generated results in more semantic scenarios. Unlike the reconstructive training method,
107   they are not limited by the number of references, effectively mitigating overfitting and demonstrating
      strong robustness, even when only a single reference image is available.

145          • We introduce a Spatial Identity Adapter to inject the identity and effectively mitigate the
146            degradation of motion and semantics. It can also seamlessly combine with other pre-trained
147            SD components, without the need for extra tuning effort.

151   Text-to-Video Generation. The topic of T2V generation has attracted considerable interest among
152   researchers for a long time. Recently, the utilization of diffusion models has become predominant
153   in the realm of T2V tasks. VDM (Ho et al., 2022b) stands as the pioneer that first leverages a
154   diffusion model for T2V generation. Subsequently, Make-A-Video (Singer et al., 2023) and Imagen
155   Video (Ho et al., 2022a) were proposed to generate high-resolution videos in pixel space. To save
156   computational resources, various frameworks have been developed to perform a latent denoising
157   process (Zhou et al., 2022; He et al., 2022; Wang et al., 2023a; Blattmann et al., 2023; Wang et al.,
158
      2023c; Chen et al., 2023). Although these methods can produce high-quality generic videos by pre-
      training on large-scale text-video pair datasets, it remains challenging to enable them to synthesize
159
      contents according to specified identities.

200   Text-to-Video Diffusion Models. Text-to-video diffusion models (T2V) (Blattmann et al., 2023;
201   Guo et al., 2023; Wang et al., 2023a; 2024a) are tailored for generating videos by adapting image
202
      diffusion models to handle video data. Specifically, the diffusion model ϵθ aims to predict the added
      noise ϵ at each timestep t based on text condition c, where t ∈ U(0, 1) is normalized. The training
203
      objective can be simplified as a noise-prediction loss:

207   where z ∈ RB×F ×H×W ×C is the latent code of video data with B, F, H, W, C being batch size,
208   frame, height, width, and channel, respectively. τθ presents a pre-trained text encoder. A noise-
209   corrupted latent code zt from the ground-truth z0 is formulated as zt = αt z0 + σt ϵ, where αt and

211   ID Customization. ID customization for text-to-image models (T2I) focuses on enabling pre-
212   trained models to generate images that reflect specific identities while adhering to the provided text
213   descriptions. Typically, they optimize a new word embedding for the user-provided ID or fine-tune
214   the generator to enhance ID fidelity. Similarly to the training objective of pre-trained diffusion mod-
215   els, they adopt the reconstruction supervision with L2 noise-prediction loss to bind the user-provided
      references with the special token to inject to identity to the pre-trained T2I models.

      4     M ETHODOLOGY
217
218
      Despite the great progress of ID-specific image generation, it is still challenging for T2V cus-
219   tomization due to the additional consistency requirements. Given few images for a specific iden-
220   tity, our goal is to generate customized videos with high ID-fidelity and preserve original motion
221   dynamics and prompt following. In this paper, we propose a novel framework, dubbed Person-
222   alVideo, for high ID fidelity video customization as shown in Fig. 3. Specifically, we propose a
223   non-reconstructive approach to directly learn the identity as depicted in Sec. 4.1. To inject the iden-
224   tity with preserved motion dynamics and prompt following, we design a spatial identity adapter in
225   Sec. 4.2. Besides, we introduce a simulated prompt augmentation to mitigate overfitting in Sec. 4.3.
226
227   4.1   N ON -R ECONSTRUCTIVE T2V C USTOMIZATION
228
      Current T2V customization methods typically adopt a reconstruction approach to train a customized
229
      T2I prior on provided images and inject it into the pre-trained T2V models to generate identity-
230
      specific videos. However, it leads to a tuning-inference gap due to the misaligned distribution be-
231   tween reference images in the tuning time and generated videos in inference time, which brings
232   inferior ID fidelity and degradation of inherent motion dynamics and prompt following.
233
234
      To bridge the gap, as shown in Fig. 2, we propose a non-reconstructive framework to directly apply
      identity supervision on the generated videos for high ID fidelity with the references. Inspired by
235
      face generation (Wang et al., 2021; Richardson et al., 2021) in the realm of GAN (Goodfellow et al.,
236
      2020), directly applying human perception supervision to the generated videos could reward the
237   T2V model generating videos with high ID fidelity. Therefore, during the tuning time, we start from
238   pure noise instead of noised references in the previous reconstructive methods. Then we directly
239   supervise the sampled videos to mimic the identity in the generated videos with that in references
240   using ID loss, which closely aligns with human perception and the distribution of the real world.
241
      Specifically, we use pre-trained ID encoder (Deng et al., 2019) ϕ to precisely extract the ID em-
242
      beddings of the references and the random i-th frame of the sample video. Then we minimize the
243   cosine similarity of them to align the identity effectively and directly. Here we crop the faces from
244   the images and adopt image augmentation techniques like color jitter to make it more robust for
245   limited references. Formally, we calculate:
246

248   where Iid are the reference images and c are the text prompts with the specific keyword.

285   4.3   S IMULATED P ROMPT AUGMENTATION
286
287
      To further enhance the robustness of the customization, we introduce simulated prompt augmen-
      tation. During the customization process, traditional reconstruction frameworks can only utilize
288
      prompts that describe the reference image, which limits the model’s generalization capabilities. Ben-
289
      efiting from the non-reconstructive framework, we can incorporate numerous reference-irrelevant
290   prompts during the optimization, e.g., ‘V’ playing the violin and ‘V’ smiling on the beach. Specif-
291   ically, we leverage the Large Language Model to create 50 prompts as our simulated prompts with
292   various motion, appearance, and backgrounds and randomly select the prompts during the cus-
293   tomization. They align well with actual test scenarios to mitigate overfitting with strong robustness,
294   even when only a single reference image is available.
295
296   4.4   T RAINING AND I NFERENCE
297
298   Following previous T2V subject customization methods (Wei et al., 2024), we adopt a two-step
299
      training strategy. In the first step, we freeze the video diffusion model and optimize a textual em-
      bedding ‘V’ using Textual Inversion (Gal et al., 2022) to achieve a coarse identity personalization
300
      and serve as an initialization. Then we train the spatial identity adapter with ID loss directly on the
301
      generated videos to enhance further identity details, which achieves high ID fidelity and preserves
302   original motion dynamics and prompt following. Building on recent advancements in fast sampling
303   methods for T2V models like AnimateLCM (Wang et al., 2024a) , we could generate a high-quality
304   video in only four steps. To preserve the motion dynamic, we inject the spatial identity adapter in
305   the last step during the training. Besides, inspired by previous work [hyperdreambooth], we also use
306   a weight-space loss to mitigate the overfitting and enhance the diversity. Overall, our training loss is
307   as follows:
308                                          Ltotal = Lid + λ∥θ̂ − θ∥22 ,                                 (4)
309   where λ is the coefficient, θ and θ̂ are the initial and optimized weights.
310
      During the inference time, we generate videos using the original T2V model with customized textual
311
      embedding and our spatial identity adapter. We only introduce our adapter in the final quarter of the
312   denoising steps, which is consistent with the training process.

526   6    C ONCLUSION & L IMITATION
527
528   In conclusion, we present PersonalVideo, a novel framework designed for identity-specific video
529   generation using only a few images, achieving high identity fidelity while preserving the motion
530   dynamics and prompt-following capabilities of the original T2V model. By applying direct super-
531   vision on generated videos and introducing a Spatial Identity Adapter, we successfully bridge the
532   tuning-inference gap, mitigating identity degradation. Furthermore, the use of simulated prompts
533   augmentation enhances robustness, allowing for high-quality results even with minimal reference
534   input. Our method demonstrates superior performance over prior approaches, offering a flexible,
535   efficient, and scalable solution for personalized video generation within the AIGC community.
536   However, our approach still has some limitations. While it enables a plug-and-play injection into the
537   pre-trained T2V model, the results are inherently constrained by the capabilities of the T2V model
538   itself. For example, it fails to generate customized videos that contain multiple identities. One
539   possible solution is to further decouple the attention map of each subject, which will be explored in
      our future work.

      A.1   I MPLEMENTATION D ETAILS
695
      During training, we optimize the textual token with the learning rate for 1e-3 and the batch size
696   fixed at 4. Each identity training consists of approximately 400 optimization steps. Then we learn
697   the spatial identity adapter for 400 iterations with a learning rate of 1e-4 with the batch size 1. We
698   default to using AdamW optimizer with the default betas set to 0.9 and 0.999. The epsilon is set to
699   the default 1e-8 and the weight decay is set to 1e-2. During inference, we use 25 steps of DDIM
700   sampler and classifier-free guidance with a scale of 7.5 for all baselines. We generate 16-frame
701   videos with 512 × 512 spatial resolution and 8 fps. All experiments are conducted on a single
      NVIDIA A800 GPU.
