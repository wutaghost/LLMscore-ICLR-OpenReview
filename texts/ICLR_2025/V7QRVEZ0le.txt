008
009
010            Current one-stream Transformer-based trackers are quality but unfriendly to mem-
011
               ory consumption of large resolution and long sequence, both of which are crucial
               keys to tracking tasks. Recently structured state space model (SSM) demonstrates
012
               promising performance and efficiency in sequence modeling but struggles to re-
013
               trieve due to the limited hidden state number. To solve the computation chal-
014            lenge and explore the potential of Mamba, we propose TrackMamba, a Mamba-
015            Transformer tracker containing TrackMamba Blocks and Attention Blocks. In
016            order to better harness the scanning in TrackMamba Blocks for inter- and intra-
017            frame modeling, we introduce various scan patterns for rearrangement and flip-
018            ping. Furthermore, we propose Target Enhancement, including Temporal Token
019            for target aggregation and search enhancement, and Temporal Mamba for target
020            information cross-frame propagation. Extensive experiments show TrackMamba
021            performs better than the first-generation one-stream Transformer-based tracker at
022
               same resolution and mitigates consumption growth when enlarging resolution, ex-
               hibiting the potential of Mamba-based model for large-resolution tracking.

026   1   I NTRODUCTION
027
028   Visual object tracking aims to locate the target object in video sequences based on its initial state,
029   which is one of the fundamental tasks in computer vision.Except for the traditional challenges that
030   most of the work endeavors to solve, such as object deformations, occlusion, and confusion with
031   similar objects, there are also challenges related to model efficiency including computation and
032   memory burden when enlarging input resolution and extending long sequence so that most trackers
033
      work on small resolution for large-resolution datasets, resulting in inadequate performance.
034   Prevailing trackers follow a three-stage pipeline that extracts features of template and search re-
035   gion separately, then models their cross-relations, and predict box finally, such as Siamese-based
036   tracker (Bertinetto et al., 2016; Li et al., 2019). With the help of Transformer (Vaswani et al., 2017),
037   several trackers (Chen et al., 2021; Yan et al., 2021) adopt Attention to enhance cross-relation. Most
038   of the recently proposed Transformer-based trackers (Cui et al., 2024; Ye et al., 2022) are changed to
039
      one-stream pipeline for joint feature learning and relation modeling inside backbone which obtains
      better target-relevant search features. Thanks to the strong global modeling ability of Transformer
040
      and their well-pretrained backbone (He et al., 2022), they have achieved remarkable success. How-
041
      ever, the quadratic complexity of attention faces challenges of computation burden when enlarging
042   the image resolution which is critical for spatial modeling and location. Meanwhile, several works
043   focused on temporal modeling to handle appearance changes and distractor, such as additional dy-
044   namic online templates (Song et al., 2023; Cui et al., 2024) or progressively learnable tokens (Shi
045   et al., 2024; Zheng et al., 2024). Unfortunately, directly extending the temporal length introduces
046   significant computation to Transformer-based methods still due to the quadratic complexity.
047
      On the other hand, structured state space models (SSMs) (Gu et al., 2022a) can model sequences
048   with linear complexity, demonstrating robust performance across a spectrum of sequence model-
049   ing tasks while maintaining efficiency. Selective State Space Model (S6), a variant of SSMs, also
050   known as Mamba (Gu & Dao, 2023), has garnered significant attention within the vision community
051   and demonstrated comparable performance to Transformer (Vaswani et al., 2017) across numerous
052   vision tasks.Selective Scan Mechanism, as the core operation of Mamba, makes SSM parameters
053   data-dependent from input sequence, which enhances context-aware sensitivity. With the ability of
      relevant context selection and linear complexity, it is natural to employ Mamba to address the pres-

054
      sures from mentioned problem of resolution expanding and sequence growth. To the best of our
055   knowledge, Mamba remains untouched for single object tracking task.
056
057   Although SSM behaves well in sequences modeling, several studies (Park et al., 2024; Wen et al.,
058
      2024; Pantazopoulos et al., 2024) have demonstrated that pure Mamba inherently lacks the ability
      of retrieval and localization (Wen et al., 2024; Pantazopoulos et al., 2024) due to the limited hidden
059
      state number and suggest to incorporate attention as hybrid model to overcome the limitations.
060
061   To solve the mentioned computation challenge and explore the potential of Mamba in single object
062   tracking tasks, we propose TrackMamba, a novel Mamba-based tracker with great performance
063   both on tracking accuracy and memory consumption. Specifically, inspired by above studies, we
064
      adopt MambaVision (Hatamizadeh & Kautz, 2024), a hybrid Mamba-Transformer model, as our
      backbone for promising performance while maintaining efficiency. We first introduce TrackMamba
065
      Block as the core design of tracker, which performs both feature extraction and interaction with
066
      scanning. In addition, considering the rearrangement and flipping of input sequence play a critical
067   role in scanning, we discuss them in detail and propose various Scan Patterns that reasonably solve
068   the information sources and disturbances problem during scanning. Furthermore, to complement
069   the lack of direct cross-frame interaction for Mamba scanning, we introduce Target Enhancement,
070   including Temporal Token that performs target aggregation and search feature enhancement, and
071   Temporal Mamba for target information propagation by transferring along these tokens with Mamba.
072
      Extensive experiments on several benchmarks demonstrate our TrackMamba performs better than
073   the first-generation one-stream tracker (Cui et al., 2024) with the same resolution. When scaling up
074   the resolution, our tracker has a strong improvement on large resolution benchmarks, such as GOT-
075   10k (Huang et al., 2021), and mitigates computational consumption. Moreover, the current frame-
076   work has untapped potential due to the limitations of the backbone and pre-training. We believe that
077   with a better backbone, it could be scaled to higher resolutions better to improve performance.
078
      Our main contributions are summarized as follows:
079
080         1. We propose a novel tracking framework, termed as TrackMamba, which adopts the hybrid
081            Mamba-Transformer model and enables accurate and low-consumption tracking.
082
083
            2. For better scanning input sequence in TrackMamba Block, we introduce various Scan Pat-
               terns to arrange and flip them, solving the source and disturbances problem.
084
085         3. We propose Target Enhancement, ina Temporal Token for target feature aggregation and
086            refinement, and Temporal Mamba for modeling them, enabling information highly propa-
087            gation across frames.
088         4. Extensive experiments on multiple benchmarks show better performance of our tracker than
089            the first-generation one-stream tracker at the same image resolution while demonstrating
090            performance growth and lower consumption at larger resolution.

      2.1   M AMBA IN V ISION
095
096   The State Space Model (SSM) (Gu et al., 2022a) can model sequences with linear complexity, and
097   Mamba (Gu & Dao, 2023) introduces a novel data-dependent parametrization approach and presents
098   an efficient hardware-aware algorithm based on selective scan, achieving comparable performance
099   and better efficiency to Transformers in language modeling of long sequence NLP tasks.
100
      Recently, Mamba, with its linear complexity in long-range modeling, has been introduced to many
101
      visual tasks and demonstrated promising performance. Vim (Zhu et al., 2024) constructs a ViT-
102   like (Dosovitskiy et al., 2021) vision backbone with Mamba. VMamba (Liu et al., 2024) proposes a
103   hierarchical vision model based on Mamba with four-directional scanning. VideoMamba (Li et al.,
104   2024) leverages the linear-complexity operator inherent in Mamba to overcome the challenges of
105   the dual challenges of local redundancy and global dependencies in video data. This success has
106   led to its adoption in subsequent tasks, such as generation (Teng et al., 2024), point cloud analy-
107   sis(Zhang et al., 2024b; Liang et al., 2024), image restoration (Guo et al., 2024), video frame inter-
      polation (Zhang et al., 2024a), medical image segmentation (Ma et al., 2024; Wang et al., 2024b).

      2.2   H YBRID MODEL
109
110   Despite the sequence modeling ability of State Space Model with linear complexity, its retrieval
111   capacity is limited by relying on the finite number of internal states (Park et al., 2024; Wen et al.,
112   2024; Jelassi et al., 2024), which results in suboptimal performance across various tasks, such as
113   multi-query associative recall (MQAR) task (Park et al., 2024) and visual grounding (Pantazopoulos
114   et al., 2024). To mitigate this issue, some research has focused on efficiently increasing the number
115   of internal states (Dao & Gu, 2024; Qin et al., 2024) or refining the update rules (Schlag et al., 2021).
116   Beyond above studies, more works explored to insert attention mechanisms in Mamba (Park et al.,
117   2024; Wen et al., 2024; Waleffe et al., 2024) to explore hybrid models, yielding strong perfor-
118   mance across various tasks, such as language modeling (Lieber et al., 2024), image classifica-
119   tion (Hatamizadeh & Kautz, 2024), point cloud (Wang et al., 2024a), and image generation (Fei
120   et al., 2024). This trend highlights the great potential of hybrid architectures across diverse applica-
121   tions. Based on the trend, we additionally found similar formalization of the MQAR and tracking
122
      tasks and therefore chose the Mamba-based hybrid model for single object tracking task.
123
124   2.3   S INGLE O BJECT T RACKING
125
126
      Classics trackers follow a three-stage architecture, including separate feature extraction of template
      and search frames, integration between them, and target location with a box head. Siamese-based
127
      trackers (Bertinetto et al., 2016; Li et al., 2019) adopt a correlation operation to model the appearance
128
      similarity and correlation. Based on the success of Transformer (Vaswani et al., 2017), some track-
129   ers, such as TransT (Chen et al., 2021) and STRAK (Yan et al., 2021) adopt attention to capture the
130   global correlation while stilling following the three-stage architecture. In contrast, MixFormer (Cui
131   et al., 2024) performs both feature extraction and interaction within the Transformer-based backbone
132   as a representative of the first one-stream generation. Despite their great performance, the quadratic
133   computational complexity of self-attention has hindered the development of long-range modeling
134   and large sizes, while both of them play a key role in tracking.
135   In fact, Mamba has already made a mark in other tracking tasks. For instance, several works (Huang
136   et al., 2024a; Xiao et al., 2024; Hu et al., 2024) leverage Mamba as a motion predictor to model
137   trajectories in multi-object tracking, MambaVT (Lai et al., 2024) jointly model RGB and TIR with
138   trajectories in RGB-T object tracking, and MambaFETrack (Huang et al., 2024b) adopt Mamba to
139   modality interaction with event streams in RGB-Event tracking. In contrast, our work is the first to
140   investigate the application of the Mamba-based backbone model for single object tracking tasks.

      In contrast to traditional models that rely heavily on linear time-invariant SSMs, Mamba (Gu &
176   Dao, 2023) extends the SSM by introducing Selective Scan Mechanism (S6) as its core operator.
177   With S6 operation, three linear projection layers S∆ (x), SB (x), SC (x) are introduced to directly
178   derived the parameter B ∈ RL×N , C ∈ RL×N , and ∆ ∈ RL×N from the input data x(t) ∈ RL×N
179   for data-dependent processing in Eq.2 which enhances its context-aware sensitivity. Additionally,
180   Mamba also presents an efficient hardware-aware implementation.
181
182   Formulate Tracking as MQAR. Multi-query associative recall (MQAR) (Park
183   et al., 2024) task provides a sequence of query {q1 , q2 , . . . , qm } and key-value pairs
184   {(k1 , v1 ), (k2 , v2 ), . . . , (kn , vn )}. For each query qj , there exist some keys that satisfy qj = kl , and
185   the model needs to recall vl for each query, producing m outputs total. Tracking can be formulated
186   as an MQAR problem by treating the template images as key-value pairs and the search image
187
      as a set of query tokens. Beyond this, tracking introduces unique challenges, such as appearance
      variations, occlusion, and distractors, requiring more robust matching.
188
189
      Tracking Challenging Pure SSMs. Due to the limited hidden state dimension for carrying infor-
190
      mation, several studies (Park et al., 2024; Wen et al., 2024; Jelassi et al., 2024; Waleffe et al., 2024)
191
      indicate that SSMs struggle to accurately retrieve the vectors in MQAR task and are overwhelmed if
192   the context increases substantially, which leads to a lack of retrieval capabilities for matching-based
193   task, such as localization (Pantazopoulos et al., 2024) and tracking. To address this, they introduced
194   attention mechanisms to yield a hybrid model. Inspired by these efforts, we adopt the hybrid frame-
195   work, MambaVision (Hatamizadeh & Kautz, 2024) rather than the pure Mamba model, so as to
196   unleash the strong power of the Mamba-based model in preserving sufficient target information and
197   integrating it into the search.

      In this section, we describe our proposed tracker, TrackMamba. First, we begin with an overview
202   description of the framework. Then, we propose the core TrackMamba Block, which replaces the
203   Attention Block of one-stream transformer-based trackers, enabling the search features to be more
204   consistent with the target in addition to feature extraction. Furthermore, since the arrangement and
205   flipping strategies of the input sequences are critical of Mamba scanning, we give a detailed discus-
206   sion on scanning patterns and introduce three various patterns in TrackMamba Block. In addition,
207   we present Target Enhancement, containing Temporal Token for target feature aggregation and en-
208   hancing target-relevant search, and Temporal Mamba for target information cross-frame propagation
209   within one token for each frame. Finally, we describe the training and inference of TrackMamba.

281   Discussion on Scan Patterns. The Scan Pattern of input sequence, including rearrangement and
282   flipping, determines the transfer flow, and it is essential to analyze which Scan Pattern is more
283
      suitable for intra- and inter-frame modeling. First, if the sequence simply flipped as a whole into
      search-templates sequence, the backward scan begins with search region that contains lots of back-
284
      ground while ends with templates that are actually needed as source. This violates the purpose of the
285
      transfer and disturbs the template features. Next, based on the cropping process of tracking, the ob-
286   ject is always at the center of the cropped-out frame and the search region has twice scale factor than
287   templates. It can be assumed that the object is at almost the same position across, i.e., the shaded
288   tokens in Fig. 2. We could interleaved rearrange tokens with the same position for direct inter-frame
289   modeling. In summary, we propose three various Scan Patterns shown in Fig. 2 as follows:
290
291         (a) sequential-rearrange-whole-flip: Flipping the sequential sequence as a whole sequence,
292         (b) sequential-rearrange-separate-flip: Replacing the whole flipping with separate flipping to
293             fix the source and disturbance problems, and keeping the sequential order unchanged,
294
            (c) interleaved-rearrange-whole-flip: Interleaved rearranging the tokens in the same center po-
295             sition of different frames and keep the position of the background tokens on the periphery of
296             the search region unchanged, generating a splice sequence for direct inter-frame modeling.
297
298   As the critical pole for scanning, these operations strongly affect the model performance. Our ex-
299   periments, in Section 5.3, will verify the performance and analyze them in further detail.

459   5.3   A BLATION AND A NALYSIS
460
      In this section, we give a thorough analysis on the TrackMamba-256 model trained on four tracking
461
      datasets, and perform detailed ablation studies on LaSOT and GOT-10k benchmarks.
462
463
      Study on Scan Patterns. The arrangement order and scanning manner of the frame tokens play
464
      a key role in intra- and inter-frame modeling. To verify the validity, as shown in Table 2, we ex-
465   perimented with different scan patterns. First, bi-directional scanning (#3 v.s. #6) expands re-
466   ceptive field, which is more convenient for extraction, interaction, and aggregation. Compared
467   to the whole flipping in sequential-rearrange-whole-flip (#1), the separate inversion in sequential-
468   rearrange-separate-flip (#6) ensures the target information flow and avoids the distraction from
469   search region. Unfortunately, even if the cropping strategy ensures the center location, interleaved-
470   rearrange-whole-flip (#2) still hardly guarantees that target exists in the same position across frames,
471   and the repeated cross-frame scanning may break the intra-frame continuity for feature extraction.
472
473   Study on Interaction Modes. Since the hybrid model adopt both Mamba and Attention mecha-
474   nisms, for more obvious comparison of interaction ability, we switch one of them to feature extrac-
475
      tion only respectively. As shown in Table 2, in addition to the optimal performance achieved by
      employing both, Mamba-only achieves better performance than Attention-only (#4 v.s. #5), demon-
476
      strated that the quality long sequence modeling capability of Mamba transfer target information with
477
      hidden state well enough. It suggests Mamba is a stronger alternative to Attention while Attention
478   could assist back during discontinuous tokens interaction, which is a good use of the hybrid model.
479
480   Study on Target Enhancement. As shown in Table 3, we ablate the impact of the Target Enhance-
481   ment. First, we remove the it (#2 v.s. #7), the performance degradation demonstrates it achieving
482   target feature aggregation and propagation simply but efficiently. Next, we explored different inser-
483   tion locations for the token, including the head(#7), middle(#3), and tail(#4) of each frame, showing
484   the head location could aggregate representation better than the others. Furthermore, we attempted
485   different layer numbers of Temporal Mamba, experiments #5-7 show that more layers achieve better
      performance, indicating the effectiveness of transferring aggregated features.


                                                         9
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


486
      Study on the various number of template frames. With more available templates, search region
487   receives target representation at various moments for robust tracking. As shown in Table 4, with one
488   more template, the performance growth shows the ability to model more than one representation.
489   Not as expected, more templates lead to worse performance. We analyze that Mamba does not
490   directly interact with discontinuous tokens so that the best feature in first template will be interfered
491   while the hidden state is difficult to carry more information well with its limited dimension.

      5.5   L IMINTIONS AND F UTURE
525
526   The most serious problem with current framework is no performance improvement with more tem-
527   plates. On the one hand, we consider applying the new proposed Mamba2 (Dao & Gu, 2024) to our
528   framework for better carrying. On the other hand, we could modify the transfer path, such as adding
529   scanning branches from the first frame to implement a longer temporal tracker in the future.

      6     C ONCLUSION
532
533
      This work proposes TrackMamba, a Mamba-Transformer tracking framework based on Track-
534   Mamba Blocks with various scan patterns and Attention Blocks, aiming to transfer target feature
535   with the scanning mechanism of Mamba. By leveraging additional Target Enhancement with Tem-
536   poral token and Temporal Mamba, it obtains target aggregation and high representation transferring
537   across frames directly. Extensive experiments the proposed tracker beats the first-generation one-
538   stream Transformer-based tracker at same resolution on performance and memory consumption,
539   especially in terms of scalability at large resolutions. We expect this work can catalyze more com-
      pelling research to Mamba-based tracker on large resolution and long sequence tracking.
