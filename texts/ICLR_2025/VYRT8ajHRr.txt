011                                               A BSTRACT
012
013            The rapid advancement of Large Language Models (LLMs) has led to an influx
014            of efforts to extend their capabilities to multimodal tasks. Among them, grow-
015
               ing attention have been focused on monolithic Multimodal Large Language Mod-
               els (MLLMs) that integrate visual encoding and language decoding into a sin-
016
               gle LLM. Despite the structural simplicity and deployment-friendliness, training
017
               a monolithic MLLM with promising performance still remains challenging. In
018            particular, the popular approaches adopt continuous pre-training to extend a pre-
019            trained LLM to a monolithic MLLM, which suffers from catastrophic forgetting
020            and leads to performance degeneration. In this paper, we aim to overcome this lim-
021            itation from the perspective of delta tuning. Specifically, our core idea is to embed
022            visual parameters into a pre-trained LLM, thereby incrementally learning visual
023            knowledge from massive data via delta tuning, i.e., freezing the LLM when opti-
024            mizing the visual parameters. Based on this principle, we present Mono-InternVL,
025            a novel monolithic MLLM that seamlessly integrates a set of visual experts via
026
               a multimodal mixture-of-experts structure. Moreover, we propose an innovative
               pre-training strategy to maximize the visual capability of Mono-InternVL, namely
027
               Endogenous Visual Pre-training (EViP). In particular, EViP is designed as a pro-
028
               gressive learning process for visual experts, which aims to fully exploit the visual
029            knowledge from noisy data to high-quality data. To validate our approach, we
030            conduct extensive experiments on 16 benchmarks. Experimental results not only
031            validate the superior performance of Mono-InternVL compared to the state-of-
032            the-art MLLM on 6 multimodal benchmarks, e.g., +113 points over InternVL-1.5
033            on OCRBench, but also confirm its better deployment efficiency, with first token
034            latency reduced by up to 67%. Our code and models will be released.

      In recent years, the rapid development of Large Language Models (LLMs) (OpenAI, 2023; Bai et al.,
      2023a; Cai et al., 2024) has spurred increasing efforts to extend their multimodal capabilities (Chen
040
      et al., 2023; Liu et al., 2023e). As shown in Fig. 1 (a), most existing Multimodal Large Language
041
      Models (MLLMs) adopt a modular architecture, where visual encoding and language decoding are
042   processed separately. This is typically achieved by combining a pre-trained visual encoder like
043   CLIP-ViT (Radford et al., 2021) with an LLM (Liu et al., 2023e; Chen et al., 2024c; Li et al., 2023a).
044   Recent research has also started exploring monolithic MLLMs (Bavishi et al., 2023; Diao et al.,
045   2024; Chen et al., 2024b), as shown in Fig. 1 (b), which integrate visual perception and multimodal
046   understanding directly into a single LLM. Due to their simplicity and unity, monolithic MLLMs can
047   be more easily deployed using existing LLM inference libraries (LMDeployContributors, 2023) and
048   show superior efficiency than modular MLLMs (Diao et al., 2024; Chen et al., 2024b).
049   Despite recent progress, training a monolithic MLLM with promising performance still remains
050   challenging. In particular, monolithic MLLMs struggle to replicate the successful usage of pre-
051   trained visual encoders in modular MLLMs for visual perception. Therefore, researchers often
052   rely on additional pre-training to compensate for the shortcomings in visual perception in mono-
053   lithic MLLMs (ChameleonTeam, 2024; Diao et al., 2024). A straightforward approach is the native
      pre-training (ChameleonTeam, 2024), which pre-trains a monolithic MLLM from scratch using a

075   mixture of text-only and multimodal data. However, it requires prohibitively high training costs and
076   often suffers from challenges of unstable optimization (ChameleonTeam, 2024). Another common
077   solution is the continuous pre-training (Diao et al., 2024), which extends the pre-trained LLM to
078   multimodality via additional visual pre-training. Benefiting from the knowledge in the pre-trained
079   LLM, the cost of continuous pre-training becomes much more affordable. Nevertheless, due to the
080   catastrophic forgetting issue (Zhai et al., 2023), the pre-trained language knowledge is inevitably
081
      undermined during continuous pre-training, thereby weakening the multimodal capabilities.
082   In this paper, we aim to address the forgetting issue of continuous pre-training through delta tun-
083   ing (Ding et al., 2022). In particular, we argue that such issue arises from the shared architecture
084   for joint vision and language modeling, where optimizations for vision can negatively impact lan-
085   guage capabilities. Therefore, it is a natural thought to introduce an independent visual parameter
086   set into the pre-trained LLM, which allows formulating visual pre-training with partial parameter
087
      tuning. This can help retain the language knowledge by freezing the entire LLM during continuous
      pre-training, while improving visual learning. This principle is also aligned with previous endeavors
088
      in modular MLLMs, e.g., QwenVL (Bai et al., 2023b) and InternVL-1.5 (Chen et al., 2024c), where
089
      the visual parameters are placed outside the LLM.
090
091   Based on the above principle, we propose a novel monolithic MLLM, namely Mono-InternVL. As
092   shown in Fig. 2, the visual parameters in Mono-InternVL are designed as a set of expert networks
093
      that are seamlessly integrated into the LLM via the mixture-of-experts mechanism. To better capture
      the visual knowledge, these experts are initialized from the Feed-Forward Networks (FFNs) in the
094
      pre-trained LLM. Based on this architecture, we present an innovative visual pre-training method
095
      called Endogenous Visual Pre-training (EViP). Specifically, EViP is formulated as a progressive
096   learning process of three stages: 1) concept learning to grasp basic visual concepts, 2) semantic
097   learning to capture high-level semantics, e.g., world knowledge, and 3) alignment learning to align
098   knowledge with downstream tasks. Benefiting from the architecture and the pre-training strategy,
099   the visual scalability of Mono-InternVL is fully unleashed, where the downstream performance
100   consistently improves as the scale of the pre-training data increases. After visual pre-training, Mono-
101   InternVL accommodates complex multimodal tasks via supervised instruction tuning.
102   To validate our method, we develop Mono-InternVL using the pre-trained LLM InternLM2-
103   1.8B (Cai et al., 2024), and conduct extensive experiments on 16 multimodal benchmarks. Exper-
104   imental results not only demonstrate the significant performance improvements of Mono-InternVL
105   against previous monolithic MLLMs, but also validate its superior efficiency compared to existing
106   modular MLLMs. For instance, Mono-InternVL with 1.8 billion activated parameters can outper-
107   form existing monolithic MLLMs with 7 billion parameters by a significant margin, e.g., +15.5%

      2   R ELATED W ORK
125
126
127   Modular multimodal large language models. Recent progress in large language models (LLMs)
128   has catalyzed the integration of vision and language modalities, giving rise to multimodal large
129   language models (MLLMs). Both commercial entities, such as GPT-4V (Yang et al., 2023) and
130
      Gemini series (Team et al., 2023), and other open-source initiatives, e.g. BLIP series (Li et al., 2022;
      2023a; Dai et al., 2023), LLaVA series (Liu et al., 2023e;d; 2024), InternVL (Chen et al., 2023;
131
      2024c), have pursued this integration, often linking LLMs (Touvron et al., 2023a;b; Team, 2023;
132
      Cai et al., 2024) with large vision models (LVMs) (Radford et al., 2021; Dosovitskiy et al., 2021;
133   Dehghani et al., 2023; Chen et al., 2023) via intermediate layers. Specifically, there are lightweight
134   MLLMs (i.e., ≤ 4B parameters), such as MobileVLM-V2 (Chu et al., 2024), Mini-Gemini (Li
135   et al., 2024), MM1 (McKinzie et al., 2024), DeepSeek-VL (Lu et al., 2024), PaliGemma (Beyer
136   et al., 2024) and MiniCPM-V (Yao et al., 2024). However, such encoder-based vision-language
137   models (modular MLLMs) encounter challenges like limitations in input processing due to pre-
138   trained visual encoders, deployment inefficiencies, and complexities in balancing the capacities of
139   LLMs and LVMs, as also pointed out by (Diao et al., 2024).
140   Monolithic multimodal large language models. The issues related to modular MLLMs have
141   steered research toward encoder-free architectures, also known as monolithic MLLMs, which can
142   be summarized into two categories. The first category obtains continuous visual tokens through a
143   lightweight structure before feeding into MLLMs. For instance, Fuyu-8B (Bavishi et al., 2023) pro-
144   cesses images directly through a simple linear projection, adeptly handling high-resolution images
145   without using a dedicated visual encoder. EVE-7B (Diao et al., 2024) prioritizes vision-language
146   pre-alignment from an LLM-centric perspective and enhances image recognition through visual rep-
147
      resentation supervision. SOLO (Chen et al., 2024b) introduces an open-source training recipe for
      developing monolithic MLLMs. In contrast, the second category introduces VQ tokenizer-based
148
      models to generate discrete visual tokens to support image generation tasks, with representative
149
      works such as Chameleon (ChameleonTeam, 2024), Show-o (Xie et al., 2024), Transfusion (Zhou
150   et al., 2024), and Emu3 (Wang et al., 2024b).
151
152   Multimodal mixture-of-experts. VLMo (Bao et al., 2022) and BEiT-3 (Wang et al., 2022) employ
153
      a pool of modality experts to replace the feed-forward network in standard Transformer to capture
      modality-specific information by switching to different modality experts, and use the shared self-
154
      attention across modalities to align visual and linguistic information. VL-MoE (Shen et al., 2023)
155
      introduces mixture-of-experts (MoE) (Yuksel et al., 2012) based on the above works for better train-
156   ing and deploying. MoMa (Lin et al., 2024) also uses multimodal mixture-of-experts for pre-training
157   of MLLMs (ChameleonTeam, 2024) and cooperates with sparse components, e.g. MoE and mixture-
158   of-depths (MoD) (Raposo et al., 2024) to improve the efficiency of pre-training from scratch with
159   trillions of mixed-modal tokens. Inspired by the above literature, we introduce multimodal mixture-
160   of-experts (i.e., a visual expert and a language expert) for pre-training monolithic MLLMs and use
161   a novel progressive learning strategy, named endogenous visual pre-training (EViP), to address the
      unique challenges faced by monolithic MLLMs.

292
      Concept learning. Concept learning aims to encourage the model to learn fundamental visual
      concepts, such as object categories or basic shapes. Therefore, we first pre-train Mono-InternVL
293
      with about 922 million noisy samples, which are sampled from Laion-2b (Schuhmann et al., 2022a)
294
      and Coyo-700M (Byeon et al., 2022). In this sub-stage, Mono-InternVL employs a simple prompt
295   to perform generative learning, i.e., “provide a one-sentence caption for the image”. Meanwhile,
296   we constrain the maximum number of image patches of the visual tokenizer to 1,280 for training
297   efficiency. To ensure that the foundational language capabilities are preserved while enabling visual
298   specialization, the entire LLM is kept frozen during concept learning, and only the patch embedding
299   and visual experts are optimized.
300
      Semantic learning. After concept learning, Mono-InternVL is able to understand basic concepts in
301   the image, but organizing this information to produce reasonable descriptions remains challenging.
302   To achieve a higher-level visual understanding, we utilize the pre-trained InternVL-8B (Chen et al.,
303   2024c) to produce short captions for 258 million images. Compared to the original noisy captions,
304   synthetic captions typically depict complex visual knowledge, such as relationship and world knowl-
305   edge, etc., while containing less noisy information unrelated to the image, e.g., time of shooting, and
306   the photographer. In this sub-stage, we adopt the same optimization strategy as concept learning,
307   except that the maximum number of image patches is increased to 1,792.
308   Alignment learning. To meet the visual requirements of downstream tasks, we further perform
309   alignment learning on Mono-InternVL. As shown in Tab. 1, our alignment data is sampled from
310   the pre-training data of InternVL-1.5 (Chen et al., 2024c), including 143 million samples of image
311   captioning, detection and optical character recognition (OCR). In particular, captioning data, detec-
312   tion data and OCR data account for about 53.9%, 5.2% and 40.9% of the total, respectively. In this
313   sub-stage, we utilize the task-specific prompts from InternVL-1.5 for the generative learning, and
314   increase the maximum number of image patches to 3,328. Compared to previous sub-stages, the
315
      multi-head attention layers are additionally optimized to achieve better vision-language alignment.

362   4.2     I MPLEMENTATION D ETAILS
363
      Mono-InternVL is implemented based on InternLM2-1.8B (Cai et al., 2024) with newly added
364
      visual tokenizer and visual experts. The visual experts are initialized from pre-trained MLPs in
365   InternLM2-1.8B to leverage existing learned representations for improved visual feature extraction,
366   which accounts for 1.2 billion parameters. We adopt a similar dynamic high-resolution strategy
367   from InternVL-1.5 (Chen et al., 2024c) to align an optimal resolution for input image, which is
368   then patchfied to visual tokens. The remaining configurations are kept identical to InternLM2-1.8B.
369   The endogenous visual pre-training and instruction tuning take approximately 16 days (646k itera-
370   tions) and 1 day (14k iterations) on 256 NVIDIA A100 GPUs, respectively. More detailed training
371   configurations are given in Appendix §A.2.

519   4.4      Q UALITATIVE E XPERIMENTS
520
      To gain in-depth insights into Mono-InternVL, we visualize its attention maps of different layers in
521
      Fig. 5. From this figure, we can draw two noteworthy findings. Firstly, despite the global connec-
522
      tivity of the Transformer architecture, locality still exists in the visual encoding of shallow layers.
523   As shown in Fig. 5, in the first layer, visual tokens only interact with their nearby content, yielding
524   patterns that closely resemble those produced by convolutional neural networks (He et al., 2016).
525   Secondly, modalities are barely interactive at shallow layers but gradually fused as the layers deepen.
526   As illustrated in Fig. 5, the attention weights between visual and textual tokens are extremely small
527   in the first layer and become larger in deeper layers. We believe these examples will provide useful
528   hints for the design of monolithic MLLMs. More examples are given in Appendix §A.4.

      5      C ONCLUSION
531
532
      In this paper, we propose Mono-InternVL, a monolithic multimodal large language model (MLLM)
533
      that integrates visual encoding and textual decoding into a single LLM. In Mono-InternVL, a set of
534   visual experts is embedded into the pre-trained LLM via a mixture-of-experts mechanism. By freez-
535   ing the LLM, Mono-InternVL ensures that visual capabilities are optimized without compromising
536   the pre-trained language knowledge. Based on this structure, an innovative Endogenous Visual Pre-
537   training (EViP) is introduced to realize the coarse-to-fine visual learning. Extensive experiments
538   demonstrate the effectiveness and efficiency of Mono-InternVL compared to existing MLLMs. Our
539   work greatly pushes the boundaries of monolithic MLLMs, providing new possibilities for the de-
      velopment of MLLMs.
