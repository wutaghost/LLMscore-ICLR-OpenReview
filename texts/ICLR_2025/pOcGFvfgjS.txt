009                                              A BSTRACT
010
011            Represented by Zero123 series of works, recent advancements in single-view 3D
012            generation research have shown prominent progress by utilizing pre-trained 2D
013            diffusion generation models. These approaches either generate multiple discrete
014            views of a 3D object from a single-view image and a set of camera poses or pro-
015            duce multiple views simultaneously under specified camera conditions. However,
016            it is hard to maintain consistency across different views and camera angles, es-
017            pecially for poses with large differences. In this paper, we introduce AR-1-to-3,
018
               a novel paradigm to generate multi-view images according to the input single
               image with significantly improved consistency in details. We achieve this by de-
019
               signing a novel auto-regressive scheme where novel views are generated based
020
               on previous views. The core of our method is first to generate views closer to
021            the input view, which is utilized as contextual information to prompt the genera-
022            tion of farther views. To this end, we propose two image conditioning strategies,
023            termed as Stacked-LE and LSTM-GE, to encode the sequence views. Particu-
024            larly, Stacked-LE encodes the previously generated views into a stack embedding,
025            which is employed as a local condition to modify the key and value matrices of the
026            self-attention layers for denoising the target views of the current step. Meanwhile,
027            LSTM-GE divides the previously generated views into two groups based on their
028            elevations, whose feature vectors are encoded by two LSTM modules into high-
029
               level semantic information for global conditioning. Extensive experiments on the
               Objaverse dataset show that our method can synthesize more consistent 3D views
030
               and produce high-quality 3D assets that closely mirror the given image. Code and
031
               pre-trained weights will be made publicly available.

034   1   I NTRODUCTION
035
036   Synthesizing 3D objects from a single image has long been a challenging problem in both computer
037   vision and graphics communities. This task not only involves reconstructing the visible regions of a
038   3D object but also necessitates extrapolating the invisible parts. Inspired by the remarkable success
039   of diffusion models in 2D image generation (Rombach et al., 2022; Peebles & Xie, 2023; Li et al.,
040   2024; Zhou et al., 2024), numerous studies have sought to leverage the powerful 2D generative
041   priors learned from large-scale image datasets and transfer this success to 3D generation (Qian
042   et al., 2023; Long et al., 2024). For example, Zero123 (Liu et al., 2023a) pioneers the synthesis
043   of novel views based on camera poses by fine-tuning pre-trained diffusion models on (input view,
044
      camera pose, output view) triplets rendered from 3D objects on the Objaverse dataset (Deitke et al.,
      2023). Many subsequent works, such as Consistent123 (Lin et al., 2023), One-2-3-45 (Liu et al.,
045
      2024b), Cascade-Zero123 (Chen et al., 2023), also adhere to this paradigm.
046
047   Considering the independence of new views specified by different cameras, Zero123++ (Shi et al.,
048   2023) proposes to merge all target views into a grid image to jointly model the distribution of multi-
049   ple views. Nevertheless, these methods may produce multi-views inconsistent with the input image,
050
      resulting in unsatisfactory synthesis quality of 3D objects, particularly when the texture information
      of the 3D objects is complex and the gap between camera poses is large. Fig. 1 illustrates some
051
      examples of these two paradigms of methods in this scenario.
052
053   How do humans imagine the 3D shape and appearance of an object given a single camera view?
      In addition to leveraging a wealth of accumulated prior knowledge, we typically also refer to the

073   the views of objects closer to the given viewpoint, which are employed as context to restore the
074   full view of the 3D object from near to far. Motivated by this, we rethink the way of generating
075   consistent views and present AR-1-to-3, a novel paradigm that progressively generates all target
076   views in an autoregressive fashion.
077   To be specific, we follow the 3×2 grid image generation strategy proposed by Zero123++ (Shi et al.,
078   2023), in which the 6 target camera poses consist of interleaving elevations of 20◦ downward and
079   −10◦ upward, combined with azimuths that start at 30◦ and increase by 60◦ for each pose. Given
080   a source image, AR-1-to-3 starts by generating the first row views of the 3 × 2 grid, and gradually
081   generates the remaining row views in the subsequent steps with a fixed row azimuth interval of 120◦
082   for each step. At each step, the views of two different elevations can exchange information, and
083   the views generated in the previous steps are utilized as conditions to generate the views for the
084   current step. To this end, we develop a conditional feature encoding scheme for both the image local
085
      conditioning and global conditioning of the diffusion models. For the former, we design a Stacked-
      LE manner where the features of previously generated views encoded by the denoising UNet model
086
      are stacked to the key and value matrices of the self-attention layers when denoising the current
087
      views. For the latter, we propose a LSTM-GE strategy, in which the features of views already
088   generated encoded by the CLIP (Radford et al., 2021) model are fed to two branches of LSTM
089   according to their elevations. The output features are concatenated together for the cross-attention
090   layers during the denoising process of the current views.
091
      We evaluate the performance of AR-1-to-3 on a wide range of 3D objects collected from the Obja-
092
      verse dataset (Deitke et al., 2023). By introducing the autoregressive manner coupled with the pro-
093
      posed Stacked-LE and LSTM-GE strategies into multi-view latent diffusion models, our AR-1-to-3
094   enables the generation of consistent and accurate 3D views for various objects. The experimental
095   results demonstrate that our AR-1-to-3 is able to produce high-quality 3D assets and outperform
096   other state-of-the-art image-to-3D methods. We hope that the auto-regressive scheme provided by
097   this paper will inspire future works and our AR-1-to-3 could become a base multi-view generative
098   model for the 3D generative AI community.
099
      To sum up, our main contributions can be summarized as follows:
100
101                           • We present a novel autoregressive framework, termed as AR-1-to-3. It takes a single-view
102                             image as input to progressively generate consistent target views from near to far.
103
                              • We develop the Stacked-LE and the LSTM-GE strategies to encode the already generated
104
                                views for the local conditioning and global conditioning of the multi-view diffusion models.
105
106                           • Extensive experiments on the large-scale 3D dataset, i.e., Objaverse, demonstrate that our
107                             approach could generate more consistent 2D multi-view images than previous works and
                                produce high-quality 3D assets.

      2.1   2D D IFFUSION M ODELS FOR 3D G ENERATION
111
112   Diffusion Models (Ho et al., 2020; Rombach et al., 2022) pre-trained on large-scale 2D datasets
113   have demonstrated remarkable performance in generating high-quality images and powerful zero-
114   shot zero-shot generalization. In recent years, a significant amount of effort has been consecutively
115   devoted to transferring the strong priors of 2D diffusion models to 3D generation. For example,
116   DreamFusion (Poole et al., 2022) and SJC (Wang et al., 2023) propose to distill the knowledge of a
117
      pre-trained 2D diffusion model by feeding the rendered views to it and performing per-shape opti-
      mization. However, these methods are typically extremely time-consuming and suffer from artifacts
118
      such as over-saturated colors and the “multi-face” problem. Zero123 (Liu et al., 2023a) pioneers an
119
      open-world single-image-to-3D framework, in which diffusion models are fine-tuned to synthesize
120   new views conditioned on an input view and a set of discrete camera poses. Many subsequent works
121   employ Zero123 as a base module to generate 3D objects. Magic123 (Qian et al., 2023) combines
122   the 3D prior of Zero123 and the 2D prior of the stable diffusion model together to enhance the qual-
123   ity of generated 3D meshes. One-2-3-45 (Liu et al., 2024b) uses Zero123 to generate multi-view
124   images, which are lifted to 3D space to assist in the generation of 3D meshes. There also have been
125   several approaches, e.g., Consistent123 (Lin et al., 2023) and Cascade-Zero123 (Chen et al., 2023),
126   proposed to improve Zero123 by incorporating extra priors, like boundary and redundant views.
127   Besides, more and more attention has been drawn to enforce consistency between the generated
128
      multiple views. SyncDreamer (Liu et al., 2023b) adopts a 3D-aware attention mechanism to corre-
      late the corresponding features across different views. MVDiffusion (Tang et al., 2023) generates
129
      multi-view images in parallel through the weight-sharing multi-branch UNet with shared weights
130
      and correspondence-aware attention. More recently, Zero123++ (Shi et al., 2023) proposes a strat-
131   egy of tiling six target views surrounding the 3D object into a grid image to simultaneously generate
132   multiple views and enable the correct modeling of their joint distribution. This strategy also has
133   been successively adopted by follow-up works like One-2-3-45++ (Liu et al., 2024a), Instant3D (Li
134   et al., 2023), and InstantMesh (Xu et al., 2024), etc.
135
      In contrast to these methods heavily relying on the 2D priors of diffusion models, our AR-1-to-3
136
      inspired by human thinking also pays attention to the contextual information of the current object.
137   The method most similar to our method is Cascade-Zero123 (Chen et al., 2023). It first uses a
138   multi-view diffusion model to generate many extra views, which, along with the input image, are
139   then fed into another diffusion model to produce a specific target view. Different from this method,
140   our AR-1-to-3 takes into account the relationship between the target views and the input image, and
141   utilizes a diffusion model to establish the potential sequence between them. Given the target views
142   closer to the input image are easier to generate, AR-1-to-3 gradually generates all target views from
143   near to far in an autoregressive manner, in which the views generation of each step could be assisted
144   by the views generated in earlier steps.
145
146   2.2   AUTOREGRESSIVE V ISUAL G ENERATION
147
      The autoregressive scheme, which is used for analyzing and predicting time series data, relies on the
148
      idea that the current value in a series can be explained by its previous values. Based on this scheme,
149
      many researchers propose a series of classic sequential modeling methods, e.g., LSTM (Hochreiter,
150   1997), ConvLSTM (Shi et al., 2015), Transformer (Vaswani, 2017), which have excelled in various
151   communities including natural language processing, computer vision, and multi-modal learning. In
152   recent years, there has been a growing interest in applying such an autoregressive manner to visual
153   generation. VQVAE (Van Den Oord et al., 2017) revolutionizes the learning of discrete representa-
154   tions by incorporating the codebook mechanism, enabling efficient encoding and decoding processes
155   of images. VQGAN (Esser et al., 2021) adopts a transformer architecture to model serialized visual
156   parts and introduces adversarial loss during the training process. Parti (Yu et al., 2022) proposes a
157   pathways autoregressive model treating the image generation as a sequence-to-sequence modeling
158
      to generate high-fidelity photorealistic images. LlamaGen Sun et al. (2024) and VAR (Tian et al.,
      2024) scale image generation by incorporating multimodal large models.
159
160   In this work, we observe there is also a potential sequential nature among the target views, which
161   can be divided into several steps with equidistant camera pose intervals. As a result, we propose to
      generate multiple novel views in an autoregressive manner.

164   3.1   P RELIMINARIES
165
166   We introduce the preliminaries of Zero123++ (Shi et al., 2023), the base multi-view latent diffusion
167   model adopted in our work, which is beneficial for understanding the designs in AR-1-to-3.
168
      Multi-View Generation. To generate consistent multi-view images, Zero123++ proposes tiling six
169   target views with a 3 × 2 layout into a single grid frame to model the joint distribution among them.
170   For the target views, a fixed set of relative azimuth and absolute elevation angles are adopted in this
171   work. Specifically, the six camera poses contain alternating elevation angles of 20◦ downwards and
172   −10◦ upwards, along with azimuth angles starting from 30◦ relative to the input azimuth angle and
173   incrementing by 60◦ for each subsequent pose.
174
      Stable Diffusion. Zero123++ chose Stable Diffusion (SD) as the generative model since it is open-
175   sourced and has been trained on various internet-scale image datasets. The geometric priors that
176   SD learns about natural images are utilized for novel view synthesis under the image and camera
177   conditions. SD performs the diffusion process within the latent space of a pre-trained autoencoder
178   whose encoder and decoder are denoted as E(·)and D(·), respectively. At the diffusion time step t,
179   the objective for fine-tuning the denoiser UNet ϵθ (·) can be formulated as:
180                                 L = Ez∼E(x),y,t,ϵ∼N (0,I) ||ϵ − ϵθ (zt , t, cθ (y)||22 ,         (1)
181   where x is the target grid image which is encoded and perturbed to a feature with Gaussian noise ϵ,
182   i.e., zt , and cθ (y) represents the embedding encoded from the condition image y.
183
      Image Condition. The image conditioning techniques employed in Zero123++, i.e., cθ (y), can be
184
      divided into two aspects, i.e., local condition and global condition. The local conditioning strategy
185
      is tailored for the pixel-wise spatial correspondence between the input and the target views. To
186   this end, Zero123++ adopts a variant of the Reference Attention operation (Zhang, 2023), which
187   runs the denoising UNet on the input image and appends the self-attention key and value matrices
188   from it to the corresponding attention layers when denoising the target views. As far as the global
189   conditioning mechanism, the CLIP text embedding of an empty text is added with the CLIP image
190   embedding of the input image multiplied by a trainable set of global weights to provide high-level
191   semantic information for the cross-attention of the denoising UNet.

193   3.2   AR-1- TO -3
194
195
      Existing methods either generate multiple discrete viewpoints from a single input view and a set of
      camera poses, like Zero123 (Liu et al., 2023a), One-2-3-45(Liu et al., 2024b), or simultaneously gen-
196
      erate multiple views in a grid layout based on specified camera conditions, such as Zero123++ (Shi
197
      et al., 2023), Instant3D (Li et al., 2023). Despite achieving excellent performance in many scenarios,
198   these methods are still prone to generating several target views that exhibit geometric and textural
199   inconsistencies with the input image. We argue that the underutilization of contextual information
200   of current objects during the generation process should be responsible for this issue.
201
      The core of this work is to progressively generate target views in an autoregressive fashion so that
202
      the closer views generated earlier can be used as supplementary information for the generation of
203
      the farther views. Fig. 2 shows the end-to-end architecture of our AR-1-to-3. In this work, we follow
204   the paradigm of Zero123++ generating 6 specific camera conditional target views. In contrast, our
205   method generates these views step by step rather than all at once. Besides, Zero123++ has demon-
206   strated that generating multiple target views simultaneously contributes to accurately modeling the
207   joint distribution of these views. Therefore, each step in our generative strategy refers to a row of
208   the 3 × 2 layout, containing two target views with different elevations and 60◦ difference in azimuth.
209   Moreover, the camera pose difference between steps is a fixed azimuth angle of 120◦ , which is suit-
210   able for the scheme of next-views generation. As a result, the different target views at each step can
211   exchange information, and the target views generated in the previous steps can be utilized as extra
212
      conditions to generate the views for the current step.
213   We achieve such autoregressive generation by designing two image conditional strategies that en-
214   code sequence view information to fine-tune the denoising UNet model. These two strategies, de-
215   noted as Stacked Local Feature Encoding (Stacked-LE) and Long Short-Term Global Feature Encod-
      ing (LSTM-GE), correspond to the local and global image conditioning techniques in Zero123++,

254   3.3   S TACKED L OCAL F EATURE E NCODING
255
      In this subsection, we introduce how our method encodes the latent features of the input image
256
      and the generated partial target-view sequence as local conditions for the operation of Reference
257
      Attention to generate the target views of the current step. Note that the denoising UNet model is a
258   multi-level architecture and the hidden dimensions may vary across different self-attention layers.
259   It is challenging to encode the latent features of the condition view sequence at these positions
260   using a single network. Considering that these reference features and the attention representations
261   in self-attention layers come from the same positions in the U-Net network, sharing the same spatial
262   and channel dimensions, we naturally thought of encoding them into a unified representation by
263   stacking them along the spatial dimension. This strategy offers two significant advantages: 1) it
264   can encode any number of reference features at the self-attention layer. 2) it allows the reference
265   features to be directly fed into the attention module, enabling the reuse of weight parameters without
266
      any additional design required. We term this local feature encoding strategy as Stacked-LE whose
      details are shown in the bottom left corner of Fig. 2.
267
268   Formally, at the k-th step of autoregression, with a total of 2k-1 reference views, we aim to predict
269   the (2k)-th and (2k+1)-th target views, where the variable k ranges from 1 to 3. We first feed the
      reference views into the denoising UNet model separately and record their key/value matrices at

273   current step. During this process, the records in each layer are stacked together to modify the key

290   3.4   L ONG S HORT-T ERM G LOBAL F EATURE E NCODING
291
      In this subsection, we present the details of encoding the CLIP features of the input images and the
292   existing target-view sequence as global conditions, which provide high-level semantic information
293   via cross-attention to generate the target views of the current step. We empirically observe that the
294   CLIP features of the conditional view sequence are vectors with the same channel dimension and
295   1D spatial dimensions. Besides, they can be divided into two sub-sequences with an azimuthal angle
296   spacing of 120◦ based on their elevation angles. Thus, it is well-suited to process such sequences
297   with the Long Short-Term Memory (LSTM) Network (Hochreiter, 1997). Furthermore, this manner
298   has two interesting merits, mitigating computational burden: 1) It can encode feature vectors from
299   the conditional views into two vectors, regardless of the number of views. 2) The LSTM structure
300
      possesses a strong ability to model sequential representations while requiring fewer parameters. This
      strategy is named as LSTM-GE, and the details are shown in the bottom left corner of Fig. 2.
301
302   Given 2k-1 conditional views at the k-th step of autoregression, we first send them to the image
303   encoder of the CLIP model for their visual features, which are represented as F ∈ RB×(2k−1)×D .
304   Then, we partition these features into two groups according to the elevation angles of their respective
305   views. Note that the feature of the input image is a special case, which is included in both groups.
306   We denote the two grouped features as F1 ∈ RB×k×D and F2 ∈ RB×k×D , and feed them to two
307
      separate LSTM modules. Next, the hidden states of the k-th step of the two LSTM modules, i.e., hkl ,
      are selected as their respective outputs, i.e., , Il . This process can be formulated as follows:
308
309                                 Il = LSTMl ([Fl , (h0l , c0l )]),    l ∈ {0, 1}                      (4)
310   where h0l and c0l are the hidden state and cell state, respectively, both initialized as zero vectors.
311   Finally, the outputs of the two LSTM modules are concatenated together along the channel dimen-
312   sion, followed by a MLP layer and a trainable set of global weights W ∈ R77×1 , to form global
313   embeddings for the cross-attention of the denoising UNet:
314

367   Implementation Details. We train AR-1-to-3 on the render images from about 800k objects of the
368   Objaverse dataset for 150k steps with a total batch size of 32 on 8 NVIDIA A100 (80G) GPUs. The
369
      learning rate is initialized as 1e-5 and changes every 25k steps in a cycle, along with the AdamW
      optimizer (Loshchilov & Hutter, 2019) and CosineAnnealingWarmRestarts scheduler (Loshchilov
370
      & Hutter, 2016). We randomly select a k from {1, 2, 3} to build the autoregressive pattern, where the
371
      first 2k-1 views form the conditional images and the following two views make up the target views.

373   capable of adjusting to different input resolutions and producing more clear images. Meanwhile, we
374   resize each target view to 320, thereby the size of the grid image during the autoregressive process
375   is 320 × 640. Besides, we employ the linear noise schedule and v-prediction loss in Zero123++ (Shi

526   5              C ONCLUSIONS
527
528   In this paper, we present AR-1-to-3, a novel paradigm that starts from the input image and generates
529   target views from near to far in an autoregressive manner. At each step of the autoregressive process,
530   the previously generated views are employed as extra contextual information to facilitate the genera-
531
      tion of the current target views. The experimental results demonstrate that our method generates new
      perspective images and 3D objects that are more consistent with the input images compared with the
532
      existing approaches generating multi-view images discretely or simultaneously. To accomplish such
533
      autoregression, we propose two image conditional strategies, i.e., Stacked-LE and LSTM-GE, that
534   encode sequence view information to fine-tune the denoising UNet model. Particularly, Stacked-LE
535   encodes the latent features of conditional images as a stacked embedding to modify the key and
536   value matrices of the self-attention mechanism in the UNet. Meanwhile, LSTM-GE adopts two
537   LSTM modules to encode the CLIP features of conditional images into two feature vectors, which
538   are concatenated together to provide high-level semantic information via cross-attention for the gen-
539   eration of the current target views. We hope our AR-1-to-3 could become one of the base multi-view
      generative models for image-to-3D, and the autoregressive idea will inspire future works.

542   In this section, we describe the reproducibility of the results in this paper. First, we use the rendered
543   images from the Objeaverse (Deitke et al., 2023) to train and evaluate our AR-1-to-3. We use the
544   Blender software to render 7 images for each object based on the settings of Zero123++, including
545   an input image and 6 target images. We also set the rendered images to a white background for
546   the convenience of 3D object reconstruction. The overall training of our AR-1-to-3 is to learn
547   the autoregressive paradigm, in which the views of the first k-1 steps are utilized to predict the
548   k-th step views. We write a data loader to load the rendered images according to this paradigm.
549   Second, our AR-1-to-3 is implemented using the PyTorch framework. We use the standard pipeline

      of the diffuser toolkit to build the Stable Diffusion network. Then, the prepared sequence data
      are employed to fine-tune the Stable Diffusion model equipped with our proposed Stacked-LE and

      LSTM-GE. The entire training process lasts for about 120k steps with a total batch size of 32 on 8
