009                                                A BSTRACT
010
011            Despite remarkable progress, there still exist several limitations in current multi-
012            view clustering (MVC) techniques. Specially, they generally focus only on the
013            affinity relationship between anchors and samples, while overlooking that between
014            anchors. Moreover, due to the lack of data labels, the cluster order is inconsistent
015            across views and accordingly anchors encounter misalignment issue, which will
016
               confuse the graph structure and disorganize cluster representation. Even worse,
               it typically brings variance during forming embedding, degenerating the stability
017
               of clustering results. In response to these concerns, in the paper we propose a
018
               MVC approach named DLA-EF-JA. Concretely, we explicitly exploit the geometric
019            properties between anchors via self-expression learning skill, and utilize topology
020            learning strategy to feed captured anchor-anchor features into anchor-sample graph
021            so as to explore the manifold structure hidden within samples more adequately. To
022            reduce the misalignment risk, we introduce a permutation mechanism for each view
023            to jointly rearrange anchors according to respective view characteristics. Besides
024            not involving selecting the baseline view, it also can coordinate with anchors in
025            the unified framework and thereby facilitate the learning of anchors. Further,
026            rather than forming embedding and then performing spectral partitioning, based on
027
               the criterion that samples and clusters should be hard assignment, we manage to
               construct the cluster labels directly from original samples using the binary strategy,
028
               not only preserving the data diversity but avoiding variance. Experiments on
029
               multiple publicly available datasets confirm the effectiveness of our DLA-EF-JA.

032   1    I NTRODUCTION
033
034   In recent years, multi-view clustering (MVC) is becoming a research hotspot because of its ability to
035   effectively mine potential patterns hidden in heterogeneous data, and is widespreadly deployed in
036   various fields such as drug design and finance analysis (Xu et al., 2024; Chen et al., 2023b; Xia et al.,
037   2022a; Wang et al., 2023b; Wen et al., 2024a; Wang et al., 2022b; Wen et al., 2023b; Xu et al., 2023b).
038   As a powerful tool in MVC, anchor technique is commonly utilized to filter noise points and decrease
039
      the computing overhead (Li et al., 2023; He et al., 2023; Li et al., 2024b). It first selects a small
      number of significant samples to represent overall samples, and then replaces the sample-sample
040
      affinity relationship by building up the anchor-sample relationship (Zhao et al., 2024; Yang et al.,
041
      2022; Nie et al., 2024b). Following this line, a series of prominent works have been successively
042   proposed. For instance, Kang et al. (2020b) regard the centroids generated by k-means on respective
043   view as anchors and merge multiple graphs by splicing their left singular vectors. Xia et al. (2022b)
044   first project samples to perform de-correlation and then select anchors in projection space according
045   to the sample variance. Wang et al. (2022a) design a hierarchical k-means model to output anchors
046   and construct sparse similarity using the learned bipartite graph. Huang et al. (2023) leverage three
047   diversity levels in neighbors to construct anchors and generate graph directly in the early-stage fusion.
048   Although generating pleasing clustering results from various aspects, current methods usually focus
049   only on the anchor-sample affinity, and fail to take into account the anchor-anchor characteristics.
050   This is not reasonable since between anchors, there generally exist informative geometric features.
051   Overlooking them will not be conductive to constructing discriminative anchors and extracting
052   the intrinsic similarity among samples. Additionally, due to the fact that clustering tasks do not
053   involve any data labels, anchors could be misaligned across views, leading to the graph structure
      becoming chaotic. Wang et al. (2022c) provide an alignment scheme from the perspectives of

      feature and structure matching, nevertheless, it requires to select the baseline view. Also, the anchor
055   generation, the anchor transformation, and the graph construction are separated from each other.

058   approaches is to first form embedding and then conduct spectral partitioning on it, which causes the

080
081   With these concerns in minds, we design a MVC method termed DLA-EF-JA in this paper, and its
082   framework is presented in Fig. 1. To be specific, we introduce self-expression learning mechanism to
083   explore the geometric characteristics between anchors, and integrate them into the topology learning
084   of anchor-sample graph so as to characterize the manifold structure inside samples more sufficiently.
085   Then, we associate each view with a permutation model, which is learnable and works jointly with the
086
      anchor generation, to rearrange anchors in their original dimension space according to view-specific
      features. Owing to the joint-optimization mechanism in the unified framework, consequently, it does
087
      not involve the selection of baseline view. Further, to eliminate variance, based on the criterion that
088
      one sample should belong to only one cluster, we avoid the formation of embedding and choose to
089   directly generate cluster indicators from original samples. When the sample belongs to its cluster, we
090   manage to optimize its indicator as 1 and otherwise 0. In addition to well preserving the data diversity,
091   this paradigm also can skip the spectral partitioning stage and thereby alleviate the computing burden.
092   The cluster indicator matrix is shared for all views, which bridges all anchors, permutations and views.
093   Not only does it play an important role in gathering multi-view information at the cluster-label level,
094   but provides consensus structure for anchors on different views to force them rearranging towards
095   correct-matching direction. Subsequently, we give a six-step updating scheme with linear complexity
096   to optimize the resultant objective loss. Experiments on various benchmark datasets demonstrate that
097
      DLA-EF-JA is effective in grouping multi-view data and owns competitive strengths against multiple
      classical MVC approaches. For more clarity, we summary the contributions of this work as below,
098
099   1. We explicitly take into account the geometric features between anchors, and successfully integrate
100   them into the anchor-sample graph through topology learning to exploit the manifold characteristics
101   hidden within samples more fully for better clustering.
102   2. We devise a joint-alignment mechanism that not only eliminates the need for selecting the baseline
103   view but also coordinates well with the generation of anchors.
104
      3. We avoid the formation of embedding by directly learning cluster indicators using a binary strategy,
105
      which effectively clears the variance in clustering results, accordingly highlighting the stability.
106
107   4. We provide a six-step optimization scheme with linear complexity for the loss function. Experi-
      ments validate the effectiveness of our proposed method from multiple aspects.

110   Based on the fact that each view data typically owns self-unique features and consequently can
111   compensate for the limitations of other views, multi-view clustering aims at integrating information
112   from diverse views to obtain more comprehensive and accurate data representation, thereby achieving
113   superior clustering effect than single-view counterparts (Xu et al., 2023a; Wang et al., 2024; Huang
114   et al., 2024b; Wen et al., 2023a; Zhang et al., 2019; Tang & Liu, 2022; Fang et al., 2023; Wang
115   et al., 2023a). Anchor technology is recently introduced into multi-view clustering to increase the
116   computing efficiency (Shi et al., 2021; Chen et al., 2024). It is intended to replace the full graph with
117   a small-sized anchor graph by utilizing some discriminative landmarks. Specially, given a multi-view

      dataset {Xp ∈ Rdp ×n }vp=1 where dp , n and v denote the dimension of data, the number of samples
      and the number of views respectively, anchor based multi-view clustering can be formulated as

138   anchors and samples when constructing anchor graph, while overlooking the influence of geometric
139
      characteristics inside anchors. This could bring about the loss of some informative features. Anchors
      on different views also could be misaligned due to the unsupervised property of data, leading to
140
      the confusion of graph structure (Wang et al., 2022c). Moreover, the clustering results outputted
141

142   the instability (Zhang et al., 2020a; Zeng et al., 2024; Chen et al., 2023a). In next section, we will
143   elaborate in detail on the principles of our devised DLA-EF-JA approach to alleviate these issues.

      Subsequently, to eliminate the anchor misalignment issue, one straightforward idea is to compute the
163   space similarity between anchor sets and then match anchors according to their distance. However,
164   multi-view data generally has various dimensions, and accordingly anchors on different views also
165   have various dimensions. It is typically difficult to directly compute the distance between anchor sets
166   with diverse dimensions. Although one can project all anchors into a common space to make them
167   have the same dimension, it can not guarantee the distance similarity after projecting to be consistent
168   with that before projecting. Additionally, determining the appropriate projection dimension needs
169   heuristic searching. The projecting operation also could lead to heavy information loss. Consequently,
170   these strategies are not that sensible. To get rid of this dilemma, considering that the nature of anchor
171
      misalignment is that the order of anchors on different views is not identical, we can alleviate the
      misalignment issue by rearranging anchors. In particular, we associate each view with a learnable
172
      permutation matrix Tp ∈ Rm×m to flexibly transform anchors according to the characteristics of
173                                              2

      respective view, i.e., ∥Xp − Ap Tp Zp ∥F . The subsequent issue is how to make anchors rearrange

      5   C OMPLEXITY A NALYSIS
271
272
      Space complexity The space complexity of DLA-EF-JA is mainly from optimization variables
273
      Ap , Tp , Bp , Sp , C and α, p = 1, 2, · · · , v. According to the fact that Ap ∈ Rdp ×m , Tp ∈ Rm×m ,
274
      Bp ∈ Rm×k , Sp ∈ Rm×m , C ∈ Rk×n and α ∈ Rv×1 , we have that storing them will require
275
      O(dp m), O(m2 ), O(mk), O(m2 ), O(nk) and O(1) memory overhead, respectively. Thus, storing
276
      all optimization variables will take O(dm + m2 v + mkv + nk) overhead where d represents the
277
      data dimension sum of all views and is independent of the sample size n. Further, since the number
278
      of anchors m is generally greater than or equal to the number of clusters k, we have m2 v ≥ mkv.
279   Besides, considering that m is generally much smaller than n and is also independent of n, we have
280   that the space complexity of the proposed DLA-EF-JA is O(nk), which is linearly related to the
281   sample size n.

      6.2    C LUSTERING R ESULTS AND A NALYSIS
371
372
373   We summarize the clustering results in Table 1, and from this table we can conclude that,
374   1. Overall Effectiveness. Our DLA-EF-JA consistently beats these twenty competitors in terms of
375   all three metrics on DERMATO, Reuters, CIF10Tra4 and FasMNI4V. Particularly, it makes 6.91%
376   improvement in Fscore than the second-best approach on DERMATO. In other cases, such as on
377   Cora, it is still able to provide comparable outcomes. These signals that our DLA-EF-JA is effective
      in partitioning multi-view data and can achieve competitive clustering outcomes.


                                                                          7
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


378
      2. Anchor Suitability. In contrast with PMSC, AMGL, MCLES, FMCNOF, OrthNTF, FMR, PGSC,
379   etc, which tackle MVC problems using tensor, kernel, latent space, co-training or matrix factorization
380   means, our DLA-EF-JA using anchor tool can produce better results than them. For instance, on
381   Cora, it surpasses them in terms of NMI with 38.59%, 42.37%, 27.00%, 42.63%, 43.20%, 23.07%,
382   42.55%, respectively. These suggest that our adopted anchor means is recommendable.
383
      3. Ample Affinity. Different from FPMVS, FMVACC, FASTMI, SFMC, etc, which concentrate only
384
      on the anchor-sample relationship, DLA-EF-JA also successfully takes anchor-anchor characteristics
385   into the measuring of overall similarity and accordingly brings performance enhancement. Taking
386   FASTMI as an example, DLA-EF-JA outperforms it on all of these seven datasets and three metrics,
387   which reveals that our dual-level affinity strategy can help extract representations more fully.
388
      4. Reliable Stability. The results outputted by our DLA-EF-JA are all not with variance. This mainly
389
      benefits from avoiding the generation of embedding. Not only does the embedding-free property
390
      enhance the stability, but allows the labels to be directly derived from original data, well maintaining
391   the diversity. Despite non-variance for MPAC, SFMC and Orth, the low-rank constraint could damage
392   potential graph structure, accordingly weakening their performance.
393
394
      5. Flexible Alignment. Compared to FMVACC that requires firstly selecting the baseline view
      and then performs alignment based on completed anchors, DLA-EF-JA exceeds it with remarkable
395
      margins. For example on CALTE7, DLA-EF-JA receives 6.84%, 41.11%, 37.11% improvement
396
      respectively. This is primarily because our joint-alignment strategy, besides not involving the baseline
397   view, can also coordinate with the generation of anchors, more flexibly transforming anchors to align.
398
399   6. Broader Applicability. Some methods like PMSC, PFSC, MFLVC, AMGL, UOMVSC, MCLES,
400
      PGSC, etc, can not work with large-sized CIF10Tra4 and FasMNI4V due to the intensive complexities
      or self-limitations, while our proposed DLA-EF-JA operates normally with its lower complexities and
401
      meanwhile can produce superior clustering outcomes. So, DLA-EF-JA enjoys broader applicability.

421   To illustrate the efficiency of DLA-EF-JA, we count the running time of each algorithm, and report
422   the comparison results in Fig. (2). From this figure, we can draw that,
423
      1. MVSC, PFSC, PGSC and MCLES consume significantly more time than others. This is mainly
424
      caused by the subspace strategy they employed, which typically requires constructing large-sized
425   similarity and needs at least cubic computational overhead.
426
427   2. MPAC, PMSC, FMR, MLRSSC, etc, take more time than us, which is mainly because MPAC and
428
      PMSC gather multi-view representations at the partition level, and FMR and MLRSSC utilize the
      kernel dependence measure to do data reconstruction.
429
430   3. FPMVS, FMVACC, MSGL and SFMC operates slower than us. Possible reasons are that the
431   connection component constraints and feature matching constraints conducted on anchor graph
      induce a large proportion of additional time expenditure.

      4. FMCNOF and FASTMI enjoy slightly faster running speed, the reasons of which could be
433   that FMCNOF decouples dense optimization matrices by sparse factorization skills and FASTMI
434   generates base clusterings via fast partitioning on the view-sharing graph.
435
436
      5. AMGL, MSCIAS, UOMVSC and OrthNTF are generally faster than PMSC, MVSC, PGSC,
      MPAC, PFSC, etc, possibly because the former ones alleviate the computing burden of spectral

      partitioning and graph mergence via low-rank approximation or non-negative factorization.

      In this work, we introduce dual-level affinity, which concurrently considers anchor-sample and
479
      anchor-anchor characteristics, to more fully extract multi-view representations for better clustering.
480   To reduce the mismatching risk, we adopt a joint-alignment mechanism that does not involve the
481   selection of baseline view and also can coordinate with the anchor generation. Furthermore, we avoid
482   forming embedding and directly generate cluster indicators via a binary learning strategy, which
483   not only effectively eliminates the variance but well preserves original diversity. For the resulting
484   optimization problem, we provide a solution with linear complexities. Experiments on multiple
485   public benchmark datasets verify the effectiveness of our proposed DLA-EF-JA. In future work, we
      will extend our DLA-EF-JA method to non-parametric scenarios to further enhance its practicality.

799   To demonstrate the strong points of the proposed DLA-EF-JA, we select 20 remarkable MVC
800   algorithms as baselines. Their brief introduction is as follows,
801
802
           1. FMR (Li et al., 2019): This method utilizes kernel dependence measure instead of pro-
              jecting original samples to enhance the correlation between different views, and highlights
803
              the comprehensiveness of potential representations through subspace reconstruction.
804
805        2. PMSC (Kang et al., 2020a): This method merges view information in the level of partition
806           spaces via ensemble learning, and integrates consensus clustering and graph generation to
807
              maintain the consistence among views.
808        3. AMGL (Nie et al., 2016): This method assigns a group of weights for all graphs to
809           increase the diversity automatically, and reformulates conventional spectral partitioning
              procedure into a convex problem so as to generate the optimal solution.

           4. MSCIAS (Wang et al., 2019): This method maximizes the dependence between intact

813
           5. MVSC (Gao et al., 2015): This method conducts subspace clustering on each view
814
              concurrently to explore specific characteristics, and employs an indicator matrix that is
815
              shared for all the views to preserve the cluster consistence.

817        6. MLRSSC (Brbić & Kopriva, 2018): This method generates a common similarity matrix
818
              with low-rank and sparsity properties to learn joint subspace representations, and utilizes

          17. PGSC (Wu et al., 2023): This method exploits the connectivity and sparsity of each

              similarity graph to achieve the pure graph with a block-diagonal structure, and assigns labels

          18. OrthNTF (Li et al., 2024c): This method establishes an orthogonal non-negative tensor

              complementary information hidden in multi-view samples through tensor regularization.

              of graph fusion by utilizing both feature and structure information.

      the construction of anchors to decrease the disagreement between views, and improve the clearness

      sets of anchors for basic clusterings so as to form discriminative subspace representations.

      information theory and decrease the view redundancy by minimizing the representation lower bound,
