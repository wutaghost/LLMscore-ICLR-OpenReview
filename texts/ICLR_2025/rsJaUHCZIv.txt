011                                              A BSTRACT
012
013            In the field of visual object tracking, one-stream pipelines have become the main-
014            stream framework due to its efficient integration of feature extraction and re-
015
               lationship modeling. However, existing methods still face the issue of seman-
               tic misalignment: firstly, the interaction of position encoding between the two
016
               branches leads to a misalignment between feature semantics and position encod-
017
               ing; secondly, traditional attention mechanisms fail to distinguish between se-
018            mantic attraction and repulsion among features, resulting in semantic misalign-
019            ment when the model processes these features. To address these issues, we pro-
020            pose an Enhanced Semantic Alignment Transformer Tracker (ESAT) with posi-
021            tion learning and force-directed attention. By leveraging self-supervised posi-
022            tion loss (SPL), ESAT separately learns the absolute position encodings of the
023            target and search branches, distinguishing the locations of various tokens and
024            their positive or negative relationships, thereby enhancing the semantic consis-
025            tency between position and features. Additionally, ESAT incorporates a repulsion-
026
               attraction mechanism applied to the self-attention module, named force-directed
               attention (FDA),simulating dynamic interactions between nodes to improve fea-
027
               ture discrimination. Extensive experiments on multiple public tracking datasets
028
               show that our method outperforms many pipelines and achieves superior perfor-
029            mance on five challenging benchmarks.

032   1   I NTRODUCTION
033
034   Visual Object Tracking (VOT) has attracted increasing attention due to its broad applications in var-
035   ious fields, including traffic monitoring (Chandrakar et al., 2022), medical science (Bouget et al.,
036   2017) and self-driving cars (Gao et al., 2019). Single Object Tracking (SOT) is a one of the popular
037   category of VOT, which can be described as follows: Capture the target’s appearance features in
038   the first frame and maintain continuous tracking in subsequent frames (Soleimanitaleb & Keyvan-
039
      rad, 2022). Notwithstanding many highly effective trackers have emerged (Bertinetto et al., 2016;
      Li et al., 2019), challenges such as occlusions and deformations continue to perplex numerous re-
040
      searchers, impinging on the accuracy and stability of tracking. In recent years, based on Transformer
041
      (Vaswani et al., 2017), Vision Transformer (ViT) (Dosovitskiy et al., 2020) with its superior atten-
042   tion mechanisms and capacity for global feature capture, have achieved remarkable success in VOT
043   tasks (Chen et al., 2022; Lin et al., 2022; Hu et al., 2024).
044
045
      Despite advancements in feature fusion, current prevailing trackers still have limitations in dealing
      with semantic alignment. On the one hand, self-attention cannot capture the ordering of input
046
      tokens, so incorporating positional encoding is particularly important. In VOT tasks, absolute po-
047
      sitional encoding (Vaswani et al., 2017) is a widely adopted method. This method assigns distinct
048   vectors to each position in the template and search token sequences, which may result in a seman-
049   tic misalignment. Meanwhile, the template contains target feature information, while the search
050   region includes potential target positions and background information. However, the differing se-
051   mantic content and context of these areas may hinder the ability of model to effectively capture
052   their relationships. On the other hand, traditional attention mechanisms in object tracking exhibit
053   a significant limitation in feature weight allocation. Traditional methods indiscriminately sum the
      weights of various features, overlooking the complex interactions between them. This ”one-size-fits-


                                                       1
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


054
      all” approach is particularly inadequate given the semantic complexity, where feature relationships
055   involving appearance, motion patterns, and background are interwoven and interdependent. For
056   instance, in SOT tasks, certain features like the target’s color may attract each other in specific con-
057   texts, while others, such as background interference, may repel due to semantic conflicts. Traditional
058   attention mechanisms struggle to capture these subtle differences and dynamic relationships, leading
059   to biases in the model’s tracking accuracy and ultimately affecting its ability to discern the target’s
060   true state and motion trajectory.
061   To alleviate these problems, we propose a novel Enhanced Semantic Alignment Transformer
062   Tracker (ESAT) with position learning and force-directed attention. Specifically, we introduce a
063   novel self-supervised position loss function and a self attention mechanism incorporating repulsive
064   and attractive forces. (1) Self-supervised position loss (SPL) allows each token to implicitly incor-
065   porate its positional information. We propose two types of positional labels: absolute positional
066   labels and positive-negative positional labels. We input the features of the template and search re-
067   gions outputted by the tracking model into two separate fully convolutional networks (FCN), each
068   consisting of L stacked Conv-BN-ReLU layers. This module outputs the corresponding position in-
069
      formation for each image patch. Based on this, we calculate the position losses using the two types
      of labels and the obtained positional information separately, distinguishing the positions of different
070
      tokens and their positive and negative values, to enhance the semantic consistency of positions and
071
      features. (2) Force-directed attention (FDA) define the repulsive and attractive forces, by calculating
072   the difference between query (q) and key (k). Repulsive force reduces the attention weight between
073   different features, while attractive force enhances the weight of similar features. By adjusting the at-
074   tention matrix, the model considers the interaction between features in attention allocation, thereby
075   reducing the problem of semantic misalignment.

      To sum up, the main contributions of this work are threefold: (1) We propose a self-supervised po-

113
      mitigates the issue of semantic misalignment between template and search region. (2) We introduce

123   2.1   T RANSFORMER BASED T RACKER
124
      Transformer-based tracking models can be categorized into two distinct groups based on the frame-
125
      work and method of feature extraction they employ: CNN-Transformer based trackers and Fully-
126
      Transformer based trackers (Kugarajeevan et al., 2023).
127
128   CNN-Transformer based trackers (Chen et al., 2021b; Yan et al., 2021b) combine the Convolu-
129   tional Neural Network (CNN) (Krizhevsky et al., 2012) and Transformer (Vaswani et al., 2017) to
130
      be a hybrid architecture. Following fully-convolutional siamese networks based trackers (Bertinetto
      et al., 2016; Li et al., 2019), they use two identical pipelines of CNNs to extracted the features of
131
      the target template and search region and then flat the features into vectors and pass this information
132
      to the Transformer which capture the similarity features of the target in the search region. Although
133   CNN-Transformer based trackers utilize the attention mechanism, they still rely on CNN for feature
134   extraction, which makes them to be difficult to capture global feature representations.
135
136
      Fully-Transformer based trackers (Lin et al., 2022; Cui et al., 2022; Ye et al., 2022; Hu et al.,
      2024) are proposed to solve this problem, which are categorized into Two-stream Two-stage trackers
137
      and One-stream One-stage trackers. Two-stream Two-stage trackers use two identical and indepen-
138
      dent Transformer based tracking pipelines to extract features and then employ another Transformer
139   network to find the relationships between these features. STMTrack (Fu et al., 2021) introduces a
140   space-time memory network that integrates historical template and search features for better adapt-
141   ing to appearance variations during tracking. One-stream One-stage trackers like SimTrack (Chen
142   et al., 2022) and OSTrack (Ye et al., 2022), using pretrained ViT as the backbone Transformer. OS-
143   Track points out that some search patches contain background information, which is not necessary
144   in the tracking process, so an early candidate elimination strategy gradually discards the background
145   regions in the search area during the inference process. ARTrack (Wei et al., 2023) is proposed to
146   treat tracking tasks as a coordinate sequence interpretation task, gradually estimating object trajecto-
147
      ries. AQAT (Xie et al., 2024) uses a simple autoregressive query to effectively learn spatial-temporal
      information, capturing the instantaneous appearance changes of the target in a sliding window man-
148
      ner.
149
150   Although the dual stream method has achieved great success, the separation of feature extraction and
151   relationship modeling has the following limitations. In this case, we use the One-stream One-stage
152   method as as the baseline model.
153
154   2.2   ATTENTION MECHANISM IN TRANSFORMER TRACKING
155
156   The attention mechanism can establish rich global contextual dependencies, excelling at extracting
157   edge features and distinguishing similar features, making it highly suitable for single object tracking
158
      tasks.
159   CNN-Transformer based trackers: STARK (Yan et al., 2021b) captures the feature dependency
160   relationships between each element in the tracking sequence and enhances the original features using
161   global contextual information, using multiple self attention layers to achieve information exchange
      and fusion between features. TransT (Chen et al., 2021b) replaces the traditional cross correlation

162
      with attention, using self attention and cross attention based feature enhancement module in the
163   feature fusion stage.
164
165   Fully-Transformer based trackers: For Two-stream Two-stage methods, building on TransT
166
      (Chen et al., 2021b), SparseTT (Fu et al., 2022) proposes a sparse multi-head attention mechanism
      to improve the ability to differentiate between foreground and background. SwinTrack (Lin et al.,
167
      2022) explore to concatenate template and search features, and use multiple self-attention layers
168
      to facilitate feature interaction and fusion, reducing computational costs and enhancing model ac-
169   curacy. CSWinTT (Song et al., 2022) proposed an attention module based on multi-scale cyclic
170   moving windows, elevating pixel level attention to window level, which helps to maintain the in-
171   tegrity of the target and preserve more bit information. Force reduces the ambiguity of the target
172   edge area. For One-stream One-stage methods, MixFormer (Cui et al., 2022) designs a set of Mixed
173   Attention Modules based on the CVT (Wu et al., 2021a) structure to simultaneously extract and
174   integrate features of the target and search region. Compared to CNN-Transformer based trackers,
175   Fully-Transformer based method has a larger network capacity, stronger representation ability for
176   its output features, and it exhibits greater robustness.
177
178   2.3   P OSITIONAL E NCODING
179
180
      Transformer (Vaswani et al., 2017) relies on positional encoding (PE) to help it understand the
      spatial structure of images, as it does not utilize convolutional operations to extract local features.
181
      PE provides explicit spatial location information for each patch in the image. Each offering different
182
      advantages depending on the context of the task.
183
184   Absolute Position Encoding (APE) assigns a unique position vector to each element in the sequence
185   or image, indicating its specific location. It provides a clear reference for the position of each
186
      element. There are several choices of APE. In Vision Transformer (ViT) (Dosovitskiy et al., 2020),
      the PE is generated by the fixed 1-D sinusoidal functions of different frequencies. At the same
187
      time, there are 2-D sinusoidal PE methods (Raisi et al., 2021; Wang & Liu, 2021) that incorporate
188
      information about the height and width of the image. Meanwhile, the APE can also be learnable, as
189   it is randomly initialized and updated with the model’s parameters during the training process.
190
191   Relative Position Encoding (RPE) encodes the relative distances or relationships between elements
192
      instead of focusing on the absolute position. It allows the model to understand how far apart ele-
      ments are from each other. Based on the APE, RPE is added into the self-attention weight calcula-
193
      tion. (Bello et al., 2019) firstly proposes a new relative positional encoding methods dedicated to 2D
194
      images, which is used in Swin-Transformer (Liu et al., 2021). (Wu et al., 2021b) further improves
195   the 2-D RPE, called image RPE (iRPE), which integrates the modeling of directional relative dis-
196   tances as well as the interactions between queries and relative positional embeddings in self attention
197   mechanism.
198
      We build upon the previous method of explicitly combining positional information by using po-
199
      sitional information as a supervised signal for the self-supervised training of the tracking model,
200
      allowing each encoded patch to implicitly contain its positional information.

      3.3   F ORCE - DIRECTED ATTENTION
312
313   Force-Directed Attention (FDA) innovatively adjusts the distribution of attention among features
314   by introducing the concept of force from physics, enabling the model to more effectively capture
315   meaningful relationships when faced with complex features. FDA defines two interacting forces:
316   repulsive force and attractive force by calculating the differences between queries and keys. Fig.4
317   illustrates an example of the FDA.
318   In FDA, the role of the repulsive force is to decrease the attention scores between different features.
319   When the difference between the query and the key is large, the model applies repulsive force, caus-
320   ing these features to be suppressed in the attention distribution. This mechanism effectively reduces
321   the model’s focus on irrelevant features, thereby avoiding information interference and enhancing
322   the model’s sensitivity to important features. As Eq.5 shown, the repulsive force is calculated by de-
323   termining the Euclidean distance between the query and the key, squaring it, and then scaling it with
      the hyperparameter δ to derive the repulsive force value Arepulsive . This value plays a suppressive

      5   C ONCLUSION
531
532
      In this paper, we propose a novel Enhanced Semantic Alignment Transformer Tracker (ESAT) to
533
      alleviate the problem of semantic misalignment. Specifically, we design the self-supervised posi-
534   tion loss (SPL) and force-directed attention (FDA). SPL learns absolute positional encoding of both
535   target and search regions, and distinguish the target and background through positive-negative la-
536   bels. FDA uses repulsive and attractive forces to adjust attention and enhance semantic relationships
537   among features. The two modules cooperate to improve semantic alignment. By embedding these
538   modules into ESAT, extensive and quantitative experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of our
539   approach. We hope that our work can provide new insights into addressing challenges related to
      semantic misalignment in Transformer-based trackers.

766

      total number of training epochs is 150, and after 120 epochs, we reduced the learning rate by 10
767
      times. The model is trained with AdamW optimizer.
