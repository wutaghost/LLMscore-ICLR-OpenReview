009                                                A BSTRACT
010
011            We propose Topic and Description Reasoning Generation (TDRG), a text infer-
012            ence and generation method based on user-contributed comments with large lan-
013            guage models (LLMs). Unlike summarization methods, TDRG can infer the topic
014            according to comments contributed by different users, and generate a readable de-
015            scription that addresses the issue of the lack of interpretability in traditional topic
016            modeling for text mining. In this paper, we adopted zero-shot and fine-tuning
017            methods to generate topics and descriptions for comments. We use a human-
018
               annotated YouTube comment dataset to evaluate performance. Our results demon-
               strate that the potential of large language models of reasoning the topic and de-
019
               scription. Generated topic titles and descriptions are similar to human references
020
               in textual semantics, but the words used are different from those of humans.

023   1   I NTRODUCTION
024
025   With the rapid development of Internet, user-generated content has grown exponentially, flood-
026   ing social media platforms, forums, and websites with an unprecedented volume of data. The
027   vast majority of user-generated content is generated on social media platforms. People who
028   frequently use social media platforms share their lives and interact with others via various for-
029   mats, including text, image, video, audio, etc. However, these data formats are usually un-
030   structured. For researchers, it is a challenge to extract meaningful insight from a massive vol-
031   ume of unstructured data. Thanks to recent advances in natural language processing, many stud-
032   ies started to explore solutions to handle unstructured data with large language models. GPT-
033
      4o (https://openai.com/index/hello-gpt-4o/), which is a powerful large language
      model released in May 2024 by OpenAI, can reason across audio, vision, and text in real time. The
034
      outstanding and cross-modal abilities of GPT-4o encourage us to deal with complex user-generated
035
      content on social media.
036
037   YouTube, which is the largest video-sharing and social media platform, is primarily focused on
038   video and audio content. People can discuss videos and interact with YouTubers, the video creators
039
      on YouTube, by commenting under the videos. In previous studies, researchers employed the nat-
      ural language processing techniques to process massive volume of text comments, including topic
040
      modeling and text summarization (Wu & Li, 2019; Chakraborty et al., 2019). The purpose of these
041
      methods is to help humans to quickly gain insights from massive textural data. It is important for
042   YouTubers to understand what their audiences’ thoughts are. Through audience feedback, YouTu-
043   bers can discover their preferences and produce more engaging content that caters to them. However,
044   users’ comments encompass a multitude of aspects, including the content of the videos themselves,
045   the personalities of the YouTubers, and other unrelated matters such as “spam comment”. Our study
046   focused on these comments related to the video content instead of the others. We proposed a Topic
047   and Description Reasoning Generation (TDRG) approach to automatically generate the topic title
048   and reasoning the description about the topic.
049   The TDRG method comprises three main components: principles, comments, and a large language
050   model. The core component is the large language model, which plays a crucial role in generating
051   high-quality topics and descriptions. In this paper, we observe that as the model size increases, its
052   performance improves significantly, especially in zero-shot learning tasks. This is because smaller
053   models do not adhere well to the principles of constraint. As a result, the generated topics differ
      significantly from human-written ones, and the descriptions are even less aligned. To improve the

071   2.1   T OPIC M ODELING
072
073   There exists a number of topic modeling approaches in text mining, which are used to identify
074   underlying themes, patterns, and structures in large volumes of text data. Blei et al. (2003) proposed
075   a “Latent Dirichlet Allocation” (LDA), which is probabilistic model for collections of discrete data.
076   It is an unsupervised approach to discover latent topics in text data. LDA has also been extended
077
      and modified to improve its performance due to its defects, including the inability to perform well
      in short text and ignoring semantic similarity. MSTM and EXTM (Ma et al., 2012) consider the
078
      relationships between comments and news articles and assume that each comment has one topic due
079
      to the short length of most comments. Lu et al. (2020) proposed a subject word feature extraction
080   method LDA-SLP (LDA-word vector Similarity & Location & Part of speech). The weight of the
081   candidate subject words is determined by the similarity between candidate subject words and article
082   category label, part of speech, and word position of the candidate subject words. However, these
083   approaches focus on optimizing topic detection and topic clustering instead of topic explanation.
084   This activates us to study and understand where the topic comes from and how to explain it, which
085   we call topic reasoning.
086
087   2.2   T EXT G ENERATION
088
089   Since the launch of ChatGPT in 2022, Natural Language Generation (NLG) has become popular
090   research. ChatGPT is a large language model (LLM) was built by OpenAI, which demonstrated a
091   powerful generative ability in various natural language processing tasks. With LLMs’ ability, many
092   studies have explored complex textual structures such as table, SQL (Structured Query Language)
093   (Gong et al., 2020; Dong et al., 2023) .In recent studies, a lot of researchers attempt to unleash
094   the LLMs’ generative abilities though prompt engineering, which is a zero-shot learning with large
095
      language methods, and fine-tuning (Sahoo et al., 2024; Hu et al., 2021). Prompt engineering is
      a straightforward method involved crafting various input texts, referred to as prompts, which in-
096
      fluenced the model’s output. Otherwise, fine-tuning represents a sophisticated method involved in
097
      preparing domain-oriented datasets for training and teaching LLMs’ how to generate the superior
098   output via human instruction and human feedback. Fine-tuning is classified as a transfer learning to
099   enhance the system’s capability in domains. However, our TDRG method aims to tackle the novel
100   topic reasoning task. In this task, we provide comments from YouTube videos to a large language
101   model, which is then required to infer the topic from these comments, generate an appropriate topic
102   title, and even produce an explanatory description of the topic.

      3.1     TASK F ORMALIZATION
109
110   At first, we divided our task of generating topics and descriptions into two subtasks: topic generation
111   and topic description generation. These two subtasks are interdependent, as the output of one influ-
112   ences the other. In essence, the topic is derived from the general subject of the comments, while the
113   description elaborates on the discussion within those comments under the topic. Typically, the topic
114   is a concise phrase or sentence, around 15 words in length, with the description providing further
115   explanation, around 150 words in length.
116
117   3.2     DATA C OLLECTION
118
      There is no public dataset suitable for our tasks. Therefore, we crawled the comments from YouTube
119
      videos and wrote topic and description manually. We collected user-contributed comments from
120   YouTube public videos via YouTube Data API.
121
122   In this paper, we mainly focus on Chinese comments instead of other languages.
123   Each video randomly selected 100 comments in order to ensure a consistent sample size.
124
      We crawled 125 videos from 25 representative YouTubers in Taiwan. Their channels mainly speak
125
      Chinese and audiences also comment with Chinese. Therefore, we calculated the number of Chinese
126
      words in each comment and remained top 25 videos, where text exceeded five Chinese word. There
127   were finally 1,972 comments for the next human-labeling phrase.
128
129
      3.3     H UMAN A NNOTATIONS
130
131   According to the task formalization for topic and description reasoning generation, We formulated
132   principles for writing topics and descriptions respectively. We manually clustered the comments and
133   wrote the topics and the descriptions for each clustered set based on our writing principles. Finally,
134   the human-annotated YouTube comment dataset contains 112 clusters derived from 25 videos.
135   Our detailed writing principles are listed below:
136
137   Topic
138
            1. Less than 15 Traditional Chinese words.
139
140         2. It must contain at least one subject, which can be a person, item, or thing
141
      Description
142
143         1. Less than 150 Traditional Chinese words.
144
            2. Use simple and direct narrative.
145
146         3. Required to be related to the content of the video.
147         4. Explain the topic from an objective point of view without personal opinions.
148
149   3.4     T OPIC AND D ESCRIPTION R EASONING G ENERATION
150
151   In this paper, we proposed Topic and Description Reasoning Generation (TDRG) method to address
152   the lack of explanation under the topics. This brief overview of the TDRG approach, which is
153
      shown in Fig. 1, consists of three required components and one optional component — principles,
      comments, large language models, and context respectively. At first, principles component is a
154
      system-level prompt to control the output format and writing style, we designed the system prompt
155
      based on our formulated principles of writing the topics and descriptions in Section 3.3. According
156   to prior research has demonstrated the efficacy of prompt engineering as a method for assigning a
157   role and describing desired output format to LLM (Zheng et al., 2023). Our system prompt template
158   for principle component is shown in Fig. 2. There are role assignment and output principles to LLM
159   in the template. Another human-level prompt component consists of comments and context, where
160   the comments represent a clustered collection with an underlying latent topic. The context includes
161   any additional information referenced or implied within those comments. Comments are required
      for the LLM, whereas the context is optional. Last but most importantly, the core component is

162
      the large language model. Selecting an appropriate large language model significantly enhances
163   performance in topic reasoning tasks based on comments. Generally, the more parameters an LLM
164   has, the better its performance. We adopted zero-shot learning and fine-tuning methods to generate
165   the topics and descriptions with LLM.

300   4.3   F INE - TUNING L LAMA 3.1-8B FOR TDRG
301
302   In this section, we adopted the fine-tuning method to generate topics and descriptions. Unlike the
303   zero-shot approach, fine-tuning helps the model learn specific language patterns and task require-
304   ments by providing a large amount of pre-labeled training data. For the description generation sub-
305   task, the fine-tuning method requires the model to use the output from the topic generation sub-task
306   as context to generate descriptions more closely related to the video. This process not only handles
307   multiple subjects that may appear in a single comment but also helps the LLM focus on a specific
308
      topic, ensuring that the description is highly relevant and accurate to the video content. Compared
      to the zero-shot method, fine-tuning relies on richer contextual learning and more precise semantic
309
      understanding, resulting in content that better meets specific requirements.
310
311   Our results of fine-tune model are presented in Table 5. In terms of topic generation, the BLEU
312   scores surpass those of other models, indicating that the fine-tuned model can closely match the
313   format of human annotations. However, the performance in description generation was less sat-
314
      isfactory, which might be due to the limited amount of data, resulting in the model’s inability to
      effectively learn how to generate long texts.

      5   C ONCLUSION
325
326
      In this paper, we have explored topic and description reasoning generation, a novel approach to
327   addressing the topic reasoning task from comments. We employed zero-shot learning with a large
328   language model to generate topics and descriptions. As a result, the GPT-4o series models performed
329   well in topic reasoning tasks due to their large parameter size. In contrast, the Llama-3.1-8B model
330   performed poorly, often generating incorrect formats. To improve the Llama model’s ability for topic
331   reasoning, we applied the LoRA method for fine-tuning. We demonstrated the fine-tuned model can
332   generated the correct format but still need to improve. In the future work, we will also explore the
333   small language models abilities to topic reasoning task.
