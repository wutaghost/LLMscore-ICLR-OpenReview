009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            Vision-Language Models (VLMs) are a significant technique for Artificial Gen-
012            eral Intelligence (AGI). With the fast growth of AGI, the security problem become
013            one of the most important challenges for VLMs. In this paper, through exten-
014            sive experiments, we demonstrate the vulnerability of the conventional adaptation
015            methods for VLMs, which may bring significant security risks. In addition, as the
016            size of the VLMs increases, performing conventional adversarial adaptation tech-
017            niques on VLMs results in high computational costs. To solve these problems, we
018
               propose a parameter-efficient Adversarial adaptation method named AdvLoRA
               by Low-Rank Adaptation. At first, we investigate and reveal the intrinsic low-
019
               rank property during the adversarial adaptation for VLMs. Different from LoRA,
020
               we improve the efficiency and robustness of adversarial adaptation by designing
021            a novel reparameterizing method based on parameter clustering and parameter
022            alignment. In addition, an adaptive parameter update strategy is proposed to fur-
023            ther improve the robustness. By these settings, our proposed AdvLoRA allevi-
024            ates the model security and high resource waste problems. Extensive experiments
025            demonstrate the effectiveness and efficiency of the AdvLoRA.

028   1   I NTRODUCTION
029
030   Artificial General Intelligence (AGI), which aims to create intelligent agents that can perform as well
031   as or better than humans on a wide range of cognitive tasks, is a promising topic for both research
032   and industrial products Pei et al. (2019); Goertzel (2014). As vision and language are the most
033
      important information of intelligence, Vision-Language Models (VLMs) have become a significant
      technique for achieving AGI Fei et al. (2022); Achiam et al. (2023).
034
035   In recent years, the adaptation of VLMs aims to improve the performance on different downstream
036   tasks and has become a hot research topic. However, through extensive experiments, we find the vul-
037   nerability of the conventional adaptation methods, e.g., Full Fine-Tuning (FFT) Wang et al. (2017);
038   Wu et al. (2022); Zhang et al. (2022a), Linear Probe (LP), LoRA Hu et al. (2021), Unidapter Lu
039
      et al. (2023), and Aurora Wang et al. (2023) for VLMs, which may bring significant security threats
      in various domains, such as facial recognition Venkatesaramani et al. (2021); Sharif et al. (2016),
040
      medical analysis Finlayson et al. (2019); Ma et al. (2021) and autonomous driving Zhang et al.
041
      (2022b); Feng et al. (2021). As shown in Figure 1, we conduct adaptation experiments of VLMs
042   on the natural and attacked data of the MSCOCO Lin et al. (2014) and MSR-VTT Xu et al. (2016)
043   datasets. From these experimental results, we find that the average performance drops about 30.98%
044   on the attacked data. To solve this problem, various techniques are proposed against adversarial
045   attacks by data augmentation Volpi et al. (2018); Morris et al. (2020), attack detection Metzen et al.
046   (2016); Liu et al. (2018) and adversarial training Goodfellow et al. (2014); Liu et al. (2020). As the
047   most effective defense strategy, adversarial training enhances the adversarial robustness of VLMs
048   by retraining the model on mined adversarial examples Madry et al. (2018); Szegedy et al. (2013);
049   Pang et al. (2020).
050   However, as the sizes of VLMs increase, the conventional adversarial training method with full pa-
051   rameter updating to improve the adversarial robustness of VLMs will lead to high computing and
052   storage costsGan et al. (2020). In recent years, Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning (PEFT) technol-
053   ogy has garnered widespread attention as a novel adaptation paradigm due to its significant success
      in adapting large-scale pre-trained models. PEFT technologies can adapt VLMs with extremely

094   To solve these problems, we aim to develop a parameter-efficient adversarial adaptation method
095   termed AdvLoRA to effectively and efficiently improve the robustness of VLMs against attacks.
096   At first, similar to LoRA, the intrinsic low-rank property of adversarial adaptation for VLMs is

098   regard the rank of LoRA as the number of cluster centers and utilize the clustering algorithm to
099
      reparameterize LoRA from the weight matrices of VLMs. The weight matrices are decoupled into
      the clustering centers and the clustering distribution matrices. Subsequently, we impose constraints
100
      on their product to align with the parameter distribution of the original weight matrix. Moreover,
101
      we design an adaptive parameter update strategy to improve the robustness further. Through these
102   settings, we effectively and efficiently facilitate the adversarial adaptation of VLMs. Our designs on
103   low-rank for adversarial adaptation are motivated by the common dense direction theoryAllen-Zhu
104   & Li (2022), which demonstrates that low-rank adaptation in shallow convolutional neural networks
105   are more suitable to effectively enhance their robustness. For the first time, this paper empirically
106   verifies the applicability of this theory to VLMs and introduces a novel clustering-based initialization
107   method for LoRA, facilitating the process of adversarial fine-tuning. The contributions of this paper
      are summarized as follows.

      2.1   PARAMETER - EFFICIENT T UNING ON V ISION -L ANGUAGE M ODELS
121
122   Vision-Language Models (VLMs) have demonstrated success in addressing diverse vision-language
123   downstream tasks, including cross-modal retrievalZeng & Mao (2022); Huang et al. (2023); Geigle
124   et al. (2022) and cross-modal generationRamesh et al. (2021; 2022); Bao et al. (2023); Rombach
125   et al. (2022). However, VLMs may underperform on specific tasks when the data distribution of
126   the task diverges from that of the training data. Consequently, VLMs typically require re-training
127
      on task-specific data to effectively adapt to downstream tasks, a process commonly referred to as
      adaptation or fine-tuning. As the size of VLMs increases, traditional adaptation technologies such
128
      as Full Fine-Tuning (FFT) become increasingly inefficient and costlyWang et al. (2017); Wu et al.
129
      (2022); Zhang et al. (2022a). Parameter-efficient tuning emerges as a promising solution to alleviate
130   the heavy training and storage costs associated with adapting VLMs.
131
132   Recently, inspired by methods from natural language processingHoulsby et al. (2019); Hu et al.
133
      (2021); Li & Liang (2021); Liu et al. (2023a); Zhang et al. (2023b); Dettmers et al. (2023) and com-
      puter visionRebuffi et al. (2017); Jia et al. (2022); Bahng et al. (2022) domains, some approaches de-
134
      signed for VLMs have been proposed. These approaches aim to adapt frozen VLMs to downstream
135
      tasks by introducing extremely small tunable parameters. Despite having fewer tunable parameters,
136   their effects can equal or even exceed that of the full-parameters tuning. These approaches can
137   be broadly categorized into three types: adapter-basedLu et al. (2023); Gao et al. (2023), prompt-
138   basedZhou et al. (2022); Lu et al. (2022); Xing et al. (2022), and LoRA-basedDai et al. (2023);
139   Dou et al. (2023); Hayou et al. (2024); Zhong et al. (2024); Qiang et al. (2024); Liu et al. (2024);
140   Wang et al. (2024); Zhao et al. (2024); Pan et al. (2024). LoRA-based approaches have received
141   considerable attention due to their fewer tunable parameters, no additional input, and no additional
142   inference latency. In this paper, we identify suboptimal initialization in the standard LoRA approach
143   and investigate a clustering-based reparameterization strategy to enhance the robustness of VLMs
144
      during adaptation.

146   2.2   A DVERSARIAL A DAPTATION ON V ISION -L ANGUAGE M ODELS
147
      Some researchers have demonstrated that artificial neural networks including Vision-Language
148
      Models (VLMs) are vulnerable to human-unrecognized attacks Li et al. (2020); Cai et al. (2023);
149
      Zhao et al. (2023). Specifically, adding additional perturbations to input can cause VLMs to make
150   the incorrect decision with high confidence. To improve adversarial robustness on VLMs, most
151   works focus on data augmentation Cai et al. (2023); Wortsman et al. (2022) and adversarial training
152   Gan et al. (2020); Mao et al. (2023). Considered one of the most effective methods, adversarial
153   training can improve the adversarial robustness of VLMs by injecting adversarial inputs into the
154   training procedure through a min-max formulation Madry et al. (2018).
155
      In the early stages of research, some efforts were directed at employing adversarial training tech-
156   niques to train VLMs from scratchGan et al. (2020). Recently, adversarial adaptation has emerged as
157   a cost-effective strategy for post-pretraining enhancement of adversarial robustnessHendrycks et al.
158   (2019); Liu et al. (2023b); Zhu et al. (2023); Li et al. (2024a;b); Xu et al. (2024); Mao et al. (2023);
159   Yuan et al. (2024); Zhang et al. (2023a). However, the majority of these methods enhance adversarial
160   robustness by updating all the parameters of the pre-trained model through adversarial adaptation,
161   while primarily focusing on the robustness of visual models. A few multi-modal parameter-efficient
      approaches, such as TeCoAMao et al. (2023), employ prompt tuning for adversarial adaptation, but


                                                         3
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


162
      they too are limited to classification tasks. In this paper, we utilize a parameter-efficient method
163   based on LoRA to achieve adversarial adaptation for cross-modal tasks. Unlike recent methods like
164   AutoLoRaXu et al. (2024), which aims to solve gradient instability issues by independently extract-
165   ing natural image features with the LoRA branch, it is essentially not a parameter-efficient approach
166   as it still updates all the parameters. Furthermore, AutoLoRa is exclusively focused on visual models
167   and single-modality tasks.
168
      Our designs to incorporate low-rank methodologies for adversarial adaptation is inspired by the well-
169   established dense direction theory proposed by Allen-Zhu & Li (2022). This theory highlights that
170   integrating low-rank adaptation in shallow convolutional neural networks is particularly effective in
171   bolstering their robustness. Significantly, this paper presents the first empirical validation of this
172   theory within the realm of VLMs. Additionally, it introduces a novel clustering-based initialization
173   method for LoRA, streamlining the adversarial fine-tuning procedure.

239   3.3     A DAPTATION M ODULE
240
241   Although adversarial training can effectively enhance VLMs’ adversarial robustness, it requires up-
242   dating all parameters based on gradient information, leading to a significant cost overhead. To
243   alleviate this issue, in this subsection, we propose an adaptation module that performs adversarial
244   training on LoRA to reduce the number of tunable parameters, achieving parameter-efficient adver-
245   sarial adaptation. We first provide a brief introduction to LoRA, followed by the introduction of
246
      clustering reparameterization and parameter alignment methods, as well as an adaptive parameter
      update strategy, to facilitate adversarial adaptation.

527   5     C ONCLUSION
528
529   In this paper, we aim to alleviate the security risks in the Vision-Language Models (VLMs). First of
530   all, we show the vulnerability of VLMs with various adaptation methods under adversarial attacks
531
      via extensive experiments. Besides, as the sizes of VLMs increase, simply applying the conventional
      adversarial adaptation methods to VLMs easily leads to 1) unpromising adversarial robustness and
532
      2) tremendous parameter and training costs. From these motivations, a novel parameter-efficient
533
      adversarial adaptation method named AdvLoRA is proposed with parameter clustering, parameter
534   alignment, and adaptive parameter update. Extensive experiments demonstrate the effectiveness and
535   efficiency of AdvLoRA. This result reveals the intrinsic low-rank property that emerges during the
536   adversarial adaptation process. Our proposed technique, which involves clustering reparameteriza-
537   tion and parameter alignment, has been instrumental in facilitating the adaptation process. We have
538   thereby offered a novel perspective for researchers in the field of security within the broader context
539   of AGI. In the future, it is worth further optimizing the memory and computational budget during
      the adaptation process.

      A    DATASETS
811
812
            • Flickr30K Plummer et al. (2015) contains 31,783 images and 158,915 captions totally.
813           Each image is often annotated with 5 captions. Following the split in Uniadapter Lu et al.
814           (2023) and Aurora Wang et al. (2023), we use 1,000 images for testing, another 1,000 for
815           validation, and the rest for training.
816
            • MSCOCO Lin et al. (2014) is a large dataset containing 123,287 images and 616,435
817           captions. Each image is annotated with 5 captions. Following the split in Uniadapter Lu
818           et al. (2023) and Aurora Wang et al. (2023), we use 5,000 images for testing, another 5,000
819           for validation, and the rest for training.
820
            • Didemo Anne Hendricks et al. (2017) contains 10,000 videos and 40,000 annotations. Fol-
821           lowing Frozen in Time Bain et al. (2021), we concatenate all descriptions corresponding to
822           the same video into a single sentence to conduct actually video-paragraphto retrieval task.
823
            • MSR-VTT Xu et al. (2016) is a popular video-text dataset. It contains 10,000 video and
824
              200,000 captions. Following the split in Uniadapter Lu et al. (2023) and Aurora Wang et al.
825           (2023), we use 1,000 videos for testing, another 9,000 for training.

828   B    BASELINES
829
830         • BLIP-FFT is a conventional adaptation technique that enhances the performance of BLIP
831
              for specific downstream tasks by retraining and updating full parameters in downstream
              tasks.
832
833         • BLIP-LP is an adaptation technique that involves adding and training a linear layer on top
834           of the frozen pre-trained model BLIP to adapt to specific downstream tasks.
835         • BLIP-LoRA is a Parameter-Efficient Fine-Tuning (PEFT) technology that adapts BLIP by
836           introducing low-rank adapters to capture task-specific information, allowing for efficient
837           adaptation to downstream tasks with minimal tunable parameter updates.
838         • Uniadapter Lu et al. (2023) is the first adapter-based PEFT technology for parameter-
839           efficient cross-modal adaptation.
840         • Aurora Wang et al. (2023) is a parameter-efficient cross-modal transfer learning framework
841           that uses mode approximation to generate a minimal set of tunable parameters, achieving
842           lightweight multi-modal adaptation.
