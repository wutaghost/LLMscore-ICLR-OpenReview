009                                                    A BSTRACT
010
011            In this work, we propose a novel Trajectory Score Matching (TSM) method that aims
012            to solve the pseudo ground truth inconsistency problem caused by the accumulated error
013            in Interval Score Matching (ISM) when using the Denoising Diffusion Implicit Models
014            (DDIM) inversion process. Unlike ISM which adopts the inversion process of DDIM to
015            calculate on a single path, our TSM method leverages the inversion process of DDIM to
016            generate two paths from the same starting point for calculation. Since both paths start
017            from the same starting point, TSM can reduce the accumulated error compared to ISM,
018            thus alleviating the problem of pseudo ground truth inconsistency. TSM enhances the
019
               stability and consistency of the model’s generated paths during the distillation process.
               We demonstrate this experimentally and further show that ISM is a special case of TSM.
020
               Furthermore, to optimize the current multi-stage optimization process from high-resolution
021
               text to 3D generation, we adopt Stable Diffusion XL for guidance. In response to the issues
022            of abnormal replication and splitting caused by unstable gradients during the 3D Gaussian
023            splatting process when using Stable Diffusion XL, we propose a pixel-by-pixel gradient
024            clipping method. Extensive experiments show that our model significantly surpasses the
025            state-of-the-art models in terms of visual quality and performance.

030   In recent years, Virtual Reality (VR) and Augmented Reality (AR) have increasingly become a part of our
031   daily lives, and the demand for high-quality 3D content has increased significantly. 3D technology has
032   become extremely important, allowing us to visualize, understand and interact with complex objects and
033
      environments. It also plays a key role in various fields such as architecture, animation, gaming and virtual
      reality. In addition, 3D technology shows broad application prospects in retail (Wodehouse & Abba, 2016),
034
      online meetings (Nakanishi et al., 1999), education (Reisoğlu et al., 2017) and other fields (Miao et al., 2024).
035

036   high-quality 3D models requires computional time, effort, and expertise. Given these challenges, methods

038   Zhuang, 2023; Ma et al., 2023; Shi et al., 2023b). These methods create accurate 3D models directly from
039   natural language descriptions, thereby reducing manual input in traditional 3D modeling processes. Once the

      In this work, we aim to overcome the above limitations. The reverse process of DDIM was adopted by (Liang

100
      reconstruction and generating relatively consistent pseudo-ground truth. However, the inherent accumulation
101
      errors in the reverse process of DDIM still result in semantic changes in these pseudo-ground truths, leading to
102   partially averaged reconstruction results in certain regions, resulting in erroneous and unrealistic outcomes in
103   these regions. To address this issue, we propose a novel method called Trajectory Score Matching (TSM). We
104   through simple improvements to Interval Score Matching (ISM) (Liang et al., 2023), effectively alleviate the
105   average effect of inconsistent pseudo-ground truths caused by inherent accumulation errors. We demonstrate
106   that our TSM has smaller accumulation errors compared to ISM, and ISM can be considered as a special
107   case of TSM. Through experiments, we prove that the effects produced by our TSM are superior to ISM,
108   yielding highly realistic and detailed results. In order to achieve high-resolution output, previous methods
109   need to undergo multi-stage training. Our model directly uses SDXL that supports high resolution as guidance
110
      without going through multi-stage training. This not only reduces training costs, but also simplifies the

111
      we propose a pixel-by-pixel gradient clipping method, which effectively alleviates the inherent gradient
112
      instability of SDXL. In summary, the contributions of our work are as follows:
113
114          • We investigate and analyze the inherent accumulated errors produced by DDIM inversion process in
115            interval score matching (ISM), resulting in the presence of inconsistent pseudo-ground truth.
116

117            method. Unlike the single path of ISM, TSM improves ISM to a dual path, effectively alleviating

120

121            introduce a novel gradient clipping method, which effectively solves the problem of SDXL in
122            gradient stability. Extensive experiments demonstrate that our method significantly outperforms the
123            current state-of-the-art methods.

174
      two interval steps. DDIM will inevitably produce inherent accumulated errors in this process, resulting in
175   inconsistent reconstruction results. In this work, we empirically follow the mature mainstream architecture
176   method of 3D Gaussian Splatting (Liang et al., 2023; Tang et al., 2023; Yi et al., 2024) as the baseline of our
177   approach. On this basis, inspired by the recent Consistency trajectory model (Kim et al., 2023), we propose
178   to optimize the 3D model from two trajectories, and use the less noisy trajectory to guide another noisier
179   trajectory to alleviate the inconsistency problem.

      3.4   T HE ABNORMAL GRADIENT FROM ADVANCED PIPELINE
312
313   Previous methods have shown that increasing rendering resolution and training batch size can significantly
314   improve visual quality. Although increasing the resolution of rendering can significantly improve the visual
315   quality, most text-to-3D generation methods mainly use guidance based on Stable Diffusion 2.1 and only
316   support 512 × 512 resolution. Due to the impact of low resolution, local details are still blurred. Consequently,
317   we experimented with using Stable Diffusion XL as guidance, which supports 1024 × 1024 resolutions. The
318   more advanced model Stable Diffusion XL has a different architecture from the previous one, and the VAE of
319   this model is unstable. Although it has a certain impact on NeRF-based methods, it is not serious. However,
320   this instability poses significant challenges for methods that employ 3D Gaussian splatting. In 3D Gaussian
321
      splatting, the reliability of operations like copying and deleting point clouds is heavily dependent on gradient
      stability. If the average positional gradient g of the Gaussian view space exceeds a preset threshold, regions
322
      with under- or over-reconstruction of color c and depth d are intensively corrected. SDXL gradients are
323
      usually large and unstable, and high average gradient values in this case may cause normal areas to still be
324   densified. An intuitive method is leveraging the gradient clip technical to handle this issue, previous related
325   work (Pan et al., 2024) has explored for NeRF-based method, which is not very suitable for 3D Gaussian
326   splatting. Thus, we propose an improved gradient clip method for 3D Gaussian splitting. Specifically, we still

      A.6   L IMITATIONS
800
801   While our method can generate high-quality and relatively realistic 3D models, qualitative results show a
802   limitation in our approach regarding light handling. Specifically, we have observed anomalous blue reflections
803   in many scenes. Through our experiments, we have identified this problem as primarily caused by our use
804   of SDXL. When SDXL is applied, the blue channel values in rendered images tend to be large, resulting in
805   numerous areas exhibiting abnormal blue hues after normalization. Despite our attempts, including parameter
806   adjustments and different normalization methods, we have yet to find a viable solution. We speculate that
807
      this may be attributed to the gradient or training data of SDXL. Additionally, it’s worth noting that while our
      work aims to enhance the quality of generated models, it may inadvertently contribute to the advancement of
808
      deepfake technology.
