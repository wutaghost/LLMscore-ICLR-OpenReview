009                                                                                         A BSTRACT
010
011              In this work, we introduce Mini-Gemini, a simple and effective framework enhanc-
012              ing multi-modality Vision Language Models (VLMs). Despite the advancements
013              in VLMs facilitating basic visual dialog and reasoning, a performance gap persists
014              compared to advanced models like GPT-4 and Gemini. We try to narrow the gap by
015              mining the potential of VLMs for better performance across various cross-modal
016              tasks from three aspects, i.e., high-resolution visual tokens, high-quality data, and
017              VLM-guided generation. To enhance visual tokens, we propose to utilize an addi-
018              tional visual encoder for high-resolution refinement without increasing the visual
019
                 token count. We further construct a high-quality dataset that promotes precise
                 image comprehension and reasoning-based generation, expanding the operational
020
                 scope of current VLMs. In general, Mini-Gemini further mines the potential of
021
                 VLMs and empowers current frameworks with image understanding, reasoning,
022              and generation simultaneously. Mini-Gemini supports a series of dense and MoE
023              Large Language Models (LLMs) from 2B to 34B. It is demonstrated to achieve lead-
024              ing performance in several zero-shot benchmarks and even surpasses the developed
025              private models. Code and models will be available to the public.

      1    I NTRODUCTION
049   With the rapid evolution in Large Language Models (LLMs) OpenAI (2023a); Zhang et al. (2022);
050   Touvron et al. (2023), empowering the impressive capabilities for multi-modality inputs is becoming
051   an essential part of current Vision Language Models (VLMs) OpenAI (2023b); Team et al. (2023).
052   To bridge the modality gap, several studies are conducted to marry vision with LLMs from images Li
053   et al. (2023c); Liu et al. (2023b); Zhu et al. (2023) to videos Zhang et al. (2023a); Li et al. (2023d).
      Despite these advancements, a significant gap remains between academic initiatives and the prowess

      of well-established models like GPT-4 OpenAI (2023b) and Gemini Team et al. (2023), which are
055   trained with huge amounts of data and resources.

057   For vision itself, image resolution is a core part of explicitly despite the surrounding environment

      with minimal visual hallucination. To this end, more attempts are performed to further improve the
      visual understanding in current VLMs. For instance, SPHINX Lin et al. (2023), Otter-HD Li et al.
059
      (2023a), and LLaVA-Next Liu et al. (2024) are proposed to enhance the ability based on previous
060
      work Dai et al. (2023); Liu et al. (2023b); Bavishi et al. (2023) by improving the image resolution.
061   Increasing the number of visual tokens with higher-resolution images undeniably enriches visual
062   embeddings in LLMs. However, this improvement comes with escalated computational demands
063   and associated costs, particularly when processing multiple images. Moreover, the existing data
064   quality, model capabilities, and application scopes remain inadequate for accelerated training and
065   development processes. This scenario prompts a critical inquiry: how to push forward the VLMs
066   approaching well-developed models with acceptable cost in an academic setting?
067   To answer this question, we explore the potential of VLMs from three strategic aspects, i.e., efficient
068   high-resolution solution, high-quality data, and expanded applications. Firstly, we utilize ConvNet
069   to efficiently generate higher-resolution candidates, thus enhancing visual detail while maintaining
070   the visual token count for LLMs. To bolster data quality, we amalgamate high-quality datasets
071   from diverse public sources, ensuring a rich and varied data foundation. Furthermore, our approach
072   integrates these enhancements with cutting-edge LLMs and generative models, aiming to elevate
073   VLM performance and user experience. This multifaceted strategy enables us to delve deeper into the
074   capabilities of VLMs, achieving significant advancements within manageable resource constraints.
075   In general, our method is adept at handling both image and text as input and output. In particular, we
076   introduce an efficient visual token enhancement pipeline for input images, featuring a dual-encoder
077   system. It comprises twin encoders, one for high-resolution images and the other for low-resolution
078   visual embedding, mirroring the cooperative functionality of the Gemini constellation. During
079   inference, they work in an attention mechanism, where the low-resolution one generates visual
080   queries, and the high-resolution counterpart provides candidate keys and values for reference. To
081
      augment the data quality, we collect and produce more data based on public resources, including high-
      quality responses Chen et al. (2023b; 2024), task-oriented instructions Goyal et al. (2017); Tito et al.
082
      (2021); Masry et al. (2022); Kembhavi et al. (2016), and generation-related data Zhou et al. (2024);
083
      Köpf et al. (2024). The increased amount and quality improve the overall performance and extend
084   the capability of model. Additionally, our model supports concurrent image and text generation,
085   facilitated by the seamless integration of our VLM with advanced generative models Podell et al.
086   (2023). It leverages VLM guidance for image generation by providing the generated text from LLMs.
087
      The Mini-Gemini framework, can be easily instantiated with a range of LLMs from 2B to 34B
088
      parameter scales, as detailed elaborated in Section 3. Extensive empirical studies are conducted
089
      in Section 4 to reveal the effectiveness of the proposed method. Remarkably, our approach attains
090   leading performance in various settings and even surpasses the well-developed Gemini Pro Team et al.
091   (2023), Qwen-VL-Plus Bai et al. (2023), and GPT 4V OpenAI (2023b) in the complex MMB Liu et al.
092   (2023c) and MMU Yue et al. (2024) dataset, respectively. These results underscore Mini-Gemini’s
093   potential to set new benchmarks in the realm of VLMs, highlighting its advanced capabilities in
094   handling complex multi-modal tasks.

140   LLM as Generation Assistant. Combining LLMs with image outputs has emerged as a pivotal
141   area in recent multimodal research. Methods like InternLM-XComposer Zhang et al. (2023b); Dong
142
      et al. (2024) utilize image retrieval to produce interleaved text and image outputs, bypassing direct
      generation. Conversely, auto-regressive token prediction approaches, exemplified by EMU Sun et al.
143
      (2023b;a) and SEED Ge et al. (2023a;b), enable LLMs to decode images through massive image-text
144
      data directly. These methods require enormous training resources, and their auto-regressive nature
145   leads to undesirable latency. Recent studies Xia et al. (2023); Chi et al. (2023); Zhan et al. (2024)
146   strive to align with latent diffusion models Podell et al. (2023) to streamline image generation.
147   They typically require designing text embeddings and additional optimization to achieve the desired
148   generation effect. This joint training can compromise the performance of VLMs in text generation.
149   Mini-Gemini distinguishes itself by adopting a text-data-driven approach to enable the model to
150   generate high-quality images. We leverage a mere 13K pure text data to activate the LLM’s ability as
151   a high-quality re-captioner Betker et al. (2023) without undermining the fundamental performance of
152   VLMs.

154   3     M INI -G EMINI
155   The framework of Mini-Gemini is conceptually simple: dual vision encoders are utilized to provide
156   low-resolution visual embedding and high-resolution candidates; patch info mining is proposed to
157   conduct patch-level mining between high-resolution regions and low-resolution visual queries; LLM
158   is utilized to marry text with images for both comprehension and generation at the same time.

      data) from the LLaVA Liu et al. (2023a) dataset, 100K QA pairs from ShareGPT4V Chen et al.

      (2023b), 10K LAION-GPT-4V eV captions, 700K GPT-4V-responded instruction pairs from ALLaVA
245   dataset Chen et al. (2024), and 6K text-only multi-turn conversations from LIMA Zhou et al. (2024)
246   and OpenAssistant2 Köpf et al. (2024). To bolster the OCR-related abilities, we further collect 28K

249   1.5M instruction-related conversations for image comprehension. Moreover, we also collect 13K

531
      We presented Mini-Gemini, a streamlined and potent framework for multi-modality VLMs. The
532
      essence of Mini-Gemini is to harness the latent capabilities of VLMs through strategic framework
533
      design, enriched data quality, and expanded functional scope. At its core, patch info mining enables
534   efficient extraction of detailed visual cues by engaging with high-resolution candidates. From the
535   data perspective, our meticulously compiled high-quality dataset ensures accurate vision-language
536   alignment and bolsters strong instruction-following ability. Furthermore, we support reasoning-based
537   generation in Mini-Gemini and empower current VLMs with image generation.Extensive experiments
538   on several zero-shot benchmarks prove the superiority of the proposed method, which surpasses
539   previous leading approaches and even private models. We hope the Mini-Gemini can serve as a
      strong benchmark for image understanding and VLM-guided generation.

      tion. Natural images can be easily annotated with detailed captions, but text-rich figures or diagrams,
816
      such as documents Tito et al. (2021), charts Masry et al. (2022), and scientific diagrams Kembhavi
817
      et al. (2016), present a more intricate challenge for models in complex questions and answers. There-
818   fore, to facilitate the optimization process, we follow the strategy in TextVQA Singh et al. (2019)
819   and incorporate OCR tokens for model reference in the training phase. In particular, we utilize
820   the PaddleOCR PaddleOCR to initially identify and extract textual elements within each image.
821   Then, we append the text characters to the original conversations in a format of Reference OCR
822   token:Text_1,...,Text_n, where Text_1 to Text_n indicates n detected text strings.
823   This approach ensures that the model has access to textual representations from the images, enhanc-
824   ing its ability to understand the image content. It is important to note that the OCR detector is utilized
825   solely for generating enriched data and is not employed during testing. This distinction underscores
826
      our objective to train Mini-Gemini with a comprehensive understanding of both textual and visual
      elements, thereby improving capability in complex image-text scenarios.
827
828
      Generation Data Collection. For the data generation collection described in Section 3.3, we
829
      provide specific examples of query prompts and their corresponding reference data sources for
830
      two generation tasks in Figure 7. We commence with a corpus comprising 10K GPT4V caption
831   data and 6K English-only LLM SFT data. After filtering out results that did not meet the format
832   requirements, we ultimately obtained 13K data points. To enhance the contextuality and quality of the
833   queries, we incorporate two distinct types of in-context examples: get_example_captions()
834   and get_example_queries(). The former function randomly selects 5 high-quality Stable
835   Diffusion (SD) Text-to-Image (T2I) prompts, while the latter extracts 3 instances from a repository of
836   simple instructional templates. These in-context examples serve as a foundational guide, providing
837   diverse and representative prompts that significantly enrich the generation process. This strategic
838   approach ensures the production of high-quality, relevant data, effectively supporting the generative
839
      capabilities.
