009                                                     A BSTRACT
010
011                Jailbreak attack can be used to access the vulnerabilities of Large Language Mod-
012                els (LLMs) by inducing LLMs to generate the harmful content. And the most
013                common method of the attack is to construct semantically ambiguous prompts
014                to confuse and mislead the LLMs. To access the security and reveal the intrin-
015                sic relation between the input prompt and the output for LLMs, the distribution
016                of attention weight is introduced to analyze the underlying reasons. By using
017                statistical analysis methods, some novel metrics are defined to better describe
018
                   the distribution of attention weight, such as the Attention Intensity on Sensitive
                   Words (Attn SensWords), the Attention-based Contextual Dependency Score
019
                   (Attn DepScore) and Attention Dispersion Entropy (Attn Entropy). By lever-
020
                   aging the distinct characteristics of these metrics, the beam search algorithm and
021                inspired by the military strategy “Feint and Attack”, an effective jailbreak attack
022                strategy named as Attention-Based Attack (ABA) is proposed. In the ABA, nested
023                attack prompts are employed to divert the attention distribution of the LLMs. In
024                this manner, more harmless parts of the input can be used to attract the attention
025                of the LLMs. In addition, motivated by ABA, an effective defense strategy called
026                as Attention-Based Defense (ABD) is also put forward. Compared with ABA,
027                the ABD can be used to enhance the robustness of LLMs by calibrating the atten-
028                tion distribution of the input prompt. Some comparative experiments have been
029
                   given to demonstrate the effectiveness of ABA and ABD. Therefore, both ABA
                   and ABD can be used to access the security of the LLMs. The comparative ex-
030
                   periment results also give a logical explanation that the distribution of attention
031
                   weight can bring great influence on the output for LLMs 1 .

034   1       I NTRODUCTION
035
036   LLMs have garnered considerable attention owing to their exceptional performance across diverse
037   tasks (Touvron et al., 2023a). However, as the deployment of LLMs becomes more widespread,
038   security concerns have been escalated, particularly in safety-critical and decision-making environ-
039   ments. A pivotal concern resides in the susceptibility of LLMs under jailbreak attacks, wherein
040   adversarial prompts are meticulously crafted to compel the model to produce content that violates
041   usage policies (Perez et al., 2022; Shen et al., 2023). The nucleus of jailbreak attack lies in crafting
042
      effective prompts that obscure malicious intent and deceive LLMs into treating harmful queries as
      benign (Zou et al., 2023). Various jailbreak techniques have been proposed, ranging from manually
043
      designed attack prompts (Shen et al., 2023) to automatically generated prompts (Zou et al., 2023;
044
      Liu et al., 2024; Chao et al., 2023), demonstrating increasing attack efficacy in both online and
045   offline LLMs.
046
047   Most current research on jailbreak methodologies primarily focuses on the development of sophis-
048
      ticated attack prompts, including role-playing (Jin et al., 2024), code injection (Ding et al., 2023),
      and distraction techniques (Xiao et al., 2024). The cornerstone of these strategies lies in embedding
049
      harmful queries within meticulously crafted legitimate contexts. Despite significant advancements in
050
      existing approaches, there exists a notable paucity of investigations into the underlying mechanisms
051   that enable such prompts to circumvent safety constraints within LLMs. An intuitive explanation
052   posits that these prompts create semantically safe and benign scenarios.

      To gain deeper insights into the success of jailbreak attacks, we propose to elucidate the underlying
076
      mechanisms from the perspective of attention mechanisms. The attention schema is foundational
077
      to most LLMs and is recognized for its robust generalization capabilities (Vaswani et al., 2017).
078   Moreover, attention mechanisms have been extensively utilized as an explanatory framework for
079   deep learning models, providing advanced interpretability (Zhang et al., 2024). Drawing on these
080   considerations, we pose a novel and significant research question: Does the success of jailbreaks
081   correlate with their influence on attention distribution within LLMs?
082
      To gain preliminary insights into this question, we analyze the distributions of attention weights
083
      associated with various input prompts, as illustrated in Figure 1. The attention weights represent
084   the average attention scores on different words from all layers of the Llama2-7B-chat model. The
085   color intensity corresponds to the relative magnitude of the assigned attention weight for each word.
086   Figure 1(a) depicts the attention distributions for a harmful input versus a harmless input. The atten-
087   tion of LLMs is notably concentrated on sensitive words (e.g., nouns), facilitating the detection of
088   harmful queries. Figure 1(b) illustrates a failure attack prompt, where the LLM’s attention remains
089   focused on sensitive terms such as “make” and “bomb”. In contrast, to further investigate the atten-
090   tion distributions of successful attacks, Figure 1(c) illustrates the attention distribution of an instance
091   generated by a SOTA attack model (Ding et al., 2023). It is obvious that the model’s attention is
092
      significantly diverted from harmful words to benign phrases such as “Enhance the Python code”,
      leading the LLM to neglect the underlying malicious intent. From this analysis, we derive a key
093
      finding: the success of a jailbreak may be attributed to its capacity to distract LLMs from focusing
094
      on sensitive words. Additional preliminary experiments that support our findings are detailed in
095   Section 2.
096
097   Preliminary experiments suggest potential correlations between attention distributions and the effi-
098
      cacy of jailbreak attacks on LLMs. However, formally defining these correlations and effectively
      integrating them to enhance both attack and defense strategies presents three significant challenges.
099
      First, the development of appropriate metrics to accurately depict attention diversion related to jail-
100
      break attacks remains obscure. Second, the majority of existing attack prompts are derived from
101   heuristic assumptions, complicating the incorporation of attention-based numerical signals as de-
102   sign guidance. Third, there is a pressing need to formulate novel defense strategies based on our
103   findings, thereby showcasing their comprehensive merits.
104
      In this paper, we introduce a novel attention-based framework to tackle the aforementioned chal-
105
      lenges by leveraging three metrics to guide both jailbreak attacks and defense strategies. Our moti-
106
      vation lies in exploring the intricate relationship between attention distribution and the success rate
107   of jailbreak attacks. Hence, the foundation for more generalizable attention-driven methodologies

108
      will be established. To ensure the proposed metrics effectively capture this relationship, they must
109   meet several key criteria: accurately reflecting the model’s attention focus on sensitive terms, elu-
110   cidating the interplay between input prompts and generated responses, and quantitatively assessing
111   the dispersion and shift in attention patterns during the attack. In response, three novel metrics are
112   defined, such as Attn SensWords, Attn DepScore and Attn Entropy. Building on theses metrics
113   and combined with the idea of “Feint and Attack”, we propose a novel semantic jailbreak attack
114   strategy which is named as Attention-Based Attack(ABA). Here, the harmless task serves as the
115   “Feint”, which is aimed at diverting attention from sensitive words. While the inner harmful task
116   constitutes the core “Attack”,which is intended to elicit harmful responses. This dual-pronged tac-
117
      tic amplifies the focus on the harmful content through positional (Hsieh et al., 2024) and semantic
      guidance, thereby enhancing the likelihood of generating harmful outputs once the harmless task
118
      is executed. To counteract such attacks, we propose the Attention-Based Defense (ABD), which
119
      capitalizes on the statistical regularity observed in both Attn DepScore and Attn Entropy. By in-
120   troducing a risk score formula that integrates these metrics, we can preemptively assess the threat
121   level of the given input. If the computed risk exceeds a predefined threshold, then the input will
122   be regarded as high risk. In this manner, a security warning will be added at the beginning of the
123   input, such as “Attention! There may be some harmful content.” As a result, the Attn Entropy can
124   be effectively decreased, and the attention of the model can also be more concentrated on a more
125   security response. We evaluate the efficacy of ABA and ABD through extensive experiments on
126   popular datasets (Chao et al., 2023; Conover et al., 2023), demonstrating the performance of state-
127   of-the-art attack and the capability of distracting the attention of the target LLMs. Compared with
128
      current baselines, ABD is shown to significantly enhance the robustness of defenses.
129   There are mainly threefold contributions in this paper as follows:

152   Attention Intensity on Sensitive Words. The metric Attn SensWords is proposed to quantify the
153
      correlations between the attention weights of sensitive words within the prompt and the success
      rate of jailbreak attacks. Sensitive words are defined as the verbs and nouns (e.g., “make” and
154
      “bomb”), which are more probability to be potentially harmful (Ding et al., 2023). The input to the
155
      model consists of two types: the original query, denoted as q, and the iteratively modified prompt,
156   denoted as p. For consistency, p is used throughout the following computations to represent each
157   input. Attn SensWords first calculates the normalized attention weights for sensitive words across
158   all layers and heads at each time step, which will be further averaged over all time steps and all
159   instances. Formally, let the target LLM be a model with L layers and H attention heads, the model

161   αt,l,h,i be the normalized attention weight which is assigned to the i-th word wi by head h in
      layer l at time step t. Let S be the set of indices corresponding to sensitive verbs and nouns in

171   Attention-based Contextual Dependency Score. In practice, the allocation of attention weight

      3.1     ATTENTION -BASED ATTACK
286
287   Given the origin malicious query q and based on the feedback of the target LLM, ABA is firstly
288   introduced to analyze the attention weight of each word. In ABA, the attention weight and corre-
289   sponding sensitive words, which are described as the verbs and nouns in q, will be input into the
290   attention distraction generator. According to the preliminary experiment results, Attn SensWords
291   and Attn Entropy play a significant role on the effectiveness of the jailbreak attack. Since
292   Attn SensWords is basically determined by the attention weight of each sensitive word, the core
293   objective of ABA is to continuously reduce the attention weight on these sensitive words while si-
294
      multaneously maximizing the model’s Attn Entropy. To achieve this, the distraction generator will
      refine and disguise the original query q by generating multiple semantic-guided scenarios. The re-
295
      fined and disguised query will be selected to input into the target LLM to generate harmful answers
296
      under a multi-round framework.
297
298
      3.1.1    ATTENTION W EIGHT A NALYSIS
299
300   To quantify the importance of each sensitive word, the attention weight on each sensitive word will
301   be calculated. The value of the attention weight can reflect the significance of each word in the set
302   of input prompt. Therefore, the most contributive word in the input prompt can be identified.
303
      Here, let q = {w1 , w2 , . . . , wM } be the set of words from the input malicious query. If the number
304   of sensitive words is supposed to be r, and these words are denoted as S = {s1 , s2 , . . . , sr }, then the
305   attention weight of these words can be described as a set Ssr = {(s1 : aws1 ), (s2 : aws2 ), . . . , (sr :
306   awsr )}. In each interaction between attention distraction generator and target LLM, the attention
307   weight analysis will be completed.
308
309   3.1.2    P ROMPT R EFINEMENT
310
311   The refinement of prompt can be realized by attention distraction generator. Based on responses
312   from target LLM, the objective of attention distraction generator is to minimize the attention weight
313   of sensitive words and maximize the Attn Entropy. In addition, these responses should be closely
314
      related to the intention of malicious inputs in q.
315   Inspired by the strategic principle of “Feint and Attack”, which is commonly employed in military
316   tactics, ABA emphasizes on the specified harmless part of the input by adding the nesting of multiple
317   tasks. Specifically, the number of tasks of LLMs can be increased by embedding multiple nested
318   objectives from the inputs. In this manner, the attention of the LLMs can be dispersed across various
319   tasks, and their focus on potentially sensitive content can also be diluted. For example, if the target
320
      LLM is required to accomplish a harmless task, then small probability may be given to reject this
      action. Since the task is nested, and the generated tokens always depend on the input, then the
321
      harmful responses will also be generated simultaneously.
322

      mented in generating jailbreak attack prompt p. Specifically, the weight of these sensitive words is

349   will be used to refine the jailbreak attack prompts. As a result, the most promising candidate can
350   be retained. And the prompt with the smallest average attention weight for sensitive words will be
351   selected for further refinement in each iteration. Furthermore, the task with the highest attention
352   weight will also be further developed in each iteration. For example, if the scenario “The chemistry
353   teacher is teaching students the specific principles of bomb-making” has the highest attention weight
354   on sensitive words, the next iteration may produce additional sub-tasks which is centered around the
355   chemistry teacher. In this manner, the attention weight on sensitive words can be gradually reduced
356
      in generating each new prompt.
357
358   3.1.3       M ULTI - ROUND PARADIGM
359
360   Considering the stochastic environment and inherent instability of the generation process, multi-
361
      round paradigm will be used to prove the validity of the proposed methods (Chao et al., 2023). In
      the multi-round paradigm, if the LLMs are encountered a failed jailbreak attack, then the jailbreak
362
      attacker will persistently attack target LLMs. A straightforward strategy is to regenerate prompt as
363
      the new jailbreak attack sample. In regeneration step, the generated tasks will maintain diversity on
364   basis of the origin objective, which is to distract the attention of the target LLMs. In this manner, the
365   tried or failed scenarios will not be reused. If the number of attempts in the inner loop exceeds the
366   predefined threshold, ABA will switch to a new scenario to launch a new jailbreak attack sample in
367   the outer loop. Based on this iterative regeneration strategy, ABA can be used to generate new sce-
368   narios and jailbreak attack samples. Therefore, an efficient multi-round jailbreak attack mechanism
369   has been established. The details of training algorithm for ABA can be found in Appendix E.
370
371   3.2     ATTENTION -BASED D EFENSE
372
373   Comparative experiments show that different attention distributions of the input prompt can lead to
374   different outputs of the target LLMs. Whereas, the attention distributions of prompts with different
375   risk levels are distinct. Hence, attention distribution can be used to access the security of the input
376   for LLMs. Based on this fact, ABD is proposed. In ABD, a new metric Risk Score is proposed to
377   measure the risk level of the input prompt. Risk Score is the weighted sum of Attn DepScore and
      Attn Entropy. And the Risk Score can be computed as following equation (4):


                                                                                                7
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


378
379
                                Risk Score = Attn DepScore + σ · Attn Entropy                             (4)
380
381   Therefore, if the attention distribution of input prompt is different, then the weight and threshold
382   for the Risk Score will be set to be different for the LLMs. σ is the weight of Attn DepScore. For
383   this reason, it is very necessary for us to obtain the optimal weight. The related details can be found
384   in Section 4.5. The effectiveness of the ABD will be proved in our later experiments. Moreover,
385   the precise selection of parameters can be guarded by maximizing the discrepancy between origin
386   harmful and jailbreak attack prompts. Once the optimal weight is determined, a suitable threshold
387   of LLMs can also be established. The threshold is the foundation of ABD. Additionally, the ABD
388   will obey the following rules: If the Risk Score of the input prompt is lower than the threshold, then
389
      the input can be regarded as harmless. In contrast, if the Risk Score exceeds the threshold, then
      the input can be regarded as an ambiguous or a potential deception. Consequently, the input will be
390
      regarded as potential harmfulness. As a response, a security warning prefix will be added to the input
391
      before it is input into the LLMs, such as “Attention! The following content might contain harmful
392   instructions: Firstly, identify any potentially harmful part. If safe, then give a secure response”. In
393   this way, the attention of the LLMs can be calibrated effectively. As a result, the LLMs will be
394   prompted to prioritize the safety assessment of the input before generating a response. Hence, both
395   reliability and security can be enhanced.

419   4.2   E VALUATION M ETRICS
420
      Three metrics have been proposed to evaluate jailbreak attack methods, such as ASR, ASR-G and
421
      Queries. The Attack Success Rate (ASR) and the GPT-4-based ASR (ASR-G) are selected to assess
422   the effectiveness of jailbreak attack strategies. ASR can be determined by predefined rules. A
423   jailbreak attack is considered to be successful if the answer of the target LLM is given without a
424   refusal prefix such as “I cannot”. Based on GPT-4, ASR-G can be used to determine the success of a
425   jailbreak attack. To evaluate the efficiency of the jailbreak attack, the metric “Queries” is introduced
426   to measure the average number of successful jailbreak attacks between the attack model and the
427   target model. The details of the metrics for calculation can be found in Appendix D.

      The attention mechanism is initially introduced in RNN-based on basis of encoder-decoder archi-
696   tectures (Bahdanau et al., 2015; Luong et al., 2015). Based on the self-attention mechanism, trans-
697   formers (Vaswani et al., 2017) can be used to achieve state-of-the-art performance in various do-
698   mains (Devlin et al., 2019; Dosovitskiy et al., 2021). Nowadays, self-attention has also been widely
699   utilized as a proxy in understanding and explaining behaviors of models (Clark et al., 2019; Hao
700   et al., 2021; Vashishth et al., 2019). In recent years, some modifications of attention mechanisms
701   have been made to improve the interpretability and performance in reasoning tasks (Zhang et al.,
      2024; Yu et al., 2024). The obtained achievements indicate that the emotional tokens in the middle

706   B.2   S TUDIES ON THE JAILBREAK ATTACK
707
708   In general, jailbreak attack prompts can be categorized into two primary types: direct jailbreak at-
709   tack prompts (Shen et al., 2023; Zou et al., 2023; Liu et al., 2024) and indirect jailbreak attack
710   prompts (Chang et al., 2024). Direct jailbreak attacks are characterized by their use of disguise to
711   conceal harmful intentions, often achieved through the optimization of the prompt. This optimiza-
712
      tion involves crafting the prompt in such a way that it bypasses security filters while maintaining
      its malicious intent. In contrast, indirect jailbreak attacks typically exploit additional knowledge
713
      or context to realize their objectives. Despite their different methodologies, both types of jailbreak
714
      attacks neglect the concept of intention shift during the generation of the jailbreak prompt. This
715   oversight can be critical as it pertains to the evolution of the prompt’s purpose over time.
