009                                                A BSTRACT
010
011            Deep learning has made tremendous progress in the last decades; however, it is
012            not robust to adversarial attacks. Perhaps the most effective approach for this is
013            adversarial training, although it is impractical as it needs prior knowledge about
014            the attackers and incurs high computational costs. In this paper, we propose a
015            novel approach that can train a robust network only through standard training with
016            clean images without awareness of the attacker’s strategy. We add a specially
017            designed network input layer, which accomplishes a randomized feature squeezing
018
               to reduce the malicious perturbation. It achieves the state of the art of robustness
               against unseen l1 , l2 and l∞ attacks at one time in terms of the computational cost
019
               of the attacker versus the defender through just 100/50 epochs of standard training
020
               with clean images in CIFAR-10/ImageNet. Both experiments and Rademacher
021            complexity analysis validate the high performance. Moreover, it can also defend
022            against the “attacks” on training data, i.e., unlearnable examples, seemingly being
023            the only solution for the One-Pixel Shortcut without any data augmentation.

026   1    I NTRODUCTION
027
028   The vulnerability of neural networks has been widely acknowledged by the deep learning community
029   since the seminal work of Szegedy et al. (2014). A lot of solutions have been proposed to solve these
030   problems. They can be categorized into three classes.
031   The first is preprocessing-based approaches which include bit-depth reduction (Xu et al., 2018),
032   JPEG compression, total variance minimization, image quilting (Guo et al., 2018), and Defense-GAN
033   (Samangouei et al., 2018). With this kind of preprocessing, the hope is that the adversarial effect can
034   be reduced. However, it neglects the fact that the adversary can still take this operation into account
035   and craft an effective attack through Backward Pass Differentiable Approximation (BPDA) (Athalye
036   et al., 2018).
037   Secondly, perhaps the most effective method is adversarial training. In the training phase, the attack
038   is mimicked through the backward gradient propagation with respect to the current network state.
039   There is a large volume of work that falls into this class which differs in how to generate extra training
040   samples. Madry et al. (2018) used a classical 7-step PGD attack, while other approaches are also
041   possible, such as Mixup inference (Pang et al., 2020), feature scattering (Zhang & Wang, 2019),
042   feature denoising (Xie et al., 2019), geometry-aware instance reweighting (Zhang et al., 2021), and
043   channel-wise activation suppressing (Bai et al., 2021). External (Gowal et al., 2020) or generated data
044
      (Gowal et al., 2021; Rebuffi et al., 2021) are also beneficial for robustness. The inherent drawbacks
      are the large computation cost and the need for prior knowledge about attacks. This is certainly not
045
      realistic in practice. Also, there is a possibility of robust overfitting (Rice et al., 2020).
046
047   The last is adaptive test-time defenses. They try to purify the input in an iterative way as in Mao et al.
048   (2021); Shi et al. (2021); Yoon et al. (2021) or adapt the model parameters or even network structures
049   to reverse the attack effect. For example, close-loop control is adopted in Chen et al. (2021), and a
050
      neural Ordinary Differential Equation (ODE) layer is applied in Kang et al. (2021). Unfortunately,
      most of them are proven to be not effective in Croce et al. (2022).
051
052   It turns out the progress is not optimistic, and even 1%-2% improvement on AutoAttack(Croce &
053   Hein, 2020a) requires huge computational cost and moreover, not effective for unseen attacks. Here
      we ask a question: “can we design a novel network and loss function thereof that can drive the

054
      network to be robust on its own without awareness of adversarial attacks?” In other words, we do
055   not intend to generate extra adversarial samples like most other approaches do, and standard training
056   with clean images is enough. Indeed, there should be no prior knowledge of attacks needed at all.
057   This certainly poses a great challenge to the construction of networks as it is not clear even whether it
058   is feasible. On the other hand, it appears to be possible since deep networks have a very high capacity.
059   Unfortunately, Ilyas et al. (2019) pointed out network tends to learn discriminant features that can
060   help correct classification, regardless of robustness. It motivates us to take the point of view from the
061   network input side. How can we make a new input layer that is most suitable for network robustness?
062   Our intuition is essentially very simple. As attacks can always walk across the class decision boundary
063
      through the malicious feature perturbations, feature squeezing might be helpful, at least reducing the
      space of being altered, which, indeed, is supported by Rademacher complexity Yin et al. (2019) in
064
      adversarial setting which depends on input feature dimension. However, fundamentally different from
065
      the preprocessing work Xu et al. (2018), the input features are randomized squeezed with parameters
066   learned during training as shown in Figure 1. Moreover, in test we simplify this layer and greatly
067   facilitate the evaluation. The experiments of CIFAR-10 and ImageNet demonstrate this approach
068   is very useful in promoting robustness of networks. It needs to be pointed out that although our
069   main motivation is adversarial defense against unseen attacks, it turns out that ours is much less
070   influenced by the unlearnable examples, i.e., data intentionally manipulated for unauthorized usage
071   for training DNNs. Recently, One-Pixel Shortcut(OPS) has been proposed in Wu et al. (2023) and
072   could effectively degrade model accuracy even to almost an untrained counterpart even equipped
073   with adversarial training, while ours sustain around 60%.
074   To our knowledge, we are the first to clear a tough bar by enhance robustness on unseen attacks
075   without adversarial training. With all the source codes and pre-trained models online A.1, our work
076   has the following contributions:
077
078          • We design a special input layer that uses the reciprocal and multiplication to implement
079
               our randomized feature squeezing, which has not been investigated before. Furthermore,
               it could be easily plunged into different networks such as WideResNet and ConvNeXt to
080
               boost their performance.
081
082          • Our work is the only one that does not require any prior knowledge about the attacks
083
               with standard training with clean images; while significantly outperforms adversarial
               ones with training costs up to 1-5 orders of magnitude higher than ours, for black-box and
084
               l1 , l2 -attacks in both CIFAR-10 and ImageNet.
085
086
             • By transforming our random network into a deterministic one, we draw a connection
               between our robust accuracy and Rademacher complexity, which guarantees our
087
               defense is fully legitimate.
088
089          • Our approach appears to be the only one that can effectively deal with unlearnable examples
090
               generated by the state of the art OPS without any data augmentation.

092   2    R ELATED W ORKS
093
094   There are some works that add some extra preprocessing steps. For example, in Yang et al. (2019),
095   pixels are randomly dropped and then reconstructed using matrix estimation. Ours is not preprocess-
096   ing. We just add an extra layer inside the network, and the network is trained and tested as usual
097   without explicit image completion. Besides this, to get high robust accuracy, Yang et al. (2019) needs
098
      adversarial training while we adopt standard training with clean images.
099   Another related work is certified adversarial robustness via randomized smoothing (Cohen et al.,
100   2019). The base classifier is trained with Gaussian data augmentation, and inference is based on
101   the most likely class of the input perturbed by isotropic Gaussian noise. Ours is based on standard
102   training and testing, and there is no perturbation-based training data augmentation involved at all.
103   Stochastic Neural Networks(SNNs) (Eustratiadis et al., 2021; Däubener & Fischer, 2022; Lee et al.,
104   2023) achieve robustness by intentionally injecting noise to the intermediate layers of the preexisting
105   networks, which is very different from ours. We are motivated by the inherent weakness of the
106   current network design and are trying to modify it such that adversarial defense can be implemented.
107   Moreover, it seems that SNNs are far behind the top-ranking methods in the robustbench leaderboard
      at https://robustbench.github.io/, which we compared with. Moreover, there is no SNNs for ImageNet.

108
      Recently, there are some works that address the robustness from the network architecture’s perspective.
109   Wu et al. (2021) investigates impact of the network width on the model robustness, and proposes
110   Width Adjusted Regularization. Similarly, Huang et al. (2021) explores architectural ingredients
111   of adversarially robust deep neural networks in a thorough manner. Liu et al. (2023a) established
112   that the higher weight sparsity is beneficial for adversarially robust generalization via Rademacher
113   complexity. Wang et al. (2022) proposes batch normalization removal, such that adversarial training
114   can be improved. Singla et al. (2021) shows that using activation functions with low curvature values
115   reduces both the standard and robust generalization gaps in adversarial training. They are in some
116   sense similar to ours, but our motivations are fundamentally different. There is no adversarial training
117
      involved in our approach at all.
118   There are some attempts (Tramèr & Boneh, 2019; Maini et al., 2020; Croce & Hein, 2022; Laidlaw
119   et al., 2021; Dai et al., 2022b) to deal with multiple attacks simultaneously. Among them, the only
120   relevant works for unseen attacks are Laidlaw et al. (2021); Dai et al. (2022b), however they
121   adopt costly adversarial training, and only for CIFAR-10. There are also some benchmarks (Dai
122   et al., 2023; Kang et al., 2019) which extend beyond the lp attacks.
123   Adversarial purification is another research line to defend against unseen attacks, but it is extremely
124   slow. For example, in Table 14 of Nie et al. (2022), the inference time is around (100-300)x of
125   standard one. Also, they need pre-trained diffusion models, which are very expensive to train.
126   Another big disadvantage is that the evaluation of robustness for these methods is very difficult due
127   to very high memory consumption. Moreover, adversarial purification cannot deal with OPS, as the
128   underlying static model has very low clean accuracy.

260   4.3   L AST M OVE
261
      We emphasize here that since our approach is random, a same sample could be classified with different
262
      logits when executed multiple times. It will seriously mislead attackers, which will report a wrong
263
      robust accuracy. For that reason, we always take the last-move advantage. In other words, in test
264   time, we always take the adversarial samples generated by attackers and feed them to our network
265   once again to test. We think that is fair in practice. Attackers can always take an arbitrarily long
266   time to figure out a malicious sample, but they only have one chance to submit it. It is the attacker’s
267   responsibility to provide a stable adversarial sample, and the last move is always the defender’s
268   privilege. In fact, other works, for example Wang et al. (2021), also adopt this under different contexts.
269   Some theoretical analysis is provided in Däubener & Fischer (2022) for this setting. Please refer to
      Appendix A.2 for more explanations.

461   5.3   A BLATION S TUDIES
462
463   As our approach is very special in that the test framework is different from the training one, a natural
464   concern will arise: “Why not directly train in the configuration of the test?” In essence, this can be
465   regarded as special case of our training framework where the parameters of all the CONV3×3 for
466   sampling matrix x̂ are manually set to be zero, then the reciprocal will become very big, and the
467
      Sigmoid is approximately equal to Sign. Accordingly there is no need to have S in loss Equation 8.
      However, very importantly, we still need to have α in the first cross-entropy term to have a stable
468
      training, which shows exactly this is just the special case of our more general training setting. We
469
      adopt this training scheme in both CIFAR-10 and ImageNet, and make some comparisons of EOT-20
470   under APGD-ce attack with our method. The results are shown in Table 7. For CIFAR-10, it is
471   very interesting to notice that training in the configuration of the test is always less robust than ours,
472   especially for l1 -ϵ=12, around 20 in performance gap, which indicates that our specially designed
473   input layer can make the training very efficient. However, for ImagetNet, the performance is quite
474   similar, this might be due to the small β = 0.01 in Equation 7 and quick convergence of S to nearly
475   zero in Equation 8.

482   of the same test image to the test framework, each with a different but fixed seed of noise, and then
483   the average logits of N outputs are used to get the final classification. It might be possible that our
484   training scheme implements implicit adversarial training due to the added random noise; however, it is
485   unclear how it relates to test robust accuracy. It appears that the feature squeezing is beneficial in this
