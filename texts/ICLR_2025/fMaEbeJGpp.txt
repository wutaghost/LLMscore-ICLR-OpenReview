                                                   A BSTRACT
010
011
               Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) combines the richness of external knowl-
012            edge bases with the generative capabilities of large language models (LLMs) to
013            provide users with more accurate and real-time responses. However, in the era
014            of information explosion, the way information is presented is increasingly be-
015            coming multimodal. Users are no longer satisfied with the information provided
016            by traditional text-based knowledge bases, making the construction of an effi-
017            cient and accurate multimodal RAG question-answering system of significant the-
018            oretical and practical importance. To address these issues, this paper proposes
019            an innovative RAG question-answering system: this approach pre-designs a rich
020
               dataset containing images, text, and question-answer pairs from external knowl-
               edge bases for subsequent model training, effectively improving the training qual-
021
               ity of the model; it builds a cross-modal retrieval model from text to images, en-
022
               suring precise matching between document content and corresponding images,
023            significantly reducing the complexity and processing time of locating relevant
024            images within long texts. Furthermore, the retrieval model and the multimodal
025            question-answering model are integrated to construct an efficient and accurate
026            RAG question-answering system. Experimental results show that this system not
027            only effectively simplifies the document formatting process and improves text-to-
028            image retrieval accuracy but also exhibits comprehensive performance in handling
029            multimodal data.

      1    I NTRODUCTION
033
      Large Language Models (LLMs) have demonstrated remarkable success in performing question-
034
      answering tasks (Brown et al., 2020). By training on vast datasets, these models leverage their
035
      extensive parameterized memory to generate query responses that meet user requirements (Kojima
036   et al., 2023). However, no training dataset can fully encompass all domains or address critical de-
037   tails, particularly in the current era of rapid data growth, where data iteration occurs at an exponential
038   rate and information is increasingly presented in multimodal formats. As a result, when LLMs are
039   required to respond to the latest information or handle knowledge-intensive questions with ambigu-
040   ous factual grounding (Petroni et al., 2021), they may produce responses that are inconsistent with
041   reality, sometimes even generating answers based on hallucinated knowledge (Huang et al., 2023).
042   Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) (Lewis et al., 2021) has gradually become a mainstream
043   solution in the industry to address these issues. By integrating information retrieved from external
044   databases into the model’s context (Gao et al., 2023), this approach effectively reduces factual errors
045   in LLMs when tackling knowledge-intensive tasks. It not only enables efficient access to external,
046   rich knowledge bases but also helps LLMs incorporate knowledge in a timely and accurate manner
047   before responding.
048   Of course, this method has its limitations. The current retrieval modules often fail to achieve the
049   desired precision for specific tasks. For instance, when answering complex questions, this method
050   frequently requires retrieving relevant information from multiple documents to ensure that the con-
051   text contains the necessary information for the response (Petroni et al., 2021). This practice increases
052   the input length for LLMs, introducing additional delays in encoding lengthy retrieval documents
053   and posing complex inference challenges (Ding et al., 2023). Furthermore, external databases often
      contain a wealth of diverse information, such as images, tables, and complex charts. The intri-

      cate information embedded in charts is often difficult to convey effectively through simple textual
055   descriptions(Kembhavi et al., 2016), and the brevity of annotations further complicates understand-
056   ing. This makes it challenging for traditional text-based retrieval methods to provide precise an-
057   swers when handling and interpreting multimodal data, thereby affecting the overall performance of
058   question-answering systems. Therefore, enhancing the retrieval and generation capabilities to han-
059   dle multimodal information is crucial to improving the accuracy of question-answering systems(Li
060   et al., 2019).
061   In summary, to address the aforementioned issues, this paper proposes an innovative multimodal
062   retrieval-augmented generation question-answering system. The system integrates both a text-image
063   retrieval module and a visual multimodal model, aiming to overcome the limitations of traditional
064   frameworks in processing chart information and to enhance the accuracy of generated answers by
065   pre-parsing documents. The main contributions of this paper are threefold:

               timodal scenarios.

               document-based multimodal RAG system. The system aims to simplify traditional doc-
077            ument processing workflows, avoiding complex preprocessing steps (such as document
078            parsing, OCR layout analysis, and text chunking) that may introduce errors and lead to in-
079            accurate information transmission. It significantly simplifies the RAG document processing
080            flow and directly answers user queries based on image content.

      2   R ELATED W ORK
083
084
      Retrieval-Augmented Generation In text-image retrieval tasks, models must align visual and
085   textual information for effective cross-modal matching(Lu et al., 2019). Recent advancements have
086   improved feature alignment, retrieval efficiency, and adaptability in multilingual contexts (e.g., Chi-
087   nese). Recent work (Chen et al., 2023; Tan & Bansal, 2019) proposed combining global and local
088   alignment, mapping image regions to text fragments for precise cross-modal matching, address-
089   ing the limitations of relying solely on global alignment. Retrieval efficiency remains a challenge
090   with large datasets. Another study (Miech et al., 2021) optimized this using hash encoding and
091   model compression, which reduces storage and computational costs but may slightly affect accu-
092   racy. Fast-slow combination strategies further balance efficiency and accuracy. In Chinese contexts,
093
      retrieval models face challenges in aligning linguistic and visual features. Recent research (Huang
      et al., 2020; Yu et al., 2020) enhanced performance using cross-modal pre-training and contrastive
094
      learning-based alignment methods. This paper adopts the ColPali model (Faysse et al., 2024), which
095
      generates high-quality contextual embeddings and employs post-interaction matching to enhance re-
096   trieval speed and performance, supporting end-to-end training.
097
098   Image-Text Retrieval In image-text retrieval, models must align visual and textual information
099
      for effective cross-modal matching. Recent work (Chen et al., 2023) proposed combining global
      and local alignment to improve fine-grained semantic matching, mapping image regions to text
100
      fragments for greater precision. Retrieval efficiency is a challenge with large data volumes. Another
101
      study (Miech et al., 2021) addressed this with hash encoding and model compression, reducing
102   storage costs but potentially affecting accuracy. ”Fast-slow combination” strategies also improve
103   the balance between efficiency and accuracy. In Chinese contexts, models often struggle with text-
104   image alignment due to linguistic and visual feature complexities. Research (Huang et al., 2020)
105   improved performance using cross-modal pre-training and contrastive learning-based alignment.
106   This paper uses the ColPali model (Faysse et al., 2024), which generates high-quality embeddings
107   from document images. It applies a post-interaction matching mechanism, enhancing speed and
      retrieval performance, while supporting end-to-end training.

      Problem Description: In knowledge-intensive tasks, each entry can be represented as (Q, A, D),
112
      where Q is a question or statement requiring external knowledge to answer; A is the expected
113   answer; and D is a set of n relevant documents retrieved from an external database. In practi-
114   cal document retrieval, aside from textual information, there are often complex charts (e.g., line
115   graphs, flowcharts) and even realistic images that are relevant to the question. These visual ele-
116   ments can effectively assist in generating the expected answer A(Kafle & Kanan, 2017). The goal
117   of the multimodal retrieval-augmented generation question-answering system is to generate high-
118   quality answers by using the top N most relevant images retrieved, combined with a pre-generated
119   answer(Zhang et al., 2020).
120
121   3.1   OVERVIEW
122
      This paper presents an innovative multimodal retrieval-augmented generation question-answering
123
      system designed to enhance performance in handling complex documents and tasks while main-
124
      taining processing speed. Unlike traditional methods that rely solely on text, this system integrates
125   cross-modal retrieval and multimodal question-answering. In response to user queries, it retrieves
126   the top K relevant images and uses a multimodal model to analyze them for direct question answer-
127   ing, enabling efficient text-image retrieval and intelligent question-answering.
128
      As shown in Figure 1, the process begins by converting the document content into Markdown-
129
      formatted text along with corresponding images, and constructing a rich dataset containing these
130
      elements. By training a text-image retrieval model, optimized with contrastive learning and a cross-
131   entropy loss function, the system ensures that the retrieval model can accurately locate relevant
132   images from the document content based on the question. The retrieved images are then passed to
133   the multimodal question-answering model, which fully understands the user’s query and combines
134   the information from the retrieved images to generate effective and accurate answers. In the system
135   constructed in this paper, the text-to-image retrieval model and the multimodal question-answering
136   model work in conjunction to achieve efficient document processing and answer generation.

153   3.2   DATASET C ONSTRUCTION
154
      Building a high-quality multimodal retrieval-augmented question-answering system depends on a
155
      diverse dataset that significantly affects model training and the knowledge base’s quality(Baltrusaitis
156   et al., 2019). Each document page from the external knowledge base is converted into Markdown
157   format (including text, tables, and charts)(Tapaswi et al., 2016), and GPT-4o is employed to extract
158   relevant question-answer pairs (standard text, chart, and image-based Q&A)(Raffel et al., 2020).
159   This pre-processed and structured information allows the retrieval module to efficiently capture rel-
160   evant data. The multimodal model then evaluates this content to complete the Q&A tasks. Dataset
161   preparation involves document collection, preprocessing, and Q&A pair generation(Kwiatkowski
      et al., 2019).

      3.2.1    D OCUMENT C OLLECTION
163
164   The core of RAG technology is to help large language models connect with external knowledge
165   bases, enabling them to provide rich answers for specific domains. A large number of PDFs and
166   Word documents are collected from multiple sources to ensure that the knowledge base covers a
167   wide range of topics and content in the field. These documents include, but are not limited to,
168   professional papers, industry reports, white papers, and are sourced from public databases, online
169   resources, etc.
170
171   3.2.2    D OCUMENT P REPROCESSING AND C ONVERSION
172
173   To construct an efficient and accurate training set, information must be extracted from a large volume
174   of PDF and Word documents, and each page of the document must undergo the following processes:
175
176           • Markdown Format Text Conversion: This study converts the text content of each
177             page into Markdown format (MD test) using layout analysis-OCR tools. Markdown is
178             lightweight(He et al., 2020), easy to read, and easy to process, and is widely used in doc-
179             ument writing and data processing. After the initial conversion, GPT-4o is used to format
180             and correct the Markdown text, ensuring clear and standardized text structures that enhance
181
                the efficiency and accuracy of subsequent text processing and analysis.
182
              • Image Generation:To ensure high-quality image generation for multimodal retrieval, PDF
183             pages are first converted into high-resolution images(Deng et al., 2009). These images
184             are optimized by adjusting resolution and cropping unnecessary edges. Data augmenta-
185             tion techniques, such as blurring and brightness adjustments, are applied to improve the
186             model’s generalization(Simonyan & Zisserman, 2015). Additionally, scanned document
187             images and diverse text-image datasets are introduced to enhance performance across vari-
188             ous scenarios(He et al., 2016). Finally, the optimized images are matched with correspond-
189             ing Markdown text for efficient multimodal processing, ensuring clarity and data diversity.
190
              • Question-Answer Pair Generation: This paper uses a high-quality, large-scale pre-
191
                trained model (such as GPT-4o)(Devlin et al., 2019) to generate question-answer pairs
192
                (QA = [(q1 , a1 ), . . . , (qn , an )]) that are highly relevant to the document content from
193             the generated Markdown text. This process covers not only textual information but also
194             charts, flowcharts, and other types of information, ensuring that the Q&A pairs compre-
195             hensively reflect the document’s overall content and details. The generated Q&A pairs
196             have the following advantages: First, the question-answer pairs generated by the large
197             model are more standardized and consistent in format and content expression, helping the
198             subsequent models more effectively capture key information in the document(Brown et al.,
199             2020). Second, the generated Q&A pairs are well-matched with the extracted text, images,
200             and charts, ensuring the overall consistency and coherence of the dataset(Radford et al.,
201
                2019). This provides rich and high-quality data (IMG, MD test, QA) support for subse-
                quent model training.
202
203
204   3.3     T EXT-I MAGE R ETRIEVAL M ODEL
205
206   This paper uses a specialized text-image retrieval model based on the ColPali architecture, fine-
207   tuned with a custom dataset for enhanced performance in document retrieval tasks. The model
208   focuses on efficiently retrieving images from text, supporting the visual question-answering model
209   and improving answer accuracy by capturing contextual information.Unlike complex semantic tasks,
210   the model emphasizes generating high-quality contextual embeddings from document images. The
211   ColPali model is fine-tuned to optimize the relationship between text tokens and image patches,
212
      improving precision through a late-stage interaction matching mechanism.
213   This approach simplifies traditional document retrieval by bypassing OCR and layout analysis,
214   achieving faster indexing by directly processing document images. The fine-tuned ColPali model
215   excels in retrieving visually rich content, demonstrating strong performance across multiple domains
      and languages.

      This section provides a detailed explanation of how to build an efficient multimodal Retrieval-
220   Augmented Generation (RAG) question-answering system by integrating dataset construction, text-
221   image retrieval models, and multimodal question-answering models. The system aims to leverage
222   the advantages of multimodal information processing to provide users with accurate and compre-
223   hensive answers, demonstrating exceptional performance in handling complex document scenarios.

228   3.4.1    S YSTEM A RCHITECTURE
229
230
231   The entire RAG question-answering system architecture consists of two main modules: the text-
232   image retrieval module and the multimodal question-answering module. These modules work col-
233   laboratively to cover the complete process from data input to answer output.
234
235
236           • Text-Image Retrieval Module: This module, based on the ColPali framework, builds
237             a retrieval model to efficiently match and retrieve images from text. By comparing the
238             user’s query with the text and images in the documents, the retrieval module quickly locates
239             relevant pages and provides them to the multimodal question-answering module for further
240             processing.
241
242
243
244           • Multimodal Question-Answering Module: Based on the GPT-4o model, this mod-
245             ule generates high-quality answers by integrating visual and linguistic features from the
246
                top NNN most relevant images retrieved. The introduction of the multimodal question-
                answering model greatly enhances the system’s ability to handle complex queries.
247

252   3.4.2    S YSTEM W ORKFLOW
253
254
255
      In the actual operation of the multimodal RAG question-answering system, the system first parses
      the user’s input query and generates embedding vectors suitable for retrieval and question-answering
256
      tasks. This step leverages a pre-trained language model to generate high-dimensional embedding
257
      representations, ensuring that the query content is accurately captured to support the subsequent
258   retrieval process. The system calculates the similarity between the query vector and image vectors
259   to retrieve relevant documents from the external knowledge base, using the retrieval model to extract
260   relevant images or pages associated with the query.
261
      During this process, the retrieval module not only integrates the semantic information from the text
262
      but also incorporates visual information, ensuring that the results reflect the document content com-
263
      prehensively. The retrieved images are then passed to the GPT-4o multimodal model for processing.
264   This model can deeply analyze the images and, in conjunction with the textual information con-
265   tained within the images, generate answers that are highly consistent with the query context. By
266   precisely aligning visual and textual features, the GPT-4o model ensures that the answers fully re-
267   flect the critical information in the images while maintaining coherence and accuracy. Finally, the
268   system optimizes the generated answers, including format adjustments, filtering of redundant in-
269   formation, and enhancing linguistic coherence to ensure that the user receives the highest-quality
      answer output. The detailed algorithm workflow is shown in Table 1.

488   Response speed (latency) is a key metric for evaluating multimodal question-answering systems,

492   but its inability to handle images and tables reduces its accuracy in multimodal tasks. Haystack 2.0
493
      RAG, with OCR integration, improves multimodal accuracy but suffers from slower response times
      due to added computational demands. Chinese-CLIP RAG offers high retrieval accuracy, but its
494
      tightly integrated retrieval and generation modules lead to longer response times.

497   tational redundancy, achieves a fast response time of 1.8 seconds while maintaining high generation

502   This paper proposed a multimodal retrieval-augmented generation question-answering system that
503   integrates a high-quality dataset, a image-text retrieval model, and a multimodal question-answering
504   model. Experimental results demonstrate that the system excels in retrieval accuracy, generation
505   quality, and response speed when processing complex documents, particularly in chart-intensive
506   scenarios, greatly enhancing the user experience.
507   Despite these positive results, the system still has room for optimization in handling complex mul-

510   systems in more real-world scenarios.
