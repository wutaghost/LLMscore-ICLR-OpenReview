009                                                A BSTRACT
010
011            Class-Incremental Learning (CIL) aims to learn knowledge from new classes se-
012            quentially, while rataining the knowledge obtained from previously encountered
013            classes, thereby mitigating the challenge of Catastrophic Forgetting. In a more
014            realistic scenario, future unseen classes may contain only a few samples, leading to
015            a new challenge of over-fitting, which is referred to as Few-Shot Class-Incremental
016
               Learning (FSCIL). Existing works explore FSCIL from various perspectives, such
               as classifier calibration and backbone extension. Most of them treat the many-shot
017
               base session and incremental few-shot sessions separately, as the model tends to
018
               overfit on few-shot classes. In this paper, we propose Singular Value Fine-tuning
019            for few-shot Class-incremental Learning (SVFCL) to constantly learn base and
020            incremental sessions based on the pre-trained ViT encoder. SVFCL incorporates
021            incremental adapters, each of which is attached to a corresponding pre-trained mod-
022            ule and contains only a small number of learnable parameters, effectively reducing
023            the risk of overfitting. Furthermore, since each adapter is task-specific, information
024            from previous tasks is well-preserved, mitigating catastrophic forgetting. Our ex-
025            perimental results demonstrate that SVFCL achieves substantial improvements over
026            state-of-the-art methods while requiring significantly less computational overhead
027
               and epochs.

029   1    I NTRODUCTION
030
031   Given the unprecedented increase in computational resources and data availability, the ability to
032   continuously learn from new data while retaining previously acquired knowledge is crucial for
033   developing truly intelligent systems. Class-Incremental Learning (CIL) (Zhou et al., 2023; Rebuffi
034   et al., 2017; Li & Hoiem, 2017; Kirkpatrick et al., 2017) seeks to address this challenge by introducing
035   new classes sequentially, requiring the model to adapt to changes in data distribution while preserving
036   knowledge of previously learned classes. However, the practical implementation of CIL is limited by
037   the difficulty of acquiring sufficiently large datasets for new classes, which significantly constrains its
038   application in real-world scenarios.
039   Few-Shot Class-Incremental Learning (FSCIL) (Tao et al., 2020; Tian et al., 2024b; Zhang et al.,
040   2021; Peng et al., 2022; Zhou et al., 2022; Yang et al., 2023) focuses on the incremental learning
041   scenario where newly introduced classes have only a limited number of samples. Unlike traditional
042   CIL, FSCIL begins by training on a task with a sufficiently large dataset to establish prior knowledge,
043   followed by subsequent tasks that include only a small number of samples. This scenario highlights
044   the model’s ability to sequentially learn new classes from limited samples while retaining previously
045
      learned knowledge, thereby enhancing its practical applicability in real-world contexts. Conversely,
      the practical setting of learning incrementally with limited samples also introduces two key problems
046
      in the demanding FSCIL scenario:
047
048       ◦ (Forgetting) During the sequential training, the model will experience a significant drop in
049
            performance on previously learned tasks after training on new tasks.
050       ◦ (Overfitting) Due to the limited data of the new tasks, the model may overfit to these examples,
051
            which negatively impacts its performance on these tasks.
052   Most previous studies primarily employed shallow models such as ResNet-18, operating solely with
053   a constrained set of training parameters to address these challenges. Specifically, Zhang et al. (2021)
      first proposed decoupling the training phases of representations and classification head to effectively

054
      mitigate forgetting. Subsequently, Shi et al. (2021) highlighted that pre-training on the base session
055   is more critical than adapting to new sessions, which inspired a wide range of works that focus on
056   training the backbone exclusively on the base session while tuning the classification head on the
057   subsequent few-shot sessions (Zhou et al., 2022; Peng et al., 2022; Wang et al., 2024; Ahmed et al.,
058   2024; Yang et al., 2023; Akyürek et al., 2021; Hersche et al., 2022). However, these methods based on
059   shallow models are inadequate for effectively transferring domain knowledge from the base session
060   to the few-shot sessions.
061   Recently, many studies have applied large pre-trained models, such as Vision Transformer (ViT)
062   (Alexey, 2020), to FSCIL, as these models can provide a backbone with strong generalization
063   capabilities (Liu et al., 2024; Park et al., 2024; D’Alessandro et al., 2023). Most of these works adopt
064   a prompt learning paradigm, which involves designing specific input prompts to guide the pre-trained
065   backbone to perform well across various few-shot sessions. However, these approaches typically
066   utilize a completely frozen backbone, which renders performance highly dependent on the underlying
067   model’s capabilities and, consequently, may result in inferior performance when suboptimal models
068   are employed. On the flip side, attempting to fine-tune all parameters of these large models can lead
069
      to catastrophic overfitting, especially considering the extremely limited samples available in few-shot
      sessions. This arises a critical question: how can we effectively fine-tune large models to address the
070
      challenging issue of overfitting in FSCIL?
071
072   To address this challenge, this paper proposes a simple yet effective framework, called Singular
073   Value Fine-tuning for Class-incremental Learning (SVFCL), to simultaneously mitigate catastrophic
074   forgetting and overfitting. The core idea of this method is to treat both the many-shot base session
075
      and the subsequent few-shot sessions equally by adding task-specific adapters for each session. This
      strategy facilitates the retention of prior knowledge within these adapters, effectively overcoming
076
      forgetting by enabling the model to access previously learned information during the continual
077
      learning stage. To prevent the adapters from introducing a substantial parameter load that could lead
078   to overfitting in few-shot sessions, we further propose a singular value fine-tuning strategy, which
079   decomposes the pre-trained weights—such as those of the linear layers in ViT—using Singular Value
080   Decomposition (SVD) to obtain two singular matrices and singular values, and tunes parameters
081   solely in the singular value positions for each incremental session. This approach reduces the number
082   of learnable parameters, thereby minimizing the risk of overfitting; furthermore, it provides valuable
083   insights into how parameters in the inner layers of ViT evolve during incremental training. Overall,
084   the main contributions of this paper are summarized as follows:
085
             • We propose the SVFCL, a simple yet effective strategy to jointly mitigate both catastrophic
086
               forgetting and overfitting in the FSCIL scenario.
087
088
             • We design lightweight adapters based on singular value fine-tuning to capture task-specific
               knowledge for each incremental session, which introduces quite few learnable parameters
089
               and thereby alleviating overfitting in FSCIL.
090
091
             • We conduct extensive experiments on three commonly used datasets, where the proposed
               SVFCL achieves new state-of-the-art results in FSCIL, demonstrating its effectiveness and
092
               robustness.

095   2    R ELATED W ORKS
096
097   Continual learning aims to address the catastrophic forgetting problem (Zhou et al., 2023). Con-
098   ventional class-incremental learning methods can be divided into four mainstream categories as
099
      follows. First, regularization-based methods (Kirkpatrick et al., 2017; Chaudhry et al., 2018; Zenke
      et al., 2017; Aljundi et al., 2018) assume that network parameters have varying importance for
100
      different tasks. The essence of these methods is to allocate importance to parameters and impose
101
      penalties in loss items to preserve old knowledge. Second, replay-based methods (Rebuffi et al.,
102   2017; Buzzega et al., 2020; De Lange & Tuytelaars, 2021; Chaudhry et al., 2018; Bang et al., 2021)
103   mitigate catastrophic forgetting by maintaining some old exemplars in the memory buffer during
104   current training. However, these methods often fall short due to real-world constraints, such as data
105   privacy and storage limitations. Third, while Hinton et al. (2015) proposed Knowledge Distillation
106   (KD) technology to transfer knowledge from a teacher model to a student model, some methods (Li
107   & Hoiem, 2017; Lee et al., 2019; Hou et al., 2018; Zhai et al., 2019; Dhar et al., 2019) incorporate
      knowledge distillation to constrain model update. They view the old model as a teacher and the


                                                         2
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


108
      current model as a student and alleviate catastrophic forgetting by transferring knowledge from the
109   old model to the current model. Fourth, architecture-based methods (Yan et al., 2021; Li et al., 2019;
110   Rusu et al., 2016) typically expand the network structure to enhance its capacity for learning specific
111   tasks.
112
      Parameter-Efficient-Fine-Tuning (PEFT) aims to adapt large pre-trained models to diverse down-
113
      stream tasks efficiently by incorporating extra lightweight learnable parameters while freezing the
114
      pre-trained backbone, thereby circumventing the computational and storage costs associated with
115   direct fine-tuning of large models. With the rapid development of large pre-trained models, a growing
116   number of PEFT methods have emerged, such as prefix tuning (Li & Liang, 2021), prompt tuning
117   (Lester et al., 2021; Jia et al., 2022), adapter tuning (Houlsby et al., 2019), scale and shift tuning
118   (SSF) (Lian et al., 2022), low-rank tuning (Hu et al., 2021; Sun et al., 2022), and so on.
119
      In the field of class-incremental learning, approaches incorporating PEFT are becoming increasingly
120
      popular. L2P (Wang et al., 2022b) first proposes the prompt pool to select task-specific prompts during
121   incremental learning. DualPrompt (Wang et al., 2022a) further introduces task-invariant prompts
122   to learn knowledge shared between diverse tasks. Numerous subsequent prompt-based continual
123   learning approaches (Liu et al., 2024; D’Alessandro et al., 2023; Tian et al., 2024a) have adopted the
124   aforementioned strategy of combining task-invariant and task-specific prompts. CodaP Smith et al.
125   (2023) further enhances the training and selecting strategies in an end-to-end manner.
126
      Few-Shot Class-Incremental Learning (FSCIL) was first proposed by Tao et al. (2020) and is
127   expected to incrementally learn new few-shot tasks while given at first a base task with abundant
128   samples for base training. Panos et al. (2023) also explores a more extreme scenario in which no
129   base task with sufficient data is given and only a small number of data are accessible in all sessions,
130   including the first. Following Tian et al. (2024b), existing FSCIL methods can be roughly categorized
131   into five groups: traditional machine learning methods, meta-learning-based methods, feature-and-
132   feature-space-based methods, replay-based methods and architecture-based methods. Additionally,
133   another taxonomy divides FSCIL methods into two categories: base-focus (Zhang et al., 2021; Zhou
134   et al., 2022; Wang et al., 2024; Yang et al., 2023; Akyürek et al., 2021; Hersche et al., 2022; Ahmed
135
      et al., 2024; Peng et al., 2022) and incremental-focus (Kim et al., 2024; Tao et al., 2020), in terms
      of their training emphasis. This distinction is supported by empirical evidence from studies such
136
      as Zhang et al. (2021) and Shi et al. (2021), which demonstrate that a backbone trained on the
137
      base session is more critical than merely calibrating the feature space for updating knowledge in
138   incremental sessions. However, these methods mainly rely on shallow models, which are insufficient
139   to effectively transfer domain knowledge from the base session. This limitation prompts our focus on
140   fine-tuning foundation models in this paper.
141
      FSCIL methods based on foundation models have gradually attracted attention in recently years.
142
      Leveraging the generalization ability of foundation models, these methods can quickly adapt to
143
      incremental tasks and achieve remarkable performance. We can roughly categorize these methods in
144   terms of their backbones, i.e. Vision Transformer (ViT) (Alexey, 2020) or large Vision-Language-
145   Model (VLM) such as CLIP (Radford et al., 2021). Liu et al. (2024) propose the Attention-aware
146   Self-adaptive Prompt (ASP) framework with ViT as its network backbone. ASP utilizes task-invariant
147   prompts to capture shared knowledge and self-adaptive task-specific prompts to obtain specific
148   information. However, ASP suffers from a high training cost, as it requires an extra pass over
149   the training data in each epoch to cluster class centers. Compared to ASP, our proposed SVFCL
150   introduces a lightweight computational overhead and benefits from enhanced training efficiency.
151   There are also works that employ vision and language models. Specifically, Park et al. (2024) propose
152
      PriViLege, which combines prompting functions and knowledge distillation. D’Alessandro et al.
      (2023) propose CPE-CLIP, a CLIP-based framework that utilizes prompting techniques to generalize
153
      pre-trained knowledge to incremental sessions. Different from these methods, our approach does not
154
      incorporate any textual priors yet achieves competitive performance.

180   matrices, which provide a powerful tool for revealing intrinsic properties and structures within

187   of singular values is equal to the rank of M , which play an important role in providing information

528   6     C ONCLUSION
529
530   In this work, we focus on jointly mitigating both catastrophic forgetting and overfitting in the
531   FSCIL scenario, particularly when leveraging pre-trained models. We propose a simple yet effective
532   framework that presents the SVFCL strategy to fine-tune the pre-trained ViT backbone for each
533   incremental session. SVFCL effectively addresses the challenges of overfitting and catastrophic
534   forgetting while incurring relatively few learnable parameters and minimal computational overhead.
535   Our final experiments demonstrate that SVFCL achieves promising performance improvements
536   compared to various state-of-the-art methods. In the future, we aim to further explore the feasibility of
537
      integrating our SVFCL with other advanced architectures to enhance its performance and applicability
      across diverse datasets.
