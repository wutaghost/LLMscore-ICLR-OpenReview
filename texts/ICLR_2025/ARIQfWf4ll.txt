                                                 A BSTRACT
014
015            Despite significant advancements in general artificial intelligence, such as GPT-4,
016            their effectiveness in the medical domain (general medical AI, GMAI) remains
017            constrained due to the absence of specialized medical knowledge. To address
018
               this challenge, we present GMAI-VL-5.5M, a comprehensive multimodal medical
               dataset created by converting hundreds of specialized medical datasets into metic-
019
               ulously constructed image-text pairs. This dataset features comprehensive task
020
               coverage, diverse modalities, and high-quality image-text data. Building upon this
021            multimodal dataset, we propose GMAI-VL, a general medical vision-language
022            model with a progressively three-stage training strategy. This approach signifi-
023            cantly enhances the model’s ability by integrating visual and textual information,
024            thereby improving its ability to process multimodal data and support accurate di-
025            agnosis and clinical decision-making. Experimental evaluations demonstrate that
026            GMAI-VL achieves state-of-the-art results across a wide range of multimodal
027            medical tasks, such as visual question answering and medical image diagnosis.
028            Our contributions include the development of the GMAI-VL-5.5M dataset, the in-
029
               troduction of the GMAI-VL model, and the establishment of new benchmarks in
               multiple medical domains.

033   1   I NTRODUCTION
034
035
      Large-scale Vision-Language Models (LVLMs) have rapidly evolved in recent years, effectively in-
036   tegrating visual perception with language understanding by leveraging large-scale multimodal data,
037   which enables them to capture complex visual and textual patterns and drive significant advance-
038   ments in image recognition, natural language processing, and multimodal tasks. With the advance-
039   ment of multimodal integration technologies, the demand for high-precision processing of diverse
040   data types in the medical field has become increasingly critical. The ability to effectively integrate
041   and analyze various data modalities, such as medical images, clinical text, and structured clinical
042   records, is pivotal for achieving accurate and comprehensive diagnostic and treatment decisions.
043   However, existing LVLMs, such as GPT-4 (Achiam et al., 2023), are limited in medical applications
044   due to their lack of domain-specific knowledge, highlighting the need for specialized solutions that
045   effectively integrate medical expertise. Addressing this challenge requires constructing a compre-
046   hensive medical vision-language dataset and developing domain-specific models. For the medical
047   dataset, it should provide high-quality medical knowledge, including the following three aspects:
048   Comprehensive Medical Task. To enhance the model’s applicability across various medical sce-
049   narios, the dataset should cover a wide range of medical contexts, such as disease types, symptoms,
050   and treatments. Comprehensive task coverage can improve the model’s generalization ability and
051   increase its reliability in real-world applications. However, existing models often focus on specific
052   domains (He et al., 2024; Li et al., 2024b; Xin Yan, 2023; Thawakar et al., 2024; Kapadnis et al.,
053   2024), limiting their broader applicability. Expanding the dataset’s scope will further enhance the
      model’s utility in clinical practice.

054
      Rich Multimodal Representation. A well-rounded medical multimodal dataset should encompass
055   various modalities, including different medical imaging types (such as CT, MRI, and X-rays) and
056   diverse forms of textual data (such as medical records and imaging reports). This would allow mod-
057   els to better integrate multi-source information and improve their analytical capabilities. However,
058   existing methods tend to focus on a single type of medical imagery (Johnson et al., 2019; Wu et al.,
059   2023b; Lu et al., 2024b), limiting the model’s adaptability to diverse clinical scenarios. A more
060   diverse multimodal dataset would provide a foundation for developing more comprehensive models,
061   better suited to the complexity of real-world medical environments.
062   High-Quality Image-Text Data. High-quality training data is crucial for model performance. For
063   medical applications, ideal image-text data should include a large collection of medical images with
064   precise textual descriptions, enhancing the model’s understanding of key medical concepts, includ-
065   ing diagnosis, treatment, and clinical workflows, ultimately improving clinical outcomes. Although
066   progress has been made by collecting data from sources like PubMed (Li et al., 2024a; Moor et al.,
067   2023; Wu et al., 2023b), which has inconsistent data quality, imprecise alignment and lack of stan-
068   dardization, limiting its potential.
069   Based on above observations, we propose a methodology for developing a comprehensive multi-
070   modal medical datasets. The methodology begins by collecting large-scale, open-source medical
071   imaging datasets and extracting key details such as modality, task type, labels, and bounding boxes.
072   A vision-language model (e.g., GPT-4o) is then used to transform these datasets, covering tasks like
073   lesion detection, segmentation, and disease diagnosis, into high-quality image-text pairs for training
074   LVLMs. To ensure data quality, extracted image information is incorporated into the prompt design,
075
      improving model performance across various clinical tasks. This results in a comprehensive mul-
      timodal dataset with 5.5M samples, named GMAI-VL-5.5M, which supports the development of
076
      general medical LVLMs. Fig. 1(a) illustrates the sources, departments, modalities, task types, and
077
      instruction formats of the constructed dataset.
078
079   With the constructed GMAI-VL-5.5M dataset, we develop a general medical vision-language model,
080   GMAI-VL. To enhance its integration of visual and linguistic features and its instruction-following
081
      abilities, a three-stage training strategy is proposed in this paper. Specifically, we sequentially im-
      plement shallow and deep alignments in the first two stages, gradually building associations between
082
      visual (medical images) and language (medical texts) elements from basic features to high-level se-
083
      mantics. Next, we fine-tune the model with cross-modal instructions, improving its understanding of
084   visual-language interactions and instruction-following in complex tasks. With this strategy, GMAI-
085   VL shows strong performance in medical tasks like visual question answering and medical image
086   diagnosis, providing a solid foundation for advancing multimodal models in the medical field.
087
      Our contributions are as follows:
088
089
      • We propose a methodology for constructing the GMAI-VL-5.5M, a comprehensive vision-
090     language dataset with extensive coverage of medical tasks, diverse multimodal representations,
091     and high-quality image-text pairs, forming a robust foundation for model training.
092
      • With GMAI-VL-5.5M, we propose a versatile medical vision-language model, named GMAI-VL.
093
        Our proposed three-stage training strategy enhances its ability to integrate visual and language
094     features, significantly improving the abilities of instruction-following and generalization across
095     various medical tasks.
096
      • GMAI-VL outperforms previous models in multimodal question-answering tasks, including
097
        PMC-VQA and VQA-RAD, setting new benchmarks on OmniMedVQA, GMAI-MMBench, and
098     the health and medicine subset of MMMU. Specifically, GMAI-VL achieves an average score of
099     88.48% on OmniMedVQA, 62.43% on the GMAI-MMBench test set, and 51.3% on the health
100     and medicine subset of MMMU.

      3      GMAI-VL-5.5M: A C OMPREHENSIVE M ULTIMODAL DATASET
195
196
      In the context of rapid advancements in medical vision-language models (VLMs), the construction
      of high-quality datasets is essential for developing general-purpose medical VLMs. Unlike previous
197
      methods that primarily rely on published literature to build medical vision-language datasets, our ap-
198
      proach focuses on utilizing specialized medical datasets to develop a more robust and high-quality
199   dataset. We introduce the GMAI-VL-5.5M, a comprehensive medical vision-language dataset that
200   aggregates data from a wide range of sources, including both open-source and proprietary resources.
201   The dataset encompasses 13 medical imaging modalities and covers 18 medical specialties, effec-
202   tively addressing a broad spectrum of common medical imaging tasks. This dataset is designed to
203   significantly enhance the model’s capacity to understand and process complex medical information,
204   thus contributing to advancements in precision medicine and intelligent diagnostics.
205

313   4.1       A RCHITECTURE
314
315   The GMAI-VL model is a vision-language model built upon the LLaVA architecture (Liu et al.,
316   2023a; Li et al., 2024a), incorporating three key components: a large language model (LLM), a
317   vision encoder, and a projector (MLP), as illustrated in Fig. 1(b). These components are designed to
318   work together seamlessly, enabling the model to deliver exceptional performance in medical appli-
319   cations.
320   We utilize InternLM2.5-7B (Team, 2023) as our language processing module, which offers out-
321   standing reasoning capabilities. With a context window up to one million tokens, it can handle com-
322   plex medical tasks and generate coherent, accurate responses. Its support for advanced instruction-
323   following makes it particularly effective in addressing intricate medical queries, thereby enhancing
      the model’s ability to understand and respond to a wide range of instructions.


                                                                                                              6
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


324
      For vision processing, GMAI-VL employs a CLIP-based vision encoder (Radford et al., 2021),
325   which transforms visual inputs into high-dimensional feature representations. CLIP’s strong perfor-
326   mance in aligning image and text representations ensures that medical image features are accurately
327   extracted and effectively integrated with linguistic information, significantly enhancing the model’s
328   ability to handle multimodal medical data.
329
      The MLP, as a projector, serves as a bridge between the vision encoder and the LLM, optimizing
330
      high-dimensional outputs and further enhancing feature representation. The seamless integration
331   of these components enables GMAI-VL to excel in processing and understanding complex medical
332   multimodal data.

      4.2   O PTIMIZATION S TRATEGY
335
336   As illustrated in Fig. 1(c), the training process of the GMAI-VL model is divided into three stages:
337   shadow alignment, deep alignment, instruction tuning, respectively. The detailed hyper-parameter
338   settings can be found in Appendix (Table. 8). To enhance the training of GMAI-VL, we supplement
339   our GMAI-VL-5.5M dataset with additional medical datasets. This supplemented data increases
340   the diversity of training data, exposing the model to a wider range of medical scenarios and visual-
341   language patterns, enhancing its generalization to complex clinical tasks and ensuring robustness
342   in real-world applications. Fig. 4 (in Appendix) provides a complete distribution of the utilized
343   datasets during the training stage. The detailed data proportions for each training stage are detailed
344
      in Table. 7 (in Appendix).
345
346   Stage I: Shadow alignment. In the shallow alignment phase, we utilize a large-scale medical
347   image-text dataset comprising approximately 11.7 million image-text pairs, sourced from a combi-
348
      nation of publicly accessible datasets and proprietary in-house data. To achieve shallow alignment,
      we freeze both the large language model and the vision encoder, optimizing only the projector. With
349
      this optimization stage, the model establishes an initial alignment between medical images and their
350
      corresponding textual descriptions. All input images are resized to 336×336 pixels, and the training
351   objective is to minimize the cross-entropy loss of the text tokens.
352
353
      Stage II: Deep alignment. Since most vision encoders in multimodal models are pre-trained on
354   natural images, we address the domain differences between medical and natural images in the deep
355   alignment stage. In this stage, we fine-tune both the vision-language projector and the vision encoder
356   to achieve better alignment between the visual features of medical images and the feature space of
357   the language model.
358
359   Stage III: Instruction tuning. At this stage, we fine-tune our GMAI-VL model (including the
360   vision encoder, the language model, and the projector parts) by instruction tuning to enhance its
361   instruction-following and dialogue capabilities. The multimodal instruction tuning data is primarily
362   derived from the training data in previous stages, by filtering high-quality and more suitable data for
363   fine-tuning. Additionally, we incorporate medical text dialogue data to ensure the model’s versatility
364   in handling various dialogue scenarios. Thus, our instruction tuning data comprises approximately
365   10 million samples.

1043   A.3        M ODEL T RAINING S ETTINGS
1044
1045   The table. 8 presents the training settings for GMAI-VL across three stages, detailing key hyperpa-
1046
       rameters.
1047
       • Stage I (Shallow alignment). The large language model (LLM) is frozen, while the MLP is train-
1048
         able, and the vision encoder is frozen. The learning rate is set to 1e−3 with a cosine decay sched-
1049     ule, using AdamW as the optimizer. Input size is 336 × 336, and the total batch size is 32 × 8 × 2,
1050     with no dropout.
1051
1052
       • Stage II (Deep alignment). Both the LLM and MLP remain frozen, but the vision encoder is
         unfrozen and trainable. The learning rate is lowered to 1e−4 , and other settings remain consistent
1053
         with Stage 1.
1054
1055   • Stage III (Instruction tuning). None of the components are frozen, allowing the entire model to
1056     be fine-tuned. The learning rate is further reduced to 1e−5 , while other parameters, including
1057     optimizer and batch size, remain unchanged across stages.
1058
1059
       Each stage utilizes DeepSpeed for mixed-precision training (bf16) on 32 A100 (80GB) GPUs.
