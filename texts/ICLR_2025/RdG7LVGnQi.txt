009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            Large Language Models (LLMs) have advanced rapidly but face significant mem-
012            ory demands. While quantization has shown promise for LLMs, current methods
013            typically require lengthy training to alleviate the performance degradation from
014            quantization loss. However, deploying LLMs across diverse scenarios with differ-
015            ent resource constraints, e.g., servers and personal computers, requires repeated
016            training per application, which amplifies the lengthy training problem. Given
017            that, it is advantageous to train a once-for-all (OFA) supernet capable of yielding
018
               diverse optimal subnets for downstream applications through one-shot training.
               Nonetheless, the scale of current language models impedes efficiency and amplifies
019
               interference from weight sharing between subnets. We make an initial attempt
020
               to extend the once-for-all framework to large language models. Specifically, we
021            decouple shared weights to eliminate the interference and incorporate Low-Rank
022            adapters for training efficiency. Furthermore, we observe the imbalance allocation
023            of training resources from the traditional uniform sampling. A non-parametric
024            scheduler is introduced to adjust the sampling rate for each quantization configura-
025            tion, achieving a more balanced allocation among subnets with varying demands.
026            We validate the approach on LLaMA2 families and Mistral on downstream evalu-
027            ation, demonstrating high performance while significantly reducing deployment
028            time faced with multiple scenarios.

      1   I NTRODUCTION
032
033
      Large Language Models have shown surprising performance in the past years. However, they suffer
      from huge storage and computational costs; for example, inference with a LLaMA (Touvron et al.,
034
      2023) model with 70B parameters needs at least 280 GB of GPU memory. To further boost the LLMs
035
      development for fitting diverse scenarios, recent studies have adopted quantization to compress the
036   model size and reduce the computational costs.
037
038   Previous works have extensively explored Post-Training Quantization (Frantar et al., 2022; Xiao
039
      et al., 2023; Lin et al., 2023) and Quantization-Aware Training (Dettmers et al., 2024; Xu et al.,
      2023) to alleviate the memory cost of LLMs. Post-training quantization (PTQ) offers a fast model
040
      compression approach but may lead to performance degradation. In contrast, Quantization-aware
041
      training (QAT) alleviates performance losses by simulating quantization errors during training,
042   although it is significantly more time-consuming than standard fine-tuning. When deploying LLMs
043   for diverse scenarios with varying resource constraints, repeating quantization-aware training for each
044   scenario is impractical, as shown in Figure 1 (a). From the above analysis, the training major the cost
045   of deployments; hence, it would be beneficial to train a once-for-all (OFA) supernet. This supernet
046   can generate optimal subnets with diverse configurations (e.g., quantization bit-width) tailored to
047   specific applications, as shown in Figure 1 (b, c).
048   To the best of our knowledge, once-for-all quantization-aware training for LLMs has not been
049   investigated, primarily due to the large scale of current language models and the high cost of
050   traditional QAT. Previous research on once-for-all strategies primarily employs a weight-sharing
051   approach to avoid the model size explosion that would result from allocating separate weights for
052   each configuration (Wang et al., 2020; Chen et al., 2021). However, the weight-sharing combined
053   with traditional QAT presents two significant challenges: 1) various quantization configurations (e.g.,
      2, 3, 4 bit-width) share the weight but have different orders of magnitude of quantization noise,

077   Furthermore, our observations reveal that the uniform sampling strategy used by traditional OFA

081
082   Integrating these aspects, we propose the LLM-QFA (Quantization-Aware Fine-tuning one LLM
083   for All scenarios) framework that efficiently fine-tunes a once-for-all supernet to later generate
084   optimal subnets for diverse scenarios. First, we introduce interference-less fine-tuning to decouple the
085
      weights of different configurations, accompanied by Low-Rank adapters to enable efficient training.
      Specifically, we quantize the weights with different quantization configurations and freeze them,
086
      then apply Low-Rank adapters to each quantized weight for later fine-tuning. Second, we propose
087
      a resource-balanced sampling strategy, which utilizes a non-parametric scheduler that dynamically
088   adjusts the sampling strategy across training steps.
089
090   To evaluate our proposed framework, we conduct experiments on LLaMA2 models and validate the
091
      performance on the MMLU and Common Sense QA benchmarks. The results show that our proposed
      framework can yield diverse optimal quantized models for various scenarios. It is worth noting that
092
      our framework can be easily scaled up to even larger models since the training time per step is the
093
      same with previous LoRA-tuning (Xu et al., 2023). We summarize our contributions as follows:
094
095              • We first introduce the once-for-all training paradigm for large language models (LLMs),
096                which helps to reduce the training cost for deploying LLMs across diverse scenarios.
097
                 • we decouple weights of configurations to mitigate interference issues and incorporate Low-
098                Rank adapters to enhance the training efficiency.
099
                 • To address the imbalance training caused by the uniform sampling strategy, we propose a
100
                   resource-balanced sampling strategy that focuses on providing fair sampled opportunity
101
                   across subnets with various resource demands.

104   2    R ELATED W ORK
105
106   LLM Quantization. Quantization is a compression technique that reduces the bit-width of weights
107   and/or activations to save memory and accelerate inference. The quantization of LLM can be
      categorized into two main lines. The first one is post-training quantization (PTQ) (Frantar et al.,


                                                                     2
108
      2022; Xiao et al., 2023; Lin et al., 2023; Kim et al., 2023), which focuses on reducing the memory
109
      footprint without retraining. Although lots of designs are designed to mitigate the degradation of
110   performance, e.g., handling outliers in parameters (Kim et al., 2023; Li et al., 2023a) and dynamic
111   quantization (Xiao et al., 2023; Lin et al., 2023), PTQ still have to drop the ultra-low bit-width (e.g.,
112   2 bit and 3 bit) to guarantee the performance. Hence, the second line, Quantization-Aware Training
113   (QAT) can help alleviate the performance drop. The first QAT method applied on LLM (Liu et al.,
114   2023) inherits the idea of traditional QAT, which is computationally expensive in the fine-tuning
115   stage. To reduce the training cost, (Dettmers et al., 2024; Xu et al., 2023; Guo et al., 2023; Li et al.,
116   2023b) utilizing LoRA-tuning on quantized weight and gain a decent performance. Specifically, (Xu
117   et al., 2023) adds constraints on LoRA to maintain the quantization property after merging between
118
      LoRA weight and quantization weight, which firstly brings LoRA-tuning to actual quantization-aware
      training. Though Lora-tuning can save memory footprint and training costs, when faced with diverse
119
      development scenarios with different resource constraints, LoRA-tuning still falls into the pitfall of
120
      repeated training.
121
122   Once for All training. Once-for-all training (OFA) methods (Wang et al., 2020; Chen et al., 2021; Yu
123   et al., 2020; Tang et al., 2023; 2022) aim to train a one-shot supernet that can serve diverse scenarios
124
      with different resource constraints and save expensive retraining per scenario. On non-LLMs, the
      success of one-shot training comes from the weight-sharing scheme between different configurations
125
      (Chen et al., 2021; Yu et al., 2020), while weight-sharing also brings interference between different
126
      bit-widths for quantization-aware training (Tang et al., 2024; 2023). Moreover, traditional OFA with
127   weight sharing necessitates fine-tuning entire parameters, which is impracticable for LLMs due to
128   their extensive size.

253   3.3   S EARCH O PTIMIZED S UBNET
254
      We decouple the fine-tuning process and the searching process. No extra retraining cost is needed
255
      when finding the optimal subnet under the given resource constraint. The searching process starts
256
      with random searching, where a few subnets are sampled. Then, correlation analysis between the
257   subnets’ performance on the validation set and the quantization bit-width of each layer is conducted.
258   Learning from the correlation, the sensitivity of each layer to quantization bit-width can be obtained,
259   and the search space can be further narrowed down. Finally, we further sample subnets from the
260   narrowed search space, and the final optimal subnet is selected based on the performance of the
261   validation set.

      4.3   A BLATION S TUDY
418
419   Ablation on Interference-Less Fine-tuning. To assess the effectiveness of decoupling shared
420   weights, we introduce a variant called shared-LoRA, wherein different quantization settings share
421   the same Low-Rank adapter. Figure 7 reports that shared-LoRA underperforms the original version
422   across all resource demands, validating the interference problem in one-shot LLM training.
423
424   Ablation on Resource-Balance Sampling. Similarly, we implement a uniform sampling version of
425   our method. Figure 7 also shows a consistently under-performing uniform sampling strategy; even the
426   resource-concentrated area (3 bit) falls short in the comparison. This has motivated the development
427   of a resource-balanced sampling strategy for training, which is designed to counteract the challenges
428   of under-fitting and over-fitting encountered in one-shot training.

474   5   C ONCLUSION
475
476   This work introduces the LLM-QFA framework, a once-for-all Quantization-Aware training approach
477   to reduce the training cost of deploying large language models (LLMs) across diverse scenarios. By
478   decoupling the weights of different configurations and incorporating Low-Rank adapters, we enhance
479
      training efficiency and mitigate interference issues. A resource-balanced sampling strategy ensures
      fair training across subnets with various resource demands. Our experiments on LLaMA2 models
480
      show that LLM-QFA deliver optimal quantized models, demonstrating its effectiveness in reducing
481
      computational and storage costs while maintaining performance. Our framework can be easily scaled
482   up to even larger models since the training time per step is the same as with previous LoRA tuning.
