               In the fields of computational mathematics and artificial intelligence, the need for precise
               data modeling is crucial, especially for predictive machine learning tasks. This paper ex-
012
               plores further XNet, a novel algorithm that employs the complex-valued Cauchy integral
013
               formula, offering a superior network architecture that surpasses traditional Multi-Layer
014            Perceptrons (MLPs) and Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks (KANs). XNet significant im-
015            proves speed and accuracy across various tasks in both low and high-dimensional spaces,
016            redefining the scope of data-driven model development and providing substantial im-
017            provements over established time series models like LSTMs.

021   1   I NTRODUCTION
022
023   We initially proposed a novel method for constructing real networks from the complex domain using the
024   Cauchy integral formula in Li et al. (2024); Zhang et al. (2024), utilizing Cauchy kernels as basis functions.
025   This work comprehensively compares these networks with KANs, which use B-spline as basis functions in
026   Liu et al. (2024), and MLPs to highlight our significant improvements.
027   Multi-layer perceptrons (MLPs) (Haykin (1994); Cybenko (1989); Hornik et al. (1989)), recognized as
028   fundamental building blocks in deep learning, have their limitations despite their wide use, particularly in
029   its accuracy, and large number of parameters needed in structures such as in transformers (Vaswani et al.
030   (2017)), and lack interpretability without post-analysis tools (Cunningham et al. (2023)). The Kolmogorov-
031   Arnold Networks (KANs) were introduced as a potential alternative, drawing on the Kolmogorov-Arnold
032   representation theorem (Kolmogorov (1956); Braun & Griebel (2009)), and demonstrate their efficiency
033
      and accuracy in computational tasks, especially in solving PDEs and function approximation (Sprecher
      & Draghici (2002); Köppen (2002); Lin & Unbehauen (1993); Lai & Shen (2021); Leni et al. (2013);
034
      Fakhoury et al. (2022)).
035
036   In the swiftly advancing domain of deep learning, the continuous search for novel neural network designs
037   that deliver superior accuracy and efficiency is pivotal. While traditional activation functions such as the
038   Rectified Linear Unit (ReLU) (Nair & Hinton (2010)) have been widely adopted due to their straightfor-
039
      wardness and efficacy in diverse applications, their shortcomings become evident as the complexity of
      challenges escalates. This is particularly true in areas that demand meticulous data fitting and the solutions
040
      of intricate partial differential equations (PDEs). These limitations have paved the way for architectures
041
      that merge neural network techniques with PDEs, significantly enhancing function approximation capabil-
042   ities in high-dimensional settings (Sirignano & Spiliopoulos (2018); Raissi et al. (2019); Jin et al. (2021);
043   Wu et al. (2024); Zhao et al. (2023)).
044
045
      Time series forecasting is critical in various sectors including finance, healthcare, and environmental sci-
      ence. While LSTM models are well-regarded for their ability to capture temporal dependencies (Yu et al.
046
      (2019); Zhao et al. (2017)), KAN models have also shown promise in managing time series predictions
047
      (Hochreiter & Schmidhuber (1997); Staudemeyer & Morris (2019); Xu et al. (2024)). Our study com-
048   pares these models, providing insights into their applications and theoretical foundations. We also examine
049   the performance of transformers and our novel XNet model in time series forecasting in the appendix,
050   highlighting their capabilities in managing sequential data (Vaswani et al. (2017); Wen et al. (2023)).
051
      Inspired by the mathematical precision of the Cauchy integral theorem, Li et al. (2024) introduced the
052
      XNet architecture, a novel neural network model that incorporates a uniquely designed Cauchy activation
053
      function. This function is mathematically expressed as:
054
055                                                    λ1 ∗ x      λ2
056
                                            ϕa (x) =           + 2       ,
                                                      x 2 + d2   x + d2
057   where λ1 , λ2 , and d are parameters optimized during training. This design is not only a theoretical ad-
058   vancement but also empirical advantageous, offering a promising alternative to traditional models for many
059   applications. By integrating Cauchy activation functions, XNet demonstrates superior performance in func-
060   tion approximation tasks and in solving low-dimensional PDEs compared to its contemporaries, namely
061   Multilayer Perceptrons (MLPs) and Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks (KANs). This paper will systematically
062   compare these architectures, highlighting XNet’s advantages in terms of accuracy, convergence speed, and
063
      computational demands.
064   Furthermore, empirical evaluations reveal that the Cauchy activation function possesses a localized re-
065   sponse with decay at both ends, significantly benefiting the approximation of localized data segments. This
066   capability allows XNet to fine-tune responses to specific data characteristics, a critical advantage over the
067   globally responding functions like ReLU.
068   The implications of this research are significant. It has been demonstrated that the XNet can serve as
069   an effective foundation for general AI applications, our findings in this paper indicate that it can even
070   outperform meticulously designed networks tailored for specific purposes.

      Principal Contributions
072
073   Our study elucidates several critical advancements in the domain of neural network architectures and their
074   applications:
075
        (i) Enhanced Function Approximation Capabilities: We conduct a comparative analysis between XNet
076
            and KAN within the context of function approximation, demonstratting the superior performance of
077
            XNet, particularly in handling the Heaviside step function and complex high-dimensional scenarios.
078         Detailed examinations are presented in Sections 3.1 through 3.3, showcasing empirical validations
079         that underscore XNet’s robust adaptability across varying dimensions.
080
       (ii) Superiority in Physics-Informed Neural Networks: Utilizing the Poisson equation as a benchmark, we
081
            demonstrate XNet’s enhanced efficacy within the Physics-Informed Neural Network (PINN) frame-
082         work. Our results indicate that XNet significantly outstrips the performance metrics of both Multi-
083         Layer Perceptron (MLP) and KAN, as detailed in Section 3.5. This investigation not only highlights
084         XNet’s prowess but also sets a new benchmark for subsequent applications in the field.
085
      (iii) Innovation in Time Series Forecasting–By innovatively substituting the conventional feedforward
086         neural network (FNN) with XNet in the LSTM architecture, we introduce the XLSTM model. In
087         a series of time series forecasting experiments, XLSTM consistently surpasses traditional LSTM
088         models in accuracy and reliability, establishing a new frontier in predictive analytics.

409
