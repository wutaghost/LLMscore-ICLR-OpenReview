009                                                                                                                      A BSTRACT
010
011                         Transformers can capture long-range dependencies using self-attention, allowing
012                         tokens to attend to all others directly. However, stacking multiple attention layers
013                         leads to attention concentration. One natural way to address this issue is to use
014                         cross-layer attention, allowing information from earlier layers to be directly ac-
015                         cessible to later layers. However, this approach is computationally expensive. To
016                         address this problem, we propose Transformer with residual value (ResFormer)
017                         which approximates cross-layer attention through adding a residual connection
018                         from the values of the the first layer to all subsequent layers. Based on this
019
                            method, one variant is the Transformer with single layer value (SVFormer), where
                            all layers share the same value embedding from first layer, reducing the KV cache
020
                            by nearly 50%. Comprehensive empirical evidence demonstrates that ResFormer
021
                            mitigates attention concentration problem in deeper layers and enhances represen-
022                         tation across most layers, outperforming the vanilla Transformer, DenseFormer,
023                         and NeuTRENO in training error as well as downstream tasks. SVFormer trains
024                         significantly faster than the vanilla Transformer and performs better than other
025                         methods like GQA and CLA, with performance influenced by sequence length
026                         and cumulative learning rate.

051   and width of a Transformer model within a fixed parameter budget is particularly difficult. While
052   research indicates that deeper models generalize more compositionally than shallower ones (Petty
053   et al., 2024), the training and deployment of deep models remain problematic. Although Transform-
      ers use residual connections (He et al., 2016) to address the vanishing gradient issue, training very

070
071   Existing solutions to alleviate the over-smoothing problem in Transformer include adding extra reg-
072   ularizers (Nguyen et al., 2023; Shi et al., 2022) and optimizing the information flow within the model
073   (Pagliardini et al., 2024). During the era of convolutional neural network architectures, Stochastic
074   Depth (Huang et al., 2016) reduces the likelihood of over-smoothing by randomly dropping layers
075
      during training and DenseNet (Huang et al., 2017) mitigates the impact of over-smoothing by allow-
      ing each layer to directly access the hidden states of all preceding layers. Recently, DenseFormer
076
      (Pagliardini et al., 2024) adopts the idea of DenseNet when training Transformer. Additionally,
077
      NeuTRENO (Nguyen et al., 2023) alleviates over-smoothing through incorporating the difference
078   between the value vectors of the first layer and the current layer to the attention output.
079
080   In this paper, we address the problem of multi-layer attention from another perspective. We in-
081
      troduce the phenomenon of attention concentration, which describes how a model’s attention in-
      creasingly focuses on fewer tokens. We quantify the degree of attention concentration using the
082
      entropy of the distribution of token importance, where lower entropy indicates a more pronounced
083
      concentration. Unlike over-smoothing, which is inherent to model architecture, attention concen-
084   tration emerges during training. Fig. 1 (Left) shows that randomly initialized models exhibit over-
085   smoothing but not attention concentration. Trained ViT models often focus on low-informative
086   background areas (Darcet et al., 2024), while language models concentrate on low-semantic tokens
087   (Sun et al., 2024), particularly the start token (attention sink (Xiao et al., 2024)). While previous
088   studies analyzed single-layer attention patterns, our research reveals a “concentration - dispersion -
089   concentration” pattern in deep models, as shown in Fig. 1 (Middle), suggesting potential loss of in-
090   formation during concentrated phases. The analysis of over-smoothing is available in Appendix A.1.
091   Mitigating attention concentration can lead to more interpretable attention maps and potentially im-
092   prove downstream task performance (Darcet et al., 2024). This phenomenon typically emerges after
093   the second or third network layer and is associated with value-state drains (decreased magnitude of
094   value states) (Guo et al., 2024b), and hidden-state peaks (increased magnitude of hidden states) (Sun
095   et al., 2024). Guo et al. (2024a) shows a mutual reinforcement mechanism exists between value-state
096   drains and attention concentration. Recent studies have linked this to implicit biases during pretrain-
097   ing, with most existing solutions focusing on the use of additional tokens (registers) (Darcet et al.,
098
      2024) or additional keys and values (explicit attention bias) (Sun et al., 2024) to redirect this.
099   Given that the first layer always shows no attention concentration, an effective method is to use cross-
100   layer attention on information from this layer. However, due to computational costs, we propose
101   ResFormer as an efficient alternative. ResFormer applies a residual connection between the value
102   vectors of the current layer and the first layer before the attention operation. Unlike cross-layer
103   attention, ResFormer indirectly mitigates attention concentration. It leverages the absence of value-
104
      state drains in the first layer by introducing a value residual connection. This alleviates value-state
      drains in deeper layers, thereby disrupting the mutual reinforcement between attention concentration
105
      and value-state drains, as shown in Fig. 1 (Right) and Fig. 2.
106

      ment (Xiao et al., 2024). Existing KV -efficient methods often process keys and values simultane-

      2.1     S HORTCUT C ONNECTIONS F OR B ETTER I NFORMATION F LOW
147
148
      Deep learning models often consist of multiple layers, posing a challenge to minimize information
149
      loss during transmission. ResNet (He et al., 2016) mitigates the vanishing gradient problem with
150   identity connections. Stochastic Depth (Huang et al., 2016) enhances training by randomly dropping
151   layers. DenseNet (Huang et al., 2017) allows subsequent layers to directly access the hidden states
152   of all preceding layers. These two methods further enhance the information flow after ResNet.
153
      Related research indicates that for advanced Transformer architectures, although increasing depth
154
      continues to yield performance improvements in language modeling tasks, the gains become less
155
      significant with further increases (Petty et al., 2024). Furthermore, Zhou et al. (2021) illustrates
156   that a 32-layer ViT underperforms a 24-layer ViT. Depth-Wise Attention (ElNokrashy et al., 2024)
157   allows each query to access the key and value at the same position from previous layers through an
158   attention-like mechanism before the output layer. DenseFormer (Pagliardini et al., 2024) integrates
159   weighted fusion of outputs from all preceding layers after each layer. To explore why increasing
160   depth in Transformers does not yield expected gains, Wang et al. (2022) finds that self-attention
161   acts as a low-pass filter, smoothing token representations in ViTs. Additionally, Shi et al. (2022) in-
      vestigates over-smoothing from a graph perspective in BERT-based language modeling tasks. Neu-


                                                            3

162
      TRENO (Nguyen et al., 2023) adds the difference between the value vectors of the first and current
163   layers to each layer’s attention output and significantly alleviates the over-smoothing problem.
164
165   In contrast to these methods, ResFormer accesses and integrates information from previous layers
166
      prior to the attention operation, as illustrated in Fig. 3. Moreover, it does not require the selection or
      tuning of additional hyperparameters.

      2.2   KV CACHE COMPRESSING
170   The KV cache is a key factor limiting the efficiency of long-text model inference. Research in this
171   area can be broadly classified into Transformer-based methods, which target redundant information
172   in Transformer models, and non-Transformer methods, which mainly addresses the quadratic time
173   complexity of attention with respect to sequence length.
174
      For non-Transformer methods, Mamba (Gu & Dao, 2023) and RWKV (Peng et al., 2023) are two
175
      popular works. They replace the original softmax-based attention with SSM (Gu et al., 2021) and
176   AFT (Zhai et al., 2021) mechanisms, respectively. Besides, several approaches have been proposed
177   to enhance models’ ability to process long texts while reducing the reliance on KV cache. Dai et al.
178   (2019) advocates segmenting long texts into smaller parts for attention computation. Furthermore,
179   Munkhdalai et al. (2024) uses a fixed-size memory matrix for storing and retrieving past information.
180
      Transformer-based methods can be categorized into three main groups. The first group consists of
181
      post-training methods like SnapKV (Li et al., 2024) and ThinK (Xu et al., 2024), which compress
182   KV cache during inference based on attention matrices at token or hidden dimension levels. The
183   second group focuses on quantization and adopts low-precision KV cache quantization rather than
184   completely eliminating them (Hooper et al., 2024). The third group aims to maximize the efficiency
185   of attention-based models via parameter or activation value sharing. The most representative works
186   include Multi-Query Attention (Shazeer, 2019) and Grouped-Query Attention (Ainslie et al., 2023)
187   which suggest to share key and value across a group of queries. MLKV (Zuhri et al., 2024) further
188   suggest to share keys and values for queries across layers and MLA (Liu et al., 2024) introduces
189   low-rank projection when processing keys and values. Besides, CLA (Brandon et al., 2024) and
190
      LISA (Mu et al., 2024) respectively point out that we can reuse keys, values, or the attention matrix
      across layers to reduce redundancy between layers. While these methods typically process both key
191
      and value simultaneously, SVFormer is the first approach to decouple value from query and key
192
      during attention computation. Moreover, it is compatible with other methods like GQA.

391      4.3                          A BLATION STUDY OF RESIDUAL CONNECTION
392
393     In Eqn. 4, we employ residual connections for the values. We compare this approach with models
394     that add residual connections to queries or keys. The results, shown in Fig. 9, indicate that only
395     residual connections for values yield positive effects. One possible explanation is that attention
396
        mechanisms are sensitive to perturbations, and modifying queries or keys significantly impacts it.
397     Moreover, we compare with the models based on Eqn. 2 and Eqn. 6. The results in Fig. 10 align
398     with Fig. 4, showing that identity mapping causes significant perturbations, leading to poor perfor-
399     mance. Interestingly, ResFormer achieves an even lower final loss than ResFormer. It suggests that
400     ResFormer’s impact on the attention optimization is better by mitigating value-state drains.
401     When determining the mapping method and target value, it is crucial to consider which historical
402     layers’ values should be included in the residual connection. Fig. 11 shows that each Transformer
403     layer should add a shortcut to the first layer’s value rather than to the nearest preceding layer or all
404     previous layers, highlighting the first-layer value’s critical importance. A potential explanation is
405     that incorporating values from other layers may dilute the impact of the first-layer value.

526   5                               C ONCLUSION
527
528   In this paper, we propose the concept of attention concentration, a problem that arises from stack-
529   ing multiple attention layers. From the perspective of cross-layer attention, we derive ResFormer,
530   which adds a residual connection between the value vectors of the current layer and those of the
531   first layer before the attention operation to alleviate attention concentration. Additionally, we intro-
532   duce SVFormer, based on ResFormer, which reduces the KV cache by nearly half. We conducted
533   comprehensive experiments on the language modeling task to validate the advantages of these two
534
      Transformer variants in different scenarios.
