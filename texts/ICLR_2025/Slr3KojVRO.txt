010
011
               Point cloud anomaly detection, particularly under the anomaly-free setting, poses
012
               a significant challenge as it requires the precise capture of 3D normal data fea-
013
               tures to accurately identify deviations indicative of anomalies. Current efforts
014            focus on devising reconstruction tasks, such as acquiring normal data representa-
015            tions by restoring normal samples from altered, pseudo-anomalous counterparts.
016            Nonetheless, such methods tend to dilute the model’s focus, as they require at-
017            tention to both normal and pseudo-anomalous data points, thereby hampering the
018            efficacy of the learning process. Moreover, the inherently disordered and sparse
019            nature of 3D point cloud data significantly complicates the task. In response to
020            those predicaments, we introduce an innovative approach that involves learning
021            point offsets for the first time, with a concentrated emphasis on more informa-
022
               tive pseudo-abnormal points, thus fostering more effective distillation of normal
               data representations. We have crafted an augmentation technique that is steered
023
               by normal vectors, facilitating the creation of credible pseudo anomalies that en-
024
               hance the efficiency of the training process. Our comprehensive experimental
025            evaluation on the Anomaly-ShapeNet and Real3D-AD datasets evidences that our
026            proposed method outperforms existing state-of-the-art approaches, achieving an
027            average enhancement of 9.0% and 1.4% in the AUC-ROC detection metric across
028            these datasets, respectively.

032   1   I NTRODUCTION
033
034   Point cloud anomaly detection aims to identify defective samples and locate abnormal regions that
035   deviate from expected data patterns (Roth et al., 2022; Zhou et al., 2024). Owing to the high cost
036   of collecting and labeling anomaly samples, this task is usually implemented in an anomaly-free
037   setting, i.e., only normal samples are available during training. The critical challenge within this
038   framework is to effectively capture the distinctive features that are characteristic of 3D normal data,
039
      enabling the system to recognize and classify instances that deviate from these normal patterns
      as anomalies. Nonetheless, the inherently disordered and sparse nature of 3D point cloud data
040
      significantly complicates the process of acquiring such discriminative knowledge.
041
042   As one reasonable way to tackle this task, anomaly detection in 3D point clouds often involves de-
043   signing reconstruction tasks to capture normal representations,as illustrated in Fig. 1(a). Anomalies
044   are detected by comparing inputs to their reconstruction outputs. For instance, IMRNet (Li et al.,
045
      2024) randomly masks training normal samples and trains a reconstruction task to restore complete
      point clouds. However, this approach may fail to detect anomalies in unmasked regions. To ad-
046
      dress this limitation, R3D-AD (Zhou et al., 2024) proposes reconstructing normal samples from
047
      their pseudo-abnormal variants. A test sample with high differences between its input and output
048   is considered an anomaly. Despite its efficacy, reconstructing each point’s coordinates in 3D space
049   causes the model to assign equal loss weight to both normal and pseudo-abnormal points, which
050   may hinder learning normal representations. Empirical evidence in Fig. 1(c) shows that the per-
051   formance degrades as the normal point loss weight increases from 0.1 to 1.0 (the loss weight of
052   pseudo-abnormal points is fixed at 1.0). Extraction of normal patterns relies on learning to restore
053   normal regions from pseudo-abnormal ones, but equal loss weights impair the network to focus on
      this process, thus limiting the detection performance.

      In this paper, we propose to predict point offsets for pseudo anomalies (as illustrated in Fig. 1(b))
      to allow the model to concentrate on pseudo-abnormal regions, ensuring the effective distillation
081
      of normal representations. Specifically, point offsets are essentially vectors characterized by two at-
082
      tributes: magnitude and direction. The offsets of abnormal points in pseudo anomalies are defined by
083   these attributes, representing their displacement distance and direction relative to their correspond-
084   ing points in original normal ones. In contrast, the offsets of normal points in pseudo anomalies
085   can be predominantly governed by their displacement distance, as they remain unchanged relative to
086   their corresponding points in original normal ones, making the direction less relevant and the magni-
087   tude zero. Therefore, learning the task of point offset prediction allows the model to estimate normal
088   points’ offset magnitude only, while requiring it to predict both offset magnitude and direction for
089   pseudo-abnormal points. This is significantly different from the current mainstream reconstruction-
090   based methods that need to precisely restore the coordinates of each point, thus leading the model to
091
      concentrate unnecessarily on both normal and pseudo-abnormal points simultaneously. Empirical
      evidence is presented in Fig. 1(d). In the right part, losses converge faster on normal points than
092
      on pseudo-abnormal points, enabling the model to focus on pseudo-abnormal points in late training.
093
      However, the losses of normal points follow almost the same trend as those of pseudo-abnormal
094   points in the reconstruction-based method, i.e., the model equally concentrates on both two kinds
095   of points. Additionally, the predicted offsets of test samples can directly assess their abnormality
096   levels during inference, while reconstruction-based methods need to design handcrafted metrics to
097   produce anomaly scores.
098
      Drawing inspiration from the aforementioned observation, we propose a novel framework named
099
      PO3AD, which efficiently predicts point offsets and adequately captures normal representations.
100   For practical implementation, in order to enable the model to learn the knowledge of predicting
101   offsets, we further propose an anomaly simulation method named Norm-AS, which is guided by
102   normal vectors 1 . Norm-AS is performed by moving points of a random region in normal data along
103   or against the normal vectors to produce pseudo anomalies. In contrast, the previous augmentation
104   method (Zhou et al., 2024) ignores point movement direction, resulting in points potentially moving
105   in any direction in 3D space. This may cause pseudo-abnormal regions to overlap with normal

      Our contributions can be summarized as follows:
115
116          • We propose a novel paradigm named PO3AD to predict point offsets, allowing the model
117            to concentrate on pseudo-abnormal regions and ensuring the effective learning of normal
118            representations for 3D point cloud anomaly detection.
119          • We design a point cloud pseudo anomaly generation method guided by normal vectors,
120            termed Norm-AS, creating credible pseudo anomalies from normal samples for improving
121            training efficiency.
122          • Extensive experiments conducted on two benchmark point cloud anomaly detection
123            datasets demonstrate the superiority of our method to state-of-the-art methods, with an
124            average improvement of 9.0% and 1.4% detection AUC-ROC on Anomaly-ShapeNet and
125            Real3D-AD, respectively.

129   2D anomaly detection. Anomaly detection methods on 2D image data under anomaly-free scenar-
130   ios have been widely studied in recent years. To address the issue that anomalies are unavailable
131   during training, a straightforward approach involves generating pseudo anomalies (Hu et al., 2024;
132   Zavrtanik et al., 2021a; Li et al., 2021; Schlüter et al., 2022; Liu et al., 2023b; Zhang et al., 2024),
133   allowing models to learn discriminative knowledge for identifying anomalies. An alternative way
134   to tackle this task relies on constructing a memory bank storing normal features produced by pre-
135   trained encoders (Bae et al., 2023; Kim et al., 2023; Roth et al., 2022; Xie et al., 2023). Such
136
      methods detect anomalies by contrasting features of test data with those of normal training samples.
      Flow-based methods (Rudolph et al., 2021; Gudovskiy et al., 2022) leverage normalizing flows for
137
      estimation of the feature distribution to detect anomalies. Reconstruction-based methods (Huang
138
      et al., 2022; Pirnay & Chai, 2022; Yan et al., 2021; Zavrtanik et al., 2021b) designs reconstruc-
139   tion tasks to capture normal representations; anomalies are detected by comparing inputs to their
140   reconstruction results. In this paper, we focus on 3D point cloud anomaly detection. This task is
141   particularly challenging due to the disordered and sparse characteristics of point cloud data.
142
      3D anomaly detection. Although significant progress has been made in 2D anomaly detection, re-
143
      search into anomaly detection for 3D data is still relatively limited. Due to the absence of point cloud
144
      anomaly detection datasets, early studies are conducted on RGB-D datasets, such as the MVTec AD-
145   3D dataset (Bergmann et al., 2022). AST (Rudolph et al., 2023) enhances the detection capability
146   by leveraging depth information to suppress background. 3D-ST (Bergmann & Sattlegger, 2023)
147   proposes a teacher-student framework to capture representations of normal samples during training,
148   and anomalies are detected by assessing regression errors between teacher and student networks.
149   BTF (Horwitz & Hoshen, 2023) proposes to utilize handcrafted 3D descriptors combined with K-
150   Nearest Neighbors (KNN) to tackle the task of 3D anomaly detection. M3DM (Wang et al., 2023)
151   designs a multimodal hybrid fusion paradigm that merges point and image features to strengthen
152   the detection performance. CPMF (Cao et al., 2024) fuses 2D and 3D features by projecting point
153
      cloud data into multi-view images to construct a memory bank. With the proposal of two point cloud
      anomaly detection datasets: Real3D-AD (Liu et al., 2023a) and Anomaly-ShapeNet (Li et al., 2024),
154
      recent efforts focus on anomaly detection for point cloud data. Reg3D-AD combines the classical
155
      2D method PatchCore (Roth et al., 2022) with RANSAC algorithm (Bolles & Fischler, 1981) to de-
156   velop a memory bank-based framework for point cloud anomaly detection. Group3AD (Zhu et al.,
157   2024) groups points into multiple clusters and designs a group-level contrastive loss to capture inter-
158   cluster dispersion and intracluster compactness features, which are subsequently stored in a memory
159   bank. Although memory bank-based methods have shown effectiveness, they suffer the prohibitive
160   computational and storage. IMRNet (Li et al., 2024) adopts the idea of 2D reconstruction-based
161   methods, randomly masking training point clouds and restoring them by training a PointMAE (Pang
      et al., 2022). While R3D-AD (Zhou et al., 2024) creates pseudo anomalies from normal samples

202   3.1     O FFSET P REDICTION L EARNING
203
204   To capture normal representation for anomaly detection, we propose to predict point offsets. Practi-
205   cally, we construct an offset prediction network and leverage an offset loss to supervise the network
206   in learning the knowledge of estimating points offsets.
207
208   3.1.1    O FFSET PREDICTION NETWORK
209
      Our network is composed of two modules: a backbone and an offset predictor. Inspired by exem-
210   plary pioneering work (Hu et al., 2021; Zhao et al., 2023; Schult et al., 2023; Delitzas et al., 2024)
211   in 3D domain, we adopt MinkUNet (Choy et al., 2019b;a) as the backbone for our method. Specifi-
212   cally, MinkUNet is a voxel-based sparse convolutional network (Graham, 2015; Gwak et al., 2020)
213   that effectively captures detailed local features from point clouds. This allows the extraction of fine-
214   grained pseudo-abnormal features during training, thus facilitating normal representation learning.
215   Given one point cloud sample P ∈ RN ×3 , it is voxelized into V ∈ RNV ×3 , where NV stands for
      the number of voxels. It is noted that NV ≤ N and NV are inversely correlated with the voxel size.

      The MinkUNet fU maps V to latent voxelized features GV ∈ RNV ×C = fU (V ), where C denotes
217   the dimension of each voxel’s feature. Then, the voxel-to-point index is leveraged to transform GV
218   to latent point features GP ∈ RN ×C , which are utilized to predict point-wise offsets. Our offset
219   predictor fO is constructed using a Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP), which takes GP as input to es-
220   timate the offset of each point Opre ∈ RN ×3 = fO (GP ). The offset of each point is composed of
221   three coordinate (xyz) offsets. Each element in Opre refers to the offset of a point along a particular
222   coordinate.

      4.1   E XPERIMENTAL S ETTINGS
300
301   Datasets. Our evaluation encompasses two 3D point cloud anomaly detection datasets: Anomaly-
302   ShapeNet (Li et al., 2024) and Real3D-AD (Liu et al., 2023a). Anomaly-ShapeNet is a synthesis
303   dataset based on ShapeNet (Chang et al., 2015) dataset. It consists of 1,600 samples belonging to
304   40 categories. The training set of each category contains 4 normal samples, and the test set includes
305   both normal and abnormal samples. Real3D-AD is a high-resolution point cloud dataset based
306   on real objects of 12 categories. Each category contains 4 training normal samples and 100 test
307
      instances. There is a large difference between training and test samples in the Real3D-AD dataset
      where training samples undergo 360◦ scan, while test samples are scanned on only one side.
308
309   Evaluation metrics. Experiments are conducted by following previous work (Liu et al., 2023a; Li
310   et al., 2024). Area Under the Receiver-Operating-Characteristic Curve (AUC-ROC) is utilized as our
311   evaluation criterion. It can objectively evaluate detection (object-level) and localization (point-level)
312   performance without making any assumption on the decision threshold.
313
314   4.2   I MPLEMENTATION D ETAILS
315
      The MinkUNet34C (Choy et al., 2019b;a) serves as our backbone for feature extraction. A three-
316
      layer MLP with PReLU activation function forms the offset predictor. We set the dimension of latent
317
      features C to 32, and the voxel size to 0.03. Our network is trained for 1,000 epochs with a batch
318   size of 32 (the training set is replicated 100 times to obtain 400 samples). The model parameters
319   are optimized by Adam with an initial learning rate of 0.001, which decays with the cosine anneal
320   schedule (Loshchilov & Hutter, 2017). Our method does not involve point cloud downsampling.
321   Training samples are applied with random rotation before normalization. All input point clouds
322   are normalized by aligning their center of gravity with the origin of coordinates and scaling their
323   dimensions to range from -1 to 1. We set the patch number J to 64 for our Norm-AS, which is
      performed after normalization. The normal vectors are obtained from official OBJ files of datasets.

      We select fifteen categories ending in 0 of the Anomaly-ShapeNet dataset to conduct the ablation

458
459   Normal representation learning heavily relies on Ldir : We design “Variant 1”, where the model
460   is supervised solely by Ldist . The absence of Ldir causes the network to struggle with precisely
461   estimating the offset direction of pseudo-abnormal points. According to the experimental results,
462
      the performance of “Variant” is much lower than that of our method, validating the significance of
      Ldir for capturing effective normal representations.
463
464   Ldist is essential for capturing effective normal representations: “Variant 2” learns a single
465   objective of predicting point offset direction. Evidently, it is significantly inferior to our method.
466   Without Ldist , the model fails to learn offset distance for both normal and pseudo-abnormal points.
467   Additionally, it completely disregards normal points as Ldir is not applicable for them. Therefore,
468
      Ldist is indispensable in our offset prediction-based framework.
469   Generating pseudo anomalies guided by normal vectors helps the normal representation learn-
470   ing: A substantial performance drop is observed in “Variant 3”, since moving points in random
471   directions may produce unsuitable pseudo anomalies that confuse the model, resulting in less effi-
472   cient learning. This indicates that the proposed Norm-AS is crucial for facilitating the extraction of
473   normal representations. Besides, the detection performance of “Variant 3” further demonstrates the
474
      superiority of our offset prediction framework compared to reconstruction-based R3D-AD (77.2%).

476   4.6        A NALYSIS ON PATCH N UMBER
477
      Fig. 4 reports the object-level and point-level AUC-ROC results vs. different patch numbers, which
478
      are average on fifteen categories ending in 0 of the Anomaly-ShapeNet dataset. The size of pseudo-
479
      abnormal regions is inversely correlated with the patch number J. An appropriate size is crucial for
480   learning normal representations. Difficulty in predicting point offset for a region that is too large
481   may hinder the model’s convergence. Conversely, learning point offsets for a region that is too small
482   may prevent the model from capturing sufficient normal representations. However, despite these
483   effects, our method is generally less sensitive to the size of pseudo-abnormal regions. According
484   to the presented results, the detection and localization performance reach their best when the patch
485   numbers are 32 and 64, respectively. We set the patch number to 64 in our implementation to achieve
      the best detection performance, at the cost of a slight sacrifice in localization performance.

528   5     C ONCLUSION
529
530   In this paper, we design a novel framework PO3AD based on point offset prediction to capture
531   effective normal representations for 3D point cloud anomaly detection. Moreover, we propose an
532   anomaly simulation method named Norm-AS guided by normal vectors, creating credible pseudo
533   anomalies from normal samples to facilitate the distillation of normal representations. Extensive ex-
534   periments conducted on the Anomaly-ShapeNet and Real3D-AD datasets evidence that our method
535   outperforms the existing best methods.
536   Limitations and future work. It is imperative to note that our current design is still under the
537   one-model-per-category learning paradigm, i.e., each category needs a specifically trained detection
538   model, leading to prohibitive computational and storage. In future work, we intend to investigate
539   the inter-category common patterns to explore a one-model-all-category learning paradigm for point
      cloud anomaly detection.
