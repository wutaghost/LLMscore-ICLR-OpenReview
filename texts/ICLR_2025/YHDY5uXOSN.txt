010
011
               Neural channel decoder, as a data-driven channel decoding strategy, has shown
012            very promising improvement on error-correcting capability over the classical
013            methods. However, the success of those deep learning-based decoder comes at
014            the cost of drastically increased model storage and computational complexity,
015            hindering their practical adoptions in real-world time-sensitive resource-sensitive
016            communication and storage systems. To address this challenge, we propose an
017            efficient variational diffusion model-based channel decoder, which effectively in-
018            tegrates the domain-specific belief propagation process to the modern diffusion
019            model. By reaping the low-cost benefits of belief propagation and strong learn-
020
               ing capability of diffusion model, our proposed neural decoder simultaneously
               achieves very low cost and high error-correcting performance. Experimental re-
021
               sults show that, compared with the state-of-the-art neural channel decoders, our
022
               model provides a feasible solution for practical deployment via achieving the best
023            decoding performance with order-of-magnitude (1000× and up) savings in com-
024            putational cost and model size.

      1   I NTRODUCTION
029
030   Channel coding has served as the fundamental and critical mechanism in numerous modern com-
031   munication and storage systems and applications, such as 5G, Wi-Fi, Starlink, optical networking,
032   solid-state drive and hard disk drive. By providing error correction functionality, channel coding
033
      aims at protecting information from various corruptions (e.g., noise) incurred by data transmission.
      To that end, most channel codes are designed by adding extra redundant bits to help detect and
034
      recover the original information after noisy transmission.
035
036   To date, most of the commercially adopted channel codes are linear block codes, which can be
037   optimally decoded using maximum likelihood (ML) decoding process. However, ML decoding,
038   which can be mathematically modeled as searching for the closest lattice point in high dimensional
039
      space (Gowaikar & Hassibi, 2007), is very expensive and computationally prohibitive. In practice, a
      more feasible and practical channel decoding solution is to use belief propagation (BP) algorithm (Su
040
      et al., 2022), which can achieve exact optimum results in the tree-structured factor graph. However,
041
      in the context of channel coding, factor graphs constructed from the parity check matrix of modern
042   channel codes are often cyclic, making the BP decoding results suboptimal (Yedidia et al., 2005).
043
044   Existing Neural Channel Decoders. Motivated by the unprecedented success of neural networks
045
      in many fields, many recent efforts leverage the advance of deep learning to develop neural channel
      decoder, successfully improving the decoding performance. Nachmani et al. (2016) propose a neural
046
      belief propagation model by adding neural weights on all propagation messages. Considering belief
047
      propagation is naturally a graph based algorithm, Nachmani & Wolf (2019) further improves by
048   using hyper-graph neural network as the decoding solution. As these models are constrained to the
049   original belief propagation form, they are not as flexible as modern neural networks in terms of layer
050   design, architecture search, etc. To overcome this limitation, Bennatan et al. (2018) reformulates
051   the decoding problem into a noise prediction task taking extra steps in pre-processing and post-
052   processing, enabling more relaxed model design. Following this philosophy, Choukroun & Wolf
053   (2022) introduces the powerful transformer (Vaswani et al., 2017) architecture-based neural channel
      decoder, and later extend it into a diffusion-based model (Choukroun & Wolf, 2023).

054
      High-Complexity Challenges of SOTA Neural Decoders. While today’s deep learning-based de-
055   coding algorithms achieve outstanding error-correcting performance, they are meanwhile suffer-
056   ing from the high computation and storage costs of neural networks, a very challenging limitation
057   severely hinders their practical deployment. More specifically, the application scenarios of chan-
058   nel coding, e.g., wireless communication, optical communication and disk drives, demand real-time
059   and low-power processing, bringing very stringent requirement on processing speed and power con-
060   sumption of channel decoder. For instance, the decoding latency in 5G is limited to millisecond level
061   (Parvez et al., 2018; Rico & Merino, 2020). Meanwhile, a massive amount of channel decoding is
062   performed at mobile devices such as smartphones, which have constrained computing resource and
063
      power budgets. Consequently, the cost of modern neural channel decoder, if cannot be properly
      trimmed down, could severely impede the widespread adoption of this emerging solution.
064
065   Technical Contributions. In this work, we propose an efficient diffusion model-based decoding al-
066   gorithm. Unlike existing diffusion decoder (Choukroun & Wolf, 2023), our method takes a different
067   formulation of diffusion process via effectively integrating the philosophy of belief propagation into
068   the model architecture, simultaneously achieving high error-correcting performance and low model
069
      complexity. More specifically, considering belief propagation is not noise oriented and its input
      format (log-likelihood ratio) needs a flexible noise design in the forward diffusion process, our pro-
070
      posed neural channel decoder is built upon the framework of variational diffusion model (Kingma
071
      et al., 2021), successfully reaping the benefits of belief propagation and diffusion process.
072
073   We summarize the contributions of this work as follow: 1) It, for the first time, studies the efficient
074   integration of belief propagation-based channel decoding to diffusion model, formulating a new
075
      variational diffusion model based neural channel decoder. 2) The proposed variational diffusion
      decoder achieves ultra-low model complexity with high error-correcting performance. Compared to
076
      the state-of-the-art neural decoder, our approach brings 1000× reduction in computational costs and
077
      memory costs, with the same or lower bit-error-rate (BER).

080   2   R ELATED W ORKS
081
082
      To date a series of research efforts have been reported in applying neural networks to channel coding.
083
      In general, from the perspective of machine learning, by defining training loss as the binary cross
084   entropy loss, the objective of channel decoding can be described as a multi-label binary classification
085   problem, which has been well studied in deep learning community. Here different from many deep
086   learning applications, the input messages of channel coding can be randomly generated, making the
087   neural channel decoders free from data hungry concern (Marcus, 2018). It is also worth noticing
088   that belief propagation-based deep learning models are found able to learn from all zero messages
089   (Lugosch & Gross, 2017); while there has not been found any significant difference between training
090   on all zero messages or random messages.
091   More specifically, neural channel decoders can be roughly categorized into belief propagation based
092   and general neural network based. Belief propagation-based neural models maintain the message
093   passing structure of BP algorithm, while adding neural weight parameters on messages (Nachmani
094   et al., 2016; Lugosch & Gross, 2017; Liang et al., 2018; Nachmani & Wolf, 2019; Liao et al., 2021).
095   Although these models provide performance improvement over classical BP approach, they are also
096   constrained to the BP structure and are lack of the flexibility of neural architecture exploration. On
097   the other hand, the structure of general neural network-based decoding models are designed without
098
      prior constraint. Gruber et al. (2017) propose the dense layer-based neural decoder, which works
      well for short channel codes with code length up to 64.
099
100   Instead of decoding the input message, Bennatan et al. (2018) take another path as directly predict-
101   ing the transmission noise. To that end, it builds a binary-input symmetric-output channels-based
102   framework to decouple message and noise. This strategy asks for additional information (syndrome)
103   as input and needs extra computation for output, since the learning model is trained for predicting
104
      noise. In addition to these changes in processing input and output, the transition from message pre-
      diction to noise prediction is also relaxed to general neural network structure, enabling the flexible
105
      model design.
106
107   Based on the framework, Choukroun & Wolf (2022) successfully applies the transformer architec-
      ture in channel decoding, and this powerful architecture is later extended to the diffusion-based


                                                        2
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


108
      model (Choukroun & Wolf, 2023). Considering denoising diffusion probabilistic models (DDPMs)
109   Ho et al. (2020) naturally learn to predict noise, the neural decoder in Choukroun & Wolf (2023) is
110   built in the format of DDPM. To accommodate the channel coding setting, the additive white Gaus-
111   sian noise (AWGN) channel is formulated as an unscaled forward diffusion process. The reverse
112   diffusion process is also modified into a decoding process, and the corresponding decoding steps
113   (sampling timesteps in reverse process) are bounded by the parity check count in the given channel
114   codes.
115   One key challenge for diffusion model-based channel decoder is the high complexity. Though pow-
116   erful, diffusion models are known for their large computation cost and slow generation process, in
117   addition to the underlying heavy deep learning model. For instance, the reverse timesteps in im-
118   age generation can be as large as 1000 (Ho et al., 2020). To address this challenging issue, many
119   research works have been proposed to improve the generation speed, such as reducing sampling
120   steps (Song et al., 2021), transforming into using faster solvers for ordinary different equation (Lu
121   et al., 2022) and knowledge distillation into a deterministic model (Salimans & Ho, 2022). These
122   existing diffusion model optimization methods are orthogonal to our proposed solution of efficient
123
      variational diffusion channel decoder, and can be potentially applied to our approach towards further
      improvement.

128   3.1   C HANNEL C ODING
129
130   For an (N, K) channel code with code length as N and information length as K, it specific
131   code format is determined by a generator matrix G ∈ {0, 1}K×N and a parity check matrix
132   H ∈ {0, 1}(N −K)×N . Given K-bit input message mb ∈ {0, 1}K , the encoded N -bit codeword
133   xb ∈ {0, 1}N can be calculated via xb = mb G with all computations in binary domain. In general,
134
      xb is transmitted over a noisy channel. The goal of channel coding is to recover the input message
      from the corrupted transmitted codeword at the receiver end. In practice, a systematic encoding
135
      approach is often adopted such that receiver can easily recover the message by taking the first K bits
136
      from decoded results (Lin & Costello, 2004).
137

202   It can be noticed that the parameterization in DDPMs is specially designed, such as the relationship
203   between α and β and between xt and x0 . Although this specific design results in clear formu-
204   lations for training and generation, such constraints are not realistic in channel coding, especially
205
      when describing the AWGN channel from the perspective of forward diffusion process. In this sec-

206
      framework (Kingma et al., 2021), i.e., variational diffusion channel decoder (VCDC).

      5      E XPERIMENTS
294
295   We set CSNR from 4-dB to 6-dB as previous work (Choukroun & Wolf, 2023) adopts and evaluate
296   our method on different linear block codes, i.e., Polar Codes (Arikan, 2009), Low-Density Parity
297
      Check (LDPC) codes (Gallager, 1962), Mackay codes and CCSDS codes, which are available on
      (Helmling et al., 2019). Modern channel coding has stringent latency and model size requirement.
298
      From the perspective of deployment, we evaluate and compare different models in experiments.
299
      Although deep learning-based decoders often benefit from the power of increasing model size, the
300   resulting deployment cost is not feasible in the case of channel coding, e.g., millisecond-level la-
301   tency tolerance (Rico & Merino, 2020). For the purpose of achieving low error rate, fast inference
302   and small model size, we compare different models in their lightest setting over the bit error rate,
303   computation cost in terms of floating-point operations (FLOPs), and storage cost in terms of bytes.
304
      To achieve maximum efficiency, we build our VDCD models using a single block neural network
305
      and set T = 20 for reverse process. For model training, Adam optimizer (Kingma & Ba, 2015) is
306   applied using learning rate 0.001 with 256 samples per batch and 20000 training iterations. Different
307   from many diffusion models, the high efficiency of our model enables training and experiments on
308   a CPU only platform, i.e., AMD EPYC 7402P 24-Core Processor. The training time varies between
309   1 to 3 hours for different codes.
310
      Comparison is made with the hyper graph neural network-based model (Nachmani & Wolf, 2019)
311
      that also maintains the belief propagation structure, which is referred as HGN. Their fastest models
312   are configured with hidden dimension 32 and 5 hidden layers. We also compare with the state-of-
313   the-art DDECC (Choukroun & Wolf, 2023), where their fastest models are configured with 2 self
314   attention layers in hidden dimension 32. The traditional belief propagation algorithm running 5
315   iterations is also listed as the baseline.

424   6   C ONCLUSIONS
425
426   This paper proposes a diffusion based decoder for channel coding. Driven by the strict requirements
427   of low latency and high reliability, our model is designed to leverage traditional belief propagation
428   and modern diffusion frameworks. The method reformulates the AWGN channel as forward dif-
429   fusion process in the VDM framework and builds a neural network architecture on top of belief
430   propagation. Experiments show our design achieves the best decoding performance with orders of
431   magnitude savings in computation and storage cost than the state-of-the-art diffusion based decoder.
      These significant results exhibit the great potential of deploying diffusion based decoder in reality.
