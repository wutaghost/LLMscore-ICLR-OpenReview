009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            Two-view correspondence learning aims to discern true and false correspondences
012            between image pairs by recognizing their underlying different information. Previ-
013            ous methods either treat the information equally or fail to discard the superfluous
014            information of false correspondences, tending to be invalid in practical scenarios.
015            Therefore, inspired by Mamba’s inherent competence of selectivity, we propose
016
               MambaMatch as a Mamba-based correspondence filter to selectively mine infor-
               mation from true correspondences and to dispose of the potentially interfering
017
               information of false correspondences. Specifically, the selection is achieved by
018
               adaptively adjusting model parameters in a high-dimensional latent space, which
019            also avoids attention leakage and implements context compression, ensuring the
020            precise and efficient exploitation of pertinent information. Meanwhile, channel
021            awareness is tailored to serve as a complementary aspect of comprehensive in-
022            formation acquisition. Moreover, we design a novel local-context enhancement
023            module to capture reasonable local context that is crucial for correspondence prun-
024            ing. Extensive experiments demonstrate that our approach outperforms existing
025            state-of-the-art methods on several visual tasks while saving time and space costs.

      attention to diverse inputs through explicit weights, but this simplistic approach is insufficient for
055   handling complex scenarios. Additionally, some vanilla attention-based methods (Liu & Yang, 2023;
056   Li et al., 2024) modulate the importance of information by constructing feature maps for the inputs
057   and assigning different weights. However, this soft attention is allocated across all data throughout,
058   leading to attention leakage and the retention of redundant context (i.e. incapacity to compress
059   context) as presented in Figure 1(c), which degrades the performance. Furthermore, attention-based
060   methods are impeded by quadratic complexity, resulting in inefficiency during both inference and
061   training. Therefore, we consider the following questions: (i) How to selectively treat inliers and
062   outliers, and allocate attention appropriately to different points? (ii) Can we fully capture the
063
      information and compress the context into a more compact representation as much as possible?
064   Recently, a novel architecture termed Selective State Space Model, i.e. Mamba (Gu & Dao, 2023),
065   has gained widespread adoption in visual tasks (Liu et al., 2024b; Zhu et al., 2024). One of its key
066   advantages is the selectivity, enabling models to focus on or disregard specific inputs. This may offer
067   a pathway to address (i). Furthermore, it achieves optimization of complexity through compressing
068   context into a compact representation and employs hardware scanning methods. This presents a
069
      potential avenue for addressing (ii). Inspired by this, we seek to introduce Mamba to tackle the
      problem of two-view correspondence learning. However, most existing Mamba-based visual models
070
      are tailored for tasks involving regular images as input (Guo et al., 2024; Zhu et al., 2024; Liu et al.,
071
      2024b), and their common structures are ill-suited for two-view correspondence learning with sparse
072   points. Although some recent endeavors have explored using Mamba for point cloud tasks (Liu et al.,
073   2024a; Zhang et al., 2024), they predominantly focus on handling 3D sequences and lack guidance
074   on adapting to our 2D data while achieving context compression and ensuring no attention leakage.
075
      Therefore, we propose a Mamba-based correspondence learning method MambaMatch, which is
076
      superior in terms of effective data selection. Specifically, the channel-aware Mamba filter (CAMF)
077
      is the main part, which can parameterize the state space model (SSM) parameters according to the
078   input data. This allows the network to distinguish inliers and outliers in the high-dimensional latent
079   space and give different focus, which is beneficial to correspondence pruning, especially with a high
080   percentage of outliers. Although Mamba is globally aware, its lack of channel awareness (Guo et al.,
081   2024) makes it underpowered for learning high-dimensional features with many channels. This may
082   lead to the model being sensitive to randomly distributed outliers. To this end, we customize its
083   channel awareness capability so that our method can better adapt to real-world scenes. Meanwhile,
084   it’s known that a fundamental principle in correspondence learning is the ability to capture local
085   context (Zhang et al., 2019; Liu et al., 2021). Therefore, to increase the capability to obtain context
086
      information (Yi et al., 2018) of the initial sequences, we also design a local context enhancement
      module (LCEM). As shown in Figure 1(d), our Mamba-based approach not only focuses on potentially
087
      correct matches with higher weights but also discards false matches, avoiding attention leakage and
088
      achieving context compression. To sum up, the contributions of this article mainly include the
089   following three folds:
090
091     i. To our best knowledge, this represents the inaugural exploration into leveraging Mamba for
092        sparse feature matching. Additionally, we pioneer the application of Mamba’s selection traits to
093        tackle scenarios characterized by a significant presence of outliers adeptly. This approach could
094        potentially offer valuable insights for a variety of other applications;
095    ii. We specifically engineer a new outlier filter based on Mamba that can more effectively filter out
096        mismatches by discarding particular inputs, trying to retain correct matches only. Concurrently,
097        we develop a local context enhancement module to address Mamba’s deficiency in capturing
098        local context, thereby enhancing the learning of correspondences;
099   iii. We design a suite of experiments to substantiate the rationality of our methodological design and
100        further demonstrate the efficacy and robust generalization capabilities of our approach through
101        various real-world datasets.

105   2.1   C ORRESPONDENCE L EARNING
106
107   A renowned paradigm involves first obtaining an initial set of Correspondences, followed by the
      application of outlier rejection to refine and achieve a more accurate correspondence set. The best-


                                                         2
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


108
      known handcrafted methods such as RANSAC (Fischler & Bolles, 1981) and its variants (Torr &
109   Zisserman, 2000; Ni et al., 2009; Chum & Matas, 2005) employ a hypothesis-verification strategy to
110   find a maximal consistent subset that fits a particular geometric model. This type of method is highly
111   dependent on the sampled subsets, leading to their failure in high outlier situations. Methods for
112   nonparametric models (Ma et al., 2014; Bian et al., 2017; Fan et al., 2023) can handle both rigid and
113   non-rigid deformations. But they are equally helpless in the face of large perspectives and repetitively
114   structured real-life scenarios. Then, with the development of deep learning, MLPs are first used as
115   a solution for correspondence pruning. Yi et al. (2018) utilizes them to extract high-dimensional
116   features of each putative correspondence individually and introduces context normalization (CN)
117
      to capture global contextual information. Zhang et al. (2019) introduces a differentiable layer that
      captures local information by softly assigning nodes to a set of clusters. Liu et al. (2021) combines
118
      global and local coherence to robustly detect true correspondences. Zhang & Ma (2023) uses a CNN
119
      as the backbone and avoids the design of additional context normalization modules of MLP-based
120   approaches. However, these methods treat each input equally and lack the ability to mine the different
121   information between inliers and outliers. Some subsequent approaches like NCMNet (Liu & Yang,
122   2023) and MC-Net (Li et al., 2024) attempt to address the divergence of information between outliers
123   and inliers by learning an attention map that softly assigns varying degrees of focus to different
124   regions. However, they suffer from attention leakage to outliers and efficiency issues due to the
125   inability to compress context.
126   Another popular pipeline involves directly obtaining an accurate set of Correspondences in one go.
127   Representative work such as SuperGlue (Sarlin et al., 2020) has made significant strides by utilizing
128   graph neural networks in conjunction with attention mechanisms (Vaswani et al., 2017). However,
129   these methods experience a quadratic decrease in efficiency as the number of keypoints increases,
130   leading to difficulties in practical application. Detector-free dense matching methods (Edstedt et al.,
131   2023; 2024) and semi-dense approaches (Sun et al., 2021; Tang et al., 2022)can achieve accurate
132   matches even in extreme scenarios like textureless regions, but the increased computational cost and
133   memory usage due to richer matches remain unresolved. Therefore, our paper focuses on the first
134
      paradigm, while the second pipeline is discussed appropriately in the analysis.

      2.2   S TATE S PACE M ODELS
137
138
      The state space model (SSM) is initially used to describe dynamic systems. It has recently been
139
      introduced as a generalized backbone for natural language processing and computer vision (Gu et al.,
140
      2021b;a; Smith et al., 2022). The problem of early SSMs is similar to RNN models that easily forget
141   global contextual information and suffer from gradient vanishing as the sequence length grows (Gu
142   et al., 2020; 2021b). The recent work Mamba (Gu & Dao, 2023) based on SSM successfully
143   overcomes these shortcomings. With the subquadratic complexity and selective scanning mechanism,
144   Mamba (Gu & Dao, 2023) has the potential to be a prospective backbone. Zhu et al. (2024) and Liu
145   et al. (2024b) innovatively introduce Mamba to vision tasks. In addition, Liu et al. (2024a) and Zhang
146   et al. (2024) first propose to use Mamba to process 3D sequence data. The methods directly employed
147   for handling image data may not directly translate to our task because of the difference in data
148   structure. At the same time, approaches for point cloud tend to overly focus on data preprocessing
149
      without significantly enhancing Mamba itself, more resembling a custom utilization. We observe
      that while Mamba exhibits strong capability in capturing global information, it falls short in channel
150
      information acquisition (Guo et al., 2024). Therefore, in this article, while we use Mamba to solve
151
      the problem of how to selectively mine and utilize inlier information while discarding outliers, we
152   design the channel-aware module to increase its ability to acquire cross-channel information. This
153   may also offer a novel approach to addressing other scenarios with a high proportion of outliers.

189
      Given an image pair (I, I0 ), we employ off-the-shelf feature detectors and descriptors to extract
190   keypoints from both images. Subsequently, employing the nearest neighbor (NN) method, we
191   establish initial putative matches. Denoted as C = [c1 ; c2 ; · · · ], where ci = {(xi , yi , x0i , yi0 )|i =
192   1, · · · , N }, (xi , yi ) and (x0i , yi0 ) represent the coordinates of the i-th keypoints in respective images.
193
      In analogy to Yi et al. (2018), we approach the two-view correspondence learning problem as an
194
      inlier/outlier classification and an essential matrix regression. For the putative correspondences C ∈
195
      RN ×4 , to extract the deep information, they are usually upscaled to get F = {f1 , f2 , · · · , fi } ∈ RN ×d .
196   Moreover, we establish an inlier predictor at each layer of the network for simultaneous training,
197   with only the last predictor yielding the probability value P = [p1 , p2 , · · · , pN ]T ∈ RN ×1 , where
198   pi ∈ [0, 1) signifies the probability that the corresponding ci is an inlier. Like other learning-based
199   methods (Liu et al., 2021; Liu & Yang, 2023; Li et al., 2024), we employ a weighted eight-point
200   method based on P to directly estimate the essential matrix. The entire process is encapsulated as
201   follows:

      4.4   I MPLEMENTATION D ETAILS
335
      For implementation, we normalize the coordinates of keypoints to [-1, 1] with image size or camera
336   intrinsic. Our MambaMatch consists of L = 4 stacked MambaMatch layers. The geometric error
337   threshold is set to 10−4 . The model is optimized by Adam (Kingma & Ba, 2022), and the learning
338   rate is set to 10−3 during the first 80k iterations then decaying with a factor of 0.999996 every step.

528   6     C ONCLUSION AND P ROSPECTS
529
530   In this paper, we design a novel network named MambaMatch for two-view correspondence learning.
531   Inspired by the selective state space model, MambaMatch can focus on or discard particular inputs.
532   Specifically, by targeting the distinguishability of inliers and outliers in the high-dimensional hidden
533   space, the network gives different focus to the two, so that it can pay more focus to the useful
534   information brought by the inliers while discarding the interfering information of the outliers to
535   realize high-precision correspondence pruning. Meanwhile, since ignoring irrelevant information will
536
      compress the context at the same time, resulting in redundant information being discarded directly,
      this makes the model have subquadratic complexity. This is relatively friendly for handling real-time
537
      tasks. A large number of experiments prove the superior performance of our method.
538
539   Additionally, our approach, which focuses on the selection characteristics of Mamba, may offer
      insights for a range of tasks with a high proportion of noisy information interference.
