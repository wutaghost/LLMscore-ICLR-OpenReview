009                                                A BSTRACT
010
011            Transformers have been successfully applied to learned image compression (LIC).
012            In fact, dense self-attention is difficult to ignore contextual information that de-
013            grades the entropy estimations. To overcome this challenging problem, we incor-
014            porate dynamic attention in LIC for the first time. The window-based dynamic
015            attention (WDA) module is proposed to adaptively tune attention based on the
016            entropy distribution by sparsifying the attention matrix. Additionally, the WDA
017            module is embedded into encoder and decoder transformation layers to refine at-
018
               tention in multi-scales, hierarchically extracting compact latent representations.
               Similarly, we propose the dynamic-reference entropy model (DREM) to adap-
019
               tively select context information. This decreases the difficulty of entropy estima-
020
               tion by leveraging the relevant subset of decoded symbols, achieving an accurate
021            entropy model. To the best of our knowledge, this is the first work employing dy-
022            namic attention for LIC. Extensive experiments demonstrate the proposed method
023            outperforms the state-of-the-art LIC methods.

026   1   I NTRODUCTION
027
028   Vision Transformer (ViT) has achieved tremendous advancements in the field of computer vision,
029   with many studies applying it to learned image compression (LIC) methods (Zhu et al., 2022; Qian
030   et al., 2022a;b; Liu et al., 2023). Efficient self-attention between all sequence elements helps the
031   model pay attention to long-range information. There are mainly two aspects of works transferring
032   CNN-based learned image compressions to ViT architectures. Utilizing Swin Transformers (Swin-
033
      T) (Liu et al., 2021b) in main encoder-decoders to build powerful nonlinear transforms (Liu et al.,
      2023; Zhu et al., 2022; Zou et al., 2022; Lu et al., 2022). On the other hand, some works leverage
034
      ViTs in entropy models to capture global contextual information, supporting a more accurate prob-
035
      ability estimation of the latent representation distributions (Qian et al., 2022a; Koyuncu et al., 2022;
036   Kim et al., 2022). However, the rate-distortion (RD) performance improvements of these methods
037   are marginal.
038
039
      Nonlinear transformations and entropy models are the key components of LIC. Despite ViTs enables
      attention to more distant context, it does not guarantee compact transformations and accurate entropy
040
      estimation. Adjacent features exhibit stronger causal relationships and the previous work (Minnen
041
      et al., 2018) reveals that convolution kernel sizes larger than 5 × 5 (with larger receptive fields)
042   unexpectedly compromise the RD performance. The works (He et al., 2021; Zou et al., 2022) also
043   prove that redundancy primarily exists in local regions. Some redundancy information indeed exists
044   in distant regions (Qian et al., 2022b), but referring global contextual information increases the risk
045   of overfitting. Previous works have focused solely on the long-range modeling capabilities of ViTs,
046   ignoring the issue of overfitting.
047
      In this paper, we analyze the challenge of applying ViTs to image compression, and a novel method
048   is proposed: dynamically sparsifying attention. Plain Swin-T compute paired attention between
049   all elements in local windows. In other works, all decoded symbols in a window are considered
050   when decoding the current node. Different from recognition tasks, the goal of compression is to
051   remove redundancy. Reference to irrelevant content can mislead probability estimations. We claim
052   that the core contradiction of overfitting is that the attention-pattern space of ViTs is much large than
053   redundancy-pattern space. To overcome this problem, we sparsify the attention-pattern space and
      propose a compression model adopts adaptive attention patterns learning from the entropy distribu-

054
      tion of the image. Specifically, the model is built on the window-based dynamic attention (WDA)
055   module, which tunes the attention matrix to ignore useless references in local windows. The WDA
056   module works in multiple feature scales to hierarchically tune long-range attention patterns. Further-
057   more, the dynamic-reference entropy model (DREM) is proposed, which builds upon the concept
058   of dynamic attention patterns, adaptively selecting reference contextual information for the current
059   encoding element based on the known entropy distribution. The aggregation of relevant decoded
060   symbol subsets significantly reduces the difficulty of probability estimation in the entropy model.
061   To the best of our knowledge, we first focus on the overfitting issue of transformer-based learned
062   image compression methods and modulate the attention by the means of dynamic sparsification
063   patterns. In summary, our contributions can be concluded as follows:
064
065          • We first integrate dynamic attention into learned image compression, which narrows the
066            gap of attention space and redundancy space. Sparse attention patterns ignore globally
067            irrelevant contexts, reducing the risk of overfitting.
068          • We propose the window-based dynamic attention (WDA) module, which adaptively learns
069            attention patterns from entropy information of latent representations. The WDA modulates
070            the attention matrix at different scales in a fine-to-coarse manner.
071
             • We present the dynamic-reference entropy model (DREM) to select subsets of decoded
072            symbols, which provide enough contextual information and simultaneously reduce the op-
073            timization difficulty.
074
             • Experiment results demonstrate that our proposed method achieves 13.42%, 17.74% and
075
               12.93% BD-rate gains over VTM-17.0 on the Kodak, Tecnick and CLIC datasets respec-
076
               tively and outperforms the state-of-the-art LIC method MLIC++.

079   2     R ELATED WORK
080
081   2.1   L EARNED IMAGE COMPRESSION
082
083   Early LIC methods adopt convolutional neural networks (CNNs) in both encoder-decoders and en-
084
      tropy models (Ballé et al., 2018; Minnen et al., 2018; Lee et al., 2018). (Cheng et al., 2020) first
      incorporates the attention mechanism into LIC, which pays more attention to regions with com-
085
      plicated textures. However, the local receptive field of CNNs limits their ability to capture long-
086
      range spatial dependencies. Some global methods utilize non-local networks (Chen et al., 2021) and
087   content-weighted attention masks (Li et al., 2018; Mentzer et al., 2018) to alloacte bits across the
088   entire image, leading to an overall improvement in RD performance. With the rise of transformers,
089   ViTs are gradually emerging in LIC. The global self-attention constructs more powerful nonlinear
090   transformations (Lu et al., 2022; Zhu et al., 2022; Liu et al., 2023; Zou et al., 2022) and provide rich
091   contextual information in entropy models (Qian et al., 2022a; Kim et al., 2022; Liu et al., 2023).
092   However, the downside of global information is that irrelevant context increases the difficulty of
093   entropy estimation and the risk of overfitting. We propose the WDA module to dynamically sparsify
094   the attention patterns to address the problem.
095
096   2.2   DYNAMIC ATTENTION
097
098   Previous works have demonstrated that a significant amount of computational redundancy exists
099
      in ViTs. Only a small proportion of tokens contribute to the final prediction, thus removing those
      useless tokens improves the computational efficiency without harming the performance (Chen et al.,
100
      2023; Wei et al., 2023). Following that, some sparse attention methods are proposed to accelerate
101
      ViTs, including token sampling (Rao et al., 2021; Fayyaz et al., 2022; Tang et al., 2022) and atten-
102   tion masking (Liu et al., 2021a; Kitaev et al., 2019). Among those methods, static sparse methods
103   (Tay et al., 2020; Kong et al., 2022) introduce heuristic sparse attention patterns with challenging of
104   generalization. While dynamic methods (Yin et al., 2022; Venkataramanan et al., 2023; Lee et al.,
105   2024) learn dynamic attention patterns from data in a flexible way. Our work is inspired by dynamic
106   sparse attention but applied in a different domain. Specifically, we apply the dynamic sparse at-
107   tention to more efficiently eliminate representation redundancy rather than to reduce computational
      complexity.

      2.3    C ONTEXT E NTROPY M ODELING
134
135   In LIC entropy models replace the marginal probability distribution of latent variables by the joint
136   probability distribution with prior variables to reduce entropy (Ballé et al., 2018; Minnen et al., 2018;
137   Lee et al., 2018). Due to the sequential property of decoding, leveraging previously decoded fea-
138   tures (i.e., context) to provide predictive information for the current decoding step can significantly
139   reduce the joint entropy. And an optimal contextual pattern determines the upper bound of predic-
140   tion accuracy. Some works divide feature channels into multiple slices and remove redundacy by
141
      leveraging correlations between channels (Minnen & Singh, 2020; He et al., 2022; Zhu et al., 2022).
      In terms of spatial redundancy, CNN-based methods capture local correlations between neighboring
142
      representations (He et al., 2021; Zou et al., 2022; Guo et al., 2021) and transformer-based methods
143
      calculate relevant information over longer ranges (Liu et al., 2023; Lu et al., 2022). Although rele-
144   vant information may exist in global regions, previous works (He et al., 2021; Minnen et al., 2018)
145   show that adjacent pixels are likely to have a stronger causal relationship. Focusing on too much
146   irrelevant information increases the difficulty of prediction. Some methods select top-K elements in
147   global regions to centralize attention (Qian et al., 2022a;b; Ma et al., 2021). However, this fixed-
148   number reference pattern fails to adapt to sample differences. We propose the dynamic-reference
149   entropy model (DREM) to adaptively select reference subsets with entropy information.

311   4.1      E XPERIMENTAL SETUP
312
      We implement the proposed compression method based on the platform CompressAI 1 (Bégaint
313
      et al., 2020). The proposed model is trained on the Flickr2W (Liu et al., 2020) dataset for 2M
314   steps. We crop images into 256 × 256 patches and set batch size as 8. The Adam optimizer
315   is utilized and the learning rate is fixed as 1e−4 for the former 1.5M steps. Then, the learning
316   rate is divided by two if the validation loss hits a bottleneck (we use a wait for 20 steps). The
317   model is trained with the RD loss in Equation 5. Following the settings of CompressAI, λ is set
318   as 0.0018, 0.035, 0.0067, 0.013, 0.025, 0.0483 for MSE and 2.4, 4.58, 8.73, 16.64, 31.73, 60.5 for
319   MS-SSIM. For evaluation we conduct test on Kodak (Kodak, 1993), CLIC Professional Validation
320   (Toderici et al., 2020) and Tecnick datasets (Asuni et al., 2014).

420   5   C ONCLUSION
421
422
423
      In this paper we first adopt dynamic attention into learned image compression. Based on the as-
      sumption that the redundancy information densely distributes in local regions and sparsely exists in
424
      long-range distance, we propose the WDA module to dynamically sparsifying the attention matrix
425
      in Swin-T blocks, making adaptive attention patterns learned from data possible. This is reasonable
426   because of the diversity of image entropy distribution. The WDA module dynamically modulate
427   the contextual information according to the local entropy, where regions with large entropy could
428   be allocated more long-range context. Appling the WDA into the entropy model, the proposed
429   dynamic-reference entropy model select a subset of reference tokens, sparsifing the optimization
430   space and decreases the risk of overfitting. Extensive experiments demonstrate the performance ad-
431   vantage of our method and proves the possibility for compression networks to evolve in a dynamic
      and flexible direction in the future.
