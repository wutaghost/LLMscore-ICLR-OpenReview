009                                              A BSTRACT
010
011            For multivariate time series (MTS) tasks, previous state space models (SSMs) fol-
012            lowed the modeling paradigm of Transformer-based methods. However, none of
013            them explicitly model the complex dependencies of MTS: the Channel Depen-
014            dency variations with Time (CDT). In view of this, we delve into the derivation of
015            SSM, which involves approximating continuously updated functions by orthog-
016            onal function basis. We then develop Poly-Mamba, a novel method for MTS
017            forecasting. Its core concept is to expand the original orthogonal function basis
018            space into a multivariate orthogonal function space containing variable mixing
019
               terms, and make a projection on this space so as to explicitly describe the CDT
               by weighted coefficients. In Poly-Mamba, we propose the Multivariate Orthog-
020
               onal Polynomial Approximation (MOPA) as a simplified implementation of this
021
               concept. For the simple linear relationship between channels, we propose Linear
022            Channel Mixing (LCM) and generate CDT patterns adaptively for different chan-
023            nels through a proposed Order Combining method. Experiments on six real-world
024            datasets demonstrate that Poly-Mamba outperforms the SOTA methods, especially
025            when dealing with datasets having a large number of channels and complex cor-
026            relations. The codes and log files are in the supplementary.

029   1   I NTRODUCTION
030
031   State space models (SSMs) Gu et al. (2021) Gu & Dao (2023) are subquadratic-time foundational
032   architectures compared with Transformers Vaswani et al. (2017), and shows great performance with
033   approximately linear complexity on long-range dependency tasks. Previous studies Zhang et al.
034   (2023) Wang et al. (2024) Ahamed & Cheng (2024) attempted to employ SSM for Multivariate
035   Time Series Forecasting (MTSF), they all follow the Transformer-based MTSF modeling paradigm:
036   learning dependencies between temporal tokens Zhou et al. (2021) Wu et al. (2021) Zhou et al.
037   (2022) Nie et al. (2022), Channel tokens Liu et al. (2023) and their concatenation Zhang & Yan
038
      (2022). However, the special complex dependency pattern of MTS is the Channel Dependency
      variations with Time (CDT), none of these methods explicitly depict it.
039
040   It is inappropriate to directly model the CDT because it not only greatly increases complexity when
041   calculating the dependency between temporal tokens of all channels but is also hard to generalize for
042   the scale of most MTS data. We delved deep into the initial development of SSM Gu et al. (2020):
043   real-time approximation of continuously updating function by orthogonal function basis Voelker
044
      et al. (2019), and we found that compared with Transformers, SSM has the potential to efficiently
      and effectively depict the CDT pattern.
045
046   We propose an improved SSM for MTSF tasks named Poly-Mamba. Based on the dynamical equa-
047   tion of its fitting coefficient, we propose three operations, Multivariate Orthogonal Polynomial Ap-
048   proximation (MOPA), Linear Channel Mixing (LCM) and Order Combining, improving the ability
049   of capturing the complex CDT pattern.
050   The idea of MOPA is to explicitly describe the inter-channel dependency pattern by using the map-
051   ping weighted coefficients of the extended multivariate polynomial function basis, which contains
052   variable mixing terms. MOPA is an efficient operation for mapping on the multivariate polynomial
053   basis space after simplification. LCM is a mapping operation used to construct a simple linear cor-
      relation between channels. In addition, in order to deal with different MTS scenarios, we propose


                                                       1
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


054
      Order Combining, which retains the low-order trend information of each channel itself and uses
055   gating Shazeer et al. (2017) to adaptively generate the CDT pattern of each channel.
056
057   The architecture of Poly-Mamba for MTSF takes time series patches as tokens and generates cor-
058
      responding multivariate hidden states. For the multivariate hidden states at each moment, LCM,
      MOPA, and Order Combining are applied to transform into hidden states with increased inter-
059
      channel correlations. Thus, this new SSM process generates a pattern representation of the time-
060
      varying dependencies between multi-channel tokens.
061
062   Our contributions are as follows:
063
             • We delve into the derivation of SSM, improve it for MTSF, and propose a new model named
064
               Poly-Mamba, which explicitly models channel dependency variations with time.
065
066          • We develop MOPA, an efficient method based on the idea of extending the orthogonal
067
               polynomial function basis space to a multivariate orthogonal polynomial basis space and
               explicitly characterizing channel dependencies with the mapped weights.
068
069          • We propose LCM to handle the simple linear correlation between channels. Additionally,
070            we propose Order Combining, retaining the trend information of each channel itself and
071
               adaptively learning different types of CDT patterns.
072          • We conduct extensive experiments on six real-world datasets. The experimental results
073            show that our Poly-Mamba outperforms the SOTA methods for the MTSF task, proving
074            that the improved SSM can effectively capture the complex dependencies of MTS.

      To effectively learn the CDT pattern of MTS, Poly-Mamba models along the temporal direction.
163   We embed sequence segment patches as tokens. This is inspired by the Transformer-based model
164   PatchTST Nie et al. (2022). Treating sequence segments as tokens can be more semantically infor-
165   mative and can effectively reduce complexity, and this is also applicable to the Mamba. As shown
166   in the Figure 1, in addition to the original parameters (A, B, C, ∆) in Mamba Gu & Dao (2023),
167   Poly-Mamba adds operations and corresponding parameters of MOPA, LCM, and Order Combining
168   to the hidden state. The complete process in the form of recurrent is shown in Algorithm 3.3. For
169   the MTSF task, after flattening the encoding result of L layers, a fully connected is used to output

      3.2   LCM
217
218   Different MTS scenarios exhibit varying degrees of CDT patterns. For example, some channels
219   may exhibit a simple linear relationship. Then, for the linear relationship between channels, it is
220   not appropriate for MOPA to capture it with refined high-order channel cross-terms. Therefore,
221   we propose the LCM method. LCM characterizes the linear relationship between channels with a
222   learnable parameter matrix L of size C × C, and multiplies L with the coefficient matrix of size
223   C ×N :
224
                                              LCM (x(t)) = L x(t)                                   (11)
225   In this way, each channel receives information from other channels and is mapped to the polynomial
226
      space linearly related to the channels.
227
228   3.3   O RDER C OMBINING
229
      LCM and MOPA model the simple linear dependency and complex relationships between channels
230
      respectively. However, the specific type of channel relationship is unknown in advance. Order
231   Combining adaptively generates the CDT pattern by applying a Gate operation Shazeer et al. (2017)
232   on the outputs of MOPA and LCM:
233
234
                       G{2,...,N } = sof tmax(PL LCM {2,...,N } , PM M OP A{2,..,N } )                  (12)
235   where PL and PM are the scalar weights. Moreover, in order to maintain the trend (low-order)
236   information of each channel itself, we perform LCM mapping on all terms but only perform MOPA
237   on high-order terms. After performing gate selection on the high-order terms of LCM and MOPA,
238
      they are spliced with the low-order terms of LCM:
239                                 OC = Concat(LCM {0,1} , G{2,...,N } )                               (13)
240
      If the overall relationship is linear, then LCM is selected by the gate; if it is a high-order complex
241   relationship, MOPA is selected by the gate, and the coefficients of the low-order terms in front will
242   be adjusted accordingly to adapt to the complex pattern.

      We comprehensively evaluate the proposed Poly-Mamba on six real-world datasets, including ETT

      (consisting of 4 subsets) Zhou et al. (2021), ECL Wu et al. (2021), Exchange Wu et al. (2021),

268   Zeng et al. (2023), TiDE Das et al. (2023); and (4) CNN-based methods: TimesNet Wu et al. (2022).
269   The experimental setting is identical to that in iTransformer Liu et al. (2023), where the input length
      L = 96 and the output length T = {96, 192, 336, 720}.

474   5      C ONCLUSION
475
476
477
      We delved into the derivation of SSM and found that it has the ability to depict the channel depen-
      dency variations with time. For Multivariate Time Series (MTS), we transformed the idea of the
478
      original SSM’s orthogonal function basis for real-time approximation of continuously updated func-
479
      tions into real-time approximation of multivariate functions with continuously updated inter-channel
480   dependencies. Based on this, we proposed the Poly-Mamba model, which contains three modules:
481   MOPA for mapping in multivariate orthogonal polynomial basis space, LCM for simple linear map-
482   ping between channels, and Order Combining for retaining trends and adaptive fitting. Experimen-
483   tally, Poly–Mamba achieves state-of-the-art MTS forecasting performance especially when facing
484   MTS scenarios with a large number of channels and real-time changing inter-correlations. In the
485   future, we will continue to further explore the application of our method in other MTS scenarios and
      explore large-scale time-series pre-training.
