010                                                 A BSTRACT
011
012            Traffic forecasting is crucial for smart cities and intelligent transportation initia-
013            tives, where deep learning has made significant progress in modeling complex
014            spatio-temporal patterns in recent years. However, current public datasets have
015            limitations in reflecting the distribution shift nature of real-world scenarios, charac-
016
               terized by continuously evolving infrastructures, varying temporal distributions,
               and long temporal gaps due to sensor downtimes or changes in traffic patterns.
017
               These limitations inevitably restrict the practical applicability of existing traffic
018
               forecasting datasets. To bridge this gap, we present XXLTraffic, largest available
019            public traffic dataset with the longest timespan collected from Los Angeles,
020            USA, and New South Wales, Australia, curated to support research in extremely
021            long forecasting beyond test adaptation. Our benchmark includes both typical
022            time-series forecasting settings with hourly and daily aggregated data and novel
023            configurations that introduce gaps and down-sample the training size to better
024            simulate practical constraints. We anticipate the new XXLTraffic will provide a
025            fresh perspective for the time-series and traffic forecasting communities. It would
026            also offer a robust platform for developing and evaluating models designed to
027
               tackle the extremely long forecasting problems beyond test adaptation. Our dataset
               supplements existing spatio-temporal data resources and leads to new research
028
               directions in this domain.

      1    I NTRODUCTION
032
033
      Rapid global population growth and vehicle proliferation have intensified urban traffic congestion.
034
      As cities expand and personal transportation reliance grows, strain on road networks leads to longer
035
      commutes, higher fuel consumption, and increased emissions. Accurate traffic prediction is vital for
036   intelligent transportation systems, informing strategies to mitigate congestion and enhance mobility
037   through improved route planning and urban development. Effective forecasting requires capturing
038   long-term spatio-temporal relationships in traffic data. Long-term analysis provides context for
039   anomalies in short-term patterns and reveals trends influenced by population cycles, seasonal shifts,
040   and yearly vehicle usage changes. These insights are crucial for developing robust models that adapt
041   to evolving urban traffic dynamics due to demographic and vehicular changes.
042   In recent years, significant work has focused on both short-term and long-term traffic flow prediction.
043   Deep learning techniques, including Graph Neural Networks (GNNs), have been employed to extract
044   spatial relationships within traffic networks Jin et al. (2023), while Transformer-based architectures
045   have been utilized to capture temporal dependencies over various timescales Shao et al. (2023a).
046   Although these methods have shown promising results, they often rely on datasets that do not fully
047   encapsulate the complexities introduced by rapid population growth and the surging number of
048   vehicles, thus limiting their applicability to real-world scenarios.
049   There is an emerging need in intelligent transportation systems to design predictive models that extend
050   beyond test adaptation, effectively generalizing to real-world conditions that evolve over time. It is
051   important to note that our concept of ’beyond test adaptation’ differs from ’test time adaptation’ Guo
052   et al. (2024) as illustrated in Fig 1 that shows the distinctions between them. This shift necessitates
053   models that can handle the multifaceted impacts of demographic changes and vehicle proliferation
      without relying solely on adaptation to specific test datasets. To achieve this, it is essential to utilize

055   the intricate patterns influenced by population and vehicular growth.

115   1.2   C HALLENGES
116
117   The ultra-dynamic challenge encompasses three key aspects: (1) Continuously evolving states of
118
      the underlying spatio-temporal infrastructures, characterized by an expanding number of nodes over
      the years. This continuous growth introduces complexity as the infrastructure adapts and expands.
119
      (2) Evolving temporal distributions over an extremely long observation period, which is crucial for
120
      extremely long forecasting beyond different non-contiguous train-test splits. This requires models to
121   adapt to changes in patterns and trends over extensive temporal spans.
122
123   We have constructed a traffic dataset with an exceptionally long temporal span and broader regional
124
      coverage, providing aggregated data and benchmarking, as well as a benchmarking setup considering
      extremely long prediction scenarios for future exploration:
125
126          • We propose XXLTraffic, a dataset that spans up to 23 years and exhibits evolutionary growth.
127            It includes data from 9 regions, with detailed data collection and processing procedures for
128            expansion and transformation. This dataset supports both temporally scalable and spatially
129            scalable challenges in traffic prediction.
130          • We present an experimental setup with temporal gaps for extremely long prediction beyong
131            test adaptation and provide a benchmark of aggregated versions of hourly and daily datasets.
132
             • We provide the exploration of input features through evolving temporal distributions over an
133            extremely long observation period. Additionally, our datasets support zero-shot forecasting
134            for new sensors.

140
      Definition 1. Traffic datasets: Traffic data primarily consists of vehicle flow detection data
141
      collected by sensors distributed across various locations in the traffic network. It is generally
142   represented by Xi ∈ RN ×T ×C , where T denotes the time steps, N denotes the number of sensors,
143   and C denotes the number of features.
144
145   Definition 2. Short-term traffic prediction: Short-term traffic prediction primarily focuses on
146   forecasting traffic speed or flow within the next hour. As shown in Equation 1, the input length α and
147   output length β are generally set to 12 steps.

258   Based on the 23 years of raw data we collected, we conducted rigorous data filtering and aggregation.
259
      The PeMS system has continuously evolved, expanding from a few sensors in 2001 to over 4,000

260
      selected a subset of sensors that were installed in the early stages and have consistently collected new
261
      data up to the present(named gap dataset), which is shown in the Appendix. This extensive gap
262   dataset effectively underpins the extremely long forecasting with gaps demonstrated in Figure 3.
263   Utilizing the gap dataset, we performed both hourly and daily aggregations, which will be
264   employed for gap-free long-term forecasting benchmarking. We will provide standard long-term

411   variate tokens. DLinear Zeng et al. (2023) challenges the effectiveness of Transformer models by
412   proposing a simple one-layer linear model that captures temporal relations in an ordered set of
413   continuous points. It employs positional encoding and uses tokens to embed sub-series, preserving
414   some ordering information in Transformers. Lastly, Mamba Gu & Dao (2023), a well-known
415   sequential model from last year, uses a bidirectional Mamba block to extract inter-variate correlations
416   and temporal dependencies. Additionally, we have selected five SOTA baselines Yu et al. (2018);
417   Guo et al. (2019); Wu et al. (2019); Bai et al. (2020); Jiang et al. (2023) from traffic forecasting
418   domain.

529   6     P ROSPECTS AND C ONSTRAINTS
530
531   Prospects. Our dataset spans the longest time period among existing datasets, and it is not only the
532   largest spatially, but also evolving in growth. It can continue to update in line with the updates from
533   the PeMS system in the future. It is specifically designed for various complex scenarios, such as
534   those already mentioned, including extremely long forecasting with long gaps, and hourly and daily
535   predictions. Additionally, zero-shot forecasting designed for evolving growth scenarios will also be
536
      included in Appendix.
537   Constraints. The limitations of our dataset are also quite evident. Due to the sheer size of our dataset,
538   it requires more computational resources. However, with the maturation of large language models
539   and foundational models, we believe its large volume will become an advantage, contributing more
      diverse data to the spatio-temporal large model community.
