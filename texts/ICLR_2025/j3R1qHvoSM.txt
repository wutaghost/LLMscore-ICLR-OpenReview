009                                                 A BSTRACT
010
011            Detecting small objects in video sequences is crucial, yet it poses significant chal-
012            lenges due to their limited visibility and dynamic nature, which complicates accu-
013            rate identification and localization. Traditional methods often employ a uniform
014            aggregation strategy across all frames, neglecting the unique spatiotemporal re-
015            lationships of small objects, which results in insufficient feature extraction and
016
               diminished detection performance. This paper introduces a long short-term trans-
               former network specifically designed for small object detection in videos. The
017
               model integrates features from both long-term and short-term frames: long-term
018
               frames capture global contextual information, enhancing the model’s ability to
019            represent background scenes, while short-term frames provide dynamic informa-
020            tion closely related to the current detection frame, thereby improving the feature
021            representation of small objects. A dynamic query generation module optimizes
022            query generation based on the implicit motion relationships of targets in short-
023            term frames, adapting to the current video framework. Additionally, the network
024            employs a progressive sampling strategy—densely sampling short-term frames
025            and sparsely sampling long-term frames—to effectively model video scenes. A
026            spatio-temporal alignment encoder further enhances pixel-level features by ac-
027
               counting for temporal and spatial transformations. Extensive experiments on the
               VisDrone-VID and UAVDT datasets demonstrate the method’s effectiveness, with
028
               an average detection precision increase of 1.4% and 2.1%, respectively, highlight-
029
               ing its potential in small object video detection.

032   1    I NTRODUCTION
033
034   Video small object detection refers to the task of identifying and localizing objects of small size
035   within video sequences, which are often characterized by limited pixel representation and subtle vi-
036   sual features. This process is essential in various fields, including self-driving cars, satellite imagery,
037   healthcare imaging, and industrial quality control (Jiao et al., 2022; Wang et al., 2020; Jiang et al.,
038   2022). The challenge lies in the inherent difficulties associated with small objects, such as their
039
      low resolution and tendency to blend into complex backgrounds. As a result, research has increas-
      ingly focused on enhancing feature detection for small objects (Lim et al., 2021a; Feng et al., 2020b;
040
      Ashraf et al., 2021b; Zhang et al., 2021). Studies have demonstrated that utilizing temporal informa-
041
      tion across video frames is crucial for improving detection performance, as single-frame detection
042   often struggles with issues like motion blur, low resolution, and the diminutive size of the objects.
043   Leveraging information from multiple frames helps to overcome these challenges (Bertasius et al.,
044   2018a; Han et al., 2020; Cui et al., 2021; Ma et al., 2022).
045
      Given the strong visual similarity and temporal continuity between adjacent frames, previous meth-
046
      ods have leveraged temporal information through pixel-level enhancements across these frames(Zhu
047
      et al., 2017; Bertasius et al., 2018b). Small objects, which may only occupy a few pixels in a single
048   frame, are particularly susceptible to pixel-level noise and environmental changes, making pixel-
049   level feature enhancement crucial(Xiao et al., 2023b). These methods typically employ deformable
050   convolutions or optical flow to align and aggregate features between adjacent frames. Although
051   pixel-level alignment has successfully enhanced features, it primarily focuses on single-pixel align-
052   ment and aggregation, neglecting the aggregation of features in adjacent pixel areas. Additionally,
053   some studies(Zhou et al., 2022) have attempted to aggregate features in neighboring regions of cor-
      responding pixels across frames using deformable attention mechanisms. However, they have not

083   The aggregation of temporal information from adjacent frames is often limited to a brief time win-
084   dow. To fully exploit the spatio-temporal information across an entire video, recent methods(Xiao
085   et al., 2023b) have focused on the roles of long-term and short-term frames in detection. Some ap-
086   proaches(He et al., 2021; Zhu et al., 2017; Bertasius et al., 2018b) use optical flow and deformable
087
      convolutions to extract and aggregate short-term frame features; however, these are challenging to
      train and costly. Other methods focus on the semantic information of long-term frames to establish
088
      long-term dependencies(Wu et al., 2019; Fujitake & Sugimoto, 2022), often involving randomly
089
      sampling frames from the video, which leads to unstable detection results and information loss.
090   Notably, some strategies(Chen et al., 2020) define long-term and short-term frames based on their
091   temporal distance, using attention mechanisms to aggregate features over time and space throughout
092   the video. However, whether aggregating long-term or short-term frames, such methods typically
093   employ a generic approach that fails to consider the varied information within frames concerning
094   small objects, resulting in suboptimal detection performance. Previous research has shown that con-
095   textual information is crucial for detecting small objects(Lim et al., 2021b; Xiao et al., 2023a). The
096   aggregation of video frames frequently lacks attention to global contextual information, particularly
097   for short-term frames, which tend to contain similar global context. In contrast, long-term frames,
098
      while rich in global context, often lack effective extraction of this information.
099   Despite the establishment of end-to-end networks by video object detectors utilizing temporal in-
100   formation, their performance in detecting small-sized targets is typically suboptimal(Zhou et al.,
101   2022). Video small object detectors often rely on complex post-processing steps, such as establish-
102   ing tubelet links among targets in video frames. Consequently, there is an urgent need for a more
103   efficient end-to-end video small object detector.
104   To address these challenges, this paper proposes an end-to-end long short-term transformer (LSTT)
105   network. Compared to previous methods, this network avoids complex post-processing steps while
106   fully accounting for the information discrepancies between different video frames. It efficiently ex-
107   tracts and aggregates information from both long-term and short-term frames, leveraging the trans-
      former architecture to enhance the detection of small objects. Given the visual similarity between

142   2   R ELATED W ORKS
143
144   Object detection from images has seen significant advancements with the introduction of several
145   leading detectors in recent years. Following the development of early detectors, video object de-
146   tection has been extensively studied as a more challenging task. Results indicate that leveraging
147
      information from other frames can significantly improve detection in the current frame. Therefore,
      temporal feature aggregation has gained increasing attention and is now a key component of video
148
      small object detection.
149
150   Temporal feature aggregation: Video object detection necessitates the propagation of temporal
151   information across frames, typically achieved by aggregating features from adjacent frames to en-
152   hance the detection frame’s feature representation. Early methods employed optical flow warping for
153
      feature aggregation(Zhu et al., 2017; Bertasius et al., 2018b). The DFF(Zhu et al., 2018) method uti-
      lizes optical flow networks to predict flow fields, aligning keyframe features with short-term frames
154
      to reduce redundant computations and accelerate the network. FGFA(Zhu et al., 2017) employs
155
      optical flow to align and fuse short-term frame features with the current frame, enhancing detec-
156   tion accuracy. MANet(Wang et al., 2018) builds upon FGFA by implementing pixel and instance-
157   level feature alignment and aggregation, and employs a motion pattern reasoning module for further
158   feature aggregation. Some methods expand the temporal window by integrating long-term frame
159   features with the detection frame to establish long-range dependencies. SELSA(Wu et al., 2019)
160   enhances feature aggregation by calculating semantic similarity between the current and long-term
161   frames. STMN(Xiao & Lee, 2018) employs recurrent computation units as spatiotemporal memory
      modules to convey semantic information across frames. MEGA(Chen et al., 2020) incorporates both


                                                       3
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


162
      global and local information from videos, utilizing long-term memory for feature enhancement. TF-
163   Blender(Cui et al., 2021), in contrast, employs a learnable network to predict aggregation weights,
164   unlike methods relying on cosine similarity.
165
166
      Most approaches are based on the classical two-stage Faster R-CNN network(Ren et al., 2015),
      which involves complex post-processing steps. Recently, Transformer-based object detection net-
167
      works have optimized the process, enabling end-to-end detection in single-frame images with com-
168
      petitive performance. However, these methods face temporal and spatial limitations when applied
169   to videos. TransVOD, TransVODLite, and TransVOD++(Zhou et al., 2022) incorporate spatiotem-
170   poral decoders and query generation across multiple video frames, balancing speed and accuracy.
171   PTSEFormer(Wang et al., 2022) employs a progressive strategy involving multi-scale feature extrac-
172   tion, focusing on temporal information and spatial transitions between frames. Sparse video object
173   detection employs an end-to-end trainable detector leveraging temporal information to propose re-
174   gion suggestions. Conversely, DAFA(Roh & Chung, 2022) emphasizes global over local temporal
175   features of videos. DEFA critiques FIFO memory inefficiencies, proposing a diversity-aware mem-
176   ory for instance-level feature retention in attention modules rather than frame-level memory. VS-
177
      TAM(Fujitake & Sugimoto, 2022) improves feature quality element-wise through sparse aggrega-
      tion before detecting object candidate regions, and utilizes external memory for long-term contextual
178
      information.
179
180   These methods propagate temporal information through feature aggregation, performing well on
181   regular objects but failing to account for intrinsic connections between video frames. They use
182   uniform aggregation strategies across the entire temporal window of a video, struggling to extract
183
      key information beneficial for small object detection, thus limiting performance improvements in
      this area.
184
185   Video small object detection: Research indicates that video analysis can enhance small object de-
186   tection performance by leveraging temporal continuity. For instance, the Motion R-CNN(Feng et al.,
187   2020a) and DogFight(Ashraf et al., 2021a) algorithms analyze dynamic changes in video sequences,
188   extracting crucial information about object movement and changes, thus enhancing the accuracy
189
      of small object detection. Algorithms like STDnet-ST++(Bosquet et al., 2021) and FANet(Cores
      et al., 2023) demonstrate how constructing and optimizing spatiotemporal trajectories improve small
190
      object recognition. These algorithms associate detection results across consecutive frames, build-
191
      ing trajectories and further refining them to eliminate noise and improve detection signals. The
192   LSTFE(Xiao et al., 2023b) method extends this concept by fusing long-term and short-term video
193   features, aggregating diverse information from different frames, significantly improving small object
194   recognition capabilities and demonstrating the effectiveness of long short-term structures. However,
195   these two-stage network-based video small object methods often require manual refinement of com-
196   plex post-processing steps, impeding the realization of end-to-end object detection.
197
      Although both short-term and long-term frame features have been used to enhance detection per-
198   formance, the information extracted from these features remains insufficient to establish stable con-
199   nections among small objects. LSTFE(Xiao et al., 2023b) indicated that distinguishing between
200   long-term and short-term frames in aggregation provides a clear advantage for small object features.
201   However, convolutional neural network-based feature extraction struggles to capture sufficient envi-
202   ronmental information crucial for small objects. Considering the global context modeling capability
203   of transformers, we applied a transformer-based detector to video small object detection for the
204   first time. Specifically, we considered both temporal and spatial information, performing sparse
205   sampling aggregation among short-term frames rather than simply aggregating weights between ad-
206
      jacent frames like TransVOD(Zhou et al., 2022). Moreover, unlike the aforementioned methods
      focusing on either short-term or long-term frame information, we defined video frames from the
207
      perspective of intrinsic connections, fully considering the different information between short-term
208
      and long-term frames, and aggregating object queries accordingly. By adopting these designs, our
209   approach is more conducive to detecting small objects in videos compared to previous works.

219   3.1   F RAMEWORK OVERVIEW
220
221   The architecture of LSTT, shown in Fig. 1, processes the current frame along with multiple short-
222   term and long-term frames sampled from the video. The feature extraction network first extracts
223   features from these frames. Short-term frames, adjacent to the current frame, share similar ap-
224   pearance information. The spatio-temporal alignment encoder uses convolutional offsets to align
225   features between the current and short-term frames. Additionally, a deformable attention mecha-
226   nism sparsely samples effective features from short-term frames, enhancing the pixel-level features
227   of the current frame.
228   For long-term frames, direct feature alignment or pixel-level fusion is impractical due to lack of
229   temporal continuity. Instead, global semantic information is extracted from long-term frames using
230   object queries and fused with short-term frame queries. Positional encoding represents the relative
231   positions of objects in short-term frames, while dynamic query generation learns relative motion
232   relationships, addressing the uneven distribution of small objects. Progressive random sampling with
233   exponential segmentation ensures coherent motion relationships in short-term frames and adequate
234   scene information from long-term frames.

356   4.1   E XPERIMENT S ETUP
357
358   Datasets:For a fair and convincing comparison, we chose to conduct extensive experiments on two
359   publicly available challenging datasets:
360
361          • VisDrone2019-VID dataset:This dataset includes 96 challenging HD video sequences from
362
               UAV perspectives, with 56 sequences for training (24,198 frames), 7 for validation (2,648
               frames), 16 for challenge testing (6,322 frames), and 17 for development testing (6,635
363
               frames). It covers 10 object categories: pedestrians, people, cars, vans, buses, trucks,
364
               motorcycles, bicycles, awning tricycles, and tricycles.
365
366          • UAVDT dataset:Designed for vehicle detection, this dataset comprises 50 videos captured
367            by drone-mounted cameras, totaling around 40,000 annotated frames. It includes 30 videos
368
               for training and 20 for testing, with resolutions of 1080x540 pixels. The test set contains
               375,884 objects across three categories: cars (361,055), trucks (7,595), and buses (7,234),
369
               with 20.3% of the objects being small.
370
371
      Implementation Details: Our model was trained and tested on four 24GB NVIDIA RTX 4090
372   GPUs, using ResNet101 and ResNet50 as backbones, and Deformable DETR as the base detector.
373   We employed ImageNet pre-trained weights, training for 10 epochs on images followed by 7 fine-
374   tuning epochs on videos. The input sizes were 1024x540 pixels for UAVDT and 1920x1080 pixels
375   for VisDrone2019-VID. Training used a learning rate of 0.0002 with AdamW optimizer, processing
376   one image per GPU batch. For reference frames, we sampled eight long-term and six short-term
377   frames relative to the current frame. The same input sizes were used during inference, and the batch
      size was kept at 1 to simplify the detection pipeline for Video Small Object Detection (VSOD).

      5   C ONCLUSION
530
531
      This paper proposes a long short-term transformer model that leverages temporal information to
532
      enhance features and improve small object detection in videos. A spatio-temporal alignment en-
533
      coder is introduced to align features between adjacent frames, eliminating spatial variations and
534   enhancing pixel-level features using deformable attention. To capture dense motion in short-term
535   frames and global context in long-term frames, a progressive random sampling strategy is used to
536   densely sample short-term frames and sparsely sample long-term frames. A long short-term module
537   then incrementally aggregates scene information from long-term frames and motion and appearance
538   details from short-term frames into the current frame, improving small object representation. Com-
539   parative and ablation studies on the Visdrones2019-VID and UAVDT datasets show that our method
      outperforms state-of-the-art approaches in detecting small objects in videos.
