009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            In recent years, numerous deep models have been proposed for the forecasting
012            of multivariate time series (MTS). Transformer-based models show significant
013            potential due to their ability to capture long-term dependencies. However, existing
014            models based on Multi-Layer Perceptrons (MLPs) or Transformers often suffer
015            from a lack of interpretability due to their large parameter sizes, which can be
016            problematic in many real-world applications. To address this issue, we propose
017            TimeKAN, a model based on Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks. The KAN model
018            offers two key advantages: (1) it achieves accuracy comparable to MLPs with
019
               significantly fewer parameters, and (2) its parameters can be symbolized, which
               makes it possible to re-interpret the meaning of the parameters. Additionally,
020
               instead of the usual attention mechanisms, we designed a Multi-Scale Patching
021
               (MSP) module for MTS that allows for a more flexible and simple multi-patching
022            and effectively extracts both temporal and cross-dimensional features. By leverag-
023            ing this strategy along with KAN, TimeKAN constructs a hierarchical structure
024            capable of utilizing information across different scales, leading to highly accurate
025            predictions. Extensive experiments on six real-world datasets demonstrate that
026            TimeKAN outperforms state-of-the-art (SOTA) methods in terms of predictive
027            performance. Furthermore, we interpret TimeKAN by visualizing its learning
028            process for extracting symbolized features, opening the black box, and revealing
029            meaningful patterns within the time series.

032   1    I NTRODUCTION
033
034   In many real-world scenarios, time series forecasting stands as a highly rewarding endeavor, offering
035   insights that approach the realm of prescience. Multivariate Time Series (MTS) forecasting, a subset
036   of this field, deals with predicting future values based on historical data where each observation
037   contains multiple variables, forming a "channel" dimension. The power of MTS forecasting lies in
038   its ability to enhance decision-making across various domains, including weather (Angryk et al.,
039
      2020), electricity (Wu et al., 2021), economic and finance (Loaiza-Maya & Smith, 2020), etc. Recent
      advancements in deep learning have significantly improved MTS forecasting performance, with
040
      methods such as multi-layer perceptrons (MLPs) (Borovykh et al., 2017), recurrent neural networks
041
      (RNNs) (Bai et al., 2018; Liu et al., 2020) and transformer-based models (Li et al., 2019).
042
043   Although deep learning-based models have made notable progress in time series forecasting, there are
044   still several challenges. Firstly, these methods may not fully capture the complex cross-time and cross-
045
      dimension dependencies present in MTS data. While attention mechanisms and Transformer-based
      models (Chen et al., 2022; Zeng et al., 2023) have improved the ability to capture long-term temporal
046
      dependency (cross-time dependency), they often come with increased computational complexity
047
      (Liu et al., 2022) and may still fall short in extracting cross-dimension dependencies present in MTS
048   (Zhang & Yan, 2023). Furthermore, the Universal Approximation Theorem (UAT), the mathematical
049   bedrock of most mainstream forecasting models, fails to provide guarantees on the required network
050   size (depth and width) for approximating a given continuous function with specific accuracy (Lu et al.,
051   2021). This limitation, coupled with UAT’s ability to only approximate rather than represent, poses a
052   significant hurdle for time series forecasting. Finally, the prediction mechanism of existing models
053   is black-box, resulting in a lack of interpretability. These nontransparent methods are suspected of
      being suitable for tasks that require a low tolerance for errors, such as medicine, law, and finance.

      In this paper, we propose TimeKAN, a novel model that leverages Kolmogorov–Arnold Networks
055   (KAN) (Liu et al., 2024) instead of traditional MLP architectures. KAN is a concise neural network
056   architecture inspired by the Kolmogorov-Arnold representation theorem (KART) (Kolmogonov,
057   1957; 1961). On one hand, KART demonstrates that a multivariate continuous function can be
058   expressed as a combination of a finite number of univariate continuous functions. This theorem links
059   network size to input shape within the context of representation. On the other hand, KAN provides a
060   pruning strategy that simplifies the trained network into a set of symbolic functions, allowing for the
061   analysis of specific modules’ mechanisms and greatly enhancing interpretability.
062   Besides using the KAN architecture, TimeKAN is designed to capture multi-period dependencies
063   between multiple variates through a multi-patching strategy. Unlike single-patching (Zhong et al.,
064   2023) or other attention mechanisms, multi-patching divides the time series data into multi-period
065   patches, enabling the model to focus on a broader spectrum of temporal patterns (e.g. trend and
066   periodicity) in different periods of the data. Moreover, we demonstrate TimeKAN’s transparency by
067   symbolizing and visualizing learned meaningful temporal features, as shown in Figure 1, throughout
068   the training process. This transparency is crucial for a wide range of applications where understanding
069
      the model’s decision-making process is as important as the accuracy of its predictions. Our main
      contributions are as follows:
070
071          • We introduce TimeKAN, a KAN-based model that outperforms existing methods, achieving
072            state-of-the-art (SOTA) results on several benchmark datasets. By utilizing kernel-based
073            attention, TimeKAN effectively captures the nonlinear relationships in multivariate time
074            series data.
075          • We demonstrate that multi-patching significantly improves the model’s ability to extract
076            relevant features compared to single-patching or traditional attention mechanisms. Multi-
077            patching allows the model to concurrently analyze multiple segments of the data, enhancing
078            its ability to capture both local and global patterns.
079          • We employ symbolization techniques to represent the learned features and visualize the train-
080            ing process, addressing the interpretability challenge. This transparency aids in validating
081            the model’s performance and understanding the features that contribute to its predictions.

086   2    R ELATED W ORKS
087
088   Multivariate Time Series Forecasting. Time series forecasting aims to predict future observations
089   based on historical data. Traditional statistical modeling methods, such as ARIMA (Box & Jenkins,
090   1968) exponential smoothing (Hyndman et al., 2008) and its variants (Li et al., 2022), have been
091   reliable workhorses for this task. In the realm of deep learning, various architectures have been
092   employed to model different dependencies in time series data. Graph Neural Networks (GNNs)
093
      capture spatial dependencies in correlated time series (Jin et al., 2024; Zhao et al., 2023; Miao et al.,
      2024), while Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs) are adept at modeling temporal dependencies (Kieu
094
      et al., 2022). Models like DeepAR (Rangapuram et al., 2018) combine RNNs with autoregressive
095
      methods for short-term forecasting. TimesNet (Wu et al., 2023) transforms one-dimensional time
096   series into two-dimensional representations to capture multi-period features using convolution and
097   shows good performance on four datasets.
098
099
      Recently, Transformer models have garnered significant attention in multivariate time series forecast-
      ing (Wu et al., 2020; Zerveas et al., 2021; Wen et al., 2022). Informer (Zhou et al., 2021) introduces
100
      prob-sparse self-attention to select important keys, and Triformer (Cirstea et al., 2022) employs a
101
      triangular architecture to reduce complexity. Autoformer (Wu et al., 2021) replaces self-attention
102   with auto-correlation mechanisms to model temporal dynamics, while FEDformer (Zhou et al., 2022)
103   utilizes Fourier transformations from a frequency perspective. However, some studies have raised
104   concerns about the effectiveness of Transformers in this domain, as simple linear models have proven
105   to be effective or even outperform previous Transformer-based models Li et al. (2022); Zeng et al.
106   (2023). Nonetheless, PatchTST (Nie et al., 2022) enhances performance by employing patching and
107   channel independence within Transformers, indicating that the Transformer architecture still holds
      potential with proper adaptations in time series forecasting.

147   studies demonstrate that KAN-based models effectively bridge the gap between predictive power and
148   interpretability in time series forecasting. However, their proposed KAN-based single-layer model is
149
      only a preliminary attempt to introduce KAN into time series forecasting. We hope to further improve
      the performance and interpretability of the KAN-based model by combining the Multi-Scale property
150
      of time series.

      3.6      KOLMOGOROV-A RNOLD N ETWORKS (KAN)
355
356   At the core of each MSP module lies the Kolmogorov-Arnold Network (KAN), a theoretical framework
357   that facilitates the approximation of multivariate continuous functions through a combination of
358   univariate and bivariate function compositions (Kolmogonov, 1957; 1961). By integrating KAN
359   structures within the MSP modules, TimeKAN effectively captures complex temporal dependencies
360   across different scales. A symbolic representation of the training results of any KAN layer inside the
361
      patch encoder is visible at a certain patching scale by taking out the TimeKAN block of any layer,
      which means the time series at that scale.
362
363   As shown in Figure 1, we can get lnpYi q “ cospx2 q ` cospx3 q ` sinpx5 q by taking the natural
364   logarithm of both side, which is equivalent to the logarithmic rate of return in finance. This equation
365   reveals that the yield Yi is influenced by the periodic functions cospx2 q, cospx3 q, and sinpx5 q. The
366   presence of cosine and sine functions indicates that there are periodic features within the return
367
      series. These components capture cyclical patterns or oscillations over the variables x2 , x3 , and
      x5 , suggesting that the underlying process has inherent periodicity. This insight is crucial for
368
      understanding and interpreting the dynamics and regularities in the series, potentially aiding in
369
      forecasting or pattern recognition tasks.

394   4.1   C OMPARED M ODELS
395
396   To demonstrate the superiority of our proposed method, we conducted experiments across 27 bench-
397   marks on the aforementioned datasets, comparing our TimeKAN against selected baselines. These
398   baselines include models based on recent MLP and Transformer architectures, as shown below.
399   Regarding Transformer-based model, Mamba (Gu & Dao, 2023) is a forecasting model that achieves
400   promising prediction accuracy while maintaining low computational complexity. TimesNet (Wu et al.,
401   2023) proposes a framework based on time-domain two-dimensional variation modeling. PatchTST
402   (Nie et al., 2022) divides time series into multiple “patches” and utilizes Transformer to capture the
403   temporal patterns in these patches. iTransformer (Liu et al., 2023) focuses on adaptive modeling of
404   time series data by introducing learnable time encoding and dynamic attention mechanisms.
405   As for the Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP) based model, DLinear (Zeng et al., 2023) decomposes the
406   time series into trend and seasonal components using moving averages and models each component
407   using linear models. LightTS (Zhang et al., 2022): introduce a lightweight time series forecasting
408   model that enhances predictive capability through auxiliary sequences and feature selection mecha-
409   nisms. MSD-Mixer (Zhong et al., 2023) utilizes a multi-scale distribution mixing method through a
410   fully connected neural network architecture to capture multi-scale features in time series data.

503   5   C ONCLUSION AND F UTURE W ORK
504
505   In this paper, we introduced TimeKAN, a novel approach for multivariate time series forecasting
506   that combines the strengths of Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks (KAN) with a Multi-Scale Patching
507   (MSP) module. Our TimeKAN addresses the critical challenges of interpretability and parameter
508   efficiency while maintaining high predictive accuracy. By leveraging KAN’s ability to symbolize
509   parameters and our innovative MSP module, TimeKAN effectively captures both temporal and
510   cross-dimensional features across various scales. Extensive experiments conducted on six real-world
511   datasets demonstrate that TimeKAN consistently outperforms state-of-the-art models, achieving
512   superior performance in long-term forecasting tasks. Additionally, our TimeKAN provides insights
513
      into its decision-making process through symbolic feature extraction, enhancing transparency and
      interpretability.
514
515   Future work will focus on further refining the MSP module to handle even more complex temporal
516   patterns and exploring the application of TimeKAN in other domains that require high interpretability,
517   such as healthcare and finance. Moreover, enhancing training speed via parallel processing, effective
518   batch management, and refined spline calculations will be essential for KAN’s practical application
519
      in real-time scenarios.

784   To further enhance the interpretability and transparency of our TimeKAN, we have developed an

786   of training, to choose whether to apply symbolic processing and to select parameters such as the
787   TimeKAN block number to be symbolized, the index of the MSP block within each block, the type
788
      of Encoder/Decoder module, the number of KANLayers, and the number of output nodes.
789   This interface is designed to provide users with "a sharp scalpel," enabling them to conveniently and
790   efficiently dissect the internal mechanisms of the model. It allows users to observe how the model
791   "thinks, decides, and acts," thereby mitigating, to some extent, the limitations of previous black-box
792   neural networks in tasks that require interpretability.
