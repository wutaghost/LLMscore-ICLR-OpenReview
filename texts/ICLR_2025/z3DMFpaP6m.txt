009                                                 A BSTRACT
010
011            Large language models (LLMs) are widely recognized for their exceptional capacity
012            to capture semantic meaning. Yet, there remains no established metric to quantify
013            this capability. In this work, we introduce a quantitative metric, Information
014            Emergence (IE), designed to measure LLMs’ ability to extract semantics from
015            input tokens. We formalize “semantics” as the meaningful information abstracted
016            from a sequence of tokens and, leveraging information theory, quantify this through
017            comparing the reduction in entropy observed for a sequence of tokens (macro-level)
018
               and individual tokens (micro-level). To achieve this, we design a light-weight
               estimator to compute the mutual information at both micro and macro levels for
019
               each transformer layer, which is agnostic to different tasks and language model
020
               architectures. We apply IE in both synthetic in-context learning (ICL) scenarios
021            and natural sentence contexts. Experiments show a high-level informativeness
022            of our metric reflected in semantic faithfulness, sensitivity and connection with
023            emergence. In addition, we highlight some interesting findings: 1) IE explains why
024            ICL offers clearer semantics and benefits compared to natural text through changes
025            in entropy. 2) We could associate certain hallucination phenomenon with increased
026            variance in IE. 3) IE can effectively differentiate between human-written and LLM-
027            generated text, proving especially useful for extremely large and closed-source
028            language models. Our codes are available at: https://anonymous.4open.
029
               science/r/Emergence/.

      1    I NTRODUCTION
033
034   One of the most elusive and captivating attributes of large language models (LLMs) is their ability
035
      to learn semantics from inputs across diverse domains(Chen, 2023; Chang et al., 2024; Minaee
      et al., 2024), a feature that owes much to a cross-pollination of unsupervised training and next-
036
      token prediction (NTP) mechanisms. It has stimulated numerous significant research directions,
037
      such as in-context learning (ICL) (Min et al., 2022a;b; Wies et al., 2024; Ye et al., 2023; Kossen
038   et al., 2023; Swaminathan et al., 2024), emergence capabilities (Wei et al., 2022; Schaeffer et al.,
039   2023; Srivastava et al., 2023; Lu et al., 2023; Yu & Dong, 2022; Liu et al., 2024), and hallucination
040   investigations (Rawte et al., 2023; Ji et al., 2023; Zhang et al., 2023; Deemter, 2024).
041
      However, the capability of LLMs to capture semantics from texts is challenging to quantify and
042
      thereby, to evaluate. Numerous existing tasks indirectly reflect similar capabilities through evaluating
043
      LLMs’ performances (e.g., accuracy) on a specific task, such as “instruction following” (Zeng et al.,
044   2023), “searching” (Sun et al., 2023), and “reasoning” (Yang et al., 2024). Nevertheless, these
045   evaluation methods rely on manually curating datasets and tasks tailoring different aspects, resulting
046   in time-consuming and domain-specific findings. In addition, these evaluations typically focus on
047   coarse-grained text, not providing interpretations for the behavior of finer-grained tokens. Lastly,
048   existing evaluation metrics which vary across different tasks can lead to varied performances, and
049   even contradicting conclusions (Schaeffer et al., 2023).
050   In response to the above limitations, we propose a task-agnostic and closed-form metric, which we
051   refer to as Information Emergence (IE)1 , designed to reflect and deterministically quantify the
052

054
      ability of LLMs to extract meaningful semantics from input tokens. To begin with, we construct
055   a mathematical formalism capable of modeling semantics. In essence, semantics naturally emerge
056   as a meaningfully organized ensemble of tokens (Hilpert & Saavedra, 2020; Apidianaki, 2023).
057   Consequently, tokens are considered microscopic (micro) observations with sophisticated patterns
058   in a sentence, whereas semantics represent macroscopic (macro) observations emerging with more
059   predictable behaviors. Inspired by information theory, we formalize the model’s proficiency in
060   semantic understanding, i.e., information emergence, as the difference of the entropy reduction
061   between micro-level and macro-level. In another word, a better model proficient in deriving
062   semantics from tokens, in comparison to other models, ought to render a higher entropy
063
      reduction for a global sequence than for a single token.
064   To compute IE in transformer models, as discussed earlier, we need to mathematically measure
065   entropy reduction for both micro and macro levels. Given the auto-regressive nature of the NTP
066   mechanism, at any layer l in transformer, the most micro-level transition can be naturally framed as
067   the probability ph0l |h0l−1 for an isolated token h0l , whereas the macro-level transition can be formulated
068   as phTl |h0l−1 ,h1l−1 ,...,hTl−1 across T tokens. We resort to the mutual information between successive
069   transformer layers and adopt a practically effective estimation algorithm motivated by (Belghazi et al.,
070   2018) which is suitable for high-dimensional continuous representations. Therefore, we can measure
071   the IE value for any token at any transformer layer, reflecting the strength of the LLM’s capability in
072   extracting semantics from the historical context.
073   We devise a suite of comprehensive experiments encompassing two different scenarios. In the
074   first scenario, we curate a group of synthetic datasets under the ICL setting with different context
075   domains. In the second scenario, we collect two wild datasets consisting of real-world natural
076   language questions/answers. Under both scenarios, we experiment with different LMs including
077   GPT-2 (Radford et al., 2019), GEMMA (Team et al., 2024), and OpenLlama (Computer, 2023).
078   In alignment with our hypothesis, we show that IE offers a high-level informativeness through
079   semantic faithfulness and sensitivity - the richer the semantics, the higher the IE. Additionally, a set
080
      of experiments conducted across model sizes have indicated the potential association between IE and
      Emergence. Furthermore, we obtain 3 interesting findings:
081
082         1. IE increases token-by-token in natural texts, whereas, in ICL-style texts, IE increases only
083            when a new demonstration appears.
084
085
            2. There is a strong correlation between specific hallucination phenomenons and a high variance
               in IE scores.
086
087         3. Distinctive patterns in IE have been observed between human-written and LLM-generated
088            texts, revealing IE’s potential in automatically recognising LLM generations.
089
      Overall, the main contributions could be summarized below:
090
091
             • We introduce IE, a novel, reasonably validated, and task-agnostic metric to deterministically
092            quantify the semantic understanding capability of LLMs.
093
094
             • We introduce a light-weight implementation method for evaluating IE, which can be applied
               to extremely large and closed-source LMs like GPT-4 (Achiam et al., 2023).
095
096          • Empirical evidence demonstrates that IE can uncover previously unknown and essential
097            patterns in areas such as ICL, Emergence, and hallucination.

      2.1   I NFORMATION E MERGENCE AND E MERGENCE
102
103   Emergence is defined as a capability that does not exist in smaller models but appears in larger
104   ones (Srivastava et al., 2023; Lu et al., 2023; Yu & Dong, 2022; Liu et al., 2024). Most commonly,
105   as the model size increases, the performance on many tasks rapidly improves. Several studies have
106   posited explanations for Emergence: the qualitative change resulting from quantitative behavior (Wei
107   et al., 2022), the random combination of linguistic skills (Arora & Goyal, 2022), and principles that
      can be extrapolated from smaller models (Schaeffer et al., 2023).

128
      The prevalent body of research extensively measures the capabilities of LLMs across various tasks
129   by employing substantial benchmark datasets (Srivastava et al., 2023; Wang et al., 2024; Zhu et al.,

      3.3     I MPLEMENTATION
271
272   Our algorithm requires that the number of samples is sufficiently large (over 300k) to provide a good
273   estimate of the mutual information. Meanwhile, the length of each sequence within a dataset should
274   be kept the same to facilitate position-wise observation and meaningful computation. Due to resource
275   constraints, our comparative analysis is limited to GPT2-large (812M), GPT2-XL (1.61B), GEMMA
276   (2.51B), and OpenLlama (3B) models. Fortunately, this parameter range is sufficient to observe
277   variations and regularities of IE. For those LLMs with extremely large size or closed resource (e.g.,
278
      GPT-4, Claude3, etc.), we design another efficient strategy that enables their IE evaluations as shown
      in Section 5.3. All computational experiments can be conducted on one NVIDIA GeForce RTX 3090
279
      GPU4 . The estimator f in Eq. 4 is a model of a 10-layer neural network comprising linear layers
280
      and leaky ReLU activation functions, where each linear layer’s output dimension was half of its
281   input dimension. We set the batch size to 300,000 to ensure the stability of the sampling distribution,
282   thereby guaranteeing robust results. Additionally, the learning rate was initially set at 1e-4 and was
283   polynomially decayed to 1e-8 within 10k epochs. We examine the IE value of LLMs under two
284   distinct settings: ICL with few-shot examples and natural sentences without demonstrations.

      6     L IMITATIONS
517
518   Position-wise Token: Given that mutual information intrinsically demands the distribution of two
519   tokens to be valid, we require every token’s position to hold a specific meaning, such as representing
520   the beginning or end of a sentence, the subject, predicate, and so forth. Hence, applying our estimator
521
      directly to existing tasks may result in a lack of interpretability as the token lengths and positions in
      the samples vary significantly.
522
523   Sample Amount: To ensure the accuracy of joint and marginal distributions of high-dimensional
524   continuous representations, a tremendous number of samples is essential. We are attempting more
525   mechanistic alternative methods, hoping to reduce sample size requirements in the future.
526   More Models and Tokens: It is evident that our experiments lack larger-sized models and analysis
527   of long-length texts, especially for emergence and hallucination analysis. Given more computational
528   resources, we would continue to expand these experiments.

531   7     C ONCLUSION
532
533
      In this paper, we mathematically model the entropy of tokens and propose a quantitative metric,
534   Information Emergence (IE), representing the LLM’s ability to obtain semantics from tokens. Under
535   the proposed low-resource estimator, we corroborate that IE possesses semantic faithfulness and
536   sensitivity not found in other metrics. under the settings of ICL and natural sentences, We conducted
537   extensive experiments explaining why ICL provides clearer semantics than natural text, as well as the
538   intrinsic relationship between IE and hallucinations. Simultaneously, we discovered that IE can be
539   utilized to distinguish whether the source of the text originates from humans or LLMs, particularly a
      simple and feasible strategy for those of significantly large LMs.

705   Algorithm 1 Estimating Mutual Information
706   Require: : A set of input tokens U ∈ RS∗T , where S denotes the total number of samples and T
707     represents the number of token in each sample. A LLM fτ with L layer of blocks and hidden state
708     dimension D. A estimator fθ .
709   Ensure: : Mutual Information M ∈ RL∗T .
710     procedure 1 Extracting Representation H ∈ RS∗L∗T ∗D from LLM
711     Initialization: H = ∅
712     for each sample s in S do
713
           H ← H + fτ (Us )
        end for
714
        procedure 2 Estimating Mutual Information M
715
        Initialization: M = ∅, l = 0, t = 0,
716     while l < L and t < T do
717        Ix ← Hl,t (Hl,t ∈ RS∗D )
718        Iy ← Hl+1,t (Hl+1,t ∈ RS∗D )
719        Shuffle Hl+1,t in the dimension S
720        Iy ∗ ← Hl+1,t (Hl+1,t ∈ RS∗D )
721        input1 ← Ix ||Iy
722        input2 ← Ix ||Iy ∗
723        Initialization: Mtmp = 0
724        for Epoch i < 10k do
725           output1 ← fθ (input1)
726
              output2 ← fθ (input2)
                      PS                       PS
727           L = S1 s=1 (output1) − log( S1 s=1 (output2)
728
              L backpropagation
              if Mtmp == 0 then
729
                 Mtmp ← −log2e L
730
              else if Mtmp ̸= 0 then
731              if Mtmp < −log2e L then
732                 Mtmp ← −log2e L
733              end if
734           end if
735        end for
736        l ← l + 1, t ← t + 1
737        Ml,t ← Mtmp
738     end while
739
        return M
740
741   Algorithm 1 is employed to elucidate the entire process of estimating mutual information. Sim-
742   plified, the method involves two primary steps: Step 1 involves extracting representative samples
743   from a LLM, and Step 2 entails estimating the mutual information between these representation
744   samples. We denote the time required to estimate a pair of representations (Hl,t and Hl+1,t ) as
745   α. Consequently, the time complexity for estimating representations from an LLM for a sequence
746
      ST = token1, token2, ,̇tokenT − 1 across L block layers is denoted as O(LT α).
747   In practical implementations, α approximately costs 40 minutes on one 3090 GPU, whereas significant
748   improvements on a 4090 GPU reduce this time to about 20 minutes.
