009                                              A BSTRACT
010
011            Recent advances in text-to-3D generation have made significant progress. In par-
012            ticular, with the pretrained diffusion models, existing methods predominantly use
013            Score Distillation Sampling (SDS) to train 3D models such as Neural Radiance
014            Fields (NeRF) and 3D Gaussian Splatting (3D GS). However, a hurdle is that they
015            often encounter difficulties with over-smoothing textures and over-saturating col-
016            ors. The rectified flow model – which utilizes a simple ordinary differential equa-
017            tion (ODE) to represent a straight trajectory – shows promise as an alternative
018
               prior to text-to-3D generation. It learns a time-independent vector field, thereby
               reducing the ambiguity in 3D model update gradients that are calculated using
019
               time-dependent scores in the SDS framework. In light of this, we first develop
020
               a mathematical analysis to seamlessly integrate SDS with rectified flow model,
021            paving the way for our initial framework known as Vector Field Distillation Sam-
022            pling (VFDS). However, empirical findings indicate that VFDS results in over-
023            smoothing outcomes. Therefore, we analyze the grounding reasons for such a
024            failure from the perspective of ODE trajectories. On top, we propose a novel
025            framework, named FlowDreamer, which yields high-fidelity results with richer
026            textual details and faster convergence. The key insight is to leverage the coupling
027            and reversible properties of the rectified flow model to search for the correspond-
028            ing noise, rather than using randomly sampled noise as in VFDS. Accordingly,
029
               we introduce a novel Unique Couple Matching (UCM) loss, which guides the 3D
               model to optimize along the same trajectory. Our FlowDreamer is superior in its
030
               flexibility to be applied to both NeRF and 3D GS. Moreover, we highlight the
031
               intriguing open questions, such as initialization challenges in NeRF and sampling
032            techniques, to benefit the research community.

034   1   I NTRODUCTION
035
036   3D generation enjoys broad applications in diverse fields, such as the Metaverse, games, education,
037   architecture design, and films, and has attracted significant research interest recently (Xie et al.,
038   2024; Wang et al., 2024b; Tang et al., 2024; Poole et al., 2022; Chen et al., 2023a; Lin et al., 2023;
039   Jain et al., 2022; Tang et al., 2023; Jiang et al., 2024). Text-to-3D generation – which generates 3D
040   contents from user-input text – has emerged as one of the promising 3D generation paradigms due
041   to its ease of use (Wang et al., 2023; Yi et al., 2023; Wang et al., 2024a; Nichol et al., 2022; Jun &
042   Nichol, 2023).
043   Recently, with the advances of text-to-2D image synthesis techniques based on the diffusion mod-
044   els, text-to-3D generation also undergoes a surge of research interest. A seminal work, Dream-
045   Fusion (Poole et al., 2022) sets a cornerstone by proposing Score Distillation Sampling (SDS) to
046   address this issue by leveraging pretrained text-to-image diffusion model, to train Neural Radiance
047   Fields (NeRF) (Mildenhall et al., 2021). It has been rapidly evolved to 3D Gaussian Splatting (3D
048   GS) (Kerbl et al., 2023; Tang et al., 2023; Yi et al., 2023) for faster training and rendering.
049   Despite the success, existing works (Lin et al., 2023; Poole et al., 2022; Zhu et al., 2023) unveil
050   that SDS suffers from issues such as over-smoothing textures and over-saturating colors. For this
051   reason, some attempts, e.g.,Wang et al. (2024a); Liang et al. (2023); Wu et al. (2024) improve
052   SDS from different perspectives. For example, ProlificDreamer (Wang et al., 2024a) introduces
053   variational score distillation (VSD), which models 3D parameters as random variables to distill 3D
      assets. However, it requires significantly more time to optimize. Consistent3D (Wu et al., 2024)

188   Diffusion and Flow based models. Recent advances in text-to-image generation have witnessed
189   a significant progress. Diffusion models define a process, which progressively converts a distribu-
190   tion of training data to pure Gaussian noise. By learning the reverse process, one can sample data
191   following the distribution. DDPM (Ho et al., 2020) employs a Markov chain to achieve the above
192
      process, while DDIM (Song et al., 2020a) proposes to reduce the iteration step by maintaining the

193

194

      3.3   F LOW D REAMER FOR N E RF AND 3D GS
325
326   Based on the UCM loss, we propose a novel FlowDreamer framework. It yields high-fidelity results
327   with richer textual details. FlowDreamer can be applied to two types of 3D models: 3D Gaussian
328   splatting (Kerbl et al., 2023) and NeRF (Mildenhall et al., 2021).
329
330
      Application to 3D Gaussian Splatting. We use points generated by the text-to-3D genera-
      tor (Nichol et al., 2022) as parameter initialization. Then, we directly train the 3D GS model using
331
      our UCM loss. Our FlowDreamer yields high-fidelity results with richer textual details (see Fig-
332
      ure 1).
333
334   Application to NeRF and the initialization issue. For NeRF, however, a direct application does
335   not perform well. We call this the initialization issue of NeRF, as we will explain the reasons below.
336   When searching the noise ϵ for a given image x, i.e., push-backward process, we use the rectified
337   flow model vϕ . It defines a mapping from data distribution π0 to noise distribution π1 , where π0 is
338   the distribution of its pre-training datasets. The effectiveness of the push-backward process depends
339
      on that the input distribution for vϕ should be aligned with or at least approximate to π0 or π1 .
      Otherwise, the input lies in an undefined area for vϕ hence the output is unreasonable.
340
341   When training NeRF from scratch, it can hardly generate reasonable images based on its randomly
342   initialized parameters. Therefore, the input distribution (denoted as π nf ) is far from π0 and π1 ,
343   causing the rectified flow model vϕ difficult to estimate the gradient of the ODE trajectory. To solve
344   this issue, we temporarily use the naive VFDS training to warm up as a remedy. We view this
345
      initialization issue as an open question and advocate further investigations.
346   As for 3D GS models, the issue does not exist. The example can be found in the supplementary
347   material, when the prompt “A English cottage with stone walls” is provided, the NeRF Initialization
348   is simply a gray image, while the output of the 3D GS model (initialized by Point-E (Nichol et al.,
349   2022)) resembles a cottage. This indicates the initial distribution of the 3D Gaussian splatting model
350   is more approximate to π0 . The result of push-backward process is also more effective, as they are
351
      closer to the gradient from images output by the trained model.

527   5        C ONCLUSION
528
529   In this paper, we explored a new direction by using the rectified flow model as an alternative prior
530   to text-to-3D generation. We developed a mathematical analysis to adapt SDS to rectified flow
531   model, resulting in the initial VFDS framework. However, VFDS still leads to over-smoothing. We
532   analyzed this issue from the perspective of ODE trajectories and proposed FlowDreamer, a text-to-
533   3D framework with a new UCM loss. Extensive experiments showed that FlowDreamer achieves
534   high-fidelity results with richer details and faster convergence in both NeRF and 3D GS settings. We
535
      also highlighted open questions, such as initialization issues for NeRF and noise search sampling.
      Limitation. The Jabus problem still exists; simply adding words like ‘front view,’ ‘back view,’ and
536
      ‘side view’ to the prompt is insufficient for supervising the generation view. Although we attempt to
537
      mitigate this issue using Perpneg (Armandpour et al., 2023), it still occasionally occurs. We consider
538   solving the Jabus problem thoroughly as a focus for the future work.
