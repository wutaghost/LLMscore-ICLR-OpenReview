009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            State Space Models (SSM), such as Mamba, have shown strong representation
012            ability in modeling long-range dependency with linear complexity, achieving suc-
013            cessful applications from high-level to low-level vision tasks. However, SSM’s
014            sequential nature necessitates multiple scans in different directions to compensate
015            for the loss of spatial dependency when unfolding the image into a 1D sequence.
016            This multi-direction scanning strategy significantly increases the computation over-
017            head and is unbearable for high-resolution image processing. To address this
018
               problem, we propose a novel Hierarchical Mamba network, namely, Hi-Mamba,
               for image super-resolution (SR). Hi-Mamba consists of two key designs: (1) The
019
               Hierarchical Mamba Block (HMB) assembled by a Local SSM (L-SSM) and a
020
               Region SSM (R-SSM) both with the single-direction scanning, aggregates multi-
021            scale representations to enhance the context modeling ability. (2) The Direction
022            Alternation Hierarchical Mamba Group (DA-HMG) allocates the isomeric single-
023            direction scanning into cascading HMBs to enrich the spatial relationship modeling.
024            Extensive experiments demonstrate the superiority of Hi-Mamba across five bench-
025            mark datasets for efficient SR. For example, Hi-Mamba achieves a significant
026            PSNR improvement of 0.29 dB on Manga109 for ×3 SR, compared to the strong
027            lightweight MambaIR.

030   1   I NTRODUCTION
031
032   Single Image Super-Resolution Yang et al. (2019); He et al. (2019); Zhang et al. (2018); Chen
033
      et al. (2022); Zhang et al. (2021) (SISR) aims to restore an authentic high-resolution (HR) image
      from a single degraded low-resolution (LR) one, which benefits plentiful downstream applications
034
      such as magnetic resonance imaging (MRI), mobile device photography, and video surveillance.
035
      Various studies have proposed Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) Ahn et al. (2018); Li et al.
036   (2021b); Zhang et al. (2019b) to learn a mapping from LR inputs to HR outputs. Despite their efficacy
037   and remarkable advances in the past, CNN-based SR models are reaching their upper-performance
038   limits even with continuously increasing model sizes, due to CNNs’ limited capability on long-range
039   dependency modeling.
040
      Transformer-based SR methods Liang et al. (2021); Chen et al. (2023b;a); Ray et al. (2024); Zhang
041
      et al. (2024) introduce self-attention mechanisms with extraordinary long-range modeling capabilities
042   to remarkably improve SR performance, while at the cost of quadratic computational complexity.
043   Numerous subsequent works have been proposed to make the vanilla Transformers more efficient
044   and powerful via shifted window attention Liang et al. (2021); Zhang et al. (2022b), transposed
045   attentions Zamir et al. (2022); Li et al. (2023b) and anchored stripe self-attention Li et al. (2023c),
046   etc. However, these studies are difficult to relieve the quadratic complexity of attention mechanisms
047   at inference in practice.
048   Recently, Mamba Gu & Dao (2024) architecture constructed on Structured State Space Models (S4)
049   has emerged as a promising technique due to its high potential in long-sequence modeling with linear
050   complexity. As S4 was originally proposed in the field of natural language processing (NLP) Gu et al.
051   (2021a); Gu & Dao (2024), several succeeding works have introduced S4 into vision recognition
052   tasks Liu et al. (2024); Zhu et al. (2024) and image processing tasks Shi et al. (2024), demonstrating
053   impressive results. For example, Vision Mamba Zhu et al. (2024) was proposed for image recognition
      tasks, manifesting that Vision Mamba can overcome the computation & memory constraints on

      Previous vision Mamba architectures typically employ a multi-direction scanning strategy to compen-
065
      sate for the loss of spatial dependencies when unfolding the image into a 1D sequence. Unfortunately,
066
      the repetitive multiple-sequence scanning overshadows the essential linear computational complexity
067   of SSMs primarily designed with single-sequence scanning to model 1D sequential relationships.
068   It significantly increases the computation overhead and is unacceptable for high-resolution image
069   processing tasks. As shown in Tab. 1, the four-sequence scanning approach effectively improves
070   the performance by 0.10 dB and 0.12 dB on Urban100 and Manga109, respectively. However, this
071   enhancement comes at a significant computational cost, increasing FLOPs by 95.2% and parameters
072   by 37.8% compared to the single-sequence scanning approach in MambaIR.
073   To address this problem, we propose a novel Hierarchical Mamba architecture, termed Hi-Mamba,
074   for image super-resolution (SR). We first propose the hierarchical Mamba block (HMB) which is
075   constructed by a local SSM and a region SSM with single-direction scanning to conduct multi-
076   scale data-dependent visual context modeling. Furthermore, we propose the direction alternation
077   hierarchical Mamba group (DA-HMG) that allocates the isomeric single-direction scanning into
078   cascaded HMBs to enrich the spatial relationship modeling. Our DA-HMG improves the recon-
079   struction performance with no extra FLOPs or parameter increases. In addition, we propose that the
080   gate feed-forward network (G-FFN) introduce additional non-linear information through a simple
081
      gate mechanism in the feed-forward network. We verify the effectiveness of Hi-Mamba on several
      classical SR benchmarks with three released versions, which makes fair comparisons with various
082
      SR models with different capacities.
083
084   We summarize our main contributions as follows:
085
               • We propose Hi-Mamba for efficient SR, incorporating hierarchical Mamba block (HMB),
086
                 specifically the Local-SSM and the Region-SSM for multi-scale data-dependent visual
087
                 context modeling.
088
               • The direction alternation hierarchical Mamba group (DA-HMG) is simple yet effective in
089
                 enriching the spatial relationship modeling, which allocates the isometric single-direction
090
                 scanning into cascaded HMBs to improve performance without incurring extra computation
091              and memory costs.
092
               • Extensive experiments demonstrate the superiority of the proposed Hi-Mamba. For example,
093
                 our Hi-Mamba achieves significant PSNR gains of 0.37dB on Urban100 for ×3 SR compared
094
                 to SRFormer Zhou et al. (2023).

      2.2    M AMBA AND A PPLICATIONS FOR S UPER -R ESOLUTION
138
139   State space models (SSM) Gu et al. (2021a;b); Smith et al. (2022), originating from classical control
140   theory Kalman (1960), are rising as novel backbones in Deep Learning. Successful applications
141   of SSM include Mamba Gu & Dao (2024), Vim Zhu et al. (2024), and VMamba Liu et al. (2024),
142   which are all tailored toward high-level image understanding tasks. Overall, the implementations
143   of SSM in low-level vision tasks remain few. MambaIR Guo et al. (2024) first introduced the
144   Mamba architecture to image super-resolution tasks, achieving impressive image restoration results.
145
      MMA Cheng et al. (2024) introduced Vision Mamba (ViM) Zhu et al. (2024) and combined it
      with convolutional structures to activate a wider pixel area, thereby enhancing SR performance.
146
      DVMSR Lei et al. (2024) was the first to attempt distilling the Mamba architecture to achieve
147
      an ultra-lightweight SR Mamba model. FMSR Xiao et al. (2024) introduced Mamba for remote
148   sensing image super-resolution, which uses frequency information to assist the Mamba architecture,
149   achieving performance surpassing Transformer methods. However, these methods all use multi-
150   sequence scanning strategies to model the image spatial relationships, which significantly increases
151   computational costs compared to the single-sequence scanning of vanilla Mamba. Different from
152   these methods, our Hi-Mamba uses only single-sequence scanning and proposes HMB to compensate
153   for SSM’s inadequacy in modeling 2D-pixel relationships. Additionally, the DA-HMG is proposed to
154   enrich spatial relationship modeling by alternatively changing the single-sequence scanning direction
155   in HMB without additional computational costs.

161   SSM can be viewed as a Linear Time-Invariant (LTI) system, which maps the input one-dimensional
      function or sequence x(t) ∈ R to the output response y(t) ∈ R through a hidden state h(t) ∈ RN .

      They are typically represented as linear ordinary differential equations:
163
164                               h′ (t) = Ah(t) + Bx(t),           y(t) = Ch(t) + Dx(t),                   (1)

      scenarios. In particular, the timescale parameter ∆ is used to convert the continuous parameters A
169
      and B into discrete ones A and B. The widely used discretization method adheres to the Zero-Order
170
      Hold (ZOH) rule, which is formulated as:
171
172                              A = exp(∆A),          B = (∆A)−1 (exp(A) − I) · ∆B.                        (2)

174

177   convolution with a structured convolutional kernel K ∈ RL :

180   where L is the length of the input sequence and ∗ denotes the convolution operation. A recent state
181   space model, Mamba Gu & Dao (2024), introduces Selective State Space Models (S6) by relaxing the
182   time-invariance constraints on B, C, and ∆ depending on the input x, which selectively propagates
183   information for 1D language sequence modeling.
184
      To expand Mamba from 1D language sequences to 2D visual inputs, various works Liu et al. (2024);
185
      Liang et al. (2024); Deng & Gu (2024); Guo et al. (2024) employ 2D selective scan (SS2D) mechanism
186   to capture spatial correlations with 2D feature sequences. For example, VMamba Liu et al. (2024)
187   employs SS2D by scanning four directed input sequences and generating the 2D feature map by
188   independently combining four feature sequences via an S6 block. Similarly, MambaIR Guo et al.
189   (2024) introduces the Vision State-Space Module (VSSM) into image restoration for information
190   interaction at the whole-image level. However, these methods employ repetitive multi-direction
191   scanning to adapt to 2D image inputs, significantly increasing computational costs.

193   3.2   A RCHITECTURE OVERVIEW
194
195   As shown in Fig. 1 (a), the proposed Hierarchical Mamba (Hi-Mamba) architecture comprises three
196
      parts: shallow feature extraction, deep feature extraction, and image reconstruction. Given a low-
      resolution (LR) input image ILR ∈ RCin ×H×W , where Cin , H, and W are the input channels, height,
197
      and width, respectively. We first use a simple convolution for shallow feature extraction HSF to
198
      generate local features Fl ∈ RC×H×W :
199
200                                                   Fl = HSF (ILR ),                                      (5)
201   where C is the embedding channel dimension. Subsequently, the local features Fl are processed in
202   the deep feature extraction module HDF to obtain deep features Fd ∈ RC×H×W :
203
204
                                                       Fd = HDF (Fl ),                                      (6)
205   where the deep feature extraction module HDF consists of multiple direction alternation hierarchical
206   Mamba groups (DA-HMG) with a total number of N2 . To ensure training stability, a residual strategy
207   is adopted within each group. Each DA-HMG contains the number of N1 Hierarchical Mamba blocks
208   (HMB), which consist of four isomeric single-direction scanning SSM denoted by HMB-H/V/RH/RV.
209   At the end of each DA-HMG, convolutional layers are introduced to refine the features.
210   Finally, we use Fl and Fd as the inputs and reconstruct the high-resolution (HR) output image HR
211   through the reconstruction module, which can be formulated as:
212
                                                  Ir = HR (Fl + Fd ),                                       (7)
213
214   where HR involves a single 3×3 convolution followed by a pixel shuffle operation. We optimize the
215   parameters θ of Hi-Mamba by the pixel-wise L1 loss between the reconstruction output Ir and the
      ground truth (GT) Igt . In the following, we will introduce the key blocks and modules in Hi-Mamba.

269   illustrated in Fig. 2 (a) and (b), respectively. L-SSM and R-SSM take the local feature Il ∈ RC×H×W

      3.4     D IRECTION A LTERNATION H IERARCHICAL M AMBA G ROUP
299
300   As depicted in Fig. 1, DA-HMG is easy to implement by alternatively allocating the isomeric single-
301   direction scanning to different HMBs. By default, we apply Horizontal HMB (HMB-H), Vertical
302   HMB (HMB-V), Reverse Horizontal HMB (HMB-RH), and Reverse Vertical HMB (HMB-RV)
303   orders to enrich the spatial relationship modeling further. DA-HMG does not incur extra parameters
304   and computational costs, compared to the HMB with the same direction, denoted by base-single.
305   Compared to the stacked multi-sequence scanning in the 2D-SSM module of MambaIR, DA-HMG
306   significantly reduces the computational and parameter overhead while achieving superior performance.
307   The more detailed difference between base-single, 2D-SSM and DA-HMG on the sequence scanning
308   strategy is presented in Fig. 7 of Appendix.

526   to patch sizes of 4×4 and 8×8. The region size of 4 × 4 yields the best trade-off between PSNR and
527   inference speed. Thus, we set the region size to 4 × 4 for our experiments.
528
529   5      C ONCLUSION
530
531   We present the Hierarchical Mamba Network (Hi-Mamba) in this paper for image super-resolution.
532   Hi-Mamba is built on multiple-direction alternation hierarchical Mamba groups (DA-HMG), which
533   allocates the isomeric single-direction scanning into cascading HMBs, enriching the modeling
534   of spatial relationships. Each HMB consists of a Local SSM and a Region SSM, utilizing uni-
535   directional scanning to aggregate multi-scale representations and enhance 2D spatial perception.
536
      Extensive experiments demonstrate that our Hi-Mamba has high potential compared to CNN-based
      and transformer-based methods.

784   A.2    C OMPARISON OF D IFFERENT S CANNING M ETHODS IN SSM
785
786   In NLP tasks, data often contains causal relationships, so a base-single scanning approach is typically
787   used, as shown in Fig. 7 (a). This single-directional scanning method has low complexity, but it is
788   designed for text data and struggles to construct 2D spatial relationships in image tasks. VMamba Liu
789   et al. (2024) and MambaIR Guo et al. (2024) designed the 2D-SSM scanning method for image tasks
790
      to model pixel information from various directions, as shown in Fig. 7 (b). 2D-SSM performs four
      different directional scanning sequences in parallel at each layer, achieving multi-directional paral-
791
      lelism. However, in visual SSM architectures, parallel scanning across four sequences significantly
792
      increases computational overhead, making it less efficient for processing high-resolution images.
793   This compromises the speed advantage of the Mamba architecture. To address this issue, we designed
794   the DA-HMG, as shown in Fig. 7 (c). In each layer, only a single directional scanning sequence is
795   computed. Compared to the base-single approach, the use of different directional scanning sequences
796   in DA-HMG allows pixels to integrate neighboring information from multiple directions. In contrast
797   to 2D-SSM, DA-HMG selects only one scanning direction per layer, significantly improving inference
798   speed while reducing computational and parameter overhead.
