009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            Large language models (LLM) in natural language processing (NLP) have demon-
012            strated great potential for in-context learning (ICL) — the ability to leverage a few
013            set of example prompts to adapt to various tasks without having to explicitly up-
014            date model weights. ICL has recently been explored for the visual domain with
015            promising early outcomes. These approaches involve specialized training and/or
016            additional data which complicate the process and limit its generalizability. In this
017            work, we show that off-the-shelf Stable Diffusion models can be re-purposed for
018
               visual in-context learning (V-ICL). Specifically, we formulate an in-place attention
               re-computation within the self-attention layers of the Stable Diffusion architec-
019
               ture that explicitly incorporates context between the query and example prompts.
020
               Without any additional fine-tuning, we show that this re-purposed Stable Diffu-
021            sion model is able to adapt to six different tasks: foreground segmentation, single
022            object detection, semantic segmentation, keypoint detection, edge detection, and
023            colorization. For example, the proposed approach improves the mean intersection
024            over union (mIoU) for the foreground segmentation task on Pascal-5i dataset by
025            8.9% and 3.2% over recent methods such as Visual Prompting and IMProv, re-
026            spectively. Additionally, we show that the proposed method is able to effectively
027            leverage multiple prompts through ensembling to infer the task better and further
028            improve the performance across all tasks.

      1   I NTRODUCTION
032
033
      In-context learning (ICL) refers to the paradigm where a large language model (LLM) or a founda-
      tion model leverages exemplar source-target pair(s), known as prompts, to infer the task and perform
034
      the inferred task on an input, known as query. ICL is an emergent property of LLMs and has been
035
      widely explored in the natural language processing (NLP) domain (Wei et al., 2022; Brown et al.,
036   2020; Hao et al., 2022; Touvron et al., 2023). ICL facilitates a model to adapt to novel or out-of-
037   domain tasks without the need for fine-tuning which not only eliminates task-specific training but
038   also reduces dependency on task-specific annotated datasets. Recent research, such as Bar et al.
039   (2022); Xu et al. (2023); Wang et al. (2023a;b;c); Liu et al. (2023); Wang et al. (2024), have made
040   promising attempts to harness the potential of in-context learning for computer vision tasks. These
041   works can be broadly divided into two categories based on the datasets that are used for training: (1)
042   uncurated datasets (e.g. CVF (Bar et al., 2022), S2CV (Xu et al., 2023)) and (2) curated/annotated
043   datasets (e.g. COCO (Lin et al., 2014), NYUDv2 (Silberman et al., 2012)).
044   The first category of approaches such as Visual Prompting (Bar et al., 2022) and Improv (Xu et al.,
045   2023) enable the foundation model for in-context learning by training with inpainting loss on uncu-
046   rated datasets (e.g. CVF, S2CV), which consists of images with grid-like structures of source-target
047   pairs extracted from computer vision papers. The grid-like structure of input images along with the
048   in-painting loss that is based on random masking allows the model to implicitly learn the relation-
049   ships between the source and target images. During inference, when the model is presented with a
050
      grid-like canvas of the query image and the example prompt(s) (source-target pairs) along with the
      location of the output for the query image masked-out (see Fig. 1a), the model would inpaint the
051
      prediction.
052
053   The second category of approaches (Wang et al., 2023a;b;c; Liu et al., 2023; Wang et al., 2024)
      enable a foundation model for in-context learning by allowing the model to train on curated datasets

079   et al., 2020)) remains an area yet to be fully explored.
080
081   Although these existing V-ICL approaches show promising outcomes, they involve the use of addi-
082
      tional training steps and/or data from out-of-domain tasks to which the model is being used to adapt.
      Different from existing approaches, in this work, we focus on an approach that is more in-line with
083
      the ICL methods of the NLP community. Specifically, we address the question — Can we use off-
084
      the-shelf foundation models for visual in-context learning without any additional training steps or
085   data? To this end, we propose the Stable Diffusion based visual in-context learning (SD-VICL)
086   pipeline where we show that an off-the-shelf Stable Diffusion model can be re-purposed, without
087   any fine-tuning, to adapt to several new out-of-domain tasks (see Fig. 2). We formulate an in-place
088   attention re-computation within the self-attention layers of the Stable Diffusion architecture that ex-
089   plicitly incorporates the context between the query and example prompts. This formulation is based
090   on our analysis of existing approaches like Visual Prompting (Bar et al., 2022) as shown in Fig. 1,
091   where these models are unable to fully leverage the example prompt.
092   Furthermore, we note that existing approaches such as Bar et al. (2022); Xu et al. (2023) demonstrate
093   the benefits of using multiple example prompts to boost the prediction abilities of the V-ICL founda-
094   tion models. However, their approach of ensembling in the image space by creating a composite of
095   multiple examples comes at the cost of reducing the resolution per prompt — limiting the potential of
096   prompt ensembling. To address this issue, inspired by feature ensembling in SegGPT (Wang et al.,
097   2023b), we perform prompt ensembling in the latent space for in-context learning. Note that the
098   feature ensembling in SegGPT involves averaging of query features after each attention layer which
099
      results in uniform weighting of all prompts. In contrast, we perform implicitly-weighted prompt
      ensembling within each attention layer thereby enabling the model to better infer the information
100
      from multiple prompts.
101
102   We conduct extensive evaluations to demonstrate the effectiveness of the proposed approach. Specif-
103   ically, we show that a pre-trained Stable Diffusion model has the ability to adapt to various tasks like
104   foreground segmentation, single object detection, semantic segmentation, keypoint detection, edge
105
      detection, and colorization.

125   To summarize, the main contributions of this work are as follows,
126   • The first training-free method to enable visual in-context learning properties within a foundation
127     model, setting a new direction for visual in-context learning research.
128   • A novel pipeline that explicitly incorporates the context between the query image and the prompts
129     by introducing an in-place attention re-computation within the self-attention layers of an off-the-
130     shelf Stable Diffusion model.
131   • Extensive evaluation of the proposed approach to demonstrate its ability to generalize to multiple
132     out-of-domain tasks.
133   • We show that implicitly-weighted prompt ensembling enables effective use of multiple prompts
134
        for visual in-context learning.

138   2.1   M OTIVATION AND P RELIMINARIES
139
      In ICL, a foundation model is expected to infer the task based on a few source-target example
140
      prompts and predict the appropriate output for the query image. More importantly, as demonstrated
141
      in the NLP community, in-context learning is supposed to be an emergent property of a foundation
142   model where the model does not require additional training. However, existing visual in-context
143   learning approaches involve training and/or use of additional task-related data. Further, when we
144   investigated V-ICL approaches based on uncurated datasets (e.g. Bar et al. (2022))2 through multiple
145   experiments, we observe the following, (1) the model fails to reconstruct the input prompt, despite
146   it being unmasked in the input, as shown in the top-left image of the foundation model’s output in
147   Fig. 1a), (2) the model struggles in the presence of multiple objects in the image as shown in Fig. 1a)
148   where it incorrectly predicts the bird feeder as the foreground, and (3) when provided with the query
149   and its ground-truth as the example prompt, the model struggles to make correct predictions despite
150
      having access to the ground-truth for the query (see Fig. 1b). These observations indicate that the
      model is unable to fully leverage the prompts. We attribute this inability of the model to lack of
151
      appropriate context interpretation (1) between the source and target of the example prompt which is
152
      essential for task inference, and (2) between the query and the source of the example prompt which
153   is essential for accurate predictions.
154
155   To alleviate these limitations, we propose a novel inference-only Stable Diffusion based visual in-
156
      context learning pipeline (SD-VICL), that unlike existing approaches, requires no additional train-
      ing. Stable Diffusion (Rombach et al., 2022) is a latent diffusion model that generates high-quality
157
      images by iteratively refining random noise through a denoising process. The denoising process at
158
      a given time step t employs a denoising U-Net which comprises of multiple self-attention layers

282   2.3   I MPLICITLY-W EIGHTED P ROMPT E NSEMBLING (IWPE)
283
      Bar et al. (2022); Xu et al. (2023) demonstrate that providing the model with multiple source-target
284
      example prompts improves the prediction performance of visual in-context learning. These methods
285   ensemble multiple prompts at the input level by stitching together prompt images and corresponding
286   ground-truths to form a grid-like structure in the form of a composite image. This composite image
287   is then input to the model, which is expected to leverage information from multiple prompts to
288   make a better prediction compared to the single prompt scenario. However, for a fixed-resolution
289   foundation model, as the number of source-target examples increases, the effective size of each
290   image in the grid is reduced. This leads to performance deterioration, mainly because of the loss of
291   details in the input (Xu et al., 2023).
292   To address this issue, instead of ensembling prompts in the image space through a composite input,
293   SegGPT (Wang et al., 2023b) proposed a feature space ensembling method. In this method, multiple
294   prompts are processed in parallel and aggregated by averaging the features at the end of each atten-
295   tion layer. This approach assumes that all the example prompts are equally informative and they are
296   uniformly weighted during the averaging process. However, such uniform weighting of prompts is
297   sub-optimal since it prevents the model from benefiting from more relevant prompts. To this end, we
298   propose a simple but effective ensembling method by integrating the ensembling into the attention
299
      computation, allowing the prompt patches to be implicitly weighted based on their correspondences
      with each query patch. Specifically, we concatenate each of the K and V vectors from each of the

528   4       C ONCLUSION
529
530   In this paper, we propose a novel pipeline for visual in-context learning that explicitly incorporates
531   the context between the query image and the prompt. Unlike existing visual in-context learning
532   methods which rely on training or fine-tuning foundation models on curated or uncurated data to
533   enable in-context learning, our approach is entirely training-free. Specifically, we introduce an in-
534   place attention re-computation within the self-attention layers of an off-the-shelf Stable Diffusion
535
      model. Additionally, the proposed implicitly-weighted prompt ensembling technique facilitates ef-
      fective integration of context through multiple prompts by implicitly weighing the prompts based
536
      on the relative correspondences. The versatility of the proposed method is demonstrated by its suc-
537
      cessful generalization across six different tasks, where it outperforms existing models that do not
538   explicitly train on the tasks of interest nor use annotated samples related to the tasks.
