011                                               A BSTRACT
012
013            In the existing model pruning literature, the weight gradient has been extensively
014            utilized to measure the importance of weight, where the gradient is well-known to
015
               be sensitive to perturbations. On the other hand, the widely used large language
               models (LLMs) have several billion model parameters, which could increase the
016
               fragility of few-shot gradient pruning. In this work, we experimentally show that
017
               one-shot gradient pruning algorithms could lead to unstable results under perturba-
018            tions to model weights. Even the minor error of switching between data formats
019            bfloat16 and float16 could result in obviously different outcomes. To address such
020            instabilities, we leverage optimization analysis and propose an LLM structural
021            pruning method, called MoreauPruner, with provable robustness against weight
022            perturbations. In MoreauPruner, the model weight importance is estimated based
023            on the neural network’s Moreau envelope, which can be flexibly combined with
024            ℓ1 -norm regularization techniques to induce the sparsity required in the pruning
025            task. We extensively evaluate the MoreauPruner algorithm on several well-known
026
               LLMs, including LLaMA-7B, LLaMA-13B, LLaMA3-8B, and Vicuna-7B. Our nu-
               merical results suggest the robustness of MoreauPruner against weight perturbation
027
               and how robust importance estimation in MoreauPruner contributes to successful
028
               accuracy-based scores compared to several existing pruning methods.

031   1    I NTRODUCTION
032   In the rapidly evolving field of Natural Language Processing (NLP), transformer-based Large Lan-
033   guage Models such as GPTs (Dettmers et al., 2022) and LLaMAs (Touvron et al., 2023; AI@Meta,
034   2024) have become foundational technologies, driving significant advances across various tasks.
035   These models excel in understanding and generating human language due to their scalable archi-
036   tecture, which allows performance to improve with an increase in parameters. However, deploying
037   these large models poses significant challenges due to their substantial computational and memory
038   demands. To address these challenges, considerable research has been directed toward model pruning
039
      (Han et al., 2015; Wen et al., 2016; Ma et al., 2023; Zhang et al., 2023), a technique aimed at reducing
      model size while maintaining or enhancing model performance.
040
041   While effective in accelerating LLMs for efficient deployment, existing pruning methods generally
042   focus on fixed pre-trained models, neglecting potential perturbations in the weights and their effect
043   on pruning outcomes. These perturbations can originate from various sources, including quantization
044   errors during transitions between precision levels, errors introduced by post-training operation
045
      merging(DeepSeek-AI, 2024), position embedding extension(Su et al., 2024) when the attention
      window is enlarged and so on. With minor changes mentioned above, the modified models usually
046
      produce similar outputs compared with unmodified models. Therefore, when there is a need to prune
047
      those slightly modified models, we may expect that the pruned modified models are similar to the
048   pruned original models. For example, some popular LLMs (Touvron et al., 2023) are trained with the
049   weight format bfloat16 (BF16) and deployed with the weight format float16 (FP16). As both BF16
050   and FP16 utilize 16-bit to represent a floating point, the negligible transition error will not affect
051   inference results in most cases. Considering that the basic idea of pruning is removing unnecessary
052   weights and keeping the essential weights, it is straightforward to believe that the models pruned from
053   BF16 and FP16 will be close to each other. However, current gradient-dependent pruning methods
      (Ma et al., 2023; Zhang et al., 2023; LeCun et al., 1989; Hassibi & Stork, 1992) utilize gradient to

070   inconsistency in pruned outcomes could be anti-intuitive, and a robust weight importance estimation
071

072   This paper introduces MoreauPruner, a novel robust structural pruning algorithm for LLMs, designed
073   to mitigate the effects of weight perturbations while preserving model performance. MoreauPruner
074   utilizes the gradient of the loss function’s Moreau envelope (Moreau, 1965; Zhang & Farnia, 2023;
075   T Dinh et al., 2020), a well-established optimization tool for function smoothing, to reduce weight
076   sensitivity to perturbations during the pruning process. We show that the gradient of the Moreau
077
      envelope remains stable within the neighborhood of given weight in parameter space. This stability
      enables MoreauPruner to generate robustness pruning result against weight perturbations, with any
078
      norm-bounded perturbation resulting in only a bounded change of the Moreau gradient. Additionally,
079
      by incorporating an ℓ1 -group-norm-based regularization penalty, MoreauPruner promotes group-level
080   sparsity in the gradient, which is suitable for structural pruning to facilitate real-life acceleration
081   on hardware platforms. Our empirical results suggest that MoreauPruner improves the robustness
082   of pruning outcomes against weight perturbations and achieves state-of-the-art post-pruning model
083   performance among baseline LLM pruning methods.
084
      Our contributions through this work are threefold:
085
086   • We emphasize the importance of consistent pruning criteria against minor weight perturbations, an
087     aspect previously neglected in the literature. This work is among the first to tackle the robustness
088     of pruning algorithms to such perturbations.
089
      • We introduce MoreauPruner, a structural pruning algorithm that offers provable robustness to
090     weight perturbations, leveraging the Moreau envelope to ensure the smoothness and stability of the
091     pruning process.
092
      • Through extensive experimentation with widely-used large language models such as LLaMA-
093
        7B, LLaMA-13B, LLaMA3-8B, and Vicuna-7B, we demonstrate that MoreauPruner achieves a
094     state-of-the-art performance in both robustness to weight perturbation and overall performance of
095     compressed models.

099   2.1   E FFICIENT L ARGE L ANGUAGE M ODELS
100
      Large Language Models (LLMs) (Touvron et al., 2023; Achiam et al., 2023; Chiang et al., 2023;
101
      AI@Meta, 2024) have achieved remarkable performance by following the scaling laws (Kaplan
102   et al., 2020). However, deploying LLMs can be challenging due to high inference costs in resource-
103   limited scenarios. Various methods have been proposed to reduce model size, including knowledge
104   distillation (Hinton et al., 2015; Sanh et al., 2019; Sun et al., 2019; 2020), which involves transferring
105   the knowledge from the original model to a smaller one; model quantization (Dettmers et al., 2022;
106   Xiao et al., 2023; Yao et al., 2022; Zafrir et al., 2019), which reduces the bit length of the model
107   weights; and model pruning (Han et al., 2015; Frankle & Carbin, 2018; Fang et al., 2023; Park et al.,
      2023), which involves removing non-essential weights to speed up inference. This work primarily

109   Ashkboos et al., 2024; Zhang et al., 2024; Yin et al., 2023; Ji et al., 2023; van der Ouderaa et al.,

112
113   2.2   P RUNING C RITERIA
114
      To determine which weights to prune during the pruning phase, the importance of each weight is
115
      assessed using various criteria. Several studies (Sun et al., 2023; Li et al., 2018; Han et al., 2015;
116   Elesedy et al., 2020) adopt a magnitude-based criterion, retaining weights with larger magnitudes
117   post-pruning. Recent approaches (Sun et al., 2023) also consider activation values to evaluate
118   weight importance. Some prevalent criteria are based on Taylor Expansion approximation (LeCun
119   et al., 1989; Ma et al., 2023; Yu et al., 2022; Hassibi et al., 1993; Hassibi & Stork, 1992), utilizing
120   differential information (zero-th, first, and second order) to estimate output changes if weights
121   are removed. Notably, (Zhang et al., 2023) highlights that, in LLMs, gradients can be efficiently
122   approximated using low-rank methods (Hu et al., 2021) when the direct computation of the gradient
123   is too costly. Nevertheless, gradients can be highly sensitive to weight modifications, rendering
124
      gradient-based pruning criteria susceptible to variations in weight. In response, MoreauPruner offers
      proven robustness against any norm-bounded weight perturbation, maintaining model performance.

353   5.2    ROBUSTNESS AGAINST W EIGHT P ERTURBATION
354
355   As we previously discussed, few-shot gradient-based pruning methods are significantly influenced by
356
      the changes of the gradient. Even minor differences on model weight can lead to markedly different
      pruning outcomes. In contrast, MoreauGrad is theoretically robust against norm-bounded weight
357
      perturbation. To validate this assertion, we adhered to the channel-wise pruning protocol established
358
      in prior research (Ma et al., 2023), removing a fixed ratio of channels based on their importance score.
359
360   When utilizing 16 bits to store a float number, BF16 has larger range while FP16 has better precision.
361   Considering that some LLMs are trained on BF16 and inferred on FP16, our experiments were
362
      conducted on the LLaMA-7B model using both BF16 and FP16 weight bit formats. We standardized
      the calibration sample selection during pruning across different settings to ensure a fair comparison.
363
      Upon completing the pruning process, we performed a zero-shot perplexity (PPL) analysis using
364
      128-token segments on the WikiText2 and PTB datasets and compared the discrepancies between
365   FP16 and BF16. The findings are presented in Table 1. Due to the space limitation, we put the full
366   evaluation results on zero-shot question-answering in appendices.
367
368
      The results indicate that, for MoreauPruner, the performance under different weight format is closer
      to each other, which indicates a better consistency of pruning outcomes. The result demonstrates the
369
      robustness of MoreauPruner against weight perturbations caused by different weight formats.

516   stability underscores the robustness and effectiveness of our pruning strategies.
517

519   MoreauPruner-GS during optimization. We conduct an ablation study on LLaMA-7B with 20%

524   6      C ONCLUSION
525   In this paper, we discussed how minor changes in model weights can lead to unstable pruning results
526   for large language models (LLMs). To address this instability, we introduced MoreauPruner, a weight-
527   perturbation structural pruning method. Our theoretical analysis demonstrates that MoreauPruner
528   is robust to norm-bounded perturbations. Numerical experiments conducted on well-known LLMs
529   suggest that MoreauPruner can efficiently compress LLMs while maintaining their performance. For
530   future work, we propose combining structural pruning technology with other model compression
531   methods to accelerate model inference and reduce computational costs.
532
      Limitations. The authors acknowledge that the number of parameters utilized in the models for
533
      this paper only reach 13B due to limited hardware budget. The performance of MoreauPruner on
534
      extremely large-scale models (e.g., 30B, 70B, etc.) will be further explored once enough hardware
535   resources are available.

797   B     E XTENDED C OMPARISON ON C HANNEL R ANKING A SSIGNMENT
798
799   In Section 5.2 of the main text, we demonstrate the inconsistency in ranking assignments across
800   different layer depth. Here, we extend the discussion by exploring how this inconsistency happens
801
      on different modules. Several examples from different layers are illustrated in Figures 5 and 6. The
      experimental results indicate that the major disagreements between MoreauPruner and gradient-based
802
      methods occur in the most shallow and deepest layers. Given that these layers are known to be
803
      sensitive to pruning (Ma et al., 2023; Yin et al., 2023; Ji et al., 2023), the performance gap between
804   gradient-based methods and MoreauPruner can be partially attributed to differences in channel
805   pruning within these layers. Additionally, we observed that the ranking stability among the middle
806   layers suggests that the weights in these layers of LLMs may have converged to a flatter minimum,
807   as both gradient-based and robust-gradient-based measurements yield similar sensitivity rankings.
808   The numerical results also suggest that while gradient-based methods and MoreauPruner generally
809   agree on the importance of channels within the attention module, there is more disagreement in the
      feed-forward network (FFN) module.

       E.2     PARAMETERS C HOOSING
987    In the pruning stage, we randomly pick a batch from BookCorpus (Zhu et al., 2015) with ten 128-
988    token truncated sentences. The batch choice remains the same among LLM-Pruner, SmoothGrad,
989    MoreauPruner, and MoreauPruner-GS in our experiments. Since deep layers and shallow layers are
990    sensitive to pruning, following previous works (Ma et al., 2023), we only prune the middle layers in
991    this stage. For example, when we aim to prune 20% parameters from LLaMA-7B, we remove 25%
992
       parameters from layer 4 to layer 30.
993    For SmoothGrad, we pass the batch to the model 100 times. We utilized a element-wised Gaussian
994    smoothing, i.e., for weight parameter w(k) , the intensity of Gaussian is σ = 0.05∥w(k) ∥1 . The
995    smooth gradient is empirically calculated by averaging the inportance scores of each forward pass.
996
       For both MoreauPruner and MoreauPruner-GS, we also apply the element-wise Gaussian smoothing
997    to the model weights during the optimization of the gradient of the Moreau Envelope, as SmoothGrad
998    does. The hyper-parameter ρ is set to 0.05 for MoreauPruner and 0.2 for MoreauPruner-GS. The
999    stepsize γ used in the optimization of the gradient of the Moreau Envelope is 1e-3 for MoreauPruner
1000   and 2e-4 for MoreauPruner-GS. The hyper-parameter η is set to 5e-6 as explained in the main text.
1001   We conducted a parameter search on LLaMA-7B to find suitable hyper-parameters.
1002
       In the fine-tuning stage, we use the protocol from previous work (Ma et al., 2023) and employ a
1003   LoRA with rank (r=8). The batch size is 64. The learning rate is 1e-4 for Alpaca (Taori et al., 2023)
1004   and 5e-5 for Lamini (Wu et al., 2023). The training length is two epochs for Alpaca and three epochs
1005   for Lamini.

1027
1028   In this section, we provide answers to frequently asked questions about our work.
1029
1030
            ▷ When pruning an existing model, the parameters are fixed. Why is it meaningful to
              consider the robustness of pruning criteria against weight perturbation?
1031
1032
              Intuitively, the basic goal of pruning is to remove unnecessary weights while retaining
              the essential ones. It is reasonable to expect that a pruning algorithm should produce
1033
              similar results for a model in BF16 and FP16 formats, as models in these formats generally
1034
              yield the same inference results. The unexpected discrepancy between the two pruned
1035          models indicates a bias in the evaluation of weight importance. Therefore, ensuring pruning
1036          consistency under minor perturbations can serve as a guiding principle during the design
1037          phase of pruning criteria. The improvements in the performance of pruned models, as shown
1038          in our numerical results, further support this argument.
1039        ▷ There are many structured pruning methods in the literature. Why does MoreauPruner
1040          only compare several of them in this paper?
1041          The main motivation behind MoreauPruner is to draw attention to the robustness of pruning
1042          criteria against weight perturbation. Given that pruning technologies have been extensively
1043          explored over the past thirty years, there is significant variation in experiment settings across
1044          different methods. These include variations in selected models, calibration/recovery sets,
1045          recovery methods, iterative versus one-shot pruning, evaluation metrics/datasets, and more,
1046          making it difficult to identify a universally optimal setting.
1047          Therefore, in our paper, we adopt the setting from a recent, powerful, gradient-based
1048          baseline, LLM-Pruner, and focus on comparing our method with several competitors that
1049          utilize similar experimental setups to ensure a clear comparison. The numerical results and
1050          conclusions presented in the main text support our motivations under the selected settings.
1051
              Our theoretical analysis holds across settings that satisfy our assumption.
1052        ▷ Quantization technologies, another widely-used model compression method, can
1053          achieve high compression ratios (≥ 50%) without significant performance drops. Why
1054          do we still need pruning methods like MoreauPruner, which typically maintain perfor-
1055
              mance only at lower pruning ratios?
1056          Pruning and Quantization, as two mainstream model compression methods, are developed
1057
              for different purposes and each has its own advantages and disadvantages. In practical
              applications, the choice of compression method should consider several factors. For example,
1058
              mainstream hardware does not support arbitrary data formats. In cases where an 8-bit model
1059
              needs to be compressed by approximately 12.5%, pruning would often be a better choice
1060          than attempting to quantize the model to 7 bits. Each method serves specific needs, and
1061          pruning remains a viable option when fine control over compression is required.
