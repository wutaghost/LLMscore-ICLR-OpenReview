011                                              A BSTRACT
012
013            In this paper, we study the ergodic convergence properties of the Peaceman-
014            Rachford (PR) method with semi-proximal terms for solving convex optimiza-
015            tion problems (COPs). By reformulating the PR method as a degenerate proximal
016
               point method, for the first time we establish the global convergence of the ergodic
               sequence generated by the PR method with broadly chosen semi-proximal terms
017
               under the assumption that there exists a Karush–Kuhn–Tucker (KKT) solution
018
               to the COPs. This result represents a significant departure from previous stud-
019            ies on the non-ergodic convergence of the PR method, which typically requires
020            strong convexity (or strong monotonicity in the reformulated operator) conditions
021            that are hardly satisfied for COPs. Moreover, we establish an ergodic iteration
022            complexity of O(1/k) of the PR method with semi-proximal terms, measured by
023            the objective error, the feasibility violation, and the KKT residual using the ε-
024            subdifferential. Based on these convergence properties, we introduce the solver
025            EPR-LP, using the ergodic sequence of the PR method with semi-proximal terms
026            for solving linear programming (LP) problems. EPR-LP incorporates an adaptive
027
               restart strategy and dynamic penalty parameter updates for efficiency and robust-
               ness. Extensive numerical experiments on LP benchmark datasets, executed on a
028
               high-performance GPU, show that our Julia-based solver outperforms the award-
029
               winning solver PDLP at a tolerance level of 10−8 .

099

101   Karush–Kuhn–Tucker (KKT) residual and the objective error. The Julia implementation of HPR-LP
102   has demonstrated superior performance compared to PDLP on classical LP benchmark datasets.

      2     E RGODIC CONVERGENCE PROPERTIES OF THE PADMM
163
164   In this section, we study the ergodic convergence properties of the pADMM method, including the
165   PR method, for solving COP. We begin by reformulating the pADMM method as a dPPM. Next,
166   we prove the ergodic convergence of the dPPM, thereby establishing the ergodic convergence of
167   pADMM. Finally, we analyze the ergodic iteration complexity of the pADMM method.

530   5     C ONCLUSION
531
532
      In this paper, we proved the ergodic convergence of the PR method with semi-proximal terms for
533
      solving convex optimization problems. We established the ergodic iteration complexity of O(1/k)
534   with respect to the objective error, the feasibility violation, and the KKT residual based on ε-
535   subdifferential. Building on these results, we developed the solver EPR-LP for solving large-scale
536   LP problems, which incorporates adaptive restart and penalty parameter updates. Extensive nu-
537   merical experiments on LP benchmark datasets highlighted the advantages of EPR-LP compared to
538   PDLP. In the future, it would be interesting to explore combining the ergodic sequence with accel-
539   eration techniques, such as Halpern’s iteration (Halpern, 1967; Lieder, 2021; Sun et al., 2024), to
      design a more efficient algorithm with an iteration complexity better than O(1/k).
