009                                              A BSTRACT
010
011            Long-context extrapolation aims to extend the contextual window of large lan-
012            guage models to process more contextual information, which is widely adopted
013            in industrial applications. Current mainstream solutions involve increasing the
014            rotation base of RoPE Su et al. (2024) to varying degrees or introducing opti-
015            mization strategies such as “low-frequency extrapolation and high-frequency in-
016
               terpolation”, in order to enhance the model’s extrapolation capabilities for long
               context. Actually, these methods alter the representation distribution of positional
017
               information by adjusting the rotation frequency of positional encoding, resulting
018
               in inevitably disrupt the attention distribution within the original training length
019            range. In this paper, we analyze this phenomenon from a theoretical perspective
020            and propose a long-context extrapolation strategy that preserves the known dis-
021            tribution via periodic extension of high-dimensional positional encoding. Based
022            on this strategy, we design two methods, namely Extra-PE and Extra-MPE, to
023            significantly enhance the models’ long-context extrapolation capabilities without
024            disrupting the positional encoding distribution within the original training length.
025            Through extensive experimental results, it is found that the long-context extrap-
026            olation method based on periodic extension can enhance the model’s capability
027
               in extrapolating long-contexts. Specifically, a model fine-tuned on 32k tokens
               can extrapolate beyond 80k tokens, surpassing the performance of the NTK-32k
028
               model and approaching that of the YaRN-64k model. Furthermore, this method
029
               demonstrates significantly superior performance in extrapolating extremely long-
030            contexts compared to other methods. Notably, a model fine-tuned on 8k tokens
031            still does not exhibit perplexity explosion when extrapolating to 80k tokens. Ad-
032            ditionally, during the fine-tuning process, our approach achieves optimal perfor-
033            mance using only one-fourth of the fine-tuning steps (100 steps) compared to the
034            YaRN Peng et al. (2023) method. Secondly, in our comparative experiments, we
035            found that the period in which the model learns a sufficient number of positional
036            encoding has a significant impact on long-context extrapolation capability. Fi-
037            nally, through attention analysis, we discovered that our method can still maintain
038
               a stable level of attention at ultra-long distances, with the mean attention value
               exceeding 0 at these distances.

041   1   I NTRODUCTION
042
043   In recent years, large language models (LLMs) based on the Transformer Vaswani (2017) has rapidly
044   developed, demonstrating a powerful reasoning ability that has played a very significant role in the
045   fields of natural language processing (NLP). Long-context extrapolation is a fundamental research
046   area in the field of LLMs, referring to the capability of processing and generating text based on an
047   extended context length.
048   In order to expand the LLMs’ context window, it is necessary to increase the positional encoding
049   to enhance the model’s ability to understand longer contexts. Currently, Rotary Position Embed-
050   ding (RoPE) Su et al. (2024) has become the mainstream positional encoding method for large
051   language models, e.g., LLaMA Touvron et al. (2023). However, when the context length exceeds
052   the training text length, directly extrapolating with RoPE does not achieve the expected results Press
053   et al. (2021). With the deepening of research, some research efforts have been proposed for Long-
      context extrapolation based on RoPE, such as positional interpolation Chen et al. (2023), NTK-

      Aware Scaled RoPE Peng & Quesnelle (2023), and the improved methods based on NTK-Aware
055   like Dynamic NTK Emozilla (2023), NTK-by-parts Bloc97 (2023), and YaRN Peng et al. (2023).
056   NTK-series methods are widely adopted in long-context extension ( Young et al. (2024), Touvron

058   increasing the base value. Another method Liu et al. (2023) involves continuously reducing the base
059   value, which significantly shortens the rotation period of the positional encoding. This allows the
060   positional encoding in high-dimensional space to cover the entire rotation period within a limited
061   training length as much as possible, and this approach performs well in terms of Perplexity (PPL).
062   However, Men et al. (2024) indicates that drastically lowering the base value also has some nega-
063
      tive impacts on the model’s attention mechanism, specifically by disrupting the long-context decay
      property of the attention mechanism. By observing how attention scores vary with relative distance
064
      within both high-frequency and low-frequency spaces, this paper finds that the low-frequency space
065
      of positional encoding is of crucial importance for capturing long-distance attenuation. The specific
066   process is detailed in Appendix A.1. In addition, some work also focuses on achieving Long-context
067   extrapolation by adjusting the attention mechanism Chiang & Cholak (2022); Su (2023).
068
069
      Previous work has demonstrated good performance in achieving long-context extrapolation, but the
      current mainstream methods mainly adjust the rotation base (base) or m of ROPE. However, the
070
      drawback of this approach is that it alters the distribution of positional encoding within the original
071
      training length range of the model, which in turn has a certain impact on the attention distribution
072   within that range. In this paper, firstly, based on the aforementioned issues, we attempt to en-
073   hance the long-context extrapolation capability without disrupting the positional encoding distribu-
074   tion within the original training length range of the model. Therefore, we propose a new theoretical
075   perspective for analyzing long-context extrapolation capabilities, and from this perspective, we offer
076   a novel viewpoint on the out-of-distribution (OOD) problem and the classification of current main-
077   stream long-context extrapolation methods. Secondly, from our new theoretical perspective, we pro-
078   pose two long-context extrapolation methods based on periodic extension: Extra-PE (Long-context
079   Extrapolation based on Periodic Extension) and Extra-MPE (Long-context Extrapolation based on
080
      Mirrored Periodic Extension). These are currently the first effective out-of-domain long-context ex-
      trapolation methods. The core idea of this method is ”direct extrapolation for high frequencies and
081
      periodic extension for low frequencies,” which differs from the ”high-frequency extrapolation and
082
      low-frequency interpolation” approach proposed by ”NTK-aware.” Finally, we conducted extensive
083   experiments to verify the effectiveness of the long-context extrapolation methods based on periodic
084   extension. In summary, the main contributions of this paper are as follows:
085
086
      1. This paper proposes a new theoretical perspective for analyzing long-context extrapolation capa-
      bilities.
087
088   2. Based on this new theoretical perspective, this paper introduces two long-context extrapolation
089   methods using periodic extension. These methods not only achieve effective extrapolation capabili-
090   ties but also offer higher training efficiency. Specifically, they are capable of sufficient extrapolation
091   to over twice the original length after only 50 fine-tuning steps. When fine-tuned for 100 steps, the
092
      model fine-tuned on 16k tokens achieves optimal extrapolation performance, demonstrating a four-
      fold improvement in fine-tuning efficiency compared to the current best long-context extrapolation
093
      method, YaRN Peng et al. (2023).
094
095   3. In terms of extrapolation capabilities, the larger the context window of the fine-tuning data,
096   the more significant the improvement in extrapolation capabilities(Figure 1). The two long-context
097   extrapolation methods based on periodic extension designed in this paper excel at extrapolation
098
      on ultra-long-context windows. The model fine-tuned on 32k tokens can effectively extrapolate
      beyond 80k, with extrapolation performance far surpassing the NTK-32k model and approaching
099
      the YaRN-64k model. Moreover, after fine-tuning the model using the Extra-PE method and data
100
      of 8k length, the model still does not show significant perplexity explosion at a text length of 80k.
101   Additionally, for models trained with the same specification of fine-tuning data (greater than 16k
102   tokens), the Extra-MPE method demonstrates significantly better extrapolation capabilities on ultra-
103   long-context windows compared to the Extra-PE method, but the Extra-PE method performs slightly
104   better on short-distance text windows.
105
      4. This paper finds that the OOD problem is not the only important factor affecting a model’s extrap-
106
      olation capabilities; simultaneously, the model’s learning of the periodic distribution of positional

      2.1   P RELIMINARY

153
      RoPE Su et al. (2024) serves as the theoretical foundation for current long-context extrapolation,
154
      which is an improved positional encoding method that has been widely adopted in large language
155
      models. RoPE improves upon traditional absolute positional encoding, e.g., sinusoidal positional
156   encoding Vaswani (2017), by aiming to better capture relative position information while avoiding
157   direct modifications to the input vectors. Unlike traditional sinusoidal positional encoding, RoPE
158   does not directly add positional information to the input vectors. Instead, it embeds positional
159   information into the vectors q and k, which are the results of transforming the input vector x through
160   the query matrix Q and the key matrix K during the attention mechanism computation. This design
161   preserves the original information of the input vectors, as well as effectively incorporates relative
      positional information, significantly enhancing the model’s performance and robustness.

      Increasing the base value is equivalent to interpolation, but decreasing the base value is entirely
258   different. By reducing the base, the period of the positional encoding becomes shorter, allowing the
259   model to learn a more diverse distribution of positional encoding during the training phase. Liu et al.
260   (2023) conducted an in-depth study on the process of scaling the base and found that decreasing the
261   base also achieves good extrapolation results. However, in Men et al. (2024), further analysis from
262   the perspective of the attention mechanism was performed on the method of decreasing the base.
263   The study found that the process of reducing the base has a certain impact on the attention scores
264   and identified an absolute lower bound for the base value.
265   Adjusting the base value undoubtedly modifies the original positional encoding distribution. Al-
266   though this can address the out-of-distribution (OOD) problem for new positional encoding, it can
267   lead to the forgetting of the originally learned positional encoding. Different from the above meth-
268   ods, this paper proposes a method for long-context extrapolation that differs from the aforemen-
269   tioned approaches. It addresses the OOD problem for new positional encoding without altering the
      original positional encoding distribution.

      3   T HEORY P ERSPECTIVE
288
289   The OOD problem is widely regarded as a key factor limiting the Long-context extrapolation ca-
290   pabilities of LLMs Ruoss et al. (2023). To address this, many researchers have proposed various
291
      positional interpolation methods to confine token position indices within a limited window Chen
      et al. (2023). This paper presents a new perspective for analyzing Long-context extrapolation capa-
292
      bilities.
293
294   Figure 3 represents the distribution of positional encoding in a certain high-dimensional subspace.
295   It is divided into three regions (A, B and C) and four types of data distribution points with different
296   colors. Region A represents the linearly increasing position indices during the encoding process. Re-
297
      gions B and C both show the positional encoding distributions after the ROPE operation, but region
      B includes the full range of encoding (even beyond the training length), while region C represents
298
      the encoding learned within the training length. The areas within B but outside C represent unknown
299
      or OOD regions. Differently colored dots illustrate the data distribution across these regions. The
300   reason for describing a high-dimensional subspace is that in a low-dimensional subspace, region C
301   would be infinitely close to region B. In contrast, the high-dimensional subspace is the focus for
302   Long-context extrapolation. When the inference text length exceeds the training text length, the
303   corresponding positional encoding are likely to fall into the unknown areas of region B, leading to
304   the OOD problem.
305
      Below, we use the Figure 3 to categorize and describe the existing mainstream methods for Long-
306   context extrapolation. These methods can be broadly classified into three categories: positional
307   index interpolation within region A, outward expansion from the visible area C of the positional en-
308   coding distribution, and inward convergence towards the complete area B of the positional encoding
309   distribution. With this theoretical perspective, direct extrapolation involves making no adjustments
310   for positions that exceed the training text length, relying solely on the good extrapolation properties
311   of ROPE. The result is that a large number of positional encoding fall within the unknown areas
312   of region B in the high-dimensional subspace, leading to very poor extrapolation performance. To
313   address this, there are three main approaches to mitigate this issue as follows.
314   Position Interpolation. PI Chen et al. (2023) involves compressing and adjusting the positions that
315   exceed the training text length within region A. This is done by reducing the spacing between posi-
316   tion indices within the training length range and directly inserting the excess position indices into the
317   training length, thereby keeping the positional encoding distribution within the visible area C. Fur-
318   ther, researchers have proposed various strategies to adjust the base of RoPE, including NTK Peng &
319   Quesnelle (2023), NTK-by-part Bloc97 (2023), dynamic NTK , and Yarn Peng et al. (2023). These
320
      methods are essentially improved versions of PI, using nonlinear interpolation based on different
      dimensions of the positional encoding.
321
322   Outward Expansion of the Visible Area of positional encoding Distribution. Randomized po-
323   sitional encoding Ruoss et al. (2023) achieves the expansion of area C by performing random sam-
      pling over longer sequences within region A, thereby reducing the likelihood of positional encoding

      falling into unknown regions. Similarly, the method of reducing the base involves compressing the
325   rotation periods across all dimensions, allowing the model to learn the positional encoding distri-

      Inward Convergence of the Complete Area of positional encoding Distribution. The advantage
329
      of this approach is that it does not disrupt the visible area C of the model’s positional encoding
330
      distribution, ensuring that the attention distribution within the training length remains intact. To
331   the best of our knowledge, no such methods currently exist. Analysis of Figure 3 reveals that this
332   approach aims to converge the unknown regions in Area B into the visible region C. To achieve
333   this, we truncate the unknown distributions that have not appeared within the training length in
334   a high-dimensional space, retaining only the unknown code distributions that have been learned
335   within the training length. Based on this, we propose a periodic extension method for long-context
336   extrapolation.

412   The Extra-MPE method addresses the issue of discontinuity in the positional encoding strategy
413   present in the Extra-PE method, albeit with an increase in computational cost. Additionally, the
414   extrapolation period of Extra-MPE is longer than that of Extra-PE, which means that when using

419   the training length Ltrain and avoiding the attention field resolution issues caused by the compres-
420   sion of distances between adjacent positions in positional interpolation methods. Additionally, by
421   converging the full positional encoding distribution in high-dimensional subspaces, it addresses the
422   OOD problem. Compared to extending the learning range of high-dimensional positional encod-
423   ing by reducing the base, this approach theoretically has a higher potential for achieving infinite

447   This paper proposes a new perspective for analyzing the extrapolation capability of long-context,
448   and based on this perspective, introduces a novel approach and specific implementation for solv-
449   ing Out-of-Distribution (OOD) problems. We achieve the purpose of long-context extrapolation by
450
      periodically extending the positional encoding beyond the length of the pre-training data in high-
      dimensional space. Additionally, this paper proposes two methods of periodic extension: peri-
451
      odic extension of high-dimensional positional encoding and mirrored periodic extension of high-
452
      dimensional positional encoding. Corresponding comparative experiments are conducted for both
453   methods to further verify the effectiveness and advantages of this approach. The specific advantages
454   of this method are summarized as follows:
455
456
      1.In terms of fine-tuning efficiency, the long-text extrapolation method based on periodic extension
      designed in this paper is four times as efficient as the YaRN method. That is, we can achieve very
457
      good extrapolation results with just a few fine-tuning steps (100 steps).
458
459   2.In terms of attention distribution, the long-context extrapolation method designed in this paper
460   does not alter the attention distribution within the pre-training length range of the model, meaning
461   it maintains the model’s attention to adjacent tokens at short distances. Additionally, the long-
462
      context extrapolation method based on periodic extension demonstrates stronger ultra-long-context
      extrapolation capabilities. This experimental result corresponds to the conclusion that this method
463
      enables the model’s attention at ultra-long distances not to decay to zero.
464
465   3.Through experimental validation, this paper finds that, besides the OOD problem, the model’s
466   ability to learn sufficient periodic positional encoding is also an important factor affecting its long-
467   context extrapolation capability.
468   4.When comparing the two extrapolation methods designed in this paper, Extra-PE performs better
469   on short-distance context windows and can learn more periodic characteristics with the same length
470   of fine-tuning data. On the other hand, the Extra-MPE method demonstrates stronger ultra-long-
471   context extrapolation capabilities after learning positional encoding information for at least four
472   periodic lengths.

565
566   In this paper, by analyzing the changes in attention scores between two identical tokens as the
567   relative distance increases, we theoretically investigate the following two questions:
568
      1. Whether adjusting the base and the Long-context extrapolation method based on periodic exten-
569   sion produce different effects on the attention distribution at short and long distances.
570
571   2. Whether the long-distance attenuation observed in the attention distribution when using the ROPE
572
      (Rotary Position Embedding) positional encoding method is primarily attributed to the influence of
      low-frequency spatial positional encoding.
573
574
575
      A.1.1 T HE INFLUENCE OF THE BASE ON THE ATTENTION DISTRIBUTION
576   The changes in attention scores for the same tokens as the relative distance increases, when the base
577   is set to 10000, 500, and 100000, are shown in Figure 6. The experimental results indicate that:
578
      1. Within a short-distance window, adjusting the base will cause significant changes in attention.
579
      Increasing the base will enhance the attention between adjacent tokens, while decreasing the base
580
      will reduce it.
581
582   2. Within an ultra-long-distance window, adjusting the base results in an attention distribution that
583   is consistent with the original base. The attention ultimately oscillates around the mean value of 0.

      DISTRIBUTION

587   By comparing the impact of the Extra-PE and Extra-MPE methods on the attention scores as the
588   relative distance increases, as shown in Figure 7, the experimental results indicate that:
589
590
      1.When using the method based on periodic extension for long-context extrapolation within the
      pretraining data window of the model, no changes occur in the attention. This performance allows
591
      the model to maintain its original ability when processing nearby tokens.
592
593   2.When the length of the inference data exceeds the size of the pretraining data window, both the
      Extra-PE and Extra-MPE methods cause a certain degree of increase in the attention distribution.

      B.1.1 TRAINING
705
706   The base model for this method is Llama2-7b Touvron et al. (2023), with a data context window

708   Peng et al. (2023) training framework. Additionally, in terms of model structure, only the strategy
709   for implementing positional encoding was adjusted accordingly, without modifying other aspects
710   of the model structure or introducing attention adjustment factors in the process of calculating the
711   attention score. To test the impact of the number of model training steps and the context length
712   of the model fine-tuning data on the model’s extrapolation performance, the model training was
713
      conducted based on two dimensions: firstly, the number of training steps was set to 50, 100, 200,
      and 400, respectively; secondly, the context length of the model fine-tuning data was set to 8k,
714
      16k, and 32k, respectively. All experiments are conducted on a machine equipped with 8 NVIDIA
715
      A100 GPUs, each with 40GB of memory. In terms of hyperparameters, we fixed the learning rate
716   at 2e-5, used the Adam optimizer, set the global batch size for model training to 64. Additionally,
717   we leveraged the deepspeed framework Rasley et al. (2020) to achieve efficient model training,
718   and utilized PyTorch Paszke et al. (2019) with Fully Sharded Data Parallelism Zhao et al. (2023)
719   and Flash Attention 2 Dao (2023), where the zero optimization adopts ZeRO3 strategies Jackson
720   et al. (2010). In terms of the dataset, we adopted the same training set as the YaRN Peng et al.
721   (2023) method. Specifically, the model was fine-tuned based on the PG19 dataset Rae et al. (2019).
722   During the model training process, we validated Motivation 1 in Section 4 and found that the use of
723   non-contiguous positional encoding did not lead to failure in the model training process.

725   B.1.2 P ERPLEXITY E VALUATIONS
726
727   During the model PPL (Perplexity) evaluation phase, we utilize the Proof-pile Azerbayev et al.
728   (2022) dataset, which comprises numerous long sequence samples. From this dataset, we have se-
729   lected 10 samples as the test set for perplexity evaluation, with each sample containing at least 100K
730   tokens in length. For the perplexity evaluation, we employ the sliding window method introduced
731   by Press et al. (2021). However, to more accurately assess the model’s perplexity on long sequence
732   texts, this paper does not set a specific size for the sliding window or a sliding step. Instead, we
733
      adopt a truncation method, where the perplexity calculation is performed in one go within the entire
      truncated text window, i.e., truncate=True.
734
735   Firstly, we tested the model’s perplexity performance within a context window of 16k tokens as the
736   window size increases. We truncated the data samples sequentially from 2k tokens to 16k tokens,
737   with a step size of 2k tokens each time.
738   In order to better analyze the performance and advantages/disadvantages of the two periodic ex-
739   tension methods mentioned in this paper in terms of long-context extrapolation and overall model
740   capability, we need to conduct detailed experimental comparisons and theoretical analyses. The
741   specific evaluation content mainly includes three aspects: Perplexity Evaluations, The long-context
742   Retrieval Capability, and The Long Context Understanding Capability.
743   Figure 8 presents a comparison of the PPL results for the Llama2-7B model extrapolated using
744   Extra-PE, Extra-MPE, ”NTK-aware” Peng & Quesnelle (2023), and YaRN methods Peng et al.
745   (2023), respectively. The results indicate:
746
      1.Without fine-tuning on new data, directly using the Extra-PE or Extra-MPE methods yields rel-
747
      atively poor results. This implies that merely addressing the OOD (Out-of-Distribution) problem
748
      does not directly lead to a comprehensive improvement in Long-context extrapolation.
749
750   2.During the fine-tuning stage, the model must learn positional encoding for at least 4 cycles (i.e.,
751   the context length of the fine-tuning data should be at least 4 times the maximum length of the pre-

      training data) for there to be a significant improvement in extrapolation capability. This suggests
      that enabling the model to learn the periodicity of the positional encoding distribution is crucial for
753
      significantly enhancing its long-context extrapolation ability.
754
755   3.Within short-distance context windows, the perplexity performance of the Extra-PE method is
      superior to that of the Extra-MPE method.
