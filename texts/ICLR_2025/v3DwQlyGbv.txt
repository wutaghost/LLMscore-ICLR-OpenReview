013
014
               In this paper, we pose the following question: whether domain specific pretrain-
015            ing of tiny generative language models from scratch with domain specialized to-
016            kenizer and Chain-of-Thought (CoT) instruction fine-tuning results in very com-
017            petitive performance on mathematical reasoning than LLMs which are trained
018            on trillion of tokens and humongous parameters? Secondly, we pose our second
019            RQ: whether domain specific pretraining from scratch is environmentally sus-
020            tainable, highly cost efficient? To address these research questions, we present
021            PARAMANU -G ANITA, a 208 million-parameter novel Auto Regressive (AR) de-
022            coder based language model on mathematics. We performed pretraining from
023
               scratch on 31.5 billion tokens using a context size of 4096 on a mixed mathemat-
               ical corpus consisting of mathematical web pages, mathematics related source
024
               code such as AlgebraStack, mathematical textbooks, Chain-of-Thought (CoT)
025
               templatised mathematical StackOverflow question answers pairs, and mathemat-
026            ical lecture notes in LATEX curated by us. We also trained a math and code spe-
027            cialised BPE tokenizer. We proposed and performed Chain-of-Thought instruc-
028            tion fine-tuning of Paramanu-Ganita on the MetaMathQA dataset. We evaluate our
029            model on GSM8K and MATH mathematical benchmarks, and on logical deduc-
030            tive reasoning (LogiQA) and multiple choice high school and college level math
031            questions from SAT (AGIEVAL-SAT-Math), GRE/GMAT questions (AGIEVAL-
032            AQuA-RAT), college and high school level math questions from MMLU. Our
033            model Paramanu-Ganita, despite being 34 times smaller than the 7B LLMs, out-
034
               performs general LLMs by approximately 30% points, and even math-specialised
               LLMs by 3-23% points in GSM8K test accuracy metric. On MATH benchmark,
035
               Paramanu-Ganita outperformed the various models by 6-8% points. On other
036
               benchmarks such as LogiQA logical deductive reasoning benchmark, mathemati-
037            cal high school level multi-choice questions (MMLU-math-high-school), GRE-
038            GMAT level quantitative questions (AGIEVAL-AQuA-RAT), SAT level math
039            questions, Paramanu-Ganita was better than the others by about 1-4% points. The
040            large significant margin improvement in performance of our math model over the
041            existing LLMs signifies that reasoning capabilities of language models are just not
042            restricted to those with humongous number of parameters. Paramanu-Ganita took
043            only 170 hours of A100 training whereas large LLMs such as the math-specialised
044            LLM, LLEMMA 7B, was trained for 23,000 A100 equivalent hours. Thus, our ap-
045
               proach of pretraining powerful domain-specialised language models from scratch
               for domain adaptation is much more cost-effective and environmental friendly
046
               than performing continual training of LLMs.

130   2     R ELATED W ORK
131
132   Mathematical reasoning plays a crucial role in artificial intelligence, enabling the comprehension
133   and resolution of intricate mathematical challenges. The incorporation of large language models
134
      (LLMs) in this area has been substantial, thanks to their capability to interpret, process, and produce
      complex natural language. In artificial intelligence, math problem solving involves utilizing algo-
135
      rithms, computational models, and use of increasingly LLMs to understand, explain, and resolve
136
      mathematical challenges. This method encompasses a wide range of topics, from basic arithmetic
137   to advanced mathematics, including areas such as algebra, geometry, statistics, and calculus. (Wei
138   et al., 2023) boosts the reasoning capacity of LLMs by supplementing the output with a series of in-
139   termediate steps leading to the answer. Several approaches have been suggested to enhance the qual-
140   ity of these reasoning paths. For instance, complexity-based CoT (Fu et al., 2023) picks examples
141   with more steps as in-context demonstrations, demonstrating that prompting with additional reason-
142   ing steps improves performance. Self-consistency (Wang et al., 2023b) generates multiple reason-
143   ing paths and selects the final answer through majority voting. Another set of techniques involves
144   fine-tuning-based methods, which adapt open-source models (like LLaMA) using insights from ad-
145
      vanced closed-source LLMs (GPT-4, GPT-3.5-Turbo). (Magister et al., 2023) explore the transfer
      of reasoning abilities through knowledge distillation. (Yuan et al., 2024) advocate for the use of re-
146
      jection sampling fine-tuning (RFT) to enhance mathematical reasoning performance. WizardMath
147
      (Xu et al., 2024) introduces a reinforced evol-instruct method for strengthening reasoning abilities
148   through supervised fine-tuning and PPO training (Schulman et al., 2017). MAmmoTH (Yue et al.,
149   2024) integrates CoT and Program-of-Thought (Chen et al., 2023) rationales to teach LLMs how
150   to utilize external tools (such as a Python interpreter) for solving mathematical problems. (Wang
151   et al., 2023a) propose a constraint alignment loss for fine-tuning LLMs to improve calibration. Go-
152   ing beyond the improvement of mathematical abilities through fine-tuning, LLEMMA (Azerbayev
153   et al., 2024) introduces the Proof-Pile-2 dataset, which combines mathematical texts and code. By
154   continuously pre-training with Code Llama, the model is equipped to utilize Python interpreters and
155   formal theorem provers, showcasing remarkable performance on the MATH benchmark.

159   3.1   ROTARY P OSITION E MBEDDING (RO PE)
160
161   Transformer-based models rely on positional embeddings to encode position and relative location
      information of words in a text. Rotary Position Embedding (RoPE) is a position encoding technique


                                                        3
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


162
      proposed by (Black et al., 2022). Instead of adding positional embeddings or relative positional
163   embeddings to token embeddings, RoPE rotates the token embedding by a fixed factor (θ) in the
164   higher-dimensional space to encode relative positional embeddings. In other words, RoPE encodes
165   the absolute positions with a rotation matrix and meanwhile incorporates the explicit relative position
166   dependency in self-attention formulation. The intuition behind RoPE is that we can represent the
167   token embeddings as complex numbers and their positions as pure rotations that we apply to them.
168   If we shift both the query and key by the same amount, changing absolute position but not relative
169   position, this will lead both representations to be additionally rotated in the same manner. Thus, the
170   angle between them will remain unchanged and, thus, the dot product will also remain unchanged.
171
      By exploiting the nature of rotations, the dot product used in self-attention will have the property for
      preserving relative positional information while discarding absolute position.
172

174   3.2   M ODEL FLOP S U TILIZATION (MFU)
175
176   Model FLOPs Utilization (MFU) (Chowdhery et al., 2023) estimate is the ratio of the observed
177   throughput (tokens-per-second) relative to the theoretical maximum throughput of a system at peak
178   FLOPs. Model flops utilization (MFU) estimate the number of flops (floating point operations) done
179   per iteration. It quantifies how efficiently the GPUs are utilized in model training.
180
181   3.3   M AXIMAL U PDATE PARAMETERIZATION
182
183   As the size of large language models (LLMs) and the scale of the dataset used in pretraining are ex-
184   pensively large, it is not feasible to perform hyperparameter tuning in LLMs. Yang et al. (2021) used
185   a technique called maximal update parameterization (µP ) to transfer the hyperparameters learnt
186   from tuning of a small model to a larger model and found that the optimal hyperparameter values
187   become stable across neural network sizes when the models have been parameterized using (µP ).

342   to arrive at the final answer. The MATH dataset consists of 12,500 problems taken from high school
343
      math competitions. Each problem includes a step-by-step solution, allowing models to learn how to
      generate answer derivations and explanations. We used the following evaluation prompt for GSM8K
344
      test set for our math model.

347   request. ### Q:{question} ### A: Let’s think step by step. The answer is: ”

351   tion’, ‘USER:’, ‘USER’, ‘ASSISTANT:’, ‘ASSISTANT’, ‘Instruction:’, ‘Instruction’, ‘Response:’,

      10    C ONCLUSIONS AND F UTURE W ORK
495
      In this paper, we presented an alternative approach to the hypothesis that LLMs can reason and they
496
      should be improved with continual pretraining and then fine-tuning approaches via reinforcement
497
      learning or vanilla supervised instruction-tuning for mathematical reasoning. Instead of continual
498   pretraining of LLMs and then various fine-tuning approaches to improve the reasoning of LLMs, we
499   introduce an exclusive tiny mathematical auto regressive decoder-based language model, Paramanu-
500   Ganita 208M, which is pretrained from scratch only on mathematical mixed corpora of mathemati-
501   cal web text, textbooks, LATEX lecture notes, math related programming source code and Chain-of-
502   Thought (CoT) templatised mathematical question answers curated from various public sources for
503   a context size of 4096. We proposed and performed CoT instruction fine-tuning of Paramanu-Ganita
504   on MetaMathQA dataset. We evaluated our mathematical model on various grade level of difficulty
505   multiple standard benchmarks across grade school, high school, college level, and competitive ex-
506
      ams of SAT. GRE. GMAT and competition MATH benchmarks . We found that Paramanu-Ganita,
      despite being 35 times smaller than 7B LLMs, outperformed general LLMs and even LLMs built
507
      specially for mathematics, such as Minerva 8B and LLEMMA 7B, in accuracy metric in GSM8K
508
      and MATH benchmarks. We further evaluated our model on logical deductive reasoning LogiQA
509   benchmark, SAT and GRE/GMAT mathematical multi-choice questions subset of AGIEVAL and on
510   high school and college level math multi-choice questions from MMLU. We achieved comparable
511   or better results than the competing 7B family of LLMs.
512
      This exhaustive evaluation of our model and other LLMs on various level of difficulty of mathemat-
513
      ical and logical deductive reasoning questions takes us to the conclusion that a tiny language model,
514
      when sufficiently trained enough from scratch with a domain specialised tokenizer, offers a more
515   cost effective (using only 1 GPU, much less pretraining time, i.e., total 170 A100 hours) and envi-
516   ronmental friendly (very less carbon footprint due to 170 A100 training equivalent) approach to even
517   specialised domain expert models as it cut down the training cost by 135 times than the continual
518   pretraining of Llama for mathematical reasoning ((Azerbayev et al., 2024)) without compromising
519   the performance of tiny generative math model, Paramanu-Ganita, on mulitple math benchmarks.
520   We are the first to show that such an approach works without limiting ourselves to the presumption
521   that “bigger means stronger” and only working on top of LLMs without creating our own models
522   from scratch.
523   For future work, we would like to extend our pretraining corpus with ArXiv math papers and perform
524   additional reinforcement learning (RL) alignment such as DPO/PPO RL training with our mathe-
525   matical model to see how the performance of our model improves after additional RL alignment.
