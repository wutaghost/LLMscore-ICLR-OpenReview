009                                              A BSTRACT
010
011            Shannon Information theory has achieved great success in not only communication
012            technology where it was originally developed for but also many other science and
013            engineering fields such as machine learning and artificial intelligence. Inspired by
014            the famous weighting scheme TF-IDF, we discovered that Shannon information
015            entropy actually has a natural dual. To complement the classical Shannon infor-
016            mation entropy which measures the uncertainty we propose a novel information
017            quantity, namely troenpy. Troenpy measures the certainty and commonness of
018
               the underlying distribution. So entropy and troenpy form an information twin.
               To demonstrate its usefulness, we propose a conditional troenpy based weighting
019
               scheme for document with class labels, namely positive class frequency (PCF). On
020
               a collection of public datasets we show the PCF based weighting scheme outper-
021            forms the classical TF-IDF and a popular Optimal Transport based word moving
022            distance algorithm in a kNN setting with respectively more than 22.9% and 26.5%
023            classification error reduction while the corresponding entropy based approach com-
024            pletely fails. We further developed a new odds-ratio type feature, namely Expected
025            Class Information Bias(ECIB), which can be regarded as the expected odds ratio
026            of the information twin across different classes. In the experiments we observe
027            that including the new ECIB features and simple binary term features in a simple
028            logistic regression model can further significantly improve the performance. The
029
               proposed simple new weighting scheme and ECIB features are very effective and
               can be computed with linear time complexity.

      1   I NTRODUCTION
033
034
      The classical information theory was originally proposed by Shannon (1948) to solve the message
035
      coding problem in telecommunication. It turned out that it has far more profound impact beyond
036   communication theory, and it has shaped all aspects of our science, engineering and social science
037   by now. The core concept entropy was coined to measure the expected rareness or surprise of a
038   random variable X across its distribution. In the literature entropy is usually taken for granted as
039   the information in many people’s mind. The mutual information (MI) between two variables is the
040   difference of the entropy of a variable from its conditional entropy given the other variable. MI
041   maximization principle also has been studied and used widely in machine learning. Recently MI has
042   also been employed as part of the objective function for optimization in neural network models based
043   representation learning(Belghazi et al., 2018; Hjelm et al., 2019).
044   Along another line, weighting scheme has been used extensively in information retrieval tasks.
045   Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency(TF-IDF), a simple statistic heuristic proposed by
046   Sparck Jones (1972) has been widely used as a weighting method over half a century in information
047   retrieval and natural language processing. It weighs down a term if its document frequency increases
048   in the corpus, as it becomes less effective to distinguish from others when it gets popular and its
049   appearance brings less surprise in the sense of Shannon self-information. This simple but effective
050
      algorithm has achieved great success as a robust weighting scheme. Even today many search engines
      and digital database systems still employ TF-IDF as an important default algorithm for ranking.
051
052   In the past decades a few researchers have intensively investigated on it for a better theoretical
053   understanding of the underlying mechanism rather than a heuristic and intuition argument. Robertson
      (2004) justified it as an approximate measure of naive Bayes based probability relevance model in


                                                        1
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


054
      information retrieval. Some researchers tried to explain from the information theory point view.
055   Aizawa (2003) interpreted it as some probability weighted amount of information. Siegler & Witbrock
056   (1999) interpreted IDF for a term exactly as the mutual information between a random variable
057   representing a term sampling and a random variable representing a document sampling from a
058   corpus. Many other variants of the term frequency have been proposed in the literature. For example,
059   BM25(Robertson, 2009) based on probabilistic retrieval framework was further proposed and it has
060   been widely used by search engines to estimate the relevance of documents to a given search query. In
061   general the derived applications go far beyond text processing and information retrieval community.
062   The connection between TF-IDF and information theory mentioned above is quite motivating. This
063   makes us wonder if there are other simple and effective weighting schemes that can be established
064   from information theory. In order to achieve this goal, it turns out that we first developed a new
065   metric of information quantity for certainty, namely troenpy, a natural dual to entropy, and then used
066   it to derive a new type of weighting scheme which works very well in the extensive experiments as
067   we hoped.
068   In the following we will first introduce troenpy and its basic properties, and share some insights we
069   have for this innovation. Then for the classical task of supervised document classification, we will
070   develop a troenpy based weighting scheme for document representation. This weighting scheme
071   makes use of the documents class label distribution and helps improving the model performance very
072   significantly. Employing both entropy and troenpy, we will also define some new odds-ratio based
073   class bias features leveraging the document class label distribution. Finally evaluating under the
074   simple KNN and logistic regression settings, we show that the proposed new weighting scheme and
075
      new features are very effective and achieved substantial error reduction compared with the TF-IDF
      and a popular optimal transport based document classification algorithm on a collection of widely
076
      used benchmark data sets.

      The binary term frequency (BTF) is simply a binary feature for each term w. BTF(w) is 1 if w is

428   Baselines: For the evaluation of supervised documents classification on term frequencies and their
429   weighting, we include the classical TF-IDF document representation as a baseline. The pairwise
430   document distance in kNN setting is computed using the TF-IDF represented vectors. For comparison
431   purpose and reference, in the experiments we also include the result of a Word Moving Distance
      (WMD) based algorithm, namely HOFTT proposed by Yurochkin et al. (2019). It is a hierarchical

432
      optimal transport distance in the topic spaces of documents. We follow closely the experiment setting
433   of HOFTT.
434
435
      kNN based Classification: The features include term frequencies only. The goal is to validate the
436
      TF-PIDF weighting and compare with TF-IDF. The data pre-processing starts with removing the
437   frequent English words in the stop word list, which can be found in the above references. To ease
438   the kNN evaluation part, we fix the number of closest neighborhoods k=7 rather than dynamically
439   selecting the optimal k. We compute the integrated weighting PIDF as the product of PCF and IDF,
440   and compare with the IDF weighting for each term frequency. Using the TF-PIDF and TF-IDF, we
441   obtain the bag-of-words vector representation of each document and take their L2 normalization,
442   and then compute the document pairwise distance following the standard kNN procedures. Again
443   our main goal here is to assess if the proposed PCF weighting is effective and can help improve the
444   classical TF-IDF method. Also we want to evaluate the entropy based approach and see if it fails as
445
      we expected.
446
447   Logistic Regression based Classification: In this setting we simply replace the simple kNN
448   with a standard logistic regression model instead. In the experiments we use the Sklearn package
449   implementation with default settings. Here we have two goals to evaluate. First we need to evaluate
450
      if the models have performance improvement when the 2B features are included, compared with
      the models using only the TF-PIDF features. So we can assess if 2B features are effective for the
451
      document classification task. Second we want to evaluate the PCF weighting effect on the ECIB and
452
      BTF features both separately and jointly.
453
454   Here the data preprocessing is identical to the kNN classification settings above. We mainly consider
455   three types of features in the experiment, namely the TF-PIDF features, binary term features (BTF)
456
      and the ECIB features.

458   6    R ESULTS
459
460   kNN based Classification Experiments: In Figure 1 we can visually observe that the TF-PIDF
461   based kNN model uniformly outperformed the classical TF-IDF based kNN across all seven datasets
462   and the improvement is quite substantial for most cases with an average overall error reduction 22.9%.
463   Noticeably the R8 dataset achieves the most 53.4% error reduction. Compared with HOFFT, the
464   TF-PIDF achieves even more error reduction with the average of 26.5%. These uniform improvement
465   can be explained as the PCF weighting does effectively leverage the certainty and common similarity
466   of class label distributions across the corpus at a term level. For a term, the more PCF it has the
467   better prediction capacity it has. For example, the different news groups in Ng20 actually share many
468
      non-stop words in common and some groups are very relevant. The learned similarity information
      about one group is helpful at predicting a relevant group. We also observe only slight improvement
469
      on the Twitter and BBC sport datasets which might be simply due to the small sample sizes. The
470
      Twitter has 3115 samples and BBCsport has only 737 samples, which are quite small compared
471   with other datasets. Additionally, the Twitter sentiment dataset has three class labels consisting of
472   positive, neutral and negative. The extreme polarity of the classes is often consistent with the fact
473   that relatively less common description words are shared across the classes.
474
475   PCF, NCF, RNCF and NEGECF Comparison: To compare the performance of TF-PIDF, TF-
476   NIDF, TF-RNIDF and TF-NEGEIDF with the baseline TF-IDF, we did another experiment, where
477   the datasets with no given train/test splits are resampled fifty times. The result is reported in Figure 2.
478   We observed that the TF-PIDF is consistently effective on reducing the errors compared with TF-IDF
479   while all TF-NIDF, TF-RNIDF and TF-NEGEIDF fail in reducing the errors as expected. Note the
480   TF-RNIDF performs badly on the BBC dataset, which suggests that RNCF could be very unstable
481   for some datasets. This clearly justifies that our proposal of Troenpy is necessary and troenpy cannot
482   be substituted by entropy or its reciprocal or difference from a constant for the weighting role.
483
484   t-SNE: We also use the popular t-SNE by van der Maaten & Hinton (2008) to visualize the TF-IDF
485   and TF-PIDF classification effect on the R8 dataset. In Figure 4, the TF-PIDF appears to cluster
      relatively closer for each class labels and clusters are relatively separated from other cluster groups.

486
      Word Moving Distance Methods: In the experiments a modern Optimal Transport (OT) based
487   Word Mover’s Distance (WMD) approach HOFTT performs poorly compared with the TF-PIDF
488   weighting on all dataset except on R8 dataset, on which it is also outperformed by TF-PIDF em-
489   ploying the additional 2B features. However we are also aware of another advanced WMD method
490   Wasserstein-Fisher-Rao(WFR) developed by Wang et al. (2020), which uses the Fisher-Rao metric for
491   the unbalanced optimal transport problem. The reported result of WFR is comparable to our proposed
492   methods across the datasets. Unfortunately there are some version mismatch for some datasets as
493   well as slightly different sampling procedure for datasets with no pre-specified train-test splits, so we
494   did not include the corresponding result in our figures. Note also that the general Sinkhorn based
495
      algorithms for such OT optimization problems have relatively high computational complexity and so
      they are quite expensive on computational cost. While the proposed weighting scheme and ECIB
496
      features can be obtained in a single scan of the data and the time complexity is linear, they are fast
497
      and a lot cheaper on computational cost.
498
499   Logistic Regression based Experiments: In Figure 1 we observed the following: (1) for the same
500   TF-PIDF feature set, the logistic regression model uniformly outperforms the kNN approach across
501   all datasets. This is not surprised as the logistic regression optimizes the term coefficients for optimal
502   fitting the data while the kNN is rigid as given. (2) adding the 2B features of binary term frequency
503   (BTF) and expected class information bias (ECIB) further significantly reduces the errors on most
504   datasets. Compared with TF-IDF, the average error reduction is 35%. Compared with HOFFT, the
505   error reduction reaches 43.4%. For the BBC dataset we observed a relatively large error increase,
506   and we hypothesize that this may be due to the very small test sample size of the dataset.
507   In Figure 3 we reported the results of using BTF and ECIB features in the logistic regression setting.
508   We observed the following. Both BTF and ECIB features are quite effective when used individually
509   alone. ECIB performs better than BTF on all datasets except on the dataset of 20 Newsgroup, where
510   they are relatively close. Simply combining the two features together not necessarily always improves
511   the performance, instead it leads to slightly more errors on a couple of the datasets. We also observe
512   that applying the PCF weighting helps on majority of the cases. Visually the left three bars of light
513
      color represent 2B features without PCF weighting while the right three bars of darker color represent
      corresponding features with PCF weighting applied.

516   7    D ISCUSSION
517
518   The current work first proposed a new information measurement of certainty and an associated
519   weighting scheme leveraging the document label information, and further demonstrated its effective-
520   ness on several popular benchmark datasets of English text documents. We also gave a couple of
521
      measures which are easy to come up as candidates for certainty. Unfortunately neither of them is
      effective as a weighting scheme. Our troenpy is mathematically the canonical dual of entropy from
522
      the definition. For documents without label information available, the current proposal cannot apply
523
      directly. However, a few unsupervised tasks often can be reformulated into popular self-supervised
524   problems. The only difference from the above supervised setting is that the labels and features are
525   from the same space, and we can apply the developed methods to process without much difference.
526   In modern NLP community distributed representations of word tokens are widely used in language
527   models for various tasks. The proposed troenpy and the weighting schemes can actually be integrated
528   into neural network based large language models and can further lower the perplexities. For computer
529   vision, the corresponding methods can be suitably modified and then applied to vision language
530   models for downstream tasks. We will leave these as future work.
