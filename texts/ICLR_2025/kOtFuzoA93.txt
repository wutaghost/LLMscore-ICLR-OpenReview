011                                              A BSTRACT
012
013            This paper presents two models - called global and local models - of neural-
014            networks applicable to neural networks of arbitrary width, depth and topology,
015
               assuming only finite-energy neural activations. The first model is exact (un-
               approximated) and global (applicable for arbitrary weights), casting the neural
016
               network in reproducing kernel Banach space (RKBS). This leads to a width-
017
               independent (under usual scaling) bound on the Rademacher complexity of neural
018            networks in terms of the spectral-norm of the weight matrices, which is depth-
019            independent for sufficiently small weights. For illustrative purposes we consider
020            how this bound may be applied to untrained networks with LeCun, He and Glo-
021            rot initialization, discuss their connect to width and depth dependence in the
022            Rademacher complexity bound, and suggest a modified He initialization that gives
023            a depth-independent Rademacher complexity bound whp. The second model is
024            exact and local, casting the change in neural network function resulting from a
025            bounded change in weights and biases (ie. a training step) in reproducing kernel
026
               Hilbert space (RKHS) with a well-defined local-intrinsic neural kernel (LiNK).
               The neural tangent kernel (NTK) is shown to be a first-order approximation of the
027
               LiNK, so the local model gives insight into how the NTK model may be gener-
028
               alized outside of the over-parameterized limit. Analogous to the global model, a
029            bound on the Rademacher complexity of network adaptation is obtained from the
030            local model. Throughout the paper (a) dense feed-forward ReLU networks and (b)
031            residual networks (ResNet) are used as illustrative examples and to provide insight
032            into their operation and properties.

035   1   I NTRODUCTION
036
037   The application of reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS (Aronszajn, 1950)) and reproducing
038   kernel Banach space (RKBS (Lin et al., 2022; Der & Lee, 2007; Zhang et al., 2009; Zhang & Zhang,
039
      2012; Song et al., 2013; Sriperumbudur et al., 2011; Xu & Ye, 2014)) theory to the study of neural
      networks has a long history (Neal, 1996; Weinan et al., 2019; Parhi & Nowak, 2021; Lee et al., 2018;
040
      Matthews et al., 2018; Rahimi & Benjamin, 2009; Bach, 2014; 2017; Daniely et al., 2016; Daniely,
041
      2017; Cho & Saul, 2009; Bartolucci et al., 2021; Spek et al., 2022). Neural tangent kernels (NTKs)
042   are an exemplar of this approach, modeling training using a first-order (tangent) model. This approach
043   has led to a wide body of work on convergence and generalization (Du et al., 2019b; Allen-Zhu et al.,
044   2019; Du et al., 2019a; Zou et al., 2020; Zou & Gu, 2019; Arora et al., 2019b;a; Cao & Gu, 2019),
045   mostly focused on the wide-network (over-parameterized) limit. In parallel, there is a growing body
046   of work investigating the uniform convergence, complexity, and capacity of neural networks under
047   various regimes (Neyshabur et al., 2015; 2018; 2019; 2017; Harvey et al., 2017; Bartlett et al., 2017;
048   Golowich et al., 2018; Arora et al., 2018; Allen-Zhu et al., 2018; Dra╠ѕxler et al., 2018; Li & Liang,
049   2018; Nagarajan & Kolter, 2019b; Zhou et al., 2019).
050   Nevertheless, as noted in eg. (Arora et al., 2019b; Lee et al., 2019; Bai & Lee, 2019), there are
051   limits to the descriptive powers of NTK models. A gap has been observed between NTK-based
052   predictions and actual performance (Arora et al., 2019b; Lee et al., 2019), and the validity of NTK
053   models naturally breaks down outside of the over-parameterized limit, which has led to attempts to
      generalize NTK models outside of the over-parameterized - for example (Bell et al., 2023) use an

054
      exact pathwise kernel, while (Shilton et al., 2023) presented an exact model for dense feedforward
055   neural networks with smooth activations in RKBS, (Bartolucci et al., 2023; Sanders, 2020; Parhi &
056   Nowak, 2021; Unser, 2021; 2019) have explored links to RKBS, and (Bai & Lee, 2019) explored
057   higher-order approximations. However the assumptions made (smoothness, over-parameterization
058   etc) and approximations used limit application and raise the question of whether it is possible to
059   instead formulate exact, non-approximate models for neural networks that may be used for similar
060   ends. With regard to uniform convergence, some authors such as (Nagarajan & Kolter, 2019a) have
061   argued that such methods may be unable to explain generalization for neural networks at all due to the
062   behavior of the Rademacher complexity (Bartlett & Mendelson, 2002; Steinwart & Christman, 2008).
063   In this paper we simultaneously address two questions, namely: (1) is it possible to formulate an
064   exact (non-approximate) model for a wide class of neural networks, thereby avoiding entirely the
065   question of gaps between real performance and model prediction; and (2) can such a model be used
066   to derive general, non-vacuous, training-independent bounds on uniform convergence. We address
067   both questions with the following contributions:
068         1. Exact global model: for arbitrary neural network topologies with arbitrary weights and
069            biases and finite-energy activations, we construct a model that recasts neural networks and
070            bilinear products in a reproducing kernel Banach space (RKBS). This leads to:
071             (a) Rademacher Complexity Bound: using the global model, we bound the Rademacher
072                 complexity as a function of the spectral-norms of the weight matrices. We show that
073                 this bound is width-independent for standard weight-scaling, and depth-independent in
074                 the unbiased case for small weight matrices if the neural activations are L-Lipschitz.
075                 For networks satisfying this constraints, we prove that the Rademacher complexity is
076
                    bounded as RN (F) РЅц Рѕџ1N for training set size N .
077             (b) Rademacher Complexity of Randomly Initialized Networks: we discuss the
078                 Rademacher complexity of randomly initialized networks, in particular following Le-
079                 Cun, He and Glorot initialization. We analyse the width- and depth- dependence of the
080
                    Rademacher complexity bound for these initializations and present modified He and
                    Glorot initializations for which RN (F) РЅц Рѕџ1N with high probability.
081
082         2. Exact local model: again for arbitrary neural network topologies with arbitrary weights
083            and biases and finite-energy activations, we construct a model that recasts the change in
084
               neural network operation due to a (spectral-norm) bounded change in weights and biases in
               a reproducing kernel Hilbert space (RKHS), with a locally-intrinsic neural kernel (LiNK)
085
               defined by the network topology, neural activations and initial weights. This leads to:
086
                (a) Rademacher Complexity Bound: analogous to the global model, the local model
087
                    leads to a bound on the Rademacher complexity as a function of the spectral-norms of
088                 the change to the weight matrices.
089
                (b) Connection with NTK: we prove that the NTK is a first-order approximation LiNK
090                 in our local model, casting light on higher-order generalization of NTK models.

529
530
      In this paper we have presented two models of neural networks and neural network training for neural
531   network of arbitrary width, depth and topology. First we presented an exact (non-approximated)
532   RKBS model of the overall network in the form of a bilinear product between a data- and weight-
533   feature map. We have used this model to construct a bound on Rademacher complexity, and for
534   Lipschitz activations we have given conditions under which the Rademacher complexity is depth-
535   independent, and how diffierent initialization schemes can achieve RN (F) РЅц Рѕџ1N . The second model
536   we have presented models the change in the neural network due to a bounded change in weights
537   and biases. This model cast the change in RKHS with the local-intrinsic neural kernel (LiNK). We
538   have shown that this can be used to bound Rademacher complexity for network adaptation. We have
539   also discussed the role of weight initialization and implications for feedforward ReLU networks and
      residual networks (ResNets), and presented the local intrinsic neural kernel for the ResNet.
