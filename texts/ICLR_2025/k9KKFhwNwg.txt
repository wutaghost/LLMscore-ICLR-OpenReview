009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011
               With the rise of 5G and the IOT, the amount of data generated by IoT devices has
012
               exploded. Ethereum has become a secure tool for storing and trading IoT data due
013
               to its openness and tamper-proof nature. However, as Ethereum becomes more
014            and more popular, the Ethereum platform has also become a hotbed for various
015            types of cybercrimes, so ensuring the security of the Ethereum network is crucial.
016            Recently, algorithms based on GNN have been seen as an effective way to detect
017            abnormal nodes in the network. However, through analysis, this work finds that
018            its original network structure is not optimal, directly applied to the existing GNN
019            model with poor results. Meanwhile, it is understood that most of the current
020            GNNs rely on the message-passing principle, which leads to slow model train-
021            ing and inference, and large model size. It is quite challenging to directly apply
022
               traditional GNN algorithms in industrial scenarios with limited space and high
               feedback time requirements. This study proposes a knowledge distillation-based
023
               algorithm called Enhanced Graph-Less Neural Network .EGLNN estimates more
024
               realistic graph structures through Bayesian graph structure estimator and solves
025            the problem of large-scale GNN models being difficult to be widely applied in
026            industry through the faculty-student distillation method.

030   1   I NTRODUCTION
031
032   The Internet of Things (IoT)Xu et al. (2022) refers to a group or groups of tightly connected devices
033   that form a network through wireless or cable communication technology and work together to the
034   common objectives of its users.With the rapid development of 5G, the amount of data generated by
035   connected devices in the Industrial IoT (IIoT) paradigm has significantly increased within Industry
036   4.0. This data holds immense value across fields such as technology, economy, energy, and smart
037   cities. An rising number of consumers and businesses see the transactional value of IoT data. Conse-
038   quently, IoT data trading platforms have extensive applications and promising prospects.Currently,
039
      most IoT data trading platforms rely on third parties for data storage and transactionsMohamed &
      Mohamed (2019). However, this data trading approach is not inherently secure and may lead to data
040
      security issues such as industrial information leaks due to platform credibility concerns. Blockchain
041
      technologyWu et al. (2019), with its attributes of decentralization, transparency, and immutability,
042   offers a potential solution to these problems. As a result, an increasing number of researchers are ex-
043   ploring the utilization of blockchain technology for storing and trading IoT data without dependence
044   on third-party platformsEsposito et al. (2018).
045
      However, with the increasing popularity of Ethereum, the Ethereum network platform has also be-
046
      come a hotbed of various cybercrimes Wu et al. (2021). Among them, phishing scams are the
047
      most harmful among all kinds of cybercrimes Li et al. (2022a).This shows that the security of the
048   Ethereum platform has become a key issue affecting the development of the Ethereum ecosystem.
049   Currently, the most popular method for detecting abnormal nodes in the Ethereum network is to
050   convert the abnormal node detection problem into a node classification problem through network
051   representation learning, and identify abnormal nodes in the network by learning the characteristic
052   information of the network Lin et al. (2020). Wang et al.Wang et al. (2022) presented a hetero-
053   geneous network-based network embedding approach to mine implicit information in Ethereum
      transactions.

      In recent research on anomaly detection in the Ethereum network, algorithms applying network
055   representation learning and graph neural networks have made significant progress. However, what
056   we still need to think about is whether this type of GNN model is enough to solve the Ethereum
057   network security problem in the context of industrial information storage transactions? In this work,
058   the topology of the Ethereum transaction network was first analyzed, and it was found that the degree
059   of nodes in the Ethereum transaction network exhibits the characteristics of a long-tail distribution
060   Liu et al. (2020). This shows that the original transaction network structure of Ethereum may not
061   be optimal, making it difficult for the GNN model trained directly using the original graph to obtain
062   optimal network structure information.Secondly, by analyzing the principle of the GNN algorithm,
063
      we know that most of the current GNNs rely on the principle of message passing Yang et al. (2023b).
      This makes the training and execution speed of the GNN model slower and the size of the trained
064
      model larger. Although this feature of GNN can guarantee good results in node classification tasks,
065
      it is only suitable for scenarios with unlimited memory and speed, and it is not suitable for industrial
066   application scenarios.
067
068   After identifying these challenges, this study proposes a model called Enhanced Graphless Neural
069
      Network (EGLNN) to Solve Ethereum network security issues in industrial information storage and
      transaction scenarios. The model’s main idea is to transfer a large amount of work from the delay-
070
      constrained teacher GNN reasoning to the less time-sensitive student MLP by adopting the method
071
      of knowledge distillation (KD) Yang et al. (2023a). The purpose is to transfer the knowledge learned
072   in the teacher model from The typical GNN large model is extracted into a smaller MLP model, such
073   that the student MLP model can perform similarly to the instructor model and has a running speed
074   that the teacher model does not have, so that it can be applied to industrial platforms.
075
      The following are the three main contributions of this paper:
076
077   (1) This paper proposes a new GNN model, which gets rid of the scalability and deployment chal-
078   lenges brought about by GNN’s data dependence in industrial environments through knowledge
079   distillation, so that it can be deployed to applications that require fast reasoning. in latency-limited
080   applications.
081   (2) Through research, it was discovered that the original Ethereum transaction network structure is
082   not reliable. This work optimizes the graph structure based on Bayesian reasoning, and replaces the
083   initial node features with position encoding (PE) vectors to ensure that the knowledge transferred by
084   the teacher model to students only contains optimized graph structure information.
085   (3) Extensive experiments are conducted on the Ethereum dataset collected in this work, and the
086   experimental results show that EGLNN has better performance compared with state-of-the-art meth-
087   ods.

      2.1   G RAPH NEURAL NETWORK
095
096   Current graphic neural network algorithms can be roughly divided into the following five categories:
097   graphical network-based (GCN), graphical attention network (GAT), graphic self-coding (GAE)
098   based, graphic generating model (GAN) based and graphical pooled neural networks (GPN) based.
099
      GCN Kipf & Welling (2016) is one of the most classic and basic algorithms in graphic neural net-
100
      works, which updates the characteristic vector of each node by aggregating the characteristics of
101
      neighbouring nodes . Chen et al.Chen et al. (2019) proposed GIN, which is a graph convolutional
102   neural network based on graph isomorphism. It constructs the embedding vector of nodes by ac-
103   cumulating and splicing the feature vectors of neighboring nodes. The algorithm based on GAT
104   Perozzi et al. (2014) is a graph neural network algorithm based on attention mechanism. Different
105   from GCN, GAT can assign different weights to each neighbor node, and weight the contributions
106   of different neighbor nodes when computing node feature vectors. GATv2 Brody et al. (2021) is an
107   improved version based on variational dropout, which introduces a variational dropout mechanism
      based on Gaussian noise, which can improve the generalization ability of the model. The algorithm


                                                         2
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


108
      based on graph autoencoder (GAE) Schulman et al. (2015) is an algorithm that utilizes autoencoder
109   structure to learn graph representation. It treats the graph structure as input, and learns the low-
110   dimensional representation of the graph through the process of encoding and decoding, so as to
111   realize tasks such as graph classification and clustering. An algorithm based on a graph generative
112   model (GAN) Creswell et al. (2018) is a neural network model capable of generating data similar
113   to the input data. In graph neural networks, GANs can be used to generate graph structures that
114   meet specific property or structural requirements. Graph Pooling Neural Network (GPN) Gao et al.
115   (2021) is a GNN based on adaptive graph pooling, which can reduce the size of the graph, improve
116   computational efficiency, and enhance the model’s generalization efficacy. It is widely used in graph
117
      classification, Graph generation etc.
118   In summary, most current graph neural network algorithms still directly take the original topological
119   graph as input without considering the incompleteness of the graph structure, which greatly hamper
120   their performance in subsequent tasks.

122   2.2   KNOWLEDGE DISTILLATION
123
124   Traditionally, large neural networks need to be run on GPU or TPU to achieve good performance,
125   but large neural networks cannot be run directly on industrial IoT devices with limited computing
126   resources and storage space.Therefore, research on GNN reasoning acceleration has attracted in-
127
      creasing attention.Hinton et al. Hinton et al. (2015) first proposed a knowledge distillation(KD)
      algorithm.Its central concept is to extract the teacher model’s knowledge from a typical large model
128
      into a smaller one. In recent years, there are many related studies on knowledge distillation. For
129
      example, Zhang et al. Zheng et al. (2021) proposed a model called Cold brew to solve the node long-
130   tail distribution problem by distilling the structural embedding SE learned by the teacher GNN into
131   the student MLP, and Huo et al. Huo et al. (2023) proposed a The double distillation mode enhances
132   the ability of the student model by distilling topological features and attribute features separately.
133
      There are many advantages of knowledge distillation: 1) It can compress the size of the model and
134
      compress the knowledge of the large model into a small model while retaining the accuracy of the
135
      large model. 2) It can speed up the inference speed. 3) It can improve the model generalization
136   ability. In short, the knowledge distillation algorithm can solve the problem of deploying the GNN
137   algorithm on the Industrial Internet of Things very well. It can greatly reduce the computing and
138   storage costs of the deep neural network, improve the efficiency and performance of the model, and
139   accelerate the deployment and operation on various devices.

196
197   The main goal of the teacher GNN is to learn an embedding with node structural features and pass
198   it to the student MLP model through distillation. In this way, the student model, given only node
199   features as input, is able to generate embeddings similar to the topological feature embeddings
200   of the optimal graph. This process is designed to enable the student model to obtain topological
201   information from the estimated optimal graph learned by the teacher model and maintain similar
202   features when generating embeddings.
203   To effectively convey the topological information from the network to the student model, while min-
204   imizing the influence of node attribute characteristics, this work uses PE vectors to replace the initial
205   node features. Positional encoding vectors can effectively model positional information in sequence
206   data. In this work, PE vectors are designed to further extract the position information of nodes in the
207   graph structure. One-hot encoding is used to calculate PE vectors, and the dimensions of the vectors
208   are converted to the same dimensions as the initial node features through linear transformation to

508   5   C ONCLUSION
509
510
      This work employs the algorithm of graph neural networks to detect anomalous nodes in Ethernet
511   networks used for IoT data storage transactions. Different from traditional IoT anomaly detection
512   algorithms, this study innovatively introduces a knowledge distillation algorithm into the traditional
513   GNN algorithm. Through this approach, a more lightweight model that occupies less storage space
514   is successfully trained to solve the problem that large-scale GNN models are difficult to be widely
515   applied in industry. Specifically, this work proposes a model named EGLNN, which consists of a
516   teacher module and a student module. In the teacher module, a more realistic graph structure is
517   first estimated by a Bayesian inference-based approach, and then the structural feature embedding
518   (SFE) of the optimal graph is learned by replacing the attribute features of the nodes with the PE of
519
      the nodes on the premise of the estimated optimal graph. Next, the structural feature embeddings
      learned by the teacher model are transferred as knowledge to the student model through knowledge
520
      distillation, so that the student model has similar capabilities as the teacher model. Finally, the
521
      structural embeddings learned by the student model are fused with the attribute embeddings using the
522   attention mechanism to obtain the most favorable embeddings for the subsequent anomaly detection
523   task. The effectiveness of the EGLNN was confirmed through extensive experiments and verified the
524   greater advantages of the model over traditional GNN models for industrial IoT anomaly detection
525   tasks. Future work should study the dynamics and heterogeneity of IoT data storage transactions in
526   more depth, and extend EGLNN to dynamic networks containing time-series information, in order
527   to better adapt to the needs of real industrial scenarios.

      A.3   BASELINES
906
907   By analyzing the comparison of baseline methods with similar work, the EGLNN method is com-
908   pared to a number of other methods, including: (1) some traditional algorithms (e.g. DeepWalk,
909   GCN) (2) some latest algorithms for graph structure learning (e.g. GEN) (3) some algorithms for
910   anomalous node detection in IoT, blockchain (e.g. trans2vec AMBGAT)) (4) Some algorithms for
911   knowledge distillation (e.g. DistillGCN, T2-GNN).
912   • DeepWalk [28] : DeepWalk is an algorithm for learning graph structure embeddings by sampling a
913   sequence of nodes through a random walk and then mapping it into a low-dimensional vector space.
914
      • GCN [18] : GCN is a deep learning model for graph data that learns node representations by
915
      aggregating node neighbor information.
916
917   • GEN [54] : GEN is a graph structure learning algorithm. By constructing and utilizing multiple
      types of information, a more realistic graph structure can be estimated.

      • trans2vec [47] : trans2vec is a method for detecting blockchain phishing scams by mining transac-

920   vectors.

922
      • AMBGAT[13]: AMBGAT is an algorithm to secure data in IoT by augmenting node features with
      attentional power and estimating graph topologies that conform to basic facts using graph structure
923

927   • T2-GNN [53]: T2-GNN is an approach to avoid interference between features and structures by
928   designing feature-level and structure-level teacher models separately to provide targeted guidance
929   to student models (base GNNs, e.g., GCNs) through distillation.

931   A.4   IMPLEMENTATION DETAILS
932
933   The implementation of this model consists of a teacher model and a student model. Among them,
934   in the teacher model, the GAT network is used, and each layer includes K = 4 attention head calcu-
935   lations. The model is trained using a 0.01 learning rate, a 5e-5 weight decay, a 50% dropout rate per
936
      layer, and an Adam optimizer.
937   Additionally, the dimensions for the Bayesian graph structure estimator and the hidden layer em-
938   beddings in the teacher model are selected from the set {64, 128, 256, 512, 768}. The embedding
939   dimensions for the output layer of the teacher model are chosen from the set {2, 4, 8, 16, 32, 64}.
940   Feature map K ∈ {2, · · ·, 10}, threshold ε ∈ {0.1 · · · 0.9}. The number of iterations for teacher
941   model optimization is set to 400. In the student model, the MLP model used is two-layer, the
942
      embedding dimension of the hidden layer is selected from {128, 512}, the distillation temperature
      parameter T ∈ {1, 2, · · ·, 5}, the balance parameter λ ∈ {0.1, 0.2, · · ·, 0.9}. During model training
943
      and testing, only the classification performance of labeled nodes was taken into account, and the
944
      parameters with the highest performance were preserved for testing. Five independent experiments
945   utilizing distinct random seeds were conducted for each technique, and the average accuracy (Pre),
946   F1 score, and recall were reported to evaluate the performance of the models.
