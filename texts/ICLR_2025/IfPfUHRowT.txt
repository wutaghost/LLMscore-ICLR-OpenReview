011                                               A BSTRACT
012
013            Computed Tomography (CT) is a widely used non-invasive imaging technique for
014            materials at microscopic or sub-microscopic length scales in synchrotron radia-
015
               tion facilities. Typically, the object is rotated relative to the X-ray beam, and 2D
               projection images are recorded by the detector at different rotation angles. The 3D
016
               object is then reconstructed by combining these projections and solving a compu-
017
               tationally demanding inverse problem. The quality of the reconstructed image is
018            critical for scientific analysis and is influenced by various factors, including the
019            number of projections, exposure time or dose, and the reconstruction algorithm.
020            In this work, we develop a foundation model by integrating a Generative AI-based
021            Latent Diffusion Model (LDM) with physics-based domain knowledge. Specifi-
022            cally, we first develop and incorporate a set of loss functions into our LDM that
023            accurately capture the physical properties of the CT data acquisition process. We
024            demonstrate that adding these loss functions aids in stable training of the autoen-
025            coder in the LDM and improves its accuracy. The autoencoder and the diffusion
026
               model of the LDM are trained with real-world experimental data. Collecting real-
               world experimental data from synchrotron beamlines is often time-consuming and
027
               challenging. We demonstrate that the autoencoder trained with a combination of
028
               real-world experimental data and phantom shapes features also performs similarly
029            to the autoencoder trained with real-world data. Second, we introduce a novel im-
030            age blending method to combine the LDM’s generated output with the original,
031            extremely sparse sinogram data. Since our model integrates physics-guided loss
032            functions focused on CT data acquisition, it simplifies the creation of downstream
033            tasks and facilitates the adaptation of new features from different experiments.
034            We demonstrate improvements of up to 23.5% in SSIM for sinogram quality and
035            13.8% for reconstructed image quality compared to state-of-the-art techniques.

133
134   facts, the reconstruction results are unsatisfactory without prior image information. A constrained
135   total variation (TV) based iterative image reconstruction algorithm has also been developed (Sidky
136
      & Pan, 2008). Despite effectiveness of TV regularization in preserving edge and restoring smooth

137
      lem using data-fidelity term with appropriate priors has been done (Kudo et al., 2013; Vandeghinste

533   5       C ONCLUSION
534
535   This paper presents a novel method combining domain-specific physics knowledge with the SDM
536   for inpainting in the sinogram domain from real-world CT experimental data. It introduces the
537   physics loss functions in the sinogram as well as in the reconstructed object domains and a novel
538   blending algorithm. It develops a foundation model pre-trained on random masking and fine tunes
539   it on tasks such as sparsely acquired data and missing wedge problems. Our model outperforms the
      state-of-the-art baselines by 23.5% for sinogram and 13.8% reconstructed object in terms of SSIM.
