009                                                   A BSTRACT
010
011             Continual learning based on prompt tuning creates a key-value pool, where these
012             key-value pairs are called prompts. Prompts are retrieved using input images as
013             queries and input into a frozen backbone network. It requires training only a few
014             parameters to quickly adapt to downstream tasks. Compared to other traditional
015             continual learning methods, it is more effective in resisting catastrophic forget-
016             ting. However, the effectiveness of these methods heavily depends on the selection
017             strategy. Most existing methods overlook the model plasticity since they focus on
018             solving the model’s stability issues, leading to a sharp decline in performance for
019
                new tasks in long task sequences of incremental learning. To address these limi-
                tations, we propose a novel prompt-based continual learning method called TIPS,
020
                which mainly consists of two modules: (1) design a novel two-level prompt se-
021
                lection strategy combined with a set of adaptive weights for sparse joint tuning,
022             aiming to improve the accuracy of prompt selection; (2) design a semantic dis-
023             tillation module that enhances the generalization ability to unknown new classes
024             by creating a language token and utilizing the encapsulated semantic information
025             of class names. We validated TIPS on four datasets across three incremental sce-
026             narios. Our method outperformed the current state of the art (SOTA) by 2.03%,
027             4.78%, 1.18%, and 5.59% on CIFAR (10 tasks), ImageNet-R (20 tasks), CUB (10
028             tasks), and DomainNet (20 tasks). Notably, our approach consistently surpasses
029             or matches SOTA in all settings, maintaining stable prompt selection accuracy
030
                throughout multiple incremental learning sessions.

033   1    I NTRODUCTION
034
035   Humans are good at gaining new knowledge while remembering past information. However, ma-
036   chine learning finds it hard to copy this ability (Van de Ven & Tolias, 2019; Masana et al., 2022).
037   Neural networks often forget learned knowledge when learning new tasks, which is called catas-
038
      trophic forgetting (De Lange et al., 2021; McCloskey & Cohen, 1989). Continual learning (CL)
      (Mehta et al., 2023; Masana et al., 2022) achieves great success in solving catastrophic forgetting
039
      and has received increasing attention in recent years (Parisi et al., 2019). To handle catastrophic for-
040
      getting, CL aims at smoothly integrating new tasks into a single model while preventing catastrophic
041   forgetting of previously learned knowledge. However, maintaining this balance (i.e., the stability-
042   plasticity dilemma (Arani et al., 2022; Wang et al., 2022a)) poses great challenges for effective CL.
043
044
      Traditional from-scratch training methods in CL aim to prevent knowledge forgetting by protecting
      important past parameters. For example, Elastic Weight Consolidation (EWC) (Kirkpatrick et al.,
045
      2017), uses regularization constraints on the loss function for new tasks to protect previously ac-
046
      quired knowledge from being disrupted by new information. DER (Yan et al., 2021) employs a
047   dynamic network architecture that creates a distinct parameter space for each task, while freezing
048   the existing parameters to ensure they remain unchanged, thereby achieving effective parameter iso-
049   lation. However, these methods not only require multiple iterations to converge but also rely on
050   rehearsal strategy1 to perform well. It poses significant challenges in scenarios that require rapid
051   generalization or involve privacy concerns.

        091    Although important progress has been achieved by existing prompt-based CL methods, they have the
        092    following drawbacks to address: (1) Most of prompt-based CL methods (e.g., (Wang et al., 2022b;
        093
               Chen et al., 2023)) heavily depend on selection strategy. During the inference phase, task identifiers
               are not available, so only the selection strategy can identify the appropriate prompt from training.
        094
               Conventional selection methods (as shown in Figure 1) that measure similarities between images
        095
               and prompts show a significant decrease in the probability of accurately selecting the correct prompt
        096    as the number of task sessions and prompts increases. (2) Many prompt-based CL methods (e.g.,
        097    (Wang et al., 2023a; Smith et al., 2023)) focus on solving the forgetting problem (stability) while
        098    neglecting the model’s generalisation ability (plasticity), resulting in a limited understanding of new
        099    tasks by specific task prompts within a few model iterations.

               In this paper, we propose a novel prompt-based CL framework, as illustrated in Figure 2. Our goals
        101
               include: (1) designing a stable prompt selection strategy to prevent a significant decline in accu-
        102    racy when selecting the correct prompt as the size of the prompt set increases; and (2) developing
        103    a simple and effective module to enhance the model’s understanding of new tasks and improve its
        104    plasticity. To achieve these goals, we construct a two-level prompt. The first-level prompt consists
        105    of a set of learnable parameters and text embeddings of class labels, it improves the probability of
        106    matching image embeddings with class prototypes by learning the high-level semantic features of
        107    images, thereby addressing the instability of the prompt selection strategy. Moreover, we propose a
               set of adaptive weight parameters, with each weight corresponding to a prompt, which dynamically

116         • We designed a two-level prompt selection strategy that leverages the semantic consistency

132   2   R ELATED W ORK
133
134   Continual learning from scratch. The first category is regularization-based (Wu et al., 2019; Kirk-
135   patrick et al., 2017; Chaudhry et al., 2018) CL, where the primary idea is to employ a penalty
136   mechanism to ensure that important parameters remain unchanged. The second category is replay-
137   based (Rebuffi et al., 2017; Li & Hoiem, 2017; Feng et al., 2024a; Douillard et al., 2020) CL, which
138   allocates extra storage space for a few exemplars of previous tasks. When learning a new task,
139   the system learns all the exemplars from the storage space, as well as the examples from the new
140   task. The third category is parameter-isolation-based (Feng et al., 2024b; Yan et al., 2021; Douil-
141
      lard et al., 2022) CL, which allocates independent learning parameters for each task. While some
      methods employ sophisticated compression strategies to reduce the number of model parameters,
142
      they often neglect rapid generalization. Consequently, these models usually require a large number
143
      of iterations to converge.
144
145   Prompt-Based Continual Learning. Prompt-based CL keeps the pre-trained model’s weights un-
146   changed while adding additional learnable prompt tuning modules to generalize to downstream
147
      tasks. These works (Wang et al., 2022b;a; Smith et al., 2023) create a prompt pool where each
      prompt contains a learnable index key and a prompt value. They uses a cosine distance function to
148
      search for the nearest in the existing pool, then optimizes weights using cross-entropy loss. In re-
149
      cent, some works (Zhou et al., 2022; Wang et al., 2023a) integrated CLIP (Radford et al., 2021) with
150   a backbone network to form a framework, inspiring recent innovations. For example, Chen et al.
151   (2023) enhances model diversity using CLIP to replace identical pre-trained models for construct-
152   ing different classifiers, dynamically combining logits during inference for comprehensive decision-
153   making. Zheng et al. (2023) applied merging techniques to CLIP models to maintain their zero-shot
154   performance during CL. Although this merging method does not require saving all historical models,
155   deciding which parameters to merge remains a challenge.
156   The key difference between our method and the aforementioned methods is that our approach does
157   not require selecting or merging parameters. Our method and others are based on the same pretrained
158   backbone (e.g., CLIP encoder). However, our approach achieves comparable or even better results
159   without necessitating fine-tuning the parameters of the backbone network. Moreover, our work
160   does not require any additional storage space for previous instances of old classes by leveraging the
161   prior knowledge of the powerful pre-trained model, making our approach suitable for data privacy
      scenarios.

189   3.1     P RELIMINARY
190
191   Problem Definition. The goal of CL is to acquire knowledge from a stream of data composed
192
      of T non-overlapping sequential datasets, denoted as D = {D1 , D2 , . . . , Dt }. Each dataset Dt
      corresponds to a specific  task t, which can be represented as a collection of data for that specific
193
      class, i.e., Dt = i (xtc , yct ), contains data samples xtc ∈ X and corresponding yct ∈ Y , where
                         S
194
      c denotes the c-th class within task t, The objective is to train a mapping function fθ : X →
195   Y parameterized by θ to handle the T incremental tasks. In the inference phase, f predicts the
196   corresponding label y based on task-agnostic samples x. Note that during the training phase of the
197   current task, data from previous tasks is not accessible.
198
199   3.2     OVERALL F RAMEWORK
200
201   In our work, we introduce a new prompt-based CL method called TIPS, which aims to address
202   the instability of the prompt selection strategy and the low plasticity of the model. Specifically,
203   TIPS consists of two modules: Two-level prompt (TP) and semantic knowledge distillation. As
204   shown in Figure 2, TP is based on a two-level prompt selection strategy. The first-level prompt
205
      generates a key for retrieval by combining class labels with learnable context parameters and feeding
      them into the CLIP text encoder. Then, the visual embedding of the input image are used as a
206
      query, which is matched with the key. Finally, we apply adaptive weight modulation to the second-
207
      level prompt, which enables joint sparse prompt tuning, thereby reducing prompt selection errors
208   in long incremental task sequences. To facilitate the model’s adaptation to CL incremental tasks, a
209   semantic knowledge distillation module was proposed. The semantic knowledge of the current class
210   is transferred to a learnable language token by using a simple distillation function.
211

452   which incorporates all three modules (as shown in the last row), achieved the best results. Therefore,
453   the experiments validate the effectiveness of the proposed three modules.
454   (2) Selection strategy analysis. We report the performance of the prompt selection strategy and
455   the results on both old and new classes for TIPS compared to other methods in Figure 3. In the

457   correct prompt, even after 10 rounds of incremental learning. We also compare the average accuracy

472   5     C ONCLUSION
473
474   In this paper, we propose a novel prompt-based continual learning framework without relying on
475   a pre-set database or manually designed prompts. The proposed framework employs a two-level
476   prompt selection strategy, maintaining the stability of prompt selection by learning the consistency
477   between high-level image information and label semantic information in long sequence settings. To
478   achieve a balance between stability and plasticity, we design a semantic knowledge distillation mod-
479   ule to reduce the impact of old class retention on the learning of new classes. Extensive experiments
480   on four public datasets demonstrate that our method achieves state-of-the-art performance and is
481   independent of any particular dataset or CL environment.
482   Border impact: The proposed framework learns from a prompt pool and label predictor, making it
483   applicable to downstream tasks such as continual fine-tuning of large language models. However, the
484   goal of our work is to provide a general framework, and the trained prompt pool may be influenced
485   by inherent data biases. Therefore, future work could extend our framework to other application
      scenarios.
