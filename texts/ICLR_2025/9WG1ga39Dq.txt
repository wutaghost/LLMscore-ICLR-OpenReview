011                                               A BSTRACT
012
013            This paper extends the traditional Optimal Transport (OT) to the Consistent Optimal
014            Transport (COT), which accommodates more than two measures while maintaining
015
               transport consistency. The problem is formulated by minimizing the transport
               costs between pairs of measures and enforcing cycle-consistency among them. We
016
               introduce both the Monge and Kantorovich formulations of COT and derive an
017
               approximate solution through the addition of entropic and consistency regulariza-
018            tion. An iterative projection algorithm, RCOT-Sinkhorn, is developed to enhance
019            the Sinkhorn algorithm. In the visual multi-point matching task, our COT solver
020            directly employs the cosine distance between learned point features from existing
021            graph matching neural networks as the pairwise cost, achieving significant improve-
022            ment in learning multiple matchings without further feature training. Additionally,
023            based on COT, we present a new formulation for the Travelling Salesman Problem
024            (TSP), termed TSP-COT. Regularization is used to relax the optimization, and the
025            modified RCOT-Sinkhorn algorithm is applied to obtain the probability matrix of
026
               TSP routing. A post-process search method is then utilized to determine the TSP
               routes, and experiments validate the superiority of our approach. The code will be
027
               made available.

      Cycle-Consistency for Visual Point Matching (PM) (Sarlin et al., 2020; Sun et al., 2021). The
128   idea of cycle consistency is widely considered in learning and vision. For example, it is applied
129   in multiple graph matching (Wang et al., 2021; Bernard et al., 2019; Tourani et al., 2023), image
130   matching (Sun et al., 2023; Bernard et al., 2019), and shape matching (Bhatia et al., 2023; Bernard
131   et al., 2019). These multiple matching instances with cycle-consistency in various fields motivate us
132   to investigate whether multiple transportation can be performed with cycle-consistent constraints in
133   the Optimal Transport problem. Thus, in this paper, we elaborate on the concept of cycle-consistency
134   in OT and introduce the definition of COT in Sec.3.1. Visual PM is a prominant area in vision
135   that aims to find optimal point correspondences between images, with wide applications, such as
136
      3D structure estimation and camera pose estimation. Graph matching (GM) (Caetano et al., 2009)
      builds upon PM and treats the point sets as graphs, aiming to find the optimal node correspondences
137
      between graph-structured data. GM can be typically formulated as Lawler’s Quadratic Assignment
138
      Problem (LAP) (Crama & Spieksma, 1992), which is known to be NP-hard and requires expensive
139   and complex solvers. Recent works (Wang et al., 2019; Yu et al., 2019) have focused on learning
140   node features using supervised or unsupervised loss functions. In this paper, our main focus is
141   on multi-point matching (Swoboda et al., 2019), where we utilize the trained models (Wang et al.,
142   2019) to extract point features and perform inference on testing data, which emphasizes the cycle-
143   consistency among multiple images, enabling more robust and accurate matching results. Traditional
144   methods apply cycle-consistency only to the model’s loss function to enhance feature learning on the
145   training set, while not utilizing cycle-consistency during inference on the test set. In contrast, our
146   method employs a training-free approach that assumes consistency is satisfied on the test set, using
147
      this prior information to improve performance during inference.
148   Travelling Salesman Problem. In recent years, there has been a surge of interest in leveraging
149   machine learning techniques to address the Travelling Salesman Problem (TSP). The most advanced
150   state-of-the-art methods do not generate the solution directly but instead output a heatmap to indicate
151   the probability of an edge being part of the ground truth routes. Various search methods are utilized to
152   obtain the final solution. The approaches for heatmap (i.e. probability matrix for routing) generation
153
      can be categorized into supervised learning, unsupervised learning, and reinforcement learning.
      Supervised methods, such as GCN (Joshi et al., 2019) and ATT-GCN (Fu et al., 2021), utilize labeled
154
      TSP instances to generate heatmaps. Similarly, DIFUSCO (Sun & Yang, 2023) employs diffusion
155
      models for heatmap construction, while T2TCO (Li et al., 2024) enhances these maps using learned
156   distributions for gradient-based searches. Unsupervised learning methods, like UTSP (Min et al.,
157   2024), train models without explicit labels, focusing on identifying Hamiltonian cycles through
158   Scattering Attention Graph Neural Networks (SAGs). Reinforcement learning strategies, including
159   those advanced by DIMES (Qiu et al., 2022), optimize sampling efficiency within reinforcement
160   frameworks. As for search methods based on generated heatmaps, greedy algorithms remain prevalent,
161   ranking edges based on their probability scores and adding them iteratively without causing conflicts
      (Graikos et al., 2022). Monte Carlo Tree Search (MCTS) (Fu et al., 2021), known for its robustness,

      simulates multiple scenarios to refine the path continuously. Additionally, local search techniques
163   like 2-opt (Croes, 1958) offer further refinements by swapping edge pairs to improve route efficiency.
164   Amidst this landscape, (Xia et al., 2024) proposed SoftDist, a method that stands out due to its
165   simplicity and effectiveness. By applying the softmax function to the distance matrix and MCTS as

170   new heatmap for enhancing the simple SoftDist.

408
      matrix exponentiation and matrix multiplication of order K. We can compute the matrix powers
409
      using eigenvalue decomposition, which reduces the time complexity of computing matrix powers
410   within the loop to O(K 3 ), which is the same as matrix multiplication. Therefore, the overall time
411   complexity of the algorithm is O(K 5 ). Although our algorithm has a high time complexity, it is a
412   training-free approach. In comparison to retraining neural network parameters, our method holds

532
533
      We have introduced a generalized form for Consistent Optimal Transport (COT), which enables trans-
534   portation among multiple measures while (softly) preserving cycle-consistent constraints. Besides,
535   we propose a new formulation of TSP based on TSP-COT, which helps the calculation of heatmap
536   for solving with regularization on TSP-COT. For the limitation, our RCOT-Sinkhorn introduces a
537   hyperparameter δ ′ for tuning. Similarly, for the regularized TSP-COT, the number of hyperparameters
538   increases by K i.e. the number of measurements. For future work, we believe that a Schrödinger
539   bridge based on cycle-consistency would be a promising direction, which will be the focus of our
      next efforts.
