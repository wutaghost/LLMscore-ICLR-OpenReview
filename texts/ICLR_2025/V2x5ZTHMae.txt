011                                               A BSTRACT
012
013            Diffusion models have emerged as a powerful foundation model for visual genera-
014            tion. With an appropriate sampling process, it can effectively serve as a generative
015
               prior to solve general inverse problems. Current posterior sampling based methods
               take the measurement (i.e., degraded image sample) into the posterior sampling to
016
               infer the distribution of the target data (i.e., clean image sample). However, in this
017
               manner, we show that high-frequency information can be prematurely introduced
018            during the early stages, which could induce larger posterior estimate errors dur-
019            ing the restoration sampling. To address this issue, we first reveal that forming
020            the log posterior gradient with the noisy measurement ( i.e., samples from a dif-
021            fusion forward process) instead of the clean one can benefit the reverse process.
022            Consequently, we propose a novel diffusion posterior sampling method DPS-CM,
023            which incorporates a Crafted Measurement (i.e., samples generated by a reverse
024            denoising process, compared to random sampling with noise in standard methods)
025            to form the posterior estimate. This integration aims to mitigate the misalignment
026
               with the diffusion prior caused by cumulative posterior estimate errors. Exper-
               imental results demonstrate that our approach significantly improves the overall
027
               capacity to solve general and noisy inverse problems, such as Gaussian deblur-
028
               ring, super-resolution, inpainting, nonlinear deblurring, and tasks with Poisson
029            noise, relative to existing approaches.

032   1   I NTRODUCTION
033
034   Diffusion models (Ho et al., 2020) have achieved remarkable generative performance on images
035   (Amit et al., 2021; Baranchuk et al., 2021; Brempong et al., 2022), videos (Singer et al., 2022; Wu
036   et al., 2023), audios (Popov et al., 2021; Yang et al., 2023a), natural language (Austin et al., 2021;
037   Hoogeboom et al., 2021; Li et al., 2022) and molecular generation (Hoogeboom et al., 2022; Jing
038   et al., 2022). Besides its strong modeling capacity for complex and high dimensional data, diffusion
039
      models have exhibited a strong generative prior to form the diffusion conditional sampling (Song
      et al., 2020) that can be harnessed for diffusion posterior sampling. In the context of noisy inverse
040
      problems, this sampling process effectively approximates precise data distributions from noisy and
041
      degraded measurements. Noisy inverse problems, such as super-resolution, inpainting, linear and
042   nonlinear deblurring, are targeted to restore an unknown image x from its noise corrupted mea-
043   surement y given the corresponding forward measurement operators A(·) : Rn → Rm . Recently,
044   Diffusion models have been extensively utilized for these tasks (Zhu et al., 2023; Song et al., 2022;
045   Wang et al., 2022), offering a robust framework for reconstructing high-quality images from de-
046   graded measurements.
047
      Current diffusion-based methods generally employ two distinct strategies to solve inverse problems.
048   The first strategy is to train problem-specialized diffusion models (Saharia et al., 2022; Whang et al.,
049   2022; Luo et al., 2023; Chan et al., 2023) given measurements and clean image pairs. In contrast,
050   methods of the second strategy only capitalize on problem-agnostic pre-trained diffusion models to
051   benefit the zero-shot diffusion restoration sampling by posterior estimate (Song et al., 2023a; Rout
052   et al., 2024; Peng et al., 2024; Mardani et al., 2023) or enforcing data consistency (Chung et al.,
053   2022b). In this paper, we concentrate on the second manner, posterior estimate for sampling, to
      solve noisy inverse problems universally.

054
      Posterior estimate based methods enable controllable generations (Dhariwal & Nichol, 2021) via
055   diffusion conditional reverse-time SDE (Song et al., 2020), such as classifier guidance (Dhariwal &
056   Nichol, 2021), loss-guided diffusion (Song et al., 2023b). Applying Bayesian, the gradient of log
057   posterior ∇xt log pt (xt | y) can be easily adopted into conditional reverse-time SDE sampling as a
058   log likelihood gradient term ∇xt log p (y | xt ) and an unconditional prior term ∇xt log pt (xt ). In
059   the context of solving inverse problems, however, p (y | xt ) is an intractable distribution due to the
060   unclear dependency between the measurement y and the diffusion generation xt at time t. To tackle
061   this issue, existing posterior estimate methods for inverse problems, such as Diffusion Posterior
062   Sampling (DPS (Chung et al., 2022a)), form the measurement model p (y | x̂0 ) as the likelihood
063
      estimate, where x̂0 is the denoising prediction given diffusion intermediate output xt . This process
      maps x̂0 into the measurement y’s space, which can be interpreted as a reconstruction loss guidance
064
      to push A (x̂0 ) close to y, while narrowing the gap between xt and the clean image x.
065
066   However, via empirical examples in Section 3.1, we show that solving inverse problems in the man-
067   ner of diffusion posterior sampling follows a similar pattern of diffusion reverse process (Yang et al.,
068   2023b), i.e., focusing on low-frequency recovery at first and posing increasing attention on high-
069
      frequency generation in the late stages. With such observation, the log posterior gradient estimate
      ∇xt log p (y | x̂0 ) in DPS with sharp measurement y will easily introduce abrupt high-frequency
070
      gradient signals for the subsequent step, which unfits the appropriate input pattern for the pre-trained
071
      model sθ (xt , t) during the early stages with large t. In fact, Song et al. (2023b) also note that DPS
072   significantly miscalculates the scale of the guidance term with different variance levels which re-
073   sults in accumulated errors in posterior sampling. In Section 3.1, we have the similar observations
074   that DPS amplifies posterior sampling errors. We also find that applying the likelihood estimate
075   p (yt | x̂0 ) with randomly sampled noisy measurement yt instead of the clean measurement y in
076   DPS for each timestep t leads to a smaller approximation error during the early stages, and thus ben-
077   efits the restoration generation. Posterior approximation with noisy measurement yt at timestep t,
078   compared with the clean one, has the advantage that it adaptively matches the frequency pattern of
079   the diffusion model’s generation at the timestep t.
080   Therefore, with this insight, we propose the posterior approximation that leads to less high-
081   frequency signal recovery during the early stages. Specifically, we propose the Diffusion Posterior
082   Sampling with Crafted Measurements (DPS-CM), which can introduce less biased posterior es-
083   timate by combining crafted measurement yt , the intermediate generation of another diffusion
084   reverse-time trajectory {yt }Tt=0 from posterior p (yt | y). As {yt }Tt=0 shares a similar frequency
085   distribution pattern with the target generation trajectory {xt }Tt=0 , i.e., low-frequency recovery at
086
      first, the approximated log likelihood gradient ∇xt log p (ŷ0 | x̂0 ) will bring in less high-frequency
      gradient signal and thus benefit the subsequent generations. Besides, leveraging crafted measure-
087
      ments yt brings the lower bias compared with directly using randomly sampled noisy measure-
088
      ment yt . Our extensive experiments results on various noisy linear inverse problems, e.g. super-
089   resolution, random masked/fixed box inpainting, Gaussian/Motion deblurring, and nonlinear inverse
090   problems such as nonlinear deblurring, demonstrate that the proposed DPS-CM significantly outper-
091   forms existing unsupervised methods on both FFHQ (Karras et al., 2019) and ImageNet (Deng et al.,
092   2009) datesets while keeping the algorithm simplicity.

      We propose DPS-CM, which perform diffusion posterior sampling from p (xt | yt ) with Crafted
      Measurement yt belonging to another diffusion reverse trajectory {yt }Tt=0 instead of the vanilla
154
      input y. Specifically, applying Eq.4 on xt and yt , we incorporate the tractable p (ŷ0 | x̂0 ) as the
155
      likelihood approximation of p (yt | xt ) to enable the posterior sampling from p (xt | yt ). DPS-
156   CM can be interpreted as pushing the bond between two noisy approximation ŷ0 and x̂0 as the
157   measurement model ŷ0 = A (x̂0 ). While ŷ0 is progressively recovered to the input measurement y
158   via its diffusion denoising process, x̂0 is expected to develop into the target ground truth x gradually
159   during the synchronous reverse process with the measurement bond ŷ0 = A (x̂0 ). In Section 3.1,
160   we explore the advantage of posterior sampling utilizing noisy measurements yt sampled from the
161   diffusion forward process over using the clean input y in reducing intermediate generation errors in
      diffusion posterior sampling. In Section 3.2, we formulate the DPS-CM in detail.

      5    C ONCLUSION
531
      This work introduces Diffusion Posterior Sampling with Crafted Measurements to solve general and
532
      noisy inverse problems. DPS-CM leverages an additional diffusion reverse process to form a crafted
533
      measurement trajectory {yt }0t=T with extensive noise levels to perform a frequency-adaptive and
534   less biased diffusion posterior sampling with the log posterior gradient ∇xt log p (xt | yt ) incorpo-
535   rating yt . Via experimental results, we show that our DPS-CM can generate improved high-quality
536   restorations compared with recent state-of-the-art and also latent diffusion approaches. For the po-
537   tential future works, following the idea of DPS-CM, improved design on forming more beneficial
538   crafted measurement, e.g., additional inverse problem-related guidance for yt diffusion sampling
539   besides the reconstruction loss guidance, is a crucial direction to explore. We discuss the limitation
      of DPS-CM and solutions in Appendix D.

790   D     L IMITATIONS
791
792
      As the high-quality generations of DPS-CM benefit from the interplay between two posterior sam-
793   pling processes for the crafted measurement’s generation and x’s restoration, the running time of
794   DPS-CM is nearly twice of DPS’s sampling as shown in Tabel 7. However, DPS-CM shows better or
795   comparable performance compared with latent diffusion based methods such as PSLD, Resample,
796   and methods with Monte Carlo such as FPS-SMC and LGD-MC with less running time. Besides, we
797   try to improve the efficiency of DPS-CM by setting µ = 0 after t during the late stages of sampling
798   as the posterior sampling with crafted measurements shows similar ϵ-prediction errors with DPS
799   during the late stages in Fig. 1b. We set the t as 400 and conduct an experiment for random inpaint-
800   ing on FFHQ dataset. From Table 9, the accelerated variant achieves comparable performance and
801
      has the over 20% efficiency improvement. Besides, the core idea of DPS-CM is based on insight-
      ful observations in posterior sampling methods. We can potentially combine crafted measurements
802
      with a more efficient method, such as DiffPIR, to achieve high-quality reconstructions and maintain
803
      efficiency. Another concern for DPS-CM is how it deals with the inverse problems with the targets
804   and measurements from very different modalities, such as phrase retriever. In this case, utilizing
805   the same pre-trained diffusion model to construct the crafted measurements for DPS-CM sampling
806   can not generate stable restorations. One solution is to train a smaller diffusion model on the mea-
807   surement modality for crated measurements. It should be noted that most of the current zero-shot
808   methods are designed for typical inverse problems in which the target and measurement lie on the
809   close manifold and perform unstably on tasks similar to phrase retriever. Thus, fitting a small model
      to facilitate DPS-CM for such tasks is acceptable and reasonable.
