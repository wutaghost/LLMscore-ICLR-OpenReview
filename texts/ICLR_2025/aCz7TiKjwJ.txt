                                                   A BSTRACT
008
009
               Transformer-based architectures have achieved unprecedented success in time se-
010            ries analysis. However, facing the challenge of across-domain modeling, existing
011            studies utilize statistical prior as prompt engineering fails under the huge distribu-
012            tion shift among various domains. In this paper, a Unified Time Series Diffusion
013            (UTSD) model is established for the first time to model the multi-domain proba-
014            bility distribution, utilizing the powerful probability distribution modeling ability
015            of Diffusion. Unlike the autoregressive models that capture the conditional prob-
016            abilities of the prediction horizon to the historical sequence, we use a diffusion
017            denoising process to model the mixture distribution of the cross-domain data and
018
               generate the prediction sequence for the target domain directly utilizing condi-
               tional sampling. The proposed UTSD contains three pivotal designs: (1) The
019
               condition network captures the multi-scale fluctuation patterns from the observa-
020
               tion sequence, which are utilized as context representations to guide the denoising
021            network to generate the prediction sequence; (2) Adapter-based fine-tuning strat-
022            egy, the multi-domain universal representation learned in the pretraining stage is
023            utilized for downstream tasks in target domains; (3) The diffusion and denois-
024            ing process on the actual sequence space, combined with the improved classifier
025            free guidance as the conditional generation strategy, greatly improves the stabil-
026            ity and accuracy of the downstream task. We conduct extensive experiments on
027            mainstream benchmarks, and the pre-trained UTSD outperforms existing founda-
028            tion models on all data domains, exhibiting superior zero-shot generalization abil-
029
               ity. After training from scratch, UTSD achieves comparable performance against
               domain-specific proprietary models. In particular, UTSD shows stable and reliable
030
               time series generation, and the empirical results validate the potential of UTSD as
031
               a time series foundational model. The source codes of UTSD are publicly avail-
032            able on https://anonymous.4open.science/r/UTSD-1BFF.

035   1   I NTRODUCTION
036
037   Time Series (TS) data widely exist in many real-world fields (Bengio et al., 2015; Sezer et al.,
038   2019; Fan et al., 2023), such as power (Wang et al., 2022), weather (Schultz et al., 2021), trans-
039
      portation (Thissen et al., 2003), finance (Chi & Chi, 2022), etc. The wide application of time series
      analysis makes it of vital research significance to many practical fields. Empirical practice illustrates
040
      that time series data from different domains perform shifted statistical properties (Wang et al., 2023;
041
      Yuan & Qiao, 2024), such as period, frequency, data distribution, number of features, and fluctua-
042   tion patterns, which poses a critical challenge to the generalizability and robustness of time series
043   analysis.
044
045
      With the continuous development of deep learning, models based on DNN (Zeng et al., 2023; Yi
      et al., 2023), RNN (Shi et al., 2015), CNN (Wu et al., 2023; Wang et al., 2023) and Transformer (Wu
046
      et al., 2021; Nie et al., 2023), have made remarkable achievements in many tasks of time series anal-
047
      ysis. With the success of generative pre-trained diffusion models in the vision domain (Esser &
048   Kulal, 2024; Peebles & Xie, 2022; Liu et al., 2024b), diffusion-based time series forecasting has
049   also shown promising results. Early efforts like TimeGrad (Rasul et al., 2021) employed RNNs to
050   capture temporal patterns, thereby predicting future series in an autoregressive fashion. Furthermore,
051   CSDI (Tashiro et al., 2021) and TimeDiff (Shen & Kwok, 2023) predict all time points simultane-
052   ously to mitigate the issue of error accumulation in long-term series forecasting. However, current
053   methods often focus on training domain-specific models tailored to individual datasets, limiting their
      ability to generalize well to a variety of new, unseen time series domains.

069   The success of unified Large Language Models (LLMs) (Touvron et al., 2023) has inspired the devel-
070   opment of a unified time series model. Trained on time series data from various domains, the unified
071   model aims to achieve strong generalization capabilities and robustness to deliver satisfactory zero-
072   shot inference performance on previously unseen domains. Previous attempts to develop unified
073   time series models can be divided into two main categories: the LLM-based approach and the multi-
074   domain generalization approach. The LLM-based approach leverages the alignment of time series
075   modalities with natural language processing (NLP), utilizing a pretrained LLM, potentially with fur-
076
      ther fine-tuning, to enhance generalization capabilities. OneFitsAll (Zhou et al., 2023) fine-tunes a
      subset of the weights from pre-trained LLMs on specific time series dataset and customizes distinct
077
      output layers for various downstream tasks. To mitigate the cross-modality challenges encountered
078
      during fine-tuning, TimeLLM (Jin et al., 2023) employs mathematical and statistical information
079   as part of its prompt engineering strategy to refine the LLM. However, the LLM-based approach
080   requires fine-tuning model weights for each individual time series domain, and the inherent dif-
081   ferences between NLP and time series modalities can result in concept drift and misalignment of
082   representation dimensions (Yang et al., 2024). On the other hand, the multi-domain generalization
083   approaches (Woo et al., 2024; Goswami et al., 2024) aim to train a broadly applicable model from
084   scratch using data from multiple time series domains. UniTime (Liu et al., 2024a), Timer (Liu et al.,
085   2024c), and Moirai (Woo et al., 2024) have focused on designing a generic architecture and train-
086   ing from scratch on comprehensive datasets with several temporal domain characteristics. Existing
087
      multi-domain generalization methods (e.g., Timer, etc.) rely on autoregressive mechanisms to estab-
      lish connections between observed and predicted sequences. However, these methods are prone to
088
      error accumulation in long-sequence predictions and often face challenges with domain confusion.
089
      In contrast, models such as UniTime and Moirai attempt to directly learn the projection from the
090   historical horizon to the future. Nevertheless, different domains exhibit varying data characteris-
091   tics, making it nearly impossible to design a shared encoder capable of effectively handling time
092   series from domains with distinct semantics. This limitation significantly restricts the zero-shot and
093   cross-domain generalization capabilities of such models.
094
      This paper establishes the unified time series diffusion model for the first time. Taking advantage of
095
      the diffusion model’s excellent capability to model probability distributions, our approach directly
096   produces diverse and high-quality forecasts by modelling a fusion probability distributions over mul-
097   tiple time series domains without establishing any inter-series projections. Further, the contextual
098   information embedded in the observation sequence is captured as a conditional variable that guides
099   the process of reconstructing the forecast results from gaussian noise, enhancing the stability and
100   accuracy. Due to the excellent cross-domain generalisation capability and robustness of the diffusion
101   approach, without any fine-tuning strategy, the pre-trained model exhibits better performance than
102   the existing LLM-based methodologies on the all benchmarks. Besides, taking advantage of the ex-
103   cellent probability distribution modelling capability of the diffusion approach, this paper models the
104
      multidomain fusion distribution directly from the integrated data domain and generates diversified
      high-quality forecast results directly, thus avoiding the inferior of the autoregressive paradigm with
105
      respect to the cumulative error and predictive coherence.
106

      condition-denoising architecture, the execution of the reverse noise reduction process in the actual

108
      sequence space, and the conditional generation strategy based on the classifier-free guidance. First,
109
      the condition-denoising architecture is designed, which contains both Condition Net and Denoising
110   Net components. (a) In the context learning stage, observation sequences from different domains are
111   fused together as inputs, from which the condition net captures a multi-scale representation of fluctu-
112   ation patterns as context to guide the conditional generation at different levels. For example, shallow
113   conditional variables will guide the trend part of the generated sequence, and deep representations
114   will guide the multi-periodic patterns of the generated sequence. This ensures that the conditional
115   information of the input data can be fully utilized to generate high-quality sequence samples. (b) In
116   the denoising stage, the contextual information embedded in the observation sequence is captured
117   as a conditional variable that guides the process of reconstructing the forecast results from gaussian
118
      noise, enhancing stability and accuracy. Another novel design is that, UTSD models in the actual
      sequence space instead of the latent space. Since the inverse denoising process often goes through
119
      a large number of iterative denoising (Li et al., 2024), each iteration of the denoising stage causes
120
      an accumulation of errors in the latent space, which are further amplified during the alignment of
121   the latent space to the actual sequence space. Therefore, this paper proposes to perform the dif-
122   fusion and denoising processes in the actual sequence space. While ensuring low time overhead,
123   iterative denoising directly in the original sequence space can alleviate the dithering problem and
124   improve the prediction accuracy. Furthermore, we propose the improved classifier free guidance as
125   the conditional generation strategy, which ensures that UTSD has sufficiently strong generalization
126   ability. Finally, we also design the efficient fine-tuning module Transfer-Adapter, which can gen-
127   erate high-quality sequence samples in a specific domain by fine-tuning only 5% of the parameters
128   while retaining the fluctuation information learned by pre-training.
129   The key contributions of our work are as follows.
130
             • We propose a novel condition-denoising architecture, an actual-space diffusion and de-
131            noising procedure, and a classifier-free guidance conditional generation strategy, which
132            improvements greatly improve the prediction accuracy of the diffusion model.
133
             • This paper establishes the time series foundation model based on a diffusion model for the
134            first time. Due to the excellent cross-domain generalization capability, the model shows the
135            potential to become an entirely new paradigm in time series.
136
             • The proposed UTSD achieves SOTA results. Extensive experiments validate the effective-
137
               ness of UTSD, with overall performance improvements of 19.6% and 21.2% compared to
138            existing foundation and proprietary baselines.

      3     UTSD A RCHITECTURE
199
200   The establishment of unified diffusion model in time series faces the challenge of learning the fusion distribution
      from multiple data domains, while the long-term forecasting requires the model to learn the enough temporal
201
      information from the observation series of the specified domain as a condition context to generate forecast
202   series that comply with the distribution of the domain. Existing condition diffusion models (Tashiro et al.,
203   2021; Li et al., 2024; Yuan & Qiao, 2024) often use simple neural network layer to capture the conditional
204   variable on single scale. However, sequences from several domains often have different multi-scale latent
205   representations (Shabani et al., 2022), such as sampling rate, periodic frequency characteristics, multi-periodic
      patterns (Ma et al., 2024), etc. Due to the difficulty for the model to learn enough fluctuation pattern information
206
      from the lookback window and the randomness of the initial state, diffusion model demonstrates low accuracy
207   in the prediction task (Alcaraz & Strodthoff, 2022). UTSD contains three pivotal novel designs: the innovative
208   condition-denoising architecture, the execution of the reverse noise reduction process in the actual sequence
209   space, and the conditional generation strategy based on the classifer-free guidance.

      Firstly, the architecture composition of the conditional learning module and the denoising generation module
251
      are introduced. For convenience, the term Block is utilized to refer to a set of consecutive neural network,
252   such as Encoder Block, Middle Block, Decoder Block, and Adapter Block, etc., which are reused as important
253   components in building the Unet structure. As shown in Figure 2, condition net and denoising net both follow
254   the encoder-decoder design, where the encoder and decoder are composed of Encoder Block-a,b,c and Decoder
255   Block-a,b,c, respectively. Besides, the Middle Block is designed to connect the encoder and decoder. Unet

256
      and skip connections are established between the two blocks, similarly, the other blocks have dimensions Pd /4
257   and Pd /8, respectively.

259

297   the timestep embedding temb through the linear layer, and the output is obtained by second convolution
298   layer (the normalization layer, activation layer, etc., are ignored in the description). The Transformer1D
299   module has two versions, writer and reader, which constitute Blocks in condition and denoising net, re-
300
      spectively. Transformer1D-writer accepts the latent representation Xemb ∈ RB·d×PL ×Pd as input, where
      self-attention mechanism is utilized to capture dependencies between global patches in the lookback window.
301
      Transformer1D-reader accepts the condition variable {hm,a,b,c }, noised sample Y1:H  t
                                                                                               ∈ RB·d×PH ×Pd , and the
302   trend part embedding pemb (as prompt) as input, where self-attention concatenates the context into the key-value
303   vector, thereby using historical fluctuation patterns as contextual information to guide the denoising process. In
304   the subsequent cross-attention mechanism, the historical trend information contained in pemb is used to model
305   the long-term trend of future sequences. The proposed condition-denoising net establishes the direct connec-
      tion between the generated sequence space and the observed sequence through Transformer1D-writer,reader,
306
      ensuring that UTSD has strong generalization capabilities to address the challenge of cross-domain probability
307   distribution modeling. In addition, each Encoder Block has Downsample1D as the end layer and each Decoder
308   Block has Upsample1D as the first layer, as shown in Figure 2. The implementation details of ResNet1D,
309   Transformer1D, Downsample1D, and Upsample1D are given in the Figure 3.

311   3.4       T RANSFER -A DAPTER M ODULE
312
      For pretrained models, directly fine-tuning with limited data at full weight or continuing training leads to catas-
313   trophic forgetting, mode collapse, and overfitting (Hu et al., 2022; Ruiz et al., 2023). Existing time series
314   models avoid forgetting by freezing the original model weights and adding a small number of new parame-
315   ters (Zhou et al., 2023), or low-rank adaptation prevents catastrophic forgetting by learning parameter shifts of
316   a low-rank matrix (Jin et al., 2023). However, in order to deal with cross-domain challenges, it is necessary to
317
      design fine-tuning strategies that can adapt to the diffusion and denoising process.
318   UTSD’s hybrid architecture supports naturally efficient fine-tuning through the ‘plug-and-play’ Adapter.
319   Specifically, pre-training to obtain a Condition-Denoising Net with a large number of weights is completely
320
      frozen, and only a small number of weights in the Adapter component need to be optimised. The effective-
      ness of the fine-tuning strategy can be intuitively explained by the fact that the pre-trained Condition Net is
321   responsible for capturing generic fluctuation patterns from observed sequences as conditional information, the
322   Denoising Net is required to reconstruct sequence samples from noise in the target domain based on specific
323   fluctuation patterns, and the fine-tuning-enabled Adapter is used to connect the unified representation space
      with the proprietary representation space.

      Diffusion-based forecasting methdologies generally face the challenge of generation dithering, and existing
464   models mitigate this difficulty through repeatedly sampling and subsequently taking the average or median
465   as the final prediction result. In real-world scenarios, models are required to generate stable predictions for
466   a fixed observation sequence. Besides, repeated sampling inevitably results in intolerable inference overhead
467   since iterative denoising is required for each sample. Based on this, improved evaluation metrics are utilized
      to simultaneously measure prediction accuracy and generation stability. Table 4 demonstrates the mse and
468
      mae between the predicted and true results at different quantile points. All probabilistic models first repeat the
469   sampling 100 times, and then take out the values at the 25, 50, and 75 percentile positions at each time point,
470   sorted from smallest to largest, and calculate the mean square error (mae is the same) between this prediction
471   and the true result, denoted as Top Quartile MSE (topQ), Middle Quartile MSE (midQ) and Last Quartile MSE
472   (lastQ), respectively. In addition, the standard deviation of the distribution consisting of the mean square error
      of all the predictions is displayed as the stability of the prediction (STA) in Table 4. Specifically, the average
473
      MSE is reduced by 32.3%, 54.3% and 90.2% compared to the existing DiffusionTS, LDT and CSDI.

529   5       C ONCLUSION AND FUTURE WORK
530
531
      In this paper, a unified time series diffusion (UTSD) model was established for the first time to model the
      joint probability distribution of multiple data domains by using the powerful probability distribution modeling
532   ability of Diffusion. To ensure that the model has sufficient generalization ability for the generation task of
533   multiple data domains, UTSD contains two pivotal modules: ConditionNet learns the general representation
534   of fluctuation patterns from multiple domains in the pre-training phase, and DenoisingNet accepts multi-scale
535   representations as conditional context in the reverse denoising process. In the fine-tuning stage, ConditionNet
536
      and DenoisingNet are frozen, and the Transfer-Adapter Module is used to transform the fluctuation patterns
      shared across domains into the corresponding latent space of the downstream data domain, so as to allow the
537   model to generate time series samples that match the style of the specified data domain. Besides, this paper
538   also designs the diffusion and denoising process on the actual sequence space, combined with the improved
539   classifier-free guidance as condition generation strategy, which greatly improves the accuracy of the model in
      the forecasting task.

      A     R ELATED W ORK

      A.1    LLM- BASED TS M ODEL
705
706   The first attempt to establish a unified time series model is OneFitsAll (Zhou et al., 2023). OneFitsAll uses
707   GPT2 (Radford et al., 2019) pretrained from billions of tokens as a backbone, where it freezes the self-attention
708   and feedforward layers in the pretrained language model and evaluates by fine-tuning the output and normal-
      ization layers on the time series data domain. However, the semantic information in the pre-trained model is
709
      difficult to be directly used in temporal scene. TimeLLM (Jin et al., 2023) uses text prototypes to reprogram
710   the input sequence data and then feed it into the frozen LLM (Touvron et al., 2023) to align the two modalities
711   of time series and natural language. In addition, to fully activate the modeling ability of LLM for time-series
712   data, TEST (Sun et al., 2023) builds an encoder to align the embedding Spaces of two modalities by comparing
713   the alignment of instances, features and text prototypes. Although LLM-based models show good zero-shot
      inference ability, these models still face cross-modal challenges, and it is still urgent to establish a time series
714
      foundation model trained from scratch.
715
716
      A.2    U NIFIED TS M ODEL
717
718   Different from NLP (Brown et al., 2020; Hu et al., 2022) and CV (Ho et al., 2020; Rombach et al., 2021),
719   the background knowledge and statistical characteristics of time series data from different domains often vary
720   greatly (Woo et al., 2024), so it is challenging to train a unified time series model by utilizing multiple data
721   domains. The first attempt to cross-domain training is UniTime (Liu et al., 2024a), which uses domain in-
      structions and a Language-TS transfer module to provide recognition information to distinguish time series
722
      data from different domains, and uses masking technology to alleviate the problem of unbalanced domain
723   convergence speed. On this basis, many time series foundation models have emerged. The first is the MO-
724   MENT (Goswami et al., 2024) of encoder-only attention architecture with input patching; Then, in order to
725   overcome the differences between data domains, MOIRAI (Woo et al., 2024) based on mask encoder architec-
726   ture includes multiple input-output projection layers to deal with different patterns of frequency-varying time
      series, and a spatio-temporal shared attention mechanism is designed. Different from the mainstream encoder-
727
      only architectures, decoder-only based timers show similar capabilities to large language models (Das et al.,
728   2024). In addition to showing strong generalization in zero-shot inference, the Timer (Liu et al., 2024c) based
729   on autoregressive generation strategy can capture the temporal representation from any length of context.
730
731   A.3    P RE - TRAINED D IFFUSION ON V ISION G ENERATION
732
733   Due to its powerful distribution modeling and generation capabilities, Diffusion has quickly become a popular
734
      component in the field of high-quality image generation (Balaji & Nah, 2022; Huang et al., 2023; Nichol &
      Dhariwal, 2022). However, the image generation paradigm of diffusion through iterative denoising process
735   leads to a large amount of time overhead. In order to ensure the quality of generated images while reducing the
736   time overhead required for inference, LDM (Rombach et al., 2021) successfully compresses the forward diffu-
737   sion process and the reverse noise reduction process from the real image space to the latent space through the
738   pre-trained VAE (van den Oord et al., 2017), which greatly reduces the computational overhead and memory re-
739
      quirements for inference. Subsequently, researchers have focused on generating high-quality images that meet
      user expectations. Large pre-trained text-image diffusion models (Rombach et al., 2021) based on CLIP (Rad-
740   ford et al., 2021) allow users to input text as prompt to generate pictures with specified styles. Since it is
741   often difficult to describe every image/video detail with text alone, there are many works (Hoe et al., 2024; Qi
742   et al., 2024) by providing additional inputs as condition context. ControlNet (Zhang et al., 2023) in particular,
743   by producing trainable copies of its encoder connected to zero convolutions, by reusing a powerful backbone
744
      derived from pre-training process, Additional images provided by the user (e.g., Canny Edge (Canny, 1986),
      Depth Map (Ranftl et al., 2019), and Normal Map (Vasiljevic et al., 2019), etc.) to enable more fine-grained
745   spatial control.
746
747
      A.4    D IFFUSION M ODEL ON TS
748
749   Since the success of diffusion models in vision, TimeGrad (Rasul et al., 2021) is the first time to use diffusion
750   model to model the probability distribution of time series data, which chooses LSTM or GRU model as the
751   architecture to predict future series in an autoregressive way. Subsequently, CSDI (Tashiro et al., 2021) and
752
      SSSD (Alcaraz & Strodthoff, 2022) designed a diffusion model with the observed data as the context condition,
      and filled the missing part by introducing the noise of the diffusion process into the missing part, and then
753   gradually denoising at each step. To improve the learning ability to model long-term dependencies in time series
754   data, TimeDiff (Shen & Kwok, 2023) introduces future-Mixup and autoregressive initialization mechanisms to
755   predict all timepoints of future sequence. DiffusionTS (Yuan & Qiao, 2024) utilizes the transformer architecture
      to model the seasonal-trend components separately, and the Fourier-based losses are designed to reconstruct the


                                                              14
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


756
      sequence sample directly rather than the noise at each diffusion step. Inspired by LDM, recent LDT (Li et al.,
757   2024) utilizes a transformer-based autoencoder to learn latent representations from raw observation sequence
758   and subsequently predicts future sequence in a non-autoregressive manner in the latent space.
759
      Although there have been a lot of methodologies on the application of diffusion to time series, to the best of
760   our knowledge, there are few researches about building a unified time series diffusion model. The establish-
761   ment of UTSD faces the challenge of capturing distributions from multiple data domains, and only introducing
762   inductive biases may not be sufficient for diffusion models to capture distribution characteristics of different
763   domains (Shen et al., 2024). The Condition-Denoising architecture is designed where the independent Condi-
      tionNet ensures that the model can capture multi-scale domain specified pattern features. In addition, LDMs
764
      are widely used in Vision (Zhang et al., 2023; Esser & Kulal, 2024; Liu et al., 2024b) because high-resolution
765   images are generally large in size (512*512), while the number of timepoints in time-series data is usually less
766   than 1,000. By modeling in the actual sequence space instead of the latent space, the proposed UTSD avoids
767   error accumulation in the latent space during multiple rounds of iterative noise reduction (e.g., the number of
768   steps of DDPM is 200).
