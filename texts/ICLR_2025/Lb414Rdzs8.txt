009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            We propose NODE-SAT, a novel temporal graph learning model that integrates
012            Neural Ordinary Differential Equations (NODEs) with self-attention mechanisms.
013            NODE-SAT’s design requires only historical 1-hop neighbors as input and com-
014            prises three key components: a temporal link processing module utilizing NODE-
015            guided self-attention layers to capture temporal link information, a node represen-
016            tation module summarizing neighbor information, and a prediction layer. Exten-
017            sive experiments across thirteen temporal link prediction datasets demonstrate that
018
               NODE-SAT achieves state-of-the-art performance on most datasets with signifi-
               cantly faster convergence. The model demonstrates high accuracy, rapid conver-
019
               gence, robustness across varying dataset complexities, and strong generalization
020
               capabilities in both transductive and inductive settings in temporal link prediction.
021            These findings highlight NODE-SAT’s effectiveness in capturing node correla-
022            tions and temporal link dynamics.

025   1   I NTRODUCTION
026
027   Temporal graphs have emerged as a powerful tool for modeling complex, evolving systems across
028   various domains Kazemi et al. (2020); Yu et al. (2017); Bui et al. (2022). These time-varying struc-
029   tures represent entities as nodes and their interactions as timestamped links, capturing the chronolog-
030   ical evolution of relationships in diverse scenarios. In social networks, temporal graphs are highly
031   effective at analyzing user interactions and predicting future connections Kumar et al. (2019); Song
032   et al. (2019). Within e-commerce platforms, they play a crucial role in modeling user-item interac-
033
      tions and recommending products Li et al. (2020); Fan et al. (2021); Yu et al. (2022); Zhang et al.
      (2022b). In the field of transportation, temporal graphs prove invaluable for analyzing traffic patterns
034
      and optimizing routes Yu et al. (2017); Wu et al. (2019); Guo et al. (2019). Furthermore, they have
035
      shown great promise in modeling and predicting the behavior of complex physical systems Huang
036   et al. (2020b); Sanchez-Gonzalez et al. (2020). Representation learning on temporal graphs can
037   be categorized into continuous-time and discrete-time Huang et al. (2020a). This study focuses on
038   continuous-time representation learning. Unlike discrete-time methods that aggregate interactions
039   into fixed intervals, continuous-time models maintain the exact timing of events, allowing for a more
040   nuanced understanding of the graph’s evolutionary patterns.
041
      Conventional approaches to temporal graph learning typically integrate Recurrent Neural Networks
042   (RNNs), temporal attention mechanisms, and Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) to model both tem-
043   poral information and structural relationships Trivedi et al. (2019); Xu et al. (2020). However, a
044   recent study introducing GraphMixer Cong et al. (2023) challenges this complexity with a simple
045   design. GraphMixer utilizes only 1-hop neighbor information, link features, and node features, pro-
046   cessing temporal graph data through an MLP-Mixer Tolstikhin et al. (2021) architecture. Despite its
047   simplicity, GraphMixer achieves performance comparable to or surpassing more complex models
048   across various temporal graph learning tasks. The success of GraphMixer raises important ques-
049   tions about the necessity of complex architectures in temporal graph analysis. Its effectiveness lies
050
      in the efficient integration of spatial and temporal information. This finding suggests that simplicity
      can match or exceed the performance of more sophisticated approaches in capturing temporal graph
051
      learning.
052
053   While GraphMixer demonstrates effectiveness in general temporal graph learning tasks, specific
      domains such as traffic prediction require tailored approaches to address their unique challenges.

054
      Traffic prediction, a critical component of intelligent transportation systems, demands models that
055   can capture complex spatio-temporal dependencies and evolving patterns in urban mobility. In this
056   context, Neural Ordinary Differential Equations (NODEs) Chen et al. (2018) have emerged as a
057   promising framework Fang et al. (2021); Choi et al. (2022). NODEs, which model continuous-time
058   dynamics, offer a mathematically approach to representing the fluid nature of traffic flows and their
059   temporal evolution.
060
      Drawing inspiration from GraphMixer Zhang et al. (2022a) and NODEs Chen et al. (2018); Poli
061   et al. (2019), we propose a novel method to model temporal graph dynamics. Our method lever-
062   ages continuum-depth models to capture the intricate evolution of temporal graphs, utilizing NODE
063   frameworks to model the continuous-time dynamics of graph structures. This method enables us to
064   learn differential equations that describe the temporal evolution of link features and node features,
065   providing a more nuanced and continuous representation of graph dynamics. By integrating these
066   concepts, our method offers a more detailed and smooth perspective on how graphs evolve over
067   time, capturing complex interactions and changes in graph structures.
068   Our method employs an ODE layer to integrate hidden representations across the temporal graph,
069   yielding continuous-time temporal embeddings for each node. Our model’s ability to identify and
070   analyze complex temporal patterns is enhanced through the incorporation of a self-attention mech-
071   anism Vaswani et al. (2017). The attention layer is applied to the representations generated by the
072   ODE solver. By combining ODE-based continuous-time modeling with self-attention, our method
073   effectively captures node features and richer temporal link information. This integration allows for
074   a more comprehensive understanding of temporal dynamics in graph data. To encourage future re-
075
      search, we have made NODE-SAT available at https://anonymous.4open.science/r/
      NODE-SAT-6F12. Our key contributions can be summarized as follows:
076
077
            1. Novel Temporal Graph Neural Network Architecture: We propose a new architecture
078            that combines continuous-time modeling with graph neural networks, specifically designed
079            to capture the dynamic nature of temporal graphs.
080
            2. Continuous-Time Temporal Embeddings: Our method generates continuous-time tem-
081
               poral embeddings for each node by integrating hidden representations using an ODE solver,
082
               capturing the evolving nature of temporal link and graph structure.
083
084         3. Robust Framework for Temporal Graph Analysis: By combining NODEs with self-
085
               attention, we provide a robust framework for modeling and predicting links in temporal
               graphs, offering a continuous-time perspective that can potentially capture subtle temporal
086
               dynamics often overlooked by discrete-time models.

      equations (ODEs) Strogatz (2018); Guckenheimer & Holmes (2013); Kuznetsov (2013). These
134

135

136   dynamics. Given the initial conditions x1 (0), x2 (0), . . . , xM (0) and the function F, the system’s
137   evolution can be solved using numerical ODE solvers such as the Runge-Kutta method Press et al.
138   (2007). The solution for any variable i at an arbitrary time τ can be expressed as:

177   4     M ETHOD
178
179   Our model learns continuous-time representations of nodes in temporal graphs through three key
180   components. The link processing module employs a neural ODE to guide a self-attention layer, cap-
181   turing complex temporal link information. The node processing module generates node represen-
182   tations that capture structural information from the graph. A prediction layer utilizes these learned
183   representations to predict links at future time points. Figure 2 is the framework of our method.
184
185   4.1   NODE-SAT
186
      In this section, we present the framework of NODE-SAT, detailing its architecture and key compo-
187
      nents. For each node vi in the graph, we represent its temporal link information as a chronologically
188
      ordered sequence:
189                              T (vi ) = (t1 , xi,j1 (t1 )) ⊕ · · · ⊕ (tn , xi,jn (tn ))              (4)
190

191
      vi and vj at time t, and ⊕ is the concatenation operator. To balance computational efficiency with
192
      the recency of information, we retain only the K most recent temporal link entries, where K is a
193   hyperparameter.
194
195   To enhance the model’s ability to capture temporal patterns, we apply a learnable time encoder Xu
196
      et al. (2020). For each timestamp tk , we compute a time encoding vector ϕ(tk ) using a learnable
      function:
197

198

200   link features:
201

202   To ensure uniform input dimensions, we apply zero-padding to standardize the length of all temporal
203   vectors T̃ (vi ). Following the padding operation, we employ a MLP to summarize the information
204   contained in these vectors:
205                                          hi (T ) = MLP(T̃ (vi ))                                  (7)
206   where hi (T ) is the embedding of vi that contains the temporal link information from t1 to tn . The

266

      We introduce NODE-SAT, a novel temporal graph learning model that integrates Neural Ordinary
      Differential Equations (NODEs) with self-attention mechanisms. Through extensive experiments on
504
      thirteen diverse datasets, NODE-SAT consistently demonstrates outstanding performance in tempo-
505
      ral link prediction tasks, exhibiting exceptional accuracy, rapid convergence, robustness across vary-
506   ing dataset complexities, and strong generalization capabilities in both transductive and inductive
507   temporal link prediction settings. The model’s innovative combination of NODEs and self-attention
508   provides a simple yet powerful framework for temporal graph modeling, allowing for nuanced rep-
509   resentation of graph evolution and enhanced capture of complex between-node relationships. By
510   leveraging a continuous-time perspective, NODE-SAT effectively models node correlations and tem-
511   poral link dynamics, potentially discerning subtle patterns that discrete-time models might overlook.
512   These results not only validate NODE-SAT’s efficacy in temporal graph learning tasks but also open
513   up new research directions in continuous-time graph modeling.

689   across all settings. Other models, particularly DyGFormer and GraphMixer, often emerge as strong
690   contenders, frequently achieving the second-best scores. The results demonstrate NODE-SAT’s
691   overall superiority in Inductive Dynamic Link Prediction tasks, with some specific exceptions, high-
692   lighting its effectiveness across different negative sampling strategies and diverse datasets.
