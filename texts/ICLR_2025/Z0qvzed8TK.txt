                                                  A BSTRACT
010
011
               Document Visual Question Answering (DocVQA) remains a significant challenge
012            in the field of document understanding and is a critical evaluation metric for
013            current general-purpose large model techniques. However, the prevailing pub-
014            lic datasets are predominantly designed for single scenarios or specific sources.
015            Furthermore, most available datasets are in English, which limits the verification
016            of model performance in other languages. This paper presents a novel multi-
017            domain Chinese document VQA dataset, which includes 39 document types from
018            7 different domains. The designed question set encompasses both common ex-
019            tractive questions and complex abstractive questions. Based on this dataset, we
020
               conducted a comprehensive review and analysis of various technical paradigms,
               including both traditional and large model-based approaches. Using the popular
021
               in-context learning framework, we propose a strong baseline that achieves com-
022
               mendable few-shot adaptation. Comparative evaluations demonstrate the superior
023            performance of the proposed method across different solution paradigms. The
024            dataset and code will be published.

027   1   I NTRODUCTION
028
029   Document understanding plays a central role in artificial intelligence, covering various industries
030   and enhancing daily operational efficiency. Previous endeavors have primarily focused on distinct
031   subfields driven by different task objectives, encompassing document classification (Mohbat et al.,
032   2023; Fronteau et al., 2023), key information extraction (KIE) (Zhang et al., 2020; Tang et al., 2021;
033
      Wang et al., 2021a), layout analysis (Zhang et al., 2021; Cheng et al., 2023; Shen et al., 2021),
      table understanding (Shigarov, 2023; Li et al., 2022), etc. Recently, the advent and proliferation
034
      of large models have instigated a shift towards a unified paradigm for end-to-end problem repre-
035
      sentation. Document Visual Question Answering (DocVQA) (Mathew et al., 2021) theoretically
036   aligns with such a paradigm, potentially addressing more intricate task demands compared to the
037   aforementioned objectives.
038
039
      Traditional approaches of DocVQA generally fall into two categories: those relying on pure lan-
      guage models (Tito et al., 2022) and those leveraging multimodal pretrained models (Xu et al.,
040
      2021a; Huang et al., 2022; Peng et al., 2022; Appalaraju et al., 2021). The latter, integrating ad-
041
      ditional modalities such as vision and layout, demonstrate superior performance. The recent emer-
042   gence of Large Language Models (LLMs) and Large Vision-Language Models (LVLMs) has intro-
043   duced innovative solutions. For example, research such as Liu et al. (2023b); Yang et al. (2023b)
044   incorporates off-the-shelf Optical Character Recognition (OCR) results into the prompt and utilizes
045   LLMs as information extractors. LVLM-based approaches directly integrate OCR capabilities into
046   the model, offering a pure end-to-end solution (Alayrac et al., 2022; Yang et al., 2023a; Anil et al.,
047   2023; Anthropic, 2024; Chen et al., 2023; Zhu et al., 2023; Bai et al., 2023b; Li et al., 2023; Wang
048   et al., 2023; Zhang et al., 2023), which exhibits promising potential in text-oriented task evaluation.
049   However, most existing models do not support Chinese document comprehension due to the lack of
050
      corresponding training and evaluation data.
051   Presently available datasets of DocVQA predominantly originate from homogeneous domains and
052   are primarily in English, restricting method generalizability to diverse scenarios. For example, the
053   images from Mathew et al. (2021) are mostly scanned industrial documents, VQA-CD (Mahamoud
      et al., 2022) focuses only on invoice scenarios, and both TAT-DQA (Zhu et al., 2022) and BD-VQA

075   population worldwide, presents additional challenges due to its extensive character set (Shi et al.,

      This paper introduces a novel Multi-Domain Chinese Document Visual Question Answering
079
      (MDCD-VQA) dataset, which aims to encompass a broad spectrum of document types across var-
080
      ious real-world domains. The construction of the dataset involved the aggregation of data from
081   multiple public and private databases. In contrast to previous datasets, which were predominantly
082   composed of documents with regular text (e.g., extracted from PDF or web pages), MDCD-VQA
083   incorporates a significant amount of real-world data sourced from photographed or scanned scenes.
084   Consequently, these introduce perceptual challenges to models like skew, curvature, blur and over-
085   lap. The MDCD-VQA dataset comprises 5,071 images and 34,170 questions, and some samples
086   from different domains are shown in Figure 1. The construction of question-answer (Q&A) pairs
087   was guided by rigorous principles to ensure diversity. The dataset encompasses both extractive ques-
088   tions, where the answer is located within the image, and more complex abstractive questions that
089
      involving tasks such as summarization, judgment, inference, and calculation.
090   Using our proposed MDCD-VQA dataset, we conduct a comprehensive evaluation and analysis of
091   various method types for DocVQA tasks. This evaluation encompasses traditional full-training-
092   based methods as well as zero/few-shot-based approaches leveraging LLMs and LVLMs. Further-
093   more, we introduce a novel baseline method based on the In-Context Learning (ICL) framework
094   (Dong et al., 2023), which integrates powerful LLMs. This model leverages the retrieval of the
095
      most similar examples from the image, text, and question perspectives in the training set to acti-
      vate the underlying capabilities of LLMs. This approach represents a viable option offering optimal
096
      comprehensive performance and generalization at the current stage of development.
097
098   The main contributions of this paper are as follows:
099
100         • We present a new Chinese DocVQA dataset, which, to the best of our knowledge, is the first
101           comprehensive dataset to include both extractive and abstractive questions across multiple
102
              domains.
103         • We conducted extensive experiments to evaluate four different architectural solutions for
104           this Chinese DocVQA task. The results underscore the substantial potential for improve-
105           ment in Chinese document understanding by current large models.
106
107         • We introduce a strong baseline model based on the ICL framework. The experimental
              results show that this method outperforms previous approaches on the proposed dataset.

123   2.1       DATASETS OF D OC VQA
124
125
      DocVQA is a subset of text-oriented VQA tasks. Unlike typical tasks where the questions focus pri-
      marily on prominent text in the image, as seen in tasks like TextVQA (Singh et al., 2019), OCRVQA
126
      (Mishra et al., 2019), and STVQA (Biten et al., 2019), DocVQA images have denser text with more
127
      fine-grained associated questions.
128
129   Most of the existing public datasets for DocVQA are tailored for specific domains or from specific
130   sources. For example, the datasets presented in Mathew et al. (2021); Tito et al. (2022) are con-
131
      structed using images selected from the UCSF Industry Documents Library. VQA-CD (Mahamoud
      et al., 2022) contains invoice images from an industry document collection. TAT-DQA (Zhu et al.,
132
      2022) and BD-VQA (Raja et al., 2023) are two datasets within the financial domain, the former con-
133
      sisting of comprehensive financial reports and the latter containing specific financial spreadsheets.
134   VisualMRC (Tanaka et al., 2021) and DuReadervis (Qi et al., 2022) are different English and Chi-
135   nese datasets for the DocVQA task, mainly containing screenshots of web pages. The collection
136   of SlideVQA (Tanaka et al., 2023) all from slide decks. A more recent addition is the multi-page,
137   multi-domain dataset DUDE (Van Landeghem et al., 2023), proposed and used as a competition
138   dataset for ICDAR-2023 (International Conference on Document Analysis and Recognition). For
139   Chinese datasets, DocVQA-ZH1 is a competition dataset that contains various types of scanned doc-
140   uments related to insurance scenarios such as medical bills or cases. However, the download for this
141   dataset is currently unavailable due to the conclusion of the competition. Notably, the question types
142
      in this dataset are exclusively in extractive form. A comprehensive comparison of these datasets can
      be found in the table 1.

145   2.2       M ETHODS OF D OC VQA
146
      Given the denser text information in DocVQA, models need to have enhanced text understanding
147
      capabilities. As a result, proposed solutions are predominantly implemented within the language
148
      model paradigm. Besides the direct adoption of language models and treating the problem as a
149   QA task (e.g., BERT (Devlin et al., 2019) or BigBird (Zaheer et al., 2020) in Tito et al. (2022)),
150   the dominant approaches in the past few years involve the use of a multimodal pre-trained model.
151   These approaches typically incorporate features from three modalities (visual, layout, semantic) into
152   a transformer-based encoder. Representative works include the LayoutLM family (Xu et al., 2020;
153   2021a; Huang et al., 2022; Xu et al., 2021b), DocFormer (Appalaraju et al., 2021; 2023), StrucText
154   (Li et al., 2021; Yu et al., 2023b), ERNIE-Layout (Peng et al., 2022),etc. Despite the use of off-the-
155   shell OCR results, there have also been some recent works like Donut (Kim et al., 2022), Dessurt
156   (Davis et al., 2022) and Pix2Struct (Lee et al., 2023) aiming to build more end-to-end OCR-free
157
      solutions. These systems usually integrate the ability of OCR in the pre-training stage, but the
      current public models mainly have the ability of English scenes only.
158
159   Recently, there has been a surge in the development of general paradigms based on LLMs and
160   LVLMs. These models aim to support different tasks, and some works such as GPT-4 (Yang et al.,

      3.1      DATA C ONSTRUCTION
191
192   Images: To build a comprehensive multi-domain dataset, we systematically explored and collected
193   Chinese document data from various sources. In this context, “documents” include traditional office
194   documents, tickets, cards, web pages, and other text-intensive image scenes. Our data collection
195   encompasses three main sources with open copyright: (1) public academic datasets, (2) datasets
196   released alongside public competitions, and (3) private datasets collected by our team. All exter-
197   nal data from these sources are publicly available. For each dataset, we selected a varying number
198   of images based on the diversity of data formats. Specifically, we computed the distribution and
199   similarity of the image features in each data source and sampled them diversely within the feature
200
      space to ensure the final dataset includes as many different styles as possible. For samples with
      very similar layouts, the number of samples was relatively small. In total, we collected 5,071 doc-
201
      ument images from 12 data sources, as shown in Table 2. Each image in the dataset is tagged with
202
      a document category label to facilitate problem analysis and indexing of different data types. Some
203   category information is taken directly from the dataset itself, while for others, such as HUST-CELL,
204   we manually assigned labels to categorize the data into predefined document categories. Overall, we
205   defined 39 document types across 7 domains (business, culture, education, finance, lifestyle, medi-
206   cal, and transportation) within the dataset. MDCD-VQA is the first multi-domain DocVQA dataset
207   to provide detailed categorization of document types, thereby significantly facilitating research on
208   system adaptation. The specific distribution can be found in the supplementary material.
209   Questions and Answers: When constructing Q&A pairs, we aim to increase question diversity
210   while maintaining alignment with real-world applications. It is important to note that the public
211   datasets used in our dataset originate from sub-domain document understanding tasks, such as KIE
212   and entity linking. These tasks provide high-quality task labels that match the primary application

227

228
      Q&A pairs and providing additional annotations for each image. During this phase, annotators were
229   encouraged to submit more complex questions, including those that require judgment, reasoning,
230   summarization, or calculation. They were also encouraged to submit questions that cannot be an-
231   swered from the image. Each Q&A pair was double-checked by at least one other annotator. The
232   final MDCD-VQA dataset consists of 34,170 Q&A pairs. Approximately 50% of the dataset was
233   created through semi-automated generation, and the rest was created entirely by hand.

294   4     P ROPOSED M ODEL
295
296   In this section, we present a baseline method based on a simple yet powerful ICL framework
297   (Dong et al., 2023) that leverages the robust capabilities of LLMs to achieve high-quality few-shot
298   DocVQA tasks. The overall framework, illustrated in Figure 3, can be broken down into two key
299
      steps: Nearest Sample Retrieval (NSR) and Prompt Construction.
300   Nearest Sample Retrieval: In context learning with LLMs, training samples typically aren’t used
301   directly to update model parameters. Instead, they serve as context input to the LLM, activating
302   its potential capabilities. Different examples presented to the model will elicit different responses
303   from the LLM. Inspired by the idea of Retrieval-augmented Generation (RAG)(Lewis et al., 2020),
304   a straightforward approach is to assign each image the most similar example from the training sam-
305
      ples. To measure the similarity between samples, we consider three different feature dimensions: the
      visual feature, the text within the image, and the question itself. Specifically, for an inference sam-
306
      ple, we first use a vision transformer (Dosovitskiy et al., 2021) to extract its visual feature, denoted
307
      as V , which is then flattened into a one-dimensional feature vector. For the text in the image, we use
308   an offline OCR engine to extract all text instances along with their positions. All texts are concate-
309   nated into a sequence according to their bounding boxes using a heuristic method from Qiao et al.
310   (2023). Both the serialized text sequence and the question are then encoded using the BGE-large
311   embedding (Xiao et al., 2023) to obtain the sentence embedding, yielding the text feature vector
312   T and the question feature vector Q, respectively. For samples in the training set, all features are
313   precomputed offline and stored in a vector database. Consequently, computing the similarity metric
314   between a given sample and any sample ([V ′ , T ′ , Q′ ]) in the database becomes straightforward:
315                               D = dis(λ1 V + λ2 T + λ3 Q, λ1 V ′ + λ2 T ′ + λ3 Q′ )                                               (1)
316
317   where λ1 , λ2 , λ3 are parameters used to balance the importance of the features, and we use cosine
318   similarity to compute the feature distances (dis()). Finally, we select the k closest examples in the
319   training set. It is worth noting that the samples are stored in the database at the granularity of the
320
      question. Once a sample (a question and its corresponding image) is retrieved, all other samples
      belonging to the same image are skipped to ensure the retrieval of new images.
321
322   Prompt Construction: Different prompts may influence the output of the LLM to varying degrees.
323   In this baseline model, we use a simple and straightforward prompt design: “Declare the task, give
      examples, and ask the question,” as illustrated in Figure 3. Specifically, the prompt comprises three


                                                              6
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


324
      key components that need to be filled in the prompt template: 1) Document Type: For the retrieved
325   samples added to the prompt, we include a document-type cue to help the model correlate underlying
326   knowledge with the textual context. For inference, the category is not added here as it is unknown.
327   2) OCR Context: The OCR result is concatenated into a long sequence using the same ordering
328   strategy and separated by whitespace to distinguish different text instances. 3) Q&A Pairs: Only the
329   retrieved Q&A pairs are concatenated into the prompt, providing format information for the LLM
330   to reference. All the prompts are in Chinese.

      5.1   C OMPARED M ETHODS
336   As is widely recognized, new large models are being continuously developed and rapidly improving
337   performance metrics. In this study, we have gathered as many advanced models as possible that
338   support Chinese document understanding and utilized their latest versions. Specifically, we com-
339   pare four types of methods: 1) Language-Model-based: BERT (Devlin et al., 2019) and ERNIE3.0
340   (Liu et al., 2023a); 2) Multimodal-Pretrained-Model-based: LayoutXLM (Xu et al., 2021b), Lay-
341
      outLMv3 (Huang et al., 2022), ERNIE-Layout (Peng et al., 2022); 3) LLM-based: InterLM2.5-7B
      (Zhang et al., 2024), Qwen-14B (Bai et al., 2023a), Qwen2-72B (Yang et al., 2024), ChatGPT
342
      (gpt-3.5-turbo) (Ouyang et al., 2022), GPT-4o (OpenAI, 2024) (with Pure Text input, gpt-4o-2024-
343
      05-13, ); 4) LVLM-based: Qwen-VL-7B (Bai et al., 2023b), InternLM-XComposer-2.5 (Dong et al.,
344   2024a), MiniCPN-V2.6 (Yao et al., 2024), CogVLM2 (Hong et al., 2024), InternVL2 (Chen et al.,
345   2024), Qwen-VL-Max (Bai et al., 2023b), Claude 3 Opus (claude-3-opus-20240229) (Anthropic,
346   2024), Gemini 1.5 Pro (Reid et al., 2024), GPT-4 (Yang et al., 2023a) (gpt-4-turbo) and GPT-4o
347   (with Multi-Modal input, gpt-4o-2024-05-13).
348
      For the previous two types of methods, we uniformly adopted the base-scale Chinese version. Fol-
349
      lowing standard practice, we trained the models using the full training set until convergence. For
350   closed-source methods, such as Qwen-VL-MAX, ChatGPT, GPT-4, GPT-4o, Claude-3 Opus, and
351   Gemini 1.5 Pro, we evaluated them through their official APIs. In the few-shot testing, we compared
352   our NSR method with the Random-Sample-Retrieval (RSR) method (Liu et al., 2023b). The pro-
353   posed few-shot approach can theoretically be applied to both LLM and LVLM models. In this paper,
354   we report only the few-shot testing performance of LVLM-based methods on certain open-source
355   models, as we found similar limitations in most LVLM models’ ability to comprehend the examples,
356   including several tests with GPT-4o.

359
360   The MDCD-VQA dataset has been randomly divided into 3,557/ 761/ 753 for training/ validation/
361   testing, respectively. They separately contain 24,044/ 4,967/ 5,159 Q&A pairs.
362
363
      Except for the LVLM-based method, the off-the-shell OCR results are obtained using the DavarOCR
      (Qiao et al., 2022) engine. For the LLMs/LVLMs-based methods, we only evaluate these methods
364
      in a zero/few-shot setting, where the training data are used as queryable examples, since tuning a
365
      large model is beyond the scope of this work. The weight balance parameters in our NSR method
366   are set as λ1 = λ2 = λ3 = 1. All experiments are performed on 8 Tesla A100-80G GPUS.
367
368   We adopt the widely used Average Normalized Levenshtein Similarity (ANLS) (Biten et al., 2019)
369
      as the primary evaluation metric, which allows partial credit for answers that are close, though not
      exact matches. Additionally, we follow the approach in Mathew et al. (2021) to report accuracy
370
      (Acc), which accounts for exact matches. In Chinese, however, different expression habits may
371
      result in answers having the same meaning but entirely different text (e.g., “BúShı̀” and “MéiYǒu”
372   both convey the meaning of “No”). To address this, we perform simple post-processing to align the
373   model output during evaluation. It is important to note that large models may produce semantically
374   similar responses that are incorrectly judged due to differences in expression. While some methods
375   use large models like GPT for semantic evaluation, they still face significant challenges in achieving
376   accurate assessments. Considering both the evaluation of previous datasets and the need for a stable
377   metric in real-world applications, we continue to use the traditional evaluation method. More details
      can be found in the Appendix.

411   5.3    R ESULTS
412
413   Table 4 presents the overall evaluation results on the MDCD-VQA dataset.
414   In the full-training setting, ERNIE-Layout demonstrates the highest performance among tradi-
415   tional methods, highlighting its effectiveness in multimodal modeling. However, its proficiency
416   varies across question types, with strong results for extractive questions but weaker performance for
417   abstractive questions, due to the limitations of the task paradigm. It is worth noting that in addition
418   to the indexing modeling paradigm, some generative-based pre-training models also exist, such as
419   UDOP (Tang et al., 2023) and GenDoc (Feng et al., 2023). However, to our knowledge, none of
420   these models provide Chinese pre-training models.
421   In the 0-shot setting, the evaluated models are all effective in understanding Chinese commands.
422   As can be seen from the results, GPT-4o(PT) and Intern-VL-26B have achieved the best results in
423   the LLM-Based and LVLM-based methods, respectively. For the open source models shown so far,
424   their performance is almost positively correlated with the number of parameters. Comparing the
425   two type of schemes, LVLM-based models still lag behind LLM-based methods in performance, as
426   compared between GPT-4o(PT) and GPT-4o(MM).This disparity is primarily due to the fact that
427   LLM-based methods integrate the expertise of OCR specialists, resulting in more accurate text
428
      recognition. However, they have an intrinsic flaw: they lose part of the visual information in the
      processing pipeline, which hampers their ability to handle questions involving visual intricacies.
429
430   In the 5-shot setting, incorporating examples extracted using our NSR method significantly en-
431   hanced the performance of zero-shot LLM-based approaches, improving overall results by 5%-7%
      and by 8%-13% for abstractive questions. One of the obvious features is that ICL has helped to


                                                              8
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


432
      constrain the model in the format of the output, thus improving the match with the ground truth.
433   This is actually of great importance for industrial production as well. Among LLM-based meth-
434   ods, NSR+GPT-4o achieved the highest performance across all compared methods. We found that
435   integrating NSR partially mitigates the issue of missing layout information by enabling the model
436   to discern similar patterns from examples. In contrast, the strategy of randomly selecting examples
437   (RSR) provided limited benefit and, in some cases, even underperformed compared to the zero-shot
438   approach. For LVLM models, most NSR/RSR methods showed limited effectiveness, with the ex-
439   ception of MiniCPM-V2.6, particularly for extractive questions. In some cases, these methods led
440   to a significant decrease in performance, with a common error pattern being the models directly
441
      replicating answers from retrieved examples instead of using them as references—indicating de-
      ficiencies in contextual comprehension (an example is provided in Appendix). This highlights a
442
      potential training data gap in the ICL capabilities of other LVLM models.

      6      C ONCLUSION
478
479
      This paper introduces a new multi-domain Chinese DocVQA dataset, comprising 39 types of doc-
480   uments from 7 different domains. The dataset includes a diverse set of Q&A pairs, encompassing
481   both extractive and abstractive questions. We conduct a comprehensive comparison of several meth-
482   ods on the Chinese DocVQA task and propose a novel approach based on the in-context learning
483   framework. This approach utilizes image features, text features, and question features to retrieve
484   similar examples from the database, thereby activating the latent capabilities of LLMs/LVLMs. Ex-
485   perimental results demonstrate that our methods establish a new advanced baseline and highlight the
      strong generalization and few-shot capabilities of the proposed framework.

905   A.2.1    P ROMPT CONSTRUCTION
906
907   Here, we provide some extra details for constructing the prompts for LLMs/LVLMs.
908   Prompt construction for few-shot LLM. Note that some documents have very long OCR contexts,
909   and LLM/LVLM prompt tokens usually have a length limit (e.g., 8K for Qwen-14B). Thus, in prac-
910   tical implementation, for samples where the support context length is insufficient for full example
911   splicing, a k-shot prompt construction is actually a max-k-shot. This means that a maximum number
912   of examples, but no more than k, will be added to the prompt. Algorithm 1 presents a simple method
913   to adaptively select the maximum-top-k examples based on the input. In practice, only about 1.6%
914
      of test samples cannot be filled with 5 examples due to their context limit.
915   In the algorithm, we consider that there may be some samples in the queue that cannot be added
916   to the prompt due to the long context or share the same image, so we will continue to traverse the
917   following adjacent samples, and add them to the prompt if there are any tokens left. Here we selected
      k + 5 samples as candidates at once.

960   A.2.2    P OST- PROCESSING FOR E VALUATION
961
962   For the outputs of LLM/LVLMs, extraneous text content or shifts in output style can make it difficult
963   to assess accuracy precisely. One approach is to send the output results and ground truth values to
964   another LLM (e.g., ChatGPT) for matching judgment, but this method also struggles to guarantee
965
      complete accuracy. Here, to prove the efficiency and consistency of the evaluation, we summarize
      the output styles of various scenarios according to Chinese language expression habits, unify them
966
      through simple post-processing, and calculate quantitative indexes through scripts.
967
968   Specifically, in the evaluation process, we design post-processing rules to align answers with the
969   same meaning but different formats:
970
971           • Fixed sentence pattern extraction: For answers in the format “key is value” or “key : value”,
                we keep the answer after the element “is” or “:”. For example, for the question “Who is the


                                                          18
       Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


972
                  passenger of this train ticket?”, the prediction “The passenger is Zhangsan” is considered
973               correct if the ground truth is “Zhangsan”.
974
975            • Numeric formatting: For answers in numeric format, such as total money, we remove other
976
                 characters and compare only the numeric values. For example, for the question “What is
                 the price of this ticket?”, the prediction “22 yuan” is considered correct if the ground truth
977
                 is “22”.
978
979            • Yes/No formatting: For yes/no type answers, we convert all similar expressions to a fixed

1256   A.3.4    FAILURE C ASES A NALYSIS
1257
1258   n this section, we provide a summary of the typical types of errors encountered in some scenarios
1259   based on large models.
1260   Errors in 0-shot/few-shot LLM-based methods. As mentioned earlier, such methods are based
1261   on a two-stage pipeline, which inevitably leads to some error accumulation throughout the process.
1262   However, the current perceptual results rely on OCR expert realization, making it the solution with
1263   the least loss of accuracy at the perceptual level. In summary, the most significant problems stem
1264   mainly from the following:
1265
               • Lack of visual information. Some problems involving color, position, size, and type have
1266
                 inherent flaws due to the absence of visual information. However, in few-shot settings,
1267
                 these issues can be somewhat mitigated. For instance, the model can infer hints from
1268             examples containing similar types of problems.
1269
               • Wrong semantic order. The current heuristic rule for text concatenation can lead to errors,
1270
                 especially in complex layouts like multi-column formats. The reading order of the text
1271             may affect the extraction accuracy of the model. This issue could potentially be resolved
1272             by introducing a more generalized reading order prediction module (Wang et al., 2021b).
1273
               • Deficiencies in Reasoning Ability: For tasks involving reasoning and computation, the per-
1274             formance of different large language models may vary significantly due to their inherent
1275             capabilities. Some models may even produce many hallucinations in their outputs.

1277   Errors in 0-shot LVLM-based methods. This type of scheme is currently more of a black-box
1278   approach, making it relatively difficult to pinpoint the root cause of its errors. However, certain
1279   types of errors can be identified through more obvious examples. For instance, in the second example
1280
       shown in Figure 12, it is evident that some of the LVLMs correctly find the position of the answers,
       but the output textual content is incorrect. This type of problem can be attributed to defects in their
1281
       perceptual capabilities.
1282
1283   Moreover, while the LVLM-based approach theoretically reduces the accumulation of errors, there
1284   are still issues due to the distribution of the large model training corpus. These issues can lead to
1285   defects in visually related or inference-related abilities.
1286   Errors in few-shot LVLM-based methods. In addition to the previously mentioned issues, we
1287   identified a prevalent flaw in approaches involving LVLMs. Specifically, when these models gener-
1288   ate answers based on provided examples, they often directly output the answers from the examples
1289   rather than performing recognition on the queried images. This observation highlights a signifi-
1290   cant limitation in the contextual capabilities of LVLMs, likely due to the insufficient inclusion of
1291   image-text interleaved data in their training samples.
1292   For instance, consider the NSR+InternVL2-26B model, as shown in Figure 15. When asked, “What
1293   is the mode of transportation?” for the queried image, the correct answer should be “river and sea
1294   transportation.” However, the nearest examples retrieved by NSR all contain the ground truth of
1295   ”waterway transport.” After incorporating these examples into the prompt, the model erroneously
       extracts the answer ”waterway transport” from the prompt and outputs it directly.

       This work still has some limitations:
1328
               • The dataset has not yet been evaluated by a third party to assess human performance. In-
1329             corporating third-party evaluations could provide more robust benchmarks and highlight
1330             areas for further improvement in both the dataset and the models tested.
1331
               • The size of the dataset can be further improved. The current Chinese dataset is relatively
1332
                 small within single domains due to the multi-domain nature of the dataset. The data se-
1333             lection strategy restricts the sampling of data with the same format, and many real-world
1334             sample types are not yet covered. We plan to continuously collect and expand the dataset
1335             to ensure wider coverage across various domains.
1336
               • The tests for the LLMs and LVLMs are not yet comprehensive. The rapid iteration and
1337             emergence of new large models in the market mean that some test conclusions may change
1338             quickly. Despite this limitation, the proposed dataset can provide a good reference bench-
1339             mark for evaluating the performance of current large models.
1340
1341   By addressing these limitations, we aim to enhance the dataset’s utility and the comprehensiveness
1342   of model evaluations, providing a more robust benchmark for future research and development in
