011                                                A BSTRACT
012
013            The self-attention mechanism is the key to the success of transformers in recent
014            Large Language Models (LLMs). However, the quadratic computational cost O(n2 )
015            in the input sequence length n is a notorious obstacle for further improvement
016
               and scalability in longer contexts. In this work, we leverage the convolution-like
               structure of attention matrices to develop an efficient approximation method for
017
               attention computation using convolution matrices. We propose a conv basis system,
018
               analogous to the rank basis, and show that any lower triangular matrix can always
019            be decomposed as a sum of structured convolution matrices in this basis. We
020            then design a fast algorithm to approximate the attention matrix via a sum of
021            such k convolution matrices. This allows us to compute the attention inference
022            via Fast Fourier Transforms (FFT) in O(knd log n) time, where d is the hidden
023            dimension, and thus achieve almost linear time n1+o(1) in the practical scenario
024            where kd = no(1) . Furthermore, the attention training forward and backward
025            gradient can be computed in n1+o(1) as well. We provide theoretical guarantees
026            on the run time and approximation error and conduct preliminary experiments to
027            evaluate its effectiveness. We hope our new paradigm for accelerating attention
028            computation in transformer models can help their application to longer contexts.

033
      Numerous notable large language models (LLMs) in natural language processing (NLP) have emerged
      in these two years, such as Mistral (Jiang et al., 2023), Gemini (Team et al., 2023), Claude3 (Anthropic,
034
      2024), GPT-4 (Achiam et al., 2023), Llama3 (AI, 2024) and so on. These models have profoundly
035
      changed the world and have been widely used in human activities, such as education (Kasneci
036   et al., 2023), law (Sun, 2023), finance (Li et al., 2023a), bio-informatics (Thirunavukarasu et al.,
037   2023), coding (Hou et al., 2024), and even creative writing (Achiam et al., 2023) such as top AI
038   conference reviews (Liang et al., 2024a). The key component of the generative LLMs success is
039   the decoder-only transformer architecture introduced by Vaswani et al. (2017). The transformer
040   uses the self-attention mechanism, allowing the model to capture long-range dependencies in the
041   input sequence. Self-attention computes a weighted sum of the input tokens, where the weights
042   are determined by the similarity between each pair of tokens. This enables the model to attend to
043   relevant information from different parts of the sequence when generating the output. However, the
044
      computational complexity of the self-attention in transformers grows quadratically O(n2 ) with the
      input length n, limiting their applicability to long context, e.g., 128k, 200k, 1000k input tokens for
045
      GPT4 (Achiam et al., 2023), Claude3 (Anthropic, 2024), Gemma (Team et al., 2024) respectively.
046
047   The complexity O(n2 ) comes from computing the similarity between each pair of tokens, which will
048   introduce an n × n size matrix. More specifically, let d be the hidden dimension and let Q, K ∈ Rn×d
049   be the query and key matrices of input. Then attention needs to compute Softmax on QK ⊤ ∈ Rn×n .
050
      Although QK ⊤ is at most rank-d, Softmax(QK ⊤ ) ∈ Rn×n may be full rank in Softmax attention.
051   To overcome the computational obstacle of Softmax(QK ⊤ ), many studies propose more efficient
052   attention computation methods that can scale gracely with the sequence length while maintaining
053   the model’s performance. Alman & Song (2023) show that if all entry of QK ⊤ is bounded and
      d = O(log n), Softmax(QK ⊤ ) will be “close” to a low-rank matrix. Then, they present an algorithm

130   previous works. (1) To guarantee a small approximation error, for the attention matrix, Alman & Song
131   (2023) needs bounded entries assumption and d = O(log n) assumption, while Han et al. (2024)

136   needs to assume d = O(log n) to get n1+o(1) time complexity. However, for our algorithm, as long

      & Song (2023) can only handle d = O(log n). (3) To guarantee a truly subquadratic running time,

      Attention matrix conv-like structure. Very recent works study the conv-like attention matrix.
149   Elhage et al. (2021); Olsson et al. (2022) find that in-context learning is driven by the formation
150   of “induction heads”–attention heads that copy patterns from earlier in the input sequence. This
151   is reflected in the attention matrix becoming more diagonal, with tokens attending primarily to
152   preceding tokens that match the current token. In Song & Zhong (2023) Figure 6, they show a similar
153   conv-like attention pattern for other important attention circuits. Figure 3 of Reddy (2024) shows
154   that in a minimal classification task, the abrupt emergence of in-context learning coincides with the
155   formation of an induction head, characterized by a diagonal attention pattern. Nichani et al. (2024)
156   proves that for a simplified task, gradient descent causes a transformer to encode the causal graph
157
      structure of the task in the attention matrix. This results in tokens attending primarily to their causal
      parents reflected in a sparse diagonal structure (Figure 2). In Li et al. (2024a), the conv-like attention
158
      matrix can also be observed when learning math tasks. Moreover, Cai et al. (2024) uses convolutional
159
      kernels to compress the KV-cache size for fast LLM generation.

       Extend to full self-attention. We can easily extend our method to full self-attention. Our proposed
1020   approach can be extended to accelerate full self-attention as well, not just the causal attention
1021   mechanism. Note that the full self-attention matrix can be split into a lower triangular matrix L and
1022   an upper triangular matrix U . Then, our conv-basis approximation method can be applied separately
1023   to L and the transpose of U . This allows the algorithm to handle both the lower and upper triangular
1024   components of the full attention matrix. The diagonal normalization step D−1 would need to be
1025   adjusted to account for the full matrix rather than just the lower triangular portion. Finally, we
       combine the approximations of L and U ⊤ to reconstruct the full self-attention output.

       Memory consumption. Our method does not increase the memory consumption because each
1027   convolution matrix can be stored as a n-dimention vector (see Definition 2.5). Therefore, our
1028   method requires O(kn) memory for k convolution matrices, O(nd) memory for the value matrix
1029   V ∈ Rn×d , and O(n) memory for the diagonal matrix D ∈ Rn×n . In total, our memory consumption
1030   is O(kn + nd). For the standard attention computation of D−1 AV , it requires O(n2 ) memory for
1031   the attention matrix A, O(nd) memory for the value matrix V ∈ Rn×d , and O(n) memory for the
1032   diagonal matrix D ∈ Rn×n . In total, the memory consumption is O(n2 + nd).

2512   Fast attention computation and long context LLM. The development of efficient attention com-
2513   putation has been an active area of research in recent years. The standard self-attention mechanism,
2514   introduced in the transformer architecture (Vaswani et al., 2017), has a quadratic complexity with
2515   respect to the sequence length, which limits its applicability to long sequences. To address this
2516
       limitation, various approaches have been proposed to improve the efficiency of attention computation.
       One line of research focuses on patterns of sparse attention that reduce the number of computations
2517
       (Child et al., 2019; Beltagy et al., 2020; Zaheer et al., 2020; Shi et al., 2023a; Han et al., 2024).
2518
       Another approach is to use low-rank approximations or random features for the attention matrix
2519   (Razenshteyn et al., 2016; Li et al., 2016; Wang et al., 2020; Choromanski et al., 2020; Zheng et al.,
2520   2022; Alman & Song, 2023; Ahn et al., 2024), which reduces the computational complexity to linear
2521   in the sequence length. In addition, using linear attention as a proxy of Softmax attention is a rich
2522   line of work (Tsai et al., 2019; Katharopoulos et al., 2020; Schlag et al., 2021; Zhang et al., 2023;
2523   Sun et al., 2023; Ahn et al., 2024; Shi et al., 2023b; Xu et al., 2024b; Zhang et al., 2024; Deng et al.,
2524   2023). These developments in efficient attention computation have enabled transformer-based models
2525   to process longer sequences and have opened up new possibilities for their application in various
2526   domains (Chen et al., 2023b; Su et al., 2024; Peng et al., 2024; Ding et al., 2024; Ma et al., 2024; Xu
2527
       et al., 2024c; An et al., 2024; Bertsch et al., 2024; Chen et al., 2024; Liang et al., 2024d; Jin et al.,
       2024; Shi et al., 2024).
2528
2529
       Convolution in language model and FFT. There are many subquadratic-time architectures are
2530
       proposed to address Transformers’ computational inefficiency on long sequences, gated convolution
2531
       recurrent models (Bai et al., 2018; Fu et al., 2023; Peng et al., 2023; Qin et al., 2023), and structured
2532   state space models (SSMs) (Gu et al., 2021; Gu & Dao, 2023). They can use global or local convolu-
2533   tion (Krizhevsky et al., 2012) operations to replace attention while keeping a comparable performance.
2534   The convolution operation can be computed by fast Fourier transform (FFT) efficiently (Pratt et al.,
2535   2017; Chi et al., 2020). Moreover, the development of efficient convolution algorithms like Winograd
2536   (Lavin & Gray, 2016) and FFT-based convolutions (Mathieu et al., 2013) has further optimized the
2537   computation, reducing the memory footprint and improving the overall speed. There are many other
       works studying Fourier transform (Price & Song, 2015; Moitra, 2015; Chen et al., 2016; Song, 2019;

       Lee et al., 2019; Chen et al., 2020; Song et al., 2022; Gao et al., 2022; Song et al., 2023a; Chen et al.,

2541   (Weighted) low rank approximation. Low-rank approximation has become an important tool
2542   in machine learning and numerical linear algebra, providing a way to extract the core structure of
2543   high-dimensional data while minimizing computational costs. Mathematically, we want to find
2544   matrices X, Y ∈ Rn×k such that ∥M − XY ⊤ ∥F is minimized. It has been applied to various fields,
2545   such as training multi-layer neural network Song et al. (2021), attention approximation Alman &
2546   Song (2023; 2024a), dynamic Kronecker product maintenance Song et al. (2023c), and tensor product
2547   regression Reddy et al. (2022). In practice, certain entries of M tend to be more important than
2548   others, leading to the study of the weighted low-rank approximation: finding matrices X, Y ∈ Rn×k
2549
       such that ∥W ◦ (M − XY ⊤ )∥F is minimized, where W ∈ Rn×n     ≥0 Li et al. (2016); Razenshteyn et al.
2550
       (2016); Song et al. (2023e); Gu et al. (2024). As data continues to grow in size and complexity,
       (weighted) low rank approximation remains an active area of research, with ongoing efforts to develop
2551
       more efficient, scalable, and robust methods for a wide range of applications.
