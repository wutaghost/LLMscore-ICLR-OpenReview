009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            The Optimal Transport (OT) problem investigates a transport map that connects
012            two distributions while minimizing a given cost function. Finding such a transport
013            map has diverse applications in machine learning, such as generative modeling and
014            image-to-image translation. In this paper, we introduce a scalable and simulation-
015            free approach for solving the Entropic Semi-Unbalanced Optimal Transport (ES-
016            UOT) problem. We derive the dynamical form of this ESUOT problem, which
017            is a generalization of the Schrödinger bridges (SB) problem. Based on this, we
018
               derive dual formulation and optimality conditions of the ESUOT problem from the
               stochastic optimal control interpretation. By leveraging these properties, we pro-
019
               pose a simulation-free algorithm to solve ESUOT, called Simulation-free ESUOT
020
               (SF-ESUOT). While existing SB models require expensive simulation costs during
021            training and evaluation, our model achieves simulation-free training and one-step
022            generation by utilizing the reciprocal property. Our model demonstrates signifi-
023            cantly improved scalability in generative modeling and image-to-image translation
024            tasks compared to previous SB methods.

027   1   I NTRODUCTION
028
029   The distribution transport problem investigates finding a transport map that bridges one distribution
030   to another. The distribution transport problem has various applications in machine learning, such
031   as generative modeling (Rout et al., 2022; Choi et al., 2023a; 2024; Shi et al., 2024; Dao et al.,
032   2023; Lübeck et al., 2022), image-to-image translation (Kim et al., 2024a), and biology (Bunne et al.,
033
      2023b; 2022). Optimal Transport (OT) (Peyré et al., 2019; Villani et al., 2009) explores the most
      cost-efficient transport map among them. For discrete measures, the OT map can be computed exactly
034
      through convex optimization, but it is computationally expensive. In contrast, the Entropic Optimal
035
      Transport (EOT) problem presents strict convexity and can be computed more efficiently through
036   the Sinkhorn algorithm (Sinkhorn, 1967; Cuturi, 2013). For continuous measures, several machine
037   learning approaches have been proposed for learning the EOT problem (Chen et al., 2021a; Shi et al.,
038   2024; Gushchin et al., 2024). These approaches typically utilize a dynamic version of EOT, known as
039   the Schrödinger Bridge problem (Léonard, 2013; Shi et al., 2024).
040
      The Schrödinger Bridge (SB) is a finite-time diffusion process that bridges two given distributions
041
      while minimizing the KL divergence to a reference process (Chen et al., 2021b; Léonard, 2013).
042   Various works for solving this SB problem have been proposed (Stromme, 2023; Finlay et al., 2020;
043   Bortoli et al., 2021; Chen et al., 2021a; Shi et al., 2024). However, these methods tend to exhibit
044   scalability challenges when the source and target distributions are in high-dimensional spaces and the
045   distance between them is large. Consequently, these approaches tend to be limited to low-dimensional
046   datasets (Stromme, 2023; Finlay et al., 2020) or rely on a pretraining process for generative modeling
047   task (Chen et al., 2021a; Shi et al., 2024). Moreover, existing approaches require simulation of the
048   diffusion process, leading to excessive training and evaluation costs (Shi et al., 2024; Chen et al.,
049   2021a; Gushchin et al., 2024).
050   In this paper, we propose an algorithm for solving the Entropic Semi-Unbalanced Optimal Transport
051   (ESUOT) problem, called Simulation-free ESUOT (SF-ESUOT). The ESUOT problem generalizes
052   the EOT problem by relaxing the precise matching of the target distribution into soft matching through
053   f -divergence minimization. Depending on the chosen divergence measure, the ESUOT problem can
      encompass the EOT problem. To solve this, our model is based on the stochastic optimal control

      Notations and Assumptions Let X = Rd where d is a data dimension. Let M2 (X ) be a set of
075
      positive Borel measures with finite second moment. Moreover, let P2 (X ) be a set of probability
076   densities in M2 (X ). Let Φ2 (X ) be a set of continuous functions φ : X → R such that |φ(x)| ≤
077   a + b∥x∥2 for all x ∈ X , for some a, b > 0. Let Φ2,b be a set of bounded-below functions
078   in Φ2 . Throughout the paper, let µ, ν ∈ P2 (X ) be the absolutely continuous source and target
079   distributions, respectively. In generative modeling tasks, µ and ν correspond to tractable noise and
080   data distributions. Π(µ, ν) denotes the set of joint probability distributions on P2 (X × X ) whose
081   marginals are µ and ν. Wt represents the standard Wiener process on X .

090   Kantorovich’s Optimal Transport (OT) Kantorovich’s Optimal Transport (OT) (Kantorovich,
091   1948) problem addresses the problem of searching for the most cost-effective way to transform the
092   source distribution µ to the target distribution ν. Formally, it can be expressed as the following
093
      minimization problem:                       Z
                                                     1
094                                        inf         ∥x − y∥22 dπ(x, y)                                   (1)
                                         π∈Π(µ,ν)    2
095
096   Here, we consider the quadratic cost 12 ∥x − y∥22 . For an absolutely continuous µ ∈ P2 (X ), the
097   optimal transport plan π ⋆ exists. Moreover, the optimal transport is deterministic. In other words,
098
      there exists a deterministic OT map T ⋆ : X → X such that (Id × T ⋆ )# µ = π ⋆ , i.e. π ⋆ (·|x) = δT ⋆ (x)
      is a delta measure.

      Entropic Optimal Transport (EOT) The Entropic Optimal Transport (EOT) problem is an entropy-
101
      regularized version of the OT problem, which introduces an entropy term for the coupling π to the
102   standard OT problem. Formally, EOT can be written as the following minimization problem:
103                                     Z
                                              1
104                               inf           ∥x − y∥2 dπ(x, y) − σ 2 H(π),                       (2)
                                π∈Π(µ,ν) X ×X 2

106   where H(π) denotes the entropy of π and σ > 0. The optimal transport plan π ⋆ of Eq. 2 is unique
107   due to the strict convexity of H(π). Moreover, when σ > 0, the optimal transport map is stochastic,
      i.e. π ⋆ (·|x) is a conditional probability distribution.

      In this section, we derive various formulations of the Entropic Semi-Unbalanced Optimal Trans-
179
      port (ESUOT). First, we introduce the ESUOT problem and derive its dynamical formulation
180   (Theorem 3.2). The dynamical formulation of ESUOT encompasses the Schrödinger Bridge (SB)
181   problem, which is a dynamical form of EOT (Sec. 2.2). Next, we derive the dynamical dual form of
182   ESUOT by leveraging the SB theory (Theorem 3.4). The dynamical dual will play a crucial role in
183   deriving our learning objective and we will utilize the reciprocal property from the dynamical for our
184   simulation-free parametrization in Sec 4.2. For detailed proof of the theorems, refer to Appendix A.1.
185
186   Entropic Semi-Unbalanced Optimal Transport (ESUOT) In this paper, we consider the ESUOT
187   problem with a fixed source measure constraint, i.e., π0 = µ (Eq. 14). While EOT assumes precise
188   matching of two measures, i.e., π0 = µ, π1 = ν (Eq. 2), ESUOT relaxes the target marginal constraint
189
      using the f -divergence DΨ . This unbalanced variant of the optimal transport problem offers outlier
      robustness (Balaji et al., 2020; Choi et al., 2023a) and the ability to handle class imbalance in datasets
190
      (Eyring et al., 2024). Formally, the ESUOT problem is defined as follows:
191                                    Z
                                               1
192                       inf                    ∥x − y∥2 dπ(x, y) − σ 2 H(π) + αDΨ (π1 |ν) ,               (14)
193                π0 =µ,π∈P2 (X ×X )    X ×X 2
194   where α > 0 denotes the divergence penalization intensity and Ψ : [0, ∞) → [0, ∞] is assumed to
195   be a convex, lower semi-continuous, non-negative function, and Ψ(1) = 0. We call Ψ an entropy
196   function of DΨ . Furthermore, due to the convexity of Ψ, the solution π ⋆ of Eq. 14 is unique.
197   Remark 3.1 (ESUOT is a Generalization of EOT). Suppose Ψ is a convex indicator ι at {1}, i.e.
198   ι(x) = 0 if x = 1 and ι(x) = ∞ otherwise. Then, the corresponding f -divergence Dι (ρ1 |ρ2 ) equals
199
      0 if ρ1 = ρ2 almost surely and ∞ otherwise. To obtain a finite objective in Eq. 14, it must hold
      π1 = ν almost surely. Therefore, when Ψ = ι, ESUOT becomes EOT.

528   6    C ONCLUSION
529
530   In this paper, we propose an algorithm for solving the Entropic Unbalanced Optimal Transport
531
      (EUOT) problem. We derived the dynamical formulation of EUOT. Then, we established the dual
      form and analyzed this dual form from the stochastic optimal control perspective. Our model is based
532
      on the simulation-free algorithm leveraging the reciprocal property of the dynamical formulation of
533
      EUOT problem. Our experiments demonstrated that our model addresses the scalability challenges
534   of previous Schrödinger Bridge models. Specifically, our model offers simulation-free training
535   and achieves state-of-the-art results in generative modeling on CIFAR-10 without diffusion model
536   pretraining. A limitation of this work is that our method demonstrates lower accuracy in learning
537   the EUOT compared to other models. We hypothesize that this is due to the inherent difficulty of
538   achieving precise matching using a PINN-style loss function. Additionally, due to computational
539   resource constraints, we were unable to test our model on high-resolution datasets such as CelebA-HQ
      (Liu et al., 2015) (256 × 256).

541
542
      Our approach significantly enhances the scalability of EOT algorithms, enabling the generation of
543   high-quality samples from large-scale datasets while maintaining an accurate representation of the
544   data distribution. As a result, we expect our model to impact various fields, including image transfer,
545   finance, image synthesis, healthcare, and anomaly detection. However, it is important to recognize
546   the potential negative societal implications of our work. Generative models can unintentionally learn
547   and magnify existing biases within the data, which may reinforce societal biases. Therefore, careful
548   monitoring and control are crucial when deploying these models in real-world applications. Rigorous
549   management of both the training data and the modeling process is essential to mitigate any potential

1040
1041   In this section, we clarify the connection of our method to various existing OT algorithms.
1042
1043   B.1     C ONNECTION TO WASSERSTEIN L AGRANGIAN F LOW
1044
1045   In this section, we clarify the relationship of our approach compared to Neklyudov et al. (2023).
1046   In Neklyudov et al. (2023), they suggests a general framework for handling dynamical optimal
1047   transport problems for various cost functionals where the marginal distributions are fixed for some
1048   timesteps {0 = t0 , . . . , tN −1 , tN = 1}. The algorithm is derived by leveraging the Lagrangian dual
1049
       formulations of these problems. Technically, this algorithm alternately updates the probability density
       ρt and value function V . It is easy to show that when the methodology proposed in Neklyudov et al.
1050
       (2023) is reduced to our problem, it becomes a max-min problem of Dual II (Proposition A.4).
1051
1052   In our approach, we similarly derive a the objective for the density ρ Appendix A.2 by leveraging the
1053   lagrangian dual formulation. The key difference to the objective derived in Neklyudov et al. (2023)
1054   lies in our active incorporation of optimality conditions of our EUOT formulation, which allowed
1055
       us to avoid the minimax objective on the HJB-like equation (Eq. 47). We believe this difference
       contributes to the scalability of our network, though the further investigation in required to fully
1056
       understand its impact.
1057
1058   Furthermore, there is a significant difference in the evaluation process. Unlike our approach, Neklyu-
1059   dov et al. (2023) does not directly learn the transport map. Instead, they train the value function vϕ
1060   and generate samples by solving stochastic differential equation (SDE) dynamics using the gradient
1061
       of vϕ , requiring approximately 100 function evaluations (NFE) for the evaluation. In contrast, our
       method directly parametrizes the transport map, enabling one-step evaluation.
1062
1063
1064
       B.2     C ONNECTION TO D IFFUSION S CHR ÖDINGER B RIDGE M ATCHING (DSBM)
1065
       A key difference between our method and Schrödinger bridge matching Shi et al. (2024); Liu et al. is
1066   that our approach eliminates burdensome simulations by directly parametrizing the transport plan Tθ .
1067   In Shi et al. (2024), the drift u := uθ of the SDE dXtu = udt + σdWt is learned in the following way:
1068   first, sample pairs {(X0u , X1u )} are obtained through SDE simulation. Then, sample an intermediate
1069   samples using the reciprocal property Xt ∼ ρt|0,1 (·|X0u , X1u ). Note that this approach requires the
1070   heavy simulation of X1u starting from X0u . Then, the drift uθ is updated by Markovian projection (or
1071   action matching) using these triplets {(X0u , Xt , X1u )}.
1072   In contrast, our model directly obtain {(X0 , X1 )} through the transport plan Tθ . Then, we directly
1073   obtain Xt ∼ ρt|0,1 (·|X0 , X1 ) by leveraging reciprocal property (Eq. 16). While our method reduces
1074   the simulation burden, it face some computational burden by the PINN-like objective.
1075
1076   B.3     OTHER R ELATED W ORKS
1077
1078   In this paragraph, we introduce several works that approximate the solution of EUOT between the
1079   continuous measures. It is challenging to directly address the EUOT problem in a continuous setting
       Gazdieva et al. (2023). Hence, several approaches utilize discrete minibatch EUOT approximations


                                                             20
       Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


1080
       based on Sinkhorn Algorithm Eyring et al. (2024); Klein et al. (2023). These works employ minibatch
1081   EUOT plans to train flow matching Lipman et al. (2023) or conditional flow matching Tong et al.
1082   (2023). On the other hand, Gazdieva et al. (2023) proposes a lightweight solver for the EUOT
1083   problem. This method is based on a Gaussian mixture approximation of the EUOT plan. These
1084   approaches rely on strong assumptions that the OT plan can be approximated by a minibatch EUOT
1085   plan or a Gaussian mixture. To the best of our knowledge, our work is the first attempt to propose a
1086   method for solving the continuous EUOT problem in Eq. 14, without any additional assumptions on
1087   the EUOT plan. Notably, our method also benefits from simulation-free training and offers one-step
1088   sample generation.
