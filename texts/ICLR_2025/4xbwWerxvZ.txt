011                                              A BSTRACT
012
013            Diffusion Inverse Solvers (DIS) are designed to sample from the conditional distri-
014            bution with a pre-trained diffusion model an operator and a measurement derived
015
               from an unknown image. Existing DIS estimate the conditional score function by
               evaluating operator with an approximated posterior sample. However, most prior
016
               approximations rely on the posterior means, which may not lie in the support of
017
               the image distribution and diverge from the appearance of genuine images. Such
018            out-of-support samples may significantly degrade the performance of the opera-
019            tor, particularly when it is a neural network. In this paper, we introduces a novel
020            approach for posterior approximation that guarantees to generate valid samples
021            within the support of the image distribution, and also enhances the compatibility
022            with neural network-based operators. We first demonstrate that the solution of
023            the Probability Flow Ordinary Differential Equation (PF-ODE) yields an effec-
024            tive posterior sample with high probability. Based on this observation, we adopt
025            the Consistency Model (CM), which is distilled from PF-ODE, for posterior sam-
026
               pling. Through extensive experiments, we show that our proposed method for
               posterior sample approximation substantially enhance the effectiveness of DIS
027
               for neural network measurement operators (e.g., in semantic segmentation). The
028
               source code is provided in the supplementary material.

049   Diffusion Inverse Solvers (DIS) are a family of algorithms designed to address the inverse problem
050   using diffusion priors (Li et al., 2023; Moser et al., 2024). Specifically, given an operator f (.), a
051   measurement y = f (x′0 ) from some unknown image x′0 , and a pre-trained diffusion model pθ (X0 ),
052   DIS aims to sample from the conditional distribution X0 ∼ pθ (X0 |y). For example, when f (.) is
053   a down-sampling operator, DIS functions as a perceptual super-resolution algorithm (Menon et al.,
      2020); when f (.) is an image segmentation operator, DIS generates an image which has the same


                                                        1
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


054
      segmentation map as the given one (Ye et al., 2024; Bansal et al., 2023) (See Figure 1). However,
055   directly sampling from conditional distribution pθ (X0 |y) is intractable. To tackle this challenge,
056   previous works have adopted a variety of techniques such as linear projection (Wang et al., 2022;
057   Kawar et al., 2022; Chung et al., 2022b; Lugmayr et al., 2022; Song et al., 2022; Pokle et al., 2024;
058   Cardoso et al., 2024), variational inference (Feng et al., 2023; Mardani et al., 2023; Janati et al.,
059   2024), Bayesian filtering (Dou & Song, 2023), sequential Monte Carlo (Wu et al., 2024a; Phillips
060   et al., 2024), proximal gradient methods (Xu & Chi, 2024) and conditional score estimation (Chung
061   et al., 2022a; Yu et al., 2023; Zhu et al., 2023; He et al., 2024; Song et al., 2023b; Boys et al., 2023;
062   Rout et al., 2023; 2024).
063   Among various DIS techniques, conditional score estimation methods (Chung et al., 2022a; Song
064   et al., 2023c) are the most widely adopted. These methods are suitable for general non-linear oper-
065   ators f (.) and are practically efficient. During the diffusion process from XT to X0 , they estimate
066   the conditional score ∇x log pθ (Xt |y) with posterior samples from pθ (X0 |Xt ). Since the posterior
067   pθ (X0 |Xt ) is intractable, various approximations to posterior sampling are proposed based on the
068   posterior mean. These approximations either directly adopt the posterior mean (Chung et al., 2022a;
069
      Yu et al., 2023; Zhu et al., 2023; He et al., 2024) or construct a Gaussian distribution centered at the
      posterior mean (Song et al., 2023b; Boys et al., 2023; Rout et al., 2023; 2024).
070
071   However, these posterior mean-based approximations often yield posterior samples that are far from
072   real images. It is well-known that the mean of a set of noisy images may not lie within the support
073   of the image distribution (Ledig et al., 2017; Blau & Michaeli, 2018; Zhang et al., 2024). While
074   these out-of-distribution approximations have been shown to be successful for simple operators f (.)
075
      such as down-sampling and motion blurring, they may fail for more complex operators, especially
      when f (.) are neural networks such as segmentation or classification (Ye et al., 2024; Bansal et al.,
076
      2023). This is partially because neural networks are particularly sensitive to out-of-distribution
077
      inputs, which can significantly degrade their performance. On the other hand, consistency models
078   (CM) (Song et al., 2023c) can effectively produce high quality images with a few steps, while their
079   relationship to the posterior samples is under-explored.
080
081
      To address the aforementioned challenges, we propose the following points:
082
             • We propose a novel approach for approximating posterior samples in DIS. Our approxima-
083
               tions are guaranteed to be valid images and perform well with neural network measurement
084
               operators.
085
086          • To justify our approximations are effective posterior samples, we first intuitions and exam-
087            ples on why the solution of the Probability Flow Ordinary Differential Equation (PF-ODE)
088            might have high density in posterior. Next, we demonstrate that the solution of PF-ODE
089
               has positive density lowerbound in posterior with high probability.
090          • Empirical results show that utilizing CM for posterior sample approximation significantly
091            improves the performance of DIS, particularly when the operators are neural networks,
092            such as semantic segmentation and image captioning.

198   It is known that the PF-ODE and reverse SDE, as defined in Eq. 3, share the same marginal distribu-
199   tion. If the score function is learned perfectly, this marginal distribution of X0 of the reverse process
200
      is the same as the original image distribution p(X0 ) (Song et al., 2020). Denote the solution of the
      PF-ODE given the initial condition Xt = xt as Φ(t, xt ). This solution lies within the support of the
201
      natural image distribution, meaning it represents a valid image with non-zero density:
202
203                                           p(X0 = Φ(t, xt )) > 0.                                       (7)
204
      Returning to the example in Figure 2, when using the PF-ODE as the posterior sample approxima-
205
      tion, the semantic segmentation operator f (.) produces a reasonable result. Furthermore, in Table 1,
206
      we quantitatively verify that the result of PF-ODE is a valid image (the sample has low FID and
207   KID scores). However, knowing that p(X0 = Φ(t, xt )) > 0 is not sufficient. Since we seek a
208   posterior sample approximation, we need to ensure that the solution Φ(t, xt ) has positive density in

      3.4   I MPLEMENTATION OF PF-ODE A PPROXIMATION USING C ONSISTENCY M ODELS
307   Directly solving the PF-ODE is also computationally intractable for DIS. Fortunately, the PF-ODE
308   can be distilled into a Consistency Model (CM) (Song et al., 2023c). Specifically, CM trains a one-
309   step neural function gθ (t, xt ) to approximate the solution of the PF-ODE, Φ(t, xt ). Its gradient is
310   cheap to evaluate. Thus, we can directly replace the step x0|t ∼ pθ (X0 |Xt = xt ) in Eq. 5 with
311   x0|t = gθ (t, xt ). As this approach is an improvement of diffusion posterior sampling (DPS) (Chung
312   et al., 2022a) using CM, we name it DPS-CM.
313
      In practice, we find that the Consistency Model (CM) often overfits the operator f (.). Specifically,
314   the CM-approximated sample x0|t is close to y after being processed by the operator f (.). However,
315   upon visual inspection, x0|t often appears misaligned with y. (See an example in Figure 6). In fact,
316   the resulting overfitted sample is an adversarial example (Szegedy et al., 2013), which aligns with
317   the label y according to the neural network but not according to human perception. To make f (.)
318   more robust, we propose adding small Gaussian noise to the output of the CM, as suggested in the
319   literature on adversarial robustness (Li et al., 2019).

      Base Diffusion Models For the pixel space diffusion model, we use the pre-trained the Elucidated
360   Diffusion Model (EDM) (Karras et al., 2022)—as provided by Song et al. (2023c). For EDM-
361   related methods, we adopt an ancestral sampler with 1000 Euler steps. For the Consistency Model
362   (CM) (Song et al., 2023c), we employ the official pre-trained model as provided by Song et al.
363   (2023c). For latent diffusion model Rombach et al. (2022), we use Stable Diffusion 1.5 (SD1.5)
364   with Dreamshaper v7 pre-trained weights. For latent consistency model (Luo et al., 2023a), we
365   adopt the official pre-trained model. The detailed setup is described in Appendix B.
366   Operators We evaluate all the methods with four different neural network measurement operators:
367   semantic segmentation, room layout estimation, image captioning, and image classification. For
368   layout estimation, we adopt the neural network proposed by Lin et al. (2018). For semantic segmen-
369   tation, we use the network developed by Zhou et al. (2017). For image captioning, we employ the
370   BLIP model (Li et al., 2022). For image classification, we use ResNet (He et al., 2015). Additionally,
371   we also evaluate the methods with a non-neural network operator: down-sampling (x4).
372   Datasets & Metrics Following the evaluation protocols of Song et al. (2023c) and Chung et al.

      5     R ELATED W ORK
514
515
516   An important branch of DIS focuses on linear operators f (.) using projection or pseudo-inverse
517
      techniques (Wang et al., 2022; Kawar et al., 2022; Chung et al., 2022b; Lugmayr et al., 2022; Song
      et al., 2022; Dou & Song, 2023; Pokle et al., 2024; Cardoso et al., 2024). For general, non-linear
518
      f (.), various approaches have been proposed, including Monte Carlo methods (Wu et al., 2024a;
519
      Phillips et al., 2024), proximal gradient methods (Xu & Chi, 2024), and variational inference (Feng
520   et al., 2023; Mardani et al., 2023; Janati et al., 2024). Among these paradigms, conditional score
521   estimation methods are the most widely adopted as they are scalable to practically large images with
522   reasonable runtime (Chung et al., 2022a; Yu et al., 2023; Zhu et al., 2023; He et al., 2024; Song et al.,
523   2023b; Boys et al., 2023; Rout et al., 2023; 2024; Bansal et al., 2023). Following this paradigm, we
524   propose to approximate the posterior sample using the PF-ODE, which improves results for neural
525   network measurement operators f (.). After the submission of this paper, we notice that Wang et al.
526   (2024); Wu et al. (2024b) also solve the DIS for general noisy operator. And Zhao et al. (2024) also
527   leverage CM to solve DIS.

      6     D ISCUSSION & C ONCLUSION
531
532   One limitation of this paper is that we only consider simple image datasets such as LSUN Bedroom
533   and LSUN Cat. Training-free generation with conditions such as segmentation can be very challeng-
534   ing on complicated image datasets like ImageNet. Consequently, most previous works have focused
535   on simpler images, such as faces and dogs (Yu et al., 2023; Bansal et al., 2023). Therefore, we also
536
      focus on simple image datasets and leave the generation of more complex images as future work.
537   To conclude, we show that the solution of PF-ODE is an effective posterior sample. Built upon this,
538   we propose to use CM as a high-quality approximation to posterior sample. Further, we propose a
539   new family of DIS using only CM. Experimental results show that our DPS-CM perform well for
      neural network measurement operators.
