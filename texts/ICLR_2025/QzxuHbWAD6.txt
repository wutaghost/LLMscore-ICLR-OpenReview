011                                               A BSTRACT
012
013            In this paper, we study the necessity of federated learning (FL) for online linear
014            regression with decentralized data. Previous work proved that FL is unnecessary
015
               for minimizing regret in full information setting, while we prove that it can be
               necessary if only limited attributes of each instance are observed. We call this
016
               problem online sparse linear regression with decentralized data (OSLR-DecD).
017
               We propose a federated algorithm for OSLR-DecD, and prove a lower bound on
018            the regret of any noncooperative algorithm. In the case of d = o(M ), the upper
019            bound on the regret of our algorithm is smaller than the lower bound, demonstrat-
020            ing the necessity of FL, in which M is the number of clients and d is the dimension
021            of data. When M = 1, we give the first lower bound on the regret and improve
022            previous upper bounds. We invent three new techniques including an any-time
023            federated online mirror descent with negative entropy regularization, a paradigm
024            for client-server collaboration with privacy protection, and a reduction from on-
025            line sparse linear regression to prediction with limited advice for establishing the
026
               lower bound on the regret, some of which might be of independent interest.

112   1.2   T ECHNICAL C ONTRIBUTIONS
113
114   In designing AMRO and FedAMRO, and in proving the lower bound, we propose three new tech-
115   niques, some of which might be of independent interest.
116   The first one is an any-time online mirror descent (OMD) with negative entropy regularization.
117   AMRO and FedAMRO use the new OMD to update sampling probability. To be specific, we use
118   time-variant decision sets and learning rates in the OMD, thereby eliminating the need on the prior
119   value of T . Our technique is more elegant than the standard doubling trick. The regret analysis is
120   also more technical than the well-known OMD with constant decision sets (Bubeck & Cesa-Bianchi,
121   2012). The time-variant decision sets and regret analysis are also suitable for other variants of OMD,
122   such as optimistic OMD (Rakhlin & Sridharan, 2013).
123   The second one is a new paradigm for cooperating among clients and server. Most of federated
124   learning algorithms transmit estimators of gradient from clients to server, while our algorithm trans-
125   mits statistics constructed from estimators of instance (the representation of each example). The
126   new information not only makes communication efficient, but also safeguards against data leakage.
127
      The third one is a non-trivial reduction from OSLR to prediction with limited advice (Seldin et al.,
128   2014) for establishing the first lower bound on the regret. By this lower bound, we further establish
129   the lower bound on the regret of any noncooperative algorithm for OSLR-DecD.

      Most of previous work proposed federated learning algorithms for online learning with decentralized
134
      data, but without demonstrating their necessity, such as federated online mirror descent (Mitra et al.,
135
      2021), communication-efficient federated online gradient descent (Gogineni et al., 2023; Kwon

137   efficient federated online multi-kernel learning algorithms (Shen et al., 2021; Ghari & Shen, 2022;
138   Hong & Chae, 2022). For distributed bandit convex optimization, Patel et al. (2023) gave an incom-
139   plete answer on the necessity of federated learning, since they did not prove a lower bound on the
140   regret of any noncooperative algorithm. A recent study on online model selection with decentral-
141   ized data (OMS-DecD) demonstrated that collaboration is essential when clients face computational
142   constraints (Li et al., 2024). Although a federated online mirror descent has been proposed, it neces-
143   sitates the prior value of T . We propose a new federated online mirror descent which does not require
144
      the prior value of T . The algorithms and results for OMS-DecD are not suitable for OSLR-DecD.
      The problem that whether federated learning is necessary for OSLR-DecD is still open.

      The first one is making communication efficient. For each j ∈ [M ], if the client maintains pt and
297    (j)            (j)
      ft,1 , . . . , ft,N , then the client must transmit estimators of gradient and loss to server and receive a
298
      global probability distribution and global models from server. The communication cost is O(M N )
299
      bits. It is necessary to limit the communication cost to O(M · poly(d)) bits.
300
301   The second one is protecting the raw data of clients. Due to the communication constraint, it is
302   challenging to avoid transmitting estimators of gradient and loss to server and avoiding privacy
303   leakage simultaneously.
304   Next we give a high-level explanation on the algorithm. We propose two techniques for addressing
305   the challenges. The first one is decoupling prediction and updating. Specifically, clients make
306   predictions, while server aggregates information from clients and updates probability distributions
307   and hypotheses. Our algorithm independently samples a hypothesis for each client, and only sends
308   the selected hypotheses to clients, thereby achieving efficient communication. The second one is a
309   new paradigm for cooperating among clients and server. To be specific, we construct novel statistics
310
      (not estimators of loss and gradient) that will be sent by clients. Benefit from our estimators of xt in
      (4), we can enhance the privacy by incorporating random noises into the estimators.

531   8     C ONCLUSION
532
533   In this paper, we have proved that federated learning is necessary for OSLR-DecD, contrasting with
534   the pessimistic result that federated learning is unnecessary for OLR-DecD in full information set-
535   ting. We proposed a federated algorithm for DecD-OSLR, and proved that its regret bound is smaller
536
      than the lower bound of any noncooperative algorithm in the case of d = o(M ). The experimen-
      tal results also verify our theoretical findings. Our work demonstrates that federated learning is
537
      necessary for online learning with decentralized data in the case of limited resource.
538
539   In the future, it would be interesting to close the gap between the lower and upper bounds on the
      regret, and explore whether the condition d = o(M ) can be relaxed, such as for any value of d > b.
