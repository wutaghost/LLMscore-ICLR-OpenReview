009   A deep neural network (DNN) is said to be undistillable if, when used as a black-box input-output
010   teacher, it cannot be distilled through knowledge distillation (KD). In this case, the distilled student
011   (referred to as the knockoff student) does not outperform a student trained independently with label
012   smoothing (LS student) in terms of prediction accuracy. To protect intellectual property of DNNs, it is
013   desirable to build undistillable DNNs. To this end, it is first observed that an undistillable DNN may
014   have the trait that each cluster of its output probability distributions in response to all sample instances
015   with the same label should be highly concentrated to the extent that each cluster corresponding to
016
      each label should ideally collapse into one probability distribution. Based on this observation and
      by measuring the concentration of each cluster in terms of conditional mutual information (CMI), a
017
      new training method called CMI minimized (CMIM) method is proposed, which trains a DNN by
018
      jointly minimizing the conventional cross entropy (CE) loss and the CMI values of all temperature
019   scaled clusters across the entire temperature spectrum. The resulting CMIM model is shown, by
020   extensive experiments, to be undistillable by all tested KD methods existing in the literature. That
021   is, the knockoff students distilled by these KD methods from the CMIM model underperform the
022   respective LS students. In addition, the CMIM model is also shown to performs better than the model
023   trained with the CE loss alone in terms of their own prediction accuracy. The code for the paper is
024   publicly available at https://github.com/ICLR2024CMIM/CMIM.

027   1    I NTRODUCTION
028
029   Originally aiming for model compression, knowledge distillation (BuciluaМҢ et al., 2006; Hinton et al.,
030   2015) (KD) has received significant attention from both academia and industry in recent years due to
031   its remarkable effectiveness. The essence of KD is to transfer the knowledge of a pre-trained large
032   model (teacher) to a smaller model (student). Building on the work of Hinton et al. (2015), numerous
033   follow-up works have endeavored to enhance the performance of KD (Romero et al., 2014; Anil
034   et al., 2018; Park et al., 2019) and to gain deeper insights into why distillation is effective (Phuong
035   & Lampert, 2019; Mobahi et al., 2020; Ye et al., 2024; Allen-Zhu & Li, 2020; Menon et al., 2021;
036
      Borup & Andersen, 2021).
037   In the scenario where the teacher does not want its knowledge to be transferred, however, KD is
038   undesirable and indeed poses a threat to intellectual property (IP) of the teacher (Shokri & Shmatikov,
039   2015). Developing and training a high-quality large DNN requires significant investments of time,
040   effort, finances, and resources, including intensive data annotation and computational infrastructure.
041   Developers of the large DNN may want to prevent the knowledge of the large DNN from being
042
      transferred by their competitors. However, once the large DNN is released as a вҖңblack boxвҖқ, anyone
      can apply a logit-based KD method (or equivalently a distribution-based KD method (Zheng &
043
      Yang, 2024)) to distill the DNN as a teacher. The goal is to train a student, referred to as a knockoff
044
      student in the context of DNN IP protection, that mimics the teacherвҖҷs behavior to gain competitive
045   advantages. As such, in this case, it would be desirable for the developers to build the large DNN so
046   that it is undistillable. The question, of course, is how.
047
048
      Before delving deeper into the above question, let us first clarify what we mean by saying that a DNN
      is undistillable. At this point, we invoke the concept of distillable DNN introduced recently in Yang
049
      & Ye (2024):
050
051   Definition 1.1. [Distillability of a DNN (Yang & Ye, 2024)] When used as a black-box input-output
052   teacher, a DNN is said to be distillable with respect to a student if there exists a KD method which,
053   when applied to the teacher and student, yields a knockoff student outperforming the student trained
      alone with label smoothing (LS student) in terms of prediction accuracy.


                                                           1
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


054
      Therefore, a DNN is undistillable if no knockoff student can outperform the respective LS student
055   regardless of which logit-based KD method is used. Since there are so many logit-based KD methods
056   and so many students, what type of a DNN is undistillable? In comparison with the training of LS
057   student, Definition 1.1 provides some insight into a trait that an undistillable DNN may possess.
058   For each label, consider the cluster of the output probability distributions of the DNN in response
059   to all input sample instances with that label. If each cluster corresponding to each label is highly
060   concentrated to the extent that all probability distributions within the cluster more or less collapse into
061   one probability distribution, then the student training within KD is similar to that of the respective
062   LS student regardless of which logit-based KD method and which student are applied. In this case,
063
      one would expect that no knockoff student would perform significantly better than the respective LS
      student. Therefore, a DNN possessing this trait will likely be undistillable.
064
065   Given a DNN, we now measure the concentration of its clusters in terms of conditional mutual
066   information (CMI) (Yang et al., 2023). Specifically, let X denote the random input sample to the
067   DNN, and Y be the ground truth label of X. Let YМӮ denote the random label predicted by the DNN
068   in response to input X. It was shown in Yang et al. (2023) that for each label y, the label specific
069   CMI I(X; YМӮ | Y = y) measures the concentration of the cluster corresponding to label y, and the
070   CMI I(X; YМӮ | Y ) measures the average concentration across all clusters. To build an undistillable
071   DNN, one then is motivated to minimize jointly the conventional cross entropy (CE) loss and the
072   CMI I(X; YМӮ | Y ).
073
      In this paper, we will go one step further. In KD (Geoffrey Hinton, 2015), temperature scaling of
074   logits is often applied. It was shown in Zheng & Yang (2024) that logit temperature scaling with
075   temperature T can be equivalently achieved by power transform of the output probability distribution
076   with power Оұ = 1/T . Further, it was demonstrated in Ye et al. (2024) that the purpose of temperature
077   scaling or power transform is to enlarge the CMI values of temperature scaled (or power transformed)
078   clusters, and enlarging CMI values in turn improves the performance of distilled students. Since here
079   we want to achieve the opposite, we want to make sure that all CMI values of all power transformed
080   clusters can be made small. To this end, we further extend the label specific CMI I(X; YМӮ | Y = y) and
081   the CMI I(X; YМӮ | Y ) to I(X; YМӮ Оұ | Y = y) and I(X; YМӮ Оұ[Y ] | Y ), respectively, so that I(X; YМӮ Оұ | Y = y)
082   measures the concentration of the power transformed cluster corresponding to label y with power Оұ,
083   and I(X; YМӮ Оұ[Y ] | Y ) measures the average concentration across all power transformed clusters with
084   power Оұ[Y ], where different clusters may be power transformed with different power Оұ.
085   Based on the above discussion and towards building undistillable DNNs, we then propose a new
086   training method called CMI minimized method, which trains a DNN by jointly minimizing the CE
087   loss and all CMI values of all power transformed clusters, i.e., jointly minimizing the CE loss and
088   I(X; YМӮ Оұ[Y ] | Y ), вҲҖОұ[Y ] > 0.
089
090
      The resulting trained DNN is referred to as the CMI minimized (CMIM) DNN. The contributions of
      the paper are summarized as follows:
091
092   вҖў An insight is provided that in order for a DNN to be undistillable, it is desirable for the DNN to
093   possess the trait that each cluster of the DNNвҖҷs output probability distributions corresponding to each
094   label is highly concentrated to the extent that all probability distributions within the cluster more or
095   less collapse into one probability distribution close to the one-hot probability vector of that label.
096   вҖў We extend the label specific CMI I(X; YМӮ | Y = y) and the CMI I(X; YМӮ | Y ) to I(X; YМӮ Оұ | Y = y)
097
      and I(X; YМӮ Оұ[Y ] | Y ), respectively, so that I(X; YМӮ Оұ | Y = y) measures the concentration of the power
098
      transformed cluster corresponding to label y with power Оұ, and I(X; YМӮ Оұ[Y ] | Y ) measures the average
099   concentration across all power transformed clusters with power Оұ[Y ], where different clusters may
100   be power transformed with different power Оұ.
101
102
      вҖў We develop a novel training method dubbed CMI minimized method to train a DNN by jointly
      minimizing the CE loss and all CMI values of all power transformed clusters with the resulting
103
      trained DNN referred to as the CMIM DNN.
104
105   вҖў We show, by extensive experiments over three popular image classification datasets, namely CIFAR-
106   100 (Krizhevsky et al., 2012), TinyImageNet (Le & Yang, 2015) and ImageNet (Deng et al., 2009),
107   that CMIM DNNs have very small CMI values and are indeed undistillable by all tested KD methods
      existing in the literature. That is, the knockoff students distilled by these KD methods from the CMIM

114   2     R ELATED W ORKS
115
116   In this section, we mention some defense methods against the threat posed by knockoff students
117   attempting to steal the IP of pre-trained DNNs via logit-based KD methods. For a thorough review of
118   related works, including detailed discussions about recent logit-based KD methods, please refer to
119   Appendix B. These defense methods can be mainly categorized into two groups: (i) model stealing
120   resistant training methods that specifically train DNNs to reduce the accuracy of knockoff students
121   while maintaining the original classification accuracy of the model (Ma et al., 2021; Wang et al.,
122
      2022); and (ii) post-training defense methods that perform minimal perturbations to the pre-trained
      modelвҖҷs predictions to mislead the knockoff student (Lee et al., 2019; Orekondy et al., 2020; Cheng
123
      & Cheng, 2023). Nonetheless, in Section 5, we will show that models trained by all these defense
124
      methods are indeed distillable.

527   6     C ONCLUSION
528
529   In this paper, from an information-theoretic perspective, we proposed a defence method against the
530   threat posed by knockoff students attempting to steal the IP of pre-trained DNNs via logit-based KD
531   methods. In particular, we proposed to minimize the CMI of the protected DNN across different power
532   transform hyper-parameter values Оұ, while minimizing the conventional CE loss simultaneously.
533   We referred to model trained by these framework as CMIM models. By conducting a series of
534   experiments, we showed that, unlike the prior defense methods proposed in the literature, a knockoff
535   student cannot render CMIM models distillable. In addition, we showed that the models trained by
536
      CMIM achieve higher classification accuracy compared to those trained by CE loss.
537   Despite these promising results, our work has certain limitations. First, the evaluation of CMIM
538   models is primarily empirical, as providing a formal theoretical proof of undistillability remains an
539   open challenge. Second, our approach introduces additional computational overhead compared to the
      conventional training using CE loss.

707   B.1   L OGIT- BASED KD METHODS
708
709
      Knowledge Distillation (KD) has become a cornerstone technique for compressing large teacher
      models into smaller student models. This section reviews key logit-based KD methods and explores
710
      their advancements.
711
712   Hinton et al. (2015) introduced the foundational concept of KD, using KL divergence to match the
713   studentвҖҷs softmax outputs to the teacherвҖҷs. This work laid the groundwork for logit-based methods, as
714   the softmax output directly relates to the logits. Later, Moldovan et al. (2019) proposed Path-KD,
715
      a method that utilizes the paths leading to the correct class in both teacher and student models for
      distillation. While not directly logit-based, it demonstrates the effectiveness of aligning decision-
716
      making processes. Guo et al. (2021) proposed Logit-Like Distillation, addressing the capacity gap
717
      by matching the ranking of logits instead of their exact values [4]. This approach allows the student
718   to learn the essential ordering of classes even with limited capacity. An et al. (2021) proposed
719   relation knowledge distillation (RKD), focusing on aligning relationships between class logits rather
720   than individual values. This approach improves the studentвҖҷs ability to generalize to unseen data.
721   Zhao et al. (2022) introduced decoupled knowledge distillation (DKD), where it decouples the
722   classical KD loss into two parts: target class knowledge distillation and non-target class knowledge
723   distillation. Huang et al. (2022b) proposed DIST where they designed a KD method to distill better
724   from a stronger teacher; indeed they claim that preserving the relations between the predictions of
725   teacher and student would suffice for an effective KD. Borup & Andersen (2021) provided theoretical
726
      arguments for the importance of weighting the teacher outputs with the ground-truth targets when
      performing self-distillation with kernel ridge regressions along with a closed form solution for the
727
      optimal weighting parameter.
728
729
730   B.2   D EFENSE METHODS AGAINST L OGIT- BASED KD
731
732
      As also discussed in Section 2, the defense methods against the threat posed by knockoff students
      attempting to steal the IP of pre-trained DNNs via logit-based KD methods can be categorized into
733
      two approaches. Here, we elaborate on these two approaches.
734
735   (I) Model stealing resistant training: In this approach, DNNs are trained to reduce the accuracy
736   of knockoff students while maintaining the original classification accuracy of the model. In partic-
737   ular, Ma et al. (2021) proposed a training algorithm named self-undermining KD to create nasty
738
      teachers (NT) that prevent knowledge leakage and unauthorized model stealing through KD, without
      compromising model accuracy. The nasty teacher is trained by minimizing the following objective
739
      function:
740
741                                     LN T = H(y, qx ) вҲ’ Пө KL(qМғx , qx ),                               (30)
742
743   where qМғx is a output of a pre-trained standard model.
744   Subsequently, Wang et al. (2022) proposed semantic nasty teachers (SNT) which improve the model
745   stealing resistance of NT by disentangling semantic relationships in the output logits during teacher
746   model training, which is crucial for successful KD.
747
      (II) post-training defence methods: The aim of these approaches is to deceive the knockoff
748
      by imposing minimal perturbations to the modelвҖҷs predictions. Lee et al. (2019) tested a variety
749
      of possible perturbation forms, and found that the reverse sigmoid perturbation (RSP) to be the
750   most effective one. Orekondy et al. (2020) introduced maximizing angular deviation (MAD), a
751   technique that perturbs the output probabilities, leading to an adversarial gradient signal that deviates
752   significantly from the original gradient of the knockoff. To this end, they applied a randomly
753   initialized model as the surrogate for the potential knockoff. More recently, Cheng & Cheng (2023)
754   proposed a plug-and-play generative perturbation model, dubbed as accuracy preserving generative
755   perturbation (APGP), which can effectively defend KD-based model cloning, while preserve the
      model utility.

768   B.3   ATTACK M ETHODS U SING L OGIT- BASED KD
769
770   Jandial et al. (2022) sought to circumvent the defense of nasty teachers and steal (or extract) its
771
      information. Specifically, they analyzed nasty teacher from two different angles and subsequently
      leverage them carefully to develop simple yet efficient methodologies, named as HTC and SCM,
772
      which enhance learning from nasty teacher.
773
774   In AVG (Keser & Toreyin, 2023), the authors noted that undistillable teachers exhibit multiple peaks
775   in their softmax response, which are transferred to the student models. These peaks are considered
776   to be the primary factor that misleads the student models. To mitigate the influence of the multiple
777
      peaks in the softmax response of teachers, they proposed transferring the mean of features with the
      same labels as the soft labels.
778
779   Orekondy et al. (2019) introduced a technique called вҖқKnockoff NetsвҖқ that allows an attacker to
780   steal the functionality of black-box models. Remarkably, the attacker only needs to interact with
781   the model by feeding it input data and observing the resulting predictions. By training a new model
782   (вҖқknockoffвҖқ) on these input-prediction pairs, the attacker can create a copycat model that performs
783
      similarly to the original black box.

786
787
      Original aiming to prevent overfitting and improve generalization, label smoothing was observed
788
      by MuМҲller et al. (2019) to reduce the accuracy of the knockoff student. The researchers found that
789   вҖқlabel smoothing encourages examples to lie in tight, equally separated clustersвҖқ. Consequently, label
790   smoothing reduces the contextual information in the teacher modelвҖҷs output (Yang et al., 2024).
791
792

796   defense methods could be made distillable (as shown in Section 5). Indeed, the reason lies in the fact
797   that by appropriately adjusting the power transform value Оұ, the DNNs trained using these defense
798   methods can potentially achieve a high CMI value compared to our proposed method CMIM (see
799   Figure 3). Thus, using this specific Оұ value during distillation, a logit-based KD method can render
800
      these DNNs distillable effectively.
