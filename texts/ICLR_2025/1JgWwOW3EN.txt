009                                              A BSTRACT
010
011            Molecular representation learning (MRL) plays a vital role in high-precision drug
012            discovery. Currently, people represent molecules in different modalities (such as
013            sequences, graphs, and images), and have developed many MRL methods. How-
014            ever, three key challenges hinder further progress in the field of MRL: (i) Lack
015            of systematic and unified evaluation on models of different modalities, resulting
016
               in unfair comparisons or being affected by randomness; (ii) The specific advan-
               tages between different molecular modalities are unclear; (iii) Lacking a unified
017
               platform to integrate data of different modalities and a large number of MRL meth-
018
               ods. Therefore, we propose the first MRL platform supporting different modal-
019            ities, called BenchMol, to integrate a large number of sing-modal MRL meth-
020            ods with different modalities and evaluate them systematically and fairly. Bench-
021            Mol has four attractive features: (i) Rich modalities: BenchMol supports 7 major
022            modalities of molecules, such as fingerprint, sequence, graph, geometry, image,
023            geometry image, and video; (ii) Comprehensive methods: BenchMol integrates
024            23 mainstream MRL methods to process these modalities; (iii) New benchmarks:
025            BenchMol constructs two new benchmarks based on PCQM4Mv2 and ChEMBL
026            34, called MBANet and StructNet, for a more systematic evaluation. (iv) Com-
027
               prehensive evaluation: evaluation covers different aspects of molecules, such as
               basic attributes and molecular types. Through BenchMol, we conduct large-scale
028
               research on methods of different modalities and report many insightful findings.
029
               We hope that BenchMol can help researchers quickly use MRL methods with dif-
030            ferent modalities on the one hand; and on the other hand, provide meaningful
031            insights into multi-modal MRL and help researchers choose appropriate represen-
032            tations in downstream tasks. We open-sourced BenchMol in Github.

053   Challenges. Despite the remarkable success of MRL, there are still three key challenges in its devel-
      opment that hinder the further development of multi-modal MRL methods:

054
         I. Unfair comparison. Differences in evaluation strategies lead to incomparable or unfair com-
055         parisons between methods. We summarize four differences in the evaluation process, includ-
056         ing the method for dividing the dataset (Rong et al., 2020; Ross et al., 2022), the range of
057         parameter optimization (Hu et al., 2020a; Wang et al., 2022), the standardization of labels
058         (Ross et al., 2022; Zhou et al., 2023), and the selection of random seed (Zeng et al., 2022;
059         Wang et al., 2022; Xia et al., 2023). For example, MoLFormer (Ross et al., 2022) uses ran-
060         dom scaffold split to divide the dataset and is compared with MolCLR (Wang et al., 2022)
061         and GraphMVP-C (Liu et al., 2022a) which use strict scaffold split. In general, it is easier to
062         achieve good performance on datasets with random scaffold split than on datasets with scaf-
063
            fold split. Compared to previous studies (Hu et al., 2020a) that use the same hyperparameters
            for different tasks and run 10 replicates, MolCLR performs independent hyperparameter op-
064
            timization for different tasks and runs 3 replicates. BARTSmiles (Chilingaryan et al., 2022),
065
            Uni-Mol (Zhou et al., 2023) and MoLFormer use label regularization to compare with other
066         methods. Hu et al. and Xia et al. use consistent random seeds from 0 to 9 to initialize the
067         model while a large number of studies (Rong et al., 2020; Zeng et al., 2022; Wang et al., 2022)
068         do not explicitly state the random seeds, which may result in a benchmark deviation. There-
069         fore, it is necessary to build a platform to fairly evaluate methods with different modalities,
070         which will pave the way for researchers to explore scientific questions instead of falling into
071         biases caused by experimental differences.
072
        II. Incomplete evaluation for different modality data. MRL is evolving towards a multi-modal
073         direction, relying on various technology stacks, such as sequence modalities based on Natural
074         Language Processing (NLP) (Devlin et al., 2019; Lewis et al., 2020), graph modality based
075         on graph deep learning (Xu et al., 2018), geometry modality based on geometry deep learning
076         (Monti et al., 2017; Atz et al., 2021), and image, geometry image and video modalities based
077         on Computer Vision (CV) (He et al., 2020; Kirillov et al., 2023). Currently, a large number
078         of methods based on different modalities have been developed in the field of MRL (Hu et al.,
079         2020a; Ross et al., 2022; Zeng et al., 2022). Intuitively, the data and encoding methods be-
080         tween different modalities are different, which may lead to their preference for different types
081
            of molecules. However, this preference is still unclear and deserves further exploration. In
            addition, researchers often focus on the task of molecular property prediction from Molecu-
082
            leNet Wu et al. (2018) in the evaluation of MRL (Xiang et al., 2023; Xia et al., 2023; Xiang
083
            et al., 2024a). However, a single evaluation is not comprehensive for studying preferences
084         in molecular representation of different modalities. Here, We introduce the Molecular Ba-
085         sic Attribute (MBANet) benchmark built from IEM (Xiang et al., 2024a) and the StructNet
086         benchmark built from the ChemBL 34 database (Zdrazil et al., 2024), as shown in Figure 2, to
087         further evaluate the ability of these models to identify essential molecular attributes and mine
088         information from different types of molecules.
089
       III. Lack of a unified platform supporting diverse modalities. MRL methods of different
090
            modalities are scattered in various corners of the Internet with different development envi-
091         ronments and different running pipelines. It is challenging to integrate various methods with
092         different modalities into a unified platform to support multiple modality data. Currently, there
093         remains a blank in the molecular-based platform supporting diverse modalities. We hope to
094         propose a unified molecular platform to provide meaningful insights for multi-modal MRL
095         and facilitate the use and development of multi-modal molecules by researchers.
096

139   2   R ELATED WORKS
140
141   Molecular representation learning (MRL). Existing MRL can be mainly divided into the fol-
142   lowing categories, including sequences, 2D topological graphs, 3D geometric graphs, 2D images,
143   3D geometry images, and videos. Sequence-based methods represent molecules as 1-dimensional
144   strings (such as SMILES) and use NLP-related techniques to learn molecular representations (Ross
145   et al., 2022; Zheng & Tomiura, 2024). Graph-based methods treat the atoms and bonds of molecules
146   as nodes and edges in a graph and use GNN and its variants to extract features (Hu et al., 2020a;
147
      Wang et al., 2022). Image-based methods treat molecules as a flat image and use computer vi-
      sion (CV)-related techniques for processing (Zeng et al., 2022; Xiang et al., 2023; Zhang et al.,
148
      2023). Subsequently, considering the importance of geometric information in molecules, geometric
149
      deep learning methods represent molecules as geometric graphs and extract information from them
150   (Schütt et al., 2021; Liu et al., 2022b). Meanwhile, image-based methods render the geometric in-
151   formation of molecules into geometry images and perform feature extraction (Xiang et al., 2024a).
152   Recently, video-based methods have been proposed, which represent the conformation of molecules
153   as a video and extract features (Xiang et al., 2024b). Given that MRL is rapidly evolving towards dif-
154   ferent modalities, it is necessary to aggregate these methods with different modalities into a unified
155   platform and accelerate the development and use of researchers.
156   Molecular benchmark platforms. OGB (Hu et al., 2020b) proposes a set of diverse, challeng-
157   ing and realistic benchmark datasets covering molecular graphs, which mainly focus on graph data.
158   Molecule3D (Xu et al., 2021) develops a benchmark that includes a dataset with precise ground-
159   state geometries of approximately 4 million molecules, and provides a few baseline methods based
160   on DeeperGCN (Li et al., 2023) and DAGNN (Yang et al., 2021). Deng et al. (2023) focuses on
161   the evaluation of molecular fingerprints and 2D graphs. MOLGRAPHEVAL (Wang et al., 2024)
      focuses on evaluating the impact of different pre-training strategies based on 2D graphs. Geom3D


                                                         3
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


162
      (Liu et al., 2024) focuses on geometric data of different biological entities (such as molecules, pro-
163   teins, crystalline materials). However, with the increase in modality data, the integration of multiple
164   fields has raised concerns about fairness and comprehensiveness in evaluation protocols. Different
165   from the above mentioned methods, BenchMol is the first molecular benchmark platform unified
166   multiple molecular modalities, which integrates 7 different modalities (fingerprint, sequence, graph,
167   geometry, image, geometry image, and video) and provides a easy-to-use interface for access.

      3     P RELIMINARIES
170
171   There is a molecule m with na atoms and nb bonds and the representation of different modalities is
172   as follows:
173
174
      Fingerprint. Molecular Fingerprints F are a compact, fixed-size representation, where F ∈ Rnf p
      and nf p represents the dimension of molecular fingerprint. Currently, there are many fingerprints
175
      developed (Mason et al., 2001), such as 167-dimensional MACCS (Molecular ACCess System)
176
      (Durant et al., 2002), ECFPx with custom dimension (Rogers & Hahn, 2010), 210-dimensional
177   RDKit2D (Landrum et al., 2016). You can read (Hou et al., 2024) for more details about fingerprints.
178
179   Sequence. A molecule is regarded as a string sequence, such as SMILES (Weininger et al., 1988),
180
      SELFIES (Krenn et al., 2020) and IUPAC (Kuhn et al., 2004). These sequences are split into tokens
      by a tokenizer with word segmentation rules, which is formalized as S = {s0 , s1 , ..., sna }, where
181
      sna represents a token. Currently, the most commonly used molecular sequence is SMILES and a
182
      large number of technologies (Kim et al., 2021; Ross et al., 2022) are developed based on it. In this
183   paper, we focus on the study of molecular SMILES because of its popularity.
184
185   Graph. A molecular graph G = (V, E) consists of a set of nodes V ∈ Rna ×nf and edges E ∈ Rnb ,
186
      where nf represents the feature number of atom (e.g., atom type) (Hu et al., 2020a; Xia et al., 2023).
      Assume that there are two atoms v and u in V, E represents an adjacency matrix A ∈ Rna ×na
187
      that indicates whether v and u are connected, where A[v, u] = 0 means there is no edge (bond)
188
      otherwise it means there is an edge (bond). In practical applications, since bonds have multiple
189   chemical properties (e.g., bond types), the adjacency matrix A can be easily extended to A∗ ∈
190   Rna ×na ×db , where db represents the number of chemical properties of the bond (Liu et al., 2022a;
191   Wang et al., 2022). For example, the type of the bond formed by nodes v and u can be formalized
192   as A∗ [v, u, it ] = {0, 1, 2, 3, 4}, where it represents the index describing the bond type, 0 represents
193   no bond, 1 represents a single bond, 2 represents an aromatic bond, and so on.
194
      Geometry. Geometry graph introduces the 3-dimensional coordinates of atoms based on graph
195
      (Zhou et al., 2023; Satorras et al., 2021), which is formalized as Ĝ = (V̂, Ê), where V̂ ∈ Rna ,nf +3 .
196
      Please note that in practical applications, models do not include edge information or use fully con-
197   nected adjacency matrices to represent edge information when processing geometric graphs.
198
199   Image, Geometry Image and Video. Molecular images (Xiang et al., 2023), geometry images
200
      Xiang et al. (2023) and videos (Xiang et al., 2024b) are based on visual representations of molecules,
      which are atom- and bond-independent and are made up of a bunch of pixels. We describe the
201
      importance of the visual modality in Appendix C.3. Using RDKit (Landrum, 2013), the SMILES of
202
      a molecule can be converted into a molecular image U I ∈ R224×224×3 (Figure 1(e)). The geometry
203   image (Figure 1(f)) and video (Figure 1(g)) of the molecule take into account the 3D structural
204   information of the molecule and are generated using PyMOL (DeLano et al., 2002). See Appendix
205   B for details of visual rendering. Formally, the geometry image and video can be represented as
206   U G ∈ R4×224×24××3 and U V ∈ R60×224×24××3 , respectively, where 4 and 60 represent the number
207   of views in the geometry image and the number of frames in the video.

      4.2   DATASET C OLLECTOR M ODULE
235
236   In BenchMol, we provide three benchmarks, MoleculeNet (Wu et al., 2018), MBANet and Struct-
237   Net. We contribute MBANet and StructNet to systematically analyze the preferences and perfor-
238   mance of different modal methods. See Appendix C.2 for details of the motivation and practicality
239   of the benchmarks. Next, we introduce the construction process of MBANet and StructNet.
240
      MBANet. MBANet aims to study the ability of different methods to capture molecular basic infor-
241   mation, including atom distributions, bond distributions, and basic attributes. As shown in Figure
242   2(a), we first sample 10,000 molecules from PCQM4Mv2 (Hu et al., 2017). Then, we count the
243   number of atoms and bonds in each molecule, which are formalized as Ka = {k1a , k2a , ..., k12 a
                                                                                                       }
             b        b  b  b   b                         a       12        b       4
244   and K = {k1 , k2 , k3 , k4 }, respectively, where K ∈ Z and K ∈ Z represent the count
245   of 12 types of atoms (C, N, O, F, S, Cl, Br, P, Si, B, Se, Ge) and 4 types of bonds (SINGLE,
246   AROMATIC, DOUBLE, TRIPLE). Subsequently, we further extracted 8 basic attributes, including
247   {molecular weight, MolLogP, MolMR, BalabanJ, NumHAcceptors, NumHDonors, NumValence-
248   Electrons, TPSA}, which are formalized as Kk ∈ R8 . Finally, we can generate three different types
249
      of datasets: MBANetatom = {m, Ka }, MBANetbond = {m, Kb }, and MBANetattr = {m, Kk },
      where m represents molecules. See Appendix D.1 for details and limitation analysis of the MBANet.
250
251   StructNet. StructNet is designed to evaluate the preference of models with varying modalities for
252   molecule types. As shown in Figure 2(b), we first collect over 14.4 million the latest molecules from
253   ChemBL 34. Then, we predefine 6 different conditions, including the number of rings, average
254   degree, presence of branches, maximum number of rings, number of amides, and molecular weight.
255
      Based on the molecular SMILES sequences, we leverage RDKit to generate these conditions. Then,
      we formulate 6 rules to classify molecules into different types: acyclic, complete chain, acyclic
256
      chain, macrocyclic peptide, reticular, and macromolecule. As shown in Figure 2(c), the application
257
      of these rules enables us to identify molecules exhibiting diverse characteristics. For example, a
258   molecule generated by the acyclic chain rule is a chain-like molecule without any rings. Ultimately,
259   we group the molecules based on the Assay ChEMBL ID defined in ChemBL 34 and select the top
260   10 assays with the largest number of samples to construct StructNet. It is worth noting that these
261   result in the creation of 60 datasets within StructNet, with 10 for each molecule type. See Appendix
262   D.2 for details of the StructNet.

      In training strategy module, we provide two training strategies, linear probing and fine-tuning. In
316   linear probing, to improve efficiency, we define a modality-specific feature extractor and pre-extract

485      and the smaller the value, the better), indicating the advantage of visual modality in learning

      6     C ONCLUSION
525
526
      We first proposed a unified and flexible platform supporting different molecular modalities, called
527
      BenchMol, to promote reproducibility in the molecular representation learning (MRL) community,
528   which provides the entire pipeline from raw data to final model evaluation and ensures fair and
529   comprehensive benchmarking. Subsequently, we proposed two new benchmarks, MBANet and
530   StructNet, to explore the performance and preferences of existing models on different modalities.
531   Finally, we used BenchMol to train at least 57,060 models and provided many meaningful insights.
532   BenchMol reviews and integrates mainstream MRL models with 7 different modalities, allowing
533   researchers to easily understand these models and quickly iterate on them.
534   Limitations and Future Works: Currently, BenchMol does not support multi-modal fusion. In
535   the future, we plan to continue maintaining BenchMol and upgrading it to a multi-modal fusion
536   platform. This enhanced platform will be capable of further improving molecular representation and
537   tackling interaction-based tasks such as drug-drug interaction (DDI), drug-target interaction (DTI),
538   and protein-protein interaction (PPI). Furthermore, while BenchMol currently focuses on evaluating
539   its performance in MRL, it is important to note that its applicability is not restricted to the molecular
      domain. We hope to leave the exploration of more fields to the community for verification.

1024        A.4    T RAINING S TRATEGY
1025
            BenchMol provides two training strategies, linear probing and fine-tuning.

1026
            Linear Probing. To improve the efficiency of linear probing, BenchMol designs 6 extractors to
1027
            extract feature representations from molecules of different modalities. A core idea of these extractors
1028        is that given a pre-trained model and a molecule, the feature extractor will return features of a specific
1029        modality. As shown in 4, BenchMol defines an interface FeatureExtractor, which is an abstract class
1030        with two abstract methods: extract features() and return features(). Subsequently, we defined an
1031        implementation class for each modality to extract features from the corresponding modality. The
1032        specific details class is as follows:
1033
1034
            • FingerprintExtractor. The features of fingerprint can be extracted by giving the fingerprint name
              and SMILES sequence.
1035
1036        • SmilesFeatureExtractor. The sequence features are generated from molecular SMILES se-
1037          quences.
1038        • GraphFeatureExtractor. The graph features can be extracted from graph modality given a pre-
1039          trained graph model.
1040        • ImageFeatureExtractor. The image feature can be extracted from image modality given a pre-
1041          trained image model.
1042        • MCImageFeatureExtractor. The features of geometry image and video can be extracted given a
1043          corresponding pre-trained model.
1044        • MVImageFeatureExtractor. The extractor is used to extract features from molecular geometry
1045          images or videos with multiple conformers.

1122

1123          However, molecules have three-dimensional conformational information. Following IEM (Xiang
1124          et al., 2024a), we use PyMOL to render the 3D structure of the molecule. Since the 3D structure
1125          is easily obscured when displayed on a single image, 4 viewing angles are used to render the
1126          molecule from different angles. Therefore, through multi-view rendering with PyMOL, we can
1127          obtain 4 geometric images with different vies for a molecule, which can be formulated as a matrix
1128          of 4 × 224 × 224 × 3.
1129        • Rendering of video: VideoMol (Xiang et al., 2024b) represents the 3D image of a molecule as
1130          a video. Specifically, a molecular video is constructed by rotating a molecule along the x-axis,
1131          y-axis, and z-axis. During the rotation process, VideoMol captures 60 frames at equal intervals
1132          to represent the molecular video. Therefore, we use PyMOL to render the molecule with 3D
1133          information and generate a 60-frame video, which can be formulated as a matrix of 60 × 224 ×
              224 × 3.

       C.1   M OTIVATION AND P OTENTIAL I MPACT OF B ENCH M OL
1137
1138   Motivation. The main motivations for BenchMol are to address the following research gaps in the
1139   field of chemical machine learning: (1) There is a lack of fair and comprehensive evaluation of
1140   methods across different modalities; (2) The strengths and differences of different modalities are
1141   still unknown, which limits the development of multi-modal fusion; and (3) There is a lack of a
1142   unified and easy-to-use platform to integrate methods across different modalities. With BenchMol,
1143   researchers can easily use and compare various molecular representation learning methods.
1144   Potential Impact. The two benchmarks MBANet and StructNet proposed in this paper have impor-
1145   tant impacts on promoting certain chemical problems, which are summarized as follows:
1146
1147   • MBANet aims to evaluate the ability of different methods to capture basic molecular information
1148     that is critical for many chemical problems. For example, the distribution of atoms and bonds is
1149     crucial for understanding the three-dimensional structure of a molecule [1] and aids in molecular
1150     dynamics simulations [2]. Predicting the basic attributes of a molecule helps design molecules
1151     with specific properties [3]. Attributes such as molecular weight, MolMR (molecular refractive
1152     index), and NumHDonors (number of hydrogen bond donors) can be used to infer the biological
1153
         activity of a molecule and its interaction with its target [4].
1154   • StructNet aims to explore the preferences of various methods for molecules of different structural
1155     types and it is of great significance for certain specific targets. For example, molecules targeting
1156     KRAS targets are often macromolecules, and the model needs to learn and predict in the sample
1157     space of macromolecules [5]; a class of antiviral and antimalarial drugs usually have chain-like
1158     molecular structures, while molecules targeting fibroblast activation protein (FAP) usually exhibit
1159
         non-cyclic structures. These chemical preferences indicate that it is important to select appropriate
         molecular modalities to work more effectively in different scenarios.

1163
1164   MBANet. The motivation and practicality of MBANet are as follows:
1165
1166
       • Motivation. Currently, a large number of benchmarks focus on mapping molecules to complex
         properties or biological activities. However, it is a complex process for models to learn to map
1167
         molecules to complex properties or biological activities, which may be related to the regulatory
1168
         network of molecules from a microscopic perspective. This complex process is not conducive to
1169     describing the model’s understanding of the basic properties of molecules. In this paper, we hope
1170     to clarify the understanding of the most basic attributes of molecules by different modalities. This
1171     basic attribute reflects the properties directly related to the molecule and has nothing to do with
1172     the complex regulatory network. This has always been a research gap but is also crucial for the
1173     model to understand molecules. Therefore, we design MBANet to evaluate the model’s ability to
1174     understand the basic attributes of molecules.
1175   • Practicality. From a practical point of view, the basic attributes evaluated by MBANet are closely
1176     related to complex properties or biological activities. For example, LogP is an important indicator
1177     of BBBP and it and TPSA are two key physicochemical parameters in drug design (Prasanna &
1178     Doerksen, 2009), affecting drug absorption, distribution, metabolism, excretion and toxicity.
1179
1180   We hope that this simple, decoupled basic task can provide reference and more thinking for modality
1181   selection on related active tasks. In addition, we will consider providing more complex prediction
1182   tasks for MBANet in future work to make it more comprehensive. However, in this paper, we prefer
1183   to study this unique aspect.
1184   StructNet. We describe the motivation and practicality of classifying molecules into different types
1185   based on their 2D structural patterns (e.g., acyclic, acyclic chain, cyclic chain, macrocyclic peptide,
1186   macromolecule, and reticular molecule). Molecular structure is intimately linked to molecular prop-
1187   erties, so that certain drug targets exhibit preferences for specific molecular structures, and some
       therapeutic applications correspond to molecules with particular structures. For instance:

1188
       • Macrocyclic peptides are increasingly being recognized for their therapeutic potential in target-
1189     ing aberrant protein-protein interactions (PPIs), with several macrocyclic peptide-based oncology
1190     drugs already approved by the U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) for clinical application
1191     (Yang et al., 2022).
1192   • Due to the mechanism of action of the KRAS target involving covalent binding with a cysteine

       C.3   T HE I MPORTANCE OF M OLECULAR V ISUAL M ODALITIES
1209
1210   Currently, molecular visual modalities consist of image, geometry image, video. Compared with
1211   previous molecular representations (such as SMILES or graph), the importance of molecular visual
1212   modalities is reflected in the following four aspects:
1213
1214
       • Direct representation of structural and geometric information: Compared with previous
         SMILES or graph representations, molecular visual modalities naturally retain structural and ge-
1215
         ometric properties such as atom type, chemical bond type, bond angle, spatial conformation, etc.
1216
         through pixel information (color, texture, etc.). Molecular videos can further describe the dynamic
1217     information of molecules, which is particularly important for tasks involving molecular geometry
1218     or dynamic behavior;
1219
       • Stronger interpretability: Molecular visual modalities show the molecular structure and its dy-
1220     namic behavior in an intuitive form, which is convenient for humans to understand and analyze the
1221     features learned by the model. For example, through technologies such as GradCAM (Selvaraju
1222     et al., 2017), researchers can intuitively understand how the model makes decisions;
1223   • Enriching molecular representation technology: Molecular visual modalities can use another
1224     technology stack (computer vision) to extract potential features from molecular images, geometry
1225     images or videos, enriching existing molecular representation technology;
1226   • Independence of the number of atoms and bonds: Molecular visual modalities are pixel-based
1227     representations, which are independent of the number of atoms and bonds. At present, drug dis-
1228     covery is increasingly biased towards large molecules. Significantly different from SMILES and
1229     graph, visual modalities have the unique advantage that the computational cost does not increase
1230     with the number of atoms and bonds, which will be of great benefit in drug development of large
1231     molecules.
1232
       Molecular visual modalities are particularly suitable for the following tasks, which rely on the spatial
1233
       configuration or dynamic characteristics of molecules:
1234
1235   • General molecular representation: Image-, geometry-image- and video-based methods have
1236     achieved excellent performance in various drug discovery tasks, just like other molecular repre-
1237     sentation methods (as shown in Table S20 and Table S21, etc.). Therefore, they can be used for
1238     general representation of drug discovery tasks;
1239   • Multi-modal fusion: Obviously, visual modalties are different from previous molecular represen-
1240     tation learning methods. Through multi-modal fusion or cross-modal contrastive learning tasks,
1241     more diversity will be provided to further improve the performance of drug discovery. As shown
         in Appendix K.1, Table S47 and Table S48, video has the largest differences with other modalities;


                                                         23
       Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


1242
       • Tasks related to atomic molecular distribution and basic properties: As shown in Table 5,
1243     video modality can achieve the best performance on atom-level and attr-level tasks of MBANet.
1244     Therefore, the video modality makes up for the lack of understanding of atomic distribution and
1245     basic properties of other modal representations;
1246
       • Virtual screening of macrocyclic or reticular structures: As shown in Table 6, images and
1247
         videos can achieve the best performance on MP and R tasks on StructNet. Therefore, images
1248     or videos make up for the lack of understanding of macrocyclic peptide (MP) and reticular (R)
1249     structures by other modalities, such as virtual screening of PPI targets (Cox & Der, 2024).
1250
1251   In general, molecules exist in the physical world. Currently, due to the limitations of molecular
1252   imaging technology, molecular images/geometry images/videos are obtained by economical image
1253   rendering technology. However, with the continuous advancement of molecular imaging technology,
1254   it is promising to directly represent molecules and inference about them in a visual way.

       D.1   MBAN ET
1261
1262   MBANet is used to evaluate the performance of deep learning models in understanding the basic
1263   information of molecules, aiming to measure whether the model can effectively capture the low-
1264   level core features related to molecules. In the fields of cheminformatics and drug development, the
1265   basic information of molecules (such as atomic information, bond information, molecular weight,
1266   TPSA and other basic attributes) is crucial for predicting molecular activity, drug-target interactions,
1267   and chemical reactivity. Therefore, MBANet attempts to examine the model’s capabilities at these
1268
       basic levels and explore whether the model can accurately capture these basic features by learning
       molecules.
1269
1270   MBANet has a total of 10,000 molecules and includes three groups of prediction tasks, namely pre-
1271   diction of atoms MBANetatom , bonds MBANetbond and basic attributes MBANetattr . We check
1272   MBANet for duplication according to canonical SMILES and find that only 25 molecules are dupli-
1273   cated in canonical SMILES. Therefore, the impact on the evaluation is negligible. In the MBANet
1274
       benchmark, the model needs to predict the atomic distribution, bond distribution, and basic attributes
       given a molecule. The Figure S1, Figure S2, and Figure S3 show the distribution information of
1275
       MBANetatom , MBANetbond and MBANetattr respectively. In particular, we describe the meaning
1276
       of each attribute in MBANetattr in detail as follows:
1277
1278   • Molecular Weight: The sum of the relative atomic masses of all atoms in a molecule, usually
1279     expressed in daltons (Da) or grams per mole (g/mol). It is a fundamental property that affects a
1280     compound’s physical and chemical behavior.
1281
       • MolLogP (LogP): The logarithmic value of the distribution coefficient ratio (P) of a compound
1282     in n-octanol (oil) and water. It indicates the hydrophobicity or lipophilicity of the molecule, with
1283     higher values suggesting greater affinity for lipid environments.
1284
       • MolMR (Molecular Refractivity): A calculated property that reflects the volume and polariz-
1285
         ability of a molecule. It is used to estimate the interactions of the molecule with its environment,
1286
         including its ability to penetrate biological membranes.
1287
1288
       • BalabanJ (Balaban’s J Index): A topological index that measures the complexity of a molecular
         structure. It is used in quantitative structure-activity relationship (QSAR) studies to correlate
1289
         molecular structure with biological activity.
1290
1291   • NumValenceElectrons (Number of Valence Electrons): The total number of valence electrons
1292     present in the atoms of a molecule. This property is important for understanding the chemical
1293
         reactivity and bonding behavior of the compound.
1294   • TPSA (Topological Polar Surface Area): A measure of the polar surface area of a molecule,
1295     calculated based on its structure. TPSA is often used to predict a compound’s absorption, perme-
         ability, and bioavailability.


                                                         24
       Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


1296
       • NumHAcceptors (Number of Hydrogen Bond Acceptors): The count of atoms in a molecule
1297     that can accept hydrogen bonds, typically involving oxygen and nitrogen atoms. This property is
1298     crucial for evaluating molecular interactions in biological systems.
1299
       • NumHDonors (Number of Hydrogen Bond Donors): The count of atoms that can donate hy-
1300
         drogen bonds, usually hydrogen atoms attached to electronegative atoms like nitrogen or oxygen.
1301     This property influences a molecule’s interaction with biological targets.

1399   StructNet has a total of 6 different molecular types, including reticular (R)-, acyclic (A)-, complete
1400   chain (CC)-, acyclic chain (AC)-, macrocyclic peptide (MP)-, macro (M)-molecules. The scenarios
1401   corresponding to these six different types of molecules are of great significance in drug discovery.
1402   For example, molecules targeting KRAS targets tend to be large molecules and the model needs to
1403   learn and predict in the sample space of large molecules (Cox & Der, 2024). Here, we first give the
       specific 6 screening rules for each type of molecule, as follows:


                                                          26
       Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


1404
       • R rule for reticular molecules. Reticular molecules exhibit better structural stability, drug load-
1405     ing capacity, and controllable release due to their complex cross-linked pore structure and larger
1406     specific surface area, making them suitable for multiple fields such as drug carriers, tissue engi-
1407     neering, and drug controlled release systems. Because reticular molecules are highly cross-linked
1408     structures composed of multiple interwoven molecular chains, similar to a network mesh. The
1409     more cross-linking (chemical bonds) between atoms, the higher the average degree. So we define
1410     molecules with average degree greater than 2.33 as reticular molecules.
1411
1412   • A rule for acyclic molecules. Acyclic molecules usually have higher reactivity and spatial adapt-
1413     ability, and can better bind to active sites that cyclic structures cannot reach smoothly due to steric
1414     hindrance in cyclic structures. Thus, We retain molecules with ring number equal to 0.
1415
1416   • CC rule for complete chain molecules. Compared to other structural molecules, chain molecules
1417     serve as drug molecules or carriers, based on their better flexibility and adaptability, high speci-
1418     ficity and affinity, targeting specific biological targets to achieve precise treatment or drug delivery.
1419
         Chain molecule is a long chain structure composed of a main molecular chain with few branches,
         where most of the atoms have a degree of 2. Therefore, We retain molecules where the ratio of
1420
         branched atoms (atoms with degree greater than 2) to the total number of atoms is less than 0.2.
1421
1422
       • AC rule for acyclic chain molecules. Strictly speaking, chain molecules do not have cyclic
1423
         structures. Therefore, based on custom chain molecules, we retain molecules where the ratio of
1424     branched atoms (atoms with degree greater than 2) to the total number of atoms is less than 0.255
1425     and the number of rings is equal to 0 as acyclic chain molecules.
1426
1427   • MP rule for macrocyclic peptide molecules. As one of the hot topics in the field of drug de-
1428     velopment today, macrocyclic peptides have high targeting, excellent pharmacokinetic properties,
1429     and low immunogenicity. Some macrocyclic peptides even have strong penetration ability. These
1430     advantages make macrocyclic peptides have broad application prospects in drug development and
1431     treatment. Therefore, we chose macrocyclic peptides as a class of structural molecules to study.
1432     We retain molecules with a maximum number of rings greater than 12 and a number of peptide
1433     bonds greater than 0 according to the definition of macrocyclic peptide, which refer to compounds
1434     connected by peptide bond (amide bond) and possessing a large cyclic structure.
1435
1436   • M rule for macro molecules. Large molecule targeted drugs, such as monoclonal antibodies,
1437     generally act on targets on the cell surface and have strong specificity. Moreover, compared to
1438
         traditional small molecule targeted drugs, large molecule drugs have a longer half-life, which
         greatly reduces the frequency of medication. Therefore, We keep molecules with molecular weight
1439
         greater than 900 as macro molecules.
1440

1445
       one and use the mean of multiple trials as the label. We show the statistics of the datasets with
