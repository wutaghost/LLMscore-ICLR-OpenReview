009                                                A BSTRACT
010
011            Denoising diffusion probabilistic models have achieved significant success in point
012            cloud generation, enabling numerous downstream applications, such as generative
013            data augmentation and 3D model editing. However, little attention has been given
014            to generating point clouds with point-wise segmentation labels, as well as to devel-
015            oping evaluation metrics for this task. Therefore, in this paper, we present SeaLion,
016            a novel diffusion model designed to generate high-quality and diverse point cloud
017            with fine-grained segmentation labels. Specifically, we introduce the semantic part-
018            aware latent point diffusion technique, which leverages the intermediate features
019
               of the generative models to jointly predict the noise for perturbed latent points and
               associated part segmentation labels during the denoising process, and subsequently
020
               decodes the latent points to point clouds conditioned on part segmentation labels.
021
               To effectively evaluate the quality of generated point clouds, we introduce a novel
022            point cloud pairwise distance calculation method named part-aware Chamfer dis-
023            tance (p-CD). This method enables existing metrics, such as 1-NNA, to measure
024            both the local structural quality and inter-part coherence of generated point clouds.
025            Experiments on the large-scale synthetic dataset ShapeNet and real-world medi-
026            cal dataset IntrA, demonstrate that SeaLion achieves remarkable performance in
027            generation quality and diversity, outperforming the existing state-of-the-art model,
028            DiffFacto, by 13.33% and 6.52% on 1-NNA (p-CD) across the two datasets. Ex-
029            perimental analysis shows that SeaLion can be trained semi-supervised, thereby
030
               reducing the demand for labeling efforts. Lastly, we validate the applicability of
               SeaLion in generative data augmentation for training segmentation models and the
031
               capability of SeaLion to serve as a tool for part-aware 3D shape editing.

      1    I NTRODUCTION
036
037   In the past few years, 3D point cloud generation based on deep neural networks has attracted
038   significant interest and achieved remarkable success in downstream tasks, such as 2D image to point
039
      cloud generation (Fan et al., 2017; Jiang et al., 2018) and point cloud completion (Yu et al., 2021;
      Huang et al., 2020). However, little effort has been devoted to the generative models capable of
040
      generating 3D point clouds with semantic segmentation labels. Exiting works (Gal et al., 2021; Li
041
      et al., 2022; Shu et al., 2019; Zhang et al., 2024) can generate point clouds composed of detachable
042   sub-parts. Nevertheless, these sub-parts lack clear semantic meaning, hindering the application of
043   generated point clouds in domains such as generative data augmentation for training segmentation
044   models and semantic part-aware 3D shape editing.
045
      Attributed to the effective approximation to the real data distribution, denoising diffusion probabilistic
046
      models (DDPMs) (Ho et al., 2020) outperform many other generative models such as variational au-
047
      toencoders (VAEs) (Kingma & Welling, 2013) and generative adversarial networks (GANs) (Creswell
048   et al., 2018) in generation quality and diversity. Current state-of-the-art diffusion-based point cloud
049   generative models (Zeng et al., 2022; Luo & Hu, 2021; Zhou et al., 2021) have achieved impressive
050   performance. However, they still lack the ability to generate semantic labels. To the best of our
051   knowledge, DiffFacto (Nakayama et al., 2023) is the only recent work capable of generating point
052   clouds with segmentation labels by utilizing multiple DDPMs to generate each part individually and
053   predicting the pose of each part to assemble the entire point clouds. However, due to the part-wise
      generation factorization, DiffFacto exhibits limited part-to-part coherence within the generated shape.

073   Inspired by (Baranchuk et al., 2021), which demonstrates that the intermediate hidden features learned
074   by DDPMs can serve as representations capturing high-level semantic information for downstream
075   vision tasks, we propose a novel approach that extends the generative model to not only predict noise
076   for perturbed data but also point-wise part segmentation labels during the generation process. Our
077   diffusion model learns the data distribution of regularized latent feature spaces, rather than directly
078   approximating the distribution of point clouds in Euclidean space, since this latent diffusion strategy is
079   proved to be more effective for complex point cloud generation (Zeng et al., 2022). Thereby, we train
080   a VAE, conditioned by the segmentation labels, to map point clouds to latent points with point-wise
081
      segmentation awareness. During inference, the diffusion model simultaneously generates both latent
      points and their associated segmentation labels. The latter serves as conditional information for the
082
      VAE decoder, leading to generated point clouds with consistent segmentation labels. We refer this
083
      approach as semantic part-aware latent point diffusion technique. Based on it, we propose a generative
084   model named SeaLion. Notably, the point-wise diffusion module in SeaLion utilizes a down-sampling
085   data path to extract the common representations for both noise prediction and segmentation tasks,
086   alongside two parallel up-sampling data paths to respectively extract task-specific features. SeaLion
087   can generate high-quality point clouds with accurate segmentation labels. Besides, SeaLion diffuses
088   on the latent points of all parts simultaneously, ensuring higher part-to-part coherence within a shape.
089
      Currently, widely-used metrics for evaluating the quality of generated point clouds, such as 1-nearest
090   neighbor accuracy (1-NNA) (Yang et al., 2019) and coverage (COV) (Achlioptas et al., 2018), fail
091   to reflect the quality of segmentation-labeled point clouds. These metrics utilize Chamfer distance
092   (CD) or earth mover’s distance (EMD) (Rubner et al., 2000) to compute the pairwise point cloud
093   distance, but neither of which considers the segmentation of point clouds. DiffFacto (Nakayama et al.,
094   2023) uses the aforementioned metrics, but it assesses each part individually and then averages the
095   results across all parts. However, this method still fails to measure the part-to-part coherence within a
096   shape. We propose a novel evaluation metric named part-aware Chamfer distance (p-CD) to address
097   these limitations and to quantify the pairwise distance between two segmentation-labeled point
098
      clouds. Using p-CD, evaluation metrics such as 1-NNA can effectively measure shape plausibility
      and part-to-part coherence of the generated point clouds.
099
100   We conduct extensive experiments on a large-scale synthetic dataset, ShapeNet (Yi et al., 2016), and a
101   real-world 3D intracranial aneurysm dataset, IntrA (Yang et al., 2020). The results show that SeaLion
102   achieves state-of-the-art performance in generating segmentation-labeled point clouds. Considering
103   that labeling 3D point clouds is tedious, we evaluate SeaLion in a semi-supervised training setting,
104
      where only a small portion of the training data is labeled. Experimental results on ShapeNet validate
      that SeaLion can leverage additional unlabeled data, highlighting its potential to reduce labeling
105
      efforts. Ablation studies validate the feasibility of generative data augmentation using point clouds
106
      generated by SeaLion and the capability of SeaLion as a tool for part-aware 3D shape editing.
107
      In summary, the contributions of this work are the following:

120   2     R ELATED W ORKS
121
122   Detachable point cloud generation. TreeGAN (Shu et al., 2019) conceptualizes point cloud genera-
123
      tion as a tree growth process, where the final generated point cloud integrates various parts at the leaf
      nodes. SP-GAN (Li et al., 2021) maps a sphere in 3D space into a point cloud like FoldingNet (Yang
124
      et al., 2018), where different sphere regions correspond to other parts of the generated point cloud.
125
      MRGAN (Gal et al., 2021) achieves explicit part disentanglement by employing multiple branches
126   of tree-structured graph convolution layers. EditVAE (Li et al., 2022) learns a disentangled latent
127   representation for each part from point clouds in an unsupervised manner. Yet, the parts in these
128   methods mentioned above do not necessarily possess clear semantic meaning. These methods are
129   designed to facilitate the replacement of specific sub-parts for subsequent point cloud editing.
130
      Diffusion-based point cloud generation. Point-Voxel Diffusion (Zhou et al., 2021) and DPM (Luo
131
      & Hu, 2021) train a diffusion model to generate point clouds directly. Instead, Lion (Zeng et al.,
132   2022) utilizes a hierarchical VAE to map the point clouds to the global and point-level latent features
133   and then trains latent diffusion models on them. Experimental results show that the latent diffusion
134   model with a hierarchical encoding method can achieve better generation quality.
135
      Representations from generative models for discriminative tasks. Some recent works explore
136
      using generative models as representation learners for discriminative tasks. (Donahue et al., 2016;
137
      Donahue & Simonyan, 2019; Chen et al., 2020) use the representations learned by GAN encoders
138   and masked pixel predictors for 2D image classification. Without any additional training, (Li et al.,
139   2023) chooses the category conditioning that best predicts the noise added to the input image as the
140   classification prediction. (Zhang et al., 2021; Tritrong et al., 2021; Xu & Zheng, 2021; Xu et al.,
141   2021; Baranchuk et al., 2021) investigate the usage of generative models on the segmentation tasks.
142   (Baranchuk et al., 2021) shows that intermediate activations capture the semantic information from
143   the input images and appear to be useful representations for the segmentation problem.

145   3     M ETHODOLOGY
146
147   In this section, we first give preliminaries on the diffusion models (Ho et al., 2020) and propose
148   the semantic part-aware latent points technique. Next, we introduce the architecture of a novel
149   generative model, SeaLion, and illustrate its usage as a part-aware 3D edition tool. Finally, we discuss
150   the limitation of current metrics for evaluating generated labeled point clouds and propose novel
151   metrics based on part-aware Chamfer distance (p-CD) that effectively measure shape plausibility
152   and part-to-part coherence of the generated point clouds.

179

180

181   Lion maps it to a global latent z0 ∈ Rdz and latent points h0 ∈ Rn×dh , and diffuses on these two
182   latent features respectively. The dz -dimensional vector z0 encodes the global shape of the point cloud
183   and serves as conditional information for point-level modules, while latent points h0 encode the
184   point-wise features and preserve the point cloud structure. However, the lack of semantic awareness
185   of latent points hinders the generation of segmentation-labeled point clouds. Although the pseudo
186   labels can be obtained by running a pre-trained segmentation model on the generated point clouds,
187   this step-by-step method is vulnerable since it relies on an accurate segmentation model.

267   3.2   M ODEL A RCHITECTURE OF S EA L ION
268
269   Based on the semantic part-aware latent point diffusion technique, we introduce a novel point cloud
      generative model named SeaLion. The architecture of SeaLion is illustrated as follows:

270
      Point-level encoder ϕh and decoder ξh . In SeaLion, ϕh and ξh adopt a similar 4-layer Point-Voxel
271   CNN (PVCNN) (Liu et al., 2019) as their backbones. PVCNN, a U-Net style architecture for point
272   cloud data, uses set abstraction layer (Qi et al., 2017) and feature propagation layer (Qi et al., 2017)
273   for down-sampling and up-sampling the points. Point-voxel convolutions (PVConv) blocks (Liu et al.,
274   2019), which merge the advantages of point-based and voxel-based methods, are utilized to extract
275   neighboring features at each layer. To incorporate the conditional information, the global latent z0
276   is integrated through the adaptive Group Normalization (Zeng et al., 2022) in PVConv, while the
277   segmentation encoding y is concatenated with the intermediate features at each layer.
278   Point-level diffusion ϵh . As discussed in 3.1, point-level diffusion ϵh contains a down-sampling path
279   to learn the shared representations for both prediction task and two parallel up-sampling paths to
280   extract the task-specific features. Accordingly, we adopt a modified PVCNN architecture with one
281   down-sampling path and two up-sampling branches.
282
      Global encoder ϕz and diffusion ϵz . We adopt the same architectures as Lion for the two global-level
283   modules. The global encoder ϕz consists of PVConv blocks, set abstraction layers, a max pooling
284   layer, and a multi-layer perceptron. The global diffusion ϵz comprises stacked ResNet (He et al.,
285   2016). More details regarding the model architecture can be found in the supplementary materials.

      3.3   PART- AWARE 3D SHAPE EDITION TOOL
288
289
      Since the latent points are semantic part-aware, SeaLion can be used as a part-aware 3D shape edition
290
      tool. Given a point cloud x consisting of |P | parts, where we aim to preserve part p ∈ P while
291   introducing variations to the remaining parts. After transforming the point cloud to the latent points
292   h, we can freeze the latent points belonging to part p and apply the diffusion-denoise process (Zeng
293   et al., 2022; Meng et al., 2022) on the unfrozen latent points. During this process, the unfrozen latent
294   points are perturbed for τ steps (τ < T ) and then denoised recursively for the same number of steps.
295   Due to the stochasticity of the denoising process, the unfrozen latent points will differ after denosing,
296   leading to deformations in the corresponding parts when decoded by ξh . The pseudo code of using
297   SeaLion as an editing tool is provided in the supplementary materials.

299   3.4   E VALUATION M ETRICS
300
301   Notions. Given a generated dataset G = {xg |xg ∈ Rn×3 } and a real dataset R = {xr |xr ∈ Rn×3 },
302   both consist of point clouds with n points. Suppose each point cloud x ∈ Rn×3 consists of |P | parts,
303   i.e. x = {xp |p ∈ P, xp ∈ Rnp ×3 }, where np is the number of points in part p. For example, if x
304   represents a car from ShapeNet (Yi et al., 2016), P = {roof, hood, wheels, body}.
305   Existing metrics. The essential of evaluating point cloud generation is to assess both the quality and
306   diversity of the generated data. Most existing works (Zeng et al., 2022; Zhou et al., 2021; Yang et al.,
307   2019) use metrics such as 1-nearest neighbor accuracy (1-NNA) (Yang et al., 2019), coverage (COV),
308   and minimum matching distance (MMD) (Achlioptas et al., 2018) for evaluation. The formulas for
309   these metrics are provided in the supplementary materials. As discussed in (Yang et al., 2019; Zeng
310   et al., 2022), COV quantifies generation diversity and is sensitive to mode collapse, but it fails to
311   evaluate the quality of G. MMD, on the other hand, only assess the best quality point clouds in G and
312
      is not a reliable metric to measure overall generation quality and diversity. 1-NNA (Yang et al., 2019)
      measures both generation quality and diversity by quantifying the distribution similarity between
313
      R and G. The aforementioned metrics rely on Chamfer distance (CD) or earth mover’s distance
314
      (EMD) (Rubner et al., 2000) to measure the distance between two point clouds. However, neither
315   CD nor EMD considers the semantic segmentation of the points, making these metrics ineffective to
316   evaluate the generated point clouds with point-wise segmentation labels.
317
318

350   anomaly of the generated sample. Based on p-CD, we can compute the 1-NNA (p-CD), COV (p-CD),
351   and MMD (p-CD) to measure the part-aware proximity of a generated set to a real set.

458   4.3   E XPERIMENTAL A NALYSIS
459
460   Compared with collecting 3D data, which can be automated using tools like web crawlers, manually
461   labeling segmentation is tedious and time-consuming. Therefore, method for extracting the infor-
462   mation from the unlabeled data attracts lots of attention in recent years. Typically, semi-supervised
463   learning effectively reduces the need for extensive data labeling by training models with a combina-
464   tion of a small amount of labeled samples and a larger set of unlabeled samples. The training process
465
      of DiffFacto (Nakayama et al., 2023) involves separate training for each semantic part, which limits
      its ability to leverage the unsegmented data. In contrast, SeaLion generates the points for all parts
466
      jointly, making it adaptable to the semi-supervised training approach. Given an unlabeled sample,
467
      we can replace the segmentation encoding y in equation 6 with zero padding of the same shape,
468   thereby transforming the corresponding modules to be unconditioned by y. Additionally, we omit the
469   second term H(y, ŷt ) in equation 8 to skip the training of segmentation prediction on unsegmented
470   samples. Consequently, SeaLion can be trained on unlabeled samples using this approach, while
471   labeled samples can still be processed using the objective functions in 3.1.
472

      experiment on the car class in ShapeNet (Yi et al., 2016). We randomly select 10% of the samples in

531   5     C ONCLUSION
532
533   In this paper, we introduce the semantic part-aware latent point diffusion technique for generating
534   point clouds with segmentation labels. Using this technique, our novel generative model, SeaLion,
535   achieves state-of-the-art performance on ShapeNet and IntrA datasets. Additionally, we discuss the
536   limitations of the existing metrics for evaluating the generated labeled point clouds and propose
537   better metrics based on a novel point cloud pairwise distance calculation method named part-aware
538   Chamfer distance. Further experiments validate the feasibility of generative data augmentation using
539   the point clouds generated by SeaLion and the utility of SeaLion as a tool for part-aware 3D shape
      editing, highlighting the broad applicability of SeaLion in various downstream tasks.
