010                                                A BSTRACT
011            Scientific documents record research findings and valuable human knowledge, com-
012            prising a vast corpus of high-quality data. Leveraging multi-modality data extracted
013            from these documents and assessing large models’ abilities to handle scientific
014            document-oriented tasks is therefore meaningful. Despite promising advancements,
015            large models still perform poorly on multi-page scientific document extraction and
016
               understanding tasks, and their capacity to process within-document data formats
               such as charts and equations remains under-explored. To address these issues, we
017
               present DocGenome, a structured document benchmark constructed by annotating
018
               500K scientific documents from 153 disciplines in the arXiv open-access commu-
019            nity, using our custom auto-labeling pipeline. DocGenome features four key charac-
020            teristics: 1) Completeness: It is the first dataset to structure data from all modalities
021            including 13 layout attributes along with their LATEX source codes. 2) Logicality: It
022            provides 6 logical relationships between different entities within each scientific doc-
023            ument. 3) Diversity: It covers various document-oriented tasks, including document
024            classification, visual grounding, document layout detection, document transforma-
025            tion, open-ended single-page QA and multi-page QA. 4) Correctness: It undergoes
026            rigorous quality control checks conducted by a specialized team. We conduct
027
               extensive experiments to demonstrate the advantages of DocGenome and objec-
               tively evaluate the performance of large models on our benchmark. DocGenome is
028
               available at https://anonymous.4open.science/r/DocGenome.

      1    I NTRODUCTION
033
      Extracting data from scientific documents and developing large models to understand them is crucial
034
      for advancing AI-assisted scientific exploration and discovery (Jumper et al., 2021; Evans et al., 2021;
035
      Baek et al., 2021). On one hand, scientific documents provide comprehensive, high-quality, logically
036   rich corpora for training large models (Lv et al., 2023; Chen et al., 2023; 2024; OpenAI, 2023). On
037   the other hand, the ability of large models (Lv et al., 2023; Chen et al., 2023; 2024; OpenAI, 2023) to
038   accurately understand scientific documents is considered as a crucial evaluation criterion.
039
      However, we observed that current Multi-modal Large Language Models (MLLMs) (Li et al., 2020;
040
      Zhong et al., 2019; Pfitzmann et al., 2022; Da et al., 2023; Wang et al., 2023b; Chen et al., 2023;
041
      2024; Bai et al., 2023; Alayrac et al., 2022; Li et al., 2023; Tian et al., 2024; Wang et al., 2024c;d; Wu
042   et al., 2023; Zhang et al., 2023; Zhu et al., 2023) still struggle to understand the content of scientific
043   documents as deeply as humans do. This challenge is primarily due to the inherently complicated
044   multi-modal information present in scientific documents, such as multi-modal charts (Xia et al., 2024),
045   intricate equations (Wang et al., 2024a), and sophisticated logical relationships. Currently, MLLMs
046   cannot effectively parse and comprehend such complicated modalities and logical relationships. To
047   alleviate this challenge, we present DocGenome, an open large-scale scientific document benchmark
048   constructed using the designed DocParser.
049   DocParser is a training-free auto-labeling pipeline, which can generate both attribute information of
050   component units and logical relationships between units by auto-annotating and structuring a large
051   amount of unlabeled arXiv papers , with four stages: 1) data preprocessing, 2) unit segmentation,
052   3) attribute assignment and relation retrieval, and 4) color rendering as elaborated in Sec. 3.1.
053   Furthermore, we utilize the proposed DocParser to label 500K scientific documents collected from
      the arXiv open-access community, and the resulting auto-annotated dataset is termed as DocGenome

206

209   to ensure a tailored and precise categorization. In the analysis of component unit relationships,
210   units are categorized into two classes: 1) fixed-form units, including Text, Title, Abstract, etc.,
211   which are characterized by sequential reading and hierarchical relationships readily discernible
212   from the list obtained in Stage 2, and 2) floating-form units, including Table, Figure, etc., which

      Stage 4: Color Rendering. The bounding box of a component unit is an additional label we aim
217   to extract. After the segmentation phase in Stage 2, we render the target unit in black and all other
218   units in white, to create two distinct PDFs. By performing a subtraction operation between these
219   documents, we can obtain the detection box containing only the current unit, as illustrated in the
220   top-right corner of Fig. 2. For component units that traverse across hurdles or pages, we standardize
221   the bounding box labels based on their unified source code information. This method effectively
222   mitigates the issue where bounding boxes may be inadvertently divided, ensuring seamless and
223   unified labeling for such units.
224   We automate the annotation process by sequentially applying DocParser’s four stages and leveraging
225   the complete LATEX source code. This yields not only the document’s PDF but also the individual
226   source code, bounding box, specific attributes for each component unit, and the relationships between
227   units. Together, these elements constitute our DocGenome dataset.

291   4.1                      P RINCIPLES OF C ONSTRUCTING E VALUATION S ET
292
      We use two principles to split the auto-annotated data into a high-quality evaluation set (termed
293
      as DocGenome-test) with precise annotation and a large-scale multi-modal training set (termed
294
      as DocGenome-train). First, the evaluation set should share the same discipline distribution as
295   the collected data. Hence, the test data are uniformly sampled across each discipline. Second, the
296   annotation of test data should be as precise as possible. Therefore, the test data are only sampled from
297   the Tier-1 set. Based on these two principles, we finally sampled 1,004 papers (covering 9K pages)
298   as the test set from the overall 500K auto-annotated papers (containing 6.8M pages). As a result, the
299   DocGenome-test covers 1,004 scientific documents with 1K document classification examples, 2K
300   visual grounding examples, 3K QA pairs, 110K layout bounding boxes, 3K Table-LATEX pairs, and
301   5K Equation-LATEX pairs.
302
303   4.2                      QA PAIR G ENERATION AND Q UALITY A SSURANCE
304
      In the DocGenome-test, we further design multiple Question-Answering (QA) pairs for each paper
305
      to comprehensively evaluate the document understanding capabilities of different models. For
306   each paper sampler, two single-page QA pairs and two multi-page QA pairs are generated using
307   GPT-4V (OpenAI, 2023). Specifically, we instruct GPT-4V to randomly select two representative
308   pages, extract useful information from the two pages respectively, and then generate corresponding
309   single-page QA pairs. Additionally, we utilize GPT-4V to search for content-related paragraphs
310   from different pages to construct the cross-page QA pairs, testing the model’s ability to understand
311   and integrate information across multiple pages. The QA pairs involve various commonly raised
312   questions whose answers can be precisely inferred from the given paper.
313   After generating QA pairs for all paper samples in the DocGenome-test, we invited professional
314   faculty members from various fields to conduct the quality assurance checks. Each QA pair is
315   reviewed by three reviewers for cross-verification. The first step involves the initial review by Kimi†† ,
316   a well-known paper understanding model, to assess the initial correctness and identify the target
317   location of QA information on the assigned page. Next, based on the provided location of QA
318   information, two professional faculty members are assigned to manually and independently check
319   each QA pair for accuracy, relevance, and clarity. At this stage, the quality evaluation involves the
320
      correctness, relevance, and rationality of the designed questions and the accuracy of the provided
      answer. Finally, the two manually-evaluated results, along with the automatically-evaluated result are
321
      cross-verified with the original text to ensure accuracy and consistency. Please refer to Appendix F
322
      for more details.

338   4.3   E VALUATION TASKS
339
      To comprehensively evaluate the models’ understanding capability of scientific documents, we
340
      design 7 tasks w.r.t each paper document for the DocGenome-test, including document classification,
341   visual grounding, open-ended single-page, and multi-page QA tasks, document layout detection,
342   Equation-to-LATEX transformation, and Table-to-LATEX transformation.
343
344
      Specifically, document classification involves recognizing the field to which a paper belongs. Visual
      grounding involves identifying the content according to the provided visual components and textual
345
      prompts. Document layout detection refers to the localization and recognition of each layout block in
346
      given papers. Document transformation encompasses two format conversions, i.e., Table-to-LATEX
347   and Equation-to-LATEX transformation. All tasks take the paper images as visual input for inference.
348   The visual examples for each task are illustrated in Fig. A.8 in Appendix I.
349
350   4.4   E VALUATION M ETRICS
351   Document Classification: Top-1 Accuracy (%) is used as the metric for document classification
352   tasks, where higher values indicate better performance.
353
      Visual Grounding: Edit Distance is used to evaluate the accuracy of visual grounding, with lower
354   values indicating better performance.
355
      Document Layout Detection: mAP@0.5:0.95 is evaluated as the metric for document layout
356
      detection, where higher values indicate better performance.
357
      Document Transformation: We utilize Edit Distance, Jaccard Similarity, Cosine Similarity, and
358
      BLEU as metrics to comprehensively evaluate the document transformation task.
359
360   Open-ended QA: GPT-acc (%) is designed for tasks with open-ended answers, where outputs are
361
      evaluated against the ground truth using GPT-4. Please refer to Appendix G for more details.

      5.4     F URTHER D ISCUSSIONS
459
460   Generalization on Out-Of-Distribution (OOD) Data. In this part, we provide a method to extend
461   DocGenome to other disciplines or domains. For disciplines not covered by the arXiv open-access
462   community, such as civil engineering, chemistry, and materials science disciplines, we assume that
463   we can collect their PDF data. Then, we can use the layout detection annotations provided by the
464
      proposed DocGenome to train a document parsing model, as demonstrated by the YOLOv8-doc
      model in Table 6. Our goal is to validate the generalization ability of this model in other disciplines
465
      or domains.
466
467   Specifically, we select data from the Scihub domain to validate the generalization ability of our models.
468   The detailed discipline information of the constructed Scihub domain is shown in Table 7. Then,
469   we conducted model evaluations on the aforementioned Scihub data, which is annotated by human
470
      experts, for the layout detection task and the Equation-to-LATEX task, respectively. As shown in Table 6,
      for the layout detection task, the YOLOv8-doc model (Jocher et al., 2023) trained using DocGenome-
471
      train presents better generalization ability than DocXChain (Yao, 2023) on human-annotated data.
472
      Regarding the Equation-to-LATEX task, although the performance of EqVLM-B declines on OOD data
473   (Scihub data), it still maintains relatively strong results with an Edit Distance of 0.6627. Considering
474   that Mathpix is closed-source with potential exposure to various data distributions in its commercial
475   usage, it is natural that our trained model performs relatively worse than Mathpix in the OOD data.
476
      Layout Understanding could Boost QA Performance. For questions related to figures or tables in
477
      DocGenome-test, we directly annotated the detection boxes for figures or tables on the document
478
      images to serve as the image input for the QA task. In Table 8, taking InternVL-1.5 as an example,
479   when the images of the papers contain layout information (this information can be considered as a
480   prompt) relevant to the questions, the performance of the QA task can be further enhanced.
481

484   the relation information between entities, we can index associated modal information for specific
485   tasks, enabling data re-annotation and expansion. For instance, in constructing table-related QA tasks,
      we can not only obtain images of tables but also index the text describing the tables in the document,

509   Representative Ability of LaTeX in Scientific Literature Domain. The collection using LATEX is
510   representative of scientific documents, as LATEX is the preferred tool for academic writing in STEM
511   fields due to its precision and professional formatting. While LATEX does impose strict formatting rules,
512   these are not constraints but rather mechanisms to ensure accuracy and consistency. For instance,
513
      the "??" marker for missing references serves as a clear indicator of errors, prompting authors to
      address them before finalizing the document. This feature actually enhances the quality of scientific
514
      writing by reducing the likelihood of overlooked mistakes. In contrast, other formatting software
515
      may not flag such issues, potentially leading to incomplete or inconsistent references. As a result, the
516   strictness of LATEX contributes to its reputation as a standard for rigorous scientific documentation.
517
518   On the other hand, the arXiv community hosts papers under the CC license, and all papers are
519
      represented in LATEX format. As a result, the structured scientific literature we obtain based on
      arXiv (using LATEX code) also complies with the CC license, which helps to widely promote the
520
      dissemination and use of our open-source dataset (DocGenome). Moreover, by leveraging LATEX code,
521
      we can automatically extract annotated structures from 600,000 scientific papers without incurring
522   any additional costs.

      F     D ETAILS OF Q UALITY A SSURANCE FOR QA DATA
893
894   Overall Process of QA Data generation. 1) GPT-4 was used to generate 7028 questions for 1757
895   paper samples. 2) Quality checkers first examine the questions, retaining or modifying them to
896   obtain correct questions. 3) Each question was then allocated to two quality checkers for review and
897   correction. 4) The checkers attempted to correct incorrect answers and assigned confidence scores.
898   5) Only QA pairs with the same answer and the highest confidence scores from both checkers were
899   retained for the final dataset. Finally, 2498 QA pairs were retained to form the QA test set, of which
900
      1672 were modified by the quality checkers.
901   The QA Generation Details. We provide a general prompt template for QA pair generation in
902   Fig. A.3. The discipline-specific guidance is imposed to generate the corresponding ground-truth
903   labels to achieve diversity and relevance.
904   The Quality Checking Details. During independent verification by professional faculty members,
905   each judgment was assigned with a confidence value ranging from 0 to 3. The confidence criterion is
906   designed as follows:
907
      Confidence 3: The reviewer is confident that the QA pair is accurate and relevant to the provided
908
      paper.
909
910   Confidence 2: The reviewer thinks the QA pair is mostly accurate and relevant to the provided paper
911   but is unsure whether it is absolutely correct.
912   Confidence 1: The reviewer has no idea about the correctness or relevance of the QA pair to the
913   provided paper.
914
      Confidence 0: The reviewer is confident that the QA pair is wrong or irrelevant to the provided paper.
915
916   During the cross-verification, the confidence values of the two professional faculty reviewers were
917   compared with the automatically-annotated correctness. The QA pairs with inconsistent results were
      re-analyzed by the two reviewers and updated to a precise version with consistent confidence.
