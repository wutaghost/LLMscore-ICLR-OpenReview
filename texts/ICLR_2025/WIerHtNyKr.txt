009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            This paper addresses the problem of Online Convex-Concave Optimization, an
012            extension of Online Convex Optimization to two-player time-varying convex-
013            concave games. Our objective is to minimize the dynamic duality gap (D-DGap),
014            a key performance metric that evaluates the playersŌĆÖ strategies against arbitrary
015            comparator sequences. Existing algorithms struggle to achieve optimal perfor-
016            mance, particularly in stationary or predictable environments. We propose a novel,
017            modular algorithm comprising three key components: an Adaptive Module that
018
               adjusts to varying levels of non-stationarity, a Multi-Predictor Aggregator that se-
               lects the optimal predictor from multiple candidates, and an Integration Module
019
               that seamlessly combines the strengths of both. Our algorithm guarantees a mini-
020
               max optimal D-DGap upper bound, up to a logarithmic factor, while also achiev-
021            ing a prediction error-based D-DGap bound. Empirical results further demonstrate
022            the effectiveness and adaptability of the proposed method.

115   by von Neumann (1928). The seminal work of Freund & Schapire (1999) connected the minimax
116   problem to online learning, sparking interest in no-regret algorithms for static environments (Anag-
117   nostides et al., 2022; Daskalakis et al., 2015; 2021; Ho-Nguyen & K─▒l─▒nc╠¦-Karzan, 2019; Syrgkanis

120   investigate OCCO and introduce the concept of saddle-point regret, later redefined as Nash equilib-
121   rium regret (Cardoso et al., 2019). Zhang et al. (2022b) further refined the concept of dynamic Nash
122   equilibrium regret and proposed a parameter-free algorithm that guarantees upper bounds for three

      vances Zhang et al. (2022b) by unifying metrics through the D-DGap, ensuring minimax optimality
124
      for arbitrary comparators, and further reducing the D-DGap using multiple predictions.

296   The rationale for designing Equations (3a) and (3d) as a joint update strategy is as follows: To
297   guarantee that the final D-DGap attains a prediction error-based bound, both the first expert and the

299   the first expert needs to be aware of the meta-layerŌĆÖs weights to update its advice, while the meta
300
      layer relies on advice from both experts to adjust its own weights. This inherent interdependence
      necessitates a joint solution.

515   6                 C ONCLUSION
516
517   This paper presents the first investigation into the dynamic duality gap (D-DGap) in Online Convex-
518   Concave Optimization (OCCO). By utilizing a modular algorithmic structure, it efficiently adapts
519   to varying degrees of non-stationarity and leverages the most accurate predictors. The Integration
520
      Module extends the idea of meta-expert framework, guaranteeing optimal performance across vary-
      ing environments.
521
522   To achieve the optimal D-DGap upper bound, our Integrated Module requires a joint optimization
523   subroutine. This subroutine involves a two-dimensional bounded optimization problem with a con-
524   tinuous objective function, which is generally not considered computationally intensive. However,
525   in online scenarios that demand rapid decision-making, the computational cost can become substan-
526
      tial. A promising direction for future research is to explore a trade-off between relaxing the strict
      guarantees on the D-DGap bound and reducing computational costs, thereby achieving a better bal-
527
      ance between these two factors in time-sensitive applications.
