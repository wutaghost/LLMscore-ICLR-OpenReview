009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            Despite significant progress has been made in image deraining, existing approaches
012            are mostly carried out on low-resolution images. The effectiveness of these methods
013            on high-resolution images is still unknown, especially for ultra-high-definition
014            (UHD) images, given the continuous advancement of imaging devices. In this
015            paper, we focus on the task of UHD image deraining, and contribute the first large-
016            scale UHD image deraining dataset, 4K-Rain13k, that contains 13,000 image pairs
017            at 4K resolution. Based on this dataset, we conduct a benchmark study on existing
018
               methods for processing UHD images. Furthermore, we develop an effective and
               efficient architecture (called UDR-Mixer) to better solve this task. Specifically,
019
               our method contains two building components: a spatial feature rearrangement
020
               layer that captures long-range information of UHD images, and a frequency feature
021            modulation layer that facilitates high-quality UHD image reconstruction. Extensive
022            experimental results demonstrate that our method performs favorably against the
023            state-of-the-art approaches while maintaining a lower model complexity. The code
024            and dataset will be available to the public.

027   1   I NTRODUCTION
028
029   Single image deraining aims to remove the undesired degradation induced by rain streaks from input
030   images, enhancing its visual quality and improving the accuracy of perception system (Chen et al.,
031   2022b). In image deraininig, deep learning-based methods become predominate ones as the formation
032   of image deraining is quite simplified compared to the conventional prior-based methods (Luo et al.,
033
      2015). One can choose deep models based on convolutional neural network (CNN) (Jiang et al.,
      2020; Zamir et al., 2021; Yi et al., 2021) or Transformer architectures (Xiao et al., 2022; Chen et al.,
034
      2023b;a; 2024) to directly estimate clear image from rainy one.
035
036   Among these approaches, most of them are trained and evaluated on low-resolution datasets (Chen
037   et al., 2023c). These commonly used benchmark datasets consist of 1K or even lower resolution
038   images, such as Rain200L/H (Yang et al., 2017) and Rain13k (Jiang et al., 2020), as illustrated in
039
      Figure 1(a). Based on the existing empirical studies (Zhang et al., 2021; Wang et al., 2023; Li et al.,
      2023), existing image deraining approaches trained on these low-resolution datasets are not likely to
040
      generalize well on high-resolution images. However, few effort has been made in ultra-high definition
041
      (UHD) image deraining due to the absence of UHD deraining dataset. As UHD devices have been
042   widely used, it is urgent and essential to build a high-resolution benchmark and pave the way for
043   future research in this field.
044
045

      large-scale dataset called 4K-Rain13k to benchmark existing methods. The proposed 4K-Rain13k
046

047

050   geometric inconsistencies in the lengths and thicknesses of rain streaks between low-resolution and
051   high-resolution rainy images. In high-resolution UHD images, rain streaks typically appear longer
052   and slighter due to the increased pixel information, whereas in low-resolution images, the same length
053   of rain streaks may appear blurred into shorter or thicker lines due to fewer pixels. To this end, by
      integrating geometric transformations (i.e., scaling) into the rain synthesis pipeline, we can enhance

      2   R ELATED W ORK
109
110   Single image deraining. When we revisit this field of single image deraining, numerous deraining
111   approaches and benchmark datasets have been proposed in recent years with demonstrated suc-
112   cess (Chen et al., 2023c). Several classic benchmark datasets are widely adopted to evaluate single
113   image deraining performance, such as Rain200L/H (Yang et al., 2017), DID-Data (Zhang & Patel,
114   2018), DDN-Data (Fu et al., 2017) and Rain13k (Jiang et al., 2020). These early benchmark datasets
115   consist of lower resolution images (1K or less). However, in this field, there is a lack of exploration
116   specifically for higher resolution images, particularly UHD images. Furthermore, when dealing with
117   UHD images, existing SOTA methods frequently encounter memory overflow issues, preventing
118
      them from conducting full-resolution inference on consumer-grade GPUs.
119   UHD image processing. With the development of photography equipment, UHD image processing
120   has emerged as a new research trend in recent years (Zheng et al., 2021; Yu et al., 2022b; Wu et al.,
121   2024). Zheng et al. (Zheng et al., 2021) formulated the UHD image dehazing network using multi-
122   guide bilateral learning. Zhang et al. (Zhang et al., 2021) explored the task of image super-resolution
123   for UHD resolutions, and further created two large-scale image datasets, UHDSR4K and UHDSR8K.
124   Ren et al. (Ren et al., 2023) developed a multi-scale separable network to address UHD deblurring
125
      problem. The UHD low-light image enhancement task has also received increasing attention from
      researchers, and representative datasets include UHD-LOL (Wang et al., 2023) and UHD-LL (Li
126
      et al., 2023). Beyond that, other related tasks have focused on the application of UHD images, e.g.,
127
      reflection removal and HDR reconstruction (Zheng et al., 2021). To the best of our knowledge, we
128   first focus on the task of removing rain from UHD images, and we propose both a benchmark dataset
129   and a baseline method.
130
131
      Vision MLP. Given the high computational cost of self-attention mechanism in vision Transform-
      ers (ViT), several researchers have designed efficient vision models comprising solely multi-layer
132
      perceptrons (MLPs). For example, MLP-Mixer (Tolstikhin et al., 2021) utilizes a straightforward
133
      token-mixing MLP instead of self-attention in ViT, leading to an all-MLP network. It employs
134   token-mixing MLP to capture token relationships and channel-mixing MLP to capture channel
135   relationships. Afterwards, some studies further improve the performance of MLP-based models by
136   designing other architectures, such as gMLP (Liu et al., 2021a) and Hire-MLP (Guo et al., 2022).
137   Recently, Tu et al. (Tu et al., 2022) formulated a multi-axis MLP-based framework MAXIM for
138   image processing tasks. Wu et al. (Wu et al., 2024) developed an efficient MixNet for UHD low-light
139   image enhancement by modeling global and local feature dependencies. Inspired by these works, we
140   leverage the vision MLP-like architecture to flexibly handle UHD image deraining.

142   3   UHD I MAGE D ERAINING DATASET C ONSTRUCTION
143
144
      To evaluate the performance of existing approaches on the UHD image deraining problem, we first
      create a large-scale benchmark dataset named 4K-Rain13k. We note that existing low-resolution
145
      rain datasets (Yang et al., 2017; Fu et al., 2017; Zhang & Patel, 2018; Zhang et al., 2019; Jiang
146
      et al., 2020; Liu et al., 2021b) simply add rain streaks into the clear backgrounds to obtain rainy
147   images. However, this copy-and-pasting approach is not suitable for synthesizing UHD rainy images
148   due to the geometric inconsistency between the low-resolution and high-resolution image synthesis
149   processes. Thus, we develop an effective method for synthesizing rainy images tailored for UHD
150   images, aiming to achieve more realistic visual effects. Our method involves background collection,
151   rain streak generation and geometric transformation, which will be presented below.
152
      Background collection. We collect numerous clear backgrounds using a Python program based on
153
      Scrapy to download high-resolution images from the web and various devices. Our ground-truths
154   includes a wide range of typical daytime and nighttime scenes in urban locations (e.g., buildings,
155   streets, cityscapes) as well as natural landscapes (e.g., lakes, hills, and vegetations).
156
157
      Rain streak generation. The diversity and fidelity of rain play crucial roles in the synthesis of rain
      streaks. Instead of using Photoshop software to render rain streaks, we synthesize corresponding rainy
158
      images by modeling the generation of rain streaks as a motion blur process to ensure diversity. In
159
      addition, we take into account the transparency of rain layer to ensure fidelity through alpha blending.
160
161   Geometric transformation. In fact, there is an easily overlooked problem of geometric inconsistency
      in the synthesis of low resolution and high-resolution rainy images, with noticeable discrepancies in

195   4      P ROPOSED UDR-M IXER
196
197   In this section, we develop an effective and efficient method (called UDR-Mixer) for UHD image
198   deraining. We first describe the overall pipeline, and then present the details of two main components,
199
      i.e., spatial feature mixing blocks (SFMB) and frequency feature mixing blocks (FFMB).
200
201   4.1     OVERALL PIPELINE
202
      As shown in Figure 3, UDR-Mixer is an end-to-end dual-branch parallel network architecture that
203
      models UHD images by exploring both spatial and frequency domain information. Specifically, we
204   first embed an input rainy image Irain ∈ RH×W ×3 into the feature space F0 ∈ RH×W ×C through a
205   3 × 3 convolution layer, where H, W and C represents the height, width, and channel, respectively.
206   To reduce computational complexity in high-resolution images, following previous studies (Li et al.,
207   2023; Wu et al., 2024), we employ a PixelUnshuffle operation to downsample the features to 1/4 of
208   the original resolution. Then, the low-level features are processed by an encoder-decoder network
209   consisting of Ni SFMBs with a 2× downsample operation and a 2× upsample operation to produce
210   output features.
211   To alleviate the issue of losing image details caused by straightforward downsampling operations,
212   we further introduce an auxiliary branch to help UHD image reconstruction. Specifically, we stack
213   Ni FFMBs to excavate the frequency information of the full-resolution UHD image. Then, the
214   learned deep features are fed to the decoder network of the main branch for guiding latent clear
215   image restoration. Finally, the output features are obtained to estimate the derained image using a
      3 × 3 convolutional layer followed by a PixelShuffle operation. To supervise the training process, we

      4.2   S PATIAL FEATURE MIXING BLOCK
246
247   Inspired by MLP-Mixer (Tolstikhin et al., 2021), we introduce a new backbone module to encode
248   feature information. Firstly, we develop a simple yet effective SFMB to aggregate global spatial
249   information. Given the input feature Xl−1 ∈ RH×W ×C , the proposed SFMB can be formulated as:
250                                   X′l = Xl−1 + SFRL (LN (Xl−1 )) ,
251                                                                                                  (2)
                                      Xl = X′l + FFL (LN (X′l )) ,
252
253   where LN refers to the layer normalization. X′l and Xl are output feature from the spatial feature
254
      rearrangement layer (SFRL) and feed-forward layer (FFL).
255   Spatial feature rearrangement layer. To model global spatial information with lower computational
256   costs, we develop a SFRL based on continuous rearrangements and channel-mixing MLPs. Different
257   from Hire-MLP (Guo et al., 2022) that utilizes region rearrangement strategy, we introduce a more
258   flexible mechanism via dimension transformation operations, which directly considers the spatial
259   properties between pixels in 3D feature maps. This enables the model to progressively capture global
260   features across the entire image by scrolling, thereby establishing a gradual perception of long-range
261
      information from UHD images (see Figure 3). Specifically, we first normalize the input features, and
      then perform multi-stage dimension transformations to rotate the spatial perspective of the tensor
262
      across three dimensions of H, W and C. Here, the 3D feature map undergoes recursive encoding from
263
      (C, H, W ) to (H, W, C) and then to (W, C, H), enabling the capture of global spatial information
264   through multi-view dimensions. Finally, we adjust the feature map to the original resolution, and
265   interact with the input features to activate useful features. Mathematically, given an input feature F0 ,
266   the feature propagation process of SFRL can be expressed as:

      4.3   F REQUENCY FEATURE MIXING BLOCK
283
284   We note that existing methods (Wang et al., 2023; Li et al., 2023; Ren et al., 2023; Wu et al., 2024)
285   mostly employ direct downsampling of UHD images to create low-resolution versions, aiming to
286   reduce computational burden. In such cases, the full-resolution restoration process is predominantly
287   governed by information learned solely from the low-resolution images, resulting in suboptimal
288   performance and a tendency to lose high-frequency details, which are abundant in UHD images. To
289   this end, we develop the FFMB as a unit for the auxiliary branch. It leverages the frequency domain
290
      information of the full-resolution images and guides the decoding restoration process of the main
      branch composed of SFMB. Given the input feature Yl−1 ∈ RH×W ×C , the proposed FFMB can be
291
      formulated as:
292
293
294

296   where Yl′ and Yl are output feature from the frequency feature modulation layer (FFML) and FFL.
297   The structure of FFL remains the same as that in SFMB.
298
299   Frequency feature modulation layer. According to the convolution theorem, convolution in
300   one domain is mathematically equivalent to the Hadamard product in its corresponding Fourier
301
      domain (Huang et al., 2023). This also motivates us to introduce a FFML for implementing frequency-
      space manipulation. Given an input feature F0 , we employ Fast Fourier Transform (FFT) to obtain
302
      the corresponding frequency representations. Then, we adopt two stacks of MLP layers with a ReLU
303
      layer in between. Finally, we perform an inverse FFT and interact with the input features to obtain
304   updated feature representations in the original latent space. This process can be formulated as:

306                              Fl = F −1 (MLP (ReLU (MLP (F (F0 ))))) ,

      6     C ONCLUDING R EMARKS
531
532   This paper explores the task of UHD image deraining for the first time and proposes a high-quality
533
      dataset 4K-Rain13k to facilitate the performance comparison. Furthermore, we develop an effi-
      cient method UDR-Mixer for UHD image deraining. Our approach utilizes a dimension rearrange
534
      mechanism to establish the global spatial context of UHD images and combines it with the original
535
      frequency representation of UHD images to help image restoration. The benchmark results show that
536   our model achieves a favorable trade-off between performance and model complexity.
537
538   Limitations. Although our method achieves favorable performance, it fails to handle the presence of
539   fog-like rain accumulation in real rainy scenes. Future work will consider expanding 4K data with
      veiling effect and introducing physical models to guide enhancing the quality of image reconstruction.

691   A.1    M ORE IMPLEMENTATION DETAILS ON DATASET CONSTRUCTION
692
693   Rain Streak Generation. We model the generation of rain streak layers as a motion blur process,
694   leveraging two essential characteristics of rain streaks: their repeatability and directionality. Formally,
695   this can be represented as:
696                                           S = K(l, θ, w) ∗ N(n),                                         (7)
697   where N represents the rain mask derived from random noise n, where we utilize uniform random
698   numbers combined with thresholds to adjust the noise level. The parameters l and θ correspond to
699                                                           R
      the length and angle of the motion blur kernel K ∈ p×p . Additionally, a rotated diagonal kernel
700   is applied with Gaussian blur to control the rain streak thickness w. The values for noise quantity
701   n, rain length l, rain angle θ, and rain thickness w are sampled from the ranges [100, 300], [20, 40],
      [40◦ , 120◦ ], and [3, 7], respectively. The symbol ∗ denotes the spatial convolution operator.

      Geometric Transformation. In the physical world, rain streaks are naturally fine and elongated,
      reflecting their slender geometry and high-speed movement. In high-resolution 4K images, with
715
      the increased pixel density and more detailed spatial representation, these characteristics can be
716
      captured more accurately. Each rain-affected region is represented with greater precision, allowing
717   the streaks to appear longer and thinner, consistent with their real-world appearance. Conversely,
718   in low-resolution images, the reduced pixel information limits the ability to preserve these fine
719   details. Rain streaks that are physically the same length may appear shorter and thicker due to the
720   loss of spatial resolution and the effects of blurring. This results in a coarser and more ambiguous
721   depiction of rain streaks, where fine structures and elongated shapes are less distinguishable. This
722   disparity highlights the importance of resolution in faithfully representing rain streaks and motivates
723   the development of high-resolution synthesis approaches. Based on these real-world observations,
724   we perform geometric transformation using scaling operations on the initial rain layer generated in
725
      the previous step.
726
727   Alpha Blending. We utilize alpha blending to combine the rain layer with the background layer.
728
      Here, the alpha value of each pixel in a layer determines the extent to which colors from underlying
      layers are visible through the current layer’s color. Mathematically, this process is expressed as:
729
730
                                        Rr,g,b = α ⊙ S + (1 − α) ⊙ B,                                     (8)
731
732   where α represents the blending ratio, which is empirically set within the range [0.8, 0.9]. The symbol
733   ⊙ indicates element-wise multiplication. The R, G, and B channels are handled independently
734   during the process. Figure 8 shows an example of synthesizing rainy images with different blending
735   ratios. It is evident that when the blending ratios are set to 0.8 and 0.9, the rain streaks become
736   invisible in the white sky region, resulting in greater harmony with real rainy images.
