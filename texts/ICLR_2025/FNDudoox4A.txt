009                                                                                        A BSTRACT
010
011                      Composed Image Retrieval (CIR) employs a triplet architecture to combine a ref-
012                      erence image with modified text for target image retrieval. To mitigate high an-
013                      notation costs, Zero-Shot CIR (ZS-CIR) methods eliminate the need for man-
014                      ually annotated triplets. Current methods typically map images to tokens and
015                      concatenate them with modified text. However, they encounter challenges dur-
016                      ing inference, especially with fine-grained and multi-attribute modifications. We
017                      argue that these challenges stem from insufficient explicit modeling of triplet re-
018
                         lationships, which complicates fine-grained interactions and directional guidance.
                         To this end, we propose a Synthetic Image-Oriented training paradigm that auto-
019
                         mates pseudo target image generation, facilitating efficient triplet construction and
020
                         accommodating inherent target ambiguity. Furthermore, we propose the Pseudo
021                      domAiN Decoupling-Alignment (PANDA) model to mitigate the Autophagy phe-
022                      nomenon caused by fitting targets with pseudo images. We observe that synthetic
023                      images are intermediate between visual and textual domains in triplets. Regard-
024                      ing this phenomenon, we design the Orthogonal Semantic Decoupling module to
025                      disentangle the pseudo domain into visual and textual components. Additionally,
026                      Shared Domain Interaction and Mutual Shift Constraint modules are proposed to
027                      collaboratively constrain the disentangled components, bridging the gap between
028                      pseudo and real triplets while enhancing their semantic consistency. Extensive ex-
029
                         periments demonstrate that PANDA outperforms existing state-of-the-art methods
                         across two general scenarios and two domain-specific CIR datasets.

049   Composed Image Retrieval (CIR) retrieves a target image by integrating a reference image and
050   modified text, achieving notable advancements recently Vo et al. (2019); Delmas et al. (2022); Yang
051   et al. (2024b). However, constructing reference-modified text-target triplets, particularly in domain-
052   specific contexts like e-commerce, is resource-intensive Karthik et al. (2024b;a). Consequently,
053   Zero-Shot Composed Image Retrieval (ZS-CIR) has emerged, focusing on scenarios that eliminate
      the need for manually annotated triplets Baldrati et al. (2023); Lin et al. (2024). The current ZS-CIR


                                                                                                        1

054
      framework trains on image-caption datasets to map images to tokens for the text encoder Tang et al.
055   (2024); Du et al. (2024). During inference, the reference image is tokenized and concatenated with
056   modified text, allowing the text encoder to extract features for target image retrieval.
057
058
      However, the existing framework struggles with fine-grained or multi-attribute modifications due
      to the lack of explicit triplet modeling. It overlooks two key roles of the modified text in CIR:
059
      Interacting with the reference. Current methods implicitly assign the crucial interaction between
060
      the modified text and reference image to the text encoder Saito et al. (2023); Baldrati et al. (2023),
061   missing essential contextual cues from complex visual-textual semantics. Guiding from reference to
062   target. The modified text should guide retrieval by outlining the differences between reference and
063   target images Kim et al. (2021). Existing methods mistakenly treat it as a direct descriptor of target
064   features, hindering effectiveness in scenarios involving multiple attributes or new elements.
065
      To address the aforementioned issues, we propose a Synthetic Image-Oriented (SIO) training
066
      paradigm tailored for the ZS-CIR task. This approach aims to automate the construction of pseudo
067   target images using generative models, thereby creating triplets similar to those encountered dur-
068   ing inference. This training paradigm presents several advantages: (i) Target Ambiguity Alignment.
069   The ZS-CIR task inherently involves target ambiguity Liu et al. (2021); Delmas et al. (2022), al-
070   lowing multiple valid options to fulfill the modified text’s objectives. Diffusion generative models
071   can produce multiple images based on the same semantics Croitoru et al. (2023); Wu et al. (2023),
072   making them well-suited for this characteristic. (ii) Efficient Triplet Construction. This paradigm
073   utilizes existing image-caption datasets to rapidly generate pseudo triplets. Current ZS-CIR meth-
074   ods implicitly learn semantic correspondences within triplets Saito et al. (2023); Tang et al. (2024),
075
      often depending on large datasets (e.g., CC3M Sharma et al. (2018)). In contrast, SIO requires
      significantly less data, up to two orders of magnitude less than existing ZS-CIR methods.
076
077   Nevertheless, recent studies Alemohammad et al. (2024) indicate that merely using synthetic images
078   for training can lead to performance degradation due to the Autophagy phenomenon. We observe
079   that synthetic images exist in an intermediate pseudo domain between the visual and textual do-
080   mains, as shown in Figure 1 (c). To prevent the model from converging excessively to the pseudo
081
      domain and to enhance the performance gains of pseudo triplets, we propose the Pseudo Domain
      Decoupling-Alignment (PANDA) model. We first introduce an Orthogonal Semantic Decoupling
082
      (OSD) module, which explicitly disentangles the features of the pseudo domain into two comple-
083
      mentary parts. The first part focuses on aligning with the visual domain of the target image in the
084   actual triplet, while the second part emphasizes constraints with the textual domain of the modified
085   text. For the first part, we propose a Shared Domain Interaction (SDI) module that employs shared
086   network weights and specific learnable tokens to model interactions among the multimodal, visual,
087   textual, and pseudo domains. By progressively interacting the real image side and the modified text
088   within the pseudo triplets, a multimodal representation that fully integrates both components is ob-
089   tained. For the second part, we design a Mutual Shift Constraint (MSC) module that captures the
090   differences from the reference to the target, constrained by the modified text.
091   In a nutshell, our contributions are summarized as follows:
092
093   • A new training paradigm is proposed to automate pseudo target image generation, facilitating
094     efficient triplet construction and addressing target ambiguity in the ZS-CIR task.
095   • We gain insight into the fact that synthetic images exist in an intermediate state between visual
096     and textual domains, underscoring the need for specialized modeling.
097
      • The proposed PANDA model focuses on mitigating the Autophagy phenomenon when using
098
        pseudo images as targets while enhancing semantic interactions and alignment among triplets.
099
100   • Extensive experiments show that PANDA outperforms state-of-the-art methods across two general
101
        scenarios and two domain-specific datasets.

105   2.1   Z ERO -S HOT C OMPOSED I MAGE R ETRIEVAL (ZS-CIR).
106
107   Currently, methods in the field of CIR can be broadly categorized into two paradigms. The first
      paradigm employs fully supervised late fusion methods Baldrati et al. (2022); Chen et al. (2024b);


                                                        2
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


108
      Jiang et al. (2024a); Yang et al. (2024b), using manually annotated reference-modified text-target
109   triplets as training data Liu et al. (2024a); Han et al. (2023); Zhang et al. (2024). For instance,
110   Baldrati et al. (2022) proposes a simple yet effective fusion model, Combiner, to combine fea-
111   tures extracted by the CLIP Radford et al. (2021) model. The second paradigm, commonly used
112   in ZS-CIR settings, adopts the word token framework Tang et al. (2024); Bai et al. (2024), initially
113   introduced by Saito et al. (2023). This method learns to map image embeddings into word tokens
114   interpretable by a text encoder during the training phase on image-caption pairs. In the testing phase,
115   the modified text is concatenated directly for target retrieval. Lin et al. (2024) further enhances the
116   fine-grained representation of reference images by mapping them into subject-oriented word tokens
117
      and several attribute-oriented word tokens. However, current methods overlook explicit modeling
      of triplet semantics, neglecting the core functions of modified text to interact with the reference and
118
      guide it toward the target. Our approach addresses these issues at both the training paradigm and
119
      methodological levels, achieving enhanced semantic interaction and alignment among triplets.

122   2.2   DATA AUGMENTATION U SING S YNTHETIC I MAGES .
123
124
      With the rapid advancement of text-to-image generation models Dhariwal & Nichol (2021); Li et al.
      (2019); Ding et al. (2021); Li et al. (2023b), an increasing number of pioneering works are apply-
125
      ing synthetic data to computer vision and multimodal tasks Wang et al. (2021); Wood et al. (2021);
126
      Yang et al. (2024a). In domains where labeled data is costly, such as medical applications, synthetic
127   images can mitigate data scarcity, facilitating model learning Chen et al. (2021); Usman Akbar et al.
128   (2024); Müller-Franzes et al. (2023). In general image tasks like classification and segmentation,
129   synthetic images serve as excellent data augmentation for real-world images and can be used as the
130   entire training dataset due to their high-quality generation. Tian et al. (2024); Fan et al. (2024); Liu
131   et al. (2024b). He et al. (2023) use high-quality synthetic images, filtering out low-quality sam-
132   ples, and achieve significant improvements over the CLIP model. Hammoud et al. (2024) propose
133   training CLIP models Radford et al. (2021) solely with synthetic text-image pairs generated by text-
134   to-image models and large language models. This scalable method eliminates manual intervention
135
      and matches the performance of CLIP models trained on real data. Inspired by pioneering studies,
      we introduce synthetic images into the ZS-CIR task for two key reasons: (i) Diffusion models can
136
      generate multiple images from the same semantics Croitoru et al. (2023); Wu et al. (2023), aligning
137
      with the inherent target ambiguity in CIR Liu et al. (2021); Delmas et al. (2022). (ii) Efficiently
138   constructs pseudo triplets from existing image-caption datasets.

150   The objective of ZS-CIR is to learn how to retrieve a target image Itar at inference time with-
151   out providing manually annotated reference-modifier-target triplets, utilizing a reference image
152   Iref and user-provided modified text tmod . We propose a novel Synthetic Image-Oriented training
153   paradigm. Given an image-caption dataset DIC = {(Ii , Ci )}N         i=1 , we construct pseudo-triplets
154   {(Iref , Tmod , Itar )} using an image generation model. Our objective is to perform associative learn-
155   ing using the embeddings extracted from Iref , tmod , and Itar , respectively, in order to learn a map-
156   ping function f : (Iref , Tmod ) → Itar . The learned function f is then evaluated on real triplets

      3.3   T HEORETICAL I NSIGHTS
187
188   In this section, we justify the rationale behind introducing pseudo-triplets, each containing multiple
189   synthetic images as possible targets, and explain how our train pattern outperforms existing solu-
190   tions. In the ZS-CIR task, we hypothesize that there is an underlying ground truth mapping function
191   F and aim to get an approximated function f that correctly retrieves the targets in available triplets
192   for training. Therefore, we theoretically construct a toy problem for the ZS-CIR task.
193
194   Toy Problem. Given a triplet Stri = {(Iref , Tmod , Itar )}, the objective is to learn an approximated
195
      mapping function f that satisfies f ((Iref , Tmod , Itar )) − F((Iref , Tmod , Itar )) = 0. In another word,
      the composed function f − F takes Stri as its root.
196
197   Existing ZS-CIR methods, where only one target image is paired with a reference and modified text,
198   define a triplet (Iref , Tmod , Itar ) that results in a linear approximation relative to f − F. In contrast,
199   our approach introduces multiple synthetic target images in a pseudo-triplet (Ii , Tmod    fine
                                                                                                      , {Iigen }), where
200   each target acts as a root for f = F, facilitating polynomial approximations.
201
202
      Weierstrass Approximation Theorem. Let F be a continuous real-valued function on the inter-
      val [a, b]. For any ϵ > 0, there exists a polynomial f such that for all x ∈ [a, b], |f (x) − F(x)| < ϵ.
203
      Furthermore, the approximation error can be bounded as follows: if F has a continuous k-th deriva-
204
      tive, then for any n ∈ N, there exists a polynomial fn of degree at most n such that:

208   Moreover, leveraging our toy problem definition and the Weierstrass approximation theorem, multi-
209   ple targets allow for higher-degree polynomial functions, resulting in more accurate approximations
210   and reduced error bounds. This offers theoretical support for the effectiveness of our approach.

      datasets. (i) CIRR Liu et al. (2021) comprises approximately 21K open-domain images sourced
334
      from the NLVR2 dataset Suhr et al. (2019). To reduce false negatives, annotations ensure the mod-
335   ification text applies to a single image pair, excluding any relevance to other pairs sharing the same
336   reference image. We evaluate our approach on the CIRR test set, consisting of 4.1K triplets. (ii)
337   CIRCO Baldrati et al. (2023), derived from the COCO Lin et al. (2014) dataset, addresses false
338   negatives more comprehensively. Unlike other datasets, each CIRCO sample includes a reference
339   image, a modification text, and multiple target images. Our evaluation uses the CIRCO test set,
340   consisting of 800 samples. (iii) FashionIQ Wu et al. (2021) focuses on fashion items from three
341   categories: Dresses, Shirts, and Tops&Tees. In line with prior studies, we use the validation set for
342   evaluation. (iv) Shoes Guo et al. (2018) is an e-commerce dataset with 4,658 validation queries,
343
      following the split used in previous work Guo et al. (2018).
344
345   Evaluation Metrics. For (i) CIRR, as suggested by prior work Saito et al. (2023); Jiang et al.
346   (2024a), we use a combination of evaluation criteria, including R@K, Rsubset @K, and the average
347
      of R@5 and Rsubset @1. Notably, Rsubset @K restricts candidate target images to those semantically
      similar to the correct target image, addressing the issue of false negatives. For (ii) CIRCO, due to
348
      the presence of multiple ground truths, we follow previous work Baldrati et al. (2023); Lin et al.
349
      (2024) and adopt Average Precision (mAP) as a more fine-grained metric. For the (iii) FashionIQ
350   and Shoes datasets, in line with previous studies Lin et al. (2024); Chen et al. (2024a), we employ
351   recall at rank K (R@K) as the evaluation metric, specifically adopting R@10 and R@50.
352
353
      Implementation Details. We use Stable Diffusion v3 Esser et al. (2024) as the pseudo target
354
      image generator, producing 5 images at 512×512 resolution per caption using 20 sampling steps.
355   We randomly sample image subsets of specified sizes from the CC3M dataset Sharma et al. (2018)
356   to construct pseudo triplets. We train the model using up to 100K pseudo-samples. Vicuna-13B-
357   V0.2 Chiang et al. (2023) serves as the LLM. Following the BLIP-2 design Li et al. (2023a), we
358   initialize the encoders using the its pretrained model with ViT-L Radford et al. (2021), and optimize
359   with AdamW Loshchilov & Hutter (2019) using a batch size of 64, an initial learning rate of 1e-5,
360   and a cosine annealing schedule over 50 epochs. All model training and inference are performed on
361   a V100 GPU. All methods utilize ViT-L as the visual backbone.

      5   C ONCLUSION
528
      In this paper, we offer the insight that current ZS-CIR training methods lack explicit semantic learn-
529
      ing for triplets, limiting their capacity for fine-grained or multi-attribute modifications. To address
530
      this, we introduce the Synthetic Image-Oriented training paradigm, leveraging synthetic images to
531   swiftly form pseudo triplets while addressing target ambiguity in CIR. Additionally, to mitigate over-
532   fitting caused by pseudo images, we propose the Pseudo domAiN Decoupling-Alignment (PANDA)
533   model, which decouples the pseudo domain and applies separate alignment constraints. Compre-
534   hensive experiments demonstrate the effectiveness of our proposed training paradigm and approach.
535
536   Limitations. Although the proposed Synthetic Image-Oriented training paradigm allows for the
537   quick construction of pseudo-triplets, enabling the model to efficiently learn the correspondence
538   between triplet components, the modified text in real-world scenarios may involve more complex
539   semantics, such as comparatives or multiple conjunctions. Our next research goal is to leverage
      synthetic images’ inherent target ambiguity to adapt to these more complex semantic cases.
