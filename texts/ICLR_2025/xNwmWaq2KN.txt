011                                               A BSTRACT
012
013
               Current generative models have shown potential performance in many tasks,
014            which typically focus on generating samples that closely adhere to a given dis-
015            tribution. However, existing methods cannot simultaneously generate diverse and
016            high-quality samples, and they fail to produce optimal diverse solutions in intra-
017            batch diversity . Recognizing that maintaining “diversity” has been a longstanding
018            challenge in multiobjective optimization, we were inspired to introduce a multi-
019            objective optimization approach to enhance diversity in a single pass. This paper
020            utilizes the in-betweening human motion generation task as an example and in-
021            troduces the multiobjective generative models to produce a batch of diverse and
022
               smooth human motion sequences in one pass. The resulting method, termed the
               Multiobjective Generation Framework with In-Betweening Motion Model (MGF-
023
               IMM), frames the human motion in-betweening task as a bi-objective optimiza-
024
               tion problem. The designed in-betweening motion model is then integrated into
025            a multiobjective optimization framework to address this bi-objective optimiza-
026            tion problem. Through comprehensive experiments, MGF-IMM has surpassed
027            the state-of-the-art methods and validated its superiority in generating diverse in-
028            betweening human motions, without introducing additional training parameters.

      1   I NTRODUCTION
034
035   Generative models Wei et al. (2024); Goodfellow et al. (2020); Ho et al. (2020); Shafir et al. (2024),
036   such as Variational Autoencoders (VAEs) Kingma et al. (2019), Generative Adversarial Networks
037   (GANs) Goodfellow et al. (2020), and Diffusion models Ho et al. (2020), have recently emerged
038   as a promising technique in many tasks, such as computer animation Harvey et al. (2020), virtual
039
      reality Zhao et al. (2022), and human-computer interactions Mohammed et al. (2020). It aims to
      learn the underlying distribution of the data, thus supporting the generation of new samples. For
040
      example, in human motion generation fields, Wei et al. Mao et al. (2022) utilized a VAE-based
041
      approach combined with Transformer network to fill long-term missing motion frames. Zhou et al.
042   Harvey et al. (2020) proposed a conditional GAN to learn in-betweening human motions. Tevet et
043   al. Tevet et al. (2023) presented a human motion diffusion model for in-betweening human motion
044   generation, they adapted a classifier-free diffusion-based generative model for this task.
045
      Despite achieving success in many applications, generative models still face challenges. For in-
046
      stance, they struggle to consistently produce diverse and high-quality samples after a single training
047
      process. Additionally, generative models are highly sensitive to hyperparameters. On large datasets,
048   setting training parameters appropriately is crucial, as retraining the model is resource-intensive and
049   time-consuming. This means that current generative models cannot guarantee intra-batch diversity .
050   It is important to note that the need for generating “multiple” and “diverse” outputs is quite common
051   in real-world scenarios. For example, in in-betweening human motion generation task Tevet et al.
052   (2023), the objective is to create a set of diverse and smooth sequences in one pass to interpolate
053   user-provided sequences, offering engineers a wide range of transitional human motions, which has
      a wide range of applications in areas, such as AI for animation Xiao et al. (2024).

054
      Coincidentally, maintaining “diversity” is also a long-standing issue in the domain of multiobjective
055   optimization Guo et al. (2024); Song et al. (2014); Hua et al. (2021); Wang et al. (2014). In general,
056   multiobjective optimization problems involve multiple conflicting objectives, which means that no
057   single solution can simultaneously minimize or maximize all objectives Liu et al. (2022); Qiao et al.
058   (2022). This impressive characteristic leads us to transform the generative task into a multiobjective
059   optimization problem. Surprisingly, due to the implicit parallelism of the population-based search
060   strategy, evolutionary algorithms (EAs) Tian et al. (2020a) can not only provide multiple Pareto
061   optimal solutions in a single pass, but their well-designed diversity maintenance strategies also fa-
062   cilitate solutions that are well-distributed along the Pareto front. Thus EAs are utilized to optimize
063
      the designed multiobjective problem and generate diverse samples. In this work, we employ in-
      betweening human motion generation task as a showcase to report the effectiveness of the designed
064
      multiobjective generative models.
065
066   Currently, it is difficult to capture human motion data, as all motion types and variations require a
067   tremendous amount of time and effort. Additionally, human motion exhibits a high degree of di-
068   versity, with significant individual differences. Therefore, efficiently generating diverse and smooth
069
      in-betweening human motion sequences remains a significant challenge. While the diversity of gen-
      erative models for human motion has been discussed in recent years Guo et al. (2020), they often fail
070
      to guarantee that human motion sequences generated through batch sampling will differ significantly
071
      from one another. Intra-batch diversity is crucial for users, who are typically more concerned with
072   the diversity of the sequences they receive. Therefore, this paper focuses on maintaining intra-batch
073   diversity to address this need.
074
075
      Motivated by this consideration, this paper designs an Multiobjective Generation Framework with
      In-Betweening Motion Model (MGF-IMM) for diverse in-betweening human motion task. We
076
      demonstrate that, without introducing any additional training parameters, the proposed MGF-IMM
077
      effectively guides the generation of diversity and smoothness in-betweening human motions. This
078   is supported mainly by the following three techniques:
079
080          • Multiobjective Modeling of Human Motion In-Betweening: We transform the human mo-
081            tion in-betweening task into a multiobjective optimization problem. We prove that for any
082            Pareto optimal solutions of the formulated problem, the corresponding human motion se-
083            quences can support smooth and diverse transitions to interpolate user-provided sequences.
084            Moreover, we also demonstrate that two points on the Pareto front that are far apart, usually
085
               correspond to two motion sequences with different motion labels.
086          • Multiobjective Generation Framework: In the optimization process, a generative model
087            is incorporated into the multiobjective EA to generate diverse human motion sequences.
088            Specifically, the generative model is used to generate in-between motion frames between
089
               the user-provided motion sequence frames, and environmental selection is employed to
               guide the generated motions to evolve toward the Pareto optimal solutions.
090
091          • In-Betweening Motion Model: We developed a Transformer-based in-betweening motion
092            model within our multiobjective generation framework to facilitate the generation of in-
093            betweening motion sequences. While we primarily focus on the designed in-betweening
094
               motion model based on the VAE generative model, the designed architecture is also adapt-
               able to other generative methods such as GAN and DDPM. Our results indicate that, re-
095
               gardless of whether the VAE, GAN, or DDPM architecture is employed, the designed mul-
096
               tiobjective generation framework successfully guides diverse and smooth in-betweening
097            human motion sequences.

116   In recent decades, researchers have developed various algorithms and frameworks to address mul-

121   led to widespread applications across real-world multiobjective optimization problems Tian et al.
122   (2020b;a); Liu et al. (2021). One of the most appealing features of EAs is their ability to maintain
123
      population diversity. Such mechanisms are crucial for discovering solutions that are well-distributed
      along the Pareto front. Therefore, we are inspired to transform our task into a multiobjective opti-

      mization problem.

      2.2   D IVERSE I N - BETWEENING H UMAN M OTION TASK
127
128   Diverse in-betweening human motion task aims to generate diverse and smooth motion sequences
129   given user-provided sequences. A plethora of generative models, such as VAEs and GANs, have
130   been applied to this task Mao et al. (2022); Fragkiadaki et al. (2015); Li et al. (2023b); Agrawal
131   et al. (2013). For example, Harvey et al. Harvey et al. (2020) utilized neural networks to gen-
132   erate plausible interpolation human motions between a given pair of keyframe poses. Tang et al.
133   Tang et al. (2022) built upon these findings by introducing a Convolutional Variational Autoen-
134   coder (CVAE) Mao et al. (2022), which utilizes the motion manifold and conditional transitioning
135   to generate real-time motion transitions. Motion DNA Zhou et al. (2020) proposed to automati-
136
      cally synthesize complex motions over a long time interval given very sparse keyframes by users for
      long-term in-betweening. However, VAEs assume a Gaussian distribution as the posterior, which
137
      can limit the diversity of the generated samples. Meanwhile, GANs tend to mainly generate sam-
138
      ples from the major modes while ignoring the minor modes, further restricting the overall diversity.
139   More recently, denoising diffusion models Ho et al. (2020); Lee et al. (2023); Guo et al. (2020)
140   have been extensively utilized for motion generation. MoFusion Dabral et al. (2023) proposed a
141   denoising diffusion-based framework to generate motion in-betweening by fixing a set of keyframes
142   in the motion sequence and reverse-diffusing the remaining frames. OmniControl Xie et al. (2024)
143   presents a novel method for text-conditioned human motion generation, which incorporates flexible
144   spatial control signals over different joints at different times. Their diffusion backbone is based on
145   the human motion diffusion method. Based on this, CondMDI Cohan et al. (2024) proposes a flexi-
146   ble in-betweening pipeline through random sampling keyframes and concatenating binary masking
147
      during training. The above state-of-the-art methods handle diverse motion sequences by enhancing
      generative models to produce a wide range of plausible samples. However, they overlook the issue
148
      of ensuring intra-batch diversity in the sampling process. In contrast, this paper introduces a multi-
149
      objective sampling method to ensure high intra-batch diversity. Our approach requires no additional
150   training and can be seamlessly integrated into various generative models as a plug-and-play solution.

      4.1   M ULTIOBJECTIVE G ENERATION F RAMEWORK
228
229   EAs are highly effective for solving multiobjective optimization problems. It is logical to employ
230   EAs to address the multiobjective optimization problem described in Section 4. However, it is cru-
231   cial to recognize that human motion sequences are represented by a high-dimensional set of vectors,
232   which introduces substantial challenges in the search for the Pareto optimal solutions. Furthermore,
233   these sequences must adhere to constraints to ensure physically meaningful representations. These
234   factors complicate the direct application of EAs for searching Pareto-optimal solutions within the
235
      human motion space. A viable solution to these challenges involves utilizing generative models in
      place of traditional EA operations, such as crossover and mutation, to produce offspring. Generative
236
      models can encode high-dimensional data into a low-dimensional space, which avoids directly gen-
237
      erating offspring within the high-dimensional human motion space and ensures that the generated
238   offspring comply with the necessary constraints Wong et al. (2023). Motivated by these considera-
239   tions, we propose a multiobjective generation framework for human motion in-betweening task.
240
241
      The designed multiobjective generation framework is illustrated in Figure. 1. In general, it follows
      the most common framework of genetic algorithms, including the following steps:
242
243   Step 1. Initialization: The pretrained generative model is employed to produce an initial popula-
244   tion of human motion sequences. These sequences are subsequently evaluated using the objective
245   functions defined in equation 4.
246   Step 2. Offspring Generation: Generating a set of offspring human motion sequences using the
247   pretrained generative model.
248
      Step 3. Evaluation: Evaluating the offspring using the objective functions defined in equation 4.
249
250   Step 4. Environmental Selection: Performing fast nondominated sorting and calculating the crowd-
251   ing distance Deb et al. (2002) for the sequences in both the current population and the offspring
252   population. Subsequently, update the population through the elite selection.
253   Step 5. Repeating steps 2 to 4 until the termination condition is met.
254
255

347   4.3   A S UMMARY FOR THE P ROPOSED M ETHOD
348
349
      In summary, our task aims to generate diverse and smooth in-betweening human motions given the
      user-provided human motion frames. The overflow of the proposed method is briefly described in
350
      Figure. 3. Specifically, given the user-provided motion sequence X1 and X2 , we first generate the
351
      initial in-betweening human motions Y through a designed and pretrained VAE model. We would
352   like to note that other generative models can also be used for this task, such as GAN and DDPM,
353   as our method can enhance the performance of these generative models. To alleviate the mode
354   collapse in VAE-based generation, while enhancing the diversity and smoothness of the generated
355   motion sequence, we transform this task into multiobjective optimization problem, and design a
356   multiobjective optimization function to optimize this process. We have demonstrated that for any
357   Pareto optimal solutions of the formulated problem, the corresponding human motion sequences
358   can support a smooth and natural transition to interpolate user-provided sequences. Therefore, the
359   multiobjective optimization process can capture multiple optimal solutions, i.e., generate diverse
360
      in-betweening human motion sequences. Moreover, our method utilizes the generative model to
      generate motion sequences. It is significant to note that the proposed method can enhance the diver-
361
      sity of generated human motions based on generative models without introducing additional training
362
      processes and parameters.
