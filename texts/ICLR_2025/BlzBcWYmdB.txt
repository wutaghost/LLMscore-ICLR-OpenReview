011                                               A BSTRACT
012
013            Recent studies have shown that pre-trained vision-language models can effectively
014            adapt to diverse downstream tasks through parameter-efficient prompt tuning. Un-
015            fortunately, the tuned models can exploit spurious correlations during prediction,
016            resulting in a failure to generalize to out-of-distribution test data, especially when
017            the tuning dataset exhibits bias. How to achieve cross-modal mitigation of spu-
018
               rious correlations during prompt tuning of vision-language models remains an
               open question. In this paper, the challenging problem is tackled by leveraging the
019
               stable relationship between necessary and sufficient causal features and the corre-
020
               sponding label. On the one hand, we constrain the learning process of prompt by
021            reinforcing the necessary and sufficient connection between the textual labels and
022            textual features. On the other hand, the probability of necessity and sufficiency
023            between the textual features and the filtered visual features is measured and max-
024            imized to enhance cross-modal feature alignment. By iteratively optimizing these
025            two objectives, we can achieve cross-modal mitigation of spurious correlations
026            because the logic equivalence between textual labels and visual features is bol-
027            stered. The theoretical analysis on generalization error indicates that our method
028            can achieve a tighter generalization error bound than existing approaches. We
029
               evaluate the proposed method on several commonly adopted out-of-distribution
               datasets, and the empirical results demonstrate the superiority of our method over
030
               the state-of-the-art competitors.

034   1   I NTRODUCTION
035
036   Vision-language models (VLMs), which integrate visual and textual data processing for complex
037   real-world tasks (Zhou et al., 2020; Radford et al., 2021; Zhao et al., 2024; Zhang et al., 2024c), have
038   become a cornerstone of multi-modal learning. Recent advancements have demonstrated the pow-
039
      erful zero-shot generalization capabilities of pre-trained vision-language models (VLMs), enabling
      them highly adaptable to a wide range of downstream tasks, especially image classification (Radford
040
      et al., 2021). To harness the flexible adaptability of pre-trained VLMs, prompt tuning emerges as
041
      a parameter-efficient tuning technique and has achieved significant success (Zhou et al., 2022b;a;
042   Chen et al., 2023). Rather than fine-tuning all model parameters, prompt tuning focuses on modi-
043   fying the text prompts while keeping the model’s pre-trained parameters largely intact. Optimizing
044   the learnable prompts can enhance the alignment between textual and visual representations, thereby
045   improving the performance of vision-language models.
046
      It has been found that modern machine learning and data-driven models can easily rely on spurious
047
      correlations to make prediction (Geirhos et al., 2020; Ye et al., 2024). Referring to statistical asso-
048   ciations between variables, spurious correlations arise from statistical bias and confounding factors
049   rather than representing a true causal relationship. Consequently, spurious correlations are unstable
050   and can vary across different data distributions. Thus, the performance of models utilizing spurious
051   correlations can degrade dramatically on test data when a distribution shift occurs between the train-
052   ing/tuning data and test data, even though they demonstrate perfect performance on training/tuning
053   data. In other words, models that employ spurious correlations exhibit poor out-of-distribution
      (OOD) generalization performance. A further complication is that this issue is especially preva-


                                                         1
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


054
      lent in complex datasets where high-dimensional inputs, including image data and text data, may
055   contain hidden biases.
056
057   Although considerable efforts have been made to mitigate spurious correlations in both visual modal-
058
      ity (Arjovsky et al., 2019; Creager et al., 2021; Yang et al., 2023b; Qiu et al., 2024) and textual
      modality (Peyrard et al., 2022; Zhou et al., 2023), these methods are primarily designed for single-
059
      modal learning and are not applicable to multi-modal learning. In contrast to single-modal learning,
060
      the critical challenge of cross-modal mitigation of spurious correlations lies in how to organically
061   integrate mitigation in visual modality, mitigation in textual modality and cross-modal alignment
062   of representations.
063
064
      Among recent studies, the cross-modal contrastive learning framework presented in (Yang et al.,
      2023c) addresses the mitigation of spurious correlations in both textual and visual modalities while
065
      requiring access to text descriptions of spurious features/objects. In general scenarios, spurious fea-
066
      tures are typically latent and unobservable. Moreover, the method proposed in (Yang et al., 2023c),
067   which is designed for fine-tuning of VLMs and alters all model parameters, cannot be applied to
068   prompt tuning of VLMs. Besides, CoOPood (Zhang et al., 2024b) focuses on mitigating spurious
069   correlations in visual modality during prompt-tuning of VLMs. It overlooks the spurious corre-
070   lations in the textual modality. Furthermore, CoOPood relies on the assumption that the spurious
071   correlations between spurious features and the target label are approximately subject to uniform
072   probability distributions. Therefore, how to organically integrate mitigation in visual modality, miti-
073   gation in textual modality and cross-modal alignment of representations, without invoking unnatural
074   assumptions, remains an open problem.
075   Inspired by the causal intervention-based calculation of the probability of necessity and sufficiency
076   (PNS) between two variables (Tian & Pearl, 2000; Wang & Jordan, 2021; Yang et al., 2023b), we
077   introduce the concept logic alignment (i.e., alignment with necessity and sufficiency) to integrate
078   mitigation of spurious correlations and cross-modal alignment of representations organically for
079   prompt tuning of VLMs. The key insight is that logic equivalence (i.e., necessary and sufficient) not
080   only facilitates mitigation of spurious correlations (Wang & Jordan, 2021; Yang et al., 2023b), but
081
      also enhances dimensionality-agnostic alignment between two variables. In the context of vision-
      language models, the overall objective is to achieve the logic equivalence between visual causal rep-
082
      resentations (denoted by Φv ) and textual label (denoted by Y ), i.e., Y ⇔ Φv . Considering spurious
083
      correlations can exist in both visual and textual modalities, the equivalence Y ⇔ Φv alone cannot
084   guarantee that the aligned textual representations exclude spurious features. Therefore, establishing
085   a stricter equivalence chain Y ⇔ Φt ⇔ Φv (where Φt represents textual causal representations) is
086   our final objective. Specifically, our framework can be divided into two components: 1) Y ⇔ Φt
087   eliminates the spurious correlations in textual modality; 2) Φt ⇔ Φv integrates mitigation of spuri-
088   ous correlations in visual modality and cross-modal alignment of representations organically when
089   Y ⇔ Φt excludes spurious features in Φt . In practical implementation, the logic equivalence be-
090   tween two variables is achieved by maximizing the probability of necessity and sufficiency (PNS)
091   between them. The main contributions of this work are summarized as follows:
092
             • We introduce the concept logic alignment to address cross-modal mitigation of spurious
093
               correlations for prompt-tuning in vision-language models. Capable of integrating miti-
094
               gation of spurious correlations and cross-modal alignment of representations organically,
095            Logic alignment can serves as a promising technique for handling spurious correlations in
096            various multi-modal learning scenarios.
097
098
             • We design a practical framework to calculate the PNS between the textual label and textual
               representations, as well as the PNS between textual representations and visual representa-
099
               tions. By maximizing these two PNS terms, the proposed objective can effectively achieve
100
               cross-modal mitigation of spurious correlations for prompt-tuning in VLMs.
101
102          • The theoretical analysis proves that our method can yield a tighter generalization error
103            bound compared to existing approaches. Moreover, the detailed components of the derived
104
               generalization error bound verify the importance of maximizing the two proposed PNS
               terms from a theoretical perspective.
105
106          • The experimental results across diverse datasets demonstrate the superiority of the proposed
107            framework in out-of-distribution generalization performance, compared with the state-of-
               the-art competitors.

110   Causal Representation Learning Attaining causally invariant predictors over varied data distri-
111   butions is proposed in the field of causal inference Peters et al. (2016), and introduced into machine
112   learning to tackle the OOD generalization problem by IRM Arjovsky et al. (2019). Then, many ef-
113   forts are dedicated to facilitating the application of invariant representation learning to more general
114   scenarios. Some works focus on achieving invariant learning when the environment label is unavail-
115   able, e.g., EIIL Creager et al. (2021), HRM Liu et al. (2021a), KerHRM Liu et al. (2021b), ED-
116   NIL Huang et al. (2022) and ZIN Lin et al. (2022). IFM Chen et al. (2022b) lowers the requirement
117   on the number of available environments. Another branch Ahuja et al. (2021); Chen et al. (2022a);
118
      Huh & Baidya (2022) completes the constraints that IRM misses. Besides, iCaRL Lu et al. (2022)
      extends causal representation learning to non-linear causal representations while ACTIR Jiang &
119
      Veitch (2022) extends causal representation learning to anti-causal scenarios. Causal representation
120
      learning is also applied to graph representation learning Li et al. (2022); Chen et al. (2022c) and
121   natural language modeling Peyrard et al. (2022). These methods are devised for handling spurious
122   correlations in single-modal learning scenarios.
123
124   Prompt Tuning of Vision-Language Models The typical vision-language model, CLIP (Radford
125   et al., 2021) is trained using a contrastive learning framework where textual and visual representa-
126   tions are aligned by maximizing the cosine similarity between the image and text embeddings of cor-
127   rect pairs. To fully exploit the powerful adaptation capability, prompt tuning is proposed to improve
128   the performance of pre-trained vision-language models (e.g., CLIP) on downstream task (Zhou et al.,
129   2022b;a). Among these attempts, CoOp (Zhou et al., 2022b) designs learnable prompts to adjust the
130
      mapping from textual label to textual representations and greatly improves the performance of pre-
      trained CLIP on downstream visual tasks. Furthermore, CoCoOp (Zhou et al., 2022a) introduce a
131
      image-conditional context generator to improve the zero-shot generalization performance of CoOp.
132
      Subsequently, MaPLe (Khattak et al., 2023a) adopts both textual and visual learnable prompts to
133   enhance the alignment of textual and visual representations in downstream tasks. Another prevalent
134   line of works utilize fine-grained learnable textual prompt to tackle the imbalance between textual
135   and visual modalities (Chen et al., 2023; Shen et al., 2024; Li et al., 2024). All above prompt tun-
136   ing methods do not consider the mitigation of spurious correlations in vision-language models. In
137   particular, CoOPood (Zhang et al., 2024b) is proposed as a pioneering work focusing on mitigating
138   spurious correlations in visual modality during prompt-tuning of VLMs. However, it overlooks the
139   spurious correlations in the textual modality. Moreover, CoOPood relies on the assumption that the
140   spurious correlations between spurious features and the target label are approximately subject to
141
      uniform probability distributions, which limits the applicability of CoOPood to general scenarios.

332   that optimizing the PNS risk in equation 8 can guarantee Y ⊥⊥ Q | Φt and optimizing the PNS risk
333
      in equation 9 can enable Φt ⊥  ⊥ X | Φv . Therefore, the proposed method can render both ‘Textual
      error term’ and ‘Alignment error term’ approach 0. In other words, our method can guarantee a
334
      tighter generalization error bound compared with the state-of-the-art prompt-tuning schemes for
335
      vision-language models. Detailed proof of Theorem 4.2 is provided in Appendix B.

350
      is the minority group. In the training set, landbirds appeared more often on land backgrounds,
351   while waterbirds appeared more often on water backgrounds, so models fine-tuned on this dataset
352   tended to rely on backgrounds rather than birds to make prediction. However, in the testing set, both
353   landbirds and waterbirds have the same probability of appearing on a land background as on a water
354   background, which leads to a degradation of the model’s performance.

      Similar to Watebirds, CelebA is a hair color prediction dataset, which also has 4 groups: non-blond

      females (G1), non-blond males (G2), blond females (G3) and blond males (G4) with proportions

419
      ‘baby’ in ImageNet-1K dataset). 2) LogicAl-PT can mitigate the ‘sufficient but not necessary’

      more consistent results across diverse data distributions compared to its competitors.

533
534   This paper investigates the cross-modal mitigation of spurious correlations in prompt tuning of
535   vision-language models. We exploit causally motivated logic alignment (i.e., alignment with ne-
536   cessity and sufficiency) to integrate mitigation of spurious correlations and cross-modal alignment
537   of representations organically. Theoretical analysis is provided to prove that our method can yield a
538   tighter generalization error bound than existing approaches. Experimental results across diverse
539   datasets demonstrate the superiority of the proposed framework, termed LogicAl-PT, in out-of-
      distribution generalization performance, compared with the state-of-the-art competitors.

      In these examples, the task is a binary classification problem aimed at distinguishing ‘cat’ class from
731
      ‘fox’ class. The learned prompt together with the frozen text encoder works as a projector which
732   projects the two text labels onto a specific feature subspace. When the text labels are projected into
733   the ‘Background’ feature subspace (as shown in Figure 4(a)), the ‘background’ feature component in
734   visual representation space determines the prediction result because prediction is made using cosine
735   similarity between visual features and text features. In this way, a spurious correlation between
736   visual representation and text label is built by this learned prompt. Similarly, the learned prompt in
737   Figure 4(b) builds a SF\NC relation from Φv to Y , since ‘cat feet’ is a sufficient but not necessary
738   feature for predicting ‘cat’; the learned prompt in Figure 4(c) builds a NC\SF relation from Φv to
739   Y , since ‘pointy ear’ is a necessary but not sufficient feature for predicting ‘cat’; the learned prompt
740
      in Figure 4(d) builds a SF and NC relation (i.e., logic alignment) from Φv to Y , since ‘short mouth’
      is a sufficient and necessary feature for predicting ‘cat’.
741
742   As illustrated in Figure 4(a), 4(b) and 4(c), all these three relations (non-causal spurious correla-
743   tion, SF\NC causal relation, and NC\SF causal relation) are unstable when data distribution varies.
744   Therefore, apart from mitigation of cross-modal spurious correlations, cross-modal logic alignment
745   (i.e., sufficiency and necessary) is also essential for enhancing the out-of-distribution generalization
746
      performance in vision-language models. This is why we utilize PNS risk in the prompt tuning of
      VLMs to achieve better out-of-distribution generalization performance.
