009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            State adversarial perturbations –such as sensor noise, environmental interference,
012            or targeted attacks– are common in real-world systems, often leading to compro-
013            mised state observations. Despite this, Inverse Reinforcement Learning (IRL) in
014            the context of State-Adversarial Markov Decision Processes (SA-MDPs) has re-
015            ceived limited attention, primarily because conventional notions of optimality do
016            not apply. In this paper, we introduce a novel definition of optimality that ensures
017            the existence of an optimal policy within SA-MDPs. Building on this foundation,
018
               we propose the State-Adversarial Max-Margin IRL (SAMM-IRL) algorithm, de-
               signed to be robust against state perturbations. Our theoretical analysis, supported
019
               by empirical validation, demonstrates that SAMM-IRL significantly enhances IRL
020
               performance in adversarial environments, providing a robust framework for real-
021            world applications that demand resilience.

024   1   INTRODUCTION
025
026   Many real-world systems, such as autonomous vehicles and robotics, are vulnerable to disruptions in
027   their sensory data, whether caused by environmental factors such as noise or deliberate adversarial
028   attacks. These state adversarial perturbations distort the agent’s perception of the environment, re-
029   sulting in compromised decision-making. In such settings, standard learning approaches, including
030   Inverse Reinforcement Learning (IRL), struggle to maintain performance because they rely on the
031   assumption of accurate state observations. This challenge is particularly critical in safety-sensitive
032   applications, where even small errors in state estimation can lead to significant risks.
033   Traditional IRL methods have been successful in recovering reward functions and replicating ex-
034   pert behavior. While some state-of-the-art approaches can handle limited static noise, they are not
035   equipped to deal with dynamic adversarial perturbations that actively interfere with state obser-
036   vations. This makes these methods vulnerable when such assumptions are violated by adversarial
037   forces. Our work focuses on State-Adversarial Markov Decision Processes (SA-MDPs) as formal-
038   ized by Zhang et al. (2020), where adversaries perturb state observations. This setting presents a
039   challenge, as a policy that is optimal for one initial state may become suboptimal for another. To ad-
040
      dress this, we introduce Resilient State Optimality (RSO), which redefines optimality by maximizing
      the expected value across initial state distributions under adversarial conditions. This guarantees the
041
      existence of robust policies, even under worst-case adversarial perturbations, enabling more resilient
042
      policy recovery.
043
044   Building on this foundation, we propose State-Adversarial Max-Margin IRL (SAMM-IRL), a novel
045   algorithm that extends the Max-Margin IRL framework (Abbeel & Ng, 2004) to address the chal-
046
      lenge of robust IRL under adversarial environments. We provide theoretical performance guarantees
      for policies learned by SAMM-IRL, and our empirical results demonstrate effectiveness in adver-
047
      sarially perturbed environments.

051
052   IRL has been extensively studied as a method to infer reward functions from expert demonstrations,
053   allowing agents to learn in environments without explicit rewards. Ng & Russell (2000) laid the
      foundation for IRL by recovering reward functions from observed expert behavior. Max-Margin

      IRL (Abbeel & Ng, 2004) improved this by focusing on feature expectation matching, addressing
055
      under-determination in reward recovery. However, these methods assume stable environments with
056   accurate observations, making them vulnerable to adversarial attacks on state observations.
057
058   Several approaches have been proposed to address adversarial challenges in Reinforcement Learning
059
      (RL). Robust Adversarial Reinforcement Learning (RARL) (Pinto et al., 2017) modeled the inter-
      action between agent and adversary as a two-player Markov game, where the adversary can modify

061
      mal policy may not exist under dynamic adversarial perturbations, proposing methods to improve
062   robustness against a fixed adversary. Their follow-up work (Zhang et al., 2021) explored learned ad-

064   convergence guarantees.
065
      Recent IRL methods, such as Generative Adversarial Imitation Learning (GAIL) (Ho & Ermon,
066
      2016) and Adversarial Inverse Reinforcement Learning (AIRL) (Fu et al., 2018), use adversarial
067
      training frameworks inspired by Generative Adversarial Networks (GAN) to enhance policy imita-
068   tion and reward recovery. However, both GAIL and AIRL assume assume relatively accurate state
069   observations, leaving them vulnerable to adversarial manipulations that target the agent’s perception
070   of the environment.
071
      In contrast, our work leverage RSO as a framework that guarantees the existence of optimal policies
072
      in SA-MDPs, enabling the development of both RL and IRL methods that can withstand adversarial
073
      manipulation of state observations. Building on this, we propose SAMM-IRL, which adapts Max-
074   Margin IRL to the SA-MDP framework, providing theoretical guarantees for robust policy and re-
075   ward recovery under adversarial conditions. Empirical results demonstrate significant improvements
076   in adversarial environments, positioning SAMM-IRL as a robust alternative to existing methods.

521   6     CONCLUSION
522
523   We developed the SAMM-IRL algorithm, an adaptation of the Max-Margin IRL algorithm to solve
524   the inverse reinforcement learning problem in SA-MDPs. Our work introduces a new definition of
525   optimality in SA-MDPs, ensuring an optimal policy by considering the initial state distribution. The-
526   oretical and numerical analysis demonstrated its effectiveness under adversarial conditions, making
527   it well-suited for real-world applications.
528   Future work could improve scalability by integrating neural networks for automated feature extrac-
529   tion and extending the method to continuous state-action spaces. Additionally, adapting the algo-
530   rithm to multi-agent settings could broaden its applicability in dynamic, high-stakes domains.

984    B.2.1     S ARSA A LGORITHM AND ITS M ODIFICATION FOR SA-MDP S

       The Sarsa algorithm is an on-policy reinforcement learning method commonly used in MDPs. It
       updates the Q-value function based on observed transitions, making it sensitive to the specific state-
987
       action pairs encountered during training. The standard update rule for Sarsa is given by:
988
989                       Q(st , at ) ← Q(st , at ) + α (rt+1 + γQ(st+1 , at+1 ) − Q(st , at )) .                (65)
990

991
       sensitive to the exact trajectories experienced.
992
993    To handle the adversarial perturbations in SA-MDPs, we modify the Sarsa update rule to incorporate
994    an expected Q-value over the initial state distribution. This modification ensures that the agent’s
995
       policy remains robust across varied starting conditions. The modified update rule is expressed as:
996                     Q(st , at ) ← Q(st , at ) + α (rt+1 + γ · Es∼P0 [Q(s, a)] − Q(st , at )) .               (66)
997
998
       The modification improves policy robustness and ensures consistent performance under varying
       starting conditions.
