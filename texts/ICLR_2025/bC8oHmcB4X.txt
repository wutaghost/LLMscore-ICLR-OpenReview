009                                                A BSTRACT
010
011            Adversarial robustness has become a major concern as machine learning models
012            are increasingly deployed in security-sensitive applications. Evaluating adversar-
013            ial robustness remains a challenging task, as current metrics are heavily affected
014            by various factors, including attack methods, attack intensities, and model ar-
015            chitecture. In this paper, we propose Steady and Fair Robustness Evaluation, a
016            novel framework designed to mitigate the impact of these factors and provide a
017            more stable evaluation of a model’s robustness. Our key insight is based on the
018
               strong correlation between the standard deviation (SD) of Shapley values, which
               measures the importance of individual neurons, and adversarial robustness. We
019
               demonstrate that models with lower SD of Shapley values are more robust to ad-
020
               versarial attacks, regardless of the attack method or model architecture. Extensive
021            experiments across various models, training objectives, and attack scenarios show
022            that our approach offers more consistent and interpretable robustness evaluation.
023            We further introduce a new training strategy that incorporates the minimization of
024            the SD of Shapley values for improving the robustness of the model. Our findings
025            suggest that analysis based on Shapley value can provide a principled and efficient
026            alternative to conventional robustness evaluation techniques.

064
065   To address this challenge, we propose a Steady and Fair Robustness evaluation framework (SF Ro-
066   bustness) that does not rely on types of adversarial attacks or the architecture of the model. The
067   fundamental idea of SF robustness is that the model is adversarially vulnerable if it heavily relies
068   on a few predictive neurons for its decisions (i.e., the importance score of individual neurons is
069
      unevenly distributed such that only a small number of predictive neurons score high importance
      while the majority of neurons are regarded redundant), and the model is adversarially robust if it
070
      makes use of its internal neuron altogether (i.e., the importance score of internal neurons are evenly
071
      distributed). In this paper, we provide a theoretical demonstration of this relationship and experi-
072   mental results supporting this tendency across a wide range of networks on different architectures
073   on various datasets.
074
075
      Based on this, we assess the robustness of models by examining the importance scores of internal
      neurons. Specifically, the reliance of the model on a few predictive neurons is reflected in the distri-
076
      bution of the importance score of each neuron. In this work, we adopt the Shapley value (Shapley,
077
      1997; Kuhn & Tucker, 1953; Lundberg & Su-In, 2017; Sundararajan & Najmi, 2020) to measure the
078   importance of internal neurons. Shapely value is a concept from Game Theory, which evaluates each
079   property’s individual and combining effects (Shapley, 1997; Kuhn & Tucker, 1953; Lundberg & Su-
080   In, 2017; Sundararajan & Najmi, 2020). However, calculating the Shapley value in a neural network
081   by definition is almost impossible due to the computational complexity. SF Robustness leverages
082   Taylor approximation of the Shapley value introduced in Khakzar et al. (2021). By examining the
083   distribution of the Shapley value, we can interpret the model’s reliance on important neurons, which
084   gives an important hint for evaluating the adversarial robustness of a neural network.
085   Section 2.1 provides a theoretical foundation for the correlation between adversarial robustness and
086   Shapley value. In Section 2.2, we empirically demonstrate this correlation. Based on our analysis in
087   Section 2, we introduce SF robustness in Section 3. In Section 4, we present extensive experiments
088   to demonstrate a correlation between the SF Robustness and the performance of various defense
089   strategies. Additionally, Section 5 discusses the relation between SF robustness and the performance
090   of various models trained with data augmentation strategies under a one-step adversarial attack. In
091   summary, our key contributions are:
092
             • We establish a strong correlation between the standard deviation of Shapley values and
093
               adversarial robustness.
094
095
             • We demonstrate that the standard deviation of Shapley values can be used as a proxy for
               evaluating adversarial robustness without relying on complex attacks. This gives an ad-
096
               vantage for stable evaluation of adversarial robustness, which was varied by the attack
097
               selection.
098
             • We introduce a novel add-on defense strategy that optimizes the standard deviation of Shap-
099
               ley values, achieving competitive performance with baseline defense methods.

      6.1   N EURON - WISE A NALYSIS ON A DVERSARIAL E XAMPLES
440
441   Kim et al. (2021) divided features (neurons) into two groups: robust features and non-robust features.
442   They utilized an information bottleneck for the feature distillation. The noise is inserted in the
443   intermediate layer to control the information flow. The noise is optimized in a way that minimizes
444   the mutual information between the input and layer-inserted intermediate layer and maximizes the
445   information between the prediction and the ground truth. This is in line with our work as they
446   analyze the robustness of individual neurons. However, their approach is unsuitable for a model
447   assessment because the noise is optimized to output the ground truth label. Therefore, it is not
448
      appropriate to quantify the properties of the network itself.
449    Zhang et al. (2020a) defined neuron sensitivity as the activation difference on adversarial and clean
450   samples. They found that sensitive neurons (i.e., neurons that undergo significant changes on adver-
451   sarial attack) play important roles in causing misclassification. This work is in line with our work
452   in that their definition of sensitive neurons is associated with important neurons. However, neuron
453   sensitivity requires adversarial attacks, and the extent to which each attack affects neuron activation
454
      is different. On the other hand, SF robustness does not rely on the types of adversarial attacks, which
      makes the evaluation process simple and stable.
455
456
457   6.2   E VALUATION OF A DVERSARIAL ROBUSTNESS
458
      There are several adversarial robustness metrics other than adversarial robustness. Minimal Per-
459   turbation is the smallest perturbation added to and input that changes the model prediction which
460   is also utilized in Carlini & Wagner (2017). Probabilistic Accuracy (Robey et al., 2022) is calcu-
461   lated by the proportion of correctly classified adversarial examples where the predicted probability
462   is above a certain tolerance level. Robustness w.r.t. predictions (Ding et al., 2019) calculates accu-
463   racy on adversarial examples in which the perturbations are produced to perturb the model’s original
464   prediction on the clean examples instead of the true label. While they provide more fine-grained
465   measurement than adversarial accuracy, they still highly depend on adversarial attacks and do not
466   provide neuron-level inspection. Adversarial Sparsity (Olivier & Raj, 2023) shares a similar con-
467
      cept with local intrinsic dimensionality (Ma et al., 2018) where they characterize the dimensional
      properties of adversarial subspaces. Adversarial Sparsity considers the number of adversarial re-
468
      gions around the sample to quantify adversarial robustness. Their approach is based on the latent
469
      space, whereas our metric focuses on the internal responses of individual neurons.
470
471
472   7     C ONCLUSION
473
474   In this paper, we introduced the Steady and Fair Robustness Evaluation (SF Robustness) frame-
475   work, which addresses the challenges of inconsistencies in adversarial robustness evaluation caused
476
      by varying hyperparameters, model architectures, and attack methods. By leveraging the standard
      deviation of Shapley values as a key metric, we demonstrated a strong correlation between neu-
477
      ron importance variability and adversarial robustness. This finding allows for a more principled
478
      and interpretable evaluation of robustness, independent of attack specifics or model configurations.
479   Through extensive experimentation, we showed that SF Robustness provides a reliable indicator of
480   adversarial robustness, with models exhibiting lower SF Robustness demonstrating stronger resis-
481   tance to attacks. Furthermore, we proposed a novel attack and defense strategy that optimizes the SD
482   of Shapley values, outperforming the baseline defenses. Our results validate that SF Robustness can
483   serve as an effective tool for both evaluating and improving adversarial robustness. This work opens
484   the door for further exploration of interpretability-driven robustness strategies, ultimately contribut-
485   ing to more secure and reliable machine learning systems in adversarial settings.
