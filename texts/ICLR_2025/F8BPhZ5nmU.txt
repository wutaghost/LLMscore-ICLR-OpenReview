009                                              A BSTRACT
010
011            Despite remarkable advances in Large Language Models (LLMs), a persistent
012            challenge remains: the potential for these models to acquire erroneous or outdated
013            information from their training data. Direct fine-tuning with data containing new
014            knowledge can be ineffective due to conflicts between old and new knowledge.
015            This paper proposes a novel fine-tuning paradigm called Delicate Fine-Tuning
016            (DFT ) that leverages parametric arithmetic to pinpoint the location of knowledge
017
               and update only the minimal set of relevant parameters. Experimental results on
               two publicly available datasets demonstrate that our proposed DFT significantly
018
               improves the knowledge updating performance of full fine-tuning, consistently
019
               outperforming existing baselines in most cases.

022   1   I NTRODUCTION
023
024   Large Language Models (LLMs) exhibit remarkable proficiency in understanding and generating
025   natural language Brown et al. (2020); Raffel et al. (2020); Ouyang et al. (2022). Despite their
026   impressive learning capabilities, LLMs are susceptible to acquiring inaccurate knowledge from their
027   training corpora. Furthermore, the dynamic nature of real-world knowledge necessitates continuous
028   updates, as information that was once accurate may become outdated or invalid over time.
029   For instance, in 2020, the query ”Who is the President of the United States?” would have yielded
030   ”Donald Trump” as the answer. However, the current answer is ”Joe Biden.” This exemplifies the
031   ongoing challenge faced by LLMs: the need for continuous updating to ensure they reflect accurate
032   and up-to-date knowledge.
033
      Current approaches to model editing and knowledge updating typically involve augmenting the net-
034
      work architecture (Dong et al., 2022; Huang et al., 2022; Raunak & Menezes, 2022), introducing
035   additional model parameters (Dai et al., 2023; Dong et al., 2022; Huang et al., 2022), or integrating
036   external knowledge bases (Dai et al., 2023; Dong et al., 2022; Huang et al., 2022). These methods
037   often necessitate more complex procedures than straightforward fine-tuning with new knowledge
038   (Zhang et al., 2022; Li & Liang, 2021; Hu et al., 2021).
039
      At present, direct fine-tuning of the model remains the predominant method for incorporating new
040
      knowledge.
041
042   During human cognitive development, individuals often encounter situations where new knowledge
043   conflicts with their existing understanding.
044   They usually remember both the new knowledge and the old knowledge simultaneously, and then
045   often get confused, leading to contradictions that make it difficult to learn the new knowledge. If
046   we directly modify the memory of old knowledge and original cognition, then the new knowledge
047   to be learned will not conflict with the original cognition and knowledge, which makes it better to
048   learn and absorb the new knowledge. For example, if people have been educated to believe that ”the
049   Earth is flat” since childhood, it would be challenging for them to accept the conflicting knowledge
050
      that ”the Earth is round” when they become adults. Conversely, if they could directly modify their
      memory of the erroneous knowledge ”the Earth is flat” to the correct knowledge ”the Earth is round,”
051
      it would be much simpler.
052
053   So how do we locate the position of old knowledge and then update it accurately? Our research has
      shown that when fine-tuning large language models, they tend to learn sentence structure, grammar,


                                                       1
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


054
      and style first, with knowledge being acquired last. Therefore, we control the variables to prevent
055   the model from learning sentence structure and stylistic information.
056
057   Inspired by the above empirical observations and (Ilharco et al., 2022)’s task arithmetic, we propose
058
      a novel paradigm of knowledge updating called DFT (Delicate Fine-Tuning ). Specifically, DFT
      begins by using the large language model to predict and generate an answer, resulting in a data point.
059
      Next, DFT modifies only the key knowledge within the sentence, keeping the sentence structure and
060
      style intact, creating a new data point. We then fine-tune the model separately with both data points,
061   recording the parameter changes. By comparing these parameter changes, we identify sections that
062   exhibit similar changes in direction. These sections, representing aspects that are not relevant to the
063   knowledge update, are discarded entirely.
064
      We retain only the parameters exhibiting contrasting change directions, then compare their differ-
065
      ences, rank those differences, and identify the top T % with the largest differences .Then update
066
      the top T % of parameters , where T is a predefined threshold ratio. The whole process is repeated
067   iteratively until the model’s knowledge update is complete.
068
069   This paper makes the following contributions:
070
071
             • We propose a novel fine-tuning paradigm “DFT (Delicate Fine-Tuning )” for knowledge
               updating in large language models.
072
073          • Our experimental results show that DFT (Delicate Fine-Tuning ) improves the knowledge
074            updating performance across various fine-tuning methods and surpasses existing baselines
075
               in most cases.

077   2   R ELATED W ORK
078
079   Currently, the method of knowledge updating and model editing (also known as knowledge editing)
080   for LLMs is mainly divided into two classes Yao et al. (2023); Wang et al. (2023b):
081
082   a. The method preserving model’s parameters
083
084   Adding Additional Parameters.
085   This approach involves injecting a small number of trainable parameters, representing new knowl-
086   edge, into the LLM while keeping its original parameters frozen . This technique, explored by
087   Dong et al. (2022); Huang et al. (2022); Raunak & Menezes (2022); Dai et al. (2023), allows for
088   efficient knowledge injection without retraining the entire model. Dong et al. (2022) proposed a
089   lightweight feed-forward network that incorporates additional parameters specifically tailored to
090   factual contexts, enabling knowledge generalization.Huang et al. (2022) developed a model edi-
091
      tor named Transformer-Patcher, which sequentially corrects errors in LLM outputs by adding and
      training a limited number of neurons within the transformer architecture.
092
093   Retrieve augmentation.
094
      These methods rely on an external knowledge base containing new or corrected information, aiming
095   to amend the output of LLMs by incorporating retrieved knowledge relevant to the given prompt or
096   question. This approach, explored by Murty et al. (2022); Mitchell et al. (2022); Li et al. (2022);
097   Madaan et al. (2022), facilitates the integration of new knowledge into the model’s responses.
098
      Mitchell et al. (2022) propose a memory module that stores manual edits, enabling a classifier to re-
099
      trieve and apply the relevant knowledge.Madaan et al. (2022) leverage the memory of user feedback
100
      to generate prompts that guide LLMs toward more accurate responses. Alternatively, Zheng et al.
101   (2023) utilize in-context learning to revise LLM outputs by extracting demonstrations from a corpus
102   based on similarity, eliminating the need for gradient calculations.
103
104   b. The method modifying model’s parameters
105
106   Fine-tuning has become a ubiquitous technique in NLP research, owing to the widespread adoption
107   of pre-trained models for downstream tasks. Its intuitive nature and effectiveness in imparting new
      knowledge make it a valuable tool for model editing Zhu et al. (2020); Zhang et al. (2022); Yao et al.

108
      (2023). Recent advancements in parameter-efficient fine-tuning methods, such as Prefix-Tuning Li
109   & Liang (2021) and LoRA (Hu et al. (2021)), have further enhanced its applicability to knowledge
110   editing. Zhang et al. (2022) proposed an adaptive fine-tuning strategy that dynamically adjusts the
111   magnitude of parameter updates based on the importance of the weight matrix, thereby improving
112   efficiency and adaptability. Zhu et al. (2020) introduced a loss constraint that minimizes the impact
113   on irrelevant knowledge during fine-tuning, preserving the integrity of the base model. Similarly,
114   Lee et al. (2022) explored large-scale continual learning for knowledge updating through regularized
115   fine-tuning.
116   Meta-learning approaches aim to update knowledge within LLMs by adjusting their parameters

118   et al. (2019); Mitchell et al. (2021); De Cao et al. (2021), enables efficient knowledge updates
119   without retraining the entire model. Mitchell et al. (2021) introduced an auxiliary network with
120   gradient decomposition, enabling efficient edits to LLMs based on a single input-output pair.De Cao
121   et al. (2021) proposed updating specific weights within a subset of modules using a hypernetwork
122   with constrained optimization.
123   Locate and edit targets the internal mechanisms of LLMs, aiming to modify specific parameters
124   and neurons to correct outputs based on knowledge-driven interventions Meng et al. (2022a); Dai
125   et al. (2022); Meng et al. (2022b); Santurkar et al. (2021); Geva et al. (2022). Geva et al. (2021)
126   discovered that the feed-forward network layers within transformers store key-value pairs associated
127   with specific knowledge. Meng et al. (2022a) employed a causal reasoning method to identify key
128   neuron activations and update factual associations by modifying feed-forward weights. To facilitate
129
      large-scale knowledge editing, they introduced Meng et al. (2022b), a method that directly updates
      thousands of memories within LLMs. Gupta et al. (2023) enhanced knowledge updating by opti-
130
      mizing edit token selection and layer selection during the editing process. Yu et al. (2023) utilized
131
      partitioned gradients to identify significant weights for unlearning biases in the model.
132
133   Hiyouga hiyouga (2023) developed the fastedit software framework, which enables convenient edit-
134   ing of models using causal reasoning. Zhang et al. Zhang et al. (2024); Wang et al. (2023a); Yao
135
      et al. (2023); Cheng et al. (2023); Mao et al. (2023); Zhang et al. (2023) developed the EasyEdit
      software framework, which makes it easy to use a variety of methods for editing models.
136
137   While numerous methods have been proposed for knowledge updating in LLMs, many necessi-
138   tate the introduction of additional knowledge bases, neural network modules, or model parameters.
139   This often leads to practical challenges, including increased model complexity and inference costs.
140   Therefore, this paper focuses on enhancing and refining fine-tuning methods as a more efficient and
141
      practical approach to knowledge updating in LLMs.

143   3    TASK D EFINITION
144
145   This paper addresses the task of knowledge updating in large language models (LLMs). Given a pre-
146   trained model fθ and a set of input-output knowledge pairs Kold = (x1 , y1 ), (x2 , y2 ), ..., (xi , yi ),
147   the objective is to modify the model parameters θ to obtain a new model fθ∗ that generates a corre-
148   sponding set of updated input-output pairs Knew = (x1 , y1new ), (x2 , y2new ), ..., (xi , yinew ). Here, i
149   represents the number of knowledge pairs to be updated.
150   Following the definition in (Yao et al., 2023), we can formally express this process and its objective
151   as:

      The knowledge update task aims to modify the model’s responses only for xi and its equivalent do-
      main N (xi ), where N (xi ) represents the neighborhood of xi encompassing semantically equivalent
159
      instances. The goal is to update the answers associated with xi and its equivalent domain without
160

161
      The effectiveness of knowledge updating is evaluated based on the following three metrics:

162
      a. Reliability
163
164   Measured as the average accuracy of the updated model fθ∗ on the new knowledge. This metric
165   assesses the effectiveness of the update process itself. For example, the answer to the question
166   ”Who is the President of the US?” should be updated from ”Donald Trump” to ”Joe Biden” after
167   knowledge updating.
168
169   b. Generalization
170
      Evaluated by the average accuracy of fθ∗ on examples drawn uniformly from the equivalence neigh-
171
      borhood N (xi ). This metric assesses the ability of the model to generalize the update to semantically
172   equivalent inputs. For example, the answer to the question ”Who holds the position of the President
173   of the US?” should also be updated from ”Donald Trump” to ”Joe Biden”.
174
175   c. Locality
176
177   Assessed by the proportion of predictions from the updated model fθ∗ that remain unchanged com-
178   pared to the pre-update model fθ on irrelevant examples. This metric evaluates the ability of the
179   model to preserve the original knowledge base while updating specific knowledge. For example,
180
      the answer to the question ”’You’re fired!’ is the catchphrase of which celebrity?” should remain
      unchanged as ”Donald Trump” after the update.

      This paper introduces a novel paradigm for knowledge updating during supervised fine-tuning,
409   termed DFT (Differential Fine-Tuning). DFT leverages parametric arithmetic to pinpoint the lo-
410   cation of existing knowledge and facilitates the acquisition of new knowledge, effectively resolving
411   potential contradictions between old and new information.
412
      Experimental evaluations conducted on the zsRE and CounterFact datasets demonstrate the superior
413
      performance of our proposed method compared to other baselines in most scenarios.

417
418   While the proposed DFT paradigm enhances the efficacy of fine-tuning methods for updating knowl-
419   edge in large language models, it incurs an increase in computational overhead due to the incorpo-
420   ration of multiple backward passes.
