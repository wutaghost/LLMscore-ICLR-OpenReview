011                                              A BSTRACT
012
013            Adaptive Moment Estimation (Adam) is a cornerstone optimization algorithm in
014            deep learning, widely recognized for its flexibility with adaptive learning rates
015            and efficiency in handling large-scale data. However, despite its practical suc-
016            cess, the theoretical understanding of AdamвҖҷs convergence has been constrained
017
               by stringent assumptions, such as almost surely bounded stochastic gradients or
               uniformly bounded gradients, which are more restrictive than those typically re-
018
               quired for analyzing stochastic gradient descent (SGD).
019
020
               In this paper, we introduce a novel and comprehensive framework for analyz-
               ing the convergence properties of Adam. This framework offers a versatile ap-
021
               proach to establishing AdamвҖҷs convergence. Specifically, we prove that Adam
022
               achieves asymptotic (last iterate sense) convergence in both the almost sure sense
023            and the L1 sense under the relaxed assumptions typically used for SGD, namely
024            L-smoothness and the ABC inequality. Meanwhile, under the same assumptions,
025            we show that Adam attains non-asymptotic sample complexity bounds similar to
026            those of SGD.

029   1   I NTRODUCTION
030
031   Adaptive Moment Estimation (Adam) is one of the most widely used optimization algorithms in
032   deep learning due to its adaptive learning rate properties and efficiency in handling large-scale data
033   (Kingma & Ba, 2014). Despite its widespread use, the theoretical understanding of AdamвҖҷs con-
034   vergence is not as advanced as its practical success. Previous studies have often imposed stringent
035   assumptions on the loss function and stochastic gradients, such as uniformly bounded loss func-
036   tions and almost surely bounded gradients (Reddi et al., 2018b; Zou & Shen, 2019), which are more
037   restrictive than those required for analyzing classical stochastic gradient descent (SGD).
038   In this paper, we introduce a novel and comprehensive framework for analyzing the convergence
039   properties of Adam. Our framework unifies various aspects of convergence analysis, including non-
040   asymptotic (average iterate sense) sample complexity, asymptotic (last iterate sense) almost sure
041   convergence, and asymptotic L1 convergence. Crucially, we demonstrate that under this framework,
042   Adam can achieve convergence under the same assumptions typically used for SGDвҖ”namely, the
043   L-smooth condition and the ABC inequality (L2 sense) (Khaled & RichtГЎrik, 2023; Bottou, 2010;
044
      Ghadimi & Lan, 2013).
045   Several recent works have attempted to relax the stringent conditions required for AdamвҖҷs conver-
046   gence, each focusing on different aspects of the stochastic gradient assumptions and convergence
047   guarantees. However, limitations still exist in terms of assumptions and the types of convergence
048   results obtained. Table 1 provides the references and a summary of the works and compares the
049   assumptions on stochastic gradients, the resulting complexities, and the convergence properties
050
      achieved.

052   eral framework for analysis. In contrast to these previous works, we study Adam under the ABC
053   inequality, which is more general and less restrictive compared to the assumptions made in the previ-
      ous studies. Our analysis successfully establishes non-asymptotic sample complexity and achieves

081   1.1     R ELATED W ORKS
082
083
      In recent years, the convergence properties of Adam have been extensively studied, with various
      works focusing on different assumptions about stochastic gradients and the types of convergence
084
      guarantees provided. In the following discussion, we categorize and review key contributions based
085
      on the different types of stochastic gradient assumptions they employ, as summarized in Table 1.
086
087
      Bounded Variance and Coordinate Weak Growth: Wang et al. (2024a) considered AdamвҖҷs con-
088   vergence under the assumption of bounded variance or coordinate weak growth. The coordinate
089   weak growth condition (Eq. 2) is particularly stringent as it requires that each component of the
090   stochastic gradient satisfies a weak growth inequality, which is stronger than the traditional weak
091   growth condition (Eq. 1) applied to the entire gradient. Under these assumptions, Wang et al. were
092   able to avoid the O(1/Вө) complexity. However, their work did not focus on analyzing almost sure
093   convergence or L1 convergence, as the primary emphasis was on the sample complexity of the al-
094   gorithmвҖҷs behavior.
095
096   Almost Surely Bounded Stochastic Gradients: Several works, including He et al. (2023) and
097
      Xiao et al. (2024), have explored AdamвҖҷs convergence under the assumption that the stochastic gra-
      dients are almost surely bounded. This is a particularly strong assumption, as it implies several other
098
      commonly made assumptions about stochastic gradients, such as bounded variance, weak growth,
099
      coordinate weak growth, and sub-Gaussian properties. The assumption is often impractical in non-
100   convex settings where gradients can become unbounded. Moreover, studies in Wang et al. (2023)
101   have highlighted that this assumption is unrealistic in many common machine learning frameworks,
102   failing to hold even for simple quadratic functions, let alone for deep neural networks. While these
103   works achieved almost sure convergence and, in some cases, L1 convergence, they did not address
104   the complexity related to the O(1/Вө) term.
105
106   L2 Bounded Stochastic Gradients: Zou et al. (2019) analyzed Adam under the assumption of
107   L2 bounded stochastic gradients. Although this condition is milder than the almost surely bounded
      gradients assumption, it is still stronger than the traditional weak growth condition and the ABC

      inequality. In the standard analytical framework, this assumption can at best be weakened to the
109   coordinate weak growth condition, which remains more restrictive than the assumptions typically
110   considered for SGD. At the same time, this work focused on complexity analysis without addressing

112
113
      Randomly Reshuffled Stochastic Gradients: In other works, such as those by Zhang et al. (2022)

521   Since we have already proved almost sure convergence in Theorem 3.2, it is natural to attempt to

527   assume that the gradients are uniformly bounded, which means we cannot directly apply the

534   We have introduced a novel and comprehensive framework for analyzing the convergence properties
535   of Adam. Our frame starts with weak assumptions such as the ABC inequality. By identifying the
536   key properties of the learning rate, handling the momentum term, and establishing the approximate
537   descent inequality, the frame concludes the sample complexity, almost surely convergence, and
538   asymptotic L1 convergence results of Adam. Our techniques overcome the limitations of existing
539   analyses, and show that AdamвҖҷs convergence guarantees can be aligned with those of SGD, which
      justifies the applicability of Adam across a wide range of machine learning problems.
