                                                   A BSTRACT
010
011
               In neural compression, vector quantization (VQ) is usually replaced by a differ-
012
               entiable approximation during training for gradient backpropagation. However,
013
               prior approximation methods face two main issues: 1) the train-test mismatch be-
014            tween differentiable approximation and actual quantization, and 2) the suboptimal
015            encoder gradients for rate-distortion (RD) optimization. In this paper, we first
016            provide new finds about how approximation methods influence the RD optimiza-
017            tion in neural compression, and then propose a new solution based on these finds.
018            Specifically, if a neural compressor is regarded as a source-space VQ, we find that
019            the encoder implicitly determines the quantization boundaries, and the decoder
020            determines the quantization centers. Suboptimal approximation methods lead to
021            suboptimal gradients for RD optimization of quantization boundaries and centers.
022
               Therefore, to address the first issue, we propose an encode-decoder alternating op-
               timization strategy. The encoder is optimized with differentiable approximation,
023
               and the decoder is optimized with actual quantization to avoid the train-test mis-
024
               match of quantization centers. To address the second issue, we propose a sphere-
025            noise based stochastic approximation method. During encoder optimization, VQ
026            is replaced with a uniform sphere noise centered at the input vector. When the in-
027            put vector is located at the quantization boundary, the encoder gradient is closer to
028            the difference in RD loss between adjacent quantization centers, facilitating bet-
029            ter encoder optimization. We name the combination of optimization strategy and
030            approximation method as Alternating Optimized Stochastic Vector Quantization.
031            Experimental results on various vector sources and natural images demonstrate
032            the effectiveness of our method.

035   1   I NTRODUCTION
036
037   Quantization is a classical lossy compression technique. In theory, vector quantization (VQ) Gersho
038   & Gray (1992) can achieve optimal rate-distortion (RD) performance in source coding. However,
039
      the exponentially increasing complexity of VQ and its non-differentiable nature limit its practical
      use in neural compression Ballé et al. (2017); Ballé et al. (2020); Lu et al. (2019); Li et al. (2021),
040
      particularly for high-dimensional data. The complexity issue can be addressed by simplifying VQ to
041
      scalar quantization Ballé et al. (2020), multistage VQ Feng et al. (2023); Zhu et al. (2022) or lattice
042   VQ Zhang & Wu (2023). In this paper, we focus on tackling the non-differentiability issue of VQ
043   for end-to-end RD optimization.
044
045
      In neural compression, quantization is performed in the latent space of an autoencoder. Since quan-
      tization is non-differentiable, optimizing the learnable encoder transform presents a significant chal-
046
      lenge. A typical solution is to introduce a differentiable approximation of quantization during train-
047
      ing, such as additive uniform noise Ballé et al. (2017) and straight-through estimator (STE) Bengio
048   et al. (2013). However, prior works mainly focus on a special case of VQ, i.e., uniform scalar quanti-
049   zation. For general vector quantization, the optimization problem remains unresolved and primarily
050   involves two issues. The first issue is train-test mismatch. Differentiable approximations Agusts-
051   son et al. (2017a); Zhu et al. (2022) often differ from actual quantization, resulting in mismatch in
052   the decoder’s reconstruction between training and testing. The second issue is the suboptimality
053   of encoder gradients. Although previous approximation methods are differentiable, the gradients
      backpropagated to the encoder remain suboptimal under the RD criterion.

054
      In this paper, we aim to design an VQ approximation method for end-to-end RD optimization in
055   neural compression. Since the gradients for the encoder and decoder vary depending on the approx-
056   imation method, the first step is to understand how they influence RD performance. By interpreting
057   a neural compressor as a source-space vector quantizer, we show that the encoder function implic-
058   itly determines the quantization boundaries, and the decoder function determines the quantization
059   centers. Suboptimal boundaries and centers directly lead to suboptimal RD performance in lossy
060   compression. Thus, the encoder gradient at the boundaries and the decoder’s gradient at the cen-
061   ters are key factors influencing compression performance. In theory, entropy-constrained vector
062   quantization (ECVQ) Chou et al. (1989) has the optimal quantization boundaries and centers.
063   To address the train-test mismatch issue, we propose an encode-decoder alternating optimization
064   strategy. When optimizing the quantization centers, the encoder is fixed, and the decoder and code-
065   book are optimized using actual quantization. When optimizing the quantization boundaries, the
066   decoder and codebook are fixed, and the encoder is optimized using the approximation method.
067   These two steps alternate during training, ensuring consistent decoder reconstruction while allowing
068   gradients to be backpropagated to the encoder.
069   To address the issue of suboptimal encoder gradients, we first provide gradient analysis and ar-
070   gue that prior approximation methods are suboptimal for RD performance due to two reasons: 1)
071   discontinuous encoder gradients result in non-smooth quantization boundaries, and 2) the encoder
072   gradients at boundaries should align with the RD loss differences when quantizing to nearby centers.
073   In theoretically optimal ECVQ, the RD loss for an input vector at the quantization boundary is equal
074   when quantized to the two neighboring centers. Therefore, if the encoder gradient at the boundary
075
      closely approximates the loss difference between neighboring centers, it will help the encoder learn
      better quantization boundaries. Based on this analysis, we propose a sphere-noise based stochas-
076
      tic approximation method. This quantization approximation follows a uniform sphere distribution
077
      centered at the input vector, with the radius of the hypersphere equal to the distance between the
078   input vector and the nearest quantization center. We further demonstrate that the encoder gradient is
079   equivalent to the integral of the RD function over the surface of the high-dimensional sphere. When
080   the input vector lies at the quantization boundary, the gradient is closer to the difference in RD loss
081   between adjacent quantization centers, leading to more effective encoder optimization.
082
      By combining the proposed alternating optimization strategy and shere-noise based stochas- tic
083
      approximation, we propose a new method named Alternating Optimized Stochastic Vector Quan-
084   tization for end-to-end RD optimization. We provide comprehensive experiments and analysis on
085   various vector sources. Experimental results on neural image compression further demonstrate the
086   effectiveness of the proposed method.

      2   R ELATED W ORK
090
      Most existing works in neural compression follows the structure of nonlinear transform coding Ballé
091
      et al. (2020), with a pair of learnable transform, an entropy model and a vector quantizer in latent
092
      space. As shown in Figure 1, the encoder transform ga maps the input vector x into latent vector y,
093   which is then quantized by a quantizer ŷ = Qy (y) = Qdy (Qey (y)). Qey is the quantization encoder
094
      that maps y to discrete index i, and the quantization decoder Qdy maps i to quantized vector ŷ. The
095
      entropy model pi is used to model the distribution of index i for entropy coding. The optimization
096   target is to minimize the RD loss L = R + λD, where R = Ex [− log pi (i)] = Ex [− log pŷ (ŷ)] is
097   rate and D = Ex d(x, gs (ŷ)) is distortion. λ is a coefficient controlling the RD trade-off and d is a
098   distortion metric.
099
      The vector quantization Q is usually simplified to uniform quantization, e.g.,, rounding to the near-
100
      est integer, where ŷ = ⌊y⌉ = i. Most previous approximation methods are designed for uniform
101
      quantization. propose to add uniform noise on y during training. use straight-though estimator
102   (STE) that copies gradient from ŷ to y to enable the training of encoder. Both and propose stochas-
103   tic rounding that randomly quantizes y into two nearest integers, where anneals stochastic rounding
104   to rounding during training. Agustsson & Theis (2020) propose a soft quantizer that smoothly in-
105   terpolate between uniform noise and rounding. propose to optimize encoder with additive uniform
106   noise and optimize decoder with rounding to reduce train-test mismatch. propose a two-stage
107   strategy which first uses uniform noise for pre-training and then uses rounding for decoder finetun-
      ing. Among these methods, we can observe that additive uniform noise perform well for encoder

309
310   As shown in Figure 5, to address the train-test mismatch issues, this paper proposes an alternating
311
      optimization strategy for the encoder and decoder. When optimizing the quantization centers, the

312

313
      tization boundaries, the decoder and codebook are fixed, and the quantization approximation ỹ is
314   used to generate the reconstruction x̃, with the RD loss used to optimize the encoder. These two
315   steps alternate during training. The entropy model is optimized during the first step.

531
      In this paper, we propose a method named Alternating Optimized Stochastic Vector Quantization to
      address the RD optimization issue in vector quantization based neural compression. We propose an
532
      encode-decoder alternating optimization strategy. The encoder is optimized with differentiable ap-
533
      proximation, and the decoder is optimized with actual quantization to avoid the train-test mismatch
534   of quantization centers. For better encoder optimization, we propose a sphere-noise based stochastic
535   approximation method. During encoder optimization, VQ is replaced with a uniform sphere noise
536   centered at the input vector. When the input vector is located at the quantization boundary, the
537   encoder gradient is closer to the difference in RD loss between adjacent quantization centers, facil-
538   itating better encoder optimization. We provide a thorough analysis using toy vector sources and
539   demonstrate through extensive experiments on neural image compression that our proposed method
      achieves a significant performance gain.
