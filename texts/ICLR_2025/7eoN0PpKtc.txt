009                                                A BSTRACT
010
011            We present FreeMorph, the first tuning-free method for image morphing that
012            accommodates inputs with varying semantics or layouts. Unlike existing methods,
013            which rely on fine-tuning pre-trained diffusion models and are limited by time con-
014            straints and semantic/layout discrepancies, FreeMorph delivers high-fidelity image
015            morphing without extensive training. Despite its efficiency and potential, tuning-
016
               free methods still face challenges in maintaining high-quality image morphing due
               to the non-linear nature of the multi-step denoising process and bias inherited from
017
               the pre-trained diffusion model. In this paper, we introduce FreeMorph to address
018
               this challenge by integrating two key innovations. 1) We first propose a guidance-
019            aware spherical interpolation design that incorporates the explicit guidance from
020            the input images by modifying the self-attention modules, addressing identity loss,
021            and ensuring consistent transitions throughout the generated sequences. 2) We
022            further introduce a step-oriented motion flow that blends self-attention modules
023            derived from each input image to achieve controlled and directional transitions that
024            respect both input images. Our extensive evaluations demonstrate that FreeMorph
025            outperforms existing methods with training that is 10× ∼ 50× faster, establishing
026            a new state-of-the-art for image morphing. The code will be released.

      Given two distinct input images, image morphing (Zope & Zope, 2017; Nri, 2022) aims to grad-
031   ually change the attributes such as shape, texture, and overall image layout to produce a series of
032   intermediate images that transit smoothly from one image to the other. This process is now widely
033   utilized in fields such as animation, film transitions, and photo-editing tools (Aloraibi, 2023; Wolberg,
034   1996; 1998), offering an effective means to enhance creative expression and imagination. Historically,
035   image morphing has relied on image warping (Smythe, 1990; Wolberg, 1990; Fant, 1986) for aligning
036   corresponding points and color interpolation (Beier & Neely, 1992; Lee et al., 1998) for blending.
037   These methods, however, often fall short in handling complex textural and semantic transitions,
038   making them less effective for images with intricate details. With the advancements in deep learning,
039
      GANs (Goodfellow et al., 2014; Karras et al., 2019; Brock et al., 2019; Sauer et al., 2023) and
      VAEs (Kingma & Welling, 2013) have significantly improved image morphing by allowing latent
040
      code interpolations. Despite their capabilities, these approaches still face challenges with real-world
041
      images due to limited training data and issues with information loss during GAN inversion. This
042   underscores the need for more identity-preserved and generalized methods in image morphing.
043
044   Recently, thanks to the collections of large datasets with extensive text-image pairs, vision-language
045
      models (e.g., Chameleon (Team, 2024)), diffusion models (e.g., Stable Diffusion (Stability.AI,
      2022; Saharia et al., 2022; Rombach et al., 2022)), and transformers (e.g., PixArt-α (Chen et al.,
046
      2023), FLUX (Black, 2024)) have demonstrated impressive capabilities in generating high-quality
047
      images from text prompts. These advancements have paved the way for the development of new
048   generative image morphing techniques. Specifically, Wang & Golland (2023) leverages the local
049   linearity of CLIP-based text embeddings to create smooth transitions between input images by
050   interpolating the latent image features; Building upon this idea, IMPUS (Yang et al., 2023) introduces
051   a multi-phase training framework that includes the optimization of text embeddings and training of
052   Low-rank Adaptation (LoRA) modules to capture the semantics better. While this method yields
053   more visually appealing results, it requires extensive training, typically around 30 minutes for each
      case. DiffMorpher (Zhang et al., 2024) proposes to directly interpolate the latent noise and leverage

081   Given these issues, our objective is to accomplish image morphing without requiring further tuning.
082   Nonetheless, this goal introduces two key challenges: 1) Inconsistent transition and identity loss.
083   While using a pre-trained diffusion model to convert input images into latent features and applying
084   spherical interpolation might seem straightforward, this approach often results in inconsistent transi-
085   tions. This is due to the non-linear characteristics of the multi-step denoising process. Additionally,
086   this method inherits biases from the pre-trained model, which can lead to a loss of identity in the
087
      generated images. 2) Limitations in directional transitions. The diffusion model does not inherently
      provide an effective "motion flow" to capture the gradual changes between images. As a result,
088
      without additional adjustments, achieving smooth and gradual transitions in a tuning-free manner
089
      remains a significant challenge.
090
091   In this paper, we present FreeMorph, a novel tuning-free method that is capable of generating
092   directional and realistic transitions between two images instantly. Our method comprises two novel
093
      components: 1) Guidance-aware spherical interpolation: We first enhance the pre-trained diffusion
      model by incorporating explicit guidance from the input images through modifications to the self-
094
      attention modules. This is achieved through spherical interpolation, which produces intermediate
095
      features used in two key ways. Firstly, we perform spherical feature aggregation to blend the key and
096   value features from the self-attention modules, ensuring consistent transitions across the generated
097   image sequences. Additionally, to address the issue of identity loss, we introduce an prior-driven
098   self-attention mechanism which incorporates explicit guidance from the input images to preserve their
099   unique identities. 2) Step-oriented motion flow: To address the limitation in directional transitions,
100   we introduce a novel step-oriented motion flow. This method blends two self-attention modules
101   respectively derived based on each input image, enabling a controlled and directional transition that
102   respects both input images. To further improve the quality of the generated image sequences, we
103   have crafted an improved reverse diffusion and forward denoising process, seamlessly integrating
104
      these innovative components into the original DDIM framework. As shown in Fig. 1 and Fig. 4, our
      approach adeptly handles diverse input types, whether they share similar or distinct semantics/layouts,
105
      producing smooth and realistic transitions between images.

      2   R ELATED W ORK
116
      Text-to-Image Generation. Recently, diffusion models (Rombach et al., 2022; Podell et al., 2023;
117
      Saharia et al., 2022; Ramesh et al., 2022) have emerged as the de facto method for text-to-image
118
      generation tasks. These models employ a series of denoising steps (DDIM, DDPM) (Ho et al., 2020;
119   Song et al., 2021) to transform Gaussian noise into images, effectively capturing and interpreting the
120   details conveyed by textual prompts. Moreover, trained on billions of text-image pairs(Schuhmann
121   et al., 2022), these methods exhibit a remarkable ability to understand the distribution of real-world
122   images, generating high-quality and diverse outputs while maintaining generalization. Our work
123   harnesses the powerful capabilities of diffusion models, particularly their ability to generate smooth
124   transition sequences between two specified images (Samuel et al., 2024; Khrulkov et al., 2023; He
125   et al., 2024) to address the image morphing task.
126
127   Image Morphing. Image morphing is a long-standing computer vision and graphics problem.
128   Before the deep learning era, techniques such as mesh warping (Smythe, 1990; Wolberg, 1990;
129
      Fant, 1986) and field morphing (Beier & Neely, 1992; Lee et al., 1998) were the primary approaches
      employed in this domain. Recently, advancements in diffusion models have led to significant progress,
130
      as demonstrated by methods like DiffMorpher (Zhang et al., 2024), IMPUS (Yang et al., 2023), and
131
      Wang & Golland (2023). These approaches focus on optimizing text embeddings for two images
132   and fine-tuning pre-trained text-to-image diffusion models to achieve smooth interpolation between
133   them. However, they often necessitate extensive fine-tuning for each image pair and are limited
134   to images with similar semantics and layouts. This can also hinder the generalizability of the pre-
135   trained diffusion models due to the constraints imposed by LoRA modules in the U-Net architecture.
136   In contrast, our method offers a tuning-free framework that makes no additional modifications to
137   the original diffusion models, thereby preserving their inherent generalizability. Additionally, our
138   approach significantly enhances training efficiency and is capable of handling images with varying
139   layouts and semantics, addressing a challenging aspect for existing techniques.
140
141   Tuning-Free Text-Guided Image Editing. Recent image translation methods have emerged that
142
      edit either generated or real-world images through text in a training-free manner, without altering
      the internal computation of the U-Net. For instance, SDEdit (Meng et al., 2022) proposes a straight-
143
      forward method to add T time steps of Gaussian noise to the original images and denoise them
144
      using the guided text. Conversely, EDICT (Wallace et al., 2023) and FPI (Meiri et al., 2023) focus
145   on inverting the reference image back to the latent space and subsequently applying the inverted
146   latent condition guided by text. Additionally, methods like P2P (Hertz et al., 2023), PnP (Tumanyan
147   et al., 2023), and MasaCtrl (Cao et al., 2023) modify the attention mechanism within diffusion
148   models to enhance the alignment between guided text and the consistency of generated images with
149   their original counterparts. Drawing inspiration from these existing techniques, our method aims to
150   facilitate image morphing in a tuning-free manner. Notably, our approach also achieves comparable
151   image editing performance by framing text-guided editing as a special case of morphing between real
152   and generated images.

           5                              C ONCLUSION
    531
    532
           We have introduced FreeMorph, a novel tuning-free pipeline that is capable of generating smooth
    533
           and high-quality transitions between two input images within 30 seconds. Specifically, we propose
    534    to incorporate explicit guidance from the input images through modifications to the self-attention
    535    modules. This is achieved by two novel components, i.e., spherical feature aggregation and prior-
    536    driven self-attention mechanism. Additionally, we introduce a step-oriented motion flow that ensures
    537    directional transitions consistent with both input images. We also carefully designed an improved
    538    reverse diffusion and forward denoising process to integrate our proposed modules into the original
    539    DDIM framework. Extensive experiments reveal that FreeMorph delivers high-fidelity results across
           various scenarios, significantly outperforming existing image morphing techniques.
