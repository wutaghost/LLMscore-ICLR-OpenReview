011                                               A BSTRACT
012
013            DNA-encoded library (DEL) screening has revolutionized protein-ligand bind-
014            ing detection, enabling rapid exploration of vast chemical spaces through read
015
               count analysis. However, two critical challenges limit its effectiveness: distribu-
               tion noise in low copy number regimes and systematic shifts between read counts
016
               and true binding affinities. We present DEL-Ranking, a comprehensive frame-
017
               work that simultaneously addresses both challenges through innovative ranking-
018            based denoising and activity-referenced correction. Our approach introduces a
019            dual-perspective ranking strategy combining Pair-wise Soft Rank (PSR) and List-
020            wise Global Rank (LGR) constraints to preserve both local and global count re-
021            lationships. Additionally, we develop an Activity-Referenced Correction (ARC)
022            module that bridges the gap between read counts and binding affinities through
023            iterative refinement and biological consistency enforcement. Another key con-
024            tribution of this work is the curation and release of three comprehensive DEL
025            datasets that uniquely combine ligand 2D sequences, 3D conformational informa-
026
               tion, and experimentally validated activity labels. We validate our framework on
               five diverse DEL datasets and introduce three new comprehensive datasets fea-
027
               turing 2D sequences, 3D structures, and activity labels. DEL-Ranking achieves
028
               state-of-the-art performance across multiple correlation metrics and demonstrates
029            strong generalization ability across different protein targets. Importantly, our ap-
030            proach successfully identifies key functional groups associated with binding affin-
031            ity, providing actionable insights for drug discovery. This work advances both
032            the accuracy and interpretability of DEL screening, while contributing valuable
033            datasets for future research.

036   1   I NTRODUCTION
037
038   DNA-encoded library (DEL) technology has emerged as a revolutionary approach for protein-ligand
039
      binding detection, offering unprecedented advantages over traditional high-throughput screening
      methods(Franzini et al., 2014; Neri & Lerner, 2018; Peterson & Liu, 2023; Ma et al., 2023). The
040
      DEL screening process involves multiple stages including cycling, binding, washing, elution, and
041
      amplification (as shown in Figure 1). This process generates large-scale read count data, serving as
042   a proxy for potential binding affinity(Machutta et al., 2017; Foley et al., 2021). The read counts rep-
043   resent the frequency of each compound in the selected pool after undergoing target protein binding
044   and subsequent processing steps (Favalli et al., 2018). These read counts typically include matrix
045   counts and target counts, representing values corresponding to the scenarios without and with spe-
046   cific protein targets, respectively.
047
      DEL screening enables rapid and cost-effective evaluation of vast chemical spaces, typically en-
048   compassing billions of compounds (Satz et al., 2022), against biological targets (Neri & Lerner,
049   2017). This approach has gained widespread adoption in drug discovery due to its ability to iden-
050   tify novel chemical scaffolds and accelerate lead compound identification (Brenner & Lerner, 1992;
051   Goodnow Jr & Davie, 2017; Yuen & Franzini, 2017). Despite its advantages, DEL screening faces
052   two critical challenges: (1) DEL screening read counts are subject to Distribution Noise, stemming
053   from both experimental factors and intrinsic library characteristics. This noise predominantly affects
      the low copy number regime (<10 copies) where read counts follow a Poisson distribution, creating

073   significant discrepancies between observed counts and true binding affinities (Ki values) (Kuai et al.,
074   2018; Favalli et al., 2018); and (2) DEL screening data faces Distribution Shift, where the mapping
075   from read counts to binding affinities is systematically biased. While read counts reflect initial bind-
076   ing events, true binding affinity (Ki value) depends on multiple molecular properties that cannot be
077   captured by enrichment data alone, creating a fundamental gap between read count-based and actual
078   affinity distributions (Yung-Chi & Prusoff, 1973; Kuai et al., 2018). This study aims to address both
079   challenges by enhancing the correlation between predicted read counts and true binding affinity.
080   To address these challenges, various approaches have been developed. Early methods focused on
081   mitigating Distribution Noise through threshold-based filtering of enrichment factors calculated
082   as target count to matrix count ratios (Gu et al., 2008; Kuai et al., 2018). While computationally
083   efficient and interpretable, these methods only consider read count properties, ignoring the com-
084   plex relationships between molecular structures and their corresponding counts (McCloskey et al.,
085   2020). Recent advances have taken two main directions. First, machine learning approaches were
086   introduced to capture non-linear relationships between ligand molecules and their count labels (Mc-
087
      Closkey et al., 2020; Ma et al., 2021). This was further enhanced by incorporating distribution-level
      constraints, using ligand sequence embeddings to jointly predict enrichment factors and ensure count
088
      consistency (Lim et al., 2022; Hou et al., 2023). Second, recognizing the limitations of 2D represen-
089
      tations, DEL-Dock (Shmilovich et al., 2023) introduced 3D conformational information to improve
090   denoising. By incorporating Zero-Inflated Poisson distribution (ZIP) modeling with 3D structural
091   information, DEL-Dock achieved superior performance in read count prediction and demonstrated
092   strong generalization ability across different protein targets.
093
      A critical yet often overlooked aspect in current approaches is the biological activity information
094
      inherent in molecular structures. While the presence of certain functional groups, such as benzene
095
      sulfonamide, can serve as reliable indicators of binding potential and provide complementary in-
096   formation to read counts (Hou et al., 2023; Blevins et al., 2024), and this prior knowledge can be
097   extended to other DEL targets and datasets, current methods still face significant limitations in fully
098   utilizing this information (Wichert et al., 2024). These methods primarily focus on absolute read
099   count values instead of more robust relative ordering information, and rely solely on enrichment
100   data without incorporating crucial biological activity labels. Although recent advanced computa-
101   tional approaches have made progress in addressing Distribution Noise, the fundamental challenge
102   of Distribution Shift between enrichment data and true binding affinities remains largely unad-
103   dressed in current literature. This gap underscores the need for methods that can better integrate
104
      both functional group activity indicators and binding affinity information to improve both read count
      regression and the discovery of novel high-affinity functional groups.
105
106   To address these multifaceted challenges, we propose DEL-Ranking, an innovative framework that
107   synergistically tackles both Distribution Noise and Distribution Shift. Our approach uniquely
      combines theoretically grounded ranking constraints with activity-referenced correction mecha-

      nisms that leverage molecular structural features, enabling robust read count denoising while pre-
109   serving biological relevance. At the core of DEL-Ranking’s Distribution Noise mitigation is a
110   novel dual-perspective ranking strategy. We introduce two complementary constraints: the Pair-
111   wise Soft Rank (PSR) that captures local discriminative features, and the List-wise Global Rank
112   (LGR) that maintains global distribution patterns. This ranking-based approach emphasizes relative

      Our method not only achieves state-of-the-art performance across multiple metrics but also success-
      fully identifies key functional groups associated with high binding affinities. Through detailed abla-
124

125
      framework can reveal structure-activity relationships that guide rational drug design. This work
126   advances both DEL screening accuracy and interpretability, potentially accelerating drug discovery
127   through more precise binding affinity predictions and actionable structural insights.

130   2   R ELATED W ORKS
131
132   Traditional DEL data analysis approaches, like QSAR models (Martin et al., 2017) and molecular
133   docking simulations (Jiang et al., 2015; Wang et al., 2015), offer interpretability and mechanistic in-
134   sights. DEL-specific methods such as data aggregation (Satz, 2016) and normalized z-score metrics
135   (Faver et al., 2019) address unique DEL screening challenges. However, these methods face limita-
136   tions in scalability and handling complex, non-linear relationships in large-scale DEL datasets.
137
      Machine learning techniques such as Random Forest, Gradient Boosting Models, and Support Vector
138   Machines have improved DEL data analysis (Li et al., 2018; Ballester & Mitchell, 2010). Combined
139   with Bayesian Optimization (Hernández-Lobato et al., 2017), these methods offer better scalability
140   and capture complex, non-linear relationships in high-dimensional DEL data. Despite outperform-
141   ing traditional methods, they are limited by their reliance on extensive training data and lack of
142   interpretability in complex biochemical systems.
143
      Deep learning approaches, particularly Graph Neural Networks (GNNs), have significantly ad-
144   vanced protein-ligand interaction predictions in DEL screening. GNN-based models predict en-
145   richment scores and accommodate technical variations (Stokes et al., 2020; Ma et al., 2021), while
146   Graph Convolutional Neural Networks (GCNNs) enhance detection of complex molecular struc-
147   tures (McCloskey et al., 2020; Hou et al., 2023). Recent innovations include DEL-Dock, combining
148   3D pose information with 2D molecular fingerprints (Shmilovich et al., 2023), and sparse learning
149   methods addressing noise from truncated products and sequencing errors (Kómár & Kalinic, 2020).
150   Large-scale prospective studies have validated these AI-driven approaches, confirming improved hit
151   rates and specific inhibitory activities against protein targets (Gu et al., 2024).
152   Existing methods demonstrate improved scalability and molecular interaction modeling. How-
153   ever, they face challenges in data interpretability and theoretical foundations. Current modeling
154   approaches for DEL read count data primarily rely on theoretical prior distribution assumptions,
155   lacking sophisticated noise handling mechanisms and robust statistical frameworks. These limita-
156   tions manifest in the inability to incorporate information beyond prior distributions and insufficient
157   reliable activity validation data for denoising, leading to suboptimal performance when addressing
158
      Distribution Noise and Distribution Shift in DEL data.
159   To address these limitations, we propose a novel denoising framework that integrates a theoretically
160   grounded combined ranking loss with an iterative validation loop. This approach aims to correct
161   read count distributions more effectively, addressing both Distribution Noise and Distribution Shift,
      while improving the interpretability and reliability of binding affinity predictions in DEL screening.

533
534   In this paper, we propose DEL-Ranking to addresses the challenge of noise in DEL screening
535   through innovative ranking loss and activity-based correction algorithms. Experimental results
536   demonstrate significant improvements in binding affinity prediction and generalization capability.
537   Besides, the ability to identify potential binding affinity determinants advances the field of DEL
538   screening analysis. Current limitations revolve around the challenges of acquiring, integrating, and
539   comprehensively analyzing high-quality multi-modal molecular data at scale. Future works will aim
      to improve multi-modal data integration and analysis to advance DEL-based drug discovery.

866   B.1   V IRTUAL D OCKING FOR DATASET C ONSTRUCTION
867
868   We employed molecular docking to define the three-dimensional conformations of molecules within
869   our DEL datasets. This method was applied to both the training and evaluation sets, generating lig-
870   and binding poses for all molecules. We concentrated on three pivotal carbonic anhydrase proteins:
871   Q16790 (CAH9 HUMAN), P00918 (CAH2 HUMAN), and O43570 (CAH12 HUMAN).
872   For the Q16790 target, we sourced the 5fl4 and 2hkf PDB structures from the PDBbind database
873   and utilized the Gerry dataset (Gerry et al., 2019). which comprised 108,529 molecules, generating
874   up to nine potential poses per molecule. For the targets P00918 and O43570, we selected 127,500
875   SMILES strings from the DEL-MAP dataset (Hou et al., 2023) and conducted self-docking using
876   the 3p3h and 5doh PDB structures for P00918, and 4kp5 and 4ht2 for O43570, as sourced from
877   PDBbind. For the validation set, we applied the same docking methodology to the corresponding
878   ligands of CA9, CA2, and CA12, involving 3,324, 6,395, and 2,690 ligands respectively.
879   In the specific docking procedures, initial 3D conformations of ligands were created using RDKit.
880   The binding sites in the protein-ligand complexes were identified using 3D structural data of known
881   binding ligands from PDBbind as reference points. Targeted docking was performed by defining
882   the search space as a 22.5 Å cube centered on the reference ligand in the corresponding PDBbind
883   complex. Using SMINA docking software, we generated 9 potential poses for each protein-ligand
884   pair.

976    In order to evaluate the robustness of our method, we conduct a comprehensive analysis of four crit-
977    ical hyperparameters: the consistency loss weight γ, ranking loss weight ρ, LGR loss weight β, and

979    logarithmic search spaces for all loss-related hyperparameters to align the magnitudes of ranking
980    and consistency losses with the ZIP loss, while adopting a linear search space for temperature.

982    performance across all search spaces. The model exhibits strong stability, with performance varia-
983    tions remaining minimal under most hyperparameter adjustments. Nevertheless, we observe dataset-

986    performance fluctuations at higher values of ranking loss and LLGR weights, which we attribute to
987    magnitude imbalances in the numerical representations.

989    ship, providing empirical support for our distribution sharpening hypothesis. These findings collec-
990    tively indicate that while our model maintains robustness across the hyperparameter search space
