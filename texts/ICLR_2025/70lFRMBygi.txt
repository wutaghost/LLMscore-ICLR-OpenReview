                                                 A BSTRACT
013
014
               Detecting auditory attention from brain signals has been a significant challenge
015
               in neuroscience and brain-computer interface research. While progress has been
016
               made in EEG-based auditory attention detection, existing methods often struggle
017            with limited data and short decision windows, particularly in complex auditory
018            environments. In this paper, we propose DBGMS (Dual-Branch Generative Ad-
019            versarial Network with Multi-Task Self-Supervised Enhancement), a novel frame-
020            work for robust auditory attention decoding from electroencephalogram (EEG)
021            signals. There are three key innovations in our approach: (1) A dual-branch ar-
022            chitecture is developed that combines temporal attention and frequency residual
023            learning, enabling more comprehensive feature extraction to be achieved from
024            EEG signals; (2) Branch-specific generative adversarial networks (GANs) are
025
               designed to generate high-quality augmented samples in both temporal and fre-
               quency domains, effectively addressing the data scarcity issue in auditory attention
026
               decoding; (3) Attention mechanisms and graph convolution operations are incor-
027
               porated in both temporal and frequency domains. (4) A multi-task self-supervised
028            learning strategy is introduced, incorporating several complementary tasks such
029            as temporal order prediction, frequency band reconstruction, and time-frequency
030            consistency. This approach leverages unlabeled data to enhance the model’s abil-
031            ity to capture subtle attention-related features from multiple perspectives, thereby
032            improving generalization across subjects and listening conditions. In contrast to
033            state-of-the-art methods, DBGMS presents significant improvements in detection
034            accuracy and robustness, particularly for short decision windows. Our framework
035            is evaluated on two public EEG datasets, including KUL and DTU, demonstrating
036
               its effectiveness across various experimental settings.

      1   I NTRODUCTION
039
040
      Decoding auditory attention from electroencephalogram (EEG) signals presents significant chal-
041
      lenges in neuroscience and brain-computer interface researchBassett & Sporns (2017); Britton et al.
042   (2016). While existing methods often struggle with limited data and short decision windows, partic-
043   ularly in complex auditory environmentsCherry (1953); Choi et al. (2013). The inherent variability
044   of EEG signals, stemming from inter-subject differences, intra-subject variations, and environmental
045   factors, further complicates this taskLuck (2014); Blankertz et al. (2016); O’Sullivan et al. (2015c).
046
      Recent advancements in deep learning and signal processing have driven substantial improvements
047
      in auditory attention decoding. Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) and Recurrent Neural Net-
048   works (RNNs) have demonstrated remarkable success, significantly outperforming traditional linear
049   methodsDeckers et al. (2021); Ciccarelli et al. (2019); O’Sullivan et al. (2015b). Concurrently, ad-
050   vanced signal processing techniques such as Independent Component Analysis (ICA) and Common
051   Spatial Patterns (CSP) have enhanced EEG signal quality and feature extractionDas et al. (2016a);
052   Wong et al. (2018b). Despite these advancements, the transition from controlled laboratory set-
053   tings to real-world applications reveals persistent challenges. Significant declines in decoding accu-
      racy are observed when moving from controlled to naturalistic listening conditionsO’Sullivan et al.


                                                        1
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


054
      (2017a); Fuglsang et al. (2017a). To address these issues, various methodological approaches have
055   been explored, including adaptive filtering techniques, transfer learning methods, and robust fea-
056   ture extraction algorithmsO’Sullivan et al. (2017b); Fuglsang et al. (2017b). These techniques aim
057   to mitigate the effects of inter-subject and intra-subject variability, enhancing model generalization
058   across different subjects and recording sessions.
059
      The challenge is further compounded by the scarcity of labeled EEG data for auditory attention tasks,
060
      which limits the diversity and representativeness of training datasetsDas et al. (2016b); Wong et al.
061   (2018a). This limitation not only constrains the development of robust and generalizable models
062   but also raises questions about how effectively these models can capture the true diversity of EEG
063   patterns across a broader population. Moreover, practical applications demand real-time processing
064   capabilities, which often leads to reduced accuracy when decision windows are shortenedAlickovic
065   et al. (2019a); Miran et al. (2018).
066
      To address these challenges, innovative approaches have begun to be explored. Generative Ad-
067   versarial Networks (GANs) have shown potential for EEG data augmentation, effectively replicat-
068   ing various sources of EEG variabilityHartmann et al. (2018); Li et al. (2021); Abdelfattah et al.
069   (2021). Self-supervised learning techniques have demonstrated promise in capturing the complex,
070   non-stationary nature of EEG data across different subjects and recording sessionsBanville et al.
071   (2019); Kostas et al. (2021). These approaches enable the extraction of robust features that are in-
072   variant to many sources of EEG variability, leveraging unlabeled data to improve downstream task
073   performance.
074   The integration of both temporal and spectral information in EEG signals has been recognized as cru-
075   cial for comprehensive auditory attention decodingDing & Simon (2012); Alickovic et al. (2019b);
076   O’Sullivan et al. (2015a). Time-frequency analysis techniques have been employed to capture the
077   dynamic nature of auditory attention, achieving robust performance across various experimental
078   conditions. Additionally, graph-based approaches for EEG signal processing have shown superior
079   performance compared to traditional architectures, leveraging the inherent spatial relationships be-
080   tween EEG electrodesWang et al. (2014); Zhong et al. (2020).
081   Despite these advancements, a unified framework that effectively combines these various techniques
082   to address the specific challenges of auditory attention decoding has yet to be developed. Such
083   a framework would need to address the data scarcity issue, account for inter-subject variability,
084   maintain high accuracy with short decision windows, and effectively integrate both temporal and
085   spectral information from EEG signals.
086   In this paper, DBGMS (Dual-Branch Generative Adversarial Network with Multi-Task Self-
087   Supervised Enhancement) is proposed as a novel framework designed to address these challenges in
088   robust auditory attention decoding from electroencephalogram (EEG) signals. This approach builds
089   upon recent advances in deep learning and signal processing, adapting these advances to the specific
090   requirements of auditory attention decoding.
091   The main contributions of our work are as follows:
092
093          • A dual-branch architecture is developed that combines temporal attention and frequency
094            residual learning, enabling more comprehensive feature extraction from EEG signals. This
095            approach allows for the capture of both temporal dynamics and spectral characteristics
096            crucial for auditory attention decoding.
097          • Branch-specific generative adversarial networks (GANs) are designed to generate high-
098            quality augmented samples in both temporal and frequency domains. This novel data
099            augmentation strategy addresses both the data scarcity issue and the inherent variability
100            of EEG signals in auditory attention decoding. By generating diverse synthetic samples,
101            these GANs enhance the model’s ability to handle varied EEG patterns, thereby improving
102
               robustness and generalization across different subjects and recording conditions.
103          • Attention mechanisms and graph convolution operations are incorporated in both temporal
104            and frequency domains, enhancing the model’s ability to capture relevant spatial-temporal
105            patterns in EEG signals.
106          • A multi-task self-supervised learning strategy is introduced, incorporating several comple-
107            mentary tasks such as temporal order prediction, frequency band reconstruction, and time-
               frequency consistency. This approach leverages unlabeled data to enhance the model’s

      6   C ONCLUSION
513
      In this work, we propose a novel dual-branch generative adversarial network with multi-task self-
514
      supervised learning enhancement (DBGMS) to improve auditory attention decoding from EEG sig-
515
      nals, specifically addressing the challenges of EEG signal variability and diversity. Our dual-branch
516   structure effectively captures both temporal and spectral features, enabling comprehensive feature
517   extraction that is robust to inter-subject and intra-subject variations. The incorporation of GANs for
518   data augmentation helps mitigate the data scarcity issue while simulating the natural variability of
519   EEG signals. Self-supervised learning strategies are employed to enhance the model’s ability to ex-
520   tract invariant features, improving generalization across subjects and diverse auditory environments.
521   Our experiments consistently demonstrate that DBGMS outperforms baseline models, particularly
522   in challenging conditions like short decision windows, indicating its enhanced ability to handle the
523   inherent variability of real-world EEG data. This work represents a significant step towards more
524
      robust and generalizable EEG-based auditory attention decoding systems, paving the way for fu-
      ture research to further explore domain-specific strategies for handling EEG signal variability and
