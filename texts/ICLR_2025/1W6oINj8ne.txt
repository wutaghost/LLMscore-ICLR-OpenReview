009                                              A BSTRACT
010
011
               In this paper, we present a novel Bangladeshi Road Scenario Segmentation Dataset
012            designed to advance autonomous driving technologies under the challenging and
013            diverse road conditions of Bangladesh. This comprehensive instance segmenta-
014            tion dataset comprised 10,082 high-resolution images captured across nine major
015            cities, including Dhaka, Sylhet, Chittagong, and Rajshahi, addressing the criti-
016            cal need for region-specific computer vision data in developing countries. Un-
017            like existing autonomous driving datasets that primarily focus on western road
018            conditions, BRSSD10k encompasses a wide range of environments unique to
019            Bangladesh, including unstructured urban areas, hilly terrains, village roads, and
020
               densely populated city centers. The dataset features instance segmentation an-
               notations with classes specifically tailored to reflect the distinctive elements of
021
               Bangladeshi roads, such as rickshaws, CNGs (auto-rickshaws), informal roadside
022
               stalls, and various nonstandard vehicles. To demonstrate its utility as a bench-
023            marking tool for autonomous driving systems, we present comparative results
024            from several state-of-the-art instance segmentation models tested on this dataset,
025            achieving an mAP of 0.441. This evaluation not only showcases the dataset’s
026            effectiveness in assessing model performance but also underscores the need for
027            adaptive algorithms capable of handling diverse and unpredictable urban environ-
028            ments in the context of autonomous navigation.

031   1   I NTRODUCTION
032
033
      Autonomous driving technologies have made substantial progress in recent years, yet their develop-
034
      ment and testing remain predominantly focused on road conditions found in Western countries. This
035
      emphasis has resulted in a significant gap in resources for developing autonomous systems capable
036   of navigating the diverse and challenging environments present in many developing nations. To ad-
037   dress this issue, we introduce the Bangladesh Road Scenario Segmentation Dataset (BRSSD10k), a
038   comprehensive instance segmentation dataset specifically designed to capture the unique road con-
039   ditions in Bangladesh.
040
      Existing datasets, such as Cityscapes Cordts et al. (2016) and Mapillary Vistas Neuhold et al. (2017),
041
      were created with a focus on Western locations. While these datasets have been instrumental in ad-
042   vancing computer vision for autonomous driving, they do not reflect the complexities of non-Western
043   environments. The Indian Driving Dataset (IDD) Varma et al. (2018), with 10,000 annotated images,
044   has advanced research in the subcontinent, yet even it does not fully encapsulate the intricate road
045   scenarios found in Bangladesh. Cityscapes, with its 5,000 finely annotated images of urban scenes
046   from German cities, remains a benchmark for structured environments, while IDD represents a step
047   toward more diverse scenarios by capturing the heterogeneous nature of Indian roads. However, nei-
048   ther dataset comprehensively addresses the unique challenges posed by Bangladeshi roads, where
049   the interaction between formal and informal transportation systems presents distinct difficulties for
050
      computer vision models.
051   Instance segmentation, which involves both classifying and delineating individual object instances
052   within an image, is crucial for autonomous navigation in complex environments He et al. (2017).
053   The dense traffic, non-motorized vehicles, and fluid road usage in Bangladeshi cities demand highly
      accurate and robust instance segmentation models. BRSSD10k was developed to meet these re-

      quirements by offering a large-scale, finely annotated dataset that reflects the specific characteristics
055   of Bangladeshi roads.

088   3.1        P ROBLEM S TATEMENT
089
090   Let D = {(Ii , Mi )}N  i=1 be a training set of N labeled images Ii ∈ X and their corresponding
091   ground-truth instance segmentation masks Mi . Each Mi is a set of instance masks {mij }K        i
                                                                                                     j=1 ,
092   where Ki is the number of instances in image Ii , and each mij ∈ {0, 1}H×W represents a binary
093   mask for the j-th instance in the i-th image, with H and W being the height and width of the image,
094   respectively.
095   The task of instance segmentation is to learn a model fθ : X → Y, where θ is a set of learnable
096   parameters. In this context, Y represents the set of instance segmentation masks for the detected
097   objects, along with their corresponding class labels and confidence scores.
098
      Given a test image I from the diverse road scenarios of Bangladesh, the trained model predicts a set
099   of instance masks Mp = {mpk }K   k=1 , where K is the number of detected instances. Each predicted
100
      mask mpk ∈ [0, 1]H×W is accompanied by a class label ck ∈ C, where C is the set of predefined
101   classes specific to Bangladeshi road scenes (e.g., cars, rickshaws, pedestrians, roadside stalls), and
102   a confidence score sk ∈ [0, 1].

189   4     DATA ACQUISITION AND L ABELING
190
191   The Bangladesh Road Scenario Segmentation Dataset (BRSSD10k) was compiled through a rigor-
192   ous process of data collection, preprocessing, and annotation. Our methodology ensured the capture
193   of authentic and diverse road scenarios specific to Bangladesh, while maintaining high-quality an-
194   notations.
195
196   4.1   DATA C OLLECTION
197
198   We collected raw data exclusively using smartphone cameras to capture real-world road scenarios
199   across Bangladesh. This approach allowed us to gather a wide range of urban and rural road scenes,
200   reflecting the true diversity and challenges of the country’s transportation infrastructure. Importantly,
201   no images were sourced from online platforms, ensuring the dataset’s originality and relevance to
202   the specific context of Bangladesh.
203   BRSSD10k includes data from nine key locations: Dhaka, Sherpur, Mymensingh, Khulna, Sylhet,
204   Maowa, Juri, Rajshahi, and Chittagong. These locations were strategically chosen to represent
205   the country’s diverse road conditions, covering major urban centers like Dhaka and Chittagong,
206   regional hubs such as Khulna and Sylhet, smaller towns like Sherpur and Juri, and areas with unique
207   geographic features like Maowa. This geographic variety ensures that the dataset reflects the full
208   spectrum of road scenarios in Bangladesh, including both congested city streets and rural roads.
209
210   4.2   P REPROCESSING
211
212   The collected videos were preprocessed to extract individual frames at a rate of one frame per sec-
213   ond. This extraction rate strikes a balance between capturing temporal variations and maintaining a
214   manageable dataset size. Each extracted frame was standardized to a resolution of 1280x720 pixels,
215   ensuring sufficient detail for complex scene analysis while considering computational efficiency for
      future model training. Additionally, some frames were extracted at a resolution of 848x478 pixels.

      4.3   A NNOTATION P ROCESS
217
218   The annotation process was carried out on the Roboflow platform, chosen for its robust features and
219   collaborative capabilities. Our annotation team consisted of 10 trained annotators who were familiar
220   with the local context and the specific requirements of our dataset.
221
222   4.4   Q UALITY A SSURANCE
223
224   To ensure the highest possible annotation accuracy, we implemented a two-stage validation process:
225
            1. Initial Annotation: Each image was manually annotated by one of the 10 trained annotators.
226
227         2. Validation: Following the initial annotation, each image underwent a secondary review by
228
               two different individuals. This dual-validation approach helped in identifying and correct-
               ing any potential errors or inconsistencies in the annotations.
229
230   This meticulous process of data acquisition, preprocessing, and multi-stage annotation validation
231   was designed to minimize errors and ensure the reliability of our dataset. The resulting BRSSD10k
232   dataset provides a high-quality, context-specific resource for advancing autonomous driving research
233   and development in Bangladesh and similar developing countries.

530   Additionally, to provide a more robust evaluation of the dataset’s effectiveness, future work should
531   include benchmarking against state-of-the-art Vision Language Models (VLMs). This comparison
532   would offer valuable insights into the dataset’s performance relative to more generalized models and
533   highlight areas where region-specific data provides significant advantages.
534   Despite these limitations, BRSSD10k represents a valuable contribution to the field of autonomous
535   driving research. By focusing on the unique challenges presented by Bangladesh’s road conditions,
536   this dataset not only advances the development of autonomous technologies for similar environments
537   but also broadens the global understanding of diverse driving scenarios. As autonomous driving
538   research continues to evolve, datasets like BRSSD10k will play a crucial role in creating more
539   inclusive and adaptable systems capable of operating safely and efficiently in a wide range of global
      contexts.
