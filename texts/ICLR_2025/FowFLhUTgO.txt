009                                              A BSTRACT
010
011            Mamba has garnered widespread attention due to its flexible design and efficient
012            hardware performance to process 1D sequences based on the state space model
013            (SSM). Recent studies have attempted to apply Mamba to the visual domain by
014            flattening 2D images into patches and then regarding them as a 1D sequence. To
015            compensate for the 2D structure information loss (e.g., local similarity) of the
016            original image, most existing methods focus on designing different orders to se-
017            quentially process the tokens, which could only alleviate this issue to some extent.
018
               In this paper, we propose a Visual 2-Dimensional Mamba (V2M) model as a com-
               plete solution, which directly processes image tokens in the 2D space. We first
019
               generalize SSM to the 2-dimensional space which generates the next state con-
020
               sidering two adjacent states on both dimensions (e.g., columns and rows). We
021            then construct our V2M based on the 2-dimensional SSM formulation and incor-
022            porate Mamba to achieve hardware-efficient parallel processing. The proposed
023            V2M effectively incorporates the 2D locality prior yet inherits the efficiency and
024            input-dependent scalability of Mamba. Extensive experimental results on Ima-
025            geNet classification and downstream visual tasks including object detection and
026            instance segmentation on COCO and semantic segmentation on ADE20K demon-
027            strate the effectiveness of our V2M compared with other visual backbones.

030   1   I NTRODUCTION
031
032   State Space Models (SSM) have recently received sustained attention in natural language processing
033
      (NLP), achieving excellent performance on various tasks because of its efficiency in handling long
      sequences (Gu et al., 2021a; 2022; 2021b). Mamba (Gu & Dao, 2024) further equip the conventional
034
      SSMs with the ability to process time-varying input and exquisitely designed hardware acceleration
035
      algorithms, demonstrating the potential to compete with transformers (Kim et al., 2018).
036
037   Although convolutional neural networks (CNNs) (He et al., 2016; Liu et al., 2022) and visual trans-
038   formers (ViTs) (Dosovitskiy et al., 2020; Liu et al., 2021) have consistently dominated computer vi-
039
      sion, recent works applying Mamba to visual data modeling emerges as promising alternatives (Zhu
      et al., 2024; Liu et al., 2024b; Huang et al., 2024). To adapt 2D visual data to Mamba with 1D
040
      sequential processing, most existing methods follow ViTs to patchify images into tokens and de-
041
      sign various ordering strategies to incorporate 2D structural prior. For example, Vision Mamba
042   (Vim) (Zhu et al., 2024) directly flattens images by rows and then employs a bidirectional model-
043   ing strategy to enhance the latent representation of the model. LocalMamba (Huang et al., 2024)
044   attempts to preserve the local invariance of images through local scanning and optimal scanning
045   direction search. However, these works are still constrained in the framework of 1-dimensional
046   Mamba, which can only approximate the 2D structural modeling of images and inevitably disrupts
047   the coherence and locality of visual data.
048   In this paper, we propose a Visual 2-Dimensional Mamba (V2M) model as a complete solution for
049   2D visual modeling. We directly processes image tokens in the 2D space instead of transforming
050   them into 1D sequences to adapt to the 1D Mamba, as illustrated in Figure 1. Specifically, we first
051   generalize the state space model to the 2-dimensional space which generates the next state consid-
052   ering two adjacent states on both dimensions (e.g., columns and rows). We then construct our V2M
053   based on the 2-dimensional SSM formulation and incorporate Mamba to achieve hardware-efficient
      parallel processing. In addition, considering the non-sequential nature of image tokens, we construct

      2   R ELATED W ORK
082
      Generic Vision Backbones. Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) and Vision Transformers
083
      (ViTs) are the most widely adopted generic backbones in computer vision. Among them, CNNs
084
      leverage shift-invariance for feature extraction for each layer, setting a series of new benchmarks on
085   numerous visual tasks (He et al., 2016; Liu et al., 2022; Krizhevsky et al., 2012; LeCun et al., 1998;
086   Szegedy et al., 2015; Simonyan & Zisserman, 2014; Huang et al., 2017). Nevertheless, the introduc-
087   tion of ViT has changed the landscape of computer vision, shifting the dominance of CNNs as the
088   backbone to a new paradigm (Dosovitskiy et al., 2020; Liu et al., 2021; Touvron et al., 2021; Carion
089   et al., 2020; Caron et al., 2021). Specifically, the plain ViT (Chen et al., 2020) divides an image
090   into multiple patches and linearly embeds these patches as the input sequence for the Transformer
091   model with positional encoding. Swin Transformer (Liu et al., 2021) introduces hierarchical feature
092   maps and a self-attention mechanism within local windows, which enables the model to effectively
093
      handle image features at different scales. Transformer-based models are also widely employed in
      multimodal tasks due to their versatility in handling data from different modalities.
094
095   In addition to CNNs and ViTs, a plethora of sophisticated visual architectures based on Mamba (Gu
096   & Dao, 2024), have recently emerged in the domain of computer vision (Zhu et al., 2024; Liu et al.,
097   2024b; Huang et al., 2024; Liu et al., 2024a; Ruan & Xiang, 2024; Yang et al., 2024; Pei et al.,
098   2024). For example, Vision Mamba (Vim) (Zhu et al., 2024) directly transfers Mamba from NLP
099
      to the visual domain, which does not incorporate significant considerations specifically for visual
      data. VMamba (Liu et al., 2024b) addresses these deficiencies through further exploration, utiliz-
100
      ing one-dimensional scans in four distinct directions to simulate the modeling of two-dimensional
101
      images, and adopting a hierarchical structure for feature extraction. LocalMamba (Huang et al.,
102   2024) further divides the input image into multiple local windows, thereby retaining the local sim-
103   ilarity of the image to a certain extent. Additionally, PlainMamba (Yang et al., 2024) is designed
104   with a non-hierarchical structure to enhance feature fusion and modality fusion, whereas EfficientV-
105   Mamba (Pei et al., 2024) conducts a streamlined and detailed study of the lightweight adaptation of
106   VMamba. These endeavors harness the high computational and memory efficiency of Mamba, yet
107   predominantly involve the naive flattening of images input for the original 1-dimensional Mamba
      formulation, which compromises the local similarity and coherence of images and thus results in

108
      suboptimality. On the contrary, we employ 2-dimensional state space equations to model image
109   features, and thereby effectively incorporates the 2D locality prior, establishing a more rational ap-
110   plication of Mamba within the realm of computer vision.

112
      State Space Models. State space models (SSMs) represent the output of the system (observation)
      as a function of the state of the system and typically assume that the evolution of the system state
113
      over time follows a Hidden Markov Model (HMM). Considering the proficiency in the modeling of
114
      extended sequences, 1-dimensional SSMs have garnered extensive and conspicuous attention within
115   the domain of natural language processing (Gu et al., 2022; 2021a;b; Gupta et al., 2022; Gu &
116   Dao, 2024). For example, S4 (Gu et al., 2021a) transforms the SSM into a large 1D convolution
117   to achieve efficient parallelization and adopts HiPPO matrices as initialization to facilitate memory
118   storage and prevent gradient explosion or vanishing. S4D (Gu et al., 2022) systematically investi-
119   gates the parameterization and initialization methods for diagonal state space models. Mamba (Gu
120   & Dao, 2024) further improves the traditional state space model, enabling it to selectively extract
121   information based on the input content, combined with a hardware-aware parallel algorithm that
122   achieves efficient processing.
123   Several studies have attempted to extend the formulation of the state space models into a 2-
124   dimensional structure (Kung et al., 1977; Eising, 1978; Kurek, 1985; Hinamoto, 1980). They typi-
125   cally increase the state dimension in the state space model and perform corresponding state iterative
126   calculations along each dimension. In computer vision, Baron et al. (Baron et al., 2023) integrates a
127   2-dimensional state space model into CNN and ViT architectures, implementing it as an additional
128   layer for the processing of features. Nonetheless, this approach neglects the input-dependent charac-
129
      teristic of SSM, which hampers the capacity of the model for adaptively extracting features from the
      input images. In contrast, our proposed V2M generalizes the SSM to the 2-dimensional space which
130
      generates the next state considering two adjacent states on both dimensions, while also preserving
131
      the input-relevant feature of the SSM.
132
133   Visual Representation Learning. Visual representation learning aims to acquire features that are
134   capable of encapsulating and portraying the essence of visual data, which are widely harnessed in
135
      a diverse array of visual tasks including image classification, object detection, image segmentation,
      pose estimation, and video analysis. Typical settings include pre-training on large datasets (such
136
      as ImageNet) and then fine-tuning on downstream tasks and specific datasets (He et al., 2020; Grill
137
      et al., 2020; He et al., 2022; Zhou et al., 2022; Chen & He, 2021; Khosla et al., 2020; He et al., 2016;
138   Liu et al., 2021), which can be divided into supervised settings (Khosla et al., 2020; He et al., 2016;
139   Liu et al., 2021) and unsupervised patterns (He et al., 2020; Grill et al., 2020; He et al., 2022; Zhou
140   et al., 2022; Chen & He, 2021). Specifically, supervised representation learning utilizes the ground
141   truth labels of samples as the supervision while unsupervised representation learning constructs a
142   label-free pretext task to conduct the training procedure, such as contrastive learning (He et al.,
143   2020; Grill et al., 2020; Chen & He, 2021) and mask image modeling (He et al., 2022; Zhou et al.,
144   2022). In this paper, we follow the setting of supervised representation learning. We pretrain the
145   model with ground truth labels on the image classification task and then transfer to object detection,
146
      instance segmentation, as well as instance segmentation for verification of transferability.

311   those adopted in Mamba, alongside expedited computations facilitated by high-speed SRAM, and a

519   SSM modeling by independently correlating the features of rows and columns without the flattening
520   of the entire image patch. Consequently, we are unable to merely append a solitary class token
521   for subsequent classification tasks. To address this limitation, we present three viable solutions,
522   including obtaining the class token through feature mean pooling, augmenting the image with an
523   additional row and column of class tokens at the edge, and incorporating a row and column of class
524   tokens at the middle of the image, shown in Figure 4. We observe that the third scheme yields a
525   commendable performance and is thus designated as the default setting.

527   5   C ONCLUSION
528
529   In this paper, we have presented a visual 2-dimensional mamba (V2M) framework for effective im-
530   age representation learning. We have employed the 2D SSM for the modeling process of images,
531   thereby preserving the inherent prior of local invariance within the images. We have concurrently
532   performed 2D SSM modeling in four directions to enhance the representational capacity of the
533   model considering the non-temporal nature of the input images. We have conducted a series of ex-
534   periments that encompass classification and various downstream tasks, including object detection,
535   instance segmentation, and semantic segmentation, to assess the effectiveness of our framework.
536
      Experimental results have demonstrated the superior classification as well as transferability perfor-
      mances of the proposed V2M framework.
537
538   Limitations. The implementation of 2D SSM modeling in four directions within each V2M block
539   necessitates a compromise in speed. Future research will concentrate on both software and hardware
      algorithm optimizations to mitigate this drawback.
