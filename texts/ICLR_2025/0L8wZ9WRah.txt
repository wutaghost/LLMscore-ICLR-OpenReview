009                                                   A BSTRACT
010
011                Quantization offers a promising solution for deploying large-scale language mod-
012                els (LLMs) on resource-constrained devices. However, early quantization meth-
013                ods, developed for smaller networks like ResNet, rely on gradient-based optimiza-
014                tion, which becomes impractical for hyper-scale LLMs with billions of param-
015                eters. While recently proposed backpropagation-free post-training quantization
016                (PTQ) methods alleviate this issue, their performance is limited by a lack of inter-
017
                   layer dependency consideration. In this paper, we introduce a novel PTQ algo-
                   rithm that incorporates inter-layer dependencies without relying on backpropaga-
018
                   tion. The key innovation is the development of attention-aware Hessian matrices
019
                   that capture inter-layer interactions within the attention module. Extensive experi-
020                ments demonstrate that our approach significantly outperforms conventional PTQ
021                methods, particularly at low bit-widths.

024   1       I NTRODUCTION
025
026   The explosive growth in complexity (parameters) of large-scale language models (LLMs) based on
027   Transformers (Touvron et al., 2023; Zhang et al., 2022) has resulted in a proportional increase in
028   computational costs, which has prompted an urgent need for efficient model processing and com-
029   pression strategies. Quantization has emerged as a pivotal solution in this context, and it serves
030
      as an essential step in deploying AI models on resource-constrained devices that primarily support
      fixed-point arithmetic. By reducing precision, the memory bandwidth requirements can be allevi-
031
      ated, and the significant parallelism of quantized models can be SIMDified using highly efficient
032
      vector processing units, such as neural processing units (NPUs).
033
034   Two main categories of quantization approaches have been proposed to preserve the performance
035   of original full-precision models: quantization-aware training (QAT) and post-training quantiza-
036
      tion (PTQ). Although QAT can potentially outperform PTQ, its practicality diminishes considerably
      when handling hyper-scale LLMs featuring billions of parameters. Consequently, recent quantiza-
037
      tion efforts have been directed toward PTQ.
038
039   Although classic PTQ methods have successfully quantized small-scale models (Nagel et al., 2020;
040   Li et al., 2021), they rely on time-consuming gradient-based optimization, so their efficacy decreases
041   when the complexity of LLMs increases. Accordingly, backpropagation-free PTQ methods have
042
      been developed for LLMs (Frantar et al., 2023; Xiao et al., 2023; Jeon et al., 2023b); however, their
      performance is somewhat limited owing to the lack of consideration of inter-layer dependencies.
043
      Recent studies have attempted to consider inter-layer dependencies (Shao et al., 2023; Ma et al.,
044
      2024), but they still rely on time-consuming gradient-based optimizations.
045
046   In this paper, we propose a novel quantization algorithm that considers inter-layer dependencies
047   without relying on backpropagation. Our primary contributions can be summarized as follows:
048
                • We propose a novel PTQ algorithm called B OA1 . To avoid time-consuming gradient-
049
                  based optimization, we adopt the Hessian-based strategy introduced by (Frantar & Alistarh,
050
                  2022). The primary contribution is to approximate the Hessian more accurately by exploit-
051               ing the attention reconstruction error, not the layer-wise reconstruction error, to capture
052               inter-layer dependencies within the attention module (Section 3.2).
053

055            requires a large amount of memory and high computational cost. Therefore, we incorpo-

058            (Section 3.3).
059          • We evaluate B OA via extensive experiments on publicly available LLMs. Our results
060            demonstrate that B OA outperforms conventional LLM PTQ methods by a significant mar-
061            gin, particularly for low-bit precision (e.g., INT2) (Section 4).

065
      When calibration data are available, PTQ primarily aims to minimize the increase in task loss in-
066
      curred by quantization. Consider a neural network parameterized by weights W. Provided that the
067   network is trained to convergence, the problem of quantizing W to minimize task loss degradation
068   can be formulated as (LeCun et al., 1989)
069
070                                          min ∆wT · H(w) · ∆w,                                           (1)
                                             ∆w
071

072
      by the quantization. Owing to the infeasibility in computing and storing the exact Hessian H(w) ,
073
      many studies have assumed independence between layers, which relaxes (1) into the following layer-
074
      wise reconstruction problem (Nagel et al., 2020):
075
                                                                      2
076                                          min     ∆W(ℓ) X(ℓ−1)         ,                                 (2)
077                                         ∆W(ℓ)                     F

078   where X(ℓ−1) is the input to the ℓ-th layer parameterized by W(ℓ) .
079
080
      To solve (2), early efforts aimed to optimize the weight-rounding mechanism (Nagel et al., 2020;
      Hubara et al., 2021; Li et al., 2021; Jeon et al., 2022; 2023a). Instead of allocating the nearest quanti-
081
      zation bin, these studies attempted to assign quantized values that minimize the reconstruction error.
082
      In (Nagel et al., 2020), a backpropagation-based optimization method, called AdaRound, has been
083   proposed. This algorithm has been extended to B RECQ where the block-wise reconstruction error
084   has been used instead of the layer-wise reconstruction error to consider the inter-layer dependencies
085   within a certain network block (e.g., Transformer block), which leads to an enhanced low-bit quan-
086   tization performance (Li et al., 2021). Although AdaRound and B RECQ have successfully quantized
087   small-sized models such as ResNet (He et al., 2016), they are heavily dependent on time-consuming
088   gradient-based optimizations. This renders their application to LLMs with billions of parameters
089   challenging. Consequently, recent efforts have shifted towards the development of cost-effective
090   quantization methods for LLMs.
091   These efforts can be classified into two orthogonal categories: 1) Hessian-based methods that opti-
092   mize a weight-rounding mechanism without relying on backpropagation (e.g., GPTQ (Frantar et al.,
093   2023)) and 2) equivalent transformation (ET)-based methods that transform a model to be robust
094   to quantization, thereby enhancing the performance of the naive rounding-to-nearest quantization
095   (e.g., SmoothQuant (Xiao et al., 2023), AWQ (Lin et al., 2024), Z-F OLD (Jeon et al., 2023b), Out-
096   lier Suppression+ (OS+) (Wei et al., 2023), OmniQuant (Shao et al., 2023), AffineQuant (Ma et al.,
097
      2024)). Among them, GPTQ has emerged as one of the most efficient quantization methods; GPTQ
      is capable of quantizing models with over 30 billion parameters in just a few GPU hours, with negli-
098
      gible performance degradation at INT4 precision. Moreover, GPTQ can be integrated with ET-based
099
      methods to enhance their performance (Lin et al., 2024; Jeon et al., 2023b).2 Similar to GPTQ, we
100   optimize a weight-rounding mechanism based on Hessian without relying on backpropagation. The
101   primary difference is that we pursue the preservation of the attention output after the quantization
102   while GPTQ aims to preserve each layer output and thus cannot consider inter-layer dependencies
103   within the attention module.
104

105
      SpQR (Dettmers et al., 2023), SqueezeLLM (Kim et al., 2023), and OAC (Edalati et al., 2024) tried

120   3.1   OVERVIEW OF P ROPOSED B OA
121
122   Similar to GPTQ, the proposed B OA algorithm quantizes weights by repeating the quantization and
123   weight-update steps; once B OA quantizes one weight, it updates the remaining (not-yet-quantized)
124
      weights by exploiting the Hessian-based weight-update formula introduced by GPTQ, compensating
      for the task loss degradation caused by the quantization. When the q-th weight wq is quantized, the
125
      weight-update δw is mathematically expressed as

136   independence has been assumed to approximate H, which yields the following Hessian equation4 :
137

141   the identity matrix. As the approximated Hessian H(w ) relies solely on the input, GPTQ is unable
142   to account for the influence of other layers when compensating for quantization error (see (3)). In
143   other words, GPTQ neglects inter-layer dependencies within the attention module, a crucial aspect
144
      of Transformers (Vaswani et al., 2017), which results in somewhat constrained performance for low

145
      inter-layer dependencies within the attention module and then use them instead of the conventional

      Hessian in (4).

234   3.3   E FFICIENT I MPLEMENTATION OF B OA
235
236
      While inter-layer dependencies within the attention module can be considered by exploiting the
      proposed Hessians, they are significantly more complex than the conventional Hessian in (4), which
237
      may incur high computational costs. For example, computing the proposed Hessians in (8) and (11)
238
      would be more expensive than computing the conventional one in (4). In this subsection, we present
239   techniques for mitigating the computational overheads incurred by the proposed attention-aware
240   Hessians.
241
242   Hessian relaxation The largest overhead related to the computation of the proposed Hessians is
243   the Jacobian matrix Jσ in (8) and (11). For one input sequence, the shape of Jσ is H × L × L × L,
244   which requires a large amount of memory and high computational cost (more than 400 GB even for
245   the OPT-125M model when L = 2048).
246   To mitigate such computational overhead, we establish a relaxed Hessian that does not require the
247   computation of Jσ . To this end, we build an upper bound for the attention reconstruction error in (7),
248   which will be used as its surrogate:
249                                                                  2
250                                               Wout,h VhT Jσ                        2
                           ∥∆ MHA(X)∥2F ≤            √                   · ∥Kh ∆WQ,h X∥F .                (14)

253
      tion.5 Thus, we do not need to consider this term when computing the Hessian. In short, we use
254
      the term ∥Kh ∆WQ,h X∥2F as a surrogate of the attention reconstruction error when deriving the
255

309   Simultaneous quantization of different heads The conventional Hessian in (4) implies the inde-
310

313   row can be quantized after being updated to compensate for the quantization error of the first row.
314   This is in contrast to GPTQ where all the rows are quantized simultaneously.

342   4.1   E XPERIMENTAL SETUP
343
344   To evaluate the performance of the proposed B OA, we quantize publicly available language models
345   including OPT (Zhang et al., 2022), BLOOM (Scao et al., 2022), and LLaMA (Touvron et al.,
346   2023)). As in (Frantar et al., 2023; Jeon et al., 2023b; Chee et al., 2023), we quantize only weights
347   and retain activations with full precision because activations do not pose a significant bottleneck
348   for the inference of LLMs (Frantar et al., 2023; Kim et al., 2023). As a calibration dataset, we use
349
      128 random 2048 token segments from the C4 dataset (Raffel et al., 2020). Thus, we do not use
      any task-specific data for quantization. We evaluate the performance of the quantized models using
350
      benchmark datasets (e.g., WikiText-2 (Merity et al., 2016), C4 (Raffel et al., 2020), and PTB (Marcus
351
      et al., 1993)) and zero-shot tasks. All experiments were conducted using a single NVIDIA A100
352   GPU (80 GB).
353
354   When determining a quantization order in B OA, the heuristic introduced by GPTQ can be employed;
355
      the column/row corresponding to the largest diag(Hcol )/diag(Hrow ) (i.e., the most quantization-

      heuristic could occasionally enhance the performance, yet at other times, it may result in inferior
357
      performance. We conduct experiments with and without this heuristic and report the better results.

      4.5   C OMPARISON OF T IME AND M EMORY C OSTS
509
      We compare the processing time and memory costs of B OA and conventional algorithms (see Ta-
510   ble 13 in Appendix C.4). We observe that the processing time of B OA is shorter than those required
511   by existing attention-aware algorithms (i.e., OmniQuant and AffineQuant), yet B OA achieves sig-
512   nificantly better performance (see Tables 6 and 11), which demonstrates the efficacy of the proposed
513   method. We also observe that B OA requires longer processing time and larger memory than those
514   required by GPTQ. This is because GPTQ quantizes all the rows of the weight matrix simultaneously
515   using only layer input. In contrast, B OA sequentially quantizes sub-weight matrices using outputs
516   of other layers as well as layer input (see Fig. 1(b)) to consider the inter-layer dependencies within
517   the attention module, which eventually leads to the better quantization performance than GPTQ.
518   Clearly, there is a trade-off between quantization speed / memory cost and accuracy. In real situa-
519   tions, when one needs to preserve the performance of the original model by considering inter-layer
520   dependencies within the attention module, the proposed B OA would be an intriguing solution. Even
521   when the memory resource is limited, B OA can be used with some relaxation. Specifically, we note
522   that the large memory cost of B OA for hyper-scale LLMs (e.g., 13B and 30B) is attributable to the
523   row-wise Hessian for the value projection (XATh Ah XT in (12)) whose shape is H × d × d. In
524
      memory-limited cases, we can mitigate the memory cost of B OA by considering inter-layer depen-
      dencies only for query and key projections and applying the standard Hessian (XXT in (4)) for the
525
      value projection. Indeed, when applying the proposed Hessians only for query and key projections,
526
      B OA requires almost same amount of memory as GPTQ, yet still exhibiting better performance (see
527   Table 14 in Appendix C.4). For more discussion on time and memory costs of the proposed B OA,
528   see Appendix C.4.

      5     C ONCLUSION
532
      In this paper, we proposed a novel PTQ algorithm called B OA. To consider the inter-layer dependen-
533
      cies within the attention module while circumventing time-consuming gradient-based optimization,
534
      we approximated the Hessian matrices by exploiting the attention reconstruction error. Furthermore,
535   to mitigate the computational overhead incurred by the proposed attention-aware Hessians, we in-
536   corporated several techniques, such as Hessian relaxation, efficient computation of inverse Hessians,
537   and head-wise simultaneous quantization. Finally, through extensive experiments, we demonstrated
538   the efficacy of the proposed B OA algorithm.

1050   Discussion on B OA The main reason why the processing time of the proposed B OA increases
1051   with the size of LLMs is that the embedding dimension of attention heads increases with the model
1052
       size. Specifically, to compensate for the error incurred by the quantization of certain rows, B OA
       needs to sequentially quantize sub-weight matrix dh times where dh is the head dimension (see
1053
       Fig. 1(b)). Because dh increases with model size, the number of sequential quantizations also in-
1054
       creases, which leads to long processing time.
1055
1056
       Comparison with OmniQuant/AffineQuant The processing time of the proposed B OA is shorter
1057
       than those required by existing attention-aware algorithms that rely on gradient-based optimization,
1058
       yet achieving significantly better performance (see Tables 6 and 11). We note that OmniQuant does
1059   not take too much time for quantizing large LLMs even though it performs gradient-based optimiza-
1060   tion. This is because OmniQuant reduces the number of learnable parameters greatly to accelerate
1061   gradient-based optimization. Specifically, instead of learning a weight-rounding policy which re-
1062   quires to learn a large number of parameters, OmniQuant learns only a small number of quantiza-
1063   tion parameters and some parameters related to the equivalent transform (Shao et al., 2023). While
1064   this strategy accelerates the quantization process greatly, OmniQuant suffers from unstable quanti-
1065   zation process or collapses for low-bit quantization (see Tables 6 and 11). AffineQuant improves
1066   OmniQuant by introducing additional learnable parameters (Ma et al., 2024), but such additional
1067
       parameters result in huge processing time (4 times longer processing time; see Table 13(a)), which
       demonstrates the inefficiency of gradient-based optimization over the proposed method.
1068
1069
1070   Comparison with GPTQ The proposed B OA requires longer processing time and larger memory
1071
       than those required by GPTQ. This is because GPTQ quantizes all the rows of the weight matrix
       simultaneously using only layer input. In contrast, B OA sequentially quantizes sub-weight matri-
1072
       ces using outputs of other layers as well as layer input (see Fig. 1(b)) to consider the inter-layer
1073
       dependencies within the attention module, which eventually leads to the better quantization per-
1074   formance than GPTQ. Clearly, there is a trade-off between quantization speed / memory cost and
1075   accuracy. In real situations, when one needs to preserve the performance of the original model by
1076   considering inter-layer dependencies within the attention module, the proposed B OA would be an
1077   intriguing solution. Even when the memory resource is limited, B OA can be used with some relax-
1078   ation. Specifically, we note that the large memory cost of B OA for hyper-scale LLMs (e.g., 13B and
1079   30B) is attributable to the row-wise Hessian for the value projection (XATh Ah XT in (12)) whose
       shape is H × d × d (H is the number of attention heads and d is the embedding dimension). In
