                                                                A BSTRACT
029
030
                    Latent diffusion models (LDMs), such as Stable Diffusion, often experience sig-
031
                    nificant structural distortions when directly generating high-resolution (HR) im-
032                 ages that exceed their original training resolutions. A straightforward and cost-
033                 effective solution is to adapt pre-trained LDMs for HR image generation; how-
034                 ever, existing methods often suffer from poor image quality and long inference
035                 time. In this paper, we propose an Attentive and Progressive LDM (AP-LDM),
036                 a novel, training-free framework aimed at enhancing HR image quality while ac-
037                 celerating the generation process. AP-LDM decomposes the denoising process of
038                 LDMs into two stages: (i) attentive training-resolution denoising, and (ii) progres-
039                 sive high-resolution denoising. The first stage generates a latent representation of
040
                    a higher-quality training-resolution image through the proposed attentive guid-
                    ance, which utilizes a novel parameter-free self-attention mechanism to enhance
041
                    the structural consistency. The second stage progressively performs upsampling
042
                    in pixel space, alleviating the severe artifacts caused by latent space upsampling.
043                 Leveraging the effective initialization from the first stage enables denoising at
044                 higher resolutions with significantly fewer steps, enhancing overall efficiency. Ex-
045                 tensive experimental results demonstrate that AP-LDM significantly outperforms
046                 state-of-the-art methods, delivering up to a 5× speedup in HR image generation,
047                 thereby highlighting its substantial advantages for real-world applications.
048

052   Diffusion models (DMs) have demonstrated impressive performance in visual generation tasks, par-
053   ticularly in text-to-image generation (Ho et al., 2020; Nichol & Dhariwal, 2021; Podell et al., 2023;
      Esser et al., 2024; Mou et al., 2024; Zhang et al., 2023; Feng et al., 2023). One notable variant

067   of DMs is the latent diffusion model (LDM), which performs diffusion modeling in latent space to

069   While it is possible to modify the input size for higher-resolution generation, this often results in
070
      severe structural distortions, as illustrated in Fig. 2(a). Therefore, a recent research focus is on
      adapting trained LDMs for HR image generation without the need for additional training or fine-
071
      tuning (i.e. training-free manner), which can inherit the strong generation capacities of existing
072
      LDMs, especially open-sourced versions like Stable Diffusion.
073
074   Existing training-free approaches for HR image generation can be roughly categorized into three
075   types: sliding window-based, parameter rectification-based, and progressive upsampling-based.
076
      Sliding window-based methods first divide the HR image into several overlapping patches and use
      sliding window strategies to perform denoising (Bar-Tal et al., 2023; Haji-Ali et al., 2023; Lee et al.,
077
      2023). However, these methods could result in repeated structures and contents due to the lack of
078
      communication between windows; see Fig. 2(b). Parameter rectification-based methods attempt to
079   correct models’ parameters for better structural consistency through the entropy of attention maps,
080   signal-to-noise ratio, and dilation rates of the convolution layers (Jin et al., 2024; Hwang et al.,
081   2024; He et al., 2023). Though efficient, they often lead to the degradation of texture details; see
082   Fig. 2(c). Unlike the two types mentioned above, progressive upscaling-based methods are to it-
083   eratively upsample the image resolution, which maintains better structural consistency and shows
084   state-of-the-art (SOTA) performance (Du et al., 2024; Lin et al., 2024). Unfortunately, these methods
085   require fully repeating the denoising process multiple times, leading to an unaffordable computa-
086   tional burden; e.g., AccDiffusion takes 26 minutes to generate a 4096 × 4096 image. In addition,
087
      their upsampling operation in the latent space may introduce artifacts; see Fig. 2(d). To sum up,
      existing methods fail to ensure the fast, high-quality HR image generation.
088
089   In this paper, we propose the attentive and progressive LDM, termed AP-LDM, a novel, training-
090   free framework aimed at enhancing HR image quality while speeding up the generation process.
091   Specifically, AP-LDM decomposes the denoising process of LDMs into two stages: (i) attentive
092   training-resolution (TR) denoising, and (ii) progressive HR denoising. The first stage aims to gen-
093
      erate a latent representation of a high-quality image at the training resolution through the proposed
      attentive guidance, which is implemented via a novel parameter-free self-attention mechanism to
094
      improve structural consistency. The second stage aims to progressively upsample the resolution in
095
      the pixel space rather than latent space, which can alleviate the severe artifacts caused by the latent
096   space upsampling. By leveraging the effective initialization from the first stage, AP-LDM can per-
097   form denoising in the second stage with significantly fewer steps, enhancing the overall efficiency
098   with 5× speedup. Extensive experimental results demonstrate the effectiveness and efficiency of
099   AP-LDM in generating HR images over the state-of-the-art baselines.
100
      Contributions. The contributions of this work are summarized as follows. (i) We propose AP-
101
      LDM, a novel, training-free framework aimed at enhancing the HR high-quality generation while
102   accelerating the generation process. (ii) We propose attentive guidance, which can utilize a novel
103   parameter-free self-attention to improve the structural consistency of the latent representation to-
104   wards high-quality images at the training resolution. (iii) We propose progressively upsampling
105   the resolution of latent representation in the pixel space, which can alleviate the artifacts caused
106   by the latent space upsampling. (iv) Extensive experimental results demonstrate that the proposed
107   AP-LDM significantly outperforms the SOTA models in terms of image quality and inference time,
      emphasizing its great potential for real-world applications.

      2     R ELATED W ORK
109

      HR image generation with super-resolution.         An intuitive approach to generating HR images
111
      is to first use a pre-trained LDM to generate TR images and then apply a super-resolution model
112
      to perform upsampling (Wang et al., 2023; Zhang et al., 2021; Liang et al., 2021; Luo et al., 2024;
113   Wang & Zhang, 2024). Although one can obtain structurally consistent HR images in this way,
114   super-resolution models are primarily focused on enlarging the image, and shown to be unable to
115   produce the details that users expect in HR images (Du et al., 2024; Lin et al., 2024).
116
      HR image generation with additional training.            Existing additional training methods either
117
      fine-tune existing LDMs with higher-resolution images (Hoogeboom et al., 2023; Zheng et al., 2024;
118
      Guo et al., 2024) or train cascaded diffusion models to gradually synthesize higher-resolution im-
119   ages (Teng et al., 2023; Ho et al., 2022). Though effective, these methods require expensive training
120   resources that are unaffordable for regular users.
121
122
      HR image generation in training-free manner. Current training-free methods can be roughly
      classified into three categories: sliding window-based, parameter rectification-based, and progres-
123
      sive upsampling-based methods. Sliding window-based methods consider spatially splitting HR
124
      image generation (Bar-Tal et al., 2023; Haji-Ali et al., 2023; Lee et al., 2023). Specifically, they
125   partition an HR image into several patches with overlap, and then denoise each patch. However,
126   due to the lack of communication between windows, these methods result in structural disarray
127   and content duplication. While enlarging the overlaps of the windows mitigates this issue, it can
128   result in unbearable computational costs. For the parameter rectification-based methods, some re-
129   searchers discovered that the collapse of HR image generation is due to the mismatches between
130   higher resolutions and the model’s parameters (Jin et al., 2024; Hwang et al., 2024; He et al., 2023).
131   These methods attempt to eliminate the mismatches by rectifying the parameters such as the dilation
132   rates of some convolutional layers. While mitigating the structural inconsistency, they often lead
133
      to the degradation of image details. Different from the aforementioned two types, the progressive
      upsampling-based methods show SOTA performance in some recent studies (Du et al., 2024; Lin
134
      et al., 2024). Though promising, they require fully repeating the denoising process multiple times,
135
      which incurs unbearable computational overhead. Additionally, these methods perform upsampling
136   in the latent space, which may introduce artifacts.
137
138   These methods aforementioned fail to improve the quality of HR images and computational effi-
139
      ciency at the same time. In contrast to them, AP-LDM aims to enhance both HR images quality and
      the generation speed towards the real-world applications.

144   3.1   OVERVIEW OF AP-LDM
145
146   Fig. 3 presents the overview of AP-LDM, which can adapt a pre-trained LDM to generate HR
147   images without further training. Formally, a pre-trained LDM utilizes a denoising U-Net model F
148   to iteratively denoise the latent representation of size h × w × c, which is then converted back to the
149   pixel space for final image generation through the decoder D of a variational autoencoder (VAE).
150   We note that the initial latent representation is sampled from a Gaussian distribution ϵ ∼ N (0, I),
151   and for the inference the encoder E of VAE is not involved.
152   Our AP-LDM extends the pre-trained LDMs for higher-resolution image generation in a training-
153   free manner; i.e., E, D and F are fixed. AP-LDM achieves this by decomposing the standard denois-
154   ing process in the latent space into two stages: (i) attentive training-resolution (TR) denoising, and
155   (ii) progressive high-resolution (HR) denoising. In the first stage, AP-LDM aims to generate a latent
156   representation of a higher-quality TR image through the proposed attentive guidance. The atten-
157   tive guidance is implemented as linearly combining the novel parameter-free self-attention (PFSA)
158
      and the original latent representation to improve the structural consistency. In the second stage,
      AP-LDM uses the latent representation provided by the first stage as a better initialization, and it-
159
      eratively obtains higher-resolution images via the pixel space upsampling and diffusion-denoising
160
      refinement.
161
      We detail the attentive TR denoising in §3.2, followed by the progressive HR denoising in §3.3.

181   Motivation. Enhancing the structural consistency helps improve image quality (Si et al., 2024).
182   However, it is challenging to do this in a training-free manner. We observe that the self-attention
183   mechanism presents powerful global spatial modeling capability (Vaswani et al., 2017; Han et al.,
184   2021; Dosovitskiy et al., 2020; Liu et al., 2021), and this capability is parameter-agnostic. It is
185   determined by the paradigm of global similarity calculation inherent to the self-attention mecha-
186   nism (Vaswani et al., 2017; Zhou et al., 2024). These insights motivate us to consider designing a
187   novel parameter-free self-attention mechanism to elegantly enhance the global structural consistency
188
      of the latent representation.
189   Denoising with attentive guidance.        To improve the structural consistency of the latent repre-
190   sentation at the training resolution z ∈ Rh×w×c , we propose a simple yet effective parameter-free
191   self-attention mechanism for attentive guidance, termed PFSA, formulated as:

      3.3    P ROGRESSIVE H IGH -R ESOLUTION D ENOISING
231
232
      Motivation.       Fig. 2(a) shows that pre-trained LDMs still retain some ability to generate high-
233   frequency information when directly used to synthesize HR images, although they exhibit structural
234   disarray. Therefore, intuitively, we can utilize the latent representation produced by the first stage
235   as a structural initialization, and generate the HR images through the “upsample-diffuse-denoise”
236   iteration in the latent space. However, this pipeline leads to severe artifacts, as shown in Fig. 4(a).
237   We speculate that this is due to the upsampling of latent representations in the latent space.
238
      Pilot study. To examine this hypothesis, we conduct the following experiments. Specifically, we
239   randomly select 10k images from ImageNet (Deng et al., 2009) to create an image set P. For each
240   image x ∈ P, we perform the following operations to obtain three additional image sets: (i) x̂ = D◦
241   E(x), which use VAE to obtain the reconstructed image set Pref ; (ii) x̂ = up ◦D◦E ◦down(x), which
242   performs upsampling in pixel space to obtain the image set Ppix ; and (iii) x̂ = D ◦ up ◦E ◦ down(x),
243   which performs upsampling in latent space to obtain the image set Plat . Both upsampling up and
244   downsampling down are performed using bicubic interpolation. Fig. 4(b) reports the quantitative
245   results, where r represents the upsampling or downsampling rate. We calculate the PSNR and
246   SSIM for pixel space upsampling set Ppix and latent space upsampling set Plat with respective to the
247
      reference set Pref . It can be clearly observed that the latent space upsampling leads to a significant
      performance decline compared to pixel space upsampling. Fig. 4(c) to (e) shows upsampling in the
248
      pixel space produces images close to the reference while upsampling in latent space leads to severe
249
      artifacts and detail loss.
250

      Experimental settings. We use SDXL (Podell et al., 2023) as the pre-trained LDM and conduct
      inference using two NVIDIA 4090 GPUs. To ensure consistency when testing inference speed, we
276
      use a single NVIDIA 3090 GPU, aligning with other methods. We randomly sample 33k images
277
      from the SAM (Kirillov et al., 2023) dataset as the benchmark. Following the released code from
278   DemoFusion (Du et al., 2024), we use the EulerDiscreteScheduler (Karras et al., 2022) setting T0 =
279   50 and the classifier-free guidance (Ho & Salimans, 2022) scale to 7.5. Pixel space upsampling is
280   performed using bicubic interpolation, and the decay factor β is fixed at 3.
281
      Evaluation metrics. The widely recognized metrics FID (Heusel et al., 2017), IS (Salimans et al.,
282
      2016), and CLIP Score (Radford et al., 2021) are used to evaluate model performance. Additionally,
283
      since calculating FID and IS requires resizing images to 299 × 299, which may not be suitable
284   for evaluating HR images, we are inspired by (Du et al., 2024; Lin et al., 2024) to perform ten
285   1024 × 1024 window crops on each image to calculate FIDc and ISc .

      6   L IMITATIONS A ND F UTURE W ORK
506
507
      AP-LDM exhibits limitations in the following aspects: (i) Effectively controlling text in images is
      challenging, as demonstrated by examples in Fig. 11. This may be due to the inherent limitations of
508
      SDXL in generating textual symbols. Text, due to its more regular structure compared to other image
509
      content, is difficult to restore by directly enhancing the structural consistency of the latent represen-
510   tation. We speculate that the most reliable approach would be to fine-tune the model specifically on
511   images containing text. (ii) When generating ultra-high resolution images, such as 12800 × 12800,
512   the second stage of AP-LDM inevitably needs to be decomposed into more sub-stages, which in-
513   creases the model’s inference time.
514
      Developing a low-cost and effective fine-tuning method to correct text generation errors may be a
515
      promising direction. Moreover, adapting attentive guidance to other tasks, such as video generation
516   can be an interesting issue.

      7   C ONCLUSION
531
532
      In this paper, we propose an effective, efficient, and training-free pipeline named AP-LDM, capable
533
      of generating HR images with higher quality while accelerating the generation process. AP-LDM
534   divides the denoising process of an LDM into two stages: (i) attentive training-resolution denoising,
535   and (ii) progressive high-resolution denoising. The first stage aims to generate a latent representation
536   through the proposed attentive guidance, which enhances the structural consistency by leveraging a
537   novel parameter-free self-attention mechanism. The second stage iteratively performs upsampling
538   in the pixel stage, thus eliminating the artifacts caused by latent space upsampling. Extensive exper-
539   iments show that our proposed AP-LDM significantly outperforms SOTA models while achieving
      5× speedup in HR image generation.
