009                                                A BSTRACT
010
011             Large language models (LLMs) have revolutionized how we interact with technol-
012             ogy, but their personalization to individual user preferences remains a significant
013             challenge, particularly in on-device applications. Traditional methods often depend
014             heavily on labeled datasets and can be resource-intensive. To address these issues,
015             we present Adaptive Self-Supervised Learning Strategies (ASLS), which utilizes
016             self-supervised learning techniques to personalize LLMs dynamically. The frame-
017             work comprises a user profiling layer for collecting interaction data and a neural
018
                adaptation layer for real-time model fine-tuning. This innovative approach enables
                continuous learning from user feedback, allowing the model to generate responses
019
                that align closely with user-specific contexts. The adaptive mechanisms of ASLS
020
                minimize computational demands and enhance personalization efficiency. Experi-
021             mental results across various user scenarios illustrate the superior performance of
022             ASLS in boosting user engagement and satisfaction, highlighting its potential to
023             redefine LLMs as highly responsive and context-aware systems on-device.

028   Adaptive self-supervised learning strategies offer innovative methods for enhancing personalization
029   in on-device LLMs. Recent advancements reveal that larger models like GPT-3 and PaLM show
030   impressive few-shot learning capabilities but may still face limitations in understanding user intent
031
      and generating accurate and helpful outputs without adequate task-specific training or fine-tuning
      techniques (Brown et al., 2020; Chowdhery et al., 2022). For effective personalization, aligning
032
      models with user intent becomes crucial, as demonstrated in methodologies like InstructGPT, which
033
      enhances performance by leveraging human feedback (Ouyang et al., 2022).
034
035   The HYDRA framework captures both individual user behaviors and shared knowledge, enabling
036   personalized responses that outperform traditional prompt-based personalization methods (Zhuang
037
      et al., 2024). Additionally, leveraging user profiles can refine information retrieval processes, tailoring
      the interaction to better suit the user’s context and language preferences (Ravichandran & Gomasta,
038
      2024). In the domain of healthcare, integrating memory mechanisms within LLMs can facilitate
039
      personalized medical assistance, thus improving user experience and efficiency across interactions
040   (Zhang et al., 2023).
041
042   The transformative potential of LLMs extends to education, where their integration into social media
043
      platforms enhances communication efficiency and collaborative learning among students, indicating
      that adaptive personalization holds significant implications for various domains (Bashiri & Kowsari,
044
      2024). These strategies collectively contribute to a more dynamic, responsive, and user-centric
045
      interaction model in natural language processing applications.
046
047   However, the personalization of large language models on-device faces significant hurdles. The

049
      has shown notable improvements in sequence recommendation tasks, as evidenced by performance

050
      impact of pedagogical guidance and interaction strategies on learner outcomes highlights the ne-

      cessity for tailored support systems to enhance user confidence and trust in LLMs (Kumar et al.,

149   3     M ETHODOLOGY
150
151
152   To enhance the personalization of large language models (LLMs) on-device, we introduce Adaptive
153
      Self-Supervised Learning Strategies (ASLS), a framework that employs self-supervised learning to
      align LLMs with individual user preferences without necessitating extensive labeled datasets. ASLS
154
      features a dual-layer design, consisting of a user profiling layer for gathering interaction data and a
155
      neural adaptation layer for dynamic model fine-tuning based on that data. This continuous learning
156   process allows LLMs to provide tailored responses that cater to the specific contexts and requirements
157   of users. By incorporating adaptive mechanisms, ASLS effectively minimizes the computational
158   overhead and time associated with personalization efforts. Experiments conducted across a range
159   of user scenarios validate the approach, revealing notable enhancements in both user engagement
160   and satisfaction when contrasted with traditional personalization techniques. The results indicate the
161   promise of ASLS in evolving LLMs into more responsive and context-sensitive systems for improved
      on-device user experiences.

297   4.4      I MPLEMENTS
298
299   The experimental setup consists of a comprehensive design aimed at evaluating the effectiveness
300   of the Adaptive Self-Supervised Learning Strategies (ASLS) for on-device large language model
301
      personalization. We employ the Llama-3-7b model as our primary architecture, conducting our
      experiments across multiple user interaction scenarios. The training phase is conducted for a total of
302
      20 epochs, allowing for adequate adaptation of the model to the user-specific data profiles. A batch
303
      size of 16 is maintained through the training process to enable efficient real-time updates, while a
304   learning rate is set at 3e-5 to balance the trade-off between convergence speed and stability.
305
306   Additionally, we implement early stopping based on validation loss, with a patience factor set to 5
307
      epochs to prevent overfitting during the adaptation process. The reinforcement learning component
      operates under a reward structure with a discount factor of 0.9 to ensure timely updates based on user
308
      feedback, and we utilize a replay buffer of size 1000 to maintain a history of user interactions for this
309
      aspect of training. Each interaction is recorded with a light-weight logging mechanism that tracks user
310   engagement metrics in real-time. Our testing scenarios vary in complexity and we randomly select
311   500 personalized prompts to evaluate performance metrics after completing the training iterations.
312   The evaluation involves measuring user satisfaction and engagement improvements, utilizing a
313   comparative analysis framework against traditional personalization methods.

      6   C ONCLUSIONS
487
488
      We present Adaptive Self-Supervised Learning Strategies (ASLS) to improve the personalization of
489   large language models (LLMs) on user devices. This framework utilizes self-supervised learning
490   techniques to tailor responses to individual user preferences without relying heavily on labeled data.
491   The ASLS consists of two main components: a user profiling layer that gathers interaction data and a
492   neural adaptation layer that dynamically fine-tunes the model based on this data. This continuous
493   learning process allows the model to adjust in real-time to user feedback, resulting in contextually
494   relevant responses. Additionally, the adaptive mechanisms incorporated in ASLS minimize the
495   computational resources and time needed for effective personalization. Experiments conducted across
496   various user scenarios show that ASLS leads to enhanced user engagement and satisfaction compared
497
      to conventional personalization methods. Our research highlights ASLS’s ability to convert LLMs
      into more context-aware systems, thereby improving the overall on-device user experience.

689   A     L IMITATIONS
690
691   ASLS, while promising, has evident challenges. One limitation pertains to its reliance on user
692   interaction data, which may not be sufficient if the user does not frequently engage with the model.
693   This could hinder the personalization process, resulting in a lack of relevant updates to the user
694
      profile. Additionally, the effectiveness of the neural adaptation layer can vary significantly based on
      the diversity of user interactions; limited data diversity may lead to suboptimal performance(Xiao
695
      et al., 2024). Moreover, while ASLS aims to reduce computational resources, the initial setup
696
      and continuous updates could still require considerable processing power, especially in resource-
697   constrained devices. Future research should investigate strategies to enhance data collection methods
698   and efficiency in high-demand scenarios while further refining user profiling techniques to improve
699   responsiveness.
