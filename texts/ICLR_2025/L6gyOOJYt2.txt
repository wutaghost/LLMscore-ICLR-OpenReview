                                                  A BSTRACT
010
011
012
               In recommender systems, selection bias arises from the users‚Äô selective interac-
               tions with items, which poses a widely-recognized challenge for unbiased evalu-
013
               ation and learning for recommendation models. Recently, doubly robust and its
014
               variants have been widely studied to achieve debiased learning of prediction mod-
015            els. However, if the users and items in the training set are not exactly the same as
016            those in the test set, even if the imputed errors and learned propensities are accu-
017            rate, all previous doubly robust based debiasing methods are biased. To tackle this
018            problem, in this paper, we first derive the bias of doubly robust learning methods
019            and provide alternative unbiasedness conditions when users and items are sampled
020            from a superpopulation. Then we propose a novel superpopulation doubly robust
021            target learning approach (SuperDR), which is unbiased when either the imputation
022            model or propensity model is correctly specified. We further derive the general-
023
               ization error bound of the proposed method under superpopulation, and show that
               it can be effectively controlled by the proposed target learning approach. We con-
024
               duct extensive experiments on three real-world datasets, including a large-scale
025
               industrial dataset, to demonstrate the effectiveness of our method.

      1   I NTRODUCTION
030
031   In the era of information explosion, recommender system (RS) plays an increasingly important
032   role in areas such as e-commerce platforms, news reading, and social media. However, due to the
033
      subjective preferences of users and the data collection process itself, selection bias always exists in
      the collected data (Pradel et al., 2012), which poses a widely-recognized challenge (De Myttenaere
034
      et al., 2014; Marlin and Zemel, 2009). Ignoring selection bias makes RS difficult to provide accurate
035
      recommendations to users, thus hurting the user‚Äôs experience and reducing social welfare.
036
037   Many methods have been proposed to address selection bias. The error imputation based (EIB)
038   method (Chang et al., 2010; Marlin et al., 2007; Steck, 2010; 2013) utilizes an imputation model to
039
      impute the missing relevance. The inverse propensity score (IPS) method uses inverse propensity to
      reweight the observed events to achieve unbiasedness (Imbens and Rubin, 2015; Saito et al., 2020;
040
      Schnabel et al., 2016). The doubly robust (DR) method combines the error imputation model and
041
      the propensity model (Wang et al., 2019; Saito, 2020; Wang et al., 2022; Oosterhuis, 2023), which
042   is unbiased if either the imputed errors or the learned propensities are accurate, which is also proved
043   to has smaller variance compared to the IPS method (Saito, 2020; Oosterhuis, 2022).
044
045
      Although previous methods have demonstrated promising performance in debiasing tasks, their un-
      biasedness relies on the assumption that the test set contains exactly the same users and items as
046
      the training set. As illustrated in Figure 1, if the users and items in the training set are randomly
047
      sampled from a larger superpopulation, previous debiasing methods are unbiased only if the test set
048   contains exactly the same users and items, and are otherwise biased, even if the test set is another
049   random sampling and the imputed errors and learned propensities are correct. For example, for the
050   user side in the e-commerce platform, the training set contains users who are active and participate
051   in transactions, while the whole population is all registered users. One may argue that the training
052   set should contain all registered users, in this case, the whole population can be regarded as both the
053   registered and non-registered users. In other words, we can always assume that there exists a larger
      population without loss of generality. At this point, for a set of users that have not appeared in the

      To this end, in this paper, we first derive the bias of doubly robust learning methods and provide al-

      2   R ELATED W ORK
094
095
      There are various biases in the data collected from RS (Chen et al., 2020; Wu et al., 2022), which
096   have been of increasing concern in recent years (Ai et al., 2018; Saito and Nomura, 2022; Liu et al.,
097   2021; Zhang et al., 2021; Luo et al., 2021; Liu et al., 2022; Lin et al., 2023). Selection bias is one of
098   the most common biases in RS and a lot of research has been done aiming to eliminate this kind of
099   bias (Chen et al., 2021; Guo et al., 2021; Liu et al., 2020; Saito, 2020; Schnabel et al., 2016; Wang
100   et al., 2019). The error imputation based method (EIB) (Chang et al., 2010; Marlin et al., 2007;
101   Steck, 2010; 2013) first imputes pseudo-labels for missing events from the observed events, and
102   then leverages these pseudo-labels to train the prediction model (DudńĪŐĀk et al., 2011; Marlin et al.,
103   2007; Steck, 2013; Wu et al., 2022). An alternative way to eliminate selection bias is to weight
104
      the inverse propensity score (IPS) on the observed data to eliminate bias (Imbens and Rubin, 2015;
      Saito et al., 2020; Schnabel et al., 2016). However, IPS will suffer from a large variance when the
105
      extreme values exist in the estimated propensities (Thomas and Brunskill, 2016).
106
107   The doubly robust (DR) method improves the weakness of EIB and IPS methods and becomes the
      mainstream model due to the weaker unbiasedness conditions and smaller variance than the IPS


                                                         2
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


108
      method (Benkeser et al., 2017; Morgan and Winship, 2015; Luo et al., 2021; Li et al., 2023d; Saito,
109   2020; Wang et al., 2019). In particular, the DR estimator is unbiased when either learned propensities
110   or imputed errors are accurate. Many augmented DR methods are developed to further enhance the
111   previous DR method performance by modifying the propensity model and imputation model or
112   the form of the DR estimator, such as MRDR (Guo et al., 2021), BRD-DR (Ding et al., 2022),
113   StableDR (Li et al., 2023d), TDR (Li et al., 2023b), DR-MSE (Dai et al., 2022), and DR-BIAS (Dai
114   et al., 2022). However, these approaches are limited to the use of deterministic error imputation
115   and propensity models and fail to be unbiased when using probabilistic models to impute errors and
116   learn propensities. To the best of our knowledge, this is the first paper that extends previous widely
117
      adopted DR methods to be compatible with probabilistic error imputation and propensity models.

472   6                   C ONCLUSION
473
474   In this paper, we addressed the critical issue of selection bias in recommender systems when users
475   and items in the training and test sets are sampled from a larger superpopulation. We demonstrated
476   that traditional doubly robust methods, though effective under certain unbiasedness conditions under
477
      a finite population, are biased when the training and test sets do not contain exactly the same users
      and items even if the imputed errors and learned propensities are correct. To overcome this limita-
478
      tion, we introduced a novel approach, Superpopulation Doubly Robust Target Learning (SuperDR),
479
      which is underpinned by a comprehensive theoretical framework. Specifically, we first derive the
480   bias in existing doubly robust estimators has two terms: a covariance term and a term that mea-
481   sures the accuracy of learned propensities and imputed errors. Then we establish new conditions for
482   unbiasedness in the superpopulation scenario. Moreover, we derived a generalization error bound
483   for SuperDR, demonstrating the practical applicability in terms of unbiased learning. In addition,
484   we conducted extensive experiments on three real-world datasets, including a large-scale industrial
485   dataset, and empirically validated the effectiveness of SuperDR in delivering unbiased and accurate
      recommendations. One of the potential limitations and research direction is how to develop a tighter

      bound for control the empirical covariance and to develop a more efficient algorithm for alternatively
487   update the prediction model, the imputation model, and the target learning parameter.
