009                                                     A BSTRACT
010
011             Score distillation sampling (SDS), the methodology in which the score from pre-
012             trained 2D diffusion models is distilled into 3D representation, has recently brought
013             significant advancements in text-to-3D generation task. However, this approach is
014             still confronted with critical geometric inconsistency problems such as the Janus
015             problem. Starting from our observation that such inconsistency problems are in-
016             duced by multiview inconsistencies between 2D diffusion scores predicted from
017
                various viewpoints, we introduce Geometry-aware Score Distillation (GSD), a
                simple and general plug-and-play framework for incorporating 3D consistency and
018
                therefore geometry awareness into the SDS process. Our methodology is composed
019
                of three components: 3D consistent noising, designed to produce 3D consistent
020             noise maps that follow the standard Gaussian distribution, geometry-based gradient
021             warping for identifying correspondences between predicted gradients of different
022             viewpoints, and gradient consistency loss to optimize the scene geometry toward
023             producing more consistent gradients. We demonstrate that our plug-and-play tech-
024             nique applied on various baseline score distillation-based methods significantly
025             improves performance, successfully addressing the geometric inconsistency prob-
026             lems with minimal computation cost.

047   1     I NTRODUCTION
048
049   Text-to-3D generation, which is the task of generating a 3D scene from a text prompt, has seen great
050   advancements in recent years due to the advent of powerful generative models such as diffusion
051   model (Ho et al., 2020; Song et al., 2020). As the main objective of this task is to generate a
052   high-quality 3D model solely from user-given text, it enables even non-professional users to create
053   3D models easily with little to no handwork. Naturally, advancements in this task have opened up
      numerous possibilities in various domains such as VR/AR, computer-generated graphics, and gaming.


                                                                 1
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


054
      However, due to the limited size and quantity of 3D ground truth datasets compared to 2D images
055   or videos, directly training a diffusion model on 3D representations is challenging. To address this,
056   most methods (Jain et al., 2022; Lin et al., 2023; Chen et al., 2023a; Wang et al., 2023) use pretrained
057   2D diffusion models to optimize 3D representations (Mildenhall et al., 2020; Müller et al., 2022;
058   Kerbl et al., 2023) through score distillation sampling (SDS) (Poole et al., 2023), where the 3D
059   representation is refined using the 2D diffusion model’s score from noised scene renderings at various
060   viewpoints. However, since the 2D diffusion model lacks explicit knowledge of 3D domain, it often
061   results in geometric inconsistencies like the Janus problem (Seo et al., 2024; Shi et al., 2023), where
062   multi-faced geometries harm the global shape, making it unsuitable for real-world applications.
063   To understand and counter this issue, we analyze the SDS process from the perspective of multiview
064   consistency, observing that such geometric inconsistency problem is correlated to the independence
065   of each SDS process, which in turn causes the lack of multiview consistency between 2D scores
066   predicted from different viewpoints. More specifically, we focus on the fact that under the naiv̈e
067   SDS setting (Poole et al., 2023), a single point in 3D receives vastly different optimization signals
068   from various viewpoints, resulting artifacts and geometrically inconsistent geometric features such
069
      as Janus problem. Under this observation, encouraging the multiview consistency of 2D diffusion
      scores between nearby viewpoints would lead to reduction in such artifacts.
070
071   In this light, we propose a novel methodology, named Geometry-aware Score Distillation (GSD),
072   which incorporates multiview correspondence awareness to the SDS process to facilitate multiview
073   consistency of predicted gradients, as described in Fig. 1. Our method is a plug-and-play module
074   that can be attached to existing SDS-based baselines for enhanced geometric consistency, with little
075
      computation cost and no need for additional networks or modules. Our method consists of three
      components. First, to encourage multiview consistency of predicted 2D scores across viewpoints, we
076
      introduce 3D consistent noising, combining point cloud representation with integral noising (Chang
077
      et al., 2024) to produce 3D geometry-aware 2D Gaussian noises in SDS process. Our 3D consistent
078   noising imbues separate SDS denoising processes implicitly with 3D awareness. Secondly, we
079   propose geometry-based gradient warping to warp the generated gradient of a viewpoint to other
080   viewpoints, allowing for the comparison of gradients between corresponding locations across various
081   viewpoints. We finally leverage the warped gradients for our novel multiview gradient consistency
082   loss, which helps to regularize and reduce inconsistent scene features such as the Janus problem.
083
      Our experimental results and analysis show that the application of our methodology strongly benefits
084   the optimization process across various SDS-based text-to-3D baselines (Yi et al., 2023; Tang et al.,
085   2024; Poole et al., 2023). Our methodology enhances the geometric consistency and fidelity of the
086   generated results, resulting 3D scenes competitive to state-of-the-art. Our ablation study demonstrates
087   that our contributions are strongly interconnected, justifying the need for all our components to be
088   used in conjunction with one another.

093   Text-to-3D generation. DreamFusion (Poole et al., 2023) and SJC (Wang et al., 2022) introduced
094
      an optimization technique called score distillation sampling (SDS), which leverages pretrained

095
      been widely adopted in various text-to-3D generation models. Magic3D (Lin et al., 2023) and

183   4.1    M OTIVATION AND OVERVIEW
184
      In the standard SDS process (Poole et al., 2023; Wang et al., 2022; 2023), the 2D noise n is sampled
185
      independently per viewpoint. This raises questions about cases where two sampled viewpoints are
186   close together, resulting in the rendered images zθ,π overlapping regions. Under the SDS setting, the
187   different renderings of the overlappings would result in largely unrelated 2D scores for supervision,
188   as the noises n are sampled independently. Put simply, it lacks multiview consistency. Our work
189   starts from this observation that such a lack of multiview consistency induces geometric inconsistency
190   problems such as the Janus problem. We seek to counter this problem by incorporating geometric
191   awareness into the SDS process (Poole et al., 2023; Wang et al., 2022; 2023).
192   Assume a mapping function W(·) that holds the 3D correspondences between viewpoints. Given the
193   explicit 3D geometry represented by θ, we can obtain W(·) by identifying which locations in 2D
194   renderings correspond to the same point in 3D space, establishing geometry-based correspondence
195   across different viewpoints. This W(·) can then be used to map an image from one viewpoint
196   to another in a geometrically consistent way – a process known as warping. Intuitively, applying
197   Wj→i (·) to the noise nj ∼ N (0, I) at viewpoint πj and mapping it to nearby viewpoint πi would
198   result in multiview-consistent noise Wj→i (nj ) for zθ,πi . We observed that this approach ultimately
