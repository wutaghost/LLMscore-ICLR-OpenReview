009                                                A BSTRACT
010
011            Federated learning facilitates collaborative model training across multiple dis-
012            tributed clients without requiring data sharing. However, conventional federated
013            methods struggle with classification tasks in an unsupervised paradigm due to the
014            absence of category knowledge. Recently, CLIP, a prominent visual language
015            model, has demonstrated impressive results, particularly its remarkable zero-shot
016            classification ability, which alleviates the dependence on labeled data. In this pa-
017            per, we first explore a new realistic problem, unsupervised federated learning us-
018
               ing CLIP, where clients with unlabeled heterogeneous data collaborate to enhance
               global performance. To address this problem, we propose FedPP, a method that
019
               incorporates a cooperative pseudo-label selection strategy and a partial prompt
020
               aggregation protocol. Our selection strategy ensures that all classes are trained
021            in a balanced manner through global pseudo-label allocation. Concurrently, the
022            aggregation protocol divides parameters into aggregated and retained components
023            to optimize global performance while supporting local personalization. Extensive
024            experiments across six datasets with various types of heterogeneity demonstrate
025            the effectiveness of FedPP. Our code is available in the supplementary materials.

028   1    I NTRODUCTION
029
030   Federated Learning (McMahan et al., 2017) is a distributed machine learning framework that enables
031   decentralized collaboration among clients without sharing their local training data. For instance, in
032   multi-institutional healthcare collaborations, participating clients (e.g., medical institutions and hos-
033
      pitals) can collaboratively train powerful models without leaking patient information (Liu et al.,
      2021). The conventional federated algorithms typically operate within a system composed of clients
034
      with labeled data, following an iterative process of local training at the client level and global aggre-
035
      gation at the server level (McMahan et al., 2017; Sheller et al., 2020; Li et al., 2020; Luo et al., 2021;
036   Zhang et al., 2023b; Guo et al., 2024). Additionally, researchers have begun to apply federated tech-
037   niques in unsupervised learning settings (Zhang et al., 2023a; Tian et al., 2024; Nardi et al., 2024;
038   Lu et al., 2022; Lubana et al., 2022). Existing unsupervised federated methods primarily concen-
039   trate on leveraging self-supervised algorithms for representation learning tasks and clustering tasks.
040   However, the absence of category knowledge presents a significant challenge for classification tasks
041   involving unlabeled datasets in federated learning.
042   Recently, pre-trained vision-language models (VLMs), such as CLIP (Radford et al., 2021), have
043   shown remarkable representation and generalization capabilities across various downstream tasks,
044   such as image classification (Cho et al., 2023; Kan et al., 2023; Liang et al., 2024), semantic seg-
045   mentation (Wang et al., 2022; Xu et al., 2022; Liang et al., 2023), and object detection (Esmaeilpour
046   et al., 2022; Chen et al., 2024). Benefiting from pre-training on large-scale image-text pairs, CLIP
047   has powerful zero-shot classification capability. This is achieved by utilizing the textual encoder to
048   generate classifier weights from a simple prompt, such as “a photo of a [CLASS]”. The impressive
049   zero-shot prediction capability of CLIP eliminates the reliance on labeled data, opening new avenues
050
      for unsupervised federated learning.
051   In this paper, we investigate a novel and realistic problem, unsupervised federated learning with
052   CLIP, where clients leverage unlabeled data to collaborate on complex problems such as image clas-
053   sification. In this setting, each client is equipped with an identically initialized pre-trained CLIP and
      the names of target categories. Similar to standard federated learning frameworks, clients periodi-

054
      cally upload their non-data knowledge (e.g., statistical information, model parameters) to the server
055   for aggregation and download global knowledge in return. However, due to the nature of unsuper-
056   vised learning and the federated paradigm, label skews among clients can easily lead to negative
057   transfer and aggregation failure.
058
      To address this problem, we propose an unsupervised federated method called FedPP. FedPP incor-
059
      porates two key components, the cooperative pseudo labels selection (CPS) strategy and the partial
060
      prompt aggregation (PPA) protocol. The CPS strategy focuses on selecting reliable pseudo labels
061   that enhance both local training and global aggregation for each client. Specifically, to improve per-
062   formance across all categories, we globally select pseudo labels by category and distribute them to
063   participating clients based on their estimated pseudo label distribution. This approach mitigates the
064   category bias caused by the inherent bias of CLIP, improving the accuracy of the pseudo labels.
065
      Bisides, the PPA protocol uploads only visual prompts to the server for aggregation, while keeping
066
      textual prompts retained locally to enhance personalization and avoid aggregation conflicts. The
067   reason for only choosing visual prompts for aggregation is that visual prompts often learn general
068   representations of the image domain while textual prompts tend to learn category-related information
069   which may introduce conflicts due to label skews. Consequently, global performance is improved
070   through the aggregated visual prompts, while clients benefit from personalized textual prompts better
071   suited to their data distribution. Experimental results across standard federated prompt learning
072   benchmarks with both Dirichlet-based and quantity-based label skews demonstrate the effectiveness
073   of the proposed FedPP method. Our main contributions are summarized as follows:
074
             • We introduce unsupervised federated learning with CLIP, a novel and realistic problem
075
               where clients with unlabeled data collaborate to improve model performance.
076
077
             • To address the unsupervised federated learning problem, we propose FedPP, which com-
               prises two key components: cooperative pseudo labels selection, which ensures balanced
078
               training across all classes through global pseudo-label allocation, and partial prompt aggre-
079
               gation, which separates parameters into aggregated and retained components.
080
             • Extensive experiments across six datasets with different types of heterogeneity demonstrate
081
               the effectiveness of FedPP.

085
086   2.1   V ISION -L ANGUAGE M ODELS
087
      Recently, vision-language models (VLMs) such as CLIP (Radford et al., 2021), ALBEF (Li et al.,
088
      2021), BLIP (Li et al., 2022a), and Flamingo (Alayrac et al., 2022), pre-trained on large-scale image-
089   text data, have achieved significant success in zero-shot learning (Lu et al., 2019; Wang et al., 2024b;
090   Liang et al., 2024; Wang et al., 2023; Kim et al., 2021; Zhang et al., 2024b), in-context learn-
091   ing (Zhou et al., 2024; Doveh et al., 2024), open-world segmentation (Tang et al., 2024; Ha & Song,
092   2022), and open-world detection (Wang et al., 2024a). Furthermore, the performance of VLMs
093   can be enhanced through fine-tuning with annotated data from downstream datasets (Zhou et al.,
094   2022b;a; Udandarao et al., 2023). For instance, CoOp (Zhou et al., 2022b) learns textual prompts
095   for downstream tasks via back-propagation on few-shot datasets, while CoCoOp (Zhou et al., 2022a)
096   incorporates visual information into textual prompts for regularization, improving base-to-new gen-
097
      eralization performance. Additionally, CLIP-Adapter (Gao et al., 2024) proposes learning both a
      visual and a textual adapter to refine the original representations of vision-language models. In this
098
      paper, we primarily focus on the performance of CLIP as a representative VLM in downstream tasks.
099

      2.2   F EDERATED LEARNING WITH V ISION -L ANGUAGE M ODELS
101
102   Federated Learning (FL) (McMahan et al., 2017) has emerged as a pivotal paradigm for decentral-
103   ized training of machine learning models on heterogeneous data, preserving data privacy and reduc-
104   ing data transfer overhead (Qu et al., 2022; Li et al., 2023b; Guo et al., 2024; Shi et al., 2023; 2024).
105   Recently, the fine-tuning of Vision-Language Models (VLMs) has been extended to the federated
106   framework to alleviate the computational load on individual devices while addressing challenges in
107   federated learning, such as subpar performance and robustness in cross-domain scenarios, as well
      as non-IID data distributions among clients (Qiu et al., 2023; Li & Wang, 2024; Halbe et al., 2023;


                                                         2
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


108
      Su et al., 2024; Yang et al., 2023). For example, FedCLIP (Lu et al., 2023) directly extends the
109   standard fine-tuning of CLIP to the federated setting to achieve strong performance and personaliza-
110   tion. PromptFL (Guo et al., 2023b) introduces a federated learning framework for prompt learning,
111   enabling participants to collaboratively learn a common prompt vector. pFedprompt (Guo et al.,
112   2023a) combines a federated prompt learning scheme with personalized spatial visual features. Ad-
113   ditionally, pFedPG (Yang et al., 2023) generates personalized prompts for each client based on their
114   visual prompts to better align with their data distribution. FedOPT (Li et al., 2024) utilizes knowl-
115   edge from both personal and global textual prompts for prediction through non-uniform optimal
116   transport. However, previous methods have primarily focused on supervised federated learning with
117
      VLMs. In this paper, we further explore leveraging VLMs for unsupervised federated learning,
      capitalizing on their zero-shot capabilities.

120   2.3   U NSUPERVISED L EARNING FOR VISION - LANGUAGE MODELS
121
122   In real-world applications, high annotation costs are often required to ensure that each data source
123   has labeled data. This necessity drives us to develop effective methods for utilizing unlabeled data
124   in downstream tasks. Pseudo-labeling strategy (Huang et al., 2022; Menghini et al., 2023; Zhang
125
      et al., 2024a; Jia et al., 2024; Tanwisuth et al., 2023) and entropy minimization (Liang et al., 2024)
      are widely studied. For instance, UEO (Liang et al., 2024) leverages sample-level confidence to
126
      minimize the conditional entropy of confident instances while maximizing the marginal entropy of
127
      less confident ones. POUF (Tanwisuth et al., 2023) fine-tunes the model or prompt by aligning the
128   discrete distributions derived from the prompts and unlabeled target data. UPL (Huang et al., 2022)
129   and FPL (Menghini et al., 2023) select an equal number of pseudo-labels for each category, while
130   CPL (Zhang et al., 2024a) generates multiple pseudo labels for each sample to enhance labeling
131   accuracy. However, existing pseudo labeling strategies have proven challenging to apply directly
132   to the zero-shot predictions of VLMs in federated settings (Huang et al., 2022). In this paper, we
133   propose a novel cooperative pseudo labels selection strategy to mitigate the category bias introduced
134   by the inherent biases of pre-trained VLMs and significantly improve the accuracy of pseudo labels.

180   3.2     U NSUPERVISED H ETEROGENEOUS F EDERATED P ROBLEM FOR VLM S
181
182   The powerful zero-shot classification capabilities of CLIP alleviate the requirement of labeled data
183   in federated learning scenarios. We investigate an unsupervised heterogeneous federated learning
184   problem involving a central server and K remote clients, as illustrated in Figure 1 (a). Each client
185
      possesses an unlabeled local dataset Dk with a capacity of nk and an identically initialized CLIP.
      Similar to standard federated learning frameworks, clients periodically upload their non-data knowl-
186
      edge (e.g., statistical information, model parameters) to the server for aggregation and download
187
      global knowledge in return. A challenge of the problem is heterogeneity, characterized by      S label
188   skew in the unlabeled datasets across clients. Specifically, the union of all clients’ datasets k Dk
189   encompasses data from all categories, while Dk may only represent a subset of these categories.
190   Additionally, the number of samples for each category can vary among clients. Figure 1 (b) and (c)
191   illustrate client data distribution under Dirichlet-based and quantity-based label skews, respectively.
192   Further details on heterogeneity can be found in Sec. 4.1. Label skews among clients’ unlabeled
193   datasets can lead to negative transfer and pose significant risks to federated aggregation.

303   4.1   S ETUPS
304
305   Datasets. We evaluated the performance of our method on six public benchmark datasets char-
306   acterized by varying types of label skew. Following previous research (Guo et al., 2023a; Li et al.,
307   2024; Cui et al., 2024), we utilized four representative visual classification datasets: DTD (Cim-
308   poi et al., 2014), RESISC45 (Cheng et al., 2017), UCF101 (Soomro, 2012), and CUB (Wah
309
      et al., 2011), along with two standard federated learning benchmark datasets: CIFAR10 and CI-
      FAR100 (Krizhevsky et al., 2009). We partitioned each dataset into distinct training and test sets,
310
      which were subsequently divided into non-overlapping subsets for different clients. To construct
311
      sample sets with label skew, we followed the settings outlined in (Li et al., 2022b) and employed
312   two prevalent forms of label skew: quantity-based and Dirichlet-based. In the quantity-based label
313   skew, all training data is grouped by label and allocated into shards with imbalanced quantities. The
314   parameter s signifies the number of shards per client, regulating the level of label skew (Lee et al.,
315   2022). In the Dirichlet-based label skew, clients receive samples for each class based on the Dirich-
316   let distribution (Zhu et al., 2021). Here, the parameter β controls the degree of label skew, with
317   lower values indicating greater label skews.

321   confidence for each class, while FPL (Menghini et al., 2023) generates multiple pseudo-labels for
322   each sample through selection at both the sample and category levels. Zero shot learning involves
323   using the pre-trained CLIP model with a hand-crafted textual prompt template, such as “a photo of a
      [CLASS],” to predict on the test data. For supervised federated learning methods, PromptFL (Guo

357
      as our base model. We set the number of clients, K, to 5 for the CUB and UCF101 datasets,
358   and to 10 for the other datasets, implementing full client participation by default. We conduct 20
359   communication rounds for all experimental datasets, regenerating pseudo-labels using the updated
360   local model every 5 communication intervals. Within each communication round, local training
361   spans 10 epochs. We optimize the prompts using mini-batch Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD)
362   with a learning rate of 0.1, a momentum of 0.9, and decay following the cosine annealing rule. For
363   our proposed method, we set the global number of pseudo-labels for each class configured to one-
364   quarter of the total data from all clients. Following the approach in FedOPT (Li et al., 2024), we
365   conduct three trials for each experimental setting and report the mean accuracy. All experiments are

      5   C ONCLUSION
475
476
477
      In this paper, we introduce an unsupervised federated learning problem with CLIP, where clients
      with unlabeled data employ collaborative training for better performance without data sharing. To
478
      address such problem, we propose FedPP, an unsupervised solution including a cooperative pseudo
479
      labels selection strategy and a partial prompt aggregation protocol. The pseudo labels selection
480   strategy allows the server to customize the selection process for each client, taking into account
481   both local and global pseudo labels distributions. The aggregation protocol only aggregates visual
482   prompts on the server for performance improvements through collaboration among clients and tex-
483   tual prompts are kept locally for better personalization by each client. Extensive results demonstrate
484   the effectiveness of both components, and proposed FedPP outpergorms baseline methods across
485   diverse datasets and various degrees of label skews. In future work, we will conduct a theoretical
      analysis of FedPP, including convergence, privacy, fairness, and other pertinent considerations.
