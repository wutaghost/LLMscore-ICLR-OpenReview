011                                               A BSTRACT
012
013            Recently, diffusion models have shown potential in 3D generation tasks, and the
014            novel view synthesis (NVS) task, a bridge between 2D and 3D generation, has
015
               received great attention. The goal of the NVS task is to generate multi-view
               images from reference images, and the core challenge is to maintain the 3D
016
               consistency between different view images. Recent works construct large 3D
017
               consistency multi-view image datasets and utilize the supervised fine-tuning (SFT)
018            method to improve the 3D consistency. However, the SFT method suffers from the
019            distribution shift, data inefficient problems, and lacks theoretical insight. To solve
020            these problems, we discuss how to provide a suitable direction to the multi-view
021            models and achieve better performance. More specifically, we first analyze the
022            training-free guidance-based method and prove that contrastive guidance, which
023            contains ground-truth and generated samples, can provide the right direction to
024            improve 3D consistency. Based on the theoretical insight, we further design a
025            contrastive 3D consistency metric and use it as the feedback in the following phase.
026
               To avoid the distribution shift problem, we use direct preference optimization
               (DPO) to fine-tune the multi-view diffusion models. Through qualitative and
027
               quantitative experiments, we demonstrate that after the fine-tuning phase with
028
               the above method, the 3D consistency of the multi-view images is significantly
029            improved and achieves better performance compared to the SFT method.

032   1    I NTRODUCTION
033
034   Recently, diffusion models have achieved impressive performance in 2D generation areas (Rombach
035   et al., 2022; Podell et al., 2023; Karras et al., 2022) and show potential in the 3D generation domain
036   (Poole et al., 2022; Zhang et al., 2024; Sun et al., 2023; Zhang et al., 2023). Since the 3D data is
037   expensive and existing 3D datasets are not sufficient to directly train 3D diffusion models, many
038   recent works focus on generating multi-view images of an object given a reference image, which is
039
      called novel view synthesis (NVS) (Liu et al., 2023b;a; Shi et al., 2023a; Long et al., 2024). We note
      that this task bridges 2D generation and 3D reconstruction. For the training phase, multi-view models
040
      can fully use the 2D prior information of large-scale diffusion models. For the 3D reconstruction
041
      task, a large reconstruction model uses the multi-view images as an input and outputs a 3D object.
042
043   An important point of the NVS task is to maintain the 3D consistency between different view images.
044   There are two common methods to generate samples with certain properties, such as 3D consistency:
045
      (1) adding guidance to a pre-trained model and (2) fine-tuning the pre-trained model using a designed
      metric. Currently, most of existing multi-view diffusion models adopt the second method to improve
046
      the 3D consistency. More specifically, most works utilize supervised fine-tuning (SFT) with perfect
047
      3D consistency multi-view image rendering from a 3D object (Liu et al., 2023a; Shi et al., 2023a;
048   Liu et al., 2023b; Shi et al., 2023b; Long et al., 2024). Though the SFT method can improve the 3D
049   consistency, it suffers from a distribution shift problem, which diminishes diversity and realism of
050   the results (Li et al., 2023). Similar to the large language model, Xie et al. (2024) use reinforcement
051   learning fine-tuning (RLFT) to improve the 3D consistency and avoid the distribution shift problem.
052   However, their reward model relies heavily on NeRF reconstruction, which is time inefficient. To deal
053   with this problem, Xie et al. (2024) use a closed-source sparse-view large reconstruction model (Li
      et al., 2023) to generate the 3D object, which is unfriendly to users. Furthermore, their 3D consistency

      metric leaks theoretical guarantee, and it is unclear which component plays an important role in 3D

      consistency. Hence, the following natural question remains open:
070
071   It is possible to design a lightweight, effective, and theoretically interpretable 3D consistency feedback
072   and use it to improve the 3D consistency of the pre-trained models?
073   In this work, we start with the guidance-based method to provide the theoretical insight of 3D
074   consistency. Then, we extend to the direct preference optimization (DPO)-based method and fine-
075   tune the pre-trained model to achieve great performance. The guidance-based method aims to provide
076   a correct direction to the pre-trained model without training. Though this method performs well
077   in 2D generation areas (Ho and Salimans, 2022; Dhariwal and Nichol, 2021; Bansal et al., 2023),
078   the suitable guidance for multi-view models to generate 3D consistent samples is unclear. In this
079   work, for the first time, we study the the design principles of guidance in the 3D generation. We first
080
      show that the universal guidance Bansal et al. (2023), which only uses the self-generated samples,
      can not provide a correct direction for the 3D consistency. To solve this problem, we design a
081
      contrastive guidance and prove that it can improve the 3D consistency of multi-view diffusion models.
082
      More specifically, we view the ground-truth sample as the positive 3D consistency sample and
083   self-generated samples as the negative samples. Then, we show that the positive samples increase
084   the 3D consistency component, and the negative samples reduce the influence of other components,
085   which leads a correct direction for improving the 3D consistency.
086
      Though the guidance-based method is training-free, it can not improve the diffusion models unlimit-
087
      edly (Guo et al., 2024). To achieve better performance, we design a 3D consistency feedback and use
088
      it to fine-tune the multi-view diffusion models. Since the reference image and the ground-truth images
089   of other views render from the same object, these images are more consistent than the generated pair
090   (reference and generated images). Inspired by our contrastive guidance, we view the ground-truth
091   pairs as positive and generated pairs as negative pairs and train a contrastive feedback model. Since
092   it is hard to guarantee absolute accuracy for trained feedback, we use the DPO method, which
093   only requires a relative preference between different generated samples, to fine-tune the multi-view
094   diffusion models. After the fine-tuning phase with the MV-D3PO-C method (the DPO method with
095   the contrastive feedback for multi-view models), the 3D consistency of multi-view models has been
096   significantly improved from qualitative and quantitative perspectives. In conclusion:
097
               • We study the design principle of the suitable guidance for 3D consistency and prove that
098
                 contrastive guidance with suitable positive and negative pairs can provide a right direction
099              for improving 3D consistency.
100
               • Inspired by the theoretical analysis, we design a contrastive metric to represent the 3D
101
                 consistency. Taking advantage of the above metric, we provide the MV-D3PO-C method
102
                 and show that it significantly improves the 3D consistency of multi-view models.

304   In this section, we start from a 3D object generation task X03D ∼ q03D ∈ RD (In this work, we use
305   X 3D and dimension D represent 3D data (3D objects).) and discuss how to design suitable guidance
306   for leading the 3D model to generate the required image given a certain view. This operation is
307   similar to "rendering" a certain view image from a 3D object. If designed guidance can achieve this
308   goal, it can generate 3D consistency multi-view images since these images come from the same
309   object. Hence, we view the design of this guidance as the first step to improve 3D consistency.

395   5     C ONTRASTIVE 3D C ONSISTENCY F EEDBACK FOR M ULTI - VIEW M ODELS
396
397   As shown in the above section, contrastive 3D guidance can help a 3D object to render a certain view.
398   Since the different view images rendering from a 3D object is 3D consistent, we think the intuition of
399   contrastive guidance can be shared with the multi-view generation task. However, the multi-view
400   generation task does not have a generated 3D object, which means we can not calculate ∇X03D
401   and ∇Xt3D . An executable solution sharing a similar idea is to design a 3D consistency feedback
402   and fine-tune the pre-trained multi-view models. In this part, we show the procedure to obtain a
403   contrastive 3D consistency feedback.
404   The contrastive training paradigm requires positive and negative pairs. To guarantee the 3D consis-
405   tency of positive pairs, we use ground-truth multi-view images and the reference image as positive
406   pairs. Then, we use images generated by Zero123 (Liu et al., 2023a) and reference images as the
407   negative samples. Since DINOv2 has shown its 3D awareness (El Banani et al., 2024), we use it as
408   an encoder to obtain the embedding of the reference images eY , the ground-truth other view images
409   eXGT
         m and other view images generated by zero123 eX m . Then, we use a linear layer f to calculate

411
      to obtain our evaluation model:
412                                                          2                      2
                        L3D−Consistency = ∥f (eY , eXGT
                                                     m ) − 1∥ + ∥f (eY , eX m ) − 0∥ .
                                                             2             0        2

414   In the training phase of the contrastive metric, we fine-tune DINOv2 and the linear layer to achieve a
415
      better result. In the evaluation phase, we calculate the contrastive score between the reference image
      and m-th generated other view image and sum up as our final contrastive score.

520   7   C ONCLUSION
521
522   In this work, we study the design principle of 3D consistency feedback of multi-view diffusion
523   models from the theoretical and empirical perspectives at the same time. For the theoretical insight,
524   we analyze the guidance-based method and design contrastive guidance consisting of ground-truth
525   images and generated images. Then, we prove that it is necessary to use contrastive guidance instead
526   of previous universal guidance to provide a correct direction for improving the 3D consistency.
527
      To achieve better performance in application, we go beyond the training-free guidance-based method,
528   propose the DPO-based method MV-D3PO and show that this method can alleviate the distribution
529   shift problem of the SFT method. For the MV-D3PO method, we propose two modified versions,
530   the matching feedback version and the contrastive feedback version and show that the multi-view
531   images generated by the MV-D3PO-C are more 3D consistent compared to MV-D3PO-M, which also
532   supports our theoretical results.
533
534   Future work and Limitation. In this work, we show the DPO-based method can effectively
535   improve the 3D consistency of multi-view diffusion models with suitable feedback. Except for the
536
      3D consistency problem, there are also some other problems, such as distorted fingers or facial
      features. It is an interesting future work to design suitable feedback for these properties and use the
537   MV-D3PO method to solve these problems. Furthermore, our work focuses on the 3D consistency
538   of the multi-view diffusion model. It is an interesting future work to achieve an end-to-end 3D
539   consistency 3D reconstruction by using a well-trained large sparse-view large reconstruction model
      with our contrastive 3D consistency feedback.

683   The Construction of Different Multi-view Pairs. Since our multi-view model is not a text-to-3D
684
      model, we can not construct negative pairs by using negative text prompts (Yang et al., 2024). To
      generate different multi-view pairs with the same reference images, as shown in Fig. 2, we add
685
      different scale noise to the embedding of reference images. In this work, we add 5 scale pure noise
686
      anoise N (0, I) with anoise ∈ {0, 0.1, 0.3, 0.5, 0.8} to the embedding of the reference image.
687
688   Training Detail of MV-D3PO. For the pre-train model, we fine-tune all parameters of reference-
689   only attention and Stable Diffusion XL on the Objaverse dataset for 400 A100 days.
690
691
      For the MV-D3PO fine-tuning phase, we first run the pre-trained model to collect data and construct
      different multi-view pairs. More specifically, we run the pre-trained models with different noise
692
      scale reference images and save πpre (ak |sk ) , ∀k ∈ [K] information. In this process, we collect 240
693
      datapoints as the training dataset of the fine-tuning phase. After that, we fine-tune the attention layers
694   of Stable Diffusion XL and reference-only attention for 1800 optimization steps, which need 4 hours
695   on 1 V100. Similarly to the ImageReward (Xu et al., 2024), we only fine-tune the last 40 denoising
696   steps of the denoising step.
