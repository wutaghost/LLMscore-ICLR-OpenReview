009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            Establishing semantic correspondences between images is a fundamental yet chal-
012            lenging task in computer vision. Traditional feature-metric methods enhance
013            visual features but may miss complex inter-image relationships, while recent
014            correlation-metric approaches attempt to model these relationships but are hin-
015            dered by high computational costs due to processing 4D correlation maps. We in-
016
               troduce MambaMatcher, a novel method that overcomes these limitations by effi-
               ciently modeling high-dimensional correlations using selective state-space models
017
               (SSMs). By implementing a similarity-aware selective scan mechanism adapted
018
               from Mamba’s linear-complexity algorithm, MambaMatcher refines the 4D cor-
019            relation tensor effectively without compromising feature map resolution or re-
020            ceptive field. Experiments on standard semantic correspondence benchmarks
021            demonstrate that MambaMatcher achieves state-of-the-art performance without
022            relying on large input images or computationally expensive diffusion-based fea-
023            ture extractors, effectively capturing rich inter-image correlations while maintain-
024            ing computational efficiency.

      1   I NTRODUCTION
027
028
      Establishing semantic correspondences between images is a fundamental problem in computer vi-
029
      sion, with wide-ranging applications in augmented and virtual reality (AR/VR), such as virtual
030   try-on (Kim et al., 2023), edit propagation (Endo et al., 2016; Peebles et al., 2022), and instance
031   swapping (Zhang et al., 2024). The task involves identifying semantically corresponding regions
032   between pairs of images depicting different instances of the same class (Cho et al., 2015; Min et al.,
033   2019; Truong et al., 2022). Despite significant advancements in deep learning, reliably establishing
034   semantic correspondences remains challenging due to substantial intra-class variations, including
035   differences in pose, scale, and appearance among instances.
036   Current state-of-the-art methods predominantly adopt a feature-metric approach, enhancing the
037   quality of features at each spatial position in the images. This enhancement is achieved by either (i)
038   employing more powerful feature extractors (Tang et al., 2023; Zhang et al., 2024) or (ii) improving
039   feature representations through aggregation with additional convolutional or attentional layers (Seo
040   et al., 2018; Lee et al., 2021b; Luo et al., 2024). While richer features can robustly identify semantic
041   similarities across local pixels, feature-metric methods may struggle to capture complex inter-image
042   relationships due to their focus on individual feature points.
043   An alternative is the correlation-metric approach, where methods aim to model inter-image rela-
044   tionships by processing the 4D correlation map between features from the two images (Rocco et al.,
045   2018; Min & Cho, 2021; Cho et al., 2021; Kim et al., 2022). Although this approach can alleviate
046   ambiguous or noisy correspondences by considering global correlation patterns, it suffers from sig-
047   nificant computational complexity. Processing the 4D correlation map incurs up to quartic complex-
048   ity with respect to the feature map dimensions, which severely limits the feature map resolution and
049   necessitates compromises on the receptive field or network expressivity—critical factors for accurate
050
      and robust correspondences. Consequently, despite their potential, correlation-metric methods are
      often outperformed by feature-metric methods that utilize stronger backbones and higher-resolution
051
      images (Luo et al., 2024; Hedlin et al., 2024; Li et al., 2023; Tang et al., 2023; Zhang et al., 2024).
052
053   In this paper, we propose MambaMatcher, a novel approach that overcomes the limitations of both
      feature-metric and correlation-metric methods by efficiently modeling high-dimensional correlation

054
      maps using selective state-space models (SSMs). To the best of our knowledge, MambaMatcher is
055   the first method to treat multi-level correlation scores at each position in the correlation map as a
056   state in a state-space model, enabling effective and efficient modeling of inter-image correlations. At
057   the core of MambaMatcher is a similarity-aware selective scan mechanism, which adapts Mamba’s
058   linear selective scanning algorithm to refine the 4D correlation tensor with linear complexity. This
059   mechanism allows us to robustly and scalably process the correlation map without compromising
060   on feature map resolution or receptive field, thereby capturing rich inter-image relationships while
061   maintaining computational efficiency.
062   The key contributions of our work are summarized as follows:
063
064          • We introduce MambaMatcher, the first method to model high-dimensional correlation maps
065            using selective state-space models, treating multi-level correlation scores as states to effec-
066            tively capture inter-image correlations.
067          • We propose a novel similarity-aware selective scan mechanism, enabling efficient and ac-
068            curate mining of inter-image correlations at high resolutions.
069          • MambaMatcher seamlessly integrates feature-metric and correlation-metric approaches
070            into a unified pipeline, leveraging the strengths of both methods without compromising
071            feature map resolution or receptive field.
072          • Extensive experiments demonstrate that MambaMatcher achieves state-of-the-art perfor-
073            mance on standard semantic correspondence benchmarks, outperforming methods that rely
074            on expensive Diffusion-based features, while incurring lower computational overhead.

      2   R ELATED W ORK
077
078
079   Feature-metric approach for semantic correspondence. Semantic correspondence methods that
080   adopt the feature-metric approach prioritize producing high-quality features to establish robust cor-
081   respondences. Traditional feature-metric methods (Liu et al., 2010; Bristow et al., 2015; Cho et al.,
082   2015; Ham et al., 2017) typically use hand-crafted descriptors (Lowe, 2004; Dalal & Triggs, 2005;
083   Bay et al., 2006), which, despite their simplicity, show satisfactory performance. With the advent
084
      of deep learning, recent methods demonstrate that using local features extracted from deep neu-
      ral networks leads to significant performance improvements (Min et al., 2020; Tang et al., 2023;
085
      Luo et al., 2024; Li et al., 2023). While ResNets (He et al., 2016) were the conventional choice
086
      for the visual feature extractor, more recent works propose employing stronger feature extractors
087   such as DINOv2 (Oquab et al., 2023) or Stable Diffusion (Rombach et al., 2022). In the presence
088   of supervision, there are attempts to yield richer features by refining the extracted features, e.g.,
089   by using additional convolutional or attentional layers (Seo et al., 2018; Lee et al., 2021b; Huang
090   et al., 2022). In our method, we leverage DINOv2 for its strong feature extraction capabilities and
091   refine these features using 2D convolutional layers tailored to enhance correspondence accuracy.
092   However, MambaMatcher takes a step further by harmoniously integrating the correlation-metric
093   approach through our proposed similarity-aware selective scan, effectively modeling the correlation
094   space and outperforming methods that use Stable Diffusion (Rombach et al., 2022) features.
095
096   Correlation-metric approach for semantic correspondence. Methods that adopt the correlation-
097   metric approach aim to refine ambiguities and noise in the correlation map so that the refined map
098   can be used to establish more robust and accurate correspondences. In the context of semantic corre-
099
      spondence, NCNet (Rocco et al., 2018) first established this idea via a 4D convolutional network to
      consider neighborhood consensus, which motivated follow-up work to formulate neighborhood con-
100
      sensus in more effective or efficient ways (Li et al., 2020; Min & Cho, 2021; Lee et al., 2021a; Kim
101
      et al., 2024). However, high-dimensional convolutional kernels are constrained by their local recep-
102   tive field and static transformations. To address this, methods such as CATs (Cho et al., 2021; 2022)
103   and TransforMatcher (Kim et al., 2022) apply the self-attention mechanism to the correlation tensor
104   to consider inter-correlation relations in a dynamic global fashion. Despite their efficacy, applying
105   self-attention to the correlation map incurs up to quartic computational complexity with respect to
106   the feature map dimensions. This necessitates a compromise on either the feature map dimensions,
107   the receptive field of inter-correlation relationship mining, or the expressivity of the algorithm used,
      leading to sub-optimal results compared to the recent success of feature-metric approaches.

      In our work, we introduce a novel approach that models the correlation space using selective state-
109   space models, applying a similarity-aware selective scan to the correlation tensor. Building upon the
110   efficiency and scalability of Mamba (Gu & Dao, 2023), this method effectively overcomes previous
111   limitations by avoiding compromises on feature map dimensions, receptive field, or network expres-
112   sivity. Our approach facilitates a harmonious integration of feature-metric and correlation-metric
113   techniques into a single pipeline, advancing the modeling of the correlation space.
114
115   State-space models for computer vision. State-space models use state variables to describe a sys-
116   tem via a set of first-order differential or difference equations and were introduced into deep learning
117   for sequence modeling (Gu et al., 2021b; Smith et al., 2022). The efficient leveraging of state-space
118   models in deep learning gained rapid interest with the advent of Mamba (Gu & Dao, 2023), which
119   showed promising results compared to transformer-based architectures for sequence modeling in
120   natural language processing. Notably, Mamba exhibits linear computational complexity at infer-
121
      ence, in contrast to attention-based methods that typically have quadratic complexity. This has
      inspired the application of the Mamba model to computer vision. VMamba (Liu et al., 2024b), Vi-
122
      sion Mamba (Zhu et al., 2024), and PlainMamba (Yang et al., 2024) concurrently propose adopting
123
      the selective scan algorithm to the 2D image domain by varying the scan directions to accommo-
124   date spatial dimensions. These endeavors show competitive or superior performance compared to
125   existing methods in the computer vision domain, motivating the application of Mamba to various
126   downstream vision tasks such as video understanding (Li et al., 2024; Chen et al., 2024), medical
127   imaging (Yue & Li, 2024; Ruan & Xiang, 2024), and point cloud understanding (Liu et al., 2024a).
128   In our work, we extend the selective scan algorithm from Mamba by introducing a similarity-aware
129   selective scan specifically designed to refine 4D correlation tensors. This adaptation enables us to
130   effectively model the correlation space using selective state-space models, allowing for seamless
131   handling of high-dimensional data in semantic correspondence tasks. By tailoring the selective scan
132
      to be similarity-aware, our method differs from previous applications by directly addressing the
      challenges of refining 4D correlation maps, which is critical for accurate semantic correspondence.

137   State-space models (SSM) can be viewed as linear time-invariant (LTI) systems that maps a 1D
138   function or sequence x(t) ∈ R 7→ y(t) through a hidden state h(t) ∈ RN . These models are math-
139   ematically formulated as linear ordinary differential equations (ODEs), with weighting parameters
140
      of A ∈ RN×N , B ∈ RN×1 , C ∈ R1×N and D ∈ R:
141                                         h′ (t) = Ah(t) + Bx(t),
                                                                                                          (1)
142                                          y(t) = Ch(t) + Dx(t)
143

144
      state that memorizes the sequence history. This is accomplished by tracking the coefficients of a
145   Legendre polynomial, allowing the HiPPO matrix to approximate all of the previous history.
146
147   The S4 and Mamba are based on discrete versions of Eq.1, which include a timescale parameter
148   ∆ to transform the continuous parameters A, B to discrete parameters A, B. The commonly used
149
      method for this transformation is the zero-order hold (ZOH), where the discretized result is:
150                                    A = exp(∆A)
151                                                                                                       (2)
                                       B = (∆A)−1 (exp(∆A) − I) · ∆B

191   We provide an overview of MambaMatcher in Fig. 1. Given a pair of images, we first extract
192   multi-level feature maps from both images using a visual feature extractor. We then enhance these
193   features using a simple yet effective convolutional feature aggregation module (Sec. 4.1). Next, we
194   construct a multi-level correlation map from these features. This correlation map is refined using our
195   correlation aggregation layers based on our novel similarity-aware selective scan (Sec. 4.2). Using
196   the ground-truth source keypoints, we transfer them through the refined correlation map to obtain
197   the predicted target keypoints (Sec. 4.3). Finally, we train the entire network by comparing these
198
      predicted keypoints with the ground-truth target keypoints (Sec. 4.4).

367   PASCAL (Ham et al., 2017) and SPair-71k (Min et al., 2019) datasets. The results are shown in
368   Table 1, where MambaMatcher outperforms existing methods on both datasets, without relying on
369   particularly large image sizes or computationally expensive backbones like Stable Diffusion. More-

      over, our method incurs a reasonable computational overhead in terms of latency and memory usage.

372   final refined correlation map Ĉ better localizes keypoints, as indicated by higher similarity scores

      5.3   A NALYSIS ON FEATURE AND CORRELATION AGGREGATION
452
453
      Feature aggregation analysis. Table 4 presents the comparative performance when using different
454   feature aggregation schemes, evaluated on the small subset of SPair-71k. As we extract multi-level
455   features and compute multi-level correlation maps, we obtain a single-level refined correlation map
456   after feature aggregation using a single 1×1 convolution layer in these experiments. Using no feature
457   aggregation (’None’) defaults to using the extracted features directly. When applying Mamba2D , we
458   flatten the multi-level feature map F ∈ R2L×H×W to a sequence in R2L×(H×W ) , which is then input
459   to a Mamba layer. We also experiment with bi-directional selective scans, considering that an image
460   does not have a fixed beginning or end, unlike a temporal sequence. The Z-order curve (Morton,
461   1966) is a representative space-filling curve that forms a path passing through every point in a high-
462   dimensional discrete space while preserving spatial proximity, which has been effective in prior
463
      work (Wu et al., 2023; Liang et al., 2024) and is applicable to the scan order of Mamba. Among
      convolution-based, attention-based, and Mamba-based feature aggregation schemes, we find that
464
      using a series of 2D convolutional layers with a kernel size of 5 performs the best.
465
466   Correlation aggregation analysis. Table 5 illustrates the comparative performance when using dif-
467   ferent correlation aggregation schemes, evaluated on the small subset of SPair-71k. Based on the
468   results of the feature aggregation comparison (Table 4), we default the feature aggregation scheme
469
      to 2D Convk=5 for these experiments. Applying a vanilla transformer (Dosovitskiy et al., 2020) to
      the correlation tensor results in out-of-memory errors even on a single batch on an RTX 3090 GPU;
470
      therefore, we opt for FastFormer (Wu et al., 2021) as the linear-complexity attention-based corre-
471
      lation aggregation scheme (Kim et al., 2022). When applying Mamba4D , we flatten the multi-level
472
      correlation map C ∈ RL×H×W ×H×W to a sequence in RL×(H×W ×H×W ) , which is then input to
473   a Mamba layer. ’Ascending order’ and ’Descending order’ indicate that the flattened correlation
474   tensor is sorted in either ascending or descending order based on the similarity scores from the final
475   correlation map of the multi-level correlation map. Among different schemes, we find that process-
476   ing a descending-order sorted multi-level correlation map shows to be the best alternative to induce
477   similarity-awareness in the selective scan algorithm, verifying the design choices of MambaMatcher.
478   Fig. 4 compares different correlation aggregation schemes, showing that MambaMatcher establishes
479   more robust and accurate semantic correspondences under large viewpoint or scale variations. We
480   provide qualitative comparisons between different selective scanning schemes in Fig. 5. We include
481
      further analyses and comparisons in Appendix C, D, E and J.

527   6       C ONCLUSION
528
529   In conclusion, we introduced MambaMatcher, a novel approach for semantic correspondence that
530   models the high-dimensional correlation space using selective state-space models (SSMs), treating
531   multi-level correlation scores as states within the correlation map. By leveraging the efficiency of
532   Mamba’s linear-complexity algorithm and implementing a similarity-aware selective scan mecha-
533   nism, MambaMatcher effectively refines 4D correlation tensors without compromising feature map
534   resolution or receptive field. Our evaluations on standard benchmarks demonstrate that Mamba-
535   Matcher significantly enhances keypoint localization by inducing high similarity values near true
536
      keypoint positions, outperforming existing methods while maintaining computational efficiency.
      This work not only advances the state of the art in semantic correspondence but also highlights
537
      the potential of applying SSMs to high-dimensional data, encouraging further exploration into inte-
538
      grating feature-metric and correlation-metric approaches in visual correspondence tasks.
