010
011
               Large language models achieve significant improvements in instruction following
012
               through training with synthetic data. The self-instruct method generates instruc-
013
               tions based on manually selected tasks, establishing an annotation-free paradigm
014            for synthesizing instructions. However, the experience of synthesizing language
015            instructions for LLMs does not directly transfer to visual instruction generation.
016            Visual instructions encompass both images and questions, and questions generated
017            directly from images often struggle to form high-quality instructions. By analyz-
018            ing real user queries, we summarize the characteristics of high-quality instruc-
019            tions: they require image perception, reasoning, and answerability. We propose
020            a three-stage visual instruction generation pipeline, named ”Seeking the Right
021            Question” (SRQ), to produce high-quality instructions. In stage 1, we select 160
022
               instructions that meet high-quality standards as seed questions, categorizing them
               into eight groups based on multi-modal task types. In stage 2, we introduce
023
               capability-driven prompting to generate high-quality questions. In stage 3, we
024
               implement an Image Dependency Scoring Mechanism to filter the generated ques-
025            tions. Additionally, we use GPT-4o to directly generate answers, forming <image,
026            question, answer> triples for model training. To demonstrate the effectiveness of
027            SRQ, we construct the high-quality instruction dataset Allava-SRQ from 125,000
028            images sampled from the Allava dataset. Experimental results show that Allava-
029            SRQ significantly improves the performance of multiple baseline models across
030            various benchmarks. We plan to open-source SRQ and the high-quality instruc-
031            tion dataset Allava-SRQ to promote advancements in the field of visual instruction
032            generation.

035   1   I NTRODUCTION
036
037   Recent advancements in large language models (LLMs) have yielded substantial improvements in
038   instruction-following ability through the utilization of synthetic data (Wang et al., 2022; 2023; Singh
039
      et al., 2023; Li et al., 2023c). Among these, self-instruct (Wang et al., 2022) exemplifies the gen-
      eration of instructions derived from deliberately curated tasks, thereby establishing a paradigm for
040
      annotation-free synthetic instruction generation. However, the methodologies employed in synthe-
041
      sizing language instructions for LLMs cannot be directly extrapolated to visual instruction tasks,
042   which inherently encompass both images and associated queries. Typically, queries generated from
043   images present challenges in formulating high-quality instructions. Through an analysis of authentic
044   user inquiries, we identify key characteristics of high-quality instructions: the necessity for image
045   perception, the requirement for reasoning, and the provision of definitive answers. Specifically,
046   high-quality instructions must not rely solely on textual questions devoid of image context, as this
047   would reduce the visual language model (LVLM) to a mere LLM. Furthermore, effective instruc-
048   tions should not merely solicit the identification of image content but should instead necessitate
049   reasoning, as failing to do so risks devolving into basic image captioning tasks. Lastly, the questions
050
      posed must be answerable to ensure their utility in training contexts.

053   image captioning task and fails to enhance the model’s ability to follow instructions as effective

      Existing methodologies predominantly generate questions based on input images. LLaVA (Liu et al.,
084
      2024c) is the first to propose the generation of visual instruction data with the assistance of GPT-
085   4V (Achiam et al., 2023). Following this, Allava (Chen et al., 2024a) employes a caption-then-QA
086   framework, wherein GPT-4V first generates captions for the input image, after which a language
087   model formulates multiple questions based on these captions. Although these methods have proven
088   effective on certain benchmarks, a significant gap remains between the generated questions and
089   those encountered in real-world scenarios. To address this limitation, MMinstruct (Liu et al., 2024d)
090   introduces enhancements by randomly sampling a subset of examples from a curated seed question
091   pool, thereby offering robust prompts for the large language model (LLM) and facilitating the gen-
092   eration of a diverse array of questions. Subsequently, it selects images from a library based on the
093
      relevance between the questions and the images to create the final inquiries. However, the questions
      produced by MMinstruct are susceptible to leaking image content, resulting in some questions be-
094
      ing answerable without reference to the images and thereby not fully addressed. Additionally, the
095
      entire instruction generation process heavily relies on the capabilities of the LLM, which makes it
096   prone to hallucinations and errors, thus rendering the effective filtering of high-quality instructions
097   a significant challenge.

      2.3   I MAGE D EPENDENCY S CORING M ECHANISM
246
247   As described in 3 (stage 3), we filter generated questions based on the characteristics of high-quality
248   instructions, which include the necessity for image perception, the requirement for reasoning, and
249   the provision of definitive answers. To achieve this, we propose an Image Dependency Scoring
250   Mechanism. We determine whether the generated questions rely on image perception and whether
251   the answers depend on the image content, scoring the questions accordingly. The specific scoring
252
      criteria are as follows:
253
            1. The question cannot be answered based on the information in the image (score = 1).
254
255
            2. The question can only be roughly answered based on the information in the image, requir-
               ing considerable inference (score = 2).
256
257         3. The question can be partially answered based on the information in the image, but key
258            details are missing and inference is required (score = 3).
259         4. The question can be mostly answered based on the information in the image, needing only
260            a few inferred details (score = 4).
261         5. The question can be completely answered based on the information in the image without
262            inference or assumption (score = 5).
263
264   Scores ranging from 1 to 5 represent the quality of the question from low to high.
265   Regarding the requirement for reasoning in instructions, most questions generated under the guid-
266   ance of high-quality seed questions necessitate either complex or simple reasoning. Concerning the
267   provision of definitive answers, even the most advanced LVLMs struggle to generate accurate re-
268   sponses for all questions (Tong et al., 2024a). Explicit judgment may lead to hallucinations, making
269   it difficult for the LVLM to assess its ability to answer a question, often resulting in forced responses.

      Multimodal Data Construction. High-quality visual instruction data is an essential component
506
      while training LVLMs. In the field of LLMs, Self-Instruct (Wang et al., 2022) introduces a semi-
507
      automated process, enabling the generation of arbitrarily large datasets. However, visual instruction
508   data necessitates the alignment of language directives with image content. InstructBLIP (Dai et al.,
509   2023) converts existing visual-language datasets into an instruction-tuned format. LLaVA (Liu et al.,
510   2024b), VisionLLM (Wang et al., 2024), and Shikra (Chen et al., 2023b) employ GPT prompts
511   during data generation, but these approaches are often constrained by whether the dataset includes
512   captions or bounding boxes, limiting their broad applicability. ShareGPT4V (Chen et al., 2023c)
513   utilizes GPT-4V to generate high-quality image captions and expands the dataset to 1.2 million
514   examples. ALLava (Chen et al., 2024a) leverages GPT-4V’s ability to generate detailed captions,
515   complex reasoning instructions, and in-depth image-based answers to create a synthetic dataset.
516
      Genixer (Zhao et al., 2023) further investigated whether self-instruction could be achieved without
      relying on GPT-4V’s capabilities MM-Instruct (Liu et al., 2024d) innovatively uses ChatGPT to
517
      automatically generate diverse instructions from a small set of seed instructions. Despite significant
518
      efforts in data construction, a fully automated pipeline that can generate and evaluate high-quality
519   data simulating real-world conditions has yet to be realized.
520
521   LLM-as-a-Judge. The use of ”LLM-as-a-Judge” prompts has become a common method for evalu-
522
      ating language models, with benchmarks like LLavaBench (Liu et al., 2023a) and MMVet (Yu et al.,
      2023) relying on LLMs for scoring. Self-Rewarding Language Models (Yuan et al., 2024) advance
523
      this by incorporating self-scoring mechanisms as a reward model to aid in training. However, some
524
      studies (Huang et al., 2023) have pointed out that LLMs still struggle with consistent self-correction.
525   This problem is even more pronounced in vision-language models, where even the most advanced
526   systems often fail at image recognition tasks that humans find trivial (Tong et al., 2024b). This raises
527   further concerns about the reliability of using large models as evaluators in visual instruction tasks.

532
533
534   In this paper, we propose Seeking the Right Question(SRQ), a method for automatically generat-
535   ing high-quality visual instructions based on the content of input images. This approach utilizes
536   Capability-Driven Prompting for Question Generation to ask questions about the image from mul-
537   tiple perspectives. It then applies the Image Dependency Scoring Mechanism to directly filter the
538   generated questions, resulting in a set of high-quality visual instruction data. Experiments have
539   shown that training various models on this dataset significantly improves their performance, demon-
      strating the effectiveness of our method.
