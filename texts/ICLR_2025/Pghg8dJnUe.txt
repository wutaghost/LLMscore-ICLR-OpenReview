009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            Current random feature models typically rely on ﬁxed activation functions, lim-
012            iting their ability to capture diverse patterns in data. To address this, we intro-
013            duce the Random Feature model with Learnable Activation Functions (RFLAF),
014            a novel model that signiﬁcantly enhances the expressivity and interpretability of
015            traditional random feature (RF) models. We begin by studying the RF model with
016
               a single radial basis function, where we discover a new kernel and provide the
               ﬁrst theoretical analysis on it. By integrating the basis functions with learnable
017
               weights, we show that RFLAF can represent a broad class of random feature mod-
018
               els whose activation functions belong in Cc (R). Theoretically, we prove that the
019            model requires only about twice the parameter number compared to a traditional
020            RF model to achieve the signiﬁcant leap in expressivity. Experimentally, RFLAF
021            demonstrates two key advantages: (1) it performs better across various tasks com-
022            pared to traditional RF model with the same number of parameters, and (2) the
023            optimized weights offer interpretability, as the learned activation function can be
024            directly inferred from these weights. Our model paves the way for developing
025            more expressive and interpretable frameworks within random feature models.

      1   I NTRODUCTION
028
029
      Kernel methods are powerful tools for solving nonlinear learning problems by leveraging kernel
030   functions to implicitly map data into high-dimensional spaces. However, they can be computation-
031   ally intensive and lack scalability with large datasets. The random feature (RF) model, introduced
032   by Rahimi & Recht (2008b), offers a solution by approximating kernel functions with a ﬁnite num-
033   ber of random features, allowing the application of linear algorithms while maintaining the kernel’s
034   essence (Li et al., 2021; Liu et al., 2021).
035
      Despite their advantages, random feature models typically use a ﬁxed activation function, limiting
036   their adaptability during data ﬁtting. This rigidity prevents the model from automatically searching
037   for activation functions for optimal performance across various tasks. Meanwhile, recent work such
038   as Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks (KANs) (Liu et al., 2024b) has witnessed the powerful expressiv-
039   ity and interpretability of the structure of learnable activation functions. Therefore, it is natural to
040   consider enhancing random feature models by incorporating trainable activation functions.
041
      Current methods involve parametrizing learnable activation functions with splines(Liu et al., 2024b;
042   Fakhoury et al., 2022; Bohra et al., 2020; Aziznejad & Unser, 2019). However, the piecewise deﬁ-
043   nition of splines precludes the extraction of a closed-form analytic kernel, hindering the derivation
044   of theoretical insights. Moreover, models equipped with splines may face difﬁculty with efﬁcient
045   convergence. For instance, KANs uses LBFGS with line search instead of common gradient descent
046   method to boost convergence.
047
      In this paper, we propose the Random Feature models with Learnable Activation Functions
048   (RFLAF), a innovative random feature model that parametrizes learnable activation functions us-
049   ing weighted sums of radial basis functions (RBFs) (Section 2). RBFs offer several beneﬁts: the
050   regularity of RBFs allows for the derivation of the kernel’s analytic form, enabling further theoreti-
051   cal analysis. In addition, RBFs and their derivatives are easily computed, which may facilitate the
052   convergence of the model. This integration aims to improve the expressivity of the class of random
053   feature models, while ensuring simple implementation and optimization of the model. Moreover,
      the introduction of trainable activation functions naturally adds interpretability to the model. Our

      study offers a comprehensive analysis of the model, covering both its theoretical foundations and
055   the empirical validations. Our contributions are summarized as follows.
056
057          • We identify an unexplored kernel through studying RFLAF of the special case activated by
058            a single RBF. We provide the ﬁrst result on the analytic form of this kernel, and investigate
059            its representation and approximation characteristics. We demonstrate that a single RBF as
060            activation does not necessarily result in stronger expressivity of the RF model (Section 3).
061          • For general RFLAF, we develop rigorous analyses on approximation and generalization.
062            Our theories guarantee that our model boosts its representational capacity at a little cost of
063            less than double parameter number (Section 4), and the number of random features only
064            need to scale with the square root of the sample size (Section 5).
065          • Experimental results verify our theoretical ﬁndings (Section 6). Our model not only sur-
066            passes random feature models of other activation functions across different tasks, but is
067            also simple to implement and shows rapid convergence rate with standard gradient descent
068            optimizers. RFLAF successfully reveal the unknown activation functions through learning,
069            presenting superior interpretability.

      sample complexity, and Section 6 presents experimental results to validate our theories.

076   1.1   R ELATED WORK
077
      Random feature (RF) models. Random feature model (Rahimi & Recht, 2008b; 2007) is initially
078
      motivated by the fact that randomization is computationally cheaper than optimization (Amit &
079   Geman, 1997; Moosmann et al., 2006). By virtue of the relations between a kernel and its Fourier
080   spectral density, random features act as a technique to scale up kernel methods (Lopez-Paz et al.,
081   2014; Sun et al., 2018; Jacot et al., 2018; Arora et al., 2019b; Zandieh et al., 2021; Du et al., 2019;
082   Zambon et al., 2020; Choromanski et al., 2020; Peng et al., 2021). When viewed as a class of
083   two-layer neural networks with ﬁxed weights in the ﬁrst layer, random feature models provide deep
084   insights for partly understanding deep neural networks (Cao & Gu, 2019; Arora et al., 2019a; Mei
085   et al., 2022; Chizat & Bach, 2020). Research effort has been devoted to deriving approximation
086   and generalization bounds with respect to random feature number and sample size (Sutherland &
087
      Schneider, 2015; Rudi & Rosasco, 2017). Li et al. (2021) contribute to a uniﬁed analysis of random
      Fourier features.
088
089
      Learnable activation functions. Several work proposes the notion of learnable activation func-
090
      tion and attempts to combine it into the structure of neural network. Activation functions are
091
      parametrized in a continous way as splines (Liu et al., 2024b;a; Fakhoury et al., 2022; Bohra et al.,
092   2020; Aziznejad & Unser, 2019), polynomials (Goyal et al., 2019), sigmoid linear unit (Ramachan-
093   dran et al., 2017) and neural network (Zhang et al., 2022), or in a dicrete way (Bingham & Miikku-
094   lainen, 2022). The similar notion of RBF network introduced by Lowe & Broomhead (1988) is
095   fundamentally distinct from our model. In our work, RBFs are used for universal approximation,
096   whereas the RBF network applies them for functional interpolation.

415   Experimental results show several advantages of our model:
416
417   Enhanced expressivity. Figure 1 shows that RFLAF signiﬁcantly outperforms other models
418
      across the three tasks. RFLAF achieves consistently low level of losses, while other models fail
      to correctly learn the target functions. Speciﬁcally, RF models with RBF as a single activation do
419
      not learn all the target functions, demonstrating that the stronger expressivity of RFLAF comes from
420
      the universal approximation characteristics of the array of RBFs. We also ﬁnd similar phenomena
421   across a wider range of activation functions. The result is contained in appendix E.
422
423   Emerged interpretability. Figure 2 shows that RFLAF provides a level of interpretability con-
424   cerning the structure of the activation function. By combining the RBFs with the optimized weights,
425   we ﬁnd that RFLAF successfully recover the activation functions of various forms, providing deeper
426   insights into the data patterns. Trainable activation functions act as the core component in a re-
427   cently proposed deep learning framework, Kolmogorov-Arnold Network (Liu et al., 2024b;a). Our
428   ﬁndings highlight the potentials of trainable activation functions to provide interpretability from the
429   perspective of random features.
430
431   Easy implementation and fast convergence. The implementation of our model is simple. It
      merely involves appending a list of modules consists of RBFs in PyTorch compared to an ordinary


                                                                                              8
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


432
      RF model. In addition, a standard gradient descent optimizer such as Adam should sufﬁce for fast
433   convergence of our model. Although a wide range of functions serve as basis functions in UAT
434   (Nestoridis & Stefanopoulos, 2007), we use RBFs instead of B-splines in (Liu et al., 2024b). The
435   reasons are twofold: (1) RBFs are regular, simplifying the theoretical analysis, (2) RBFs and their
436   derivatives are easily computed, facilitating the convergence process. In contrast, models with B-
437   splines typically require LBFGS with line search for better convergence performance. Figure 1
438   provides evidence for the rapid convergence of our model, occurring within a duration of 5 epochs.

440   7   C ONCLUSION
441
442   In this work, we propose the random feature model with learnable activation functions. We provide
443   theoretical results on the approximation and generalization properties, and validate its superior ex-
444   pressivity and interpretability empirically. These ﬁndings deepen our comprehension of the beneﬁts
445   of learnable activation functions, and lay the groundwork for the advancement of more expressive
446   and interpretable machine learning paradigms that incorporate such mechanism. Our work initiates
447   an array of open problems for future work, including the derivation of tighter generalization bounds,
448   the exploration of basis functions for more effective approximation and faster convergence, and the
449   extension of learnable activation functions to a broader spectrum of statistical learning models.
