011                                               A BSTRACT
012
013            An LLM is pretrained on trillions of tokens, but the pretrained LLM may still
014            generate undesired responses. To solve this problem, alignment techniques such as
015            RLHF, DPO and KTO are proposed. However, these alignment techniques have
016
               limitations. For example, RLHF requires training the reward model and policy
               separately, which is complex, time-consuming, memory intensive and unstable
017
               during training processes. DPO proposes a mapping between an optimal policy and
018
               a reward, greatly simplifying the training process of RLHF. However, it can not
019            take full advantages of a reward model and it is limited to pairwise preference data.
020            In this paper, we propose UNified Alignment (UNA) which unifies RLHF/PPO,
021            DPO and KTO. Firstly, we mathematically prove that given the classical RLHF
022            objective, the optimal policy is induced by a generalize implicit reward function.
023            With this novel mapping between a reward model and an optimal policy, UNA
024            can 1. unify RLHF/PPO, DPO and KTO into a supervised learning of minimizing
025            the difference between an implicit reward and an explicit reward; 2. outperform
026            RLHF/PPO while simplify, stabilize, speed up and reduce memory burden of RL
027
               fine-tuning process; 3. accommodate different feedback types including pairwise,
               binary and scalar feedback. Downstream experiments show UNA outperforms
028
               DPO, KTO and RLHF.

      1   I NTRODUCTION
032
033
      LLMs are trained on extensive and diverse corpora, enabling them to develop robust language
034
      capabilities and a deep understanding of various contexts OpenAI et al. (2024); Anthropic (2024).
035
      However, during inference, LLM can generate undesired responses, which should be avoided.
036   Supervised fine-tuning (SFT) though can improve an LLM on downstream tasks like question
037   answering, it cannot solve these problems. To address these problems, alignment techniques like
038   RLHF Ouyang et al. (2022) and DPO Rafailov et al. (2023) are proposed.
039
      RLHF involves two stages of training from the SFT models as shown in part (b) of Figure 1. Firstly,
040
      it trains a reward model (RM) using a preference dataset consisting of tuples (input, desired response,
041
      undesired response). Next, during the RL fine-tuning stage, the policy generates responses to given
042   prompts. These responses are evaluated by the reward model and then used to fine-tune the policy with
043   RL through PPO. However, several problems exist in RLHF. First of all, there exists an overfitting
044   problem in the training stage of the reward model. In addition, RL fine-tuning stage is inherently
045   unstable due to the nature of RL. Lastly, RL increases memory requirements for elements like the
046   policy, reference policy, reward model and value model.
047
      DPO addresses these issues by creating a mapping between the reward model and the optimal policy,
048   combining the RM and RL training stages into a single process as shown in part (c) of Figure 1. This
049   approach simplifies the two-stage optimization into one stage, eliminating the need to train an explicit
050   reward model, reducing memory costs, and transforming the unstable RL process into a stable binary
051   classification problem. Given a prompt along with desired and undesired responses, the implicit
052   rewards for both responses are calculated. The differences in these rewards are then used to optimize
053   the policy. However, DPO has its own set of challenges. It cannot produce an explicit reward model
      and will require more preference data to fine-tune the LLM. Moreover, in RL, the pretrained RM can

175   Several limitations exist in RLHF. To begin with, the reward model may suffer from overfitting during
176   training, which can adversely affect the RL fine-tuning process. Then, unlike traditional supervised
177   learning, RL does not have explicit labels for each prompt and response. To address this, the authors

      2.3.4    UNA: S IMPLIFICATION OF RLHF
328
329   When utilizing reward model for online evaluation, UNA will greatly simplify RL fine-tuning stage
330   of RLHF/PPO with superior performances as shown in part (d) of Figure 2. Assuming the reward
331   model has already been trained, the focus now shifts exclusively to the RL fine-tuning stage. Prompts
332   are firstly sent to an LLM for online response generation and implicit reward estimation. Then,
333   the prompt and response are sent to the reward model for explicit reward estimation. The last step
334   minimize the differences between implicit and explicit rewards to align the LLM policy. Eventually,
335   the original RL objective in Equation 3 can be transformed to difference minimization like MSE of
336
      implicit reward and explicit reward or scores as shown in Equation 13
337   UNA has several benefits over PPO in RL fine-tuning stage. First of all, it transforms the original
338   unstable RL problem into a stable supervised learning problem by minimizing the difference between
339   implicit and explicit rewards. In addition, UNA removes the necessary of value model in PPO, and
340   partially reduce the burden of memory cost. Then, the computation cost of MSE is much lower
341   compared with the multiple terms in PPO to maintain performance, and as a result, UNA will speed
342
      up the training process. Lastly, UNA outperforms RLHF/PPO on downstream tasks.

474   4   R ELATED W ORK
475
476   The field of LLM has been greatly revolutionized with billions of parameters, trillions of tokens in
477
      parallel during the pretraining stage OpenAI et al. (2024); Anthropic (2024); Team et al. (2023).
      After pretraining, SFT will be applied to enhance its capability on downstream tasks. However, both
478
      pretraining and SFT can not solve the bias and ethic problem of LLM as they exist in the pretriaing
479
      data OpenAI et al. (2024). To solve this problem, RLHF with PPO Ouyang et al. (2022); Bai et al.
480   (2022a) have been proposed, and it is the mostly accepted method to align LLM including GPT and
481   Claude. However, lots of problems exist for RLHF/PPO including large memory burden, unstability
482   of RL and multiple stages of training, i.e. RM training and RL fine-tuning Rafailov et al. (2023). To
483   decrease the cost of human labelling, AI feedback can be utilized to replace human feedback, which
484   will be termed as RLAIF Bai et al. (2022b); Lee et al. (2023). RLOO considers PPO an overkill for
485   LLM alignment as LLM has been pretrained, and RLOO should be good enough Ahmadian et al.
      (2024).


                                                            9
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


486
      To simplify RLHF, DPO is proposed to map the optimal policy and reward model, and the two stages
487   can be merged into one step Rafailov et al. (2023). This process transforms the initial unstable RL
488   into a binary cross entropy problem. DPOP Pal et al. (2024) mathematically prove that during DPO,
489   the reward of desired responses will go down and proposed a maximum term to prevent the rewards
490   of desired responses going down. IPO discovered that under nearly deterministic condition between
491   desired and undesired responses, the effectiveness of the KL divergence constraint imposed by β
492   diminished, potentially leading to overfitting, and they proposed a new loss term to prevent this
493   problem Azar et al. (2023). sDPO proposed to divide given dataset into splits and use these splits
494   to sequentially align the model will achieve performance than using all of them at once Kim et al.
495
      (2024). Iterative DPO argued that LLM can be both response generator and evaluator so that it can
      iterate and improve it continuously Yuan et al. (2024); Xu et al. (2024). TDPO provided an idea to
496
      provided reward to each token generation Rafailov et al. (2024); Zeng et al. (2024).
497
498   There have also been some works on merging SFT with alignment. ORPO proposed a new loss
499   function to increase the ratio of desired responses over undesired responses to realize the goal of both
500   SFT and alignment Hong et al. (2024). PAFT proposed to conduct SFT and alignment in parallel and
501
      merge them together afterward Pentyala et al. (2024). Some works, i.e., R-DPO Park et al. (2024)
      and SimPO Meng et al. (2024) have also discovered the verbose problem of LLM generation, and
502
      included some length control methods to reduce the length of generated responses while minimizing
503
      the impact of LLM performances.
504
505   The previous work focused on pairwise dataset, which was more tough to gather. In comparison,
506   binary feedback like ”thumb up” and ”thumb down” will be easier to gather. KTO borrowed the idea
507
      of human aversion to desired over undesired data and it can handle binary feedback successfully
      Ethayarajh et al. (2024). DRO focused on binary data by estimating the policy and value functions
508
      and optimize each sequentially while maintaining the other fixed Richemond et al. (2024). However,
509
      there have not been a work that can unify both pairwise and binary feedback. Nash learning model
510   the LLM improvement as a minmax problem and propose a iterative method to gradually approach
511   the optimal solution Munos et al. (2024). It can solve the intranstivity problem of human preference.
512   SPPO utilized one model as two sides of the competition Wu et al. (2024). Though Nash learning
513   provides some hints, it will increase the time of alignment as it will increase the number of iteration
514   before convergence.
515
      LiPO Liu et al. (2024), RRHF Yuan et al. (2023) and PRO Song et al. (2024) utilized the ranking of a
516   list of responses, and the relative score between these methods were utilized. RPO proposd to utilize
517   KL divergence to mimize the difference between predicted reward and labelled reward by human or
518   AI, which is closer to our idea in this work Nvidia et al. (2024).

      5    C ONCLUSION
524
525
526   Despite the trillions of tokens used to pretrain LLMs with billions of parameters, undesired responses
527
      persist. RLHF, DPO and KTO can improve the alignment quality. However, RLHF, DPO and KTO
      each have their own strengths and drawbacks, but they cannot be unified into a single approach. In this
528
      work, we propose UNA to integrate the benefits of RLHF, DPO, and KTO into  a unified framework.
529
530
      Based on the RLHF objective, the optimal policy is induced by r(x, y) = β log ππθ (y|x)        +f (x)+c.

      When f (x) = c = 0, the reward can be simplified to r(x, y) = β log π (y|x) . With this derived
532                                                                               ref
      implicit reward function, it can be utilized to build UNA, which unifies RLHF, DPO and KTO as a
533
      task of minimization between implicit and explicit reward functions. As a result, UNA simplifies,
534   stabilizes and reduces memory cost of RLHF. Downstream tasks demonstrate that UNA significantly
535   outperforms RLHF. Then, UNA can deal with pairwise, binary and score-based feedback. For
536   pairwise feedback, UNA is mathematically equivalent to DPO. For binary feedback, UNA can
537   improve over KTO. For score-based feedback, UNA outperforms non-score-based methods including
538   DPO and KTO, and it can be regarded as a distillation of RM and LLM or an offline RLAIF. In
539   conclusion, UNA has introduced a unified, stable, and efficient approach to LLM alignment that
      delivers high-quality results.
