011                                                A BSTRACT
012
013            Open Vocabulary Object Detection (OVD) aims to extend to identify novel classes
014            solely through text descriptions, by learning the mapping between images and text
015
               from the base class. However, current methods focus on linking the visual features
               of target objects to their corresponding category names for prompt learning, ignor-
016
               ing richer contextual information and shared knowledge about these categories,
017
               which can easily lead to overfitting on base categories and exhibit poor general-
018            ization to novel classes. To address the above problems, we propose Hierarchical
019            prompts with Context-Aware calibration (HiCA) for open-vocabulary object de-
020            tection, which integrates high-level semantic and contextual information into the
021            detector from both linguistic and visual perspectives. Hierarchical prompts ef-
022            fectively map regions with superior-level semantics, which encompasses shared
023            knowledge of both base and novel classes, thereby enhancing the model’s general-
024            ization ability to novel classes. Meanwhile, context-aware calibration utilizes the
025            visual context of the image to establish the correlation between contextual infor-
026
               mation and categories, thereby minimizing the adverse effects of the background
               and enhancing generalization to novel classes. Extensive experiments demon-
027
               strate that the hierarchical prompts with context-aware calibration can effectively
028
               improve the performance of the open vocabulary detection methods. Especially on
029            the OV-COCO, we achieve 57.2% mAPB , surpassing the current state-of-the-art
030            by 2.4% while achieving the best mAP50 .

033   1   I NTRODUCTION
034
035   In recent years, advancements in deep neural networks have propelled significant progress in object
036   detection (He et al., 2017; Lin et al., 2017; Ren et al., 2015), leading to its broad application across
037   various downstream tasks (Li et al., 2023; Ma et al., 2023). However, most of the existing studies
038   have focused on closed-set scenarios that necessitate extensive labeled data. In contrast, a real
039
      open-world environment continuously presents novel categories, making the collection and labeling
      of samples for each novel class increasingly challenging. The challenge significantly limits the
040
      practical usability of these methods. To address the issue, Open-Vocabulary Object Detection (OVD)
041
      tasks (Zareian et al., 2021; Gu et al., 2022) have been proposed, aiming to recognize novel classes
042   during the testing phase by leveraging semantic embeddings of category names as classifiers. This
043   approach enables the classification of regions into appropriate categories, facilitating the detection of
044   previously unseen objects without the need for extensive retraining. Consequently, OVD enhances
045   the adaptability and generalization of object detection models in dynamic and open-world settings.
046
      By leveraging large-scale pre-trained Vision and Language Models (VLMs), such as CLIP (Radford
047
      et al., 2021) and ALIGN (Jia et al., 2021), recent advances in OVD have employed knowledge
048   distillation to transfer the insightful knowledge of VLMs to the object detection task. This approach
049   enables the models to generalize and identify unknown object categories effectively. Substituting
050   traditional classifiers with embeddings of class names and prompts allows for seamless adaptation to
051   emerging classes. The prompts can either be handcrafted, as in RegionCLIP (Zhong et al., 2022) or
052   learnable, as in PromptDet (Feng et al., 2022). Overall, these techniques distill visual features from
053   VLMs into detection frameworks and utilize learnable multi-modal prompts to facilitate knowledge
      extraction from large models.

074   Current methods primarily focus on learning the mapping between visual features of objects and
075   their corresponding class prompts, neglecting to capture the shared knowledge between both base
076   and novel classes, as well as the rich information present in the visual context. This oversight leads
077   to misclassifications, particularly in cases where objects from different superclasses share similar
078   appearances. Consequently, detectors tend to favor the heavily trained base classes, resulting in
079   overfitting and diminished generalization capabilities for novel classes. Our approach, in contrast,
080   incorporates high-level semantic information from both linguistic and visual perspectives. This
081
      integration allows us to leverage coarse-grained and contextual knowledge, ultimately enhancing the
      overall performance of open-vocabulary detection and ensuring robust generalization of the model
082
      across various classes.
083
084   Despite the rapid advancements in OVD, several challenges remain. Firstly, as shown in Figure 1,
085   current approaches predominantly map regions to classes to learn the prompts, which only learn the
086   relationship between object regions and base classes during the training process. As a result, the
087
      learned prompts tend to favor detecting regions as base classes, exhibiting poor generalization to
      novel classes. Simultaneously, prevailing methodologies often treat visual context as purely neg-
088
      ative examples, inadvertently creating barriers to distinguishing novel categories from background
089
      elements. The rich information contained in the context is largely underutilized, as these methods
090   fail to leverage the explanatory power of the context by clearly establishing connections between
091   contextual features and class identifiers. This oversight limits the potential for detailed understand-
092   ing and adaptive learning in the detection of novel classes.
093
      To address the aforementioned issues, we propose hierarchical prompts with context-aware calibra-
094
      tion (HiCA) for open-vocabulary object detection. Our framework enhances semantic and visual
095
      alignment with more generalization ability through the integration of high-level languages and vi-
096   sual context. Specifically, hierarchical prompts decompose the original region-category mapping
097   into a two-step process: coarse-grained mapping between objects and superclasses and fine-grained
098   between class and superclasses. The coarse-grained superclass knowledge effectively encompasses
099   both base and novel classes, thereby ensuring that the prompts do not overly favor base classes dur-
100   ing the training phase. Furthermore, context-aware calibration fosters a strong connection between
101   contextual information and categories through the context-aware matrix, which obtains visual em-
102   beddings of contexts by unsupervised clustering and constructs the context-superclass distribution
103   using a Distribution Generation Layer (DG Layer). Moreover, the association between superclasses
104
      and categories is used to fit the context-class distribution. By selecting the appropriate class distri-
      bution based on the specific context, we calibrate the detection results, ensuring a more precise and
105
      context-sensitive classification.
106
107   The effectiveness of HiCA is evaluated on popular open-vocabulary object detection benchmarks
      OV-COCO and OV-LVIS. We establish a baseline based on a knowledge distillation framework on

112   2     R ELATED WORK
113
114   Open-Vocabulary Object Detection Open-vocabulary object detection has recently become a
115   focus in the modern object detection area, which aims to detect objects of unlimited categories.
116   OVR-CNN (Zareian et al., 2021) is the first work that put forth the OVD task, which pre-trains
117   the detector with image-caption pairs to enable generalization to novel classes. Since pre-trained
118   vision-language models like CLIP (Radford et al., 2021) emerged and demonstrated strong capabil-
119   ities, various research has incorporated VLMs in their methods. CORA (Wu et al., 2023b) proposed
120
      region prompting and anchor pre-matching to tackle the whole-to-region distribution gap and make
      the object queries class-aware, avoiding the low efficiency of OV-DETR. ViLD (Gu et al., 2022)
121
      employs a knowledge distillation approach that aligns the region embeddings of detected objects to
122
      visual and text representations inferred by the teacher VLM. SAMP (Zhao et al., 2024) designed
123   a mechanism to generate scene-adaptive and region-aware multi-modal prompts to enhance knowl-
124   edge transfer. In this paper, we pay more attention to high-level semantic knowledge and introduce
125   coarse-grained information to improve the detection and generalization performance of the model
126   simultaneously.
127
128   Prompt tuning Prompt tuning has emerged as a significant advancement in natural language pro-
129   cessing and found its way into the realm of computer vision, where it has been adapted to enhance
130   performance in tasks such as image classification and object detection. CLIP demonstrated how
131   textual prompts could be paired with images to create a joint vision-language embedding space.
132   This approach allows the model to classify images based on textual descriptions, thereby leveraging
133   large-scale pre-trained language models for visual tasks. CoOp (Zhou et al., 2022a) learns con-
134   tinuous prompt vectors, enabling the model to adaptively modify the prompts for better alignment
135   with the image data. VPT (Jia et al., 2022) prepends a set of learnable parameters to transformer
136
      encoders and remarkably beats full fine-tuning on 20 downstream recognition tasks. We use hier-
      archical prompts to fuse coarse-grained and fine-grained knowledge and construct a multi-modal
137
      prompts architecture to further optimize the detector performance.

      3.3       H IERARCHICAL PROMPTS FOR MULTI - MODAL KNOWLEDGE DISTILLATION
205
206   Existing OVD methods generally train prompts to directly associate proposal regions to predefined
207   object categories, creating a reliance on a fixed set of base categories and restricting their adaptabil-
208   ity to novel ones. To overcome this limitation, we propose hierarchical prompts for the multi-modal
209   knowledge distillation method, which learns the mapping between regions and categories in a grad-
210   uated manner, from shallow to deep by incorporating coarse-grained textual knowledge between
211   visual regions and category descriptions. Thereby improving generalization to unseen novel cate-
212
      gories.
213   This novel approach structures prompt construction into two hierarchical levels: coarse-grained shal-
214   low prompts that capture the superclass-to-region relationship, and fine-grained prompts that map
215   regions to specific categories. Superclasses encapsulate broader, higher-level semantic information,
      facilitating better generalization to novel categories. By progressively structuring trainable prompts

      3.4    C ONTEXT- AWARE C ALIBRATION
257
258   When transferring features from the VLM model to the detector network, the emphasis is often
259   placed on cropped region features, which tend to neglect the surrounding environmental background.
260   However, the contextual information in the background can enhance the model’s ability to generalize
261   to novel classes by expanding high-level visual semantic understanding. To harness this potential,
262   we propose a context-aware probability matrix that establishes connections between context and
263
      categories, calibrating the logits produced by the detector classifier. It embodies the probability of
      a category’s occurrence in a given context, utilizing unsupervised contextual clustering alongside
264
      textual cues that pertain to both the category and its superclass.
265

267   bution of superclasses in the environmental context, where K represents the number of contextual

269   text embeddings in an unsupervised condition and subsequently refine the matrix. Specifically,
      we employ the visual encoder Ev of pre-trained VLMs to extract the global features eg ∈ Rd of

      3.5   T RAINING AND I NFERENCE
301
302   Training During training, we use the distillation framework of OADP to preserve not only the loss
303   function of Faster R-CNN Lf rcnn = Lrpn + Lcls + Lreg , but also the global LG , block LB , and ob-
304   ject LO distillation losses. At the same time, based on the original distillation loss, we optimize the
305   object distillation loss LO
                                vp by adding learnable visual prompts to improve the distillation efficiency
306   of the knowledge in the object regions. After computing the context-aware matrix M̂ca ∈ RK×C ,
307   we select the context-aware vector {M̂kca }k∈K based on the context to which the current image be-
308   longs and calibrate the logits obtained from the hierarchical prompts. When calculating the visual
309   context embedding using clustering, we save the global features of each image through a queue and
310   update the visual context embedding in a fixed number of rounds divisible by one thousand.
311
312   Inference During inference, we use the hierarchical prompts saved during training to calculate the
313   classification score of the detector and do not use the test data to update novel categories’ hierarchical
314   prompts. Meanwhile, when leveraging the context-aware matrix in the inference phase, we also
315   select the context-aware vector to calibrate the region-category logits calculated by the knowledge
316   distillation framework according to the current context.
317
318   Discussion Both our proposed hierarchical prompts and context-aware calibration are indepen-
319   dent of the knowledge distillation framework and are plug-and-play flexible modules. Hierarchical
320   prompts only adjust category and superclass prompts according to different datasets and can di-
321   rectly replace prompts in various methods. When the subordination matrix A = {aij } is difficult
322   to generate with annotations, the superclass-category similarity can be used instead. Context-aware
323   calibration uses context embeddings and text prompts independently to learn the distribution of cat-
      egories and directly acts on region-category logits.

      4.1   DATASETS
327
328   Building on various open-vocabulary detection methodologies (Gu et al., 2022; Wang et al., 2023;
329   Wu et al., 2023a), we adopt two widely-used open-vocabulary object detection datasets, OV-COCO
330   and OV-LVIS, to thoroughly assess the performance of our approach.
331
332   OV-COCO In accordance with the setup of (Zareian et al., 2021), the categories in the COCO
333   dataset are re-classified into 48 base categories and 17 novel categories. There are three main metrics
334   to evaluate the performance of the model, mAPN , mAPB , and mAP50 , where mAPN represents
335
      the mAP of the novel categories with an IoU threshold of 0.5, while mAPB and mAP50 represent
      the base categories and all categories, respectively.
336
337
      OV-LVIS The dataset originally contained three broad classes: common, frequent, and rare. Fol-
338
      lowing the setting of (Gu et al., 2022), the original rare class has been further subdivided into novel
339   classes, while the common and frequent classes are jointly divided into base classes. Consequently,
340   the OV-LVIS package now includes 337 novel classes and 866 base classes. For this dataset, we use
341   the same metrics names as APr , APc , APf , and AP .
342
343   4.2   I MPLEMENTATION D ETAILS
344
345   We train our model using 8 V-100 GPUs with a total batch size of 16. Adhering to the implementa-
346   tion details of (Wang et al., 2023), we use SGD as the optimizer with an initial learning rate of 0.02,
347   momentum of 0.9, and weight decay of 0.0001. We use ViT-B/32 CLIP as a teacher model, and its
348   text and visual encoders are used to generate multimodal prompts. The student model is based on
349
      the classic Faster RCNN, initializing its ResNet-50 backbone with SoCo. We trained on OV-COCO
      for a total of 40,000 iterations and reduced the learning rate at 30,000 iters. For OV-LVIS, We use
350
      2× (24 epochs) training schedule, and the learning rate is divided by 10 at the 16th and 22nd epochs.

    523      6   C ONCLUSION
    524
    525      In this paper, we propose a Hierarchical prompts with Context-Aware calibration (HiCA) for open-
    526      vocabulary object detection to enhance the ability of knowledge distillation framework to transfer
    527      high-level semantic knowledge. The core idea of HiCA is to make full use of superior-level semantic
    528      information in vision and language and maintain the generalization ability to novel classes while im-
    529      proving the performance of the open vocabulary detector. The hierarchical prompts integrate coarse-
    530      grained superclass knowledge as an intermediary step, thereby transforming the region-category into
    531      a two-stage process. It ensures that coarse-grained knowledge mitigates potential biases towards
    532      base classes during training. Context-aware calibration revises detector results by learning cate-
    533
             gory distribution through environmental context knowledge. We conduct sufficient comparison and
             ablation experiments to demonstrate the superior performance of our proposed method.
