009                                                A BSTRACT
010
011            Recent advancements in multimodal large language models (MLLMs) have broad-
012            ened the scope of vision-language tasks, excelling in applications like image cap-
013            tioning and interactive question-answering. However, these models struggle with
014            accurately processing visual data, particularly in tasks requiring precise object
015
               recognition and fine visual details. Stringent token limits often result in the omis-
               sion of critical information, hampering performance. To address these limitations,
016
               we introduce Zoomer, a novel visual prompting mechanism designed to enhance
017
               MLLM performance while preserving essential visual details within token limits.
018            Zoomer features three key innovations: a prompt-aware strategy that dynamically
019            highlights relevant image regions, a spatial-preserving orchestration schema that
020            maintains object integrity, and a budget-aware prompting method that balances
021            global context with crucial visual details. Comprehensive evaluations across mul-
022            tiple datasets demonstrate that Zoomer consistently outperforms baseline meth-
023            ods, achieving up to a 26.9% improvement in accuracy while significantly reduc-
024            ing token consumption.

026   1   I NTRODUCTION
027
028   Recent advancements in multimodal large language models (MLLMs), such as GPT-4o, Gemini Pro,
029   and Claude 3, have significantly expanded the capabilities of vision-language tasks. These models
030   now excel in applications such as image captioning, object recognition, and interactive question-
031   answering systems (Li et al., 2024; Gu et al., 2024). These models are able to process and integrate
032   both text and images, creating opportunities for applications spanning diverse fields from creative
033
      writing to technical problem-solving. Yet, their reliance on textual prompts limits their performance
      when tasked with precise object recognition and interpreting intricate visual details (Cui et al., 2024).
034
      This limitation becomes evident when users attempt to guide the model using text prompts alone, as
035
      shown in Figure 1. While these models possess the capability to capture and analyze subtle visual
036   nuances, current text-based prompting methods often fail to fully unlock this potential. We argue
037   that more sophisticated approaches, where vision is not only analyzed but also serves as part of
038   the prompt, are essential for refining the interaction and fully harnessing the power of MLLMs in
039   high-stakes visual environments.
040
      One of the primary limitations of current MLLMs arises from their unified image processing strat-
041
      egy. In visual tasks, there are often regions of interest (RoI) corresponding to the parts of concern
042   and auxiliary understanding information, sometimes even including irrelevant and redundant back-
043   ground. However, most models process images uniformly without selectively filtering these regions.
044   This lack of targeted processing leads to decreased fidelity in critical areas, severely affecting tasks
045   that rely on small object detection or fine visual details. Although some recent studies (Jin et al.,
046   2023) attempt to address this issue by modifying the model architecture, picking the valuable token
047   and fine-tuning training, such approaches are impractical for black-box MLLMs.
048   Meanwhile, black-box MLLMs impose strict token limits on both text and visual prompts to ensure
049   computational efficiency and user fairness (Chen et al., 2024). These constraints further compound
050   the problem, as downscaling high-resolution images to fit within token budgets leads to the omission
051   of vital details. As demonstrated in Figure 2, when high-resolution images are resized to meet the
052   token limits imposed by these models, vital visual information is lost. In critical domains such
053   as medical imaging or satellite-based analysis, missing such details can result in inaccurate or even
      dangerous outcomes (Mahapatra et al., 2022). Moreover, the black-box nature of these models limits

169   3.1   M ULTIMODAL LLM S : O PEN -S OURCE AND B LACK -B OX M ODELS

171
      The integration of visual and textual modalities in large language models (LLMs) has led to signifi-
      cant advancements in multimodal models (MLLMs) like GPT-4o, Gemini Pro and Claude3-Sonnet.
172
      These models rely on effective visual encoding strategies to bridge the gap between language and
173
      vision. Approaches such as CLIP (Yang et al.) align visual and language embeddings through
174   contrastive learning, while models like Flamingo (Alayrac et al.) and BLIP-2 (Dai et al.) use cross-
175   attention mechanisms or pretraining modules to link vision encoders with LLMs. However, these
176   methods often rely on fixed low-resolution inputs (e.g., 224x224), limiting their ability to process
177   high-resolution images or non-standard aspect ratios (Liu et al., a), which hampers performance on
178   fine-grained tasks such as OCR and small object detection
179
      In contrast, open-source multimodal models (Li et al., c; Xu et al.; Zhang et al., a; Li et al., a;
180   Zhao et al.) allow for architectural modifications and fine-tuning to accommodate any-resolution
181   inputs. However, black-box MLLMs such as GPT-4o and Gemini Pro, which impose strict token
182   limits for computational efficiency, require alternative solutions. The need to downsample or crop
183   images to meet these constraints often results in the loss of crucial visual details, particularly in tasks
184   requiring detailed visual understanding. While position embedding interpolation (Bai et al.; Wang
185   et al.; Luo et al.; Hong et al.; Chen et al.) and patch-based cropping (Xu et al.; Li et al., a) widely
186   adpoted in open-soure models offer promising directions for any aspect ratio and any-resolution
187   image processing, they are not applicable to black-box models, where architectural changes and
188
      extra training/fine-tuning are not permitted.
189
190   3.2   O BJECT D ETECTION
191
      Traditional object detection models, such as Faster R-CNN (Ren et al.) and YOLO (Redmon et al.),
192
      effectively identify and localize objects within predefined categories. However, they struggle with
193   open-set scenarios, where novel objects not seen during training need to be detected.
194
195   Recent advances address this limitation through open-set detection models that leverage natural
196
      language processing. For instance, OV-DETR (Zang et al.) integrates CLIP with object detection
      to generate category-specific bounding boxes from textual prompts, enabling detection in open-
197
      world settings. Similarly, GLIP (Li et al., b) reframes detection as a grounding problem, improving
198
      alignment between visual regions and textual descriptions. DetCLIP (Yao et al.) extends this fur-
199   ther using pseudo labels from large-scale captioning datasets, enhancing generalization. Grounding
200   DINO (Liu et al., b), built on the DETR framework (Carion et al.), also advances open-set detection
201   through natural language integration.
202
      In addition, SAM (Kirillov et al., 2023) and SAM-2 (Ravi et al., 2024) offer zero-prompt or minimal-
203
      prompt segmentation for arbitrary objects but lack robust text-prompt handling. EVF-SAM (Zhang
204
      et al., b) overcomes this by extending SAM’s capabilities to better manage complex text-based object
205   segmentation.
206
207   By incorporating these models, Zoomer enhances its ability to dynamically detect and emphasize
208
      regions of interest (RoIs), enabling black-box MLLMs to focus on the most relevant visual content
      without losing critical details, which is essential for maintaining high performance across varied
209
      resolutions.

      4.1      P ROMPT- AWARE V ISUAL E MPHASIZER
249
250
      The prompt-aware visual emphasizer utilizes a multi-scale emphasizing strategy to prioritize image
251
      slices that are most relevant to the input prompts. By analyzing the semantic content of the prompts,
252   this component dynamically selects and enhances specific regions of the image at varying resolu-
253   tions. This approach not only enriches the contextual information available to the model but also
254   mitigates the adverse effects of losing critical details during the resizing process.
255
      Prompt Tokenization Prompt tokenization is a critical first step in which input prompts are parsed
256
      into meaningful tokens. This process segments the prompt into components that can be easily an-
257
      alyzed for semantic relevance. Specifically, the prompt is divided into structural components, and
258   our focus is on processing the relevant sections that contribute directly to visual emphasis.
259
260   To enhance the extraction of semantically relevant tokens, we apply advanced natural language
261
      processing (NLP) techniques. First, we use the NLTK library5 to remove stopwords, reducing noise
      and ensuring that the model’s attention remains on the most critical visual elements. By eliminating
262
      these non-essential words, we concentrate on key terms that directly influence the visual emphasis.
263
264   In addition to basic stopword removal, we utilize dependency parsing (Sarthi et al., 2024; De Marn-
265   effe & Manning, 2008) to analyze the syntactic structure of the prompt. This deeper analysis iden-
266   tifies core entities and relationships, such as subject-object pairs and action verbs, which are crucial
267
      for interpreting the user’s intent. By focusing on these core semantic elements, we ensure that the
      visual emphasis aligns precisely with the underlying meaning of the prompt.

      4.2   S PATIAL - PRESERVING O RCHESTRATION S CHEMA
305
      Building upon the Multi-Scale Emphasizing Algorithm, we introduce a Spatial-preserving Orches-
306   tration Schema to maintain the structural integrity of the image during the encoding process. This
307   schema filters the bounding boxes obtained from the Multi-Scale Emphasizing Algorithm and en-
308   sures that the relative positions of the selected image slices are preserved, facilitating a more faithful
309   representation of the original image layout and enabling coherent reconstruction when processed by
310   the multimodal LLM. To refine the selection of bounding boxes, we implement a Non-Maximum
311   Suppression (NMS) based slice filtering method. NMS is employed to eliminate redundant and
312   overlapping slices, retaining only the most salient features that align with the prompt. The process
313   works as described in Algorithm 2.
314   By setting an appropriate threshold T for the Intersection of Union (IoU) of bounding boxes around
315   the selected regions, we ensure that only the highest-quality slices are retained for the encoding
316   process. This filtering step enhances computational efficiency by reducing the number of slices to
317   be processed and improves the clarity and relevance of the visual information provided to subsequent
318   stages of the model.
319   The resulting set of filtered slices are then orchestrated to preserve their original relative positions
320   within the image. This orchestration process involves the following steps: Slice Extraction: For
321   each bounding box bi in the filtered set F , we extract the corresponding image slice from the orig-
322   inal image. Blank Image Creation: We create a new blank image with the same dimensions as
323   the original image. Slice Placement: We place each extracted slice onto the blank image at its
      original position, leaving the rest of the image blank. Image Shrinking: The resulting image, con-

      4.3   B UDGET- AWARE P ROMPTING S TRATEGY:
328
329   Our approach incorporates a sophisticated budget-aware prompting strategy that optimizes the allo-
330   cation of token budget for image processing. This strategy begins with a user-specified total token
331   budget Btotal , allowing for customization based on specific task requirements or computational con-
332   straints. We propose four varieties of Zoomer to accommodate different budget scenarios and task
333   requirements:
334
      • Zoomer-Local(④): This variant utilizes only the spatial-preserving schema to consolidate all fo-
335
      cused image slices into a single image patch(④ in Figure 3). It is optimal for scenarios with very
336   limited token budgets, prioritizing the most relevant visual information.
337
      • Zoomer-Adaptive (④ + ♢ ③ ): This approach dynamically includes a global view of the original
338
      image if the cropped portion falls below a certain threshold TA . This allows the MLLM to better
339   understand the overall scene context when the budget permits, while still focusing on key areas of
340   interest.
341
      • Zoomer-Global (④ + ③): This variant assigns a global view to all images, regardless of the
342
      specific regions of interest. It is suitable for tasks that require consistent overall context and when
343   the token budget is sufficient to include both global and local information.
344
      • Zoomer-Patches(② + ③): This is the most token-intensive approach, assigning each image slice
345
      its own patch without spatial preservation, along with a global view. It provides the most detailed
346   information but requires the largest token budget.
347
348   The selection among these varieties depends on the user-specified budget and the nature of the
349   task. For each variant, the number of high-resolution slices or patches N is calculated based on the
350   available budget and the token cost per slice or patch. These slices are selected from the output of
351   our Multi-Scale Emphasizing Algorithm, prioritizing based on their relevance to the key term of text
352
      prompts. To present the methods more clearly and vividly, we refer to Figure 4, which outlines the
      methodology, and Figure 5, which showcases a specific case study.

      6    C ONCLUSION
517
518
      In this paper, we introduced Zoomer, a novel visual prompting mechanism designed to overcome
      the limitations of black-box MLLMs in processing images while adhering to token constraints. Our
519
      approach effectively balances the need to capture essential visual details without exceeding token
520
      budgets, a challenge commonly encountered in existing models like GPT-4o and Gemini Pro.
521
522   Through a comprehensive evaluation across datasets such as Vstar and RealWorldQA, Zoomer
523   demonstrated significant improvements, particularly in fine-grained visual tasks. Our results show
524
      that Zoomer-Patches achieved a 26.9% accuracy gain over baseline methods in Vstar, and Zoomer-
      Adaptive provided a 12.1% improvement in RealWorldQA. These gains were achieved while dras-
525
      tically reducing token usage, with Zoomer delivering 6.4% higher accuracy in the TerraIncognita
526
      dataset using 67% fewer tokens.
527
528   Although this work primarily focuses on improving the efficiency of visual processing in black-box
529   MLLMs, another potential issue that arises in real-world applications is communication cost. For
530
      example, transferring large images from edge devices (e.g., wearable cameras or glasses) to cloud
      servers can be expensive in terms of bandwidth, latency, and energy consumption. While Zoomer is
531
      designed to reduce token usage, its application in minimizing data transmission costs is an area that
532
      could be explored in future work.
533
534   As part of future research, we plan to investigate how Zoomer could be adapted for edge ML ap-
535   plications, enabling local processing on devices such as wearable cameras. This would allow for
536
      more efficient handling of visual inputs at the edge, reducing the need for extensive data transfers
      to the cloud. We aim to measure latency, power consumption, and the overall impact on system
537
      performance to assess the feasibility of applying Zoomer in these scenarios.
538
539   In summary, Zoomer offers a practical solution for enhancing visual processing in constrained
      MLLMs and opens up new directions for future exploration.
