011                                               A BSTRACT
012
013            Uncertainty quantification for deep neural networks is crucial for building reli-
014            able modern AI models. This challenge is particularly pronounced in deep rein-
015            forcement learning, where agents continuously learn from their interactions with
016            stochastic environments, and the uncertainty of the value function is a key con-
017
               cern for ensuring reliable and robust RL applications. The complexity increases
               in actor-critic methods, as the training process alternates between optimizing the
018
               actor and critic networks, whose optimization nature makes the uncertainty of the
019
               value function hard to be quantified. To address this issue, we introduce a novel
020            approach to RL training that conceptualizes transition trajectories as latent vari-
021            ables. Building on this framework, we propose an adaptive Stochastic Gradient
022            Markov Chain Monte Carlo (SGMCMC) algorithm for training deep actor-critic
023            models. This new training method allows for the implicit integration of latent
024            transition trajectories, resulting in a trajectory-independent training process. We
025            provide theoretical guarantees for the convergence of our algorithm and offer em-
026            pirical evidence showing improvements in both performance and robustness of
027            the deep actor-critic model under our Latent Trajectory Framework (LTF). Fur-
028
               thermore, this framework enables accurate uncertainty quantification for the value
               function of the RL system, paving the way for more reliable and robust RL appli-
029
               cations.

032   1   I NTRODUCTION
033
034   Reinforcement learning (RL) solves sequential decision-making problems by designing an agent that
035   interacts with its environment to learn an optimal policy, with the goal of maximizing the value func-
036   tion, i.e., total amount of expected rewards. Therefore, accurately quantifying the uncertainty of the
037   value function has been a critical concern for ensuring reliable and robust RL applications. However,
038   achieving this goal in the context of deep RL is notably challenging, despite the universal approxi-
039
      mation capabilities of deep neural networks (DNNs) having significantly expanded the applicability
      of RL. The primary difficulty in uncertainty quantification for deep RL arises from two aspects: the
040
      complex architecture of DNNs and the adaptive nature of the RL process. Even within the context
041
      of supervised learning, accurately quantifying uncertainty for DNNs is a challenging problem (see,
042   e.g., (Blundell et al., 2015) and (Lakshminarayanan et al., 2017)), and the adaptive process in RL
043   further complicates this issue (Osband et al., 2016; Bellemare et al., 2017). A significant step to-
044   ward addressing this challenge has been made in Shih & Liang (2024), where deep Q-networks are
045   simulated from their posterior distribution under the Kalman temporal difference (KTD) framework
046   (Geist & Pietquin, 2010; Shashua & Mannor, 2020), enabling accurate quantification for the uncer-
047   tainty of the Q-value function throughout the RL process. However, their methods are challenging to
048   extend to deep RL settings involving actor-critic architectures. The inclusion of an additional actor
049   network significantly complicates the process of uncertainty quantification, as the actor‚Äôs stochastic
050
      policy directly influences the distribution of the critic‚Äôs value estimates, creating interdependencies
      that are not easily captured by traditional approaches.
051
052   To address the challenge of uncertainty quantification in actor-critic models, we propose a novel
053   framework for deep RL training, which conceptualizes transition trajectories in the RL process as
      latent variables. Building on this perspective, we introduce an adaptive Stochastic Gradient Markov

054
      Chain Monte Carlo (SGMCMC) algorithm, which simultaneously updates the actor network through
055   a stochastic gradient descent (SGD) step and samples from the conditional distribution of the critic
056   network ‚ÄĒ conditioned on the current actor network ‚ÄĒ via an SGMCMC step. Under mild regu-
057   larity conditions, we establish the convergence of this adaptive SGMCMC algorithm. Specifically,
058   we show that the parameters of the actor network converge in probability to a fixed point, while
059   the parameters of the critic network converge weakly to a target distribution, thereby enabling accu-
060   rate quantification of the associated value function‚Äôs uncertainty. In summary, our study offers two
061   primary contributions:
062
063          ‚ÄĘ We introduce a novel latent trajectory framework for training deep actor-critic models that
064            inherently accounts for the dynamic nature of complex RL processes.
065
             ‚ÄĘ We establish a convergence theory for the SGMCMC algorithm used for training deep
066
               actor-critic models under the latent trajectory framework, which ensures effective training
067
               for the actor network while enabling proper uncertainty quantification for the critic network
068            and thus the value function.
069
070   To our knowledge, this is the first work for training deep actor-critic models with uncertainty quan-
071   tification.

074   Related Works Bayesian methods has been generally considered as a standard approach for uncer-
075   tainty quantification in machine learning. However, a rigorous implementation of Bayesian methods
076   for online RL is challenging. Specifically, Bayesian methods aim to infer unknown model parame-
077
      ters (denoted by őł) based on their posterior distribution ŌÄ(őł|D) for a given dataset D of identically
      distributed samples; however, in online RL, the data are drawn from a dynamic system where the
078
      samples are non-identically distributed. Furthermore, under the deep learning setting, it is challeng-
079
      ing to specify the prior distribution that ensure the posterior consistency property holds when the
080   deep neural networks involve more parameters than the training sample size. While there is existing
081   work on Bayesian RL, such as Fellows et al. (2024) and Osband et al. (2018), these challenges are
082   not adequately addressed. For instance, Osband et al. (2018) introduces uncertainty through ran-
083   domized priors in a maximum a posteriori (MAP) framework for approximate inference. However,
084   the theoretical property of posterior consistency for such randomized priors has not been established.
085   The actor-critic architecture introduces further complexity due to the interaction between the actor
086   and critic networks, which exacerbates the challenges in uncertainty quantification. Some other
087   methods, such as bootstrapping (Osband et al., 2016; Tasdighi et al., 2024), Gaussian processes
088
      (Geist & Pietquin, 2010; Engel et al., 2003), distribution RL (Bellemare et al., 2017), have also been
      proposed for uncertainty quantification in online RL. However, their theoretical guarantees remain
089
      unestablished in the context of the deep actor-critic setting. In this paper, we propose an innova-
090
      tive and theoretically rigorous framework for uncertainty quantification in online RL. Our approach
091   explicitly models the dynamics of online RL, ensuring that the uncertainty in the critic network is ac-
092   curately captured, even under the challenges posed by non-identically distributed data and multiple
093   deep neural networks.
