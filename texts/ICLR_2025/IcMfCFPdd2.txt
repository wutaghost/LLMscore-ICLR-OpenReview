011                                               A BSTRACT
012
013            The sharpness-aware minimization (SAM) algorithm and its variants, including
014            gap guided SAM (GSAM), have been successful at improving the generalization
015            capability of deep neural network models by п¬Ғnding п¬Ӯat local minima of the em-
016            pirical loss in training. Meanwhile, it has been shown theoretically and practi-
017            cally that increasing the batch size or decaying the learning rate avoids sharp local
018
               minima of the empirical loss. In this paper, we consider the GSAM algorithm
               with increasing batch sizes or decaying learning rates, such as cosine annealing
019
               or linear learning rate, and theoretically show its convergence. Moreover, we nu-
020
               merically compare SAM (GSAM) with and without an increasing batch size and
021            conclude that using an increasing batch size or decaying learning rate п¬Ғnds п¬Ӯatter
022            local minima than using a constant batch size and learning rate.

533
      First we gave upper and lower bounds of the search direction noise of the GSAM algorithm for solv-
534   ing the SAM problem. Then, we examined the GSAM algorithm with two mini-batch and learning
535   rate schedulers based on the bounds: an increasing batch size and constant learning rate scheduler
536   and a constant batch size and decaying learning rate scheduler. We performed convergence analyses
537   on GSAM for the two schedulers. We also provided numerical results to support the analyses. The
538   numerical results showed that, compared with SGD/Adam, SAM/GSAM with an increasing batch
539   size and a constant learning rate converges to п¬Ӯatter local minima of the empirical loss functions for
      ResNets and ViT-Tiny on the CIFAR100 dataset.
