489
490
      During the process of unlearning, we form two dynamic sets, the remember and the forget set. The

491

492

493   upon the arrival of a remove request to adjust the parameters of the model, so that the accuracy of
494   the model on the forget set is as similar as possible to that of a model which has never seen these
495   data, while maintaining high test accuracy on the remember set.

527
      In this work, we provide a first-order method (CSVRG − PL) for Continual Finite Sum Min-
      imization under the Polyak-Łojasiewicz condition. For accuracy√         ϵ > 0, CSVRG − PL com-
528
      putes an ϵ-approximate solution for all stages with O(n/ ϵ) FOs under the PL condition, for
529
      non-convex functions and recovers the rate of O(n/ϵ1/3 ) of CSVRG for the strongly convex
530
      case with improved computation, simultaneously improving over the O(n/ϵ) FO complexity of
531   StochasticGradientDescent and over the O (n log (1/ϵ)) FO complexity of variance reduction
532   methods. We also provide experimental evaluations indicating the effectiveness of our method in
533   settings with time-evolving datasets like continual learning and unlearning.
534                                                                      √
      Limitations: There is still an efficiency gap between the O(n/ ϵ) FO complexity of our method
535
536
      that applies to the non-convex setting and the Ω(n/ϵ1/4 ) FO lower bound (Mavrothalassitis et al.,
      2024) developed in the convex setting. Closing this gap is a very interesting future research direction.
537
538   Broader Impact: This paper provides an efficient first-order method for continual finite-sum mini-
539   mization. Our work contributes on the theoretical foundations of optimization and machine learning.
      We do not expect any negative societal impact from this work.
