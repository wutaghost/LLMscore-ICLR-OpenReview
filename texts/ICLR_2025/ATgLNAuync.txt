034                                                 A BSTRACT
035
036
                  Multimodal image fusion and semantic segmentation are critical for autonomous
037
                  driving. Despite advancements, current models often struggle with segmenting
038               densely packed elements due to a lack of comprehensive fusion features for guid-
039               ance during training. While the Segment Anything Model (SAM) allows precise
040               control during fine-tuning through its flexible prompting encoder, its potential re-
041               mains largely unexplored in the context of multimodal segmentation for natural
042               images. In this paper, we introduce SAM into multimodal image segmentation
043               for the first time, proposing a novel framework that combines Latent Space Token
044               Generation (LSTG) and Fusion Mask Prompting (FMP) modules. This approach
045               transforms the training methodology for multimodal segmentation from a tradi-
046
                  tional black-box approach to a controllable, prompt-based mechanism. Specif-
                  ically, we obtain latent space features for both modalities through vector quan-
047
                  tization and embed them into a cross-attention-based inter-domain fusion mod-
048
                  ule to establish long-range dependencies between modalities. We then use these
049               comprehensive fusion features as prompts to guide precise pixel-level segmen-
050               tation. Extensive experiments on multiple public datasets demonstrate that our
051               method significantly outperforms SAM and SAM2 in multimodal autonomous
052               driving scenarios, achieving at least a 3.9% improvement in segmentation mIoU
053               over state-of-the-art methods.

      1   I NTRODUCTION
055
056   Accurate and comprehensive scene understanding is crucial for autonomous driving (Zhang &
057   Demiris, 2023). Due to the limitations of sensor imaging devices, no single modality sensor can
058   independently provide a complete description of the scene (Zhou et al., 2024; Xue & Marculescu,
059   2023; Cao et al., 2023b; Xu et al., 2023). For instance, infrared sensors capture thermal radiation in-
060   formation, highlighting objects of interest such as pedestrians and vehicles (Bellagente et al., 2024).
061   Conversely, visible light sensors capture reflected light, generating scenes rich in texture details (Liu
062   et al., 2024). By combining these modalities, complementary details that might be missed by indi-
063   vidual sensors can be captured, enhancing the model’s ability to perform semantic segmentation of
064
      the complete scene (Cao et al., 2023a). Therefore, the fusion of infrared and visible light images
      has become a mainstream solution for improving scene understanding and semantic segmentation.
065
      However, current semantic segmentation models struggle to comprehend densely packed elements in
066
      multimodal driving scenes, failing to fully represent the captured information for better subsequent
067   segmentation results.
068
069   In recent decades, advancements in semantic segmentation within deep learning have significantly
070
      propelled the understanding of multimodal scenes. Capturing efficient multimodal fusion represen-
      tations is key to enhancing segmentation performance. A common approach involves feature-level
071
      fusion of infrared and visible light images using Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) to extract
072
      rich semantic representations, but the local constraints of CNNs make it challenging to effectively
073   merge information from different modalities. As an alternative, Transformer architectures, with
074   their attention mechanisms and ability to model long-range dependencies, facilitate better global
075   fusion and utilization of complementary information (Li et al., 2023; Zhang et al., 2024). How-
076   ever, pure transformer architectures lack the flexibility required for scene understanding, especially
077   in autonomous driving scenarios where elements are densely packed (Cao et al., 2023c), and edge
078   textures of segmented categories are blurred due to varying lighting conditions and nighttime en-
079   vironments. Without intermediate fine-tuning guidance to focus on critical regions, segmentation
080   distortions can occur, hindering better scene parsing. The Segment Anything Model (SAM) has
081
      emerged as a transformative method for single-modal natural scene segmentation due to its flexible
      prompting architecture (Ravi et al., 2024; Kirillov et al., 2023). Remarkably, the prompt architecture
082
      of SAM enhances the model’s ability to focus on detailed features. Through the guiding mechanism
083
      of prompts, SAM can more effectively direct the segmentation process compared to transformers
084   that lack fine-tuned control. This is crucial for the dense element segmentation required in au-
085   tonomous driving scenarios. However, SAM has not yet been extensively studied in the realm of
086   multimodal fusion.
087
      To address these challenges, we innovatively propose FusionSAM, a Latent Space driven Segment
088
      Anything Model for Multi-Modal Fusion and Segmentation, which endows SAM with efficient
089
      multimodal image fusion and segmentation capabilities. Specifically, we first capture latent space
090   feature embeddings of the two modalities through vector quantization to obtain efficient downsam-
091   pled representations. Then, we establish long-range dependencies between the modalities using a
092   cross-attention-based inter-domain fusion module, capturing comprehensive information as fusion
093   features to guide precise pixel-level segmentation. To the best of our knowledge, this is the first
094   study to apply the SAM to multimodal visual segmentation tasks in natural images, and it outper-
095   forms current state-of-the-art methods as shown in Figure 1. Our main contributions are as follows:
096
097
            • We extend SAM to multimodal image segmentation in natural images for the first time.
              Through SAM’s flexible prompt encoder we achieve efficient fusion and segmentation of
098
              multimodal images, meeting the complex requirements of autonomous driving scenarios
099
              with dense elements and varying lighting conditions.
100
            • We propose a novel FusionSAM framework that includes the Latent Space Token Gen-
101
              eration (LSTG) and Fusion Mask Prompting (FMP) Module. By capturing latent space
102
              representations through vector quantization and performing cross-domain fusion of these
103           features, we generate precise segmentation prompts.
104
            • Extensive experiments on public datasets and benchmarks show that FusionSAM signifi-
105
              cantly outperforms state-of-the-art methods, including SAM and SAM2, in multimodal au-
106
              tonomous driving scenarios, achieving a notable 3.9% improvement in segmentation IoU,
107           validating its effectiveness and robustness.

      2.1   S EGMENT A NYTHING M ODEL (SAM)
132
133   The SAM enables efficient object segmentation through simple prompt embeddings, like points or
134   bounding boxes, guiding the model to focus on specific regions (Ke et al., 2023; Schön et al., 2024;
135   Ren et al., 2024; Shen et al., 2024a; Wang et al., 2023; Ma et al., 2024; Huang et al., 2024). Derived
136   methods in single-modality segmentation include RobustSAM (Chen et al., 2024) by Chen et al.,
137   which improved SAM’s performance on low-quality images, and Crowd-SAM (Cai et al., 2024)
138   by Cai et al., which enhanced segmentation in crowded scenes with an Efficient Prompt Sampler
139
      and Part-Whole Discriminator Network. SAM has also been adapted for cross-modal tasks in fields
      like medical imaging and remote sensing. For example, Pandey et al. used YOLOv8 and SAM for
140
      cross-modal segmentation (Pandey et al., 2023), while Yan et al. introduced RingMo-SAM (Yan
141
      et al., 2023) for segmenting optical and SAR data. However, these methods only linearly adapt
142   SAM for multimodal tasks, missing the full potential of multimodal features. They also overlook
143   SAM’s powerful prompting architecture, which could better activate multimodal fusion features
144   during training to guide segmentation. Our proposed FusionSAM, on the other hand, captures latent
145   space representations through vector quantization, enabling comprehensive cross-domain fusion and
146   using these features as precise segmentation prompts.
147
148   2.2   M ULTI -M ODALITY I MAGE F USION
149
150   In autonomous driving, integrating various sensors is essential for accurate scene understanding, as
151   single-modality data is insufficient (Liang et al., 2022; Li et al., 2024; Zhang et al., 2021; Sun et al.,
152   2022; Wang et al., 2020a; 2022; Zhang et al., 2020). Wang et al. proposed AsymFusion (Wang
153
      et al., 2020b), which enhances multimodal feature interaction using a dual-branch structure with
      asymmetric fusion blocks. Zhang et al. developed MRFS (Zhang et al., 2024), combining CNN-
154
      based Interactive Gated Mixed Attention with transformer-based Progressive Cycle Attention to
155
      overcome bottlenecks in infrared-visible fusion. Feng et al. introduced MAF-Net (Feng et al., 2022),
156   which effectively segments road potholes by fusing RGB and disparity data. Ma et al. proposed
157   SwinFusion (Ma et al., 2022), leveraging cross-domain long-range learning and Swin Transformer
158   for global information integration and complementary feature extraction. Most existing methods
159   rely on convolutional networks or transformers, which struggle with global information extraction
160   and flexible segmentation in dense scenes. To overcome these limitations, we apply multimodal
161   fusion within SAM, using its flexible prompting to enhance segmentation in complex autonomous
      driving scenarios.

179
180   We propose FusionSAM, which enhances image fusion while preserving the segmentation capabil-
181   ity of the SAM architecture. By integrating a fusion module that enables latent space representation
182   embedding and cross-modal consistency fusion into the original SAM architecture, so that its per-

185   3.2.1    M ODEL OVERVIEW
186
      Figure 2 presents an overview of the proposed FusionSAM. The key contribution of FusionSAM
187   is its Latent Space Token Generation (LSTG) and Fusion Mask Prompting (FMP) modules. Unlike
188   methods that fine-tune or add adapters to SAM and SAM2, FusionSAM’s strength lies in its rigorous
189   and well-considered approach to efficient multimodal fusion and segmentation. This efficiency is
190   achieved by fusing compact and comprehensive latent space representations of both modalities,
191   rather than the original large-scale images, enabling more thorough and effective fusion.
192
      Training. To train FusionSAM, we first generate efficient fused modality representations, which are
193   then input into the model. Initially, a vector encoder creates latent space representations for both
194   modalities, followed by cross-attention-guided fusion to achieve a comprehensive representation.
195   Unlike the original SAM, we modify the input tokens for segmentation into Full-fledged Output
196   Tokens (FOT), which are enhanced versions of the latent representations designed to capture the
197   full spectrum of fused features for segmentation. These FOTs, along with the prompt token, are
198   processed through the SAM decoding layers to generate the segmentation mask.
199   The LSTG block processes the raw images from both modalities and transforms them into efficient
200   latent space features. Simultaneously, the FMP module performs multimodal fusion on the obtained
201   latent features. It uses cross-attention mechanisms to learn features from different modality domains,
202   producing refined and comprehensive features. These refined fusion features are then fed into the
203   mask encoder to enhance segmentation quality.
204
      In summary, the robust segmentation capability of the completed FusionSAM framework primar-
205   ily stems from the training of the LSTG and the FMP modules. Additionally, the decoder and
206   segmentation head from the original SAM architecture are also involved in the learning process.
207   This integration ensures that the model comprehensively understands the fused features from both
208   modalities, thereby enhancing segmentation performance.
209
      Inference. In the FusionSAM framework, the ViT-driven image encoder is not involved in training,
210
      it is solely used for inference to generate inputs for the mask decoder.

276   3.2.3   F USION M ASK P ROMPTING M ODULE
277

278
      infrared modalities, enabling comprehensive scene understanding in autonomous driving scenarios.
279

280   fusion mask. By leveraging the rich and comprehensive features present in the fusion representation
281   as prompts, FMP module provides flexible fine-tuning guidance for the segmentation process, lead-
282   ing to improved segmentation performance. For instance, if multimodal fusion feature map contains
283   complete information, using local area features as point prompts during training can further enhance
284   the model’s segmentation accuracy.
285

      During 100 epochs of training, multimodal images are subjected to 4× downsampled features by
345
      the LSTG module, and the FMP module further captures efficient fusion representations, combined
346   with 10-point mask prompts and 1-box mask prompt to facilitate effective segmentation of SAM.
347   Our initial learning rate is set to 1e-4, using the Adam optimizer with a weight decay of 1e-3, the

350   the performance of semantic segmentation. mIoU is the average result of summing the ratio of the
351   intersection over the sum of the predicted true values for each class.

      6     C ONCLUSION
472
473
      A key challenge in multimodal semantic segmentation for autonomous driving is developing a
474   framework that can effectively fuse and utilize multimodal data as prompts during training, guiding
475   the model to achieve high-performance segmentation in dense distribution scenes— an issue that
476   previous multimodal segmentation approaches have not fully addressed. We have innovatively pro-
477   posed FusionSAM, a latent space driven SAM framework for multimodal semantic segmentation,
478   which endows the SAM architecture with robust capabilities in multimodal fusion, understanding,
479   and segmentation. Our approach performs comprehensive cross-domain fusion of the latent space
480   representations from two modalities, using this fused information as prompts to guide segmentation.
481   This is the first study to leverage SAM in multimodal semantic segmentation of natural scenes, uti-
482
      lizing fusion as a guiding prompt. Extensive experiments demonstrate that FusionSAM significantly
      outperforms existing state-of-the-art methods in multimodal autonomous driving scenarios, offering
483
      a novel approach for future multimodal semantic segmentation tasks.
