                                                 A BSTRACT
009
               Powerful deep learning methods based on Transformers are used to model diverse
010
               data modalities such as sequences, images, and graphs. These methods typically
011
               use off-the-shelf modules like self-attention, which are domain-agnostic and treat
012            data as an unordered set of elements. To improve performance, researchers employ
013            inductive biasesтАФsuch as position embeddings in sequences and images, and
014            random walks in graphsтАФto inject the domain structure, or topology into the model.
015            However, these inductive biases are carefully engineered heuristics that must be
016            designed for each modality, requiring significant research effort. In this work, we
017            propose Chimera, a unified framework that mathematically generalizes state space
018
               models to incorporate the topological structure of data in a principled way. We
               demonstrate that our method achieves state-of-the-art performance across domains
019
               including language, vision, and graphs. Chimera outperforms BERT on the GLUE
020            benchmark by 0.7 points, surpasses ViT by 2.6% on ImageNet-1k classification
021            accuracy, and outperforms all baselines on the Long Range Graph Benchmark
022            with a 12% improvement on PascalVOC. This validates ChimeraтАЩs methodological
023            improvement which allows it to directly capture the underlying topology, providing
024            a strong inductive bias across modalities. Furthermore, being topologically aware
025            enables our method to achieve a linear time complexity for sequences and images,
026            in contrast to the quadratic complexity of attention.

028   1   I NTRODUCTION
029
      Real-world data is heterogeneous, ranging from sequential language data to high-dimensional image
030
      data, and structured data of proteins and molecules. Despite this heterogeneity, many domains
031
      exhibit an inherent topology that encodes the neighborhood of each element (node) of the data. For
032   instance, language and audio have a directed line graph topology, where each node (token) is arranged
033   sequentially (Fig 1a). Similarly, images possess an undirected grid-graph topology, where each node
034   (image patch) is connected to its immediate local neighbors in a grid (Fig 1b). Structured data like
035   proteins have predefined nodes (atoms) and edges (bonds), which constitute their topology (Fig 1c).
036   Typical approaches to model data build upon Transformers (Vaswani et al., 2017) with self-attention
037   at their core (Devlin et al., 2019; Dosovitskiy et al., 2021; Ramp├б┼бek et al., 2022). However, since
038   self-attention is permutation invariant, it treats data as an unordered set of elements and completely
039   disregards the dataтАЩs topology. To address this, significant research effort has focused on developing
040   domain-specific heuristics, such as position embeddings (Su et al., 2023; Devlin et al., 2019), and
041   random walks (Behrouz and Hashemi, 2024; Wang et al., 2024), to serve as the inductive bias for the
042
      underlying topology. However, developing these heuristics requires navigating a large search space for
      each domain. For instance, RoPE embeddings (Su et al., 2023) work well in language (Touvron et al.,
043
      2023); in vision, absolute position embeddings are widely used (Dosovitskiy et al., 2021; Heo et al.,
044
      2024); while laplacian embeddings are used in graphs (Ramp├б┼бek et al., 2022). Moreover, given the
045   lack of systematic underpinnings, it is unclear how effectively they capture the underlying topology.
046
      In this paper, we consider the following problem: тАЬCan we develop a principled method that captures
047
      the underlying data topology, and achieves state-of-the-art performance across domains?тАЭ. We propose
048
      Chimera, a domain-agnostic framework built on recent State Space Models (SSMs)тАФMamba-2 (Dao
049   and Gu, 2024a), RetNet (Sun et al., 2023), Linear Attention (LA) (Katharopoulos et al., 2020)тАФthat
050   mathematically generalizes SSMs to any topology and achieves state-of-the-art performance across
051   diverse domains including language, images, and graphs. These consistently superior results validate
052   ChimeraтАЩs methodological improvement which allows it to directly capture the underlying topology,
053   providing a strong inductive bias across various modalities. This contrasts with existing approaches
      that instead apply attention or SSMs as a black box to тАЬflattened dataтАЭ, supplemented by heuristics.

105   process sequential data, such as language and audio. We first formulate SSMs in their recurrent form
106   and then introduce the Structured Masked Attention (SMA) (Dao and Gu, 2024a) representation that

171   3     C HIMERA : B UILDING M ODELS FOR A NY T OPOLOGY
172
173   In this section, we introduce Chimera, a unified framework that generalizes SSMs to any arbitrary
174
      topology, enabling the development of performant models across diverse domains. Existing approaches
      such as Behrouz and Hashemi (2024); Devlin et al. (2019); Liu et al. (2021), treat attention and SSMs
175
      as black-box modules operating on fixed topologies such as sets or sequences and rely on heuristics
176   to incorporate structural information. In contrast Chimera opens up this black box and mathematically
177   adapts it to handle any topology.

328
329   Linear-time Complexity The intuition for the linear complexity is that the resolvent operation for
      DAGs is finite because of the lack of cycles. From the adjacency matrix perspective, A is nilpotent, i.e.
330
      Ak = 0, where k is the diameter of the graph (Prop 4.1). Since Chimera can be equivalently viewed as
331
      a recurrence on the DAG, the resolvent operation converges after one pass through the graph in the
332   topological order which takes linear time.
333
      Proposition 4.4. The Chimera structured mask matrix L can be computed in O(|V +|E|) complexity
334
      where |V|,|E| is the number of vertices and edges of the graph, respectively.

337   seen as a special case of Theorem 4.4 specialized to the directed line graph, where both |V| and |E| is

514   6      C ONCLUSION AND F UTURE W ORK
515
      In this work, we propose Chimera, a unified framework that mathematically generalizes State Space
516   Models (SSMs) to incorporate the underlying data topology. Unlike previous approaches that rely
517   on carefully engineered heuristics and treat attention and SSMs as black boxes, our method breaks
518   open this black box by providing a principled, domain-agnostic framework for modeling diverse data
519   modalities. We show that Chimera achieves state-of-the-art performance across domains including
520   language, vision, and graph tasks, consistently surpassing highly tuned domain-specific baselines,
521   which validates our premise and the proposed solution. Furthermore, we also show that for structured
522   domains like sequences and images, Chimera has an efficient linear complexity by leveraging our DAG
523
      decomposition technique, recovering the complexity of SSMs like Mamba-2.
524   Our work is the first step toward developing unified models for diverse data modalities. We believe
525   that extending the DAG decomposition technique to general graphs to achieve linear complexity is an
526   exciting direction for future work. Furthermore, we hope that the research community applies Chimera
527
      to more domains with an inherent underlying topology, and establishes Chimera as a strong baseline
      for further research in those domains.
