009                                              A BSTRACT
010
011            Diffusion models are predominantly used for generative modeling, which synthe-
012            size samples by simulating the reverse process of a stochastic differential equation
013            (SDE) that diffuses data into Gaussian noise. However, when simulating the re-
014            verse SDE, the SDE solver suffers from numerical instability near the time bound-
015            ary; hence, in practice, the simulation is terminated before reaching the boundary
016            point. This heuristic time truncation hinders the rigorous formulation of diffu-
017            sion models, and requires additional costs of hyperparameter tuning. Moreover,
018
               such numerical instability often occurs even in training, especially when using a
               maximum likelihood loss. Therefore, the current diffusion model heavily relies
019
               on the time truncation technique in both training and inference. In this paper,
020
               we propose a method that completely eliminates the heuristic of time truncation.
021            Our method eliminates numerical instability during maximum likelihood training
022            by modifying the parameterization of the noise predictor and the noise schedule.
023            We also propose a novel SDE solver that can simulate without time truncation by
024            taking advantage of the semi-linear structure of the reverse SDE. These improve-
025            ments enable stable training and sampling of diffusion models without relying on
026            time truncation. In our experiments, we tested the effectiveness of our method on
027            the CIFAR-10 and ImageNet-32 datasets by evaluating the test likelihood and the
028            sample quality measured by the Fréchet inception distance (FID). We observe that
029
               our method consistently improve performance in both test likelihood and the FID
               compared to the baseline model of DDPM++.

      1   I NTRODUCTION
033
034
      Diffusion probabilistic models (Sohl-Dickstein et al., 2015; Ho et al., 2020) and score-based gener-
035
      ative models (Song & Ermon, 2019; 2020) have achieved state-of-the-art performance in terms of
036   sample quality especially for image generation. Both models consider to pertub data with a sequence
037   of noise distributions, and generate samples by learning to reverse the diffusion process from noise
038   to data. Song et al. (2020b) have shown that these two types of models can be interpreted using a
039   single framework, which we refer to as diffusion models in this paper.
040
      The framework of diffusion models (Song et al., 2020b) involves gradually diffusing the data dis-
041
      tribution towards a simple noise distribution, such as the standard Gaussian distribution, using a
042   stochastic differential equation (SDE), and learning the time reversal of this SDE for generative mod-
043   eling. The reverse-time SDE has an analytic expression which only depends on a time-dependent
044   score function of the perturbed data distribution. This score function can be efficiently estimated
045   by training a neural network (called a score-based model (Song & Ermon, 2019; 2020)) with a
046   weighted combination of score matching losses (Hyvärinen & Dayan, 2005; Vincent, 2011; Song
047   et al., 2020a) as the objective. After training, we can obtain samples from the model by simulating
048   the reverse SDE from a simple noise using the estimated score function.
049   However, when simulating the reverse-time SDE, the SDE solver suffers from numerical instabil-
050   ity near the time boundary. This is mainly because the estimated score function diverges near the
051   boundary, and simulation around the boundary region becomes infeasible with a numerical SDE
052   solver. To avoid the numerical instability, the simulation is terminated before reaching the bound-
053   ary point in practice. Moreover, such numerical instability is often observed even during training,
      especially when the model is trained with a maximum likelihood objective. Therefore, heuristics

      like time truncation is widely used in both training and inference of diffusion models. Although
055   time truncation is one of the most naive ways to avoid numerical instability, it requires tuning of the
056   truncation time and also breaks the rigorous formulation of the diffusion model.
057
058
      In this paper, we propose a method to completely eliminate the heuristic of time truncation from
      both training and inference of diffusion models. First, to eliminate time truncation during training,
059
      we consider sufficient conditions for the maximum likelihood objective not to diverge. Specifically,
060
      by using a specific noise schedule and parameterization, we show that the objective becomes always
061   finite even around the boundary points. This prevents the diffusion model from suffering from
062   numerical instability when training with the maximum likelihood objective. We also provide a way
063   to reduce variance of the Monte-Carlo estimate of the objective. Second, we propose a new SDE
064   solver to eliminate time truncation time during sampling. This solver avoids numerical instability at
065   boundary points by taking advantage of the semi-linear structure of the reverse SDE.
066
      By combining these techniques, we successfully remove the dependence on time truncation from
067   both training and inference of the diffusion model. We name this framework FullDiffusion. In
068   experiments, we validate the effectiveness of FullDiffusion on CIFAR-10 and ImegeNet 32x32 using
069   DDPM++ as a baseline and confirm that it consistently outperforms the baseline in terms of both
070   likelihood and sample quality measured by the Fréchet inception distance (FID).

200   3     M ETHOD : F ULL D IFFUSION
201
202   In this section, we provide a way to eliminate the time truncation from both training and sampling of
203
      diffusion models. Specifically, we first demonstrate that the divergence of the ELBO objective at the
      boundary points can be avoided by carefully designing the parameterization and the noise schedule.
204
      By this modification, we can eliminate time truncation from training especially for the maximum
205
      likelihood objective. Furthermore, we provide a way to reduce the variance of the Monte-Carlo
206   estimation of the ELBO objective using stratified sampling. Finally, to eliminate time truncation
207   from sampling, we introduce a novel numerical SDE solver to avoid the divergence during the SDE
208   simulation. By combining all of them, we can stably train and sample from diffusion models without
209   relying on any heuristics like time truncation. We name the framework of this training and sampling
210   scheme for diffusion models FullDiffusion.
211

353   4     R ELATED W ORKS
354
355   4.1   N UMERICAL I NSTABILITY IN D IFFUSION M ODELS
356
      The numerical instability of continuous-time diffusion models around the boundary points has been
357
      widely recognized ever since the original paper by Song & Ermon (2019). However, to the best of
358   our knowledge, almost all previous works still rely on time truncation to deal with it (Kingma et al.,
359   2021; Karras et al., 2022). One of the most related attempts regarding this topic is a technique called
360   soft truncation (Kim et al., 2022), in which the truncation time tmin is randomly chosen during
361   training. Although soft truncation alleviates the numerical instablity during training, it still requires
362   the choice of a minimum truncation time. Yang et al. (2024) have also tackled the issue of the
363   numerical instability, and pointed out that the Lipschitz constant of the noise predictor ϵ̂θ tends to
364   diverge near the boundary point at t = 0. To alleviate it, they propose to round the time variable
365   t near the boundary point with a staircase function when inputting small t to the noise predictor.
366
      While they experimentally demonstrate the effectiveness of this method, they only apply it to the
      discrete-time diffusion model, so the applicability to the continuous-time model is still unclear.
367
      Moreover, the rounding operation loses information about time near the boundary point, which may
368
      leads to performance degradation especially for continuous-time models. On the other hand, our
369   method can fundamentally solve the problem of numerical instability by the design of the model
370   parameterization, the noise schedule, and the numerical solver.
371

397   4.3     PARAMETERIZATION & N OISE S CHEDULE
398
399   In the original paper by Song & Ermon (2019), the noise predictor ϵ̂θ is directly parameter-
400
      ized by a neural network (e.g., U-Net), and many subsequent works follow that parameteriza-
      tion. However, some variants are also proposed in the previous works, such as the signal predictor
401
      x̂θ = (xt − ϵ̂θ ) /αt , the velocity predictor v̂θ = (ϵ̂θ − σt xt ) /αt (Salimans & Ho, 2022). How-
402
      ever, these variants also suffer from the numerical instability around the boundary points, so they do
403   not contribute to our motivation.
404
405   On the noise schedule, Song & Ermon (2019) use the linear gt2 schedule as described in Section 2.2,
406
      but many variants have been proposed in previous works. For example, the cosine αt schedule is
      often used (Nichol & Dhariwal, 2021; Salimans & Ho, 2022; Choi et al., 2022). In this paper, we
407
      show that the combination of the linear σt schedule and the parameterization in Eq. (15) contributes
408
      to the stable maximum likelihood training without time truncation. However, there might be other
409   variants to achive the same goal, which we leave as future work.

480   6   C ONCLUSION
481
482   In this paper, we propose FullDiffusion, a framework to train and infer score-based diffusion models
483   without relying on time truncation around the boundary points. To overcome inherent numerical in-
484   stability of diffusion models, we reformulate the parameterization and the noise schedule so that the
485   maximum likelihood objective does not diverge around the boundary points. Moreover, to avoid the
      divergence during SDE simulation, we propose a special SDE solver named FullDiffusion-Solver.
