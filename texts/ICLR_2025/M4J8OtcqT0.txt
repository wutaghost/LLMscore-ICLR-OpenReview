011                                                A BSTRACT
012
013            Vision-Language Models (VLMs) have demonstrated remarkable generalization
014            across tasks by aligning visual and linguistic representations. High-quality vi-
015
               sual instruction data is critical for enhancing the performance of Vision-Language
               Models. However, current visual instruction tuning datasets, which are primarily
016
               derived from past visual tasks, have several limitations. For instance, the range of
017
               question types is often restricted and closely tied to the original visual tasks. Fur-
018            thermore, image diversity is limited, as images collected for various specialized
019            vision tasks clearly fail to adequately represent real-world user queries. Addi-
020            tionally, previous instruction datasets tend to lack complexity, focusing on sin-
021            gle tasks like captioning or OCR, which makes it challenging to train models
022            for more complex, multi-skill scenarios. To address these limitations, we pro-
023            pose a novel paradigms called strategy-centric synthesis: automatically synthe-
024            sizing high-quality instruction data from large-scale image-text pairs. First, we
025            employ an efficient heuristic method to select high-quality, complex images from
026
               DataComp-1B image-text pairs. Carefully crafted prompts and these images are
               fed to VLMs to extract high-quality query strategies and generate corresponding
027
               image descriptions. These descriptions are subsequently used to retrieve images
028
               aligned with specific questioning strategies. Finally, the retrieved images and
029            their matching strategies are used to synthesize high-quality instructional data.
030            Our experiments indicate that with continued instruction fine-tuning via LoRA on
031            only 3,000 newly synthesized data samples, 0.45% of the LLAVA-1.5 instruction
032            tuning dataset, the model significantly outperforms the original LLAVA-1.5-7B
033            across multiple benchmarks, thereby demonstrating the effectiveness of our ap-
034            proach.

047   diverse user requests that involve visual content. For the pretraining stage, the abundance of image-
048   text pairs accumulated from prior research means that data is not a significant bottleneck. However,
049   in the visual instruction tuning stage, there is a clear lack of sufficient high-quality instruction data.

      erate instruction data.

            1. Lack of Diversity: Since most instruction datasets are derived from previous vision tasks,
082            these datasets face limitations in diversity, specifically in the following aspects: (1) Limited
083            Question Types: The types of questions are highly correlated with the original tasks, and
084            the categories of tasks themselves are limited. For instance, Xu et al. (2023) compiled
085            nearly all past vision tasks but only managed to obtain 200+ diverse vision-language tasks,
086            resulting in a limited variety of question types. (2) Limited Image Distribution: The images
087            collected for specialized vision tasks are clearly insufficient to cover the distribution of
088            real-world user queries. (2) Limited Variety of Prompt Templates Used for Synthesis:
089            Past work has often used a static template to prompt VLMs (vision-language models) to
090
               synthesize instruction data for different images, such as detailed captions (Chen et al.,
               2023b) or complex reasoning tasks (Liu et al., 2024c; Chen et al., 2024), which restricts
091
               the full potential of the VLMs.
092
093         2. Lack of Complexity: In most previous queries, only one basic visual ability, such as cap-
094            tioning or OCR, is typically involved. However, real-world scenarios often require a com-
095
               bination of multiple abilities to resolve queries. Fine-tuning on previous instruction data
               does not adequately teach VLMs to master a combination of multiple capabilities.
096
097         3. Mismatch Between Images and Prompt Templates: Each image has an optimal ques-
098            tioning approach, but previous synthesis methods have not sufficiently considered this. For
099
               instance, simple images selected from image caption datasets are sometimes forced into
               generating complex reasoning instruction data (Liu et al., 2024c; Chen et al., 2024), which
100
               can lead the model to produce divergent questions rather than high-quality reasoning prob-
101
               lems.
102
103   To address the aforementioned issues, we propose a novel paradigms called strategy-centric synthe-
104   sis. Before presenting our method, we introduce the query strategy for visual instructions, which
105   refers to a general questioning perspective applicable to images with some shared characteristics.
106   As illustrated in Figure 1, the strategy can be used to guide the question synthesis for a category of
107   images. It is evident that query strategies are more fine-grained than foundational visual task de-
      scriptions, making them more suitable for handling complex scenarios. In fact, various basic visual

      tasks can be viewed as specific query strategies within our method. For instance, an OCR-related
109   task can correspond to the strategy: designing questions about text recognition in the image.

111   Our method centers around strategies and consists of two primary components: (1) automated strat-
112
      egy mining from seed images using visual language models, and (2) strategy-guided multimodal in-
      struction synthesis. First, we introduce a heuristic and efficient approach for selecting high-quality,
113
      complex images from large-scale image-text pairs by leveraging domain-specific visual keywords to
114
      filter image captions and identify relevant images. Using this approach, we construct a diverse and
115   complex seed image library from the recaptioned DataComp-1B dataset (Li et al., 2024). Carefully
116   designed prompts are then applied to mine high-quality query strategies and generate corresponding
117   image type descriptions. After eliminating redundant strategies, we use the image type descriptions
118   to retrieve matching images, guide question generation based on the associated strategies, and pro-
119   duce detailed, step-by-step answers. Finally, a self-reflection step is implemented to evaluate the
120   quality of the synthesized instructions.
121   Our core contributions are as follows:
122
123         1. The proposed strategy-centric data synthesis approach effectively addresses several clear
124            limitations observed in existing visual instruction datasets. By integrating query strategies
125            into the synthesis process, we enhance the diversity of both prompt templates and question
126            types. Moreover, these strategies guide visual language models to generate higher-quality,
127            complex instruction queries at a finer granularity. During synthesis, retrieving matching
128
               images based on the strategy’s corresponding image type descriptions also significantly
               mitigates the mismatch between images and prompt templates.
129
130         2. We introduce an automated strategy mining approach, starting with a heuristic retrieval
131            method to efficiently collect images suitable for generating complex queries. Using these
132            seed images, we prompt the visual language model to generate query strategies. Our meth-
133
               ods connects billions of image-text pairs to high-quality visual instruction data, providing
               potential scalability for high-quality data synthesis.
134
135         3. After continued LoRA instruction tuning using only 3k synthesized data samples, 0.45% of
136            the LLAVA-1.5 instruction tuning dataset, the model significantly outperforms the original
137
               LLAVA-1.5-7B across multiple benchmarks.

139   2   R ELATED WORK
140
141   Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs) have made significant strides in recent years, driven
142   by the success of Large Language Models (LLMs). Typical architectures consist of a pre-trained
143   visual backbone for encoding visual features, a pre-trained LLM to interpret user instructions and
144   generate responses, and a vision-language cross-modal connector to align visual encoder outputs
145   with the language model. Models such as LLaVA (Liu et al., 2024c;a) and MiniGPT-4 (Zhu et al.,
146   2023) have demonstrated strong cross-task generalization. mPLUG-Owl (Ye et al., 2023; 2024),
147
      Shikra(Chen et al., 2023a), and KOSMOS-2 (Peng et al., 2023) have introduced novel data types
      and training methods, such as grounding data, aimed at reducing hallucinations and improving the
148
      grounding capabilities of LLMs. LLaVA-NeXT (Liu et al., 2024b) has significantly enhanced vi-
149
      sual perception by utilizing dynamic resolution techniques, while Cambrain1 (Tong et al., 2024)
150   has improved model robustness through visual encoder routing. Recently, Luo et al. (2024); Xie
151   et al. (2024); Zhou et al. (2024) have combined diffusion models with LLMs to enhance both the
152   generative and understanding capabilities of MLLMs.
153
      Training an instruction-following LMM typically follows a two-stage protocol. First, the vision-
154
      language alignment pretraining stage leverages image-text pairs to align visual features with the
155
      language model’s word embedding space. Second, the visual instruction tuning stage fine-tunes the
156   model on visual instructions, enabling it to handle diverse user requests that involve visual content.
157   For the pretraining stage, the abundance of image-text pairs accumulated from prior research means
158   that data is not a significant bottleneck. However, in the visual instruction tuning stage, there is
159   a clear lack of sufficient high-quality instruction data. Previous approaches have transformed data
160   from single-task visual datasets using templates (Xu et al., 2022), manual annotations (Xu et al.,
161   2023), language models (Liu et al., 2024c; Tong et al., 2024), or vision-language models (Zhao et al.,
      2023; Wang et al., 2023; Chen et al., 2023b) to generate instruction data. Unlike these datasets,

      3     METHOD
199
      As shown in Figure 2, our approach consists of two key components: (1) the automated mining
200
      of high-quality strategies from seed images using visual language models, and (2) strategy-guided
201
      multimodal instruction data synthesis.
202

      3.1     AUTOMATED S TRATEGY M INING
204
205   Manually annotating query strategies is prohibitively costly and time-consuming. In practice, we
206   have found that with carefully designed prompts, powerful visual language models can mine high-
207   quality potential query strategies directly from representative images. This insight led us to develop
208   the automated mining method.
209
      High-Quality Seed Image Library Construction The construction of a high-quality seed image
210   library focuses on two main criteria: (1) Diversity: Traditional instruction datasets built from indi-
211   vidual academic tasks often contain many homogeneous images. To cover a broader range of image
212   types, we leverage recaptioned DataComp-1B image-text pairs (Li et al., 2024). We start by identi-
213   fying common real-world domains, such as science, medicine, and business. For each domain, we
214   generate a series of visual keywords using large language models (LLMs) through prompts, then
215   use these keywords to filter captions from the large-scale image-text pairs, allowing us to acquire
      matching images. (2) Complexity: We assume that the more keywords in the caption of the image,

252   on a threshold determined through empirical testing.

334   Our dataset construction primarily builds on the recaptioned dataset (Li et al., 2024), which employs
335
      a vision-language model (VLM) to recaption billions of text-image pairs. We began by identifying
      several distinct domains: Tech, Art, Business, Medicine, Science, and Sociology. With the help of
336
      GPT-4 and manual verification, we curated a set of visualization-related keywords for each domain,
337
      which can be found in the Appendix. We hypothesize that the more keywords in the caption of the
338   image, the greater its potential for generating complex questions. Therefore, if a caption contains
339   more than four visualizable keywords and the image resolution exceeds (336, 336), we download
340   and save the image. By traversing billions of text-image pairs, we successfully downloaded a diverse
341   set of images across these domains, as detailed in Table 1.
342
      Next, we applied k-means clustering techniques to these images, grouping them into 1,00 clusters
343
      for each domain. It is important to emphasize that more clusters can be set here; however, due to
344   our limited budget, we only need a small number of images, so we choose to use a relatively small
345   clustering cluster. For each cluster, we selected the image closest to the centroid as the representative
346   image, while the remaining images were reserved for future retrieval. Using the GPT-4o visual
347   language model, we generated general and detailed questioning strategies from multiple perspectives
348   for each image. Using regular expressions, we extracted approximately 2,000 strategies and their
349   corresponding image type descriptions from the response. We then applied semantic deduplication
350   to these strategies, utilizing OpenAI’s ”text-embedding-3-small” model to obtain their embedded
351   representations. By setting a cosine similarity threshold of 0.65, we reduced the set to about 1000
352
      unique questioning strategies.
353   When retrieving images using image type descriptions, we employed the CLIP model (Li et al.,
354   2024), which was trained on a large corpus of internet images. Given that different strategies might
355   correspond to similar image types, some overlap in the retrieved images was anticipated. To address
356   this, we randomly selected one image from the top k (k=5) retrieval results to increase diversity.
357   Finally, considering budgetary constraints, we synthesized three data cases per strategy using the
358
      GPT-4o model. Therefore, the final synthesized dataset consists of 3k instances, which is approx-
      imately only 0.45% of the original 665k instruction fine-tuning data. The code and data will be
359
      open-sourced.

      We selected the popular instruction-tuned LLaVA-v1.5-7B (Liu et al., 2024a) model as our base-
364
      line and adopted the LoRA technique for further instruction tuning. There are two reasons for this
365
      choice: (1) Due to limited budget and computational resources, the scale of our synthetic dataset is
366   relatively small, making it challenging to perform full-scale instruction tuning from scratch. (2) The
367   newly constructed instruction data is often more complex, involving combinations of multiple sub-
368   tasks, which is more suitable for continued learning in a model that already possesses foundational
369   capabilities. The format of the synthetic data is parsed to be consistent with the original instruction
370   dataset of LLaVA-1.5 (Liu et al., 2024a). Additionally, to ensure reproducibility, all hyperparam-
371   eters used during the LoRA fine-tuning process are kept identical to the original script (Liu et al.,
372   2024a), as detailed in the appendix.
373   We evaluated the model on multiple mainstream benchmarks: ChartQA (Masry et al., 2022),
374   MME(Fu et al., 2023) MMBench (Liu et al., 2023), MMMU(Yue et al., 2024), POPE (Li et al.,
375   2023b) , ScienceQA IMG (Lu et al., 2022) , TextVQA (val)(Singh et al., 2019) ,VizWiz (Gurari
376   et al., 2018), DocVQA (Mathew et al., 2020). We utilized LLMs-Eval (Bo Li* & Liu, 2024) as the
377   evaluation tool and, to ensure reproducibility, maintained all evaluation parameters at their default
      settings without any modifications.

      We used GPT-4 to synthesize 1,000 data instances under this setting. (2) The contribution of GPT-4o

      4o without strategies to synthesize the instruction data. This experiment fully ablates our method,

      5     CONCLUSION
478
479
      To address the key limitations of existing visual instruction tuning datasets, we introduced a novel
480   strategy-driven approach that synthesizes high-quality instruction data from large-scale image-text
481   pairs. Through a carefully designed pipeline, we automated the process of mining strategies and
482   generating detailed, multimodal instructions tailored to the characteristics of each image. Empiri-
483   cal results demonstrated the effectiveness of our approach, with significant improvements observed
484   after continue fine-tuning using only 3,000 synthesized data samples. The model outperformed
485   LLAVA-1.5-7B across multiple benchmarks, validating the potential of strategy-guided multimodal
      data synthesis in advancing the performance of vision-language models. For future work, we plan
