011                                               A BSTRACT
012
013            Extending the context window in large language models (LLMs) is essential for
014            applications involving long-form content generation. However, the quadratic com-
015
               plexity of self-attention and the linear increase in key-value (KV) cache memory
               requirements with respect to sequence length present significant challenges dur-
016
               ing fine-tuning and inference. Although LongLoRA achieves efficient fine-tuning
017
               by employing shifted sparse attention, inference remains inefficient due to the re-
018            quirement for dense global attention. In this work, we introduce a novel context
019            extension method that optimizes both fine-tuning and inference efficiency. Our
020            method exploits a key observation: in the frequency domain, the energy distri-
021            bution of the KV cache is primarily concentrated in low-frequency components.
022            By filtering out the high-frequency components, the KV cache can be effectively
023            compressed with minimal information loss. Building on this insight, we propose
024            an efficient compression technique, FreqKV, that iteratively reduces the increasing
025            KV cache to a fixed size in the frequency domain, applicable to both fine-tuning
026
               and inference. With minimal fine-tuning, LLMs can learn to leverage the lim-
               ited cache that is compressed in the frequency domain and extend the context
027
               window efficiently. FreqKV introduces no additional parameters or architectural
028
               modifications, ensuring compatibility with the original full attention post-training.
029            Experiments on long context language modeling and understanding demonstrate
030            the efficiency and efficacy of the proposed method.

033   1   I NTRODUCTION
034
035   Large language models (LLMs) typically have a limited size of context window, which is pre-defined
036   during the pre-training process. However, it is inevitable for LLMs to process sequences that exceed
037   the preset context size. LLMs struggle to maintain their performance when generalized to longer
038
      contexts. Additionally, the computation cost of the self-attention mechanism (Vaswani et al., 2023)
      grows quadratically with the context length, meaning that doubling the context window results in a
039
      fourfold increase in the computational cost of attention modules.
040
041   For efficiency, existing efforts aim to compress the key-value (KV) cache for long contexts dur-
042   ing inference. They evict (Xiao et al., 2024; Li et al., 2024) or merge (Zhang et al., 2024b; Wan
043   et al., 2024) KV states of less important tokens following certain rules. They use attention scores to
044
      measure the importance and approximate the original full attention. However, while these methods
      provide an approximation of full computation on existing tokens through different strategies, they
045
      can not fully prevent performance degradation when decoding future tokens.
046
047   Recent studies propose to fine-tune LLMs to longer contexts to extend the context window. Lon-
048   gLoRA (Chen et al., 2024) trains LLMs using shifted sparse attention. Despite training efficiency,
049   their sparse attention fails to be applied during inference, and they still require the original atten-
050
      tion on the full sequence. Concurrently, LoCoCo (Cai et al., 2024a) and Activation Beacon (Zhang
      et al., 2024a) introduce additional modules to compress KV states. They incorporate the fine-tuned
051
      compressing pattern into the decoding procedure of LLMs.
052
053   In the field of computer vision, studies have shown that low-frequency channels are more important
      for convolutional neural networks (CNNs) (Xu et al., 2020). Moreover, Fourier Transformer (He

054
      et al., 2023) discards the high-frequency parts of the contexts and downsample the hidden states
055   in the encoders. Inspired by these work, we seek to compress KV states in the frequency domain
056   without the need for additional compression modules in decoder-only LLMs. We transform key
057   states and value states in LLaMA-2-7b (Touvron et al., 2023) from the time domain to the frequency
058   domain for power spectrum analysis. As shown in Figure 1, the energy distribution increasingly
059   concentrates on low-frequency components as the computation process progresses inside the model.
060   This suggests that high-frequency components, which contribute less to the overall information,
061   can be discarded without a significant impact on performance, thereby enhancing computational
062   efficiency.
063   In this paper, we introduce FreqKV, an efficient context extension method that iteratively com-
064   presses key-value states in the frequency domain. Compression is triggered only when the KV cache
065   reaches the predefined context window size. We keep the first few tokens uncompressed due to the
066   attention sinks of LLMs (Xiao et al., 2024; Han et al., 2024). During each compression step, the
067   low-frequency components of the KV states are preserved at a specified retaining ratio. Subsequent
068   tokens are appended to the compressed cache until it is filled again. This ensures that the maximum
069
      number of cached KV states that each query token can attend to is limited below the context window
      size. To reduce memory and computational costs, the compressed cache will be further compressed
070
      together with the incoming tokens. This iterative compression mechanism leads to an increased
071
      compression level of the earlier contexts as the sequence length grows. Without introducing ad-
072   ditional compression modules, LLMs could learn to utilize the compressed cache efficiently when
073   extending to longer contexts. FreqKV demonstrates comparable performance to other methods that
074   employ full KV cache or additional compressors in long context language modeling. Furthermore,
075   experiments on LongBench (Bai et al., 2024) indicate that FreqKV surpasses recently studied KV
076   compression methods in long-context understanding, achieving higher scores on open-ended text
077   generation tasks.

      2   R ELATED W ORK
080
081
      KV Compression for LLMs. To extend the context window of LLMs efficiently, researchers
082
      attempt to compress the KV cache as more tokens are fed into the model. One common approach is
083
      selective token eviction (Xiao et al., 2024; Li et al., 2024; Cai. et al., 2024b), where less significant
084   tokens are discarded. Although the eviction strategies ensure that the size of KV cache involved
085   in each decoding step does not exceed the pre-defined context window size, LLMs suffer from
086   the permanent loss of the information associated with evicted tokens. To address this limitation,
087   some researchers introduce cache merging techniques to approximate the original full attention of
088   the existing contexts (Zhang et al., 2024b; Wan et al., 2024; Wang et al., 2024). However, these
089   inference methods often sacrifice performance for efficiency.
090
091   Context Extension for LLMs. Recent advancements in context extension for LLMs have fo-
092   cused on efficiently scaling models to handle longer input sequences without significantly increas-
093   ing computational costs. LongLoRA (Chen et al., 2024) employs shifted sparse attention during
094   the parameter-efficient fine-tuning. However, this sparse attention mechanism is not applicable dur-
095
      ing inference, necessitating a return to the original full attention post-training. Other techniques,
      such as LoCoCo (Cai et al., 2024a), integrate convolutional operations into LLMs for compressing
096
      long contexts. They fine-tune the compression modules together with LLMs. Landmark attention
097
      (Mohtashami & Jaggi, 2023) uses landmark tokens to retrieve previous input blocks. Similarly, Ac-
098   tivation Beacon (Zhang et al., 2024a) proposes the use of a special token to represent the previous
099   context for compression. However, they introduce a copy of multi-head attention parameters, which
100   can amount to approximately 2 billion for 7 billion parameter models. In contrast, our proposed
101   method achieves context extension without introducing any additional parameters.
102
103   Learning in the Frequency Domain. Learning in the frequency domain is a well-established
104   technique to compress images and accelerate CNNs (Gueguen et al., 2018). It has been observed
105   that CNNs are more sensitive to low-frequency channels than high-frequency channels (Xu et al.,
106   2020). These works have inspired efforts to process natural language. FNet (Lee-Thorp et al., 2022)
107   enhances the efficiency of Transformer encoder architectures by replacing the self-attention layers
      with the Fourier transform to serve the purpose of mixing tokens. Additionally, Fourier Transformer


                                                         2
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


108
      (He et al., 2023) eliminates redundancies in the context through frequency domain processing within
109   encoder architectures.
110
111   However, because of the auto-regressive nature, it remains unclear how to leverage frequency com-
112
      ponents for decoder-only Transformer, which is the main architecture of generative LLMs. To the
      best of our knowledge, FreqKV is the first work that explores compressing key-value states in the
113
      frequency domain for decoder-only LLMs.

      4.3     C ONTEXT E XTENSION WITH THE C OMPRESSED KV
240
241   When extending the context window, the memory requirement of KV cache increases linearly and
242   the computation cost of the original full attention grows quadratically with the length. To limit the
243   size of KV cache for each query token to attend below the context window size, we will compress
244   KV cache in the frequency domain iteratively as the cache is filled. We illustrate our FreqKV in
245   Figure 2.
246
      For tokens within the context window, the standard attention will be conducted. For tokens out of
247   the window, we will compress the cached KV states in the frequency domain. Because the retaining
248   size L is smaller than the cache size N , subsequent tokens could get in and fill the cache. We discard
249   the original KV states of the prefilling tokens and maintain the compressed KV states. They will be
250   concatenated with the KV states of the incoming tokens and compressed together when the cache
251   is filled again. Tokens that appear earlier in the sequence undergo more iterations of compression
252   as the context window expands, whereas less compression will be performed on the more recent
253   tokens. With the iterative FreqKV, the KV cache size is not fixed and is reduced below the context
254   window size during decoding. Since the compression is only performed when the sum of cached KV
255
      and incoming tokens reaches the preset size, the computation overhead of the compression could be
      negligible. For example, with the context size of 4096 and the retaining size of 2048, compression
256
      is performed every 2048 tokens for contexts exceeding the original window.
257
258   Recent work has found the phenomenon of attention sinks that LLMs tend to assign high attention
259   scores to initial tokens (Xiao et al., 2024; Han et al., 2024). Therefore, we maintain these initial
260   tokens uncompressed in the cache and only compress tokens that come after them.
261   During training and the prefilling stage of inference, the whole sentence is tokenized and fed into
262   the model. The attention is computed chunk-wise interleaved with the compression operation. After
263   each compression, N − L − S incoming tokens are regarded as a chunk and fill the cache, with
264   S sink tokens uncompressed. As shown in Figure 2 (b), the incoming tokens in each chunk can
265   not attend to the subsequent tokens. The newly incoming token xM will attend to S sink tokens, L
266   compressed “tokens”, M − N previous incoming tokens, and xM itself. The calculation of attention

      5.1    I MPLEMENTATION
302
303
      We conduct experiments on long context language modeling and understanding tasks with LLaMA-
304
      2-7b (Touvron et al., 2023) base and chat models. Minimal training is introduced to adapt the model
305
      to this frequency-domain compression method. For long context language modeling, we fine-tune
306   LLaMA-2-7b on the RedPajama (Computer, 2023) pre-training dataset for 1000 steps, extending
307   the context window size from 4K to 8K, 16K, and 32K. Perplexity (PPL) evaluation is conducted on
308   PG-19 (Rae et al., 2019) and Proof-pile (Azerbayev et al., 2022). For long context understanding,
309   the instruction following dataset LongAlpaca (Chen et al., 2024) is used for the supervised fine-
310   tuning (SFT) of the chat model. The context window size is extended from 4K to 8K. The model
311   is trained on 6.28K long-context QA samples for 5 epochs and evaluated on LongBench (Bai et al.,
312   2024).
313   The total batch size (GP U number × Batch size per device × Gradient accumulation steps)
314   is 64. The learning rate increases linearly from 1e-6 to 2e-5 with 20 warm-up steps and remains
315   constant in the following steps. The rank used in the LoRA (Hu et al., 2021) fine-tuning is set to
316   8. Following LongLoRA (Chen et al., 2024), the embedding and normalization layers are learnable
317   during training.
318   The preset context window size of LLaMA-2 is 4096, which is also the maximum KV cache size
319   N . We maintain S = 4 sink tokens uncompressed. The retaining ratio γ in compression is set to
320   0.5. Therefore, the retaining size during each compression is L = γ · (N − S) = 2046. As long
321   as the cache size reaches its capacity of 4096, the 4092 states since the 5-th state in the cache will
322   be compressed into 2046 states. All the experiments are conducted on ADA6000 and RTX4090
323   GPUs. Moreover, we equip our method with FlashAttention-2 (Dao, 2023) for further acceleration
      and memory saving.

414
415   We conduct further studies on the computation cost and performance regarding different retaining
416   ratios. As introduced in Section 4.3, chunk-wise attention is performed for the prefilling tokens. The
417   size of the attention matrix in each chunk is (N −L−S)·N except for the last few tokens. Therefore,
418   the computational cost of self-attention grows approximately linearly with the input length like
419   sliding window attention (Beltagy et al., 2020).
420   We use torchprofile1 to count the number of Floating Point Operations (FLOPs) with input sequences
421   of different lengths for LLaMA-2-7b. The statistics given in Table 4 show that FreqKV reduces more
422   FLOPs as the input length grows from 4K to 12K. Despite more compressions will be performed
423   with the retaining ratio ranging from 0.1 to 0.75, the growth in FLOPs is minimal. This is because
424   the compression is performed every N − L − S tokens with the complexity of O(N logN ), which
425   is negligible compared to the quadratic self-attention.

427   FreqKV with different retaining ratios. The evaluation context length is 8192. Results are shown in
428   Figure 4. While the model performs better in long context language modeling as the retaining ratio

      7   C ONCLUSION
487
488
      In this paper, we introduce FreqKV to compress KV states iteratively in the frequency domain for
489   LLMs. We exploit the energy concentration of KV states in the frequency domain within the de-
490   coder layers. Specifically, we filter out the high-frequency components that are of low magnitude
491   and retain the low-frequency components for compression. The KV cache is compressed in the fre-
492   quency domain without introducing additional compression modules. Iteratively compressing the
493   KV cache, FreqKV could extend the context window efficiently for LLMs. With minimal training
494   of low-rank adaption, LLMs learn to leverage the compressed KV cache. Through extensive experi-
495   ments and analysis on long context modeling and understanding, FreqKV demonstrates its efficiency
496   and effectiveness in context extension.
