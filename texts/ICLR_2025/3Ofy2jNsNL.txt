011                                                A BSTRACT
012
013            High-resolution inputs empower Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs)
014            to capture intricate visual details, thereby enhancing comprehension. However, the
015            self-attention mechanism’s quadratic complexity poses significant computational
016            and memory challenges as image resolution increases, particularly with long-vision
017            tokens. Existing approaches generally alleviate these issues by reducing vision
018
               tokens before feeding them into LLMs. Although efficient, this Pre-LLM com-
               pression strategy fails to match the performance of models utilizing all tokens,
019
               particularly on high-resolution benchmarks. Our experiments reveal that the perfor-
020
               mance gap arises from this strategy’s limitation in selecting important visual tokens
021            in early LLM layers, leading to the irretrievable loss of critical information. To
022            overcome these challenges, we propose a new strategy that Adaptively Compresses
023            vision Tokens within different LLM layers, named ACT- IN -LLM. Our innovative
024            approach retains all tokens throughout the layers to ensure no vital information
025            is lost while compressing key and value tokens in the self-attention mechanism,
026            to reduce computational costs. The layer-wise compression of ACT- IN -LLM is
027            guided by the interaction information between vision and text tokens, leading
028            to more accurate selections. Our theoretical analysis and extensive experiments
029
               demonstrate the effectiveness of ACT- IN -LLM, showing a 6.3% improvement
               over existing token compression techniques. It also achieves the competitive per-
030
               formance with non-compression methods, while reducing training/inference time
031
               by ∼ 20% and vision tokens by ∼ 60%.

080   of the average attention weights of LLaVA-1.5-HD (Fig.2 (b)) shows that vision tokens receiving
081   low attention in early layers become critical in the latter, showing the risks of compressing tokens
082   prematurely. In summary, existing Pre-LLM approaches compress vision tokens too early, leading to
083   irreversible performance degradation, potentially due to: (1) early-layer’s insufficient interaction
084   between vision and text tokens, (2) varying token importance across layers, making it difficult to
085   decide which tokens to drop, and (3) the inability to recover lost information in latter layers.
086   To address the above drawbacks, we propose a novel compression strategy, ACT- IN -LLM, which
087   Adaptively Compresses vision Tokens within different LLM layers. Unlike existing methods that
088   discard tokens prematurely, ACT- IN -LLM retains all tokens across layers, ensuring an implicit
089   error correction mechanism that mitigates the loss of critical information (see Fig. 1 (b)). To reduce
090   computational and memory overhead, ACT- IN -LLM uniformly integrates an adaptive compression
091   module (ACM) into various transformer decoder layers, selectively compressing only the key and
092   value tokens within the self-attention mechanism. Specifically, ACM utilizes the final token in each
093
      layer’s hidden states— which encodes the complete multimodal context— to guide visual token
      compression, ensuring more accurate token selection, compared with early-layer selection.
094
095   We theoretically demonstrate that this key-value compression used in ACT- IN -LLM provides a
096   better low-rank approximation of the full-token self-attention mechanism compared to the query or
097   all compression used in existing vision token compression techniques. Extensive experiments on
098   high-resolution and general benchmarks, across LLMs of varying sizes (0.5B to 7B parameters), show
099
      that ACT- IN -LLM achieves a 6.2% improvement over existing token compression techniques, and
      competitive performance compared with non-compression models while reducing training/inference
100
      time by ∼ 20% and vision tokens by ∼ 60%.

103   2    R ELATED W ORKS
104   Multimodal Large Language Models. Advanced Large Language Models (LLMs) like GPT-
105   4 (OpenAI, 2023), Mistral (Jiang et al., 2023), and Gemini (Team et al., 2023) excel in reasoning,
106   while Multimodal LLMs (MLLMs), such as LLaVA (Liu et al., 2023b), MiniGPT-4 (Zhu et al.,
107   2023), and QwenVL (Bai et al., 2023), extend this to images, though limited resolution hampers
      fine-grained visual understanding. To address this, splitting images into patches (Bavishi et al.,


                                                          2
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


108
      2023; Li et al., 2023a) and up-resizing methods (Bai et al., 2023) improve resolution, though they
109   introduce issues like poor visual representation and positional encoding disruptions (Radford et al.,
110   2021). Dual-branch methods add high-resolution branches but increase complexity and training
111   data requirements (Hong et al., 2024; Ding et al., 2023). Cropping strategies offer a more efficient
112   approach by dividing high-resolution images into patches without increasing model parameters (Li
113   et al., 2024d; Xu et al., 2024; Huang et al., 2024). However, increasing image resolution leads to
114   higher computational costs due to the quadratic complexity of self-attention (Vaswani, 2017). This
115   paper is aimed at developing efficient high-resolution MLLMs.
116   Vision Token Compression in MLLMs. Vision Token Compression in MLLMs. Existing methods
117   for vision token compression can be categorized into interaction-based and pre-LLM strategies.
118   Interaction-based approaches (Hong et al., 2024; Li et al., 2024b; Tong et al., 2024) process low-
119   resolution tokens in the LLM while using a high-resolution branch with lightweight cross-attention
120   for feature interaction. However, these methods fail to fully align high-resolution visual inputs with
121
      the LLM’s low-resolution and text representations, requiring additional parameters and training
      data (Huang et al., 2024; Yao et al., 2024). Pre-LLM approaches, on the other hand, reduce tokens
122
      before entering the LLM, employing either parameterized (Bai et al., 2023; Li et al., 2023c; Cha et al.,
123
      2024; Xu et al., 2024) or non-parameterized (Liu et al., 2024a; Yao et al., 2024; Shang et al., 2024)
124   techniques. Recently, Early-LLM methods such as FastV (Chen et al., 2024a) discard tokens during
125   early LLM layers at inference but risk losing critical information due to suboptimal token selection.
126   In contrast, ACT- IN -LLM introduces adaptive token compression at multiple LLM layers, retaining
127   all tokens for final predictions to minimize information loss. Theoretical analysis and experiments
128   demonstrate that ACT- IN -LLM outperforms existing approaches in efficiency and effectiveness.
129   Efficient Attention in Transformer Models. To reduce the computation and memory costs associ-
130   ated with the self-attention mechanism in Transformers, various alternative attention mechanisms
131   have been proposed (Child et al., 2019; Zaheer et al., 2020; Wang et al., 2020; Choromanski et al.,
132   2020). For instance, Sparse Transformer (Child et al., 2019) employs fixed sparse attention pat-
133   terns to reduce complexity. BIGBIRD (Zaheer et al., 2020) extends this approach by combining
134   multiple attention patterns, including window, random, and global attention, for further efficiency.
135
      Reformer (Kitaev et al., 2020) replaces traditional self-attention with locality-sensitive hashing (LSH)
      to reduce computation costs. Similarly, Axial Transformer (Beltagy et al., 2020) applies attention
136
      along single axes of input tensors, significantly lowering the computational burden. PvT-V2 (Wang
137
      et al., 2022) leverages the average-pooling to reduce the tokens of the key and value. All of the above
138   methods primarily target single-modal tasks. In contrast, our work focuses on reducing vision tokens
139   based on the multi-modal information in MLLMs for high-resolution multimodal tasks.

468   5.4   A BLATION S TUDY
469
      In this section, we evaluate the effectiveness of the Adaptive Compression Module (ACM), a pivotal
470
      component of ACT- IN -LLM. Using the baseline configuration outlined in Section 5.1, our ablation
471   study addresses three critical aspects: (i) compression ratios—quantifying vision token reduction,
472   (ii) compression implementation methods—strategies for token compression, and (iii) compression
473   layers—optimal layers for token reduction.
474
      Compression Ratios. Compression ratios dictate the number of vision tokens reduced, specifically
475
      characterized by rihr and rilr , which represent the reduction ratios for high-resolution and low-resolution
476
      tokens at layer i. We categorize the LLM layers into three types: the early layers with compression
477   ratio of rihr /rihr , the middle with compression ratio of rjhr /rjhr and the latter layers with compression
478
      ratio of rkhr /rkhr . To explore the best configuration of the compression ratios, our ablation study consists
479
      of two steps. First, we maintain equal compression ratios for he high-resolution and low-resolution
480   vision tokens (rhr = rlr ) and change r across different layer groups, i.e., plain type (ri = rj = rp )
481   and hierarchical type (ri ̸= rj ̸= rp ). Results (rows (a) to (e) in Table 4a) indicate that a hierarchical
482   approach (ri < rj < rp ) outperforms the plain type, aligning with the observed trend of sparser
483   attention in deeper layers (Fig.2(b)). Subsequently, we investigate distinct ratios for high- and low-
484   resolution tokens within each layer. The results from row (f) to row (i) in Table 4a demonstrate that
485   rhr > rlr performs better than rhr ≤ rlr , likely due to the higher low-rank nature of high-resolution
      tokens (Fig. 5).

      6       C ONCLUSION
530
531   In this paper, we introduce ACT- IN -LLM, which enhances the efficiency of multimodal large
532   language models (MLLMs) by adaptively compressing vision tokens across different LLM layers.
533
      Unlike prior methods that reduce vision tokens before LLM processing, our approach retains all
      tokens, providing an inherent error correction mechanism to prevent the loss of critical information.
534
      Additionally, the layer-wise compression is guided by interactions between vision and text tokens,
535
      ensuring precise token selection. Our theoretical analysis and extensive experiments demonstrate that
536   ACT- IN -LLM outperforms existing vision token compression techniques. Moreover, we reveal the
537   potential for scaling up ACT- IN -LLM to achieve competitive performance even with SOTA MLLMs
538   without vision token compression.

783   of the sum of the top r% singular values to the total sum of all singular values. A larger LDR indicates
784   a higher degree of low-rankness in the matrix, and vice versa.
