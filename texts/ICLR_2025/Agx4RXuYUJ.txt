009                                                A BSTRACT
010
011            This paper undertakes meticulous scrutiny of the pure logit-based distillation un-
012            der multi-label learning through the lens of activation function. We begin with
013            empirically clarifying a recently discovered perspective (Yang et al., 2023b;a) that
014            vanilla sigmoid per se is more suitable than tempered softmax in multi-label distil-
015            lation, is not entirely correct. After that, we reveal that both the sigmoid and tem-
016
               pered softmax have an intrinsic limitation. In particular, we conclude that ignoring
               the decisive factor temperature τ in the sigmoid is the essential reason for its unsat-
017
               isfactory results. With this regard, we propose unleashing the potential of temper-
018
               ature scaling in the multi-label distillation and present Tempered Logit Distillation
019            (TLD), an embarrassingly simple yet astonishingly performant approach. Specif-
020            ically, we modify the sigmoid with the temperature scaling mechanism, deriving
021            a new activation function, dubbed as tempered sigmoid. With theoretical and vi-
022            sual analysis, intriguingly, we identify that tempered sigmoid with τ smaller than
023            1 provides an effect of hard mining by governing the magnitude of penalties ac-
024            cording to the sample difficulty, which is shown as the key property to its success.
025            Our work is accompanied by comprehensive experiments on COCO, PASCAL-
026            VOC, and NUS-WIDE over several architectures across three multi-label learning
027
               scenarios: image classification, object detection, and instance segmentation. Dis-
               tillation results evidence that TLD consistently harvests remarkable performance
028
               and surpasses the prior counterparts, demonstrating its superiority and versatility.

      Based on the above analysis, we are strongly encouraged to take the tempered sigmoid as a re-
109   sponse to the question ❸. Building upon the behavior of multi-label learning(Yang et al., 2023b)
110   and temperature scaling (Li et al., 2022b; 2023b), tempered sigmoid brings two desirable proper-
111   ties: 1) activate each class in the logit individually, successfully aligning the training protocol of the
112   original tasks; 2) bring back the critical factor τ , endowing us to unleash its effects for multi-label
113   KD. Particularly, the tempered sigmoid derives two intriguing findings regarding the selection of τ
114   in logit KD. First, a lower value (τ < 1) achieves more satisfactory performances with tempered
115   sigmoid, which is exactly opposite to the optimal setting (τ > 1) for tempered softmax. One is
116   encouraged to think about the reason. The theoretical and empirical answers will be provided later.
117
      Besides, we find that L2D and BCKD are the special case of the proposed method but with τ = 1.
118   Our method also possesses two appealing merits: it is compatible with different distance functions
119   (i.e., LKL or LDIST ); it is flexible cooperating with feature-based approaches. Massive empirical re-
120   sults on COCO (Lin et al., 2014), PASCAL-VOC (Everingham et al., 2015), and NUS-WIDE (Chua
121   et al., 2009) evidence that our method achieves state-of-the-art performance on image classifica-
122   tion, object detection, and instance segmentation tasks. The embarrassing simplicity and excellent
123
      performance of the proposed method may foresee its broad application in multi-label logit KD and
      warrant future research. To sum up, this paper makes the following contributions:
124

             • We clarify that the vanilla sigmoid per se does not bring significant practical benefits than
126
               tempered softmax in multi-label logit distillation, as claimed in previous publications. The
127            limitation of vanilla sigmoid and tempered softmax is further discussed.
128
129          • In particular, we disclose that ignoring the temperature parameter is the essential bottleneck

               causing the vanilla sigmoid to produce poor results. This motivates us to introduce the

               lation approaches to further enhance KD.

137            in multi-label distillation and beats its prior counterparts by a large margin.

      2    R ELATED W ORK
140
141
      Multi-Label Learning. Multi-label learning, in which each sample is generally associated with
142
      multiple class labels, is more applicable for real-world applications (Yang et al., 2023b). Thanks to
143
      the recent advance of backbones (e.g., ResNet (He et al., 2016) and Swin (Liu et al., 2021)), multi-
144   label learning supports several visual recognition tasks, including image classification (Chen et al.,
145   2019b), object detection (Lin et al., 2017b), and instance segmentation (Wang et al., 2020).
146
147
      Knowledge Distillation. Knowledge distillation (KD), which aims to learn a compact yet powerful
      model by inheriting knowledge from a high-capacity one, is popularized in single-label learning
148
      (Hinton et al., 2015). Several papers have attempted to investigate KD in multi-label learning tasks.
149
      L2D (Yang et al., 2023b) studied this problem in the classification task and introduced a one-versus-
150   all distillation manner. In object detection, BCKD (Yang et al., 2023a) further formulated the logit
151   maps as multiple binary-classification maps with sigmoid and then performed distillation. However,
152   these methods left some critical questions unanswered and did not explore this problem in general.
153
      Temperature Scaling in KD. In temperature scaling, a parameter τ is involved to adjust the slope of
154
      predictive distributions. Its pivotal role and practical benefits in logit KD are justified with sufficient
155
      evidence (Xu et al., 2020; Kim et al., 2021; Chandrasegaran et al., 2022; Li et al., 2022b; Kobayashi,
156   2022; Li et al., 2023b; Zheng & Yang, 2024; Sun et al., 2024; Wang et al., 2024). For example, ATS
157   (Li et al., 2022b) teaches better students by applying different τ to the different classes. CTKD (Li
158   et al., 2023b) adjusted the τ through an easy-to-hard curriculum learning strategy. TTM (Zheng &
159   Yang, 2024) dropped the temperature τ on the student side, further boosting the standard logit KD.
160   LSKD (Sun et al., 2024) assigned distinct τ between the teacher and student and across samples.
161   However, all these methods are based on tempered softmax and devoted to single-label classification
      KD. This paper, with distinct motivation, explores the specific properties of τ in multi-label distilla-


                                                          3
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


162
      tion and introduces tempered sigmoid. In addition, using the τ as a proxy, we recognize and verify
163   some intriguing phenomena among different logit KD under different learning tasks.
164
165   Hard Samples Mining in KD. Mining and prioritizing hard/important samples is a long-standing
166
      issue in knowledge distillation. For example, (Hu et al., 2024; Li et al., 2022a; Yang et al., 2023a)
      find that the samples exhibiting larger discrepancy between the teacher and student is hard for distil-
167
      lation and paying more learning effort on them favors the student. This issue is more critical for the
168
      detection KD, where the training samples have an extreme imbalance between positive and negative
169   pixels. To alleviate this, numerous methods propose to distill the “knowledge-dense” locations by
170   various sophisticatedly-designed strategies (Wang et al., 2019; Dai et al., 2021; Huang et al., 2023;
171   Li et al., 2022a; Yang et al., 2022b; Zheng et al., 2022). For instance, (Wang et al., 2019) selected an-
172   chors overlapping with the ground-truth object anchors as distillation regions. (Huang et al., 2023)
173   leveraged multiple receptive tokens to perceive each pixel via attention calculation, generating a
174   pixel-wise distillation mask. (Dai et al., 2021; Li et al., 2022a) distills student detectors based on
175   valuable locations selected by the predictive discrepancy between the teacher and student. In this
176   paper, we revealed that the simple temperature scaling operation is capable of providing the effect
177
      of hard mining and consistently outperforms the previous publications.

261   (i) When τ < 1 and τ → 0, the p̃ti,τ starts resembling to the ground-truth [1, 0]. Since the student

513   5   C ONCLUSION
514
515   In this paper, we investigated knowledge distillation under multi-label learning. Our empirical re-
516
      sults clarify that the vanilla sigmoid and tempered softmax are both inferior in performing distil-
      lation in multi-label learning. As a simple yet effective solution, we introduced tempered sigmoid
517
      and proposed the tempered logit distillation (TLD). We provided theoretical and visual justification,
518
      showing that our method realizes hard mining during distillation, which is the primary attribute of
519   its success. Our TLD is general and achieves outstanding distillation results in various vision tasks,
520   including image classification, object detection, and instance segmentation. Only distilling with the
521   logit, TLD even outperforms state-of-the-art KD methods designed specifically for the correspond-
522   ing tasks. Besides, we notice some quite interesting observations regarding the temperature τ , which
523   may supplement and consummate its behavior in logit distillation.

      A.1   DATASETS
705
706   COCO. COCO (Lin et al., 2014), covering 80 categories, is the standard benchmark for classifica-

708   and validation. For image classification, we use COCO-2014 following L2D (Yang et al., 2023b).
709   COCO-2017 is used for detection and instance segmentation as the common practice.

      PASCAL-VOC. PASCAL-VOC (Everingham et al., 2015) consists of 5K and 4K images, across 20
711
      classes, for training and validation.
712
713   NUS-WIDE. NUS-WIDE (Chua et al., 2009), which is a large-scale dataset commonly used for
714   image classification with 81 concept categories, includes 161K and 107K annotated images for
715
      training and validation, respectively.

718

720
      age per-category F1-score (CF1) as the metrics. For object detection and instance segmentation, we
      report mAP as the main metric, together with AP under different IoU thresholds AP50 , AP75 and
721
      object scales APS , APM , APL .

724   A.3   N ETWORK A RCHITECTURES
725
      For backbones, we consider several models: ResNet (He et al., 2016), WRN (Zagoruyko & Ko-
726
      modakis, 2016), RepVGG (Ding et al., 2021), MobileNet (Sandler et al., 2018). Besides, vision
727
      transformer-based networks, such as Swin (Liu et al., 2021), are also included in the image classi-
728   fication task. For the detection task, various dense detectors are selected, i.e., RetinaNet (Lin et al.,
729   2017b), FCOS (Tian et al., 2019), ATSS (Zhang et al., 2020), GFocal (Li et al., 2020), and RepPoints
730   (Yang et al., 2019). For the instance segmentation task, we choose SOLOV2 (Wang et al., 2020).
731   All the backbones are pre-trained on ImageNet (Deng et al., 2009).
732
733   A.4   T RAINING D ETAILS
734
735   For image classification, we use the L2D codebase (Yang et al., 2023b). Specifically, we respectively
736   train the teachers and students for 30 and 80 epochs with the Adam optimizer (Kingma & Ba,
737   2015). The one-cycle policy is used with a maximal learning rate of 1e-4 and a weight decay of
738   1e-4. The batch size is 64 and the input size is 224×224. For each training image, we apply a
739   weak augmentation consisting of random horizontal flipping and a strong augmentation consisting
740   of Cutout (DeVries & Taylor, 2017) and RandAugment (Cubuk et al., 2020). We fix the τ = 0.75
741
      and tune the λ in a reasonable range.
742   For object detection and instance segmentation, our implementation is built upon the MMDetection
743   (Chen et al., 2019a) framework with default configure files. All the student models are trained under
744   the 1× learning schedule without any tricks, e.g., multi-scale training. We fix the τ = 0.5 and tune
745   the λ in a reasonable range.
