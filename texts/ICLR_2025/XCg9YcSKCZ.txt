009                                               A BSTRACT
010
               We present SelfPrompt, a novel prompt-tuning approach for vision-language mod-
011
               els (VLMs) in a semi-supervised learning setup. Existing methods for tuning
012
               VLMs in semi-supervised setups struggle with the efficient use of the limited
013            label-set budget, the negative impact of the miscalibrated VLMs on pseudo-
014            labelling, and the accumulation of noisy pseudo-labels. SelfPrompt addresses
015            these challenges by introducing (a) a weakly-supervised sampling technique that
016            selects a diverse and representative labelled set, (b) a cluster-guided pseudo-
017            labelling method that improves pseudo-label accuracy, and (c) a confidence-aware
018            semi-supervised learning module that maximizes the utilization of unlabelled data
019            by combining supervised learning and weakly-supervised learning. We conduct
020            extensive evaluations across 13 datasets, significantly surpassing state-of-the-art
021
               performances with average improvements of 6.23% in standard semi-supervised
               learning, 6.25% in our proposed active semi-supervised learning, and 4.9% in
022
               base-to-novel generalization, using a 2-shot setup. Furthermore, SelfPrompt
023
               shows excellent generalization in single-shot settings, achieving an average im-
024            provement of 11.78%.

      1   I NTRODUCTION
028   Vision-language models (VLMs) (Radford et al., 2021) pre-trained on large-scale datasets of image-
029   text pairs have shown strong generalization on a wide range of tasks. Nonetheless, prior works
030   (Zhou et al., 2022a;b) have demonstrated that VLMs require fine-tuning on a considerable amount
031   of labelled data to perform well on downstream tasks. Additionally, the size of the foundation model
032   makes fine-tuning in a limited labelled data setting difficult without losing generalization (Roy &
033
      Etemad, 2024). To reduce the reliance on labelled data, some recent works have explored solutions
      that utilize auxiliary unlabelled data (Huang et al., 2022; Menghini et al., 2023; Zhang et al., 2024)
034
      to improve learning from a limited set of labelled data.
035
036   Although prior works that leverage unlabelled data for tuning VLMs show substantial performance
037   gains, we identify several limitations in such approaches. (a) First, given a limited budget for the
038   labelled data (few samples per class), existing methods (Zhang et al., 2024; Menghini et al., 2023)
039
      typically select the labelled sample set randomly. However, a randomly selected set of samples may
      not adequately represent the underlying data distribution, leading to inefficient use of the limited
040
      label budget. (b) Next, given the unlabelled set, prior works (Menghini et al., 2023; Zhang et al.,
041
      2024) utilize the zero-shot capabilities of pre-trained VLMs to predict pseudo-labels for the unla-
042   belled data to then use as labelled samples. However, pre-trained VLMs do not necessarily possess
043   adequate knowledge of the downstream domain, which could lead to incorrect pseudo-labels. Such
044   wrong labels, especially in few-labelled settings, can negatively impact the final performance of the
045   model. (c) Finally, to learn from unlabelled data more effectively, previous works (Menghini et al.,
046   2023; Zhang et al., 2024) have employed incremental pseudo-labelling, wherein the labelled set is
047   continuously expanded by iteratively adding to the pseudo-label set from the unlabelled set. Never-
048   theless, as Figure 1 illustrates, this method often results in the accumulation of noisy pseudo-labels,
049   ultimately leading to performance degradation.
050   To solve the above-mentioned problems, we propose SelfPrompt, a new prompt tuning approach
051   that uses weak supervision by the pre-trained VLM itself to fine-tune the model with a confidence-
052   aware semi-supervised learning approach. SelfPrompt comprises three components. (a) A weakly-
053   supervised labelled set sampling module: To select the most representative set of samples for
      the labelled set, we propose a novel sampling technique. First, the VLM’s predictions are used

070

071   Instead, by leveraging the clusters formed in the labelled set sampling module, we select samples that
072   are near the centroids of the above-mentioned clusters to which we assign the corresponding class
073   labels. (c) Confidence-aware semi-supervised learning: To make the best use of the unlabelled
074   data, we propose a confidence-aware semi-supervised module. This hybrid approach leverages high-
075   confidence pseudo-labels in a fully supervised learning setting, while learning from low-confidence
076   samples in a weakly-supervised manner.
077   Since our approach of sampling a labelled set is slightly different from standard semi-supervised
078   learning (where the labelled set is provided), our approach can be defined as active semi-supervised
079   learning. Accordingly, we evaluate SelfPrompt in comparison to existing methods in both standard
080   semi-supervised learning and active semi-supervised learning setups. While previous works report
081   these results on six datasets, we evaluate our solution (and the previous methods) on 13 datasets.
082   Our evaluation shows that SelfPrompt outperforms prior works by 6.23% in the standard semi-
083   supervised learning setup and by 6.25% on the active semi-supervised learning setup, with up to
084   11.23% and 13.82% improvements on individual datasets, respectively. Additionally, we show gen-
085
      eralization by reducing the size of the labelled set to just one sample-per-class while outperforming
      prior methods by an average of 11.78%. For completeness, we also compare our solution to few-
086
      shot tuning methods (Zhou et al., 2022a; Khattak et al., 2022; Roy & Etemad, 2024; Li et al., 2024)
087
      on base-to-novel generalization, where we also show an average improvement of 4.9% over prior
088   works in a 2-shot evaluation setup, with improvements in both base and novel classes. Finally, we
089   present extensive ablation and sensitivity studies on different components of our proposed method.
090   Overall, our contributions are:
091      • We introduce SelfPrompt, a novel prompt-tuning approach for VLMs that uses weak supervi-
092        sion from the pre-trained VLM itself to learn effective representations from limited labelled
093        data while improving generalization.
094      • SelfPrompt comprises three novel components: (a) Weakly-supervised labelled set sampling
095        technique that selects an effective representative and diverse set of labelled samples; (b) Cluster-
096        guided pseudo-labelling, which improves pseudo-label accuracy by assigning labels based on
097
           cluster proximity; and (c) Confidence-aware semi-supervised learning, which adapts learning
           based on the confidence of pseudo-labels to maximize the use of the unlabelled data.
098
         • Our comprehensive experiments across 13 datasets demonstrate that SelfPrompt surpasses ex-
099        isting methods on various benchmarks, achieving average improvements of 6.23% in standard
100        semi-supervised learning, 6.25% in active semi-supervised learning, and 4.9% in base-to-novel
101        generalization, in the standard 2-shot evaluation setup. Additionally, SelfPrompt exhibits strong
102        generalization in a single-shot setting, resulting in an even higher improvement of 11.78%.
103      • To enable fast reproducibility and contribute to the area, we will make our code public upon
104        acceptance of the paper.

106   2 R ELATED W ORKS
107   Vision-language models. Vision-language models (VLMs), pre-trained on vast web-scale datasets
      of image-text pairs, have demonstrated remarkable generalization across a wide range of down-


                                                                                                      2

108
      stream tasks (Radford et al., 2021; Jia et al., 2021; Alayrac et al., 2022). These models are com-
109   posed of a vision encoder and a text encoder, jointly trained to align the representations of an image
110   and corresponding text. Once pre-trained, VLMs can perform zero-shot image classification by
111   matching an image embedding with the text embedding of the class name with a prompt, such as ’a
112   photo of a [class].’ While this zero-shot capability has delivered impressive results in various do-
113   mains, fine-tuning is often necessary to achieve optimal performance on new tasks or domains (Zhou
114   et al., 2022a). However, fine-tuning large models with limited labelled data presents significant chal-
115   lenges, including overfitting and reduced generalization (Roy & Etemad, 2024). To address these
116   issues, recent approaches have proposed semi-supervised tuning, which incorporates unlabelled data
117
      alongside limited labelled data to improve the fine-tuning of VLMs on downstream tasks. In the fol-
      lowing subsections, we first explore prompt tuning, a prominent approach for parameter-efficient
118
      fine-tuning of pre-trained models by introducing a small set of additional parameters while keep-
119
      ing the pre-trained encoder frozen. These methods help mitigate the risk of overfitting on limited
120   downstream labelled data. We then review prior work on the semi-supervised tuning of VLMs.
121
122   Prompt tuning. Prompt tuning is a parameter-efficient technique for adapting foundation models
123
      to downstream tasks by learning soft prompts (textual (Ge et al., 2023; Zhou et al., 2022c) or vi-
      sual (Bahng et al., 2022a; Jia et al., 2022)) from limited labelled data (Ge et al., 2023; Jia et al.,
124
      2022). Text-based prompt tuning (Zhou et al., 2022c) optimizes learnable prompt vectors, which
125
      are embedded within the input sentence tokens and fed into the sentence encoder. Extending this
126   idea, Zhou et al. (2022b) introduced soft prompt learning that conditions the prompt on the image
127   input. In contrast, visual prompt tuning (Bahng et al., 2022b) introduces a small number of trainable
128   parameters into the visual input tokens. PLOT (Chen et al.) fine-tuned VLM by learning multiple di-
129   verse prompts per category via a two-stage optimization strategy. MaPLe (Khattak et al., 2022) later
130   introduced a multi-modal approach that trains both textual and visual prompts simultaneously, lever-
131   aging their synergy to facilitate multi-modal representation learning while avoiding an overemphasis
132   on unimodal features. A similar multi-modal approach was proposed in PromptSRC (Khattak et al.,
133   2023), which, unlike MaPLe, focused on learning task-agnostic and independent prompts for text
134
      and images. Later, CoPrompt (Roy & Etemad, 2024) proposed to enforce consistency between the
      pre-trained and learnable encoders, aiming to reduce overfitting and enhance generalization. AWT
135
      (Zhu et al., 2024) enhances vision-language models by augmenting inputs with diverse perspectives
136
      and enriched descriptions, dynamically weighting inputs by prediction uncertainty, and transporting
137   semantic correlations into a shared embedding space. While these methods have demonstrated im-
138   proved performance on downstream tasks compared to pre-trained VLMs, they still face challenges
139   in domains where the target data significantly deviates from the pre-training distribution, primarily
140   due to the limited availability of domain-specific data.
141
      Semi-supervised tuning. Recently, a new stream of research has focused on semi-supervised tun-
142
      ing that leverages unlabelled data alongside a small set of labelled data to enhance downstream task
143   performance. The core idea behind these methods is pseudo-labelling (Sohn et al., 2020; Lee et al.,
144   2013), where the model predicts labels for unlabeled samples and then uses these pseudo-labels to
145   learn from the unlabeled data. For example, GRIP (Menghini et al., 2023) utilizes CLIP’s zero-shot
146   capabilities to generate pseudo-labels for unlabelled data and select the most confident samples to
147   serve as labelled data. However, this approach introduces a considerable amount of wrong pseudo-
148   labels due to the inherent miscalibration (LeVine et al., 2023) and imbalanced predictions (Wang
149   et al., 2022a) issues of the pre-trained VLM. To address these issues, CPL (Zhang et al., 2024) pro-
150   poses to generate refined candidate pseudo-labels through intra- and inter-instance label selection,
151
      using a confidence score matrix to improve label accuracy and class balance during fine-tuning. Both
      GRIP and CPL adopt an iterative process, where the model is used to continuously refine and select
152
      additional samples from the unlabelled set. Although these methods have demonstrated promising
153
      improvements in VLM performance on downstream tasks, they still face several key limitations.
154   These include the under-utilization of the labelling budget, the negative impact of miscalibrated
155   pseudo-labels, and the declining quality of pseudo-labelling as the number of samples increases.
156
157
      Weakly-supervised learning. Weakly supervised learning encompasses a class of methods that
      train models with limited or imprecise supervision (Peyre et al., 2017; Li et al., 2019; Zhou, 2018).
158
      Unlike fully supervised learning, which requires large amounts of precisely labelled data, weakly
159
      supervised learning leverages weak annotations, such as noisy, incomplete, or coarse-grained la-
160   bels. This paradigm significantly reduces the reliance on costly and time-intensive data annotation
161   processes. This paradigm has demonstrated broad applicability across various domains, includ-


                                                        3
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


162
      ing vision-language models (Wang et al., 2022b), medical image analysis (Kanavati et al., 2020).
163   Despite its potential, it has been underexplored in the context of semi-supervised learning, where
164   pseudo-label predictions frequently introduce noise. This positions weakly supervised learning as
165   an ideal candidate to address the challenges posed by noisy labels in semi-supervised frameworks.

      Despite recent progress in semi-supervised prompt tuning for VLMs, we have identified three key
189
      open challenges in this area:
190
      (a) In a few-shot learning setting with N samples, the common strategy is to randomly selected n
191   samples per class from the unlabelled set U to form the labelled set (Zhang et al., 2024; Menghini
192   et al., 2023). However, in such a setting, random selection may fail to adequately represent the data
193   distribution of the target domain, leading to inefficient use of the limited label budget.
194   (b) Given an unlabelled set U , the typical approach is to initiate the training protocol by using
195   the pre-trained VLM to generate pseudo-labels for U (using Eq. 1) and selecting the most confident
196   M/S samples (Zhang et al., 2024; Menghini et al., 2023). Here, S represents the number of sessions
197   over which this pseudo-labelling and selection process is repeated. However, pre-trained VLMs of-
198   ten lack adequate knowledge of the target domain, i.e., domain miscalibration (LeVine et al., 2023),
199
      thus resulting in considerable numbers of incorrect pseudo-labels. This in turn can misguide the
      fine-tuning of the model, leading to degraded final performances.
200
      (c) To maximize the information obtained from unlabelled data, prior works have employed an
201
      iterative pseudo-labelling strategy, where unlabelled samples are progressively added to the pseudo-
202   labelled set. However, as shown in Figure 1, this approach results in an accumulation of noisy
203   samples, leading to a drop in the model’s final performance. While CPL (Zhang et al., 2024) pro-
204   posed avoiding pseudo-labels in favour of using multiple top predictions as partial labels, this results
205   in underutilizing the unlabelled data by avoiding correct pseudo-labels as well.

215   that leverages the VLM predictions as weak supervision to remove uninformative samples, and (ii)
      a clustering-based selection method to ensure diversity among the selected samples. By leveraging

304   4.1 E XPERIMENT S ETUP
305   We report the results of our experiments on 13 datasets of diverse semantic concepts. For evaluating
306   the proposed solution, we closely follow the training and evaluation protocol established by Zhang
307   et al. (2024) and Menghini et al. (2023). Specifically, we utilize few samples from the training set
308
      of a dataset as the labelled set and the remaining samples as the unlabelled set. All experiments are
      conducted in a 2-shot setup, with ten sessions of iterative pseudo-labelling (50 epochs per session).
309
      The model is optimized using SGD with a learning rate of 0.02 and a batch size of 64. The main
310
      results are reported with the value of q = 5, τ = 0.05, and λ = 1. Results are reported as the

      4.2 S TANDARD S EMI - SUPERVISED LEARNING
314
315   First, we present our results on standard semi-supervised learning in a 2-shot setup. The results
316
      of this experiment are presented in Table 1, where we observe that SelfPrompt shows large and
      consistent improvements over prior works across the 13 datasets. On average, SelfPrompt achieves
317
      an accuracy of 76.12% with just two labelled samples per class, which is a 4.71% improvement over
318
      the previous SOTA CPL. Notably, SelfPrompt shows up to 10.54% improvement over the previous
319   SOTA on individual datasets. More importantly, SelfPrompt shows higher improvements on datasets
320   with lower zero-shot (VLM) accuracies (e.g., FGVCAircraft and MNIST). We also report the results
321   for visual prompt tuning in Table 2. Similar to textual prompting, SelfPrompt with visual prompt
322   tuning outperforms CPL by up to 19.50%, with a 6.23%, which is higher than the improvement
323   with text prompts. On average, SelfPrompt with visual prompt tuning achieves an overall average

528   of SelfPrompt is on par with the previous SOTA CPL (see Table 9 for training time on EuroSAT).

530   5    C ONCLUSION
531
      In this paper, we propose SelfPrompt, a novel prompt-tuning approach for vision-language mod-
532
      els in semi-supervised setups. Our method addresses three key limitations of prior works: under-
533
      utilization of the limited label-set budget, reliance on miscalibrated VLMs for pseudo-labelling, and
534   the accumulation of noisy pseudo-labels. We demonstrate SelfPrompt’s superior performance across
535   13 datasets, showing an average improvement of 6.23% in standard semi-supervised learning, 6.25%
536   in active semi-supervised learning, and 4.9% in base-to-novel generalization tasks compared to prior
537   methods. Notably, SelfPrompt achieves significant improvements with as few as one labelled sample
538   per class. Our extensive evaluations highlight the effectiveness of our proposed solution in adapting
539   VLMs to downstream tasks with limited labelled data, paving the way for real-world applications
      where data annotation is a critical bottleneck.
