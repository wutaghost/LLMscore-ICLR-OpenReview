009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            Variational quantum algorithms is one of the most representative algorithms in
012            quantum computing, which has a wide range of applications in quantum machine
013            learning, quantum simulation and other related fields. However, they face chal-
014            lenges associated with the barren plateau phenomenon, especially when dealing
015            with large numbers of qubits, deep circuit layers, or global cost functions, making
016
               them often untrainable. In this paper, we propose a novel parameter initializa-
               tion strategy based on Gaussian Mixture Models. We rigorously prove that, the
017
               proposed initialization method consistently avoids the barren plateaus problem
018
               for hardware-efficient ansatz with arbitrary length and qubits and any given cost
019            function. Specifically, we find that the gradient norm lower bound provided by the
020            proposed method is independent of the number of qubits N and increases with the
021            circuit depth L. Our results strictly highlight the significance of Gaussian Mixture
022            model initialization strategies in determining the trainability of quantum circuits,
023            which provides valuable guidance for future theoretical investigations and practi-
024            cal applications.

      1   I NTRODUCTION
028
      In recent years, the rapid advancement of quantum computing technology has drawn attention to
029
      Variational Quantum Algorithms (VQAs) McClean et al. (2016); Cı̂rstoiu et al. (2020); Cerezo et al.
030
      (2022) as a promising quantum algorithm with broad application prospects. In the current era of
031   Noisy Intermediate-Scale Quantum (NISQ) devices Bharti et al. (2022); Arrasmith et al. (2019);
032   Preskill (2018), VQAs provides a feasible approach to solving complex problems, where challenges
033   such as noise and errors in quantum computing devices make large-scale fully quantum computa-
034   tions difficult Benedetti et al. (2019); Jerbi et al. (2023); Cerezo et al. (2021a); Moll et al. (2018).
035   On the other hand, VQAs utilizes Parametrized Quantum Circuits (PQCs), denoted as V (θ), as
036   its quantum computing framework. PQCs serving as a trainable model adjusts its parameters θ
037   through classical optimization to minimize or maximize a specified cost function. By employing
038   parametrized quantum circuits, VQAs can adapt flexibly to the characteristics of different problems,
039
      providing a robust and practical option for quantum computation on NISQ devices Peruzzo et al.
      (2014); Zhou et al. (2020); Tabares et al. (2023); Pan et al. (2023). VQAs exhibit immense potential
040
      across a spectrum of applications, showcasing efficient quantum algorithms that excel in tasks rang-
041
      ing from chemical molecular structure and energy calculations McArdle et al. (2020); Kandala et al.
042   (2017); Hempel et al. (2018) to combinatorial optimization problems Amaro et al. (2022); Akshay
043   et al. (2020) and machine learning Havlı́ček et al. (2019); Saggio et al. (2021); Schuld et al. (2020);
044   Schuld & Killoran (2019); Zhang et al. (2021); Tian et al. (2023); Zhang et al. (2020); Chen et al.
045   (2020). These applications not only have profound implications for scientific research but also offer
046   innovative solutions for practical applications.
047
      Training VQAs encompasses various methodologies, including gradient-based Sweke et al. (2020);
048   Basheer et al. (2023); Qi et al. (2023) and gradient-free Nelder & Mead (1965); Powell (1964) ap-
049   proaches. However, regardless of the sampling method employed, it is susceptible to encountering
050   the notorious barren plateaus (BP) problem McClean et al. (2018); Arrasmith et al. (2021); Liu et al.
051   (2022); Larocca et al. (2024). The phenomenon of the barren plateau is characterized by the random-
052   ized initialization of parameters θ in VQAs, leading to an exponential vanishing of the cost function
053   gradient along any direction with the increasing number of qubits. We have observed that recent
      work has employed Lie groups and Lie algebras to provide a unified framework for understanding

      the emergence of BP Ragone et al. (2024); Fontana et al. (2024). This framework reveals a close
055   relationship between BP and factors such as noise, the loss function, and circuit structure. Specif-
056   ically, noise is a significant cause of the barren plateau problem Wang et al. (2021); Stilck França
057   & Garcia-Patron (2021). While depolarizing noise leads to the emergence of BP when the circuit
058   depth L becomes sufficiently large Wang et al. (2021), however, in the case of non-unital noise,
059   barren plateaus do not appear for local cost functions, regardless of the circuit depth Singkanipa &
060   Lidar (2024). The essential cause of the emergence of BP lies in the entanglement within quantum
061   circuits Ortiz Marrero et al. (2021). Numerous strategies have emerged to address this issue, such
062   as optimizing initialization policies Zhang et al. (2022a); Wang et al. (2023); Friedrich & Maziero
063
      (2022); Liu et al. (2023), refining circuit structures Liu et al. (2024); Zhao & Gao (2021); Pesah
      et al. (2021); Cong et al. (2019); Martı́n et al. (2023); Park & Killoran (2024), or employing local
064
      cost functions Arrasmith et al. (2021); Liu et al. (2022). However, whether noise is present or not,
065
      avoiding the BP phenomenon for global loss functions remains a challenging problem Cerezo et al.
066   (2021b); Mele et al. (2024). The design of the circuit ansatz is crucial for capturing quantum cor-
067   relations, including physics-inspired Taube & Bartlett (2006); Wecker et al. (2015); Peruzzo et al.
068   (2014) and hardware-efficient ansatz designs Zhang et al. (2022b). While physics-inspired ansatz
069   exhibits some advantages in certain aspects Wecker et al. (2015); O’Malley et al. (2016), they also
070   face serious challenges in terms of computational resources. On the other hand, hardware-efficient
071   ansatz Kandala et al. (2017) caters to the limitations of NISQ devices, striking a balance between
072   achievability and performance Zhang et al. (2024). At the same time, in this structure, deeper layers
073   exhibit stronger expressibilityRagone et al. (2024); Fontana et al. (2024), and as a result, the BP
074
      emerges regardless of the form of the measurement operator or the initial state. The quest for an
      effective solution to mitigate BP and enhance the versatility of addressing linear combinations in the
075
      context of a hardware-efficient ansatz continues to be a forefront challenge in the training of VQAs.
076
077   The Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM) Reynolds (2015) is a probability distribution model com-
078   posed of multiple Gaussian distributions. Each Gaussian distribution, referred to as a component,
079   contributes to the overall mixture distribution. Every component is characterized by its own mean,
080
      variance, and weight. This versatile model finds widespread applications in statistics and machine
      learning Rasmussen (1999); Xuan et al. (2001); Zong et al. (2018), particularly in tasks such as
081
      clustering Yang et al. (2012); Manduchi et al. (2021), density estimation Glodek et al. (2013), and
082
      generative modeling GM et al. (2020). GMM excels at fitting complex data distributions and, owing
083   to its flexibility and expressive power, is frequently employed for modeling diverse categories of
084   data.
085
086
      In the training of VQAs, the parameter update expression for the cost function f (θ) based on
      gradient optimization methods is f (θk+1 ) = f (θk ) − α||∇θ f (θk )||22 + o(α), where θk+1 =
087
      θk − α∇θ f (θk ), α is the learning rate. Therefore, typically ||∇θ f (θ)||22 is used to determine
088
      whether the cost function f (θ) = Tr[OV (θ)ρin V † (θ)] can be updated. Here, O is an observable,
089   V (θ) is a parameterized quantum circuit, and ρin is the input quantum state. In this paper, we em-
090   ploy GMM for parameter initialization in VQAs to address the barren plateau problem. Considering
091   arbitrary observables O which can be a single term or a linear combination of terms, by designing
092   specific GMM initialization methods based on O, we rigorously prove the following conclusions:
093   (1) When the observable O consists of a single term, the lower bound of ||∇θ f (θ)||22 is independent
094   of the number of quantum bits N and increases with the circuit length; (2) When O is a linear com-
095   bination of many terms, the lower bound of ||∇θ f (θ)||22 increases compared to the single-term case
096   and not decrease; (3) When O consists of non-negative terms, by adjusting GMM, we may achieve a
097
      larger lower bound. Therefore, the barren plateau problem does not occur in these scenario, and the
      model can undergo effective training. This is significant for reducing the cost and saving quantum
098
      resources during model training. Additionally, numerical experiments show excellent performance
099
      for both local and global cost functions using our method.

      We observe that when Pauli matrices are limited to I and Z, the CZ gate does not alter their forms.
453
      In other words, CZ † (oi ⊗ oj )CZ = oi ⊗ oj for all oi , oj ∈ {I, Z}. Therefore, CZl can be any
454
      combination of CZ gates, and it only changes the conditions for ’flip,’ which does not affect our
455   results. Also, although our method is specifically effective for the Rx − Ry gate structure, it can be
456   readily extended to other combinations of rotation gates. For instance, as shown in Theorem 2, if we
457   interchange the positions of Rx and Ry in the arrangement of rotation gates, i.e., the arrangement is
458   Ry −Rx , then we initialize the parameters of the last block according to Table 6, and the initialization
459   of parameters in other layers follows the distribution G1 (σ 2 ). Alternatively, when the rotation gates
460   consist of three Rx − Ry − Rx gates, under the same conditions as in Theorem 1, we initialize the
461   parameters of the last block as shown in Table 7, and the initialization of parameters in other layers
462   follows the distribution G1 (σ 2 ). In both cases, the results are consistent with those of Theorem 1.
463   Certainly, our analysis method remains applicable when using CNOT to provide entanglement.

465   6   C ONCLUSION
466
467
      In this paper, we introduce GMM into the parameter initialization of PQCs to circumvent the notori-
468   ous barren plateau problem. Results indicate the universality of our approach, as it applies to various
469   cost functions, and we rigorously prove that its gradient norms is no less than 81 . We validate our
470   algorithm for diverse problems, which is crucial for VQAs as it enables the training of larger and
471   deeper quantum circuits, unlocking the potential of quantum computation.
472
      While the theorems presented in our paper are tailored to the ansatz in Fig. 1, the applicability of
473
      our theorems and proof techniques can extend to other ansatz structures. Furthermore, considering
474   the analogous BP issues in tensor network simulations Liu et al. (2022); Garcia et al. (2023), we
475   anticipate incorporating our method into the initialization of tensor networks in the future. However,
476   due to the sharp-P completeness of classical simulations in tensor networks, even without facing BP,
477   computing their derivatives remains challenging for large-scale problems. In contrast, VQAs can
478   efficiently obtain expected values through quantum devices, making them implementable. Certainly,
479   for effective training of VQAs, overcoming the barren plateau is just one step, as they still face
480   challenges such as local minima Bittel & Kliesch (2021); Anschuetz & Kiani (2022) that need to
481   consider.
482   We note that recent articles claim all BP-free ansatzes are classically simulable Cerezo et al. (2023).
483   As stated in Ref. Park et al. (2024), HEA can be interpreted as a many-body localized (MBL) system
484   Shtanko et al. (2023), and currently, no efficient classical algorithm can simulate MBL systems for
485   exponentially long times. Additionally, even when using tensor networks to simulate, the barren
      plateau problem arises when dealing with global loss functionsLiu et al. (2022). Although the work
