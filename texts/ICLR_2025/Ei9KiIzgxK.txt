009                                                         A BSTRACT
010
011            Offline reinforcement learning (RL) offers a promising framework for training agents using pre-
012            collected datasets without the need for further environment interaction. However, policies trained
013            on offline data often struggle to generalise due to limited exposure to diverse states.The complexity
014            of visual data introduces additional challenges such as noise, distractions, and spurious correlations,
015            which can misguide the policy and increase the risk of overfitting if the training data is not suffi-
016            ciently diverse. Indeed, this makes it challenging to leverage vision-based offline data in training
017            robust agents that can generalize to unseen environments. To solve this problem, we propose a sim-
018
               ple approach—generating additional synthetic training data. We propose a two-step process, first
               augmenting the originally collected offline data to improve zero-shot generalization by introducing
019
               diversity, then using a diffusion model to generate additional data in latent space. We test our method
020
               across both continuous action spaces (Visual D4RL) and discrete action spaces (Procgen), demon-
021            strating that it significantly improves generalization without requiring any algorithmic changes to
022            existing model-free offline RL methods. We show that our method not only increases the diversity
023            of the training data but also significantly reduces the generalization gap at test time while maintain-
024            ing computational efficiency. We believe this approach could fuel additional progress in generating
025            synthetic data to train more general agents in the future.

031   1   I NTRODUCTION
032
033   Offline reinforcement learning (RL) offers a compelling approach for training agents using pre-collected datasets with-
034   out additional environment interaction (Levine et al., 2020). This paradigm is particularly valuable in domains like
035   healthcare (Liu et al., 2020), robotics (Singla et al., 2021), and autonomous driving (Kiran et al., 2021), where real-
036   time data collection can be costly or risky. However, generalizing policies trained on high-dimensional visual inputs
037   in offline RL remains a significant challenge, and it has received relatively little attention in the research community.
038   Agents may learn irrelevant correlations between visual features and actions, reducing their ability to perform well
039   in new settings (Song et al., 2019; Raileanu & Fergus, 2021). Additionally, offline RL policies tend to exhibit risk-
040   averse behavior, avoiding novel actions in unfamiliar states, which further hampers generalization (Mediratta et al.,
041
      2024). To tackle these challenges, we propose a two-step method that combines data augmentation with diffusion
      model-based upsampling to improve generalization in offline RL. While both data augmentation (Laskin et al., 2020;
042
      Yarats et al., 2021a;b; Raileanu et al., 2021) and the use of diffusion models for replay buffer upsampling (Lu et al.,
043
      2023b) have been explored independently in the online RL domain, our contribution lies in their integration and adap-
044   tation for the unique challenges of offline RL to achieve greater diversity and more robust generalization. First, we
045   apply data augmentation techniques to the offline dataset, introducing variability that helps reduce overfitting. Then,
046   we use a diffusion model to upsample the augmented dataset in the latent space, generating additional synthetic data
047   points that capture unseen transitions. This approach broadens the distribution of experience replay data without in-
048   curring significant computational overhead, allowing policies to generalize more effectively to new environments.Our
049   results demonstrate that our method significantly reduces the generalization gap across various difficulty levels in two
050   recent visual offline RL benchmarks, highlighting the effectiveness of combining augmentation and diffusion-based
051   upsampling.

      2     BACKGROUND
091
092   2.1   O FFLINE R EINFORCEMENT L EARNING FROM V ISUAL O BSERVATIONS
093
      Reinforcement Learning (RL) typically involves an agent learning by interacting with an environment modeled as a
094
      Markov Decision ProcessP(MDP) (Sutton & Barto, 2018), where the objective is to optimize the expected cumulative
095                                ∞
      return J(π) = Eπ,P,ρ0 [ t=0 γ t R(st , at )], where J(π) represents the expected return of a policy π, Eπ,P,ρ0 is the
096   expectation over the policy π, the environment dynamics P (s′ |s, a), and the initial state distribution ρ0 . The term
097   γ ∈ [0, 1) is the discount factor, controlling how much future rewards are valued, and R(st , at ) is the reward function
098   at time step t, depending on the state st and action at . The goal is to find a policy π that maximizes this cumulative
099   discounted return over time.
100
      In offline RL, however, the agent must learn from a static dataset D = {(oi , ai , ri , o′i )}N
                                                                                                    i=1 , without any interaction
101
      with the environment during training (Levine et al., 2020). This dataset typically consists of observations collected by
102   one or more behavior policies. When dealing with visual observations (high-dimensional inputs), additional challenges
103   arise.Unlike proprioceptive observations in standard RL, visual inputs are prone to noise and spurious correlations (Lu
104   et al., 2023a), making offline RL particularly vulnerable to overfitting. Small environmental changes (e.g., lighting
105   or backgrounds) can cause significant shifts in data distribution. Without interaction to correct for these shifts, agents
106   struggle to generalize from visual observations (Raileanu & Fergus, 2021). Given these challenges, the core problem
107   is: How can we improve the generalization performance of model-free offline RL methods from visual observations
      and ensure the robust deployment of agents in unseen environments for both continuous and discrete action spaces?

110   Diffusion models generate data by reversing a noise-adding process, starting from noise and gradually denoising to
111   recover the original data distribution (Ho et al., 2020; Rombach et al., 2022). The noise removal is guided by a learned
112   denoising model Dθ (x; σ), trained using an L2 objective:

116   This allows the model to estimate the data distribution at different noise levels. Additional details, including the use
117   of ODEs or SDEs for the reverse process, can be found in Karras et al. (2022). Diffusion models have shown supe-
118   rior performance in generating diverse synthetic datasets compared to Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs) and
119   Variational Autoencoders (VAEs), making them particularly effective for improving generalization in reinforcement
120   learning Lu et al. (2023b). This is why we chose diffusion models, as their ability to generate diverse data makes them
121   ideal for our approach.

274   4.3.2    L ATENT S PACE D ISTRIBUTION A NALYSIS
275
276   To gain deeper insights into the impact of our method on learned representations, we performed a latent space distribu-
277   tion analysis using the Jensen-Shannon (JS) divergence (Menéndez et al., 1997). For each dataset variant, we extracted
278   latent space representations by passing both training and testing observations (collected during the evaluation steps,
279   as detailed in supplementary material through the trained encoder fξ and the actor-critic networks. Let htrain and htest
280
      represent the sets of latent representations for training and testing observations, respectively. For each environment,
      we estimated probability distributions over the latent space dimensions using kernel density estimation (KDE) to non-
281
      parametrically capture the distributions of htrain and htest . We then computed the JS divergence between the training
282
      and testing distributions for each dimension, resulting in a divergence vector d = [d1 , d2 , . . . , dn ], where n repre-
283   sents the dimensionality of the latent space. The mean JS divergence d¯ across all dimensions was used to summarize
284   how closely the training and testing distributions aligned, with lower divergence indicating better generalization. To
285   facilitate comparison across different environments, we normalized the mean JS divergence values within each en-
286   vironment, ensuring consistency in scale. A closer match between training and test distributions suggests improved
287   generalization performance by our method.

473   6    R ELATED W ORK
474
475   Generalization in RL has been extensively studied, primarily in the context of online RL. A substantial body of work
476   has focused on training agents to generalize across novel transition dynamics and reward functions (Rajeswaran et al.,
477   2018; Machado et al., 2017; Packer et al., 2019; Cobbe et al., 2020; Kirk et al., 2023; Justesen et al., 2018; Nichol et al.,
478   2018; Küttler et al., 2020; Bengio et al., 2020; Bertran et al., 2020; Ghosh et al., 2021; Lyu et al., 2024; Ehrenberg
479   et al., 2022; Lyle et al., 2022; Dunion et al., 2023; Almuzairee et al., 2024). RL environments such as Procgen (Cobbe
480   et al., 2020) and the NetHack Learning Environment (Kumar et al., 2020) have been specifically developed to assess
481   generalization in online RL. However, these studies largely focus on interactive settings where agents can gather new
482
      data during training, leaving generalization in offline RL relatively unexplored.
483   Visual offline RL introduces additional challenges, particularly when using large-scale datasets. Datasets like Atari,
484   StarCraft, and MineRL contain millions of samples but require significant computational resources, limiting their
485   accessibility to many researchers (Agarwal et al., 2020; Vinyals et al., 2017; Fan et al., 2022). In contrast, benchmarks
      such as V-D4RL and offline Procgen (Lu et al., 2023a; Mediratta et al., 2024) offer more accessible alternatives,

487   generalization in continuous and discrete control tasks. Both benchmarks highlight the generalization challenges in
488   offline RL, especially with model-free methods. Our work extends these efforts by evaluating generalization across
489   diverse, procedurally generated environments in both continuous and discrete control tasks.

502   Diffusion models have emerged as a promising tool for improving RL solutions, though they have primarily been
503
      used as policies or planners rather than as data synthesizers (Zhu et al., 2024; Jackson et al., 2024). Recent works,
      such as ROSIE (Yu et al., 2023) and GenAug (Chen et al., 2023), have employed diffusion models for synthetic data
504
      generation to improve generalization. However, these methods operate in online robotic learning contexts, relying on
505
      continuous interaction with the environment. In contrast, our approach applies diffusion-based upsampling in offline
506   RL, where additional interaction with the environment is not possible. Inspired by SynthER (Lu et al., 2023b), our
507   method improves data diversity through upsampling, making it the first diffusion model-based data synthesis aimed
508   at solving the generalization problem in model-free offline RL with visual inputs, across both continuous and discrete
509   control tasks.

      7    C ONCLUSION
513
514   We presented a practical two-step approach that improves generalization in offline reinforcement learning from visual
515   inputs. By combining targeted data augmentation with diffusion model-based synthetic data generation in the latent
516
      space, our approach increases training data diversity without significant computational overhead, allowing model-
      free offline RL algorithms to better handle risk-averse behavior in unseen environments. Our experiments on the
517
      V-D4RL benchmark (continuous control) and Procgen benchmark (discrete control) demonstrate that our approach
518
      consistently reduces the generalization gap and improves performance in unseen environments. Additionally, our
519   method effectively leverages small amounts of hand-crafted, fixed distracting data to further improve generalization,
520   suggesting potential applications for few-shot learning in offline RL. These benchmarks challenge the development
521   of better offline RL algorithms for visual observations, and to our knowledge, we are the first to apply this approach
522   across both continuous and discrete action spaces. By broadening the data distribution in both pixel and latent spaces,
523   we provide a scalable two-step solution to the generalization challenges in offline RL.
524
      While our method shows significant improvements, there are certain limitations. Although working in the latent space
525   keeps computational overhead relatively low, extending this approach to the pixel space would introduce significantly
526   higher costs, especially in environments with high-resolution visual inputs. Additionally, our method required exten-
527   sive experimentation to identify the best settings for data augmentation and diffusion model parameters, which may
528   limit its immediate applicability to more complex environments like robotics or autonomous driving. We opted for
529   model-free algorithms in this work due to their sampling efficiency and lower computational load. However, these
530   algorithms may face limitations in handling more complex tasks, particularly those requiring long-horizon planning.
531   Future work will focus on scaling this approach to such environments and exploring its integration with various RL
532   algorithms, including model-based ones, to improve generalization in offline RL settings.
