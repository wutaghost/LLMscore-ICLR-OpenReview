                                                   A BSTRACT
010
011
               While visual language model (VLM) architectures and training infrastructures
012
               advance rapidly, data curation remains under-explored where quantity and quality
013
               become a bottleneck. Existing work either crawls extra Internet data with loose
014            guarantee of quality or distills from black-box proprietary models (e.g., GPT-4V
015            / Gemini) that is API frequency and performance bounded. This work enables
016            a VLM to improve itself via data enhancement, exploiting its generative nature.
017            We introduce a simple yet effective VLM augmentation scheme that includes a
018            self-augment step and a specialist-augment step to iteratively improve data quality
019            and hence, model performance. In the self-augment step, the instruction-finetuned
020            VLM recaptions its pretraining caption datasets and then retrains from scratch
021            leveraging refined data. Without any expensive human-in-the-loop annotation, we
022
               observe improvements in data quality and downstream accuracy boosts with three
               self-augmentation rounds – a viable free lunch to current VLM training recipe.
023
               When self-augmentation saturates, we augment the caption diversity leveraging
024
               specialty skills picked up from instruction finetuning. We finetune VLM specialists
025            from the self-augmented VLM with domain-specific experts, including spatial,
026            grounding, and OCR, to fuse task-aware synthetic data into the pretraining stage.
027            Data quality improvements and hallucination reductions are cross checked by
028            VLM (GPT-4V, Gemini) and human judges. Combining self-augmentation and
029            specialist-augmented training, VILA2 consistently improves the accuracy on a
030            wide range of benchmarks over prior art , producing a reusable pretraining dataset
031            that is 300x more cost-efficient than human labeling. We will open source the code
032            upon publication.

      1    I NTRODUCTION
036
037   The success of large language models (LLMs) (Raffel et al., 2020; Dai et al., 2019; Brown et al.,
038   2020; OpenAI, 2023b; Touvron et al., 2023a;b; Taori et al., 2023; Chiang et al., 2023; Karamcheti
039
      et al., 2021; Chowdhery et al., 2022; yi, 2023; Bai et al., 2023a) has offered the cornerstone for
      cross-modality tasks. Through the alignment of visual encoders to LLMs, visual language models
040
      have enabled myriad appealing capabilities to visual tasks, such as instruction following, zero-shot
041
      generalization, few-shot in-context learning (ICL), and enhanced world knowledge (Liu et al., 2023c;
042   Alayrac et al., 2022; Driess et al., 2023; Chen et al., 2023c; Li et al., 2023b; fuy, 2023; Bai et al.,
043   2023b; OpenAI, 2023a; Zhu et al., 2023a; Lin et al., 2024). The field has progressed rapidly in the
044   past two years, yielding effective alignment training recipes (Driess et al., 2023; OpenAI, 2023a; Lin
045   et al., 2024) and model architectures (Liu et al., 2023c; Alayrac et al., 2022; Driess et al., 2023; Chen
046   et al., 2023c; Li et al., 2023b).
047
      Contrary to the fast-evolving training enhancement, the underlying human-generated datasets and
048   tasks remain simple (Zhu et al., 2023b; Schuhmann et al., 2022; Byeon et al., 2022; Sharma et al.,
049   2018). Given the costly nature of VLM training, most methods are confined with coarse-quality
050   large-scale captioning image-text pairs (pretraining), followed by fine-grained small-scale supervised
051   finetuning (SFT). Enhancement of image-text pairs with millions and billions of instances can in-
052   evitably impose a huge amount of human effort, and thus not realistic. Recent methods have observed
053   rewarding distillation possibilities from proprietary commercial models like GPT-4V (OpenAI, 2023c)
      and Gemini (Gemini Team & other authors, 2023). However, the performance is upper bounded by

068   In this work, we aim to answer “whether it is possible that the VLM itself can remedy dataset
069   deficiency and enhance its training.” We delve deep into the potential of using VLM itself to refine
070   and augment pretraining data and performance iteratively. Our new training regime, summarized in
071   Figure 1, consists of two main steps: a self-augment
                                                             step and a specialist-augment step. We start
072
      with the self-augment loop Figure 1 (a) that leverages VLMs to enhance the quality of pretraining
      data. We demonstrate that synthetic data, combined with the original data, can collaboratively
073
      generate stronger models in a bootstrapped loop manner. Intuitively and as we observed, the loops
074
      offer performance boosts for free, but suffers diminishing returns after 3 rounds. To facilitate further
075

      learning, we reformulate a more challenging task-specific loop Figure 1 (b) . In this loop, specialists
076   with a focus on new knowledge or tasks, such as a spatial-aware expert, OCR expert or grounding
077   expert, are finetuned from the self-augmented VLM using a limited amount of additional SFT data.
078   The specialists can then recaption a massive amount of pretraining data. Finally, the self-augmented
079   VLM can be retrained on the specialist-recaptioned pretraining data to further boost the performance.
080
      The insights yield a novel VLM augmentation training regime progressively improves data quality, by
081   transferring knowledge from the higher-quality but small-scale SFT stage back to the larger-scale
082   but coarse pretraining phase. This improvements cover enhanced visual semantics (Figure 1)
083   and reducing hallucinations (Table 7- 8). We also observed consistent agreements on data quality
084   improvements when cross checking the data by VLM models (GPT-4V, Gemini) and humans (Ph.D.
085   students). This offers a direct performance boost to VLMs. We introduce a new family of VILA2
086   models, as in VLM-augmented-VLM. VILA2 outperforms state-of-the-art methods across main
087   benchmarks, all enhanced without bells and whistles via self-bootstrapped training. We hope that
088   the insights and release of VILA2 ’s recipe, data, and code can assist with our community for better
089
      understanding and usage of synthetic data to train stronger VLMs.

091   2      M ETHODOLOGY
092
093   In this paper, we focus on auto-regressive VLMs where image tokens are projected into the textual
094   space and concatenated with text tokens, in line with (Liu et al., 2023c; Gemini Team & other authors,
095   2023; Lin et al., 2024). This approach is chosen because of its flexibility when handling multi-modal
096   inputs. We follow the widely adapted three-stage training paradigm, i.e., align-pretrain-SFT, to ablate
097
      our studies. We start to self-augment VLM training by constructing a bootstrapped loop leveraging
      VLM’s general captioning capability. After the bootstrapping saturates, we then introduce specialist
098
      augmenting exploiting VLM’s skills picked up during SFT across additional visual tasks as specialist
099
      feedback to its pretraining stage. We next elaborate on these steps in detail.

185   2.2     S URPASSING THE L IMIT WITH S PECIALIST VLM AUGMENTATION
186
187   While self-augmentation provides a simple yet effective way to boost VLM’s performance, we notice
188   that the improvement starts to saturate with all free lunches having been squeezed (Table 3). We
189   hypothesize that this shortcoming stems from the monotonic task of general descriptive captioning,
190   which is also heavily influenced by language modeling priors.
191   To advance the boundary of self-augmentation, we propose the integration of extra task-specific
192   knowledge into a generalist VLM to create several specialist VLMs. Each specialist model is finetuned
193   with data that demands a deeper understanding of image components and semantics, e.g., spatial
194   relations, localization, and OCR. A bootstrapped loop can then transfer such specialist knowledge
195   from small-scale SFT data onto a large number of pretraining images.
196
197   2.2.1     ACQUIRING S PECIALIZED K NOWLEDGE
198
199
      We focus on three challenging tasks: spatial relations understanding, grounded narration, and OCR
      (Figure 3), and then elaborate the specialist construction as follows:
200
201   Spatial Specialist. To explicitly challenge the model to acquire additional spatial awareness, we
202   curated SpatialRelationQA, a dataset containing 1 million conversations about spatial relations within
203   scenes. Our dataset is built on LV3D, a comprehensive collection of both indoor and outdoor 3D
204   datasets from Cube-LLM (Cho et al., 2024) that is designed to enhance the understanding of 3D
205
      spatial relations requiring both perceptual and grounding skills.
206   We formulated a two-step process to create the QA pairs.
207
              1. For each cleaned 3D scene, we iterated through all 3D bounding boxes and randomly sampled
208
                 from object-object relations (closest, in front of, behind, left, right) and
209              object-camera relations (close, far, closest, farthest, left, right);
210
211           2. Next we checked if any remaining bounding boxes matched these sampled relations. For
212
                 each matched results, we randomly selected question templates to construct the QA pairs,
                 incorporating instances, their projected 2D bounding boxes, and relations.
213
214   A sample question can be constructed as: “Where is the chair closest to the table
215   [xleft ,ytop ,xright ,ybottom ] in the image?”, with answer being the target bounding box. During re-
      captioning, we guide the model to answer with more spatial information by prompting the specialist

      Training Strategies. We conduct VILA2 training following widely used three-stage settings.
299
300         1. Projector Initialization. The language models and ViT are separately pretrained, while the
301            projector is randomly initialized. To initially align the feature space between the visual and
302            text modalities, we utilize the LLaVA align dataset (Liu et al., 2023c).
303         2. Vision-Language Pretraining. We then pretrain the model (LLM and the projector) on the
304            visual language corpus. We consider interleaved image text corpus (e.g., MMC4 (Zhu et al.,
305            2023b)) and image-text pairs (e.g., COYO (Byeon et al., 2022)). We apply our VILA2 for
306
               the pretraining data and the augmented data will be applied in this stage to replace original
               COYO captions.
307
308
            3. Visual Instruction-tuning. Finally, we perform instruction tuning of the pretrained model on
               visual language instruction datasets. The blending details is attached in the appendix.
309
310   Without specifically mentioned, our experiments are conducted with 128 GPUs and a global batch
311   size of 1024. We employ AdamW optimizer with learning rate {10−3 , 5 × 10−5 , 2 × 10−5 } for
312   aforementioned three stages respectively. Each stage is trained with one epoch with a 3% warmup
313   strategy. No weight decay is applied. In some self-/specialist augmented training, VILA2 may involve
314
      extra stage to further improve. Please refer to Section. 2.2 and Appendix A.5 for more details.

316   Coyo (Byeon et al., 2022), and the full ShareGPT4V (Chen et al., 2023b). To ensure a fair comparison,
317   we only replace the text captions during our experiments while keeping all image sources the same.
318   We use two SFT data blends for different purposes: a smaller blend of 1.8M samples for exploratory

343   Our goal is to "augment" existing pretraining datasets by rewriting captions with dense and informative
344   texts. Instead of relying on human labor or black-box APIs, we use VILA to generate these captions.
345   Therefore, the enriched caption can help develop better VILAs based on which VILA can also
346   feedback to further enhance the captions for the training dataset.

      VILA2 Enriches Dataset Text Quality. The main VLM’s performance boost stems from improved
348   caption quality. As shown in Table 1, caption length increases rapidly after self-augmentation and

352   self-augmentation beyond round-1 primarily enhances caption quality by providing more accurate

355   clothing and surroundings. Although early iterations may contain some hallucinations (such as a

      non-existent basket and misread "Barcardi" text), subsequent iterations refine the caption to include

      The “self-augmentation then training” cycle reaches a plateau after three iterations, as illustrated

395
      Surpassing the Limit with VILA2 Specialist. The caption augmented by the specialist (the last
      example) retains the most visible details and provides more information about spatial relations
396
      compared to the "Round-4" caption (Figure 4). This additional information includes object-to-
397
      object relations, as well as localization and clear pose of the major focus, which are not present
398   in the SpatialRelationQA dataset. We hypothesize this improvement might result from combining
399   knowledge in specialist data and VLM’s training data. The effectiveness of these enriched captions is
400   demonstrated in Table 3 (VILA3 + spatial specialist). Following the same SFT stage, we observe
401   notable improvements in 5 out of the 8 benchmarks.
402
      The More Specialists, The Better The Performance. We next explore the significance of scaling up
403
      our VILA2 specialists with more pretraining data using recent state-of-the-art settings. We demon-
404   strate the effectiveness of each specialist using S2 (Shi et al., 2024) with Llama 3-8B-Instruct (meta,
405   2024). On a 10% subset of pretraining data, specialist VLMs show overall improvements across most
406   VQA benchmarks in Table 4’s first part. We then combine annotations from all three specialists into
407   multi-round QA pairs for each image and retrain VILA. This synergy among the specialists proves
408   highly effective, with scaling up to the full pretraining set yielding significant improvements. Results

      3.6   E FFICIENCY AND E FFECTIVENESS OF VILA2
493
494   Cost Analysis – Labeling. Data quantity and quality are critical factors in model training. While
495   VILA2 involves three rounds of recaptioning, it is still far more cost-efficient than traditional human
496   re-labeling. For example, a standard re-labeling on Amazon Turk costs 36 USD per 1k images, while
497   VILA2 costs only 0.12 USD per 1k images. The cost breakdown includes AWS pricing for H100
498   GPUs: USD 4.91 per hour for one H100, or USD 39.33 per hour for eight. With an inference speed
499   of 10.6 images per second per H100, VILA2 processes around 38,340 images per hour, making it
500   300x cheaper and significantly faster.
501   Cost Analysis – Training. As shown in Table 10, VILA2 achieves better accuracy with significantly
502   less data (51M) compared to other works like MM1 (>2B) and Idefics2 (>600M). Even with three
503   rounds of iteration, VILA2 remains more cost-effective in terms of training computation. Further,
504   VILA2 is a one-time cost that can be leveraged for training multiple models. Once recaptioning is
505   complete, this data can be shared with the community, reducing the need for expensive data pipelines.

      4     R ELATED W ORK & L IMITATIONS
510
511   Visual language models (VLM). Visual language models have rapidly progressed in recent years
512   (Radford et al., 2021; Li et al., 2022a; Dai et al., 2023; Liu et al., 2023c; Ye et al., 2024; Cheng et al.,
513
      2024). The success mainly comes from pretraining visual and language models on the internet-scale
      data. Kosmos-2 (Peng et al., 2023) and PaLI-X (Chen et al., 2023c) largely scaled the pretraining data
514
      by pseudo-labeling bounding boxes from performant open-vocabulary object detectors (GLIP (Li
515
      et al., 2022b) and OWL-v2 (Minderer et al., 2024), respectively). They examined that strong
516   perception capabilities such as object detection and OCR translate to better high-level reasoning tasks
517   like visual question-answering (VQA).
518
519
      Contributions & Novelty. Our work expands the horizon of data-scaling through our self-augmenting
      paradigm. ShareGPT4V (Chen et al., 2023b) applied a single round of recaptioning by distilling
520
      from GPT-4V. In contrast, we focus on a more general approach of using VLM to augment VLM
521
      itself without relying on commercial APIs or distilling from larger models. We provide 1) a detailed
522   analysis of self-augmentation, covering prompt templates, iteration rounds, saturation points; 2) a
523   practical method that uses specialist-augmentation to continually improve; 3) curated datasets that
524   can be reused for future research. Our solution efficiently enhances SOTA VLM performance without
525   requiring extra data or expensive APIs for closed-source models.
526
      Limitations. Due to resource constraints, we concentrate on the design of a self-augmented data
527
      curation pipeline and verify the 7B, 8B, and 40B models with 51M data. Larger models (e.g., 70B and
528   405B) and more data (>0.5B pretrain data) can have the potential to lead to better VLM capabilities
529   with self-augmenting abilities. We will address these aspects in future work.
530

532   5     C ONCLUSIONS
533
534   This work has explored the techniques, insights, and benefits of using VLMs to self-improve its pre-
535   training. We introduced two primary augmentation loops, one leveraging VLM’s general captioning
536   capacities and the other harnessing their strength in specialized visual tasks. We demonstrated the
537   feasibility of three ‘free lunch’ rounds for VLMs through self-bootstrapping, with further improve-
538   ments via knowledge distillation from specialist VLMs. Our new VILA2 models demonstrate SOTA
539   performances across a comprehensive set of benchmarks. Fruitful future directions include a deeper
      delve into the potential synergy between synthetic and real data to train stronger foundation models.

      like OWLv2(Minderer et al., 2024) to automatically label bounding boxes, VLMs to generate detailed
903   captions, and LLMs such as Llama3-70B-Instruct to merge the information for the grounded narration
904   specialist. However, we found that language models introduced more hallucinations into the merged
905   grounded narration. This is because many different detection labels can share the same meaning
906   and refer to the same instance, making it difficult for the language model to perform the bipartite
907   matching between bounding boxes and their text correspondence.
