009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            Efficient long-context language modeling remains a significant challenge in Nat-
012            ural Language Processing (NLP). While Transformers dominate language tasks,
013            they struggle with long sequences due to quadratic computational complexity in
014            training and linearly scaling memory costs during inference. Recent State Space
015            Models (SSMs) such as Mamba offer alternatives with constant memory usage,
016            but they underperform in tasks requiring extensive in-context retrieval. We in-
017            troduce Taipan, a novel hybrid architecture that combines Mamba-2 with Selec-
018
               tive Attention Layers (SALs). These SALs identify tokens requiring long-range
               interactions, remove less important features, and then augment their representa-
019
               tions using the attention module. This approach balances Mamba’s efficiency
020
               with Transformer-like performance in memory-intensive tasks. By constraining
021            the attention budget, Taipan extends accurate predictions to context lengths of up
022            to 1 million tokens while preserving computational efficiency. Our experiments
023            demonstrate Taipan’s superior performance across various scales and tasks, offer-
024            ing a promising solution for efficient long-context language modeling.

027   1   I NTRODUCTION
028
029   Transformer-based architectures Vaswani (2017); Brown (2020) have revolutionized Natural Lan-
030   guage Processing (NLP), delivering exceptional performance across diverse language modeling
031   tasks Touvron et al. (2023). This success stems from their ability to capture complex word de-
032   pendencies using the self-attention mechanism. In addition, Transformers are highly scalable and
033
      well-suited for parallel training on large datasets. However, despite their success, they still face no-
      table challenges when handling long-context sequences. Specifically, the self-attention mechanism
034
      suffers from quadratic computational complexity, and the memory requirement grows linearly with
035
      context length during inference, as the model must store key-value vectors for the entire context.
036   These factors impose practical constraints on sequence length due to the high computational and
037   memory costs.
038
039
      To this end, recent advancements in recurrent-based architectures, particularly State Space Models
      (SSMs) Gu et al. (2021b;a), have emerged as promising alternatives for efficient language modeling
040
      Gu & Dao (2023); Dao & Gu (2024). SSMs offer constant memory usage during inference, and
041
      architectures like Mamba-2 Dao & Gu (2024), a variant of SSMs, have demonstrated performance
042   comparable to Transformers in certain language tasks Waleffe et al. (2024). Some studies even
043   suggest that SSMs can outperform Transformers in areas like state tracking Merrill et al. (2024) due
044   to their Markovian nature. However, despite these advancements, SSM-based models still fall short
045   in scenarios requiring in-context retrieval or handling complex long-range dependencies Arora et al.
046   (2024); Waleffe et al. (2024).
047
      To address these challenges, we introduce Taipan, a hybrid architecture that combines the effi-
048   ciency of Mamba with enhanced long-range dependency handling through Selective Attention Lay-
049   ers (SALs). While Mamba is highly efficient, it relies on the Markov assumption—where predictions
050   are based solely on the last hidden state—which can lead to information loss for tokens that need
051   interactions with distant tokens. To mitigate this, Taipan incorporates SALs that strategically select
052   key tokens in the input sequence requiring long-range dependencies. These selected tokens first
053   undergo feature refinement to remove unimportant information, and then are passed through an at-
      tention module to capture long-range dependencies. Less critical tokens bypass the attention step, as

096
097    This section briefly overviews the foundational architectures relevant to our work. We first review
098    Causal Self-Attention Vaswani (2017), the core mechanism of Transformer models. We then discuss
099    Linear Attention Katharopoulos et al. (2020), an efficient variant that achieves linear complexity. Fi-
100    nally, we examine Mamba-2, a recent architecture that generalizes Linear Attention using structured
101    state-space models (SSMs) Dao & Gu (2024). We emphasize how each model balances computa-
102    tional efficiency and recall accuracy, particularly in memory-intensive tasks .

200   3        TAIPAN M ODEL
201
202   To address the limited modeling capabilities of Mamba-2 and Linear Attention while preserving
203   their computational efficiency, we introduce Taipan, a new architecture for sequence encoding in lan-
204   guage modeling. In Taipan, we strategically incorporate Selective Attention Layers (SALs) within
205   the Mamba framework, as shown in Figure 2. SALs are inserted after every K Mamba-2 blocks,
206
      creating a hybrid structure that combines Mamba-2’s efficiency with Transformer-style attention for
      effective sequence representation.
207
208   The core of SALs is a gating network that identifies important tokens for enhanced representation
209   modeling. These tokens undergo two phases: (1) feature refinement to filter out irrelevant infor-
210   mation and (2) representation augmentation via softmax attention. This allows Taipan to capture
211   complex, non-Markovian dependencies when necessary.
212   Taipan processes input through Mamba-2 blocks, with SALs periodically refining key token rep-
213   resentations. These enhanced representations are then passed into the subsequent Mamba-2 layers,
214   influencing further processing. This hybrid structure balances Mamba-2’s efficiency with the expres-
215   sive power of SALs, enabling Taipan to excel in tasks requiring both speed and accurate information
      retrieval. The following sections detail each component’s structure and function.

230   3.1   S ELECTIVE ATTENTION L AYERS
231
232   Selective Attention Layers (SALs) are the key innovation in Taipan, designed to enhance the model’s
233   ability to focus on critical tokens while maintaining overall efficiency. These layers employ a
234   lightweight gating network Gθ to dynamically determine which tokens should undergo softmax
235
      attention processing.
236   For each token hidden representation hi in the input sequence, the gating network G computes a
237   score vector:
238                                               si = Gθ (hi )                                          (1)
239   where Gθ : Rd → R2 is parameterized by θ. This score vector si = [si,0 , si,1 ] serves two purposes:
240   1) it is used to generate a binary mask mi for token selection, and 2) it guides feature refinement.
241
      To maintain differentiability while allowing for discrete token selection, we employ the Straight-
242   Through Gumbel-Softmax trick Jang et al. (2017). A binary mask mi is generated from si to select
243   tokens during the forward pass of the network:
244
245

246   where τ is the temperature parameter. hi will only be selected for attention processing if mi = 1.
247

248
      computation differentiability for the network:
249
250                            I[mi = 0] exp((si,0 + g0 )/τ ) + I[mi = 1] exp((si,1 + g1 )/τ )

261   In our model, the score vector si is also used to refine the representations of selected tokens. We
262   employ the softmax of si to compute the mixing weights: [1 − αi , αi ] = softmax(si ). The final

269   attention outputs. Here, it is important to note that unselected tokens (i.e., mi = 0) skip the attention

278   3.2   S LIDING W INDOW ATTENTION
279
280   To maintain linear time complexity while leveraging the benefits of attention, Taipan employs Slid-
281
      ing Window Attention (SWA) Beltagy et al. (2020). SWA’s computational complexity scales linearly
      with sequence length, allowing Taipan to handle theoretically unlimited context lengths during infer-
282
      ence. Importantly, the combination of Selective Attention and Sliding Window Attention in Taipan
283
      leads to a significantly sparser attention weight map compared to full attention or standard windowed
284   attention (Figure 3), thus enhancing the computational efficiency of Selective Attention for process-
285   ing long sequences for our model. In addition, the sparser attention map allows us to afford a longer
286   sliding window (i.e., w = 2048 in our work) to effectively capture longer-range dependencies for
287   input sequences. In this way, our designed Taipan architecture offers a mechanism to balance the ef-
288   ficient processing of long sequences with the ability to capture important long-range dependencies,
289   thereby addressing a key limitation of existing efficient attention mechanisms. Finally, removing
290   positional embeddings from the Attention Module improves extrapolation capabilities, suggesting
291   that the model can better generalize temporal relationships. We explore this impact of positional
292
      embeddings in more detail in Section 5.2.

      Taipan presents a significant advancement in long-context language modeling by combining the effi-
533
      ciency of Mamba with strategically placed Selective Attention Layers. Our experiments demonstrate
534   Taipan’s superior performance across various scales and tasks, particularly in scenarios requiring ex-
535   tensive in-context retrieval, while maintaining computational efficiency. A key insight is that not all
536   tokens require the same computational resources. Taipan’s architecture leverages this observation
537   through its selective attention mechanism, which dynamically allocates computational resources
538   based on token importance. This hybrid approach addresses limitations of both Transformers and
539   SSMs, offering a promising solution for efficient, large-scale language processing. Future work
      could explore further optimizations and applications of this architecture.
