                                                 A BSTRACT
008
009
010
               Recently, there has been an increasing demand for privacy-preserving techniques
               in numerous machine learning algorithms, elevating it to a critical concern. One
011
               promising solution involves the application of homomorphic encryption (HE).
012
               This study focuses on obtaining statistics based on the ranks of HE-encrypted
013            data as a vital tool for robust data analysis. However, computing ranks in HE
014            comes with significant computational costs due to the necessity of comparison
015            operations, and there is currently no efficient method available. To address this
016            gap, we propose an approximate rank method that exploits pairwise comparisons
017            of data to derive ranks for encrypted information. This method effectively mea-
018            sures the association between two-dimensional ranks. Specifically, by utilizing
019            approximate ranks of two variables, we estimate Spearman rank correlation with-
020            out relying on perfect sorting and introduce a technique to reduce the number of
021
               required comparisons. Numerical experiments have been conducted to validate
               our approach, demonstrating that the disparity in values between rank correlation
022
               and approximate rank correlation is not substantial. Notably, the processing of
023
               one block comprising 32,768 ciphertexts took approximately one minute, exhibit-
024            ing observed linear complexity dependent on the number of blocks.

027   1   I NTRODUCTION
028
029
      The demand for privacy-preserving techniques in numerous machine learning algorithms has been
030   on the rise, emerging as a crucial concern. One potential solution to address this concern is the
031   adoption of homomorphic encryption (HE) (Rivest et al., 1978). HE offers a promising solution
032   for safeguarding data privacy while enabling computations on encrypted data. In our research, we
033   concentrate on acquiring statistics based on the ranks of HE data. However, the computation of
034   ranks in HE involves substantial computational costs due to the requisite comparison operations.
035   Utilizing pairwise comparisons for any two data points allows us to determine the individual rank of
036   encrypted data with a computational complexity of O(n2 ), where n is the sample size (Chatterjee &
037   Sengupta, 2015; Cheon et al., 2019; Chatterjee & Sengupta, 2020; Çetin et al., 2021).
038   To mitigate the complexity associated with finding  ranks in HE, the Bitonic sort method (Nakatani
039   et al., 1989) offers a solution with O n(log n)2 complexity, ensuring a perfect alignment of data.
040   However, this method cannot track the index of encrypted data during sorting, preventing the iden-
041   tification of individual data ranks even after sorting encrypted data. Additionally, the Cheon, Kim,
042   Kim, and Song (CKKS) Scheme (Cheon et al., 2017), the sole HE system capable of handling real
043   numbers, faces challenges in precise comparison operations. Determining the exact rank becomes
044
      impossible when given numbers share the same value or exhibit slight differences.
045   Comparison operations in HE typically involve high computational complexity. This complexity
046   becomes particularly significant when dealing with statistics, such as those in Eq. (2), which are
047   based on ranks of HE data. To address this computational challenge, we propose a method for
048   approximating ranks.
049   In the case of one-dimensional data, the approximate rank represents ordered groups that categorize
050   and segment the data into grades. Conversely, for two-dimensional data, the approximate rank
051   delineates grades that spatially partition the data. Consider a scenario where the data exist on two
052   dimensions. By combining two marginal approximate ranks, a data point obtains a clearer spatial
053   rank compared to its rank in just one dimension. Leveraging this property, our proposed method
      effectively evaluates the correlation between ranks in two dimensions. An advantageous feature of

      this method is that, as the dimensionality increases, it enables more accurate identification of the
055   location of data points in multi-dimensional space. This capability proves effective in measuring the
056   correlation between ranks, offering a valuable tool for analyzing complex data structures.

058   1.1   C ONTRIBUTIONS
059
060   This paper presents a method for efficient approximate computation based on the group rank of
061   homomorphic encryption (HE). The main idea is to introduce a grouped rank within HE, allowing
062   us to estimate ranks that are HE-friendly for large-sized HE data.
063   In order to improve the computational complexity, currently at O(n2 ), required for precise ranking
064   in homomorphic encryption, we introduce the notion of grouped ranks, denoted as ’group rank.’
065   This concept proves effective in estimating correlations among variables in multidimensional space,
066   even though the approximation accuracy is substantial in one-dimensional space. To the best of our
067   knowledge, this work represents the first exploration in homomorphic encryption. The proposed
068   method specifically tackles the challenge of estimating Spearman rank correlation (Kruskal, 1958)
069   in two-dimensional space, showcasing a reduction in estimation error. Additionally, the utilization
070   of group rank in homomorphic encryption suggests the potential for facilitating HE-friendly compu-
071
      tations based on ranks for various statistical estimation problems. Furthermore, we enhance speed
      by implementing an efficient memory utilization of the comparison operations.

075   analysis. Section 5 concludes and discusses future works.

077   1.2   R ELATED W ORKS
078
      HE enables computations on encrypted data without decryption, facilitating data analysis while pre-
079
      serving data privacy. The concept of HE was initially proposed by Rivest et al. (1978), and Gentry
080
      (2009) demonstrated the existence of homomorphic encryption schemes that allow for unlimited
081   multiplications. Subsequently, several HE schemes have been developed. Cheon et al. (2017) pro-
082   posed an approximate HE scheme that supports particular fixed-point arithmetic commonly referred
083   to as block floating-point arithmetic, known as the Cheon, Kim, Kim, and Song (CKKS) scheme.
084   The CKKS scheme is recognized as one of the most efficient HE schemes that support computation
085   on real/complex data. Unlike other HE schemes designed for integer (Brakerski et al., 2012; Fan
086   & Vercauteren, 2012) or binary (Chillotti et al., 2016) messages, the CKKS scheme is intended for
087   real/complex messages.

094   H OMOMORPHIC ENCRYPTION
095
096   HE is a class of encryption schemes that enables computation over encrypted data. Fully HE refers
097   to an encryption system that preserves operations such as addition and multiplication in an encrypted
098   state. That is, for encryption homomorphism (Enc) and decryption homomorphism (Dec), the fol-
099
      lowing holds.
100                                      Dec(Enc(x) + Enc(y)) = x + y,
101                                      Dec(Enc(x) · Enc(y)) = x · y.
102
103   To estimate the ranking, comparison operations between data are necessary. For example, the 0-1
104   function with a single discontinuity point is commonly used to compare two numbers for finding var-
105   ious statistics. However, since the CKSS scheme is designed to handle only polynomial functions,
106   one must approximate non-polynomial functions with polynomial ones. To approximate the 0-1
107   function, the CKKS scheme employs a rational function that can be represented as the Taylor series,
      leading to time-consuming calculations in HE (Cheon et al., 2019; 2020). Their algorithms achieve

      5   D ISCUSSION
523
524
525
      In this study, we introduce a method for estimating group ranks of homomorphic encryption data by
      estimating the ECDF comparison with pre-defined uniformly or randomly selected knots. The pro-
526
      posed approach effectively estimates the Spearman rank correlation, which measures the correlation
527
      between the ranks of two variables. Our simulations assessed accuracy and computational com-
528   plexity across various distribution types, the number of knots, and selection methods. Additionally,
529   we successfully demonstrated the estimation of Spearman rank correlations among the measured
530   variables when applying this method to preoperative blood marker data.
531
      We enumerate topics for future work. First, statistical survival analysis frequently employs ranking-
532
      based methods. These methods can be utilized in survival regression models, such as the Kaplan-
533
      Meier estimator and Cox regression, which involve estimating the cumulative survival probability
534   up to a specific time. The group rank estimation method proposed for homomorphic encrypted
535   data in this study can also be extended to Kendall’s τ statistic, a distribution-agnostic measure of the
536   association between two variables. Moreover, when two populations, denoted as X1 , X2 , . . . , Xm ∼
537   F , and Y1 , Y2 , . . . , Yn ∼ G, are available, it is expected that the Mann-Whitney U-statistics can be
538   used to test the null hypothesis H0 : ∆ = 0, concerning the difference (∆) in means between the
539   two populations. This is further elaborated in the appendix. We will explore these topics in our
      future research.
