009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            Tensor Attention, a multi-view attention that is able to capture high-order corre-
012            lations among multiple modalities, can overcome the representational limitations
013            of classical matrix attention. However, the O(n3 ) time complexity of tensor at-
014            tention poses a significant obstacle to its utilization in transformers, where n is
015            the input sequence length. In this work, we prove that the backward gradient
016            of tensor attention training can be computed in almost linear time n1+o(1) , the
017            same complexity as its forward computation under the bounded entries assump-
018            tion. We provide a closed-form solution for the gradient and propose a fast com-
019            putation method utilizing polynomial approximation methods and tensor algebraic
020
               techniques. Furthermore, we prove the necessity and tightness of our assumption
               through hardness analysis, showing that slightly weakening it renders the gradient
021
               problem unsolvable in truly subcubic time. Our theoretical results establish the
022
               feasibility of efficient higher-order transformer training and may facilitate practi-
023            cal applications of tensor attention architectures.

033   2023), finance (Li et al., 2023b), law (Sun, 2023), and even writing NeurIPS conference reviews

040   The above open-sourced large models use two-view matrix attention, i.e., each attention score/entry
041   is related to two tokens (one query token and one key token) to capture the data correlation. More
042
      specifically, let Z be hidden representations and Q = ZWQ , K = ZWK , V = ZWV be the corre-
      sponding query, key, and value matrices after projections using weights WQ , WK , WV respectively.

045   On the other hand, many studies find that multi-view is crucial for high-order correlation in various
046   kinds of data, e.g., math (Sanford et al., 2023), graph (Demirel et al., 2022; Luo et al., 2023), and
047   multi-modality (Lahat et al., 2015). For example, recently, OpenAI released GPT-4o (OpenAI,
048   2024), and Google released Project Astra (Google, 2024), two flagship multi-modality models that
049   aim to reason across three views, i.e., audio, vision, and text in real-time.
050   However, Sanford et al. (2023) theoretically and empirically shows that classical attention can cap-
051   ture pairwise correlation but not triple-wise correlation due to the representational limitations of
052   matrix attention. Sanford et al. (2023) introduces a triple detection task, demonstrating that classical
053   attention layers have complexity scaling linearly with the input size, while high-order attention can
      efficiently perform this computation within a single head. In other words, one classical matrix

089   There are several recent works to overcome the time complexity bottleneck above, e.g., O(n2 ) for
090   matrix attention and O(n3 ) for tensor attention. Zandieh et al. (2023) accelerate matrix attention
091   forward via kernel density estimation and get truly sub-quadratic time running time. Alman & Song
092   (2023) uses the polynomial approximation method to map the matrix attention into low-rank matri-
093   ces during forward computation, leading to an almost linear time complexity n1+o(1) when entries
094   are bounded. Similarly, under sparsity assumptions, Han et al. (2024) achieves nearly linear time
095
      computation for matrix attention forward by identifying the larger entries in the attention matrix. On
      the one hand, with fine-grained analysis, Alman & Song (2024a) proposes a new backward algo-
096
      rithm to compute the gradient of matrix attention in almost linear time complexity n1+o(1) as well,
097
      under the same bounded entry assumption. On the other hand, Alman & Song (2024b) surprisingly
098
      finds that the forward computation of tensor attention can also be achieved in almost linear time
099
      n1+o(1) rather than almost quadratic time n2+o(1) , under similar assumptions as Alman & Song
100   (2023). See a summary in Table 1. Thus, it is natural to ask,
101
102       Can we achieve almost linear time for gradient computation in Tensor Attention Training?
103
104   We provide a positive answer in this work. Under the same bounded entries assumption as Alman &
105   Song (2024b), we propose Algorithm 1 to fast compute the backward gradient of Tensor Attention
106   Training in almost linear time n1+o(1) as its forward computation. Thus, our results may make the
107   tensor attention practical, as we can get around the O(n3 ) complexity barrier both in its forward and
      backward computation. Our contributions are summarized as follows:

118   2   R ELATED W ORK
119
120
      Fast attention computation. In recent years, significant advances have been made in the devel-
      opment of efficient attention computation. One research direction involves employing low-rank
121
      approximations, polynomial kernel, or random features for the attention matrix (Zheng et al., 2022;
122
      Alman & Song, 2023; Kacham et al., 2023; Song et al., 2024; Gu et al., 2024), which scales the com-
123   putational complexity sub-quadratically with sequence length. Another method explores patterns of
124   sparse attention that lessen the computational load (Han et al., 2024). Additionally, using linear at-
125   tention as an alternative to softmax attention has emerged as a substantial area of study (Katharopou-
126   los et al., 2020; Schlag et al., 2021; Zhang et al., 2023b; Ahn et al., 2024; Zhang et al., 2024). These
127   innovations have enhanced the capability of transformer-based models to handle longer sequences,
128   thereby broadening their potential applications across various fields (Chen et al., 2023; Su et al.,
129   2024; Peng et al., 2024; Ding et al., 2024; Ma et al., 2024; Bertsch et al., 2023; Jin et al., 2024).
130
131   Tensor computation for high-order representation. Tensors excel over matrices in capturing
132
      higher-order relationships within data. Calculating low-rank factorizations or approximations of
      tensors is essential in a wide range of computer science applications, such as natural language pro-
133
      cessing (Lei et al., 2015; Bouchard et al., 2015), computer vision (Lu et al., 2016; Chen et al.,
134
      2017), computer graphics (Wang et al., 2005; Vasilescu, 2009), security (Acar et al., 2006; Kolda &
135   Bader, 2006), and data mining (Karatzoglou et al., 2010; Rendle & Schmidt-Thieme, 2010; MГёrup,
136   2011). Moreover, tensors are crucial in numerous machine learning applications (Podosinnikova
137   et al., 2015; Zhong et al., 2017; Yang et al., 2019) and other diverse fields (Reps et al., 2016; Yi
138   et al., 2016; Ray et al., 2016).

498
499
      In this work, we proved that the backward gradient of tensor attention training can be computed
500
      in almost linear n1+o(1) time, the same complexity as its forward computation, under a bounded
501
      entries assumption. We provided a closed-form solution for the gradient and proposed a fast com-
502   putation method utilizing polynomial approximation and tensor algebraic techniques. Furthermore,
503   we proved the necessity and tightness of our assumption through hardness analysis, showing that
504   slightly weakening it renders the tensor attention gradient problem unsolvable in truly subcubic
505   time. Our theoretical results establish the feasibility of efficient higher-order transformer training
506   and may facilitate practical applications of tensor attention architectures. Due to space limits, we
507   provide our further discussion and extension in Appendix A. Future work can perform empirical
508   evaluations of the method in practical large language models, and explore how these findings can be
509   implemented in real-world scenarios to enable the development of powerful higher-order models.

893   Connection to real applications. There are some empirical studies attempting to implement sim-
894   ilar tensor attention (three order) in language modeling (Ma et al., 2019) and 3D medical image
895   segmentation (Wang et al., 2023). However, due to cubic time complexity, their models remain rela-

      tively small, e.g, 12M parameters in Ma et al. (2019). Although small scale, Ma et al. (2019); Wang
      et al. (2023) demonstrates the significant potential of tensor attention. Our work proves that an al-
897
      most linear time algorithm for tensor attention mechanisms exists (Algorithm 1). This advancement

      could enable the scaling up of tensor attention and facilitate novel model designs in multi-modality,

901   a solution. Our work introduces a new concept to the community, suggesting that cubic time com-
902   plexity may not be the bottleneck in implementing three-order attention during training. Practical
903   implementation poses additional significant challenges, considering numerous other techniques and
904   operations, such as dropout, layer normalization, residual connections, position encoding, and many
905   others. We hope our work inspires further algorithmic design.
