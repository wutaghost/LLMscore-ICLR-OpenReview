009                                              A BSTRACT
010
011            Multi-hop complex reasoning over incomplete knowledge graphs has been ex-
012            tensively studied, but research on numerical knowledge graphs remains relatively
013            limited. Recent approaches focus on separately encoding entities and numerical
014            values, using neural networks to process query encodings for reasoning. However,
015            in complex multi-hop reasoning tasks, numerical values are not merely symbols;
016            they carry specific semantics and logical relationships that must be accurately rep-
017            resented. Directly encoding numerical values often leads to the loss of such se-
018            mantic information. In this work, we propose a Complex Numerical Reasoning
019
               with Numerical Semantic Pre-Training Framework (CNR-NST). Specifically, we
               designed a joint link predictor to learn numerical semantics. The proposed frame-
020
               work is the first to enable binary operations on numerical attributes in numerical
021
               knowledge graphs, allowing new numerical attributes to be inferred from existing
022            knowledge. The CNR-NST framework can perform binary operations on numer-
023            ical attributes in numerical knowledge graphs, enabling it to infer new numerical
024            attributes from existing knowledge. Our approach effectively handles up to 102
025            types of complex numerical reasoning queries. On three public datasets, CNR-
026            NST demonstrates SOTA performance in complex numerical queries, achieving
027            an average improvement of over 40% compared to existing methods. Notably,
028            this work expands the range of query types for complex multi-hop numerical rea-
029            soning and introduces a new evaluation metric for numerical answers, which has
030
               been validated through comprehensive experiments.

033   1   I NTRODUCTION
034
035   Complex Query Answering (CQA) refers to the process of reasoning and performing computations
036   on knowledge graphs by combining multiple entities and relationships to retrieve entities that fulfill
037   specific logical conditions (Kotnis & Garcı́a-Durán (2018)). This field has seen significant advance-
038   ments, with research increasingly focused on enhancing the accuracy of models in handling intricate
039
      query tasks.(Zhu et al. (2022)) (Arakelyan et al. (2020)) Despite this progress, real-world knowledge
      graphs are not limited to discrete entity-relation knowledge; they also contain numerous numerical
040
      attributes, such as birth dates, event times, and territorial sizes of countries. Numerical knowledge
041
      graphs, therefore, offer a more nuanced approach to modeling real-world query (Xue et al. (2022)).
042   Figure 1 presents an example of the FB15K numerical knowledge graph (Kotnis & Garcı́a-Durán
043   (2018)) illustrating three distinct query types: Complex Queries Involving Only Entities, such as
044   Q1: Which film directors are married?; Complex Numerical Queries, such as Q2: Who are the
045   married individuals born in August 1955?; and Complex Numerical Queries with Calculations,
046   such as Q3:What is the combined population of Schleswig-Holstein and Dakar?
047
      Traditional CQA methods are effective for multi-hop queries but encounter challenges in accurately
048   capturing the subtle nuances of numerical semantics (Ren et al. (2023)). These methods neglect the
049   inherent meanings of numerical attributes. Existing approaches, such as the Numerical Reasoning
050   Network (NRN) (Bai et al. (2023a)), present a framework that encodes entities and numerical val-
051   ues separately. However, when using Sinusoidal (Sundararaman et al. (2020)) and DICE (Vaswani
052   (2017)) encoding methods for numerical values, this approach encounters issues with sparsity in the
053   knowledge graph. Many encoding regions lack corresponding numerical mappings in the knowledge
      graph, leading to incomplete learning of numerical semantics.

      Handling complex numerical query tasks presents several significant challenges. First, current meth-
      ods are constrained by their reasoning answers, which are limited to the numerical knowledge graph
083
      itself and cannot compute or infer new numerical answers from multiple values (like Q3). The
084
      ability to infer new numerical attributes from existing values in knowledge graphs is essential for
085   practical applications. Second, unlike entities, numerical values are inherently continuous (Ren et al.
086   (2023)), with their semantics influenced by factors such as units, ranges, and precision, establishing
087   an intrinsic connection with their context (Kim et al. (2023)). Moreover, the sparsity of numeri-
088   cal data within knowledge graphs introduces additional challenges (Li et al. (2022)) that must be
089   addressed to effectively tackle complex queries.
090
      To tackle the challenges in complex numerical reasoning, we propose the CNR-NST framework:
091   Numerical Binary Operation Operator. As illustrated in Figure 2a, CNR-NST is capable of han-
092   dling queries that involve numerical answers not present in the original knowledge graph, introduc-
093   ing new numerical query operators. Utilizing relevant theorems from fuzzy mathematics, we can
094   perform operations on two fuzzy numbers within the real number domain. By representing numer-
095   ical values as fuzzy sets, these operations can be effectively mapped to mathematical calculations
096   between two numerical attributes within the knowledge graph (Detailed mathematical proof can be
097   found in Appendix A.1).
098   Numerical Semantic Learning. To effectively capture the semantics of numerical values, we uti-
099
      lize a joint predictor to learn the relationships between entity attributes and their corresponding
      numerical values, thus facilitating knowledge transfer across different tasks. Figure 2b provides a
100
      comprehensive overview of this architecture.
101
      Complex Numerical Reasoning and Computation. CNR-NST utilizes fuzzy sets to represent
102   numerical values and entities, thereby avoiding the training of neural operators in an unrestricted
103   numerical embedding space (as illustrated in Figure 2c). Fuzzy sets not only capture the uncertainty
104   of numerical values during complex reasoning but also effectively represent the inherent fuzzy rela-
105   tionships within numerical data. During the reasoning process, the values of intermediate variables
106   reflect the probability scores of corresponding entities or numerical values, thereby greatly enhanc-
107   ing the accuracy of numerical inference and reasoning.

146   Knowledge Graph With Numerical Attributes. Knowledge Graph G = (V, R, ε) contains the
147   set of all entities V and the set of all relations R. Each triplet (h, r, t) ∈ ε is the set of facts in
148   the knowledge graph. And Numerical Knowledge Graph GN = (V, R, A, N , ε) not only contains
149   entities V and relations R, but also contains the set of all attributes A and the set of all attribute
150   values N (Pai & Costabello (2021)). Meanwhile, the triplet (h, r, t) in the Numerical Knowledge
151   Graph is defined as GN = {(h, r, t)} ⊂ (V × R × V) ∪ (V × A × N) , where A ∩ R = ∅.
152   Numerical FOL Query. A complex numerical query is defined in existential positive first-order
153   logic form, which can be recursively defined as:
154   1. Atomic Formulas: If t1 , t2 , . . . , tn are variables or constants, and P is an n-ary predicate, then
155   P (t1 , t2 , . . . , tn ) is an atomic formula.
156   2. Compound formulas: It can be constructed using logical connectives ∧ (and), ∨ (or), and quanti-
157   fiers ∀ (for all) and ∃ (there exists).

      New Evaluation Metrics for Numerical-Type Answers. In complex multi-hop numerical rea-
466
      soning, many query types return numerical values as answers. Previous approaches used the same
467
      evaluation metrics for these queries as for entities, but this method has limitations. It does not
468   consider the differences between numerical values and entities, especially the continuous nature of
469   numerical data. Additionally, it cannot effectively evaluate queries where the numerical answers are
470   not present in the original knowledge graph. To address these issues, we propose a new evaluation
471   metric for numerical answers, analogous to Mean Reciprocal Rank (MRR). Instead of ranking based
472   on the exact match of numerical nodes, we compute the RANK using the probability ranking of nu-
473   merical nodes whose relative error compared to the correct answer is below a specified threshold
474   (typically set at 0.001). This new metric is denoted as MRR0.001 , and the ranking calculations are
475   applied only to hard answers.
476   As shown in Table 3, we re-evaluated the numerical answers across the 102 sub-queries that CNR-
477   NST can handle and compared the results with the baseline model, Q2P. CNR-NST significantly
478   outperformed Q2P on the new evaluation metric, demonstrating that although CNR-NST may not
479   always generate completely accurate answers in numerical reasoning, its performance improves
480   substantially when a margin of error is permitted. This suggests that CNR-NST’s inferred answers
481   are often very close to the correct values. Furthermore, the experimental results show that our
482
      approach offers a significant advantage over baseline models in numerical reasoning tasks, with an

519
520
      Complex Query Answering over conventional Knowledge Graph. Complex Query Answering
521
      (CQA) focuses on reasoning over relationships and entities in a knowledge graph to address complex
522
      logical queries. Early CQA methods relied on logical reasoning rules to query knowledge graphs.
523   Methods like GQE (Hamilton et al. (2018)), Q2B (Ren et al. (2020)), Q2P (Bai et al. (2022)), ConE
524   (Zhang et al. (2021)) and BetaE (Ren & Leskovec (2020)) represent entity sets and queries using
525   geometric shapes or probability distributions, utilizing geometric operations (e.g., intersections, pro-
526   jections) or probabilistic operations for reasoning. Later, CQD (Arakelyan et al. (2020)) introduced
527   a framework capable of handling complex logical expressions without explicit training on complex
528   queries. QTO (Bai et al. (2023b)) further optimized the query computation tree during complex
529   query processing, improving reasoning accuracy and reducing the search space.
530   Numerical reasoning over Knowledge Graph. Numerical reasoning tasks in knowledge graphs
531
      involve making logical inferences or predictions based on numerical values associated with enti-
      ties and relationships. Methods such as RAKGE (Kim et al. (2023)) , KR-EAR (Lin et al. (2016))
532
      , TransEA Wu & Wang (2018) and Lacroix et al. (2018) utilize attribute learning to improve nu-
533
      merical reasoning within knowledge graph embeddings. LiteralE (Kristiadi et al. (2019)) enhances
534   knowledge graph embeddings by incorporating textual information through learnable parametric
535   functions. HyNT (Chung et al. (2023)) uses the expressive power of Transformers to capture com-
536   plex relational structures and numerical attributes in knowledge graphs. Neural-Num-LP (Wang
537   et al. (2020)) learns numerical rules within knowledge graphs.
538   LLMs’ numerical reasoning. Large model numerical reasoning refers to extracting relevant numer-
539   ical information from textual descriptions and performing mathematical calculations (Zhang et al.
      (2024)). MathPrompter (Imani et al. (2023)) introduces the ”Chain-of-Thought” (CoT) approach,


                                                                        10
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


540
      utilizing a step-by-step prompting technique to guide the model through solving complex arithmetic
541   problems incrementally. NumeroLogic (Schwartz et al. (2024)) defines new numerical formats to
542   handle and execute arithmetic operations. PoT (Chen et al. (2022)) employs the Codex model to
543   represent the reasoning process as a program, which is then executed by an external system to per-
544   form the necessary computations and derive the final answer.
545   Complex Query Answering over Numerical Knowledge Graph. Research on CQA with numer-
546   ical values is still limited. LitCQD (Demir et al. (2023)) decomposes complex numerical queries
547   into smaller subqueries, solving them by integrating symbolic and numerical reasoning. NRN (Bai
548   et al. (2023a)) embeds entities, relations, and numerical attributes from the knowledge graph into a
549
      shared vector space, using neural networks to learn and reason about numerical relationships.
550
551   6   C ONCLUSION
552
553   In this paper, we present a novel framework for reasoning and computation on numerical knowledge
554   graphs, leveraging a pretrained multi-relational link predictor to infer over 102 types of complex
555   numerical queries. Extensive experiments on three publicly available KGs demonstrate that our
556   proposed model, CNR-NST, significantly surpasses previous SOTA methods. Furthermore, we in-
557   troduce additional categories of numerical reasoning tasks and new evaluation metrics for numerical
558
      answers, contributing to the broader research of multi-hop numerical reasoning. Future work could
      explore expanding the variety of numerical queries supported by CNR-NST, further enhancing its
559
      applicability in diverse reasoning tasks.
