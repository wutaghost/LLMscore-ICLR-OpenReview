               Using the same architecture for text and image is important for AI standardization.
               Recent multimodal models use a decoder-only Transformer to generate text and
011
               an encoder-only Transformer to extract image features. Can images use exactly
012
               the same language architecture? To answer this question, we aim at a LLaMa
013            decoder as vision Transformer (ViT) classifier in this paper. Specifically, we start
014            our trajectory by “LLaMAfy” a standard ViT step-by-step, i.e., feed-forward net,
015            normalization layer, causal self-attention and positional embedding, and point out
016            a key issue—attention collapse—that result in the failure to the network training.
017            Motivated by this observation, we propose post-sequence class token, enabling
018            causal self-attention to efficiently capture the entire image’s information. To im-
019            prove model optimization behavior and enhance performance, we then introduce
020            a soft mask strategy to gradually transform the attention from bi-directional to
021
               causal mode. The tailored model, dubbed as image LLaMA (iLLaMA), maintains
               high consistency with LLaMA architecture, while matching up well against ViT,
022
               achieving 75.1% ImageNet top-1 accuracy with only 5.7M parameters. Scaling
023
               the model to ∼310M and pre-training on ImageNet-21K further enhances the ac-
024            curacy to 86.0%. Its causal self-attention boosts computational efficiency and
025            learns complex representation by elevating attention map ranks. Extensive exper-
026            iments demonstrate iLLaMA’s reliable properties: shape-texture bias, calibration,
027            quantization compatibility, ADE20K segmentation and CIFAR transfer learning.
028            We hope our study can kindle fresh views to visual architectures in the era of
029            LLMs and contributes to standardized AI models.

032   1   I NTRODUCTION
033
034   Using the same architectures for both text and images is important for building standardized AI
035   systems. If architectures dealing with both modalities can be fully aligned, we can economically
036   develop one set of operators to implement both models, and acceleration and optimization algo-
037   rithms (Kwon et al., 2023; Dao et al., 2022; Dao, 2024; Shah et al., 2024) designed for one modality
038
      (e.g., text) can be seamlessly transferred to another (e.g., image).
039   Recent multimodal models use decoder-only large language models (LLMs, e.g., LLaMA (Touvron
040   et al., 2023a)) to generate text, given their superior scaling capabilities and performance. For image
041   feature extraction, however, encoder-only ViTs (Dosovitskiy et al., 2020) are still used. a natural
042   question is: can decoder-only language architectures be used to handle images?
043   However, the answer to this question is not intuitive. First, decoder-only architectures take a causal
044   mode attention to process 1D text tokens, while encoder-only counterparts use a bi-directional
045   mode attention to process 2D image tokens. Such intrinsic difference may affect the effective-
046   ness of LLaMA training on visual tasks. Second, besides the attention mode, several differences
047   still exist in architectural design choices between LLaMA and ViT, i.e., feed-forward network
048   (SwiGLU (Shazeer, 2020) vs MLP), normalization layer (RMSNorm (Zhang & Sennrich, 2019)
049   vs LayerNorm (Ba et al., 2016)), and positional embedding (rotary (Su et al., 2024) vs learnable).
050   In this paper, we move closer to answering this question by introducing a decoder-only vision Trans-
051   former—image LLaMA (iLLaMA), which adapts LLaMA decoder to an image classifier, as shown
052   in Figure 1. Our exploration roadmap starts with an empirical study: replacing the components of
053   LLaMA, i.e., SwiGLU, RMSNorm, causal self-attention, and RoPE into a standard ViT step-by-
      step, and learn useful lessons along this adaptation process. Importantly, we observe an attention

086

089
090               • We investigate several designs of using LLaMA decoder as an image classifier and learn
091                 useful lessons along the adaptation way. iLLaMA fully aligns with LLaMA in architecture.
092               • We identify the attention collapse issue when applying causal mode attention, and thus
093                 introduce a PS [cls] technique and a soft mask strategy to respectively to address this issue
094                 and improve model training behavior.
095               • Extensie experiments on ImageNet, transfer learning, along with practical properties such
096                 as quantization compatibility, calibration, shape-texture bias demonstrate that iLLaMA can
097                 be safely used as an efficient and reliable ViT alternative for image feature extraction.

      2.1     T RANSFORMER E NCODER AND DECODER .
102
103   We briefly summarize the encoder and decoder in Transformer (Vaswani et al., 2017). Both of
104   them basically consist of attention module and a MLP module, each followed by a residual con-
105   nection. The key difference between them is the mask scheme in their self-attention. Encoders use
106   bi-directional self-attention, and decoders employ causal self-attention and cross-attention. How-
107   ever, the latter is typically omitted in decoder-only LLMs (Touvron et al., 2023a;b), we thus focus
      on comparing causal and bi-directional attention as follows, in terms of the mask setting. Denote

135   conduct an empirical study of gradually aligning LLaMA designs to a standard ViT step-by-step in

139
      map rank. We use ViT-T/16 and ViT-B/16 with around 5.7M and 86.4M parameters. We conduct

      et al., 2023) (details in Appendix C.1). Considering the differences between visual perception and
141
      text generation tasks, we maintain ViT’s non-autoregressive manner in our network. Each step

      3.1   P OST-S EQUENCE C LASS T OKEN : ACHIEVING A RCHITECTURE A LIGNMENT
145
146   Feed-forward network (FFN) module is implemented as multi-layer perceptron (MLP) in ViT and
147   SwiGLU (Shazeer, 2020) in LLaMA. MLP consists of two sequential linear mappings. Meanwhile,
148   SwiGLU combines three linear mappings, allowing for the modulation of high-dimensional fea-
149   tures. We substitute the Transformer’s MLPs with SwiGLUs, while maintaining comparable com-
150   putational cost. As shown in Figure 1, this improves performance from 73.8% to 74.3%, and from
151   81.3% to 82.0% for the ViT-T/16 and ViT-B/16 regime. This highlights SwiGLU’s effectiveness not
152
      only in language models but also in vision, inspiring further exploration of other components. We
      will now use SwiGLU to substitute MLP in each block.
153
154   Normalization layer is the key module in Transformers for stable training i.e., layer normalization
155   (LN) (Ba et al., 2016) in ViT and root mean square layer normalization (RMSNorm) (Zhang &
156   Sennrich, 2019) in LLaMA. We replace all LNs with RMSNorms in our network and empirically
157   observed that the accuracy of the ViT-T/16 regime increased from 74.3% to 74.5%. However, sim-
158
      ilar improvements in precision were not observed in the ViT-B/16 regime (from 82.0% to 81.7%).
      Nonetheless, compared to LN, RMSNorm removes the shift term computation, bringing simplicity
159
      to the network. We will use RMSNorm instead of LN as the normalization layer in each block.

200
      to the patch embedding. Meanwhile, rotary positional embedding (RoPE) (Su et al., 2024) is gener-
201
      ally applied in LLMs (Touvron et al., 2023a;b), which functions in the attention of each block. We

205   Transformer blocks, keeping it does not disrupt the alignment with LLaMA within each block. Thus,
206   we reintroduce the LPE, which improves the accuracy of ViT-T/16 regime from 72.6% to 73.2%,

208   network performance. We will use both LPE and RoPE for positional embedding.

459   FFN and causal self-attention layers to 8 bits. Quantization recipes and results are shown in Table 15
460
      of Appendix C.4 and Table 7. Different sizes of low-bit iLLaMA maintain accuracy well, and 8-bit
      iLLaMA-T is even compete favorably with the full-precision DeiT-T (72.4% v.s. 72.2%).

      5     C ONCLUSIONS
478
479
      In this paper, we systematically studies whether Transformer decoder, an architecture that has shown
480   amazing potential in LLMs, can also take root in learning visual representation through straightfor-
481   ward supervised training. The key component – causal self-attention we used – is not novel and
482   is inherited from existing LLM architectures, but we propose pivotal techniques, i.e., PS [cls] and
483   soft mask strategies, to effectively adapt them to visual tasks. The proposed iLLaMA outperforms
484   many ConvNets, ViTs, and MLPs on imagenet, and demonstrates robust quantization compatibility,
485   calibration, and shape-texture bias, thereby showing its practicality. We hope that this work will
      inspire a rethinking of generic yet practical architecture that can fully unify both vision and text.

704   import torch
      import torch.nn as nn
705
706   def reshape_for_broadcast(freqs_cis: torch.Tensor, x: torch.Tensor):
         ndim = x.ndim
707      assert 0 <= 1 < ndim
         assert freqs_cis.shape == (x.shape[1], x.shape[-1])
708      shape = [d if i == 1 or i == ndim - 1 else 1 for i, d in enumerate(x.shape)]
709      return freqs_cis.view(*shape)
710   def apply_rotary_emb(
711      xq: torch.Tensor,
         xk: torch.Tensor,
712      freqs_cis: torch.Tensor,
      ) -> Tuple[torch.Tensor, torch.Tensor]:
713      xq_ = torch.view_as_complex(xq.float().reshape(*xq.shape[:-1], -1, 2))
714      xk_ = torch.view_as_complex(xk.float().reshape(*xk.shape[:-1], -1, 2))
         freqs_cis = reshape_for_broadcast(freqs_cis, xq_)
715      xq_out = torch.view_as_real(xq_ * freqs_cis).flatten(3)
716      xk_out = torch.view_as_real(xk_ * freqs_cis).flatten(3)
         return xq_out.type_as(xq), xk_out.type_as(xk)
717
718   class Attention(nn.Module):
719      def __init__(self, dim, num_heads=8, qkv_bias=False, qk_scale=None, attn_drop=0.,
             proj_drop=0.):
720         super().__init__()
721         self.num_heads = num_heads
            head_dim = dim // num_heads
722         # NOTE scale factor was wrong in my original version, can set manually to be
                compat with prev weights
723         self.scale = qk_scale or head_dim ** -0.5
724
             self.qkv = nn.Linear(dim, dim * 3, bias=qkv_bias)
725          self.proj = nn.Linear(dim, dim)
726
         def forward(self, x: torch.Tensor, freqs_cis: torch.Tensor, mask: Optional[torch.
727          Tensor]):
            B, N, C = x.shape
728         qkv = self.qkv(x).reshape(B, N, 3, self.num_heads, C // self.num_heads).permute(2,
729              0, 1, 3, 4) # [3, B, N, self.num_heads, C // self.num_heads]
            q, k, v = qkv[0], qkv[1], qkv[2] # make torchscript happy (cannot use tensor as
730             tuple) # [B, N, self.num_heads, C // self.num_heads]
731
             q, k = apply_rotary_emb(q, k, freqs_cis=freqs_cis)
732
             q = q.transpose(1, 2) # [B, self.num_heads, N, C // self.num_heads]
733          k = k.transpose(1, 2) # [B, self.num_heads, N, C // self.num_heads]
734          v = v.transpose(1, 2) # [B, self.num_heads, N, C // self.num_heads]
             attn = (q @ k.transpose(-2, -1)) * self.scale # [B, self.num_heads, N, N]
735          attn = attn.softmax(dim=-1)
736          if mask is not None:
                attn = attn * mask # (B, H, N, N)
737
             x = (attn @ v).transpose(1, 2).reshape(B, N, C)
738          x = self.proj(x)
739
             return x

1158   L    L IMITATIONS
1159
1160   We have shown that the LLaMA architecture, enhanced by the developed post-sequence [cls] and
1161   soft mask techniques, is adept at adapting to tasks such as visual recognition and semantic segmen-
1162   tation. However, iLLaMA’s application remains predominantly within the realm of perception. In
1163   fact, such decoder-only architecture, favored by LLMs in the NLP field, can do more complex tasks,
1164   such as reasoning and generation. This may be due to their massive training data and the next sen-
1165
       tence prediction training paradigm, which is not explored by iLLaMA. Thus, a critical validation
       step of aligning the architectures of text and visual models would be to construct a multi-modal
1166
       large language model that fully leverages LLaMA components. In this envisioned model, both vi-
1167
       sual and textual feature extractors would be realized through the LLaMA architecture. Futhermore,
1168   we strongly argue that iLLaMA’s successful attempts at basic supervised training strategies and
1169   classification tasks provide a foundation for more complex tasks, such as object detection and depth
1170   estimation. This represents a compelling avenue for future research.

       M    S OCIETAL I MPACT
1174
       After the ChatGPT milestone in 2022, open-source architectures like LLaMA began to shine in the
1175
       text domain. In the real world, images and text are the two main mediums of information and data
1176
       types. For neural networks, having a unified architecture for language and vision models allows
1177   people to process these two distinct types of information using the same structure, which aids in
1178   the specialization of hardware implementation. This paper transfers the architecture widely used in
1179   language models to vision models, facilitating the achievement of this goal. The pretrained models
1180   and code provided in this paper can be used in a plug-and-play manner to serve this objective.
