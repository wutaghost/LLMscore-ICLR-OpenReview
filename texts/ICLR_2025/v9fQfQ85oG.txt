                                                 A BSTRACT
012
               Multi-objective multi-agent reinforcement learning (MOMARL) problems fre-
013            quently arise in real world applications (e.g., path planning for robots) but have
014            not been explored well. To find Pareto-optimum is NP-hard, and thus some multi-
015            objective algorithms have emerged recently to provide Pareto-stationary solution
016            centrally, managed by a single agent. Yet, they cannot deal with MOMARL prob-
017            lem, as the dimension of global state-action (s, a) grows exponentially with the
018
               number of spatially distributed agents. To tackle this issue, we design a novel
               graph-truncated Q-function approximation method for each agent i, which does
019            not require the global state-action (s, a) but only the neighborhood state-action
020            (sNiκ , aNiκ ) of its κ-hop neighbors. To further reduce the dimension to state-
021            action (sNiκ , ai ) with only local action, we further develop a concept of action-
022            averaged Q-function and establish the equivalence between using graph-truncated
023            Q-function and action-averaged Q-function for policy gradient approximation.
024            Accordingly, we develop a distributed scalable algorithm with linear function ap-
025            proximation and prove that it successfully converges Pareto-stationary solution at
026            rate O(1/T ) that is inversely proportional to time domain T . Finally, we run sim-
               ulations in a robot path planning environment and show our algorithm converges
027
               to greater multi-objective values as compared to the latest MORL algorithm, and
028            performs close to the central optimum with much shorter running time.

031   1   I NTRODUCTION
032
033   As real-world applications become increasingly complex, multi-objective optimization problems are
034
      becoming more prevalent. For example, in the e-commerce domain (Weck et al., 2022; Xu et al.,
      2024), platforms aim for product recommendations that are not only clickable and purchasable but
035
      also engaging enough to encourage user sharing and collection. This scenario involves optimiz-
036   ing multiple objectives, including the click-through rate, purchase rate, and collection rate of the
037   products. For such scenarios involving multiple optimization objectives, the traditional setting of a
038   single reward structure in the reinforcement learning (RL) framework (Sutton & Barto, 1998) is ob-
039   viously insufficient to describe. Therefore, it is necessary to establish multi-objective RL (MORL)
040   problems.
041   Different from the rapid development of traditional RL (Grondman et al, 2012; Zhang et al, 2021),
042   the research in MORL (Ge et al., 2022; Stamenkovic et al, 2022) is still in its infancy to address the
043   potential conflicts between multiple objectives. One common approach to solving MORL problem
044   involves assigning weights to different objectives and transforming the multi-objective problem into
      a single-objective problem (Blondin & Hale, 2020). However, this approach has the limitation of
045
      assuming known objective weights, which can restrict its applicability. In the MORL problems, a
046   more appropriate and relevant metric is to find a Pareto-optimal solution for all objectives, where
047   no objective can be unilaterally improved without sacrificing another. As many real-world MORL
048   problems are typically non-convex, finding the Pareto-optimal solution is NP-hard (Yang et al.,
049   2024).
050   To address the NP-hard nature of non-convex MORL problems, Pareto-stationary solutions (a neces-
051   sary condition for Pareto optimality) are employed (Sener & Koltun, 2018). For the MORL problems
052   with continuous action space, (Chen et al., 2021) proposed an actor-critic MORL algorithm based
053   on the deterministic policy-gradient (Silver et al., 2014). More generally, for the MORL problem
      with non-continuous action space, a unified multi-objective actor-critic algorithmic framework was


                                                        1
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


054
      proposed for both discounted and average reward settings in (Zhou et al., 2024), where the update
055   of stochastic policy parameters employs the multi-gradient descent method in (Désidéri, 2012).
056
057
      The aforementioned methods are all directed towards addressing the MORL problem in a central-
      ized setting or for a single agent. However, practical applications of MORL problems often involve
058
      multi-agents. For instance, teams of robots need to decide themselves how to explore distinct regions
059   by simultaneously minimizing energy consumption and travel time. In comparison to the MORL
060   problem with single-agent, the multi-objective multi-agent problem (MOMARL) is more intricate
061   as it encompasses not only potential conflicts among different objectives but also interactions be-
062   tween the distributed agents with limited communication. An intuitive approach to the MOMARL
063   problem is to consider it as a MORL problem with a single agent, where the state and action are rep-
064
      resented by the joint states and joint actions of all agents, respectively. However, as the number of
      agents increases, the size of their joint state-action space will exponentially grow. This characteristic
065
      renders the current algorithms used for solving MORL problems with a single agent in (Chen et al.,
066   2021; Zhou et al., 2024) unsuitable for large-scale scenarios with multi-agents. Consequently, the
067   MOMARL problem poses new challenges to the design of scalable algorithms and their theoretical
068   analysis.
069   This paper aims to address the following problem: How to develop a scalable algorithm for the
070   MOMARL problem and ensure its convergence to Pareto-stationary of the multi-objective function?
071   The contributions of this paper are described as follows.
072
      (i) In order to improve the scalability of the algorithm and avoid using the global state-action, we
073   design a novel graph-truncated Q-function approximation for each agent i, which only requires the
074   neighborhood state-action (sNiκ , aNiκ ) of its κ-hop neighbors, instead of the global state-action. Ad-
075   ditionally, we introduce a new concept of action-averaged Q-function and establish the equivalence
076   between using the graph-truncated Q-function and action-averaged Q-function for policy gradient
077   approximation.
078   (ii) Based on the concept of action-averaged Q-function, we propose a distributed scalable actor-
079   critic algorithm for the MOMARL problem. In critic step, we use linear function to approximate the
080   action-averaged Q-function, which further reduces the dimension of state-action to (sNiκ , ai ) with
081   local action. In addition, we use the multi-gradient descent method in actor step to update the policy
082   parameter for finding a Pareto-stationary solution.
083   (iii) We prove that the proposed scalable algorithm for MOMARL successfully converges to the
084   Pareto-stationary solution at rate O(1/T ) that is inversely proportional to time domain T . Moreover,
085   we run simulations in a robot path planning environment and show our algorithm converges to
086   greater multi-objective values as compared to the latest MORL algorithm (Zhou et al., 2024), and
087   performs close to the central optimum with much shorter running time.

     534                 In this paper, we proposed a distributed scalable actor-critic algorithm for the MOMARL problem
     535                 and proved that this algorithm reaches a close-to-Pareto-stationary point of J (θ). In the proposed
                         algorithm, each agent only requires state-action information (sNiκ , ai ), which can effectively im-
     536
                         prove the scalability of the algorithm. The underlying framework of distributed scalable actor-critic
     537
                         algorithm, which includes the graph-truncated Q-function (12) and the action-averaged Q-function
     538                 (16), constitutes a significant contribution in its own right and has the potential to pave the way for
     539                 other scalable reinforcement learning methods in networked systems.
