009                                                       A BSTRACT
010
011            We consider the distributed learning problem with data dispersed across multiple workers
012            under the orchestration of a central server. Asynchronous Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD)
013            has been widely explored in such a setting to reduce the synchronization overhead associated
014            with parallelization. However, prior works have shown that the performance of asynchronous
015            SGD algorithms depends on a bounded dissimilarity condition among the workers‚Äô local
016            data, a condition that can drastically affect their efficiency when the workers‚Äô data are highly
017
               heterogeneous. To overcome this limitation, we introduce the Incremental Aggregated Asyn-
               chronous SGD (IA2 SGD) algorithm. With a server-side buffer, IA2 SGD makes full use of stale
018
               stochastic gradients from all workers to neutralize the adverse effects of data heterogeneity. In
019
               an asynchronous implementation setting, the algorithm entails two distinct time lags in the
020            model parameters and data samples utilized in the server‚Äôs iterations. Furthermore, by adopting
021            an incremental aggregation strategy, IA2 SGD maintains a per-iteration computational cost
022            that is on par with traditional asynchronous SGD algorithms. Our analysis demonstrates that
023            IA2 SGD achieves a consistent convergence rate for smooth nonconvex problems for arbitrarily
024            heterogeneous data. Numerical experiments indicate that IA2 SGD compares favorably with
025            existing asynchronous and synchronous SGD-based algorithms.

      1    I NTRODUCTION
029
      In traditional machine learning, training often occurs on a single machine. This approach can be restrictive when
030
      handling large datasets or complex models that demand substantial computational resources. Distributed machine
031
      learning overcomes this constraint by utilizing multiple machines that work in parallel. This method distributes
032   the computational workload and data across several nodes or workers, enabling faster and more scalable training.
033
034   We focus on a common distributed machine learning paradigm, known as the data parallelism approach. In this
035
      setup, the training data are distributed among multiple workers, with each worker independently conducting
      computations on its local data. As an extension of stochastic gradient descent (SGD) used on a single machine,
036
      synchronous SGD (Cotter et al., 2011; Dekel et al., 2012; Chen et al., 2016; Goyal et al., 2017) stands as a
037
      prominent example of data-parallel training algorithms. In synchronous SGD, the server broadcasts the latest
038   model to all workers, who then simultaneously compute stochastic gradients using their respective datasets.
039   After local computation, these workers send their stochastic gradients back to the central server. The server then
040   aggregates these stochastic gradients and updates the model accordingly.
041
      However, variations in computation speeds and communication bandwidths across workers, typically due to
042
      the differences in hardware, are common. In synchronous SGD, this disparity forces all workers to wait for the
043
      slowest one to complete its computations before proceeding to the next iteration. This issue, often referred to as
044   the straggler effect, leads to significant idle times, severely limiting the efficiency and scalability of the approach.
045   To address this problem, asynchronous SGD (ASGD) algorithms have been extensively studied to mitigate the
046   synchronization overhead among workers. Since nodes operate independently, each can proceed at its own pace
047   without waiting for others. This attribute is especially beneficial in ad-hoc clusters or cloud environments where
048   hardware heterogeneity is prevalent (Assran et al., 2020).
049
      The primary challenge faced by asynchronous training is that its efficiency can be compromised by data
050   heterogeneity. This issue arises because fast workers are able to send more frequent updates to the server,
051   while slower workers participate less frequently. Consequently, the training process may become biased, as
052   the data from fast and slow workers are not equally represented in the server‚Äôs model updates. Recent research
053   efforts have addressed the problem of data heterogeneity in ASGD (Gao et al., 2021; Mishchenko et al., 2022;
054   Koloskova et al., 2022; Islamov et al., 2024). These studies focus on the convergence properties of ASGD

055
      under conditions where the dissimilarity of local objective functions is bounded. However, if the local datasets
056   are highly heterogeneous, leading to significant differences in local objective functions, then the convergence
057   performance of these algorithms can be substantially reduced.

059   1.1   O UR C ONTRIBUTIONS
060
061   The aim of this paper is to tackle the above limitations of existing ASGD algorithms in handling heterogeneous
062   data. Our main contributions are summarized as follows:
063
064
      1) We propose the incremental aggregated ASGD (IA2 SGD) algorithm for distributed training, with the following
         key features:
065
066       ‚ÄĘ IA2 SGD handles the data heterogeneity issue through using an aggregated stochastic gradients from all
067         workers, which are computed based on both stale models and stale data samples. This leads to a dual-
068
            delayed aggregated stochastic gradient at the server, contrasting sharply with existing ASGD algorithms
            that use stale models but fresh data samples for each iteration.
069
          ‚ÄĘ IA2 SGD can operate in a fully asynchronous manner, meaning that the server updates the model as soon
070
            as it receives a stochastic gradient from any worker, without the need to wait for other workers.
071
          ‚ÄĘ Although IA2 SGD requires aggregation of stochastic gradients from all workers in every iteration, it can be
072
            implemented incrementally by storing each worker‚Äôs latest aggregated stochastic gradient at a server-side
073
            buffer, ensuring a per-iteration computational cost comparable to existing ASGD algorithms.
074
075
      2) Through a careful analysis accounting for the time lags inherent in the dual-delayed system, we demonstrate
         that IA2 SGD achieves a sublinear convergence rate for general smooth but nonconvex optimization problems
076
         under mild assumptions. Our analysis does not depend on bounded function dissimilarity conditions,
077
         indicating that IA2 SGD can achieve rapid and consistent convergence on arbitrarily heterogeneous data.
078
079
      3) We perform experiments comparing IA2 SGD with other ASGD and aggregation-based algorithms in training
         deep neural networks on the CIFAR-10 dataset. We show that IA2 SGD delivers competitive runtime
080
         performance relative to asynchronous and synchronous SGD-based algorithms, validating its effectiveness
081
         and efficiency in practical applications.
082
083   To our best knowledge, the proposed IA2 SGD algorithm is among the first ASGD algorithms with guaranteed
084   exact convergence without the assumption of bounded function dissimilarity.

536   6                  C ONCLUSIONS
537
538   This paper introduces the Incremental Aggregated Asynchronous SGD (IA2 SGD), a novel approach to distributed
539   machine learning that effectively counteracts the challenges posed by data heterogeneity across workers. By
540
      leveraging an asynchronous mechanism that utilizes stale gradients from all workers, IA2 SGD not only alleviates
      synchronization overheads but also balances the contributions of diverse worker datasetsp to the learning process.
541
542
      Our comprehensive theoretical analysis shows that IA2 SGD achieves an O((1 + ŌÉ 2 ) ŌĄmax /nT ) convergence
      rate for solving nonconvex problems, regardless of variations in data distribution across workers. This significant
543
      advancement highlights the robustness and efficiency of IA2 SGD in handling highly heterogeneous data scenarios,
544
      which are prevalent in modern distributed environments. Experiments on real-world datasets validate its superior
545   runtime performance under high data heterogeneity in comparison to other leading distributed SGD-based
546   algorithms, thereby confirming its potential as an effective tool for large-scale machine learning tasks. While our
547   approach was to focus comprehensively on data heterogeneity, there can be room for improvement in reducing
548   the dependence of our convergence rate on maximum delay. Whether our algorithmic framework is able to
549   achieve both unbounded data heterogeneity and unbounded delay is worth further exploring.

735   Another line of research explores ASGD for homogeneous data without parameter servers (Recht et al., 2011;
736   De Sa et al., 2015; Mania et al., 2017), where multiple threads perform updates to the shared model parameters
737   simultaneously without explicit synchronization. This means that while one thread is updating certain parameters,
738   another thread can compute gradients and update other parameters concurrently. These algorithms operates
739
      most effectively when the data is sparse, so that concurrent updates are unlikely to interfere with each other

743   Asynchronous Federated Learning. Federated learning (FL) is an emerging distributed machine learning
744   paradigm that pays particular attention to data privacy and heterogeneity. Asynchronous federated learning
745
      algorithms (Xie et al., 2019; Chen et al., 2020; Nguyen et al., 2022; Zakerinia et al., 2022; Wang et al., 2023b;
