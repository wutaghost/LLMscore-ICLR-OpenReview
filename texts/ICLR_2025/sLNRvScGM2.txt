                                                    A BSTRACT
006
007
               Federated Learning faces significant challenges arising from two critical uncer-
008
               tainties: the validity of a client’s participation, which can be compromised by
009            network and system heterogeneity, and the utility of the data contributed by each
010            client, which varies due to heterogeneous statistical data. Traditional client se-
011            lection methods often treat these uncertainties as a whole, leading to suboptimal
012            performance. To address this issue, we propose FedSUV, an innovative client
013            selection framework that decouples validity and utility uncertainties. FedSUV
014            approaches client selection from a multi-objective optimization perspective, em-
015            ploying advanced bandit algorithms: a confidence bound-based linear contextual
016            bandit for assessing validity and a Gaussian Process bandit for evaluating utility.
017
               We validate the effectiveness of FedSUV through both theoretical analysis and
               large-scale experiments conducted within our physical cluster.

      1    I NTRODUCTION
021
022
      Federated learning (FL) is a distributed machine learning paradigm that trains models locally on
023
      multiple clients and only sends model updates to a central server for aggregation, thereby protect-
024
      ing data privacy Konečnỳ et al. (2015); McMahan et al. (2017). Fig. 2 shows the workflow of
025   FL and highlights several challenges in its practical deployment. One challenge is communication
026   limitations. The frequent exchange of model parameters between clients and the central server is
027   constrained by limited bandwidth and channel capacity Li et al. (2020b); Shi et al. (2023). To ad-
028   dress this issue, client selection—choosing a subset of clients to participate in the FL training—has
029   been widely considered an effective method for optimizing FL.
030
      Common barriers in client selection include network heterogeneity, system heterogeneity, and het-
031   erogeneous statistical data Ying et al. (2020); Tian et al. (2024). On one hand, local training on
032   certain clients is likely to fail due to system heterogeneity, such as hardware constraints and runtime
033   variance. Even if local training is successful, the model may fail to be transmitted during the ag-
034   gregation process due to network heterogeneity, such as poor channel conditions and high latency.
035   Consequently, a selected client may become an invalid participant, requiring additional communica-
036   tion rounds Abdelmoniem et al. (2023). We refer to the ability of a client to successfully participate
037   within one round as validity. On the other hand, the contribution of each client to the overall FL
038   system varies due to heterogeneous statistical data Yang et al. (2024), which we refer to as utility.
039
      Naturally, we aim to select clients with high utility in each round of FL. However, a client with
      high utility but low validity is not beneficial. The fundamental challenge is finding the right balance
040
      between validity and utility. Adding to the complexity, both these factors are inherently uncertain,
041
      highlighting the crucial need to adeptly address these uncertainties for optimal client selection.

      may amplify uncertainties rather than mitigate them Iqbal et al. (2023). Treating uncertainties as
055   a combined metric, whether by addition or multiplication, overlooks the complex dynamics intro-

061   In this paper, we propose FedSUV, a new and efficient framework for client selection in FL. As
062   illustrated in Fig. 1, FedSUV decouples the two uncertainties of validity and utility in FL, aiming
063   to explicitly explore their interaction. Selecting participants from numerous clients can be modeled
064   as a multi-armed bandit problem Lai et al. (2021). Consequently, FedSUV employs the confidence
065   bound-based linear contextual bandit algorithm Li et al. (2010); Shi et al. (2023) to predict the
066   expected validity of clients and applies the confidence bound-based Gaussian Process(GP) bandit
067   algorithm Krause & Ong (2011); Liu et al. (2019); Li et al. (2010) to estimate the expected utility
068   of clients. Leveraging these two estimates, FedSUV identifies the optimal subset of clients in each
069
      round. Specifically, it strives to select Pareto-optimal clients or clients near the Pareto-optimal front,
      drawing inspiration from the Pareto Learning Algorithm Zuluaga et al. (2013).
070
071   In summary, this paper makes the following contributions. First, we decouple the uncertainty fac-
072   tors in FL and address them from multi-objective MAB perspectives. Second, we introduce a novel
073   method that effectively balances validity and utility by employing a dual-model approach—training
074   both a linear model and a GP model to tackle the distinct uncertainties in FL. Furthermore, we es-
075
      tablish an analytical framework that demonstrates FedSUV’s performance, showing it effectively
      captures and reduces uncertainty and achieves a sublinear regret bound with high probability, ensur-
076
      ing rapid convergence from a theoretical standpoint. Meanwhile, FedSUV speeds up convergence
077
      by up to 3.54× and improves final accuracy by up to 30.54% in extensive cluster experiments.

080   2    R ELATED W ORK
081
082
      Fig. 2 illustrates a typical FL framework and highlights the challenges encountered in real-world
083
      implementations. Effective client selection is crucial to addressing these challenges. Client selection
084   methods can be broadly categorized into three approaches: heuristic-based selection, reinforcement
085   learning-based selection, and bandit-based selection.
086
087
      Traditional client selection methods predominantly rely on heuristics, evaluating factors such as
      data resources (e.g., FedCS Nishio & Yonetani (2019), MCFL Li et al. (2020a), Harmony Tian et al.
088
      (2022)) and distinct training processes (e.g., SAFA Wu et al. (2020), REFL Abdelmoniem et al.
089
      (2023)). However, heuristic-based approaches may lack robustness in new or unseen scenarios,
090   often requiring extensive domain expertise and tuning.
091
092   Reinforcement learning-based solutions, such as FAVOR Wang et al. (2020), FedMarl Zhang et al.
093
      (2022), and FedL Su et al. (2022), utilize reinforcement learning techniques for client selection.
      However, these methods can incur additional training time overhead due to the complexity of deep
094
      reinforcement learning and lack theoretical guarantees.
095
096   Bandit-based solutions present a promising alternative. For instance, Xia et al.2020 demonstrated
097   that MAB-based methods can achieve significantly lower training loss compared to various bench-
098   marks. Lai et al.2021 introduced a framework that prioritizes client participation based on the utility
099
      of existing data to enhance model accuracy and training speed, treating client selection as a standard
      MAB problem without incorporating contextual features. Huang et al.2022a proposed a contextual
100
      MAB-based client selection algorithm for mobile edge computing systems, considering computa-
101
      tion and communication times as context. Shi et al.2023 designed a client selection scheme based
102   on MAB for volatile FL, optimizing convergence rates. MAB-based methods offer theoretical guar-
103   antees and can be adapted for exploration, making them a valuable approach for reference. Collec-
104   tively, these approaches address FL uncertainties from a holistic perspective, yet they often overlook
105   the benefits of decoupling. In this paper, we leverage multi-objective MAB principles to enhance
106   client selection in FL. Our approach aims to decouple and balance two critical factors—validity
107   and utility—through a refined selection process, thereby improving the overall performance and
      efficiency of client selection.

      7     C ONCLUSIONS
481   FedSUV represents a significant advancement in FL by introducing a dual-model approach from
482   multi-objective MAB perspectives for client selection. By decoupling the uncertainties of validity
483   and utility, FedSUV effectively mitigates the impact of uncertainty. Meanwhile, it utilizes both linear
484   and GP models to intelligently capture and reduce these uncertainties, continuously identifying the
485   optimal client group. Theoretical guarantees and empirical validation of FedSUV demonstrate its
      superiority and potential to revolutionize client selection strategies in FL.
