009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            The field of image captioning has witnessed a surge in attention, particularly in
012            the context of e-commerce, where the exploration of automated fashion descrip-
013            tion has gained significant momentum. This growing interest can be attributed to
014            the increasing influence of visual language and its impact on effective commu-
015            nication within the fashion industry. However, generating detailed and accurate
016            natural language descriptions for fashion items remains a topic of intense discus-
017            sion. This paper introduces an innovative approach that specifically addresses this
018
               challenge. Our approach integrates a knowledge base into the widely adopted
               end-to-end architecture, thereby enhancing the availability of comprehensive data
019
               about fashion items. We design a mode mapping network that facilitates the fu-
020
               sion of attribute features extracted from the knowledge base with image features.
021            Additionally, we introduce a filter strategy to enhance the quality of the generated
022            descriptions by selecting the best result among the candidate sentences generated
023            through beam search using a language model. Through extensive experimenta-
024            tion and evaluation, our proposed method demonstrates superior performance in
025            the task of fashion description, surpassing the performance of state-of-the-art ap-
026            proaches in this domain.

029   1   I NTRODUCTION
030
031   The task of describing fashion items in the e-commerce domain differs from traditional image cap-
032   tioning tasks, as highlighted in previous works (Xu et al., 2015; Anderson et al., 2018; Huang et al.,
033
      2019), which primarily focus on generating descriptive sentences from a given image. This differ-
      ence stems from the unique requirements of fashion product descriptions. Not only should these
034
      descriptions accurately reflect the product’s intricate details and features, but also they should be
035
      vivid and illustrative, effectively capturing users’ attention while showcasing the distinctive charac-
036   teristics of the item.
037
038   With the rise of fashion networking platforms, there is a noticeable trend towards automating the
039
      product description process, especially when dealing with a large volume of images (Hacheme &
      Sayouti, 2021). However, existing models that are primarily designed for natural images have limi-
040
      tations when it comes to generating diverse and non-repetitive descriptions (Vinyals et al., 2015; Xu
041
      et al., 2015). These models often produce descriptions that follow a templated structure and lack the
042   desired level of diversity and comprehensive details. Consequently, effectively highlighting the key
043   elements and attributes depicted in the image becomes challenging.
044
045
      To address these challenges, we present a novel approach named KEIC (i.e. Knowledge Enhanced
      Image Captioning). Our approach aims to capture and represent the fine-grained features of products
046
      depicted in images, while also emphasizing key information during the caption generation process.
047
      We integrate a fine-grained knowledge base for products into the conventional end-to-end architec-
048   ture. This enhancement enriches the image encoding process and enables a more profound semantic
049   analysis of the images. The method enhances the overall representation of the images, leading to
050   more accurate and contextually relevant descriptions of the fashion products. We incorporate the
051   fused feature as a prefix to facilitate the generation of image descriptions. By leveraging this ap-
052   proach, it is anticipated that the limitations of existing models, such as the lack of diversity and
053   comprehensive details, can be overcome, resulting in more accurate and compellingly product de-
      scriptions in the e-commerce context.

069   2   R ELATED W ORK
070
071   In recent years, significant research efforts have been dedicated to the field of image captioning (Hos-
072   sain et al., 2019). Initially, a common approach involved generating simple template sentences that
073   were then populated with the outputs of object detectors or attribute predictors (Farhadi et al., 2010).
074   However, with the emergence of deep learning, captioning methods started adopting recurrent neu-
075
      ral networks as language models, leveraging the outputs of convolutional neural networks (CNN) to
      encode and guide the generation of captions (Kiros et al., 2014). Building upon this foundation, Xu
076
      et al. (Xu et al., 2015) introduced an attention mechanism that operates on the spatial output grid of
077
      the convolutional layer. This mechanism enabled the model to influence the generation process by
078   selectively attending to specific elements in the grid while generating each word. By dynamically
079   attending to different regions of the image, the model could generate more contextually relevant and
080   detailed descriptions.
081
      The method of Anderson et al. (Anderson et al., 2018) combines bottom-up Faster R-CNN object
082
      detection and top-down weighted region prediction, which enables the model to generate dense de-
083
      tection sets and enhance feature representations through auxiliary training on the visual genome
084   dataset. The reticulation decoder designed by Cornia et al. (Cornia et al., 2020) contains a reticula-
085   tion operator that independently regulates the contributions of all coding layers and a gate mecha-
086   nism that weights these contributions under the guidance of textual queries to exploit the information
087   of all coding layers. They consider all the encoding layer information in order to better meet the
088   requirements of text queries.
089
      Huang et al. (Huang et al., 2019) proposed an extended attention operator to refine visual features
090   by weighting the final attention information through a context-guided gate mechanism, where the
091   output of self-attention is multiplied with the query join computing information and the gate vector.
092   In recent years, e-commerce has attracted more and more attention, and many scholars have launched
093   attempts in the field of e-commerce to provide different solutions. Han et al. (Han et al., 2022)
094   proposed fashion-centric visual and language (V+L) representation learning framework FashionViL,
095   but there are still challenges in fine-grained cross-modal alignment.
096   Zhu et al. (Zhu et al., 2021) proposed a new method, K3M, which designed a structural aggre-
097   gation module to integrate information from image, text, and knowledge modalities. Moratelli et
098   al. (Moratelli et al., 2023) proposed grid relational Self-attention (GSA) and Gated Augmented
099   decoder (GED) to strengthen visual representation and dynamically measure the contribution of dif-
100   ferent views to the target word and applied it to a Transformer model for the fashion item captions
101   task. However, these methods mainly focus on entities in text modality and are still insufficient for
102   cross-modal interaction.
103   The object detection approach proposed by Li et al. (Li et al., 2020) focuses on identifying specific
104   objects within an image, which is useful for general image understanding but still does not address
105   the specific requirements of e-commerce tasks. Similarly, scene graph parsing, as introduced by
106   Cui et al. (Cui et al., 2021), aims to extract structured representations of objects and relationships in
107   an image. While these techniques consider fine-grained information has shown promise in various
      domains, their effectiveness in e-commerce tasks appears to be limited.

108
      Considering the recent advancements in multimodal large-scale models for visual language under-
109   standing, a prominent approach entails leveraging pre-trained models and fine-tuning them using
110   e-commerce data. This approach has demonstrated promising results in various studies (Mirchan-
111   dani et al., 2022; Zhuge et al., 2021; Wang et al., 2022).
112
      Clipcap is an innovative approach that shows great potential in the field of image captioning.
113
      It capitalizes on the utilization of pre-trained models CLIP (Contrastive Language-Image Pre-
114
      Training) (Radford et al., 2021) and introduces a streamlined architecture to generate captions for
115   images. The key idea behind this approach is to leverage the existing semantic understanding en-
116   coded in pre-trained models and adapt it to the style of the target dataset, rather than learning entirely
117   new semantic entities (Mokady et al., 2021). Although Clipcap demonstrates great effectiveness in
118   image captioning tasks, its performance in fine-grained image tasks is comparatively subpar in prac-
119   tical applications (Zhong et al., 2022).
120
      In the context of e-commerce, the challenges go beyond mere object detection and scene understand-
121   ing. The goal is to generate comprehensive and accurate descriptions that capture the fine-grained
122   details and relevant attributes of the products. Therefore, addressing the unique characteristics of
123   e-commerce data, such as product-centric information and the importance of relevant attributes,
124   are essential for achieving satisfactory results in e-commerce image captioning tasks (Wang et al.,
125   2024).

224   and corresponding attribute tag features can be perfectly aligned and smoothly inputted into the

226   product features and modality transformation.

327   4.1.3     E XPERIMENTAL PARAMETERS
328
329   When we build the product attribute knowledge base, we extracted 14, 920 noun phrases from the
330   Fashion-Gen dataset as attribute tags. We set k to 5 to select the top-5 attribute tags. To extract
331   the attribute tags, we use cosine similarity as the similar measure to rank the attribute tags in the
332
      knowledge base, and we set λ = 0.5 to optimal balance diversity and relevance.
333   The remaining configuration parameters were defined as follows. The temperature parameter, which
334   controls the randomness of the generated text, was set to 1. A higher temperature value allows for
335   more diverse and exploratory outputs. For the top-k filtering technique, the parameter k was set to
336   10. Similarly, for the top-p filtering technique, the parameter p was set to 0.7. During the training
337   phase, the number of epochs was set to 80. The batch size was set to 40. We set the prefix length
338
      l = 10 for the concatenated embedded matrix learned from the mapping network. For optimization,
      we use AdamW (Kingma & Ba, 2015) with learning rate 0.00002 and 5000 steps to warm up.
339
340   All comparisons are conducted under the same experimental environment, and all training and in-
341   ference tasks are completed on a single NVIDIA A4000 server GPU to evaluate the performance of
342   the models.

474   5   C ONCLUSIONS
475
476
477
      Through this research, we develop a model called KEIC (i.e. knowledge enhanced image caption-
      ing) that can effectively learn the relationship between images and their corresponding captions and
478
      generate high-quality descriptions for fashion products. By incorporating additional knowledge into
479
      the image captioning task, KEIC enhances the model’s ability to generate more accurate and infor-
480   mative captions. Through extensive experiments and evaluations, we demonstrate the effectiveness
481   of KEIC in generating meaningful and contextually relevant captions for product images. The results
482   highlight the advantages of incorporating domain knowledge and fine-grained features in the image
483   captioning task, showcasing the potential of our approach to improve the performance and accuracy
484   of image captioning systems. In future work, we aim to delve deeper into the task of fine-grained la-
485   bel extraction for product images, focusing on accurately identifying unique geographical indicators
      for different products in the e-commerce domain and generating corresponding descriptions.
