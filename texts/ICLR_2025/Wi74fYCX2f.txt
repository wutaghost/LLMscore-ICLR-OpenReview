009                                                A BSTRACT
010
011            Diffusion or score-based models recently showed high performance in image gen-
012            eration. They rely on a forward and a backward stochastic differential equations
013            (SDE). The sampling of a data distribution is achieved by solving numerically the
014            backward SDE or its associated flow ODE. Studying the convergence of these
015            models necessitates to control four different types of error: the initialization error,
016            the truncation error, the discretization and the score approximation. In this paper,
017            we study theoretically the behavior of diffusion models and their numerical im-
018
               plementation when the data distribution is Gaussian. In this restricted framework
               where the score function is a linear operator, we derive the analytical solutions of
019
               the backward SDE and the probability flow ODE. We prove that these solutions
020
               and their discretizations are all Gaussian processes, which allows us to compute
021            exact Wasserstein errors induced by each error type for any sampling scheme.
022            Monitoring convergence directly in the data space instead of relying on Inception
023            features, our experiments show that the recommended numerical schemes from
024            the diffusion models literature are also the best sampling schemes for Gaussian
025            distributions.

028   1    I NTRODUCTION
029
030   Over the last five years, diffusion models have proven to be a highly efficient and reliable framework
031   for generative modeling (Song & Ermon, 2019; Ho et al., 2020; Song et al., 2021a;b; Dhariwal
032   & Nichol, 2021; Karras et al., 2022). First introduced as a discrete process, Denoising Diffusion
033
      Probabilistic Models (DDPM) (Ho et al., 2020) can be studied as a reversal of a continuous Stochastic
      Differential Equation (SDE) (Song et al., 2021b). A forward SDE progressively transforms the initial
034
      data distribution by adding more and more noise as time grows. Then, the reversal of this process,
035
      called backward SDE, allows us to approximately sample the data distribution starting from Gaussian
036   white noise. Moreover, the SDE is associated with an Ordinary Differential Equations (ODE) called
037   probability flow (Song et al., 2021b). This flow preserves the same marginal distributions as the
038   backward SDE and provides another way to sample the score-based generative model.
039
      An important issue about diffusion models is the theoretical guarantees of convergence of the model:
040
      How close to the data distribution the generated distribution is? There are four main sources of
041
      errors to study for deriving theoretical guarantees for diffusion models: (a) the initialization error is
042   induced when approximating the marginal distribution at the end of the forward process by a standard
043   Gaussian distribution. (b) The discretization error comes from the resolution of the SDE or the ODE
044   by a numerical method. (c) The truncation error occurs because the backward time integration is
045   stopped at a small time ϵ > 0 to avoid numerical instabilities due to ill-defined score function near
046   the origin. (d) The score approximation error accounts for the mismatch between the ideal score
047   function and the one given by the network trained using denoising score-matching.
048   Despite these numerous sources of errors, a lot of numerical and theoretical research has been led
049   to assess the generative capacity of diffusion models. Several articles (Franzese et al., 2023; Karras
050   et al., 2022) provide strong experimental studies for the choices of sampling parameters. On the
051   theoretical side, several works derive upper bounds on the 1-Wasserstein or TV distance between
052   the data and the model distributions by making assumptions on the L2 -error between the ideal and
053   learned score functions and on the compacity of the support of the data (Chen et al., 2023b; Lee et al.,
      2024; De Bortoli et al., 2021; Chen et al., 2023c; Lee et al., 2022; Benton et al., 2024), eventually


                                                         1

054
      under an additional manifold assumption (De Bortoli, 2022; Wenliang & Moran, 2022; Chen et al.,
055   2023a). Yet, on one hand, to the best of our knowledge, the derived theoretical bounds mostly rely on
056   worst case scenario and are not tight enough to explain the practical efficiency of diffusion models.
057   On the other hand, numerical considerations mostly rely on Inception feature distributions through
058   the FID metric (Heusel et al., 2017).
059
      Ideally, given a data distribution of interest, one would like to have an adapted estimation of the
060
      discrepancy between the data and the diffusion model samples, thus enabling adaptive hyperparameter
061   selection for the sampling procedure. As a first step towards reaching this goal, in the present work
062   we study diffusion models applied to Gaussian data distributions. While this setting has a priori no
063   practical interest, since simulating Gaussian variates does not require a diffusion model, it provides a
064   large parametric family of distributions for which the errors involved in diffusion model sampling
065   can be completely understood.
066
      When restricting the data distribution to be Gaussian, the resulting score function is a simple linear
067   operator. Exploiting this specificity allows us to derive the following contributions under the
068   assumption that the data distribution is Gaussian:
069
070          • We give the exact solutions for both the backward SDE and the probability flow ODE.
071          • We fully describe the Gaussian processes that occur when using classical sampling dis-
072            cretization schemes.
073
             • We derive exact 2-Wasserstein errors for the corresponding sample distributions and are able
074
               to assert for the influence of each error type on these errors, as illustrated by Figure 1.
075
076   Our theoretical study allows for an analytical evaluation of any numerical sampler, either stochastic
077   or deterministic. In particular, it confirms the strength of best practice scheme such as Heun’s method
078   for the ODE flow (Karras et al., 2022). We provide our source code that can be applied to any
079   Gaussian data distribution of interest and gives insight to calibrate parameters of a diffusion sampling
080   algorithm, e.g. by straightforwardly generalizing our study to higher order linear numerical schemes.
081   While our theoretical analysis relies on an exactly known score function, we conduct additional
082   experiments to assess for the influence of the score approximation error. Surprisingly, in the context
083   of texture synthesis, we show that with a score neural network trained for modeling a specific
084   Gaussian micro-texture a stochastic Euler-Maruyama sampler is more faithful to the data distribution
085   than Heun’s method, thus highlighting the importance of the score approximation error in practical
086   situations.
087   Plan of the paper: First, we recall in Section 2 the continuous framework for SDE-based diffusion
088   models. Section 3 presents our main theoretical results detailing the exact backward SDE and
089   probability flow ODE solutions when supposing the data distribution to be Gaussian. Section 4
090   gives explicit Wasserstein error formulas when sampling the corresponding processes, yielding to
091   an ablation study for comparing the influence of each error type on several sampling schemes. In
092   Section 5, we study numerically a special case of Gaussian distribution for texture synthesis in
093   order to evaluate the influence of the score approximation error occurring with a standard network
094
      architecture. Finally, we address discussion and limitations of our framework in Section 6.

251   these works discuss the mismatch between the OT map and the initialization of y0 . Our Proposition 3

416

      6       D ISCUSSION AND LIMITATIONS
511
512
      The main limitation of our work is that our results are limited to Gaussian distributions. Resorting
513   to diffusion models for sampling Gaussian distributions is not necessary in practice, rather we use
514   Gaussian distributions as a test case family to provide insight on diffusion models.
515
516
      A natural extension of this work is to compute error types for more complex distributions (e.g
      multimodal) such as Gaussian mixtures models (GMM). However, generalizing our results for these
517
      more complex distributions one faces two main difficulties. First, to the best of our knowledge, we
518
      are unable to derive exact solutions to the backward SDE or the flow ODE under GMM assumption,
519   even though the score has a known analytical expression (Zach et al., 2024; 2023; Shah et al., 2023).
520   Another key feature of this study is to evaluate exactly the Wasserstein error by using Equation (19),
521   strongly relying on the Gaussian assumption. A closed-form of the Wasserstein distance between
522   two GMMs is not known, leading to alternative distance definitions for such models (Delon &
523   Desolneux, 2020). Hence, to compare the distributions generated in practice with exact solutions of
524   time continuous equations under GMM assumption, as we do for the Gaussian case, one should solve
525   two open theoretical problems.
526
527   7       C ONCLUSION
528
529   By restricting the analysis of diffusion models to the specific case of Gaussian distributions, we
530   were able to derive exact solutions for both the backward SDE and its associated probability flow
531   ODE. We demonstrate that regarding the initialization error, the SDE sampler is more resilient than
532   the ODE sampler for Gaussian distributions. Additionally, we characterized the discrete Gaussian
533   processes arising when discretizing these equations. This allowed us to provide exact Wasserstein
534   errors for the initialization error, the discretization error, and the truncation error as well as any of
535   their combinations. This theoretical analysis led to conclude that Heun’s scheme is the best method
536
      out of the four considered schemes, in accordance with empirical previous work (Karras et al., 2022).
537   To conclude our work we conducted an empirical analysis with a learned score function using standard
538   architecture which showed that the score approximation error may be the most important one in
539   practice. This suggests that assessing the quality of learned score functions is an important research
      direction for future work.
