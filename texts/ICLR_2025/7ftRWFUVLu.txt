011                                               A BSTRACT
012
013            Convolutional neural networks (CNNs) and vision Transformers (ViTs) play key
014            roles in few-shot fine-grained image classification (FSFGIC). One of the main chal-
015            lenges of FSFGIC is how to consistently learn high-quality feature representations
016            from different very limited fine-grained datasets. CNNs struggle with long-range
017            dependencies due to their inherent localized receptive fields, and ViTs might impair
018
               high-frequency information, e.g., local texture information. Furthermore, ViTs
               require a large number of training samples to infer feature properties such as
019
               translation invariance, locality, and the hierarchy of visual data, while FSFGIC’s
020
               training samples are extremely limited. To address the problems mentioned, a
021            new lightweight Transformer guided by features from multiple receptive fields
022            (LT-FMRF) is proposed which has considered how to manage long-range depen-
023            dencies and how to extract local features with multiple scales, global features, and
024            fused features from input images for increasing inter-class differences and consis-
025            tently obtaining high-quality feature representations from different types of limited
026            training datasets. Furthermore, the proposed LT-FMRF can be easily embedded
027            into a given few-shot episodic training mechanism for end-to-end training from
028            scratch. Experimental results conducted on five widely used FSFGIC datasets
029
               consistently show significant improvements over twenty state-of-the-art end-to-end
               training-based methods.

033   1   I NTRODUCTION
034
035   Few-shot fine-grained image classification (FSFGIC) (Zhang et al., 2024) aims to use a limited
036   number of training samples to train a network for accurately classifying images (e.g., with birds (Wah
037   et al., 2011)) belonging to subordinate object categories of the same entry-level category. Existing
038   FSFGIC methods can be roughly classified into two groups (Zhang et al., 2021): meta learning-based
039
      and metric learning-based FSFGIC methods. Meta learning-based methods aim to enable a given
      model to learn quickly and obtain good generalization performance when adapting to new tasks in a
040
      scenario with limited data by optimizing model parameters or learning strategies. Metric learning-
041
      based methods use metric functions such as cosine distance or Euclidean distance to determine the
042   category of samples based on the similarity between different samples.
043
044   Convolutional neural networks (CNNs) and vision Transformers (ViTs) play a key role in FSFGIC.
045
      Although CNNs have the capability to obtain local feature information from images well, they
      have difficulties handling long-range dependencies due to their inherent localised receptive fields.
046
      Transformers have the capability to effectively capture low-frequency signals from images, i.e., global
047
      feature information (e.g., global shapes and structures). However, Transformers (Park & Kim, 2022)
048   might also impair high-frequency information (e.g., local textures information) to a certain extent.
049   Currently, pre-trained Transformers or some branches of Transformers (e.g., encoder or decoder)
050   have been widely employed in FSFGIC for improving classification accuracy. It was indicated in (Liu
051   et al., 2021) that existing pre-trained Transformer models (Sun et al., 2022; Dosovitskiy et al., 2021)
052   for FSFGIC require significantly more training data compared to CNNs to infer feature properties
053   such as translation invariance, locality, and the hierarchy of visual data, for FSFGIC tasks where
      the training samples are extremely limited. These give us the following inspiration: With limited


                                                        1
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


054
      training samples and in a given few-shot scenario for a training mechanism, how to train a lightweight
055   Transformer model end-to-end from scratch to perform FSFGIC tasks well?
056
057   In this work, a new lightweight Transformer network (LT-Net) is proposed for addressing the
058
      aforementioned problems. The designed LT-Net in this paper is mainly based on the following
      two considerations: (1) Vision Transformers (ViTs) have the capability to capture dependencies
059
      between image patches and capture long-range global pattern information well. However, due to
060
      the lack of convolutional inductive biases (Liu et al., 2021), ViTs rely more on large-scale image
061   datasets than CNNs. (2) In the human visual system (Bullier, 2001; Kauffmann et al., 2014),
062   information from different frequency bands is indispensable and is fused in some ways to extract
063   more important and unique features. In this manner, a new lightweight Transformer guided by
064   features from multiple receptive fields (LT-FMRF) is proposed which has considered how to manage
065   long-range dependencies and how to extract local features with multiple scales, global features, and
066   fused features from input images for increasing inter-class differences, and consistently obtaining
067   high-quality feature representations from different training datasets. Furthermore, with limited
068   training samples, the proposed LT-FMRF can be easily embedded into a given few-shot episodic
069
      training mechanism for end-to-end training from scratch. Extensive experiments on five benchmark
      datasets (i.e., CUB-200-2011 (Wah et al., 2011), Stanford Dogs (Khosla et al., 2011), Stanford
070
      Cars (Krause et al., 2013), meta-iNat (Van Horn et al., 2018; Wertheimer & Hariharan, 2019), and
071
      tiered meta-iNat dataset (Van Horn et al., 2018; Wertheimer & Hariharan, 2019)) demonstrate the
072   effectiveness and superiority of the proposed LT-FMRF over twenty state-of-the-art end-to-end
073   training-based methods.

      2.1   M ETA LEARNING - BASED FSFGIC
084
085   The core concept of meta-learning is “learning to learn”. Attention mechanisms were widely used in
086   meta learning-based FSFGIC methods which aim to learn salient feature representations from input
087
      images. In the work of (Ruan et al., 2021), a spatial attention comparison network was proposed
      which contains a feature selective comparison module to fuse multi-scale feature maps of support
088
      and query images by arranging different weights pixel by pixel. Wang et al. (2024) introduced a
089
      dual-channel attention meta-learning architecture which contains an embedding module and a feature
090   calibration module for addressing the problem that traditional FSFGIC methods indiscriminately
091   obtain semantic feature information from each part of the input image.
092
093
      Alternately, feature alignment techniques have been proposed, which aim to align the spatial locations
      of objects between support images and query images to capture subtle differences. In (Song et al.,
094
      2024), a feature disentanglement alignment network was introduced that aims to enhance the model’s
095
      generalizability by maintaining the consistency of extraneous features throughout the fine-tuning
096   process. In the work of (Leng et al., 2024), a feature distribution alignment architecture was proposed,
097   which takes into account the differences in feature distributions between tasks that are ignored by the
098   current meta-learning based methods, and then the Kullback-Leibler divergence method is used to
099   improve the similarity of the extracted feature distributions.
100
      In addition to the above methods, knowledge distillation techniques were proposed to improve
101
      learning efficiency and generalization ability by transferring knowledge from complex models to
102   simplified models. In (Wu et al., 2023), a task-specific meta-distillation was presented in which the
103   teacher and student models are trained simultaneously in the procedure of meta-learning. Then in the
104   validation process, the teacher model is first fine-tuned, and the adjusted teacher model guides the
105   adjustment of the student model. Peng et al. (2024) proposed a semantic-guided visual adaptation
106   architecture which intends to extend the vision-language pre-trained model by integrating implicit
107   knowledge distillation, vision-specific contrastive loss, and cross-modal contrastive loss to generate
      discriminative and adaptive visual features.

      2.2   M ETRIC L EARNING - BASED FSFGIC
109
110
      Attention mechanisms were also widely employed in metric learning-based FSFGIC algorithms which
111
      have the capability to provide effective feature weighting strategies for metric learning, enabling a
112
      model to process complex data more effectively, thereby improving the classification performance
113   and generalization ability of the model. In (Tang et al., 2022), used a combination of multi-scale
114   feature pyramids and multi-level attention pyramids to enhance the internal representation of features
115   and reduce the uncertainty caused by the background mediated by limited samples. In (Li et al.,
116   2023b), visual self-attention mechanisms are used to to infer local feature relationships, model
117   spatial long-distance dependencies, estimate representative prototypes, and develop discriminative
118   prototype-query pairs.
119   Feature alignment techniques have been introduced for learning feature embedding. A background
120   suppression and foreground alignment network (Zha et al., 2023) was presented which aims to
121   suppress the background content of images and align the foreground of support and query image pairs.
122   Ma et al. (2024) proposed a cross-layer and cross-sample feature optimization network (C2-Net)
123   which integrates feature maps from multiple network layers and improves the matching results
124   between query features and support samples by adjusting the query features from both channel and
125   spatial perspectives.
126   Feature reconstruction techniques have also been widely applied in metric learning-based FSFGIC
127   tasks. In (Wertheimer et al., 2021), a feature map reconstruction network (FRN) was introduced
128   which reconstructs query features directly from support features by ridge regression in closed form.
129   Li et al. (2024) argued that existing reconstruction methods do not address the overfitting problem
130   due to the scarcity of samples during training. In Li et al. (2024), a self-reconstruction metric module
131   and a constrained cross-entropy loss based on FRN (Wertheimer et al., 2021) were proposed. In the
132   work of (Li et al., 2023a), a local content enrichment cross reconstruction network (LCCRN) was
133
      proposed, in which a local content enrichment module was designed to learn discriminative local
      feature representations and a cross reconstruction module was introduced to combine these local
134
      features with the appearance details obtained from a separate embedding module to enhance the
135
      semantic understanding of the network.
136
137
138
      2.3   T RANSFORMERS FOR FSFGIC
139
140
      In (Wang et al., 2023), the encoder, decoder, and cross-attention in the Transformer architec-
141
      ture (Vaswani et al., 2017) were utilized to model the support image representation, query image
142
      representation, and metric learning for performing different FSFGIC tasks. Huang & Choi (2023) pre-
143   sented a self-attention-based prototype enhancement network that integrates discriminative features
144   with channel information to obtain representative class prototypes for addressing feature redundancy
145   in prototype networks. In (Wu et al., 2024), a self-attention module (Vaswani et al., 2017) was
146   employed to reconstruct the query set from the support set for increasing inter-class difference and
147   the support set was reconstructed from the query set for reducing intra-class difference.
148
      Alternatively, pre-trained Transformers were also widely utilized in FSFGIC. In the work of (Sun
149   et al., 2022), global and local feature interaction based on a pre-trained vision Transformer, named
150   GL-ViT, was proposed to mine few-shot feature attributes. To address the overfitting problem caused
151   by insufficient data, He et al. (2022) introduced a hierarchical cascaded Transformer that leverages
152   intrinsic image structures through spectral token pooling and optimizes learnable parameters via
153   latent attribute surrogates. Hao et al. (2023) proposed a class-aware patch embedding adaptation
154   (CPEA) network which aims to remove the noise of single-label annotations and avoid supervision
155   collapse without aligning semantically related regions.

      3.1        P ROBLEM S TATEMENT
163
164   A typical FSFGIC setting contains a support set S and a query set Q. Support set S contains N
165   different image classes and each class in N is composed of K labeled samples. Query set Q is
166   composed of unlabeled samples. Set S and set Q have the same data-label space. The goal of
167   FSFGIC is to train a model which has the ability to classify each query sample q (q ∈ Q) into its
168   corresponding class in N . Thus, the FSFGIC task is called a N -way K-shot task (Vinyals et al.,
169   2016).

308   We conduct experiments in the 5-way 1-shot and 5-way 5-shot FSFGIC settings on the above five
309   datasets. All experiments in this work are conducted using the PyTorch framework on 2 NVIDIA 3090
310   Ti GPUs through data parallelism. ResNet-12 and Conv-4 are selected as the backbones for obtaining

      after 400 iterations. We employed standard data augmentation techniques, including random crop,

314
      validates the average accuracy of 10,000 randomly generated tasks for obtaining the top-1 mean
315
      classification accuracy results under the standard 5-way 1-shot and 5-way 5-shot settings. Meanwhile,
316   the 95% confidence intervals are obtained and reported.

526   the LT-FMRF framework. Together, they effectively capture both global and local feature information,
527   manage long-range dependencies, and amplify the differences between different types of features,

531   5     C ONCLUSION
532
533
      In this paper, a new lightweight Transformer guided by features from multiple receptive fields (LT-
534   FMRF) is proposed for FSFGIC. The designed LT-FMRF has the capability to manage long-range
535   dependencies and extract local features with multiple scales, global features, and fused features from
536   input images for increasing inter-class differences and consistently obtaining high-quality feature
537   representations from different types of limited training datasets. Furthermore, the proposed LT-FMRF
538   can be easily embedded into any given few-shot episodic training mechanisms for end-to-end training
539   from scratch. Experimental results conducted on five widely used FSFGIC datasets consistently show
      significant improvements over twenty state-of-the-art end-to-end training-based methods.
