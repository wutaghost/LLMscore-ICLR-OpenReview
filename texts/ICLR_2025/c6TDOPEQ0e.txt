009                                                A BSTRACT
010
011            Linear sequence modeling approaches, such as linear attention (Katharopoulos
012            et al., 2020), provide advantages like linear-time training and constant-memory
013            inference over sequence lengths. However, existing sequence parallelism (SP) meth-
014            ods are either not optimized for their right-product-first feature or use ring-style
015            communication as in LASP (Sun et al., 2024), which results in lower computation
016
               parallelism, limits their scalability for longer sequences in distributed systems. In
               this paper, we introduce LASP-2, a new SP approach designed to enhance both
017
               communication and computation efficiency in linear (attention) transformer models
018
               with very-long input sequences. Compared to LASP, LASP-2 rethinks the mini-
019            mal communication requirement for SP on linear attention, reorganizes the whole
020            communication-computation order of LASP. In this way, only one single all-gather
021            collective communication is needed on intermediate memory states, whose sizes
022            are independent of the sequence length, leading to significant improvements of
023            both communication and computation parallelism, as well as their overlap. Ad-
024            ditionally, we extend LASP-2 to LASP-2H by applying similar communication
025            redesign to standard attention modules, offering an efficient SP solution for hybrid
026            models that combine linear and standard attention layers. Our evaluation on a
027
               Linear-Llama3 model, a variant of Llama3 with linear attention replacing standard
               attention, demonstrates the effectiveness of LASP-2 and LASP-2H. Specifically,
028
               LASP-2 achieves throughput improvements of 15.2% over LASP and 36.6% over
029
               Ring Attention, with a sequence length of 2048K across 64 GPUs.

032   1    I NTRODUCTION
033
034   Transformer, originally introduced by Vaswani et al. (Vaswani et al., 2017), has become the back-
035   bone of modern models across a wide range of domains, including language, vision, audio, video,
036   graphs, and even time-series data. Although the Transformer dates back to 2017, its adaptability and
037   robustness have made it indispensable for a variety of tasks. Central to its success is the self-attention
038   mechanism, which is highly effective for sequence modeling, but has quadratic complexity (w.r.t.
039
      sequence length), leading to significant computational costs during training. However, the ability to
      handle long-context sequences is crucial for large model applications, not only for language tasks but
040
      also for multi-modal tasks, where sequences naturally tend to be long (Xue et al., 2024). FlashAtten-
041
      tion series (Dao et al., 2022; Dao, 2023; Shah et al., 2024) have provided substantial advancements
042   in scaling attention to handle longer sequences by optimizing the CUDA-level computations for
043   better hardware utilization. However, the theoretical complexity of FlashAttention remains quadratic.
044   Moreover, the need to maintain the KV cache presents further difficulties in managing memory as the
045   sequence length extends (Yang et al., 2024). As a result, long-sequence processing in Transformer
046   models continues to be a complex and resource-intensive problem.
047
      Recently, numerous variations of attention have been proposed, primarily aimed at addressing its
048   quadratic computational and memory complexity, as well as the growing size of the KV cache (Peng
049   et al., 2023; 2024). One promising approach line is linear attention (Katharopoulos et al., 2020),
050   which replaces the exponential kernel in softmax attention with a simpler dot product between key
051   and query vectors. This shift allows linear attention to be structured as a linear recurrent neural
052   network (RNN) with matrix-valued hidden states, thereby eliminating the need for a KV cache. In
053   consequence, it supports constant-memory inference and reduces training complexity from quadratic
      to linear (Yang et al., 2023). A parallel line of research focuses on State Space Models (SSMs), such

055   control theory. Both linear attention and SSMs share a common recurrent formulation, expressed as

059   et al., 2024), Needle In A Haystack (Briakou et al., 2023)) and long-context reasoning. Empirical
060   research (Lieber et al., 2024; Ren et al., 2024; Waleffe et al., 2024) has shown that models relying
061   solely on linear sequence modeling struggle to excel in these domains. However, a hybrid architecture
062   combining linear sequence modeling layers with standard transformer layers has been demonstrated
063   to significantly enhance model performance on tasks that are recall-intensive.
064   Sequence Parallelism (SP) techniques (Korthikanti et al., 2022; Jacobs et al., 2023; Liu et al., 2023)
065   are commonly employed to partition long sequences into smaller sub-sequences, allowing them to
066   be processed across multiple GPUs in parallel. Despite the advantages offered by SP for handling
067   large sequences, current SP methods do not fully exploit the right-product-first feature of linear
068   attention, which can lead to inefficiencies in parallelism and communication. LASP (Sun et al.,
069   2024) (referred to as LASP-1) introduced a SP approach specifically tailored for linear attention,
070   that uses a point-to-point (P2P) communication strategy. In this method, intermediate states are
071
      transferred across GPUs in a ring-style pattern within the distributed world. However, although such
      P2P ring-style communication offers certain benefits, part of its computation has to be executed
072
      sequentially, which leads low computation parallelism. In addition, too many small P2P operators
073
      make the overlapping of communication and computation difficult.
074
075   In this paper, we introduce LASP-2 by rethinking the minimal communication requirement involved
076   in SP of linear attention. Specifically, we innovatively reorganize the whole computation and
077
      communication procedure with an optimized execution order. In this way, only a single all-gather
      collective communication is needed in the forward or backward of each iteration. These bring both
078
      communication and computation efficiency improvements: 1) the size of the intermediate memory
079
      state tensor the all-gather operator works on is independent of the sequence length, making the
080   communication burden insignificant in the context of long sequences. The communication parallelism
081   and accessibility to overlap with computation are notably improved. 2) the refactored computation
082   order improves computation parallelism over multiple devices. Additionally, we separately present
083   LASP-2 with and without masking for autoregressive and bidirectional tasks, respectively, as the
084   presence of a mask matrix significantly impacts the execution mechanism of LASP-2. To extend
085   LASP-2 to hybrid models with both linear and standard attention modules, we introduce LASP-2H.
086   This extension employs the same all-gather-based communication for standard attention layers, with
087   a similar designing philosophy on linear attention. We conduct experiments with up to sequence
088
      length of 2048K to verify the efficiency advantages of LASP-2 and LASP-2H.
089   The main contributions of this paper can be summarized as follows:
090
             • We rethink the communication design for the current SP on linear attention, reorganize its
091
               whole communication and computation process with an optimized execution order. This
092            involves using a single all-gather collective communication on intermediate memory states,
093            whose sizes are independent of sequence length. The resulted LASP-2 significantly improves
094            both communication and computation parallelism, as well as their overlap.
095          • We extend LASP-2 to LASP-2H, offering an efficient SP solution for hybrid models that
096            integrate both linear and standard attention layers, employing an unified all-gather-based
097            communication strategy.
098          • We construct a series of Linear-Llama3 models, including both purely linear and hybrid
099            versions. Extensive experimental results on these models with up to a sequence length of
100
               2048K, validate the efficiency improvement and performance of LASP-2 and LASP-2H.

146   2.2    S EQUENCE PARALLELISM
147
      SP (Li et al., 2022) is a distributed technology designed for training language models more efficiently,
148
      which is implemented by dividing a long sequence into multiple shorter subsequences and processing
149   these subsequences in parallel on multiple computing devices. Existing SP methods (Korthikanti et al.,
150   2022; Jacobs et al., 2023) whose parallelism degree cannot exceed the number of attention heads,
151   which limits their scalability. Ring Attention (Liu et al., 2023) is proposed to address high memory
152   cost in long sequence modeling by distributing subsequences across different devices and overlapping
153   the communication of KV blocks. LASP (Sun et al., 2024) proposes a new linear attention-tailored
154   SP strategy based on GPU friendly implementation by utilizing a P2P ring-style communication
155   strategy, but still lacks of optimizations for hybrid model architecture.

198   4.1   LASP-2 WITHOUT M ASKING
199
      Sequence parallelism methods work by dividing long input sequences into several smaller chunks,
200
      which are then distributed across multiple computational devices. Each device independently pro-
201   cesses the queries, keys, and values for its assigned chunk in parallel. To complete the attention
202   computation for the entire sequence, necessary communication steps are performed to either gather
203   the results from all devices or exchange information between them. LASP (Sun et al., 2024) was
204   introduced as a sequence parallelism technique designed specifically for the linear attention module.
205
      Let us consider a distributed computing setup where there are W devices, and the input sequence is
206
      divided into T chunks, referred to as the sequence parallel size. In the usual case, T is evenly divisible
207   by W , and we often assume W = T . It means each chunk is assigned to a single device, ensuring
208   that every chunk is processed in parallel across the distributed system. This scenario exemplifies pure
209   sequence parallelism. Additionally, in Sec.A.4.1, we will explore cases where W ̸= T , representing
210   a hybrid approach that combines sequence parallelism with data parallelism.
211

212
      chunk is distributed across the devices in the distributed system. For each chunk Xt , its corresponding
213   query, key, value, and the linear attention memory state can be computed in parallel across all chunks.

252

321   4.3   LASP-1 VS LASP-2
322
323   LASP-2, as well as its previous version LASP-1, both aim on efficient SP on linear attention. Although,
      in theory, LASP-1 and LASP-2 share similarity on communicating the KV activation (d × d), whose


                                                        6
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


324
      size is independent of the sequence or chunk length. They have fundamental distinctions where the
325   key differences lie in their communication manners and the computational order reorganization, as
326   elaborated as below:
327
328
             • LASP-1 utilizes a ring-style P2P communication, which needs to launch many send &
               receive operators between devices, to sequentially transfer the KV activation one-by-one
329
               among the devices. This makes the communication process relatively slow and hard to
330
               adequately overlap with intra-chunk computations.
331          • While LASP-2 uses a single AllGather collective communication operator to exchange
332            KV activation concurrently among all decices. This offers practical advantages: (1) Only one
333            well-optimized collective communication operator needs to be launched, and the exchange
334            of KV activation on all devices can be finished concurrently all at once; (2) the collective
335            communication can be more easily overlapped with computations. Like in LASP-2 with
336            masking, the AllGather communication is able to overlap with the intra-chunk output
337            computations. And, in addition, LASP-2 reorganizes the whole computation order to make
338            the AllGather based communication strategy feasible and efficiency.

349   Communication traffic in each communication step: LASP-1: BHd2 , LASP-2: BHd2 . This
350   is because both LASP-1 and LASP-2 transfer linear attention memory states (not keys and values)
351
      among devices. The memory state corresponding to each chunk (located at each device) has a tensor
      shape of [B, H, d, d]. Thus in each communication step, their communication traffic are both BHd2 .
352
353   For a practical Linear-Llama3-1B model with B = 16, H = 16 and d = 2048, each memory
354   state will has BHd2 ≈ 1.07 billion parameters, which takes around 2.14GB memory in FP16. For
355   a Linear-Llama3-8B model with B = 16, H = 32 and d = 4096, each memory state will has
356   BHd2 ≈ 8.59 billion parameters, which takes around 17.18GB memory in FP16.
357   Number of communication steps in each iteration: LASP-1: 2(W − 1), LASP-2: 2. This
358   depends on the different communication manners of these two algorithms. During the forward of
359   an iteration, LASP-2 launches a single all-gather operation to gather all memory states Mt to all
360   devices, i.e., [Mt ]T1 = AllGather([Mt ]T1 ). This collective operation is concurrently executed
361   on all devices. While in backward, another all-gather is performed on the gradients of Mt , i.e.,
362   [dMt ]T1 = AllGather([dMt ]T1 ). Thus in each iteration, LASP-2 has 2 communication steps.
363
      While LASP-1 uses a pair of send & receive operation to sequentially exchange the memory
      state from one device to another device. During forward, device i sends its memory state to device
364
      i + 1, and device i + 1 receives the memory state from device i, and so on. Computations of Ot,inter ,
365
      Ot and updates of Mt are followed behind each receive operation on that device. Thus in the
366   process of forward, LASP-1 has W − 1 communication steps. In the backward, this process is
367   repeated reversely from the last device to device 0. Thus in each iteration, LASP-1 have totally
368   2(W − 1) communication steps.
369
      Given that both LASP-1 and LASP-2 perform a total of I iterations, their communication traffic
370
      models can be expressed as follows: LASP-1: 2(W − 1)IBHd2 and LASP-2: 2IBHd2 . Ideally,
371
      LASP-2’s communication traffic would be reduced by a factor of W − 1 compared to LASP-1.
372   However, the actual communication cost depends on factors like communication bandwidth, which
373   is typically faster within nodes and slower across nodes. As a result, LASP-2’s benefits become
374   more evident in clusters with slower interconnects, and vice versa. It is important to note that this
375   cost model only accounts for communication, excluding computation or data loading. In practice,
376   communication represents a smaller portion of the total cost, so the overall training speedup achieved
377   by LASP-2 is less than W − 1 times. LASP-2 performs best in scenarios involving long sequences,
      large clusters, slow communication links, and efficient data loading and computation.

      4.5    H YBRID M ODEL S EQUENCE PARALLELISM
399
400
      The hybrid model, which combines linear transformer layers with standard transformer layers that
401   utilize softmax self-attention, has been demonstrated to effectively enhance long-context capabilities,
402   particularly in tasks such as recall and retrieval. To optimize SP in such hybrid models, we propose
403   an extended version of LASP-2, referred to as LASP-2H. This approach introduces a comprehensive
404   solution by incorporating SP into both the linear attention and standard attention modules. The
405   structure of LASP-2H is illustrated in Fig. 2.
406
      On Linear Attention Module. As outlined in Algorithm 1 and Algorithm 2, LASP-2H handles linear
407   attention modules by performing a single all-gather communication operation on the memory state
408   Mt ∈ Rd×d . The communication complexity remains independent of both sequence or chunk length,
409   and only scales linearly with the SP size T , making this method efficient in distributed clusters.
410
      On Standard Attention Module. Context Parallelism (CP) is a SP technique in Megatron-LM that
411
      divides network inputs and all activations along the sequence dimension. This approach is specifically
412
      tailored for standard softmax attention. While traditional CP implementations in Megatron-LM
413   rely on overlapping communication and computation in a ring-like structure (Liu et al., 2023), our
414   LASP-2H adopts a different method, following the best practice in Llama3 (Dubey et al., 2024).
415   Instead of the ring-style strategy, LASP-2H employs AllGather-based communication on standard
416   attention, where the Kt and Vt tensors are first gathered across devices, after which the attention
417   output is computed locally for the Qt tensor chunk. Although the all-gather communication has a
418   higher latency compared to ring-based methods, it provides greater ease and flexibility in handling
419   various types of attention masks, such as document-level masks. This flexibility is particularly
420   beneficial in scenarios where different attention patterns are needed. Additionally, the all-gather
421
      latency is minimized because the Kt and Vt tensors are significantly smaller than the Qt tensor,
      especially when using Grouped Query Attention (GQA) (Ainslie et al., 2023). As a result, the time
422
      complexity of computing the attention output far exceeds the complexity of all-gather operation. We
423
      present the description of AllGather-based Context Parallelism in Algorithm 7 in Appendix A.3.

528   6                          C ONCLUSION
529
530   This paper presents LASP-2, a new SP method that addresses the inefficiencies of existing SP
531
      approaches for linear sequence modeling. By reorganizing the whole algorithm execution order
      and leveraging an all-gather communication strategy, LASP-2 enhances both communication and
532
      computation parallelism, and enables easier communication-computation overlapping, comparing
533
      with LASP-1. Our results demonstrate that LASP-2 offers significant improvements in throughput
534   and scalability, especially in the context of very-long sequence length. Furthermore, the extension
535   to LASP-2H enables efficient SP in hybrid models that integrate both linear and standard attention
536   modules, both utilize an unified all-gather-based communication primitive. Experimental evaluations
537   on the Linear-Llama3 models validate these advancements, with LASP-2 outperforming previous
538   methods like LASP-1 and Ring Attention by substantial margins, particularly at extreme sequence
539   lengths. These findings confirm the practical utility of LASP-2 for large-scale distributed systems,
      making it a promising approach for future applications in long-sequence linear transformer models.

793   LASP-2 also offers support for both tensor parallelism (TP) and pipeline parallelism (PP). In the case
794   of TP, its integration with LASP-2 is straightforward and efficient. Linear attention layers apply TP
795   to break down matrix operations across both intra-chunk and inter-chunk computations. At the same
796   time, the MLP layers are processed as usual under TP, without any modification. When LASP-2 is
797   paired with PP, instead of using traditional micro-batches, it substitutes them with sub-sequences

      A.4.2    VARIABLE L ENGTH
803
      During pretraining, the batch typically contains sequences of uniform length. However, when
804   finetuning or during inference, the model might encounter input sequences of varying lengths. A
805   straightforward solution to address this is to right-pad all sequences in a batch to match the length
806   of the longest sequence. Unfortunately, this method can be inefficient, especially when the lengths
807   differ significantly across sequences. For standard transformers, more sophisticated approaches
808   have been developed to handle this challenge. These include techniques like load-balancing across
809   GPUs without padding (Zeng et al., 2022; Zhai et al., 2023) or packing multiple sequences into a
      single batch and adjusting the attention mask accordingly (Ding et al., 2024; Pouransari et al., 2024).
