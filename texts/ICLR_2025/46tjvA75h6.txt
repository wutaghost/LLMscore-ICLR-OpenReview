009                                             A BSTRACT
010
011           Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) sampling-based maximum likelihood estima-
012           tion is a standard approach for training Energy-Based Models (EBMs). However,
013           its effectiveness and training stability in high-dimensional settings remain thorny
014           issues due to challenges like mode collapse and slow mixing of MCMC. To address
015           these limitations, we introduce a novel MCMC teaching-free learning framework
016           that jointly trains an EBM and a diffusion-based generative model, leveraging the
017           variational formulation of divergence between time-reversed diffusion paths. In
018
              each iteration, the generator model is trained to align with both the empirical data
              distribution and the current EBM, bypassing the need for biased MCMC sampling.
019
              The EBM is then updated by maximizing the likelihood of the synthesized exam-
020
              ples generated through a diffusion generative process that more accurately reflects
021           the EBM’s distribution. Moreover, we propose a novel objective function that
022           further improves EBM learning by minimizing the discrepancy between the EBM
023           and the generative model. Our proposed approach enhances training efficiency
024           and overcomes key challenges associated with traditional MCMC-based methods.
025           Experimental results on generative modeling and likelihood estimation demonstrate
026           the superior performance of our method.

029   1   I NTRODUCTION
030
031   Energy-based models (EBMs) are an appealing class of probabilistic models that can model the
032   data distributions and generate various types of samples, such as images (LeCun et al., 2006a; Kim
033
      & Bengio, 2016), reinforcement learning (Haarnoja et al., 2017; Boney et al., 2019), language
      (Mireshghallah et al., 2022), and out-of-distribution detection (Liu et al., 2020). The maximum
034
      likelihood estimation (MLE) based on Markov chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) is widely used to learn
035
      EBMs. However, training EBMs on high-dimensional data is still a challenging problem since
036   MCMC is prone to mode collapse and slow mixing in high dimensions.
037
038   Recently, a line of work has been proposed to improve the learning of EBM by accelerating the
039
      MCMC sampling process based on MCMC teaching. In MCMC teaching, a complementary generator
      model is cooperatively learned to serve as the amortized initialization of MCMC (Xie et al., 2022).
040
      Specifically, the synthesized samples generated by the generative model are revised by a short-run
041
      MCMC toward the current EBM. Then, the refined synthesized samples serve as fair samples to
042   update the EBM and the generator by maximizing the likelihood of each model. Although the
043   MCMC revision process in MCMC teaching provides a useful gradient information for updating
044   the synthesized examples, the non-convergent nature of short-run MCMC typically generates biased
045   samples, leading to inaccurate estimations for updating generators and EBMs . To mitigate the bias
046   of short-run MCMC when updating the generator, Cui & Han (2023) proposed to train the generator
047   to match both the empirical data distribution and the EBM via dual-MCMC teaching. However, the
048   biased short-run MCMC can still lead to conventional biased training of the generator and the EBM.
049   In this paper, we propose a novel MCMC teaching-free diffusion-based EBM (DiffEBM) learning
050   framework to remove the bias of the short-run MCMC by jointly training an EBM and a diffusion-
051   based generative model. In DiffEBM, we introduce an objective function to train the EBM and the
052   generator to match with each other without MCMC revisions by leveraging the variational formulation
053   of divergence of the path measures between the inference process and the generative process of
      probabilistic diffusion models (Richter & Berner, 2024). Training the EBM and the generator to


                                                       1
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


054
      match each other implies that we can directly regard the samples from the generator as fair samples
055   from the current EBM and avoid biased short-run MCMC revisions when updating both models. In
056   such a way, our method does not suffer from the bias issue of short-run MCMC revision of the current
057   MCMC teaching framework. Specifically, the generator is trained by maximizing the likelihood of
058   the empirical data and minimizing the difference with the current EBM. The EBM is then learned by
059   maximizing the likelihood of the empirical data where the synthesized examples are simulated from
060   the diffusion model within finite time and minimizing the discrepancy with the generator. Therefore,
061   we can avoid the non-convergent MCMC during the iterative joint learning process.
062   In summary, our innovations are:

             • We propose an MCMC teaching-free framework to learn energy-based models based on
               the variational formulation of divergence between forward and backward time-reversed
066
               diffusion paths.
067
068
             • Our method can avoid mode collapse and slowing mixing issues of traditional MCMC
069
               sampling-based EBM learning methods.

             • We demonstrate that our method achieves significant improvements in sample quality
072
               compared to existing EBM learning approaches on benchmark simulation tasks.

077
078   Training Energy-based models Given the computational costs of MCMC inference until conver-
079   gence, numerous methods have been proposed to improve the learning of EBMs. For example, (Xie
080   et al., 2018; 2016; 2020) proposed amortizing MCMC sampling with learned networks by leveraging
081   the cooperative learning scheme. In (Xie et al., 2022), the authors train an energy-based model with a
082   normalizing flow as an amortized sampler to initialize the MCMC chains of the energy-based model.
083   Carbone et. al. (Carbone et al., 2023) point out that the approximations of the estimate of the gradient
084   of the cross entropy are uncontrolled and known to induce biases similar to those observed with
085
      score-based methods and cannot handle well multimodal distributions. Therefore, they proposed a
      sequential Monte Carlo sampling procedure with Jarzynski correction to estimate the cross-entropy
086
      and its gradient with theoretical guarantees.
087
088
089   Diffusion probabilistic models for Energy-based models Diffusion models (Sohl-Dickstein et al.,
090   2015; Song et al., 2020; Ho et al., 2020) learn to reverse a constructed noising process to generate
091   high-quality data, which inspires a line of works to improve the training of EBM on generative
092   modeling. (Gao et al., 2021) proposed to learn a sequence of EBMs for the marginal distributions
093   of the diffusion process, where each EBM is learned with recovery likelihoods that are defined as
094   the conditional distributions of the reverse process. Zhu et al. (2023) introduced the cooperative
095   diffusion recovery likelihood that jointly trained EBMs and initializer models at multiple noise
096   levels to improve sample quality and generation performance, bridging the gap between EBMs
097
      and other generative models like GANs and diffusion models. (Luo et al., 2023) proposed a novel
      diffusion contrastive divergence, which replaces MCMC sampling in contrastive divergence with
098
      diffusion processes, offering a more computationally efficient training method for EBMs that avoids
099
      the difficulties of handling non-negligible gradient terms.
100
101
102   Diffusion probabilistic models for sampling The goal of sampling is to generate samples from
103   a known (unnormalized) density function (Liu & Wang, 2016; Cheng et al., 2023). Richter et al.
104   (Richter & Berner, 2024) propose a general framework for learning a diffusion model tailored to the
105   sampling task. Their approach involves minimizing the divergence between the diffusion sampling
106   process and the time-reversal of the noising process. Sampling has a close connection to EBM
107   learning since training EBM via MLE requires sampling from the current energy model. Our work is
      inspired by the diffusion-based sampling method.

458   6           C ONCLUSION
459
460   In this paper, we propose a novel MCMC teaching-free framework to learn energy-based models. We
461   leverage the variational formulation of divergence between time-reversed diffusion paths to jointly
462   train an EBM and a diffusion-based generative model. By training the generator and the EBM to
463   match with each other, the samples simulated from the generator can be viewed directly as fair
464   synthesized data to update EBM via MLE, and the refined EBM model can directly be used to update
465   the generator based on the unnormalized density-guided path divergence, avoiding mode collapse and
466
      slowing mixing issues of traditional MCMC sampling-based EBM learning methods. We verify the
      effectiveness of the proposed method on the energy estimation, density estimation, score estimation,
467
      and the quality of synthetic data on simulations.
