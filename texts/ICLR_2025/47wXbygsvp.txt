012            creating fully flexible, animatable avatars. In this work, we introduce TAGA
013            (Template-free Animatable Gaussian Avatars), the first template-free, Gaussian-
               based solution for the reconstruction of animatable avatars from monocular videos,

               reliable skinning anchors to ensure consistent geometry and movement. TAGA
017

130   Templates-free Reconstruction Methods. To eliminate reliance on parametric templates, various
131   methods focus on building template-free animatable avatars. One prominent direction [15, 17–24],
132   exemplified by HumanNeRF [15], compensates for the lack of shape priors by learning inverse
133   skinning to map observed poses to a canonical space, but struggles with generalization to new
134   poses. Another mainstream approaches [14, 25–30], represented by TAVA [14] and ARAH [30],
135
      perform complex and time-consuming iterative root-finding algorithm to search for correct canonical
      correspondences of points in observation space. More recently, NPC [16] uses sparse feature point
136
      clouds as anchors to accelerate the backward mapping of query points. However, since each sampling
137
      point requires querying K-nearest anchors during both forward rendering and backward mapping, it
138   fails to fully leverage the speed advantages of explicit representations. All the above methods incur
139   significant computational overhead during the mapping process, as they require extensive querying to
140   establish correspondences between observation space points and their canonical counterparts. As a
141   result, both training and rendering speeds are significantly slowed.
142
      The 3D Gaussian representation, which has been widely adopted in SMPL-based human models [11,
143
      33–41], holds the potential to overcome the aforementioned limtations, with enhanced speed, superior
144   quality, flexible topology, and natural one-to-one correspondence [31–33]. Despite these advantages,
145   it is surprising that template-free approaches for animatable avatar reconstruction based on Gaussian
146   representations remain unexplored. In this work, we extend 3D Gaussian splatting to template-free
147   avatars, achieving state-of-the-art synthesis quality on both novel view synthesis and unseen pose
148   synthesis with just minutes of training and real-time rendering at over 140+ FPS. Table 1 provides a
149   comparison between TAGA and recent representative animatable avatar reconstruction methods.
150   Template-free Canonical Appearance Modeling. In template-free scenarios, the absence of para-
151   metric templates requires learning canonical body geometry from scratch. NPC [16] sidesteps the
152   problem by extracting explicit point clouds from existing part-based body models. Despite that, the
153   use of fixed point clouds lacks flexibility [42], making it difficult to handle complex deformations and
154   hindering end-to-end learning. Other traditional methods, whether relying on root-finding [14, 27, 30]
155   or inverse skinning [15, 43], require rich pose data as input. When pose diversity is limited, these
156   methods tend to learn spurious correlations in self-contact regions.
157   TAGA uses self-supervised learning to reconstruct the geometry and skinning of animatable avatars
158   from a limited set of videos and poses, without relying on predefined templates. A conceptually
159   related approach is SCANimate [48], which weakly supervises the reconstruction of clothed human
160   bodies from raw scans by enforcing consistency between forward and inverse skinning. However,
161   SCANimate requires learning inverse skinning and depends on skinning from SMPL template to
      supervise both forward and inverse skinning. In contrast, TAGA learns only forward skinning and

      For implicit representation, there is no direct correspondence between points in the canonical and
288
      observation space, and it is difficult to ensure bijectivity [58–60]. Classical backward mapping
289   strategy primarily sought to establish correspondences from observation space (xo ) to canonical
290   space (xc ), often expressed mathematically as solving for xc where LBS(w(xc ), xc , B) = xo .
291   Since implicit representations do not explicitly store points, we denote positions in 3D space with
292   x, while w establishes a continuous skinning field in the canonical 3D space. As the relationship
293   between xo and xc remains unknown, it is impossible to obtain an analytical solution for xc
294   directly [61, 62]. Therefore, existing methods resort to cumbersome and time-consuming iterative
295   root-finding algorithms, which often require tens of hours of training.
296   In contrast, the explicit nature of Gaussians gives a one-to-one correspondence [63, 64] between
297   the canonical Gaussians G c and those G o in the observation space. The skinning weights W in
298   the canonical space are directly associated with the Gaussians themselves. The Gaussians act as
299   anchors for transferring the skinning weights from the canonical space to the observation space. Thus,
300   according to Eq. 3, pc can be elegantly obtained as T −1 po . Given the estimated weights W ′ derived
301   from observation space, the positions pc∗ of the corrected canonical Gaussians are computed by
302   applying an inverse LBS transformation using the adjusted skinning weights W ′ as follows:

327   Remark: Our backward mapping strategy boasts several attractive qualities. ❶ Transparency:
328   Central to our backward mapping strategy is the detection of Ambiguous Gaussians – a process
329   that is straightforward, intuitive, and readily interpretable by humans. Unlike traditional implicit
330   methods that operate as black boxes, our approach ensures full transparency in both detection and
331   correction stages. Whether identifying or correcting Ambiguous Gaussians, or dealing with the
332   resulting canonical Gaussians, each intermediate steps can be viewed and inspected (See Fig. 4). ❷
333   Flexibility: Our anomaly detection framework is not tied to a specific algorithm. Since we focus
334   solely on using anomaly detection algorithms to perform unsupervised detection of Ambiguous
335
      Gaussians, TAGA can seamlessly integrate other point cloud anomaly detection methods into the
      current framework. ❸ Robustness: By integrating anomaly detection to capture overlooked spatial
336
      and semantic information, TAGA enables template-free reconstruction from limited pose data while
337
      resolving ambiguities and spurious correlations that typically arise from this ill-posed problem in the
338   canonical space. ❹ Efficiency: TAGA utilizes the one-to-one correspondence of Gaussian represen-
339   tations to efficiently refine the canonical space, avoiding unnecessary exploration and focusing on
340   incremental improvements from a suboptimal reconstruction.

                                 527   5      C ONCLUSION
                                 528
                                 529
                                 530   In this study, we tackle the challenge of reconstructing a canonical avatar from monocular videos with
                                 531
                                       limited poses, without relying on parametric templates. We demonstrate that leveraging semantic and
                                       spatial cues from observations can compensate for the limited visual information during canonical
                                 532
                                       reconstruction. Following this insight, we utilize the inherent bijectivity of Gaussians to design a
                                 533
                                       coarse-to-fine forward-backward framework named TAGA that self-supervises the optimization of
                                 534   skinning and geometry in the canonical space. To this end, we propose a new backward mapping
                                 535   strategy that integrates anomaly detection to capture robust spatial and semantic inductive biases
                                 536   from the observed space, allowing for transparent correction of erroneous geometric artifacts caused
                                 537   by Ambiguous Gaussians in the canonical space. Extensive experiments demonstrate the robustness
                                 538   and efficiency of TAGA. We believe our contributions provide novel insights into template-free
                                 539   reconstruction, taking an important step towards overcoming the limitations imposed by parametric
                                       templates and observations with low diversity.

      C    I MPLEMENTATION D ETAILS
838
839
      Training. The training of TAGA is organized into several phases aimed at optimizing skinning and
840   canonical appearance in a template-free environment. We begin with a warm-up phase to learn a rigid
841   skinning field, during which the skinning weight field is optimized independently. Following this, we
842   enter the main training phase. For the first 1.5K iterations, all components are frozen except for the
843   3D Gaussians. These Gaussians, driven by the pre-trained rigid skinning field, autonomously refine
844   the positions and appearance in canonical space. Subsequently, we commence the optimization of the
845   voxel-based skinning field, continuing to enforce the skinning weights regularization Lbone . After
846   2.5K iterations, the backward mapping stage is activated, utilizing a cycle consistency loss Lcycle
847   to address geometrical errors within the canonical space and further refine the skining field. The
848
      backward mapping phase is introduced only after the forward mapping reconstruction has stabilized,
      thereby ensuring that it serves to refine the geometry rather than disrupt it. To mitigate computational
849
      overhead, backward mapping is performed every 150 steps, with the positions and skinning weights
850
      of corrected Gaussians cached as soft constraints to continuously guide the optimization of canonical
851   Gaussians. This strategy distributes the cost of ambiguity detection and correction across iterations,
852   minimizing the impact on computational efficiency.
853
854
      Due to the extremely limited pose in the PeopleSnapshot, some regions such as the armpits are
      occluded during training and remain unseen. To mitigate this issue and improve the model’s ability to
855
      reconstruct these occluded areas, we add noise to the pose during the backward phase. Specifically,
856
      throughout the entire backward phase, we perturb the bone transfromation matrix B by adding noise
857   sampled from a normal distribution N (0, 0.1) with a probability of p = 0.5.
858
859   Moreover, given the sparsity of skinning weights -— where each Gaussian is typically influenced
860
      by at most a few bones —we focus only on the bone with the highest posterior probability and its
      immediate neighboring bones when estimating the coarse skinning weights.
861
862   Inference. For Inference, we solely employ forward-mapping, leveraging the optimized skinning
863   weights and the refined geometry. Similar to InstantAvatar [12], test-time pose refinement is also
      employed to enhance the results.

      Voxel Resolution. Table S2 shows the impact of voxel grid resolution on novel pose performance.

907

911   times the memory usage, yet do not yield improvements in novel pose performance. This may be
912   because the high resolution of the grid makes the voxel-based skinning field less stable.

      Limitation. While TAGA demonstrates significant advancements in template-free modeling, it is
966   important to acknowledge certain limitations that could impact its applicability in more complex
967   scenarios: i) Non-rigid Deformations: TAGA struggles with excessively loose clothing or extreme
968   non-rigid deformations. Such scenarios can disrupt the learning process for template-free skinning
969   and pose challenges in generalizing to unseen poses. ii) Unseen Details and Artifacts: Although
970   TAGA reduces the reliance on precise pose input and effectively addresses geometric artifacts of
971   self-contact regions, it is still challenging to handle unseen details in the input data. Even when a
      Gaussian is placed correctly, issues such as holes or rendering artifacts may persist, especially in
