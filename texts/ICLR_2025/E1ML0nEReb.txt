009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            Point cloud segmentation is an important topic in 3D understanding that has tradi-
012            tionally has been tackled using either the CNN or Transformer. Recently, Mamba
013            has emerged as a promising alternative, offering efficient long-range contextual
014            modeling capabilities without the quadratic complexity associated with Trans-
015            former’s attention mechanisms. However, despite Mamba’s potential, early ef-
016
               forts have all failed to achieve better performance than the best CNN-based and
               Transformer-based methods. In this work, we address this challenge by identi-
017
               fying the key components of an effective and efficient point cloud segmentation
018
               architecture. Specifically, we show that: 1) Spatial locality and robust contex-
019            tual understanding are critical for strong performance, and 2) Mamba features
020            linear computational complexity, offering superior data and inference efficiency
021            compared to Transformers, while still being capable of delivering strong contex-
022            tual understanding. Additionally, we further enhance the standard Mamba specif-
023            ically for point cloud segmentation by identifying its two key shortcomings. First,
024            the enforced causality in the original Mamba is unsuitable for processing point
025            clouds that have no such dependencies. Second, its unidirectional scanning strat-
026            egy imposes a directional bias, hampering its ability to capture the full context
027
               of unordered point clouds in a single pass. To address these issues, we carefully
               remove the causal convolutions and introduce a novel Bidirectional Strided SSM
028
               to enhance the model’s capability to capture spatial relationships. Our efforts
029
               culminate in a novel architecture named MEEPO that effectively integrates the
030            strengths of CNN and Mamba. MEEPO surpasses the previous state-of-the-art
031            method, PTv3, by up to +0.8 mIoU on multiple key benchmark datasets, while
032            being 42.1% faster and 5.53× more memory efficient. Our code will be released.

035   1   I NTRODUCTION
036
037   Point cloud segmentation is an important topic in 3D understanding that has gained significant atten-
038   tion from the research community in recent years. Numerous neural network architectures have been
039
      proposed for this task, with CNN-based and Transformer-based designs being the two most promi-
      nent. Currently, Transformer-based methods consistently deliver the highest performance across
040
      numerous benchmarks. Their success is frequently credited to the attention mechanism, which can
041
      adequately capture and model context. However, the quadratic complexity of self-attention in Trans-
042   formers poses a significant challenge for point cloud processing, especially when handling a large
043   number of points. To address this, researchers have explored more efficient strategies, such as ag-
044   gressive downsampling (Zhao et al., 2021; Wu et al., 2022; Pan et al., 2021), efficient attention algo-
045   rithms (Yang et al., 2023), and windowed attention mechanisms (Wang, 2023; Lai et al., 2022; Wu
046   et al., 2024). While these methods help reduce computational costs, they achieve this at the expense
047   of valuable spatial and geometric information, potentially weakening the Transformer’s modeling
048   capability and hindering its contextual understanding of the point cloud (Shen et al., 2021).
049   Recently, State Space Models (SSMs) like Mamba (Lieber et al., 2024; Gu & Dao, 2024; Pióro
050   et al., 2024; Wang et al., 2024b) have emerged as a promising alternative to Transformers. These
051   models combine aspects of recurrent neural networks (Cho et al., 2014; Hochreiter & Schmidhuber,
052   1997) and convolutional neural networks (LeCun et al., 1989) within a framework rooted in classical
053   state space theory. Similar to Transformers, SSMs provide robust contextual modeling capabilities.
      However, unlike Transformers, which scale quadratically with sequence length, SSMs scale linearly

      With the macro-level design established, we turn our attention to the micro-level design, specifically
      assessing whether the standard Mamba module, originally designed for sequential processing, is
113
      suitable for point cloud segmentation. Our investigation shows that it is not. In particular, we
114
      identify two major shortcomings of the standard Mamba module when applied to this task:
115
116         1. Loss of spatial information due to enforced causality: Mamba’s use of causal convo-
117            lutions introduces unnecessary artificial dependencies that can disrupt the inherent spatial
118            relationships in point cloud data, ultimately reducing its effectiveness for point cloud data.
119         2. Directional bias due to unidirectional scan: Mamba’s unidirectional scan strategy inher-
120            ently favors certain data points over others, creating a directional bias. This bias under-
121            mines its ability to fully grasp the context of unordered point clouds in a single pass.
122
      To address these issues, we propose two corresponding improvements to the standard Mamba mod-
123
      ule. Specifically, we 1) remove the causality constraint, and 2) incorporate a novel Bidirectional
124   Strided SSM to enhance its context and spatial understanding.
125
126   Our work culminates in M EEPO, a novel point cloud segmentation architecture which seamlessly in-
127
      tegrates Mamba’s efficiency and strong contextual understanding with the local sensitivity of CNN.

128
      passes the performance of other leading segmentation models across both indoor and outdoor scenes.
129

130   ScanNet200, S3DIS, and nuScenes datasets, with 42.1% smaller latency and 5.53× smaller memory
131   usage. M EEPO also scales much better with respect to the size of point cloud. As shown in Fig. 1(d),
132   it achieves up to 12× fewer GFLOPs than PTv3 when processing a scene with 217 (131, 072) points.

136            tance of spatial locality and robust contextual understanding in achieving high perfor-
137            mance. We then reveal that Transformer’s global attention is unnecessary and inefficient
138            for this task as Mambas offer comparable contextual modeling with linear complexity.
139         2. We propose two corresponding solutions to address the limitations of Mamba when applied
140            to point cloud segmentation, namely the removal of the causality constraint and the incor-
141            poration of an innovative Bidirectional Strided SSM to enhance contextual understanding.
142         3. We introduce M EEPO, a novel architecture that solely utilizes the efficient sparse convo-
143            lution to provide spatial locality and the efficient SSM to provide robust contextual under-
144            standing. M EEPO not only consistently outperforms previous best method, PTv3 across
145            multiple key benchmark datasets, but is also much faster and much more memory efficient.

178   3    A NALYSIS
179
180
      In this section, we delve into the key requirements for effective point cloud segmentation, showing
181   the importance of capturing both local and contextual features via model architecture. Robust local
182   modeling is essential for maintaining point-level consistency, especially when object boundaries are
183   unclear, while strong contextual modeling is crucial for identifying occluded or ambiguously shaped
184   objects. With these needs in mind, we evaluate various architectures, assessing their strengths and
185   weaknesses in providing these important properties for effective point cloud segmentation.
186
187
      Meta-architecture for analysis. To facilitate our analysis, we first propose a meta-architecture for
      point cloud segmentation. This meta-architecture follows an encoder-decoder framework, featuring
188
      a 5-stage encoder and a 4-stage decoder. Following PTv3 Wu et al. (2024), the input points are first
189
      voxelized into non-overlapping segments and arranged into an ordered sequence using alternating Z-
190   order and Hilbert space-filling curves (Morton, 1966; Peano, 1890). These voxels are then processed
191   by an embedding module that employs a single submanifold sparse convolution layer (Graham et al.,
192   2018). GridPooling and GridUnpooling operations are applied at the end and beginning of each
193   encoding stage, respectively, to downsample and upsample the point cloud (Wu et al., 2024). Within
194   this meta-architecture, we train three similarly sized models, each using either the CNN, Mamba,
195   or Transformer blocks, as shown in Fig. 2, to evaluate their performance both qualitatively and
196   quantitatively. To account for the different parameter densities of these blocks, their channel sizes
197   are adjusted accordingly to ensure similar parameter counts. These models are referred to as Pure
198
      CNN, Pure Mamba, and Pure Transformer, respectively. We hypothesize that the global attention
      mechanism of the Pure Transformer and the long-range sequential modeling capabilities of the Pure
199
      Mamba are highly effective for contextual modeling. In particular, Pure Mamba offers additional
200
      advantages, including enhanced inference efficiency due to its linear computational complexity and
201   improved data efficiency enabled by the inductive bias of the forget gate. On the other hand, Pure
202   CNN excels in local modeling by leveraging its inherent spatial locality.

344   4.2   M ICRO A RCHITECTURE D ESIGN
345
      Optimizing Mamba for point cloud segmentation. Despite its impressive speed and performance,
346
      M EEPO without micro-level optimizations still does not surpass the leading point cloud segmen-
347
      tation network, PTv3, in accuracy. This aligns with previous research, which consistently demon-
348   strates that attempts to apply Mamba have all failed to outperform well-established models for this
349   task (Zhang et al., 2024; Liu et al., 2024b). A closer analysis reveals that the standard Mamba, origi-
350   nally designed for sequential data, is inherently unsuited for processing unordered point clouds. We
351   identify two key limitations in the standard Mamba and propose the solutions to overcome them.
352
      1. Loss of spatial information due to enforced causality: Mamba was originally designed for se-
353
      quential data with clear causal relationships. However, point clouds lack such dependencies, as their
354   spatial relationships are multidimensional, requiring simultaneous consideration of points holisti-
355   cally. The causal convolutions in standard Mamba impose a causal dependency that disrupts these
356   essential spatial relationships, making it ineffective for handling spatial data like point clouds.
357
      Proposed Solution: Causal-Free Mamba. To address this issue, we propose replacing the causal
358
      convolution with a standard convolution, resulting in the Causal-Free Mamba module, as illustrated
359
      in Fig.6(b). This modification eliminates the limitations of the original Mamba architecture when
360   processing spatial data, greatly enhancing its performance in point cloud segmentation tasks.
361
362   2. Directional bias due to unidirectional scan: Mamba’s unidirectional scanning method intro-
363
      duces a directional bias in the representation learning of point clouds because some parts are scanned
      first while others are scanned later. Such sequential processing is ill-suited for orderless point cloud
364
      data, where all information should be treated equally. This approach can lead to models prioritizing
365
      information from later stages of the scan, overlooking details captured earlier. The bias is further
366   amplified by factors such as noise, occlusions, or reduced data density at the beginning of the scan,
367   which can degrade the quality of the early data. As a result, important features captured early in the
368   scan may be missed or inaccurately interpreted, leading to segmentation errors where boundaries
369   are poorly defined, and features are incorrectly merged or omitted.
370
      Proposed Solution: Bidirectional Strided SSM. To address this issue, we propose an innovative
371
      multi-directional scanning approach to reduce the directional bias of Mamba. Unlike the standard
372   Mamba, which employs a unidirectional scan, this scanning method processes data in four distinct
373   scanning directions: forward, backward, n-strided forward, and n-strided backward. In an n-strided
374   forward scan, every n-th token is skipped, and the scan pattern restarts from the beginning once the
375   end is reached. For example, given the sequence 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, a 2-strided forward scan would
376   process it as 1, 3, 5, 2, 4, 6. The backward scan operates similarly but in reverse order. This multi-
377   directional scanning approach can effectively expand Mamba’s receptive field, reduce information
      loss, and improve overall performance by shortening the information flow path.

493   6   R ELATED W ORK
494
495   Architectures for point cloud segmentation fall into three main categories: point-based (Thomas
496   et al., 2019; Qi et al., 2017a;b; Ma et al., 2022), voxel-based (Maturana & Scherer, 2015; Song
497   et al., 2017), and projection-based (Chen et al., 2017; Lang et al., 2019; Li et al., 2016; Su et al.,
498   2015). Although they differ in pre-processing strategies, all these methods are designed with careful
499
      consideration of the unique characteristics of point clouds. They mainly differ in how they integrate
      local and contextual features and manage irregular point distributions. Recently, transformer-based
500
      models (Wu et al., 2024; Robert et al., 2023; Yang et al., 2023; Lai et al., 2022) have emerged in
501
      this field, achieving higher accuracy but suffering from significant time and memory complexities
502   relative to the size of the point cloud. To address this, more efficient attention mechanisms, such as
503   vector attention (Zhao et al., 2020), grouped vector attention (Wu et al., 2022), local window-based
504   attention (Lai et al., 2022), and memory-efficient attention (Yang et al., 2023), have been developed.
505   However, these strategies approximate original attention, resulting in a loss of global modeling ca-
506   pability (Shen et al., 2021), which may impede performance on long range modeling task like point
507   cloud segmentation. Our research examines the strengths and weaknesses of several widely-used
508   architectures for point cloud segmentation and introduces a novel architecture that effectively inte-
509   grates their best features, culminating in a new state-of-the-art model for point cloud segmentation.
510   State space models (SSMs) (Gu & Dao, 2024; Wang et al., 2024b; Lieber et al., 2024) have emerged
511   as a promising alternative to Transformers (Vaswani et al., 2017) in natural language processing
512   (NLP) for capturing long-range dependencies. Unlike Transformers that scale quadratically with se-
513   quence length, SSMs (Gu et al., 2022; Nguyen et al., 2022; Smith et al., 2023) achieve linear scaling
514   during inference. The seminal Mamba model (Gu & Dao, 2024) greatly improves the performance
515   and efficiency of SSM by introducing input-specific parameterization and a scalable, hardware-
516
      optimized method, allowing it to outperform Transformers for the first time. Driven by the success
      of SSMs in NLP, recent works have also explored their application to visual tasks. S4ND (Nguyen
517
      et al., 2022) marks the introduction of SSM modules for processing visual data across 1D, 2D, and
518
      3D domains. Subsequent works, such as VMamba (Liu et al., 2024c), Vim (Zhu et al., 2024), and
519   Bi-Mamba+ (Liang et al., 2024a), address the directional sensitivity in SSMs with bi-directional and
520   cross-scan mechanisms, allowing SSMs to achieve performance that rivals that of CNN and Trans-
521   former models. Mamba-based models have also delivered impressive performance in many other
522   vision tasks, such as image segmentation (Xing et al., 2024; Liu et al., 2024a), image synthesis (Yan
523   et al., 2024), graph modeling (Wang et al., 2024a), and low-level vision (Guo et al., 2024). Despite
524   some initial attempts (Liu et al., 2024b) to apply Mamba for point cloud segmentation, these models
525   have all failed to outperform existing architectures. In this work, we identify the shortcomings of
526   SSM when applied to this task and propose simple solutions to greatly improve its performance.

      7   C ONCLUSION
529
530
531
      In this work, we present a detailed analysis of existing network architectures for point cloud seg-
      mentation, highlighting their strengths and weaknesses. This evaluation provides valuable insights
532
      into designing more efficient and effective architectures for this task. Our findings emphasize the
533
      crucial role of spatial locality and robust contextual understanding in achieving strong performance.
534   Specifically, we identify convolution and the Mamba module as essential components for efficient
535   and accurate point cloud segmentation. Convolution provides spatial locality, while the Mamba
536   module enhances the understanding of context. Additionally, we improve the standard Mamba mod-
537   ule by removing the causality constraint and introducing Bidirectional Strided SSM, which further
538   enhances its ability to capture and utilize contextual information. Following these design principles
539   and applying targeted optimizations, we introduce M EEPO, a novel architecture that outperforms
      previous state-of-the-art models across multiple key benchmark datasets and efficiency metrics.

842   Effectiveness of Mamba in Processing Long Context. Due to their linear complexity, Mamba-

      are overlooked. Conversely, segmenting a point cloud into windows may conceal crucial interactions
852   across boundaries, resulting in a potentially incomplete or inaccurate scene representation.

      A.4   L IMITATIONS
919
920   While our work has significantly advanced the performance of point cloud segmentation models,
921   many challenges and opportunities for improvement remain. For instance, although M EEPO exhibits
922   notable efficiency gains, there is still room for further optimization to enhance its computational and
923   memory efficiency. Additionally, segmentation quality, especially in handling fine details and com-
924   plex geometries within point clouds, can be further improved. Enhancing the model’s ability to
925   accurately segment objects in diverse and densely populated scenes is another critical area for future
926
      research. Moreover, the potential of pretraining through self-supervised learning is unexplored in
      this work. Leveraging large-scale unlabeled point cloud data for pretraining could help the model
927
      learn more robust and generalizable features, ultimately boosting performance across various tasks
928
      and datasets. Future work should explore and integrate self-supervised learning techniques to har-
929   ness this potential fully. Addressing these challenges will ensure that Mamba-based architectures
930   continue to evolve and set new benchmarks in the field of point cloud segmentation.
931
932   A.5   B ROADER I MPACTS
933
934   Accessibility and Resource Efficiency. By demonstrating that our proposed method, M EEPO can
935   achieve superior performance with reduced latency and memory consumption, this research pro-
936   motes the development of more accessible and resource-efficient machine learning models. This
937   is particularly important for applications in resource-constrained environments, such as mobile and
938   embedded systems, where computational power and memory are limited. As a result, more organiza-
939   tions and developers can leverage advanced point cloud segmentation techniques without requiring
940   extensive computational resources.
941
      Environmental Impact. The reduction in computational cost and memory usage directly translates
942
      to lower energy consumption. Given the growing concern over the environmental impact of large-
943
      scale machine learning models, M EEPO’s efficiency can contribute to more sustainable AI practices.
944   By minimizing the energy required for training and inference, this work aligns with global efforts to
945   reduce the carbon footprint of technology.
