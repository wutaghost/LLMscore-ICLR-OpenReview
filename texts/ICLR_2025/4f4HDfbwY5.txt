011                                              A BSTRACT
012
013            The generation of time series has increasingly wide applications in many fields,
014            such as electricity and transportation. Generating realistic multivariate long time
015            series is a crucial step towards making time series generation models practical,
016
               with the challenge being the balance between long-term dependencies and short-
               term feature learning. Towards this end, we propose a novel time series genera-
017
               tion model named Compressed Patch Denoising Diffusion-model (CPDD). Con-
018
               cretely, CPDD first employs the Time-series Patch Compressed (TPC) module
019            based on the patch mode decomposition method to obtain the latent encoding
020            of multi-scale feature fusion. Subsequently, it utilizes a diffusion-based model
021            to learn the latent distribution and decode the resulting samples, thereby achiev-
022            ing high-quality multivariate long-time series generation. Through extensive ex-
023            periments, results show that CPDD achieves state-of-the-art performance in the
024            generation task of multivariate long-time series. Furthermore, TPC also exhibits
025            remarkable efficiency in terms of robustness and generalization in time series re-
026            construction.

029   1   I NTRODUCTION
030
031   The analysis of long-term time series data is of paramount importance in many real-world applica-
032   tions (Zhou et al. (2022); Wu et al. (2021); Zhou et al. (2021); Wang et al. (2023)). For instance,
033
      in power load forecasting, longer historical data spanning months or even years is crucial for cap-
      turing seasonal patterns and long-term trends, enabling more accurate predictions of future energy
034
      demand. However, the increasing complexity and volume of time series data, particularly in sce-
035
      narios involving long sequences and multiple variables, present significant challenges for analysis
036   and modeling. These challenges are further exacerbated by limited access to high-quality, diverse
037   datasets, especially when data sharing is restricted due to privacy concerns or proprietary reasons.
038
039
      Time series generation has emerged as a promising solution to address these issues. By artifi-
      cially creating realistic and diverse time-series data, researchers and practitioners can overcome
040
      data scarcity and privacy constraints. Generating long time series presents its own set of difficul-
041
      ties, including maintaining temporal consistency, capturing complex long-term dependencies and
042   short-term features, and ensuring the generated data accurately reflects the statistical properties of
043   real-world time series.
044
045
      To address the challenges of time series generation, various deep learning approaches have been
      proposed in recent years. These approaches aim to create synthetic time series data that closely
046
      resemble real-world data in terms of statistical properties and temporal dynamics. Some notable
047
      approaches include TimeGAN (Yoon et al. (2019)), TimeVAE (Abhyuday Desai & Beaver (2021)),
048   and Diffusion-TS (Yuan & Qiao (2024)). TimeGAN leverages the Generative Adversarial Networks
049   (GANs) framework (Goodfellow et al. (2014)) and introduces the supervised loss in training to
050   improve the generation quality. TimeVAE utilizes variational auto-encoders to capture and repro-
051   duce the underlying distribution of time series data using the trend-season decomposition technique.
052   More recently, diffusion-based models such as Diffusion-TS have shown promising results in gen-
053   erating high-quality time series data. However, these existing approaches face several challenges
      when it comes to generating multivariate long-term time series: 1) Cumulative errors: Recurrent

067   Neural Networks(RNN-based) methods exhibit limited performance in capturing long-range depen-
068   dencies due to cumulative errors. 2) High computational demands: Transformer-based approaches
069   lead to quadratic memory complexity for sequence length, requiring substantial computational re-
070   sources in long-term time series generation. 3) Difficulty in capturing both long-term dependencies
071   and short-term features: As the length of time series and the number of variables increase, the short-
072   term features in time series will become increasingly complex due to additional seasonal changes,
073   cyclical changes, sudden events, and other short-term fluctuations. Simultaneously, this heightened
074   complexity may lead to long-term dependencies becoming more prominent and intricate. These
075
      challenges pose difficulties for current approaches in producing high-quality multivariate long-term
      time series that accurately reflect the complex temporal dynamics found in real-world scenarios.
076
077   One viable solution to the aforementioned issue is to employ a framework akin to the latent layer
078   diffusion model (Rombach et al. (2022)), necessitating techniques capable of effectively compress-
079   ing and characterizing multivariate long-term time series data. In this paper, we introduce a novel
080   multivariate long-term time series generative model named Compressed Patch Denoising Diffusion-
081
      model (CPDD), which utilizes the mode functions decomposition technique to achieve cross-scale
      features fusion for obtaining the integrated representation of long-term dependence and short-term
082
      features. Concretely, We employ a Time-series Patch Compression (TPC) module of CPDD to de-
083
      compose the patches into mode functions (Dragomiretskiy & Zosso (2014)), enabling a consistent
084   characterization of both long-term dependencies and short-term features through these mode func-
085   tions. In contrast to conventional time series representation approaches (Chowdhury et al. (2022);
086   Yue et al. (2022); Zheng & Zhang (2023)), the TPC module learns a versatile combination of mode
087   functions from patches to accommodate various patterns and achieve generalized compressed rep-
088   resentation of time series data. To learn the latent distribution of the TPC outputs, CPDD introduces
089   a trend-seasonal decomposition diffusion-based generative model (Ho et al. (2020)) which utilizes
090   a novel Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN-based) Backbone named Depthwise separable Star
091   Convolution (DSConv) block to capture complex nonlinear features and Transformer blocks to learn
092
      the trend in time series.
093   The major contributions of this paper are as follows:
094
095          • We propose CPDD, a novel compression-based denoising diffusion generative model de-
096            signed for multivariate long-term time series. CPDD leverages a patch compression method
097            to capture complex long-term and short-term dependencies more effectively, ultimately
098
               leading to the high-quality generation of multivariate long-term time series.
099          • We introduce the TPC module, which guides the model to learn the general mode functions
100            of the patches and then fits it by mode functions combination to obtain the generalized
101            time-compressed representation model, based on the patch mode decomposition technique.
102            The TPC module can achieve robust reconstruction under high noise levels on some unseen
103            time series data.
104          • We propose a novel CNN-based backbone network named DSConv block apply to adap-
105            tively learn the patch mode functions, which employs an element-wise multiplication oper-
106            ation akin to the kernel trick, integrating the Depthwise separable Convolution (DWConv)
107            (Howard (2017)) technique with the ConvFFN structure (Luo & Wang (2024)) to dynami-
               cally learn the general mode functions.

184   3.1   CPDD M ETHOD
185
      CPDD is composed of the TPC module and a diffusion-based generative model, designed for ef-
186
      ficient time series compression and reconstruction. The TPC module employs an encoder-decoder
187
      framework and integrates hierarchical components for long-term, short-term, and residual process-
188   ing, which facilitate the learning of multi-scale temporal features and patch mode functions. The
189   diffusion generative model, built upon the Transformer architecture, aims to capture the distribution
190   of compressed representations more effectively.
191
192
      3.2   T IME - SERIES PATCH C OMPRESSION MODULE
193
194   The TPC module is composed of the DSConv block and the Transformer block components, as
195   shown in Figure 2 and Figure 3. The DSConv block is employed to handle short-term and residual
196   features due to its advanced complex nonlinear modeling capabilities, while the Transformer block
197   is used to deal with long-term dependencies.
198   Multi-scale Feature Extraction. Several studies, such as ModernTCN (Luo & Wang (2024)), have
199   demonstrated that CNNs can significantly improve temporal feature extraction in time series mod-
200   eling by expanding the receptive field. To construct a comprehensive initial representation of time
201   series data, we combine linear layers, dilated convolutions, and patch embedding layers (C. Zhang
202   & Wu (2022); Zheng & Zhang (2023)) to form the embedding module, referred to as Integrated
203   Feature Convolution (If Conv). This module is specifically designed to capture multi-scale features
204   by effectively converting patches into representative tokens.
205   Adaptive mode Function Learning. To achieve adaptive learning of mode functions, we aim to de-
206   sign a model that learns a latent space where each point corresponds to a unique mode function, with
207   smoothly distributed representations for both long-term and short-term functions. This enables cap-
208   turing diverse morphological characteristics across different time scales. We propose the DSConv
209   block, which incorporates structural regularization to support this adaptive learning. In the following
210   subsections, we first describe the components of DSConv and then provide a detailed formal proof
211   of the effectiveness of its structural regularization.
212   As illustrated in Figure 3, the DSConv block integrates a modified StarNet structure (Ma et al.
213   (2024)) with three core components: DWConv, Single Channel Convolution, and two ConvFFN
214   modules. Concretely, the DWConv is responsible for learning temporal multi-scale information.
215   The Single Channel Conv aggregates information across channels to capture global dependen-
      cies. The ConvFFN modules, analogous to the Feed-Forward Networks (FFN) in Transformers,

      5   C ONCLUSSION
487
488
      In this work, we introduce a novel approach for realistic multivariate long-time series generation
489   through a model named CPDD. CPDD effectively addresses the fundamental challenges of balanc-
490   ing long-term temporal dependencies and short-term feature representations by integrating the TPC
491   module and a diffusion-based generative model. The TPC module leverages adaptive patch mode de-
492   composition to capture and compress multi-scale temporal features, ensuring a robust and compact
493   latent encoding. The diffusion-based framework then models the latent distribution and synthesizes
494   high-quality long-time series data through an iterative denoising process. Extensive experiments
495   validate that CPDD not only achieves state-of-the-art performance in generating complex multivari-
496   ate long-term time series but also demonstrates remarkable generalization and robustness in various
497
      time series reconstruction tasks. In future work, we will focus on enhancing the TPC module and
      DSConv block to handle more complex temporal structures and explore their integration into hybrid
498
      architectures for broader applicability across diverse domains.
