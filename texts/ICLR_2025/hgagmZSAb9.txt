011                                               A BSTRACT
012
013            Recent advancements in large language models (LLMs) with extended context
014            windows have significantly improved tasks such as information extraction, question
015            answering, and complex planning scenarios. In order to achieve success in long-
016            context tasks, a large amount of work has been done to enhance the long-context
017            capabilities of the model through synthetic data. Existing methods typically utilize
018            the Self-Instruct framework to generate instruction-tuning data for better long-
019            context capability improvement. However, our preliminary experiments indicate
020
               that less than 35% of samples generated by Qwen-272B are multi-hop, and more
               than 40% exhibit poor quality, limiting comprehensive understanding and further
021
               research. To improve the quality of synthetic data, we propose the Multi-agent
022
               Interactive Multi-hop Generation (MIMG) framework, incorporating a Quality Ver-
023            ification Agent, a Single-hop Question Generation Agent, a Multiple Question
024            Sampling Strategy, and a Multi-hop Question Merger Agent. This framework im-
025            proves the data quality, with the proportion of high-quality, multi-hop, and diverse
026            data exceeding 85%. Furthermore, we systematically investigate strategies for
027            document selection, question merging, and validation techniques through extensive
028            experiments across various models. Our findings show that our synthetic high-
029            quality long-context instruction data significantly enhances model performance,
030            even surpassing models trained on larger amounts of human-annotated data.

      1    I NTRODUCTION
034
035
036
      Recently, large language models (LLMs) with long-context windows have significantly improved
      tasks such as information extraction, question answering, and even complex planning scenarios (Liu
037
      et al., 2024a; Bai et al., 2024b; Hu et al., 2023; 2024; Xu et al., 2024b). Research on developing long-
038
      context LLMs has predominantly focused on extending the context window (Ding et al., 2024; Jin
039   et al., 2024; Peng et al., 2024). Nevertheless, in practical applications, merely expanding the context
040   window is insufficient for effectively utilizing long-context (Hsieh et al., 2024; Huang, 2024), which
041   presses a need for training to optimize utilization of long-context (Zhang et al., 2024), especially in
042   instruction-tuning (IT) (Fu et al., 2024b). In the IT phase, a large amount of high-quality long-context
043   IT data is required. However, acquiring such data is challenging, with annotation costs significantly
044   higher than those for short-context data (Bai et al., 2024b; Xiong et al., 2024). To address this, Xiong
045   et al. (2023) and Bai et al. (2024a) have explored leveraging LLMs to generate IT data using the
046   Self-Instruct framework (Wang et al., 2023b), thereby mitigating the scarcity of long-context IT data.
047   Moreover, the challenge often lies not in extracting single-hop information, but in integrating multiple
048   hops of data from the long context to reach complex conclusions. Despite this, existing studies
049   struggle to produce high-quality, multi-hop IT data. This gap stems from insufficient attention to the
050   data synthesis process and factors influencing data effectiveness. As illustrated in Figure 1 (a), our
051   preliminary manual annotation experiments show that direct self-instruction yields less than 35%
052   multi-hop samples, with high-quality examples representing only 60%. Additionally, sample diversity
053
      remains problematic, with over 45% of the samples exhibiting semantic duplication. These issues
      hinder comprehensive understanding and further advancement in this domain.

095   2     F RAMEWORK
096   Our framework consists of four main components: Quality Verification Agent (§ 2.1), Single-hop
097   Question Generation Agent (§ 2.2), Multiple Question Sampling (§ 2.3), and Multi-hop Question
098   Merging Agent (§ 2.4). Specifically, the Quality Verification Agent is first designed as a validator to
099   control and supervise the data quality at each stage. The Single-hop Question Generation Agent then
100   generates simple, direct single-hop questions. Next, Multiple Question Sampling strategies expand on
101   this by sampling questions that cover various documents, enhancing multi-hop instruction generation.
102   Finally, the Multi-hop Question Merging Agent integrates these single-hop questions into coherent
103
      multi-hop questions, requiring information synthesis from multiple document parts. The detailed
      architecture is illustrated in Figure 2.

      2.2   S INGLE - HOP Q UESTION G ENERATION AGENT
163
164   This phase generates single-hop questions and answers from individual documents, encompassing
165   the following components:
166
167   Generation Backbone: This component utilizes a robust LLM to generate valid and relevant
168
      single-hop questions and answers from each document. Multiple questions and answers are produced
      per document to ensure a diverse foundation for multi-hop question development. We thoroughly
169
      examine various LLMs, including both open-source and close-source models, across different scales.
170
171   Generation Strategy: The strategy employs a structured approach to extract potential questions
172   from the text, using the following techniques:
173
        • Rationale-based Question Generation: Chain-of-Thought (CoT) prompting (Wei et al., 2022)
174
          has been recognized for its role in improving performance on long-text tasks (Li et al., 2024).
175
          Building on this, our study investigates whether generating questions from a long document,
176       supported by rationale, can enhance the understanding of the document’s inherent reasoning.
177
178     • Question-Answering Generation Order: Furthermore, we aim to evaluate whether the sequence
179
          of generating questions and answers impacts the overall effectiveness. Specifically, generating
          the question prior to the answer may reduce the reasoning complexity and improve the quality of
180
          the model’s output compared to a simultaneous generation approach.

183   2.3   M ULTIPLE Q UESTION S AMPLING
184
      In order to further optimize the diversity of generated samples, we introduce Multiple Question
185
      Sampling strategy to create multi-hop questions by sampling and combining questions from multiple
186   questions and documents. It mainly involves the following two strategies:
187
188   Retrieval Strategy: This strategy identifies relevant questions and documents for multi-hop ques-
189   tion creation. Using relevance sampling, a question semantic relevance matrix is generated, assessing
190   the semantic connections between questions across different documents and guiding the sampling
191
      process. The strategy includes:
192     • Probability-Based Sampling: This method evaluates document relevance based on the probabil-
193       ity and occurrence of specific keywords related to the questions, like BM25 (Robertson et al.,
194       1995; 2009), and LDA (Hoffman et al., 2010).
195
        • Semantic-Based Sampling: This approach assesses the relevance by analyzing the semantic
196
          similarity between questions and documents, like embedding similarity.
197
198   Sampling Strategy: Based on the relevance matrix, the most related questions are selected for
199   merging. This involves choosing questions that are both relevant and complementary, ensuring that
200   the resulting multi-hop questions are coherent and contextually rich. The strategy includes:
201
202
        • Intra-Document Sampling: This strategy focuses on selecting questions within the same
          document to ensure internal coherent multi-hop data.
203
204     • Inter-Document Sampling: This strategy involves selecting questions from different documents
205       to ensure a broader contextual coverage.

      3.1.1              V ERIFICATION S TRATEGY
257
258   Currently, the most widely employed strategies for model verification are scoring and direct classi-
259   fication. We evaluated the consistency and precision of both approaches by comparing them with
260   human annotations in the sample analysis of data generated from long contexts.
261   Scoring is a Better Verification Strategy Compared with Classification. As shown in Figure 3
262   (a), the scoring strategy shows significantly higher kappa and precision scores compared to binary
263   quality classification. This statistical improvement suggests that scoring better captures the nuances of
264   human judgments. This observation aligns with findings in short-context scenarios (Fu et al., 2024a),
265   reinforcing the generalizability of scoring strategies across different lengths of textual data.
266
      LLM is not a long-context annotator but a good selector. As depicted in Figure 3 (a),
267   in contrast to their performance in short-context verification (Wang et al., 2023a; Fu et al.,
268   2024a), LLMs demonstrate minimal agreement with human annotators in long-context scenar-
269   ios, reflected in low kappa scores. This suggests challenges in maintaining annotation con-
      sistency due to the cognitive load and interpretative variations over extensive information.

      3.1.2                     V ERIFICATION C ONDITIONS
289   To deeply understand what factors affect the verification of long text data quality, we further explored
290   from three perspectives: scoring perspective, guidelines, and whether rationale is included for scoring.
291
292   More scoring perspectives reduce long-context bias. As illustrated in Figure 5 (a), incorporating
293   more scoring perspectives significantly enhances the accuracy and robustness of filtering long-context
294   data. Unlike short contexts, long contexts introduce noticeable bias in judgments. When fewer
295   than 3 perspectives are used, performance gains are minimal, and the model often overestimates
296   irrelevant samples, leading to poor selection results. However, increasing the number of perspectives
297   markedly improves labeling accuracy, effectively mitigating biases associated with longer contexts.
298
      See Appendix A.2.2 for more details.
299
      Effective verifiers adhere to annotation standards aligned with human judgment. To assess
300
      whether incorporating additional scoring criteria enhances the model’s verification performance, we
301
      specify the criteria for each score in detail. As illustrated in Figure 5 (b), interestingly, the guideline
302   does not include supplementary information during the annotation process for advanced models. This
303   observation suggests that effective verifiers inherently follow annotation standards that well align
304   with human judgment.
305
306   Incorporating rationale enhances robustness in diverse long contexts. Our methodology neces-
307   sitates extension across numerous domains, emphasizing the criticality of robustness across diverse
308   domains. Contextualizing the role of CoT (Wei et al., 2022; Qin et al., 2023), we evaluate model
309   performance across various domains, specifically in wiki-like knowledge and paper analysis domains.
310   As illustrated in Figure 5 (c), incorporating rationale enables the model to maintain high performance
311   across diverse contexts. Without rationale, performance decreases by more than 8.6% when con-
312
      fronted with different domains. Conversely, adding rationale during validation results in minimal
      performance variation, with fluctuations in precision limited to 1.8% at most.

501   Data Scaling Analysis To evaluate how the size of high-quality data affects model performance,

      Hop Scaling Analysis To assess the impact of multi-hop data on model performance, we increased
      the number of hops in the dataset while keeping the training data volume constant. This approach
508
      isolated the effect of multi-hop reasoning on model outcomes. As indicated in Figure 15, there is a
509
      clear positive correlation between the number of hops and model performance. The data demonstrate
510   that with more hops, the model achieves higher accuracy and robustness. These results demonstrate
511   the effectiveness of using high-quality multi-hop data to enhance the model’s capability for complex
512   reasoning tasks.

      5     R ELATED W ORK
515
516
      Recent efforts have aimed to enhance the performance of LLMs in handling longer contexts.
517
      LongLLaMA (Xiong et al., 2023) demonstrates the impact of incorporating long text data dur-
518
      ing various pre-training stages. LLaMA2-80K (Fu et al., 2024b) highlights the significance of using
519   a domain-balanced, upsampled long text corpus to improve long text capabilities, requiring only a
520   5B-token corpus for effective comprehension. ICLM (Shi et al., 2024) enhances long-text reasoning
521   by transforming pre-training data into knowledge graphs and splicing adjacent documents. To improve
522   the model’s ability to follow long text instructions, LongAlpaca (Chen et al., 2024b) combines a 9K
523   paper question-answering (QA) corpus with 3K short instruction samples. In contrast, LongAlign (Bai
524   et al., 2024a) utilizes Claude (Anthropic, 2023) to produce 10K QA pairs for training. Additionally,
525   ChatQA (Liu et al., 2024b) enhances long-context QA performance by incorporating manually anno-
526   tated data. Building on these approaches, ChatQA2 (Xu et al., 2024a) further incorporate existing
527
      long-text datasets, such as Natural Questions (NQ) (Kwiatkowski et al., 2019).
528   The method closest dataset is Quest (Gao et al., 2024), which constructs QA pairs from document
529   data and splices documents based on QA pair correlations, resulting in a close-sourced single-hop QA
530   corpus. In contrast, our approach models document correlations first, then create multi-hop QA pairs
531   using related intra-document data. Additionally, we offer systematic analysis, open-source datasets,
532   and significantly improved models.
533
534   6     C ONCLUSION
535
536   In conclusion, our proposed Multi-agent Interactive Multi-hop Generation (MIMG) framework, which
537   includes a quality verification agent, a single-hop question generation agent, a multiple question
538   sampling strategy, and a multi-hop question merger agent, achieves high-quality, diverse instruction
539   data. Our experiments show that this synthetic data notably enhances performance, even surpassing
      models trained on larger human-annotated data, highlighting the effectiveness of our approaches.

      B      H IGHEST Q UALITY S TRATEGIES D ETAILS
919
920
      To achieve the highest quality data, we deliberately prioritize the use of GPT-4o as the backbone for
921   all processes, fully disregarding cost constraints. This decision is driven by the understanding that
922   ensuring the best data quality is paramount for the success of our project. Furthermore, to maintain
923   and enhance performance during the exploration phase, we implement a comprehensive range of
924   strategies aimed at maximizing the data retention rate.
925
      Specifically, for the Quality Verification Agent, we employ a multi-faceted approach that includes
926
      more-perspectives scoring mechanisms, the addition of rationales, the integration of multiple perspec-
927   tives, and the application of detailed guidelines. For the Single-hop Question Generation Agent, we
928   have adopted a question-then-answer strategy. This approach is complemented by the incorporation
929   of rationales, which provide context and justification for each query generated. Additionally, we
930   require LLMs to generate only one question per query, which is intended to reduce the logical burden
931   on the model, thereby improving the coherence and relevance of the questions produced. In the case
932   of Multiple Question Sampling, we utilize BGE embeddings for the retrieval of questions. This tech-
933   nique is applied both within individual documents (intra-document) and across multiple documents
934   (inter-document). Finally, for the Multi-hop Question Merging Agent, we employ a strategy that
935
      involves merging questions and answers using document references. This method ensures that the
      merged questions and answers are contextually aligned and coherent. Notably, we have opted to
936
      remove the rationale for merging in this process, as we found that it adds unnecessary complexity
937
      without significantly improving the quality of the merged content.
