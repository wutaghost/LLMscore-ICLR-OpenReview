031                                                 A BSTRACT
032
033               Single image-to-3D generation is pivotal for crafting controllable 3D assets. Given
034               its under-constrained nature, we attempt to leverage 3D geometric priors from a
035               novel view diffusion model and 2D appearance priors from an image generation
036               model to guide the optimization process. We note that there is a disparity between
037               the generation priors of these two diffusion models, leading to their different
038               appearance outputs. Specifically, image generation models tend to deliver more
039               detailed visuals, whereas novel view models produce consistent yet over-smooth
040               results across different views. Directly combining them leads to suboptimal effects
041
                  due to their appearance conflicts. Hence, we propose a 2D-3D hybrid Fourier
                  Score Distillation objective function, hy-FSD. It optimizes 3D Gaussians using
042
                  3D priors in spatial domain to ensure geometric consistency, while exploiting
043
                  2D priors in the frequency domain through Fourier transform for better visual
044               quality. hy-FSD can be integrated into existing 3D generation methods and produce
045               significant performance gains. With this technique, we further develop an image-to-
046               3D generation pipeline to create high-quality 3D objects within one minute, named
047               Fourier123. Extensive experiments demonstrate that Fourier123 excels in efficient
048               generation with rapid convergence speed and visually-friendly generation results.

090   Motivation. (1) 2D SDS-based 3D generation. With the powerful generation capability of 2D Stable
091   Diffusion (SD) (Rombach et al., 2022), DreamFusion (Poole et al., 2023) utilizes SD to generate
092   pseudo-GT views for training NeRF to achieve text-to-3D. However, SD is a 2D generative model
093
      that struggles to ensure good multi-view consistency, so a high Classifier-Free Guidance (CFG)
      value (Ho & Salimans, 2021) is necessary but it decreases generation quality. Additionally, SD tends
094
      to generate forward-facing images (Liu et al., 2023), which is known as the Janus problem. These
095
      limitations restrict the ability to use SD for 3D generation directly. (2) 3D SDS-based 3D generation.
096   Zero123 (Liu et al., 2023) fine-tunes the pre-trained 2D SD on 3D object-level datasets, improving its
097   multi-view consistency and 3D geometry. Similarly, DreamGaussian utilizes Zero123 to generate
098   multi-view pseudo-GT images, training a Gaussian-based 3D representation, resulting in a better
099   one-image 3D generation. However, Zero123 is fine-tuned on limited-scale 3D datasets, inevitably
100   reducing its generation capability and resulting in limited generation quality. (3) 2D SDS and 3D
101   SDS combined 3D generation. Intuitively, Magic123 (Qian et al., 2024) proposes a 3D generation
102   method that combines the generative capabilities of SD with the multi-view consistency of Zero123,
103   achieving impressive results. However, it still inherits the limitations of SD (e.g., the Janus problem
104
      and content distortion) and requires a time-consuming iterative textual inversion to fit the learnable
      text prompt to the given image, significantly restricting the generative capacity of this text-to-image
105
      (T2I) model. Therefore, our motivation is raised:
106
107   How to avoid the limitations of using 2D SD, a text-to-image model, for image-to-3D generation
      and fully unleash its generative capability?

      Insight. High-quality 3D generation requires three conditions: multi-view consistent geometry, clear
109   textural details, and content that matches the input image. (1) Spatial Domain: As shown in Fig. 2
110   (a), we visualize the generation results of SD (S2D ) and Zero123 (S3D ) in the spatial (RGB) domain.
111   Although the generated image by Zero123 ensures reasonable 3D structure and content matching with
112   the input, it is overly smooth and lacks details. Conversely, the generated image by SD exhibits clear
113   textures but alters content, leading to mismatches with the input image. Therefore, combining SD
114   and Zero123 in the spatial domain is not the optimal solution. (2) Frequency Domain: We further
115   transform the images to the frequency domain using Discrete Fourier Transform (DFT), as shown
116   in Fig. 2 (b). The result of SD (F2D ) exhibits more mid-to-high frequency components (frequency
117
      increases outwards) compared to the result of Zero123 (F3D ). According to the principle of Fourier
      transform, higher frequencies represent finer textures. Consequently, the generation result of SD
118
      maintains its advantage of detailed textures in the frequency domain, and since it is not in the spatial
119
      domain, it does not forcibly constrain the RGB content, but only the degree of texture details. Based
120   on the above analysis, we propose our perspective on one-image 3D generation:
121
122   In the spatial domain, use Zero123 to ensure reasonable 3D geometry and content that matches
123
      the input. In the frequency domain, use SD to enrich the texture details.
124   This solution effectively addresses the two motivations we outlined above, achieving high-quality
125   one-image 3D generation. (1) Avoiding the limitations of SD: Since the Janus problem and content
126   distortions arise in the spatial domain, we avoid using SD in the spatial domain. (2) Unleashing
127   the generative power of SD: Similarly, by avoiding strong supervision on the input content in the
128   spatial domain, we can discard the textual inversion and a high CFG value, avoiding SD fitting to
129
      local solutions and limiting the generation capability.
130   In summary, our main contributions are outlined as follows:
131
132         1. We use both spatial and frequency supervision for image-to-3D. The proposed hybrid Fourier
133            Score Distillation (hy-FSD) fully unleashes the generation ability of the text-to-image model
134            while mitigating the Janus problem and integrates its generation capabilities with the 3D
135            priors of the novel view generation model.
136
            2. We develop an efficient image-to-3D generation pipeline, Fourier123, utilizing hy-FSD. It
137
               enables high-quality 3D generation in one minute on a single NVIDIA 4090 GPU.
138
139         3. Extensive experiments confirm that our method significantly enhances the performance of
140            existing optimization-based 3D generation methods, effectively produces 3D assets with
141            reliable structure and elegant appearance.

146   2.1   3D R EPRESENTATION
147
148   Recently, various 3D representation techniques have been proposed for a range of 3D tasks. Wang et
149   al. (Wang et al., 2021a) employed volumetric rendering and reconstructed object surfaces by training
150   an implicit network. Mildenhall et al. (Mildenhall et al., 2021) further proposed NeRF, an end-to-end
151   model popular for enabling 3D optimization with only 2D supervision. NeRF has inspired numerous
152   subsequent studies, including 3D reconstruction (Barron et al., 2021; Park et al., 2021; Wang et al.,
153
      2022; Li et al., 2023; Cui et al., 2024) and generation (Jain et al., 2022; Poole et al., 2023; Wang et al.,
      2023; Lee et al., 2024), but it consumes excessive time for optimization due to its computationally
154
      expensive forward and backward passes. Although some methods (Yu et al., 2021a; Fridovich-Keil
155
      et al., 2022; Müller et al., 2022) attempted to accelerate training, the recent developed 3D Gaussian
156   Splatting (3DGS) (Kerbl et al., 2023) achieved real-time rendering with faster training speed, and
157   is considered a viable alternative 3D representation to NeRF. Its efficient differentiable splatting
158   mechanism and representation design enable fast convergence and faithful reconstruction (Luiten
159   et al., 2023; Meng et al., 2024; Yang et al., 2024). Recent studies on 3D generation (Yi et al., 2023;
160   Tang et al., 2024b) have adopted 3DGS to achieve faster and higher quality generation. In this
161   work, we also employ 3DGS as the representation technique and make the first attempt to realize the
      optimization-based methods in both spatial and frequency domains, improving generation quality.

      2.2   I MAGE - TO -3D G ENERATION
163
164   Image-to-3D generation aims to create 3D assets from a single reference image. This problem is also
165   known as single-view 3D reconstruction, but such reconstruction settings (Trevithick & Yang, 2021;
166   Yu et al., 2021b; Duggal & Pathak, 2022) are limited by uncertainty modeling and often produce
167   blurry results. Recently, diffusion models (Ho et al., 2020; Song et al., 2021) have achieved notable
168   success in image generation, including text-to-image (T2I) (Saharia et al., 2022; Rombach et al.,
169   2022) and novel view synthesis (Nichol et al., 2022; Liu et al., 2023; Wang & Shi, 2023; Long et al.,
170
      2023; Melas-Kyriazi et al., 2023). Several methods have attempted to extend 2D image models for
      3D generation (Poole et al., 2023; Wang et al., 2023; Lee et al., 2024; Wu et al., 2024; Zhu et al.,
171
      2024), but they suffer from long optimization times as they require frequent generation of 2D images
172
      to train 3D representations. To address this, some studies explicitly injected camera parameters into
173   2D diffusion models for zero-shot novel view synthesis (Liu et al., 2023; Long et al., 2023). Other
174   methods have tried to build end-to-end large reconstruction models to generate 3D assets with a single
175   forward process (Hong et al., 2024; Tang et al., 2024a; Wang et al., 2024; Xu et al., 2024). However,
176   the quality of the 3D assets generated by these two methods remains rough. Qian et al. (Qian et al.,
177   2024) found that the T2I model (Rombach et al., 2022) has impressive 2D generation capabilities,
178   while Zero-1-to-3 (Liu et al., 2023) tends to generate reliable 3D structures. They used both 2D and
179   3D priors to generate 3D. However, it still remains serious issues in tedious time costs and limited
180   generation capacity. In this paper, we combine 2D and 3D priors from the spatial and frequency
181
      domains, respectively, fully utilizing their respective advantages and enhancing 3D generation quality.

234   4     P ROPOSED F OURIER 123
235
236   In this section, we first illustrate the proposed novel score distillation sampling function, hy-FSD
237   (hybrid Fourier Score Distillation), that supervises 3D generation in both spatial and frequency
238   domains. hy-FSD enables the produced 3D assets to benefit from the high-quality generation
239   capability of a text-to-image (T2I) model and the geometric prior of a novel view generation diffusion
240   model simultaneously. Experiments validate that hy-FSD can be applied to existing 3D generation
241   baselines for performance gains. Next, we present an efficient image-to-3D generation pipeline,
242   Fourier123. It generates more reliable geometric structures and high-quality appearances.

      4.2     OVERALL P IPELINE
318
319   Our Fourier123 pipeline is simple yet effective, which generates high-quality 3D assets based on a
320   single image within 1 minute. We employ 3D Gaussian as our 3D representation due to its superior
321   optimization speed. Our overall framework consists of two steps: initialization and optimization.
322   As shown in Fig. 3, we parameterize the 3D Gaussians as ϕ. We first initialize ϕ and this step is
323   formulated as F(·). F(·) can be implemented by sphere initialization used in Tang et al. (2024b) or
      other point cloud generation models (Nichol et al., 2022; Tang et al., 2024a). The latter leads to better


                                                              6
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


324
      quality and is used in our main pipeline, but the former is still feasible. We use sphere initialization
325   in the ablation experiments of Sec. 5.2. Next, we use Stable Diffusion (SD) (Rombach et al., 2022)
326   and Zero-1-to-3XL (Zero123) (Liu et al., 2023) to optimize ϕ via the proposed hy-FSD, obtaining
327   generated 3D assets. This process takes about 52 seconds on a single NVIDIA 4090 GPU.
328
      Gaussian initialization. We employ differentiable 3D Gaussians as 3D representations. Their
329
      initialization is illustrated in Fig. 3 i), which can be modelled as: ϕ = F(I), where I is the given input
330
      image. ϕ is the initialized Gaussians, which will be iteratively optimized in subsequent processes
331   (see Fig. 3 ii)). F is the initialization operation, and there are typically two choices: one is to
332   randomly initialize Gaussians within a sphere in 3D space, as DreamGaussian (Tang et al., 2024b)
333   does. The other option is to employ a pre-trained large Gaussian generative model (e.g., LGM Tang
334   et al. (2024a)) for initialization. In our implementation, we default to using LGM for initialization.
335   However, our method is not initialization-sensitive, meaning that random sphere initialization is
336   also feasible. To demonstrate the generalizability of our method, we analyze the initialization
337   configurations in Sec. 5.2 and Sec. D of the Appendix.
338   Optimization with the hy-FSD. Fourier123 uses hy-FSD to generate high-quality 3D assets with
339   satisfactory appearances and geometries. Specifically, we use the proposed hy-FSD to optimize ϕ.
340   As depicted in the first term of Eq. 11, SD is employed for appearance guidance with its generations
341   in frequency amplitude. Note that since the classifier-free guidance (Ho & Salimans, 2021) in SD
342   is critical to its generation ability, a textual prompt is essential. This prompt can be generated by
343   ChatGPT based on the input image, or it can be a universal text such as “A high-quality image”. We
344   use the latter in this paper to prove superior convenience and generalization of our method, but in
345
      fact, using texts generated by ChatGPT leads to better performance and we conduct such experiments
      in Sec. F of our appendix. The detailed workflow is shown in Fig. 3. We first render an image from
346
      ϕ, then add some noise and input it to SD. SD performs a denoising process for a few steps to get
347
      2D supervision. Both the addition and removal of noise follow the DDIM schedule (Song et al.,
348   2021). Considering that the products of SD tend to exhibit distorted but high-quality appearances,
349   we extract their amplitude components in the frequency domain to utilize desired appearance priors
350   while avoiding training being misled by the distorted structure. This loss indirectly supervises 3D
351   assets from the frequency perspective, avoiding conflicts between the generation priors of SD and
352   other diffusion models or ϕ itself. Benefiting from this, we can combine Zero123 more effectively.
353
      As depicted in the second term of Eq. 11, we use Zero123 to calculate 3D-SDS in the spatial domain,
354   following similar operations as described in Liu et al. (2023); Qian et al. (2024). Zero123 can generate
355   novel views that are geometrically consistent with the input image, while strictly adhering to the
356   given input camera pose. However, it cannot improve visual quality of the input image, but SD can.
357   Taking advantage of hy-FSD, ϕ is trained with rich geometric and appearance priors.

      Optimization details. We only optimize the 3D Gaussian ellipsoids for 400 iterations, where the
366   learning rate of position information decays from 1 × 10−3 to 2 × 10−5 . The Stable Diffusion (Rom-
367   bach et al., 2022) (SD) model of V2 is selected. We set its classifier-free guidance as 7.5 because, on
368   the one hand, a too high guidance scale (e.g., 100 used in (Poole et al., 2023)) leads to low generation
369   quality (Wang et al., 2023). On the other hand, in our method, the views generated by SD are used
370   for supervision in the frequency domain rather than the RGB domain, which means guidance scale is
371   not required to be too high for cross-view consistency at the pixel-level. Additionally, we leverage
372   Zero-1-to-3XL for 3D structural supervision and its guidance scale is set to 5 according to (Liu et al.,
373   2023). Finally, the rendering resolution is set to 512 × 512.
374   Camera setting. Due to the lack of pose information in the input reference image, we set its camera
375   parameters as follows. First, we regard the reference image as being shot from the front view, i.e.,
376   azimuth angle is 0◦ and polar angle is 90◦ , which is similar to (Qian et al., 2024). Second, the camera
377   is placed 1.5 meters from the content in the image, consistent with LGM (Tang et al., 2024a). Third,
      as we use Zero123 to supervise distillation, the field of view (FOV) of the camera is 49.1◦ .

532   6   C ONCLUSION
533
534   In this work, we present Fourier123, a 3D object generation framework that achieves high-quality
535   image-to-3D generation. Two key contributions of our work are: 1) We propose a 2D-3D hybrid
536   Fourier score distillation function, which attempts to fully unleash generation ability of T2I model
537   for efficient image-to-3D generation. 2) We design a generative Gaussian splatting pipeline, called
538   Fourier123. It can produce ready-to-use 3D assets from a single image within one minute. We
539   believe that this method of distilling 3D assets from a frequency domain perspective provides a novel
      approach for the 3D generation task, and this work demonstrates its effectiveness.
