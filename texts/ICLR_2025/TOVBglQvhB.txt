008                                               A BSTRACT
009
010
               Several properties of raw data exhibit significant potential for enhancing images
               under extremely low-light conditions. Recently, many deep-learning methods
011
               for raw-based low-light image enhancement (LLIE) have demonstrated excellent
012
               performance. However, deploying them on resource-limited devices is restricted
013            by high computational and storage demands. In this work, we propose a novel
014            low-bit quantization method for raw-based LLIE model to improve their efficiency.
015            Nevertheless, directly adopting existing quantizers for LLIE networks leads to
016            obvious performance drop due to two main reasons. i) The U-Net model, com-
017            monly employed in LLIE, faces challenges in identifying a suitable quantization
018            range due to disparities in distribution between the encoder and decoder features.
019            ii) Low-bit quantized LLIE networks struggle to restore clear details in low-light
020            images because their features have a constraint capacity. We address these issues
021
               by introducing a novel low-bit quantization method, the Distribution-Separative
               Asymmetric Quantizer (DSAQ), designed specifically for U-Net architectures used
022
               in LLIE. In order to accurately determine the quantization intervals, DSAQ sepa-
023
               rates the distribution of encoder and decoder features before they are concatenated
024            by the skip connection. We also make the quantizer asymmetric with trainable
025            scale and offset parameters to suit skewed activation ranges caused by non-linear
026            functions. To further enhance performance, we propose a uniform feature distil-
027            lation technique, which allows the low-bit student model to effectively assimilate
028            knowledge from the full-precision teacher model, bridging the gap in representa-
029            tion capability. Extensive experiments show that our approach not only greatly
030            reduces the memory and computational requirements of raw-based LLIE models
031            but also has a promising performance. Our low-bit quantized model can achieve
032
               comparable or superior results to full-precision counterparts.

034   1    I NTRODUCTION
035
036   Capturing high-quality images under extremely low-light conditions is important for night surveillance
037   and various downstream computer vision tasks Hong et al. (2021); Chen et al. (2023). However, the
038   degradations including low signal-to-noise ratio (SNR) and obvious color cast caused by limited
039   photon count make it challenging. Low-light image enhancement (LLIE) methods provide a post-
040   capture solution that prevents noise amplification at high ISO settings and motion blur due to long
041   exposure time.
042   Recently, deep-learning LLIE methods trained with paired datasets Chen et al. (2018); Dong et al.
043   (2022); Wei et al. (2018) have shown outstanding performance. In this work, we focus on enhancing
044   low-light raw images because of their inherent advantages for LLIE Wei et al. (2022); Huang et al.
045   (2022). On the one hand, they maintain a linear relationship with photon counts and a tractable noise
046   distribution before passing through the image signal processing (ISP) pipeline. On the other hand,
047   they have a higher bit-depth that can distinguish subtle low-intensity details. Although deep-learning
048   models for raw-based LLIE Chen et al. (2018); Zhu et al. (2020); Jin et al. (2023) have achieved
049   promising results, the deployment of these neural networks on edge devices like mobile phones or
050
      embedded cameras is hindered by their high computational and storage demands.
051   A potential solution to this problem lies in the technique of network quantization Zhou et al. (2016);
052   Li et al. (2020); Qin et al. (2023). Quantization involves converting the continuous weights and
053   activations (features) of a neural network into discrete low-bit representations, significantly reducing
      the model’s memory footprint and accelerating its inference speed. In this work, we intend to quantize

054
      the LLIE model to a range of 2-4 bits to achieve a higher compression ratio. Despite its benefits in
055   terms of efficiency, low-bit network quantization may lead to a deterioration in model performance.
056
057   For raw-based LLIE methods, we recognize two main reasons for this performance degradation.
058
      Firstly, many of raw LLIE networks are based on U-Net Ronneberger et al. (2015) structure Chen
      et al. (2018); Dong et al. (2022); Huang et al. (2022). In the U-Net architecture, we observe that
059
      the features from the encoder and decoder, concatenated through skip connections, show notable
060
      differences in distribution. Moreover, the use of non-linear activation functions, such as LeakyReLU
061   Chen et al. (2018); Lamba & Mitra (2021), results in asymmetric distributions of positive and negative
062   values. These factors pose challenges in accurately determining the quantization range. Secondly,
063   features in low-bit quantized networks exhibit a representation capability gap compared to those in
064   full-precision networks. Therefore, existing knowledge distillation schemes Li et al. (2020); Zhong
065   et al. (2022), which directly impose constraints on normalized features, cannot fully transfer intrinsic
066   semantic information from full-precision teacher model to the low-bit quantized student model.
067   In this paper, we present a novel low-bit quantization method for raw-based LLIE to solve the above
068   problems. Specifically, we propose a Distribution-Separative Asymmetric Quantizer (DSAQ) that is
069   tailored for U-Net based LLIE method. It quantizes the encoder and decoder features respectively
070   before concatenation to facilitate the learning of quantization interval. In order to mitigate the
071   influence of non-linear functions on the distribution of activations, we introduce trainable scale and
072   offset parameters to implement the asymmetric quantizer. We further introduce a uniform feature
073   distillation that maps features of quantized student model and full-precision teacher model into
074   a uniform latent feature space. So low-bit network can better obtain intrinsic information from
075
      teacher model and restore clearer details from low-light images. Through extensive experiments, we
      demonstrate that our quantization method surpasses previous quantizers in raw LLIE and the low-bit
076
      network achieves comparable enhancement results to their full-precision counterparts. Our main
077
      contributions can be summarized as follows:
078
079          • We propose a compact low-bit quantized model for low-light raw image enhancement, which
080            can achieve satisfactory results with low memory and computation.
081
             • We build a Distribution-Separative Asymmetric Quantizer (DSAQ) for U-Net structure. It
082            separately determines the quantizer of different features before concatenation and introduces
083            asymmetric quantization for activations with skewed distribution.
084
             • We design a uniformed feature distillation that reduces capacity difference between features
085
               in quantized and full-precision models. So the knowledge from teacher model can be easily
086            transferred to student model.

      2.1   R AW- BASED L OW- LIGHT I MAGE E NHANCEMENT
095
      Because of the merits of raw images discussed in Section 1, they are commonly used for LLIE in
096   extremely dark environments. The pioneering work Chen et al. (2018) builds a large-scale paired
097   short/long exposure raw image dataset for LLIE, dubbed See-in-the-Dark (SID). A U-Net is employed
098   for restoring noisy low-light raw input into bright RGB images. A parallel work DeepISP Schwartz
099   et al. (2019) uses an end-to-end neural network to process low-light raw images, which achieves
100   better visual quality than manufactured ISP. Based on the SID dataset, following work also introduces
101   residual learning Maharjan et al. (2019), self-guidance strategy Gu et al. (2019) and multi-criterion
102   loss Zamir et al. (2021) for single-stage raw to RGB LLIE.
103   Another line of methods decompose the problem of raw-based LLIE into different aspects and design
104   multi-stage networks. EEMEFN Zhu et al. (2020) sequentially performs multi-exposure fusion and
105   edge enhancement for LLIE. LDC Xu et al. (2020) enhances the low-frequency part and reconstructs
106   the high-frequency details of the low-light images in two consecutive stages. MCR Dong et al.
107   (2022) first learns to synthesize monochrome images with additional supervision. Then a dual-branch
      network is leveraged to fuse generated monochrome and color images to produce enhanced RGB

      results. Huang et al. Huang et al. (2022) proposes a raw-guiding exposure enhancement network,
109   which consist of three cascaded U-Nets for unprocessing, denoising and processing. DNF Jin et al.
110   (2023) decouples raw-based LLIE into raw image denoising stage and RGB image color correction
111   stage to mitigate the domain ambiguity. A feedback module enables feature interaction across two

115   LLIE models by designing lightweight network architectures. In this work, we resort to network
116   quantization to achieve efficient LLIE.

118   2.2   N EURAL N ETWORK Q UANTIZATION
119
120   Neural network quantization involves reducing the precision of weights and activations in a neural
121   network, representing them with a lower-bit (usually 2-8 bits) discrete representation Nagel et al.
122   (2021). This process can effectively reduce the model size and computation cost, and it can be
123   incorporated with other network compression techniques like parameter pruning Zhang et al. (2022);
124   Wang & Fu (2023) and knowledge distillation Zhu et al. (2023); Li et al. (2020). There are two
125
      primary paradigms for network quantization: Post-Training Quantization (PTQ) Hubara et al. (2021);
      Li et al. (2021) and Quantization Aware Training (QAT) Zhou et al. (2016); Choi et al. (2018); Li
126
      et al. (2022; 2023); Esser et al. (2020). PTQ methods allow for the efficient quantization of pre-
127
      trained neural networks with minimal data and no retraining. However, they suffer from sub-optimal
128   performance due to fixed parameters and limited fine-tuning capabilities. In this paper, we adopt
129   QAT that retrain the network parameters with simulated quantization and full training data to achieve
130   a better performance in low-bit (i.e., less than 4 bits) quantization. Additionally, 1-bit quantization
131   methods (also known as binary neural networks) are not discussed because they often rely on specific
132   designs to avoid severe performance degradation Liu et al. (2018); Cai et al. (2023) and have different
133   hardware implementations Qin et al. (2023).
134   Low-bit network quantization with QAT has been widely applied to various computer vision tasks,
135   with early efforts primarily focusing on the quantization of classification models Choi et al. (2018);
136   Jacob et al. (2018); Gong et al. (2019); Jung et al. (2019); Esser et al. (2020); Bhalgat et al. (2020).
137   These methods incorporate either a learnable quantization interval Choi et al. (2018) or a learnable
138   step size Esser et al. (2020) within the quantizer, optimizing these parameters along with network
139   weights to minimize task-specific loss Jung et al. (2019). In low-level vision, much work has explored
140   low-bit quantization for super-resolution networks, which typically consist of a head, main body,
141   and upsample tail Qin et al. (2023); Li et al. (2020); Wang et al. (2021). PAMS Li et al. (2020)
142
      introduces a trainable clamp function and proposes a structured knowledge transfer strategy, enabling
      the learning of high-level representations from the full-precision model. FQSR Wang et al. (2021)
143
      fully quantizes all the layers including head and upsample tail in super-resolution networks. DDTB
144
      Zhong et al. (2022) adopts trainable upper and lower bounds for the highly asymmetric activations.
145   DAQ Hong et al. (2022) uses a distribution-aware quantization that defines a quantize function for
146   each channel. QuantSR Qin et al. (2023) leverages a redistribution-driven learnable quantizer to
147   diversify the low-bit quantized representation. A depth-dynamic quantized architecture is designed to
148   achieve resource adaptive inference. In this work, we aim to quantize a U-Net-based model, which is
149   widely used in raw LLIE.

258   after quantization is to find a proper scaling factor s for each activation. Existing QAT methods Choi

      3.3       U NIFORM F EATURE D ISTILLATION
302
303   Inspired by previous work Li et al. (2020); Zhong et al. (2022), incorporating network quantization
304   with knowledge distillation can achieve a better performance. Full-precision networks can learn
305   more representative features, which provide abundant details and high-level semantic information
306   for low-bit quantized networks. The structured knowledge transfer Li et al. (2020); Zhong et al.
307   (2022) used in previous quantized super-resolution networks directly minimize pixel-wise distance of
308   normalized features from full-precision teacher model and low-bit student model. However, there is
309   great capability gap between features from quantized models and their full-precision counterparts,
310   which makes it challenging for low-bit features to mimic float-point features. Existing work Zhu et al.
311
      (2023) also leverages the quantized feature from the full-precision model for knowledge distillation
      in the classification task. Although it makes low-bit models easier to learn the feature representation,
312
      quantizatied float-point features lose the detailed information for enhancing low-light images. In
313
      this work, we propose a uniform feature distillation for feature alignment and knowledge transfer.
314   Specifically, we introduce a full-precision feature uniform module (FUM) to process features from
315   the quantized network, which can be excluded during inference. The FUM projects the low-precision
316   feature to a uniform space with the full-precision features and mitigates the capability disparity.
317   Therefore, our uniform feature distillation facilitates the knowledge transfer from the teacher model
318   to the low-bit student model without losing essential details in the full-precision features, which is

      5      C ONCLUSION
518
519
      In this paper, we propose a low-bit quantization method for raw-based LLIE networks. First, we
520
      present a novel low-bit quantizer DSAQ for the U-Net architecture. In order to match the distribution
521   range of the features concatenated via skip connections, DSAQ employs two sets of quantization
522   parameters to separately quantize the two parts of the activations, thereby better fitting these two
523   different distributions. It also exploits the asymmetric activation quantizer for the skewed features
524   activated by LeakyReLU non-linear function. Second, we introduce uniform feature distillation,
525   which employs a feature uniform module to reduce the capability gap between low-bit features
526   and full-precision features, facilitating knowledge transfer from the teacher model. However, it
527   shows limitations in the 2-bit setting, which is worth to explore in the future work. Extensive
528   experiments demonstrate that our low-bit quantized LLIE model can yield satisfactory results with
529
      low computational and memory costs.
