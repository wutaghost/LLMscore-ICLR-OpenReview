009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            We identify sufficient conditions to avoid known failure modes, including represen-
012            tation, dimensional, cluster and intracluster collapses, occurring in non-contrastive
013            self-supervised learning. Based on these findings, we propose a principled design
014            for the projector and loss function. We theoretically demonstrate that this design
015            introduces an inductive bias that promotes learning representations that are both
016            decorrelated and clustered without explicit enforcing these properties and leading
017
               to improved generalization. To the best of our knowledge, this is the first solution
               that achieves robust training with respect to these failure modes while guarantee-
018
               ing enhanced generalization performance in downstream tasks. We validate our
019
               theoretical findings on image datasets including SVHN, CIFAR10, CIFAR100 and
020            ImageNet-100, and show that our solution, dubbed FALCON, outperforms existing
021            feature decorrelation and cluster-based self-supervised learning methods in terms
022            of generalization to clustering and linear classification tasks.

025   1   I NTRODUCTION
026
027   Self-supervised learning (SSL) has unlocked the potential of learning general-purpose representations
028   from large amounts of unlabeled data. Despite its successes, important challenges remain, hindering
029   the applicability of SSL to a broader spectrum of real-world tasks and its widespread adoption and
030
      democratization. One such challenge is the presence of failure modes occurring during the training
      of SSL models. Several heuristic strategies have been proposed and analyzed in the literature, such
031
      as momentum encoder, stop gradient and asymmetric projector heads (Chen et al., 2022; He et al.,
032
      2020; Grill et al., 2020; Tao et al., 2022; Chen & He, 2021; Tian et al., 2021; Halvagal et al., 2023;
033   Wang et al., 2022). However, these heuristics do not always come with universal guarantees, making
034   it unclear whether failure modes can be avoided in all situations.
035
036
      In this work, we focus on the family of non-contrastive SSL approaches, aiming to distill the essential
      principles to guarantee the avoidance of known failure modes. More concretely, we identify sufficient
037
      conditions to avoid representation, dimensional, cluster, and intracluster collapses, and correspond-
038
      ingly devise a projector and loss function enforcing them by design. In particular, we demonstrate that
039   minimizing invariance to data augmentations while matching priors suffices to avoid representation
040   and cluster collapses, whereas normalized embeddings, orthogonal frozen weights, and large predic-
041   tion outputs in the projector are key to avoiding dimensional and intracluster collapses. Moreover, we
042   prove that these principles are sufficient to guarantee (i) the decorrelation of embeddings, without
043   any explicit computation of their covariance matrix, and (ii) the clustering of embeddings, without
044   the use of specialized clustering layers. We experimentally validate the theory on four image datasets,
045   including SVHN, CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100 and ImageNet-100, showcasing superior performance
046   in terms of training robustness to failure modes and generalization to downstream clustering and
047
      classification tasks compared to popular feature decorrelation and cluster-based SSL.
048   To summarize, our key contributions are:
049
050          • At a conceptual level, we provide sufficient conditions to avoid known failure modes and
051            accordingly devise FALCON, the first FAiLure-proof non-CONtrastive SSL approach.
052          • These conditions are shown to be sufficient to jointly decorrelate and cluster embeddings,
053            thus providing evidence on the feasibility of unifying non-contrastive and cluster-based SSL
               families of approaches.

             • We establish the first connection between SSL and hyperdimensional computing, thus
055            supporting training for large projector outputs (reaching similar order of magnitudes to the
056            size of the dataset).
057
             • At a practical and computational level, we simplify the design and training of non-contrastive
058            SSL and provide a proof-of-concept demonstration of the properties of FALCON.

060   The structure of the paper is organized as follows: In §2, we relate our work to theory of SSL, clarify
061   the relation between different failure modes and review recent efforts aiming to address them. In §3,
062   we provide design principles for the loss and projector head of FALCON. Subsequently, we provide
063
      the theory supporting its design. In §4, we compare our solution to existing feature decorrelation and
      cluster-based SSL strategies and analyze the different properties highlighted by the theory. Finally,
064
      we conclude with §5 by summarizing the main findings and discussing future work.

520   5   C ONCLUSIONS
521
522
523   We have distilled the essential principles of non-contrastive self-supervised learning into the design
524
      of a projector and loss function that prevent known failure modes, including representation, cluster,
      dimensional, and intracluster collapses. This approach enables robust training of a backbone network,
525
      achieving representations that are both decorrelated and clustered. We have also demonstrated that
526
      the resulting solutions improve generalization to supervised downstream tasks when large dictionaries
527   are used in the projector. ‘Collapsing’ to low-level features like color is a potential failure mode
528   in non-contrastive learning (Chen et al., 2020; Caron et al., 2020; He et al., 2020), often mitigated
529   through data augmentation. Exploring the impact of augmentations on generalization is a promising
530   research direction. Another key focus is improving scalability. The main bottleneck lies in storing and
531   using large dictionaries. Leveraging their bipolar nature, instead of handling them as floating-point
532   collections, could significantly enhance efficiency. Finally, other future directions include scaling
533   the experiments to larger networks and datasets, addressing the non-uniform setting and tackling
534   additional learning and downstream tasks.
