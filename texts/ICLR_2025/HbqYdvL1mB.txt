                                                   A BSTRACT
008
009
               Two typical neural models have been extensively studied for operator learning,
010
               learning in spatial space via attention mechanism or learning in spectral space via
011            spectral analysis technique such as Fourier Transform. Spatial learning enables
012            point-level flexibility but lacks global continuity constraint, while spectral learn-
013            ing enforces spectral continuity prior but lacks point-wise adaptivity. This work
014            innovatively combines the continuity prior and the point-level flexibility, with the
015            introduced Point-Calibrated Spectral Transform. It achieves this by calibrating the
016            preset spectral eigenfunctions with the predicted point-wise frequency preference
017            via neural gate mechanism. Beyond this, we introduce Point-Calibrated Spectral
018            Neural Operators, which learn operator mappings by approximating functions with
019
               the point-level adaptive spectral basis, thereby not only preserving the benefits of
               spectral prior but also boasting the superior adaptability comparable to the attention
020
               mechanism. Comprehensive experiments demonstrate its consistent performance
021
               enhancement in extensive PDE solving scenarios.

024   1    I NTRODUCTION
025
026   Partial differential equations (PDEs) are widely used across a wide range of scientific and engineering
027
      tasks, such as airfoil design, plastic structure design, and blood flow simulation. However, traditional
      PDE solvers depend on high-precision meshes and substantial computational requirements, which
028
      significantly impedes efficiency in many engineering applications.
029
030   To resolve these limitations, recent works (Li et al., 2020; Lu et al., 2019; Tripura & Chakraborty,
031   2022) introduce neural operators, a class of data-driven approaches that directly learn mappings
032   between continuous function spaces for solving parametric partial differential equations. The most
033
      performed neural operators could be classified into two groups, i.e., attention-based neural opera-
      tors (Cao, 2021; Hao et al., 2023; Wu et al., 2024) and spectral-based neural operators (Li et al., 2020;
034
      Tran et al., 2021; Gupta et al., 2021). Attention-based methods directly learn operators in the original
035
      physical space, devoid of prior constraints. In contrast, spectral-based methods learn operators in a
036   truncated spectral space via spectral transformation such as Fourier Transform, seamlessly integrating
037   the resolution-invariant prior.
038
039
      Both attention-based neural operators and spectral-based neural operators exhibit distinct advantages
      and limitations. Attention-based neural operators (Hao et al., 2023; Xiao et al., 2023; Wu et al.,
040
      2024) can adapt to various physical systems and obtain consistent leading performance on problems
041
      with sufficient training data amount, benefiting from the flexible spatially point-wise learning on
042   the physical domain. However, the pure data-driven framework without spectral prior limits their
043   generalization capability, thus suffering serious performance drops in scenarios with scarce data,
044   which is common in practical applications. In contrast, Spectral-based neural operators (Li et al.,
045   2020; Kovachki et al., 2023; Tran et al., 2021) can efficiently learn operator mappings between
046   continuous spaces with limited training data, through approximating physical functions in the
047   truncated spectral space. However, the classical spectral processing mechanism lacks point-level
048   flexibility for adaptively handling the spatially varying phenomenon in physical systems. This
049   makes them struggle to resolve complex PDEs and constrains their performance promotion with the
050
      increasing of training data amount. Therefore, both attention-based neural operators and existing
      spectral-based neural operators struggle to manage various PDE solving scenarios.
051
052   This work aims to develop more advanced neural operators that not only have strong generalization
053   capability like previous spectral-based methods (Li et al., 2020; Tran et al., 2021) but also possess
      point-level flexibility like the attention-based methods (Hao et al., 2023; Wu et al., 2024). We present


                                                         1
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


054
      Point-Calibrated Spectral Transform, an improved spectral transform technique that integrates
055   point-wise frequency preference for flexible spectral feature learning. Specifically, we first predict
056   the frequency preference of each physical point via neural gate mechanism, and then the frequency
057   preference is used to calibrate spectral eigenfunctions. This enables feature learning in a point-
058   adaptive spectral space where spatially point-wise status is integrated, rather than point-irrelevant
059   spectral space like previous approaches (Li et al., 2020; Tran et al., 2021). Next, we introduce
060   Point-Calibrated Spectral Mixer based Neural Operator (simply denoted as PCSM), where
061   operator mappings are learned by approximating functions with adaptive spectral basis based on
062   Point-Calibrated Spectral Transform. In PCSM, the spectral prior enables efficient operator learning
063
      even under scarce training data amount, and the point-level calibration enables adaptively handling
      spatially varying phenomena e.g. adding high-frequency features in regions with sharp status changes.
064
065   Extensive experiments are conducted to validate the superiority of Point-Calibrated Spectral Mixer.
066   (a) First, we compare PCSM with previous most performed neural operators on diverse PDE solv-
067   ing problems, demonstrating its leading performance over both existing spectral-based methods
068   and attention-based methods. (b) Additionally, our experimental results validate that PCSM simul-
069
      taneously has the advantages of spectral-based and attention-based neural operators. Similar to
      spectral-based methods, PCSM can be efficiently optimized during training, performs well under
070
      limited training data, and possesses great zero-shot resolution generalization capability. Similar
071
      to attention-based methods, PCSM can flexibly manage different PDE problems and continuously
072   achieves significant performance gains as training data amount increases. And we find that PCSM
073   performs well even under extremely little spectral frequencies, different from previous spectral-based
074   methods that rely on sufficient spectral frequencies. (c) Furthermore, visualization analysis of the
075   learned frequency preference is provided. We find that the learned frequency preference by PCSM
076   can instruct the frequency design for constructing better fixed spectral-based neural operators.
077
      Our core contributions are summarized as follows:
078
079   • We present Point-Calibrated Spectral Transform, pioneeringly learning features in a point-status
080     integrated spectral space, holding potential applications in diverse spectrum-related tasks.
081   • We present Point-Calibrated Spectral Neural Operator (PCSM), which (a) performs well under
082     limited training data and unseen resolutions like spectral-based methods, (b) flexibly handles
083
        various PDEs and efficiently utilizes training data like attention-based methods.
084   • We demonstrate the superiority of PCSM in various scenarios through comprehensive experiments,
085     and find the frequency preference learned by PCSM can help design spectral-based neural operators.

090   2.1.1    P ROBLEM F ORMULATION
091
092
      Following previous works (Li et al., 2020; Lu et al., 2021; Kovachki et al., 2023), we formulate the
      solution of parametric partial differential equations as the operator mapping between two infinite-
093
      dimensional function spaces:
094
095                     G † : A → U, A = {a|a : Ω → Rda }, U = {u|u : Ω → Rdu },                         (1)
096
097
      where Ω denotes the physical domain, da and du represent the channel number of input functions and
      output functions respectively. The function a and u are the state functions defined on the problem
098
      domain. They are differently instantiated for different PDE problems. For example, in the steady-state
099
      problem Darcy Flow, a denotes the diffusion coefficient and u represents the solution function. In
100   the time-series problem Navier-Stokes, a is the vorticity states in previous time steps and u is the
101   vorticity states of following time steps. The operator learning problem is to learn a parameterized
102   surrogate model Gθ† for the operator mapping G † . Specifically, we need to train a neural operator
103
      network Gθ† , which takes (a, g) as input and produces the output function u.
104

131

145
      In this section, we propose a new transformer-based neural operator that achieves superior perfor-
146   mance across various scenarios by integrating the point-level flexibility of attention-based mixers
147   with the spectral prior of spectral-based mixers.

149   2.2.1    L APLACE -B ELTRAMI T RANSFORM
150   While coordinate-based spectral transforms such as Fourier Transform and Wavelet Transform are
151   prevalent in existing neural operators (Li et al., 2020; Tran et al., 2021; Gupta et al., 2021), they are
152   limited by uniform discretization requirements and applicability to regular domains. To address these
153   constraints and enable spectral processing on general physical domains, we introduce the Laplace-
154   Beltrami Transform, following Chen et al. (2023). This approach adapts to irregular geometries and
155   non-uniform meshes, potentially enhancing the versatility and accuracy of neural operators across
156   diverse physical scenarios.

158   Chen et al. (2023) and utilize Laplace-Beltrami Operator (LBO) eigenfunctions (Rustamov et al.,

160   constitute a set of spectral basis (Patanè, 2018) of manifolds, which have been demonstrated as the
161   optimal basis for function approximating on Riemannian manifold (Aflalo et al., 2015). The LBO
      eigenfunctions can be represented as a list of functions ϕi ∈ RN ×1 , where each ϕi is an eigenfunction

201   2.2.2     P OINT-C ALIBRATED L APLACE -B ELTRAMI T RANSFORM
202   We propose the Point-Calibrated Laplace-Beltrami Transform to enhance spectral neural operators
203   by combining the spatial learning of attention-based mixers with the strong priors of spectral-based
204   mixers. By integrating Laplace-Beltrami Operator (LBO) eigenfunctions with point-wise calibration,
205   we allow frequency selection to vary across physical points. This mechanism can effectively capture
206   spatially varying phenomena in PDE systems. Our method maintains the computational efficiency
207   and generalization strengths of spectral approaches while incorporating the spatial adaptability of
208   attention-based methods, boosting performance across diverse PDE problems.

225   As shown in Figure. 2, the point-wise calibrated eigenvector modulates ϕi individually for each point,
226   integrating varied frequency preferences across physical points. Unlike the standard Laplace-Beltrami
227   Transform, where each point’s importance is tied solely to its geometric location, our approach
228   considers both location and physical state. This allows emphasis on points experiencing significant
229   physical changes and enables different spectral modulations for latent features across layers.
230   Discussion. Prior adaptive frequency selection methods (Guibas et al., 2021; George et al., 2022;
231   Li & Yang, 2023) attempt to directly select frequencies in the spectral domain, resulting in a shared
232   frequency filter for all points, labeled as global-level frequency selection. Conversely, we cultivate
233   spectral gates in the spatial domain and utilize the gates during spectral transformation, allowing each
234   point to select its own suited frequencies, labeled as point-level frequency selection.
235   Point-Calibrated Spectral Transform enjoys three advantages: (a) PCSM automatically determines
236   appropriate frequency ranges and combinations for various PDE problems, adapting to domain
237   geometry and operator types without manual spectrum design. This allows flexible frequency
238   assignment to each point based on both location and physical state. (b) PCSM combines the efficient
239   convergence and strong generalization of spectral-based methods with the scalability of attention-
240   based approaches. This fusion enables effective performance across diverse PDE problems and data
241   scales. (c) The learned spectral gates reflect the frequency preference of physical points, which can
242
      be used for additional applications such as guiding spectral design for fixed spectral models.

477   4    R ELATED W ORK
478
479
      Spectral-based Operator Learning. Stem from Li et al. (2020), numerous works have explored
480   learning operator mappings in spectrum space, which significantly reduces learning difficulty through
481   efficient function approximation with spectral basis function. FNO (Li et al., 2020) learns operators
482   in fourier spectral space, LNO (Cao et al., 2024) learns in laplacian spectral space, and WMT (Gupta
483   et al., 2021) learns in wavelet spectral space. In addition, a line of works investigates the issue
484   of spectral-based neural operators, including the complex physical domain processing (Li et al.,
485   2023c; Bonev et al., 2023; Liu et al., 2024), computational efficiency enhancement (Poli et al., 2022;
      Tran et al., 2021), multi-scale feature processing (Rahman et al., 2022; Zhang et al., 2024), and


                                                          9
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


486
      generalization capability improvement (Brandstetter et al., 2022; Yue et al., 2024). Moreover, Li et al.
487   (2024) and Du et al. (2023) explored physics-driven neural operators.
488
489   Prior studies, however, employ static spectral feature processing, which restricts the networks’ point-
490
      level adaptability and makes the network struggle to handle spatially varying phenomena. In contrast,
      we calibrate the spectral eigenfunctions with the point-wise frequency preference learned by spectral
491
      gates, significantly enhancing the feature learning flexibility of spectral-based methods.
492
493   Attention-based Operator Learning. Recently, learning operator mappings based on attention
494   mechanism (Vaswani, 2017) draws extensive studies. The primary benefits of attention are the
495   capability to handle any physical domains and point-level flexibility for learning high-quality operators
496
      for diverse PDEs. To resolve the quadratic complexity of attention mechanism, previous works (Li
      et al., 2023a; Cao, 2021; Hao et al., 2023) employ efficient attentions for operator learning, and
497
      Factformer (Li et al., 2023b) enhance model efficiency with multidimensional factorized attention
498
      mechanism. Besides, Liu et al. (2023) introduce a hierarchical transformer based neural operator for
499   learning better multi-scale features, Xiao et al. (2023) alleviate the overfitting of neural operators with
500   orthogonal attention mechanism. In addition, Transolver (Wu et al., 2024) introduces a new operator
501   learning framework by first predicting slices and then learning attentions between different slices.
502
      While attention-based neural operators achieve impressive performance on various PDEs (Wu et al.,
503
      2024), their lack of spectral constraints results in subpar performance under limited training data
504
      amount and unseen resolutions samples as compared to spectral-based methods. Instead, we develop
505   point-adaptive spectral processing for learning neural operators, thus simultaneously preserving the
506   continuity prior of spectral-based methods and point-level flexibility of attention-based methods.
507
508
      Neural Gate Mechanisms. Gate modules are widely employed in deep neural models. In Mixture-
      of-Experts models (Jacobs et al., 1991; Shazeer et al., 2017), the gate layer determines which expert
509
      networks to activate for processing each input. Besides, gated recurrent unit (Cho, 2014) uses the
510
      gate layer to control the flow of information, enabling the model to update the memory cell based on
511   the relevance of the previous and current inputs. Additionally, Dauphin et al. (2017) shows the gate
512   mechanism can help select words or features for next word prediction in language modeling.
513
514
      This work introduces the concept of Calibrated Spectral Transform via neural gate mechanism, which
      performs spectral transform integrating point-wise frequency preference. We show its significance for
515
      operator learning, by providing point-level flexibility akin to attention mechanism while maintaining
516
      the advantages of spectral basis.

519   5    L IMITATION AND F UTURE W ORK
520
521   Despite obtaining superior performance in a variety of scenarios, the introduced Point-Calibration
522   methods inevitably suffer certain limitations that do not affect the core conclusion of this work, and
523   they are worth further exploration in future works.
524   • Firstly, the introduced spectral feature calibration technique remains a fundamental design, without
525     considering demands in particular circumstances. Therefore, it is meaningful to develop more
526     customized calibration methods such as learning frequency preference with physical-driven losses.
527   • Additionally, although this work has explored a broad PDE solving problems, numerous real-
528     world physical systems still warrant further investigation. And it is significant to investigate the
529     application of Point-Calibrated Transform in general deep learning tasks such as time-series signal
530     prediction and computer vision learning.

532   6    C ONCLUSION
533
534   This work presents Point-Calibrated Spectral Mixer based Neural Operators (PCSM), enabling
535   the point-level adaptivity in spectral-based neural operators by integrating point-wise frequency
536   preference for spectral processing. The proposed Calibrated Spectral Transform holds significant
537   potential applications in numerous spectrum-related deep models. Comprehensive experiments
538   validate the superior performance in various PDE solving scenarios of PCSM, benefiting from the
539   combination of spectral prior of spectral-based methods and point-level adaptivity of attention-based
      methods. We hope PCSM can provide insights for future exploration of PDE solving tasks.

722   frequencies, we uniformly take N k frequencies from the lowest N k × r frequencies, where r is
723   the sparsity ratio. Higher r indicates using more high frequencies and we set r = 2 and r = 4 for
724   different layers.
725
      We find that using Sparse-Frequency Spectral Mixer in partial network layers effectively improves
726   the performance of fixed spectral methods. However, such manual frequency design relies on prior
727   knowledge and repeated experiments to select appropriate layers and sparsity ratios, and additional
728   computational cost is required for calculating LBO eigenfunctions with N K × r frequencies. To
729   address this issue, we experiment fixed spectral design guided by learned spectral gates of PCSM, as
730   shown in Table. 5.

       Spectral-based neural modules. We note that the spectral processing has been extensively employed
       as basic neural modules for operator learning (Li et al., 2020; Chen et al., 2023; Tran et al., 2021), as
1176
       well as general deep learning tasks (Guibas et al., 2021; Lee-Thorp et al., 2021). Unlike the classical
1177
       neural modules such as CNN (LeCun et al., 1995), RNN (Chung et al., 2014), and Self-Attention
1178   mechanism (Vaswani, 2017) that directly mix features of different tokens or spatial locations in the
1179   spatial domain, these spectral-based neural modules learn features in the spectral domain.
1180
1181
       Specifically, consider the input features x ∈ RN ×d , where N is the number of tokens or spatial points
       and d is the number of latent dimensions. The spectral-based neural module first transforms the
1182
       features in the spatial domain into the spectral domain via spectral transform, after a few processes
1183
       such as MLPs, the features are then transformed back to spatial domains. The spectral-based neural
1184   modules could be defined as follows:
1185                                       mixer

1187   where T and T −1 represent the spectral transform and inverse spectral transform respectively. They
       could be instantiated in multiple ways, such as (inverse) Fourier Transform in Li et al. (2020) and

1199   feature x̂i via matrix multiplication.
1200
       One property of the Laplace-Beltrami Spectral Transform is that the eigenfunctions associated with
1201   lower eigenvalues transform the signal to features of lower frequencies, whereas those with higher
1202   eigenvalues transform them to features of higher frequencies. Therefore, the frequency features
1203   x̂∗ ∈ Rd with lower indexes correspond to lower-frequency features, while those with high indexes
1204   correspond to higher-frequency features.
1205
       The roles of high-frequency and low-frequency features in operator learning. Intuitively, different
1206
       frequency features help learn various aspects of function transformations for operator learning. As
1207   illustrated in Figure. 2, in the eigenfunctions with smaller eigenvalues (e.g. ϕ56 , a lower frequency
1208   eigenfunction), the variation of importance across different spatial locations is smoother, which
1209   facilitates the learning of coarser-grained, slowly varying function transformations. Conversely, in the
1210   eigenfunction with larger eigenvalues (e.g. ϕ72 , a higher frequency eigenfunction), the signal changes
1211   rapidly across different locations, aiding in learning finer-grained, faster-changing transformations.
1212
       As shown in Figure. 2, unlike conventional frequency eigenfunctions that are independent of the
1213   physical state of points, the introduced Point-Calibrated Spectral Transform calculates the features of
1214   different frequencies integrated with the physical state. Specifically, in Figure. 2 (b), regions with
1215   physical quantities changing rapidly could be automatically assigned stronger importance in the
1216   calculation of higher-frequency features. This could help them achieve enhanced prediction accuracy
1217   by integrating more local details.
