009                                             Abstract
010
011           We consider decentralized time-varying stochastic optimization problems
012           where each of the functions held by the nodes has a finite sum structure.
013           Such problems can be efficiently solved using variance reduction techniques.
014           Our aim is to explore the lower complexity bounds (for communication
015           and number of stochastic oracle calls) and find optimal algorithms. The
016           paper studies strongly convex and nonconvex scenarios. To the best of our
017           knowledge, variance reduced schemes and lower bounds for time-varying
018           graphs have not been studied in the literature. For nonconvex objectives, we
019
              obtain lower bounds and develop an optimal method GT-PAGE. For strongly
              convex objectives, we propose the first decentralized time-varying variance-
020
              reduction method ADOM+VR and establish lower bound in this scenario,
021
              highlighting the open question of matching the algorithms complexity and
022           lower bounds even in static network case.

097   Related Work. Decentralized optimization over static and time-varying networks has been
098   actively developing in recent years. In (Scaman et al., 2017), dual-based methods and lower
099   bounds for (non-stochastic) strongly convex optimization over static graphs were proposed.
100   Optimal primal methods were obtained in (Kovalev et al., 2020b). For time-varying networks,
101   non-accelerated primal (Nedic et al., 2017) and dual (Maros and JaldГ©n, 2018) methods were
102   proposed. After that, accelerated algorithms were given in (Kovalev et al., 2021b) for dual
103   oracle and in (Kovalev et al., 2021a; Li and Lin, 2021) for primal oracle. These methods
104
      match the lower complexity bounds for time-varying graphs developed in (Kovalev et al.,
      2021a).
105

107   such as SAGA (Defazio et al., 2014) and SVRG (Johnson and Zhang, 2013) allow to

119   In strongly-convex decentralized optimization over static graphs, optimal dual variance
120   reduced method ADFS was proposed in (Hendrikx et al., 2019). The corresponding lower

      of this algorithm remains an open question. For variational inequalities, variance reduction is

125
      time-varying networks. See an overview of methods for strongly-convex objectives in Table 1.

127   In the nonconvex case, the result of first application of variance reduction and gradient

129
      2020). Later, algorithms GT-SAGA (Xin et al., 2021b), GT-HSGD (Xin et al., 2021a) and

      In this section, we propose new methods for decentralized finite-sum optimization: Algorithm 1
203
      for strongly convex case and optimal Algorithm 2 for nonconvex case. Both algorithms use
204   a variance reduction technique. The main idea of variance reduced methods is a special
205   gradient estimator. The estimator is computed w.r.t. a snapshot of the full gradient. If
206   the objective is a sum of q functions, one recomputes the full gradient (over all samples)
207   once in O(q) iterations (Johnson and Zhang, 2013; Allen-Zhu, 2017). In a loopless approach
208   (Kovalev et al., 2020a) the full gradient is computed with a probability of order O(1/q) at
209   each iteration. In this paper, we use the latter technique.
210   We measure the complexity in two ways: number of communications and number of stochastic
211   oracle calls. The computational complexity of the algorithm iterations can be controlled
212   using mini-batching of the gradient. That is, we take b gradient estimations and average
213   them. If the batch size is large, the number of algorithm iterations decreases, but the number
