009                                               A BSTRACT
010
011            Among the most commonly utilized parameter-efficient fine-tuning (PEFT) meth-
012            ods, LoRA and its variations have achieved significant popularity. The Vector-
013            based Random Matrix Adaptation (VeRA), one typical variant, utilizes random
014            weights and projections to reduce the number of trainable parameters greatly.
015            However, it requires additional GPU memory and computational resources, prob-
016            ably resulting in a lack of scalability that leads to performance bottlenecks in com-
017            plex tasks. Besides, the inappropriate initialization of random matrices may affect
018
               model performance. To address these problems, we propose a new method called
               Vector Segmented and Recombined Adaptation (SeRA). SeRA segments input
019
               vectors into sub-vectors for individual dimensionality reduction, then introduces
020
               a square matrix to combine the information from the reduced sub-vectors, and fi-
021            nally expands the dimensionality independently to adapt the size of pre-trained
022            model. SeRA allows for flexible increase of trainable parameters to enhance
023            performance in complex tasks, and avoids the problem caused by random ma-
024            trices initialization. Through evaluations on the image classification, cross-modal
025            image-text retrieval, instruction-tuning and GLUE benchmark, we demonstrate
026            the scalability and efficiency of SeRA. Furthermore, we utilize Singular Value
027            Decomposition on the adaptation matrices of SeRA, to analyze how the informa-
028            tion characteristics of the matrices change in different ranks and tasks. The results
029
               can serve as the guide for selecting appropriate parameter amounts in different
               tasks.

      1   I NTRODUCTION
033
034
      With the rapid development of intelligent models, the demands for them from society and commerce
035
      have become increasingly diverse. Although general models like GPT-4 (OpenAI, 2023) can meet
036   most daily needs, there is still considerable room for improvement in specialized domains. For
037   example, Codex (Chen et al., 2021), which is a code generator developed by OpenAI based on GPT,
038   demonstrates superior performance to general models after being fine-tuned on open-source code
039   data from GitHub.
040
      Compared to full-parameter fine-tuning, LoRA (Hu et al., 2022) is a simple and efficient fine-tuning
041
      method. It introduces low-rank matrices, updating only a small number of parameters while keeping
042   most pre-trained parameters fixed, to achieve good performance in specific tasks. Many variants of
043   LoRA have been proposed to enhance parameter efficiency or performance. VeRA (Kopiczko et al.,
044   2024), a recently proposed efficient LoRA variant, uses “scaling vectors” to adjust frozen random
045   matrices shared across layers, achieving comparable performance with only one-tenth parameters
046   compared to LoRA. Although models utilizing random weights and projections can significantly
047   reduce parameter usage (Peng et al., 2021; Ramanujan et al., 2020), the fixed, randomly initialized
048   matrices still occupy GPU memory, increasing resource consumption, which may limit its scalabil-
049   ity. We report VeRA’s GPU memory consumption in the Appendix B. Additionally, experiments
050
      show that VeRA may fail to converge under certain seed initializations.
051   In recent fine-tuning study, MELoRA (Ren et al., 2024) and MoSLoRA (Wu et al., 2024) have
052   demonstrated remarkable advantages in terms of parameter efficiency and flexibility, respectively.
053   MELoRA achieves efficient feature extraction by segmenting and reducing the dimensionality of
      the input vector, while MoSLoRA introduces a matrix adjustment mechanism to bolster the model’s

054
      adaptability to intricate tasks. Drawing inspiration from the multi-head attention mechanism, this
055   paper proposes a novel method Vector Segmented and Recombined Adaptation (SeRA), which for-
056   mally integrates these two techniques. SeRA features two key steps as follows. (1) Vector seg-
057   mentation: the input vectors of the fine-tuning layers are split into several sub-vectors. SeRA
058   independently reduces and expands their dimensionalities, reducing the number of SeRA’s param-
059   eters. (2) Sub-vector aggregation: after dimensionality reduction, a square matrix is introduced
060   to adjust the reduced sub-vectors. This process is equivalent to expanding their dimensionalities
061   and adding them together, which fuses the information of the sub-vectors, ensuring performance
062   while maintaining efficiency. This method aims to provide a flexible and efficient solution that can
063
      navigate the evolving complexity of tasks. Take the autonomous driving image classification task
      as an example, a fine-tuning method is required to quickly adapt to data changes in order to iterate
064
      the model in the early stages of training, which requires the method to be highly efficient; as the
065
      scale of the task increases, the lack of scalability may lead to performance bottlenecks. We design
066   increasingly complex classification modes to simulate the real situation for experiment, and show
067   that SeRA can adapt well to this increasingly complex task.
068
069
      Hu et al. (2022) has shown that for tasks requiring only minor parameter adjustments to achieve
      good performance, increasing the rank of trainable adaptation matrix does not improve performance.
070
      However, many studies have argued that increasing the rank of the adaptation matrix leads to better
071
      model performance in memory-intensive tasks, so to analyze the reasons for this, we conducted
072   experiments across tasks with different ranks with SeRA. By applying singular value decomposition
073   (SVD) to the fine-tuned adaptation matrix, we aim to explain this phenomenon from the perspective
074   of singular vector subspaces and singular values. ALL in all, the key innovations and contributions
075   of this paper are as follows:
076
077          • We propose SeRA, a scalable and efficient method that avoids the problems caused by
078            random weights and projections. We demonstrate its effectiveness through image classi-
079
               fication, cross-modal image-text retrieval, instruction-tuning, and natural language under-
               standing (GLUE), as well as its scalability in the image classification.
080
081          • Using SeRA, we conducted experiments across tasks with varying ranks, applying sin-
082            gular value decomposition (SVD) to analyze how the rank of adaptation matrix affects
083
               information and performance across different tasks. This analysis will aid in selecting the
               appropriate number of trainable parameters for different tasks.

086   2   R ELATED W ORK
087
088   Multi-Head Attention (MHA) Multi-Head Attention (MHA) is a core component of the Trans-
089   former model, first introduced by Vaswani et al. (2017). It enhances the model’s ability to capture
090   complex patterns by computing correlations in different subspaces in parallel across multiple atten-
091   tion heads. Each head learns distinct attention patterns, and the results are concatenated and pro-
092   jected back to the original dimension. This mechanism significantly improves the model’s capability
093
      to capture global information. Subsequent research has further expanded the theory and applications
      of MHA. For instance, Cordonnier et al. (2020) demonstrated that multi-head attention has the ca-
094
      pability to approximate convolutional kernels, providing additional insights into its representational
095
      power. Various optimization strategies for MHA have been proposed, such as hierarchical attention
096   mechanisms (Yang et al., 2020) and sparse attention mechanisms (Child et al., 2019), which pri-
097   marily aim to improve computational efficiency and model adaptability. SeRA introduces a novel
098   lightweight adaptation approach by segmenting input vectors, which is closely related to the group-
099   ing computation concept in multi-head attention (MHA). Additionally, the matrices in SeRA share
100   the same optimization design objectives as the projection matrices in MHA, further enhancing the
101   model’s representational capacity across different tasks.
102   Vector-based Random Matrix Adaptation (VeRA) VeRA is a more efficient method than
103   LoRA, which achieves efficient training by introducing trainable “scaling vectors” and freezing
104   random matrices during training, achieving comparable performance to LoRA in GLUE with only
105   one-tenth of LoRA’s parameters. However, despite the low number of fine-tuned parameters in
106   VeRA, the random matrix still needs to be involved in the computation and save the activations
107   in the GPU. When the “scaling vectors” are relatively large, the random matrix takes up a lot of
      memory, limiting the scalability of VeRA.

      Recent different variants of LoRA Since LoRA was introduced, numerous improved variants
109   have been proposed, with previous work focusing on three main areas: Firstly, enhancing LoRA’s
110   parameter efficiency, which further reduce parameter usage without sacrificing performance (Zhou
111   et al., 2024; Zhang et al., 2023a; Kopiczko et al., 2024; Ren et al., 2024); Secondly, addressing the
112   limitations of LoRA’s low-rank update in certain tasks by increasing the matrices’s rank without
113   adding extra trainable parameters (Jiang et al., 2024; Wu et al., 2024); Lastly, improving LoRA’s
114   performance through optimized training strategies (Hayou et al., 2024; Zhang et al., 2023b; Liu
115   et al., 2024). While many optimization strategies can be applied simultaneously, balancing the
116   goals of parameter efficiency and scalability remains a challenge. An scalable and efficient method
117
      capable of handling tasks of varying complexity forms the foundation of our proposed SeRA.

      This analysis compares the performance of different fine-tuning methods on the image classification
204   task, choosing the Road Surface Classification Dataset (RSCD) which is a valuable resource for
205   research in the field of autonomous driving (Zhao et al., 2023). RSCD contains about one million
206   image data, due to time and budget constraints, we performed a random selection of 10% of the
207   training data from each category in the RSCD and used the full validation set to test. ViTLarge
208   (Dosovitskiy et al., 2021), which was pre-trained on 21K imagenet, was chosen as the pre-trained
209   model.
210   The RSCD categorizes road surface conditions into three main attributes: friction level, road ma-
211   terial, and road unevenness. The friction level attribute is subdivided into six categories based on
212   varying weather conditions: dry, wet, water, fresh snow, melted snow, and ice. The road mate-
213   rial attribute includes asphalt, concrete, mud, and gravel, while road unevenness is classified into
214   smooth, slight unevenness, and severe unevenness, depending on the road’s undulation amplitude.
215   The dataset defines its classes by combining these three attributes. The road material and uneven-
      ness annotations are absent when the friction level is fresh snow, melted snow, or ice. Additionally,

346
347   We evaluated our method on the General Language Understanding Evaluation (GLUE) benchmark
348   (Wang et al., 2019) using RoBERTalarge model (Liu et al., 2019). In addition to LoRA and VeRA, we
349   also compare SeRA to the following baselines. Adapter tuning: Initially introduced by Houlsby
350   et al. (2019), it involves integrating adapter layers between the self-attention and MLP modules
351   followed by a residual connection. This configuration, denoted as AdapterH, includes two fully
352
      connected layers and a nonlinearity. A variation, AdapterP by Pfeiffer et al. (2021), employs the
      adapter layer only after the MLP module and subsequent to a LayerNorm. LoRA-FA: This baseline
353
      freezes the A matrix during training and is able to reduce the number of parameters by about half
354

519   5   C ONCLUSION
520
521
      In this paper, we propose a scalable and efficient PEFT method, called SeRA. It saves a large number
      of trainable parameters by segmenting the input vectors into multiple sub-vectors then calculating
522
      them separately, and recombines the information of sub-vectors by introducing a square matrix to
523
      improve the expressive power of the method. The performance of SeRA is demonstrated in a wide
524   range of experiments. We show that different tasks have different requirements on the number of
525   trainable parameters, and analyze the reasons from the perspective of singular value and singular
526   vector subspace. Our method can be combined with a variety of advanced training strategies, such
527   as AdaLoRA (Zhang et al., 2023b) and LoRA+ (Hayou et al., 2024). The variants of LoRA are rich
528   and diverse. Investigating how to combine multiple advanced methods to ultimately come up with
529   a widely applicable and efficient method is a promising research direction in the field of PEFT. In
530   addition to the visual and textual domains, in this paper, we have also conducted experiments with
531   SeRA in the multimodal domain with excellent performance. We haven’t completely analyzed the
532
      reasons. It is worthwhile to continue exploring and investigating the superiority of this method in
      the multimodal domain.
