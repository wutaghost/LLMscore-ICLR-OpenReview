                                                     A BSTRACT
010
011
                Neural Networks (NN) with ReLU activation functions have been used to model
012             multiparametric quadratic optimization problems (mp-QP) in diverse engineering
013             applications. Researchers have suggested leveraging the piecewise affine property
014             of deep NN models to solve mp-QP with linear constraints, which also exhibit
015             piecewise affine behaviour. However, traditional deep NN applications to mp-QP
016             fall short of providing optimal and feasible predictions, even when trained on large
017             datasets. This study proposes a semi-supervised NN (SSNN) architecture that di-
018             rectly represents the mathematical structure of the global solution function. In
019             contrast to generic NN training approaches, the proposed SSNN method derives
020
                a large proportion of model weights directly from the physical characteristics of
                the system, producing more accurate solutions despite significantly smaller train-
021
                ing data sets. Since many energy management problems are formulated as QP,
022
                the proposed approach has been applied in energy systems to demonstrate proof
023             of concept. Model performance in terms of solution accuracy and speed of pre-
024             dictions was compared against a commercial solver and a generic DNN model
025             based on classical training. Results show KKT sufficient conditions for SSNN
026             consistently outperform generic NN architectures with classical training using far
027             less data, including when tested on extreme, out-of-training distribution test data.
028             Given its speed advantages over traditional solvers, the SSNN model can quickly
029             produce optimal and feasible solutions within a second for millions of input pa-
030             rameters sampled from a distribution of stochastic demands and renewable gener-
031
                ator dispatches, which can be used for simulations and long term planning.

      1    I NTRODUCTION
034
035   There has been increasing interest in using Neural Networks (NN) to predict the solutions to com-
036   plex nonlinear optimization problems in energy management, chemistry, control theory, and other
037   domains. Effectively, this goal amounts to estimating a global solution function 1 that predicts opti-
038   mal solutions based on a given set of input parameters, such as the right hand side (RHS) vector and
039   cost coefficients of an optimization model. Most applications treat the NN model as a black-box and
040   employ standard training methods using datasets of input-output pairs, representing particular val-
041   ues of system parameters and corresponding optimal solutions. Most also ignore the mathematical
042   structure of the underlying function and utilize a generic NN architecture to approximate solutions.
043
      Such modelling approaches necessitate large, computationally expensive training datasets to achieve
      satisfactory performance, yet cannot guarantee the feasibility or optimality of results.
044
045   The multiparametric programming literature similarly seeks to represent the global solution function
046   to optimization problems by characterizing the optimal solutions as a function of feasible problem
047   parameters, and identifying the regions of parameter space where these functions are valid (Pis-
048   tikopoulos et al., 2020). Recent work has attempted to integrate multiparametric programming with
049
      DNNs, based on recognition of their similar underlying mathematical characteristics (Karg & Lu-
      cia, 2020; Katz et al., 2020). Specifically, for multiparametric Linear Programming (mp-LP) and
050
      Quadratic Programming (mp-QP) with linear constraints, the optimal primal and dual solutions are
051
      piecewise linear (PWL) functions of the parameters (Pistikopoulos et al., 2020). Similarly, a NN
052

054
      with ReLU activation functions is a general form of PWL function with trainable weights and biases
055   (Montufar et al., 2014). In theory, therefore, for any mp-LP or mp-QP, one should be able to find an
056   optimal set of weights and biases such that a NN can exactly represent the corresponding solution
057   function (Karg & Lucia, 2020).
058
      In practice, however, DNN applications of multiparametric programming face significant training
059
      challenges to guarantee global solution optimality and feasibility. First, obtaining the optimal NN
060
      weights and biases is difficult due to nonconvexity of the training loss function, whereby multiple
061   sets of weights and biases can result in different local minima for a given dataset. Second, NN
062   prediction accuracy is highly dependent on the training set, which makes it hard to generalize pre-
063   dictions outside the training distribution. Overcoming this problem to find the global solution func-
064   tion requires sampling very large datasets that cover all feasible regions of the parameters, which
065   is impractical for problems with high dimensions. Specifically, for an n dimensional domain, con-
066   structing a dataset that includes only boundary points for each feasible region requires sampling 2n
067   points, which would still be insufficient because additional interior points are needed within each
068   region for good estimation.
069   To address these concerns, this paper proposes a semi-supervised NN (SSNN) architecture and train-
070   ing procedure for mp-QP problems that directly reflects the mathematical structure of the global
071   solution function. The proposed approach dramatically reduces training dataset demands, while
072   satisfying sufficient optimality and feasibility requirements. In general, while the solution function
073   to mp-QP for uncertain parameters is piecewise linear, the slope changes in the solution function
074   can be decomposed into two components: piecewise linear changes in the value of dual variables
075
      associated with inequality constraints, and the remaining terms that form a linear function. The
      proposed SSNN implements these components separately in a single NN architecture, whose calcu-
076
      lations can be easily parallelized using GPU to predict solutions for a large set of input parameters.
077
      The proposed SSNN approach was used to model DC optimal power flow (DC-OPF) problems in
078   the domain of electricity power management for proof-of-concept support.
079
080   Unlike black-box NN models, the proposed SSNN for QP is largely constructed unsupervised by
081
      deriving NN weights directly from the problem. The coefficients are calculated prior to the train-
      ing by expanding the Lagrangian function with each possible combination of binding constraints,
082
      calculating partial derivatives and inverting the resulting Jacobian matrices of the derivatives of
083
      the Lagrangian function. Theoretically, the prior computation of coefficients can be infeasible for
084   large problems, as the number of constraint combinations increases exponentially. Nevertheless, in
085   practical applications, many constraint combinations are not possible due to characteristics of the
086   problem, such as the topology of the electricity grid in energy management. To scale down the set
087   of all possible binding constraint combinations, we use a commercial solver to solve the problem
088   for a set of input system parameters sampled equidistantly from minimum to maximum feasible
089   input demand for a number of arbitrarily chosen parameters. Note that this procedure is used only
090   to filter out unused cases and is run only about 500 times for even the largest empirical test systems
091   investigated. Once the set of possible binding constraints is discovered, this information is used to
092
      construct datasets with minimal computational costs without using solvers in a loop.
093   Our study makes the following contributions:
094
095
             • A semi-supervised NN model is proposed to provide optimal solutions to QP with inequal-
               ity constraints. The model is based on an explainable architecture that aligns with the
096
               piecewise linearity of the global solution function, and is supported by a training approach
097
               that decomposes the learning process to sub-problems to overcome the training challenges.
098
             • The proposed model generalizes outside the training distribution well, thanks to a procedure
099
               that samples model weights analytically from the underlying optimization problem.
100
101
             • The model is applied to DC-OPF with upper and lower bound generator limits, and provides
               precise predictions that satisfy KKT optimality and feasibility conditions. The model can
102
               rapidly create a distribution of optimal and feasible solutions to a large dataset sampled
103
               from an empirical distribution of uncertain demands and renewable generator dispatches.
104
105   The paper is organized as follows: Section 2 discusses related studies in the literature. Section 3 de-
106   tails our methodology, discusses how QP solutions form a PWL function that can be decomposed to
107   linear and piecewise parts, and outlines our semi-supervised NN architecture and training procedure.
      Section 4 presents numerical results, and section 5 discusses conclusions and future work.

      2    R ELATED W ORK
109
110
      Using NNs for surrogate modeling of optimization problems is an established field with various
111
      applications in engineering. The approaches leverage DNNs as universal function approximators
112   to estimate the nonlinear relationship between the problem parameters and the optimal solutions,
113   which has found success in power management (Nellikkath & Chatzivasileiadis, 2022; Fioretto et al.,
114   2020; Lotfi & Pirnia, 2022), control Karg & Lucia (2020) and chemistry (see Katz et al. (2020) for
115   a review). Despite their advantages as nonlinear solvers, guaranteeing feasability and optimality is
116   challenging, and finding the best model parameters from a large parameter space is data-greedy with
117   significant computational training burdens.
118   To improve feasibility, Zamzam & Baker (2020) and Chen et al. (2023) proposed two-stage models
119   that first predict optimal solutions with a NN and then post-process the solutions to enforce feasi-
120   bility. Lotfi & Pirnia (2022) proposed a NN architecture that enforces optimal dispatch generator
121   limits using a sigmoid activation function in the output layer. Fioretto et al. (2020) processed optimal
122   solutions through sequentially connected sub-networks reflecting problem characteristics. Physics
123   Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) use DNNs that are also trained to satisfy KKT optimality condi-
124   tions, resulting in reduced dataset requirements, more accurate predictions with less violations, and
125   more generalizability power (Nellikkath & Chatzivasileiadis, 2022). Instead of predicting optimal
126
      solutions directly, others proposed training different types of classifiers to predict active constraints,
      thereby reducing the problem dimensions, providing more accurate predictions and quicker solu-
127
      tions (e.g. Misra et al. (2021); Deka & Misra (2019); Ng et al. (2018); Chen et al. (2022)).
128
129   Examining mathematical properties of mp-LP and mp-QP problems under a multiparametric setting
130   led researchers to recognize their similarity to the PWL characteristics of DNNs. Katz et al. (2020)
131   attempted to integrate PWL behaviour of the problem solution analytically. Karg & Lucia (2020)
132
      examined the functional similarities between DNNs and model predictive control (MPC) laws, and
      trained a model to estimate MPC solutions for a time variant system. Similarly, Huo et al. (2022)
133
      proposed an approach to integrate MPC laws of a MILP problem for a microgrid system. However,
134
      none of these works compare optimality and feasibility between their models and traditional solver
135   benchmarks or DNN based approaches. This paper proposes a custom NN architecture that closely
136   aligns with the PWL structure of the global solution function to improve accuracy and generalizabil-
137   ity of mp-QP predictions.

358   4.1   T EST R ESULTS
359
360   Our method was evaluated using IEEE-6, IEEE-30, IEEE-57 bus test systems. We used our SSNN
361   model to predict optimal solutions to the DC-OPF problem, and compared them to solutions pre-
362   dicted by a NN model trained using classical methods (i.e., with paired input-output data represent-
363
      ing particular values of system parameters and corresponding optimal solutions), and also to solu-
      tions generated by the Gurobi solver (v10.0.3). The SSNN models were trained using only 1000 data
364
      points, and validated and tested on datasets of 1000 and 4000 observations, respectively. To generate
365
      training data, demand of all buses was set to 0.01, then one bus was chosen and its load incremen-
366   tally increased from 0 to the maximum allowed by the system, and the procedure was repeated for
367   one other bus. For the classically trained NN model, we generated training, validation and test sets
368   of 5000, 1000 and 4000 observations, respectively, by setting the demand to the default load values
369   in the Pandapower package and multiplying with a constant sampled from Uniform(0.6, 1.4). Two

      5       C ONCLUSION AND F UTURE R ESEARCH
463
      This paper proposes an explainable semi-supervised NN modeling approach for multiparametric QP
464
      optimization problems with linear constraints that provides precise solutions with generalizability
465   power. The model aligns the piecewise linear nature of NN architecture with the underlying mathe-
466   matical structure of the optimization problem by deriving the model weights directly from the linear
467   sensitivity function of solutions in each critical region defined by the sets of binding constraints. To
468   train the model, a cost efficient data generation approach was proposed that bypasses using solvers in
469   a loop. As a proof of concept, the SSNN modeling approach was applied to DC-OPF problems with
470   upper and lower bound generator limits. SSNN models trained on only 1000 data points achieved
471   higher KKT optimality and feasibility results than generic DNN models trained classically on five
472   times as much data. The SSNN models generated optimal solutions to large input sets much faster
473
      than the Gurobi solver, with little sacrifice in KKT performance. As such, the models can rapidly
      create a distribution of optimal and feasible solutions to inputs sampled from empirical distributions
474
      of uncertain demand and renewable generator dispatches.
475
476   This work has focused on the SSNN model architecture, its training, and providing proof-of-concept
477   support. We acknowledge that further work is needed to develop an efficient discovery algorithm for
478   identifying active constraint combinations that is applicable to a general class of empirical applica-
479
      tions. Our results indicated some declining performance with the larger DC-OPF systems. Further
      work is also needed to identify the difficulties in training SSNN models to scale up to larger systems.
