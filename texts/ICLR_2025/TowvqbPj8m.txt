011                                               A BSTRACT
012
013            Controllable artistic image stylization and generation aims to render the content
014            provided by text or image with the learned artistic style, where content and style
015
               decoupling is the key to achieve satisfactory results. However, current methods for
               content and style disentanglement primarily rely on image information for supervi-
016
               sion, which leads to two problems: 1) models can only support one modality for
017
               style or content input;2) incomplete disentanglement resulting in semantic interfer-
018            ence from the reference image. To address the above issues, this paper proposes a
019            content-style representation disentangling method for controllable artistic image
020            stylization and generation. We construct a WikiStyle+ dataset consists of artworks
021            with corresponding textual descriptions for style and content. Based on the multi-
022            modal dataset, we propose a disentangled content and style representations guided
023            diffusion model. The disentangled representations are first learned by Q-Formers
024            and then injected into a pre-trained diffusion model using learnable multi-step
025            cross-attention layers for better controllable stylization. This approach allows
026
               model to accommodate inputs from different modalities. Experimental results
               show that our method achieves a thorough disentanglement of content and style in
027
               reference images under multimodal supervision, thereby enabling a harmonious
028
               integration of content and style in the generated outputs, successfully producing
029            style-consistent and expressive stylized images. The code of our method will be
030            available upon acceptance.

033   1    I NTRODUCTION
034
035   Artistic image stylization and generation task aims at creating new images by applying specific artistic
036   styles to content. In stylization, the concept of “content” is well-defined, typically referring to the
037   subject of an image or the semantics of input text. However, the definition of “style” is relatively
038   vague and lacks consistent standards. Art historian, Meyer Schapiro, has defined artistic style as:
039
      “The constant form and sometimes the constant elements, qualities, and expression in the art of an
      individual or a group” (Karkov & Brown, 2003). For example, Impressionism emphasizes the natural
040
      representation of light and color. Therefore, to truly capture and reproduce artistic style, stylization
041
      models must learn the unique ways of artists handle and present content in their creative process.
042   To achieve this, the model needs to effectively disentangle and control content and style during the
043   stylization process, thereby producing visually coherent and stylized results.
044
045
      Recently, diffusion models (Ho et al., 2020; Rombach et al., 2022) have demonstrated great potential
      in text-to-image stylization tasks (Ramesh et al., 2022; Ye et al., 2023; Mou et al., 2024; Chen
046
      et al., 2024a) due to their powerful generative capabilities. These methods typically extract reliable
047
      features in the reference images serving as conditional information to guide the diffusion model to
048   follow the predetermined style. However, the features extracted by the encoder often couple style and
049   semantics, leading to semantic conflicts between the text and the reference image . This in turn causes
050   content leakage from the reference image, as shown in Fig. 1 (a). Some approaches (Xing et al.,
051   2024) (Qi et al., 2024) attempt to achieve disentanglement by using separate encoders to extract style
052   and content representations. Still, they encounter the following issues: 1) These methods depend on
053   datasets consisting of paired content and stylized images for training. Besides, the disentanglement
      process is solely reliant on image-based supervision, which limits the style input to only image

055   not effectively decouple the content and style. This results in a mismatch between style and content

057   Additionally, they can only handle visual elements from single image and unable to mimic the style

077   To address the aforementioned issues, this paper proposes a content-style representation disentan-
078   glement method for controllable artistic image stylization and generation. First, we constructed a
079   multimodal art image-text dataset, WikiStyle+. We collected art images and their corresponding
080   style information from the WikiArt website, including details such as artists, creation periods, genres,
081
      and painting medias, as references for style descriptions. Additionally, we used large language
      model to generate textual descriptions of the content of these artworks. In this way, we tackled the
082
      problem of the lack of explicitly disentangled data for style and content in artistic images from a
083
      multimodal perspective. Based on the constructed WikiStyle+ dataset, we proposed a disentangled
084   content and style representations guided diffusion model. Through multimodal alignment tasks, the
085   Q-former aligns the learned image style features with style descriptions and the learned content
086   features with content descriptions. This method utilizes multimodal data to provide the style and
087   content information of the reference images for disentanglement supervision, achieving explicit
088   separation of content and style. As a result, the model learns style representations consistent with the
089   definition of style in art history, which reflects the distinct ways artists depicts the world. Building on
090   this, we inject the learned style and content representations into the multi-step cross-attention layers
091   of the diffusion model, enabling text-to-image stylization and generation.
092   With the explicit disentanglement of style and content, our method can adjust visual elements based
093   on content and different style prompts, making stylization go beyond mere visual imitation and
094   achieve a harmonious integration of content and style in the generated results, as shown in Fig.
095   1 (c). Our approach can effectively prevent content leakage from reference image. Besides, the
096   multimodal disentangle supervision allows us to accommodate style and inputs from either image
097   or text modalities flexibly, which is typically restricted by previous image-supervised decoupling
098
      methods (e.g., DeaDiff Qi et al. (2024)). In summary, our contributions are threefold:
099
              • We constructed the art image-text dataset, WikiStyle+, addressing the problem of the lack of
100
                explicitly disentangled data for style and content from a multimodal perspective.
101
102           • We propose a disentanglement method that explicitly decouples style and content represen-
103             tations through multimodal supervision, enabling the model to accept inputs from different
104
                modalities as control conditions.
105           • We constructed a disentangled representation-guided diffusion model, where the disen-
106             tangled content and style representations are injected into the cross-attention layers at
107             different time steps of the diffusion model, successfully generating images with harmonious
                integration of content and style.

      2.1   D IFFUSION - BASED IMAGE STYLIZATION WITH MULTIMODAL LATENTS
111
112
      In recent years, Diffusion Probabilistic Models Sohl-Dickstein et al. (2015) have demonstrated
113   immense potential in the field of image generation Dhariwal & Nichol (2021); Ho et al. (2020); Song
114   et al. (2020).Thanks to large-scale multimodal pre-trained models Radford et al. (2021); Li et al.
115   (2023), diffusion models have achieved remarkable success in text-to-image generation tasks Ramesh
116   et al. (2022); Rombach et al. (2022); Saharia et al. (2022). Text-to-image stylization tasks based
117   on diffusion models are primarily divided into two categories: one is optimization-based methods,
118   which involves fine-tuning the diffusion model to enable it to generate images for a specific style
119   Ruiz et al. (2023); Kumari et al. (2023); Sohn et al. (2023), or utilizing textual inversion to redefine or
120   optimize textual embeddings related to a specific style Gal et al. (2022); Zhang et al. (2023), thereby
121
      generating more accurately stylized images that match with descriptions. The other category involves
      conditional diffusion models based on pre-trained encodersHuang et al. (2023); Li et al. (2024); Mou
122
      et al. (2024); Wang et al. (2023b); Ye et al. (2023); Zhao et al. (2024), which extract stylistic features
123
      from reference images using pre-trained encoders and then inject these features as conditions into
124   diffusion model to generate images with specific styles.
125
126
127   2.2   C ONTENT-S TYLE D ECOUPLING
128
      In image stylization tasks, decoupling content and style is key to enhancing the quality of generated
129
      images. Text inversion methods (such as InST (Zhang et al., 2023), VCT (Cheng et al., 2023),
130
      DreamBooth (Ruiz et al., 2023), Textual Inversion (Gal et al., 2022), Custom Diffusion (Kumari
131   et al., 2023), and ArtBank (Zhang et al., 2024)) map reference images the embedding space of special
132   text tokens through a reversal module. They construct text prompts to specify whether the special
133   tokens represent content or style, thereby separately decoupling content and style, such as “a dog in
134   [v] style” or “a [v] dog in Van Gogh style,” where [v] represents either style or content information.
135
      For unoptimized attention mechanism methods like StyleAligned (Hertz et al., 2024) and Visual
136
      Style Prompting (Jeong et al., 2024) typically achieve zero-shot content-style decoupling for image
137   stylization by sharing or optimizing attention mechanisms in SD. Cross-domain alignment methods
138   like StyleDiffusion (Wang et al., 2023a) and OSASIS (Cho et al., 2024) use cross-domain and
139   intra-domain losses in CLIP space to achieve latent content-style decoupling.
140
      Adapter-based methods (such as IP-Adapter (Ye et al., 2023), T2I-Adapter (Mou et al., 2024), and
141
      StyleAdapter (Wang et al., 2023b)) adjust specific layers or channels of the model, allowing it to
142
      effectively separate and blend style and content without altering its original architecture. Adapter
143   methods based on pre-trained encoders (such as InstantStyle (Wang et al., 2024) and DEA-Diffusion
144   (Qi et al., 2024)) decouple the content and style of reference images in the encoding space. However,
145   current methods fail to achieve a thorough decoupling of content and style from an artistic creation
146   perspective, resulting in content leakage from the reference image in the generated results.

205   et al. (2023) to separate the style and content from images and align the feature spaces of images
206   and text accordingly. Different from Qi et al. (2024) that uses two independent Q-Formers, we adopt
207   a simpler design with two sets of learnable query embeddings, one dedicated to extracting content
208   embeddings from multimodal inputs and the other for extracting style embeddings. There are two
209   advantages by doing so: 1) Smaller model and faster convergence rate; 2) Physical decoupled query
210   embeddings are crucial for explicit disentanglement of content and style.

214   >is <value >". The content text is derived from the description field in the dataset, generated by a
215   multimodal large language model. We achieve content and style disentanglement by minimizing

344   Evaluation Metrics. Based on previous works (Qi et al., 2024) (Jiang & Chen, 2024), the following
345   metrics are used in the style transfer task. For content similarity, we use text alignment capability
346   (TA), which calculate the cosine similarity in the CLIP embedding space between stylized images
347
      and their corresponding text prompts. For Image Quality (IQ), the LAION-Aesthetics Predictor V2 1
      is adopted to evaluate the generation quality of the image. For style similarity (SS), we use a text
348
      prompt template to express style, namely “the painter is [v], the theme is [v]”. Then, we measured
349
      the cosine similarity between the generated image and the corresponding style template in the CLIP
350   text-image embedding space. We also conduct a user study to reflect the subjective preference (SP)
351   for the results.

829   mentioned in the prompts. When ITM is removed, the generated images show a loss of detail in
830   both style and content alignment. For instance, in Figure 10 (c), the “house” element explicitly
831
      mentioned in the content prompt is missing in the generated image. This indicates that, without ITM,
      the model’s capacity to maintain fine-grained alignment is compromised, resulting in less coherent
832
      and contextually relevant outputs.
833
834   4) Full-loss setting: When all three loss functions are used together, the SS metrics achieve the
835   highest scores. From Figure 10 (d), the generated images align closely with both style and content
836   prompts, showcasing strong disentanglement and alignment. The style is faithfully preserved, while
837
      the content, such as the "house" or "bus," is accurately represented in the generated outputs. This
      demonstrates the complementary nature of the three loss functions in ensuring both disentanglement
838
      and multimodal coherence. While ITC prioritizes content alignment, the inclusion of ITM and
839
      ITG shifts the focus toward achieving a better balance, where both content and style are accurately
