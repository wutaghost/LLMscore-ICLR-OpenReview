011                                          Abstract
012
013           The J-orthogonal matrix, also referred to as the hyperbolic orthogonal ma-
014           trix, is a class of special orthogonal matrix in hyperbolic space, notable for
015
              its advantageous properties. These matrices are integral to optimization un-
              der J-orthogonal constraints, which have widespread applications in statisti-
016
              cal learning and data science. However, addressing these problems is gener-
017
              ally challenging due to their non-convex nature and the computational inten-
018           sity of the constraints. Currently, algorithms for tackling these challenges
019           are limited. This paper introduces JOBCD, a novel Block Coordinate
020           Descent method designed to address optimizations with J-orthogonality
021           constraints. We explore two speciﬁc variants of JOBCD: one based on a
022           Gauss-Seidel strategy (GS-JOBCD), the other on a variance-reduced and
023           Jacobi strategy (VR-J-JOBCD). Notably, leveraging the parallel frame-
024           work of a Jacobi strategy, VR-J-JOBCD integrates variance reduction
025           techniques to decrease oracle complexity in the minimization of ﬁnite-sum
026
              functions. For both GS-JOBCD and VR-J-JOBCD, we establish the
              oracle complexity under mild conditions and strong limit-point convergence
027
              results under the Kurdyka-Lojasiewicz inequality. To demonstrate the eﬀec-
028
              tiveness of our method, we conduct experiments on hyperbolic eigenvalue
029           problems, hyperbolic structural probe problems, and the ultrahyperbolic
030           knowledge graph embedding problem. Extensive experiments using both
031           real-world and synthetic data demonstrate that JOBCD consistently out-
032           performs state-of-the-art solutions, by large margins.

064   1.1 Related Work
065   ▶ Block Coordinate Descent Methods. Block Coordinate Descent (BCD) is a well-
066   established iterative algorithm that sequentially minimizes along block coordinate directions.
067   Its simplicity and eﬃciency have led to its widespread adoption in structured convex appli-
068
      cations [36]. Recently, BCD has gained traction in non-convex problems due to its robust
      optimality guarantees and/or excellent empirical performance in areas including optimal
069
      transport [22], matrix optimization [12], fractional minimization [54], deep neural networks
070
      [5; 55; 31], federated learning[46], black-box optimization [4], and optimization with orthog-
071   onality constraints [52; 14]. To our knowledge, this is the ﬁrst application of BCD methods
072   to optimization under J-orthoginality constraints, with a focus on analyzing their theoretical
073   guarantees and empirical eﬃcacy.
074
      ▶ Minimizing Smooth Functions under J-Orthogonality Constraints. The J-
075
      orthogonal matrix belongs to a subset of generalized orthogonal matrices [16; 35; 23]. How-
076
      ever, projecting onto the J-orthogonality constraint poses challenges, complicating the ex-
077   tension of conventional optimization algorithms to address optimization problems under
078   these constraints [1; 16]. This contrasts with computing orthogonal projections using meth-
079   ods such as polar or SVD decomposition, or approximating them via QR factorization.
080   Existing methods for addressing Problem (1) can be categorized into three classes. (i) CS-
081   Decomposition Based Methods. These approaches involve parameterizing four orthogonal
082   matrices (as described in Proposition 2.2) and subsequently minimizing a smooth function
083   over these matrices in an alternating fashion. The involvement of 3 × 3 block matrices
084   makes the implementation of these methods very challenging. Consequently, the work of
085
      [49] focuses on optimizing a reduced subspace of the CS decomposition parameters, albeit
      at the expense of losing some degrees of freedom. (ii) Unconstrained Multiplier Correction
086
      Methods [47; 48; 13; 14]. These methods leverage the symmetry and explicit closed-form ex-
087
      pression of the Lagrangian multiplier at the ﬁrst-order optimality condition. Consequently,
088   they address an unconstrained problem, resulting in eﬃcient ﬁrst-order infeasible approaches.
089   (iii) Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers [19]. This method reformulates the origi-
090   nal problem into a bilinear constrained optimization problem by introducing auxiliary vari-
091   ables. It employs dual variables to handle bilinear constraints, iteratively optimizing primal
092   variables while keeping other primal and dual variables ﬁxed, and using a gradient ascent
093   strategy to update the dual variables. This approach has become widely adopted for solv-
094   ing general nonconvex and nonsmooth composite optimization problems. Notably, all the
095   aforementioned methods solely identify critical points of Problem (1).
096   ▶ Finite-Sum Problems via Stochastic Gradient Descent. The ﬁnite-sum struc-
097   ture is prevalent in machine learning and statistical modeling, facilitating decomposition
098   into smaller, more manageable components. This property is advantageous for developing
099   eﬃcient algorithms for large-scale problems, such as Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD). Re-
100   ducing variance is crucial in SGD because it can lead to more stable and faster convergence.
101   Various techniques, such as mini-batch SGD, momentum methods, and variance reduction
102
      methods like SAGA [10], SVRG [25], SARAH [33], SPIDER [11; 43], SNVRG [57], and
      PAGE [30], have been developed to address this issue. Additionally, SGD for minimizing
103
      composite functions has also been investigated by the authors [15; 24; 29].
104
105   1.2 Contributions
106   This paper makes the following contributions. (i) Algorithmically: We introduce the
107   JOBCD algorithm, a novel Block Coordinate Descent method speciﬁcally designed to
      tackle optimizations constrained by J-orthogonality. We explore two speciﬁc variants of


                                                    2
108
      JOBCD, one based on a Gauss-Seidel strategy (GS-JOBCD), the other on a variance-
109   reduced and Jacobi strategy (VR-J-JOBCD). Notably, VR-J-JOBCD incorporates a
110   variance-reduction technique into a parallel framework to reduce oracle complexity in the
111   minimization of ﬁnite-sum functions (See Section 2). (ii) Theoretically: We provide com-
112   prehensive optimality and convergence analyses for both algorithms (see Sections 3 and 4).
113   (iii) Empirically: Extensive experiments across hyperbolic eigenvalue problems, structural
114   probe problems, and ultrahyperbolic knowledge graph embedding, using both real-world and
115   synthetic data, consistently show the signiﬁcant superiority of JOBCD over state-of-the-art
116   solutions (see Section 6).

528   7              Conclusions
529
      In this paper, we propose a new approach JOBCD, which is based on block coordinate de-
530
      scent, for solving the optimization problem under J-orthogonality constraints. We discuss
531   two speciﬁc variants of JOBCD: one based on a Gauss-Seidel strategy (GS-JOBCD), the
532   other on a variance-reduced Jacobi strategy. Both algorithms capitalize on speciﬁc struc-
533   tural characteristics of the constraints to converge to more favorable stationary solutions.
534   Notably, VR-J-JOBCD incorporates a variance-reduction technique into a parallel frame-
535   work to reduce oracle complexity in the minimization of ﬁnite-sum functions. For both
536   GS-JOBCD and VR-J-JOBCD, we establish the oracle complexity under mild condi-
