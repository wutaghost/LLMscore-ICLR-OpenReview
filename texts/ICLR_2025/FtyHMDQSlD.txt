011                                               A BSTRACT
012
013            Text-to-3D generation based on score distillation of pre-trained 2D diffusion mod-
014            els has gained increasing interest, with variational score distillation (VSD) as a
015            remarkable example. VSD proves that vanilla score distillation can be improved
016            by introducing an extra score-based model, which characterizes the distribution
017            of images rendered from 3D models, to correct the distillation gradient. Despite
018
               the theoretical foundations, VSD, in practice, is likely to suffer from slow and
               sometimes ill-posed convergence. In this paper, we perform an in-depth investiga-
019
               tion of the interplay between the introduced score model and the 3D model, and
020
               find that there exists a mismatching problem between LoRA and 3D distributions
021            in practical implementation. We can simply adjust their optimization order to
022            improve the generation quality. By doing so, the score model looks ahead to the
023            current 3D state and hence yields more reasonable corrections. Nevertheless, naive
024            lookahead VSD may suffer from unstable training in practice due to the potential
025            over-fitting. To address this, we propose to use a linearized variant of the model
026            for score distillation, giving rise to the Linearized Lookahead Variational Score
027            Distillation (L2 -VSD). L2 -VSD can be realized efficiently with forward-mode
028            autodiff functionalities of existing deep learning libraries. Extensive experiments
029
               validate the efficacy of L2 -VSD, revealing its clear superiority over prior score
               distillation-based methods. We also show that our method can be seamlessly
030
               incorporated into any other VSD-based text-to-3D framework.

034   1   I NTRODUCTION
035
036   3D content creation is important for a variety of applications, such as interactive gaming (Bruce
037   et al., 2024; Xia et al., 2024), cinematic arts (Conlen et al., 2023), AR/VR (Creed et al., 2023;
038   Li et al., 2024), and building simulated environments for training agents in robotics (Team et al.,
039
      2024). However, it is still challenging and expensive to create a high-quality 3D asset as it requires a
      high level of expertise. Therefore, automating this process with generative models has become an
040
      important problem (Jiang, 2024), while remaining non-trivial due to the scarcity of training data and
041
      the complexity of 3D representations.
042
043   Score distillation has emerged as an attractive way for 3D generation given textual condition (Poole
044   et al., 2022; Lin et al., 2023; Chen et al., 2023; Wang et al., 2023; 2024a). It leverages pretrained 2D
045
      diffusion models (Ho et al., 2020; Rombach et al., 2022) to define priors to guide the evolvement
      of 3D content without reliance on annotations. Score Distillation Sampling (SDS) (Poole et al.,
046
      2022) is a seminal work in this line, but it is widely criticized that its generations suffer from the
047
      over-smoothing issue. Variational Score Distillation (VSD) (Wang et al., 2023) remediates this by
048   introducing an extra model that captures the score of the images rendered from the 3D model to
049   correct the distillation gradient. However, VSD often requires a lengthy optimization through 3 stages:
050   NeRF generation, geometry refinement, and texture refinement. The outcomes obtained in the initial
051   stage are often blurry, prone to collapsing, and not directly applicable (Wei et al., 2023). Though
052   existing works begin to understand and improve SDS (Wang et al., 2024a; Yu et al., 2023; Katzir
053   et al., 2023), there are great but less efforts dedicated to improving the more promising VSD (Ma
      et al., 2025; Wei et al., 2024).

      To identify the root of VSD’s drawback, we conduct a comprehensive analysis of the interaction
070   between the introduced score model and 3D model revealing that adjusting their optimization order
071   can sometimes lead to a considerable enhancement in generation quality. This adjustment allows the
072   score model to look ahead to the current 3D state, resulting in more accurate and sensible corrections
073   for the distillation gradient. Yet, naive lookahead VSD can encounter unstable training due to the risk
074   of the score model overfitting the single 3D particle and the sampled camera view.
075
      To address this issue, we formally compare the correction gradients before and after looking ahead
076   and identify two major differences—a linear first-order term and a high-order one. Upon closer
077   examination, we observe that the former accommodates subtle semantic information, whereas the
078   latter contains non-trivial high-frequency noises. Given these findings, we propose to use only the first-
079   order term for correction, yielding Linearized Lookahead Variational Score Distillation (L2 -VSD), to
080   reliably and consistently boost the generation quality of VSD. L2 -VSD is both easy to implement and
081   computationally efficient—the added linear term can be computed by only one additional forward
082   process of the score model under the scope of forward-mode automatic differentiation, which is
083   supported in many deep learning libraries (Paszke et al., 2017; Ketkar et al., 2021).
084   Through extensive experiments, we demonstrate the significant superiority of the proposed L2 -VSD
085   in improving 3D generation quality compared to competing baseline methods, as shown in Fig. 7.
086   L2 -VSD can even produce realistic generation results with low resolution directly in the first stage.
087   Moreover, we empirically show that L2 -VSD can be seamlessly integrated into other VSD-based
088   3D generation pipelines and combined with other parallel techniques for VSD, e.g., Entropy Score
089   Distillation(ESD) (Wang et al., 2024a) which mitigates the Janus problem, for further improvement.
090   We summarize our technical contributions as follows:
091
             • For VSD, a fundamental method in text-to-3D generation, we carefully identify the gaps
092
               between its theory and implementation and analyze the potential impact the gaps may bring
093            us, providing a direction for possible improvements.
094
             • We propose L2 -VSD, an easy to implement and computationally efficient variant of VSD,
095
               which mitigates the mismatching problem to some extent. We demonstrate its significant
096
               improvement over baselines and comparable to other state-of-the-arts.
097
             • We demonstrate that our method can be seamlessly combined with other VSD-based im-
098
               proving techniques without much effort.

      2.2   T EXT- TO -3D G ENERATION WITH S CORE D ISTILLATION
127
128   Text-to-3D generation aims to identify the parameters θ ∈ RN of a 3D model given a text condition
129   y. Neural radiance field (NeRF) (Mildenhall et al., 2020) is a typical 3D representation based on
130   neural networks. In particular, NeRF renders a new view of the scene with the input of a sequence of
131   images as known views. Additionally, textured mesh can be applied to represent the geometry of a
132   3D object with triangle meshes and textures with color on the mesh surface.
133   Denote g(θ, c) as the differential rendering function projecting the 3D scene to a 2D image given a
134   camera angle c. Score distillation approaches for text-to-3D generations demand the image sample
135   g(θ, c) to respect the prior specified by a text-to-2D diffusion model ϵpretrain (·, ·, y) pretrained on
136   vast real text-image pairs, based on which the optimization goal is constructed (Poole et al., 2022;
137   Wang et al., 2023; 2024a; Yu et al., 2023; Tang et al., 2024; Yang et al., 2023; Katzir et al., 2023).
138
      Score Distillation Sampling (SDS) (Poole et al., 2022) updates the 3D model using view-dependent
139   prompt y c :
140                                                                                 ∂g(θ, c)
141                       ∇θ LSDS (θ) := Et,ϵ,c [ω(t)(ϵpretrain (xt , t, y c ) − ϵ)          ],            (4)
                                                                                      ∂θ
142   where c is a randomly sampled camera angle and xt := αt g(θ, c) + σt ϵ. The gradient is a simplifi-
143   cation of that of the denoising objective w.r.t. θ (Poole et al., 2022). Intuitively, it encourages the
144   rendered images to move toward the high-probability regions of the pretrained model, thus a good 3D
145   model emerges. SDS is a seminal work in the line of text-to-3D generation, but it is sensitive to the
146   CFG scale s (Ho & Salimans, 2022). A small s often results in over-smooth outcomes, whereas a
147   large s leads to over-saturation.
148   Variational Score Distillation (VSD) (Wang et al., 2023) addresses the issue of SDS with a thorough
149   theoretical analysis and proposes a new algorithm. In particular, apart from the pretrained model
150   ϵpretrain (xt , t, y) for capturing the data distribution, VSD introduces a tunable model ϵϕ (xt , t, c, y),
151   often instantiated as a LoRA (Hu et al., 2021) adaptation of ϵpretrain , to account for the distribution
152   of images rendered from all possible 3D models given condition y and camera angle c. We refer the
153   introduced model as the LoRA model hereinafter. VSD proves the LoRA model can reliably correct
154   the original distillation gradient.

      6     D ISCUSSION , C ONCLUSION AND L IMITATION
525
526
      In this paper, we dive deep into the theory of VSD, having a comprehensive understanding of inner
527
      process and identify two potential directions for improvement. In terms of the algorithm formulation,
528   we propose Linearized Lookahead Variational Score Distillation(L2 -VSD), a novel framework based
529   on VSD that achieves state-of-the-art results on text-to-3D generation. The linearized lookahead term
530   enables us to benefit from both better convergence and lookahead for next iteration. More importantly,
531   our method can be incorporated into any VSD-based framework in the future.
532
      Limitations and broader impact. Firstly, although L2 -VSD achieves remarkable improvement on
533
      text-to-3D results, the generation process still takes hours of time, which is a common issue for score
534   distillation based methods. We believe orthogonal improvements on distillation accelerating (Zhou
535   et al., 2023) can mitigate this problem. Secondly, while we find the first-order term shows regular
536   pattern and some similarity may exist between our method and SiD (Zhou et al., 2024), which is
537   briefly discussed in Appendix E, we have not yet been able to formulate a distribution-based objective
538   that guides this optimization. Investigating the underlying reasons is of significant interest in the
539   future. Lastly, as for broader impact, like other generative models, our method may be utilized to
      generate fake and malicious contents, which needs more attention and caution.
