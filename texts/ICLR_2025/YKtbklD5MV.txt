009                                                 A BSTRACT
010
011            3D Gaussian Splatting has emerged as a powerful representation of geometry and
012            appearance for RGB-only dense Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM),
013            as it provides a compact dense map representation while enabling efficient and
014            high-quality map rendering. However, existing methods show significantly worse
015            reconstruction quality than competing methods using other 3D representations,
016
               e.g. neural points clouds, since they either do not employ global map and pose
               optimization or make use of monocular depth. In response, we propose the first
017
               RGB-only SLAM system with a dense 3D Gaussian map representation that utilizes
018
               all benefits of globally optimized tracking by adapting dynamically to keyframe
019            pose and depth updates by actively deforming the 3D Gaussian map. Moreover,
020            we find that refining the depth updates in inaccurate areas with a monocular depth
021            estimator further improves the accuracy of the 3D reconstruction. Our experiments
022            on the Replica, TUM-RGBD, and ScanNet datasets indicate the effectiveness
023            of globally optimized 3D Gaussians, as the approach achieves superior or on
024            par performance with existing RGB-only SLAM methods methods in tracking,
025            mapping and rendering accuracy while yielding small map sizes and fast runtimes.
026            The source code will be publicly available.

      A common factor within the recent trend of dense SLAM is that the majority of works reconstruct a
031   dense map by optimizing a neural implicit encoding of the scene, either as weights of an MLP Azi-
032   nović et al. (2022); Sucar et al. (2021); Matsuki et al. (2023b); Ortiz et al. (2022), as features anchored
033   in dense grids Zhu et al. (2022); Newcombe et al. (2011); Weder et al. (2020; 2021); Sun et al. (2021);
034   Božič et al. (2021); Li et al. (2022); Zou et al. (2022); Sandström et al. (2023), using hierarchical
035   octrees Yang et al. (2022a), via voxel hashing Zhang et al. (2023b;a); Chung et al. (2022); Rosinol
036   et al. (2022); Matsuki et al. (2023c), point clouds Hu et al. (2023); Sandström et al. (2023); Liso
037   et al. (2024); Zhang et al. (2024) or axis-aligned feature planes Mahdi Johari et al. (2022); Peng et al.
038   (2020). We have also seen the introduction of 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) to the dense SLAM
039
      field Yugay et al. (2023); Keetha et al. (2023); Yan et al. (2023); Matsuki et al. (2023a); Huang et al.
      (2023).
040
041   Out of this 3D representation race there is, however, not yet a clear winner. In the context of dense
042   SLAM, a careful modeling choice needs to be made to achieve accurate surface reconstruction as well
043   as low tracking errors. Some takeaways can be deduced from the literature: neural implicit point cloud
044   representations achieve state-of-the-art reconstruction accuracy Liso et al. (2024); Zhang et al. (2024);
045
      Sandström et al. (2023), especially with RGBD input. At the same time, 3D Gaussian splatting
      methods yield the highest fidelity renderings Matsuki et al. (2023a); Yugay et al. (2023); Keetha et al.
046
      (2023); Huang et al. (2023); Yan et al. (2023) and show promise in the RGB-only setting due to their
047
      flexibility in optimizing the surface location Huang et al. (2023); Matsuki et al. (2023a). However,
048   they are not leveraging any multi-view depth or geometric prior leading to poor geometry in the
049   RGB-only setting. The majority of the aforementioned works only deploy so called frame-to-model
050   tracking, and do not implement global trajectory and map optimization, leading to excessive drift,
051   especially in real world conditions. Instead, to this date, frame-to-frame tracking methods, coupled
052   with loop closure and global bundle adjustment (BA) achieve state-of-the-art tracking accuracy Zhang
053   et al. (2023b;a; 2024). However, they either use hierarchical feature grids Zhang et al. (2023b;a), not
      suitable for map deformations at e.g. loop closure as they require expensive reintegration strategies,

081

146   3       M ETHOD
147
148   Splat-SLAM is a monocular SLAM system which tracks the camera pose while reconstructing the
149   dense geometry of the scene in an online manner. This is achieved through the following steps: We
150   first track the camera by performing local BA on selected keyframes by fitting them to dense optical
151   flow estimates. The local BA optimizes the camera pose as well as the dense depth of the keyframe.
152   For global consistency, when loop closure is detected, loop BA is performed on an extended graph
153   including the loop nodes and edges (section 3.1). Interleaved with tracking, mapping is done on a
154
      progressively growing 3D Gaussian map which deforms online to the keyframe poses and so called
      proxy depth maps (section 3.2). For an overview of our method, see fig. 2.
155
156
157   3.1        T RACKING
158
159   To predict the motion of the camera during scene exploration, we use a pretrained recurrent optical
160   flow model Teed & Deng (2020) coupled with a Disparity, Scale and Pose Optimization (DSPO)
161   layer Zhang et al. (2024) to jointly optimize camera poses and per pixel disparities. In the following,
      we describe this process in detail.

      We first describe our experimental setup and then evaluate our method against state-of-the-art dense
      RGB and RGBD SLAM methods on Replica Straub et al. (2019) as well as the real world TUM-
297
      RGBD Sturm et al. (2012) and the ScanNet Dai et al. (2017a) datasets. For more experiments and

299
300   Implementation Details. For the proxy depth, we use η = 0.01 to filter points and use the condition
301   nc ≥ 2 to ensure multi-view consistency. For the mapping loss function, we use λ = 0.8, λreg = 10.0.
302
      We use 60 iterations during mapping. For tracking, we use α1 = 0.01 and α2 = 0.1 as weights
      for the DSPO layer. We use the flow threshold τ = 4.0 on ScanNet, τ = 3.0 on TUM-RGBD and
303
      τ = 2.25 on Replica. The threshold for loop detection is τloop = 25.0. The time interval threshold is
304
      τt = 20. We conducted the experiments on a cluster with an NVIDIA A100 GPU.
305
306   Evaluation Metrics. For rendering we report PSNR, SSIM Wang et al. (2004) and LPIPS Zhang
307   et al. (2018) on the rendered keyframe images against the sensor images. For reconstruction, we first
308
      extract the meshes with marching cubes Lorensen & Cline (1987) as in Sandström et al. (2023) and
      evaluate the meshes using accuracy [cm], completion [cm] and completion ratio [%] (threshold 5 cm)
309

469
      Limitations. We currently do not model the appearance with spherical harmonics, since it only
      yields a marginal gains in rendering accuracy, while requiring more memory. It is is straightforward
470
      to add. We only make use of globally optimized frame-to-frame tracking, which fails to leverage
471
      frame-to-model queues from the 3D Gaussian map. Another limitation is that our construction of
472   the final proxy depth D is quite simple and does not fuse the monocular and keyframe depths in an
473   informed manner, e.g. using normal consistency. Finally, as future work, it is interesting to study how
474   surface regularization can be enforced via e.g. quadric surface elements as in Peng et al. (2024).

      5    C ONCLUSION
478
      We proposed Splat-SLAM, a dense RGB-only SLAM system which uses a deformable 3D Gaussian
479
      map for mapping and globally optimized frame-to-frame tracking via optical flow. Importantly, the
480
      inclusion of monocular depth into the tracking loop, to refine the scale and to correct the erroneous
481   keyframe depth predictions, leads to better rendering and mapping. By using the monocular depth
482   for completion, mapping is further improved. Our experiments demonstrate that Splat-SLAM
483   outperforms existing solutions regarding reconstruction and rendering accuracy while being on par or
484   better with respect to tracking as well as runtime and memory usage.
