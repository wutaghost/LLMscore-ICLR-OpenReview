011                                                    A BSTRACT
012
013                Improving the generalization of Bird’s Eye View (BEV) detection models is essen-
014                tial for safe driving in the real world. In this paper, we consider a realistic yet more
015                challenging scenario, which aims to improve the generalization with single source
016
                   data for training, as collecting multiple source data is time-consuming and labor
                   intensive in autonomous driving. To this end, we rethink the task from a frequency
017
                   perspective and exploit the cross-view consistency between adjacent perspectives.
018
                   We propose the Fourier Cross-View Learning (FCVL) framework including Fourier
019                Hierarchical Augmentation (FHiAug), an augmentation strategy in the frequency
020                domain to boost domain diversity and Fourier Cross-View Semantic Consistency
021                Loss to facilitate the model to learn more domain-invariant features. Furthermore,
022                we provide theoretical guarantees via augmentation graph theory. To the best of
023                our knowledge, this is the first study to explore generalizable 3D Object Detection
024                in BEV with single-source data. Extensive experiments on various testing domains
025                have demonstrated that our approach achieves the best performance on various test
026                domains with single-source data.

      1       I NTRODUCTION
030
      Recent advances in Bird’s Eye View (BEV) representations have shown significant potential for multi-
031
      camera 3D object detection, as they capture both spatial locations and semantic features without being
032
      heavily affected by occlusions. While existing camera-based BEV models (Philion & Fidler, 2020;
033   Huang et al., 2022; Li et al., 2022b;c) have achieved excellent performance on in-distribution datasets
034   like nuScenes (Caesar et al., 2020), they struggle in real-world settings where the environment and
035   conditions vary widely. This performance drop occurs because camera data in practical applications
036   often has different distributions compared to the limited training data. As a result, enhancing the
037   generalization of these models is critical for their safe deployment. Domain generalization (DG) aims
038   to generalize a model to an unseen target domain by learning from multiple source domains. However,
039   collecting diverse source data for training is time-consuming and labor-intensive, especially in
040   autonomous driving scenarios, and cannot always guarantee improved performance. In this paper, we
041
      tackle a more practical yet challenging problem: improving the generalization of 3D object detectors
      when trained on a single source domain. Focusing on single-domain generalization (SDG) not only
042
      addresses practical constraints but also provides a more robust evaluation of model adaptability.
043
044   In SDG for 2D image classification, previous works (Zhao et al., 2023; Qiao et al., 2020) aim to
045   enhance data diversity using common 2D data augmentation techniques1 , such as geometric transfor-
046   mations, style transfer, or adversarial data generation. However, directly applying these approaches
047
      to BEV-based tasks introduces several challenges. First, BEV representations are generated by pro-
      jecting multi-view 2D features using real-world physical constraints, which limits the use of strong
048
      geometric transformations, such as 270-degree rotations, as they would disrupt the spatial consistency
049
      of the BEV space. Second, style transfer techniques (Zhao et al., 2023) replace the original image
050   statistics with those from the target style, but this often blurs the boundary between style and content
051   (Lee et al., 2023), distorting important features and ultimately harming model generalization. Third,
052   adversarial generation methods(Goodfellow et al., 2020) suffer from unstable training and mode
053

              We have provided a more detailed introduction to these techniques in the Appendix A.

069   collapse. While diffusion-based techniques(Ho et al., 2020) are more stable, they add significant
070   computational and storage overhead, making them impractical for complex 3D detection models.
071   Therefore, common 2D data augmentations cannot be effectively leveraged to create diverse training
072   samples for BEV-based tasks. More importantly, for multi-camera 3D object detection, the natural
073   availability of multi-view data offers a unique opportunity to learn domain-invariant features, a
074   potential that remains underexplored in scenarios with limited training data.
075   In response to these limitations and challenges, we propose the Fourier Cross-View Learning (FCVL)
076   framework including Fourier Hierarchical Augmentation (FHiAug), an augmentation strategy in the
077   frequency domain to boost domain diversity and Fourier Cross-View Semantic Consistency Loss
078   to facilitate the model to learn more domain-invariant features. Different from Zhao et al. (2023)
079   expanding style statistics in the pixel domain, we utilize the Fourier transform to introduce style
080   variations while minimizing content distortion. This is motivated by the well-known property of the
081   Fourier transformation: the phase component encodes high-level semantic information, while the
082   amplitude component captures low-level image statistics (Xu et al., 2021). This separation allows
083
      us to independently manipulate style (low-level statistics) and content (high-level semantics) in the
      frequency domain. At the image level, we introduce Frequency Jitter, which perturbs both amplitude
084
      and phase components to create diverse samples that complement the single source domain. At
085
      the feature level, we propose Amplitude Transfer, a novel method for generating fine-grained style
086   variations, ensuring domain diversity in the latent space. For multi-camera setups, FHiAug applies
087   cross-camera augmentation, creating surrounding views with varied “styles” to simulate realistic
088   variations. To leverage the natural multi-view input, we design the Fourier Cross-View Semantic
089   Consistency Loss, which aligns adjacent perspectives to help the model develop robust features
090   against domain shifts. Furthermore, using augmentation graph theory (HaoChen et al., 2022; Wang
091   et al., 2024), we provide a unique theoretical perspective on FCVL and establish its theoretical
092   soundness.
093   In summary, our major contributions are as follows:
094
095          • Towards SDG for multi-camera 3D object detection, we present the Fourier Cross-View
096            Learning framework to fully exploit natural cross-view inputs.
097          • We propose FHiAug, a novel, efficient, plug-and-play augmentation strategy that operates
098            on both image and feature levels, to enhance domain diversity without requiring additional
099            modules or specialized training strategies.
100
             • We propose Fourier Cross-View Semantic Consistency Loss to facilitate the model to learn
101
               more domain-invariant features from adjacent perspectives.
102
103          • Using augmentation graph theory, we provide a valid theoretical foundation for the effec-
104
               tiveness of FCVL.
105          • To the best of our knowledge, this is the first work to address generalizable 3D object
106            detection in BEV using single-source data. Extensive experiments across various test
107            domains demonstrate that our approach achieves superior performance compared to existing
               domain generalization methods (See Fig.1(a)).

136   2.1     OVERVIEW OF F OURIER C ROSS - VIEW L EARNING (FCVL) F RAMEWORK
137
      In this section, we elaborate the Fourier Cross-View Learning (FCVL) framework. The FCVL
138
      framework is motivated by the cross-view consistency in BEV 3D object detection. For example, as
139
      shown in Fig.1(b), objects such as cars or pedestrians are often visible across multiple adjacent camera
140   views. This overlap results in similar BEV features across these cameras, providing an inductive bias
141   to guide the learning process. To capture this cross-view relationship, we implement a Fourier Cross-
142   View Semantic Consistency Loss, where features from nearby camera views are considered positive
143   samples, while those from distant views are treated as negative samples. To enhance feature diversity
144   and improve domain generalization, we propose Fourier Hierarchical Augmentation, which applies
145   frequency-based transformations to different camera views. This method enriches the feature space,
146   promoting the learning of domain-invariant BEV features. The overall structure of our framework is
147   depicted in Fig.2 and the process is outlined in Algorithm 1 in Appendix D. In the following sections,
148
      we provide an in-depth explanation of both the Fourier Hierarchical Augmentation and the Fourier
      Cross-View Semantic Consistency Loss.
149

155   strategies.

      5.1   M ULTI - VIEW 3D O BJECT D ETECTION
498
499   The recent advances in BEV representation exhibit great potential for multi-view 3D Object Detection
500   Dong et al. (2024); Yang et al. (2023); Pan et al. (2024); Qi et al. (2024); Li et al. (2024); Zhang
501   et al. (2022); Jiang et al. (2023b). The camera-based BEV models (Philion & Fidler, 2020; Huang
502   et al., 2022; Li et al., 2022b;c) have achieved excellent performance on in-distribution datasets but
503   improving the generalization of such detection models in real-world application scenarios is remains
504   under-studied. PD-BEV renders diverse view maps from BEV features and rectify the perspective
505   bias of these maps to help the learning of features resilient to domain shifts (Lu et al., 2023). DG-BEV
506   creates multiple pseudo-domains and construct an adversarial training loss to encourage the feature
507
      representation to be more domain-agnostic (Wang et al., 2023a).
508
509   5.2   S INGLE D OMAIN G ENERALIZATION
510
511   Domain Generalization (DG) aims to generalize a model trained on multiple source domains to a
512
      target domain which is distributionally different. CIRL(Lv et al., 2022) generates augmented images
      by a causal intervention module with intervention upon non-causal factors. AGFA (Kim et al., 2023)
513
      trains the classifier and the amplitude generator adversarially to synthesise a worst-case domain for
514
      adaptation. This paper focuses on single domain generalization (Wang et al., 2023b) which is a more
515   challenging yet realistic setting. Wang et al. (2023b) propose a style-complement module to enhance
516   the generalization power of the model by synthesizing images from diverse distributions that are
517   complementary to the source ones. Chen et al. (2023) propose a new learning paradigm, namely
518   simulate-analyze-reduce, which first simulates the domain shift by building an auxiliary domain as
519   the target domain, then learns to analyze the causes of domain shift, and finally learns to reduce
520   the domain shift for model adaptation. Qiao et al. (2020) leverage adversarial training to create
521   “fictitious” yet “challenging” populations and use a Wasserstein Auto-Encoder (WAE) to relax the
522   widely used worst-case constraint in a meta-learning scheme. Zhao et al. (2023) propose CPerb,
523
      a simple yet effective cross-perturbation method to enhance the diversity of the training data and
      introduce multi-route perturbation to learn domain-invariant features. As can be seen from previous
524
      work, increasing data diversity is a key ingredient for single domain generalization.
525
526
527   6     C ONCLUSION
528
529   In conclusion, this paper addresses the challenge of Single Domain Generalization in multi-camera 3D
530   object detection via Fourier Cross-View Learning framework. We propose a non-parametric Fourier
531   Hierarchical Augmentation at both image and feature levels to enhance data diversity and Fourier
532   Cross-View Semantic Consistency Loss to facilitate model to learn more domain-invariant features
533   from adjacent perspectives. Besides, via augmentation graph theory, we make valid theoretical
534   guarantees. Extensive experiments on various testing domains of different datasets have demonstrated
535   that our approach achieves the best performance across various domain generalization methods.
536   Limitations. At present, there are several hyperparameters to be tuned. In the future work, we
537   can explore additional techniques to avoid spending too much time on tuning hyperparameters.
538   Additionally, for snowy weather, we have already improved by 10 points, but the performance is still
539   much worse compared to the performance in other scenarios such as low light. Consequently, there is
      a substantial potential for enhancement in adverse weather conditions.

      A    I NTRODUCTION TO C OMMON 2D DATA AUGMENTATION TECHNIQUES
703
704
      In SDG for 2D image classification, previous works (Zhao et al., 2023; Qiao et al., 2020) aim
705   to enhance data diversity using common 2D data augmentation techniques, such as geometric
706   transformations, style transfer, or data generation. However, directly applying these approaches to
707   BEV-based tasks introduces several challenges.
708
      First, BEV representations are generated by projecting multi-view 2D features using real-world
709
      physical constraints, which limits the use of strong geometric transformations, such as 270-degree
710
      rotations, as they would disrupt the spatial consistency of the BEV space. We add a strong geometric
711   enhancement experiment including significant rotation and translation on the image and the results are
712   as follows in the Table 6. For one thing, it shows that strong geometric enhancement hurts in-domain
713   performance, as large scale rotation or translation may destroy physical restraints in the real driving
714   scenario. For another, geometric enhancement is not very effective in improving OoD performance.
715
716   Table 6: Strong geometric enhancement experiments including significant rotation and translation on
717   the images.

        BEVDet      0.3880   0.2508   0.1955   0.2409   0.2201   0.2591   0.1112   0.2633   0.0728    0.2017
721    strong geo   0.3505   0.2338   0.1875   0.2249   0.2030   0.2371   0.1188   0.2511   0.0639    0.1900
722
723
      Second, some style transfer techniques (Zhao et al., 2023; Nuriel et al., 2021) replace the original
724
      image statistics with those from the target style, but this often blurs the boundary between style and
725
      content (Lee et al., 2023), distorting important features and ultimately harming model generalization.
726   These methods need to remove the "style" in the pixel domain first. Some content cues will be
727   removed inevitably.
728
729
      Third, data generation methods including adversarial generation and diffusion-based techniques.
      Training a Generative Adversarial Network (GAN) (Goodfellow et al., 2020) involves a competitive
730
      process between two neural networks: the generator and the discriminator. Adversarial generation
731
      can suffer from unstable training and mode collapse. It often requires a lot of experiments and
732   fine-tuning to get a GAN to work well. While diffusion-based techniques(Ho et al., 2020) are
733   more stable, they need significant computational and storage overhead, making them impractical for
734   complex 3D detection models. Additionally, although we can spend much time generating a large
735   number of samples, we would also require extra storage space. However, our method involves online
736   augmentation and does not require any additional storage space.
737
      Therefore, common 2D data augmentations cannot be effectively leveraged to create diverse training
738   samples for BEV-based tasks.
739
740   Besides, we further clarify the differences between our method and other frequency-domain ap-
741
      proaches. Compared with these methods(Xu et al., 2021; Lv et al., 2022; Kim et al., 2023), our
      method has major strengths in two aspects including accuracy and efficiency. Firstly, in the setting
742
      of single source data, our proposed method can enhance the generalization ability of the detectors
743
      by large margin. FACT (Xu et al., 2021) mixes up two different domains’ data in frequency, e.g.
744   Cartoon and Photo from dataset PACS and achieves great OOD performance in the paper. But when
745   training with only single domain, FACT can only mix the samples within the single domain and it
746   indeed improves the in-domain clean set a little, but the improvement of OOD sets is very slim in
747   the single domain setting. Different from FACT, we first propose Frequency Jitter at image level to
748   create diverse samples that are complementary to the single source domain. Then, at feature level,
749   we introduce a novel method Amplitude Transfer to achieve style transfer without content distortion.
750   Through uncertainty estimation, we can obtain uncertain feature statistics, which can gradually shift
751   the features to more diverse domains through continuous training.
752   Secondly, due to the high complexity of BEV-based 3D object detection models, our plug-and-
753   play and non-paramerter data augmentation method can achieve better generalization results more
754   efficiently. CIRL (Lv et al., 2022) generates augmented images by a causal intervention module
755   with intervention upon non-causal factors. AGFA (Kim et al., 2023) trains the classifier and the
      amplitude generator adversarially to synthesise a worst-case domain for adaptation. Compared with

      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


756
      these methods, our proposed method is simple, stable, yet effective without extra module designing
757   or special training strategies.

762   B.1   DATASETS
763
764   To verify different methods’ single domain generalization ability, we first utilize nuScenes (Caesar
765   et al., 2020) as the single training source and nuScenes-C (Xie et al., 2023) as the testing sets.
766   NuScenes-C is comprehensive dataset that encompasses eight distinct corruptions, including Bright,
767   Dark, Fog, Snow, Motion Blur, Color Quant, Camera Crash, and Frame Lost. Each type of corruption
768   has three different levels of corruption intensity (i.e., easy, moderate, and hard). These eight corrup-
769   tions include different weather conditions, different light conditions, potential equipment damage
770
      situations. These scenarios are common out-of-distribution problems in real-world application. We
      use eight distinct corruptions as our multi testing domains to evaluate the effectiveness of different
771
      DG methods.
772

774   scenarios from different cities. We sample 20 % data to achieve single domain training set and evaluate
775   five categories including ’BICYCLE’, ’LARGE VEHICLE’, ’MOTORCYCLE’, ’PEDESTRIAN’,
776
      ’REGULAR VEHICLE’.
