011
               Scene generation is crucial to many computer graphics applications. Recent ad-
012            vances in generative AI have streamlined sketch-to-image workflows, easing the
013            workload for artists and designers in creating scene concept art. However, these
014            methods often struggle for complex scenes with multiple detailed objects, some-
015            times missing small or uncommon instances. In this paper, we propose a Training-
016            free Triplet Tuning for Sketch-to-Scene (T3 -S2S) generation after reviewing the
017            entire cross-attention mechanism. This scheme revitalizes the existing Control-
018            Net model, enabling effective handling of multi-instance generations, involving
019            prompt balance, characteristics prominence, and dense tuning. Specifically, this
020
               approach enhances keyword representation via the prompt balance module, re-
               ducing the risk of missing critical instances. It also includes a characteristics
021
               prominence module that highlights TopK indices in each channel, ensuring es-
022
               sential features are better represented based on token sketches. Additionally, it
023            employs dense tuning to refine contour details in the attention map, compensating
024            for instance-related regions. Experiments validate that our triplet tuning approach
025            substantially improves the performance of existing sketch-to-image models. It
026            consistently generates detailed, multi-instance 2D images, closely adhering to the
027            input prompts and enhancing visual quality in complex multi-instance scenes.

030   1   I NTRODUCTION
031
032   Scene generation plays a significant role in visual content creation across various domains, including
033
      video gaming, animation, filmmaking, and virtual/augmented reality. Traditional methods heavily
      rely on manual efforts, which require designers to transform initial sketches into detailed multi-
034
      instance scene concept art through numerous iterations. Recently, technological innovations such as
035
      Stable Diffusion (Rombach et al., 2022; Podell et al., 2023) equipped with ControlNet (Zhang et al.,
036   2023) and integrated with advanced text-to-image technologies (Kim et al., 2023), have streamlined
037   this process. These advancements have notably decreased the workload for designers by automating
038   the conversion of simple sketches into complex scenes. While these technologies perform well with
039   common scenes involving typical instances, they struggle with generating complex multi-instance
040   scenes, particularly with unusual and small instances.
041
      Alternatively, multi-instance synthesis involves incorporating layouts of multiple instances as ad-
042   ditional input through bounding boxes, and can effectively manage the generation of multiple in-
043   stances. However, most methods (Yang et al., 2023; Li et al., 2023; Liu et al., 2023; Sun et al., 2024;
044   Wang et al., 2024b; Zhou et al., 2024) are training-based and require further training when integrated
045   with sketches that contain minimal semantic information, necessitating the collection of numerous
046   scene images. In sectors such as gaming, animation, and film, copyright restrictions significantly
047   hinder scene generation and cannot be disregarded. Conversely, some training-free efforts (Xie et al.,
048   2023; Chen et al., 2024; Feng et al., 2022), like Dense Diffusion (Kim et al., 2023), focus primarily
049   on the impact of the attention map, but they overlook the interaction between the attention map and
050
      the value matrices, failing to accurately align with the designer’s sketches.
051   Our strategy involves maintaining ControlNet’s sketch-following capabilities while exploring the
052   challenges of synthesizing multiple instances. We aim to develop a training-free tuning mechanism
053   that harnesses the inherent creative capabilities of existing models, eliminating the need for extensive
      data collection or additional training. We conduct a comprehensive analysis of the cross-attention

      Text-to-Image Synthesis. In the rapidly evolving field of text-based image generation, various
101
      model architectures and learning paradigms have emerged, as highlighted by several key stud-
102   ies (Mansimov et al., 2015; Reed et al., 2016; Xu et al., 2018; Qiao et al., 2019; Zhu et al., 2019;
103   Ramesh et al., 2021; Ding et al., 2021; 2022; Yang et al., 2022; Croitoru et al., 2023; Dhariwal &
104   Nichol, 2021; Kingma et al., 2021). Recently, diffusion models (Rombach et al., 2022; Podell et al.,
105   2023; Saharia et al., 2022) have marked a major breakthrough, significantly improving the fidelity
106   and realism in text-to-image generation, which rely on structured denoising (Ho et al., 2020) with
107   latent diffusion (Rombach et al., 2022). Among these, the SDXL model and its variants, which are
      widely adopted in both academia and industry, are chosen as the baseline for our work.

      Sketch-to-image Synthesis. While text-to-image models can generate high-fidelity, realistic im-
109   ages, they struggle to accurately convey complex layouts with text prompts alone. In tasks such as
110   scene design for games, animation, film, or virtual reality, hand-drawn sketches with semantic infor-
111   mation provide a more effective way to express design ideas. In the field of diffusion-based genera-
112   tion, notable works include ControlNet (Zhang et al., 2023), Make-a-scene (Gafni et al., 2022), and
113   T2I Adapter (Mou et al., 2023) handle various additional visual conditions, including sketches, while
114   methods like Dense Diffusion (Kim et al., 2023), SpaText (Avrahami et al., 2023) and MultiDiffu-
115   sion (Bar-Tal et al., 2023) focus specifically on sketch-based inputs. In particular, Dense Diffusion
116   is a training-free approach that adjusts the attention map by amplifying sketch-relevant tokens and
117
      downplaying less important ones, allowing the model to better distinguish between instances. Con-
      trolNet is a powerful solution for sketch-to-scene generation, recognized for its exceptional ability
118
      to accurately follow conditions. However, these models often struggle with complex multi-instance
119
      scene generations, particularly when handling unusual or unique instances, and frequently overlook
120   smaller instances. Recently, Xu et al. (2024) proposed an efficient pipeline for automatically gener-
121   ating interactive 3D game scenes from users’ natural input sketches using the SDXL and ControlNet
122   models. However, the approach is also limited by the diversity and multi-instance representation in
123   the intermediate 2D isometric image generation.
124
125   Multi-instance Synthesis. Multi-instance synthesis is closely related to sketch-to-scene generation
126
      due to its controllable layout. Training-free modulations (Xie et al., 2023; Chen et al., 2024; Lian
      et al., 2023; Feng et al., 2022) and training-based fine-tuning methods (Yang et al., 2023; Li et al.,
127
      2023; Liu et al., 2023; Sun et al., 2024; Wang et al., 2024b; Zhou et al., 2024) tackle the challenge of
128
      diffusion models accurately representing multiple instances with bounding boxes. For example, Li
129   et al. (2023) (GLIGEN) used bounding box coordinates as grounding tokens, integrating them into
130   a gated self-attention mechanism to improve positioning accuracy, while Liu et al. (2023) employed
131   a latent object detection model to separate objects, masking conflicting prompts and enhancing
132   relevant ones. Despite existing methods of generating images with correct positions, these box-
133   based approaches struggle with simple sketch inputs and fail to strictly follow the designer’s sketch.
134   Our work leverages ControlNet’s sketch-following capabilities and investigates the challenges of
135   synthesizing multiple instances. We aim to design a training-free tuning mechanism to enhance
136   modeling within cross-attention operations, addressing these challenges effectively.

197   3.2                             I MBALANCE OF P ROMPT E NERGY
198
199   In extensive practice, two commonly observed phenomena are worth noting: (1) When generating
200   a single instance, the model responds well and rarely misses instances, but in multi-instance gener-
201   ation, some instances are easily lost; (2) In multi-instance generation, if an instance is overlooked,
202   techniques like increasing the prompt weight, such as “(houses:1.5)” in WebUI, can enhance the
203   weight of that prompt after embedding. To quantify this difference, we analyze the text embeddings
204   of multi-instance prompts in Figure 1 (b) and their corresponding single words, using energy (L2
205   norm) and cosine similarity metrics to measure discrepancies, as shown in Figure 2. For example,
206   in Figure 2a, the word “houses” exhibits lower energy compared to other words, which may explain
207
      why the instance “houses” is easily overlooked in Figure 1 (b). Low energy likely results in lower
      values in the Km and Vm matrices during the transformation from text embeddings, leading to
208
      diminished attention. In contrast, words encoded separately in Figure 2b tend to show higher en-
209
      ergy levels, which aligns with the observation that single instances are generally well-represented.
210   Additionally, cosine similarity analysis reveals that embedding full sentences alters the distribution
211   of word importance, reducing the emphasis in some instances. Scaling up the embeddings directly
212   enhances the energy levels, thereby increasing the competitiveness of instances in the generation
213   process by boosting their influence in both the attention map Am and the value matrix Vm . Un-
214   derstanding the imbalance of prompt energy in text embeddings highlights the importance of bal-
215   ancing and scaling energy levels, which offers an interesting perspective to improve multi-instance
      scene generation.

      4.1   OVERVIEW
255
      To address the challenges of the cross-attention mechanism highlighted in Section 3, we introduce
256   the training-free triplet tuning strategy, which builds on the strengths of the pre-trained SDXL and
257   ControlNet models, incorporating textual prompts cg = {ci }li=0 (l is the number of words) and
258   corresponding sketch images Cs ∈ Rh×w , as detailed in Figure 5.
259
260   The proposed training-free triplet tuning can be divided into the following three modules.
261
      (1) Prompt Balance: This module identifies instance keywords within global text prompts, replaces
      their embeddings with corresponding single-word embeddings, and adjusts the energy of these key-
262
      word embeddings to maintain balance. By balancing the energy of the keyword embeddings, the
263
      method enhances the representation of instances within key and value matrices. This process im-
264   proves the competitiveness of instance tokens among all tokens, ensures consistency across instance
265   tokens, and reduces the likelihood of overlooking rare or unusual instances.
266   (2) Characteristics Prominence: This module selects instance-related tokens and their sketches
267   by identifying the TopK values for each channel in the value matrices, creating an instance-specific
268   mask. The mask is then used to scale up the feature map for the corresponding channel. This
269   approach enhances the distinction of instances within the multi-channel feature space without addi-
      tional parameters, ensuring that instances’ characteristics are more prominently emphasized.

      4.3   C HARACTERISTICS P ROMINENCE
325
326   While utilizing balanced text embeddings helps balance the competition among instances, diffusion
327   models still face challenges with entity coupling in cross-attention layers, lacking a mechanism to
328   address the interaction between the attention map and value matrices. As discussed in Section 3.3,
329   increasing the TopK values in each channel of the value matrices reduces value homogeneity, but
330   requires a trade-off between instance completeness and noise clarity. In this subsection, we introduce
331   a characteristics prominence technique, applied after feature map computation in the original cross-
332
      attention layers, without introducing any additional trainable parameters.

356   5.1   I MPLEMENTATION D ETAILS
357
358   Baselines. We leverage the sketch-processing capabilities of the SDXL-base model (Podell et al.,
359   2023) and the ControlNet model (Zhang et al., 2023; Xinsir, 2023), serving as our foundational
360   models. Additionally, we extend our comparison to include two sketch-oriented approaches: the
361   training-based T2I Adapter (Mou et al., 2023) and the training-free Dense Diffusion (Kim et al.,
362
      2023), both integrated with the SDXL-base model.
363   Setup. In the triplet tuning scheme, the prompt balance module is integrated into the text encoding
364   process, while the characteristics prominence modules are incorporated across all cross-attention
365   layers. Additionally, the dense tuning module is specifically added to the “down blocks 2” layers
366   and the “mid blocks 0” layers within the ControlNet branch. The TopK value K is set to 2, and β is
367   kept at 1. During inference, we use the default Euler Discrete Scheduler (Karras et al., 2022) with
368   32 steps and a guidance scale of 9 at a resolution of 1024 × 1024. All experiments are conducted on
369   a single Nvidia Tesla V100 GPU.
370
