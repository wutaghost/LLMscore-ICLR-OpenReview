011                                                   A BSTRACT
012
013             Reinforcement learning (RL) is serving as the cornerstone of aligning large
014             language models (LLMs) to human behavior, by providing an appealing
015             formulation and a suite of effective algorithms for learning behavior strategies
016             through interacting with the underlying environment. Current paradigm of
017             RL-based methods for LLM alignment, such as reinforcement learning with human
018
                feedback (RLHF) involves utilizing a reward function learned from extensive offline
                datasets to expediate the online training of reinforcement learning. The reward
019
                function learned is then used for policy optimization to obtain an improved policy
020
                (i.e. the LLM). Despite the success of RL approaches in aligning LLM with offline
021             datasets, there are significant computational/limit of resources concern on applying
022             RL-based methods for LLMs. For example, standard RLHF requires simultaneous
023             loading of four models to the computing unit. In this paper, we develop a novel
024             policy optimization algorithm named Successive Policy Re-weighting (SPR),
025             matching the peak memory consumption of standard supervised fine-tune (SFT).
026             Further, SPR can leverage both offline and online datasets to expediate online
027             training and improve the sample efficiency. Specifically, SPR leverages a supervised
028             learning subroutine to achieve policy improvement through re-weighting the policy
029
                according to the importance/performance of executed actions. Such simple and
                effective method is computationally inexpensive, requiring loading only one model
030
                at each update step, matching the computational cost of standard supervised
031
                fine-tuning procedure. Experimental results show that the proposed method can
032             significantly outperform benchmark algorithms and accelerate the online training
033             with available offline dataset.

066   using the standard Bradley-Terry model (Bradley & Terry, 1952). Given a reward model, RLHF
067   utilizes standard policy optimization algorithm such as PPO at the policy optimization step, whose
068   naive implementation requires loading four models at the same time, namely the reward, the policy,
069
      and two value function models. In contrast, DPO only requires loading two models (a policy and a
      reference model) simultaneously, but is arguably more sensitive to the distribution shift between the
070
      base model outputs and preference data, and it is not quite effective on challenging tasks (Xu et al.,
071
      2024; Lin et al., 2024; Ivison et al., 2024). Witnessing the success of RLHF, the memory efficiency
072   issue of both RL-based method and DPO, as well as the limitation of offline nature of algorithms
073   such as DPO, we pose the following question:
074
          Can we reduce the compute and memory requirement of policy optimization to SFT level,
075
               while still leveraging an explicit reward function for effective online training?
076
077
      In this paper, we provide an affirmative answer to above question by proposing a novel algorithmic
      framework named Successive Policy Re-weighting (SPR), which is a RL-based method, whose
078
      (peak) computational cost matches that of supervised fine-tuning (SFT). The proposed framework
079
      is developed from carefully examining the objective of TRPO/PPO and considers a constrained
080   optimization reformulation from the original problem. A successive minimization algorithm is then
081   proposed to iteratively optimize for the value function and the policy. When online data collection is
082   available, we can collect online data from the current policy and add them to update the data buffer.
083   This simple supervised learning update scheme makes off-policy online reinforcement learning more
084   practical for real implementations. Specifically, our contributions are summarized as follows:
085   • We revisit the constrained policy optimization problem proposed in TRPO and a partial Lagrangian
086   reformulation of such problem inspired by the advantage-weighted regression (AWR, see Peng et al.
087   (2019)) method. We provide closed-form policy solution of the partial Lagrangian (see Theorem 1),
088
      and such a solution is exact and takes the normalization factor of the optimal policy into account
      (which is a departure from Peng et al. (2019), see Remark 2.2);
089
      • We propose a novel iterative Successive Policy Re-weighting method (SPR, Algorithm 1). SPR
090
      utilizes an explicit reward to learn the Q-value function and the log-sum-exp value function, then
091   learns the policy through re-weighting the policy probability based on the Q-value function. SPR
092   only loads one (value or policy) model at every step, achieving policy optimization with the same
093   memory consumption as standard supervised fine-tuning (SFT) (see Figure 1). We thus believe
094   that the proposed SPR can serve as a powerful alternative to the memory-costly PPO for the RLHF
095   pipeline when aligning LLMs.
096   • We conduct extensive numerical experiments to verify the effectiveness of the proposed
097   method. Specifically, we compare the SPR with standard PPO and DPO, as well as a Best-of-N
098   algorithm (Dong et al., 2023) on both 1b and 8b models. We compare both the reward value and
099
      win-rate of 1b model trained by different methods on a text-summarization dataset, and evaluate the
      8b model trained by different methods on OpenLLM leaderboard. All our results show the superior
100
      performance of the proposed method over standard baselines.

113

117   In the LLM context, S and A correspond to the space of input prompts and the space of output
118   continuations, respectively. If we consider the entire input sentences as state space and the entire
119   output continuations as action space, then there is no transition dynamics, i.e. horizon = 1. On
120   the other hand, if we consider the token-level state/action space2 , i.e. the action is modeled as the

      5         C ONCLUSIONS AND L IMITATIONS
518   In this paper we proposed a memory-efficient policy optimization approach named successive policy
519   re-weighting (SPR), matching the peak memory consumption of standard supervised fine-tune (SFT).
520   SPR is flexible for offline and online policy training and achieves state-of-the-art performance on
521   our experiment for aligning 1b and 8b models. The computational time of the proposed method is
522   in general higher than SFT or DPO, and the performance is largely dependent on the number of
523
      samples N to estimate the reference function in (22). Future works include tuning SPR pipeline to
      make it work for very large LLMs (>50b) and exploring alignment methods that are both time- and
524
      memory-efficient.

      A    R ELATED W ORK
814
815
      In this section, we discuss related work on both the LLMs and reinforcement learning with offline
      datasets.
816
817   RL for LLMs alignment. RL-based methods have show great success in improving the abilities of
818   LLMs, including text-summarizing (Liu et al., 2020; Ziegler et al., 2019), story-telling (Ziegler et al.,
819   2019), instruction-following (Ouyang et al., 2022; Ramamurthy et al., 2022), problem-solving (Trinh
820   et al., 2024), etc. The most popular RL pipeline for LLM alignment is RLHF (Ouyang et al., 2022;
821
      Christiano et al., 2017), which models the reward using the popular Bradley-Terry model (Bradley &
      Terry, 1952) and optimize the policy by policy optimization methods, such as REINFORCE (Williams,
822
      1992; Ahmadian et al., 2024; Li et al., 2023b), PPO (Schulman et al., 2017), GRPO (Shao et al.,
823
      2024) and R-max (Brafman & Tennenholtz, 2002). On the other hand, the extra computational cost
824   introduced by RL-based method is not negligible. DPO (Rafailov et al., 2024) serves as a powerful
825   substitute for RL-based method by directly optimizing for the LLM/policy (and the reward is implicitly
826   represented by the policy), making the memory consumption as low as the standard SFT. However
827   the absence of reward model in DPO pipeline is drawing discussions on its limited generalization
828   ability in the LLM alignment community (Li et al., 2023a; Xu et al., 2024). Standard DPO utilizes an
829   offline pre-collected preference dataset. Several other methods, such as KTO (Ethayarajh et al., 2024),
830   sequence likelihood calibration (SLiC, see Zhao et al. (2023)) and identity preference optimization
831   (IPO, see Azar et al. (2024)), also lie in the scope of offline preference data alignment. On the
832
      other hand, methods like online DPO (Dong et al., 2024) and Reinforced Self-Training (ReST,
      see Gulcehre et al. (2023)) utilize new samples with higher rewards from current model for further
833
      policy improvements.
834
835   Offline RL. Offline RL considers the problem of learning a policy from a fixed datasets where the
836   reward value is provided for each collected transition samples. In (Liu et al., 2019; Nachum et al.,
837   2019), model-free offline RL algorithms are proposed to solve the importance sampling problem. In
838
      (Kumar et al., 2020; Cheng et al., 2022), conservatism is incorporated into the value function to avoid
      overestimation in the offline RL setting. In (Kostrikov et al., 2021), a general algorithm for offline
839
      RL is proposed which can avoid any queries to values of out-of-sample actions during training while
840
      still enabling multi-step dynamic programming. For the model-based offline RL algorithms, Kidambi
841   et al. (2020) first constructs the estimated world model by utilizing diverse and large transition dataset
842   and then sets hard threshold on the model uncertainty for constructing terminating states to avoid
843   dangerous explorations. In (Yu et al., 2020), the authors proposes a model-based offline policy
844   optimization algorithm (MOPO) which utilizes uncertainty estimation techniques to construct a
845   penalty function to regularize the reward function. Therefore, MOPO can learn a conservative policy
846   which stays in the low-uncertainty region to avoid the distribution shift issue. As a follow-up work,
847   Lu et al. (2021) revisits the design choices of several key hyperparameters in MOPO and fine-tune
848   the corresponding hyperparameters in MOPO to guarantee strong performance. In Yu et al. (2021),
849
      the authors propose a model-based offline RL algorithm called COMBO which does not rely on
      explicit uncertainty estimation. By regularizing the value function on out-of-distribution state-action
850
      pairs generated in the estimated world model, COMBO can benefit from the conservatism without
851
      requiring explicit uncertainty estimation techniques. As a remark, the algorithms proposed in (Yu
852   et al., 2020; Kidambi et al., 2020; Lu et al., 2021; Yu et al., 2021) all perform conservative policy
853   optimization in a well-constructed dynamics model and the estimated dynamics model keeps fixed
854   during the training of the RL agent. Different from those algorithms mentioned above, (Uehara
855   & Sun, 2021; Rigter et al., 2022) incorporate conservatism into the constructed dynamics model.
856   By adversarially modifying the estimated dynamics model to minimize the value function under
857   the current policy, the proposed methods can learn a robust policy with respect to the environment
858   dynamics and can obtain probably approximately correct (PAC) performance guarantee.
859   Hybrid RL. In Nair et al. (2020), the authors provide a simple framework which can first leverage
860   large offline dataset for pre-training and then quickly perform online fine-tuning of RL policies. In
861   the proposed algorithm, sample-efficient dynamic programming method is used for policy evaluation
862   and a maximum likelihood estimation problem is further designed for solving the optimal policy. In
863   Garg et al. (2022), the authors develop a new framework to solve maximum entropy reinforcement
      learning, which directly estimates the optimal Bellman operator without replying on explicit access


                                                         16
      Under review as a conference paper at ICLR 2025


864
      to the underlying policy. The proposed framework can be used to develop simple but effective
865   reinforcement learning algorithms, which have the flexibility to well in both fully offline or hybrid RL
866   settings. In Ball et al. (2023), the authors first revisit the design choices in existing off-policy methods.
867   Moreover, with a set of minimal but important changes to the existing off-policy RL algorithms, the
868   authors further show that the existing off-policy RL algorithms can achieve reliable performance by
869   leveraging offline data when learning online.
